<p>Each piece of candy in a store costs a whole number of cents. Casper has exactly enough money to buy either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> pieces of red candy, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> pieces of green candy, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pieces of blue candy, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> pieces of purple candy. A piece of purple candy costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> cents. What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/1/0418597254d4d10ac7ec4376edd558f2f9f607f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 18 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 21 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 24\qquad \textbf{(D) } 25 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 28$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If he has enough money to buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> pieces of red candy, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> pieces of green candy, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pieces of blue candy, then the smallest amount of money he could have is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/e/c2e3ab5fadb35345365aac9ef75bac1196d4eb53.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}{(12,14,15)} = 420$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="18" /> cents. Since a piece of purple candy costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> cents, the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7c6c20de8f845bb1a51316c9cf0561d15d15816.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{420}{20} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }  21}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="120" height="37" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We simply need to find a value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/3/f23d6089b918bbc73770d2268d5d0ba5c8f71554.png" class="latex" alt="$20n$" width="28" height="12" /> that is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/1/a8161760db62f0f07e4792d70ebc390aaec54850.png" class="latex" alt="$20 \cdot 18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, but not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a1db789c06c2b52b70874900920054cae79b3c.png" class="latex" alt="$20 \cdot 21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="13" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, meaning that we have exact change (in this case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfb2fec2f1cde0059d8eb9c11bcb2bd63142401b.png" class="latex" alt="$420$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> cents) to buy each type of candy, so the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/9/649ec527ecdfeab96f4fba3e02e7db4f47321849.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }  21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can notice that the number of purple candy times <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> has to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, because of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> green candies, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, because of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> red candies. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f8641497d2b041134c1eaa48b2c4b5e81fea7f5.png" class="latex" alt="$7\cdot3=21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />, so the answer has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/9/649ec527ecdfeab96f4fba3e02e7db4f47321849.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }  21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
