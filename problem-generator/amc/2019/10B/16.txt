<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Without loss of generality, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ae177bee89e4c1255d4e88a7934a44ee797c07.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = CD = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="115" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/f/0df637c7e5d21045f84cb4a1b48b84e35ee192c5.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = EB = 3$" width="116" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/6/f369266e464a676c25b9b02f73b874edb27e9ffb.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A = \alpha$" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/2/442eca2e1a78ef29fe406750a91560e2a8f0b435.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B = \beta = 90^{\circ} - \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="146" height="16" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7a55113a84ee4c04eb66f66d08310c32533ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/4/8c45c55274427f284efb69f46e4c5917a5ae7f3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEB$" width="60" height="13" /> are isosceles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/3/d432bcab13b0aac869092e0b341e8ddeb4186994.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC = \alpha$" width="91" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/a/dfae8079a8d7f39bed761e08c17253e357fca530.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDE = \beta$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/8/4183a8199a3fe0113fac1eb25d0ff8674fbef172.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CDE = 180^{\circ} - \alpha - \beta = 90^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="232" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b984c55719cc5f992ee62656d920ac15c78d9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDE$" width="60" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/f/ebf7200c227276a7507b17341f46fc3d5a0bbd16.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = 5$" width="61" height="12" />.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/4/94432b32b7de2903b65d75f243d7124e5cfd39eb.png" class="latex" alt="$CB = 5+3 = 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="126" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/7/45755a53eaf9419774614cdba9dfbe8927b27bca.png" class="latex" alt="$1-2-\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="18" /> triangle.
</p><p>In isosceles triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7a55113a84ee4c04eb66f66d08310c32533ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/4/8c45c55274427f284efb69f46e4c5917a5ae7f3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEB$" width="60" height="13" />, drop altitudes from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />; denote the feet of these altitudes by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/e/e9e17d85b5f5e7a48245e2662246a88ed0d253b4.png" class="latex" alt="$P_C$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/1/711d39585594f410327fda0915f4ce6cad872b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$P_E$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> respectively. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/995c16d9e5de5fe1980e6007ec30ea48c8dc454d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACP_C \sim \triangle ABC$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="149" height="16" /> by AAA similarity, so we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/b/a8bdd710a34edf2e21a165707b0fd884ddba1421.png" class="latex" alt="$AP_C = P_CD = \frac{4}{\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="150" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/5/7155e079877da0f16f9a654f95072e83b2846cf2.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = 2 \times \frac{4}{\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="111" height="41" />. Similarly we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/2/a221b8b5ac16eca9593ba83ded71edd2ca812644.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = 2 \times \frac{6}{\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="112" height="42" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/3/9d32cdc358307802be0bfbf2a3cf9cbdba92e3a5.png" class="latex" alt="$AD:DB = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 2:3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="179" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
Alternatively, once finding the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> one could use the Pythagorean Theorem to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and consequently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" />, and then compute the ratio.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/8/6481d8c7286cae111d77b62c95d3c9863eb8b50d.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=CD=4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="125" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80d1573aae5cc87bf54a9a25bcf848a261fbcf10.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=EB=3x$" width="126" height="12" />. (For this solution, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is to the right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />). Also let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b8919729d9fdfe021f1649a9eb1a40baa6ffad07.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A = t^{\circ}$" width="63" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/5/585789f9a8291ebaeeb7697e5e279aac86675a42.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACD = \left(180-2t\right)^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="19" />, which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/8/2a8740afe3ffe62d4b5a89fbf1e88c5dd2ef6ea3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DCB = \left(2t - 90\right)^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="158" height="19" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/2/ba2c5ba9c78f49c3730b7a7dc97237ec5ed534e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B = \left(90-t\right)^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="19" />, which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/087720e86fc05ee70599980886cf9e3f5c454779.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BED = 2t^{\circ}$" width="103" height="13" />. This further implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/6/5260e19c04988a7ef3f3805cb76b762293f14a57.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DEC = \left(180 - 2t\right)^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Now we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/6/9563fa4d6cf22371144b97006c967292a04b5529.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CDE = 180^{\circ} - \angle ECD - \angle DEC = 180^{\circ} - 2t^{\circ} + 90^{\circ} - 180^{\circ} + 2t^{\circ} = 90^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="578" height="14" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b984c55719cc5f992ee62656d920ac15c78d9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDE$" width="60" height="13" /> is a right triangle, with side lengths of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" /> (by the Pythagorean Theorem, or simply the Pythagorean triple <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />). Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/7/ab7625157b4820fa276a4bc4e6c7981f5c06e312.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> (by definition), <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee5d7dfc7b44fa3b9dc4e66daba4023a9ef019dd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=5x+3x = 8x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="157" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc90ca77fea2abdc02123687dcc4e020ef7bcf4e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=4\sqrt{5}x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="96" height="18" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/343b9c3d65bbd9726f65a0358b4ae68865a836f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{\left(2t^{\circ}\right)} = 2 \cos^{2}{t^{\circ}} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="19" /> (by the double angle formula), giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/1/7e1635f2fdc2ff296098aac282221537ba63b2d4.png" class="latex" alt="$2\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\right)^2 - 1 = -\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="155" height="46" />.
</p><p>By the Law of Cosines in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/0/f007680c47a068a3aef3f4c57e9484da78281a63.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BED$" width="60" height="13" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/d/c9d2c7dd2dfed34fd7540b7a28f7f4735824fccd.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = d$" width="64" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ebbf71bcd07aabf481f5815f049be6dbdb6a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}&amp;d^2 = (3x)^2+(3x)^2-2\cdot\frac{-3}{5}(3x)(3x) \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;d^2 = 18x^2 + \frac{54x^2}{5} = \frac{144x^2}{5} \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;d = \frac{12x}{\sqrt{5}}\end{split}\]" width="305" height="130" /> Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/3/3c3f6418894e78bc008dba563ed425d563350522.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = AB - BD = 4x\sqrt{5} - \frac{12x}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{8x}{\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="308" height="42" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/d/8dd4a11881a128607df5e844f2a97d898130b456.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\left(\frac{8x}{\sqrt{5}}\right)}{\left(\frac{12x}{\sqrt{5}}\right)} = \frac{8}{12} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }2:3}$" style="vertical-align: -30px" width="201" height="69" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/4815797dac93e88d573872883cf189cb1b345711.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=CD=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="115" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59f2b2844a277868ff62df840e00801b29f16c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=EB=3$" width="116" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d30a587c22234a1e11eeaca03ed110e7de4cad6.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CDE = 180^{\circ} - \angle ADC - \angle BDE = 180^{\circ} - \angle DAC - \angle DBE = 90^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="540" height="13" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44bb493d6663354613438a96e312f7c8fcae3611.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{DF}{AC} = \frac{BD}{AB} = \frac35$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="128" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a734cf9a56f3bcb7607275bbc8dd7451d3d634ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac35$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/5/d655213558d61b1f944b62d4485ccf86d2073f37.png" class="latex" alt="$AD:DB$" width="74" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b69c84a65ba2fe6274bbeb39f53f53d7f5120d67.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 2:3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="80" height="28" />.
