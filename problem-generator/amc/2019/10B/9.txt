<p>The function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/9/289baf6aac9fddce693a26364041d4d5b550c685.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) = \lfloor|x|\rfloor - |\lfloor x \rfloor|\]" width="152" height="19" />for all real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af185d2067f3958364022a1d2cc3b49403514b39.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor r \rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="22" height="19" /> denotes the greatest integer less than or equal to the real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. What is the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/b/d0b4bd61c6d940d2253580d57a5b9cecbb38c166.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \{-1, 0\} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \text{The set of nonpositive integers} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \{-1, 0, 1\} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \{0\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="624" height="18" /> <br /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/3/a338f3cc3b8394542e7ae3badfec750747712f01.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E) } \text{The set of nonnegative integers}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="261" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>There are four cases we need to consider here.
</p><p><b>Case 1</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a positive integer. Without loss of generality, assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/2/512d457997cfdf02335cf27ee27cccd66372ebea.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1) = 1 - 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Case 2</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a positive fraction. Without loss of generality, assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a14649b556d4c316856b101be1b84a92084b195b.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/0/2602e51b2350647f2760a13efc7c0820c18e7c64.png" class="latex" alt="$f\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = 0 - 0 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="150" height="43" />.
</p><p><b>Case 3</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a negative integer. Without loss of generality, assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86c2e8c17cff668023b2f29f2e95e4a0fafa6271.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/4/93410fd0831d0aaa3621ce4dba7dd395842c05dc.png" class="latex" alt="$f(-1) = 1 - 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Case 4</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a negative fraction. Without loss of generality, assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/2/9a2ba57c2738a318a9a95c1a2a53691ee1581875.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/6/8a61ee8bacb2ef8bec62382a9ef5c45a6107e42a.png" class="latex" alt="$f\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = 0 - 1 = -1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="178" height="43" />.
</p><p>Thus the range of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/9/ff9faa304ddf5f32c93b1a750046a3f0e238472e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } \{-1, 0\}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="106" height="29" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>It is easily verified that when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is zero. We therefore need only to consider the case when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is not an integer.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/c/a1c3e8b7088ecd8405ccc717e56cc5ce9a444765.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor x\rfloor \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="59" height="19" />, so 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/4/ae40b05e3befd3c7e209d489bc5a47d49cd83f23.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}f(x)&amp;=\lfloor|x|\rfloor-|\lfloor x\rfloor| \\ &amp;=\lfloor x\rfloor-\lfloor x\rfloor \\ &amp;=0\end{split}\]" width="152" height="64" />
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is negative, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/f/c9f5d276f9ed06dd1a7aca19cb69243503cb2d77.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-a-b$" width="88" height="12" /> be composed of integer part <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and fractional part <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> (both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e100ee688f8f0162a35c7f18712c28592f7c5916.png" class="latex" alt="$\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="15" />):
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/0/9c0097ae9154a8bbf4dc0af789e1dc87a94a906b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}f(x)&amp;=\lfloor|-a-b|\rfloor-|\lfloor -a-b\rfloor| \\ &amp;=\lfloor a+b\rfloor-|-a-1| \\ &amp;=a-(a+1)=-1\end{split}\]" width="247" height="68" />
</p><p>Thus, the range of x is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/9/ff9faa304ddf5f32c93b1a750046a3f0e238472e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } \{-1, 0\}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="106" height="29" />.
</p><p><i>Note</i>: One could solve the case of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> as a negative non-integer in this way:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/8/368c7126c1387fa681036ae041a8e246023f78e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}f(x)&amp;=\lfloor|x|\rfloor-|\lfloor x\rfloor| \\ &amp;=\lfloor -x\rfloor-|-\lfloor -x\rfloor-1| \\ &amp;=\lfloor -x\rfloor-(\lfloor -x\rfloor+1) = -1\end{split}\]" width="254" height="69" />
</p>
