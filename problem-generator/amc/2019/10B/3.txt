<p>In a high school with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" /> students, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/e/f4e4e5c6d549ce47918b94180cad5c1644d9ea88.png" class="latex" alt="$40\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the seniors play a musical instrument, while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the non-seniors do not play a musical instrument. In all, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/4282af23ed227721fc5c07c3a27f24991cc4e04a.png" class="latex" alt="$46.8\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> of the students do not play a musical instrument. How many non-seniors play a musical instrument?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/c/2dce93191c3cfc083d8412854e37c09ed639cf44.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 66 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 154 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 186 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 220 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 266$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="434" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/7/fd7edd3842c40dbc1e54e522917a6e07b43c92e2.png" class="latex" alt="$60\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of seniors do not play a musical instrument. If we denote <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> as the number of seniors, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/b/39beb27287fd9606cb72594254ed23d8f84d808a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3}{5}x + \frac{3}{10}\cdot(500-x) = \frac{468}{1000}\cdot500\]" width="257" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/b/3bbf60a030fd69addddedd21963193e62f2f4e54.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3}{5}x + 150 - \frac{3}{10}x = 234\]" width="177" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/0/ae0b9942f3104e50463964898645e3071ad35d75.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3}{10}x = 84\]" width="72" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/1/e917d53d7f0e260365f5b83bbee0d622ee11793d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 84\cdot\frac{10}{3} = 280\]" width="138" height="37" />
</p><p>Thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47ef014290c26c25db4e335747340b12dbc08006.png" class="latex" alt="$500-x = 220$" width="110" height="12" /> non-seniors. Since 70% of the non-seniors play a musical instrument, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04cef0977ce5174289db5199dd3009f63e6ce5dc.png" class="latex" alt="$220 \cdot \frac{7}{10} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 154}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="160" height="37" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of seniors, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the number of non-seniors. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7f0504b3bfa775132523a004e290ee0d48efc5e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3}{5}x + \frac{3}{10}y = \frac{468}{1000}\cdot500 = 234\]" width="231" height="38" />
</p><p>Multiplying both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> gives us
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/d/eed3fa790604393aa68809319e61bca0eba6aee2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6x + 3y = 2340\]" width="119" height="16" />
</p><p>Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/a/e7aa5b9336ea438e5c68fa1fd1d1885c89074e7e.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 500$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" /> because there are 500 students in total.
</p><p>Solving these system of equations give us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/c/bbcb8f6a3a3c3257b4bbcf9cb4c5a5d276de063a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 280$" width="61" height="12" />,   <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/19247c32847819879cb0039f1afce2c7465a8d3b.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 220$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282fdef75f579c92565cd7e0734d83ccfd38bfdc.png" class="latex" alt="$70\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the non-seniors play a musical instrument, the answer is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282fdef75f579c92565cd7e0734d83ccfd38bfdc.png" class="latex" alt="$70\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/6/2c6f941fab390e81243e1cd2b40c6178f03ab3f1.png" class="latex" alt="$220$" width="26" height="12" />, which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/c/eec26dec7f825f60497daaca444608ac32f9099e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 154}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p>
