<p>Henry decides one morning to do a workout, and he walks <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8646894f09910d68bdc1aabc0d22bb3e643194.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the way from his home to his gym. The gym is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> kilometers away from Henry's home. At that point, he changes his mind and walks <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8646894f09910d68bdc1aabc0d22bb3e643194.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the way from where he is back toward home. When he reaches that point, he changes his mind again and walks <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8646894f09910d68bdc1aabc0d22bb3e643194.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the distance from there back toward the gym. If Henry keeps changing his mind when he has walked <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8646894f09910d68bdc1aabc0d22bb3e643194.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the distance toward either the gym or home from the point where he last changed his mind, he will get very close to walking back and forth between a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> kilometers from home and a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> kilometers from home. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/e/90e6514b5bc09c17cb37f1b9e5b1fe956e9680cf.png" class="latex" alt="$|A-B|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e589ac1503a857f8573ddf097da9a0f0f79fb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{2}{3} \qquad \textbf{(B) } 1 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 1 \frac{1}{5} \qquad \textbf{(D) } 1 \frac{1}{4} \qquad \textbf{(E) } 1 \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="398" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the two points that Henry walks in between be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> being closer to home. As given in the problem statement, the distances of the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> from his home are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> respectively. By symmetry, the distance of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> from the gym is the same as the distance from home to point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e89850f4ccec707cff60c0f8eff20e13b2550bf.png" class="latex" alt="$A = 2 - B$" width="83" height="13" />. 
</p><p>In addition, when he walks from point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> to home, he walks <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> of the distance, ending at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. Therefore, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/3/dc38b36ce42fa08888210e4360755619335b1bfa.png" class="latex" alt="$B - A = \frac{3}{4}B$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="102" height="38" />. 
</p><p>By substituting, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/0/550fe95fcd6c45020fdd7486c50b9346a45a76ca.png" class="latex" alt="$B - (2-B) = \frac{3}{4}\cdot B$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="161" height="38" /> and we solve to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/c/91cd9cb178c5a47121b5a887d23ff986b6cc7500.png" class="latex" alt="$B=\dfrac{8}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/a/9aaa5529ffc7ebe9728563dfda77884d22834a6f.png" class="latex" alt="$A=2-\dfrac{8}{5}=\dfrac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="118" height="37" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/a/85a903c95e2a2199fa20de120d809e90f400f035.png" class="latex" alt="$|A-B|=\left|\dfrac{2}{5}-\dfrac{8}{5} \right|=\frac{6}{5}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 1 \frac{1}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="277" height="49" />.
</p>
