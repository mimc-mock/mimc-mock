<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/6/ab695ddb0448ec0c5073a9f5598368421c86acf0.png" class="latex" alt="$A=(6,13)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/0/2e0b5a1d1a7bfbd5506eb93eee02448726f4def4.png" class="latex" alt="$B=(12,11)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" /> lie on circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" /> in the plane. Suppose that the tangent lines to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> intersect at a point on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/8/0f8c2e028e77068a8869ba18639ca673c66f671b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }\frac{83\pi}{8}\qquad\textbf{(B) }\frac{21\pi}{2}\qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{85\pi}{8}\qquad\textbf{(D) }\frac{43\pi}{4}\qquad\textbf{(E) }\frac{87\pi}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="472" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, observe that the two tangent lines are of identical length. Therefore, supposing that the point of intersection is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb99be0632ae9be9bbeeec99f75e4a71c2595180.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />, the Pythagorean Theorem gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ff7121d71ea2901102ce62fa2a8cbd1945bca20.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(x-6)^2 + 13^2} = \sqrt{(x-12)^2 + 11^2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="291" height="32" />. This simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" />.
</p><p>Further, notice (due to the right angles formed by a radius and its tangent line) that the quadrilateral (a kite) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/3/4c33db19dd18b4c580861bc41e39c697914aafd2.png" class="latex" alt="$AOBX$" width="58" height="13" /> is cyclic. 
</p><p>Therefore, we can apply <a href="/wiki/index.php/Ptolemy%27s_Theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Ptolemy&#39;s Theorem">Ptolemy's Theorem</a> to give:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/c/afcf606dccb526c1a6847c4ff0b224c69d046bd8.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{170}r = d \sqrt{40}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="127" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the radius of the circle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is the distance between the circle's center and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/5/b25d38afb38cb21e729f74fc5a880d03d5368e3c.png" class="latex" alt="$(5, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/9/939305492bbbf9dcd37cdc399912e1da2693e372.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \sqrt{17}r$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Using the Pythagorean Theorem on the right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/a/dba7c6aabf1498eac50bdf863d0bb4f55a5dea17.png" class="latex" alt="$OAX$" width="43" height="13" /> (or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/b/37bb08e2f8129e0b7938e8061b7e0a71d3483d0f.png" class="latex" alt="$OBX$" width="44" height="12" />), we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/7/13797989c444a0856f6cff6326cd19a68a8f8001.png" class="latex" alt="$170 + r^2 = 17r^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/a/24ad0cdfc7df990e5c222f720d44096b156be9ce.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2 = \frac{85}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="61" height="37" />, and thus the area of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7ceab134dfb67b8386f4506e740605c5f3c50b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\frac{85}{8}\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="80" height="48" />.
</p>
