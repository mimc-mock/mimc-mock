<p>Consider the statement, "If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is not prime, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9b02e32cfba0db0f9038a8d077596720f62e91.png" class="latex" alt="$n-2$" width="41" height="12" /> is prime." Which of the following values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a counterexample to this statement?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/1/ca13699e50734f90862a4be804e3bd8cdc9ccc75.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 11 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 15 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 19 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 21 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 27$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since a counterexample must be a value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> which is not prime, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be composite, so we eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c43070a2acf8f4e25d98e48c404d9348a26d40b.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{A}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/0/aa04d7a9bcbf1fe3f2037cbc5e763c4b6d2661d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{C}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="11" height="12" />. Now we subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> from the remaining answer choices, and we see that the only time <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9b02e32cfba0db0f9038a8d077596720f62e91.png" class="latex" alt="$n-2$" width="41" height="12" /> is <b>not</b> prime is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/2/61241a052dbdc7dd87c5b3c3e595d91fbcc7b749.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }27}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="98" height="28" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p><p>minor edit (the inclusion of not) by AlcBoy1729
