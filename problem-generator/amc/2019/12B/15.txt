<p>As shown in the figure, line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> is trisected by points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/9/999d7441d5e2700b8d711d2a01c1aba1ca7045a3.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=BC=CD=2.$" width="172" height="13" /> Three semicircles of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/6/ac603131fa15dbbe8226da13de0c060d3ab83494.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{AEB},\overarc{BFC},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="27" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/5/f6525c28738046d9a3d2a3c859581d43e01071d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{CGD},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="27" /> have their diameters on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/f/43f0fccd896266f9c6a2c01ec87ae77e449d7df7.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="19" /> and are tangent to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927d4689159fbeb80f7b468ca9ae96b48e926312.png" class="latex" alt="$EG$" width="28" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/15702fb3f7b1aeaefff24913baf2a387bdd2d54a.png" class="latex" alt="$E,F,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/195d683ae02c57abf49ebe0889c6f2ea7bcc513e.png" class="latex" alt="$G,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="16" /> respectively. A circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> has its center on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/9/f2920fa2bc40ed68149a854f8b32c09f619b0d70.png" class="latex" alt="$F.$" width="15" height="12" /> The area of the region inside the circle but outside the three semicircles, shaded in the figure, can be expressed in the form 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8bdd0e53bdd2b5b50aea9510be4bd6c45e2b867.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a}{b}\cdot\pi-\sqrt{c}+d,\]" width="117" height="32" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8b8490a47ba3063da2731316f5d65dc5cf731ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are positive integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are relatively prime. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/4/e947add5766b9f26f6dfe73d9425d87664416459.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/9/ac9c68e87cdfb2dde115a063764bd2d48bf835e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(6cm); filldraw(circle((0,0),2), grey); filldraw(arc((0,-1),1,0,180) -- cycle, gray(1.0)); filldraw(arc((-2,-1),1,0,180) -- cycle, gray(1.0)); filldraw(arc((2,-1),1,0,180) -- cycle, gray(1.0)); dot((-3,-1)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-3,-1),S); dot((-2,0)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,(-2,0),NW); dot((-1,-1)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(-1,-1),S); dot((0,0)); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,(0,0),N); dot((1,-1)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(1,-1), S); dot((2,0)); label(&quot;$G$&quot;, (2,0),NE); dot((3,-1)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, (3,-1), S); [/asy]" width="285" height="178" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fba780123ac73ec65eb5e929185eb76a0dea32fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 13 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 14 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 15 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 16\qquad\textbf{(E) } 17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Divide the circle into four parts: the top semicircle by connecting E, F, and G(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />); the bottom sector (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />), whose arc angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/4/9b49fcb2f74ccae5ec0e722a8c65b5b9bbd93720.png" class="latex" alt="$120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> because the large circle's radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and the short length (the radius of the smaller semicircles) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, giving a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb4e8f1484876dd33e74dde765bfff59c9e6939.png" class="latex" alt="$30^{\circ}-60^{\circ}-90^{\circ}$" width="120" height="13" /> triangle; the triangle formed by the radii of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and the chord (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />); and the four parts which are the corners of a circle inscribed in a square (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />). Then the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7cacf2a85ca3bceefbccb74c3b6a84536e9ec0c6.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B - C + D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="124" height="14" /> (in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/a/ceac4f26197426e9ae8760335079b4f39d6ad384.png" class="latex" alt="$B-C$" width="51" height="12" />, we find the area of the bottom shaded region, and in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> we find the area of the shaded region above the semicircles but below the diameter).
</p><p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8ec22e90d2c4598d74b497b4aa2d214c6beac11.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \pi \cdot 2^2 = 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="37" />.
</p><p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/d/0ad2a89446b57c2fcf094b889b3a29fbda9486fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{120^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}} \pi \cdot 2^2 = \frac{4\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />.
</p><p>For the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, the radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and the distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> (the smaller semicircles' radius) to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, creates two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb4e8f1484876dd33e74dde765bfff59c9e6939.png" class="latex" alt="$30^{\circ}-60^{\circ}-90^{\circ}$" width="120" height="13" /> triangles, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />'s area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/3/053b9c1cc912e7a83a6165254dc67cd70bd75845.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1 \cdot \sqrt{3} = \sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="144" height="37" />.
</p><p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/231ba2e20348795690b206a5c9945291e8feffe5.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 1-\frac{1}{4}\pi \cdot 2^2=4-\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="173" height="37" />.
</p><p>Hence, finding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11fc0342ea4c49e7a2b7bc124cdc34caf097420d.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B-C+D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="124" height="14" />, the desired area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/a/d8ad6eaba7cee31b71f28736df782bc33fcc1157.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7\pi}{3}-\sqrt{3}+4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a897fbc1328b915d5defc3bac5f047d6d386a77a.png" class="latex" alt="$7+3+3+4=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 17}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="191" height="28" />.
</p>
