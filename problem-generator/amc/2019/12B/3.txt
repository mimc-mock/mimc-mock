<p>Which of the following rigid transformations (isometries) maps the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> onto the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/6/a66bcf1b702aa4823d591a3a43757c06b3e45ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{A&#39;B&#39;}$" width="38" height="15" /> so that the image of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/3/773607b0656ef0f780a0ebd88051aa5346049af1.png" class="latex" alt="$A(-2, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15f38507562904aa8801848e27e9c18a2d895c05.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;(2, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and the image of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9a121fda9963da659463fed9e605c8f92ac7db7.png" class="latex" alt="$B(-1, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/0/ba08bb89624855f4e129808bdcae281cdcd14f70.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;(1, -4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/7/e8738bbc91069d2db6a97b6ab9b1e13052ac4606.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> reflection in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/6/506e909041d36bd739c8c959d341fcfbba3e9ad7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> counterclockwise rotation around the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab3a9c13a5c376b1660f46d00b9007dbae240cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> translation by 3 units to the right and 5 units down
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/3787f1b7ce783a57c3a0d02ca4d485ccc950ff0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> reflection in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/d/81d05263c2e18459105296c8e3e06c9fddddb735.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> clockwise rotation about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We can simply graph the points, or use coordinate geometry, to realize that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" /> are, respectively, obtained by rotating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> about the origin. Hence the rotation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> about the origin maps the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> to the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/6/a66bcf1b702aa4823d591a3a43757c06b3e45ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{A&#39;B&#39;}$" width="38" height="15" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/949f672c5f79b83098c9e42cdbdb5fbe99e78899.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(\text{E})}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="29" />.
</p><p>~Dodgers66
</p>
