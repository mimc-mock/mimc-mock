<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/7/73736fea7e76f0d5d8ff01f1c459c0b5f50ed572.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A_0B_0C_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="16" /> be a triangle whose angle measures are exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/0/7e068fef02d41172c115f8c9d5eeb7d413cfd9bf.png" class="latex" alt="$59.999^\circ$" width="56" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/9697ce1d61360c7061c6305e5ca8a494017c0280.png" class="latex" alt="$60.001^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" />. For each positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/c/1eccfbd65334353f671fb71c27b9b9f2c689b0bf.png" class="latex" alt="$A_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> to be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/b/bcb9b7352942e18a3df49fadca0f6a49f3508209.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="37" height="15" /> to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35b6bc1eb8298a1b4aabdf835f228cfed94cc758.png" class="latex" alt="$B_{n-1}C_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" />. Likewise, define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/e/9be2b1de79fff66db67de3b18303bdd2912b7ae6.png" class="latex" alt="$B_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="15" /> to be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/8/e48bfec2c31c2b18baee6d1e13e5a9fd2c1e3c82.png" class="latex" alt="$B_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="38" height="15" /> to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/2/2d2224f11d0bf72d2a1c9ccaecf3c308e5de225e.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{n-1}C_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e173907ca992482385a50c6f62ede920362b1bf.png" class="latex" alt="$C_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> to be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/e/7eeb85f27b76e421673cf261935886286041d551.png" class="latex" alt="$C_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="37" height="15" /> to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/5/075835162ba77e39ed7a0863e21a5f2b454f7668.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{n-1}B_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" />. What is the least positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7a226d7c599ac27fbe16cb7ab5fe5d45a9a2e64.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A_nB_nC_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="82" height="16" /> is obtuse?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/c/99cad74f3b0eddd8482105f387097fb2f1ef1f82.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 10 \qquad \textbf{(B) }11 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 13\qquad \textbf{(D) } 14 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For all nonnegative integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/8/7883dd7fcce6cfcaf5024261d111eedb332baee2.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C_nA_nB_n=x_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="123" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4fe087f7d2a1cb6397f7993a7afc5b716f00c7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A_nB_nC_n=y_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="121" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf49a56504862e9f4df3fb4dc47effae52a5276.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B_nC_nA_n=z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="121" height="15" />.
</p><p>Note that quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/d/79dc9dac276fc83f492261a8ddd61d8156a7c2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$A_0B_0A_1B_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" /> is cyclic since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/e/d1e1c2e3d9299ca2b41e211edeb8220be0d4e764.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A_0A_1B_0=\angle A_0B_1B_0=90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="225" height="15" />; thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e818749d312127cb3ed980e0dd381b9e82148c73.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A_0A_1B_1=\angle A_0B_0B_1=90^\circ-x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="265" height="15" />. By a similar argument, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9d2b1a65c3840e91d8248dc2413f5d53b65ccbd.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A_0A_1C_1=\angle A_0C_0C_1=90^\circ-x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="263" height="15" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8e3ab6c9506606dde187b96455a2f4c1c665c77.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1=\angle A_0A_1B_1+\angle A_0A_1C_1=180^\circ-2x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="323" height="15" />. By a similar argument, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c472755e0e0ed765f1e9a18b6f471756296395ac.png" class="latex" alt="$y_1=180^\circ-2y_0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="121" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/d/85d34910afd0e6c6b2740b3aa1cb01ee358d1ab3.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1=180^\circ-2z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="121" height="15" />.
</p><p>Therefore, for any positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/3/0635d997e68da7460785dd3dc63b19f638b9afbf.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n=180^\circ-2x_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="144" height="15" /> (identical recurrence relations can be derived for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/2/3b23a00ac79117133abc0c4e699668642494fc66.png" class="latex" alt="$y_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6046b5fea971a2f95348ba9ece8e46d4b947c57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />). To find an explicit form for this recurrence, we guess that the constant term is related exponentially to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> (and the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17f1249ad95b7682b8316ad21de8ce4ee9fdcf93.png" class="latex" alt="$x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/f/0bfcc697d0f9180f5bb0f43fdb531cd143653884.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2)^n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" />). Hence, we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/5/8150083406de290a1d78f430f42dcb7afebcd2ef.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n=pq^n+r+(-2)^nx_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="186" height="18" />. We will solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> by iterating the recurrence to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3dc10b0fe29ecb493017a813a6169eea5b6996.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1=180^\circ-2x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="124" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a50779e93c0ebb9d1a2001fa8f93d82755905ff.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2=4x_0-180^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="124" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/e/91e94a314127f368027fb269d12a49ae3e5e285b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3=540-8x_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="117" height="15" />. Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/e/e5e67114e90e4c0f12e82962e548d38df58a5b4d.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1,2,3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="78" height="16" /> respectively, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2bdb4275e05e405e34bf5f0eff0a44d4b3a8777.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align} pq+r&amp;=180 \\ pq^2+r&amp;=-180 \\ pq^3+r&amp;=540 \end{align}" width="300" height="72" />
</p><p>Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b88ea3916e8089c7edbccc3ff91e58e01a6e2682.png" class="latex" alt="$(3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/16449910a66d008c1574a2f8db35a460f7f735dd.png" class="latex" alt="$pq(q^2-1)=360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="19" />, and subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638cbe5f0fb4eaf47cd3e0f91006008872552c3e.png" class="latex" alt="$pq(q-1)=-360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="138" height="18" />. Dividing these two equations gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67ae48d1c6b7c9806e4c342db38dca38f5b6ded.png" class="latex" alt="$q+1=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="86" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/0/1b00c96499972dcece6a927ae01957dc79c0be1b.png" class="latex" alt="$q=-2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="15" />. Substituting back, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27a54ce58b37911ca6c6f6ea1855bfb70e885110.png" class="latex" alt="$p=-60$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05faf604d21738855665fadf6bd0ba36fc0939a2.png" class="latex" alt="$r=60$" width="50" height="12" />.
</p><p>We will now prove that for all positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e565803ad4ec05b3b4b3fd9ab4ed883b3e07273e.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n=-60(-2)^n+60+(-2)^nx_0=(-2)^n(x_0-60)+60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="432" height="18" /> via induction. Clearly the base case of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> holds, so it is left to prove that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2d6a02af7bd3c00cc1fed6c9521cb7b86790245.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{n+1}=(-2)^{n+1}(x_0-60)+60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="236" height="19" /> assuming our inductive hypothesis holds for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Using the recurrence relation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/e/e2e5c7e9263c0f62b1c0e7664c66bfd87aad66c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} x_{n+1}&amp;=180-2x_n \\ &amp;=180-2((-2)^n(x_0-60)+60) \\ &amp;=(-2)^{n+1}(x_0-60)+60 \end{align*}" width="290" height="69" />
</p><p>Our induction is complete, so for all positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/b/8abfe4e3c6117345153bbdadaf5f65519bc77f4a.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n=(-2)^n(x_0-60)+60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="18" />. Identical equalities hold for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/2/3b23a00ac79117133abc0c4e699668642494fc66.png" class="latex" alt="$y_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6046b5fea971a2f95348ba9ece8e46d4b947c57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />.
</p><p>The problem asks for the smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/195db3e04ee69d85a0aeaedbb1cf9afa97ff4546.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/2/3b23a00ac79117133abc0c4e699668642494fc66.png" class="latex" alt="$y_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="11" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6046b5fea971a2f95348ba9ece8e46d4b947c57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />. WLOG, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/c/3bc35e5d49bc454baa256a70810842b6bb42d569.png" class="latex" alt="$x_0=60^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/92631d476a9f407785608d509ce30a935d48a23a.png" class="latex" alt="$y_0=59.999^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fa60f81176e854955f0f16829bdf4844abd27.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=60.001^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="96" height="15" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39c60638bfbe5458e4babd89e809c2168331e144.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n=60^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="67" height="15" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/0/5a0f58fc2da0bb82a0ab5349edfd6b8d2c673a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$y_n=-(-2)^n(0.001)+60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a53e09c61106bbfad2ec381aaf4ff2304a32a0f.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n=(-2)^n(0.001)+60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="183" height="18" />. Solving for the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> in each sequence, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/c/21cee94ee92654e843d9cf2f18541e37eab00b47.png" class="latex" alt="$n=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/5/e75c5e6c58a350d5105ae6ef6dab232b7a3a3cb2.png" class="latex" alt="$y_n&gt;90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" />. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/d/64d90d3891dde649e46ff7a1a7f89d06c22222e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We start from Solution 1 until we reach the recurrence relation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6730a0ef1e461b5f3d315b6cabf3bb1a6dd2f18e.png" class="latex" alt="$x_n = 180 - 2x_{n - 1}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="142" height="15" /> Iterate this again, to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/7/0a729a7081105e4bbad2c50274819dd773d5eedc.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{n - 1} = 180 - 2x_{n - 2}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="159" height="15" /> Subtract the two, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/6/eb6bb853498d48e60aa264ea859e2722b4aa955b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{n} = -x_{n - 1} + 2x_{n - 2}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="165" height="14" /> This recurrence has characteristic equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/4/a847706e2b0a99823d5b7b5761223e4c3250589f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + x - 2 = 0 = (x + 2)(x - 1) = 0 \iff x = -2, 1.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="416" height="19" /> Now, write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/0/7502d7becf8dd6947c4ae1608611c816318cb251.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_n = p + q \cdot (-2)^n.\]" width="146" height="18" /> We obtain similar recursions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/8/9488a1599539128afb5aeff0927de2be6410ebd3.png" class="latex" alt="$y, z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="11" /> that can be easily solved by getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/e/d0eebd00c02420b72303b8c5f521965fe6456258.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1, y_1, z_1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="11" /> from the original recursive formula and then using those two values to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/f/9cf560f497c604f9cdc8256b4352f8d49b0a43f4.png" class="latex" alt="$q.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="11" /> Then proceed with the last paragraph of Solution 1.
</p>
