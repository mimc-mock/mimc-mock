<p>What is the greatest number of consecutive integers whose sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a5abde77ec0f8749ba4cbcaa1fc0d396e9dc111.png" class="latex" alt="$45?$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/3137a4844774edcb19aa61861b797e0b803f0213.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 9 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 25 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 45 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 90 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 120$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We might at first think that the answer would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9bf3ac6720d52c3a43c6aeac056747ed5cc2c55.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3 \dots +n = 45$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="171" height="14" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/1/1819f547a53f667e1a44a49ba2bec08c28d7f222.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 9$" width="43" height="12" />. But note that the problem says that they can be integers, not necessarily positive. Observe also that every term in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/1/721891109e94d1e041bb5b6729da784d0a19ff30.png" class="latex" alt="$-44, -43, \cdots, 44, 45$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="160" height="16" /> cancels out except <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.  Thus, the answer is, intuitively, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/023b49e0ef7624a116be68cdd57c45fa2c163220.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 90 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" /> integers.
</p><p>Though impractical, a proof of maximality can proceed as follows:  Let the desired sequence of consecutive integers be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/7/957c6c7b52d8586979b72f64453c27714f370ed2.png" class="latex" alt="$a, a+1, \cdots, a+(N-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="194" height="18" />, where there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> terms, and we want to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />.  Then the sum of the terms in this sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286a2a896edcf569d927f28adc7c026d2cd431a6.png" class="latex" alt="$aN + \frac{(N-1)(N)}{2}=45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="187" height="38" />.  Rearranging and factoring, this reduces to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/1/e01af6bab5a202eab7a15695ffe818c92841eba4.png" class="latex" alt="$N(2a+N-1) = 90$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="18" />.  Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> must divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" />, and we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> is an attainable value of the sum, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> must be the maximum.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>To maximize the number of integers, we need to make the average of them as low as possible while still being positive. The average can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/c/bdc960c165b21ba43f7a90caa2893d40409d6944.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> if the middle two numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/1039ad33905e3c09dd5e8c404f56364866d810b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{45}{\frac12}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 90 }$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="112" height="44" />.
</p>
