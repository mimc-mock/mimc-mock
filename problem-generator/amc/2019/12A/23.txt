<p>Define binary operations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/d/c6dda9b4c504bad80fd4308d62fb52fb41f3aefc.png" class="latex" alt="$\diamondsuit$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c6b4ac9e30aa1bbcdf4529130bc58e4b392e83.png" class="latex" alt="$\heartsuit$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="14" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/0/5f0ef0b94787e769e936ea5ec8657b108b356c9e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a \, \diamondsuit \, b = a^{\log_{7}(b)} \qquad \text{and} \qquad a  \, \heartsuit \, b = a^{\frac{1}{\log_{7}(b)}}\]" width="314" height="22" />for all real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> for which these expressions are defined. The sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> is defined recursively by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/8/7f8c79062a37c8b1e1676449576c37ef10c2e97c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 = 3\, \heartsuit\, 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="79" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b2d6d1783ff5064ff088b0b0ff162c3de58efc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_n = (n\, \heartsuit\, (n-1)) \,\diamondsuit\, a_{n-1}\]" width="197" height="18" />for all integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/8/83870118206bd0e612b5c9e3def2ef95460e368b.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="14" />. To the nearest integer, what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/9/889842af297f354db8b10cc53c858d8d0aea541b.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{7}(a_{2019})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/9/8f9a0d17019476ed84e7289d79c3e1fd056757b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 8 \qquad  \textbf{(B) } 9 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 10 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 11 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="380" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By definition, the recursion becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/6/00680eb657de6ff5fb9bf18d56064b3ea2d0dc84.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = \left(n^{\frac1{\log_7(n-1)}}\right)^{\log_7(a_{n-1})}=n^{\frac{\log_7(a_{n-1})}{\log_7(n-1)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="293" height="36" />.  By the change of base formula, this reduces to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/b/2bb4506605b5d3bebdb0b919d509f566d2ce6314.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = n^{\log_{n-1}(a_{n-1})}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="133" height="18" />.  Thus, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/5866e9508c5518f1902599a82a370e488771fdd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_n(a_n) = \log_{n-1}(a_{n-1})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="18" />.  Thus, for each positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/8/d58a6fa40bd832d51c131c0d95505309ba92cffb.png" class="latex" alt="$m \geq 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />, the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/d/30d7dc8d87103b679e58d2a869d8d8d69e83f895.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_m(a_m)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> must be some constant value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.  
</p><p>We now compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />.  It is given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/f/6ffbf19bad97e9af11623d54a5fb33289ff4426d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 = 3\,\heartsuit\,2 = 3^{\frac1{\log_7(2)}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="151" height="21" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/e/53ea955334bc42ed1f1e345a5265606764105cf8.png" class="latex" alt="$k = \log_3(a_3) = \log_3\left(3^{\frac1{\log_7(2)}}\right) = \frac1{\log_7(2)} = \log_2(7)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="384" height="41" />.
</p><p>Now, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5b3b8a5c2c916e0c3058578d4e00dc0e378eb27.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{2019}(a_{2019}) = k = \log_2(7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="213" height="18" />. At this point, we simply switch some bases around. For those who are unfamiliar with logarithms, we can turn the logarithms into fractions which are less intimidating to work with.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/1/7f16acceae54a4ae49c01f99484541a4b645c5be.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\log{a_{2019}}}{\log{2019}} = \frac{\log{7}}{\log{2}}\implies \frac{\log{a_{2019}}}{\log{7}} = \frac{\log{2019}}{\log{2}}\implies \log_7(a_{2019}) =\log_2(2019)$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="574" height="41" />
</p><p>We conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b3e87d91336d09dec999c534055a23d7dae786e.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_7(a_{2019}) = \log_2(2019) \approx \boxed{11}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="241" height="23" />, or choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bf9089f3c0ad5ee9fca030cfb06b487e559902.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Using the recursive definition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2e2185621c09379c60a5f69d803f3ad43ae375.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4 = (4  \, \heartsuit \, 3) \, \diamondsuit\, (3 \, \heartsuit\, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35a68756492eb49e20064709207533a6c52ea71.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4 = (4^{m})^{k}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="20" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/812b01195f194ad56f30c9060657ace0e8422ba5.png" class="latex" alt="$m = \frac{1}{\log_{7}(3)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="96" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174c47c11b4348a9707ee3cf13e0717c44614550.png" class="latex" alt="$k = \log_{7}(3^{\frac{1}{\log_{7}(2)}})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="23" />. Using logarithm rules, we can remove the exponent of the 3 so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/2/fb2a5fd4f2d016fb2f11557eb8b3d2e0f3976258.png" class="latex" alt="$k = \frac{\log_{7}(3)}{\log_{7}(2)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="91" height="43" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/5/035b09aea270251389acb80d567701f1e960a58d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4 = 4^{\frac{1}{\log_{7}(2)}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="88" height="21" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cb1892d60b184b13964af88bca7c454eecca57.png" class="latex" alt="$4  \, \heartsuit \, 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />.
</p><p>We claim that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/5/2d50c9a4c7b97af04b333180521bd6b1abfa1519.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = n  \, \heartsuit \, 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="82" height="16" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/e/49e6a2d706867d6652d7edadccc3fd034130bd98.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />. We can prove this through induction.
</p><p>Clearly, the base case where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c342b01f0cdef0f23d11a2bc9c5aa67f6d83ccb.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 3$" width="43" height="12" /> holds.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/4/e242e545eb91a085a2d08a723589f719835066ac.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = (n\, \heartsuit\, (n-1)) \,\diamondsuit\, ((n-1)  \, \heartsuit \, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="263" height="18" />
</p><p>This can be simplified as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b2505d1ae9e89c68dd4af90f5533e73827d2cd.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = (n^{\log_{n-1}(7)}) \, \diamondsuit \, ((n-1)^{\log_{2}(7)})$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="260" height="23" />.
</p><p>Applying the diamond operation, we can simplify <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/0/540d4168bfed99e9fe315e4f78da20d42d79026c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = n^h$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="61" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00e98e7a3c83ad2d3f785081e37e54695192df18.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \log_{n-1}(7) \cdot \log_{7}(n-1)^{\log_{2}(7)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="20" />. By using logarithm rules to remove the exponent of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/2/90213349661b3aba731681050eb4287bee5c87bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{7}(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="18" /> and after cancelling, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601870dd7c3833e85c49675709305b19208fedea.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \frac{1}{\log_{7}(2)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="91" height="41" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/0/d2041f05524f69c0280c14342945165394903920.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = n^{\frac{1}{\log_{7}(2)}} = n  \, \heartsuit \, 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="158" height="21" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/e/49e6a2d706867d6652d7edadccc3fd034130bd98.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />, completing the induction.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/f/0afc32ea02ac4de277c4a8dfc3138e63363f445a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2019} = 2019^{\log_{2}(7)}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="136" height="18" />. Taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/6/a36a7de667b385402bdd5bcf0d76f56a4cb296ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{2019}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="17" /> of both sides gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5994bdad1c6e6203f781a71d90b8bde5e0bda7.png" class="latex" alt="${\log_{2019}(a_{2019})} = {\log_{2}(7)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />. Then, by changing to base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and after cancellation, we arrive at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac08ff825c63615c8738d17bcd27c5e5a556fad4.png" class="latex" alt="${\log_{7}(a_{2019})} = {\log_{2}(2019)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="185" height="18" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/6/ad6ef8bc8354f707cf4ec040d38c02dbaa9f88c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{11} = 2048$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/8/cf856775e3de1168a9a4f29bc68d3651d0bf6051.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{10} = 1024$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="15" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/b/74b8b314042db8e94dbd16cb864a20d025b868b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 11}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We are given that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/f/25fb65ab4ad3b409bac6e3b9ae16ce5409136343.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_n=(n\, \heartsuit\, (n-1)) \,\diamondsuit\, a_{n-1}\]" width="197" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/b/a2b6222f8bc69f0b6ec15d6847fcbee827dd2013.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_n=(n^{\frac{1}{\log_7(n-1)}})^{\log_7(a_{n-1})}\]" width="187" height="23" />
Since we are asked to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/6/bf66f9077c8e2529a2782ef28d40b4efdb09f3f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_7(a_{2019})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />, we directly apply
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50f010951470df725264c573ad320bc9267efe5d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=\log_7(n^{\frac{1}{\log_7(n-1)}})^{\log_7(a_{n-1})}\]" width="263" height="23" />
Using the property that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bb222cc97d9315c80e08aaff3b5e50c8f832c0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_ab^c=c\log_ab$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/1/f8147b4cdefad97a08a0e78fdeca6b26db83a0dc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7a_{n-1})(\log_7(n^{\frac{1}{\log_7(n-1)}}))\]" width="296" height="23" />
Now using the property that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084334e256fd81a162475b0d7322ec89f3275f2b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{\log_ab}=\log_ba$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="113" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/b/2ab1ec29de0869e19edd81ccd1e508be9b167ab9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7a_{n-1})(\log_7n^{\log_{n-1}7})\]" width="278" height="20" />
Once again applying the first property yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/5/3b5f41980f433c2fbfdb8722094348d1e6a3731b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7a_{n-1})(\log_{n-1}7)(\log_7n)\]" width="304" height="18" />
Rearranging the expression,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd353b9061d11e31436153fa7dd09f3a3bd53df4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_{n-1}7)(\log_7a_{n-1})\]" width="304" height="18" />
</p><p>Now expressing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/d/34d6d56da47389540a7776f5f254d36bf602918f.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_7a_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="17" /> in a similar expression as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/1/161ad02e6d74ff9a01b60593fe9e6cabf573bf89.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_7a_n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="17" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10f92dd00d0b2982e3a0be409f66a0dd784326d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_{n-1}7)(\log_7n-1)(\log_{n-2}7)(\log_7a_{n-2})\]" width="470" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/7/98770fe401b1ec70498b1334053a3ab4f0d4948f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_{n-1}7)(\log_7n-1)(\log_{n-2}7)(\log_7n-2)(\log_{n-3}7)...(\log_74)(\log_37)(\log_7a_3)\]" width="731" height="18" />
</p><p>Because of the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/0/dd0ec6b42a24413d4ca048b2e19bd6201d107751.png" class="latex" alt="$(\log_ab)(\log_ba)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />, we can cancel out the terms to get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea78b345ecb229f43f34cad64623bf21957a7be9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_37)(\log_7a_3)\]" width="267" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5bf354a651caf447be10ac71a4f344e2a6f083a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_37)(\log_7(3^{\frac{1}{\log_72}}))\]" width="304" height="23" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/1/13177f94c21f361f5ee8a1f969e371f1f2b9ae65.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_7n)(\log_37)(\log_27)(\log_73)\]" width="316" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de80b223ac48ca848e1a04058d9b3f58e771f80.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_27)(\log_7n)\]" width="202" height="18" />
</p><p>Using the Chain Rule for Logarithm, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df2777d2b1f2593b5384d116aa3593b164d61114.png" class="latex" alt="$(\log_ab)(\log_bc)=\log_ac$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />, yields
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f0823682192336c8f6722cd7d4405b45c3f0f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_n)=(\log_2n)\]" width="145" height="18" />
Finally, substituting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/0/850a4492adea07362e187c351ad94f3156e57206.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67f84d2dff4c942a5f17eccdc807ea31cc4fe7da.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_{2019})=(\log_22019)\]" width="188" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/4/a44e11e396b66b8e8dfc2ebb5a6a7ebc78ef884e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_7(a_{2019})\approx11\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}}\]" width="163" height="30" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
