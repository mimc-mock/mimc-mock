<p>Circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, both centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, have radii <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, respectively. Equilateral triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />, whose interior lies in the interior of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" /> but in the exterior of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, has vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d7d48553f4d9e7ab418118607ea324cbfddfda.png" class="latex" alt="$\omega$" width="11" height="8" />, and the line containing side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> is tangent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />. Segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51eee107f2b552caca08ea4f3cd1078a32b22ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AO}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/a/aea6d895bbea72f6c2affb547c4b49731f6f09d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{BP}{CP} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> can be written in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/1/fd1b574f8baf6ba4524bd9e9715f7f60eed62211.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{m}{\sqrt{n}} - \dfrac{p}{\sqrt{q}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="78" height="39" /> for positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/d/41dd88ffd5a00b4c32a030d6ebb2e25bd076d456.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{gcd}(m,n) = \text{gcd}(p,q) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/0/740b7804fdc9546a926406b6b1e2b10490f85a06.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n+p+q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="14" />?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/b/b8baa288cc33e3c4093b5c696b57db8a5a9d18a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\phantom{}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="2" height="2" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/6/e2672158d4244028c2b3b7a35575a6703f273e7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 42 \qquad \textbf{(B) }86 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 92 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 114 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 130$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="416" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/7/8374efc0c580bf65c2bd3438f17939e93bfd601a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(20cm); draw(circle((0,0), 20)); label(&quot;$\omega$&quot;, (0,0), 4.05*20*dir(149)*20/21); draw(circle((0,0), 17)); label(&quot;$\gamma$&quot;, (0,0), 4.05*17*dir(149)*20/21); dot((0,0)); label(&quot;$O$&quot;, (0,0), E); pair aa = (-20, 0); dot(aa); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, aa, W); draw((-20,0)--(0,0)); real a = (-20 + (80/sqrt(13) - 34/sqrt(3))*(sqrt(13)/sqrt(12))*(sqrt(3)/2)); real ans = (80/sqrt(13) - 34/sqrt(3)); dot((a,0)); label(&quot;$P$&quot;, (a, 0), dir(290)*0.58); pair s = ((12*a + 0)/13, 0-sqrt(12)*a/13); dot(s); label(&quot;$S$&quot;, s, dir(135)); pair c = (a + 1/4*ans*1/sqrt(13), 0 + 1/4*ans*sqrt(12)/sqrt(13)); dot(c); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, c, dir(110)); pair m = (a - 1/4*ans*1/sqrt(13), 0 - 1/4*ans*sqrt(12)/sqrt(13)); dot(m); label(&quot;$M$&quot;, m, dir(285)); pair b = (a - 3/4*ans*1/sqrt(13), 0 - 3/4*ans*sqrt(12)/sqrt(13)); dot(b); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, b, S); draw(b--s); draw(s--(0,0)); draw(aa--b); draw(aa--c); draw(aa--m); markscalefactor=0.1; draw(rightanglemark(s,m,aa,3.4)); draw(rightanglemark((0,0),s,m,3.4)); [/asy]" width="945" height="925" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the point of tangency between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/f/a8f4d402805185ed8801a24712f8b2a2c0c508ae.png" class="latex" alt="$AM \perp BS$" width="84" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cec5e275057b37f63334b19c85045a32122b7c86.png" class="latex" alt="$OS \perp BS$" width="77" height="12" />. This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/6/b268142ac98f21461bfc9a1e691651bd2da36a9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OAM \cong \angle AOS$" width="137" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/4/294347a3de9a7ba97d50c7b610955e7095b31e02.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AMP \cong \angle OSP$" width="138" height="13" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb0720d99fe0a566012e99f18d2fd84bc50c8cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PMA \sim \triangle PSO$" width="144" height="13" />.
</p><p>If we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be the side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, then it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/1/cc168c1d0589e9c6b9dcbe784244e8170d5bf677.png" class="latex" alt="$AM = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}s$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="93" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/5/b05ed26c236384064b8db6c2593c541c1c4c1e39.png" class="latex" alt="$PM = \frac{s}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="69" height="33" />. This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/6/396b67c512d3c1bfdb46115f4dd48cba84063bac.png" class="latex" alt="$AP = \frac{\sqrt{13}}{4}s$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="97" height="41" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a94a923451a074ad7376351b1fdd0cce095d9ded.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AM}{AP} = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{13}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="97" height="45" />. Furthermore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/2/cb2e254d880bbe2e5e53ad7655bf03a1af3e37dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AM + SO}{AO} = \frac{AM}{AP}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="38" /> (because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb0720d99fe0a566012e99f18d2fd84bc50c8cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PMA \sim \triangle PSO$" width="144" height="13" />) so this gives us the equation
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f0577c95257dbfec03c9739060758943698cd3a0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}s + 17}{20} = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{13}}\]" width="132" height="48" />
to solve for the side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. Thus,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/9/d5951625083116db014037931a6e4186fb924568.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\sqrt{39}}{2}s + 17\sqrt{13} = 40\sqrt{3}\]" width="184" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/3/ad3fd3076309d82e5cd8be1b043b4b772292ca6c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\sqrt{39}}{2}s = 40\sqrt{3} - 17\sqrt{13}\]" width="184" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/c/bacde4156a3a29777f52fc27aa0c822aa79c4b99.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s = \frac{80}{\sqrt{13}} - \frac{34}{\sqrt{3}} = AB\]" width="171" height="42" />
The problem asks for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/2/4a2cf3b4d9d0de2a48fdda28cef0c44201d3955b.png" class="latex" alt="$m + n + p + q = 80 + 13 + 34 + 3 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 130}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="363" height="28" />.
</p>
