Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/e/71e2bad46c1fe9c86d121bc7811d42e2fd20f067.png" class="latex" alt="$a=10^{4m^2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="80" height="20" />; similarly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/6/196a57e4d86ba0ff214ba97e83036d2b48704384.png" class="latex" alt="$b=10^{4n^2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="74" height="20" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since all four terms on the left are positive integers, from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/1/0c1350b64d255e92383b112531f2b450d27f67b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\log{a}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="54" height="22" />, we know that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/e/ade52869f7c1bbdd276e4a62518f23c0b86f4439.png" class="latex" alt="$\log{a}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="36" height="16" /> has to be a perfect square and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> has to be a power of ten. The same applies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> for the same reason. Setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5bd31ff092ef7fffbb5a7a40ce55ba12cfe8c6.png" class="latex" alt="$10^x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="25" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/3/be30b6f4f1cd96f21e994983367203c432766e59.png" class="latex" alt="$10^y$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="25" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are the perfect squares, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/1/b1156f9db9cefeebc98bba3d603ebb1a8034e7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = 10^{x+y}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="84" height="15" />. By listing all the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/Perfect_square">perfect squares</a> up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/0/3109e9835beb0c0d1b81d84e79a1b92dc266b8dd.png" class="latex" alt="$14^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> (as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/6/5962bd17aa962087061b2201d2e0a8026ebd5cea.png" class="latex" alt="$15^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> is larger than the largest possible sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> from answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5bb2aa78132b4b716cfde4a9136c884d94c753.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{E}$" width="11" height="11" />), two of those perfect squares must add up to one of the possible sums of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> given from the answer choices (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1d1611143f5792dc0f61f3b130af88cb790c04.png" class="latex" alt="$52$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, <img <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/1/0c1350b64d255e92383b112531f2b450d27f67b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\log{a}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="54" height="22" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/4/b946c71e31e33f93eccfb922f86ff5e12f706d8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\log{b}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="52" height="22" /> are both integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/8/408d65d8c8ff9e0d9af2ab47861161bdc9d65149.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{m^2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b50c7c4a5d9a8d7a85c0608b337be55f34cccc75.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{n^2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="19" />, respectively for some positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/0/7d006f7ff5d167ff4fc7980e8d804e571f815ade.png" class="latex" alt="$\log \sqrt{a} = \frac{m^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="39" />. By substituting for a and b, the equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/0/ab0312a59da0705ad14523be4acfe74089dd15bd.png" class="latex" alt="$m + n + \frac{m^2}{2} + \frac{n^2}{2} = 100$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="194" height="39" />. After multiplying the equation by 2 and completing the square with respect to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, the equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245a45983627cbbcd7eb71d10731434a520876de.png" class="latex" alt="$(m + 1)^2 + (n + 1)^2 = 202$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="205" height="19" />. Testing squares of positive integers that add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/4/594b4629a09e61341c38216de1aa1fa8f7ee44a7.png" class="latex" alt="$202$" width="26" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/5/da528928c528150d2820e1657171a9870a2e5fbc.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2 + 9^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="16" /> is the only option. Without loss of generality, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/9/5f9c5bf97ea0e3c3046ed67ba3329e47397921dc.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/8/26858c2becbfe024f2a38f506fce66d56f3e0b66.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 8$" width="43" height="12" />. Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/a/57a16f776a64bd9e0516c70edc6d004170c04e55.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 10^{100}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/2/8c21d40a918ef4bc0694be2114f326fa31c6e93b.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 10^{64}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="15" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/4/a34cc136b521800af3f4f031ec6dadfeb860ab05.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 10^{164}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="128" height="31" />.
</p>
