<p>Travis has to babysit the terrible Thompson triplets. Knowing that they love big numbers, Travis devises a counting game for them. First Tadd will say the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, then Todd must say the next two numbers (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />), then Tucker must say the next three numbers  (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />), then Tadd must say the next four numbers (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />), and the process continues to rotate through the three children in order, each saying one more number than the previous child did, until the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/b/50b19142d92b7b4f1a115c4ec91766582e2b8faf.png" class="latex" alt="$10,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" /> is reached. What is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />th number said by Tadd?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4ee27125e94fea70a4999e5ec406affd9eff8fb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5743 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 5885 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 5979 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 6001 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 6011$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Define a <i>round</i> as one complete rotation through each of the three children, and define a <i>turn</i> as the portion when one child says his numbers (similar to how a game is played).
</p><p>We create a table to keep track of what numbers each child says for each round.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/a/20aeebcd8293eb9840e94c00ba3213667097fdb4.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{tabular}{||c c c c||}   \hline  Round &amp; Tadd &amp; Todd &amp; Tucker \\ [0.5ex]   \hline\hline  1 &amp; 1 &amp; 2-3 &amp; 4-6 \\   \hline  2 &amp; 7-10 &amp; 11-15 &amp; 16-21 \\  \hline  3 &amp; 22-28 &amp; 29-36 &amp; 37-45 \\  \hline  4 &amp; 46-55 &amp; 56-66 &amp; 67-78 \\ [1ex]   \hline \end{tabular}$" style="vertical-align: -54px" width="262" height="118" />
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Tadd says <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> number in round 1, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> numbers in round 2, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> numbers in round 3, and in general <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/1/4f155deac9cf1b4fa7ed99ae71d77bc7631c9fb8.png" class="latex" alt="$3n - 2$" width="50" height="12" /> numbers in round n. At the end of round n, the number of numbers Tadd has said so far is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/8/868ae63032b34fbb48b6fdf6c9f747b73ef57e0a.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 4 + 7 + \dots + (3n - 2) = \frac{n(3n-1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="305" height="38" />, by the sum of arithmetic series formula. 
</p><p>We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/8/1b8e0f5a9fdf89e0f4b4a515d83c8c2dd5179650.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{37(110)}{2}=2035$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="38" />, so Tadd says his 2035th number at the end of his turn in round 37. That also means that Tadd says his 2019th number in round 37. At the end of Tadd's turn in round 37, the children have, in total, completed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/3/d43f6e1b353bd2721b1c0c659baa6a0995018329.png" class="latex" alt="$36+36+37=109$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" /> turns. In general, at the end of turn <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, the nth triangular number is said, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a0833a92c440efbf9e7eb11f4a346e48b90fbfc5.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" />. So at the end of turn 109 (or the end of Tadd's turn in round 37), Tadd says the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/3/d43cb2e0975ddcc6dd9d80e1e14f6a226592e5ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{109(110)}{2}=5995$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="132" height="38" />. Recalling that this was the 2035th number said by Tadd, so the 2019th number he said was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/c/46cc3652bac793954a16d19cd96e36cace9f7b95.png" class="latex" alt="$5995-16=5979$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="136" height="13" />.
</p><p>Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/7/e077a870386b95154bc766de3eb416cc4a6922b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }5979}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Firstly, as in Solution 1, we list how many numbers Tadd says, Todd says, and Tucker says in each round.
</p><p>Tadd: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c415b33228715e04c77300fb7d7ae78b3911b75.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 4, 7, 10, 13 \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" />
</p><p>Todd: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/b/0fb05d4e823310708d2f48dfa25af60afc9f7ac1.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 5, 8, 11, 14 \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" />
</p><p>Tucker: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/8/a18e7383baee6f417ddc88c53f64fcf4dbd7d141.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 6, 9, 12, 15 \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" />
</p><p>We can find a general formula for the number of numbers each of the kids say after the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th round. For Tadd, we can either use the arithmetic series sum formula (like in Solution 1) or standard summation results to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/1/2a1696bb46015bdad1b16c8ac886af158070f727.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{i=1}^n 3n-2=-2n+3\sum_{i=1}^n n=-2n+\frac{3n(n+1)}{2}=\frac{3n^2-n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="460" height="48" />.
</p><p>Now, to find the number of rotations Tadd and his siblings go through before Tadd says his <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />th number, we know the inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93e73fe6bd9a07acd643f67be223be5c40a8242d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3n^2-n}{2}&lt;2019$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="124" height="39" /> must be satisfied, and testing numbers gives the maximum integer value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>The next main insight, in order to simplify the computation process, is to notice that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />th number Tadd says is simply the number of numbers Todd and Tucker say plus the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> Tadd says, which will be the answer since Tadd goes first.
</p><p>Carrying out the calculation thus becomes quite simple:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6ac4eef184440af7a5f819346f06f7bd63d42a06.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\sum_{i=1}^{36} 3n+\sum_{i=1}^{36} 3n-1\right)+2019=\left(\sum_{i=1}^{36} 6n-1\right)+2019=(5+11+17...+215)+2019=\frac{36(220)}{2}+2019\]" width="765" height="53" />
</p><p>At this point, we can note that the last digit of the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, which gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/7/e077a870386b95154bc766de3eb416cc4a6922b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }5979}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="28" />. (Completing the calculation will confirm the answer, if you have time.)
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Think of a turn as adding a block or an interval of numbers to each person. Here are the first couple "blocks" that Tadd has (a block will be denoted in interval notation): [<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />], [<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />], [<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" />], [<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abef9fdb0235b2ede2639538b5bd76711e058fa.png" class="latex" alt="$46$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" />]. From simple inspection, we can notice two things.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />) The start points of each interval is increasing arithmetically by  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />) The length of each "block" is increasing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Since each block length is increasing by 3, then we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />+<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />+<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />+..<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/8/ba8f6f2a200e3663ac81579a346a83a75a4765e7.png" class="latex" alt="$3n-2$" width="50" height="12" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45fe2c2c6319e42ebf7d448c562e6b664f4751dd.png" class="latex" alt="$n(3n-1)/2 = 2019$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="157" height="20" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the amount of blocks we have. In a short amount of time, you will see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is not an integer from that equation, but thats perfectly okay since we can just find an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> that gets it close to 2019, then we can work from there. From inspection, you will see that a good n value that does that exact job is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We can then deduce that the start point of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />th block will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/4/ea4ec1f6c3edccf9a8cda324cfb369e1015fd1bb.png" class="latex" alt="$1+(6+15+24..+321) = 5887$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="257" height="20" /> if we refer to fact <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. We can easily find the length of 37th block by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/f/8cf0f0cf09306f84d85b10fda5a92fac3947016e.png" class="latex" alt="$36*3+1 = 109$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="129" height="15" /> if we refer to fact <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Remember that the length increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> for each block. So we then can deduce that our endpoint for the 37th block is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c62b9063cfac9a97a468b0eec624f4e157c52c4.png" class="latex" alt="$5887+109-1 = 5995$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="180" height="15" />. The endpoint of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />th block will be the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/f/21fea9777cdb54e491a901f803dbdd97c18d2a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$2035$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />th number said because we can plug <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/6/2e6a35446a46f2a2b0320375fc6deb546ab9d9f4.png" class="latex" alt="$n(3n-1)/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />, which tells us how many numbers Tadd has said where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of blocks Tadd has. From there we know that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />th number is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />th block, so that means we can take our endpoint and subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/3/923429452d0d515028b44fb95eada080188001f4.png" class="latex" alt="$2035-2019 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="15" /> from the endpoint of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />th block, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/9/e69c433bbae8a343534e6ea88920839c2f3540c7.png" class="latex" alt="$5995$" width="35" height="12" />, so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420a1871746e5a815d52c683dfdf8f4abb2f8abf.png" class="latex" alt="$5995-16 = 5979$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="15" /> or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/7/e077a870386b95154bc766de3eb416cc4a6922b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }5979}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="28" />.
