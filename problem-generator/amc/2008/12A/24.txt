<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">Triangle</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/2/8f2d7232554ee892487761ea219eb5eefbcfd4a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C = 60^{\circ}$" width="75" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/5/c95c180b12878c1f359607e0af47b48fcf4950d0.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />.  Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Midpoint" title="Midpoint">midpoint</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />.  What is the largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/a/54a35df05bc75683a9a61066cb7291c841b1f41a.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan{\angle BAD}$" width="85" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/4/2842015b8edd6b7692bd70b91b9959f04cf58ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4\sqrt{2}-3}\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="467" height="45" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/b/ddb951a2e48a00e182fdf063f5d6ab5622700c10.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]unitsize(12mm); pair C=(0,0), B=(4 * dir(60)), A = (8,0), D=(2 * dir(60)); pair E=(1,0), F=(2,0); draw(C--B--A--C); draw(A--D);draw(D--E);draw(B--F); dot(A);dot(B);dot(C);dot(D);dot(E);dot(F); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,SW); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,N); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,SE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;\(F\)&quot;,F,S); label(&quot;\(60^\circ\)&quot;,C+(.1,.1),ENE); label(&quot;\(2\)&quot;,1*dir(60),NW); label(&quot;\(2\)&quot;,3*dir(60),NW); label(&quot;\(\theta\)&quot;,(7,.4)); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(.5,0),S); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(1.5,0),S); label(&quot;\(x-2\)&quot;,(5,0),S);[/asy]" width="498" height="242" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/e/44e17d8c676d1b564fe0d53d521e22086465df29.png" class="latex" alt="$x = CA$" width="62" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/d/15d9c68fae5f74461c5450b47213f6668dd63031.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\theta = \tan(\angle BAF - \angle DAE)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="235" height="18" />, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/7/99771cca71358e003caf5735afe40ea33eb3fea4.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\angle BAF = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{x-2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="153" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/d/70dcfdbca20209f1bfb4868dff2ae5dcefb6ce7f.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\angle DAE = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{x-1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="154" height="41" />, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/7/0e7e40fad497d77cfc1c1273257c72565ec82619.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\tan\theta = \frac{\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{x-2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{x-1}}{1 + \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{x-2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{x-1}}= \frac{x\sqrt{3}}{x^2-3x+8}\]" width="285" height="54" />
</p><p>With calculus, taking the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Derivative" title="Derivative">derivative</a> and setting equal to zero will give the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/7/567744e2ef4f7e63710dfcaa23fb8f836c352a9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan \theta$" width="38" height="13" />. Otherwise, we can apply <a href="/wiki/index.php/AM-GM" class="mw-redirect" title="AM-GM">AM-GM</a>:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/0/670903a99d3d8effc4d39e818c372527fac93cb5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{x^2 - 3x + 8}{x} = \left(x + \frac{8}{x}\right) -3 &amp;\geq 2\sqrt{x \cdot \frac 8x} - 3 = 4\sqrt{2} - 3\\ \frac{x}{x^2 - 3x + 8} &amp;\leq \frac{1}{4\sqrt{2}-3}\\ \frac{x\sqrt{3}}{x^2 - 3x + 8} = \tan \theta &amp;\leq \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4\sqrt{2}-3}\end{align*}" width="430" height="145" />
</p><p>Thus, the maximum is at  
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/195fe7c81dec521eb1d5e3e6fb76e90da186b62c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4\sqrt{2}-3} \Rightarrow \mathbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="125" height="45" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d82825425db1307377b60edb9fde02896e0b02d7.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" /> is strictly increasing on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a465bcb8f871e3cdcbd1f9d5bc4d377942fc9b83.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, \frac{\pi}{2})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="33" /> (if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/2/4d25f109f2648113e2eba5818b40f72e35b62bff.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAD\ge 90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="105" height="15" />, then it is impossible for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/3/3735b2683d9ec9e8549168e30e092151963880a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C=60^\circ$" width="75" height="13" />), so we want to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/5/cb55dc9c50a80e1d9eb23fe53e45f464c0ac755e.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAD$" width="56" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Consider the circumcircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/5/7e538978649c14e1616fac744e983b5981d0ea8e.png" class="latex" alt="$BAD$" width="43" height="13" /> and let it meet <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> again at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. Any point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is inside this circle, so it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/5/e75d7552727f6a0338960fd87c129fa8872917a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BPD&gt;\angle BAD$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="137" height="13" />. Therefore to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/5/cb55dc9c50a80e1d9eb23fe53e45f464c0ac755e.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAD$" width="56" height="13" />, the circumcircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/5/7e538978649c14e1616fac744e983b5981d0ea8e.png" class="latex" alt="$BAD$" width="43" height="13" /> must be tangent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. By PoP we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/2/5d2c1e12ecd4ebb792328523e9495d3c9766e366.png" class="latex" alt="$CA^2=CD\cdot CB \Rightarrow AC = 2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="246" height="18" />.
</p><p>Now our computations are straightforward:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcff624a0cc256fad91f496abb71bf1423cdbe39.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\tan\angle BAD = \frac{\sin \angle BAD}{\cos \angle BAD} = \frac{\frac{2\sin\angle ABD}{AD}}{\frac{AB^2+AD^2-BD^2}{2AB\cdot AD}}\]" width="332" height="49" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/9/da9e6eee652ee13abafd44c2c7a45fd765377a70.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=\frac{4\sin \angle ABD\cdot AB}{AB^2+AD^2-4} = \frac{4 AC \sin \angle ACB}{AB^2 + AD^2 - 4}\]" width="310" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/d/ffd86fe9e02826f6e7fbc70a053486f19f91b67b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=\frac{4\sqrt{6}}{(4^2+(2\sqrt{2})^2-4\cdot 2\sqrt{2}) + (2^2+(2\sqrt{2})^2 - 2\cdot 2\sqrt{2}) - 4} = \frac{4\sqrt{6}}{32-12\sqrt{2}}\]" width="556" height="47" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/f/38ff92185ce27b5ff61a940cf18d9d32283cd288.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=\boxed{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4\sqrt{2}-3}}\]" width="98" height="57" />
</p>
