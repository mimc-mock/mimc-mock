<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/e/43ec2086760ce93aaca1938b12c93cbf7cf9dc76.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1,a_2,\ldots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="11" /> be a sequence determined by the rule <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b1c2e08f6bbbcf7c5cc64f53365cdbb19c9e1b1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=a_{n-1}/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" /> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/6/896d56965b17443a55b2b5e55f798adf6465380d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="33" height="10" /> is even and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/d/d8df54e3b84063c1be0bfae4f7daf50d48a235c2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=3a_{n-1}+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" /> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/6/896d56965b17443a55b2b5e55f798adf6465380d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="33" height="10" /> is odd. For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/3/b03a47d044f9e0bd9666519d7c79affb9f00f5fb.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 \le 2008$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" /> is it true that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is less than each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e28850813a5321d2a8ae3c0510457a7337975ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/5/555010b284aa17f892666e5567d3e6cc872d17b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 250\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 251\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 501\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 502\qquad\mathrm{(E)} 1004$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="436" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>All positive integers can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b417a127620d5b467fe7a48ad5f7d843e5c390b5.png" class="latex" alt="$4n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/5/bd562ceaa0577b4a7f23a01f24b17de924542bd8.png" class="latex" alt="$4n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/2623d8aec5bfc8565c8d35b6694000c972e9fc8d.png" class="latex" alt="$4n+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5dac6132726ffa3d36b5e91034f7e39fa0af3f9a.png" class="latex" alt="$4n+3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a nonnegative integer. 
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/7/297d1e4970c1fad5999b43fda2dea3d8a0e977a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=4n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="61" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/1/dc148750d521df05deff635604689b7e981faf08.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=\frac{4n}{2}=2n&lt;a_1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="149" height="37" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1fa8ae90f591ece3f22d17fe2e15ff1bdaff7c57.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=4n+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="91" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10df694e869dd0e69f425c71ce29449f3cda9d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=3(4n+1)+1=12n+4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/6/ff6e0527af4e52edda1beea002a993be4ff2e04d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=\frac{12n+4}{2}=6n+2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="180" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d82293a7b38d042c36a6439c6e92d74f788a043.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=\frac{6n+2}{2}=3n+1&lt;a_1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="211" height="37" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/b/00be6351f2bd7c53ecdbd2d3b5cb9137b76df63d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=4n+2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5e892beab68523959e3a7b584692c4ba48a2c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=2n+1&lt;a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="132" height="14" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca75bdabb1a7191ad6cbfa12d8b81aab1e8338ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=4n+3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="15" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/2/eb2b54962bdbc45536994912e6c5592cc0916b42.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=3(4n+3)+1=12n+10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/c/56c12bb84ed140cef6e33fad1691670604ceee71.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=\frac{12n+10}{2}=6n+5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="190" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/a/e1af4535dcc6f42c23cf4938f180b0c7998cf356.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=3(6n+5)+1=18n+16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="18" />.</li></ul>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/2/9d2beb6dbb0390a90d44cfa206cfd23a318c24e7.png" class="latex" alt="$12n+10, 6n+5, 18n+16 &gt; 4n+3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="281" height="16" />, every positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca75bdabb1a7191ad6cbfa12d8b81aab1e8338ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=4n+3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="15" /> will satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/d/aade51fb35432020d17abc780f2d4acaaa884656.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1&lt;a_2,a_3,a_4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="107" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Since one fourth of the positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/3/b03a47d044f9e0bd9666519d7c79affb9f00f5fb.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 \le 2008$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5dac6132726ffa3d36b5e91034f7e39fa0af3f9a.png" class="latex" alt="$4n+3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a nonnegative integer, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e56a2933cf3bff6723564b2494feaf1d92e40e91.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}\cdot 2008 = 502 \Rightarrow D$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="157" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>After checking the first few <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> such as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, we can see that the only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> that satisfy the conditions are odd numbers that when tripled and added 1 to, are double an odd number. For example, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f78dc60f470ddfefd5bc127b3db63b6c7013b778.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" />, we notice the sequence yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, a valid sequence.
</p><p>So we can set up an equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ad6ca913b22d14e662ee1d6af8a5daab244fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$3x + 1 = 2(2k - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="147" height="18" /> where x is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />. Rearranging the equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/3/1233b3f1617155e41730ac341a5897a4a108edb5.png" class="latex" alt="$(3x + 3)/4 = k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="18" />. Experimenting yields that every 4th <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> after 3 creates an integer, and thus satisfies the sequence condition. So the number of valid solutions is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/9/909b80466ee1a71cdf47c7935d8719bc64a8cfc9.png" class="latex" alt="$2008/4 = 502 \Rightarrow D$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="18" />.
</p>
