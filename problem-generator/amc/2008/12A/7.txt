<p>While Steve and LeRoy are fishing 1 mile from shore, their boat springs a leak, and water comes in at a constant rate of 10 gallons per minute. The boat will sink if it takes in more than 30 gallons of water. Steve starts rowing towards the shore at a constant rate of 4 miles per hour while LeRoy bails water out of the boat. What is the slowest rate, in gallons per minute, at which LeRoy can bail if they are to reach the shore without sinking?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/e/5eed124f5796be2140fd910d72f98fa7aee3cdc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 2\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 4\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 6\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 8\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>It will take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of an hour or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes to get to shore.
</p><p>Since only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> gallons of water can enter the boat, only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/8/138cf33f863ee785160df94b65ae2bb94d0c644f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30}{15}=2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="55" height="38" /> net gallons can enter the boat per minute.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> gallons of water enter the boat each minute, LeRoy must bail <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/5/115e11f472361e53f627a66417f96cdfed51a037.png" class="latex" alt="$10-2=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> gallons per minute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/b/e5b957b70c707673c489a77f4bb52c694c727027.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We set up the following equation, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the answer:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/5/3453e8bf923f3289c5890211704e003ddd86a682.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30}{10-x} = 15 \Rightarrow\mathrm{x=8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="168" height="38" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b4066cd24c53cd4053f840d210f974f46e1d913.png" class="latex" alt="$(D)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the slowest rate LeRoy can bail out water. It will take them <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes to get to shore. As stated in solution 1, the number of gallons that enter the boat can be at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> for the boat to stay afloat. Water enters at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> gallons per minute. 
</p><p>Therefore:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/7/c378b40d54e97e408dd72850b7cfb00bc0801571.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \geq 10-r$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="81" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa06369d3dd17e89cf4c8b53d2485ee62cfc15cf.png" class="latex" alt="$r \geq 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />
</p><p>The minimum possible value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b860f249008c6174e1a1509e083d2f090b9555c6.png" class="latex" alt="$8\ \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" />.
</p><p>~mobius247
</p>
