<p>Older television screens have an aspect ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9a03b059b84a40291c4c20ea6a12c7127bff4c3.png" class="latex" alt="$4: 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />. That is, the ratio of the width to the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9a03b059b84a40291c4c20ea6a12c7127bff4c3.png" class="latex" alt="$4: 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />. The aspect ratio of many movies is not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9a03b059b84a40291c4c20ea6a12c7127bff4c3.png" class="latex" alt="$4: 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />, so they are sometimes shown on a television screen by "letterboxing" - darkening strips of equal height at the top and bottom of the screen, as shown. Suppose a movie has an aspect ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/8/c38896966af12256c9dae24b447911c290015bfc.png" class="latex" alt="$2: 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="12" /> and is shown on an older television screen with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/1051e7d41ec0b8bd688a872be17538413f4ee97b.png" class="latex" alt="$27$" width="18" height="12" />-inch diagonal. What is the height, in inches, of each darkened strip?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e0eb295ca042287f3d625b9ac6eed49532ef1ab.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1mm); filldraw((0,0)--(21.6,0)--(21.6,2.7)--(0,2.7)--cycle,grey,black); filldraw((0,13.5)--(21.6,13.5)--(21.6,16.2)--(0,16.2)--cycle,grey,black); draw((0,0)--(21.6,0)--(21.6,16.2)--(0,16.2)--cycle); [/asy]" width="105" height="78" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/8/458a7d19dbdfe9f7207a59179bc4c078a335e0a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 2\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 2.25\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 2.5\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 2.7\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the width and height of the screen be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" /> respectively, and let the width and height of the movie be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cab3f0704de3232f601b03d02a45d46103c172.png" class="latex" alt="$2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> respectively. 
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, the diagonal is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/c/0fc4655334aa2779ff83cbefb35fd28114acba8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(3x)^2+(4x)^2}=5x = 27$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="208" height="32" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/5/015f1122a7aec7339d14e68fa6a0e3535343f5a4.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{27}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" />. 
</p><p>Since the movie and the screen have the same width, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/8/e083c30723a7e6e3d9ed42d03df268cf2c4a44d7.png" class="latex" alt="$2y=4x\Rightarrow y=2x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="143" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Thus, the height of each strip is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/1/971a7e7da6b014eb1c08f227c550921ec593d9bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3x-y}{2}=\frac{3x-2x}{2}=\frac{x}{2}=\frac{27}{10}=2.7\Longrightarrow\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="343" height="38" />. 
</p>
