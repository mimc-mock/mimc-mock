<p>The numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a52c34211b243d3edc979e13630439887e5b09f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^3b^7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/1/43166258b4d395e1a0d3b4777f4367e01776ff09.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^5b^{12})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/d/57db576a6f9ac3d95f89269a6cde27c2f2727c63.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^8b^{15})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="19" /> are the first three terms of an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_sequence" title="Arithmetic sequence">arithmetic sequence</a>, and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/654bf1c1dacc0893a258b5c0eda6d98fc44c085f.png" class="latex" alt="$12^\text{th}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="16" /> term of the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8dee6ee2fa2458be81ac366528e8f2b6089243d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\log{b^n}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/1/3816dc42c240982afc5b52b0356197a19f9d9d63.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 40\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 56\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 76\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 112\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 143$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="405" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab4d57c5322c149cc2db0859c88a8fcb636fd65.png" class="latex" alt="$A = \log(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/f/6df5c4925733199eec534f8410339635c82932e7.png" class="latex" alt="$B = \log(b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="18" />. 
</p><p>The first three terms of the arithmetic sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/97996278bee8a7211347d472aa40054f7a7665d5.png" class="latex" alt="$3A + 7B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/c/e2c423834bbabbccb2805144ee2606f5f31f4ada.png" class="latex" alt="$5A + 12B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/a/88a2d1a7d63b516608eb0d59ab72e5c80beb85bf.png" class="latex" alt="$8A + 15B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="14" />, and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/654bf1c1dacc0893a258b5c0eda6d98fc44c085f.png" class="latex" alt="$12^\text{th}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="16" /> term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/0/8502fdfd1fe1d0560a2279cf2d8193195d914df1.png" class="latex" alt="$nB$" width="25" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/f/c0fea760b91fd76e5ccb71c42c37758267312b96.png" class="latex" alt="$2(5A + 12B) = (3A + 7B) + (8A + 15B) \Rightarrow A = 2B$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="411" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Since the first three terms in the sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/5/215205fd7f49cc87aac99fad47438ad759381d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$13B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/9/c4937f12943065c21f2c08a913199e21237aa4f6.png" class="latex" alt="$22B$" width="32" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/b/e7b3879ea86d2f3ecd8b2df07dcc00e7af988be5.png" class="latex" alt="$31B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />th term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/3/5331092dc7e53a6de1e011384ec2c9537970337f.png" class="latex" alt="$(9k + 4)B$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/654bf1c1dacc0893a258b5c0eda6d98fc44c085f.png" class="latex" alt="$12^\text{th}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="16" /> term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39fbe569fa7ef20d7c9532027b2834e72bd2a51c.png" class="latex" alt="$(9\cdot12 + 4)B = 112B = nB \Rightarrow n = 112\Rightarrow \boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="363" height="24" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a52c34211b243d3edc979e13630439887e5b09f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^3b^7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/1/43166258b4d395e1a0d3b4777f4367e01776ff09.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^5b^{12})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/d/57db576a6f9ac3d95f89269a6cde27c2f2727c63.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(a^8b^{15})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="19" /> are in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Arithmetic progression">arithmetic progression</a>, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/2/4a281c05a7c993369d15d5c4db7c3f64cef22543.png" class="latex" alt="$a^3b^7$" width="31" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35b1030536d482ba39866e423b985f535406ab2d.png" class="latex" alt="$a^5b^{12}$" width="37" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/0/e3020da8b241a9adc69fff3ea841d9692c40b088.png" class="latex" alt="$a^8b^{15}$" width="37" height="15" /> are in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Geometric progression">geometric progression</a>. Therefore,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/7/267dc9ebb14c8c501f62a7892f64e67ce84e034d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2b^5=a^3b^3 \Rightarrow a=b^2\]" width="164" height="15" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/5/c05e3561ea2dca3e6ad303a9b92af57c47e1debf.png" class="latex" alt="$a^3b^7=b^{13}$" width="77" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/b/7bbcd891327dff7f24b040cfdd1e714589f332ef.png" class="latex" alt="$a^5b^{12}=b^{22}$" width="83" height="15" />, therefore the 12th term in the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c483a294ea7ba889b9332610981498ac2b1348d.png" class="latex" alt="$b^{13+9*11}=b^{112} \Rightarrow \boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="167" height="24" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/e/a0e291c588a0392df375ff94122e241f7fd098e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\ \text{If a, b, and c are in a arithmetic progression then } b = \frac{a+c}{2} \text{ which means}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="545" height="35" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/0453e29470c44e0ab1e6d4d5b0c2d111ae4bdba0.png" class="latex" alt="$\ \log(a^5b^{12}) = \frac{\log(a^3b^7) + \log(a^8b^{15})}{2}= \frac{\log(a^{11}b^{22})}{2} \text{ therefore}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="460" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/7/6b79bdf2f505925f77e1d22f9b3b69d8530946b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\ 2\log(a^5b^{12}) = \log(a^{10}b^{24}) = \log(a^{11}b^{22}) \Rightarrow a=b^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/6/9960a6316615d09f9d48dd9deb05a967f8a75fb7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ \text{This means that the Kth term of the series would be } \log(b^{13+9(k-1)})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="508" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/7/097a383315251b6239aced16c0c57c783cf19047.png" class="latex" alt="$\ \text{The 12th term would be } \log(b^{112})  \Rightarrow n=112  \Rightarrow D$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="391" height="19" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_(mimimal_manipulation)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.28mimimal_manipulation.29">Solution 4 (mimimal manipulation)</span></h3>
<p>Given the first three terms form an arithmetic progression, we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34a328a70fff5b774ea3296c57085c69e2862e22.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a = \log(a^3b^7)\]" width="102" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da850d42442e95448e0acd969d4ae4983ec23db.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+d = \log(a^5b^{12})\]" width="140" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/8/f8889b61a8154819cdb7b2fdfdf531692aa1a7b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+2d = \log(a^8b^{15}).\]" width="154" height="20" />
Subtracting the first equation from the second and the third from the second, respectively, gives us these two expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/d/aad8a482af40866f0790006f0c71ac40954bb396.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[d = \log \left( \frac{a^5b^{12}}{a^3b^7} \right) = \log a^2b^5\]" width="210" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/b/57b4e19973d3efe99c3fdad4248c49225faa32db.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[d = \log \left( \frac{a^8b^{15}}{a^5b^{12}} \right) = \log a^3b^3.\]" width="215" height="43" />
The desired <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />th term in the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/5/b85ebd60fe8f36e7a03a3c1f1710b3aaa5eb0ddb.png" class="latex" alt="$a+11d$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="61" height="16" />, so we can substitute our values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> (using either one of our two expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />):
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/2/e92cf3dc9319f4b967f61c8f8ca50e5f722d8695.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+11d = \log a^3b^7 + 11\log(a^2b^5)\]" width="254" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/0/92040cfc1fc20a508eea4ec1da21ce740d38f018.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \log a^3b^7 + \log(a^{22}b^{55})\]" width="181" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/4/f34d634bd52a4ec285df45208ed5126b4cbbce7d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \log a^{25}b^{62}.\]" width="93" height="19" />
The answer must be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/c/7dcbf8540134623b9e9531ef580f33c5e1eff5c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(b^n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, however. We're in luck: the two different yet equal expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> allow us to express <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> in terms of each other:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d071fe882d4a675ec7a38d9770d983765a46dbb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log a^2b^5 = \log a^3b^3\]" width="140" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/f/99f6c5331e686044a9ea96eb95e09bfc66236964.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2b^5 = a^3b^3\]" width="87" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/2/27254abc6aced133913020ef90e3a49b56f9702d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a=b^2.\]" width="52" height="15" />
Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/2/d92a240b063f2ce40c321e69f8eea525f4c05d28.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="17" />, we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699d17968c9a6bae2da9e662c6c57b86874184cf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+11d = \log b^{50}b^{62}\]" width="152" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/9/3d9f0b5f0edc3dfbb281e77c6c0d819e8e4ea74c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \log b^{112} \Rightarrow \boxed{D}.\]" width="131" height="24" />
~ Jingwei325 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/541e9aef85db57b7dbef677e59a04d09b2e8b58a.png" class="latex" alt="$\smiley$" width="12" height="11" />
</p>
