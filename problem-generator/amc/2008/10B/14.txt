<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/e/a1e220396bf8f6704ea4a5f1745cc64ce339090b.png" class="latex" alt="$OAB$" width="42" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/e/94e1baf8d541d0a7887587d42b5175432b680eb5.png" class="latex" alt="$O=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/6/216851317d987ff878a0c80ae029282c45f12e3a.png" class="latex" alt="$B=(5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> in the first quadrant. In addition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/4/2e4cf8cb702b64fa45671bdbfd5ea32ee0f800a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO=90^\circ$" width="104" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/5/4b55125f320c5d99d79472241a7c86b5ca6555ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB=30^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a762ac8b4e23ffdd778ad6d5a8b97df8c7ce01.png" class="latex" alt="$OA$" width="28" height="13" /> is rotated <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> counterclockwise about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />. What are the coordinates of the image of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/7/307c9de17ef1feb0978bf2b0a242998528c67539.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \left( - \frac {10}{3}\sqrt {3},5\right) \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \left( - \frac {5}{3}\sqrt {3},5\right) \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \left(\sqrt {3},5\right) \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \left(\frac {5}{3}\sqrt {3},5\right) \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \left(\frac {10}{3}\sqrt {3},5\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="752" height="43" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/4/2e4cf8cb702b64fa45671bdbfd5ea32ee0f800a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO=90^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/5/4b55125f320c5d99d79472241a7c86b5ca6555ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB=30^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />, we know that this triangle is one of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Special_Right_Triangles" title="Special Right Triangles">Special Right Triangles</a>.
</p><p>We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38ec6a35045b7cc0f04c4989a99570bcc29aee43.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> lies on the x-axis. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/c/9acb67fc171344f8990859a3dd358853015f6bfa.png" class="latex" alt="$OB = 5$" width="62" height="12" />.
</p><p>Then, since we know that this is a Special Right Triangle(30-60-90 triangle), we can use the proportion <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/d/1dd6a99c1edb087b7b020a3eb35dd6c2d69d3dc9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{5}{\sqrt 3}=\frac{AB}{1}\]" width="81" height="42" /> to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />.
</p><p>We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/a/18a51a6c061fc449d5f6d6e8ca523ec22c2f186f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AB=\frac{5\sqrt 3}{3}\]" width="88" height="41" />
</p><p>That means the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6baa281843dddf5798cd66aa295e387f20dd1b61.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(5,\frac{5\sqrt 3}3\right)$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="78" height="53" />.
</p><p>Rotate this triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> counterclockwise around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, and you will find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> will end up in the second quadrant with the coordinates
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbef19366418c5ceb6198b48b7cd5cfb178eabf0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ \left( -\frac{5\sqrt 3}3, 5\right)}$" style="vertical-align: -29px" width="110" height="67" />.
</p><p>Note: To better visualize this, one can sketch a diagram.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/4/2e4cf8cb702b64fa45671bdbfd5ea32ee0f800a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO=90^\circ$" width="104" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is in the first quadrant, we know that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. We now need to pick a positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> coordinate for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> so that we'll have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/5/4b55125f320c5d99d79472241a7c86b5ca6555ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB=30^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />. 
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/5/935400cef6ffb8f3d303973c7e1da4075b93efe8.png" class="latex" alt="$AO^2 = AB^2 + BO^2 = AB^2 + 25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="254" height="16" />. 
</p><p>By the definition of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Sine" class="mw-redirect" title="Sine">sine</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af037c3044bb4664c9a57a9fc333a7b1ffd3cb21.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{AO} = \sin AOB = \sin 30^\circ = \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="236" height="38" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/d/72d1d1aff3c1057151748d63f3bc63b7f94cf2e3.png" class="latex" alt="$AO=2\cdot AB$" width="102" height="13" />.
</p><p>Substituting into the previous equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/6/506f042789a519a80c6d610015cd5ef2430a1f10.png" class="latex" alt="$AB^2 = \frac{25}3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41cb92ace7e7bf9c682b9fc4bc1690453add9ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=\frac{5\sqrt 3}3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="41" />.
</p><p>This means that the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6baa281843dddf5798cd66aa295e387f20dd1b61.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(5,\frac{5\sqrt 3}3\right)$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="78" height="53" />. 
</p><p>After we rotate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a762ac8b4e23ffdd778ad6d5a8b97df8c7ce01.png" class="latex" alt="$OA$" width="28" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> counterclockwise about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, it will be in the second quadrant and have the coordinates 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc141d9901435e7a5e00f3747582f558334348dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ \left( -\frac{5\sqrt 3}3, 5\right) }$" style="vertical-align: -27px" width="108" height="64" />.
</p>
