<p>A class collects 50 dollars to buy flowers for a classmate who is in the hospital. Roses cost 3 dollars each, and carnations cost 2 dollars each. No other flowers are to be used. How many different bouquets could be purchased for exactly 50 dollars?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/6/d9687860bc5de103fa30993f89a451cd2cc65166.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 7 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 9 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 16 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="360" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The cost of a rose is odd, hence we need an even number of roses. Let there be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a5745df50c8607bdc9b8de4968315633a3047dc.png" class="latex" alt="$2r$" width="17" height="12" /> roses for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/a/39ad7957a2fccdb01077981b253b51d278609565.png" class="latex" alt="$r\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />. Then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed73b1c2accc493935629550279b8f9f2609234.png" class="latex" alt="$50-3\cdot 2r = 50-6r$" width="162" height="12" /> dollars left. We can always reach the sum exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> by buying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/d/bddd899a12c662f5d581a01b19bc063ad7b6ad7e.png" class="latex" alt="$(50-6r)/2 = 25-3r$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" /> carnations. Of course, the number of roses must be such that the number of carnations is non-negative. We get the inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e81446542d95f4a19150be979fcb099d1c6c95c2.png" class="latex" alt="$25-3r \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="91" height="15" /> which solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/0/1f083d1eb399c5109a154909e457b55fe7563952.png" class="latex" alt="$r\leq 8 \frac13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must be an integer, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/a/3aa914c619be3f1b1d3ec6d672d46a5962fd3723.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{9 \text{ (C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, and each gives us one solution.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the number of roses and carnations bought. The equation should be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/383d728e1eddb93e65b9fff2c7a4b15112a375a6.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+2y = 50$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="102" height="16" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> is an even number, the product of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" /> must be even and smaller than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" />. You can try nonnegative even integers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and you will end up with the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in total, so the answer is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/a/3aa914c619be3f1b1d3ec6d672d46a5962fd3723.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{9 \text{ (C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> represent the number of roses, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> represent the number of carnations. Then, we get the linear Diophantine equation,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc7a73fe70a00f049b0261b1f6333e4f86908cc5.png" class="latex" alt="$3r+2c=50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="14" />.
Using the Euclidean algorithm, we get the initial solutions to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/8/868f802dff3f9f1ff7899a8fd8cbf045c2749f72.png" class="latex" alt="$r_0=50$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/3/133b6ed388b24e2a49c60f740db920c72ef957b2.png" class="latex" alt="$c_0=-50$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="71" height="15" />, meaning the complete solution will be,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d829cb9d09b411d8d8b002b3f55c6e8e40be9d08.png" class="latex" alt="$r=50+\frac{2}{\gcd(2,3)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="143" height="41" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/a/02a92c223bdfae3b9d001e98f15ddbbd3f335ddf.png" class="latex" alt="$k=50+2k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/3/923d25ce77a4b1309a171c2f0a115f361049f55f.png" class="latex" alt="$c=-50-\frac{3}{\gcd(2,3)}k=-50-3k$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="265" height="42" />
</p><p>The solution range for which both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are positive is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4f08038ab1fa2a97bea4f0f98ddca5a74478302.png" class="latex" alt="$\leq k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/163b0f326b6f8a5fa311f179ad175849b828e468.png" class="latex" alt="$\leq$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/a/3aa914c619be3f1b1d3ec6d672d46a5962fd3723.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{9 \text{ (C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" /> possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p>
