<p>Michael walks at the rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> feet per second on a long straight path. Trash pails are located every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> feet along the path. A garbage truck traveling at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> feet per second in the same direction as Michael stops for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds at each pail. As Michael passes a pail, he notices the truck ahead of him just leaving the next pail. How many times will Michael and the truck intersect? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a3149923c94d02071e6e8d97c866ad6d471a571.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 5\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 6\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 7\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Pick a coordinate system where Michael's starting pail is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and the one where the truck starts is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" />.
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/354db4123321a716dc7f21e1ae53f61b79ea7b5f.png" class="latex" alt="$M(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/4/03495b3b1b09ec4c6247d56444917a48b790936c.png" class="latex" alt="$T(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> be the coordinates of Michael and the truck after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds.
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/a/93a017704a79c5fa1cf1b6673a73930406f7f011.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)=T(t)-M(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="155" height="18" /> be their (signed) distance after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds.
Meetings occur whenever <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94c22dcdc99957400fdb157994a7fab12152f40a.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />.
We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/7/ec7d17c9254031beb7d3f37ab25ccd18db6c81c2.png" class="latex" alt="$D(0)=200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="18" />. 
</p><p>The truck always moves for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds, then stands still for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />. During the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds of the cycle the truck moves by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> feet and Michael by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, hence during the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds of the cycle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
During the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> decreases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e10e5f3aa7c766cb27c3ad319fa526f71588cd.png" class="latex" alt="$150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
</p><p>From this observation it is obvious that after four full cycles, i.e. at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/0/be070dbd6e6f571e998a593ab37019d71f5a7347.png" class="latex" alt="$t=200$" width="57" height="12" />, we will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94c22dcdc99957400fdb157994a7fab12152f40a.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" /> for the first time.
</p><p>During the fifth cycle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> will first grow from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, then fall from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb90d025068754ea0b56f2889bbbe4e93119683.png" class="latex" alt="$-50$" width="31" height="12" />. Hence Michael overtakes the truck while it is standing at the pail. 
</p><p>During the sixth cycle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> will first grow from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb90d025068754ea0b56f2889bbbe4e93119683.png" class="latex" alt="$-50$" width="31" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" />, then fall from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/0/060d8850e65d5781ce12d9fe2681f401dd6ee77c.png" class="latex" alt="$-100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. Hence the truck starts moving, overtakes Michael on their way to the next pail, and then Michael overtakes the truck while it is standing at the pail. 
</p><p>During the seventh cycle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> will first grow from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/0/060d8850e65d5781ce12d9fe2681f401dd6ee77c.png" class="latex" alt="$-100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, then fall from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b106a021335321ab013caaebac9c0966655d4659.png" class="latex" alt="$-150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. Hence the truck meets Michael at the moment when it arrives to the next pail.
</p><p>Obviously, from this point on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94ce0dc767f1fc482d9b79a1aaafc42bd94f1ee4.png" class="latex" alt="$D(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> will always be negative, meaning that Michael is already too far ahead. Hence we found all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/d/60dc511b0eebd7d0836065af478260c1706becab.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{5 \Longrightarrow B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="75" height="24" /> meetings.
</p><p>The movement of Michael and the truck is plotted below: Michael in blue, the truck in red.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/4/b9418ee09131439f5566d386095e5665437c05ce.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph;  size(400,300,IgnoreAspect);  real[] xt = new real[21]; real[] yt = new real[21]; for (int i=0; i&lt;11; ++i) xt[2*i]=50*i; for (int i=0; i&lt;10; ++i) xt[2*i+1]=50*i+20; for (int i=0; i&lt;11; ++i) yt[2*i]=200*(i+1); for (int i=0; i&lt;10; ++i) yt[2*i+1]=200*(i+2);  real[] xm={0,500}; real[] ym={0,2500};  draw(graph(xt,yt),red); draw(graph(xm,ym),blue);  xaxis(&quot;$\mathrm{time}$&quot;,Bottom,LeftTicks); yaxis(&quot;$\mathrm{position}$&quot;,Left,LeftTicks); [/asy]" width="672" height="502" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The truck takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds to go from one pail to the next and then stops for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds at the new pail. Thus it sets off from a pail every 50 sec. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> denote the time elapsed and write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/8/6b8e4570378abaaf00e175fa35979c4344bb4e39.png" class="latex" alt="$t=50k + \Delta$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="95" height="14" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/1/5c1f4726562dd916aee8cc1375aaae4438c60c46.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta \in [0,50)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="83" height="18" />. In  this time Michael has traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb85eb9cc1f42015e9f96629b30a012a94d7b25c.png" class="latex" alt="$5t = 250k+5\Delta$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="14" /> feet. What about the truck? In the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/addfc9b7717a25bd31143bf4e9c59ca6fd53f0a6.png" class="latex" alt="$50k$" width="27" height="12" /> seconds the truck covers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> pails, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/d/73d486e2db159c7a419a6ee7a3a463646dab5bef.png" class="latex" alt="$200k$" width="36" height="12" /> feet so it moves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/f/0bfb223104c816d2fbbc1a4fb8be43920efcedab.png" class="latex" alt="$200+200k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="14" /> feet from Michael's starting point. Then we have two cases: 
</p><p><b>(a)</b> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c93f23fb1cbc9e9b71dcc1ff09d50f093943ab2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta &lt; 20$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />, then the truck travels an additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/b/ecb8fa0d4aaaede6f43bc65eab9a08135f647d1b.png" class="latex" alt="$10\Delta$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" /> feet. For them to intersect we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/3/ff311760ae0703e1f1feab76a672a0284107ed9e.png" class="latex" alt="$200+200k + 10\Delta = 250k + 5\Delta$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="249" height="14" />. Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/a/c8afa1fd6b65e02b5937a62d65f0bb511abfdcc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta = 10k - 40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="107" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/7/0a737670d481129e2ead6d7223437f3cf70ea53f.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta$" width="14" height="13" /> must lie in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/5/6f56edf6922f0a573f2e2dc4291be50d2c311bd7.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,20)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="18" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22e3bb74bc98dd760b0126f1a0cda5b09b0241ca.png" class="latex" alt="$k \in \{4, 5\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>(b)</b> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/1/d11e6d732f54d6669c6072b2e20729fcd344f152.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta \in [20, 50)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="92" height="18" />, then the truck travels an additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> feet. For them to intersect we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/3/b530e68fe8ed530989c5aa65a38103866974341c.png" class="latex" alt="$200+200k + 200 = 250k + 5\Delta$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="243" height="14" />. Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb686c4c7798aa67d5262e1d34bd8d7656ab5489.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta = 10(8-k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/7/0a737670d481129e2ead6d7223437f3cf70ea53f.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta$" width="14" height="13" /> must lie in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/8/648824b450a0d6b3b760ae9c4f37e4bfd8321673.png" class="latex" alt="$[20,50)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="55" height="18" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/1/f51bb2b403e8971b0ffa6c491d276e74b430eaef.png" class="latex" alt="$k \in \{4, 5, 6\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Thus Michael intersects with the truck <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> times, which is option <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We make a chart by seconds in increments of ten.
