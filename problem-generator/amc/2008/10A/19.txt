<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">Rectangle</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> lies in a plane with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/2/0e24acfced9d21611d63754d54b56e9f30b348fb.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ=RS=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/e/76edf040de21300dec2c521c4e6240f396a936ff.png" class="latex" alt="$QR=SP=6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />. The rectangle is rotated <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> clockwise about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, then rotated <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> clockwise about the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> moved to after the first rotation. What is the length of the path traveled by point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d90d7843d54b9248ed930ecfd69a4e203b7992c.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \left(2\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5}\right)\pi\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 6\pi\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \left(3+\sqrt{10}\right)\pi\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \left(\sqrt{3}+2\sqrt{5}\right)\pi\\\mathrm{(E)}\ 2\sqrt{10}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="590" height="56" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd4d160e94d32f55888e6872d9a4748c44bab08.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(220);pathpen=black+linewidth(0.65);pointpen=black; /* draw in rectangles */ D(MP(&quot;R&quot;,(0,0))--MP(&quot;Q&quot;,(-6,0))--MP(&quot;P&quot;,(-6,2),N)--MP(&quot;S&quot;,(0,2),NW)--cycle); D((0,0)--MP(&quot;Q&#39;&quot;,(0,6),SW)--MP(&quot;P&#39;&quot;,(2,6),SE)--MP(&quot;S&#39;&quot;,(2,0))--cycle); D(MP(&quot;R&#39;&#39;&quot;,(2,2),NE)--MP(&quot;Q&#39;&#39;&quot;,(8,2),N)--MP(&quot;P&#39;&#39;&quot;,(8,0))--(2,0)--cycle); D(arc((0,0),(2,6),(-6,2)),dashed);D(arc((2,0),(8,0),(2,6)),dashed);D((2,6)--(0,0)--(-6,2),dashed); D(rightanglemark((2,6),(0,0),(-6,2),12));D(rightanglemark((2,6),(2,0),(8,0),12)); MP(&quot;2&quot;,(-6,1),W);MP(&quot;6&quot;,(-3,0),S); [/asy]" width="368" height="178" /></center>
<p>We let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/9/ad90b8cba1421a2b0ed9f356b286dd7a06768e6a.png" class="latex" alt="$P&#39;Q&#39;R&#39;S&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="17" /> be the rectangle after the first rotation, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/9/22955cb663a8e6866e73aa16a3f013709e3b0966.png" class="latex" alt="$P&#39;&#39;Q&#39;&#39;R&#39;&#39;S&#39;&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="17" /> be the rectangle after the second rotation. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> pivots about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> in an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arc" title="Arc">arc</a> of a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1cde7aefd2d9109eb97e9da410461285d77a201.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2^2+6^2} = 2\sqrt{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="137" height="19" />, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/c/decb01c40a2a4f5728871caa733ab34d76d5554e.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle PRS,\, \angle P&#39;RQ&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="127" height="17" /> are complementary, it follows that the arc has a degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> and length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/d/4cd0f9f9869b39e79527f9010293adf7bfe5210f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circumference" title="Circumference">circumference</a>. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> travels <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/5/205f73db3b47be22856e4d7f355d8ee00e027fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14 \left(4\sqrt{10}\right)\pi = \sqrt{10}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="162" height="37" /> in the first rotation.
</p><p>Similarly, in the second rotation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> travels in a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> arc about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/3/bf344aa376979a8a81e8784f92fcb2381897ba65.png" class="latex" alt="$S&#39;$" width="15" height="14" />, with the radius being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. It travels <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/5/8155969eb62cfec7dfb11f1110b1ade794d855f3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14(12)\pi = 3\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="100" height="37" />. Therefore, the total distance it travels is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/874d1b75dfd02c4111a4a1727a027e263d991749.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(3+\sqrt{10}\right)\pi\ \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="131" height="32" />.
</p>
