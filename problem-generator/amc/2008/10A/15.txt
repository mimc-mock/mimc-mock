<p>Yesterday Han drove 1 hour longer than Ian at an average speed 5 miles per hour faster than Ian. Jan drove 2 hours longer than Ian at an average speed 10 miles per hour faster than Ian. Han drove 70 miles more than Ian. How many more miles did Jan drive than Ian?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/3/3b38835561afb45e31c03fc924472da9fe0d327b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 120\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 130\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 140\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 150\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 160$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="432" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Set the time Ian traveled as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" />, and set Han's speed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />. Therefore, Jan's speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/9/c19e853c32fdca757cba0d0883faa3fb6dbbd4ee.png" class="latex" alt="$H+5.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="14" />
</p><p>We get the following equation for how much Han is ahead of Ian:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf4a7aad0b462a00a4734c12ecc81ce1bca9a84.png" class="latex" alt="$H+5I = 70.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" />
</p><p>The expression for how much Jan is ahead of Ian is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c1457be128c859fc1bc05582f08c24c733e15263.png" class="latex" alt="$2(H+5)+10I.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" />
</p><p>This simplifies to: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb0b31c66ab3d8ae3ee3b556fa39350818dc2606.png" class="latex" alt="$2H+10+10I.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="14" />
</p><p>However, this is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/c/71cb5d966d4d30c42f1e580b357fd8b7dde2f965.png" class="latex" alt="$2(H+5I)+10.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" />
</p><p>Substitute, from the first equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/f/73f3ee1e2f9a9411593eaeaa56f6fd63f41a7d3c.png" class="latex" alt="$H+5I$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="14" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/8/df8f84ec7aa690c07175434b64b9ea581403ad54.png" class="latex" alt="$70.$" width="21" height="12" />
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/f/6af7736fbfc7783495e1979f94139237ac090b6f.png" class="latex" alt="$140 + 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e10e5f3aa7c766cb27c3ad319fa526f71588cd.png" class="latex" alt="$150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/3/d53b37edf32cfbc0179e6e6cd9b2ff4d482b6634.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We let Ian's speed and time equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/69339359f104b04f7010295938318fcb3537f0ca.png" class="latex" alt="$I_s$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/2/e82aca3b77354c7d456dabf032e4f36fd1495fe8.png" class="latex" alt="$I_t$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" />, respectively. Similarly, let Han's and Jan's speed and time be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169629ae0683940dd26e0e59ae53c7dfd302306d.png" class="latex" alt="$H_s$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/a/9aa0d029169a3a98422d0e3060e97cdd9d724089.png" class="latex" alt="$H_t$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/6/f36c0ed2e2fda8b6c7c371082737c7a135282ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$J_s$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/a/57a0b798844c43ef3a389afb5ae5b4de5b24d875.png" class="latex" alt="$J_t$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="15" />. The problem gives us 5 <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equation" title="Equation">equations</a>:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/4/1442b59cfafc09c58e0830997efe54bd06722fba.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align} H_s&amp;=I_s+5 \\ H_t&amp;=I_t+1 \\ J_s&amp;=I_s+10 \\ J_t&amp;=I_t+2 \\ H_s \cdot H_t &amp; =I_s \cdot I_t+70 \end{align}" width="319" height="116" />
</p><p>Substituting equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/7/737a366a904e00f936f8728869ad8f2587dab46f.png" class="latex" alt="$(5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> gives:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/959c4ab7dc335d539d5d0e1edd070aa6c29de010.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(I_s+5)(I_t+1)=I_s I_t+70 \Longrightarrow I_s I_t+I_s+5I_t+5=I_s I_t+70 \Longrightarrow I_s+5I_t=65 \quad (*)\]" width="603" height="18" />
</p><p>We are asked the difference between Jan's and Ian's distances, or
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/b/90bfcf79cfe47ece92e7c7c48a8d0a1536625f89.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[J_s J_t-I_s I_t=x,\]" width="122" height="16" />
</p><p>Where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the difference between Jan's and Ian's distances and the answer to the problem. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b88ea3916e8089c7edbccc3ff91e58e01a6e2682.png" class="latex" alt="$(3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/4/104992efa4deaa9a2dfeeec9b331ad0bf628f80a.png" class="latex" alt="$(4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> equations into this equation gives:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc251ae3c94cbac68f792c1a5844f68e12d781ea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(I_s+10)(I_t+2)-I_s I_t=x \Longrightarrow I_s I_t+2I_s+10I_t+20-I_s I_t=x \Longrightarrow\]" width="485" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/a/00a7eed557bc9d5cd8132f3055c34976067dcf7b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2I_s+10I_t+20=x \Longrightarrow 2(I_s+5I_t)+20=x\]" width="350" height="18" />
</p><p>Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/0/9d02e715e7f7195675151570530c5b62ba7b2744.png" class="latex" alt="$(*)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> into this equation gives:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b74cd0faa3b7a2d71e6c0589c59dc87925f188e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2(65)+20=x \Longrightarrow 130+20=x \Longrightarrow 150=x\]" width="359" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e10e5f3aa7c766cb27c3ad319fa526f71588cd.png" class="latex" alt="$150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> miles or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/3/d53b37edf32cfbc0179e6e6cd9b2ff4d482b6634.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let Ian drive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> miles, at a speed of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, for some time <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> (in hours). Hence, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b5548d6919e956b1edf9ddf9392b7fdde6563149.png" class="latex" alt="$D=RT$" width="66" height="12" />. We can find a similar equation for Han, who drove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56a55e9fe26b54518920216452bd68205e8fa44e.png" class="latex" alt="$D + 70$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="14" /> miles, at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e7b9e6ab9c9457faa5466f2e48fe717e1a8aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$R+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="44" height="14" />, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/2/702b3f764621a26e2bababbd6f0a872cd6fbb1cd.png" class="latex" alt="$T+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="14" /> hours, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/8/028efdb414876e6b86a3fb550f2cd2175da021d2.png" class="latex" alt="$D + 70 = (R + 5)(T + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="18" />. We can do the same for Jan, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/0/41059b0dc107613475aed1511d156bc45075b73d.png" class="latex" alt="$D + x = (R + 10)(T + 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is how much further Jan traveled than Ian. We now have three equations:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d5cbfc18b3322e0788409ed138a8a1ac4b99155.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[D= RT\]" width="66" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/0/31027237132b64757f91712ff52e33fc2cbd3018.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[D + 70 = (R+5)(T+1) = RT + R + 5T + 5\]" width="360" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/d/a9d1f5b641067dd0d155883fa393b8c412522701.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[D + x = (R + 10)(T + 2) = RT + 10 T + 2R + 20.\]" width="393" height="18" />
Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8fe0bc1f0ab90d60df20bc9e4ccac77d35238e1.png" class="latex" alt="$RT$" width="27" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> in the second and third equations and cancelling gives us:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/9/c690bd68c5e5f0156d4ebb0a47ac6e80f960b39c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[70 = 5T + R + 5 \Longrightarrow 5T + R = 65\]" width="270" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e24637771f1bb2acebbb8fa1d079798bda10b0e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 10T + 2R + 20  \Longrightarrow x = 2(5T + R ) + 20  \Longrightarrow x= 2(65) + 20 = 150.\]" width="517" height="18" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/1/00164188825712672a8a19af354054877180ed83.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/3/d53b37edf32cfbc0179e6e6cd9b2ff4d482b6634.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let Ian drive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> miles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> hours, and at speed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />.
</p><p>Ian's Equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/5/4453d4a121b7a9caffc96e9b8961dffe665d3ebf.png" class="latex" alt="$d=s \cdot t.$" width="65" height="12" />
</p><p>Han drove 70 more miles, traveled 5 miles per hour faster and traveled 1 more hour than Ian.
</p><p>Han's Equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/d/1cd6121291ed095a2618ce3211a3bb6ea0d53d0f.png" class="latex" alt="$d+70=(s+5) \cdot (t+1).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="18" />
</p><p>Let Jan have driven <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> miles. Jan also has driven 10 miles per hour faster and traveled 2 more hours than Ian.
</p><p>Jan's Equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8fc6a8c50c8543fd1598bad1ba7e94618e064bf.png" class="latex" alt="$m=(s+10) \cdot (t+2).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" />
</p><p>Let's group the equations together:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/c/6dca9ff0601fa42d8615c414da9aefc8aeb33ec3.png" class="latex" alt="$(1) \phantom{a} d=s \cdot t$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6a4d6a67a56623f422480db3d2f83ca7f064a9d.png" class="latex" alt="$(2) \phantom{a} d+70=(s+5) \cdot (t+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="224" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/0/eb02bf97f0676ccc7fd0d22df16d5a2993312730.png" class="latex" alt="$(3) \phantom{a} m=(s+10) \cdot (t+2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="199" height="18" />
</p><p>Let's see what we want to find: We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. The equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/562f7ce6eeb4261169529198821406639cba6b99.png" class="latex" alt="$m=d+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of more miles traveled by Jan than Ian.
</p><p>Onto the calculating part.
</p><p>Expanding the second equation, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/3/143d11d7ebfc292ac8a43736aea3191159dc9213.png" class="latex" alt="$d+70=st+5t+s+5.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="192" height="14" />
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/b/29b3eadbbdcc1c247196d6f1f50d208374909a2b.png" class="latex" alt="$st=d$" width="49" height="12" /> by the first equation, so substituting we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/5/0f577e96cdec785cbf8dc437a0109e3ab0097482.png" class="latex" alt="$d+70=d+5t+s+5.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="187" height="14" />
</p><p>Simplifying gets us 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/b/9fb6e082ea9b8de4fb5879f15feed253e16e20aa.png" class="latex" alt="$(4) \phantom{a} s+5t=65.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />
</p><p>(Note for the above process: You could have substituted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/6/38611983906dda3650ff03bf618700fda7a09a4e.png" class="latex" alt="$st$" width="14" height="11" /> but that would lead you to the same result since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/4/8e4c5c60940afe085816b70318b5d66679a33b9d.png" class="latex" alt="$d-d=st-st=0.$" width="154" height="12" />)
</p><p>Let's look at the third equation. Expanding, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/d/a8d3ad5d28f9a323c56e51a9bc2d3da8564e1982.png" class="latex" alt="$m=st+10t+2s+20.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="186" height="14" />
</p><p>Since we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> we want the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/562f7ce6eeb4261169529198821406639cba6b99.png" class="latex" alt="$m=d+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />. We write the expanded third equation into this form since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169bf9fc307e8845b56786cc97780eed13232e7c.png" class="latex" alt="$st=d.$" width="52" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27ef79c501a5c940d8d2f7862414d09dba390ef4.png" class="latex" alt="$(5) \phantom{a} m=d+(10t+2s+20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="222" height="18" />.
</p><p>Let's take a closer look at the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> section of the equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/2/902947a2282d03f320be4e891ea4c2a5c16c4ba9.png" class="latex" alt="$(6) \phantom{a} n=10t+2s+20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" />
</p><p>This looks very similar to equation 4, if you multiply equation 4 by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> you get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aac42edb58f097a549cbbf553d8f9582004a5c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(7) \phantom{a} 10t+2s=130$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" />.
</p><p>Plugging equation 7 into equation 6 we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/598bb678f5d9c906b7a6344aa6576214af643905.png" class="latex" alt="$n=(10t+2s)+20 \Rightarrow n=130+20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="284" height="18" />
</p><p>Calculating gets us
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e64f4eb32c0f137682e99636816085c2553db94b.png" class="latex" alt="$(8) \phantom{a} n=150.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" />
