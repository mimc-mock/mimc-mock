<p>A rectangular floor measures <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> feet, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are positive integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/18850c5a6f67139956c8c1e35094c0a864ecb8ca.png" class="latex" alt="$b &gt; a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />. An artist paints a rectangle on the floor with the sides of the rectangle parallel to the sides of the floor. The unpainted part of the floor forms a border of width <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> foot around the painted rectangle and occupies half of the area of the entire floor. How many possibilities are there for the ordered pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/9959d47fffb760b6cf89894df252b99436e4d8f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/e/2fed69843311a5e5216d87e85fe02feac60a5d79.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{outer}=ab$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="88" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/c/35c33579cbe80bd0e5bd3415ba477b4caa17935b.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{inner}=(a-2)(b-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="179" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/757c962ba6ebb6e9ad18ac6a2ee5c1ffd1e162c9.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{outer}=2A_{inner}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="127" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4b925e74f1354017676a76257486639387f9ee0.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=2(a-2)(b-2)=2ab-4a-4b+8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="320" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ff18e6eb0f9a8e0219403005539eb6477d214cb.png" class="latex" alt="$0=ab-4a-4b+8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="161" height="14" />
</p><p>By Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/2/682a01457c9addb2ee8ad8c70cfa3948b30c2a50.png" class="latex" alt="$8=ab-4a-4b+16=(a-4)(b-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="302" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/2/9627c731ffbdf790e34a767c172cc85661cc20e7.png" class="latex" alt="$8=1\times8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/c/b3ca5723cc6684d22228499b8dd4b117e66b0504.png" class="latex" alt="$8=2\times4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> are the only positive factorings of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/4/8f48773d0453ebdf4578b43503eb2c8f3dbb9219.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)=(5,12)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/7/2b7955dbde20972bec72a6d2d6ba9f11fb41d9ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)=(6,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" /> yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/5/92582466d4e1ab0c4d7745fc87114975cd3517b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="53" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions. Notice that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9dfc685c058a3923bc44dc7a591ea65066c69dea.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, the reversed pairs are invalid.
</p>
