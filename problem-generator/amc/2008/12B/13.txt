<p>Vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> of equilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245ff420d808b2e09a77194551ff12dbfda05a19.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABE}$" width="58" height="13" /> is in the interior of unit square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> be the region consisting of all points inside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> and outside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245ff420d808b2e09a77194551ff12dbfda05a19.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABE}$" width="58" height="13" /> whose distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/6/d46ae55588d82d574ce8f6641b183151e0c39892.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{12-5\sqrt3}{72} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{12-5\sqrt3}{36} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{\sqrt3}{18} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{3-\sqrt3}{9} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{\sqrt3}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="573" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The region is the shaded area:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/3/c138ff4f242efb62680b69d5db830a4762be1b42.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C,D,E; A=(0,1); B=(1,1); C=(1,0); D=(0,0); E=(1/2,1-sqrt(3)/2); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,NW); dot(A); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,NE); dot(B); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,SE); dot(C); label(&quot;D&quot;,D,SW); dot(D); draw(A--E--B--cycle); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,S); dot(E); draw((1/3,0)--(1/3,1)); draw((2/3,0)--(2/3,1)); fill((1/3,0)--(1/3,1-sqrt(3)/3)--E--(2/3,1-sqrt(3)/3)--(2/3,0)--cycle,Black);[/asy]" width="252" height="248" />
</center>
<p>We can find the area of the shaded region by subtracting the pentagon from the middle third of the square.  The area of the middle third of the square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/6/3a6a945fea92dad88830947871c74ffe9c9c54db.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac13\right)(1)=\frac13$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="101" height="43" />.  The pentagon can be split into a rectangle and an equilateral triangle.
</p><p>The base of the equilateral triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/a/ebafcc60a28d8759185d7808c60ea53efc73938e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d43c3b121f052fbd6bdff8e00b2c0d1869e7c5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac13\right)\left(\frac12\right)(\sqrt{3})=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="174" height="45" />.  Thus, the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/8/038472e937aeb576b47ab90b60827e5e3f9ce86c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{6}\right)\left(\frac13\right)\left(\frac12\right)=\frac{\sqrt3}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="192" height="53" />.
</p><p>The base of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/a/ebafcc60a28d8759185d7808c60ea53efc73938e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and the height is the height of the equilateral triangle minus the height of the smaller equilateral triangle.  This is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/6/a06cd3ac5b3221a4286dccf7628dff86254acd51.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt3}{2}-\frac{\sqrt3}{6}=\frac{\sqrt3}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="129" height="41" />
Therefore, the area of the shaded region is
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/b/4cb577d918454570f76d3849dc7774824a1ba4a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac13-\frac{\sqrt3}{9}-\frac{\sqrt3}{36}=\boxed{\text{(B) }\frac{12-5\sqrt3}{36}}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="261" height="53" /></center>
