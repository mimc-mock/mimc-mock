<p>Two circles of radius 1 are to be constructed as follows. The center of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is chosen uniformly and at random from the line segment joining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c67f1e6c098104b1cb8f1cdd6bb16f689ac3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. The center of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is chosen uniformly and at random, and independently of the first choice, from the line segment joining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6bb1c456d71ee24e7a0ee603d226a7992e5da3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. What is the probability that circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> intersect?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/d/58db93d2e4390d8aa01383468cc1ea9e4aa5c78f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \; \frac {2 + \sqrt {2}}{4} \qquad \textbf{(B)} \; \frac {3\sqrt {3} + 2}{8} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \; \frac {2 \sqrt {2} - 1}{2} \qquad \textbf{(D)} \; \frac {2 + \sqrt {3}}{4} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \; \frac {4 \sqrt {3} - 3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="624" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Circles centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> will overlap if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are closer to each other than if the circles were tangent. The circles are tangent when the distance between their centers is equal to the sum of their radii. Thus, the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are separated by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> vertically, they must be separated by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> horizontally. Thus, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a0fef1ac0db8c65ee53c4430ac15b6a4734ec0b.png" class="latex" alt="$|A_x-B_x|&lt;\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="21" />, the circles intersect.
</p><p>Now, plot the two random variables <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/9/f894538898de931390d7742272be03b04449f4ed.png" class="latex" alt="$A_x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/0/b60c2060c41bb9e8c426356128fba3f111348014.png" class="latex" alt="$B_x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> on the coordinate plane. Each variable ranges from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. The circles intersect if the variables are within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> of each other. Thus, the area in which the circles don't intersect is equal to the total area of two small triangles on opposite corners, each of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/3/6f35ee52cd1cc2341606f72b23d9a97a4dc38985.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2-\sqrt{3})^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="41" />. So, the total area of the 2 triangles sums to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/1/09128bdeb5dd9a691577eacd6eca1334000caa1e.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-\sqrt{3})^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="21" />. Since the total <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />x<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> square has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, the probability of the circles not intersecting is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6c37db607dc32ed88d4b1c05589a7b4fd229afe.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2-\sqrt{3})^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="79" height="41" />. But remember, we want the probability that they do intersect. We conclude the probability the circles intersect is:<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/a/01a32a94e3b5c0a95d824b2e9102c14b1b7e1c05.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1-\frac{(2-\sqrt{3})^2}{4}=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\frac{4\sqrt{3}-3}{4}}.\]" width="246" height="53" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Two circles intersect if the distance between their centers is less than the sum of their radii. In this problem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> intersect iff 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/4/db406836f6ac08f24d64540ee5ae9aecca85d7af.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(A_X-B_X)^2+(A_Y-B_Y)^2}\leq2 \Rightarrow (A_X-B_X)^2+1\leq4 \Rightarrow (A_X-B_X)\leq \sqrt{3}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="622" height="32" />
</p><p>In other words, the two chosen <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates must differ by no more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />. To find this probability, we divide the problem into cases:
</p><p>1) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> is on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/a/c0a900c11637ef3f8813cc50016ab31fd5bb66e3.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,2-\sqrt{3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="21" />. The probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/f/fefc94b199d8736f0aae02486458306efdfa5da0.png" class="latex" alt="$B_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="25" height="15" /> falls within the desired range for a given <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> (on the left) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/6/8d6dd14313a3776364d5e8bc7608c1472ca28119.png" class="latex" alt="$+\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="18" /> (on the right) all over <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (the range of possible values). The total probability for this range is the sum of all these probabilities of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/f/fefc94b199d8736f0aae02486458306efdfa5da0.png" class="latex" alt="$B_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="25" height="15" /> (over the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" />) divided by the total range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> (which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />). Thus, the total probability for this interval is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/f/c0f848643cb581923471f5070b217c6b44756624.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2}\left(\int_{0}^{2-\sqrt{3}} \frac{x+\sqrt{3}}{2}\,dx\right) =\frac{1}{2}\left(x^2/4+\frac{x\sqrt{3}}{2} \Big |_{0}^{2-\sqrt{3}}\right)\]" width="384" height="53" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/7/ac76fc20002adae3d5b8ba27ea5fcea089745165.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \frac{1}{2}(\frac{4-4\sqrt{3}+3}{4} + \sqrt{3}-\frac{3}{2}) =\frac{1}{8}.\]" width="268" height="41" />
2) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> is on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d81742d9bc159a7b5bd5d82249a96da4717784d.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-\sqrt{3},\sqrt{3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="21" />. In this case, any value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/f/fefc94b199d8736f0aae02486458306efdfa5da0.png" class="latex" alt="$B_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="25" height="15" /> will do, so the probability for the interval is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/3/9a3d1ca91a0b6fab0d8475952216ec228df5e7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}-(2-\sqrt{3})}{2} = \sqrt{3}-1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="199" height="41" />.
</p><p>3) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/2804229f2accc08b1d448b6819eaf334306f17fb.png" class="latex" alt="$A_X$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="24" height="15" /> is on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/5/2a586ddf47f9ef71bec44b6da3cc2f018cef8cb0.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{3},2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="21" />. This is identical, by symmetry, to case 1.
</p><p>The total probability is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d9c5e0ef203b9558780283d9d6d25d3980d1294.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}-1+\frac{1}{4} = \frac{4\sqrt{3}-3}{4} \Rightarrow \boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="238" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We first calculate the probability that the circles at are their maximum possible distance while still intersecting. Since the difference in heights is 1, and the two radii add up to 2 (at their point of tangency), we can see that the maximum possible distance for the two centers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99f089046b752269e77100c333b11d8d72e5895.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />. If we look at possible placements for the lower point, we see that at placement <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> there is exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99f089046b752269e77100c333b11d8d72e5895.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> of space wherein we can put the top point. Similarly, at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a306093bbd9e0699c65dba033c9483cd379dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> we can put the top point anywhere, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of space. If one looks at a diagram (which I can't put here), we can see that the space available for the top point increases linearly from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/5/0f59fa2bd7a8b20c777c2c68617520eb377db55e.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-\sqrt3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="21" />. From then on to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a306093bbd9e0699c65dba033c9483cd379dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> it stops at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (this is symmetric for the other half of placements). If one draws a plot of the space available (which I again can't show here) with x determined by the x-value of the bottom point and y determined by space available for the top point, we notice two things: first, since for each (theoretically infinitesimally) small x-slice there is a line segment determining how much space there is available, if we sum up all of the line segments and divide by the total space (technically speaking by enumerating all the valid combinations and dividing by all of the general combinations), we will end up with a plot that looks like a rectangle with two small triangles cut out of it. Two, that since this is symmetric, we can just focus on the first half as the ratio of valid to total is the same. We see that the rectangle has a height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and a width of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, thus its area is 2. The triangle's height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0cfc1cb55f7ff4838f913d39d3bd1ed34cfca2.png" class="latex" alt="$2-\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="56" height="18" />, and its width is the same thus its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/a/1ea7ad536e31bc4597709b32f16f363599e3dcb1.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-\sqrt3)^2/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="21" /> =&gt; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/4/8147d7cd00356b41c73ab10da271813a2adf2280.png" class="latex" alt="$(7-4\sqrt3)/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="21" />. Now we simply subtract to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/a/acabd784e16e806c76f1a756e019575c4e5fb4bc.png" class="latex" alt="$(4\sqrt3-3)/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="21" /> is the valid area, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/4/a24e1fc9977dcaacdd5ecfc9642395b7cd861e68.png" class="latex" alt="$(4\sqrt3-3)/4 \Rightarrow \boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="151" height="24" /> is our solution.
</p>
