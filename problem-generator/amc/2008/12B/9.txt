<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are on a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/9596b5a2993d0f1ce48e542106bde5b216018861.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 6$" width="61" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of the minor arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. What is the length of the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/2/a120efd2fb6e7261a78f8962f82e20cccf62ba7f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \sqrt {10} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac {7}{2} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \sqrt {14} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \sqrt {15} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="426" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10f32377ac67d94f764f12a15ea987e88c85d3e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha$" width="11" height="8" /> be the angle that subtends the arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. By the law of cosines,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/c/2cc0e94690429f98559b390b4942de4d2d3ca920.png" class="latex" alt="$6^2=5^2+5^2-2\cdot 5\cdot 5\cos(\alpha)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="223" height="19" /> implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/2/062039b2a7f76a9ac40862a85ba9fad0d7d8dd74.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(\alpha) = 7/25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" />.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Trigonometric_identities#Half_Angle_Identities" title="Trigonometric identities"> half-angle formula</a> says that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/85734339a5e0cebf841cfdee30d16aa04890c62b.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(\alpha/2) = \frac{\sqrt{1+\cos(\alpha)}}{2} = \sqrt{\frac{32/25}{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{16}{25}} = \frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="387" height="44" />. The law of cosines tells us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/6/046b1e34ca0071d9191d219144ae1c99e49fa40d.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = \sqrt{5^2+5^2-2\cdot 5\cdot 5\cdot \frac{4}{5}} = \sqrt{50-50\frac{4}{5}} = \sqrt{10}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="399" height="43" />, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/9/d79e0e8ce41b5c08729443bcc258d13ca061ff35.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> as the midpoint of line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> the center of the circle. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> are collinear, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/8/8d8e334863383e1304e64c7c4b51cc7c26685ba1.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle ODA=90\deg$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="145" height="16" /> and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ecd101b6c2c77868420ee0455c332846d5b4058.png" class="latex" alt="$OD=\sqrt{5^2-3^2}=4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="158" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/0/f60db66b3eb0599603ec8bb64aacf34269b88291.png" class="latex" alt="$OD=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/f/a1f560ec4cefd1167823d5ffea2c025cfcee5990.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=5-4=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="126" height="13" />, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/061de056542e64695f2646f3a9ca3c02efe324bb.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=\sqrt{3^2+1^2}=\sqrt{10} \rightarrow \boxed{\text{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="232" height="23" />.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72fbe8b90fcde66500984b26ba2b38b0f7765119.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pen d = linewidth(0.7); pathpen = d; pointpen = black; pen f = fontsize(9); path p = CR((0,0),5); pair O = (0,0), A=(5,0), B = IP(p,CR(A,6)), C = IP(p,CR(A,3)), D=IP(A--B,O--C); D(p); D(MP(&quot;A&quot;,A,E)--D(MP(&quot;O&quot;,O))--MP(&quot;B&quot;,B,NE)--cycle); D(A--MP(&quot;C&quot;,C,ENE),dashed+d); D(O--C,dashed+d); D(rightanglemark(O,D(MP(&quot;D&quot;,D,W)),A)); MP(&quot;5&quot;,(A+O)/2); MP(&quot;3&quot;,(A+D)/2,SW); [/asy]" width="668" height="652" /></center>
