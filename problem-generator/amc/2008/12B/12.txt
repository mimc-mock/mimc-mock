<p>For each positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, the mean of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> terms of a sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. What is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc1744a459ad135efd3fd45fca7285cae133717.png" class="latex" alt="$2008$" width="35" height="12" />th term of the sequence?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/a/73aad1bea175917bdca8557210d0355f4ffa7a31.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2008 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 4015 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 4016 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 4030056 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 4032064$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="539" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74a070eea97d9363f58a257e05e75fc69efa753.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> be the nth partial sum of the sequence:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/7/b979bcd88927b4efea9c21c9ebade914eccf85d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S_n}{n} = n$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/4/8241d02262e92eeb6cc6b8bc18df9ad36b07486e.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n = n^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="61" height="17" />
</p><p>The only possible sequence with this result is the sequence of odd integers.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8f535140f474638c10267c95ed8340650b1fc8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = 2n - 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="93" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22e897f86520b884dddac9ea807585cae5b6b4bb.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2008} = 2(2008) - 1 = 4015 \Rightarrow \textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="268" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternate_Solution">Alternate Solution</span></h2>
<p>Letting the sum of the sequence equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b900957204f8957ec64356d7d0b64ece496647c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="13" /> yields the following two equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/c/05c056fbdde43ae8373cdeca059be1ed28931694.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2008}}{2008}=2008$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="222" height="35" /> and
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/9/1398e636e6002412eae3ed288397032425509705.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2007}}{2007}=2007$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="223" height="35" />.
</p><p>Therefore:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f992eda554939b2330b9f211d23a9f502fe0e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2008}=2008^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="225" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/0/6c00c7ea00b537ad8f298d9fa04eb684578d0340.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2007}=2007^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="225" height="17" />
</p><p>Hence, by substitution, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/7/d2784b2ebe29b0ed1fa2c0eda299f20acab140e0.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2008}=2008^2-2007^2=(2008+2007)(2008-2007)=4015(1)=4015\implies\boxed{\textbf{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="632" height="23" />
</p>
