<p>For how many values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/e/ece27ba559bd93cdf2416aa0f88fc8d7d85e5791.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,\pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/a/7bacd55a115826eb9a72c6d2c871b924bd9f11ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{ - 1}(\sin 6x) = \cos^{ - 1}(\cos x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="216" height="20" />?
Note: The functions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/3854566d017574445ba10fee430643e24c5be5e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{ - 1} = \arcsin$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="110" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/c/afc7390fa46f7c04c4d569d7c7ca91138bb447c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{ - 1} = \arccos$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="15" /> denote inverse trigonometric functions.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7cc1959fee43bf3d107adec56bb55735561f9856.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 4\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 5\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 6\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="349" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First of all, we have to agree on the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/b/d2ba28528011a90b40b11f8a3486205394f63a82.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}$" width="38" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/b/73b6c19dfb0fffdc5ae28b32e3c34d24a0e4fc3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1}$" width="40" height="15" />. This should have been a part of the problem statement -- but as it is missing, we will assume the most common definition: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef89c26327237366643348d2d8b4d59d6191dc7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\forall x: -\pi/2 \leq \sin^{-1}(x) \leq \pi/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="220" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/7/847b4d5536dbb26a973e1152c3a17cfab361995e.png" class="latex" alt="$\forall x: 0\leq \cos^{-1}(x) \leq \pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="19" />.
</p><p>Hence we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d61fa64760b1399a7fd7241a506d8d0ceaeea37.png" class="latex" alt="$\forall x\in[0,\pi]: \cos^{ - 1}(\cos x) = x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="224" height="20" />, thus our equation simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/a/69aa42cdaf5fa5040b7bd33281da11c0778afc25.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{ - 1}(\sin 6x) = x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="20" />.
</p><p>Consider the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/0/da068d8d2a4fabe3a4b354319c7f91ac94c0d43d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sin^{ - 1}(\sin 6x)  - x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="191" height="20" />. We are looking for roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/e/ece27ba559bd93cdf2416aa0f88fc8d7d85e5791.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,\pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" />.
</p><p>By analyzing properties of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/b/6fb300dacfcde1b57ad070409262b1743e0cd966.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/b/d2ba28528011a90b40b11f8a3486205394f63a82.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}$" width="38" height="15" /> (or by computing the derivative of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />) one can discover the following properties of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82d0e714dd3855ef2e740b789ae984e1d7cd5f39.png" class="latex" alt="$f(0)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is increasing and then decreasing on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/2/6c2b88355429e009436df68cc912104a62f90dad.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,\pi/6]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="54" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is decreasing and then increasing on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b42b75565813a9ea75bd5a661689508d13d3022.png" class="latex" alt="$[\pi/6,2\pi/6]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="83" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is increasing and then decreasing on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c4b5e589270e2fed2f1e01488199a3b8ba7b165.png" class="latex" alt="$[2\pi/6,3\pi/6]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="92" height="18" />.</li></ul>
<p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/7/e07bcf763e1bd2c2ca89cd31cb0cbf615156d3b0.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\pi/6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/b/b6b1aee07a77899102af092c112b7c914a153055.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sin^{-1} (\sin \pi) - \pi/6 = -\pi/6 &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="302" height="20" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> has exactly one root on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/1/e71ecb3fed909569db6f49f6ad922180d12cedc6.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,\pi/6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/d/81da3429f8948b4231a5733fc06621f75122c747.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2\pi/6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/3/903c86eec090a44b82955f4f24dcbbcf6b33f07a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sin^{-1} (\sin 2\pi) - 2\pi/6 = -2\pi/6 &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="329" height="20" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is negative on the entire interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b42b75565813a9ea75bd5a661689508d13d3022.png" class="latex" alt="$[\pi/6,2\pi/6]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="83" height="18" />.
</p><p>Now note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8ce4373b14ab71303fbad296f73a5e9b7de3244a.png" class="latex" alt="$\forall t: \sin^{-1}(t) \leq \pi/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="20" />. Hence for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/3/cc3493307d96e840605b2a4f0ab544f719f92979.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; 3\pi/6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93e817ec91cc1d083397e9cbef9bed3b8677a0c4.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />, and we can easily check that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/f/34fbc14a3da825fb88b0a8d88500ba750bfa2dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$f(3\pi/6)&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" /> as well.
</p><p>Thus the only unknown part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/2/e12935daa7f15650c994946a6cfcf708c0053a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$(2\pi/6,3\pi/6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />. On this interval, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is negative in both endpoints, and we know that it is first increasing and then decreasing. Hence there can be zero, one, or two roots on this interval.
</p><p>To prove that there are two roots, it is enough to find any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> from this interval such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/8/1e8ca0c3bf089cc7a01c1a854ad5d4cd5db42917.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />.
</p><p>A good guess is its midpoint, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/b/feb5f0acfe65454d1a6a2861c0cc90b3449ad21c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=5\pi/12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />, where the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/7/cb7046761ee60c14acd1e8b853aeab9d2f0deb45.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin 6x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="20" /> has its local maximum. We can evaluate:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5aa5507f678958cd098ae9411fa00125b0b9356.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sin^{-1}(\sin 5\pi/2) - 5\pi/12 = \pi/2 - 5\pi/12 = \pi/12 &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="465" height="20" />.
</p><p>Summary: The function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/2494711a9f0508844ca4716f548a3252bc93cc6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> roots on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/e/ece27ba559bd93cdf2416aa0f88fc8d7d85e5791.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,\pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" />: the first one is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, the second one is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/1/e71ecb3fed909569db6f49f6ad922180d12cedc6.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,\pi/6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />, and the last two are in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/2/e12935daa7f15650c994946a6cfcf708c0053a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$(2\pi/6,3\pi/6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />.
</p><p>Actual solutions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d33dfe8048117855dfe817e152bec860d0a1fccb.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\pi/7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/6/fd64db33151693347b14047e91d81090da887943.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2\pi/5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/5/545942f01f38ea02d4b82385b5aec17c36baccd8.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3\pi/7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/e/1fee8ef6d622b0f5faf5c5e815e0f5d22e6cf33a.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq \cos^{-1}(a) \leq \pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="19" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, the equation reduces to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/5/855d25d21489a366f6a03425aa0c91beb9f85dee.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="20" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820446dd181a8d498054668e7ab6964e3dda62ef.png" class="latex" alt="$-\pi/2 \leq \sin^{-1}(a) \leq \pi/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="20" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f57b6e3d3e6e0301492a7024abb407f45b8066d0.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \leq x \leq \pi/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />. To make the problem easier, we will measure angles in degrees. We will consider each sixth of the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5daf9a3453957d06229794a69a60a1b9be9a3b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, 90]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="43" height="18" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/2/7a2d8c1990612cd9aea964683d26d52ed79f4734.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \leq x \leq 15$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the first quadrant. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/e/7eebc6cc7d107e4d4b5bc5f74894a66ff6ca92b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 6x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="153" height="20" />. Setting this equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> yields the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d348bde3e15456e71734dc2c56fc7425c95927f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0$" width="43" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/b/48b784a99e7b2ddbfff40ce11f2c5b180c24cdc8.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \leq x \leq 30$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the second quadrant. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/604850293452bcf3b23ed961ca50b5ae9ac01a8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 180 - 6x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="20" />. This yields the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/3/283ef348f8eeac5f68ab5562cb819ee110b18c57.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{180}7$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c7c4610f2baf66571791c69c9065421aa319ab6.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \leq x \leq 45$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the third quadrant. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/604850293452bcf3b23ed961ca50b5ae9ac01a8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 180 - 6x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="20" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/9/c3918445349e52b82e281569fafd062ac074b498.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{180}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="29" height="37" /> is not on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c7c4610f2baf66571791c69c9065421aa319ab6.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \leq x \leq 45$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, this yields no solution.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/5/d0563a9f657ca7d7fa3c8abba62b61aac5826ffc.png" class="latex" alt="$45 \leq x \leq 60$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the fourth quadrant. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/c/a1c8fed63e0f7b40120cc06909c221c31d39290a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 6x - 360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="20" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" /> is not on the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/5/d0563a9f657ca7d7fa3c8abba62b61aac5826ffc.png" class="latex" alt="$45 \leq x \leq 60$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, this yields no solution.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/8/4686863c5a654d6e7071791f2cd7fe3ae7b056bd.png" class="latex" alt="$60 \leq x \leq 75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the first quadrant plus a full revolution. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/c/a1c8fed63e0f7b40120cc06909c221c31d39290a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 6x - 360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="20" />. This yields the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d019a7f54fe21eaf1d20e17e48a48c9e7052fac.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 72$" width="52" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/9/5d985fc17de71dd1abe613312fb982b08c5f5161.png" class="latex" alt="$75 \leq x \leq 90$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3c6262fbacae6ba07fea6f24758dde08078e97.png" class="latex" alt="$6x$" width="19" height="12" /> is in the second quadrant plus a full revolution. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff584902fe6a5c679354b72ac34605358f6d293d.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1}(\sin(6x)) = 540 - 6x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="20" />. This yields the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/8/a889e6a0b541fd69aef2aea41b91846abb30855e.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{540}7$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/2494711a9f0508844ca4716f548a3252bc93cc6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> solutions, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d33dfe8048117855dfe817e152bec860d0a1fccb.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\pi/7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/6/fd64db33151693347b14047e91d81090da887943.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2\pi/5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/5/545942f01f38ea02d4b82385b5aec17c36baccd8.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3\pi/7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Algebraically, the inverse function of a function should just cancel out, leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/e/b2e00b276aa82612b45b6bc192850ef997131dd1.png" class="latex" alt="$6x=x$" width="53" height="12" />. However, upon inspection we find that the graphs of these "inverse function of the function" equations are also periodic, like their normal trig function counterparts, due to the fact that inverse trig functions will never return any angle value higher than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />. But instead of a smooth wave, these graphs are made up of zigzags with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />. Trying a few values, we see that
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f701224374da8d8948314d6ff0d73bc4930811.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \arcsin{\sin{x}}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="15" />
</p><p><br />
peaks at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/e/aeebb746f8c0d3dac5219c3635617032430afee9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" /> and
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/1/ac14a5d69789d4c06a52b714ea50072dd5a800df.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \arccos{\cos{x}}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="11" />
</p><p><br />
peaks at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />
</p><p><br />
But we want the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/4/6c45b6c4c288b443d100df25014ca11748d36c0c.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \arcsin{(\sin6{x})}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="18" />, which has a period of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/2/2d2d0dc818ffa40c824ec87c9ad9c51fa86f458d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" /> instead of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />. So this means the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a151f5b93ce5735f72cdd79c46051f52b9d820d.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" /> will show <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> periods instead of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a period. Visually it would be lines with slopes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/8/3683f227b0b53e6a30087db2fa3b752965ecf05d.png" class="latex" alt="$-6$" width="22" height="12" />. Using the graph paper given to us, we plot out the two equations according to the above and we see that they intersect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f2c5dde6d634addd96d3374cc9f3729329de82b.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \boxed{\text{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="46" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The most conventional domain and range for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e146c26dbab001e3b9a71335366fee61bb807f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1} x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="17" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/4/f849ad24b7130dd7c6714e9399745fa166300829.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in [-1, 1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="81" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/5/96567fb1bc7e61a3353f952ca28e0ed98331ec7f.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1} x \in [0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="118" height="21" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11f986c49f29d7a2ed4c28cab7319fa2ee3ee978.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x$" width="37" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5d34ca397a7ca42a7d29fe0e311c5f486cd5721.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in [0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/9/7f97ae4b3d5a75d2842b61d066fa40021a184e91.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x \in [-1, 1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="112" height="20" />. Since the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11f986c49f29d7a2ed4c28cab7319fa2ee3ee978.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x$" width="37" height="8" /> is equal to the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e146c26dbab001e3b9a71335366fee61bb807f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1} x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="17" />, and the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11f986c49f29d7a2ed4c28cab7319fa2ee3ee978.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x$" width="37" height="8" /> is equal to the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e146c26dbab001e3b9a71335366fee61bb807f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1} x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="17" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/d/cbdfd3d3355746d9064c9cc08ac629a6a8f29d08.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{-1} (\cos x) = x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="131" height="21" />.
</p><p>Meaning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/a/69aa42cdaf5fa5040b7bd33281da11c0778afc25.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{ - 1}(\sin 6x) = x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/a/40adef2b5b0b6af5a896feb841c575baabbababa.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin x = \sin (6x)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="118" height="20" />.
</p><p>By graphing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/8/6e8f3a072de64627a11a2df663e577eb43d60c77.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin x$" width="35" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5b0d55491470ce8102abcb996a2d217b22dd428.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin 6x$" width="44" height="12" /> together, it can be seen that there are 7 intersections for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5d34ca397a7ca42a7d29fe0e311c5f486cd5721.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in [0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="18" />.
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2009AMC12BP24.png" class="image"><img alt="2009AMC12BP24.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/2/25/2009AMC12BP24.png/500px-2009AMC12BP24.png" width="500" height="423" srcset="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/2/25/2009AMC12BP24.png/750px-2009AMC12BP24.png 1.5x, https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/2/25/2009AMC12BP24.png/1000px-2009AMC12BP24.png 2x" /></a></div></div>
<p>However, the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/e/3be4749990d7cabfc10fa21eec347a839f8b1d2c.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{-1} x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="17" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/f/fdf06c5032ed273c13ec85180eddd75c8c5c163f.png" class="latex" alt="$[-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="33" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/4/2f42efc828c1ded6bdadb488a646baf9ff6eb45c.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in [0, \frac{\pi}{2}]$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="33" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/1/0418408e800325466692d985566ab8a8d169301d.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \sin^{ - 1}(\sin 6x)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="136" height="22" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/2494711a9f0508844ca4716f548a3252bc93cc6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/4/2f42efc828c1ded6bdadb488a646baf9ff6eb45c.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in [0, \frac{\pi}{2}]$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="33" />.
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> solutions are respectively:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/9/3c9e11bf1ce45bd9dab455bd8a77c57279601f56.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 6x$" width="53" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d348bde3e15456e71734dc2c56fc7425c95927f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0$" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/3/a131bf06922898a8ab26d29c3f14902ba2cfe394.png" class="latex" alt="$x + 2 \pi = 6x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/9/c296f3ebd2dcbb43ba292914e321d3bd28c2591b.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac25 \pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="61" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/3/1230123d99a5a09e44622b506726257a1a30499b.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi - x = 6x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="89" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/4/4743d4804b2433a07c0f67e93240cd124af67ed8.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{\pi}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="48" height="33" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/a/98a0aaef2ff06027b4524dc00781a8dcf7913aa5.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \pi - x = 6x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/4/fa4e8adec2f169aa7780fa3891f3925824cea2e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac37 \pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="61" height="38" />
</p><p>~<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/User:Isabelchen">isabelchen</a>
