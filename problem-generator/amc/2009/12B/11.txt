<p>On Monday, Millie puts a quart of seeds, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of which are millet, into a bird feeder.  On each successive day she adds another quart of the same mix of seeds without removing any seeds that are left.  Each day the birds eat only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the millet in the feeder, but they eat all of the other seeds.  On which day, just after Millie has placed the seeds, will the birds find that more than half the seeds in the feeder are millet?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e8bbe5dcc18c8b53e23c4f4979f9a427c00da04.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{Tuesday}\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \text{Wednesday}\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \text{Thursday}\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \text{Friday}\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \text{Saturday}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="625" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>On Monday, day 1, the birds find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d48fb052d7fa270cfe6d5a0b5ef1f1cb1c2ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> quart of millet in the feeder.  On Tuesday they find
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/0/c4050b4283bef8e8479cea9c214ea95a608e378a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac 14 + \frac 34 \cdot \frac 14\]" width="71" height="38" />
quarts of millet.  On Wednesday, day 3, they find
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/4/6c4238653fc34a7f23ef0e4efcd926c173ee0c21.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac 14 + \frac 34 \cdot \frac 14 + \left(\frac34\right)^2 \cdot \frac 14\]" width="166" height="46" />
quarts of millet.  The number of quarts of millet they find on day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d743236100c927b7f7d555e4cb9fbd2deaa41520.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac 14 + \frac 34 \cdot \frac 14 + \left(\frac34\right)^2 \cdot \frac 14 + \cdots + \left(\frac 34\right)^{n-1} \cdot \frac 14 = \frac {(\frac 14)(1 - (\frac 34)^n)}{1 - \frac 34} = 1 - \left(\frac 34\right)^n .\]" width="502" height="48" />
The birds always find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/1/d21715ef3fb94d12fc089969a648efb480cd5b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> quart of other seeds, so more than half the seeds are millet if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/5/5b584986ae0df9a125d12335f74c5d19a1877bfb.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - \left(\frac 34\right)^n &gt; \frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="115" height="44" />, that is, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93d34b223c61801ea86f7dac9e5a28bae538194.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 34\right)^n &lt; \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="84" height="44" />.  Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a753d74be0fdd6c3a04c049d1fd316fe5763a7c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 34\right)^4 = \frac {81}{256} &gt; \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="138" height="46" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e86c0cace261a5fac8006ed6f3ada2a3c6c658cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 34\right)^5 = \frac {243}{1024} &lt; \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="147" height="46" />, this will first occur on day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/4/a3462a48474f58c9f03792f3861279165d70485e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\text{Friday}}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="58" height="26" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>

</p><p>~savannahsolver
