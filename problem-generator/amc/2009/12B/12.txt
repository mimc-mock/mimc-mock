<p>The fifth and eighth terms of a geometric sequence of real numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce11f9bca5e74ba532323e045b62e2793d120b7e.png" class="latex" alt="$7!$" width="12" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/7/9370839bbb8a8037471714de7c7c5a52804b5a75.png" class="latex" alt="$8!$" width="12" height="13" /> respectively.  What is the first term?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/9/8b9d35841e130f115b44c92328db73716d0cfe81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 60\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 75\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 120\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 225\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 315$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="414" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th term of the series be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/495f96bd9e71dffc512f0370f4708797192b52f9.png" class="latex" alt="$ar^{n-1}$" width="42" height="15" />.  Because
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4acfa5ec8bb1e8e6fc08dbc68222f4e3c6fa3a4f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac {8!}{7!} = \frac {ar^7}{ar^4} = r^3 = 8,\]" width="147" height="39" />
it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4bf9ecce9aa4520dc266d8e492bbf44fbacfd19.png" class="latex" alt="$r = 2$" width="41" height="12" /> and the first term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/4/364490180e78a9040a4075dffc113c90539c5494.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \frac {7!}{r^4} = \frac {7!}{16} = \boxed{315}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="163" height="38" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/3/3a3faf1572d785ef1d5d72f120fb3a16190b4b61.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
