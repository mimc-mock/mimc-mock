<p>Trapezoid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/4/834829da086ed651644d6091100aba2bc9611eee.png" class="latex" alt="$AD||BC$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/7/c17586f266d8a614e45964038dbd11de47c7389e.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/8/298b55d1b105f19e8ec15cd3cf37c1dedce6ff52.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DBA = 23^{\circ}$" width="105" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/c/b9ca5e8a969703d4b2af0f496dd416ed320f2ade.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDC = 46^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" />.  The ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fc0676c527bea73515b0eeef409cd7154ac3a0e.png" class="latex" alt="$BC: AD$" width="72" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/e/49e8520691de7e23593f5612af80aaa342b5f158.png" class="latex" alt="$9: 5$" width="32" height="12" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58c11b9b80ccae0f60fa77285d28cd0e3435eef.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 79\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 45\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac {13}{15}\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 89\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac {14}{15}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="379" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Extend <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/8/238f77f5bd36cadced68f029e4554015289e50ee.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/e/c4e6778bf0a1850f765de0f9458f3cb37a155c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {DC}$" width="30" height="15" /> to meet at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />.  Then
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/1/a0170f547f192b5dc52d51ebcf9d047f80424a8c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \angle BED &amp;= 180^{\circ} - \angle EDB - \angle DBE\\ &amp;= 180^{\circ} - 134^{\circ} -23^{\circ} = 23^{\circ}. \end{align*}" width="275" height="38" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/b/16b4072dec30604cbea5998c12fddd9eacfeb564.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDE$" width="60" height="13" /> is isosceles with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8cee5a981c016f765420bc29f4b3c86ac9a53468.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = BD$" width="84" height="12" />.  Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/3/74373ddd84ad3fccb8a7371621568b9bb3a23979.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {AD} \parallel \overline {BC}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="77" height="20" />, it follows that the triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/91275aa1f85be8f39ea3ae3cef68f10100fc664b.png" class="latex" alt="$BCE$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70422100db8de33f8e5e0ab5062c5bb6a5ac969d.png" class="latex" alt="$ADE$" width="43" height="13" /> are similar.  Therefore
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/4/934256dbe10381b77781fcd59b44f2e9f2b58dd1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac 95 = \frac {BC}{AD} = \frac {CD + DE}{DE} = \frac {CD}{BD} + 1 = CD + 1,\]" width="358" height="37" />
so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/d/04da009c941d413d2d4c814be25ec449b5e98dd2.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = \boxed{\frac 45}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="82" height="48" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/d/4fdb04dc5fb97c6e3d0ce8bf6c006af2f123bbb7.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> and the line through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79bed271f95e561702a143b5ac163281a17df8ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {AB}.$" width="32" height="15" />  By construction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0aba4c354d1368128375fae0b08beebda2851b94.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = AD$" width="82" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfc4d0b6f948c6842a9e3da80d839ef808af4ddc.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDE = 23^{\circ}$" width="106" height="13" />; it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> is the bisector of the angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4057c3e63298c2ed6715003afb6f2d9dc6272037.png" class="latex" alt="$BDC$" width="44" height="12" />. So by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Angle_Bisector_Theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Angle Bisector Theorem">Angle Bisector Theorem</a> we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/9/fb9c80b3d0fa143e0ad56d426d4b41b5abcf9c40.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CD = \frac {CD}{BD} = \frac {EC}{BE} = \frac {BC - BE}{BE} = \frac {BC}{AD} -1 = \frac 95 - 1 = \boxed{\frac 45}.\]" width="463" height="48" />
The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
