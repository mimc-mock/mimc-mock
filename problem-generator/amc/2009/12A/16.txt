<p>A circle with center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is tangent to the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axes and externally tangent to the circle centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. What is the sum of all possible radii of the circle with center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c636728460bb7e2dfea229a64f8705023390f8f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 8 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the radius of our circle. For it to be tangent to the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> axes, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/7/2a72eda99c2e130d05a4d80be9b85f2fa0460ae2.png" class="latex" alt="$C=(r,r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />. For the circle to be externally tangent to the circle centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the distance between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> must be exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/0/080bdd4b53cf3c5d997f0ee91d272b451f0827d6.png" class="latex" alt="$r+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="13" />.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> the distance between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/6/a76bb04cc1ad44439278c1c3e84ee59386dff41f.png" class="latex" alt="$(r,r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b5e69f80156b6e186ac0d57ce541bc70182c3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{ (r-3)^2 + r^2 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="118" height="32" />, hence we get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/8/aa88b75f8bd2aa6bcc3e28d1a85a3cb242f22c59.png" class="latex" alt="$(r-3)^2 + r^2 = (r+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="19" />.
</p><p>Simplifying, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/2/6720be6ba31893c0dcc2b9382167dc73c7009876.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2 - 8r + 8 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="16" />. By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a> the sum of the two roots of this equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e81ca9c5c8715be868438500743202d6773d05bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{8}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p><p>(We should actually solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> to verify that there are two distinct positive roots. In this case we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/8/0c890370c352c3013e8f5d42ae72a3a3438dd3e1.png" class="latex" alt="$r=4\pm 2\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="18" />. This is generally a good rule of thumb, but is not necessary as all of the available answers are integers, and the equation obviously doesn't factor as integers. You can also tell that there are two positive roots based on the visual interpretation.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/9/1f9e3359dbc3787009e504d0ccc9d485a3c74ef1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.5cm); defaultpen(0.8); filldraw( Circle( (3,0), 1 ), lightgray, black ); draw( (0,0) -- (15,0), Arrow ); draw( (0,0) -- (0,15), Arrow ); draw( (0,0) -- (15,15), dashed ); real r1 = 4 - 2*sqrt(2), r2 = 4 + 2*sqrt(2); pair S1=(r1,r1), S2=(r2,r2); dot(S1); dot(S2); dot((3,0)); draw( Circle(S1,r1) ); draw( Circle(S2,r2) ); [/asy]" width="362" height="382" />
</p>
