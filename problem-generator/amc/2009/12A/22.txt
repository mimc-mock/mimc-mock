<p>A regular <a href="/wiki/index.php/Octahedron" title="Octahedron">octahedron</a> has side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Plane" title="Plane">plane</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parallel" title="Parallel">parallel</a> to two of its opposite faces cuts the octahedron into the two congruent solids. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Polygon" title="Polygon">polygon</a> formed by the intersection of the plane and the octahedron has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/6249def9636bd181110169c4a038967452998189.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {a\sqrt {b}}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are positive integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are relatively prime, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is not divisible by the square of any prime. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/d/2cdfeae33c1e09de6207d446beb72396c04be481.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b2184568eb34bf760b3c0b512f45a828cf19fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 11\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 12\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 13\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Firstly, note that the intersection of the plane must be a hexagon. Consider the net of the octahedron. Notice that the hexagon becomes a line on the net. Also, notice that, given the parallel to the faces conditions, the line must be parallel to precisely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the sides of the net. Now, notice that, through symmetry, 2 of the hexagon's vertexes lie on the midpoint of the side of the "square" in the octahedron. In the net, the condition gives you that one of the intersections of the line with the net have to be on the midpoint of the side. However, if one is on the midpoint, because of the parallel conditions, all of the vertices are on the midpoint of a side. Thus, we have a regular hexagon with a side length of the midline of an equilateral triangle with side length 1, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Thus, the answer is<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/0/440e38a01f971bb0c511362a32dd7164f3b3c65b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {3\sqrt {3}}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/7/c971086992d81bdf87485975c0baedf0a210400a.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 14\ \mathbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />.
</p><p>(Can somebody clarify this and provide a diagram?)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The plane cuts the octahedron into two congruent solids, which allows us to consider only the cut through the top half (a square pyramid). Because the cut is parallel to one side of the pyramid and must create two congruent solids, we can see that it must take the shape of a trapezoid with right, left, and top sides being 0.5 and a bottom side of 1. This is equivalent to a unit equilateral triangle with the tip cut off at the midpoint. Accordingly, the area of the polygon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/1/5a19082a74dc724a6b944143d955e27ae2e083bf.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="101" height="41" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/7/5a7b8a8cd19b0f12c14b69446954d57f67adbc71.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/d/6ddc12c3a7e7649e8e9276403f1253c06871591f.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 14$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />.
</p>
