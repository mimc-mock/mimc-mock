</p><p>Note: here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/1/fd1bf954f4f3fd597d4b04bd5e283b16d646b571.png" class="latex" alt="$i = \sqrt { - 1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/b/ecbfc33a6bee1480ed396cd653ebfc64aed7b0f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 48 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 49 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 97 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 98$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/c/d9c05382bed2cb37291348dff6dc10c063db6580.png" class="latex" alt="$i^x$" width="13" height="12" /> cycles every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> powers so we group the sum in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />s. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f09b0de8df95e7e02cf393fe2956958f07dea08.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[i+2i^2+3i^3+4i^4=2-2i\]" width="210" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/9553c2d3cf26301ec0593bdd2f366cab20807545.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5i^5+6i^6+7i^7+8i^8=2-2i\]" width="227" height="16" />
</p><p>We can postulate that every group of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/1/c9142d256076c13f861b1db42ae0c98ad7c474bf.png" class="latex" alt="$2-2i$" width="46" height="12" />.
For 24 groups we thus, get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/8043fc7f48909e67f147dff8d98e9b766d6b617e.png" class="latex" alt="$48-48i$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" /> as our sum. 
We know the solution must lie near
The next term is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/9/73931ba6918dc6bee28054cccd4c98cebee2e475.png" class="latex" alt="$24*4+1=97$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="118" height="14" />th term. This term is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/0/7c0b9c038311446a77988cf11315aa70b54da817.png" class="latex" alt="$97i$" width="23" height="12" /> (first in a group of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/8/d88552e0d89c8c157f42fd5c649ee01b7dd964bf.png" class="latex" alt="$i^{97}=i$" width="50" height="15" />) and our sum is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/9/d49375eee67c4142d47fd3a92c6f27fda75a2852.png" class="latex" alt="$48+49i$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="64" height="14" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/236345faf4068ea8198ad1392795284184fe1b3b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=97\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="109" height="24" /> is our answer
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Obviously, even powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> are real and odd powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> are imaginary. 
Hence the real part of the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/1/371b5863222df9a2c0dbed2adb9f338179a06f90.png" class="latex" alt="$2i^2 + 4i^4 + 6i^6 + \ldots$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="16" />, and 
the imaginary part is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/f/43f43f77660880f73c9dcb44f8549ba6c68ea60c.png" class="latex" alt="$i + 3i^3 + 5i^5 + \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="138" height="16" />.
</p><p>Let's take a look at the real part first. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/6/a56bfe567aec24da391224dc2f10e8cc4ef626dc.png" class="latex" alt="$i^2=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />, hence the real part simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/8/d58a5c0b66e1ed969b1e5d8bc93288591b3493f8.png" class="latex" alt="$-2+4-6+8-10+\cdots$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="199" height="14" />.
If there were an odd number of terms, we could pair them as follows: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db0a2c9ce5cb58f3f4ad3b531eed137bdea6a879.png" class="latex" alt="$-2 + (4-6) + (8-10) + \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="227" height="18" />, hence the result would be negative. As we need the real part to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, we must have an even number of terms. If we have an even number of terms, we can pair them as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/a/91a87e8d3a2783b1cc1086013a1d4a0b32a92085.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2+4) + (-6+8) + \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="18" />. Each parenthesis is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> of them, and the last value used is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" />. This happens for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/480b030f764b16a048ab438ab9199bc3faebab0b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=96$" width="52" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/d/1bdee9fb80fb747c1ffb74890edba0489ad7b4f4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=97$" width="53" height="12" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/480b030f764b16a048ab438ab9199bc3faebab0b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=96$" width="52" height="12" /> is not present as an option, we may conclude that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/c/7ac249b5f3b49a06acf3967a4dc70def1d8e7e89.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{97}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />.
</p><p>In a complete solution, we should now verify which of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/480b030f764b16a048ab438ab9199bc3faebab0b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=96$" width="52" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/d/1bdee9fb80fb747c1ffb74890edba0489ad7b4f4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=97$" width="53" height="12" /> will give us the correct imaginary part.
</p><p>We can rewrite the imaginary part as follows: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f23c603a806ae92cc7d204bf4fe67f60d613d53.png" class="latex" alt="$i + 3i^3 + 5i^5 + \cdots = i(1 + 3i^2 + 5i^4 + \cdots) = i(1 - 3 + 5 - \cdots)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="491" height="19" />. We need to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/8/2183dd48c7c08b1a96f54fc75e1bb5c1780738a7.png" class="latex" alt="$(1 - 3 + 5 - \cdots) = 49$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="18" />. Once again we can repeat the same reasoning: If the number of terms were even, the left hand side would be negative, thus the number of terms is odd. The left hand side can then be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/48194240a5b0ec295891c37016b150e3c242f927.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + (-3+5) + (-7+9) + \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="233" height="18" />. We need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> parentheses, therefore the last value used is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/239430b0fc1c7fe38f296a8845ed7446198bda7e.png" class="latex" alt="$97$" width="18" height="12" />. This happens when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/d/1bdee9fb80fb747c1ffb74890edba0489ad7b4f4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=97$" width="53" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b282a90bb302e7975eaa294c0791da4d8614522.png" class="latex" alt="$n=98$" width="52" height="12" />, and we are done.
</p>
