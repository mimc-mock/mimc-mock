<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/8/008b0301eafca4213de4a432183d30f4d9fa83ae.png" class="latex" alt="$p(x) = x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="197" height="19" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are complex numbers.  Suppose that
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/2/1724d4fbd29ca94c593585be9881983817b0e269.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[p(2009 + 9002\pi i) = p(2009) = p(9002) = 0\]" width="337" height="18" /></center>
<p>What is the number of nonreal zeros of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/5/1b5cdc37f358cfc03115a3b9c4a99b5403bf6316.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{12} + ax^8 + bx^4 + c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="152" height="16" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/b/2eb4e03c07624ae92d7c219a385e4db23af0b018.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 6\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 8\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 10\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>From the three zeroes, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/c/cbc9577a341663a12e26968c2685a4398ab0b5ef.png" class="latex" alt="$p(x) = (x - (2009 + 9002\pi i))(x - 2009)(x - 9002)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="396" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/0/2d09873577510e1d7b9910a69a47f30c8ec81728.png" class="latex" alt="$p(x^4) = (x^4 - (2009 + 9002\pi i))(x^4 - 2009)(x^4 - 9002)=x^{12}+ax^8+bx^4+c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="604" height="19" />.
</p><p>Let's do each factor case by case:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/0/92071145c521aa088fdd9a9190d5fd6e9fd80c50.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4 - (2009 + 9002\pi i) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="199" height="19" />: Clearly, all the fourth roots are going to be complex.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd30381aebf08886cf91949178ed2c93b83d5666.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4 - 2009 = 0$" width="109" height="15" />: The real roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/4/6b4295126a7f866b0b02c2a27927d6fbdb37db8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm \sqrt [4]{2009}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />, and there are two complex roots.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/0/920601fc68363fc52b7e56c0f7666fc3b01ad8ce.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4 - 9002 = 0$" width="109" height="15" />: The real roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626b95f70ce28fb6a5281a84e3c3bf1a3469101f.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm \sqrt [4]{9002}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />, and there are two complex roots.</li></ul>
<p>So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/7/a47d90bd5a2bead78c9f8176b4830683ab62f2ed.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 2 + 2 = 8\ \mathbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="138" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_Thinking">Alternative Thinking</span></h2>
<p>Consider the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/0/2f0078b54474db2dbf6219e8386b9b62c4405c94.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4$" width="17" height="15" />. It is similar to a parabola, but with a wider "base". First examine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a9f6671242b5611706ade720c3feacf7428c1f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-2009$" width="75" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/9/53947f331db67dd2d7376cb10dc020f3444d39a3.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-9002$" width="75" height="15" />. Clearly they are just being translated down some large amount. This will result in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis being crossed twice, indicating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> real zeroes. From the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Fundamental_Theorem_of_Algebra" title="Fundamental Theorem of Algebra">Fundamental Theorem of Algebra</a> we know that a polynomial must have exactly as many roots as its highest degree, so we are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/3/e83804a0dc46a7c2245f307fc9dfc287ac6d531b.png" class="latex" alt="$4-2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> nonreal roots for each of the graphs. For the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/d/eade63db2a77005757691401cf99141409674add.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-(2009+9002\pi i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="19" />, it's not even possible to graph it on the Cartesian plane, so all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> roots will be nonreal. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/1584655eafb8eb7fa5db57f0b45cc950247d2e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$2+2+4 = 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="104" height="14" /> total nonreal roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/2/9a2fcf7ed738da955f8e2c141cb39c144cc8b1a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \boxed{\text{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="47" height="23" />.
</p>
