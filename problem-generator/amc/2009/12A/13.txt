<p>A ship sails <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles in a straight line from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, turns through an angle between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d5c45388de5c75af641c046d466de3592ffa98.png" class="latex" alt="$60^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />, and then sails another <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> miles to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> be measured in miles. Which of the following intervals contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" />?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/b/42be593c3f7699bcb004014182d54b6832e3e173.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair B=(0,0), A=(-10,0), C=20*dir(50);  draw(A--B--C); draw(A--C,linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;));  dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),S); label(&quot;$20$&quot;,midpoint(B--C),SE); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NE); [/asy]" width="255" height="182" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84fccd7eb1e9400e2a0aa5812bff8a514ff809e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ [400,500] \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ [500,600] \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ [600,700] \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ [700,800]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="526" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/2/8826e8fe741a9605e10471adb1e265a77bac9485.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}\ [800,900]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Answering_the_question">Answering the question</span></h3>
<p>To answer the question we are asked, it is enough to compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> for two different angles, preferably for both extremes (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees). You can use the law of cosines to do so.
</p><p>Alternately, it is enough to compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> for one of the extreme angles. In case it falls inside one of the given intervals, we are done. In case it falls on the boundary between two options, we also have to argue whether our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> is the minimal or the maximal possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" />.
</p><p>Below we show a complete solution in which we also show that all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> do indeed lie in the given interval.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Complete_solution">Complete solution</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70b97eb4ad6e4e7a5a97e2547d9ed6e965960d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> be the point the ship would reach if it turned <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb649f063149ebc97d3ee58c4fd38d4c440846.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b65e6facfe122c182fe14d503aeace2c52a9739d.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> the point it would reach if it turned <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />. Obviously, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70b97eb4ad6e4e7a5a97e2547d9ed6e965960d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> is the furthest possible point from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b65e6facfe122c182fe14d503aeace2c52a9739d.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> is the closest possible point to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />.
</p><p>Hence the interval of possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/5/9359fc01c92a02b8e99e2d08b755e4aed83627d3.png" class="latex" alt="$[AC_2^2,AC_1^2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="20" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/1/1215197384d9ae9699a6f019b0e503fbff3646b0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair B=(0,0), A=(-10,0), C1=20*dir(45), C2=20*dir(60);  draw(A--B--C1); draw(A--C1,linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;)); draw(A--B--C2); draw(A--C2,linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;));  draw( arc(A, length(C1-A), 0, 55 ), dotted ); draw( arc(A, length(C2-A), 0, 55 ), dotted ); draw( arc(B, C1, C2) );  dot(A); dot(B); dot(C1); dot(C2); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),S); label(&quot;$20$&quot;,midpoint(B--C1),SE); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C_1$&quot;,C1,NE); label(&quot;$C_2$&quot;,C2,N); [/asy]" width="418" height="345" />
</p><p>We can find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/1/c71d112b8ca199da66fff4ba11f06480fefa465a.png" class="latex" alt="$AC_1^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/9/89988e02f9c13400e3957fc964eb8105fe145d10.png" class="latex" alt="$AC_2^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="19" /> as follows:
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/c/88c7f79185a0b48d33454e85f44b4bfdf81d2420.png" class="latex" alt="$D_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/0/4d01cc94a123a0c2b5239d4342be2e6ce7f3fc53.png" class="latex" alt="$D_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> be the feet of the heights from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70b97eb4ad6e4e7a5a97e2547d9ed6e965960d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b65e6facfe122c182fe14d503aeace2c52a9739d.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. The angles in the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5a92d82d58eec3ea61b271d95017ffd94cf076e.png" class="latex" alt="$BD_1C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="55" height="15" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb649f063149ebc97d3ee58c4fd38d4c440846.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb649f063149ebc97d3ee58c4fd38d4c440846.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/c/e8c9a5f0fe54b958e919fbcf4beba124092c7809.png" class="latex" alt="$BD_1 = D_1C_1 = BC_1 / \sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="197" height="21" />. Similarly, the angles in the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8bb3e5907791396b9b7e0d4f6ad3a1101f89f47.png" class="latex" alt="$BD_2C_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="56" height="15" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919bf5d461970fc8d905d63704ef763c08594184.png" class="latex" alt="$30^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/d/5dd2223e2e50a706b8a59999794d33c3183d156e.png" class="latex" alt="$BD_2 = BC_2 / 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/9/6a96542b0bc40dbda0a7cebe70e18276e65167bc.png" class="latex" alt="$D_2C_2 = BC_2 \sqrt 3 / 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="21" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/881ac37790730b4a4a8e3e259563d0fe59c665fa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair B=(0,0), A=(-10,0), C1=20*dir(45), C2=20*dir(60);  draw(A--B--C1); draw(A--C1,linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;));  dot(A); dot(B); dot(C1);  pair D1 = (C1.x,0); dot(D1); draw(B--D1--C1);  label(&quot;$10$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),S); label(&quot;$20$&quot;,midpoint(B--C1),SE); label(&quot;$20/\sqrt 2$&quot;,midpoint(B--D1),S); label(&quot;$20/\sqrt 2$&quot;,midpoint(D1--C1),E); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C_1$&quot;,C1,NE); label(&quot;$D_1$&quot;,D1,S); [/asy]" width="415" height="248" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5f4b973bc11f7d51a212320bde0357fd3b9a4ea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair B=(0,0), A=(-10,0), C1=20*dir(45), C2=20*dir(60);  draw(A--B--C2); draw(A--C2,linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;));  dot(A); dot(B); dot(C2);  pair D2 = (C2.x,0); dot(D2); draw(B--D2--C2);  label(&quot;$10$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),S); label(&quot;$20$&quot;,midpoint(B--C2),SE); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,midpoint(B--D2),S); label(&quot;$10\sqrt 3$&quot;,midpoint(D2--C2),E); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C_2$&quot;,C2,NE); label(&quot;$D_2$&quot;,D2,S); [/asy]" width="348" height="288" />
</p><p>Hence we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/c/2cc8db3e9b8bfee19910c003a26bf607135e7fa2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AC_2^2 = AD_2^2 + D_2C_2^2 = 20^2 + (10\sqrt 3)^2 = 400 + 300 = 700\]" width="450" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/6/cc6f9e03769370419dc74f43b9f99f0ccc253320.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AC_1^2 = AD_1^2 + D_1C_1^2 = (10 + 20/\sqrt 2)^2 + (20\sqrt 2)^2 = 100 + 400/\sqrt 2 + 200 + 200 = 500 + 200\sqrt 2 &lt; 500 + 200\cdot 1.5 = 800\]" width="860" height="21" />
</p><p>Therefore for any valid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> the value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff3d60c8fd112aac57841ddab6d625dff304193.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2$" width="34" height="15" /> is surely in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/6/936326b38aed511c565cb095ca4e9a89da613b51.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ \textbf{(D)}[700,800] }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="114" height="29" />.
</p>

This is essentially the same solution as above.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693c4044f8526a8179956e8ac7a3f2f9d61e2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
