<p>What is the sum of the digits of the square of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/d/1fdd1ecf8935a47956330c3a216fb003c0fa0167.png" class="latex" alt="$\text 111111111$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/481b9dba5499d18771af82b00d276675804095dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 18\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 27\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 45\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 63\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 81$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Using the standard multiplication algorithm, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/125164fccfa2010c2b1b71fedac1555eb7d7dee7.png" class="latex" alt="$111,111,111^2=12,345,678,987,654,321,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="328" height="18" /> whose digit sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/9/df92903f798da4de27b25a23df64a500698a6a57.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(E)\text{ }81.}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="71" height="32" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We note that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/312d643c239f9fd6054701a8cba664779329cf72.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/3/cf307af5793b1820789a10c54f8240188778f66e.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2 = 121$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/4/4145a06985618b682a498fde6af02c947dfcab7e.png" class="latex" alt="$111^2 = 12321$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="15" />,
</p><p>and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/5/c1514d30add3c8e58d6ab22f7969aa5cb0f7d3fe.png" class="latex" alt="$1,111^2 = 1234321$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="138" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can clearly see the pattern: If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/a/2aa75d393c0018241cc8ef2574769737b66fb608.png" class="latex" alt="$111\cdots111$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="80" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> ones (and for the sake of simplicity, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/e/d3e6f0c3c9d936a99e222471be73bb8fa3d243c1.png" class="latex" alt="$n&lt;10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />), then the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/0872ca2f62e98f7846c7ed24f6953417c2643dbf.png" class="latex" alt="$X^2$" width="22" height="15" /> is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/e/92e8115162b14832def838dbbcc63b6ace0c948d.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+4+5\cdots n+(n-1)+(n-2)\cdots+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="384" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/5/c55022cc68c5d34ca1609e91c3da60cf404b87ef.png" class="latex" alt="$=(1+2+3\cdots n)+(1+2+3+\cdots n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="338" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/4/8b4deaad0b3cfe001c389df2592b1edbe0c46cd5.png" class="latex" alt="$=\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}+\dfrac{(n-1)n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="182" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/f/faf71e6f7ee0bd804521d07afbe025e700df6ac1.png" class="latex" alt="$=\dfrac{n(n+1+n-1)}{2}=\dfrac{2n^2}{2}=n^2.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="256" height="39" />
</p><p>Aha! We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/7/2975b7bd780b46cc9e02a61f6ebe1e7dcaf1a359.png" class="latex" alt="$111,111,111$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="15" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> digits, so its digit sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/4/394776726077dd525a71d6a142dbd047df3b12a7.png" class="latex" alt="$9^2=\boxed{81(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="99" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05fb15e35c9dcd641c347a238e30c0cc36bfddfc.png" class="latex" alt="$111^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="15" /> can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/9190dd3baff01f4a8937710477560759d6d98086.png" class="latex" alt="$111(100+10+1)=11100+1110+111=12321$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="386" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can apply this strategy to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/7/ac79334a384b49b5a03ae275072ce5121160c4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$111,111,111^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="18" />, as seen below.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/765ee723c0a0e7ca3c1a1a83383d73125019ef53.png" class="latex" alt="$111111111^2=111111111(100000000+10000000\cdots+10+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="480" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843ee4e8332fd7146c15b6fd325aa3af280e91e4.png" class="latex" alt="$=11111111100000000+1111111110000000+\cdots+111111111$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="487" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/c/7fcb794e0680e364409619be15e1193ae8b774e6.png" class="latex" alt="$=12,345,678,987,654,321$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="213" height="16" /> 
</p><p>The digit sum is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/2/3824608674a44bc5e7a96f73c7d83dbd45368c7e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8+9+8+7+6+5+4+3+2+1=81 \boxed{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="592" height="29" />.
</p>
