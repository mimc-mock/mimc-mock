<p>A circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is inscribed in a semicircle, as shown. The area inside the semicircle but outside the circle is shaded. What fraction of the semicircle's area is shaded? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/7/b576665e4dd6ec10b3ec91bcda2f520bbd9d9846.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(6mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); dotfactor=4;  filldraw(Arc((0,0),4,0,180)--cycle,gray,black); filldraw(Circle((0,2),2),white,black); dot((0,2)); draw((0,2)--((0,2)+2*dir(60))); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,midpoint((0,2)--((0,2)+2*dir(60))),SE); [/asy]" width="228" height="115" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/762269adbf0c2160c98d1b02b5bb99be3ca00821.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{1}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{\pi}{6} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{2}{\pi} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{2}{3} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac{3}{\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="367" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Area of the circle inscribed inside the semicircle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/b/bcbbdf19dcfb48114201b52c86f0f23e6e89a911.png" class="latex" alt="$= \pi r^2 \Rightarrow \pi(2^2) = 4 \pi .$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="19" />
Area of the larger circle (semicircle's area x 2)<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/0/b706ba927d9def9dc8ac251b58d8e25c9e9aaf41.png" class="latex" alt="$= \pi r^2 \Rightarrow \pi(4^2)= 16 \pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="169" height="19" /> (4, or the diameter of the inscribed circle is the same thing as the radius of the semicircle). Thus, the area of the semicircle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/b/8bb23482fa7271b8698e5bb6800b22af69af8d0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(16 \pi) \Rightarrow 8 \pi .$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" />
Part of the semicircle that is unshaded is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/2/3522c2eec5c7da80e189a311f5fdcde792f0352e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4 \pi}{8 \pi} = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> Therefore, the shaded part is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/f/bff3a3b0be103073aad3b2d2cb7b6ccbb52b2b44.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/9/6d9cdc39d9fbf7a3a9bbefaf8e7f0cf02e670de0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\Rightarrow \fbox{A}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The solution above is the standard method of solving it, but we can also realize something about their radii. If we see, we know that the smaller circle has a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> while the larger semicircle has a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. We know for a fact that if we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> normal full circles and one has a radius that is double the other, the smaller circle will always be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times smaller than the larger one (if you didn't know, now you know). Now, we have a semicircle, so the same thing applies but the larger circle is cut in half, so therefore, the semicircle (which has the bigger radius), will be double the size of the small circle that is inscribed in it. Double means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of the small full circles will be able to fit the larger semi-circle. So, therefore, the area that is shaded is equal to the area of the small full circle, making <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> equal parts, so the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the whole semi-circle
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/7/6071c5c1c09da5384310ba0aa19b49ce5705011c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\Longrightarrow \fbox{A}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />
</p>
