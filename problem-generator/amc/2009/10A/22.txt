<p>Two cubical dice each have removable numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. The twelve numbers on the two dice are removed, put into a bag, then drawn one at a time and randomly reattached to the faces of the cubes, one number to each face. The dice are then rolled and the numbers on the two top faces are added. What is the probability that the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/e/b1e82009f3e852730025c8210178f99bf94f7b2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{1}{9} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{1}{8} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{1}{6} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{2}{11} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="370" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>At the moment when the numbers are in the bag, imagine that each of them has a different color. Clearly the situation is symmetric at this moment. Hence after we draw them, attach them and throw the dice, the probability of getting some pair of colors is the same for any two colors.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f363e54394035b19d42157360920421dd1f88b0.png" class="latex" alt="${12 \choose 2} = 66$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="88" height="53" /> ways to pick two of the colors. We now have to count the ways where the two chosen numbers will have a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. 
</p><p>A sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> can be obtained as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/c/55c2e51ab0f15889defecd8e444222f43d5cf604.png" class="latex" alt="$1+6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/8/4886e28554d678d6758dfa9e44bc9a333a62f0da.png" class="latex" alt="$2+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/f/13fda9769ebcc081704bcd6a1bdd32e91df779da.png" class="latex" alt="$3+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />. Each number in the bag has two different colors, hence each of these three options corresponds to four pairs of colors. 
</p><p>Out of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d40f3652c1962e98ce050a88c04ed37997c67df.png" class="latex" alt="$66$" width="17" height="12" /> pairs of colors we can get when throwing the dice, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d4ca54ab08f1bcaeba2f971a6f613e62372f272.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 4=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> will give us the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. Hence the probability that this will happen is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f44301473923e9174522bcad3b747bb24f477d79.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{66} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }\frac 2{11}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="118" height="49" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Ignoring the numbers that do not affect the probability of the desired outcome (the ones that are not on top of the dice), say that the number on top of the first die is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. For the sum of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, the second die must have the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/e/c7e71ddd5ea8c89acf5068026107b269247836a4.png" class="latex" alt="$7-n$" width="42" height="12" /> on top. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> remaining numbers that could be on top of the second die, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/e/c7e71ddd5ea8c89acf5068026107b269247836a4.png" class="latex" alt="$7-n$" width="42" height="12" /> (since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/0/200c9d3f693c912ea3d9b0e97589e4245feca582.png" class="latex" alt="$n\ne7-n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="17" /> in all cases and since there are 2 faces for each number 1-6).
</p><p>Thus, the probability of the sum of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/f/21f6f288bdd772b21d155e98fc72a79870b1fc96.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Taking out the rolling dice part, we see that we're just taking two numbers out of a bag. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/6/836675b2c80108e5b6e889f92ebe40d98dd0f673.png" class="latex" alt="${12 \choose 2}=66$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="88" height="53" /> ways to pick two, and there are 12 ways to pick one, and 2 ways to pick the other. (After you pick one, the other one you pick is fixed). Now say you pick n. The other number you pick has to be 7-n. But if you picked 7-n first and n second, that would be the same thing. So we need to divide by 2. 
</p><p>This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa59b666c1fa9a0c6f1816dc2526f781ed4440a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{12 \cdot 2}{2}}{66}=\boxed{\frac{2}{11}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="90" height="48" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
