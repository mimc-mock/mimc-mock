<p>Circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. Circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> has an integer radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/e/76e03a12434dd7d85770688c60258725c21748d9.png" class="latex" alt="$r&lt;100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="13" /> and remains internally tangent to circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> as it rolls once around the circumference of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. The two circles have the same points of tangency at the beginning and end of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />'s trip. How many possible values can <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> have?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/1/141590f6c43fdcfeb877c8a0b57191d42536ee51.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4\ \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 8\ \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 9\ \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 50\ \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 90\ \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="382" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The circumference of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/c/45cd4cb916f80262a8f8fa797e5ae35a2641c312.png" class="latex" alt="$200\pi$" width="37" height="12" />, and the circumference of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/870ee58cf1ce6e6b945cd3364fe2a65d5c1f782b.png" class="latex" alt="$2r\pi$" width="28" height="12" />. Since circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> makes a complete revolution and <i>ends up on the same point</i>, the circumference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> must be a multiple of the circumference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, therefore the quotient must be an integer.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/d/87d3886b02d043168539795fdc31c9bb1611df5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{200\pi}{2\pi \cdot r} = \frac{100}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="99" height="37" />.
</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must then be a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, excluding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> because the problem says that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/e/76e03a12434dd7d85770688c60258725c21748d9.png" class="latex" alt="$r&lt;100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a5200cf67dde054def335b42b9738ac65fdb836.png" class="latex" alt="$100\: =\: 2^2\; \cdot \; 5^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="15" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/3/f63890eb21bbbf402f1e06084d64f14169f354cb.png" class="latex" alt="$(2+1)\; \cdot \; (2+1)\;$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" /> factors*. But you need to subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, in order to exclude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e81ca9c5c8715be868438500743202d6773d05bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{8}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p>
<pre>*The number of factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/8/4b8cf4bab2aa160b3f554cb6feb8bc0413e88e18.png" class="latex" alt="$a^x\: \cdot \: b^y\: \cdot \: c^z\;...$" width="110" height="12" /> and so on, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are prime numbers, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/1/f817662154640ab286149bb2c7c90a267083c250.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)(y+1)(z+1)...$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="179" height="18" />.
</pre>
