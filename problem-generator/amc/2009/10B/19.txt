<p>A particular <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />-hour digital clock displays the hour and minute of a day. Unfortunately, whenever it is supposed to display a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, it mistakenly displays a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. For example, when it is 1:16 PM the clock incorrectly shows 9:96 PM. What fraction of the day will the clock show the correct time?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/2/7f2b56c3326a4060627928fb132143123da12f0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 12\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 58\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 34\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 56\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac {9}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="370" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The clock will display the incorrect time for the entire hours of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/a/c0a2509ca46519eac6066ac0c6b0f077e76d58e3.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 10, 11$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />.  So the correct hour is displayed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcfeb8cb09b8af3dae2cf162c738f90021a90849.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 23$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the time.  The minutes will not display correctly whenever either the tens digit or the ones digit is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so the minutes that will not display correctly are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/6/856f944e685abf0538ab51be947dddc50490a62e.png" class="latex" alt="$10, 11, 12, \dots, 19$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="128" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/3/5a3b312695b20b6ed8d9cd8d44d26cd2f1654df8.png" class="latex" alt="$01, 21, 31, 41,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1fd7cb028e27b8648efbfb002f3e6d4fcb33a5.png" class="latex" alt="$51$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.  This amounts to fifteen of the sixty possible minutes for any given hour.  Hence the fraction of the day that the clock shows the correct time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/7/bc70d3611d6d0a32f5e983850afbdbc61537420d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 23 \cdot \left(1 - \frac {15}{60}\right) = \frac 23 \cdot \frac 34  = \boxed{\frac 12}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="218" height="48" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f4283cfd441270cb90bfd2cf89a533f3dde81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The required fraction is the number of correct times divided by the total times. There are 60 minutes in an hour and 12 hours on a clock, so there are 720 total times.
</p><p>We count the correct times directly; let a correct time be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/a/87a030c20573cea5dc2d01fb88a05ceb770084df.png" class="latex" alt="$x:yz$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a number from 1 to 12 and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> are digits, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53b1aeb9976dea14cd71fe6ae5b911016c775752.png" class="latex" alt="$y&lt;6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. There are 8 values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> that will display the correct time: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9. There are five values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> that will display the correct time: 0, 2, 3, 4, and 5. There are nine values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> that will display the correct time: 0, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9. Therefore there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/914ae252f5f1170ecf182348c9fc2c9f96e8ad96.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot 5\cdot 9=40\cdot 9=360$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="169" height="13" /> correct times.
</p><p>Therefore the required fraction is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f28ac3c56cc74b126911dba343bb3988b7369692.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{360}{720}=\frac{1}{2}\Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="136" height="37" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
