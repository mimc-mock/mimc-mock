<p>Rachel and Robert run on a circular track.  Rachel runs counterclockwise and completes a lap every 90 seconds, and Robert runs clockwise and completes a lap every 80 seconds.  Both start from the same line at the same time.  At some random time between 10 minutes and 11 minutes after they begin to run, a photographer standing inside the track takes a picture that shows one-fourth of the track, centered on the starting line.  What is the probability that both Rachel and Robert are in the picture?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c50102db0750173a6242ce3b2db3d4eb7f9ee28.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\frac {1}{16}\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\frac 18\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\frac {3}{16}\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\frac 14\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\frac {5}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="360" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/3/ce3b33448115127f15871da09d1ab63a1cddd435.png" class="latex" alt="$(600$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> seconds<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8ff48facb2d169243b88fba455ef6c48e0b0504.png" class="latex" alt="$),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="11" height="18" /> Rachel will have completed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> laps and be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds from completing her seventh lap. Because Rachel runs one-fourth of a lap in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/d/91d413c0964b8ba3371f882fd21fa87baeb74e92.png" class="latex" alt="$22.5$" width="31" height="12" /> seconds, she will be in the picture between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/f/6af17885838cfff31a3105dbea2d198fd16788df.png" class="latex" alt="$18.75$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> seconds and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42ca2a2ba7ab76e1101c3f3aeb5775c4035e1df9.png" class="latex" alt="$41.25$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> seconds of the tenth minute.  After 10 minutes, Robert will have completed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> laps and be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> seconds from completing his eighth lap.  Because Robert runs one-fourth of a lap in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds, he will be in the picture between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds of the tenth minute.  Hence both Rachel and Robert will be in the picture if it is taken between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42ca2a2ba7ab76e1101c3f3aeb5775c4035e1df9.png" class="latex" alt="$41.25$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> seconds of the tenth minute.  So the probability that both runners are in the picture is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/d/6ed529199646ae67b9a04dd4b29b4387bf770b70.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {41.25 - 30} {60} = \boxed{\frac {3}{16}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="144" height="48" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484f88154fadcf9c781bba19becdae88696b77f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
