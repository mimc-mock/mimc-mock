<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on a circle centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/b/6bba4a6433bf5e62d09d40bfbb8a5db10ef9d19a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BA}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> are tangent to the circle, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is equilateral. The circle intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/2/5d29ab3cedcc24c862f97e5d7f2bb1da18b7067e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BO}$" width="29" height="15" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420de0fc294e1b3ca2f3e13621421ef7ba1de06b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BD}{BO}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="37" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/5/52549af4856084e27864d0c1fc7100c916ec0c1d.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } \frac {\sqrt2}{3} \qquad \text{(B) } \frac {1}{2} \qquad \text{(C) } \frac {\sqrt3}{3} \qquad \text{(D) } \frac {\sqrt2}{2} \qquad \text{(E) } \frac {\sqrt3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="409" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/f/4cf8bc3872a8098bf821097d42ec20d1c0563ebe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.5cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair B=(0,0), A=(3,0), C=3*dir(60), O=intersectionpoint( C -- (C+3*dir(-30)), A -- (A+3*dir(90)) ); pair D=intersectionpoint(B--O, circle(O,length(A-O))); draw(circle(O,length(A-O))); draw(A--B--C--O--A); draw(B--O); draw(rightanglemark(B,A,O)); draw(rightanglemark(B,C,O)); draw(A--C, dashed ); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NW); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,(S+SSW)); [/asy]" width="362" height="268" />
</p><p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is equilateral, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/7/cc7b0c1b7f70b993bcd18e59f51399c257ef02fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC = \angle BCA = 60^\circ$" width="183" height="13" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e80f42b3a07aebf42d652477fa5c9847e4a82cc5.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OAC = \angle OCA = 30^\circ$" width="183" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/4/b54bc2e0809db37abf644b5458a3017f2edce421.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOC = 120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="112" height="13" />, and from symmetry we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/2/712611bc4e2ae3e3b3aab37ca0373541a2cc5319.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB = \angle COB = 60^\circ$" width="184" height="13" />. Thus, this gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/6/ca697d7addeb3f459c17ef54acb0f6d549737f3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO = \angle CBO = 30^\circ$" width="184" height="13" />.
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/75992b2a606e35499fa9290595e00607eae1fd9d.png" class="latex" alt="$DO = AO$" width="81" height="13" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> lies on the circle. From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/2/632b43a80c4323401f3a392c6d05e9a1477abd26.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABO$" width="58" height="13" /> we also have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/163906348ef495e2aca1a99da602fe3beb6cf959.png" class="latex" alt="$AO = BO \sin 30^\circ = \frac{BO}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="191" height="37" />, Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b76ca049c0a10f09f48927627d7e40b9776d837c.png" class="latex" alt="$DO = \frac{BO}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="85" height="37" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33bf3958c73017b91f76521badfc0dd2246760b.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = BO - DO = \frac{BO}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="191" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aae8271b55868ff2ccbe86af12646d41c7695bc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BD}{BO} = \boxed{\frac 12 \Longrightarrow B}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="137" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/c/48c3786f1ab8d096092a74fd7b4e7bcd48a5375d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.5cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair B=(0,0), A=(3,0), C=3*dir(60), O=intersectionpoint( C -- (C+3*dir(-30)), A -- (A+3*dir(90)) ); pair D=intersectionpoint(B--O, circle(O,length(A-O))); draw(circle(O,length(A-O))); draw(A--B--C--O--A); draw(B--O); draw(rightanglemark(B,A,O)); draw(rightanglemark(B,C,O)); draw(A--C--D--A, dashed ); pair Sp = intersectionpoint(D--O,A--C); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NW); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,(S+SSW)); label(&quot;$S$&quot;,Sp,(S+SSW)); [/asy]" width="362" height="268" />
</p><p>As in the previous solution, we find out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/2/712611bc4e2ae3e3b3aab37ca0373541a2cc5319.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB = \angle COB = 60^\circ$" width="184" height="13" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/6/e66430739a35447f7cda251eb512d585dc2641c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AOD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/4/8f44214e5c66155bbcf0dd1b466ad394037eb30f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle COD$" width="60" height="13" /> are both equilateral.
</p><p>We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aae436a12231b7fffddf1d4dfe77f2a2d8c67312.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle SCD = \angle SAD = 30^\circ$" width="181" height="13" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the incenter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, and as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is equilateral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is also its centroid. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e62d97c2a1f1da7d3f2583d7b30db7b30e8c10e4.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot SD = BD$" width="104" height="12" />, and as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/500d00237f7999d2766e9d2ff337bd80631edda2.png" class="latex" alt="$SD = SO$" width="77" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/f/bef09da6a052c225a1ec88789fb2b91ec3f69f1d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot SD = SD + SO = OD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="204" height="14" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/e/7ee1c87c79bfe27e3e79c60a6c32e3987296a23a.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=OD$" width="84" height="12" />, and as before we conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/3/bd3882ec401cfb8b023a38d89b08935d5b0dc864.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BD}{BO} = \boxed{\frac 12}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="83" height="48" />.
</p>
