<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> has a right angle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/4/4e4e71e9c0786b12fb9b4b5c67364147ed6e402f.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/1/8b1929493a2042af1208c7b6bb285c11b19a6108.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=2$" width="61" height="12" />. The bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/5862947b38a495ec9be80e5b9c7582de6cf0d269.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC$" width="55" height="13" /> meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0b6594577eb2fc6916bc732b5f99843acda5c60.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(0,1), B=(0,0), C=(2,0); pair D=extension(A,bisectorpoint(B,A,C),B,C); pair[] ds={A,B,C,D};  dot(ds); draw(A--B--C--A--D);  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SE); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NW); draw(rightanglemark(C,B,A,2)); [/asy]" width="222" height="125" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/6/3468e42356950b1d1916e01e180ddd2c2b9a7d0e.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } \frac {\sqrt3 - 1}{2} \qquad \text{(B) } \frac {\sqrt5 - 1}{2} \qquad \text{(C) } \frac {\sqrt5 + 1}{2} \qquad \text{(D) } \frac {\sqrt6 + \sqrt2}{2} \qquad \text{(E) } 2\sqrt 3 - 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="602" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By the Pythagorean Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/0/7d080380fb2eda22d597e35957f097f7055f964e.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=\sqrt5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />. Then, from the Angle Bisector Theorem, we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/7/9578bcce980cf296e9a6e6d63e972f1199b3f6b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BD}{1}=\frac{2-BD}{\sqrt5}\\ BD\left(1+\frac{1}{\sqrt5}\right)=\frac{2}{\sqrt5}\\ BD(\sqrt5+1)=2\\ BD=\frac{2}{\sqrt5+1}=\boxed{\frac{\sqrt5-1}{2} \implies B}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="277" height="163" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/d/a7d3dbc9f9659fd19a6e4a7b20cc6ddcb64b98e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta = \angle BAD = \angle DAC$" width="170" height="13" />. Notice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/9/9c9276f35fa9ed931d2358426438466df3b85370.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan \theta = BD$" width="92" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/2/a02fc54e9a3f32ab2b9bbf8b373175b84c3f2a93.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan 2 \theta = 2$" width="80" height="13" />. By the double angle identity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/f/40fe0cdac1eb06b3a723c98fa33b0cbe5afdb94b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2 = \frac{2 BD}{1 - BD^2} \implies BD = \boxed{\frac{\sqrt5 - 1}{2} \implies B}.\]" width="348" height="53" />
</p><p>Remarks: You could also use tangent half angle formula
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/5/d8553582f0b6fb27e92102a8ba39b074975ab770.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />.
</p><p>Make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> a line so that it is perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> is an angle bisector, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/1/18167d788fd698322ba516cf8bfdba2c931fa17d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AED$" width="59" height="13" /> is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />.
Using the Pythagorean Theorem:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/d/85df678441f26e9c1e11179c993abf214d0183a6.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2=1^2+2^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/4/1a45031fc88c4acee01ed2b66a46ec49772f978a.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2=5$" width="68" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba36cbbcccc0c3b99c21887f8f6a6281585c245.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/0/24096979a27c72ccde79e7e4889bf4e4be7a632b.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" /> by the congruent triangles, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/100d141326af0311cd2b0a55a7f24207b91b172d.png" class="latex" alt="$EC=\sqrt{5}-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="18" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/2/7b2e18cd757eb3201f8e8edabaf8568c7b33ae7a.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/100d141326af0311cd2b0a55a7f24207b91b172d.png" class="latex" alt="$EC=\sqrt{5}-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/5/655fe60148c5b76f319d2aba43f4056d4956dbc2.png" class="latex" alt="$DC=2-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" />. We now have right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a9191aefcf2eb237d1999486083ed78f6d515f.png" class="latex" alt="$DEC$" width="44" height="12" /> and its 3 sides. Using the Pythagorean Thereom, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e45504626304e28f931914cc4bc8ca85acbb26e7.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2+(\sqrt{5}-1)^2=(2-y)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/6/e1625c6a8480da5cd2248ff12cca0579dd39bcf9.png" class="latex" alt="$-4y=2-2\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="20" />
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/3/d1308943149b096c0b73add8c7343814a908610c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=BD=\boxed{\frac{\sqrt5-1}{2} \implies B}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="227" height="53" />
</p><p>~HelloWorld21
</p>
