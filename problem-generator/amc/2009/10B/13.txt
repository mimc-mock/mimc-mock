<p>As shown below, convex pentagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a96240c51d61ba3733d9f5c3020fa92ec4136.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDE$" width="72" height="13" /> has sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c56ff6dcfd62e4e93c0469964d28b362ccff9c2b.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=3$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a5322c7f896502c208bbb4a354efc8d212620667.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/7/457c786d44511f906a48d347140798f514c83311.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=6$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/3/f93177aa484f3d901b77587ad2ed451dbb9d96b3.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=3$" width="63" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/6/566ac1c70b7e66e9f74957f36e5e9e3b72969d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$EA=7$" width="61" height="13" />. The pentagon is originally positioned in the plane with vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> at the origin and vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> on the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. The pentagon is then rolled clockwise to the right along the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. Which side will touch the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ede76a20da0615982f01248525595f42229d075.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2009$" width="70" height="12" /> on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/6/fd6ac5c8a85c125cb27930bfe3c7aa4b9cd98e12.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(0,0), Ep=7*dir(105), B=3*dir(0); pair D=Ep+B; pair C=intersectionpoints(Circle(D,6),Circle(B,4))[1]; pair[] ds={A,B,C,D,Ep};  dot(ds); draw(B--C--D--Ep--A); draw((6,6)..(8,4)..(8,3),EndArrow(3)); xaxis(&quot;$x$&quot;,-8,14,EndArrow(3));  label(&quot;$E$&quot;,Ep,NW); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,E); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$(0,0)=A$&quot;,A,SW);  label(&quot;$3$&quot;,midpoint(A--B),N); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,midpoint(B--C),NW); label(&quot;$6$&quot;,midpoint(C--D),NE); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,midpoint(D--Ep),S); label(&quot;$7$&quot;,midpoint(Ep--A),W); [/asy]" width="668" height="238" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/9/769bbc4eadbaede70c82a88ef9c61f6e4b022fa5.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } \overline{AB} \qquad \text{(B) } \overline{BC} \qquad \text{(C) } \overline{CD} \qquad \text{(D) } \overline{DE} \qquad \text{(E) } \overline{EA}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="431" height="19" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The perimeter of the polygon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/c/89cf1eb8cff0522187b8b774ac5f4a252515d310.png" class="latex" alt="$3+4+6+3+7 = 23$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="176" height="14" />. Hence as we roll the polygon to the right, every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> units the side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> will be the bottom side.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/c/70c7b2fc8432f0b11697c1bd25d9f880673b00bf.png" class="latex" alt="$2009 = 23 \times 87 + 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="150" height="14" />. Thus at some point in time we will get the situation when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/1/4d170a60530295d34be30512534fa1fcee6b659a.png" class="latex" alt="$A=(2001,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> is the bottom side. Obviously, at this moment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/3/333f93e180d11b2368c1f415ab21d4c145b933d3.png" class="latex" alt="$B=(2004,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" />. 
</p><p>After that, the polygon rotates around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> until point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> hits the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaab62cb65e46d295bb696045601ebdf218de7f9.png" class="latex" alt="$(2008,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />.
</p><p>And finally, the polygon rotates around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> until point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> hits the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/2/ec2346b93152c0f32e75643d01acd706f59e8adc.png" class="latex" alt="$(2014,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />.
At this point the side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/f/66f0f80faa2ebb109d0e109182a72d0be3550e0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\overline{CD}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="41" height="27" /> touches the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/8/8f89123ebb1e348ad7f3e651fb400dd2cc068b3b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2009,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f58d2efaaa17d28cec9dd927d78708a63b43e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2:_Mod_Arithmetic">Solution 2: Mod Arithmetic</span></h2>
<p>The perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/c/81c4fb6aa61fa922a943b3a97016f19b4e68e30d.png" class="latex" alt="$2009\equiv8($" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />mod <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/673f176b6acea1c48c6ff4a9d73aa452fa2db11f.png" class="latex" alt="$23)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="18" />, so it will end up on side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> + a total of 8 more units. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/9170b77a2a79f744b5dabf67908e5ec47f359d59.png" class="latex" alt="$4&lt;8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/f/7bf698bf5820c63a00bd7cc91b71c79a470d27c2.png" class="latex" alt="$4+6=10&gt;8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="115" height="14" />, so it ends on side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> for an answer of  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f58d2efaaa17d28cec9dd927d78708a63b43e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
