<p>Circles with centers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> have radii 2 and 4, respectively, and are externally tangent.  Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are on the circle centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, and points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> are on the circle centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> are common external tangents to the circles.  What is the area of hexagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/6/616e20a79cdf94c3a000d13cf84d5f4191b0603f.png" class="latex" alt="$AOBCPD$" width="86" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/5/985d18b9bd1bc04f16562ee9a3eabc5afe6efcbd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] // from amc10 problem series unitsize(0.4 cm); defaultpen(linewidth(0.7) + fontsize(11)); pair A, B, C, D; pair[] O; O[1] = (6,0); O[2] = (12,0); A = (32/6,8*sqrt(2)/6); B = (32/6,-8*sqrt(2)/6); C = 2*B; D = 2*A; draw(Circle(O[1],2)); draw(Circle(O[2],4)); draw((0.7*A)--(1.2*D)); draw((0.7*B)--(1.2*C)); draw(O[1]--O[2]); draw(A--O[1]); draw(B--O[1]); draw(C--O[2]); draw(D--O[2]); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, SW); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, NW); dot(&quot;$O$&quot;, O[1], SE); dot(&quot;$P$&quot;, O[2], SE); label(&quot;$2$&quot;, (A + O[1])/2, E); label(&quot;$4$&quot;, (D + O[2])/2, E);[/asy]" width="235" height="182" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/b/71b0886516b7f0690995ea1857172c14b567bfff.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 18\sqrt {3} \qquad \textbf{(B) } 24\sqrt {2} \qquad \textbf{(C) } 36 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 24\sqrt {3} \qquad \textbf{(E) } 32\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="496" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Draw the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/3/783d500281f8f46167dff3b071c543d42150e67f.png" class="latex" alt="$DP$" width="29" height="12" /> and call the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/e/33e1a0e36462d111a2434c734655b0a033cbd7a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OAD$" width="55" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/8/fe832af47de93bf6ef1d09f48a49c90c81500bf7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADP$" width="56" height="13" /> are right angles due to being tangent to the circles, and the altitude creates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/1/dc1b0e99acc52ed6b2eb190435a294dd12ba7ed8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OHD$" width="59" height="12" /> as a right angle. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/0/670babf672e4fddb8f64dc7e3909afc731f4981b.png" class="latex" alt="$ADHO$" width="59" height="13" /> is a rectangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/b/e7b315b83c0b7c2f735f66c03cf870a83da077d4.png" class="latex" alt="$OH$" width="31" height="12" /> bisecting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/3/783d500281f8f46167dff3b071c543d42150e67f.png" class="latex" alt="$DP$" width="29" height="12" />. The length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/d/25dcc9ecb8e7c6fb80766c186721353e6c3dc398.png" class="latex" alt="$OP$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/a/24a62631a12f3fa8bcef26abd1ecc86746318481.png" class="latex" alt="$4+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/1/7c1fed8aeed306b0636afee88e692cf058552f7d.png" class="latex" alt="$HP$" width="30" height="12" /> has a length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so by pythagorean's, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/b/e7b315b83c0b7c2f735f66c03cf870a83da077d4.png" class="latex" alt="$OH$" width="31" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/d/86d0aa7b903e0abe865f49e1a18c19f41dd8862a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{32}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2d16ce13ad2bc954b87d7940a2246cc64de6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \sqrt{32} + \frac{1}{2}\cdot2\cdot \sqrt{32} = 3\sqrt{32} = 12\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="293" height="37" />, which is half the area of the hexagon, so the area of the entire hexagon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64f7d2675598409b808e08c8c3079bcec70a0f51.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot12\sqrt{2} = \boxed{(B)24\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="172" height="33" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/4/d6412e52ede0a27380dd6febd11a841d0aea25a0.png" class="latex" alt="$ADPO$" width="57" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/7/94789109d9356339c135a629c14944696db62c87.png" class="latex" alt="$OPBC$" width="57" height="12" /> are congruent right trapezoids with legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/d/25dcc9ecb8e7c6fb80766c186721353e6c3dc398.png" class="latex" alt="$OP$" width="28" height="12" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Draw an altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> to either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/3/783d500281f8f46167dff3b071c543d42150e67f.png" class="latex" alt="$DP$" width="29" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/3/d13fd509f3062449aaea6e10e1040b1d585063f0.png" class="latex" alt="$CP$" width="28" height="12" />, creating a rectangle with width <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and a right triangle with one leg <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, the hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and the other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Using
the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3f43ae8f8f1306b3547283e641e1df970167ff.png" class="latex" alt="$4\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is also equal to the height of the trapezoid. The area of the trapezoid is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45f0a6e5a2c8e2e7a091c306f46064fa4a3909e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot(4+2)\cdot4\sqrt{2} = 12\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="194" height="37" />, and the total area is two trapezoids, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/5/cc58bba3a5b3391c96e852638214c9b80bd8b463.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{24\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="54" height="30" />.
</p>
