<p>A sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/d/3cdf697be1e2fbf30c3a7969525a6f643ded3dc5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1,a_2,\dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="11" /> of non-negative integers is defined by the rule <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12de01175c4747c4455151b196503a91768bd8da.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{n+2}=|a_{n+1}-a_n|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/c/9ccd696a5d0cbab8337ceb8a4cdfa202a54a6eae.png" class="latex" alt="$n\geq 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/f/e3f09b99de7cd1abde0a11a594918f12237e6bd9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=999$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2af8d020093b9818ae52d81ec9a76b0f9139e1b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2&lt;999$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6e95045f6aff128c5b8a05d4d27797e25aee7b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2006}=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="14" />, how many different values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> are possible?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/5/755ea2dbfbd2e4b10b285235e0dc94a01219fb6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 165 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 324 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 495 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 499 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 660$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="432" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We say the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> is the minimal positive integer such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/c/55c96144fe00d7ba56beffd353bc4ccd65760cc4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = a_{i + 1} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="16" />. Otherwise, we say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> does not complete.
</p><p><br />
Note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/6/1c6ebcea7548c222ded587ca0f1949a4b5b90f72.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \gcd(999, a_2) \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/4/864273ae304adf40e40a232c74e5866ec6ff6756.png" class="latex" alt="$d|a_n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/8/578cf6c5b8058f6f8e6369416da6dcaf73f8fc01.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> does not divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/d/25d20b89c2999edc583eebb303c9c5e99c9b0cb5.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(999, a_2) \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> does not complete. (Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5033830f7a46e393b1f2c6cba9de59d64a7a87b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2006}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="10" /> cannot be 1 in this case since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> does not divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so we do not care about these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> at all.)
</p><p>From now on, suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/a/acadb5a65e61b02f58198e5cc7cb9ea9e2042595.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(999, a_2) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
We will now show that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/f/bffe240e9713925d30b25d372cfac777a38baaf6.png" class="latex" alt="$i \leq 2006$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="15" />. We will do this with 3 lemmas.
</p><p><br />
<b>Lemma:</b> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb06533df6ffeb319260af02ab2a11ff9ad0c180.png" class="latex" alt="$a_j \neq a_{j + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, and neither value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/1/091b1bad16107860fad92161d6976ba6598d54be.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(a_j, a_{j + 1}) &gt; \max(a_{j + 2}, a_{j + 3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="251" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Proof:</b> There are 2 cases to consider.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/d/92dc4e2fbacc92c0e39b3bbb8fe5a608192f11c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_j &gt; a_{j + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/2/6426f1cc2bc0ea7865ba19418ee672a4cc8c1445.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 2} = a_j - a_{j + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758166ef8436b84be70e0ef1862eba97a2e12aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 3} = |a_j - 2a_{j + 1}|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="147" height="18" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/d/c4d88ac7bb3b788fe0df1a7ce43b5cadb347ac95.png" class="latex" alt="$a_j &gt; a_{j + 2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b69a55bb7ed96e59c5b0c44a05701a471ea4f22c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_j &gt; a_{j + 3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="14" />.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/6/2f6652779ce32ed3dbd3c2604dc6d29c56b5127b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_j &lt; a_{j + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/d/abd9a14f15073ba0422dd79e24e0dcf6921b915f.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 2} = a_{j + 1} - a_j$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33210be03ac9a80e12e3b06fcc8f0fbcb026a50.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 3} = a_j$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="12" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b184407d00bfa136612e408fa60f89850fe98e73.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 1} &gt; a_{j + 2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/a/d6a2d807e632e64afd830e2701bab9535c4e82b7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{j + 1} &gt; a_{j + 3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="14" />.
</p><p>In both cases, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/1/091b1bad16107860fad92161d6976ba6598d54be.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(a_j, a_{j + 1}) &gt; \max(a_{j + 2}, a_{j + 3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="251" height="18" />, as desired.
</p>
<hr />
<p><b>Lemma:</b> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/a/2aad11f7078ac61267f8c3c22363a7294fb4ec55.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = a_{i + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76050d28ae21fcb36c0e2573fcb6afbb52c362a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="14" />. Moreover, if instead we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/a/75a4e1ded2e5e0a480e07eac91c6731cb8cbf116.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/0/aa001d833faade905df65b73d615b0e97bd3f555.png" class="latex" alt="$i &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/05596d87dedccc8a0cb8a2cc6b5039ebf5daffe2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{i - 1} = a_{i - 2} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="121" height="14" />.
</p><p><b>Proof:</b> By the way <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> is constructed in the problem statement, having two equal consecutive terms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/a/2aad11f7078ac61267f8c3c22363a7294fb4ec55.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = a_{i + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="12" /> implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/412787c048e28774dc63fc27db42dc52ca858de7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> divides every term in the sequence. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/b/7cbd42c60717b332efb5176ccdc71c81aa84e7c8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i | 999$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/8/6c868047a78505a6c7c9f85fde6db0f6b314635e.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i | a_2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/d/0bd350be20203a5ba79524b4ab519b44cabbd22c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i | \gcd(999, a_2) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76050d28ae21fcb36c0e2573fcb6afbb52c362a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="14" />. For the proof of the second result, note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/a/75a4e1ded2e5e0a480e07eac91c6731cb8cbf116.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a12cad1042429cff7d4516a00753ec735de27a9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{i - 1} = a_{i - 2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="87" height="10" />, so by the first result we just proved, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/5165dd3e032ed7e07ad06b47b80858370d58a7dc.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{i - 2} = a_{i - 1} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="121" height="14" />.
</p>
<hr />
<p><b>Lemma:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/7/3b744920fd14f0010a20d975d63990bd3b1c2ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$i \leq 2000$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="15" />.
</p><p><b>Proof:</b> Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completed at some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/f/b4ff1b85501120465c95f60beb7f1ed3f1e617ac.png" class="latex" alt="$i &gt; 2000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" /> or not at all. Then by the second lemma and the fact that neither <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> nor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, none of the pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/8/5584e7c383dd5d9000784737006e35ef7125c6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_1, a_2), ..., (a_{1999}, a_{2000})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="182" height="18" /> can have a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31fa6b94e5a84b15c9a080cd52edc749580d90c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. So the first lemma implies
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/e/b2e59c16f539aa6b405c250a9dcdc4231d41feb1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(a_1, a_2) &gt; \max(a_3, a_4) &gt; \cdots &gt; \max(a_{1999}, a_{2000})  &gt; 0,\]" width="441" height="18" />
so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/9/6295aba8be003511e7483724aec607746bfcd728.png" class="latex" alt="$999 = \max(a_1, a_2) \geq 1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="18" />, a contradiction. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/7/3b744920fd14f0010a20d975d63990bd3b1c2ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$i \leq 2000$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="15" />.
</p>
<hr />
<p><br />
Now we're ready to find exactly which values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> we want to count.
</p><p>Let's keep in mind that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/9302eae6ec2d9c3915795842cd605a9163c113b5.png" class="latex" alt="$2006 \equiv 2 \pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/6/ae666f679cfa5ed84d5e1ab949ae283edc960c8e.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 = 999$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="15" /> is odd. We have two cases to consider.
</p><p><br />
<b>Case 1:</b> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is odd, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is even, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e28850813a5321d2a8ae3c0510457a7337975ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is odd, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/5/0d5d34d49a7eb6afd4874d7bdaf23946dbf63cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is odd, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ace040475607683853fcfd4960a8f7fa6678c7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is even, and this pattern must repeat every three terms because of the recursive definition of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" />, so the terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> reduced modulo 2 are
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/6/be6313a9a003378dac25c21797df71e1377f8994.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1, 1, 0, 1, 1, 0, ...,\]" width="120" height="16" /> 
so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5033830f7a46e393b1f2c6cba9de59d64a7a87b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2006}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="10" /> is odd and hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> (since if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> completes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/a/bea4d576937a04ec200b1037fc8ee33e8f744cb1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/9/ac9240bafa42750323158471b79fc091bfc4938b.png" class="latex" alt="$k \geq i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="15" />).
</p>
<hr />
<p><b>Case 2:</b> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is even, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is odd, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e28850813a5321d2a8ae3c0510457a7337975ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is odd, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/5/0d5d34d49a7eb6afd4874d7bdaf23946dbf63cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is even, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ace040475607683853fcfd4960a8f7fa6678c7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is odd, and this pattern must repeat every three terms, so the terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> reduced modulo 2 are 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/1/d3159621e94ec5652a5eaefb12bb761ac5c253b8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1, 0, 1, 1, 0, 1, ...,\]" width="120" height="16" />
so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5033830f7a46e393b1f2c6cba9de59d64a7a87b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2006}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="10" /> is even, and hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p>
<hr />
<p><br />
We have found that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/7/257c8c7b72a0e2ccce4b2fe4f804b8573a37fbba.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{2006} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="14" /> is true precisely when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/a/acadb5a65e61b02f58198e5cc7cb9ea9e2042595.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(999, a_2) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is odd. This tells us what we need to count.
</p><p><br />
There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/5/2d5e7eabeae3ce267725a768f8fe2f673b7a9b8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\phi(999) = 648$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" /> numbers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> and relatively prime to it (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/1/2c175f60eecef1de7560c3bdea495d69f26f719d.png" class="latex" alt="$\phi$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is the Euler totient function). We want to count how many of these are odd. Note that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/1/3212167e82b39cd519cb61185dde2be3c6189ca6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[t \mapsto 999 - t\]" width="90" height="13" />
is a 1-1 correspondence between the odd and even numbers less than and relatively prime to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" />. So our final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e61038dc9b22e8a391668e823fb4635b6f6ca30c.png" class="latex" alt="$648/2 = 324$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/d/f0d5b8fdfd2bd98c8be0bad569663660c33f3438.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="23" height="23" />.
</p>
