<p>The lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1a72996d692e777acdcd59c098442e971dede61.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac 14y + a$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="89" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/5/ed5b05b6118a5aaeedc44cbbd469faa7ca696afb.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac 14x + b$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="87" height="37" /> intersect at the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bb913ad463dcc2916d4135977f0d06f20ad93cd.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df6a591474ffd81edd37d8d9f52f6f476caa531.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/9/fa9e2a82c0cc640362304114a2286318dbebf546.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A) } 0 \qquad \text {(B) } \frac 34 \qquad \text {(C) } 1 \qquad \text {(D) } 2 \qquad \text {(E) } \frac 94$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="337" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a2546afe8b74e292a3afa36110ac5bd3c6fd6e1c.png" class="latex" alt="$4x-4a=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2fbec7ec913fcb4c704cff4e18e7f940ad06121.png" class="latex" alt="$4x-4a=\frac{1}{4}x+b$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="138" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/c/bdcd50070686379c290589b0256468a0dbb7feda.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot1-4a=\frac{1}{4}\cdot1+b=2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="194" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/3/58371499ac4be5e5fbf324de21151edfd8a2c0ac.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/3/993d9a67b9e763db9a29ac78ad75f0f2abde7e62.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac{7}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="43" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/5/1b5009e73ac4cbb498586229937ff41d13fefb11.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=\frac{9}{4} \Rightarrow \text{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="128" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Add both equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32f2bff6341e4b1809abd79864ab24d4c265a716.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{cases}x=\frac{1}{4}y+a\\ y=\frac{1}{4}x+b \end{cases}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="101" height="53" />
</p><p>Simplify:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a43b672c69bd11ceed9d0cb5663aaabd33994b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{4}(x+y)=\frac{1}{4}(x+y)+(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="237" height="37" />
</p><p>Isolate our solution:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f639f83de812d1fe186d7b615e9d397b107b5af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}(x+y)=a+b$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="133" height="38" />
</p><p>Substitute the point of intersection <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad79d3d6eb440dc49abd1c788dc470b850022748.png" class="latex" alt="$[x=1, y=2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="102" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e133b6c8382c12b94faaa14a5090e51d825de02d.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=\frac{3}{4}\cdot3=\frac{9}{4} \Rightarrow \fbox{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="202" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bb913ad463dcc2916d4135977f0d06f20ad93cd.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> into the first equation, and solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.
</p><p>Doing the same for the second equation for the second equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/5/70564be23564e35c9b8e70aceebe110199950f79.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />
</p><p>Adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/1/f51612f9fef6b5052477b87fc18cdf5a52603306.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{7}{4} = \frac{9}{4} \Rightarrow \text{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="201" height="38" />
</p>
