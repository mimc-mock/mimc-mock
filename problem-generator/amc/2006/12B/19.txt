<p>Mr. Jones has eight children of different ages. On a family trip his oldest child, who is 9, spots a license plate with a 4-digit number in which each of two digits appears two times. "Look, daddy!" she exclaims. "That number is evenly divisible by the age of each of us kids!" "That's right," replies Mr. Jones, "and the last two digits just happen to be my age." Which of the following is not the age of one of Mr. Jones's children?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a3149923c94d02071e6e8d97c866ad6d471a571.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 5\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 6\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 7\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>First, The number of the plate is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and in the form of
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/0/4d00e7247c23f117f199c38dc42e282cb4ac046e.png" class="latex" alt="$aabb$" width="35" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8ea7e886a23b05912c996d7cdb6d08f7bc7868c.png" class="latex" alt="$abba$" width="35" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/2/03263ee56e786052628a28200aaab932c0928ac3.png" class="latex" alt="$abab$" width="35" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We can conclude straight away that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/3/a43e9d6457f0fb05bb007d2c40413f2d299e4ae6.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b= 9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" /> using the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> divisibility rule. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf590e9abb33aa9776ddc386b1ba6ee7b43fa413.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, the number is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (unless it's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/d/47dd1cbaeecea388a7c6b19d5064fe0ff2643a52.png" class="latex" alt="$1818$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, which is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />), which means there are no <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> year olds on the car, but that can't be true, as that would mean there are less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> kids on the car. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />, then the only possible number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06bff02d591ad0e29e616e7a4f7f191d03a34182.png" class="latex" alt="$7272$" width="35" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06bff02d591ad0e29e616e7a4f7f191d03a34182.png" class="latex" alt="$7272$" width="35" height="12" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, but not by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, so that doesn't work. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />, then the only number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e56f8b581235ba3d1f1fe54d76842c9e2be5a54a.png" class="latex" alt="$6336$" width="35" height="12" />, also not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/a/fda5328bc4703ff88e62aa83ea58ae4e8361f5ee.png" class="latex" alt="$b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, the only number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4240476d0dffb419ae5dd6c1f2c489c6f2f70e27.png" class="latex" alt="$5544$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. It is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, we conclude that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/4/a048067584293c2726ac950f1d7248b56fb75375.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" />
</p><p><b>NOTE</b>: Automatically, since there are 8 children and all of their ages are less than or equal to 9 and are different, the answer choices can be narrowed down to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternate_Solution">Alternate Solution</span></h2>
<p>We know that the number of the plate is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/0/4d00e7247c23f117f199c38dc42e282cb4ac046e.png" class="latex" alt="$aabb$" width="35" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8ea7e886a23b05912c996d7cdb6d08f7bc7868c.png" class="latex" alt="$abba$" width="35" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/2/03263ee56e786052628a28200aaab932c0928ac3.png" class="latex" alt="$abab$" width="35" height="12" /> for distinct digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />.
</p><p>Using the divisibility rule for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, we can conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c539f0c20c6fc4ed2d916457e78f0fdb3d59539.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b\equiv 0\pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />.
</p><p>We also know that the number of the plate is even, because you can only discard one number from the integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, inclusive (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> children, oldest is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />), and there's always going to be an even number left.
</p><p>If one of the children was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> years old, then the plate number would be divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, the units digit must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. 
Then, the possible form of the plate number would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/e/86e48e1fc655548c4881639be832436988d75c9b.png" class="latex" alt="$aa00$" width="36" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b3ffdb0b9f5d804be45de6f4327bd7d4b160a78.png" class="latex" alt="$0bb0$" width="33" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a72eb41a0f991bed78ace8a7b3582d12c77a2fe.png" class="latex" alt="$a0a0$" width="36" height="12" />. The first case is not possible because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/f/b1f2c746327924273ba209f0879ea846a0b8dd6b.png" class="latex" alt="$00$" width="17" height="12" /> is not a possible age for the father.
</p><p>We have the remaining forms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b3ffdb0b9f5d804be45de6f4327bd7d4b160a78.png" class="latex" alt="$0bb0$" width="33" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a72eb41a0f991bed78ace8a7b3582d12c77a2fe.png" class="latex" alt="$a0a0$" width="36" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/6/726d1e22c1c358881e730ae86d2c21488da766d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{Case 1: 0bb0:}$" width="113" height="11" />
We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/e/a5ec7f786bb95a416304b0b5eee210b2092475a6.png" class="latex" alt="$2b\equiv 0\pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="121" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> has to be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can't be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3834f0cef2a1f4d8524aa5bc8e3faca298550862.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> are distinct. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/c/15cc8804d5482ae978d97e7912aa9c768329ad49.png" class="latex" alt="$b=9$" width="40" height="12" />. However, the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/b/9cb7071e7697a2a0827041b3d5cacedc1e48c5e0.png" class="latex" alt="$0990$" width="35" height="12" /> is not divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, so whichever number you discard, the other one will still be there.  This creates a contradiction, so Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> cannot be true.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a9414f57129b628339e3e7ffa910dec11a0e439e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{Case 2: a0a0:}$" width="111" height="11" />
Similarly to Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, we can determine that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. However, the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a6ed311694f85c3a50f0018a200efd0dd75d74.png" class="latex" alt="$9090$" width="35" height="12" /> is not divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. Using the same logic in case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, we conclude that Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot be true.
</p><p>Since we have disproven all of our cases, we know that it is impossible for one of the children to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/4/a048067584293c2726ac950f1d7248b56fb75375.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> years old.
</p><p><b>NOTE</b>: This might look tedious, but it only took me around 30 seconds to do on paper.
</p>
