<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/e/72efd3db67cf713f3b071e62906194f4f942ae50.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pen f = fontsize(10); size(5cm); pair B = (0,sqrt(85+42*sqrt(2))); pair A = (B.y,0); pair C = (0,0); pair P = IP(arc(B,7,180,360),arc(C,6,0,90)); D(A--B--C--cycle); D(P--A); D(P--B); D(P--C); MP(&quot;A&quot;,D(A),plain.E,f); MP(&quot;B&quot;,D(B),plain.N,f); MP(&quot;C&quot;,D(C),plain.SW,f); MP(&quot;P&quot;,D(P),plain.NE,f); MP(&quot;\alpha&quot;,C,5*dir(80),f); MP(&quot;90^\circ-\alpha&quot;,C,3*dir(30),f); MP(&quot;s&quot;,(A+C)/2,plain.S,f); MP(&quot;s&quot;,(B+C)/2,plain.W,f); [/asy]" width="238" height="235" />
Using the Law of Cosines on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/8/458bae4d809ef768d59b1cb4ea8d8f895bc4149a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PBC$" width="59" height="13" />, we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1c6741eec77cf7438b29795d5ab4b49f15df728.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} PB^2&amp;=BC^2+PC^2-2\cdot BC\cdot PC\cdot \cos(\alpha) \Rightarrow 49 = 36 + s^2 - 12s\cos(\alpha) \Rightarrow \cos(\alpha) = \dfrac{s^2-13}{12s}. \end{align*}" width="729" height="39" />
</p><p>Using the Law of Cosines on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/e/d9e2286f54a8da97297935452f9b0ecc9069d9a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PAC$" width="58" height="13" />, we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/f/88ff6eb3086352c811938014cb21ac4cb815d4be.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} PA^2&amp;=AC^2+PC^2-2\cdot AC\cdot PC\cdot \cos(90^\circ-\alpha) \Rightarrow 121 = 36 + s^2 - 12s\sin(\alpha) \Rightarrow \sin(\alpha) = \dfrac{s^2-85}{12s}. \end{align*}" width="778" height="39" />
</p><p>Now we use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6f5d2779d6c1dce24e69d71776c9767ef4951e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^2(\alpha) + \cos^2(\alpha) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="20" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/7/257e4105c294e66fe9664e8c16c57f6d29a5ee54.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sin^2(\alpha)+\cos^2(\alpha) = 1 &amp;\Rightarrow \frac{s^4-26s^2+169}{144s^2} + \frac{s^4-170s^2+7225}{144s^2} = 1 \\ &amp;\Rightarrow s^4-170s^2+3697 = 0 \\ &amp;\Rightarrow s^2 = \dfrac{170 \pm 84\sqrt{2}}{2} = 85 \pm 42\sqrt2  \end{align*}" width="521" height="115" />
</p><p>Note that we know that we want the solution with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/7/58715a5b0dc981e147580a1fd0718614ff8535bc.png" class="latex" alt="$s^2 &gt; 85$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="15" /> since we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/6/9b62aefa94f1bc70d5cf25069249b43779665188.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(\alpha) &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />.  Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/c/98c63099c87840fd56ac1b241b346f0672317c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=85+42=\boxed{127}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="185" height="23" />.
</p>
