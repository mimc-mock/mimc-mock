<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since the two <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hexagon" title="Hexagon">hexagons</a> are going to be repositioned to form a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Square_(geometry)" title="Square (geometry)"> square</a> without overlap, the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Area" title="Area">area</a> will remain the same. The rectangle's area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee18ed2baf1e20b6f8eaa881b723506edb550b4e.png" class="latex" alt="$18\cdot8=144$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />. This means the square will have four sides of length 12. The only way to do this is shown below.<br />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/1794d4dcd643f680a8bcabca8f5ff34ed2b54a70.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(175); pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H; A=(0,8); B=(12,12); C=(12,4); D=(0,0); E=(0,12); F=(12,0); G=(6,4); H=(6,8); draw(A--E--B--C--G--H--A--D--F--C); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(12.6,4)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,SW); label(&quot;$12$&quot;,E--B,N); label(&quot;$12$&quot;,D--F,S);  label(&quot;$4$&quot;,E--A,W); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(12.4,-1.75),E); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,A--D,W); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,(12.4,4),E); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,A--H,S); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,G--C,N); [/asy]" width="292" height="288" />
</p><p>As you can see from the diagram, the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Line_segment" title="Line segment">line segment</a> denoted as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is half the length of the side of the square, which leads to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee619f9cece0d0453951a0da693e407e6a5a893.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{12}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="138" height="38" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_1_(Shortcut)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_.28Shortcut.29">Solution 1  (Shortcut)</span></h3>
<p>As solution 1 says, the two hexagons are going to be repositioned to form a square without overlap. Thus we create this square out of the original rectangle.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/c/5dc022e81e0d002b0a5b02d0aeef8906a0a7c4d0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(175); pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H; A=(0,8); B=(12,12); C=(12,4); D=(0,0); E=(0,12); F=(12,0); G=(6,4); H=(6,8); draw(A--E--B--C--G--H--A--D--F--C); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,A--H,S); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,G--C,N); [/asy]" width="292" height="292" />
</p><p>As you can see from the diagram, the length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> fits into the previously blank side, so we know that it is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/6/426d7ffa69ed286558677973799500f7f705626a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)+linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=4; draw((0,4)--(18,4)--(18,-4)--(0,-4)--cycle); draw((6,4)--(6,0)--(12,0)--(12,-4)); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(9,-2),NW); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,4),NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(18,4),NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(18,-4),SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,(0,-4),SW); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(3,4),S); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(15,-4),N); label(&quot;$18$&quot;,(9,4),N); label(&quot;$18$&quot;,(9,-4),S); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,(0,0),W); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,(18,0),E); dot((0,4)); dot((18,4)); dot((18,-4)); dot((0,-4));[/asy]" width="295" height="152" />
</p><p><br />
From there we can say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/e/7ceb55bf52a42744ba9527bcdd50ab0b438e42ad.png" class="latex" alt="$3y = 18$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb625edfa95624ca8e91c44d48950d6ed426467.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{18}{3} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="138" height="38" />.
