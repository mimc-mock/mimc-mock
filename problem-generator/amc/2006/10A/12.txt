<p>Rolly wishes to secure his dog with an 8-foot rope to a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Square_(geometry)" title="Square (geometry)"> square</a> shed that is 16 feet on each side.  His preliminary drawings are shown.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66db58ca0de01a6dab5b8ddb18e671f265759bfa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); pathpen = linewidth(0.6); defaultpen(fontsize(10)); D((0,0)--(16,0)--(16,-16)--(0,-16)--cycle); D((16,-8)--(24,-8)); label(&#39;Dog&#39;, (24, -8), SE); MP(&#39;I&#39;, (8,-8), (0,0)); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (16,-4), W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (16,-12),W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (20,-8), N); label(&#39;Rope&#39;, (20,-8),S); D((0,-20)--(16,-20)--(16,-36)--(0,-36)--cycle); D((16,-24)--(24,-24)); MP(&quot;II&quot;, (8,-28), (0,0)); MP(&#39;4&#39;, (16,-22), W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (20,-24), N); label(&quot;Dog&quot;,(24,-24),SE); label(&quot;Rope&quot;, (20,-24), S); [/asy]" width="202" height="252" />
</p><p>Which of these arrangements give the dog the greater <a href="/wiki/index.php/Area" title="Area">area</a> to roam, and by how many square feet?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f4dae42577fde29a11c1103d70d8b8a16be96d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } I,\,\textrm{ by }\,8\pi\qquad \textbf{(B) } I,\,\textrm{ by }\,6\pi\qquad \textbf{(C) } II,\,\textrm{ by }\,4\pi\qquad \textbf{(D) } II,\,\textrm{ by }\,8\pi\qquad \textbf{(E) } II,\,\textrm{ by }\,10\pi$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="708" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3baf66050887becefe97193b59ba30afd2e6b9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); defaultpen(linewidth(0.7)+fontsize(10)); filldraw(arc((16,-8),8,-90,90)--cycle, rgb(0.9,0.9,0.6)); filldraw(arc((16,-26),8,-90,90)--cycle, rgb(0.9,0.9,0.6)); filldraw(arc((16,-22),4,90,180)--(16,-22)--cycle, rgb(0.9,0.9,0.6)); D((0,0)--(16,0)--(16,-16)--(0,-16)--cycle); D((16,-8)--(24,-8)); label(&#39;Dog&#39;, (24, -8), SE); MP(&#39;I&#39;, (8,-8), (0,0)); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (16,-4), W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (16,-12),W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (20,-8), N); label(&#39;Rope&#39;, (20,-8),S); pair sD = (0,-2); D(shift(sD)*((0,-20)--(16,-20)--(16,-36)--(0,-36)--cycle)); D(shift(sD)*((16,-24)--(24,-24))); MP(&quot;II&quot;, (8,-28)+sD, (0,0)); MP(&#39;4&#39;, (16,-22)+sD, W); MP(&#39;8&#39;, (20,-24)+sD, N); label(&quot;Dog&quot;,(24,-24)+sD,SE); label(&quot;Rope&quot;, (20,-24)+sD, S); [/asy]" width="195" height="252" />
Let us first examine the area of both possible arrangements. The rope outlines a circular boundary that the dog may dwell in. Arrangement <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> allows the dog 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80dcd51acc3cf8b57846415ea581b30f34c55381.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12\cdot(\pi\cdot8^2) = 32\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="134" height="37" /> square feet of area. Arrangement <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/3/af3906651d16a548230a504127bc5b308a64068f.png" class="latex" alt="$II$" width="19" height="12" /> allows <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/0/e3035399f56def19538c8f76e1b1798ae8103457.png" class="latex" alt="$32\pi$" width="28" height="12" /> square feet plus a little more on the top part of the fence. So we already know that Arrangement <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/3/af3906651d16a548230a504127bc5b308a64068f.png" class="latex" alt="$II$" width="19" height="12" /> allows more freedom - only thing left is to find out how much. The extra area can be represented by a quarter of a circle with radius 4. So the extra area is 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/e/91e8a16e2306f045aeeda0a6115f1b8996f2fdd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac14\cdot(\pi\cdot4^2) = 4\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="125" height="37" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/5/205b6a41fc1e86a1c6485729b139a920c11c9b66.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } II,\,\textrm{ by }\,4\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="130" height="30" />.
</p>
