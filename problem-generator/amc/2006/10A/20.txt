<p>Six distinct positive integers are randomly chosen between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/2/d1216dd3855e1bb88b6136b4ce49c58b57747417.png" class="latex" alt="$2006$" width="35" height="12" />, inclusive. What is the probability that some pair of these integers has a difference that is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/a/93a5f28e11e150ee277f2f21c8711c4f951e6404.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{1}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B) } \frac{3}{5}\qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{2}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D) } \frac{4}{5}\qquad\textbf{(E) } 1\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="404" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>For two numbers to have a difference that is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, the numbers must be congruent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/84027ab631566ed39d8e16d466c9ce6a0b315061.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod{5}$" width="45" height="12" /> (their remainders after division by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> must be the same).
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/823db59ba5d3dbe69d8abcd62cb81b17cd3ef605.png" class="latex" alt="$0, 1, 2, 3, 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> are the possible values of numbers in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/84027ab631566ed39d8e16d466c9ce6a0b315061.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod{5}$" width="45" height="12" />. Since there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> possible values in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/84027ab631566ed39d8e16d466c9ce6a0b315061.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod{5}$" width="45" height="12" /> and we are picking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers, by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pigeonhole_Principle" title="Pigeonhole Principle">Pigeonhole Principle</a>, two of the numbers must be congruent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/84027ab631566ed39d8e16d466c9ce6a0b315061.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod{5}$" width="45" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore the probability that some pair of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> integers has a difference that is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/b/c6ba8d576d943711052a5a444c3211a6e555cb57.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }1}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="56" height="30" />.
</p>
