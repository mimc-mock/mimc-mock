<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Symmetry" title="Symmetry">symmetry</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b076f2e7f124fc5d8b5ef7b30b95bfcfe85449f.png" class="latex" alt="$WXYZ$" width="64" height="12" /> is a square.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/6/0e69e8151c49e0c62ddcaf2e32d408891558b3e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7cm); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pointpen = black; pointfontpen = fontsize(10); pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H,W,X,Y,Z; A=(0,2); B=(1,2); C=(2,2); D=(3,2); H=(0,0); G=(1,0); F=(2,0); E=(3,0); D(&#39;A&#39;,A, N); D(&#39;B&#39;,B,N); D(&#39;C&#39;,C,N); D(&#39;D&#39;,D,N); D(&#39;E&#39;,E,SE); D(&#39;F&#39;,F,SE); D(&#39;G&#39;,G,SW); D(&#39;H&#39;,H,SW); D(A--F); D(B--E); D(D--G); D(C--H); Z=IP(A--F, C--H); Y=IP(A--F, D--G); X=IP(B--E,D--G); W=IP(B--E,C--H); D(&#39;W&#39;,W,1.6*N); D(&#39;X&#39;,X,1.6*plain.E); D(&#39;Y&#39;,Y,1.6*S); D(&#39;Z&#39;,Z,1.6*plain.W); MP(&quot;1&quot;,(A+B)/2,2*N); MP(&quot;2&quot;,(A+H)/2,plain.W); D(B--Z); MP(&quot;1&quot;,(B+Z)/2,plain.W); MP(&quot;\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}&quot;,(W+Z)/2,plain.SE); D(A--D--E--H--cycle); [/asy]" width="332" height="235" />
</p><p>Draw <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed6279f22023a8383cfc839a32e878a4c1e3e2b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BZ}$" width="28" height="15" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/9/3996443794ddc26b46ab069a9195686ef30e300c.png" class="latex" alt="$BZ = \frac 12AH = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="128" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/0/260c952a22d2e37aa68822a758f9dba2fb5f40e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BWZ$" width="64" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4fe048f5c1382916a77470eb42a56f76de35b789.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90 \triangle$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="14" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f4860e4f3943921fd678f9be1e2ee9ba1ebd2e54.png" class="latex" alt="$WZ = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="84" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/7092e3fa4c752feddfe44b5b8f4809d3e2bc00f2.png" class="latex" alt="$[WXYZ] = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 =\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac 12}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="247" height="49" />.
</p><p>There are many different similar ways to come to the same conclusion using different <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">45-45-90 triangles</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a9844cabb16af8eebe5cd42a6be9927e8d83689f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7cm); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pointpen = black; pointfontpen = fontsize(10); pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H,W,X,Y,Z; A=(0,2); B=(1,2); C=(2,2); D=(3,2); H=(0,0); G=(1,0); F=(2,0); E=(3,0); D(&#39;A&#39;,A, N); D(&#39;B&#39;,B,N); D(&#39;C&#39;,C,N); D(&#39;D&#39;,D,N); D(&#39;E&#39;,E,SE); D(&#39;F&#39;,F,SE); D(&#39;G&#39;,G,SW); D(&#39;H&#39;,H,SW); D(A--F); D(B--E); D(D--G); D(C--H); Z=IP(A--F, C--H); Y=IP(A--F, D--G); X=IP(B--E,D--G); W=IP(B--E,C--H); D(&#39;W&#39;,W,1.6*N); D(&#39;X&#39;,X,1.6*plain.E); D(&#39;Y&#39;,Y,1.6*S); D(&#39;Z&#39;,Z,1.6*plain.W); D(B--D((A+H)/2)--G);D(C--D((E+D)/2)--F); D(A--D--E--H--cycle); [/asy]" width="332" height="232" />
</p><p>Drawing lines as shown above and piecing together the triangles, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is made up of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> squares congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b076f2e7f124fc5d8b5ef7b30b95bfcfe85449f.png" class="latex" alt="$WXYZ$" width="64" height="12" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/b/ecb9d4c5a9adf027e04a51a2def416d5ed0b7db5.png" class="latex" alt="$[WXYZ] = \frac{2\cdot 3}{12} =\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac 12}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="220" height="49" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>We see that if we draw a line to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/a/1dae295f7ba255af12a078f5ea4e35c009b82e70.png" class="latex" alt="$BZ$" width="28" height="12" /> it is half the width of the rectangle so that length would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and the resulting triangle is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> so using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> we can get that each side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/0/d70a820870539b5b807a6a5ed51acf564d8a6974.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\frac{1^2}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="39" height="43" /> so the area of the middle square would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/f/1ff64602684289da873ff155e086440241928eb7.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{\frac{1^2}{2}})^2=(\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}})^2=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="223" height="50" /> which is our answer.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> are trisection points and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286e989082359d570543d0269004ce484008ce68.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 2$" width="60" height="13" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/4/f142d92351c7c9e5a27f8a5c133742642df1c495.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = 3$" width="62" height="13" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/d/84d2107ab7da6ef477ebbf010ecff02f5436cb25.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = AH$" width="82" height="13" />, so triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/6/2a673270b385175f167608a4dac92da48129fb5d.png" class="latex" alt="$ACH$" width="44" height="13" /> is a right isosceles triangle, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/b/77b555f89871714f4bbe8ae6cd5d767d6fba9863.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACH = \angle AHC = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="187" height="13" />. By symmetry, triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/4/154cffe3871a3ef13710844a1a96c55fd9e7d5a2.png" class="latex" alt="$AFH$" width="44" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/7/65772dfa055c928d9b85ea6be539a35c1dff1b38.png" class="latex" alt="$DEG$" width="44" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/0/2c0afb40c0a08457a36ebab89b23c17b40b9eff8.png" class="latex" alt="$BED$" width="44" height="12" /> are also right isosceles triangles. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e45a20da8cfd012b7aa67ca7c7529acbf8d9cfcd.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle WAD = \angle WDA = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="196" height="13" />, which means triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb35ae52ec6902ca538f725331e7ff70792eade3.png" class="latex" alt="$AWD$" width="48" height="13" /> is also a right isosceles triangle. Also, triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a83f718b5548bdac1f5c7a32217941c1c739ebef.png" class="latex" alt="$AXC$" width="44" height="13" /> is a right isosceles triangle.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/f/02fcf46f9da86ee3a22d601c6670b1ca0f1c682d.png" class="latex" alt="$AW = AD/\sqrt{2} = 3/\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="21" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/2/7a2e33462ba74e174220d1153287bbf2ed0fe652.png" class="latex" alt="$AX = AC/\sqrt{2} = 2/\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="182" height="21" />. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d10b17c7918e974ec1d834921e31f89de268ea.png" class="latex" alt="$XW = AW - AX = 3/\sqrt{2} - 2/\sqrt{2} = 1/\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="344" height="21" />.
