<p>Mr. Jones has eight children of different ages. On a family trip his oldest child, who is 9, spots a license plate with a 4-digit number in which each of two digits appears two times. "Look, daddy!" she exclaims. "That number is evenly divisible by the age of each of us kids!" "That's right," replies Mr. Jones, "and the last two digits just happen to be my age." Which of the following is <b><i>not</i></b> the age of one of Mr. Jones's children? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/6/c3666861b0032bb3da464d793c0af8565580ce7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 4\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 5\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 6\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 7\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of the ages of Mr. Jones' children (in other words <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286e5b654719c01185e5135ebb764e591679f1f5.png" class="latex" alt="$i \in S$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" /> if Mr. Jones has a child who is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> years old). Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/8/9281aaaaf8711b313657da81b4c4b06db0b3e30b.png" class="latex" alt="$|S| = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a60f9dbc8df75ddb4223e9e8895a575ab4c69ea.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \in S$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> be the positive integer seen on the license plate. Since at least one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> is contained in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/0924344717e74106864143d5d4eb22700bf445d6.png" class="latex" alt="$4 | m$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" />.
</p><p>We would like to prove that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f370c0758128bbebbec6d377da5c3114899a469.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \not\in S$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" />, so for the sake of contradiction, let us suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108c0554cdd706cc17f8b9a0b30e62ed5e41971e.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \in S$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/164dfcb4f9ab638cca459d0d7ecfee5c9a3b8413.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 4 = 20 | m$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> so the units digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Since the number has two distinct digits, each appearing twice, another digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Since Mr. Jones can't be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/f/b1f2c746327924273ba209f0879ea846a0b8dd6b.png" class="latex" alt="$00$" width="17" height="12" /> years old, the last two digits can't be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/f/b1f2c746327924273ba209f0879ea846a0b8dd6b.png" class="latex" alt="$00$" width="17" height="12" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> must be of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d3be1593435cf3b5706ae081df4dee76beb4817.png" class="latex" alt="$d0d0$" width="36" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is a digit. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> (see <a href="/wiki/index.php/Divisibility_rules/Rule_for_3_and_9_proof" title="Divisibility rules/Rule for 3 and 9 proof"> Divisibility rules for 9</a>). Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/3/5b3ad3da7849270fd2199b8910f044efc6ec553c.png" class="latex" alt="$9 | 2d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e9bff534770db92aae32442b1c3176f818c2aab.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 9$" width="42" height="12" />. But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/4/e44a914a2c006c373e649e41f286611661c42ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 9090$" width="75" height="12" /> is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, contradiction. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f370c0758128bbebbec6d377da5c3114899a469.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \not\in S$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is not the age of one of Mr. Jones' kids. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6cf7de7cc0a8d328f0e48f0710c007abf84aa73.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ B }$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p><p>(We might like to check that there does, indeed, exist such a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is not an age of one of Mr. Jones' kids, then the license plate's number must be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/485ef3056c9cc339935d6ed2e5b0776d8ddb66b3.png" class="latex" alt="$lcm(1,2,3,4,6,7,8,9) = 504$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="224" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/9/899c863425efa5d929a4ff358c93421ad2b69ec4.png" class="latex" alt="$11\cdot504 = 5544$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="119" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4240476d0dffb419ae5dd6c1f2c489c6f2f70e27.png" class="latex" alt="$5544$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> is the only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> digit multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f385769245cc3c249ec34e15661c168bfb48e5.png" class="latex" alt="$504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> that fits all the conditions of the license plate's number, the license plate's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4240476d0dffb419ae5dd6c1f2c489c6f2f70e27.png" class="latex" alt="$5544$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />.)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, we can see that if one of Mr. Jones' children is of the age 5, then the license plate will have to end in the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> . The license plate cannot end in the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is a factor of the number, so it must be even. This means that the license plate would have to have two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> digits, and would either be of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8e08d1962e1bd8315ff8fb2b4912bad743b692c.png" class="latex" alt="$XX00$" width="50" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/576bea425612020d020a2e03d47021f00e20215a.png" class="latex" alt="$X0X0$" width="50" height="12" /> (X being the other digit in the license plate) . The condition <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8e08d1962e1bd8315ff8fb2b4912bad743b692c.png" class="latex" alt="$XX00$" width="50" height="12" /> is impossible as Mr. Jones can't be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/f/b1f2c746327924273ba209f0879ea846a0b8dd6b.png" class="latex" alt="$00$" width="17" height="12" /> years old. If we separate the second condition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/576bea425612020d020a2e03d47021f00e20215a.png" class="latex" alt="$X0X0$" width="50" height="12" /> into its prime factors, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1149598f84fc6ca03ccf59d13c99d940ba8541.png" class="latex" alt="$X\cdot 10\cdot 101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="87" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> is prime, and therefore can't account for allowing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/576bea425612020d020a2e03d47021f00e20215a.png" class="latex" alt="$X0X0$" width="50" height="12" /> being evenly divisible by the childrens' ages.  The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> accounts for the 5 and one factor of 2. This leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, but no single digit number contains all the prime factors besides 5 and 2 of the childrens' ages, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6cf7de7cc0a8d328f0e48f0710c007abf84aa73.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ B }$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" /> can't be one of the childrens' ages.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Another way to do the problem is by the process of elimination. The only possible correct choices are the highest powers of each prime, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/57421b8557f6aa4384653806bfc7a5b80faf557a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3=8$" width="49" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee19f2230cc721282aa8253c8d9b7e5ff8ffbe9.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2=9$" width="49" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/1/32115eecc1fb065e5cee4ac42ceaaae727698d47.png" class="latex" alt="$5^1=5$" width="49" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/b/48b9bd1f4ae512ed331bd0401208caea8fb9b89a.png" class="latex" alt="$7^1=7$" width="50" height="15" />, since indivisibility by any powers lower than these means indivisibility by a higher power of the prime (for example, indivisibility by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/c/3fcc33a7c840ca476cc3df92991ac6edc1acb78d.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="15" /> means indivisibility by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/b/7fbca198ac6f2f0c343d1de3b5cf8af5c1e9d32a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3=8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="19" />. Since the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, it is not the answer, and the digits have to add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> be the digits: since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/a/71a90bc434bbf8b31045b3f5e8cf98910deea145.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot(a+b) | 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/3/8934cdf15a3938158d6c0e043e4105c4d7da305f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)|9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />. The only possible choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a08a01be19d538198ba97192d0e85d40442f39c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/f/b0fd8270f7d7f37bb87d25ef2d7bfc81f148f9c9.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8bd5db8574209fe06220a5d9407e04faa1b1658.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bdab922049ed5ced6b8202f91456dcc1124904c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc04892c672addda50fb21cd723f160be2443f72.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. The only number that works is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4240476d0dffb419ae5dd6c1f2c489c6f2f70e27.png" class="latex" alt="$5544$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />, but it is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6cf7de7cc0a8d328f0e48f0710c007abf84aa73.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ B }$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p><p>~edited by mobius247
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Since 5 has the "strictest" divisibility rules of the single digit numbers, we check that case first. To be divisible by 9, the other repeating digit must be 4. Seeing that 5544 is the only arrangement of 2 4's and 2 5's that is divisible by the other single digit numbers and itself is not divisible by 5, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/2/352fe4877433492e5933518c7c5f594ad7c2b20e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(B) 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="49" height="29" />.
-liu4505
~edited by mobius247
</p>
