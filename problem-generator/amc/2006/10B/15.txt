<p>Rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is similar to rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" />. The area of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7ad08337ee8955c04fd241de1518797a535258a.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAD = 60^\circ$" width="105" height="13" />. What is the area of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09dc560e2b95497143ac284151b3cf4bd64ee463.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] defaultpen(linewidth(0.7)+fontsize(10)); size(120); pair A=origin, B=(2,0), C=(3, sqrt(3)), D=(1, sqrt(3)), E=(1, 1/sqrt(3)), F=(2, 2/sqrt(3)); pair point=(3/2, sqrt(3)/2); draw(B--C--D--A--B--F--D--E--B); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, dir(point--A)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, dir(point--B)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, dir(point--C)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, dir(point--D)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;, E, dir(point--E)); label(&quot;$F$&quot;, F, dir(point--F)); [/asy]" width="202" height="132" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f22501cc30f8dcc8c1d4643946c805e46e9f0ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 6\qquad \textbf{(B) } 4\sqrt{3}\qquad \textbf{(C) } 8\qquad \textbf{(D) } 9\qquad \textbf{(E) } 6\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="404" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Using the property that opposite angles are equal in a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Rhombus" title="Rhombus">rhombus</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6ac92a2bea820e6f7ccaa5adcbfadc7c1fdc2737.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DAB = \angle DCB = 60 ^\circ$" width="186" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f8348f8c6f68a35b760d9cf7651d030128e82b.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC = \angle ABC = 120 ^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="193" height="13" />. It is easy to see that rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is made up of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equilateral_triangle" title="Equilateral triangle">equilateral triangles</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e94f1abd32d37d04a5b77e333689bf70ef83864.png" class="latex" alt="$DAB$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf51b47182ee2e945de88c04f653d01d4919d104.png" class="latex" alt="$DCB$" width="44" height="12" />. Let the lengths of the sides of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. 
</p><p>The longer <a href="/wiki/index.php/Diagonal" title="Diagonal">diagonal</a> of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" /> is a side of an equilateral triangle with a side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/5/e659b87449f2abdb7e676412b47a4163c9b0bee1.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = s$" width="62" height="12" />. The longer diagonal of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is twice the length of an altitude of of an equilateral triangle with a side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/9/b29e5c1402c7399cfb1971ed1ef32aa0d56b8495.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 2 \cdot \frac{s\sqrt{3}}{2} = s\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="168" height="41" />.
</p><p>The ratio of the longer diagonal of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" /> to rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/3/7132eb23fd1de9b624cee57a54ce48f99cf4e8e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{s}{s\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="87" height="45" />. Therefore, the ratio of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Area" title="Area">area</a> of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" /> to rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a60de6cdbc0895c123812c1db50c55d59b5ee6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \right) ^2 = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="99" height="56" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the area of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/3/5f36890d22350b1f79b34fb107bba0227c58f4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{24} = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="58" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/2417ce09d518536397929339219f68d9f3d69539.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="92" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Triangle DAB is equilateral so triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/291a26605884e355c23c524c392c07ad49e5df5a.png" class="latex" alt="$DEA$" width="43" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/c/9ece55f9a0c8c69d74514764c04b2a52b1cf734e.png" class="latex" alt="$AEB$" width="42" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/0/2c0afb40c0a08457a36ebab89b23c17b40b9eff8.png" class="latex" alt="$BED$" width="44" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7a8dfd3e68089fff4bb33022c7eb7d21b74a2ae.png" class="latex" alt="$BFD$" width="44" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/d/efdd26b7cbfad505c1adbdfccc28d3ab1eb91872.png" class="latex" alt="$BFC$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/9/ad9b7c63ecfeea6fff934a7c02b93a4cf9671f9a.png" class="latex" alt="$CFD$" width="43" height="12" /> are all congruent with angles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919bf5d461970fc8d905d63704ef763c08594184.png" class="latex" alt="$30^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919bf5d461970fc8d905d63704ef763c08594184.png" class="latex" alt="$30^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/f/f3fd424e953ac76b50bf6103875acc049d21d3c2.png" class="latex" alt="$120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> from which it follows that rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac20853d56aed0edba1c70db16d44d3ca40402cd.png" class="latex" alt="$BFDE$" width="58" height="12" /> has one third the area of rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/2/2a221fe8374d59209bbd900b2024e9648d09ceda.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \Longrightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(C) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="106" height="30" />.
</p><p>Note: A quick way to visualize this method is to draw the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" /> and notice the two equilateral triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/8/e08af8f3acc34996e3cba566dc970b7ed496ba4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADB$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/fee181f0e279c48906d10847f1986f902dc84efe.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DBC$" width="60" height="13" />.
</p>
