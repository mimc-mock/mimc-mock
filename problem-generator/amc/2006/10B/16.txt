<p>Leap Day, February 29, 2004, occurred on a Sunday. On what day of the week will Leap Day, February 29, 2020, occur? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f8ddeef0274b7b6b2575ef4ded6da82cd7f32bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \textrm{Tuesday} \qquad \textbf{(B) } \textrm{Wednesday} \qquad \textbf{(C) } \textrm{Thursday} \qquad \textbf{(D) } \textrm{Friday} \qquad \textbf{(E) } \textrm{Saturday}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="631" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988ab73be88b10c8cbf520db5e7cb4b3c6531963.png" class="latex" alt="$365$" width="26" height="12" /> days in a year, plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> extra day if there is a Leap Day, which occurs on years that are multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> (with a few exceptions that don't affect this problem). 
</p><p>Therefore, the number of days between Leap Day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ae760d1ea10eb7a90d22087b3c7e2393d02117.png" class="latex" alt="$2004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> and Leap Day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/9/509a9369d5c55fd98dc99cb2938fb44f0101f90a.png" class="latex" alt="$2020$" width="35" height="12" /> is: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07fe0333728372482545f25f039fecd0d3dc6ba3.png" class="latex" alt="$16 \cdot 365 + 4 \cdot 1 = 5844$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="172" height="14" />
</p><p>Since the days of the week repeat every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> days and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/9/d39395a3a9be034eb1e92e1e0d8b69b86b86ab90.png" class="latex" alt="$5844 \equiv -1 \bmod{7}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="138" height="13" />, the day of the week Leap Day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/9/509a9369d5c55fd98dc99cb2938fb44f0101f90a.png" class="latex" alt="$2020$" width="35" height="12" /> occurs is the day of the week the day before Leap Day <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ae760d1ea10eb7a90d22087b3c7e2393d02117.png" class="latex" alt="$2004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> occurs, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/f/09f3b13308558237c3796834b1e70d22adfb0568.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }\textrm{Saturday}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="112" height="30" />.
</p>
