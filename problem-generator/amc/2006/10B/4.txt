<p>Circles of diameter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> inch and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> inches have the same center. The smaller circle is painted red, and the portion outside the smaller circle and inside the larger circle is painted blue. What is the ratio of the blue-painted area to the red-painted area? 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2006amc10b04.gif" class="image"><img alt="2006amc10b04.gif" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//4/4a/2006amc10b04.gif" width="80" height="80" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/1/2d1406ece86163432de4a6d530399586657459af.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2\qquad \textbf{(B) } 3\qquad \textbf{(C) } 6\qquad \textbf{(D) } 8\qquad \textbf{(E) } 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The area painted red is equal to the area of the smaller circle and the area painted blue is equal to the area of the larger circle minus the area of the smaller circle. 
</p><p>So we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d37c9f484cc23142d62c049c710cdd9492af7cef.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} A_{red}&amp;=\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2=\frac{\pi}{4}\\ A_{blue}&amp;=\pi\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2-\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2=2\pi\\ \end{align*}" width="250" height="99" />
</p><p>So the desired ratio is: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/c/ecc284c5f19869d04e3dc6aa1db4377dc331da45.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{A_{blue}}{A_{red}}=\frac{2\pi}{\frac{\pi}{4}}=2\cancel{\pi}\cdot \frac{4}{\cancel{\pi}}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }8}.$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="251" height="46" />
</p>
