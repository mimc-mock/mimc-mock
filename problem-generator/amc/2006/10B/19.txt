<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The shaded area is equivalent to the area of sector <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f433b683ff8ceb019b8940b8a8c403afb6656e65.png" class="latex" alt="$DOE$" width="44" height="12" /> minus the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f433b683ff8ceb019b8940b8a8c403afb6656e65.png" class="latex" alt="$DOE$" width="44" height="12" /> plus the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd086bfde3cbcd3899095243a222eee23e40b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$DBE$" width="44" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/7/237ae3619645e900fe99f9d535c417dcdcf28d25.png" class="latex" alt="$(DA)^2=(CE)^2=2^2-1^2=3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="237" height="19" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/6/7e6af671ba8694bf09b71cb0ef8cc03eb9886364.png" class="latex" alt="$DA=CE=\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="18" />.
</p><p>Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/4/1449f9c78df10e5e3afe22131e754813b5bdf856.png" class="latex" alt="$DOA$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/3/b33dc6f29fb980fcdf0551a878a8bfb7f7a4de74.png" class="latex" alt="$EOC$" width="43" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangles with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/c/51c95ac412f6963c7df84ea44a9ee0ad954d0769.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EOC = \angle DOA = 60^\circ$" width="185" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b91381b367f9753ded66ab981c7451f2cb117578.png" class="latex" alt="$OABC$" width="56" height="13" /> is a square, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/7/0e7dbea252ee8764607eec6d303c31fffab4f149.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle COA = 90^\circ$" width="103" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c1c372a7653084bd8f4913d852a9f56bba50f5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DOE$" width="57" height="12" /> can be found by doing some subtraction of angles. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/5/735d59bc76c8b253255341f4032a3eff047019d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle COA - \angle DOA = \angle DOC = \angle EOA$" width="294" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/2/2a21723f4abba6ee2b4271edcc0964e3a7a768df.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ - 60^\circ = \angle EOA = 30^\circ$" width="201" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/7472f53a8d674c681a9e8dc112a46a24f1989ced.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DOA - \angle EOA = \angle DOE$" width="215" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/5/5e59a846f6452a5ccd46f5d987f2f9afb70fbced.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ - 30^\circ = \angle DOE = 30^\circ$" width="203" height="13" />
</p><p>So, the area of sector <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f433b683ff8ceb019b8940b8a8c403afb6656e65.png" class="latex" alt="$DOE$" width="44" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e9282e78facf7fe97dc487a4c1caaa65e0d9851.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30}{360} \cdot \pi \cdot 2^2 = \frac{\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="37" />.
</p><p>The area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f433b683ff8ceb019b8940b8a8c403afb6656e65.png" class="latex" alt="$DOE$" width="44" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/7/d67bc2a188e9626406b74ee5308da27c68890ed4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot \sin 30^\circ = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="154" height="37" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/4/a5482e2df77a27420825081bb54e937069afc406.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=CB=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="13" /> , <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/f/caf3439e55c1afbdf15c96fccf39c731bb726381.png" class="latex" alt="$DB=EB=(\sqrt{3}-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="21" />. So, the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd086bfde3cbcd3899095243a222eee23e40b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$DBE$" width="44" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/6/af6f7ded5326ee34e85010e5bfb3cd416f1cafaa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \cdot (\sqrt{3}-1)^2 = 2-\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="183" height="37" />. Therefore, the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/8/5286dd5473d045933993507794a26edcdc263b89.png" class="latex" alt="$(\frac{\pi}{3}) - (1) + (2-\sqrt{3}) = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{\pi}{3} + 1 - \sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="332" height="46" />
</p><p>OR
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/d/34db479782fad4b5b377a6840ac23bfcfef4e871.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ODA}$" width="59" height="13" /> has the same height as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c1650795e456a6fb48c9aad446b1239df04d0f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{OBD}$" width="60" height="13" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdd1b53e5faf7a118222e4b7571d1e9afa0cf56.png" class="latex" alt="$1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>We already know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/1/891b266697238fa7df21b7cbb1b9453f5b4ce1c6.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = \sqrt{3} - 1.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c1650795e456a6fb48c9aad446b1239df04d0f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{OBD}$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/d/20db91f337449a2d0337cb2e834e5072a2880acf.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{3}-1) \cdot 1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="205" height="41" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/e/5de746609b4d2207294f4d12055ede3d2e54e546.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{OBD} = \triangle{OBE} = \frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="232" height="41" />
</p><p>Therefore the sum of the areas is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/a/8ca44df0074de8880e51a8e759c2a4e61f11b1d7.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2} = \sqrt{3}-1.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="165" height="41" />
</p><p>Then the area of the shaded area becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/a/88a6dbb2c1537e35a482d40da0c78638efe2bd80.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{3} - (\sqrt{3} - 1) = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{\pi}{3} +1 - \sqrt{3}}.$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="278" height="46" />
