<p>Oscar buys <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pencils and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> erasers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/8/078860bc3c75731e529cf5127116af83716a0631.png" class="latex" alt="$1.00$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" />. A pencil costs more than an eraser, and both items cost a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Whole_number" title="Whole number">whole number</a> of cents. What is the total cost, in cents, of one pencil and one eraser?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b969d790c2ba774132bc3639a2f19d69573baf8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 10\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 12\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 15\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 18\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the price of a pencil be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and an eraser <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" />.  Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/8/508b507d02265fe591fe2532b9fdc9068f6f5607.png" class="latex" alt="$13p + 3e = 100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/d/d7d204abb3c771b3364e473c7ec58ae405050b2f.png" class="latex" alt="$p &gt; e &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />.  Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Positive_integer" title="Positive integer">positive integers</a>, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/d/86d2d5f719eca09511b6b93b79c1c65b65bd9705.png" class="latex" alt="$e \geq 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2c333bdfe7b62c79680a0ee5dfb631ae623ebc4.png" class="latex" alt="$p \geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />.
</p><p>Considering the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equation" title="Equation">equation</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/8/508b507d02265fe591fe2532b9fdc9068f6f5607.png" class="latex" alt="$13p + 3e = 100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" /> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Modulo" class="mw-redirect" title="Modulo">modulo</a> 3 (that is, comparing the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Remainder" title="Remainder">remainders</a> when both sides are divided by 3) we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/1/121bfeb6665c785d9037d2b97703916b020fe7dd.png" class="latex" alt="$p + 0e \equiv 1 \pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="154" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> leaves a remainder of 1 on division by 3.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2c333bdfe7b62c79680a0ee5dfb631ae623ebc4.png" class="latex" alt="$p \geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />, possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> are 4, 7, 10 ....
</p><p>Since 13 pencils cost less than 100 cents, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b8306907b8e48878b51fea7c6ca41de4c773b709.png" class="latex" alt="$13p &lt; 100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="78" height="16" />.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/6435d1848d08dd53184290e30cbf3a64918f606e.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \times 10 = 130$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> is too high, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> must be 4 or 7.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/e/6be241415c542a527773b9c883ce3cba4c07f1d9.png" class="latex" alt="$p = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c7714f45486409c278605e2157eced2973acc76.png" class="latex" alt="$13p = 52$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="16" /> and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d4aa09f1e8ceaa1e066d6e6796db29677d24331.png" class="latex" alt="$3e = 48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="13" /> giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a193db3110e4b85c13e92d77d99909f5325a5884.png" class="latex" alt="$e = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" />.  This contradicts the pencil being more expensive.  The only remaining value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> is 7; then the 13 pencils cost <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/ace8a83e2baed234f90eeb1186d9d661f93fd715.png" class="latex" alt="$7 \times 13= 91$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="13" /> cents and so the 3 erasers together cost 9 cents and each eraser costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46bb2b0ea935df4882cab786b730e29cec50248f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9}{3} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" /> cents.
</p><p>Thus one pencil plus one eraser cost <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec992ac90008edc74e0d2606116924dc3d59791.png" class="latex" alt="$7 + 3 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> cents, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/a/6fa6b62530ea1c0cb8381755b782ccaadbd652ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since we know that the values of pencils and erasers are both whole numbers, and that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> erasers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pencils, we can choose numbers to be the value for the pencil, and if the remainder to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/8/078860bc3c75731e529cf5127116af83716a0631.png" class="latex" alt="$1.00$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" /> is divisible by 3, we know our answer is correct. There are 7 numbers to check, although we can eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f55e0a11e29af7baee666c19c147e6f7c1923e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> mentally, since those numbers would probably have the cost of erasers higher than the cost of pencils (6 would too, but it is faster to just test 6). Trying 6, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/2/ed258993e885295d997b135a00b795f4b65a43de.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \cdot 6 = 72$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />. 28 is not divisible by 3, so we know that this number is not correct. Moving on to 7, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1fdf20f0bb89111a5e5ff4e487bdbdc37d33c6e.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \cdot 7 = 91$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="13" />. We know that 9 is a multiple of 3, so this value works! 
</p><p>Since the total cost of erasers is 9 cents, we know the individual eraser is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46bb2b0ea935df4882cab786b730e29cec50248f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9}{3} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/4/6845da8ef8a2820b712c96fb513a53d20c9af197.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 7 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> so our answer is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/a/6fa6b62530ea1c0cb8381755b782ccaadbd652ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />
</p><p>~Shadow-18
</p>
