<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">Circles</a> with <a href="/wiki/index.php/Center_(geometry)" title="Center (geometry)"> centers</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f4d1e83d6eef61f31d78844deb189c54a7abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/4/544dead885d6b05b8f8bc3ff3b1bdb320505c146.png" class="latex" alt="$(14,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" /> have <a href="/wiki/index.php/Radius" title="Radius"> radii</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, respectively. The equation of a common external <a href="/wiki/index.php/Tangent_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tangent line">tangent</a> to the circles can be written in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/335977913022df1fb3a473f90be2743ad42ada67.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx+b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/3/073b68444caca3c51f4ce50ee7fe4e5939755e22.png" class="latex" alt="$m&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="13" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p>
<center><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:AMC12_2006A_19.png" class="image"><img alt="AMC12 2006A 19.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//8/8d/AMC12_2006A_19.png" width="236" height="168" /></a></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/6/d764946775c90a868586b5ab6aa873e27465e32f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{908}{119}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{909}{119}\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac{130}{17}\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{911}{119}\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ }  \frac{912}{119}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="451" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> be the line that goes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f4d1e83d6eef61f31d78844deb189c54a7abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/4/544dead885d6b05b8f8bc3ff3b1bdb320505c146.png" class="latex" alt="$(14,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> be the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/335977913022df1fb3a473f90be2743ad42ada67.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx+b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" />. If we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> be the measure of the acute angle formed by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and the x-axis, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/3/3936324ce18ebc1ca767f49dd72de7661748b2e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\theta=\frac{5}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="83" height="38" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> clearly bisects the angle formed by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and the x-axis, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/4/de488b26ee338843bc74bcb671b2a3121de4ec2d.png" class="latex" alt="$m=\tan{2\theta}=\frac{2\tan\theta}{1-\tan^2{\theta}}=\frac{120}{119}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="246" height="38" />. We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> intersect at a point on the x-axis. The equation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b69f121c5c9235a0d5474db98072246f45d6a099.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{5}{12}x+\frac{19}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="109" height="38" />, so the coordinate of this point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/2/d129e2b5d52e12dfc10d4c65f5c1c996495fc599.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(-\frac{38}{5},0\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="76" height="43" />. Hence the equation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/5/925dff20451974fc04473ac9e1463c672c164937.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{120}{119}x+\frac{912}{119}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="127" height="38" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aae9aa5a7a3a8f9d7c0ea9bb0c24d0c4f48c0195.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac{912}{119}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="61" height="38" />, and our answer choice is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea2b8f6b62721fc3e02ca622cd6da092a31bbc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="24" />.
</p>
