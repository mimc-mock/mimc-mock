<p>Let 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/f/d3fc93b8ee594418078878bdd062d66625965e0d.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1=\{(x,y)|\log_{10}(1+x^2+y^2)\le 1+\log_{10}(x+y)\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="399" height="19" />
</p><p>and 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/a/18a637503eddd87e3ce6303c51c0889578137713.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2=\{(x,y)|\log_{10}(2+x^2+y^2)\le 2+\log_{10}(x+y)\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="399" height="19" />.
</p><p>What is the ratio of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f4caea56d945d0b6e49701b42ce06e0fb92e663.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10d5ee88ffc7d59f9c282b366953fb4fa4051f6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/1/3e10f0c2a5863ffecfe6b1e0e34a6d276061e3d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 98\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 99\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 100\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 101\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ }  102$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="414" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Looking at the constraints of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10d5ee88ffc7d59f9c282b366953fb4fa4051f6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/4/c8428959aacc9e0ad5512ebfcaa7fd6c4bed7a00.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/188997a5208b244950fb734d2456a67a50db08b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(1+x^2+y^2)\le 1+\log_{10}(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="288" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/f/a3f8c02e1337513bc170ee9ce5a6cf8ac5a21d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(1+x^2+y^2)\le \log_{10} 10 +\log_{10}(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="337" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/e/6beee7198fcc98dd80fd2df00ca8dbcf3677a591.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(1+x^2+y^2)\le \log_{10}(10x+10y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="293" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/5/8b52b467c984ef9cd882c670a2b7bf64a7c7d58a.png" class="latex" alt="$1+x^2+y^2 \le 10x+10y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="190" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/0/610d3a9a06e9b52a13c2e215a49c3d29043040bd.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-10x+y^2-10y \le -1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="204" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2f5c1abb84197bee43b209ac24800d33360362f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-10x+25+y^2-10y+25 \le 49$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="280" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/8/3d87910d7b871ee24cae33babfaa9d0e7034f185.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-5)^2 + (y-5)^2 \le (7)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10d5ee88ffc7d59f9c282b366953fb4fa4051f6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> is a circle with a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. So, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10d5ee88ffc7d59f9c282b366953fb4fa4051f6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/9/989c1024557838c086f0bb2c1f53e496cfaced8b.png" class="latex" alt="$49\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" />.
</p><p>Looking at the constraints of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f4caea56d945d0b6e49701b42ce06e0fb92e663.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/4/c8428959aacc9e0ad5512ebfcaa7fd6c4bed7a00.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/2/402fbc600fbe930a8f7982534f0cbfc9d0d74820.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(2+x^2+y^2)\le 2+\log_{10}(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="288" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/9/1892f7cd65e19cd2757063dd3bc8d45769f6f711.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(2+x^2+y^2)\le \log_{10} 100 +\log_{10}(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="346" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/6/5461d57aa1382d1d4523cc0aea63c78c012cc58b.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(2+x^2+y^2)\le \log_{10}(100x+100y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="311" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/b/b0b05853068ff8298d8f337fc5417d19090be498.png" class="latex" alt="$2+x^2+y^2 \le 100x+100y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="208" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/1/e515265b9692c3327bb56fdeb11b5a67950be7fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-100x+y^2-100y \le -2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="223" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f4d37bcb595725814324ca52067926c547c3a9d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-100x+2500+y^2-100y+2500 \le 4998$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="353" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/7/bf7b3318d592421cba4660dff97234a2ee47be5f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-50)^2 + (y-50)^2 \le (7\sqrt{102})^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="262" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f4caea56d945d0b6e49701b42ce06e0fb92e663.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> is a circle with a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/2/892521ea813888521d59f16cb17f546cacaa2460.png" class="latex" alt="$7\sqrt{102}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="18" />. So, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f4caea56d945d0b6e49701b42ce06e0fb92e663.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/3/da386c867b551bfb5aa1ad5fca0fbdfc54003cf7.png" class="latex" alt="$4998\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" />.
</p><p>So the desired ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/4/9746b998ed253b4e02c83fac35afe1a8e3f2f9a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4998\pi}{49\pi} = 102 \Rightarrow \boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="157" height="38" />.
</p>
