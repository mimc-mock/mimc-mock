<p>For some particular value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6fdf1f461bbf51b9a303c8e0a07d67092f54959.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b+c+d+1)^N$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="158" height="19" /> is expanded and like terms are combined, the resulting expression contains exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/8/888c508062c09735bab7ff0e11e7dc80cc7580db.png" class="latex" alt="$1001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> terms that include all four variables <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d0f68c04ccf111a809e060df4dd77c2ae895ab3.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, each to some positive power. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a9233553e03996a652917504e717cc3826c870b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }9 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 14 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 16 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 17 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 19$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>All the desired terms are in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/6/eb66244d9bb340efafb96412cf69171ad0ee1e26.png" class="latex" alt="$a^xb^yc^zd^w1^t$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="15" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17fd22f28771ef51da717c4955a71966b6c300a.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y + z + w + t = N$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="179" height="16" /> (the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/b/c2b44a56be6b7b7e6f249ebc3834c44711b126a6.png" class="latex" alt="$1^t$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="13" height="15" /> part is necessary to make stars and bars work better.)
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> must be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />), let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b211625e3c85aa917584d4f75c6c6220a6043074.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39; = x - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="80" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b76638032b49c8934aea45a0396d3752acef3284.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; = y - 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="78" height="17" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf0be85ef06d160ccfb1b32b63d988545474a145.png" class="latex" alt="$z&#39; = z - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/2451dcb45bb010da63d6c5400adab467ddacadb2.png" class="latex" alt="$w&#39; = w - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/f/27fa5c2f6fb7681e881112482e5d10b9824f4ab6.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39; + y&#39; + z&#39; + w&#39; + t = N - 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="229" height="17" />. Now, we use stars and bars (also known as ball and urn) to see that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e626c312dd19426a6f77e4245e2dfb2101e79b55.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{(N-4)+4}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="117" height="53" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/1/6d10e947d642ae5b5c9d646cf3e60f6bbbf58809.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{N}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="40" height="53" /> solutions to this equation. We notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/a/8ea2d9d944cb64bba38978dd664e79e5c5090c22.png" class="latex" alt="$1001=7\cdot11\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="131" height="13" />, which leads us to guess that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is around these numbers. This suspicion proves to be correct, as we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/2/c3270fd55c2d10438f5c03e1d815d666ab0b20fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{14}{4} = 1001$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="105" height="53" />, giving us our answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4be219751c6088a5dc30450d5528cc50aac3483e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }14.}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="71" height="30" />
</p><p>Note: An alternative is instead of making the transformation, we "give" the variables <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/671fcdad94abd4db28c0060628336bc3f70aebe8.png" class="latex" alt="$x, y, z, w$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="11" /> 1, and then proceed as above.
</p><p>~ Mathkiddie

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hockey_Stick_Identity" class="mw-redirect" title="Hockey Stick Identity">Hockey Stick Identity</a>, the number of terms that have all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> raised to a positive power is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/4/8c44b727b133e4a0121b9bf9986ddc1ebe488400.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{N-1}{3}+\binom{N-2}{3}+\cdots + \binom{4}{3}+\binom{3}{3}=\binom{N}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="401" height="53" />. We now want to find some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/d/23dbc16ca9872fd7b2a8a22e7db4119c519d1b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{N}{4} = 1001$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="104" height="53" />. As mentioned above, after noticing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/0/2a0712850bbb763e9c997dfc78e70d333f01acc5.png" class="latex" alt="$1001 = 7\cdot11\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="131" height="13" />, and some trial and error, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/2/c3270fd55c2d10438f5c03e1d815d666ab0b20fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{14}{4} = 1001$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="105" height="53" />, giving us our answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4be219751c6088a5dc30450d5528cc50aac3483e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }14.}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="71" height="30" />
</p>
