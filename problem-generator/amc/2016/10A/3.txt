<p>For every dollar Ben spent on bagels, David spent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> cents less. Ben paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/e/25ebe155234384607a9fcdd394c24d01fc95e966.png" class="latex" alt="$$12.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> more than David. How much did they spend in the bagel store together?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/f/6afae1ed4d9336c250a3e3ca857f7ca0a31552a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ $37.50 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ $50.00\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ $87.50\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ $90.00\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ $92.50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="555" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If Ben paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/1/a11bf142d85629208372525554361f78fd46140a.png" class="latex" alt="$$ 12.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> more than David, then he paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead4b27011e0c8526e155c2636244f5eea968a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12.5}{.25}= $ 50.00$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="110" height="37" />. Thus, David paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f3cad855f3a0dfbd937023ff9b03dd09964d2c.png" class="latex" alt="$$ 37.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" />, and they spent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/7/bf7a1ad646ff3f90b2bed51dfb487bdc3e5e4dd2.png" class="latex" alt="$50.00+37.50 =$ 87.50 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(C) }$ 87.50}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="326" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If for every dollar Ben spent on bagels David spent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> cents less, then the ratio of the money that Ben spent to David spent is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/def7726c2babb527f7ab2ae4c436cc012ebbd351.png" class="latex" alt="$4:3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />. We know that Ben paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/e/25ebe155234384607a9fcdd394c24d01fc95e966.png" class="latex" alt="$$12.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> more than David, so we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/6998ae2d8f3fa781fa38156fcb6d7ef7530c8d93.png" class="latex" alt="$$12.50\cdot (4+3)=$ 87.50.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="200" height="20" /> Then the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/9/ee90f076869827dbb3b46ebe83c1a7399f1182dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(C)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="28" />
</p><p>~@azure123456
