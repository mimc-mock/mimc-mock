<p>How many ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/3/c73ed093cc73a8d9a14185fa525e7067e5c3725d.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y,z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" /> of positive integers satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/2/c52c751d7690d4997dc14060e34f21d46e0ca527.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(x,y) = 72, \text{lcm}(x,z) = 600 \text{ and lcm}(y,z)=900$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="396" height="20" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/0/8b01c857b5599e38f6a0dca743dddffb53bba64a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 15\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 24\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 27\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 64$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We prime factorize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/0/e3045d2706164241e6b209b04cc0c9932d5bf37a.png" class="latex" alt="$72,600,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" />. The prime factorizations are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb604e555192dd7fe9545b2c46dcff7d5ae77141.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3\times 3^2$" width="54" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cada094e88a4b3b2be1c0cf922ddd02e3b3211db.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3\times 3\times 5^2$" width="85" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/d/a1d7f44db35b70d699abf7b2dd0e85bcaeabc302.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2\times 3^2\times 5^2$" width="92" height="15" />, respectively. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/1/cd109fcc87b1858310b503f57ef3869f8227eec3.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2^a\times 3^b\times 5^c$" width="127" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/5/0d5455dc4b43aed6319d6090fe87a22ed8f0f47d.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2^d\times 3^e\times 5^f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="128" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/5/ec519ce5d64eadc63a9e2f2136fb1b4b6deac5b6.png" class="latex" alt="$z=2^g\times 3^h\times 5^i$" width="126" height="15" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/54144303e6de4ae84935ad1df9a2e569c7041808.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(a,d)=3\]" width="107" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/d/b7de5a5aefbaccf608fb441e29df3ac33370880c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(b,e)=2\]" width="104" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f6de12d6a6405690950d990a3222e90f0b561b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(a,g)=3\]" width="107" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a83271852abe6343879fad35f475114d4bdbb656.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(b,h)=1\]" width="106" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/0/4006861c44f3e57be3b6e3c63512dd130924b51a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(c,i)=2\]" width="102" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/7/947289a810fdb4c7fec0f66f8d18c74ef47cd61c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(d,g)=2\]" width="107" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9eef758965cfbad23241d45c675de9a45f148d0e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(e,h)=2\]" width="107" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c5064494de6f4aa86e6938be4b64e828c1b0002.png" class="latex" alt="$c=f=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/8/9f8577142b4a9d3858370b2d0705982b032f21e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" /> isn't a multiple of 5. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/9/f89301570507382e19a780347b813deff97871a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(d,g)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340605c5f88cf6864adc5b146c3dffca7a45df22.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3$" width="42" height="12" />. We also know that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bdafa0d0f88e43f58b9a7a0b61da8e22592949b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(b,h)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" /> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3dba6df0b85b5e84ac8db1c9560a086d8c76676a.png" class="latex" alt="$e=2$" width="41" height="12" />. So now some equations have become useless to us...let's take them out. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a83271852abe6343879fad35f475114d4bdbb656.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(b,h)=1\]" width="106" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/7/947289a810fdb4c7fec0f66f8d18c74ef47cd61c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\max(d,g)=2\]" width="107" height="18" /> are the only two important ones left. We do casework on each now. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bdafa0d0f88e43f58b9a7a0b61da8e22592949b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(b,h)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/b/0fba2d2c1ee713bedd8941946ed637de383c600d.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,h)=(1,0),(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Similarly if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/9/f89301570507382e19a780347b813deff97871a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\max(d,g)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/8/3e85d0d6a342f1a91ba628daffd5748c069d17ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(d,g)=(2,0),(2,1),(2,2),(1,2),(0,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="298" height="18" />. Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/0/220b50629d70f44789bd99c75d1ac9e105f8444d.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times 3=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }15}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="137" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>It is well known that if the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/b/1cb6dbc5f7db702219c287ab79b0852776a1ebf8.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(a,b)=c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/0/9c03ef80d00c658f3df00a001b3ad3175e0a1893.png" class="latex" alt="$p_1^ap_2^bp_3^c\dots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="20" />, then the highest power of all prime numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e480bb2ca9f52067b49398665762d69125c11ec.png" class="latex" alt="$p_1,p_2,p_3\dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="11" /> must divide into either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and/or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Or else a lower <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f09d50b5d1d48ebaf72218624428e2a99879182.png" class="latex" alt="$c_0=p_1^{a-\epsilon}p_2^{b-\epsilon}p_3^{c-\epsilon}\dots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="20" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aa935b5a960da182fbdd986bca927c62b83d506.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}$" width="25" height="12" />.
</p><p>Start from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />:<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2c59538be928c71924c8d01ec7fd1fddc6392c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(x,y)=72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3dbcad918782211455fe19a39b543c5da429ac33.png" class="latex" alt="$8\mid x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/9/839c60f534d4c61f85a63b6cb73fe2da3bc6fc30.png" class="latex" alt="$9\mid x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> or both. But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce89ac1ad0d5b0c836adaf47198be89877eac12.png" class="latex" alt="$9\nmid x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/4/e14736fcc0bb0efd29cd9e0d3b18555809fe7c9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(x,z)=600$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/d/f1d130d00e32646fedf42ef8596471e2fd7f4f86.png" class="latex" alt="$9\nmid 600$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />.
So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/5/2c5dce910bab6c211e7b2d83e3232b7ec93fd08e.png" class="latex" alt="$x=8,24$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108a5efd46bca5480f119231b08e5a4a3f74e746.png" class="latex" alt="$9,18,36$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> in both cases of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> but NOT <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b9e5743a693824380b1dc89304becf3ac151fc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(y,z)=900$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/6/cd67e1d663aa248e157061157b1b5bb01197b6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$72\nmid 900$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />.
</p><p>So there are six sets of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/4/b6400c6fe3f1ed70e5afd387a9686cdc3fa09531.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="11" /> and we will list all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> based on those.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/a/cda1b637425e69194d390b874a905dad396f8e97.png" class="latex" alt="$25\mid z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> must source all powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/b/2fbb5b27fbfe6f0b3282bb6b13aa7d94dddebff8.png" class="latex" alt="$z\in\{25,50,75,100,150,300\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="225" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/9/539ba62d5ac3d0bff4904702aa21456595d5e32e.png" class="latex" alt="$z\ne\{200,225\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" /> because of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aa935b5a960da182fbdd986bca927c62b83d506.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}$" width="25" height="12" /> restrictions.
