<p>For what value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> does <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/0/f20a8e2deb823896ee3fcdac38de7ae52f1fb42b.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{x}\cdot 100^{2x}=1000^{5}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="148" height="15" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eefb164682b5c68e13f8eac4b682fdc58485673e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can rewrite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/0/f20a8e2deb823896ee3fcdac38de7ae52f1fb42b.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{x}\cdot 100^{2x}=1000^{5}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="148" height="15" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d66882f3552f06b16e0329a02da2ff91d3b9e68.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{5x}=10^{15}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="88" height="15" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/d/79d64c5be65781d58e39c297545f5c2ceb9ba6f5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split} 10^x\cdot100^{2x} &amp; =10^x\cdot(10^2)^{2x} \\ 10^x\cdot10^{4x} &amp; =(10^3)^5 \\ 10^{5x} &amp; =10^{15} \end{split}\]" width="197" height="70" />
Since the bases are equal, we can set the exponents equal, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/5/8a50ea624703ad0d8ea25ac8f7b5a7cfdf618ec8.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Solving the equation gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/f/98f4c4505833bdd0802ba93838c7952c7647fd1e.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\;3}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="92" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can rewrite this expression as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/e/1ae1705a7db891d9685d4faf4b223ee2c1d15d81.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(10^x \cdot 100^{2x})=\log(1000^5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="223" height="19" /> , which can be simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/e/9ae7032523267efeda48d7da5cf0b714b9135547.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(10^{x}\cdot10^{4x})=5\log(1000)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="219" height="19" />, and that can be further simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/301eabe578471bac269664c20833df5a14ea3015.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(10^{5x})=5\log(10^3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="169" height="19" /> . This leads to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/5/8a50ea624703ad0d8ea25ac8f7b5a7cfdf618ec8.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Solving this linear equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/f/98f4c4505833bdd0802ba93838c7952c7647fd1e.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\;3}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="92" height="28" />
</p>
