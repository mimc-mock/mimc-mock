Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/4/e84fa54f1f4c06247f291d06bbac485b2a72b086.png" class="latex" alt="$E, F, G$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> be the centers, respectively, of equilateral triangles with bases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b7fe9cd149206f72ed64d16e4d93a16a09cb8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}, \overline{BC}, \overline{CD},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af6b80669b5ed740c0aef3fab0456e164cc7367.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DA},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="19" /> each exterior to the square. What is the ratio of the area of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> to the area of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The center of an equilateral triangle is its centroid, where the three medians meet.
</p><p>The distance along the median from the centroid to the base is one third the length of the median.
</p><p>Let the side length of the square be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. The height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efaa0c7cda9b10387b96d2de2cd49a0500bdba8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle E$" width="30" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8e512a82fc8e81b4791173e033baf975ba0505b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2},$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="41" /> so the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> to the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/8/528b4cd2ea0ec61662073a9ecbf98b06af74a6e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="105" height="41" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/b/3ab0d213167514722a71d34802c303287286292c.png" class="latex" alt="$EG = 2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="41" /> (from above) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/e/b6edc6c19b4dd2b4e552f28dba752c04c5e1b7ad.png" class="latex" alt="$+ 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> (side length of the square).
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927d4689159fbeb80f7b468ca9ae96b48e926312.png" class="latex" alt="$EG$" width="28" height="12" /> is the diagonal of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/3/cb3262100140d3d4d9c031ed59694f5fa0ceaeba.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[EFGH]}{ABCD} = \frac{\frac{EG^2}{2}}{1^2} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{2 + \sqrt{3}}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="263" height="53" />
