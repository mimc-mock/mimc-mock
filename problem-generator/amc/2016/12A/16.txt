<p>The graphs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/d/8ed1860f7430f32fd30415f6145039b336154103.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\log_3 x, y=\log_x 3, y=\log_\frac{1}{3} x,$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="257" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce01728d7cd3bb6a465d988bc92c83471adb3edc.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\log_x \dfrac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" /> are plotted on the same set of axes. How many points in the plane with positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates lie on two or more of the graphs? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/8/f68a620e8d126d7a4d48fcd5878a055aaa4ab6db.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Setting the first two equations equal to each other, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/1/0f19a7d51f87fafc57e414459b7ad5ac1f088eec.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_3 x = \log_x 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="17" />.
</p><p>Solving this, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/e/76e5934cf8a0f61505e3670125041bc35685a645.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(3, 1\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/4/544d2e9cafa24153a40d17d57ebe490db6cb95f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{1}{3}, -1\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="66" height="43" />.
</p><p>Similarly with the last two equations, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a6036b6e5e217bb50ad2fdc28d54711ff20cdb39.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(3, -1\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/1/781016657744476fc4a55bac773d681aa9ecf059.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{1}{3}, 1\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="52" height="43" />.
</p><p>Now, by setting the first and third equations equal to each other, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/2/d121c46659965a9b478d73042a83a638fbdc0f10.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(1, 0\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Pairing the first and fourth or second and third equations won't work because then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/070c1187cbcaab1724dc7168b94569e056429c2d.png" class="latex" alt="$\log x \leq 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="16" />.
</p><p>Pairing the second and fourth equations will yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/34310f2f36ed6d724838edb08788ee62acb33386.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, but since you can't divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/3/af3624d9bd37dc93c79527d3af56532e19f1469a.png" class="latex" alt="$\log 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="68" height="16" />, it doesn't work.
</p><p>After trying all pairs, we have a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/a/16a4e516be1dffd7a35c3080362198ba076c1c40.png" class="latex" alt="$\rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(D)} 5}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="75" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/e/b9e54c352186ef03fda93692f8caa5a55bb69402.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_b a =\log_c a / \log_c b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/d/06d22963cecc1f3f295feb47de7856bab437b28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_b a = \log_a a / \log_a b = 1/ \log_a b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="253" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97dcf7e051a803ae4e2e45bc7c2cddac6e0b9a66.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_\frac{1}{a} b = \log_a \frac{1}{a} / \log_a b = -1/ \log_a b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="274" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a3932c7e2f422af7fabcad5668813b64f61a15bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_\frac{1}{b} a = -\log_a b$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="130" height="21" />
</p><p>Therefore, the system of equations can be simplified to:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/2/442763f02c8abe0ff2542f403d3d03453f1d2420.png" class="latex" alt="$y = t$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/231f80b34aff6cf61a1751ef0350a385a1aed4e9.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -t$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/a/94ae12b241ae5dae72a42efc4d50fb9753127df4.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{1}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/5/f651386be9b49c592541fc38c6832e762ddebdf6.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -\frac{1}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />
</p><p>where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/e/dde1e99ff2a4c245a441987e5d4ae2c26e99feb7.png" class="latex" alt="$t = \log_3 x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="17" />. Note that all values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> correspond to exactly one positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> value, so all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/2/f529f1ec25f8294f000275a3634a36a6015c36a3.png" class="latex" alt="$(t,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> intersections will correspond to exactly one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> intersection in the positive-x area.
</p><p>Graphing this system of functions will generate a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/a/16a4e516be1dffd7a35c3080362198ba076c1c40.png" class="latex" alt="$\rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(D)} 5}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="75" height="30" />
</p>
