<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/0/f60eda23e098229c26f4a92a2422e53765c97836.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  size(6cm); defaultpen(fontsize(9pt)); draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,5)--(0,5)--cycle); filldraw((7,0)--(8,0)--(8,1)--(0,4)--(0,5)--(1,5)--cycle,gray(0.8));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(1/2,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$7$&quot;,(9/2,5),dir(90));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(8,1/2),dir(0));   label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(15/2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$7$&quot;,(7/2,0),dir(270));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(0,9/2),dir(180)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(0,2),dir(180));  draw((0,5)--(8,0));  [/asy]" width="285" height="195" />
</p><p>The bases of these triangles are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and by symmetry, their heights are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Thus, their areas are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/3628e955d7e4a0e6322ada48bc33c003f9b25dbf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/3628e955d7e4a0e6322ada48bc33c003f9b25dbf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />, which add to the area of the shaded region, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/f/c6f58dc126f0e3d6efbd6c22cbefc03fd6f3c857.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{6\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="34" height="48" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Find the area of the unshaded area by calculating the area of the triangles and rectangles outside of the shaded region. We can do this by splitting up the unshaded areas into various triangles and rectangles as shown.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/c/82cdaaa75ab582149da4c5e56880faabde9f14bd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  size(6cm); defaultpen(fontsize(9pt)); draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,5)--(0,5)--cycle); filldraw((7,0)--(8,0)--(8,1)--(0,4)--(0,5)--(1,5)--cycle,gray(0.8));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(1/2,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(6,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,(5/2,5),dir(90));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(8,1/2),dir(0)); label(&quot;$5/2$&quot;,(8,15/4),dir(0)); label(&quot;$3/2$&quot;,(8,7/4),dir(0));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(15/2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,(11/2,0),dir(270));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(0,9/2),dir(180)); label(&quot;$5/2$&quot;,(0,5/4),dir(180)); label(&quot;$3/2$&quot;,(0,13/4),dir(180));  draw((0,5/2)--(8,5/2)); draw((4,0)--(4,5));  [/asy]" width="285" height="175" />
</p><p>Notice that the two added lines bisect each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> sides of the large rectangle.
</p><p>Subtracting the unshaded area from the total area gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/b/9db7a808fd496c8735e685fa235af98470f68e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$40-33\frac{1}{2}=\boxed{6\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="129" height="48" />, so the correct answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693c4044f8526a8179956e8ac7a3f2f9d61e2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Notice that we can graph this on the coordinate plane.
</p><p>The top-left shaded figure has coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/4/524b999081f085cddf4ae0cb50e3ae017fe4c48e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,5), (0,5), (0,4), \left(4,\frac{5}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="204" height="45" />.
</p><p>Notice that we can apply the shoelace method to find the area of this polygon.
</p><p>We find that the area of the polygon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/9/4f926e78edb1fc7bf25a7ec0b08bc93b33145c11.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{13}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />.
</p><p>However, notice that the two shaded regions are two congruent polygons.
</p><p>Hence, the total area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c39b511dbe55ed5cde4c8bf59ed84141cdc8d37.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{13}{2}\implies \boxed{6\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="105" height="48" />  or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55bdade53f471aab49f63d1a734979cc0c636640.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="27" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/6/4b6192c64c68e3231dc8068e13b8c72669f681e3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  size(6cm); defaultpen(fontsize(9pt)); draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,5)--(0,5)--cycle); filldraw((7,0)--(8,0)--(0,5)--(1,5)--cycle,gray(0.7)); filldraw((8,0)--(8,1)--(0,4)--(0,5)--cycle,gray(0.9));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(1/2,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$7$&quot;,(9/2,5),dir(90));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(8,1/2),dir(0));   label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(15/2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$7$&quot;,(7/2,0),dir(270));  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(0,9/2),dir(180)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(0,2),dir(180));  [/asy]" width="285" height="195" />
</p><p>Split the region into four parts by the diagonal from the top left to the bottom right. Slide the top and bottom pieces next to each other to form a parallelogram with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, and slide the left and right pieces next to each other to form a parallelogram with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/e/09e35b79099fc525d6865f4330b99868d813ade1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. The total area is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/0/fb0ecb8cac5abb148f029faa0b16c0785ac529b4.png" class="latex" alt="$1\cdot\frac{5}{2}+1\cdot\frac{8}{2} = \frac{(5+8)(1)}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 6\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="293" height="49" />. ~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
</p><p><br />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/b/9eb39ef68c092bd1a44e629723aac9cd271369bf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  size(6cm); defaultpen(fontsize(9pt)); draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,5)--(0,5)--cycle); filldraw((7,0)--(8,0)--(8,1)--(0,4)--(0,5)--(1,5)--cycle,gray(0.8)); filldraw((1,0)--(0,0)--(0,1)--(8,4)--(8,5)--(7,5)--cycle,gray(0.8)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(1/2,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$6$&quot;,(4,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(15/2,5),dir(90)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(8,1/2),dir(0)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,(8,5/2),dir(0)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(8,9/2),dir(0)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(15/2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$6$&quot;,(4,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(1/2,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(0,9/2),dir(180)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,(0,5/2),dir(180)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(0,1/2),dir(180)); [/asy]" width="285" height="195" />
</p><p>We can subtract the 4 triangular areas from the overall rectangle. It can be noticed that two triangles have area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5efd5ec500dbcb682b034fae3b1b97a87213eb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6 \cdot \frac{5}{2}}{2} = \frac{15}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="42" />, and the other two triangles have area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/2/822789bd1846659a55b1be0a84a200a70268db9a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3 \cdot \frac{8}{2}}{2} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="72" height="42" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5def12cad1dd413f666974ca9688264d9f0ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$(8 \cdot 5) - 2(\frac{15}{2}) - 2(6) = 40 - 15 - 12 = 13$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="335" height="38" />. Since the desired area is half the shaded region, our area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/7/e1733166ccf16d5b51d5dd90ecb4c803e2e60705.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 6\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="70" height="49" />.
