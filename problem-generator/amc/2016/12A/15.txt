Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7ddd42e9c3e5bbb25571f24032e65e1a366b27.png" class="latex" alt="$[Q&#39;QRR&#39;]=5\sqrt6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="134" height="22" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/604a622f5931fe83c7182ea69cbb64e7c15b93c9.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ = 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c7839136323f37e68df220031e73b7f2838ef60.png" class="latex" alt="$QR = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> because they are the sum of two radii. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/a/74a66676922f9db997fa6c46c28cde3e3d9dabbb.png" class="latex" alt="$QQ&#39; - PP&#39; = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="121" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/5/815f0b2121f3fbc404956ce5b27656e9cf78b595.png" class="latex" alt="$RR&#39; - QQ&#39; = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="120" height="17" />, the difference of the radii. Using pythagorean theorem, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/2/58235ee706438b477de873005a8a7dec97ebb377.png" class="latex" alt="$P&#39;Q&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/c/0fccb766eefa99b8b49d8df6b3c0ba5334037b92.png" class="latex" alt="$Q&#39;R&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="36" height="17" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca7b4acb8eeb90e37fa1434cb61472589e78a050.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{8}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/8/ed8dcdaee5c02f5afb2e8774644fc8fbc0b614f3.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{24}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2062113f0e84baa9e885d6d04667e5ae327f612f.png" class="latex" alt="$P&#39;R&#39; = \sqrt{8} + \sqrt{24}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="142" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Draw a perpendicular from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c077caf1edeae5078f4966d66747364f531ece3f.png" class="latex" alt="$RR&#39;$" width="31" height="14" />, then we can use the Pythagorean theorem to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/6/ec6916b50feec24773d374eaada26a95bcabc6fd.png" class="latex" alt="$PR$" width="28" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32f93c98af3ca1df1aadac06e88e1d8063c92daf.png" class="latex" alt="$RR&#39; - PP&#39; = 2$" width="120" height="14" />. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/4/34425c835139b064faae59c0cbd1e0a8d2012ae2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[PR^2 = (\sqrt{8} + \sqrt{24})^2 + 4 = 36 + 16\sqrt{3} \Rightarrow PR = \sqrt{36 + 16\sqrt{3}} = 2\sqrt{9 + 4\sqrt{3}}\]" width="572" height="32" />
</p><p>To make our calculations easier, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd66af07d2faa5e0c48f87d96609a61ee40e263.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{9 + 4\sqrt{3}} = a$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="116" height="32" />. The semi-perimeter of our triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a9810e24954b76ecbdb5ca91fd21b9f2227cbf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3 + 5 + 2a}{2} = 4 + a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="150" height="37" />. Symbolize the area of the triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. Using Heron's formula, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/1/331e5ba62e524cfefcaea43fc18d1b965bdb15e5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A^2 = (4 + a)(4 + a - 2a)(4 + a - 3)(4 + a - 5) = (4 + a)(4 - a)(a + 1)(a - 1) = (16 - a^2)(a^2 - 1)\]" width="664" height="19" /> We can remove the outer root of a. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dccce5a67bae050a8e10d08e26c37a52c46ec59c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A^2 = (16 - 9 - 4\sqrt{3})(9 + 4\sqrt{3} - 1) = (7 - 4\sqrt{3})(8 + 4\sqrt{3}) = 8 - 4\sqrt{3} \rightarrow A = \sqrt{8 - 4\sqrt{3}}\]" width="637" height="32" />
</p><p>We solve the nested root. We want to turn <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/f/88f5a6720283d79403e2915bfc46549b837b4495.png" class="latex" alt="$8 - 4\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="18" /> into the square of something. If we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/6/2e60124d6234e6e11be1a6171b64e0e5eb66070c.png" class="latex" alt="$(a - b) ^ 2 = 8 - 4\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="21" />, then we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95a82c36c18445bf7df0cd1722b79596f5e5774d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{cases} a^2 + b^2 = 8 \\ ab = 2\sqrt{3} \end{cases}\]" width="99" height="53" /> Solving the system of equations, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f34927dd09938a9ad9d5e3275a9d37458daa2e.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \sqrt{6}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/5012487a078b2b28d9a0dda438801818a54ce71c.png" class="latex" alt="$b = \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="56" height="18" />. Alternatively, you can square all the possible solutions until you find one that is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/0/2306b1b9d8ae667fb97dedfd775462702d564382.png" class="latex" alt="$8 - 4\sqrt{3}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="72" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/a/5fa1d9bbb019b6aa0e9a7dfa38515f51cdb112c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\newline A = \sqrt{8 - 4\sqrt{3}} = \sqrt{(\sqrt{6} - \sqrt{2})^2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{2}.}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="1052" height="40" />
~ZericH

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
