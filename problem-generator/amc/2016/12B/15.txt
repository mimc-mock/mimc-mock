<p>All the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380264eeecdaa2e001ecc6a81415cefc2aff6a31.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" /> are assigned to the six faces of a cube, one number to each face. For each of the eight vertices of the cube, a product of three numbers is computed, where the three numbers are the numbers assigned to the three faces that include that vertex. What is the greatest possible value of the sum of these eight products?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e0b40f41a69edf0717127cf181c173cd4d27d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 312 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 343 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 625 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 729 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 1680$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="448" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First assign each face the letters <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67f089fdbe6e5066ae93dfdec4438bc1bba0ff82.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d,e,f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" />. The sum of the product of the faces is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f0964c8657b987e4f96a87dc1916a7edb0a3bbf7.png" class="latex" alt="$abc+acd+ade+aeb+fbc+fcd+fde+feb$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="372" height="16" />. We can factor this into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adcd1ac78aad6ccfc44d8780eebd7a08efc2a448.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+f)(b+c)(d+e)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="18" /> which is the product of the sum of each pair of opposite faces. In order to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adcd1ac78aad6ccfc44d8780eebd7a08efc2a448.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+f)(b+c)(d+e)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="18" />  we use the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d5f71f00958fa926347c125680e8e86305606c6.png" class="latex" alt="$(7+2)(6+3)(5+4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/d/cedece32bb60cb67e63e24c637b5221a124745a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 729 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We'll proceed from the factoring process above.
By the AM-GM inequality,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa67eef8049086302c27d7648d8e0d17e704e7dd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a_1+a_2+a_3}{3}\geq\sqrt[3]{a_1a_2a_3}\]" width="189" height="35" />
</p><p>Cubing both sides,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/3/233c106eb78fd6060382033db69c868915a0e2ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{a_1+a_2+a_3}{3}\right)^3\geq{a_1a_2a_3}\]" width="203" height="46" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/f/e8ff7ae5b392c1403f2bd600bb711810adbb8802.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=(a+f)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927fdd8169cdfc9fff64bf0147a3dfacc9088502.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=(b+c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93eabba6a5324e9f04a5809ad1a44684ec3816f2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=(d+e)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />. Let's substitute in these values.
