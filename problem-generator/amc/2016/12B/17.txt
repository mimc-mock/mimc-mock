<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Get the area of the triangle by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/Heron%27s_Formula">Heron's Formula</a>: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f5397e0210b5bd8a769a9c43747459c4327bf096.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)} = \sqrt{(12)(3)(4)(5)} = 12\sqrt{5}\]" width="396" height="32" />
Use the area to find the height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/3/bb3ddf9a5fa2fca5c4787d4f989af0eef1387bf0.png" class="latex" alt="$AH$" width="30" height="13" /> with known base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/5/2e5591c09999692603dbd49fb7507d22bf40df56.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[Area = 12\sqrt{5} = \frac{1}{2}bh = \frac{1}{2}(8)(AH)\]" width="265" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54ff1cb156818a28a44bc2448d45ca305e284ff.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AH = 3\sqrt{5}\]" width="87" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/5/8651c0ac9c0d33fdde298515c81ce3e3d98122f8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[BH = \sqrt{AB^2 - AH^2} = \sqrt{7^2 - (3\sqrt{5})^2} = 2\]" width="334" height="32" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06b6caacad0c19015083f03a3145e7839594868d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CH = BC - BH = 8 - 2 = 6\]" width="234" height="12" />
Apply the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/Angle_Bisector_Theorem">Angle Bisector Theorem</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/b/eabc00e3e07090a48a3155e93649e2aee77a368e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACH$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ed19718d84ea66bd08cd0ce70a5a9350d58c6c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABH$" width="61" height="13" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3bafc43cb0dac0c23c70c33a2293277bcf8da9e.png" class="latex" alt="$AP:PH = 9:6$" width="131" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/5/365252e0ef5f2c25c161b96d0481b424cb57569d.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ:QH = 7:2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="16" />, respectively.
To find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3bb4eeb347e74e025fd175c22f1f7275af0555e.png" class="latex" alt="$AP$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/1643dfe1eb38c118fdbeee8c69759015b03a46eb.png" class="latex" alt="$PH$" width="30" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/7563031eab26ad18658cf2f6a8e4948861bb9013.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/f/ddf8a6421a4abc4c283cb44d0203358c198f05f6.png" class="latex" alt="$QH$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="31" height="16" />, apply variables, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3bafc43cb0dac0c23c70c33a2293277bcf8da9e.png" class="latex" alt="$AP:PH = 9:6$" width="131" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/c/48cf42f3ad921bfaf5aa1cf7bcbd04a8cfcf2755.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3\sqrt{5} - x}{x} = \frac{9}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="105" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/5/365252e0ef5f2c25c161b96d0481b424cb57569d.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ:QH = 7:2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/d/63d37ebee8cc82cc1fe95e8d6481abe8ccbea55e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3\sqrt{5} - y}{y} = \frac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="105" height="44" />. Solving them out, you will get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/8/2b83b13b04390c424054884c9d5be278117f5a64.png" class="latex" alt="$AP = \frac{9\sqrt{5}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="41" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a48d4c0815afa06633a47c360bf8c288f994f3.png" class="latex" alt="$PH = \frac{6\sqrt{5}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="41" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58c10add32a900b05d58b09aa9dbd2f594122937.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ = \frac{7\sqrt{5}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/e/7ee934d115e668b9b613495eb809c0cd5f6e0d2f.png" class="latex" alt="$QH = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="41" />. Then, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/c/0ac76bdee47e2aa98cc3e75933f1e0e13629f3eb.png" class="latex" alt="$AP + PQ = AQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="16" /> according to the Segment Addition Postulate, and thus manipulating, you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f437f1de9b9d6efbded4068b84c363ce5e9ee3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ = AQ - AP = \frac{7\sqrt{5}}{3} - \frac{9\sqrt{5}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="250" height="41" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/312801052f1cdddaec059047af665f2d5818dc63.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\frac{8}{15}\sqrt{5}}\]" width="87" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c970cbf5e2e7029089517d5e8efbb8cade6d8a.png" class="latex" alt="$CE$" width="28" height="12" /> be the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" />. Then let the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/1/ff16a7e92720b003fe5e507efb28cec1a1783b71.png" class="latex" alt="$I&#39;$" width="13" height="14" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cfe3dfdb2ef831f14001802715a866cc59f6374.png" class="latex" alt="$II&#39;$" width="22" height="14" /> is an inradius and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d7700f8af8ff8714c719220763ad73844a8dc232.png" class="latex" alt="$II&#39; \cdot s = [ABC]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="120" height="19" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semiperimeter of the triangle.
</p><p>Using Heron's Formula, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/0/4606c7b7fd7a0b0f053167251e8e76e429d2c8fd.png" class="latex" alt="$II&#39; \cdot 12 =  \sqrt{12 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5} = 12\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="246" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/3/e93935e77b8b736159167b59f97fdc06b77ec839.png" class="latex" alt="$II&#39; = \sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cfe3dfdb2ef831f14001802715a866cc59f6374.png" class="latex" alt="$II&#39;$" width="22" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/3/bb3ddf9a5fa2fca5c4787d4f989af0eef1387bf0.png" class="latex" alt="$AH$" width="30" height="13" /> are parallel, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/fee2af0c2ca50c839c546b84616a9df1c69834e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CI&#39;I \sim \triangle CHP$" width="139" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/3/df325fd86fda92373d94c172862abac5114fd1d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BHQ \sim \triangle BI&#39;I$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="17" />.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/f/30fcd7dde2c2215c3ffa850b55e156ebc697a175.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{II&#39;}{PQ + QH} = \frac{CI&#39;}{CH}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="143" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/74195c518097187198a7f38e4321c7ca5263f893.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{II&#39;}{QH} = \frac{BI&#39;}{BH}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="92" height="41" />, so 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/8/d684478c799aa60d7925ace13418e79f42116f18.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ = \frac{II&#39; \cdot CH}{CI&#39;} - \frac{II&#39; \cdot BH}{BI&#39;}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="216" height="38" />.
</p><p>By the Dual Principle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78eac37336a78c48f82fce187eb05fb9db6ef6cb.png" class="latex" alt="$CI&#39; = 5$" width="61" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/8/d8830a33c62be763d8b3b234b3737d79e5a2cf0d.png" class="latex" alt="$BI&#39; = 3$" width="61" height="14" />. With the same method as Solution 1, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/c/35cddc9935bbd5feb2378d47aafef4d8ce47e48a.png" class="latex" alt="$CH = 6$" width="63" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc2684432ddfafc6774165e7878e10aceaa5ecad.png" class="latex" alt="$BH = 2$" width="64" height="12" />.
Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/7/b57fb237ba32cb485951373f206193885938e928.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ  = \frac{8}{15} II&#39; =$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="117" height="38" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/312801052f1cdddaec059047af665f2d5818dc63.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\frac{8}{15}\sqrt{5}}\]" width="87" height="48" />
