<p>The sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> is defined recursively by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/6/a16f2d4ee29c282b705559743af4c40867c16c88.png" class="latex" alt="$a_0=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f48795a343e5664fd2ccc6c53462f61a7e32da2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\sqrt[19]{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="72" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/d/05d47547c4e00b49b88d782be5f834f58e6fe0e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=a_{n-1}a_{n-2}^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="19" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47affecc93688552c8eca8bc13560d52b4cc28a9.png" class="latex" alt="$n\geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />. What is the smallest positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that the product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/6/9b69caa06cffd7e0a2f14723773cf1c01aa387ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1a_2\cdots a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="78" height="10" /> is an integer?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/4/0649c25432fabb529a427cbf08385947c8789f55.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 17\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 19\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 20\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 21$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/9/379ecc5696e3a9da3505b8e0d0731429a98852e6.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i=19\text{log}_2a_i$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="16" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/8/cb8659a1b8bb24dac6709e3ea35ebee7128e3dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$b_0=0, b_1=1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="109" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/e/a4ea91e9331eeda1926526b12424ab7a9cdde3d8.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n=b_{n-1}+2b_{n-2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="138" height="15" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47affecc93688552c8eca8bc13560d52b4cc28a9.png" class="latex" alt="$n\geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />. The characteristic polynomial of this linear recurrence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/5/5c5854810f34246d5915e7413f00e8478c948fa6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-x-2=0$" width="114" height="15" />, which has roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f44764928de4dbd860303093015d07045f22880d.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n=k_12^{n}+k_2(-1)^n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="18" /> for constants to be determined <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a9d2a2274d2af312043a1a9a2bed07cbb781cf0.png" class="latex" alt="$k_1, k_2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="16" />.  Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/8/cb8659a1b8bb24dac6709e3ea35ebee7128e3dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$b_0=0, b_1=1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="109" height="16" /> we can solve a pair of linear equations for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a9d2a2274d2af312043a1a9a2bed07cbb781cf0.png" class="latex" alt="$k_1, k_2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="16" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/9/3d9634ab26447cfc0a2c992e5234f990d7440ad0.png" class="latex" alt="$k_1+k_2=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="89" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/8/fe84bf15fa4096aeb97c2d8bafe5be8807bc8708.png" class="latex" alt="$2k_1-k_2=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="97" height="15" />.
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/3/a33d709462690a8f46ae014ccc256b78b0ead776.png" class="latex" alt="$k_1=\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/994dcef7de3c86d962c22c7994d8381f7910f693.png" class="latex" alt="$k_2=-\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/8/8b88f6d982df54ddb8a184ad068b9b72c26b8192.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n=\frac{2^n-(-1)^n}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="130" height="38" />.
</p><p>Now, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db7a9a27057fda7ff9bbd70076426be437eb9b9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1a_2\cdots a_k=2^{\frac{(b_1+b_2+\cdots+b_k)}{19}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="197" height="23" />, so we are looking for the least value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> so that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/d/c1dc13207b078faa0a1ba46a98a5058cbeff9409.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1+b_2+\cdots+b_k \equiv 0 \pmod{19}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Note that we can multiply all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d9565abaadf04609a2e9941aa2d20b0a299b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" /> by three for convenience, as the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d9565abaadf04609a2e9941aa2d20b0a299b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" /> are always integers, and it does not affect divisibility by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>Now, for all even <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> the sum (adjusted by a factor of three) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/4/aa40fba285c545d58145564d55a6e31f013f403f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^1+2^2+\cdots+2^k=2^{k+1}-2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="227" height="17" />. The smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> for which this is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/7/3078aa78ca1dcbb30a568c894d0e39ddb2666a01.png" class="latex" alt="$k=18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> by Fermat's Little Theorem, as it is seen with further testing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is a primitive root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/0/dc04e677c6c9637b4eefab4ef361ed56065dc673.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{19}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />.  
</p><p>Now, assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is odd. Then the sum (again adjusted by a factor of three) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53b141fc61f53bd54e53b35a8fbb50eccf0ddf6a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^1+2^2+\cdots+2^k+1=2^{k+1}-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="258" height="17" />. The smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> for which this is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/7/f47539c1a0629a2ac310c7b044b8d60a5f12c939.png" class="latex" alt="$k=17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />, by the same reasons. Thus, the minimal value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/4/e540e0f4fc1ee1c8512af0b867bb59cda14aae10.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="17" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/6/9b69caa06cffd7e0a2f14723773cf1c01aa387ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1a_2\cdots a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="78" height="10" /> is an integer, it must be a power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so the sum of the base-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> logarithms must be an integer. Multiply all of these logarithms by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> (to make them integers), so the sum must be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>The logarithms are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e4359498bc1652367a176e3bfb0d6434a97614e8.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n = 19\log_2 a_n$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="117" height="19" />. Using the recursion <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2f8e1f352860ee73fb3ebf64bef9add77a86635.png" class="latex" alt="$b_0 = 0, b_1 = 1, b_n = b_{n-1}+2b_{n-2}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="255" height="18" /> (modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to save calculation time), we get the sequence
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/2/0c293b201163e0cf01f9f4b12a3d5e89e93a48e9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0,1,1,3,5,11,2,5,9,0,-1,-1,-2,-5,-11,-2,-5,-9,0,\dots\]" width="477" height="16" />
Listing the numbers out is expedited if you notice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/d/63db28b6a9c030a2ebb7a307cc6e0ea51902523c.png" class="latex" alt="$b_{n+1}=2b_n+(-1)^n$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="154" height="20" />.
</p><p>The cycle repeats every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/e/29e0a7dd842377240046d537f17ab0137270ce07.png" class="latex" alt="$9+9=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> terms. Notice that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c44838d14448bbe1660771148f24f76e3086b81d.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n+b_{n+9} \equiv 0 \pmod{19}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="189" height="20" />, the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> terms sum up to a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee5e0fcfa56e6821bdcbc6a1b92bc066fbc1defa.png" class="latex" alt="$b_{18}=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="17" />, we only need at most the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc26d9ea934090cb4c7bd6b9b427f06db398b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 17}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="30" /> terms to sum up to a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and this is the lowest answer choice.
</p><p><b>Note:</b> To rigorously prove this is the smallest value, you will have to keep a running sum of the terms and check that it is never a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> before the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />th term.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Like in <a href="#Solution_2">Solution 2</a>, calculate the first few terms of the sequence, but also keep a running sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/d/73dad573cdaca95ffccd89d8931d5c1f85c4186a.png" class="latex" alt="$c_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> of the logarithms (not modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> here):
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/1/d615f9f9aadfb4110ee009974a4488bea64ec089.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0,1,2,5,10,21,42,\dots\]" width="166" height="16" />
Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/8/fc8a2e37520e6dccf1f5c020d56e34db6c4e477a.png" class="latex" alt="$c_n=2c_{n-1}+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="114" height="14" /> for odd <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/b/4ab084a8fbf1d64e383fec909ec33e5226c405b6.png" class="latex" alt="$c_n=2c_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="86" height="18" /> for even <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is relatively prime to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, we can ignore even <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and calculate odd <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/3/f93e4f9f23a1816b221e2dcd57cdfdbb009a7c27.png" class="latex" alt="$c_1 = 1, c_{n} = 4c_{n-2}+1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="174" height="18" /> (modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />):
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8624986712ed7098fe38b3fc8556250d15e86d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[,1,,5,,2,,9,,-1,,-3,,8,,-5,,0,\dots\]" width="288" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/d/73dad573cdaca95ffccd89d8931d5c1f85c4186a.png" class="latex" alt="$c_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is first a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/d/47d94ffbbff66d1da1ff53288d35077a0855cd5c.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 17}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="101" height="30" />. ~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
