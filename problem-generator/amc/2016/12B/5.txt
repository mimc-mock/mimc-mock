<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By: dragonfly
</p><p>To find what day of the week it is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/5/30594dd22addac3e935fddb35618393899ee46e6.png" class="latex" alt="$919$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> days, we have to divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/5/30594dd22addac3e935fddb35618393899ee46e6.png" class="latex" alt="$919$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> to see the remainder, and then add the remainder to the current day. We get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1edf2bdf6e93ec28d5b07df205d01a954301a16a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{919}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="29" height="37" /> has a remainder of 2, so we increase the current day by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6f1653d1ca49c5a791261096da23b306127100.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ \text{Saturday}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="113" height="30" />.
</p><p>Note that the dates themselves (and thus leap years) can be ignored, as we only need the number of days that passed to figure out the day of the week.
</p>
