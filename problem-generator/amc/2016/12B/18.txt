<p>What is the area of the region enclosed by the graph of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/4/864191ae88a08202ee04fd5d9d1ddd4b594b4e94.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=|x|+|y|?$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/4/f94da8073549d81e04fef66f1a1f40dbf4e60d1d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \pi+\sqrt{2} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \pi+2 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \pi+2\sqrt{2} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2\pi+\sqrt{2} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2\pi+2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="613" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Consider the case when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/d/44df9cb6ce6f056c64c197a004b64fc4dd3a5aa5.png" class="latex" alt="$x \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/5/e55b6810db781e7d0c3f70e981fae71bd68c2040.png" class="latex" alt="$y \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/9/579c6d245f6bbc2bf41fcc62182d7d032c5e7ee0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2+y^2=x+y\]" width="122" height="18" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/5/2e585b5d19dc88abd1cd8d083f79cf210658ac93.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x - \frac{1}{2})^2+(y - \frac{1}{2})^2=\frac{1}{2}\]" width="190" height="37" />
Notice the circle intersect the axes at points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/6457ca2dcd40dc21d9aa0db18c66bbac9af4d899.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/754e946d1a160f767f0f61cbd9460401c2d1c17f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Find the area of this circle in the first quadrant. The area is made of a semi-circle with radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/7/cc7c4b51e06b26289d792878cbf9efac28aac903.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" /> and a triangle:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/5/a45b86b9a5f727c22135e8c436797d6b574c59df.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A = \frac{\pi}{4} +\frac{1}{2}\]" width="86" height="37" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/77121b786ebf303837357aa5aab877da041db676.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]draw((0,-1.5)--(0,1.5),EndArrow);draw((-1.5,0)--(1.5,0),EndArrow);draw((0,1)--(1,0)--(0,-1)--(-1,0)--cycle,dotted);draw(arc((1/2,1/2),sqrt(2)/2,135, 315),dotted);for(int i=0;i&lt;4;++i){draw(rotate(i*90,(0,0))*arc((1/2,1/2),sqrt(2)/2,-45,135)); dot(rotate(i*90,(0,0))*(1/2,1/2));}[/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
Because of symmetry, the area is the same in all four quadrants.
The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff46cec0a025fed22b794987d9a3e00bcc1e834a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ \pi + 2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="88" height="28" />
</p>
