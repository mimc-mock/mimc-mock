<p>In how many ways can <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c72d8d34783d3db7b917c953ca22225f513823.png" class="latex" alt="$345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> be written as the sum of an increasing sequence of two or more consecutive positive integers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fbae31ce1e7a9e307bbeab198ed0f576ef22fdfd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="349" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We proceed with this problem by considering two cases, when: 1) There are an odd number of consecutive numbers, 2) There are an even number of consecutive numbers.
</p><p>For the first case, we can cleverly choose the convenient form of our sequence to be
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a04526e358b74f6c02e0d6197b3ccfda2b03a8e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a-n,\cdots, a-1, a, a+1, \cdots, a+n\]" width="279" height="15" />
</p><p>because then our sum will just be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67a7ffe85f273308031d2e8aba462431ac08520.png" class="latex" alt="$(2n+1)a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />. We now have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/31121d247e7bb15216675853125c76f8f7916c9d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2n+1)a = 345\]" width="124" height="18" />
and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> will have a solution when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/76576c3e70ee27c8d5a79d0d891dbfa511e8bf05.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{345}{2n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="53" height="39" /> is an integer, namely when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/639e85fa2f2bb450812e9c9de2067851e11a04d1.png" class="latex" alt="$2n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="13" /> is a divisor of 345. We check that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/e/46e8888ffd22a53e9d54b886173734fb9e0dffda.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2n+1 = 3, 5, 15, 23\]" width="153" height="16" />
work, and no more, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/c/cace54ad97746d43fec567341c220462a01e9aac.png" class="latex" alt="$2n+1=1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="13" /> does not satisfy the requirements of two or more consecutive integers, and when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/639e85fa2f2bb450812e9c9de2067851e11a04d1.png" class="latex" alt="$2n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="13" /> equals the next biggest factor, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80952a9a250e7257f7c6d64e763dae189dbb271.png" class="latex" alt="$69$" width="17" height="12" />, there must be negative integers in the sequence. Our solutions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/8/548556f16d131156a2cd3c2b72c6e63914a23f6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\{114,115, 116\}, \{67, \cdots, 71\}, \{16, \cdots, 30\}, \{4, \cdots, 26\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="424" height="18" />.
</p><p>For the even cases, we choose our sequence to be of the form:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/c/ddc4567a8fec244e7d8b199e289a10b2a5730020.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a-(n-1), \cdots, a, a+1, \cdots, a+n\]" width="275" height="18" />
so the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/f/2cfc79172020a3e77da45fa159eac9ae4d02a326.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2n)(2a+1)}{2} = n(2a+1)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="200" height="38" />. In this case, we find our solutions to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd571483afd6200c692675ee88444f1ef8045f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\{172, 173\}, \{55,\cdots, 60\}, \{30,\cdots, 39\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="291" height="18" />.
</p><p>We have found all 7 solutions and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d1038fea1e97b5c99e0c34562a8c0c693ff57ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \, 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The sum from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/082852571ecc48ed136ba13dcffa8f6e40064bbd.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5f6957cb62ab3fc5fca2e8189772e12e116b87.png" class="latex" alt="$S=\dfrac{(a-b+1)(a+b)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="177" height="38" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/d/89deca721df40034bb18271f189afff651f1b878.png" class="latex" alt="$345=\dfrac{(a-b+1)(a+b)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="192" height="38" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/5/d7534c864e6cef506ba828531f2658d6edf8abc4.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 23=(a-b+1)(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="18" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/aba00850007b2e62b2a6f62b8ac2dbcf8abba465.png" class="latex" alt="$c=a-b+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/0/0d0c6c2afa38c2e40ffac3ae40e7dc907ebab9a4.png" class="latex" alt="$d=a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/3/54308ad0b02129943f2845fc711bf3c2edb11894.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 23=c\cdot d$" width="139" height="12" />
</p><p>If we factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd2065782067101d1311681df1de20f0e1a7af9.png" class="latex" alt="$690$" width="26" height="12" /> into all of its factor groups <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f78b780628d9a267ad148c0d60cc099f8c2658c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(\text{exg}~ (10,69) ~\text{or} ~(15,46))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="185" height="18" /> we will have several ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/e/79e31171b9eaf222673b566ed3cf773e0f0b532c.png" class="latex" alt="$(c,d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/c/e8c4caf5be63fefba3acfbae78c71c7af81bdd8b.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;d$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />
</p><p>The number of possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is half the number of factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd2065782067101d1311681df1de20f0e1a7af9.png" class="latex" alt="$690$" width="26" height="12" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/3/95357da0ce57d6bbd10da1876986fdc9e88c0f13.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2=8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="134" height="37" /> 
</p><p>However, we have one extraneous case of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/a/5ca5aa3bc83b305771d9ba852c12b7854d6f25fb.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,690)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> because here, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abd2c942ae299fd26e18d0ca5d390a7ca4a8c34.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b$" width="42" height="12" /> and we have the sum of one consecutive number which is not allowed by the question.
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/5/cb565cb8cd6a6236d4de94f7d55238e0ae60870a.png" class="latex" alt="$8-1=7$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c95d7e537f941020be5bf4f40a7f3297de8ef1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \,7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />.
</p>
