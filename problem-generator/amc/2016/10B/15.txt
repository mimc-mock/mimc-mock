<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Consecutive numbers share an edge. That means that it is possible to walk from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> by single steps north, south, east, or west. Consequently, the squares in the diagram with different shades have different parity:<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/9/f39f0e7b752d26dd1dc58cc402af636029b64af9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]size(4cm); for(int i=0;i&lt;3;++i)for(int j=0;j&lt;3;++j)filldraw(box((i,j),(i+1,j+1)),gray((i+j)%2*.2+.7));[/asy]" width="192" height="192" />
But since there are only four even numbers in the set, the five darker squares must contain the odd numbers, which sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96290ff849fc7a1335ca7e6ca3f51c880f3c694.png" class="latex" alt="$1+3+5+7+9=25.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="180" height="14" /> Therefore if the sum of the numbers in the corners is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, the number in the center must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/e/a8e28dcbdcd6be4be02beb725aa6dd025ac87f6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf{(C) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="57" height="30" />.
</p>
