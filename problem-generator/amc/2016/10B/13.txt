<p>At Megapolis Hospital one year, multiple-birth statistics were as follows: Sets of twins, triplets, and quadruplets accounted for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> of the babies born. There were four times as many sets of triplets as sets of quadruplets, and there was three times as many sets of twins as sets of triplets. How many of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> babies were in sets of quadruplets?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29f143bf4399a3455595ae0762c353a013c5f3b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 25\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 64\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 100\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 160$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can set up a system of equations where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sets of twins, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is the sets of triplets, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is the sets of quadruplets.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e1922ea77df0f34afd0ad8dd22675f0e1c25e3dd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split} 2a + 3b + 4c &amp; = 1000 \\ b &amp; = 4c \\ a &amp; = 3b \end{split}\]" width="156" height="62" />
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> in the second and third equations and substituting into the first equation yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/9/93942a59ca8959ac88e8b5ae99818ebf315ca679.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split} 2 (3b) + 3b + 4 (0.25b) &amp; = 1000 \\ 6b + 3b + b &amp; = 1000 \\ b &amp; = 100 \end{split}\]" width="224" height="64" />
</p><p>Since we are trying to find the number of babies and <b>NOT</b> the number of sets of quadruplets, the solution is not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, but rather <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5a644e784380bf70cd389a206ae0475c1458dfe.png" class="latex" alt="$4c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Therefore, we strategically use the second initial equation to realize that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5a644e784380bf70cd389a206ae0475c1458dfe.png" class="latex" alt="$4c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, leaving us with the number of babies born as quadruplets equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/3/2e3f613160ac97a93aa44106a056382080c3ff0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 100}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Say there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/38588fafb659d83dc4c41024358d54ce788611d3.png" class="latex" alt="$12x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" /> sets of twins, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> sets of triplets, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> sets of quadruplets. That's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/7/357f9416e9534190dac1b8b6d1ae1aa72d43a102.png" class="latex" alt="$12x\cdot2=24x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="12" /> twins, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/7/a575f2e6d9e7641e356cf5b2a01e46d5aeba30ff.png" class="latex" alt="$4x\cdot3=12x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="94" height="13" /> triplets, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79893fbd5f442c189f98dda22bdb3c02345d27c.png" class="latex" alt="$x\cdot4=4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="12" /> quadruplets. A tenth of the babies are quadruplets and that's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/9/769ead1cce9e96318456de3107caa1d801ecafc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{10}(1000)=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }100}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="171" height="37" />
</p>
