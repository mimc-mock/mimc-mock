<p>All the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380264eeecdaa2e001ecc6a81415cefc2aff6a31.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" /> are assigned to the six faces of a cube, one number to each face. For each of the eight vertices of the cube, a product of three numbers is computed, where the three numbers are the numbers assigned to the three faces that include that vertex. What is the greatest possible value of the sum of these eight products?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e0b40f41a69edf0717127cf181c173cd4d27d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 312 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 343 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 625 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 729 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 1680$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="448" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let us call the six sides of our cube <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/3/cb3a6878fef6ec913f2941426896391fcff07f3d.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d,e,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> (where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is opposite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is opposite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is opposite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />.
Thus, for the eight vertices, we have the following products: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52bb30bd17de53404ca563bc4a71c3c9d8be7410.png" class="latex" alt="$abc,abe,bcd,bde,acf,cdf,aef,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="237" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/f/5ef63538d31df384b0d9f0db118b07c2bccd6970.png" class="latex" alt="$def$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="16" />.
Let us find the sum of these products:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/48296a0cea1154a2a446e58b23eaebe3eded84c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[abc+abe+bcd+bde+acf+cdf+aef+def\]" width="368" height="16" />
We notice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is a factor of the first four terms, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is a factor of the last four terms.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/8/508d065c08d7d279de23bb5587a7ba11072729a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b(ac+ae+cd+de)+f(ac+ae+cd+de)\]" width="342" height="18" />
Now, we can factor even more:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb12bc6bc22b8d0a853a6b371560829c0fb6593e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} &amp; (b+f)(ac+ae+cd+de) \\ = &amp;(b+f)(a(c+e)+d(c+e)) \\ = &amp;(b+f)(a+d)(c+e) \end{align*}" width="227" height="68" />
We have the product. Notice how the factors are sums of opposite faces. The greatest sum possible is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5a0df440c2171eadf0d8d7e9d23003b02c0ddb.png" class="latex" alt="$(7+2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a95c1d8fc477ed2fd05c050be8eda9b789f207.png" class="latex" alt="$(6+3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb6128f5426de9f4b8cfcf8790f92562df61ec5c.png" class="latex" alt="$(5+4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> all factors.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/501eaf1a24c8b1c27da14bfa81c48b260d7aa7be.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} &amp; (7+2)(6+3)(5+4) \\ = &amp; 9 \cdot 9 \cdot 9 \\ = &amp; 729. \end{align*}" width="175" height="63" />
Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/f/03f76f4236a1ffb385da616044337b2ff327d9e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }729}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="76" height="30" />.
</p>
