<p>Carl decided to fence in his rectangular garden. He bought <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> fence posts, placed one on each of the four corners, and spaced out the rest evenly along the edges of the garden, leaving exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> yards between neighboring posts. The longer side of his garden, including the corners, has twice as many posts as the shorter side, including the corners. What is the area, in square yards, of Carl’s garden?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/d/78da4658b9b0ec6afea24e98da4874676555ef58.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 256\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 336\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 384\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 448\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 512$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If the dimensions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f2c7873b5607de62c94adc7780aeac4a5fc1710.png" class="latex" alt="$4a\times 4b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, then one side will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/b/64be5731bafe97ab8fb5d0f9c52c1c5362f963d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" /> posts (including corners) and the other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/91927ed6d1093e6ce84e3aa8446121218cb17b71.png" class="latex" alt="$b+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" /> (including corners).
</p><p>The total number of posts is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/2/3929bfa0feb9e901070e191e39bf793bc76b37be.png" class="latex" alt="$2(a+b)=20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
This diagram represents the number of posts around the garden.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3330e0e1124978ddeb7b2f002fd64388a1cc5a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]size(7cm);fill((0,0)--(5,0)--(5,7)--(0,7)--cycle,lightgreen); for(int i=0;i&lt;5;++i)dot((i,0),red);for(int i=0;i&lt;7;++i)dot((5,i),blue);dot((5,7)); draw(arc((0,0),.5,-90,-270)--arc((4,0),.5,90,-90)--cycle,gray+dotted); draw(arc((5,0),.5,-180,0)--arc((5,6),.5,0,180)--cycle,gray+dotted); draw((0,-1)--(5,-1),Arrows);draw((6,0)--(6,7),Arrows); label(&quot;$a+1$&quot;,(0,-1)--(5,-1),S);label(&quot;$b+1$&quot;,(6,0)--(6,7),E); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,(1,1));label(&quot;$b$&quot;,(4,5)); [/asy]" width="302" height="332" />
</p><p>We solve the system <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/6/b06f0d9284f11f51b46df041024dcb7c539ebdd6.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{cases}b+1=2(a+1)\\a+b=10\end{cases}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="140" height="53" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fa11411ad863917af44cea4d6438496ebf29c92.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3,\ b=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="16" />. Then the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/5/ae5502ce182eafac5bafa762c35735ca757fe8c9.png" class="latex" alt="$4a\cdot 4b=336$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="99" height="13" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/6/566869f4a0acaa5c3472f1a37de65e42a4a61887.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 336}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="75" height="30" />.
</p><p>~Edits by BakedPotato66
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>To do this problem, we have to draw a rectangle. We also have to keep track of the fence posts. Putting a post on each corner leaves us with only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> posts. Now there are twice as many posts on the longer side then the shorter side. From this, we can see that we can put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> posts on the longer side and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> posts on the shorter side. On the shorter side, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> spaces between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> posts. On the longer side, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> spaces between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> fence posts. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> yards between each post. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/1/901b71efdb19842620e6951f0f06a9114e46bcd0.png" class="latex" alt="$12\times28=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 336}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="157" height="30" />.
</p>
