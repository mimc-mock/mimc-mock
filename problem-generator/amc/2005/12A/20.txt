<p>For each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e861e10e1c19918756b9c8b7717684593c63aeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />, define
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/5/c25a89c6f31ca5a9ad24ed419fdf61430fad13e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{clr} f(x) &amp; = 2x, &amp; \text { if } 0 \leq x \leq \frac {1}{2}; \\ f(x) &amp; = 2 - 2x, &amp; \text { if } \frac {1}{2} &lt; x \leq 1. \end{array}\]" width="246" height="42" />
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19a1e133f69e72ede551e9a9ac13d6fc753631b6.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2]}(x) = f(f(x))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="20" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/8/b28e24ffc0a9d6d078390a1cc78472f25aad30de.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[n + 1]}(x) = f^{[n]}(f(x))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="20" /> for each integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/2/72287b09043ed45b372a517d764e12d07aff8071.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />. For how many values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e861e10e1c19918756b9c8b7717684593c63aeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/8/cf8eae18d34cdc34dd8eb5c5567f33e726ef8bb4.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2005]}(x) = \frac {1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="106" height="37" />?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/d/9bd6c0aa1515605b08a16a33ca750b38371d9417.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\text {A}) \ 0 \qquad (\text {B}) \ 2005 \qquad (\text {C})\ 4010 \qquad (\text {D}) \ 2005^2 \qquad (\text {E})\ 2^{2005}\]" width="450" height="19" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For the two functions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/e/30e75168687bbcb2a4340fadb463f42d198c736e.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=2x,0\le x\le \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="166" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/d/52d68eeacb56c94fa7988d42e0481312aa000606.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=2-2x,\frac{1}{2}\le x\le 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="198" height="37" />,as long as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> will be in the right domain, so we don't need to worry about the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.  
</p><p><br />
Also, every time we change <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, the expression for the final answer in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> will be in a different form(although they'll all satisfy the final equation), so we get a different starting value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.  Every time we have two choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/c/80cb14b749dccaefe2d21370a777201a35814139.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="18" />) and altogether we have to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5b442c275b4324f100c365b774e872b6b21f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2005$" width="35" height="12" /> times. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/7/017ae3dfa330979fa196f57e6f64e825f03ace6d.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}\Rightarrow\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="91" height="24" />.
</p><p>Note: the values of x that satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/7/b774d366e9a86636a0a3997073f80ee5a0149743.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[n]}(x) = \frac {1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/e/43eb25f80ff62093367a265c72f88b0b435622fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2^{n+1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/704ab2ab97465a9fb82c59fe66654f4666700868.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2^{n+1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/1/8b160ac95d2d07a624c03688b670b2322e45dcd8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2^{n+1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/087594ea26fa196b8e61bc20a36228270dc695f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\cdots$" style="vertical-align: 3px" width="19" height="2" /> ,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/c/9dc29018e2e82da97ddbcd7e665d7e84c3f1fdaf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2^{n+1}-1}{2^{n+1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="68" height="39" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/8/cf8eae18d34cdc34dd8eb5c5567f33e726ef8bb4.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2005]}(x) = \frac {1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="106" height="37" />. Thus,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f668ee7c291e18b1df5169984a5e7eafcf5e67a6.png" class="latex" alt="$f(f^{[2004]}(x))=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="131" height="37" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/5/285d6d53dec29d3b1f4452b5cf413e8d4c072e0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2004]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/e/1deab21e83af79102d5d31364ab01aac2cf3204a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(y)=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/0/11073eab014b76706300e1bf7c6125137134a037.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="45" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />. Now we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/5/285d6d53dec29d3b1f4452b5cf413e8d4c072e0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2004]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />. Now we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a0962b1a52904e89b2f327367cc00444084fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$f(f^{[2003]}(x))=\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="131" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />. Now we solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfbad44ab10772cc79ef6fedb6f2377099af4e68.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2003]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> and let  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/e/9fe4a36537e191815c0f7506fe4b2c0f84a22f80.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2003]}(x)=z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="20" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/301469a1d6b6bd4ddb15b26b27e22c03d5611b2e.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db9e227e8177d26de452c2a2faeb6d45cc53d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/7/717366d5cd3f9573014d073ca33467c37c12a364.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f4344e4ce1c2a266b885d9b160544e3036de15f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />,and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/639534d18207fde5d468bcf2bb90aa2d7ad517ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. As we see, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a7847a0f23d0dd2ddbc119cccf0265be6933d1c3.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2005]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> has 1 solution, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/5/285d6d53dec29d3b1f4452b5cf413e8d4c072e0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2004]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> has 2 solutions, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfbad44ab10772cc79ef6fedb6f2377099af4e68.png" class="latex" alt="$f^{[2003]}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> has 4 solutions. Thus for each iteration we double the number of possible solutions. There are 2005 iterations and thus the number of solutions is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/a/ceaede13e4a7750e7d95af5d1a7c1f71ec944efb.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}$" width="34" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e53b122c7353446fc55a2e7736f57d27db682d.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="49" height="24" />
(not rigorous)
</p>
