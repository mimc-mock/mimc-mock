<p>A rectangular box <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is inscribed in a sphere of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. The surface area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is 384, and the sum of the lengths of its 12 edges is 112. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a193fdc02ba5e2119ed54b22de44c79050f0fb25.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 8\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 10\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 12\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 14\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Box P has dimensions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />. 
Its surface area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/6/a76b6404f3224d41af7694e99206d9c5b4b67f1f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2lw+2lh+2wh=384,\]" width="186" height="16" /> 
and the sum of all its edges is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/3/86391c1d6be352d4b06253140f65e260ee99b750.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[l + w + h = \dfrac{4l+4w+4h}{4} = \dfrac{112}{4} = 28.\]" width="304" height="38" />
</p><p>The diameter of the sphere is the space diagonal of the prism, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/31384be5c4dd31ee79fefadd8f754b0e543415a6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{l^2 + w^2 +h^2}.\]" width="114" height="18" />
Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/e/20ea50479e3eb41006e5cba6901f12d434e6abda.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(l + w + h)^2 - (2lw + 2lh + 2wh) = l^2 + w^2 + h^2 = 784 - 384 = 400,\]" width="477" height="19" /> so the diameter is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/a/b8ab71d91a8a41cd30d5290648d0c972a78086d5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{l^2 + w^2 +h^2} = \sqrt{400} = 20.\]" width="223" height="18" /> The radius is half of the diameter, so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a0ac99e9fe30e6457565990dbbf925afcada5c3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r=\frac{20}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(B)} 10}.\]" width="140" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As in the previous solution, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/7573e1ffb29899fde3036b4740490455539640cf.png" class="latex" alt="$2lw+2lh+2wh=384$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="182" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/c/fdc6741dcd1c99f0cc64e882d627ee2f4d8f9a73.png" class="latex" alt="$l + w + h = \dfrac{4l+4w+4h}{4} = \dfrac{112}{4} = 28$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="301" height="38" />, and the diameter of the sphere is the space diagonal of the prism, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/e/29e8e6e3d99747113eb02f253c58ad85c84a5e9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{l^2 + w^2 + h^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="112" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Now, since this is competition math, we only need to find the space diagonal of any one box that fits the requirements, so assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/7/af7e5b7d062655d4be01f4d56b203954a93cf7eb.png" class="latex" alt="$h=0$" width="43" height="12" />. (This essentially means that we have an infinitesimally thin box.) We now have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/b/71b1eb3e739a9dff4e57ce5e4f6f8181e99a8b7f.png" class="latex" alt="$2lw = 384$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4fde207552da64bf77596e6c2c6fce77a6794b7.png" class="latex" alt="$l + w = 28$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="14" />, and we are solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/2/cb20539f40591605344b5b0baf7769ec68f510be.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{l^2 + w^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="71" height="18" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/d/dcd84a9ca03a7b3c3a5c636af15345ebfb0b94d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(l + w)^2 - 2lw = l^2 + 2lw + w^2 - 2lw = l^2 + w^2,\]" width="378" height="19" /> this means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/3/bf3fd795d2a9dc3f688e52f7a4b66dcd9e5fba2f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[l^2 + w^2 = 28^2 - 384 = 400,\]" width="210" height="18" /> so the space diagonal is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e20ed59fa013138bee79301b0dd31534880a23.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{400} = 20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="18" />. Since the diameter of the sphere is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, the radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a368110dca16f12cbb152ecfdb72ec11029ee9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />. ~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
</p>
