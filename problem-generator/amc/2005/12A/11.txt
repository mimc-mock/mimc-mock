<p>How many three-digit numbers satisfy the property that the middle digit is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Mean" title="Mean">average</a> of the first and the last digits?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/1/c91029d19b7e64f91f0632ce57c0ee16e8529bbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A}) \ 41 \qquad (\mathrm {B}) \ 42 \qquad (\mathrm {C})\ 43 \qquad (\mathrm {D}) \ 44 \qquad (\mathrm {E})\ 45$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the digits be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/9/299ddf7c0f03ea46bf09f81f93adeea0756aa15c.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B, C$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="16" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/4/374968c4480920b4a5f87a8f197cd4ba97c34cc9.png" class="latex" alt="$B = \frac {A + C}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="38" />. In order for this to be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> have to have the same <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parity" title="Parity">parity</a>. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> depends on the value of both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and is unique for each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/6/b0655ad32e6d1a40429a867397c94a1826876577.png" class="latex" alt="$(A,C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />. Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/1/a71cdc4e63932b57934750697124c35ae24812a2.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \cdot 5 \cdot 1 = 45 \implies E$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="159" height="13" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Thus, the three digits form an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_sequence" title="Arithmetic sequence">arithmetic sequence</a>. 
</p>
<ul><li>If the numbers are all the same, then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> possible three-digit numbers.</li>
<li>If the numbers are different, then we count the number of strictly increasing arithmetic sequences between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and multiply by 2 for the decreasing ones:</li></ul>

<tbody><tr>
<td>Common difference</td>
<td>Sequences possible</td>
<td>Number of sequences
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>1</td>
<td><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb3f469084a2f441ddf2ce046453dc12f458e4b.png" class="latex" alt="$012, \ldots, 789$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" /></td>
<td>8

<td>6

<td>4

<td>4</td>
<td><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/f/f3f78c70f027ae1dd3f1d2caf438550702955046.png" class="latex" alt="$048, \ldots, 159$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" /></td>

<p>This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/3/28370d64da69664e9cfe17dc5cce437902681e4b.png" class="latex" alt="$2(8+6+4+2) = 40$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" />. However, the question asks for three-digit numbers, so we have to ignore the four sequences starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/0/c207d7df01fc964c3bc2d2d897ffdabe4b79a7e2.png" class="latex" alt="$40 + 9 - 4 = 45 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="18" />.
</p>
