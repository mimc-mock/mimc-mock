<p>In the five-sided star shown, the letters <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are replaced by the
numbers 3, 5, 6, 7 and 9, although not necessarily in that order. The sums of the
numbers at the ends of the line segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/3/f03a5d1a4c953313b40254abc33a95b7adabc9d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{EA}$" width="28" height="15" /> form an
arithmetic sequence, although not necessarily in that order. What is the middle
term of the arithmetic sequence?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/4/b742a9eaa62d04f46229732736080eeb97f05475.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(0.5,1.54)--(1,0)--(-0.31,0.95)--(1.31,0.95)--cycle); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0.5,1.54),N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(1,0),SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(-0.31,0.95),W); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,(1.31,0.95),E); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,(0,0),SW); [/asy]" width="252" height="235" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/8/2a8f88dd020315bc8e51f0b60ec9d78878263e77.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A}) \ 9 \qquad (\mathrm {B}) \ 10 \qquad (\mathrm {C})\ 11 \qquad (\mathrm {D}) \ 12 \qquad (\mathrm {E})\ 13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/f/a8ff6e790aa369050be53b3049bf8e4e28ab94d5.png" class="latex" alt="$(A+B) + (B+C) + (C+D) + (D+E) + (E+A) = 2(A+B+C+D+E)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="623" height="18" /> (i.e., each number is counted twice). The sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79beafef51578909489be5664f582ce5b4e83385.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B + C + D + E$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="161" height="14" /> will always be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/2/5f24b8065cb7c11a175d95c41dfdcf4ed966018d.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 9 = 30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="176" height="14" />, so the arithmetic sequence has a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/0/680df397231168ecac2d932d41fcde9aa4670da1.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 30 = 60$" width="82" height="12" />. The middle term must be the average of the five numbers, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/9/4f9454256f3cd04bddeedb06d390ade3ce5fa17c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{5} = 12 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="130" height="37" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let the terms in the arithmetic sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c117a4838e2be0c981f35f324153248f63e1ec.png" class="latex" alt="$a + d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/e/eaed2bcccb46f120de651287aca90d73b3c476db.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/8/f98014b8a564f6a2cafeab9787671fb9f1bc29e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 3d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/b/62bf33e92e91c961e62cefa29a1d2a2ea7e08903.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 4d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />. We seek the middle term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/e/eaed2bcccb46f120de651287aca90d73b3c476db.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />.
</p><p>These five terms are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bf77c6740d00af2866f26718d628a60c667ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/9/8c9d45b704ffb63f5e9284bb62220ddc955522e0.png" class="latex" alt="$B + C$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/5/265656713bba48754d61c1d5bac40bf2c340ebfe.png" class="latex" alt="$C + D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/0/7107ec9b1cfad062ed99dc987909aecef903d7d6.png" class="latex" alt="$D + E$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/874bb320e89a302b02e0c0954b3ac05c76a7a878.png" class="latex" alt="$E + A$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, in some order. The numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are equal to 3, 5, 6, 7, and 9, in some order, so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/9/0b950e851b01fdef4121776d71bceb64dea71e98.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A + B + C + D + E = 3 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 9 = 30.\]" width="365" height="14" />
Hence, the sum of the five terms is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/b/96b4e29fc241c3a8aa1f8d21141905fb3dc1ea96.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(A + B) + (B + C) + (C + D) + (D + E) + (E + A) = 2A + 2B + 2C + 2D + 2E = 60.\]" width="586" height="18" />
But adding all five numbers, we also get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/9/c39d455b6de0d1bd8ae08a8b7406d04eedfc6393.png" class="latex" alt="$a + (a + d) + (a + 2d) + (a + 3d) + (a + 4d) = 5a + 10d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="18" />, so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/d/4ad7963ccb0acc510587c8046427c72115b6e099.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5a + 10d = 60.\]" width="113" height="14" />
Dividing both sides by 5, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/2/3a2b77b30e5e1c37c0f4c1966987d71361c06642.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 2d = \boxed{12}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="104" height="23" />, which is the middle term. The answer is (D).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Not too bad with some logic and the awesome guess and check. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af04466ac38b7b0d0f451b3280c38f861ea13822.png" class="latex" alt="$A=6$" width="46" height="13" />. Then let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/d/1cd232b27c7bd446c8fa856ad08e6d166f3e09b0.png" class="latex" alt="$B=7,E=5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/2/9324b954ba60afe9f4410f500208022a37d22b47.png" class="latex" alt="$C=3,D=9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="104" height="16" />. Our arithmetic sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/d/30d14acb631f41d1e4a8a87afcd0fe466317f4dc.png" class="latex" alt="$10,11,12,13,14$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="16" /> so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/3/4e3dbdf5abbcdadd27e735f1f3e93a934d920c51.png" class="latex" alt="$12 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />.
</p><p>Solution by franzliszt
</p>
