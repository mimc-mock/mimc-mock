<p>How many distinct four-tuples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/e/faedb9d30dc3763d92cbcacf2cc17e2bf64f3e8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> of rational numbers are there with
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/5/9153c41adf40f93e619e0362b706aa5160874e9c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a\cdot\log_{10}2+b\cdot\log_{10}3+c\cdot\log_{10}5+d\cdot\log_{10}7=2005?\]" width="420" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638923196694a77c65e56e84d8b691cb2f77b29f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 0      \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 1      \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 17     \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 2004   \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \text{infinitely many}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="479" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Using the laws of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Logarithms" class="mw-redirect" title="Logarithms">logarithms</a>, the given equation becomes
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/3/9b367a566b912c5625212b4998ae7e4acb4a4918.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_{10}2^{a}+\log_{10}3^{b}+\log_{10}5^{c}+\log_{10}7^{d}=2005\]" width="353" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/b/34b1fdf053c6b026b0976f3f18c9a30db5ee001a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Rightarrow \log_{10}{2^{a}\cdot 3^{b}\cdot 5^{c}\cdot 7^{d}}=2005\]" width="228" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859b1435302b7be43757fbe0695894a5fc73e38b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Rightarrow 2^{a}\cdot 3^{b}\cdot 5^{c}\cdot 7^{d} = 10^{2005}\]" width="195" height="16" />
</p><p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> must all be rational, and there are no powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/5865c4fb44f1c6ccbe8ceb7773bed0e3a0aa3645.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2005}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/7/ed7de3fc50cca2e447f373e6f9430665852d75b7.png" class="latex" alt="$b=d=0$" width="74" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/061bffb6b21bca10d27f69fa9589dbd10f0faf62.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{a}\cdot 5^{c}=2^{2005}\cdot 5^{2005} \Rightarrow a=c=2005$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="286" height="15" />.
</p><p>Only the four-tuple <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebc95cf85705799c5dd7af9ecd82cbacfe2d3622.png" class="latex" alt="$(2005,0,2005,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="18" /> satisfies the equation, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/1/961f5ccad482b5aee0ddc944b076c23e97ea8c2e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="74" height="23" />.
</p>
