<p>What is the area enclosed by the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc6fef6dd077cc39c7492faf4b54d56bb0d6f867.png" class="latex" alt="$|3x|+|4y|=12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/1/ba1f027368b47d6fb691fecb0153478eec7f4da0.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 6      \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 12      \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 16      \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 24      \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If we get rid of the absolute values, we are left with the following 4 equations (using the logic that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de907587d0f88e2cc674171d292d80c0098eabd.png" class="latex" alt="$|a|=b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa30e2beb1820f6b39f6251023709c8165cefc6.png" class="latex" alt="$-b$" width="22" height="12" />):
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c40c481511750766f665255f34ffe39e8459a0a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 3x+4y=12 \\ -3x+4y=12 \\ 3x-4y=12 \\ -3x-4y=12 \end{align*}" width="114" height="91" />
</p><p>We can then put these equations in slope-intercept form in order to graph them.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/0/860d1524c5b2f1300856502c58fb854953e3045e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 3x+4y=12 \,\implies\, y=-\dfrac{3}{4}x+3\\ -3x+4y=12\,\implies\, y=\dfrac{3}{4}x+3\\ 3x-4y=12\,\implies\, y=\dfrac{3}{4}x-3\\ -3x-4y=12\,\implies\, y=-\dfrac{3}{4}x-3\end{align*}" width="273" height="167" />
</p><p>Now you can graph the lines to find the shape of the graph:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a19def037694543a1771b3897d68d519dd066fcf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] Label f;  f.p=fontsize(6);  xaxis(-8,8,Ticks(f, 4.0));  yaxis(-6,6,Ticks(f, 3.0));  fill((0,-3)--(4,0)--(0,3)--(-4,0)--cycle,grey); draw((-4,-6)--(8,3), Arrows(4)); draw((4,-6)--(-8,3), Arrows(4)); draw((-4,6)--(8,-3), Arrows(4)); draw((4,6)--(-8,-3), Arrows(4));[/asy]" width="668" height="505" />
</p><p>We can easily see that it is a rhombus with diagonals of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. The area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/e/a2e04b8013c8e3230eb2ae243c30cf41d562af3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2}\times 6\times8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="75" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/6/1260e1f36eef835a548a3bef2d39b52af3aa4189.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 24}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>You can also assign <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Then you can easily see that the diagonals are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. Multiply and divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to get D. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The graph is symmetric with respect to both coordinate axes, and in the first quadrant it coincides with the graph of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/157aaf716d5598966d900aa7a85a6ffb1869e1e0.png" class="latex" alt="$3x + 4y = 12.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> Therefore the region is a rhombus, and the area is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/0/ef0949e38da672b8251f851ec7d51ec63056bfa9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Area} = 4\left(\frac{1}{2}(4\cdot 3)\right) = 24 \rightarrow \boxed{D}\]" width="253" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/f/f6fb4f11d88c24aa05f736f273aa28bf02060d80.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((-5,0)--(5,0),Arrow); draw((0,-4)--(0,4),Arrow); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(5,0),S); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(0,4),E); label(&quot;4&quot;,(4,0),S); label(&quot;-4&quot;,(-4,0),S); label(&quot;3&quot;,(0,3),NW); label(&quot;-3&quot;,(0,-3),SW); draw((4,0)--(0,3)--(-4,0)--(0,-3)--cycle,linewidth(0.7)); [/asy]" width="252" height="205" />
</p><p>~Alcumus
</p>
