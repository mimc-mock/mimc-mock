<p>How many three-digit numbers satisfy the property that the middle digit is the average of the first and the last digits? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/633494602d86fd2f37d378787f5d044d24ceb813.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 41\qquad \textbf{(B) } 42\qquad \textbf{(C) } 43\qquad \textbf{(D) } 44\qquad \textbf{(E) } 45$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="401" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If the middle digit is the average of the first and last digits, twice the middle digit must be equal to the sum of the first and last digits. 
</p><p>Doing some <a href="/wiki/index.php/Casework" title="Casework">casework</a>: 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/d/58d1eed7be2e1dcbfd4f7fe86fba2eb7566d8bbf.png" class="latex" alt="$111$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/3/b4328d77715c539955c57ce1aee7b7bc9cdea458.png" class="latex" alt="$210$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7e25f7c53c416ec43ea033a38f680639c5bcfe3.png" class="latex" alt="$123$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfb2fec2f1cde0059d8eb9c11bcb2bd63142401b.png" class="latex" alt="$420$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8efc4595ab8d0d7d43d5ec1988b1b7dc8ecc7fc.png" class="latex" alt="$135$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/0/970a9644fb50e7a7520c3046182fe4c6e6adc11f.png" class="latex" alt="$630$" width="26" height="12" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b554c3eb69d2f5e6625bd31774ea2be775dd9ec3.png" class="latex" alt="$147$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd39493493659fb36ab2875b266a4874b475f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$840$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/a/9aa7d80c1f73b04239eb5ff245318e8aca86421d.png" class="latex" alt="$159$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a087d2a8acf12358ba780168dfbcad381d138f.png" class="latex" alt="$951$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/7/507dc94e37f15efe056cec8cd7c5e52f0556ba00.png" class="latex" alt="$369$" width="26" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/a/a8a7f8642347a2f8c5ab9742779ba01716f3ffd4.png" class="latex" alt="$963$" width="26" height="12" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ff85cd3945fd0988e93ff8b965511ca8e37566c.png" class="latex" alt="$579$" width="26" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/efff00574137efc9e9d43218dadf631aaa4bfeff.png" class="latex" alt="$975$" width="26" height="12" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, possible numbers range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/6256355766a07e3e41d401968db104687d4d3ea7.png" class="latex" alt="$789$" width="26" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d4db801f17f083699a1a8ddae5731d1d012b0b4.png" class="latex" alt="$987$" width="27" height="12" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers in this case. 
</p><p>If the middle digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, the only possible number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" />. So there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> number in this case. 
</p><p>So the total number of three-digit numbers that satisfy the property is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/b/fcb6418a74548056c0d418ee205c78f4cf9aa2a9.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4+6+8+9+7+5+3+1=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="349" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Alternatively, we could note that the middle digit is uniquely defined by the first and third digits since it is half of their sum. This also means that the sum of the first and third digits must be even. Since even numbers are formed either by adding two odd numbers or two even numbers, we can split our problem into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> cases:
</p><p>If both the first digit and the last digit are odd, then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/0/8302a520387ac35652ef30ab035f68c87b6911c2.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 3, 5, 7,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="17" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> as choices for each of these digits, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/a/1ca607239b2bbed826a2c11f87539c44e424fdf5.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot5=25$" width="73" height="12" /> numbers in this case.
</p><p>If both the first and last digits are even, then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/1/7612fb2343cf5754f9a7ca1550b400a6cbf1bd3b.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 4, 6, 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> as our choices for the first digit and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/1/ef1c69750366d38516dfda6352e5d1848e28a082.png" class="latex" alt="$0, 2, 4, 6, 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> for the third digit. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a35170ea01aa737ea8f28795d20ba67c6f6c3fc.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot5=20$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> numbers here.
</p><p>The total number, then, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/a/51a4dadfdb7099d1c5c78e9af8140839e4162459.png" class="latex" alt="$20+25=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="148" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>As we noted in Solution 2, we note that the sum of the first and third digits has to be even. The first digit can have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/3/7e3085e2a093a52511235588d27ec0b22d7a6de9.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, and the third digit can have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> possibilities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dba2662ce8f63c21d122d610da0493dcc915f82.png" class="latex" alt="$(0-9)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="56" height="20" />. This means there can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/d/81de1abdfdede1c060fce10e441912fe8ab460d9.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot10=90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> possible two-digit numbers in which the first digit and the third digit are digits. Exactly half of these would have their sum be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> is even), so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/8/e98b3d52fa9b97c5dd126ef10f0bedb5cb7611ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{90}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="111" height="38" />
</p><p>- <a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Sweetmango77" title="User:Sweetmango77">SweetMango77</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>If our first digit is odd, the last digit also has to be odd for there to be a whole number middle digit that is the average of the first and the last digits.
</p><p>If our first digit is even, the last digit has to be even, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> for there to be a whole number middle digit that is the average of the first and the last digits.
</p><p>As there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> different possible values for each starting digit of our number, we can multiply that by the number of possible first digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/3/7e3085e2a093a52511235588d27ec0b22d7a6de9.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> to achieve our desired solution. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f05ea4d8f005146dadae289fc65a357706bdd45c.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="121" height="30" />
</p><p>-JinhoK
</p>
