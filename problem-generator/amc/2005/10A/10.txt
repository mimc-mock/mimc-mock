<p>There are two values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> for which the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/2/782a7d47e8f237b0150c0a449b6144e2521d2736.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2 + ax + 8x + 9 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="175" height="16" /> has only one solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. What is the sum of those values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/2/e024fe29ea499fc18c2fd3657eb8d5c6f7475258.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }-16\qquad\textbf{(B) }-8\qquad\textbf{(C) } 0\qquad\textbf{(D) }8\qquad\textbf{(E) }20$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="418" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_equation" title="Quadratic equation">quadratic equation</a> has exactly one <a href="/wiki/index.php/Root" class="mw-redirect" title="Root">root</a> if and only if it is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perfect_square" title="Perfect square">perfect square</a>.  So set
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/8/5c830b92250273692425ea1fe03447eb243d586b.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2 + ax + 8x + 9 = (mx + n)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/6/a76fde11dadbf6077f6135ae447ca61bacfe81da.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2 + ax + 8x + 9 = m^2x^2 + 2mnx + n^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="315" height="16" />
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/index.php/Polynomial" title="Polynomial">polynomials</a> are equal only if their <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coefficient" title="Coefficient">coefficients</a> are equal, so we must have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/e/e6e532040a0d661a3b7ed0dbb8e9ad8364362822.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2 = 4, n^2 = 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="116" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/5/875c81c29e81e5c93f1d2c06b049d833318ebdb8.png" class="latex" alt="$m = \pm 2, n = \pm 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="129" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/d/8dde4a4de65c1fd24e24b7c86d0a20fbb0b6b144.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 8= 2mn = \pm 2\cdot 2\cdot 3 = \pm 12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="249" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f98b3f405113241659d8c8db2ea3480346db4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/4/bd4c04e3891a120c9a9d1fa2e33455d70054b025.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -20$" width="65" height="12" />.
</p><p>So the desired sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/a/f1a1d2da4bdb0426e5871bbc3ae6954715ec3afb.png" class="latex" alt="$(4)+(-20)=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}-16}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="199" height="30" />
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Alternatively, note that whatever the two values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> are, they must lead to equations of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/7/707f0f7c6f1b2a8a9c1a3b3d97392e92d56b8a96.png" class="latex" alt="$px^2 + qx + r =0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="133" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/6/706765780ad6c688c085634a7b5681c4a826f9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$px^2 - qx + r = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="133" height="18" />.  So the two choices of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce9d7f5c05fae544efe5a8ef80ea27942ee455f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + 8 = q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/5/755f79d18e50b54afd5389f1dd03e97d914e16af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 + 8 = -q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/c/b6ccf690555da784be20703ae10d2dc1aac07ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + a_2 + 16 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="129" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8943dcf4083732bd32691b0786e37e08e4c1fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + a_2 =\boxed{\textbf{(A)}-16}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="163" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since this quadratic must have a double root, the discriminant of the quadratic formula for this quadratic must be 0. Therefore, we must have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/4/46400a4b9f2c514b954d34e89d4abe951e677ca0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a+8)^2 - 4(4)(9) = 0 \implies a^2 + 16a - 80.\]" width="332" height="19" /> We can use the quadratic formula to solve for its roots (we can ignore the expression in the radical sign as it will cancel out due to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/91205e81c98c486418902c88d5c396f5355491f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="13" height="12" /> sign when added). So we must have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/0/5d0f6161fdc7314df85dcc6b76f4880ec3302e90.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{-16 + \sqrt{\text{something}}}{2} + \frac{-16 - \sqrt{\text{something}}}{2}.\]" width="319" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/4/3f4fd622897883a6388e570d20953dada1e3b1c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-32}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}-16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="143" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>There is only one positive value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that the quadratic equation would have only one solution.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/4/3347335f66ca1420c3b32e9993c4ed841917726a.png" class="latex" alt="$k-8$" width="41" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/9/0990c5b43f9d325baf9a0a6388fbb219533750aa.png" class="latex" alt="$-k-8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="16" /> are the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/7/ce7a0970a53224e48e46c45be363b196cc925605.png" class="latex" alt="$-8-8=-16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="113" height="15" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/1/8c1420bcb64a0a09a252267f8e6bcd533fe2e328.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)} -16}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="83" height="30" />
</p><p>Another way of thinking of this is letting the two values of k be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee5c7c921b3334f6ecf58c241eeea42acf0ce71e.png" class="latex" alt="$a_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />. Since this is obviously a square of a binomial, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/907d5d00a68892ff70f9f60826cf379a31d1a836.png" class="latex" alt="$k+8 = m$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/d/85d34cf82d4a01e8ccd77957b91fadb13d5a676c.png" class="latex" alt="$-m$" width="29" height="8" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. Thus, we can say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/5/525b2c4c6c1e0ff75e4b1f0b448a4cc2171c11f2.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 8 = m$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/d/19d6be4df69ebd345ef164152a4c685cb1cabc03.png" class="latex" alt="$a_0 + 8 = -m$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="17" />. Combining these gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d3304f0f67fc5579a4e16a728fca2a3be44dc877.png" class="latex" alt="$a_0 + a + 16 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="17" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/e/8aea0c49612749dacb942d61d80e8b4ef879edc8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_0 + a = 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="17" />. Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/1/8c1420bcb64a0a09a252267f8e6bcd533fe2e328.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)} -16}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="83" height="30" />.
</p><p>~Extremelysupercooldude (Second solution)
</p>
