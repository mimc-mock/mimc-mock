<p>In the figure, the length of side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/6/8a6d1b584a42c88275d2ae86a59079aad25c034f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{50}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/b/1ebdd60090c4cc0f5101f76c7e7c412a20652e59.png" class="latex" alt="$BE=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. What is the area of the inner square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" />?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:AMC102005Aq.png" class="image"><img alt="AMC102005Aq.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//5/5f/AMC102005Aq.png" width="229" height="205" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0c1ef2cb12923ea6ed664f51697a35262ab0cfe.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 25\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 32\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 42$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We see that side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7baf95ecafc2d7791788ec79aeeae390d60b119.png" class="latex" alt="$BE$" width="28" height="12" />, which we know is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, is also the shorter leg of one of the four right triangles (which are congruent, I won’t prove this). So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e756fad58399e4d0aad3551d44e57643155a73e.png" class="latex" alt="$AH = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/8/d68dfd296312a761474a3f8070a95fda611a4cb9.png" class="latex" alt="$HB = HE + BE = HE + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="223" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/8/7381d73a5fd25fe335bba4cd0b2219adaf09ed3f.png" class="latex" alt="$HE$" width="30" height="12" /> is one of the sides of the square whose area we want to find. So:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/3/7c37e12e7b6f1641b19a232b4fdbdb6f03349aac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1^2 + (HE+1)^2=\sqrt{50}^2\]" width="185" height="25" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/7/2d7e5f6ccce6d23fa35a57d269737c7e9b494cfb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1 + (HE+1)^2=50\]" width="155" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cad7941ecb53cad597279cc85a31fa4983b80f0b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(HE+1)^2=49\]" width="124" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb2c8f74471c971c81fcf1364d33b5fbc98787b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[HE+1=7\]" width="95" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb09cde4ae1381e2d11cd524c1aab06d502fb90e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[HE=6\]" width="63" height="12" /> 
So, the area of the square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/f/d5f383383d02954ed10dabfd822fce78dfd5bbb0.png" class="latex" alt="$6^2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }36}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="107" height="30" />
</p>
