<p>Team A and team B play a series. The first team to win three games wins the series. Each team is equally likely to win each game, there are no ties, and the outcomes of the individual games are independent. If team B wins the second game and team A wins the series, what is the probability that team B wins the first game? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/2/f82a6e9c04b32a77f6cf2bebb11766de8425bf39.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{1}{5}\qquad \textbf{(B) }  \frac{1}{4}\qquad \textbf{(C) }  \frac{1}{3}\qquad \textbf{(D) }  \frac{1}{2}\qquad \textbf{(E) }  \frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="375" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> games played. 
</p><p>If team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> won the first two games, team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> would need to win the next three games. So the only possible order of wins is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/0/47032edaf1fba0072b08b1aafae1cbed6e8c8582.png" class="latex" alt="$BBAAA$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. 
</p><p>If team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> won the first game, and team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> won the second game, the possible order of wins are: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/c/f6c3967d56d092941716b213e3c6c7fb452f44b0.png" class="latex" alt="$ABBAA, ABABA,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/8/e2805092f67aea71dc6d2e677f5a1f245ba6cf3f.png" class="latex" alt="$ABAAX$" width="71" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> denotes that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />th game wasn't played. 
</p><p>There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> possibility where team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> wins the first game and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> total possibilities when team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> wins the series and team <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> wins the second game. Note that the fourth possibility <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876de446be0539066ad612d3891c0e499a1a9ded.png" class="latex" alt="$(ABAAX)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="88" height="20" /> occurs twice as often as the others, so we put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> over <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> total possibilities. The desired probability is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10c87abfc18885b659a4f1a3d7f7c173bae76182.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{1}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="62" height="49" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/8/e2805092f67aea71dc6d2e677f5a1f245ba6cf3f.png" class="latex" alt="$ABAAX$" width="71" height="13" /> counts as two cases because it can be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/8/968600a628e418d5543ac90349371d1173409c71.png" class="latex" alt="$ABAAB$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/a/28a67b8209229c49144a646cc573fa6208c135f5.png" class="latex" alt="$ABAAA$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" />.
</p><p>~lnzhonglp
</p>
