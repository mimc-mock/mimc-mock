<p>For each positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/d/49d98ffe996c0f0d3b82b284cba2113439fefea9.png" class="latex" alt="$n &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/f/7dfd17dc4d3100462df726e8eb7f9262783429eb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> denote the greatest prime factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is it true that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/a/83abfdfd91ae9f816d90905e6fec1bad8cee25bc.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n) = \sqrt{n}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb62334d346f790219248106a2da7359769e7f20.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n+48) = \sqrt{n+48}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="20" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/3433ac96e7e8879e3e82f62a0d7742879ce8f031.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 0\qquad \textbf{(B) } 1\qquad \textbf{(C) } 3\qquad \textbf{(D) } 4\qquad \textbf{(E) } 5$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="355" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/a/83abfdfd91ae9f816d90905e6fec1bad8cee25bc.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n) = \sqrt{n}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="19" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2fd78656bafb635390f847eee3208453411177e.png" class="latex" alt="$n = p_{1}^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="19" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0945a10572335a30ce5f3e8e6dcc8abc1752d0e5.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="15" height="11" /> is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Prime_number" title="Prime number">prime number</a>.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb62334d346f790219248106a2da7359769e7f20.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n+48) = \sqrt{n+48}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="20" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d3783b0730c15a1b064adb27a7e76d830e93df8d.png" class="latex" alt="$n + 48$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> is a square, but we know that n is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/9/d99b85a721665eb5f758c0ad6f6ef44cbf38231e.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{1}^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="16" height="19" />.
</p><p><br />
This means we just have to check for squares of primes, add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and look whether the root is a prime number.
We can easily see that the difference between two consecutive square after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/8/7080fdbf6e7385fb60b23d885788d3bd7eda12a9.png" class="latex" alt="$576$" width="26" height="12" /> is greater than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />,
Hence we have to consider only the prime numbers till <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>Squaring prime numbers below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> including <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> we get the following list.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/4/034674c98233f0a7427459fec583eb8fa5670da3.png" class="latex" alt="$4 , 9 , 25 , 49 , 121, 169 , 289 , 361 , 529$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="255" height="16" />
</p><p>But adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to a number ending with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> will result in a number ending with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, but we know that a perfect square does not end in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, so we can eliminate those cases to get the new list.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/1/4a16673d2e4d6d41976142334b98edcf297572d5.png" class="latex" alt="$4 , 25 , 121 , 361$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" />
</p><p>Adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76ad7692f5d86be790209a56a68c2a3488235054.png" class="latex" alt="$121$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> as the only possible solution.
Hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e101201addd96f637069ef5899fa2b44223efc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p><p>edited by mobius247
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Note:_Solution_1">Note: Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since all primes greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> are odd, we know that the difference between the squares of any two consecutive primes greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/6776f8ba2f546ce0c9631ea890a8f28936dc84c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(p+2)^2-p^2=4p+4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="176" height="21" />, where p is the smaller of the consecutive primes. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/4/d44e74bedc35135ff49606762920ff5ab5b13ee2.png" class="latex" alt="$p&gt;11$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/e/03e5333cc57f181130e8c14b4b4ae179f1ff46d3.png" class="latex" alt="$4p+4&gt;48$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="17" />. This means that the difference between the squares of any two consecutive primes both greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac3a1e56c7d64e3b6ea16aa944abe965fe50a3da.png" class="latex" alt="$n+48$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> can't both be the squares of primes if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/a/5cafe2ec47609fea69da05e31308d5f9642c2e7c.png" class="latex" alt="$n=p^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/4/d44e74bedc35135ff49606762920ff5ab5b13ee2.png" class="latex" alt="$p&gt;11$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="15" />. So, we only need to check <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/0/010f2cc611263f4948be18517e728c897b367f4a.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2^2, 3^2, 5^2, 7^2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="132" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c932acac3f8af148abfd610f23ef8d45a84b8071.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />.
