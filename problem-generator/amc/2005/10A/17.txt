<p>In the five-sided star shown, the letters <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/6521ad84932f05bbfa1185b24d793813c7aefc2d.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B, C, D,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are replaced by the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/5/aa5a33b0160737a99232499be265cabccf6aeffc.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 5, 6, 7,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, although not necessarily in this order. The sums of the numbers at the ends of the line segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d9456e0361813641f4ce80980839e9038fe8f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$EA$" width="28" height="13" /> form an arithmetic sequence, although not necessarily in that order. What is the middle term of the arithmetic sequence? 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2005amc10a17.gif" class="image"><img alt="2005amc10a17.gif" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//d/da/2005amc10a17.gif" width="220" height="220" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/0/8b01be4a7563dbf5928a55e2e6603a2d66cc2786.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 9\qquad \textbf{(B) } 10\qquad \textbf{(C) } 11\qquad \textbf{(D) } 12\qquad \textbf{(E) } 13$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="392" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Each corner <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e894aacf461d6cc63d0ddd9c8e0f472a9eb217ce.png" class="latex" alt="$(A,B,C,D,E)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" /> goes to two sides/numbers. (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> goes to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> goes to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e525b776c49bbe7d0ae66cfc7ab8f58f40624e.png" class="latex" alt="$DC$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" />). The sum of every term is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/1/f61c981ba4b3e5be0fb1bc1cdef341d2b01ad2be.png" class="latex" alt="$2(3+5+6+7+9)=60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="199" height="18" />
</p><p>Since the middle term in an arithmetic sequence is the average of all the terms in the sequence, the middle number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/8/07818b59c2fa658dbcb25538d56ed713545fba5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{5}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know that the smallest number in this sequence must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108880936ebf093c88308f58baa4abe040466366.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 5 = 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />, and the biggest number must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee57915ae4e0a978e7690eaeb389982b973304fa.png" class="latex" alt="$7 + 9 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />. Since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> terms in this sequence, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/0/880587cb98ff1fb7c5724816703c0f0b6a6b2366.png" class="latex" alt="$8 + 4d = 16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="16" />, or that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/d/fbd68fc9b0e901c556adb5c363357d3afbf1b4ce.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 2$" width="42" height="12" />. Thus, we know that the middle term must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e0a6b32d0dd4ceceade718344eefc1ed0f0af3.png" class="latex" alt="$8 + 2 \cdot 2 = \boxed{12}.$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="123" height="25" /> 
~yk2007 (Daniel K.)
</p>
