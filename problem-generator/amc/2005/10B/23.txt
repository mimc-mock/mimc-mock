<p>In trapezoid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/b/63b7e5ec2db31e9aded4bb09881b42d1c439fce9.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DC}$" width="30" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> as the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> as the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/6/b6691251a362837413262d094d29f92be951d579.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DA}$" width="29" height="15" />. The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e91fa584790bd73c21e57d1c0bdc5199237b961.png" class="latex" alt="$ABEF$" width="57" height="13" /> is twice the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/5/025e25a12bab06678895bfd77a533bdd219e1f09.png" class="latex" alt="$FECD$" width="58" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/2/a422b0afb689f94399dc5d56451162bc5b24fae1.png" class="latex" alt="$AB/DC$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/7/a67fa1091b0d2e188c66b65c1574326d71a8d4a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 3 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 5 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 6 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 8$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="355" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since the heights of both trapezoids are equal, and the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e91fa584790bd73c21e57d1c0bdc5199237b961.png" class="latex" alt="$ABEF$" width="57" height="13" /> is twice the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/5/025e25a12bab06678895bfd77a533bdd219e1f09.png" class="latex" alt="$FECD$" width="58" height="12" />, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/5/9d5d14d6ee900e1584ee070e29e90fc37cf10522.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB+EF}{2}=2\left(\frac{DC+EF}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="227" height="43" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/6/8f6ec1fe8411c5eb582634c589f9a1e361a00f90.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB+EF}{2}=DC+EF$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="188" height="38" />, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a469648d6894f1e69f6254e44695750272a8002f.png" class="latex" alt="$AB+EF=2DC+2EF$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="202" height="14" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> is exactly halfway between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e525b776c49bbe7d0ae66cfc7ab8f58f40624e.png" class="latex" alt="$DC$" width="29" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4cbf23ed210f08a24257d84111e47ea9b3169f0.png" class="latex" alt="$EF=\frac{AB+DC}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="135" height="38" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/827fb1e9a78adecc729135be711f34101c935826.png" class="latex" alt="$AB+\frac{AB+DC}{2}=2DC+AB+DC$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="300" height="38" />, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f445abbdbd58ff387bdd624e25f7f05d3e741e68.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2}AB+\frac{1}{2}DC=3DC+AB$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="219" height="37" />, and 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/9/8c97175e1a016e4b55f4b022f2feb475e3917f5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}AB=\frac{5}{2}DC$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db72ec2b06387d89e2ee4f3894bc850e35d1107.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{DC} = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="115" height="39" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Mark <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/d/e8dc01cadd3631e737d566071ad81b7441bfbe5e.png" class="latex" alt="$DC=z$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/598141cec89bb44405e53c44ba75a25f8b5981bd.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=x$" width="62" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/0/d90e337cb2b816722270478889b7fcdb7ddaf921.png" class="latex" alt="$FE=y.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" />
Note that the heights of trapezoids <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e91fa584790bd73c21e57d1c0bdc5199237b961.png" class="latex" alt="$ABEF$" width="57" height="13" /> &amp; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/5/025e25a12bab06678895bfd77a533bdd219e1f09.png" class="latex" alt="$FECD$" width="58" height="12" /> are the same. Mark the height to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />.
</p><p>Then, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/6772b8ac0eb8c6b7d0f60109b4256437ff989dd4.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{x+y}{2}\cdot h=2(\tfrac{y+z}{2} \cdot h)$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="150" height="21" />.
</p><p>From this, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/6/ec676c018c29574bc984c6ba49bac747a0fd281c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2z+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="15" />.
</p><p>We also get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/8/4186ccadec5cea8f50f7fcec4282da8453668845.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{x+z}{2} \cdot 2h= 3(\tfrac{y+z}{2} \cdot h)$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="159" height="21" />.
</p><p>Simplifying, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/4/3c4bcc0f3957dc1333f2eb2321c8efb4aebbfa1a.png" class="latex" alt="$2x=z+3y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" />
</p><p>Notice that we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed6064e626f93a2248588bbf85c5656eb2bc6d28.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{AB}{DC}=\tfrac{x}{z}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="60" height="21" />.
</p><p>Dividing the first equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/5/c9509dcb10d8a9d447ab2ac4a8c2b273bc54271b.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{x}{z}=2+\tfrac{y}{z}\implies 3(\tfrac{x}{z})=6+3(\tfrac{y}{z})$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="251" height="20" />.
</p><p>Dividing the second equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/1/c91c59e921d84ddd73ebdda6b846c70d36000e10.png" class="latex" alt="$2(\tfrac{x}{z})=1+3(\tfrac{y}{z})$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="123" height="20" />.
</p><p>Now, when we subtract the top equation from the bottom, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/787819f69c1fd75454544cac7909c9786b0f4a03.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{x}{z}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }5}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="93" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Since the bases of the trapezoids along with the height are the same, the only thing that matters is the second base. Denote the length of the bigger trapezoid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The area of the smaller trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5b9c2cc3a4c545b0069da59bbcd3cd1197dbd1.png" class="latex" alt="$h \cdot \frac {b_1 + b_2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="82" height="39" />. The area of the larger trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/3/803547c98b932722bf5c104ec605106dcb87b400.png" class="latex" alt="$h \cdot \frac {b_1 + x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="77" height="39" />. Since this problem asks for proportions, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad79bea94962f7b6a51157fd8465a31a32eb4b7e.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/b/85b0a2ba0313bce65af0e4add1b8375e1e757545.png" class="latex" alt="$b_2 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />. 
</p><p>The smaller trapezoid has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> while the larger trapezoid must have area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbfe6df20ff971e6138215d24c77a4a2ab0f3340.png" class="latex" alt="$5h$" width="19" height="12" />. We have the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/d/46d30694544f37f28f0cce84c31670de0fb8e783.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {x}{2} = 5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="50" height="34" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> = 10, and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6aca2b6bca42d2ed0539075dd6698440de083b6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }5}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="57" height="30" />
</p><p>~Arcticturn
</p>
