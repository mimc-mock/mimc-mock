<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span id="Solution_1_(trig)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_.28trig.29">Solution 1 (trig)</span></h3>
<p>The area of a triangle can be given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a2861e0e1333e467eb1ad74a61449f8597183367.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12 ab \sin C$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc523ef4c2ff5be8dd4f8dc222d13f206d086c05.png" class="latex" alt="$MC=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" /> because it is the midpoint of a side, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/f/e2fb6f4572576df0b7fd91965771bba13959aeb3.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=2$" width="62" height="12" /> because it is the same length as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />. Each angle of an equilateral triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/2/2722c3a56c8f3d2963983ecb0fb357bf1aedf7b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle MCD = 120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="119" height="13" />. The area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3ba733c00972e7378ebb2b362eb3bdc0d55f29f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12 (1)(2) \sin  120^\circ = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="221" height="53" />.
Note: Even if you don't know the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/4/ee45f5d49f06fa23b0d6c053e87ca0eb14e11cc7.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin 120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, you can use the fact that  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/a/b2ab81bf19b69f41ec6d92f437b363f5fbffde0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin x = \sin (180^\circ - x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd500174679a3a6af200fb48fb63c39ebd9b6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin 120^\circ = \sin 60^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="134" height="13" />.
You can easily calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/2396e05a25e87d6dc5f9d10978a59b4442121c9e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin 60^\circ$" width="49" height="13" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/1/471b8f058b0b966f478688febc32fbef03432011.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" /> using equilateral triangles.
</p><p>~Minor Edits by doulai1
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>In order to calculate the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a8945cc639df250c096deb4e5a706035da87fd45.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDM$" width="65" height="13" />, we can use the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/d/2bd38eff54ef7dc0e188ef1149b0c64f1efcf10f.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\dfrac{1}{2}bh$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> is the base. We already know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/1/ec1b644ec706069b4a2c9c56b826bbd71ba88950.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}=2$" width="62" height="15" />, so the formula becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/1/331b30a8dc7458ae2625ef2f20ccf5de8ff0fc21.png" class="latex" alt="$A=h$" width="48" height="13" />. We can drop verticals down from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> to points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />, respectively. We can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/8/3585a839707dd789928b448120d2e4ea537efce0.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AEC \sim \triangle MFC$" width="147" height="13" />. Now, we establish the relationship that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2dd91aa6f83eafd6c24b4b6fe0a5ff2cffe8ca0.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{AE}{MF}=\dfrac{AC}{MC}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="38" />. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/f/55f242ca45f96d4a7c3705b7bff72760ccf6cbdc.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}=2$" width="60" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/5/165d9bfceb8375fe979640426e50a99d5ed62ba0.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MC}=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="16" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/b/a1b3b8188354494cb248bd9965a24d0b9f1ef814.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AEB$" width="58" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangle and the ratio of the sides opposite the angles are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37a1c8cd544ba73e959a4b5ed2441bf9653efe7d.png" class="latex" alt="$1-\sqrt{3}-2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/8/5e8bc36793a7c64d05fff20d519f0f72d9256fab.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AE}$" width="28" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />. Plugging those numbers in, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e813dda4fca3b738303536964c0420bf37bfe27d.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{MF}=\dfrac{2}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="73" height="41" />. Cross-multiplying, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8a4301feba41049214e8e505da6738058a8c88.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times\overline{MF}=\sqrt{3}\times1\implies \overline{MF}=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="279" height="41" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e36928adcfc38a926c66c5f77cf2f6d6449bc526.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MF}$" width="34" height="15" /> is the height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a8945cc639df250c096deb4e5a706035da87fd45.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDM$" width="65" height="13" />, the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20fe91441e98a6969598b7464ebfb0c374fdede2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="76" height="54" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Draw a line from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> to the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />. Call the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. This is an equilateral triangle, since the two segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a894a620b157358dab619a863fdea6c368d3d0cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/a/e6a5dab0650ea78f80ca12d62afe83905de42ceb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MC}$" width="34" height="15" /> are identical, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4056d41f42aa8a00adca0ff5d1209b6bb0e92d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C$" width="27" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d5c45388de5c75af641c046d466de3592ffa98.png" class="latex" alt="$60^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. Using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/7/c778d6190a62180381c8864640d46fa3f00866b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />. Also, the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> is 2, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/4373e7b4be983c0315e82e1fda1df66ed34dd7fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BD}$" width="30" height="15" />. So, our final equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/5/e15c61380f67ebbf63e95741d79f4536b2d6d83a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\times2\over2$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="62" height="53" />, which just leaves us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/b/cbb116c62b64e4de261dd284d7e08fdd9d341684.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="76" height="54" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Drop a vertical down from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />. Let us call the point of intersection <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> and the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />. We can observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/5/895b2bee900078f464f96cd50364a250f1885945.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AYC$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/d/c1d9b9509264bedd449bff5746bf200fce4f5594.png" class="latex" alt="$\bigtriangleup MXC$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> are similar. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/a/03a548f8f2bc6dfce44eed33b1da3c8afd1b118f.png" class="latex" alt="$AY$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99f089046b752269e77100c333b11d8d72e5895.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/2/c426f3a67ed3c724e8559275f8a98f734f791a80.png" class="latex" alt="$AC:MC=2:1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="138" height="16" /> we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a538def15c55fe2da727ba1eecc79921ce174e24.png" class="latex" alt="$MX=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="92" height="41" /> Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/4/394a09127351f604ebc21e87412150da4fc6a43d.png" class="latex" alt="$BD,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="34" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/8/3286e1f36069b5b53de8fb75631cf6f0aed5e54d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/3/e23b358cd3c8f5f18c6d70169315f64d75d3617e.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=2.$" width="66" height="12" /> Using the area formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/8/d683d318a9c3a00639a21ff4a9611c2acb17cfa6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{CD*MX}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}.$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="193" height="54" />
