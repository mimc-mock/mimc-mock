<p>An envelope contains eight bills: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ones, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> fives, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> tens, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> twenties.  Two bills are drawn at random without replacement.  What is the probability that their sum is &#36;<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> or more?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/1925481673aac1e5b54fe39080c98d64f4f868e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }\ {{{\frac{1}{4}}}} \qquad \textbf{(B) }\ {{{\frac{2}{5}}}} \qquad \textbf{(C) }\ {{{\frac{3}{7}}}} \qquad \textbf{(D) }\ {{{\frac{1}{2}}}} \qquad \textbf{(E) }\ {{{\frac{2}{3}}}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="403" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The only way to get a total of &#36;<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> or more is if you pick a twenty and another bill, or if you pick both tens. There are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/8/5a819257864b33269141d19f1d7f14adecdc88ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{8}{2}=\dfrac{8\times7}{2\times1}=28$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="147" height="53" /> ways to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> bills out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> ways to choose a twenty and some other non-twenty bill. There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> way to choose both twenties, and also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> way to choose both tens. Adding these up, we find that there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> ways to attain a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> or greater, so there is a total probability of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9afac7412a7a7f338c7dee18a29ec83ed9604fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{14}{28}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }\frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="108" height="49" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Another way to do this problem is to use <a href="/wiki/index.php/Complementary_counting" title="Complementary counting">complementary counting</a>, i.e. how many ways that the sum is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. Now, you do not have to consider the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> twenties, so you have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> bills left. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/3/a138d1cd3d5560e0c9e7bec9576f68ea73d54cef.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{6}{2} = \dfrac{6\times5}{2\times1} = 15$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="147" height="53" /> ways. However, you counted the case when you have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> tens, so you need to subtract 1, and you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. Finding the ways to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> or higher, you subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/3/a63a0e529e87987f9803008af272714e110746fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{14}{28} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }\dfrac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="108" height="49" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>There are two cases that work, namely getting at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> twenty, or getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> tens.
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c1be0245389ea2fdad5a0eb26a17b9f812e0bf2.png" class="latex" alt="$P(\text{Get at least one twenty}) = 1-P(\text{Do not get a single twenty})=1- \frac{\binom{6}{2}}{\binom{8}{2}}=\frac{28-15}{28}=\frac{13}{28}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="689" height="49" />
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />&#160;: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/b/80b10462ec3bf763a1f65a850b22aeea89b8c127.png" class="latex" alt="$P(\text{Get two tens}) = \frac{1}{\binom{8}{2}} = \frac{1}{28}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="217" height="44" />
</p><p>Summing up our cases, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/7/cf76b2bb92f792d964698fb471fb227a6a982c46.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{13}{28}+\frac{1}{28}=\frac{14}{28}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } \dfrac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="199" height="49" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Note that if a twenty is drawn, anything else that is drawn will create a total greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />; The probability of a twenty being drawn first is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/f/5cf72c5136384cd1309126dbfb90b4237a20df2b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="16" height="37" /> The same could be said for drawing anything, and then drawing a twenty. However, we can only draw something that isn't a twenty first (since we've already accounted for the probability of drawing two twenties). 
</p><p>The probability of drawing a non-twenty first, then a twenty second is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1c0b19a7ad365afa4a92567c6e1abff611c9de9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}\cdot\frac{2}{7}=\frac{3}{14}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="93" height="38" /> Finally, we can draw two tens. The probability of this occuring is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d0e070f0b18293608d360f86e89c150722b313.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}\cdot\frac{1}{7}=\frac{1}{28}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="93" height="38" /> 
</p><p>Adding these three probabilities gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed004f5b587c5d63907619e3ecb4ba79ccc9770a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}+\frac{3}{14}+\frac{1}{28}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } \dfrac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="188" height="49" />
</p><p>-Benedict T (countmath1) (edited by AMC_8)
</p>
