<p>The first term of a sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5b442c275b4324f100c365b774e872b6b21f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2005$" width="35" height="12" />. Each succeeding term is the sum of the cubes of the digits of the previous term. What is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/2/582ea0f3eb219a104673436f686669ef5d1bffb9.png" class="latex" alt="${2005}^{\text{th}}$" width="48" height="15" /> term of the sequence?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df228150ab22b1274f6c387ce7840a1adfab7d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 29 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 55 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 85 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 133 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 250$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="419" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Performing this operation several times yields the results of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/0/9501915f08ed1cf295fdd389481801277bcb2e94.png" class="latex" alt="$133$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> for the second term, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" /> for the third term, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" /> for the fourth term. The sum of the cubes of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" /> equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/0/9501915f08ed1cf295fdd389481801277bcb2e94.png" class="latex" alt="$133$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, a complete cycle. The cycle is, excluding the first term, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbdd606f9a293cef748b15755d8d0991765f38bb.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{\text{nd}}$" width="23" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/8/1c829ec23ba9c6994a639da67d878e22d151a7ff.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{\text{rd}}$" width="21" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c402a8d37bae8bb31fa855956ad7c2303549ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{\text{th}}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="16" /> terms will equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/0/9501915f08ed1cf295fdd389481801277bcb2e94.png" class="latex" alt="$133$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" />, following the fourth term. Any term number that is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/b/07bdc1cc8efa7f7f1c736c4efad05c7c7cac3db3.png" class="latex" alt="$1\ (\text{mod}\ 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" /> will produce a result of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" />. It just so happens that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/a/b3a7db1aee1d70559b3a4527151341eb6ae2887b.png" class="latex" alt="$2005\equiv 1\ (\text{mod}\ 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="18" />, which leads us to the answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8efd78c61b0c5abb3e340788350e4b23573b29d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 250}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="74" height="30" />.
</p>
