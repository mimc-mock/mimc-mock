<p>What is the value of the following expression?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/4/5f4f7b3d646f920fd9ef3f767aad765860768b41.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{100^2-7^2}{70^2-11^2} \cdot \frac{(70-11)(70+11)}{(100-7)(100+7)}\]" width="236" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/b/afb3d180373767f77bb6754fd11911437c1e62b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 1 \qquad \textbf{(B) } \frac{9951}{9950} \qquad \textbf{(C) } \frac{4780}{4779} \qquad \textbf{(D) } \frac{108}{107} \qquad \textbf{(E) } \frac{81}{80}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="449" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Using difference of squares to factor the left term, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bd5ad760781142ae19f9ab08f756ab9c74008f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{100^2-7^2}{70^2-11^2} \cdot \frac{(70-11)(70+11)}{(100-7)(100+7)} = \frac{(100-7)(100+7)}{(70-11)(70+11)} \cdot \frac{(70-11)(70+11)}{(100-7)(100+7)}.\]" width="561" height="44" />
Cancelling all the terms, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/b/d3b0a524fcc5f01e0060f6aff04c67ded46bb245.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" /> as the answer.
</p>
