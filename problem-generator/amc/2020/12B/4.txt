What is the least possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />?

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since the three angles of a triangle add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> and one of the angles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> because it's a right triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/a/0ca2a00fecab06c6f7c3f0c09d772ef5b3046e7c.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{\circ} + b^{\circ} = 90^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" />.
</p><p>The greatest prime number less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/0/e609f93104b193a2355181ca62ff083ea9fd3821.png" class="latex" alt="$89$" width="17" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/8/3086bb8e1b6d1f58366ab0e0cfa782de7f0254fb.png" class="latex" alt="$a=89^{\circ}$" width="58" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/2/86290e8097e111116145cdf4bdaa9c83db2acff5.png" class="latex" alt="$b=90^{\circ}-89^{\circ}=1^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="145" height="13" />, which is not prime.
</p><p>The next greatest prime number less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c1ad4a48099624f3bbfc16590f9be2d29351d61.png" class="latex" alt="$83$" width="17" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/1/3a11ca42d3d227474e5ddb9b218794167863df35.png" class="latex" alt="$a=83^{\circ}$" width="58" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/e/d3eb56956bcc53f1c44341cfcc3d0db580b15b39.png" class="latex" alt="$b=7^{\circ}$" width="47" height="13" />, which IS prime, so we have our answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6cecfe394f16d2d6d7bc6844d2d845b2a014f077.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> ~quacker88
</p>

Testing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, the smaller angle, makes the other angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c1ad4a48099624f3bbfc16590f9be2d29351d61.png" class="latex" alt="$83$" width="17" height="12" /> which is prime, therefore our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6cecfe394f16d2d6d7bc6844d2d845b2a014f077.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
