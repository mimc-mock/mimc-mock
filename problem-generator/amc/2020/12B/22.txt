<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We proceed by using AM-GM. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/d/a1d10d15f47aae497eaf0c90b7fa725674e47f70.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2^t-3t) + 3t}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="39" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/b/cbba187e93d2b0619abf3862fffc9f9a08b08c51.png" class="latex" alt="$\ge \sqrt{(2^t-3t)(3t)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="134" height="32" />. Thus, squaring gives us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a1f0af16b5d52da563a6cc6e464bf01a5d1ed3e.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{t-1} \ge (2^t-3t)(3t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="19" />. Remembering what we want to find, we divide both sides of the inequality by the positive amount of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c4ed93e90bc57ec56b23125883d27c6f0ee772.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3\cdot4^t}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="39" height="37" />. We get the maximal values as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e10f7b229549d9afbdef6ae2862bc89bacfe16cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />, and we are done.
</p>
