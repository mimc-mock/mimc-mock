<p>A three-quarter sector of a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> inches together with its interior can be rolled up to form the lateral surface area of a right circular cone by taping together along the two radii shown. What is the volume of the cone in cubic inches?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/d/0fd309aa42ba1222ee60252a6c9cb5860f74c4c4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw(Arc((0,0), 4, 0, 270)); draw((0,-4)--(0,0)--(4,0)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;, (2,0), S); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b1f58739039d84996842ce9fd26c68e045100cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3\pi \sqrt5 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4\pi \sqrt3 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3 \pi \sqrt7 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 6\pi \sqrt3 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 6\pi \sqrt7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="526" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Notice that when the cone is created, the 2 shown radii when merged will become the slant height of the cone and the intact circumference of the circle will become the circumference of the base of the cone. 
</p><p>We can calculate that the intact circumference of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/8/9a89f98c543af6930a0ad3221e081f6054039625.png" class="latex" alt="$8\pi\cdot\frac{3}{4}=6\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="90" height="38" />. Since that is also equal to the circumference of the cone, the radius of the cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. We also have that the slant height of the cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Therefore, we use the Pythagorean Theorem to calculate that the height of the cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/8/4a8e1b6e34c985945fbdcdcae40015a4cfe3cfe9.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{4^2-3^2}=\sqrt7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="119" height="19" />. The volume of the cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/c/56c2d316caebfc758f7bf16ee62a8bb484180d11.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\cdot\pi\cdot3^2\cdot\sqrt7=\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 3 \pi \sqrt7 }$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="218" height="37" /> -PCChess
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_2_(Last_Resort/Cheap)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_.28Last_Resort.2FCheap.29">Solution 2 (Last Resort/Cheap)</span></h3>
<p>Using a ruler, measure a circle of radius 4 and cut out the circle and then the quarter missing. Then, fold it into a cone and measure the diameter to be 6 cm <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/e/2de415aa93ea4222bae043d345cdcb51a1f1afb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\implies r=3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" />. You can form a right triangle with sides 3, 4, and then through the Pythagorean theorem the height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is found to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/045215a4e78522474532f09dd2156c29a343448b.png" class="latex" alt="$h^2 = 4^{2} - 3^{2} \implies h = \sqrt{7}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="206" height="18" />. The volume of a cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10df676b1e02c93559e08cfc3ea10c4cb6fc3e6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\pi r^{2}h$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />. Plugging in we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/6/416d370545622609c59a00a1232e5b3ec81a2c6d.png" class="latex" alt="$V = 3\pi \sqrt{7} \implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="173" height="28" />
</p><p>- DBlack2021
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>The radius of the given <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> - circle will end up being the slant height of the cone. Thus, the radius and height of the cone are legs of a right triangle with hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. The volume of a cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10df676b1e02c93559e08cfc3ea10c4cb6fc3e6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\pi r^{2}h$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />. Using this with the options, we can take out the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> and multiply the coefficient of the radical by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31fc57e38bd41dc81568b6607fb22595d36fdf00.png" class="latex" alt="$r^{2}h$" width="26" height="15" />. We can then use the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> values to see that the only option that satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/d/56dda999d5a46ddff81416761d59e280e34b959e.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2+h^2=4^2=16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />
</p><p>- ColtsFan10
</p>
