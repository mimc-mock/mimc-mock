<p>Two different cubes of the same size are to be painted, with the color of each face being chosen independently and at random to be either black or white. What is the probability that after they are painted, the cubes can be rotated to be identical in appearance?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791b0631cfa9f1903e296000ff2f6fd6e54f9984.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{9}{64} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{289}{2048} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\  \frac{73}{512} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{147}{1024} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{589}{4096}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="476" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Define two ways of painting to be in the same <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" /> if one can be rotated to form the other.
</p><p>We can count the number of ways of painting for each specific <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" />.
</p><p>Case 1: Black-white color distribution is 0-6 (out of 6 total faces)
</p><p>Trivially <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/312d643c239f9fd6054701a8cba664779329cf72.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" /> way to paint the cubes.
</p><p>Case 2: Black-white color distribution is 1-5
</p><p>Trivially all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc8f52bf67c1dbbf535064a174258e004290cea.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{6}{5} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="70" height="53" /> ways belong to the same <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/4/e8406a2a50c1aa1040c8862b912a5f3d703d9569.png" class="latex" alt="$6^2$" width="15" height="15" /> ways to paint the cubes.
</p><p>Case 3: Black-white color distribution is 2-4
</p><p>There are two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/0/7404027149aa420073f35adcb6f27e38f55e4e80.png" class="latex" alt="$classes$" width="56" height="12" /> for this case: the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" /> where the two red faces are touching and the other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" /> where the two red faces are on opposite faces. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> members of the latter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" /> since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> unordered pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> opposite faces of a cube. Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/9/ca9881be30eb90a2fdd3f5c28fb7c543312c010b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{6}{4} - 3 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="110" height="53" /> members of the former <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/0/97065b47ac6db102d941b516e56eb3e722d35099.png" class="latex" alt="$12^2 + 3^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="16" /> ways to paint the cubes for this case.
</p><p>Case 4: Black-white color distribution is 3-3
</p><p>By simple intuition, there are also two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/0/7404027149aa420073f35adcb6f27e38f55e4e80.png" class="latex" alt="$classes$" width="56" height="12" /> for this case, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f82af2613f097a42f0014aa3494fcefcd8299d0.png" class="latex" alt="$class$" width="39" height="12" /> where the three red faces meet at a single vertex, and the other class where the three red faces are in a "straight line" along the edges of the cube. Note that since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> vertices in a cube, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> members of the former class and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/4/a840835070c7d75a4fa7a6b94da1a552fed8e5d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{6}{3} - 8 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="110" height="53" /> members of the latter class. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/9069c03678392a4e2d75303ba7a41c6992fdbc21.png" class="latex" alt="$12^2 + 8^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="16" /> ways to paint the cubes for this case.
</p><p>Note that by symmetry (since we are only switching the colors), the number of ways to paint the cubes for black-white color distributions 4-2, 5-1, and 6-0 is 2-4, 1-5, and 0-6 (respectively).
</p><p>Thus, our total answer is<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/4090b7116c16c14b3f455aea87e8b6520c02044a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2(6^2 + 1^2 + 12^2 + 3^2) + 12^2 + 8^2}{2^{12}} = \frac{588}{4096} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } \frac{147}{1024}}.\]" width="433" height="48" />
</p><p><br />
-fidgetboss_4000
</p>
