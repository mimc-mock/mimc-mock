<p>Jason rolls three fair standard six-sided dice. Then he looks at the rolls and chooses a subset of the dice (possibly empty, possibly all three dice) to reroll. After rerolling, he wins if and only if the sum of the numbers face up on the three dice is exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17ce6fcb2994d4241d780dd5311d828db133411.png" class="latex" alt="$7.$" width="12" height="12" /> Jason always plays to optimize his chances of winning. What is the probability that he chooses to reroll exactly two of the dice?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5dc37bb5d62ef04a58026290ab37458c501169.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{7}{36} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \frac{5}{24} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{2}{9} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \frac{17}{72} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="399" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Consider the probability that rolling two dice gives a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35b9e2eca6880f9f0a10564b4f7212d97f4f0afd.png" class="latex" alt="$s \leq 7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/1/441d61e9bcac5055a73c499eb0b40ac62b35fe73.png" class="latex" alt="$s - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="38" height="12" /> pairs that satisfy this, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/7/e57227dcbbf26d607581e95f1830113f835b3d72.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, s - 1), (2, s - 2), \ldots, (s - 1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="264" height="20" />, out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/2/242bade7eecb8dbe7d1ef62e8008cfb581dbe77d.png" class="latex" alt="$6^2 = 36$" width="58" height="15" /> possible pairs. The probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/d/e1d07bd2ccdc6e536ea5978d7b0245751d02a9fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{s - 1}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" />.
</p><p>Therefore, if one die has a value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and Jason rerolls the other two dice, then the probability of winning is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4bef8135dd0f1a48a5bb04cf6f4df82b455382ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7 - a - 1}{36} = \frac{6 - a}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="143" height="37" />.
</p><p>In order to maximize the probability of winning, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be minimized. This means that if Jason rerolls two dice, he must choose the two dice with the maximum values.
</p><p>Thus, we can let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/e/b4eb173fd518ca131dac2a900d0ed8d158865e78.png" class="latex" alt="$a \leq b \leq c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" /> be the values of the three dice. Consider the case when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76af50b849ab1500cc86346d174be7967b635ab5.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b &lt; 7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/4/9243aa2a65c4dcbfac8566b25a76c13190b9c096.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" />, then we do not need to reroll any dice. Otherwise,
if we reroll one die, we can reroll any one dice in the hope that we get the value that makes the sum of the three dice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. This happens with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/b/a8b7dd31c63b399cd72fe186a628adba5df3cad3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. If we reroll two dice, we will roll the two maximum dice, and the probability of winning is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/1/221e6f88c38d70f90b38160c7b18d889fad55fa4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6 - a}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" />, as stated above. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/2/992f6a4d00e2fe099283b4471fd7bf89afab9147.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac16 &gt; \frac{6 - a}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />, so rolling one die is always better than rolling two dice if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76af50b849ab1500cc86346d174be7967b635ab5.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b &lt; 7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />.
</p><p>Now consider the case where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/5/475c46e303b42aad8746df78592d0caeff29b439.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b \geq 7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" />. Rerolling one die will not help us win since the sum of the three dice will always be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. If we reroll two dice, the probability of winning is, once again, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/1/221e6f88c38d70f90b38160c7b18d889fad55fa4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6 - a}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" />. To find the probability of winning if we reroll all three dice, we can let each dice have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dot and find the number of ways to distribute the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> dots. By stars and bars, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/5/e859212f0add0877214a474b8a02b7495ba90bb9.png" class="latex" alt="${6\choose2} = 15$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" /> ways to do this, making the probability of winning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/a/aca265317852383ae8eadbe51ded4c014548a72f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{6^3} = \frac5{72}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="37" />.
</p><p>In order for rolling two dice to be more favorable than rolling three dice, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a25457eaad9a7fa19d936f00ad1b534346eaafed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6 - a}{36} &gt; \frac5{72} \rightarrow a \leq 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="162" height="37" />.
</p><p>Thus, rerolling two dice is optimal if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbe81cdd8946bad083474fd212df7052dba3ed3c.png" class="latex" alt="$a \leq 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/5/475c46e303b42aad8746df78592d0caeff29b439.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b \geq 7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" />. The possible triplets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/0/d80414f2f94f70731e91b6e12c4e6934c8fa65de.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b, c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> that satisfy these conditions, and the number of ways they can be permuted, are
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/8/0a8974722659f8b633440a7a048c4a92ff6c5d89.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 4, 4) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/7/e3728c57d9504c89fbe34432fc5d3e36def650af.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 4, 5) \rightarrow 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/f/f8f20e0e0352307738a699a6634f43c0e7229587.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 4, 6) \rightarrow 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab66c17f2e5bb0baf011d312877e5749a16954f.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 5, 5) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/8/c7862c89e1b9ce833d0a32d12d77e18e3513dc8c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 5, 6) \rightarrow 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/9/159965f71577ba987d3aac9221067d07cd258fcd.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 6, 6) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/b/0cbc6edafea59449c21c121e7f5c71a7c4a57dbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 5, 5) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/b/d3b7f0f94f1c4ff36e9734ae8f83b21677e58481.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 5, 6) \rightarrow 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/b/99bd01e609e395a041fc6c0efea941c839df546c.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 6, 6) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/0/8009d441ee32e544a3e6d2a53d1c3e3139c86b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 6, 6) \rightarrow 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" /> ways.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/818b7fbca9c462f2e69f054dc792aec03e7d7311.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 6 + 6 + 3 + 6 + 3 + 3 + 6 + 3 + 3 = 42$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="332" height="14" /> ways in which rerolling two dice is optimal, out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/a/94a096b91beea88234ffd24fc45279b43a87480a.png" class="latex" alt="$6^3 = 216$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="15" /> possibilities, Therefore, the probability that Jason will reroll two dice is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/0/730e8d575bd0a39e7c965b6dd13f9a59f99ab1cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{42}{216} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } \frac{7}{36}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="126" height="49" />.
</p>
