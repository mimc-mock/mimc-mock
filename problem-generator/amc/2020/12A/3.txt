<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since the driver travels <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> miles per hour and each hour she uses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> gallons of gasoline, she spends <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/7624dcadc484bda9f22dbd28147054ace34802b5.png" class="latex" alt="$$4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="18" height="14" /> per hour on gas. If she gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d8fd61137f32d81435ba367a81cd8c4a9d550e.png" class="latex" alt="$$0.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> per mile, then she gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/4/f3493ed3e65fc639c760d4739aa3fb72ee444fff.png" class="latex" alt="$$30$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="29" height="16" /> per hour of driving. Subtracting the gas cost, her net rate of money earned per hour is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ffe7b389669d4b913e179995e5e340d7acca75e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="28" />.
