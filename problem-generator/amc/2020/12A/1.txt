<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If Carlos took <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282fdef75f579c92565cd7e0734d83ccfd38bfdc.png" class="latex" alt="$70\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the pie, there must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1ed0f8aec0440ba9a832086dbfd52a2532614751.png" class="latex" alt="$(100 - 70)\% = 30\%$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="156" height="20" /> left. After Maria takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d00a60c7b2aa767c32a146a64527d8e860dded.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%, \ 1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="130" height="38" /> of the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> is left.
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2ca433b1e9d5bb62cb0b4538ee383341c491aed.png" class="latex" alt="$30\% \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 20\%}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="169" height="38" />
</p><p>~Awesome2.1 (Solution)

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Like solution 1, it is clear that there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the pie remaining. Since Maria takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the remainder, she takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/5/4d55da94ff835d97bb3b679ce4eae4bc52362d19.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3} \cdot 30\% = 10\%,$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="124" height="38" /> meaning that there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e404f6d4726da82cfb3b1ae7ad428d9b0ce67d23.png" class="latex" alt="$30\% - 10\% = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 20\%}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="194" height="31" /> left.
