<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The slope of the line is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. We must transform it by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> creates an isosceles right triangle, since the sum of the angles of the triangle must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> and one angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. This means the last leg angle must also be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p>In the isosceles right triangle, the two legs are congruent. We can therefore construct an isosceles right triangle with a line of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> slope on graph paper. That line with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> slope starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and will go to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/a/16a91f66d5f9be31b8dd447d12e092e63a9f741d.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,3)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="42" height="20" />, the vector <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edfea67b3530c8d8d535cabdd195ab42de10c034.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;5, 3 &gt;$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="17" />.
</p><p>Construct another line from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/d/68d212afba3ebe50fb3f396f6495e35cb6d13988.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,-5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, the vector <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/9/e69ccc898a41c622534476ce3775a173ef6f6fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;3,-5&gt;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" />. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/29501a90018e83d6d3e88b1f83bdfb71e692561e.png" class="latex" alt="$\perp$" width="13" height="12" /> and equal to the original line segment. The difference between the two vectors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/0/d20ecc5fdad1319eb3f9d58477bc8e890ba6bcf1.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;2,8&gt;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />, which is the slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and that is the slope of line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Furthermore, the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/6/bd68a48f71ae138181657b25043ae2b0eb180c02.png" class="latex" alt="$3x-5y+40=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="133" height="16" /> passes straight through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edda7e6080349147509eb85e15e22a13cca6f801.png" class="latex" alt="$(20,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70afc2ca9c889e8a10b5bc266206393272e764cb.png" class="latex" alt="$3(20)-5(20)+40=60-100+40=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="310" height="18" />, which means that any rotations about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edda7e6080349147509eb85e15e22a13cca6f801.png" class="latex" alt="$(20,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> would contain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edda7e6080349147509eb85e15e22a13cca6f801.png" class="latex" alt="$(20,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />. We can create a line of slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edda7e6080349147509eb85e15e22a13cca6f801.png" class="latex" alt="$(20,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercept is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/4/c640964665dab714b5db8d3d40d9315492c12292.png" class="latex" alt="$20-\frac{20}{4} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15.}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="156" height="38" />~lopkiloinm
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
