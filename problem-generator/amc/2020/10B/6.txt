<p>Driving along a highway, Megan noticed that her odometer showed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe03798054ce5c2d1aef8db8237a5bdbb4b063ae.png" class="latex" alt="$15951$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" /> (miles). This number is a palindrome-it reads the same forward and backward. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hours later, the odometer displayed the next higher palindrome. What was her average speed, in miles per hour, during this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />-hour period?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/0/2504de9a10e83a09cd5d5852042f3a61b6c1c32c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 50 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 55 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 60 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 65 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 70$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>In order to get the smallest palindrome greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe03798054ce5c2d1aef8db8237a5bdbb4b063ae.png" class="latex" alt="$15951$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />, we need to raise the middle digit. If we were to raise any of the digits after the middle, we would be forced to also raise a digit before the middle to keep it a palindrome, making it unnecessarily larger.
</p><p>So we raise <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> to the next largest value, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, but obviously, that's not how place value works, so we're in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/53130630759e032f794984432ae7baad714fbc9f.png" class="latex" alt="$16000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />s now. To keep this a palindrome, our number is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/f/1ff7b4b72784a06b0f0864bf2053e783cd4c7272.png" class="latex" alt="$16061$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />. 
</p><p>So Megan drove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/a/c3aec0e6087346f76e40332116c31ad9875224a9.png" class="latex" alt="$16061-15951=110$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="164" height="13" /> miles. Since this happened over <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hours, she drove at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/e/c4eeee546b069bb869376d1a08463637c5a0427b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{110}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }55}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="120" height="37" /> mph. ~quacker88
</p>
