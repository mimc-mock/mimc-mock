<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> be a right rectangular prism (box) with edges lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f885f96d2fb7e65f800de4ff71d7dcd0a5d2db5.png" class="latex" alt="$3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, together with its interior. For real <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/3/623bd6fa8205b33b9857e6001f091f949d457db1.png" class="latex" alt="$r\geq0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc701e00ae222f04544bdfd81edfacf3fefc76d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> be the set of points in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-dimensional space that lie within a distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> of some point in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. The volume of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc701e00ae222f04544bdfd81edfacf3fefc76d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/6397311ef9509cd7a9456ef506b2d0617c17b190.png" class="latex" alt="$ar^{3} + br^{2} + cr +d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="142" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/8/7c8acfd7d5ee559262593701b8dbd02e43ad96e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d6265e67657b5886ce257671d45ff9c0282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421dbe5ac249bea2c9d25145a7eb9b73644c5c61.png" class="latex" alt="$c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are positive real numbers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/c/5bcac6f22b6255846dc8d5218abb1b5faa70970b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{bc}{ad}?$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="31" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/a/63afde35a5177bd286f438c233d6af9eace065f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 6 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 19 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 24 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 26 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 38$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Split <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc701e00ae222f04544bdfd81edfacf3fefc76d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> into 4 regions: 
</p><p>1. The rectangular prism itself
</p><p>2. The extensions of the faces of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />
</p><p>3. The quarter cylinders at each edge of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />
</p><p>4. The one-eighth spheres at each corner of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />
</p><p><br />
Region 1: The volume of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/b/bcb25bf4f368d94e9ec2d91cf923ea1eb1cc5f5b.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="95" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/1/8a1547b17e452b32d2ae0e4ef898d680a1872583.png" class="latex" alt="$d=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />.
</p><p>Region 2: This volume is equal to the surface area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> times <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> (these "extensions" are just more boxes). The volume is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/7517829d081d3a92b37e7b89a139ad99bf2d908f.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{SA} \cdot r=2(1 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot 4)r=38r$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="288" height="18" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/9/29901e1aecc69fa74b71e3f9b4a033566fe93d70.png" class="latex" alt="$c=38$" width="49" height="12" />.
</p><p>Region 3: We see that there are 12 quarter-cylinders, 4 of each type. We have 4 quarter-cylinders of height 1, 4 quarter-cylinders of height 3, 4 quarter-cylinders of height 4. Since 4 quarter-cylinders make a full cylinder, the total volume is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33fa32ce495a163e8e713053c265b083a4ec37f7.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot \dfrac{1\pi r^2}{4} + 4 \cdot \dfrac{3\pi r^2}{4} + 4 \cdot \dfrac{4 \pi r^2}{4}=8 \pi r^2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="291" height="39" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8e525b07f9592ca4e6b91f921becc62b4e368a7.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8\pi$" width="51" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Region 4: There is an eighth-sphere of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> at each corner of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. Since there are 8 corners, these eighth-spheres add up to 1 full sphere of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. The volume of this sphere is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/c/5dc89724cab56f72f92b8588881eb90bdf3ee3a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{3}\pi \cdot r^3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/4/394580226bacc0e5ef27b1b49a6063901b20eb86.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{4\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="56" height="37" />.
</p><p>Using these values, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e47a9589a0e266667ab576fc5f3e327c97fec97f.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{bc}{ad}=\frac{(8\pi)(38)}{(4\pi/3)(12)} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }19}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="225" height="43" />.
</p><p>To see a diagram of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc701e00ae222f04544bdfd81edfacf3fefc76d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" />, view TheBeautyofMath's explanation video (Video Solution 1). 
</p><p><br />
~DrJoyo
