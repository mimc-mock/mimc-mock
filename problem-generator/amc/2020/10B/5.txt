<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let's first find how many possibilities there would be if they were all distinguishable, then divide out the ones we overcounted.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce11f9bca5e74ba532323e045b62e2793d120b7e.png" class="latex" alt="$7!$" width="12" height="13" /> ways to order <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> objects. However, since there's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/2/3920c5e3f6b2e5749cd75c3853b63ae714d00561.png" class="latex" alt="$3!=6$" width="47" height="13" /> ways to switch the yellow tiles around without changing anything (since they're indistinguishable) and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/3/46377b53287d0b7f76190da9e05acbd0fdedd334.png" class="latex" alt="$2!=2$" width="47" height="13" /> ways to order the green tiles, we have to divide out these possibilities: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/d/8fd4e87be76d2bb89f666262d59e474f6a77e7da.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7!}{3! \cdot2}=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }420}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="138" height="38" />. 
</p><p>~quacker88
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to arrange the yellow tiles which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7f6c79f2148f0cec592c9517b40ee1b82ef22e.png" class="latex" alt="$35$" width="17" height="12" />.
Then from the remaining tiles there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/9/5c9b68a04fadb4cbe90954c537de424c1519ef51.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{4}{2}=6$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="70" height="53" /> ways to arrange the green tiles.
And now from the remaining two tiles and two slots we can see there are two ways to arrange the purple and brown tiles,
giving us an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/b/c8b540c814c67a468bf08a6ba8f2b34e6394f36f.png" class="latex" alt="$35\cdot6\cdot2=420$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="14" />.
</p><p>~noahdavid (Edited by starshooter11)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can choose a different frame to solve this problem. Our tile combination can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/2/bc2c72015d290b051926efbdcacc787f917e6ca9.png" class="latex" alt="$Y, Y, Y, G, G, B, P.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" /> We can focus on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/9/c69d85278ac6299c9ac487c6a10026a6a3546dd6.png" class="latex" alt="$Y, Y, Y, G, G$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" /> first, which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/9/4390223acc5001312b7775e3f7b64511943810a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{5}{3}=10.$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="83" height="53" /> Now we can insert our brown tile into this which only has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> choices(like <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/d/aadd869424eec757bd71259bbe9e0ae07e7b11d2.png" class="latex" alt="$Y,B, Y, Y, G, G$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/8/828f86a8cdf037faa960e0d733b7f47c2eb0029d.png" class="latex" alt="$Y, Y, Y, G, G, B$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" /> etc.), then insert purple tile which only has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> choices(like <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e401adc3bd3e9ae26522c3193c658ff779a8ba75.png" class="latex" alt="$B,Y, P, Y, Y, G, G$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/4/744cd080c529d6e4ac8c9f045cd1e268810efee5.png" class="latex" alt="$B, Y, Y, Y, G, G, P$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" /> etc.).
