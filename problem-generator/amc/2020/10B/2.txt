<p>Carl has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cubes each having side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and Kate has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cubes each having side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the total volume of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> cubes?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63f923fed936e6e182d8d50299c1bcb3f39b0a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 24 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 25 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 28 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 40 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 45$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>A cube with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e908de3ae99d512f26bb0f453718039e9c9742a.png" class="latex" alt="$1^3=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> of these will have a total volume of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/1/7c1cc2fd4891a1cb5ac2103de0f4e9a2d9a61594.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot1=5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" />.
</p><p>A cube with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/57421b8557f6aa4384653806bfc7a5b80faf557a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3=8$" width="49" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> of these will have a total volume of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/9/b09299df32221470e9d8218a401b196305cb99dd.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot8=40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/a/31a063c087288f4f67edf9b3bd7b182b561449fe.png" class="latex" alt="$5+40=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="137" height="28" /> ~quacker88
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The total volume of Carl's cubes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. This is because to find the volume of a cube or a rectangular prism, you have to multiply the height by the length by the width. So in this question, it would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/8/c58deba0f41c4da22eb2c1016638fd874231c042.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 1 \times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="12" />. This is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Since Carl has 5 cubes, you will have to multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, to account for all the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cubes.
</p><p>Next, to find the total volume of Kate's cubes you have to do the same thing. Except, this time, the height, the width, and the length, are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so it will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/2/9d24580cae56fa90234ef7ff31a26d085b41d07a.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="112" height="15" /> Now you have to multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> to account for all the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> blocks. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. So the total volume of Kate's cubes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>Lastly, to find the total of Carl's and Kate's cubes, you must add the total volume of these people. This is going to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/9/5f9e082522d6caa278548c4b51b860ae74d490ee.png" class="latex" alt="$5+40=\boxed{\textbf{(E)} ~45}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="138" height="30" />.
</p><p>- BrightPorcupine
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
