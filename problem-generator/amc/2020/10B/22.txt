<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/3/c03c96b69609359d1b5087202ae9c2957f934288.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2^{50}$" width="56" height="15" />. We are now looking for the remainder of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/e/15e05d6191b3eabb319eae8aacaef92989781404.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4x^4+202}{2x^2+2x+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="102" height="40" />.
</p><p>We could proceed with polynomial division, but the numerator looks awfully similar to the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Sophie_Germain_Identity" title="Sophie Germain Identity">Sophie Germain Identity</a>,  which states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b100b5bcacb4bcafb2d261cb92d9f8d145d89ebc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^4+4b^4=(a^2+2b^2+2ab)(a^2+2b^2-2ab)\]" width="339" height="19" />
</p><p>Let's use the identity, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb06a95428e057b1ddcda318a7768aaa73641527.png" class="latex" alt="$b=x$" width="42" height="12" />, so we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/8/6d81826c91cc16610cc5b8b43a5d25dba7ac0ffe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1+4x^4=(1+2x^2+2x)(1+2x^2-2x)\]" width="307" height="19" />
</p><p>Rearranging, we can see that this is exactly what we need:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/e/c0e010372051a195c274880c199e616600c4a8ad.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{4x^4+1}{2x^2+2x+1}=2x^2-2x+1\]" width="225" height="40" />
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/0/be06c275b6cbeb278bb73d756532e63391bdc2af.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{4x^4+202}{2x^2+2x+1} = \frac{4x^4+1}{2x^2+2x+1} +\frac{201}{2x^2+2x+1}\]" width="354" height="40" />
