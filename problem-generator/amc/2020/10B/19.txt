<p>In a certain card game, a player is dealt a hand of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> cards from a deck of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1d1611143f5792dc0f61f3b130af88cb790c04.png" class="latex" alt="$52$" width="17" height="12" /> distinct cards. The number of distinct (unordered) hands that can be dealt to the player can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/8/148796eba947fa39cc403b3aff6a46755fa8cdec.png" class="latex" alt="$158A00A4AA0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="117" height="13" />. What is the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/8/b485774600f8dd1b249a7c79932060d7c570c412.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 3 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 4 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 6 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/2/3a2d19da6b9f090cf972ed67b7ffd57a09090d1b.png" class="latex" alt="$158A00A4AA0 \equiv 1+5+8+A+0+0+A+4+A+A+0 \equiv 4A \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="600" height="18" />
</p><p>We're looking for the amount of ways we can get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> cards from a deck of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1d1611143f5792dc0f61f3b130af88cb790c04.png" class="latex" alt="$52$" width="17" height="12" />, which is represented by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80d2cb6ceb9ccc79c0540dba669820418a2b12f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{52}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="42" height="53" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081bd51421e71090217140fa49e528cf31026f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{52}{10}=\frac{52\cdot51\cdot50\cdot49\cdot48\cdot47\cdot46\cdot45\cdot44\cdot43}{10\cdot9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="372" height="53" />
</p><p>We need to get rid of the multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, which will subsequently get rid of the multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> (if we didn't, the zeroes would mess with the equation since you can't divide by 0)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/a/92aa47bf8813a90fc2ef6d9544f7b2cf279830da.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot5=45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27f4d628f8dc7a1332e001084fc6ce7839367fd.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot6=48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/270733f733a5db1c15e23dca8bf4c19e08f269e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{51}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> leaves us with 17.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1fada50307be38a99b6e7c086a851f172217be0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{52\cdot\cancel{51}^{17}\cdot50\cdot49\cdot\cancel{48}\cdot47\cdot46\cdot\cancel{45}\cdot44\cdot43}{10\cdot\cancel{9}\cdot\cancel{8}\cdot7\cdot\cancel{6}\cdot\cancel{5}\cdot4\cdot\cancel{3}\cdot2\cdot1}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="316" height="42" />
</p><p>Converting these into<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/a/d3ab9092decd2590f8cca3122a89c5f6ea289888.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" />, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/9/ac9c79475e9b19be67dae4a47af95f3faf3980a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{52}{10}\equiv \frac{(-2)\cdot(-1)\cdot(-4)\cdot4\cdot2\cdot1\cdot(-1)\cdot(-2)}{1\cdot(-2)\cdot4\cdot2\cdot1} \equiv (-1)\cdot(-4)\cdot(-1)\cdot(-2) \equiv 8 \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="697" height="53" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484fd5c945e3d30c998c40b17653a10403161a1.png" class="latex" alt="$4A\equiv8\pmod{9} \implies A=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="272" height="28" /> ~quacker88
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75fdfc3eb516a4a83c8b05428a92642a2992241.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{52}{10}=\frac{52\cdot51\cdot50\cdot49\cdot48\cdot47\cdot46\cdot45\cdot44\cdot43}{10\cdot9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1}=26\cdot17\cdot5\cdot7\cdot47\cdot46\cdot11\cdot43$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="617" height="53" />
</p><p>Since this number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> but not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> digits must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> but the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> digits cannot be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. This narrows the options down to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Also, the number cannot be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Adding up the digits, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/0/850a4b69ed0b38013a2f439f49e3f24610fadf94.png" class="latex" alt="$18+4A$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="14" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af04466ac38b7b0d0f451b3280c38f861ea13822.png" class="latex" alt="$A=6$" width="46" height="13" />, then the expression equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. This would mean that the entire number would be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, which is not what we want. Therefore, the only option is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a83327d2dffcf40a2a6c6815d397a28a208480a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />-PCChess
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>It is not hard to check that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> divides the number,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54363b08f3587c3cb2e21f7e3d64bdbfafa24ef.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\binom{52}{10}=\frac{52\cdot51\cdot50\cdot49\cdot48\cdot47\cdot46\cdot45\cdot44\cdot43}{10\cdot9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1}=26\cdot17\cdot5\cdot7\cdot47\cdot46\cdot11\cdot43.\]" width="560" height="53" /> As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/2/0a2b3cf936e198f3bbe109c0d2850e6b40d8d41e.png" class="latex" alt="$10^3\equiv-1\pmod{13}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="19" />, using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/343cfbde0b7a8df0524887a862ea4854c6cfda73.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{13}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/e/55e0a138b90ef3288e9c6ee4ff890105c8c4d13d.png" class="latex" alt="$13|\overline{AA0}-\overline{0A4}+\overline{8A0}-\overline{15}=110A+781$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="321" height="20" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/9066675caac3aa1cae89880a30bbf2212f2caae1.png" class="latex" alt="$6A+1\equiv0\pmod{13}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="18" />, implying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/49484a3aa563adeeb82f4ed628b375ea19b0222a.png" class="latex" alt="$A\equiv2\pmod{13}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a83327d2dffcf40a2a6c6815d397a28a208480a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/5/8b5d72d1cc7f823de9c657979bd8570941acccf3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{- Emathmaster}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="125" height="12" />
