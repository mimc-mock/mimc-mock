<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/7/8f74ad016db02c1a73a29041ffc26b28f8acb946.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(n+57,63)=21$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="163" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/a/93a56b784ba3895a2f912524e6c4dc93257c9b66.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(n-57, 120)= 60$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="172" height="20" /> by the Euclidean Algorithm. Hence, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/e/8aec321b600ef9ce2d155e456c57739b00c90954.png" class="latex" alt="$n+57=21\alpha$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="107" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/1/4d1d6efe082eeaaf9beba96537cf4f845fd76e34.png" class="latex" alt="$n-57=60 \gamma$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="17" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f9703ae61047629e344ed4c68745807235cbd558.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(\alpha,3)=1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="105" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/c/efcacac47902a84479e3c90599ba69be814d0d5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(\gamma,2)=1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="104" height="20" />. Subtracting the two equations, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/5/dd5b0a6c93322a78e1bcfae123051c357545ff55.png" class="latex" alt="$38=7\alpha-20\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="113" height="16" />. Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35b82a607f5dcab8ef19f738eef1e789f43b82b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma = 2s+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="15" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/e/81e8a6bca655f94f92543dde4f903f935198062b.png" class="latex" alt="$58=7\alpha-40s$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="111" height="13" />. Taking modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/f/a7f0aaebd9ff6b0a0c801ae1dda7775e9b0a9aa7.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha \equiv{14} \pmod{40}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" />. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/598ae8ca527db5df74f224ae5b36504284c4cfd3.png" class="latex" alt="$n=21\alpha -57 &gt;1000$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="168" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030b58bbe27fa5112bb361f75dc340e8ff046451.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha \geq 51$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" />. Notice that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/6/a169f157816c4032ecf1370db00b9fec90045935.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha =54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" /> then the condition <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f9703ae61047629e344ed4c68745807235cbd558.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(\alpha,3)=1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="105" height="20" /> is violated. The next possible value of  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e10f8e8a3ba252bea002b5fdf28dcb968999d413.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha = 94$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" /> satisfies the given condition, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/e/2be557827517d524ed36aa675ef95593d6f803ae.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1917$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />.
</p><p>Alternatively, we could have said <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62eb72f5d4a1fcdd44d5bd24ef78cd197887a904.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha = 40k+14 \equiv{0} \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="208" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/1055a8d6efe19b728e32f382584703c97fc44603.png" class="latex" alt="$k \equiv{1} \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" /> only, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59184ff818771cdbad3e8baf026678663715311.png" class="latex" alt="$k \equiv{0,2} \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />, giving us our answer. Since the problem asks for the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/e/a8eb94fa54d4a43c2293274bdc73e1f167de6879.png" class="latex" alt="$1+9+1+7=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="192" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Prabh1512

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The conditions of the problem reduce to the following: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/128b1a2512e24a983da4a66f6ccbc2d4622f1a06.png" class="latex" alt="$n+120 = 21k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/3/55344ebd960dc6618bc65481b2169aa0907dec7e.png" class="latex" alt="$n+63 = 60\ell$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="103" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca51496e13e9d6b59811c7f369cf9857d05488a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(k,3) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac041c2f13412df7c3fd29867f5fc5425313ab04.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(\ell,2) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="101" height="20" />. From these equations, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/b/09bcf5876cd7a72f4b95c1470dc63de1b6fb5c64.png" class="latex" alt="$21k - 60\ell = 57$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="120" height="16" />. Solving this Diophantine equation gives us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/2/8b2103108194a07d6a25631ec021263afaf2c110.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 20a + 17$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/e/cae0661e9ce90952c9128785cc96b0f8a51d047e.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell = 7a + 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="84" height="16" />. Since, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/5/d75ab56486993ac835cf57bc4aade730eab03d85.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, we can do some bounding and get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/2/0c254dbd17fc2429344f06b99a285d457eebd41c.png" class="latex" alt="$k &gt; 53$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/812b9bdaa2d2cdc04c9ec79e0ebe7f1b97f721e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell &gt; 17$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" />. Now we start bashing by plugging in numbers that satisfy these conditions: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> is the first number that works so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/e/2fe5cb7e1a388606bd6e0e37b91b6eeda78dad11.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell = 33$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/a/e8a4fd6133473b6b1010df08f959c3daa08bab80.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 97$" width="52" height="12" />. Therefore, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/1/70108b696695008c43b1b2958fcfc6cc6c5bd30f.png" class="latex" alt="$n=21(97)-120=60(33)-63=1917$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="310" height="18" />, and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/e/a8eb94fa54d4a43c2293274bdc73e1f167de6879.png" class="latex" alt="$1+9+1+7=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="192" height="28" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We first find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/5/4e5ede8ff17b12389fa69329f95c8ddda757262b.png" class="latex" alt="$n\equiv6\pmod{21}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="127" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/8/328114b2066bf6f281c236eea2db5e15fe5bfb0f.png" class="latex" alt="$n\equiv57\pmod{60}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="136" height="20" />, then we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/e/3be083e10f441bbafdfec0e511e40a792d42c5c4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=21x+6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/157e06d235baf2fe5e7453eee19a675cf51ecc8b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=60y+57$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="16" /> by definitions, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are integers. Also, the unit digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, since the unit digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/1/c31d57efbafac28f1a2822b54a14bb7f883ddf3d.png" class="latex" alt="$60y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="30" height="17" /> is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and the unit digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. Therefore, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must end in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> having a unit digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6">Solution 6</span></h2>
<p>We are able to set up the following system of congruences: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/2/182428a98365978b0cc7edec8f429477f0ff36ae.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} n &amp;\equiv 6 \pmod {21}, \\ n &amp;\equiv 57 \pmod {60}. \end{align*}" width="147" height="43" /> 
Therefore, by definition, we are able to set-up the following system of equations: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38dd659c2e9e8de590ef3a8af1db24b69a96a6b3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} n &amp;= 21a + 6, \\ n &amp;= 60b + 57. \end{align*}" width="104" height="38" /> 
Thus, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a3642d37592be3fa165347024185adad7f250f.png" class="latex" alt="$21a + 6 = 60b + 57,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="157" height="18" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/f/0df90814659ba36475d073bdd481a4a9a715004b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7a + 2 = 20b + 19.\]" width="143" height="14" />
We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f3bdfd4da37f8bda44ba2c39100d34b3cc3769b.png" class="latex" alt="$7a \equiv 0 \pmod {7},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" /> and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/8/e68c8fc901aaffc342ee4ecdd6ef12023c15b3ae.png" class="latex" alt="$7a = 20b + 17,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="113" height="16" /> therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/2/d720ae696f4f7b0170c35f845c1766eadba0a13a.png" class="latex" alt="$20b + 17 \equiv 0 \pmod{7}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="175" height="18" /> Simplifying this congruence further, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/d/eedfb3cc6db33d958162d8a48b6cbe282b2c8f70.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b\equiv 3 \pmod{7}.\]" width="126" height="18" />
Thus, by definition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/5/7e5e45fc3c23f7c75ec33eecbf8a367709fff8e0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 7x + 3.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="14" /> Substituting this back into our original equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/225f2284036df1260c529afc203ce674b5e586e4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n = 60(7x + 3) + 57 = 420x + 237.\]" width="272" height="18" />
By definition, we are able to set up the following congruence:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/a/51a84576cb05d0521d03ffe2ec7d04c156152dcc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n \equiv 237 \pmod{420}.\]" width="165" height="18" />
Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/f/35f28494d91d2e3d4d464338179c1cb45e10dbf2.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1917$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, so our answer is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/e/a8eb94fa54d4a43c2293274bdc73e1f167de6879.png" class="latex" alt="$1+9+1+7=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="192" height="28" />.
</p><p><u><b>Remark</b></u>

</p><p>Remember that  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/973e526e7b8701879e058c682dc8396442bb50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$b \equiv 3 \pmod{7}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="120" height="20" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_7">Solution 7</span></h2>
<p>First, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. We know that it is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, so we first input <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/1/1712c68d8307d6b4c34a16a8cab9eea64f2c8094.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. To get to the nearest number divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25d31faf472ddb4c698a14522fe8125c06f8dd8.png" class="latex" alt="$21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, we have to add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> to cancel out the remainder.
