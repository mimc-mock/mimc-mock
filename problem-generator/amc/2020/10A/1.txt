<p>What value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> satisfies
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bfe02ab9c82120a6496887cf9a11c3438b9be67.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x- \frac{3}{4} = \frac{5}{12} - \frac{1}{3}?\]" width="135" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/7/0177a0cf82431c3f5df2fa167496c183bccbfead.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ {-}\frac{2}{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{7}{36}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{7}{12}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{2}{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{5}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="403" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> to both sides, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/6/6468ce1e02aa688f9cde93e198ca07895a8c64fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x= \frac{5}{12} - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{3}{4} = \frac{5}{12} - \frac{4}{12} + \frac{9}{12}=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }\frac{5}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="345" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Multiplying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> on both sides gets us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/6877717d809d8b211f08f09ca04f79809d48fd4d.png" class="latex" alt="$12x-9=1 \Rightarrow 12x=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="192" height="13" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/4942815aa9496bd273d3d068546ef404941a2091.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{x=\textbf{(E)}~\frac{5}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="92" height="48" />.
</p>
