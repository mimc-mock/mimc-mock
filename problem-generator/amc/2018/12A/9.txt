<p>Which of the following describes the largest subset of values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> within the closed interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/e/ece27ba559bd93cdf2416aa0f88fc8d7d85e5791.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,\pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" /> for which
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/b/b9b2c0b1c3b4bd2cd0535f1bcd6552a064d603ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin(x+y)\leq \sin(x)+\sin(y)\]" width="216" height="18" />for every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />, inclusive?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/8/01880704ce76279becf4da3cfbee26e326d89573.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\textbf{(A) } y=0 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 0\leq y\leq \frac{\pi}{4} \qquad \textbf{(C) } 0\leq y\leq \frac{\pi}{2} \qquad \textbf{(D) } 0\leq y\leq \frac{3\pi}{4} \qquad \textbf{(E) } 0\leq y\leq \pi\]" width="682" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>On the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a151f5b93ce5735f72cdd79c46051f52b9d820d.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" /> sine is nonnegative; thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/3/1e32d38fb72f609bfde37807498393750ab12299.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x + y) = \sin x \cos y + \sin y \cos x \le \sin x + \sin y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="18" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/7/447a91e1512333fc639da02e03bf52a259f38101.png" class="latex" alt="$x, y \in [0, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="86" height="18" /> and equality only occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8133b5800b18e2848c082f5d3f7c2c9a6b75cc59.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x = \cos y = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="15" />, which is cosine's maximum value. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0ef915acbcedcac7bbc707b87b4e7f8f9d6569.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 0\le y\le \pi}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="123" height="28" />. (CantonMathGuy)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Expanding, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a916ee8c05e199ecfa5fb02e05d7868542a433.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos y \sin x + \cos x \sin y \le \sin x + \sin y\]" width="290" height="15" /> Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f5735fa897775c9de24396a5e7708b2a41ae92d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin x =a \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/3/8b37f8d2e03f225e1ea031be774f9bd6fefb0246.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin y = b \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="16" />. We have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/9/81993ddeff536aa854fdcff82c41fd83230cb359.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\cos y)a+(\cos x)b \le a+b\]" width="205" height="18" /> Comparing coefficients of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gives a clear solution: both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c942a316ee4efd35e46023dcf3256ef90e9814db.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="36" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11f986c49f29d7a2ed4c28cab7319fa2ee3ee978.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos x$" width="37" height="8" /> are less than or equal to one, so the coefficients of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> on the left are less than on the right. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/d/29d5886e00dedd073f57500e340a0eec42783ed9.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="16" />, that means that this equality is always satisfied over this interval, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0ef915acbcedcac7bbc707b87b4e7f8f9d6569.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 0\le y\le \pi}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="123" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>If we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />, we can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/184911dcfe7d6e007429d3197c2daaef8c115f1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x+\pi) \le \sin(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="18" />. Note that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081bf0417da2140657aad909a14ca6c3ff8b77d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" /> is always nonnegative, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/a/1da2564b9cbb4ce6887ccb9d48d942f84c4da5f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x+\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> is always nonpositive. So, the inequality holds true when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/2916ed0f56f21d142a58d28c6aab6e5d27160a4d.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\pi$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. The only interval that contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> in the answer choices is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0ef915acbcedcac7bbc707b87b4e7f8f9d6569.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 0\le y\le \pi}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="123" height="28" />.
</p>
