<p>How many nonnegative integers can be written in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12e09c391eb94ff988e6dcfc490ddb6c72e79342.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_7\cdot3^7+a_6\cdot3^6+a_5\cdot3^5+a_4\cdot3^4+a_3\cdot3^3+a_2\cdot3^2+a_1\cdot3^1+a_0\cdot3^0,\]" width="478" height="18" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/c/f3c5e8424e376d02b3e116ed42e8c526821771ea.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i\in \{-1,0,1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/5/d55b4a57fcff346516e9bbec7f578395ac5fa341.png" class="latex" alt="$0\le i \le 7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/8/1782e14685bd389b46aaf81a667d765119877f32.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 512 \qquad  \textbf{(B) } 729 \qquad  \textbf{(C) } 1094 \qquad  \textbf{(D) } 3281 \qquad  \textbf{(E) } 59,048$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="488" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>This looks like balanced ternary, in which all the integers with absolute values less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/9/429dd9fafbfe90517395bcf24da8c701b421d206.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3^n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> are represented in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> digits. There are 8 digits. Plugging in 8 into the formula for the balanced ternary gives a maximum bound of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/c/5ac5ab68188daef0841e72f757a4a564f5281a06.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|=3280.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />, which means there are 3280 positive integers, 0, and 3280 negative integers. Since we want all nonnegative integers, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/c/29c5394714396c550db44bbd57b18180906c56bc.png" class="latex" alt="$3280+1=\boxed{3281}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="140" height="23" /> integers or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cfb0d12967a18f3020a605b42d458edc87eea8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that all numbers formed from this sum are either positive, negative or zero. The number of positive numbers formed by this sum is equal to the number of negative numbers formed by this sum, because of symmetry. There is only one way to achieve a sum of zero, if all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/1/e21bbcc8b1a1db90a76c7cda79b37d049509a5a8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />. The total number of ways to pick <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/412787c048e28774dc63fc27db42dc52ca858de7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/3/183dca59bc4e62bde64657608c38729dfe473b25.png" class="latex" alt="$i=0, 1, 2, 3, ... 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adc91b1d25a0aa8298fb168f12b1a150c7dfcdd.png" class="latex" alt="$3^8=6561$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0724261cc90561b3e42949be902019459e9e8ff0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6561-1}{2}=3280$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="131" height="37" /> gives the number of possible negative integers. The question asks for the number of non-negative integers, so subtracting from the total gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35f2a2454e4686de3f2f4369d3304dce77938f4.png" class="latex" alt="$6561-3280=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 3281}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="203" height="28" />. (RegularHexagon, KLBBC minor changes)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that the number of total possibilities (ignoring the conditions set by the problem) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adc91b1d25a0aa8298fb168f12b1a150c7dfcdd.png" class="latex" alt="$3^8=6561$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" />. So, E is clearly unrealistic. 
</p><p>Note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/2/012aa2573a77f1bfe01a31fdebe941ee94b10808.png" class="latex" alt="$a_7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is 1, then it's impossible for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12e09c391eb94ff988e6dcfc490ddb6c72e79342.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_7\cdot3^7+a_6\cdot3^6+a_5\cdot3^5+a_4\cdot3^4+a_3\cdot3^3+a_2\cdot3^2+a_1\cdot3^1+a_0\cdot3^0,\]" width="478" height="18" /> to be negative. Therefore, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/2/012aa2573a77f1bfe01a31fdebe941ee94b10808.png" class="latex" alt="$a_7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is 1, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/7/187205554f8300782a03d878a07c61ad13890cc3.png" class="latex" alt="$3^7=2187$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="15" /> possibilities. (We also must convince ourselves that these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/3/c83e3f84869c291f33c93bd87bc6df516503d67b.png" class="latex" alt="$2187$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> different sets of coefficients must necessarily yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/3/c83e3f84869c291f33c93bd87bc6df516503d67b.png" class="latex" alt="$2187$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> different integer results.)
</p><p>As A, B, and C are all less than 2187, the answer must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0bcfe6f20bc928c07859e857cb44a6d242ad1dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 3281}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="84" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Note that we can do some simple casework:
If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/1/861aada2fc8eb3dabf32c68c9304b278142f5f5e.png" class="latex" alt="$a_7=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />, then we can choose anything for the other 7 variables, so this give us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b07648d6e77e40902829c261a2e6d8e1a897e4bb.png" class="latex" alt="$3^7$" width="15" height="15" />.
If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e20d86cf427a30914414f2440dc73156914b6ea6.png" class="latex" alt="$a_7=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/0/1602323fa7d98399a2cf750d6f539e0956a1c59d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />, then we can choose anything for the other 6 variables, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d440ada8a3f3b7ea6f5beb66cb1834ededc2399.png" class="latex" alt="$3^6$" width="15" height="15" />.
If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e20d86cf427a30914414f2440dc73156914b6ea6.png" class="latex" alt="$a_7=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/d/c3df30155252acb6d7eceb86efb3fa3aa3026d00.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/7/0e77466c6d502ce2d990ddde7b6ae90ea57f5762.png" class="latex" alt="$a_5=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />, then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef89940c9d0d4b76ae12725c459ab738decd42d4.png" class="latex" alt="$3^5$" width="15" height="15" />.
Continuing in this vein, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7ca225a1ba06d85c90de1ba317fb7692d8e004e.png" class="latex" alt="$3^7+3^6+\cdots+3^1+3^0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="174" height="16" /> ways to choose the variables' values, except we have to add 1
because we haven't counted the case where all variables are 0. So our total sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0bcfe6f20bc928c07859e857cb44a6d242ad1dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 3281}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="84" height="28" />.
Note that we have counted all possibilities, because the largest positive positive power of 3 must be greater than or equal to the largest negative positive power of 3, for the number to be nonnegative.
</p>
