<p>Alice, Bob, and Charlie were on a hike and were wondering how far away the nearest town was. When Alice said, "We are at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> miles away," Bob replied, "We are at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> miles away." Charlie then remarked, "Actually the nearest town is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> miles away." It turned out that none of the three statements were true. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the distance in miles to the nearest town. Which of the following intervals is the set of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/1/7f1ac07bf6394983fd2b652c65343f073acbc7e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }   (0,4)   \qquad        \textbf{(B) }   (4,5)   \qquad    \textbf{(C) }   (4,6)   \qquad   \textbf{(D) }  (5,6)  \qquad  \textbf{(E) }   (5,\infty)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="517" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For each of the false statements, we identify its corresponding true statement. Note that:
</p>
<ol style="margin-left: 1.5em;">
  <li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/e/0fe6dc48b595a2cadc50a15f4d6c23701ae43c2e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{False}\cap\mathrm{True}=\varnothing.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="142" height="16" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e4d1151fb5b5583e177d288c8e032803a410eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{False}\cup\mathrm{True}=[0,\infty).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="175" height="20" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p></ol>
<p>We construct the following table:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/1645d4ff8df04a7bdc85c3c64aac7453f53711a4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{c||c|c} &amp; &amp;  \\ [-2.5ex] \textbf{Hiker} &amp; \textbf{False Statement} &amp; \textbf{True Statement} \\ [0.5ex] \hline &amp; &amp; \\ [-2ex] \textbf{Alice} &amp; [6,\infty) &amp; [0,6) \\  &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex] \textbf{Bob} &amp; [0,5] &amp; (5,\infty)  \\ &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex] \textbf{Charlie} &amp; [0,4] &amp; (4,\infty) \end{array}\]" width="378" height="99" />
Taking the intersection of the true statements, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37e8ff50fd0dbecfbb52f8742566d5fd1241f893.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[0,6)\cap(5,\infty)\cap(4,\infty)=(5,6)\cap(4,\infty)=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } (5,6)}.\]" width="425" height="30" />
~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Think of the distances as if they are on a number line. Alice claims that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e0cbca851f5bc206fcf12525b35af8ae98c53d2.png" class="latex" alt="$d &gt; 6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, Bob says <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/3/b43ad1bb1ad72a0e1c7166b276d28421a8d3ef24.png" class="latex" alt="$d &lt; 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, while Charlie thinks <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/c/17c0e2600d06800097672d53f7d816c438cd248d.png" class="latex" alt="$d &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. This means that all possible numbers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> are included. However, since the three statements are actually false, the distance to the nearest town is one of the numbers not covered. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/7/3978c6790fea080c150457b476ff05655f3cd7ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } (5,6)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="88" height="29" />.
</p>
