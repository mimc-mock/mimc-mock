<p>A unit of blood expires after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/c/a6c2900d2ca3ac63e345991f8fa2aef50e1b5c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$10!=10\cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdots 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="145" height="13" /> seconds. Yasin donates a unit of blood at noon of January 1. On what day does his unit of blood expire?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/6/846754d150e9dfb2adb0bc06e9a232326affb9c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }\text{January 2}\qquad\textbf{(B) }\text{January 12}\qquad\textbf{(C) }\text{January 22}\qquad\textbf{(D) }\text{February 11}\qquad\textbf{(E) }\text{February 12}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="715" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The problem says there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/b/4fba0acd922af92590cc757b46246bcf0272d9f2.png" class="latex" alt="$10! = 10\cdot 9\cdot 8\cdot 7\cdot 6\cdot 5\cdot 4\cdot 3\cdot 2\cdot 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="264" height="13" /> seconds.
Convert <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c824ea490ff073932a1c17d8ce0b9a4215f90d95.png" class="latex" alt="$10!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" /> seconds to minutes by dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e43f15d8ff5b6a16dc17068f13581ddb9a1843c0.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot 8\cdot 7\cdot 5\cdot 4\cdot 3\cdot 2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="142" height="13" /> minutes.
Convert minutes to hours by dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> again: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bde90a3ef514ffa8e96fb993a6fb0b3ec3eb616.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot 8\cdot 7\cdot 2$" width="75" height="12" /> hours.
Convert hours to days by dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/c/92c4b83064ab64508eabc7ef9047457f5bf321ad.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 7\cdot 2 = 42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="95" height="13" /> days.
</p><p>Now we need to count <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> days after January 1. Since we start on Jan. 1, then we can't count that as a day itself. When we reach Jan. 31(The end of the month), we only have counted 30 days. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/3/b7399cec178b6d3eb1c7b29a67dfdf0645e5b3e7.png" class="latex" alt="$42 - 30 = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />. Count <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> more days, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/8/778a807cdf09507e6233226a670806bdfdc56fed.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{\textbf{(E) }\text{February 12}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="133" height="28" />.
