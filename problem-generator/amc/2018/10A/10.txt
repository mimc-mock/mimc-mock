<p>Suppose that real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/2/60260471de484d4c7c3af264a757d4ff5de2ad3d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{49-x^2}-\sqrt{25-x^2}=3\]" width="200" height="18" />What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5ab28cdf200347891a719c96614ec4cc5a7c3f1a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2}+\sqrt{25-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="169" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/8/238a79e0d099c672d9c284d8344a3a6bbb53e60b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }8\qquad \textbf{(B) }\sqrt{33}+8\qquad \textbf{(C) }9\qquad \textbf{(D) }2\sqrt{10}+4\qquad \textbf{(E) }12\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="482" height="21" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>In order to eliminate the square roots, we multiply by the conjugate. Its value is the solution. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> terms cancel nicely. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/3/5a36a5f24ecad3728a199d873fe0210f807ac2c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt {49-x^2} + \sqrt {25-x^2})(\sqrt {49-x^2} - \sqrt {25-x^2}) = 49-x^2 - 25 +x^2 = 24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="571" height="21" />
</p><p>Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/a/2ea7bbbe4a6bea7b4aa3716b406fee9dff324bba.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt {49-x^2} - \sqrt {25-x^2}) = 3, (\sqrt {49-x^2} + \sqrt {25-x^2}) = \frac {24} {3} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="535" height="37" />. - cookiemonster2004
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/4/34402c21bc16133550df0c9a86cf817ef8e156fd.png" class="latex" alt="$u=\sqrt{49-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="18" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/6/5c667602be8a90e9f9b0213e54ec49f3705245bb.png" class="latex" alt="$v=\sqrt{25-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="107" height="18" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/1/721769a65d7e632257df6e64b9de8286cba97dab.png" class="latex" alt="$v=\sqrt{u^2-24}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="107" height="18" />. Substituting, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/c/1bcf79d8cfcefe7780d2dd8dc3af31fb6e73732d.png" class="latex" alt="$u-\sqrt{u^2-24}=3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="18" />. Rearranging, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/c/75c31b78db27b227fc0638615477ea41411c5a30.png" class="latex" alt="$u-3=\sqrt{u^2-24}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="18" />. Squaring both sides and solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f2e2576659411e0d5454a53d976739f30df7cfb.png" class="latex" alt="$u=\frac{11}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c94f212232d547a4db8c4665844a104a3610b4cb.png" class="latex" alt="$v=\frac{11}{2}-3=\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="37" />. Adding, we get that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/c/f6cc090e3d1b769c310885b1ec5ee58d52ce99d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Put the equations to one side. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c429bbe0be6def69aabbb5947e33e640f769b82.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2}-\sqrt{25-x^2}=3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="202" height="18" /> can be changed into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/673d26a0081323974c73e3c93d98597ca0225e98.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2}=\sqrt{25-x^2}+3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="202" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can square both sides, getting us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39fef0ea1bc4a46d7e66346384934f273be794a6.png" class="latex" alt="$49-x^2=(25-x^2)+(3^2)+ 2\cdot 3 \cdot \sqrt{25-x^2}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="21" />
</p><p>That simplifies out to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f58b5e75ff1055b7678104fcab60b38953819769.png" class="latex" alt="$15=6 \sqrt{25-x^2}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="128" height="18" /> Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />  gets us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/2/392f33dd5a6cbd6012badef237d0419990499e0e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}=\sqrt{25-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="111" height="37" />.
</p><p>Following that, we can square both sides again, resulting in the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/4/3b4ef38284a17d457d260a1e2f6a4247cbb9a70c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{4}=25-x^2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="103" height="38" />. Simplifying that, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36ff3e15eabd07eabd0d0bdf4238e372363e5560.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = \frac{75}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="62" height="38" />.
</p><p>Substituting into the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5ab28cdf200347891a719c96614ec4cc5a7c3f1a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2}+\sqrt{25-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="169" height="18" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/3/ad36d5e03626ecb5bf6a6f5fdce4aa05049d7438.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-\frac{75}{4}}+\sqrt{25-\frac{75}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="183" height="43" />. Immediately, we simplify into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/f/0bf761ec5742295c58a1a92e90006d8760273811.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\frac{121}{4}}+\sqrt{\frac{25}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="111" height="43" />. The two numbers inside the square roots are simplified to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/a/53a36e5a7787e442ab3272b7417996dd0c372b24.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{11}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, so you add them up: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/3/9b3fbfc66a7027faccc55936991b6208877f44c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{11}{2}+\frac{5}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="139" height="38" />.
</p><p>~kevinmathz

Isolating the term with the square root, and squaring again, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/5/3a569b06f4c9f4f5413e728ab5e72d2c1d782292.png" class="latex" alt="$4y^2-36y+81=4y^2-576 \implies y=\frac{73}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="320" height="38" />. <h3><span id="Solution_9_(Pythagorean_Theorem)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_9_.28Pythagorean_Theorem.29">Solution 9 (Pythagorean Theorem)</span></h3>
<p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/e/efe8546da80884ddcdddf2f3c9858aceed44f24b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2} = \sqrt{7^2-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="171" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/b/77b5809a4746be074e8f28437c98fd19bd7fbf15.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{25-x^2} = \sqrt{5^2-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="171" height="21" /> This is also the equation of finding a leg of a right triangle given the hypotenuse and the other leg using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>. 
</p><p>Now, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are the hypotenuses of the two triangles, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the one leg from each of the triangles. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d2938a180aecbec23d3a6846acb23812fb921768.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{7^2-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="21" /> is the other leg of the 1st one, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba788de4a54d3d2e5c66a24a330457558ca3aba.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{5^2-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="21" /> is the other leg of the 2nd one. 
</p><p>For convenience, we name the other leg of the 1st triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> (the one that's not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />), and the other leg of the 2nd one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> (the one that's not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />). Using the Pythagorean Theorem, we set up 2 equations.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/7/2271394f816754eaa4b53da0b29545810af139cc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a^2 + x^2 &amp;= 7^2 \\ b^2 + x^2 &amp;= 5^2 \end{align*}" width="96" height="44" />
</p><p>Subtracting the two equations and canceling out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/5/1854d1847f18ee89b0dde04061291cd37188d0a8.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - b^2 = 49-25$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="18" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340defde1f9b12f74ccd51fe4defc30d59c35e61.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a+b)=24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />.
</p><p>We already know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> (or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc2e9d02ea73a5d12550fb841f491ea288e872e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{49-x^2}-\sqrt{25-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="171" height="21" />) is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, so plugging it in, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84e7721789dc82fdbda86e5a3caf73c0c605c5f2.png" class="latex" alt="$3(a+b)=24$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="107" height="20" />, and dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/1/931954a49aac413d44b487bf7449843bed2520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="121" height="30" />
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
