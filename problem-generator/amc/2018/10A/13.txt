<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a68aa31b9e41e7d870e335dde8ac2ebc415639.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(4,0)--(4,3)--(0,0)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, (0,0), SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, (4,3), NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, (4,0), SE);  label(&quot;$D$&quot;, (2,1.5), NW); label(&quot;$E$&quot;, (3.125,0), S); draw ((2,1.5)--(3.125,0),linewidth(1));  draw(rightanglemark((0,0),(2,1.5),(3.125,0))); [/asy]" width="252" height="198" />
</p><p>First, we need to realize that the crease line is just the perpendicular bisector of side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, the hypotenuse of right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />. Call the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. Draw this line and call the intersection point with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />. Now, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/1/e5170b3a48d96da708932026f0e94194f415b0b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACB$" width="58" height="13" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/b/b0b3dbb5e0c16cabfa02f10ace626714ac385dbd.png" class="latex" alt="$AA$" width="27" height="13" /> similarity. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h4>
<p>In general, whenever we are asked to make a crease, think about that crease as a line of reflection over which the diagram is reflected. This is why the crease must be the perpendicular bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> must be reflected onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p>
