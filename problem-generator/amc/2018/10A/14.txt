<p>What is the greatest integer less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/9/7e9fe91da45cd2ff16c0da3c9b0d7ba0a4f1d510.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3^{100}+2^{100}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}?\]" width="91" height="40" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/0/fd0ddaa65c0cb8b45b5cb296a5621267c789cc18.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }80\qquad \textbf{(B) }81 \qquad \textbf{(C) }96 \qquad \textbf{(D) }97 \qquad \textbf{(E) }625\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="407" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We write
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1fa62dfccf5d1bae8067501d6c0b7d3bd8d6fb25.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3^{100}+2^{100}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}=\frac{3^{96}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}\cdot\frac{3^{100}}{3^{96}}+\frac{2^{96}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}\cdot\frac{2^{100}}{2^{96}}=\frac{3^{96}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}\cdot 81+\frac{2^{96}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}\cdot 16.\]" width="578" height="40" />
Hence we see that our number is a weighted average of 81 and 16, extremely heavily weighted toward 81. Hence the number is ever so slightly less than 81, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edd16ae3e459a5025430d223f7a0ed0803f85976.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }80}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let's set this value equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. We can write
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/2/0c28b87f333620e9b3ee344cfcf661cc90e51901.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3^{100}+2^{100}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}=x.\]" width="122" height="40" />
Multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/0/47035c5ad4780087b6537f0ad95588ef1358dc37.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{96}+2^{96}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="16" /> on both sides, we get 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba3fec961dea84ed97e532bcd451833a01c9cb1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3^{100}+2^{100}=x(3^{96}+2^{96}).\]" width="202" height="19" />
Now let's take a look at the answer choices. We notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/2259c263bde7b68aadfeca3df73046a41c953212.png" class="latex" alt="$3^4$" width="15" height="15" />. Plugging this into our equation above, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/8/a680f030db02393f3931ba39886de20bec31b181.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3^{100}+2^{100} \stackrel{?}{=} 3^4(3^{96}+2^{96}) \Rightarrow 3^{100}+2^{100} \stackrel{?}{=} 3^{100}+3^4\cdot 2^{96}.\]" width="447" height="22" />
The right side is larger than the left side because 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/7/1e72ea05972b498da810513bede5558a648c10b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2^{100} \leq 2^{96}\cdot 3^4.\]" width="110" height="17" />
This means that our original value, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, must be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. The only answer that is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea5fcc6d66685ee21f722efcd228eb44d38f262.png" class="latex" alt="$80$" width="17" height="12" /> so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6eb65962292d994ce2f9a8af63728883f256b18.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="24" />.
</p><p>~Nivek
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c63aac72241541d52a0b423e7797ee855ad6b81.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3^{100}+2^{100}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}=\frac{2^{96}\left(\frac{3^{100}}{2^{96}}\right)+2^{96}\left(2^{4}\right)}{2^{96}\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}+2^{96}(1)}=\frac{\frac{3^{100}}{2^{96}}+2^{4}}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}+1}=\frac{\frac{3^{100}}{2^{100}}\cdot2^{4}+2^{4}}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}+1}=\frac{2^{4}\left(\frac{3^{100}}{2^{100}}+1\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}+1}.\]" width="624" height="62" />
</p><p>We can ignore the 1's on the end because they won't really affect the fraction. So, the answer is very very very close but less than the new fraction.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b9393970ce24db1960ce0e92f43d8c69dc2d468.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2^{4}\left(\frac{3^{100}}{2^{100}}+1\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}+1}&lt;\frac{2^{4}\left(\frac{3^{100}}{2^{100}}\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}},\]" width="204" height="62" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/2/ff267bf66858851c0a180190129f066294973f66.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2^{4}\left(\frac{3^{100}}{2^{100}}\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{96}}=\frac{3^{4}}{2^{4}}*2^{4}=3^{4}=81.\]" width="236" height="62" />
</p><p>So, our final answer is very close but not quite 81, and therefore the greatest integer less than the number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/c/45cf67efbf7de4f6e5957bef3e06d30627304ed6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A) 80}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="57" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f960ef6bf2632763cc95ebe50ca74217ebf524fd.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3^{96}$" width="56" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/6/2169f868b2b15a95d644765440abedc45318595f.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2^{96}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" />. Then our fraction can be written as
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/0/3b05470024663edc9081ab375577a10e39b3a18e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{81x+16y}{x+y}=\frac{16x+16y}{x+y}+\frac{65x}{x+y}=16+\frac{65x}{x+y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="367" height="41" />.
Notice that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/1/6d1de27295ed5de0c60d6e5790daf7cb707abe3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{65x}{x+y}&lt;\frac{65x}{x}=65$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="144" height="41" />.
So ,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/e/1aeb331d8ffbfec47e29d3990968e0fad36320db.png" class="latex" alt="$16+\frac{65x}{x+y}&lt;16+65=81$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="210" height="41" />.
And our only answer choice less than 81 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/c/45cf67efbf7de4f6e5957bef3e06d30627304ed6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A) 80}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="57" height="29" /> (RegularHexagon)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea8bca78eab68efd4fff2fb5453f35df27d68bdc.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{3^{100}+2^{100}}{3^{96}+2^{96}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="118" height="40" />. Multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/a/9cae8f951a0f36c0332d6b3d1ddf4ed35b2911b8.png" class="latex" alt="$(3^{96}+2^{96})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="19" />, and expand. Rearranging the terms, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/0/850c68746e654f0790c0951a63f137b13b7ff193.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{96}(3^4-x)+2^{96}(2^4-x)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="228" height="19" />. The left side is decreasing, and it is negative when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/3/d8376f047f20943a48ec6209aba7870617d91da0.png" class="latex" alt="$x=81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />. This means that the answer must be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />; therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70432e471002b1c1f363f2d085b0f3dff8d930a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
