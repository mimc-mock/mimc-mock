<p>How many ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28e24c43456d95b5ab8d4f2f8f85a644e98b7ed3.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> of positive integers satisfy the equation 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18dfc63240b01569b535ba05a0d53841ad6cfe8b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a\cdot b + 63 = 20\cdot \text{lcm}(a, b) + 12\cdot\text{gcd}(a,b),\]" width="313" height="18" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/f/dff0965f3110a4b6dc0b69e8aa5cabecaab653c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{gcd}(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> denotes the greatest common divisor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/c/7bc7ea17e24bf9f5800940d8858ee3af0ecf4582.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="64" height="18" /> denotes their least common multiple?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce516a86a77759c0696a641f14335cc08da1be22.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \text{ 0} \qquad \textbf{(B)} \text{ 2} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \text{ 4} \qquad \textbf{(D)} \text{ 6} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \text{ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="345" height="17" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/428f1394d22004ad9f2270f64eec9706bce991b4.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \text{lcm}(a, b)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="102" height="20" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/6/e66706a694e1ee0b8ace35b68bc9c3465aaef721.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \text{gcd}(a, b)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="100" height="20" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0d7192fd5faf1265e1e0bc0b7496a738b9b3766.png" class="latex" alt="$a\cdot b = lcm(a, b)\cdot gcd(a, b) = x\cdot y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="260" height="18" />. Thus, the equation becomes
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fabf3251ab45c9a46352eae222bfb87de8a20b11.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x\cdot y + 63 = 20x + 12y\]" width="175" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/b/82bc19a6185adaf76a5daddc460ab02f14038347.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x\cdot y - 20x - 12y + 63 = 0\]" width="206" height="16" />
</p><p>Using <a href="/wiki/index.php/Simon%27s_Favorite_Factoring_Trick" title="Simon&#39;s Favorite Factoring Trick">Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick</a>, we rewrite this equation as
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/5/f15f9030f3a91e97f5177a080c2c67d39bb18996.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x - 12)(y - 20) - 240 + 63 = 0\]" width="250" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/a/5fa4468ebc4559a0d0b039d0184912c1b30586cc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x - 12)(y - 20) = 177\]" width="179" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0ca2a9ea48473aa074ead204a709d33561cc08f.png" class="latex" alt="$177 = 3\cdot 59$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/7/ab7da572d60053bdb3ab92f2dda44f87cd9dc78d.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/6/3063ff89e3f5da0bcda72985181ff609623e0ca7.png" class="latex" alt="$x  - 12 = 59$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="92" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/6/d162d2a824d4fc37562fda575edd114acdfb1836.png" class="latex" alt="$y - 20 = 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/0/310d23ba8472cdfbe1d1a964112655f27e65ec31.png" class="latex" alt="$x - 12 = 177$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="102" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/d/a5da9c4d81bb35045944f9f044f6df3cf279fd28.png" class="latex" alt="$y - 20 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" />. This gives us the solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/e/5be416b454efce5d2c8ae197236cd20b061d0997.png" class="latex" alt="$(71, 23)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/1/471767c687786e3621c3b846c4d437103cf083a4.png" class="latex" alt="$(189, 21)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />. Since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/fee0bac9892398b786946d4829d082e3c3c70041.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{GCD}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="12" /> must be a divisor of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/c/40c90c80a99ee3736d931647e05b88d1f3ec6321.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{LCM}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="12" />, the first pair does not work. Assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/082852571ecc48ed136ba13dcffa8f6e40064bbd.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. We must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/b/a2b993de9e1eb92a149caf15d40137901728a59a.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 21 \cdot 9$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/e/02ee40a14f381cd4b402bfc2e0bc77e3ba6667ec.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />, and we could then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/0/710e3e4f18d7c0270ab719a00adc61fa12363b1e.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/d/8cdb0dfdd4fb60dccd2ff46af2eac89a17db6fe1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~2}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="56" height="30" /> solutions.
(awesomeag)
</p><p>Edited by IronicNinja, Firebolt360, and mprincess0229~
