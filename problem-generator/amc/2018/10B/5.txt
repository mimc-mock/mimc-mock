<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_-_Complementary_Counting">Solution 1 - Complementary Counting</span></h2>
<p>We use complementary counting, or 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c7bd6550e6179156059d67a831b78df0c09de69.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{total - what we don&#39;t want = what we want}$" width="319" height="12" />. 
</p><p>There are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/1635abf3f228d02f20315b199c17f21dc0a49ccd.png" class="latex" alt="$2^8$" width="15" height="15" /> ways to create subsets (consider including or excluding each number) and there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420813c47b9ba0edd71bba84716bd485967ac81a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^4$" width="15" height="15" /> subsets only containing composite numbers (the composite numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/c/64c8cbc02382a70d36b3afcca0acbe8abeeb77c1.png" class="latex" alt="$4, 6, 8, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />). Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/2/532ece88be0faa550b8d60da1c3b8035a82aea3f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^8-2^4=240$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="15" /> total ways to have at least one prime in a subset.
