<p>In rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/0/9102816304aa6a66edc87c364f93db592e92f303.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ=8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/b/d7b58c1b930c57fc398fe86f899c78866d1059e3.png" class="latex" alt="$QR=6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />. Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/e/0deaae00f6af4ac1f5882568e7b6667bb334a2ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PQ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="19" />, points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/e/1ce7f54c5de33dbbc4547cb4227a50c6fe35ec09.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{QR}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="19" />, points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843d282b682b661d2298f2a32e322e364ccac625.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{RS}$" width="26" height="15" />, and points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b6e8f5072caa455b141c9533a77d0df4766cf3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{SP}$" width="26" height="15" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/7/6e7952be2fe0c55e997de0ee701da034ff22cab4.png" class="latex" alt="$AP=BQ&lt;4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="114" height="16" /> and the convex octagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/055dcba798b1424c44c2606d913e0f2edab1c5ad.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDEFGH$" width="118" height="13" /> is equilateral. The length of a side of this octagon can be expressed in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b99311fa3cf2b4dba4e5d024ffa981e7e047421.png" class="latex" alt="$k+m\sqrt{n}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is not divisible by the square of any prime. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/51672e6f4ad019fc6b1eeab51fda992ea9830996.png" class="latex" alt="$k+m+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4ac1b5df75534a289decfc2506a0b4c4a1d83119.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 1 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 7 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 21 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 92 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 106$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/241644308eb659591364d4bccb1033d94355f16d.png" class="latex" alt="$AP=BQ=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="115" height="16" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/a/b4a0719d75c29e25bc96b38b326cdcba9cf26333.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=8-2x$" width="103" height="13" />.
</p><p>Now notice that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/0/4302b27408745f4f8b046f6dd0e8bf64e9134bbf.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=8-2x$" width="104" height="12" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/d/00d555cc52bb66509dbe38856a0154f41ed184bd.png" class="latex" alt="$QC=DR=x-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="147" height="16" />.
</p><p>Thus by the Pythagorean Theorem we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/4/6e4672b56ecfd23c40a7115053656285c8059832.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+(x-1)^2=(8-2x)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="19" /> which becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286a4fd4b9998f963665012e368547d3f2091d84.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2-30x+63=0\implies x=\dfrac{15-3\sqrt{11}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="320" height="41" />.
</p><p>Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/f/f3f5c5560bbf8c9b60d6b13dc458fd15b1767538.png" class="latex" alt="$8-(15-3\sqrt{11})=3\sqrt{11}-7\implies \boxed{\text{(B)}~7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="327" height="28" />. (Mudkipswims42)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Denote the length of the equilateral octagon as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b18928339c213c3e404a0605cf86e27a33c8d970.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BQ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="19" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8ed4fa8e80da6d31b8f2e85dcd55d72d7699206.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8-x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="37" />. By the Pythagorean Theorem, we find that:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c36482f39ca3d042c3e67d1483de9f67be142f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{8-x}{2}\right)^2+\overline{CQ}^2=x^2\implies \overline{CQ}=\sqrt{x^2-\left(\frac{8-x}{2}\right)^2}\]" width="415" height="53" /> 
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/6/5d64e615d09333d2331448dd217c271536daffe9.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CQ}=\overline{DR}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="19" />, we can say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e1942f3f7ad5bafcecf8f457fa2f71dc972a84ee.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2\sqrt{x^2-\left(\frac{8-x}{2}\right)^2}=6\implies x=-7\pm3\sqrt{11}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="384" height="53" />. We can discard the negative solution, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/0/a703bf9fe16c47695333e75ec8a88a2cb84d0f06.png" class="latex" alt="$k+m+n=-7+3+11=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="280" height="28" /> ~ blitzkrieg21
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let the octagon's side length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b18ae9cd990d805f67ae38c4a85e2f84bb82853a.png" class="latex" alt="$PH = \frac{6 - x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="99" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/e/b7e1dd0ac79a42aab6513fccf2660447631e66c0.png" class="latex" alt="$PA = \frac{8 - x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />. By the Pythagorean theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d2947af340a709a6da29dc2c9851354dc1c2c9a0.png" class="latex" alt="$PH^2 + PA^2 = HA^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="156" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/3/fe3da9ecfc69cf64b76963675a59d6464351cccb.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{6 - x}{2} \right)^2 + \left(\frac{8 - x}{2} \right)^2 = x^2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="221" height="46" />. By expanding the left side and  combining the like terms, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/2/122b4f49da725399ff886f841604fc5e77c9d1e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x^2}{2} - 7x + 25 = x^2 \implies -\frac{x^2}{2} - 7x + 25 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="346" height="39" />. Solving this using the quadratic formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38d1ef524cabc85a98aaa10f8cf52138b7793228.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="40" />, we use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/03052bbbe6b519d59a4f15ee7e366973b8e4ca33.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/8/f18b293a98a70d0397614c05897e3756c8f6e438.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -7$" width="55" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/f/3cfbb8e1be1db5ee818bfee30c98c5d678b37836.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 25$" width="49" height="12" />, to get one positive solution, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/6/af6c4e239eb00c170fb8d37d4eb34484888326d8.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-7+3\sqrt{11}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/0/a703bf9fe16c47695333e75ec8a88a2cb84d0f06.png" class="latex" alt="$k+m+n=-7+3+11=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="280" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, or the side of the octagon, be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef80fb994adaaac7c667c5d46ade00cfd509011e.png" class="latex" alt="$BQ = \left(\frac{8-x}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="127" height="45" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/b/fcb132a6678a92fa798c6b418cc049378c13d2db.png" class="latex" alt="$CQ = \left(\frac{6-x}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="127" height="45" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/1250d284723d11b8f5326ee531455390d096f688.png" class="latex" alt="$BQ^2+CQ^2=x^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="20" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/e/98e8b787357bd963b599e6ee8bdf889c7762f515.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{8-x}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{6-x}{2}\right)^2 = x^2$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="225" height="48" />. Multiplying this out, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd4d189958d49e794bb7094b90010e66e57ef89e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = \frac{64-16x+x^2+36-12x+x^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="288" height="41" />. Simplifying, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/e/36ebdfc0c571e9330af1d6a060855004dd702e1a.png" class="latex" alt="$-2x^2-28x+100=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="177" height="18" />. Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/7/447a6a9a0cdbf065ee25305f7446375d621ec8d5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+14x-50=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="144" height="18" />. Therefore, using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_formula" title="Quadratic formula">quadratic formula</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/5/d8542ac57a7cb181a26c68359bc68d2d0d6c6d11.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-7 \pm 3\sqrt{11}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="124" height="21" />. Since lengths are always positive, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/4/bf49e06258d95e58fdf2f278dde1f816d7a03b3d.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-7+3\sqrt{11} \Rightarrow k+m+n=-7+3+11=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="432" height="30" />
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
