<p>How many of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/4/1a46b5b3929665fcb2500f3583ab77297a85afd4.png" class="latex" alt="$2018$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> numbers in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/b/8fb25d6eef2d4fc00877a6a8884d454d13c7cd15.png" class="latex" alt="$101, 1001, 10001, 100001, \dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="215" height="16" /> are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/652eb946279986558772ea0074ff13bbb74ddf48.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }253 \qquad \textbf{(B) }504 \qquad \textbf{(C) }505 \qquad \textbf{(D) }506  \qquad \textbf{(E) }1009 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="452" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/d/ddd926faf63f71e9d7dbd6079f6e492962ccaedf.png" class="latex" alt="$10^n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="14" /> is divisible by 101 if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/77144265110e54c0f1e3407870bc282de8e7c767.png" class="latex" alt="$10^n\equiv -1\pmod{101}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />. We note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c59a8ba6b146604531dcb3ec617128907d18b946.png" class="latex" alt="$(10,10^2,10^3,10^4)\equiv (10,-1,-10,1)\pmod{101}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="367" height="19" />, so the powers of 10 are 4-periodic mod 101.
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/77144265110e54c0f1e3407870bc282de8e7c767.png" class="latex" alt="$10^n\equiv -1\pmod{101}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47aeaad4133d3c4aafd2f4d68763fe8a2ad7357c.png" class="latex" alt="$n\equiv 2\pmod 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" />.
</p><p>In the given list, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e782ba117fb42ebab0ea5956feaff9ef7f46d568.png" class="latex" alt="$10^2+1,10^3+1,10^4+1,\dots,10^{2019}+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="303" height="18" />, the desired exponents are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/b/80b4976af7d899fe60b8165fd2004f383dc1f5e5.png" class="latex" alt="$2,6,10,\dots,2018$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="128" height="16" />, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b2176525189d6c7517eccf12b141d7fb47ba8e97.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2020}{4}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 505}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="138" height="38" /> numbers in that list.
<br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/0/c40f25368cb3c7565f708c96bcc103d4aac19139.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2k}+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="17" /> for some odd <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> will suffice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/d/96d8e2bf5b27d6d7f14bb46fc25f8b16c0f4461d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mod {101}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" />. Each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/e/9de0061941a7dd13f43162112c1efe0a5eb1e762.png" class="latex" alt="$2k \in \{2,6,10,\dots,2018\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="18" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/aba6195f3774dca14f257e5004c1e3bc867645f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 505}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>If we divide each number by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, we see a pattern occuring in every 4 numbers. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/b/20bd331e0bc0751f9818678b9fcbe403bbe738ea.png" class="latex" alt="$101, 1000001, 10000000001, \dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="235" height="16" />. We divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/4/1a46b5b3929665fcb2500f3583ab77297a85afd4.png" class="latex" alt="$2018$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f385769245cc3c249ec34e15661c168bfb48e5.png" class="latex" alt="$504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> left over. Looking at our pattern of four numbers from above, the first number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. This means that the first of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> left over will be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/aba6195f3774dca14f257e5004c1e3bc867645f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 505}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96ee0197ce1c570ba61aaced28fe9a71a177a420.png" class="latex" alt="$909$" width="26" height="12" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" /> is too. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> is divisible and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/8/888c508062c09735bab7ff0e11e7dc80cc7580db.png" class="latex" alt="$1001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> isn't so let us start from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/8/de84c0b376e89ec3e59a710eff23de3350bec3c9.png" class="latex" alt="$10001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />. We subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" /> to get 2. Likewise from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/5/a85e53769d0b4bf3274332b13d8be6e162becaea.png" class="latex" alt="$100001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="13" /> we subtract, but we instead subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" /> times <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f07fcdd5d8909370b52ef42f62d445d4407225d4.png" class="latex" alt="$99990$" width="44" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. We do it again and multiply the 9's by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. Following the same knowledge, we can use mod <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to finish the problem. The sequence will just be subtracting 1, multiplying by 10, then adding 1. Thus the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/8/3e8222afa043edcb1d90d008053bf6a9a84418d1.png" class="latex" alt="${0,-9,-99 ( 2),11, 0, ...}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="169" height="18" />. Thus it repeats every four. Consider the sequence after the 1st term and we have 2017 numbers. Divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> by four to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f385769245cc3c249ec34e15661c168bfb48e5.png" class="latex" alt="$504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> remainder <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f385769245cc3c249ec34e15661c168bfb48e5.png" class="latex" alt="$504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> plus the 1st term or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/aba6195f3774dca14f257e5004c1e3bc867645f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 505}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/6/3a6a8852c0794767efdc8a632cfb1ec34f575e66.png" class="latex" alt="$101=x^2+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/1/ad1992cebc3e8007e8e75f7ff346cdcda129ab98.png" class="latex" alt="$100...0001=x^n+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="156" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/1/ec186fd1809357d1f530bee2334ceb59d405b4f6.png" class="latex" alt="$x=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. We have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/1/93181923b1f26e690a3002ff07ec934bd350c936.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x^n+1}{x^2+1}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="57" height="40" /> must have a remainder of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. By the remainder theorem, the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/5/8056cc6de12dd137809e48395b29e481fe3e38ff.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="16" /> must also be roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8debda55cb36e43070d67f9efd56e1f7084cc0ab.png" class="latex" alt="$x^n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" />. Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/b/37bb61969fb86463257abc437f453fd09465b3d3.png" class="latex" alt="$i,-i$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="17" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8debda55cb36e43070d67f9efd56e1f7084cc0ab.png" class="latex" alt="$x^n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> yields that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00e8d4fe55601eded7285fc4db13c8f1a3900c0e.png" class="latex" alt="$n\equiv2\mod{4}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="108" height="14" />. Because the sequence starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/0/6a0820b09be57820d447dda06efadacdbe3e8c18.png" class="latex" alt="$10^2+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="56" height="16" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/1/8f196d53ecf214b8e5b1f2abcd5945d80f26082f.png" class="latex" alt="$\lceil 2018/4 \rceil=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 505}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="170" height="30" />
</p>
