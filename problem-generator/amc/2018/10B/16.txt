</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3ad07ba45d9a4dcd46fbd971320abb2585f7ce0e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="349" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that, for any integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/5151abe96580b1b373f2f2d3d4c6adb9af656ace.png" class="latex" alt="$a\equiv a^3 \pmod{6}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="125" height="21" />. Therefore,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/5/b75377e890f4cbd8c84cf7e94582b435fb966f32.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018} \equiv a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3 \pmod{6}.\]" width="427" height="20" />
</p><p>Therefore the answer is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63cc0fc8a8a912362ce33d924bf5a810a1a9a46e.png" class="latex" alt="$2018^{2018}\equiv 2^{2018} \pmod{6} = \boxed{ (E)4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="270" height="29" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/0/da0e8eaf92090fade8099e83d28191ca138d750a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^n \pmod{6}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="92" height="20" /> alternates with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> increases.
</p><p>~Dolphindesigner
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/a/93abdc8a9572ad74989fe01323c6a2ddd9bf01be.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}\right)^3=a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3+3a_1^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_1\right)+3a_2^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_2\right)+\cdots+3a_{2018}^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_{2018}\right)+6\sum_{i\neq j\neq k}^{2018} a_ia_ja_k$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="1049" height="78" />
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197340650e3c2c9bc6755fdc2373be762ccef242.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3+3a_1^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_1\right)+3a_2^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_2\right)+\cdots+3a_{2018}^2\left(a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{2018}-a_{2018}\right)+6\sum_{i\neq j\neq k}^{2018} a_ia_ja_k\equiv a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3+3a_1^2({2018}^{2018}-a_1)+3a_2^2({2018}^{2018}-a_2)+\cdots+3a_{2018}^2({2018}^{2018}-a_{2018}) \equiv -2(a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3)\pmod 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="1049" height="97" />
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/4/9a4699aa0a2c27c1825b922fbf1bb09cffb82158.png" class="latex" alt="$-2(a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3)\equiv \left(2018^{2018}\right)^3\equiv\left( 2^{2018}\right)^3\equiv 4^3\equiv 4\pmod{6}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="537" height="25" />.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/8/79868c7c333745def943e99e5418e69888dd1ab1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1^3+a_2^3+\cdots+a_{2018}^3\equiv 1\pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="263" height="19" />. However, since cubing preserves parity, and the sum of the individual terms is even, the some of the cubes is also even, and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b4198920681ecb231a4fd1c42717a4272ee37de4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(E) }4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="50" height="28" />
</p>
