<p>The faces of each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> standard dice are labeled with the integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> be the probabilities that when all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> dice are rolled, the sum of the numbers on the top faces is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What other sum occurs with the same probability as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/d/0ade5d80c9703a12e0a4b63c0216ded4cc263767.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \text{ 13} \qquad \textbf{(B)} \text{ 26} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \text{ 32} \qquad \textbf{(D)} \text{ 39} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \text{ 42}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="386" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>It can be seen that the probability of rolling the smallest number possible is the same as the probability of rolling the largest number possible, the probability of rolling the second smallest number possible is the same as the probability of rolling the second largest number possible, and so on. This is because the number of ways to add a certain number of ones to an assortment of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> ones is the same as the number of ways to take away a certain number of ones from an assortment of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />s.
</p><p>So, we can match up the values to find the sum with the same probability as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. We can start by noticing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is the smallest possible roll and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is the largest possible roll. The pairs with the same probability are as follows:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40e7a9427b93fcc8f03df01c70490668cc80de66.png" class="latex" alt="$(7, 42), (8, 41), (9, 40), (10, 39), (11, 38)...$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="311" height="18" />
</p><p>However, we need to find the number that matches up with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. So, we can stop at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33d161420cd8df4cff596dd188e1f1e8a5d2361f.png" class="latex" alt="$(10, 39)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> and deduce that the sum with equal probability as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/5/e058d401a1b942f106f081ff1fcca8a8b1d42a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$39$" width="17" height="12" />. So, the correct answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/270eb4fe4a255d40ebfa6fb4e7c022f44b5f656f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{39}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="57" height="28" />, and we are done.
</p><p>Written By: Archimedes15
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Add-on by ike.chen: to see how the number of ways to roll <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/5/e058d401a1b942f106f081ff1fcca8a8b1d42a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$39$" width="17" height="12" /> are the same, consider this argument:
</p><p>Each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> dice needs to have a nonnegative value; it follows that the number of ways to roll <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/3/b9312d23241da92eee9864bfb10576effec3a373.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom {10-1}{7-1}=84$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="120" height="53" /> by stars and bars. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0ea7c9bccd0fe3c94d33f6a2c4ccbb93a5b51707.png" class="latex" alt="$10-7=3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />, so there's no chance that any dice has a value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f09822df35793025120c76e53e6704ae3a87510d.png" class="latex" alt="$&gt;6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" />.
</p><p>Now imagine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> piles with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> blocks each. The number of ways to take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> blocks away (making the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/7/267ebb7a0db5f55fc493ac4d075b7e52a37a9a02.png" class="latex" alt="$7\cdot 6-3=39$" width="104" height="12" />) is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/f/8bfbcee9caecbd92e305610f56654b2f80178470.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom {3+7-1}{7-1}=84$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="142" height="53" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let's call the unknown value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. By symmetry, we realize that the difference between 10 and the minimum value of the rolls is equal to the difference between the maximum and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. So, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c4b302574891066d6d2a19b7c6bdfefc128bf33.png" class="latex" alt="$10 - 7 = 42- x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="124" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/0/2a0bf6ec4f82d68799314c52a724058e8e22b7de.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 39$" width="52" height="12" /> and our answer is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c9b74d5345edf4ec513a28db40b604a61e6da9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{ 39}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />
By: Soccer_JAMS
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>For the sums to have equal probability, the average sum of both sets of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> dies has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/b/deb397750a7e6849fe12f2e2293e5b0c7746683d.png" class="latex" alt="$(6+1)\cdot 7 = 49$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" />. Since having <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is similar to not having <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, you just subtract 10 from the expected total sum. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f2fd72f55e69704c04aace346483bd7d347690.png" class="latex" alt="$49 - 10 = 39$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c9b74d5345edf4ec513a28db40b604a61e6da9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{ 39}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />
</p><p>By: epicmonster
</p><p>Revised solution by Williamgolly (includes bijections):
Notice that we first must have at least a 1 on each die.  Now, we form the following bijection: biject each value on the original die, say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to a value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/5/1f50ad524e676579df070adcb5377c8c3c494a01.png" class="latex" alt="$7-x$" width="41" height="12" /> on the new die. Notice how now we need to 'take away' 3 from seven dies that all show 6.  Therefore, the answer is 39.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The expected value of the sums of the die rolls is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/a/26a3d3effe4aa51a162affe19772a5ff1e688da9.png" class="latex" alt="$3.5\cdot7=24.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="101" height="13" />, and since the probabilities should be distributed symmetrically on both sides of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/b/9bbab57580b74c71d1e10bbebf35f67a3dcb677e.png" class="latex" alt="$24.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/8/db8a8fa2a9fc97470fa13e41cbab5bb401e9432b.png" class="latex" alt="$24.5+(24.5-10)=39$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="186" height="20" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c9b74d5345edf4ec513a28db40b604a61e6da9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{ 39}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p><p>By: dajeff
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Another faster and easier way of doing this, without using almost any math at all, is realizing that the possible sums are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/9/1396794b12e471659f90a8db515c22d442a5fadf.png" class="latex" alt="${7,8,9,10,...,39,40,41,42}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="197" height="16" />. By symmetry, (and doing a few similar problems in the past), you can realize that the probability of obtaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is the same as the probability of obtaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/2/992d7e0a1895bd91121075c07c3b428b39fccfef.png" class="latex" alt="$P(8)=P(41)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" /> and on and on and on. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/1/1415423289a1465d458cc35a31f5a8d3b4ed5ecf.png" class="latex" alt="$P(10)=P(39)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" />, and thus the correct answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c9b74d5345edf4ec513a28db40b604a61e6da9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{ 39}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p><p>By: fhdsaukfaioifk
</p>
