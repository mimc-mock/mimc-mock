<p>Sara makes a staircase out of toothpicks as shown:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/d/7ddaec3128435e9f9478a1e8a9e3e11e3817572b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)); path h = ellipse((0.5,0),0.45,0.015), v = ellipse((0,0.5),0.015,0.45); for(int i=0;i&lt;=2;i=i+1) { for(int j=0;j&lt;=3-i;j=j+1) { filldraw(shift((i,j))*h,black); filldraw(shift((j,i))*v,black); } } [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p>This is a 3-step staircase and uses 18 toothpicks. How many steps would be in a staircase that used 180 toothpicks?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/4/324b3177b0de08e5449d89b97f2a4d4dfceb27cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 11\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 24\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>A staircase with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> steps contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/5/da513c9c0cee26d8f7d5d2c2ff7b06af6bec0593.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 6 + 8 + ... + 2n + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="182" height="14" /> toothpicks. This can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80238840e1636067011eac06d0d7acb11ebb2a0.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+2) -2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />. 
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/f/d9f43d84f1abcf8361d130608e71944f93ce8b07.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+2) - 2 = 180$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="18" />
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/2/012b998fb4b6a4687527b8564c9c6722d05ded8e.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+2) = 182.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="18" /> 
</p><p>Inspection could tell us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/5/7952972f8f8c657a0ca36bae1a8fa7ae549beb2a.png" class="latex" alt="$13 * 14 = 182$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/7/647e8022472ddbbc5917b324068ae428bb37c599.png" class="latex" alt="$13 - 1 = \boxed {(C) 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="132" height="29" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Layer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> steps
</p><p>Layer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d338f393d100febd19e4284537ac40517b48340.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="15" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> steps
</p><p>Layer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/c/f3c13f88915d101b401e1fdeac1b195262655caa.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> steps
</p><p>Layer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2ac154585e6c931b152e388f889dc90cabfdfe6.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> steps
</p><p>From inspection, we can see that with each increase in layer the difference in toothpicks between the current layer and the previous increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Using this pattern:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7ca34d81444749bf83c7fcf16ed1cf3f3116cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$4, 10, 18, 28, 40, 54, 70, 88, 108, 130, 154, 180$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="333" height="16" />
</p><p>From this we see that the solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/6/53657681d50b7b690d848b2f1836914876340355.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(C) 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="29" />
</p><p>By: Soccer_JAMS
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>We can find a function that gives us the number of toothpicks for every layer. Using finite difference, we know that the degree must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and the leading coefficient is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. The function is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/1/f418600dc45bd78ffc9478c991a2edfcb0f05d1e.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n)=n^2+3n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="19" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the layer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef0203e65664bd00b5f0325c5c5fcd81bcead8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is the number of toothpicks.
</p><p><br />
We have to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/9/b29c56734383c70103c4a9753f140f00e42e4be5.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2+3n=180\Rightarrow n^2+3n-180=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="283" height="16" />. Factor to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/6/166b624edffc3159ad00361ab7401a02d51d482f.png" class="latex" alt="$(n-12)(n+15)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />. The roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b1c7423bc2cabc550417c27a3760b8a998318a7.png" class="latex" alt="$-15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" />. Clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b1c7423bc2cabc550417c27a3760b8a998318a7.png" class="latex" alt="$-15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" /> is impossible so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/6/53657681d50b7b690d848b2f1836914876340355.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(C) 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="29" />.
</p><p>~Zeric Hang
</p>
<h3><span id="Not_a_Solution!_Just_an_observation."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Not_a_Solution.21_Just_an_observation.">Not a Solution! Just an observation.</span></h3>
<p>Notice that the number of toothpicks can be found by adding all the horizontal and all the vertical toothpicks. We can see that for the case of 3 steps, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a530c7108cac293cd497028fcfd3035c2a2a9d98.png" class="latex" alt="$2(3+3+2+1)=18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" /> toothpicks. Thus, the equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/5/6b510c334d849f27db65618114f85e3bcb350a06.png" class="latex" alt="$2S + 2(1+2+3...+S)=180$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="239" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> being the number of steps. Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5d2cecf9d177343b03d8f23a459a7a9c9cd2739.png" class="latex" alt="$S = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/6/53657681d50b7b690d848b2f1836914876340355.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(C) 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="29" />.
-liu4505
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_General_Formula">Solution 5 General Formula</span></h3>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64f8070867b03b0dc224db7e741135e07f4502c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" /> squares. Each has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> toothpick sides. To remove overlap, note that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b417a127620d5b467fe7a48ad5f7d843e5c390b5.png" class="latex" alt="$4n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> perimeter toothpicks. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/a/ceabd0611e2afb1b5b1d9baf3ee1fa061fd57495.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\cdot 4-4n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="114" height="43" /> is the number of overlapped toothpicks
Add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b417a127620d5b467fe7a48ad5f7d843e5c390b5.png" class="latex" alt="$4n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> to get the perimeter (non-overlapping). Formula is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/4/8845721ec1f0c5de8cc9edd789aee6588ca3876f.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{number of toothpicks} = n^2+3n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="239" height="18" /> Then you can "guess" or factor (also guessing) to get the answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f212ec5fc9c62e2e79e48207ca61e3014660f3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(C) }12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" />.
~bjc
</p>
<h3><span id="Not_a_solution!_Just_an_observation._2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Not_a_solution.21_Just_an_observation._2">Not a solution! Just an observation.</span></h3>
<p>If you are trying to look for a pattern, you can see that the first column is made of 4 toothpicks. The second one is made from 2 squares: 3 toothpicks for the first square and 4 for the second. The third one is made up of 3 squares: 3 toothpicks for the first and second one, and 4 for the third one. The pattern continues like that. So for the first one, you have 0 "3 toothpick squares" and 1 "4 toothpick squares". The second is 1 to 1. The third is 2:1. And the amount of three toothpick squares increase by one every column.
</p><p>The list is as follow for the number of toothpicks used...
4,4+3,4+6,4+9, and so on.
4, 7, 10, 13, 16, 19, ...
</p><p>- Flutterfly
</p>
