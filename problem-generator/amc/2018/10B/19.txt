<p>Joey and Chloe and their daughter Zoe all have the same birthday. Joey is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> year older than Chloe, and Zoe is exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> year old today. Today is the first of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> birthdays on which Chloe's age will be an integral multiple of Zoe's age. What will be the sum of the two digits of Joey's age the next time his age is a multiple of Zoe's age?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2d7ce88a6c065df7402c2eb32407f5bb4bbdc0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }7 \qquad \textbf{(B) }8 \qquad \textbf{(C) }9 \qquad \textbf{(D) }10 \qquad \textbf{(E) }11 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="370" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that Chloe is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> years old today, so Joey is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a398005d35072ae993a9ac8b72dd8e746fcc416e.png" class="latex" alt="$c+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="13" /> years old today. After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> years, Chloe and Zoe will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbdc3067560df57a6f830b44ee93db78beffddb7.png" class="latex" alt="$n+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e3efd9b14723a92c2ae891fe27780d5f8e2b215.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> years old, respectively. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/4/d8435d9904bfe5080a9ba268a2a1f6791c1bd715.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{n+c}{n+1}=1+\frac{c-1}{n+1}\]" width="144" height="38" /> is an integer for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> nonnegative integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd923e41635af40a1abe58f230732f2280b0723a.png" class="latex" alt="$n.$" width="14" height="8" /> It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f2924e05b65d0a1887d309cb3aad442e9ccee0f.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="38" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> positive divisors. The prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f2924e05b65d0a1887d309cb3aad442e9ccee0f.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="38" height="12" /> is either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/a/f8aaf0557cade138229010df80bfb511001b14fb.png" class="latex" alt="$p^8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/1/3618caa5e7857fb435829933d3c792ed211e1237.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2q^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="20" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/8/8985203a4d5fc2525422d5649f03b8ab04f90db3.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1&lt;100,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="17" /> the only possibility is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7ae958482c8c1b2f329bf02feff2bccabc93269.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1=2^2\cdot3^2=36,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="20" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70ee433d54fb69862ec2ca853a4d73034ace73d1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=37.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="14" /> We conclude that Joey is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/625fa93c57bca33e85278ee71c2afe52fdf7f47c.png" class="latex" alt="$c+1=38$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" /> years old today.
</p><p>Suppose that Joey's age is a multiple of Zoe's age after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> years, in which Joey and Zoe will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5bb3f6ccabb4ff28b011e7eb2f0c1622673bc4.png" class="latex" alt="$k+38$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f05f085f774fc8ab50676b778b86a1f1d1114fc8.png" class="latex" alt="$k+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> years old, respectively. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/681b3d7a8817955f05bd6bc7d26a623bdb8f5e6e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{k+38}{k+1}=1+\frac{37}{k+1}\]" width="151" height="39" /> is an integer for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d48e0968a64d782fe75bca620ce749938b7d0bb.png" class="latex" alt="$k.$" width="13" height="12" /> It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50d302794b205b5019bf2d13385ea7c1a92a4056.png" class="latex" alt="$k+1,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" /> so the only possibility is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/1/d51dd57f98ff07d73f8fdd075f183e4406d7efb1.png" class="latex" alt="$k=36.$" width="56" height="12" /> We conclude that Joey will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/b/9abe68a67d7f53f92998e28909d8e852392b3ee6.png" class="latex" alt="$k+38=74$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="16" /> years old then, from which the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/2/112987cf132849501279855b27ca73607a2d87d9.png" class="latex" alt="$7+4=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="135" height="30" />
</p><p>~Supercj ~MRENTHUSIASM ~Zeric
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let Joey's age be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" />, Chloe's age be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and we know that Zoe's age is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We know that there must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/8/5586db7ead68a6ecd88ebc290818d5f1f5309e48.png" class="latex" alt="$k\in\mathbb{Z}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="44" height="13" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/7/5a7506794c5eb6e572508b6785eac678bef4f2be.png" class="latex" alt="$c+k=a(1+k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is an integer.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3e2823bbd439600acd4c37845c0fca2c5fcfc2d.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1+(1+k)=a(1+k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/4/5348be87ef203684267d415a8ba000228301e3a7.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1=(1+k)(a-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" />. Therefore, we know that, as there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, there must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f2924e05b65d0a1887d309cb3aad442e9ccee0f.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="38" height="12" />. We know that this must be a perfect square. Testing perfect squares, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/c/ddcde0d012ff7ed52bd42663afd72777ef6710d5.png" class="latex" alt="$c-1=36$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/d/4fd2ef02173a8e2fd282c3b548f84f373a74e079.png" class="latex" alt="$c=37$" width="50" height="12" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/1/eb131b5d4e7acedbf35d98f2b3dfb5e220309524.png" class="latex" alt="$j=38$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />. Now, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2ca3a5945823c5e040863891da2c1f6bcd0c45b.png" class="latex" alt="$j-1=37$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" />, by similar logic, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/4/14431214ec3f038166ae95661812f4ede69b6b85.png" class="latex" alt="$37=(1+k)(a-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/5/4f5199e107f26a45ab7a959fc96ed93c4e1eb31c.png" class="latex" alt="$k=36$" width="52" height="12" /> and Joey will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906b5cae6cb4d8fb22a31376247ab37a14af7477.png" class="latex" alt="$38+36=74$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="14" /> and the sum of the digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/e/18e8197c1f407d14348beed1a2dfa72335b8355c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="65" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Here's a different way of stating Solution 2:
</p><p>If a number is a multiple of both Chloe's age and Zoe's age, then it is a multiple of their difference. Since the difference between their ages does not change, then that means the difference between their ages has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> factors. Therefore, the difference between Chloe and Zoe's age is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />, so Chloe is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />, and Joey is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c5e2e4c4950cff11894abd202aa2949e21819c5.png" class="latex" alt="$38$" width="17" height="12" />. The common factor that will divide both of their ages is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />, so Joey will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/1/90100e7ec57be3145070551137e9c47224adb4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$74$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43debe5654566785b070b222c769d5546edd480a.png" class="latex" alt="$7 + 4 =  \boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="130" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Similar approach to above, just explained less concisely and more in terms of the problem (less algebraic).
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbec19c713de9418e01c686bea17266f591cdcf.png" class="latex" alt="$C+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" /> denote Chloe's age, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d5d3fa9fbb7ca7cde6905ee6564cda5aec7241a.png" class="latex" alt="$J+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="44" height="14" /> denote Joey's age, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> denote Zoe's age, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of years from now. We are told that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbec19c713de9418e01c686bea17266f591cdcf.png" class="latex" alt="$C+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> exactly nine times. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" /> and will increase until greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" />, it will hit every natural number less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" />, including every factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbec19c713de9418e01c686bea17266f591cdcf.png" class="latex" alt="$C+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" /> to be an integral multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" />, the difference <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" /> must also be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, which happens if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" /> is a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf68ec248c46a45475bbcad22d6029f5573244a.png" class="latex" alt="$C-Z$" width="50" height="12" /> has nine factors. The smallest number that has nine positive factors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/8/3682dca34e29aeb5f229be77963f5c208ad8df34.png" class="latex" alt="$2^23^2=36$" width="75" height="15" /> . (We want it to be small so that Joey will not have reached three digits of age before his age is a multiple of Zoe's.) We also know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/5/f35a1872492632e0806bd35ee14c84138dcc7305.png" class="latex" alt="$Z=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/2/82237f733ca4f27be5d4e8de3fef2e2855a0f7ee.png" class="latex" alt="$J=C+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="80" height="14" />. Thus,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859458eab545d3c20c39c0f3d4e8eccc02fe0f1d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} C-Z&amp;=36, \\ J-Z&amp;=37. \end{align*}" width="96" height="37" />
By our above logic, the next time <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76be2824000ec151a51f12fdaa803453b2a0c741.png" class="latex" alt="$J-Z$" width="47" height="12" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> will occur when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4120fa7ecfd897bbf48be7be629a4aa35a3ca639.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" /> is a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76be2824000ec151a51f12fdaa803453b2a0c741.png" class="latex" alt="$J-Z$" width="47" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" /> is prime, the next time this happens is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/6/d0680433582eac864cc446bafd6da2beab5395c8.png" class="latex" alt="$Z+n=37$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" />, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/2/00248c7af1cafa259ea266405ce2270047d952b4.png" class="latex" alt="$J+n=74$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="14" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/3/b73c86150289e5ba762ad23eda2acd44182c3ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$7+4=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="130" height="30" />.
