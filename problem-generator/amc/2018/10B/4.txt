<p>A three-dimensional rectangular box with dimensions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" /> has faces whose surface areas are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" /> square units. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> + <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> + <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/4/ec42889bab8f60120619349190d29140f61861ee.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }18 \qquad \textbf{(B) }22 \qquad \textbf{(C) }24 \qquad \textbf{(D) }30 \qquad \textbf{(E) }36 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> be the length of the shortest dimension and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" /> be the length of the longest dimension. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45f4862f5341ba4081911b57c5ffcbd77588b1f4.png" class="latex" alt="$XY = 24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/c/02c6dc668d3fd6954f1cd5d0d8d8c44646f79e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$YZ = 72$" width="70" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/a/b8acce8bf8459bc5cb2457c1cc16ae0ded938b47.png" class="latex" alt="$XZ = 48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />.
Divide the first two equations to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/3/453bd7729aff206958577eaf5f21110f7d50046d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{Z}{X} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="37" />. Then, multiply by the last equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a3084676741076a80840a27f1eacbe16499929.png" class="latex" alt="$Z^2 = 144$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="15" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/0/9901435fd585237103f96ac7445953c182652d35.png" class="latex" alt="$Z = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />. Following, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b3019aa161a29f20e67e2a7660f20d82c8d2573.png" class="latex" alt="$X = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/4/c84064a370f909fee73ba528860c52f5ac99d35d.png" class="latex" alt="$Y = 6$" width="47" height="12" />.
</p><p>The final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/7/607c6992d0667577abf471ae03ce28edfef62c55.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 6 + 12 = 22$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Simply guess and check to find that the dimensions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/7/607c6992d0667577abf471ae03ce28edfef62c55.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 6 + 12 = 22$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>If you find the GCD of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" /> you get your first number, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. After this, do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/7/447be73ee4469b54f92d15c8506f4bdba3d265f4.png" class="latex" alt="$48 \div 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/9/62926bd52a6780574985021148fd0cd4d989fb6c.png" class="latex" alt="$72 \div 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, the other 2 numbers. When you add up your <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers, you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Since the surface areas of the faces are the product of two of the dimensions. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9a2f4b4ca90475fc5a19e6ddcafb9733ead333.png" class="latex" alt="$XY=24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c7437ae6dcdf5f365311618ebd97ceefeb6759f6.png" class="latex" alt="$XZ=48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/1/3d13ef41d7d3e1f3d76588326dfd0a0305229b1f.png" class="latex" alt="$YZ=72$" width="70" height="12" />. You can multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/4/d84df6afd9a741fcb76bd1b60d35bf9468fd8916.png" class="latex" alt="$XY \times XZ \times YZ$" width="134" height="12" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/1082a2c6a2e98d7e8e741b92a5a09fa9f5ff4f19.png" class="latex" alt="${XYZ}^2=24 \times 48 \times 72$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="175" height="16" /> which means that the volume<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/2/0526e1f5ad1412706cf938657f754604e672a671.png" class="latex" alt="$XYZ$" width="44" height="12" /> equals<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/4/ee4ac821ec9c1d956ca0ae079a438d963f86f97f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{24*48*72}=\sqrt{24^2*12^2}=24*12=288$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="340" height="19" />. The individual dimensions, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" /> can be found by doing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/f/d0f0537130ba6c176b4896a31c0a5dabba042f21.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{XYZ}{XY}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="47" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/2/1b26ee311b3c1e627cf865bafef5f1dc9fea268c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{XYZ}{YZ}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="47" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc6f18dd53b471691519ade8718f6d6531dbab21.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{XYZ}{XZ}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="47" height="37" />, which yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/e/69ec3c81db87b4cbb4f2b07cc29e50d0ca8b2f09.png" class="latex" alt="$Z=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/1/d614c4fb0f73528942bace8926fbe646803f36dd.png" class="latex" alt="$Y=6$" width="47" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/4/14425c57745d2c13c539e983550d2ef419b78242.png" class="latex" alt="$X=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />. Adding this up, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e43824492bdedb709c08da6583b6ad8276df7472.png" class="latex" alt="$X+Y+Z=22$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="14" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
- Solution by smartninja2000
</p>
