<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" /> with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/8/ca8d0ec6dc74f13176a38469882c5e79894f7409.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/d/d6ddf755b50701c255c065a1c2b6f40c089175d4.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/18873ba5dae90bd0238752b4cf8a422fedc84cc6.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 5$" width="61" height="12" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> denote the circumcenter and incenter, respectively. A circle with center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is tangent to the legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> and to the circumcircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" />. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/c/a2ce5a0ce1e2ce3c00ede67a2a3dcf07de70328f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{MOI}$" width="59" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/b/45bb383513cb5334a45bc655faae25b21ea981dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac52\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{11}{4}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{13}{4}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac72$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="385" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diagram">Diagram</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/0/dc005b1acbae74fc0295b565fa151b3a7a9b9a66.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Made by MRENTHUSIASM */ size(250);  pair A, B, C, O, I, M; C = origin; A = (12,0); B = (0,5); C = origin; O = circumcenter(A,B,C); I = incenter(A,B,C); M = (4,4); fill(M--O--I--cycle,yellow); draw(A--B--C--cycle^^circumcircle(A,B,C)^^incircle(A,B,C)^^circle(M,4)^^M--O--I--cycle); dot(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,1.5*SE,linewidth(4)); dot(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,1.5*NW,linewidth(4)); dot(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,1.5*SW,linewidth(4)); dot(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,1.5*dir((5,12)),linewidth(4)); dot(&quot;$I$&quot;,I,1.5*S,linewidth(4)); dot(&quot;$M$&quot;,M,1.5*N,linewidth(4)); [/asy]" width="418" height="402" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>In this solution, let the brackets denote areas.
</p><p>We place the diagram in the coordinate plane: Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/f/06f5484358e5613b8d3c7ed90920a9c194487c43.png" class="latex" alt="$A=(12,0),B=(0,5),$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="182" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6cee320822416e48e4f3ab5dbf3f284ac97c8619.png" class="latex" alt="$C=(0,0).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="86" height="20" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is a right triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b69b5a7b440dfdd90ac1129132d5c284d8678731.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB=90^\circ,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" /> its circumcenter is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f694ab8315c055ae7dd5d9fe5ba57a1a7c97a99.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="19" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/f/fef2bdfd8128b979c582eb240d4bc4d5cd1235da.png" class="latex" alt="$O=\left(6,\frac52\right).$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="105" height="45" /> Note that the circumradius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/3/c73e0e25277235bdfd48ff2ca97b8800c6e4ca3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{13}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" /> 
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the semiperimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919b9f77a6985e760286799909be5bfe0152f40e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC.$" width="61" height="13" /> The inradius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7dac590efbcdc713adeaf7868a9f6f63510dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[ABC]}{s}=\frac{30}{15}=2,$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="143" height="40" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0206b1a5c66e4b56069662168592d57a020b3f.png" class="latex" alt="$I=(2,2).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="81" height="20" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a4cb4eb4e4bcb249953ed1575bd5a0fab9ff96.png" class="latex" alt="$\odot M$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="33" height="14" /> is also tangent to both coordinate axes, its center is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/0/d20bc7756f1ab8a22f20c60c92c1cc0086591101.png" class="latex" alt="$M=(a,a)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="20" /> and its radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> for some positive number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45ef9cdc7c7d46920eac603663914e0d9261a99b.png" class="latex" alt="$a.$" width="13" height="8" /> Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> be the point of tangency of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c748db2ab4a67c0aa0dace8827ba7cbc3a48aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\odot O$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/d/f8d9bc9de3aaca4a32c41d5b593b4c1dabf01034.png" class="latex" alt="$\odot M.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="36" height="14" /> As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/e/18e9b0bb78fb03b2ca84b3dcab43d6afe16880f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OP}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/3/c738ae7fb31d9b886913df31ae07f109b43c5a51.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MP}$" width="34" height="15" /> are both perpendicular to the common tangent line at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/e/ede78a06018011f48c24a76284afce482ebc6c47.png" class="latex" alt="$P,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="16" /> we conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/c/89cebcc8b03f40ceb7ee2149435d90359125f5c8.png" class="latex" alt="$O,M,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> are collinear. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b60a5d64d236128eea7b2e8ac45628dd091e03.png" class="latex" alt="$OM=OP-MP,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/8/9c8742c1d692fe9d5a2a1498b057a7d1b9725547.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{(a-6)^2+\left(a-\frac52\right)^2}=\frac{13}{2}-a.\]" width="261" height="53" />
Solving this equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/5411cdd14c51889f23956e271006734f3004f7f3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="17" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/9/d496fa3b749f1b5e9d9512bd2f0c26b6cd114818.png" class="latex" alt="$M=(4,4).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="91" height="20" />
</p><p>Finally, we apply the Shoelace Theorem to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/4/b947db7983e4aa07179f1157bf19e0c90875a35e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MOI:$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10efbf5d5dcb67008fa807694669753888357e1e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[MOI]=\frac12\left|\left(4\cdot\frac52+6\cdot2+2\cdot4\right)-\left(4\cdot6+\frac52\cdot2+2\cdot4\right)\right|=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ \frac72}.\]" width="548" height="48" />
<u><b>Remark</b></u>
</p><p>Alternatively, we can use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/9/bb953239e8f2213e04f7c546bca445229fde7f63.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MI}$" width="29" height="15" /> as the base and the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9b10336bb1df5ca510fe5baa66a4e4d392a6ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overleftrightarrow{MI}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="31" height="21" /> as the height for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/4/b947db7983e4aa07179f1157bf19e0c90875a35e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MOI:$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />
</p>
<ul><li>By the Distance Formula, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/e/42e6901948257efb3dfbc58663e989be97299c95.png" class="latex" alt="$MI=2\sqrt2.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="93" height="21" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>The equation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9b10336bb1df5ca510fe5baa66a4e4d392a6ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overleftrightarrow{MI}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="31" height="21" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/6/0766d215a15b818cba91f2c763aac004ec176da3.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y+0=0,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="17" /> so the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9b10336bb1df5ca510fe5baa66a4e4d392a6ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overleftrightarrow{MI}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="31" height="21" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15f76d73cf1249a4e16ca0facd3d7def507a3f71.png" class="latex" alt="$h_O=\frac{\left|1\cdot6+(-1)\cdot\frac52+0\right|}{\sqrt{1^2+(-1)^2}}=\frac74\sqrt2.$" style="vertical-align: -21px" width="276" height="50" /></li></ul>
<p>Therefore, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/9/ac957bcab6b6a5ba39cb3edf81c93cb76b4f0c3a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[MOI]=\frac12\cdot MI\cdot h_O=\frac72.\]" width="206" height="38" />
~pieater314159 ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
