<p>A circle has a chord of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and the distance from the center of the circle to the chord is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the area of the circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/2/54227df9b2bd1786f564a7d299e24ed2a7f4807b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }25\pi \qquad \textbf{(B) }50\pi \qquad \textbf{(C) }75\pi \qquad \textbf{(D) }100\pi \qquad \textbf{(E) }125\pi \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="471" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> be the center of the circle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> be the chord, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f694ab8315c055ae7dd5d9fe5ba57a1a7c97a99.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="19" /> as shown below.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a98a012a46954b990fd2592abb76aa48f7e6cef5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Made by MRENTHUSIASM */ size(200); pair O, A, B, M; O = (0,0); A = (-5,5); B = (5,5); M = midpoint(A--B);  draw(Circle(O,5sqrt(2)));  dot(&quot;$O$&quot;, O, 1.5*S, linewidth(4.5)); dot(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, 1.5*NW, linewidth(4.5)); dot(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, 1.5*NE, linewidth(4.5)); dot(&quot;$M$&quot;, M, 1.5*N, linewidth(4.5)); draw(A--B^^M--O^^A--O^^M--O^^B--O); label(&quot;$5$&quot;, midpoint(A--M), 1.5*N); label(&quot;$5$&quot;, midpoint(B--M), 1.5*N); label(&quot;$5$&quot;, midpoint(O--M), 1.5*E); label(&quot;$r$&quot;, midpoint(O--A), 1.5*SW); label(&quot;$r$&quot;, midpoint(O--B), 1.5*SE); [/asy]" width="335" height="335" />
Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/2/2b28326eab546f9014a52a4e0f9bfbbdf3f0576e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OM}\perp\overline{AB}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="18" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/90064472691e89b8fa5d346d164022b10a189db6.png" class="latex" alt="$OM=AM=BM=5,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="18" /> we conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/d/47d8720cc891188cc83f7032ea5cba0f1fd31a19.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle OMA$" width="63" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/6/f56c6b549f1e7be150552138f1562aa03ded184b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle OMB$" width="64" height="13" /> are congruent isosceles right triangles. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/1/ba13bce93c8c157908c98da0f4cad5205e4412be.png" class="latex" alt="$r=5\sqrt2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="23" /> so the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c748db2ab4a67c0aa0dace8827ba7cbc3a48aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\odot O$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/6/38603fc90d36fae6cd305ed544feb7da08be7fdf.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^2=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }50\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="129" height="30" />.
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
