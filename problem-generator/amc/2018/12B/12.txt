<p>Side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. The bisector of angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/3/b53e0064e5c0075ceeb38a4e29b4fa1ed396dd85.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = 3$" width="62" height="12" />. The set of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is an open interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02dbab3601fa6cce490d54263017048f69e1a35d.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/0/cd025452546f3a8ec48d7d287e6cfebc117a78df.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }16 \qquad \textbf{(B) }17 \qquad \textbf{(C) }18 \qquad \textbf{(D) }19 \qquad \textbf{(E) }20 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5000627d977251fab80100e224f275486eb94c3.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=x.$" width="66" height="13" /> By Angle Bisector Theorem, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448215f07f0984e8149815c37692bd2c2932f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{AC}=\frac{BD}{CD},$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="98" height="39" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/7/eb7b3117be23980668425613c0428aaaf4fdcd4b.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=CD\cdot\frac{AB}{AC}=\frac{30}{x}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="183" height="39" />
</p><p>Recall that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbc84e38fcf36b728033e68ab60cb4be6fee9d7.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;0.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="13" /> We apply the Triangle Inequality to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/6/4a6210a7880978cd56f453775f36d8ca6327adc9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC:$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" />
</p>
<ol style="margin-left: 1.5em;">
  <li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/9/c39246f30212631f37d808c3f6f0c0b03137314c.png" class="latex" alt="$AC+BC&gt;AB \iff x+\left(\frac{30}{x}+3\right)&gt;10$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="342" height="45" /> <p>
We simplify and complete the square to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/a/a5a573754a287e2867a18f715762e0ecb9842a24.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(x-\frac72\right)^2+\frac{71}{4}&gt;0,$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="164" height="48" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbc84e38fcf36b728033e68ab60cb4be6fee9d7.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;0.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="13" />
</p></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/5/82590f072597663bfbfac81c30f09208dac48aa5.png" class="latex" alt="$AB+BC&gt;AC \iff 10+\left(\frac{30}{x}+3\right)&gt;x$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="342" height="45" /> <p>
We simplify and factor to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/5/165e05ea0f0cd5340f75b9862d38bd449cf7d105.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+2)(x-15)&lt;0,$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="161" height="20" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/7/5970c4b577ee37abb8f63d5eb2b5ad54ae8e5b61.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x&lt;15.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="93" height="15" />
</p></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/1/341ed2e81c82b8474a9fc0313bc1a5d3c179d11b.png" class="latex" alt="$AB+AC&gt;BC \iff 10+x&gt;\frac{30}{x}+3$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="316" height="38" /> <p>
We simplify and factor to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc28012c469c30829cec27b729881c2613cf72d1.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+10)(x-3)&gt;0,$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="161" height="20" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/5/635baffe031c13a27f0ee834dd05de57d600dc98.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;3.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="13" />
</p></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p></ol>
<p>Taking the intersection of the solutions gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35b758e0fc823c4281469eed321885cf9207510d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(m,n)=(0,\infty)\cap(0,15)\cap(3,\infty)=(3,15),\]" width="338" height="18" /> so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/8/fa898477801dba2a98cb458aa8f8da5890a19d59.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }18}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="145" height="30" />
</p><p>~quinnanyc ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
