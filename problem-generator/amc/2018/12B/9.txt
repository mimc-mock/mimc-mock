</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/0/650de08bdc2710ecd75f77056e3619e3378d79d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }100{,}100 \qquad \textbf{(B) }500{,}500\qquad \textbf{(C) }505{,}000 \qquad \textbf{(D) }1{,}001{,}000 \qquad \textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="669" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Recall that the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb5779c70f0e2e5ca01ebebb9d9ea030fb8cc55.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum^{100}_{k=1} k = \frac{101\cdot100}{2}=5050.$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="202" height="56" /> It follows that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66dc9b993df3ad05437f321f1d0bfba6ace9f3e1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{j=1} (i+j) &amp;= \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{j=1}i + \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{j=1}j \\ &amp;= \sum^{100}_{i=1} (100i) + 100 \sum^{100}_{j=1}j \\ &amp;= 100 \sum^{100}_{i=1}i + 100 \sum^{100}_{j=1}j \\ &amp;= 100\cdot5050 + 100\cdot5050 \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000}. \end{align*}" width="304" height="240" />
~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Recall that the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb5779c70f0e2e5ca01ebebb9d9ea030fb8cc55.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum^{100}_{k=1} k = \frac{101\cdot100}{2}=5050.$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="202" height="56" /> It follows that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/7/1a79b3e855440a338a60309a61adbb43291db010.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{j=1} (i+j) &amp;= \sum^{100}_{i=1} \left(\sum^{100}_{j=1} i + \sum^{100}_{j=1} j\right) \\ &amp;= \sum^{100}_{i=1} (100i+5050) \\ &amp;= 100\sum^{100}_{i=1} i + \sum^{100}_{i=1} 5050 \\ &amp;= 100\cdot5050+5050\cdot100 \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000}. \end{align*}" width="304" height="237" />
~Vfire ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Recall that the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb5779c70f0e2e5ca01ebebb9d9ea030fb8cc55.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum^{100}_{k=1} k = \frac{101\cdot100}{2}=5050.$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="202" height="56" /> Since the nested summation is symmetric with respect to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd49ba3dba42310ae25ee9972e7bd1ed90c7cc61.png" class="latex" alt="$j,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="16" /> it follows that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/8/8c8f620c3c2b73a41fa424eeadb04e2d3ba8a361.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{j=1} (i+j) &amp;= \sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{i=1} (2i) \\ &amp;= 2\sum^{100}_{i=1} \sum^{100}_{i=1} i \\ &amp;= 2\sum^{100}_{i=1} 5050 \\ &amp;= 2\cdot(5050\cdot100) \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000}. \end{align*}" width="251" height="236" />
~Vfire ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The sum contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/771ef4092d4105545b3e8ac0cfaa24d0bb250fca.png" class="latex" alt="$100\cdot100=10000$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" /> terms, and the average value of both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/b/aab304b08fed74663f391c7ed0c462fb7d2a91b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{101}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="38" height="38" /> Therefore, the sum becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/8/138e491e752345f21664292e5cd3897464b0ce7f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[10000\cdot\left(\frac{101}{2}+\frac{101}{2}\right)=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000}.\]" width="314" height="43" />
~Rejas ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>We start by writing out the first few terms:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/b/28b38041be1d966548a3ea535833719f4bef7a90.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{ccccccccc} (1+1) &amp;+ &amp;(1+2) &amp;+ &amp;(1+3) &amp;+ &amp;\cdots &amp;+ &amp;(1+100) \\ (2+1) &amp;+ &amp;(2+2) &amp;+ &amp;(2+3) &amp;+ &amp;\cdots &amp;+ &amp;(2+100) \\ (3+1) &amp;+ &amp;(3+2) &amp;+ &amp;(3+3) &amp;+ &amp;\cdots &amp;+ &amp;(3+100) \\ [-1ex] &amp;&amp;&amp;&amp;\vdots&amp;&amp;&amp;&amp; \\ (100+1) &amp;+ &amp;(100+2) &amp;+ &amp;(100+3) &amp;+ &amp;\cdots &amp;+ &amp;(100+100). \end{array}\]" width="524" height="104" />
From the first terms in the parentheses, the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/2/6d2539bfd22d0331762a7f91ba4024076e07ddc3.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots+100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="164" height="14" /> occurs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> times vertically.
</p><p>From the second terms in the parentheses, the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/2/6d2539bfd22d0331762a7f91ba4024076e07ddc3.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots+100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="164" height="14" /> occurs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> times horizontally.
</p><p>Recall that the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b415e6ea98f8d6a5dbc875b4c9fb0e787628995.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots+100=\frac{101\cdot100}{2}=5050.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="328" height="38" /> Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/1/9f17812793fa6d7b944c485358dccc18d7f23724.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2\cdot\left(5050\cdot100\right)=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }1{,}010{,}000}.\]" width="259" height="28" />
~RandomPieKevin ‎~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
