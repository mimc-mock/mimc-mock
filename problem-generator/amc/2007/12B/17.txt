<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is a nonzero integer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is a positive number such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/0/4c0e2859688e1c63db0603217a957a5ecc097fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$ab^2=\log_{10}b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="19" />, what is the median of the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4ed5ec3eccd52ad1bbb18afe1924d0c93176ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\{0,1,a,b,1/b\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/818e129d118f75247216378926a023238a88d105.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 0 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ a \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ b \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac{1}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="344" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is positive, then, the equation will have no solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. This becomes more obvious by noting that at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf590e9abb33aa9776ddc386b1ba6ee7b43fa413.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/4/cb47ab1777d77a0796b7d7732b5e33ca9e669091.png" class="latex" alt="$ab^2 &gt; \log_{10} b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="19" />. The LHS quadratic function will increase faster than the RHS logarithmic function, so they will never intersect.
</p><p>This puts <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> as the smallest in the set since it must be negative.
</p><p>Checking the new equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/8/508641f491c8e01ddd75283359b5936ce18dae55.png" class="latex" alt="$-b^2 = \log_{10}b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="19" />
</p><p>Near <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc43f0ea59e862d7db01586894e9eea730d427b7.png" class="latex" alt="$b=0$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/c/7fc7e934718c71515bf5c0d7933d2dd1586f59b3.png" class="latex" alt="$-b^2 &gt; \log_{10} b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="19" /> but at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf590e9abb33aa9776ddc386b1ba6ee7b43fa413.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/d/fbd9b97b24cea9873f383d6e5479f35f02efa75f.png" class="latex" alt="$-b^2 &lt; \log_{10} b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="19" />
</p><p>This implies that the solution occurs somewhere in between: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/a/9cabde1685a6e1d28759e7155a6ac61afd3e7409.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; b &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />
</p><p>This also implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c7a12b6bedb3d3c655c04b26eda94ea8f1f2246.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{b} &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />
</p><p>This makes our set (ordered)
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/76698d8d32d66cf96d89e453e08d6e0008f6c1b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a,0,b,1,1/b\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="18" />
</p><p>The median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/e/16ebdf7cba3993cfe8dee188add6692b713ac46a.png" class="latex" alt="$b \Rightarrow \mathrm {(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cheap_Solution_that_most_people_probably_used">Cheap Solution that most people probably used</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4fecd255e5da022c505de5e7a71f37afaf02c31d.png" class="latex" alt="$b=0.1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/0/1f0bb2e5450c9d47e0c08599001d2632a67a7590.png" class="latex" alt="$a\cdot0.01 = -1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/2/24250c0db4b09bd91d676d98768ce3ab7ad0f747.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />. Then the ordered set is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7c04e8d3225419fbadff55a2637398fb1f4caf1.png" class="latex" alt="$\{-100, 0, 0.1, 1, 10\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> and the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/f/19fa6fc465ee382cbe2b0daa7e8b5549b2c22350.png" class="latex" alt="$0.1=b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/1/931c46d937d19ddb7256f87d68149cf5729ddeb6.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
