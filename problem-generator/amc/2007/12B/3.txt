<p>The point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> is the center of the circle circumscribed about triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/6/336358a364ce49b354744d52dd99f5b0b69a48b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC = 120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/b/05b58a8ef08202a51a05a99afd914c57fe7a1937.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AOB = 140^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />, as shown. What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3ad57710b6810476e6017ea2c2cc6d41219c218.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABC$" width="55" height="13" />?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007_12B_AMC-3.png" class="image"><img alt="2007 12B AMC-3.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//8/8a/2007_12B_AMC-3.png" width="292" height="297" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/a/30abbe541457f5cf23163efa890a9ef10a527309.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)} 35 \qquad \mathrm {(B)} 40 \qquad \mathrm {(C)} 45 \qquad \mathrm {(D)} 50 \qquad  \mathrm {(E)} 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="359" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/890bd5d40a42153edcb992ad0a14cac52ca2eaf8.png" class="latex" alt="$ABO$" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/d/76d9b534de592b2a7fc63b97e1f0faab799204e9.png" class="latex" alt="$BOC$" width="43" height="12" /> are isosceles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84edf39f2b0f30efa403bd0237fe917372fa4326.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO=20^o$" width="104" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/9/de976d018105538a8152aadf5894f15e36f9f0ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OBC=30^o$" width="105" height="12" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1484f37b5fc4f5d56ad36d9b2c4c8758a51f7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABC=50^o$" width="104" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
