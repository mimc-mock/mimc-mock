<p>All sides of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Convex_polygon" title="Convex polygon">convex</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pentagon" title="Pentagon">pentagon</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a96240c51d61ba3733d9f5c3020fa92ec4136.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDE$" width="72" height="13" /> are of equal length, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/5/c55d22ef9032402d68b10b00b4eacd933803515d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A = \angle B = 90^{\circ}$" width="126" height="13" />. What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef820980d93fb66d6f4b2883b9630d6bbdfd2667.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle E$" width="27" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/8/a48fe47c0f91c727abfd5a7daade4b9179bb6f91.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)}\ 90 \qquad \mathrm {(B)}\ 108 \qquad \mathrm {(C)}\ 120 \qquad \mathrm {(D)}\ 144 \qquad \mathrm {(E)}\ 150$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="423" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007_12B_AMC-7.png" class="image"><img alt="2007 12B AMC-7.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//d/d5/2007_12B_AMC-7.png" width="197" height="332" /></a>
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_angle" title="Right angle">right angles</a>, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/7/897c5861ff0e7e01fa2ad067a42326369f5ae338.png" class="latex" alt="$AECB$" width="56" height="13" /> is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Square" title="Square">square</a>, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a771251838750a4cb9926c5e2b03324b2b2edae7.png" class="latex" alt="$EC$" width="28" height="12" /> is 5. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/2/bc2afec04e3636c80fa3d89e5468045595151c4d.png" class="latex" alt="$ED$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> are also 5, triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/b/c7bc69328ef74607767b467b2a851f202438edec.png" class="latex" alt="$CDE$" width="44" height="12" /> is <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equilateral_triangle" title="Equilateral triangle">equilateral</a>. Angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/90735d0a5827bbec0e4e82b406e2c5f6a5fa0150.png" class="latex" alt="$90+60=150 \Rightarrow \mathrm {(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />
</p>
