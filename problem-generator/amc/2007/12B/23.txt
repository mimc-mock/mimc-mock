<p>How many non-congruent right triangles with positive integer leg lengths have areas that are numerically equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> times their perimeters?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/2/fb25e467882081e30623f66a7eeee7a6ae7c3e86.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)} 6\qquad \mathrm {(B)} 7\qquad \mathrm {(C)} 8\qquad \mathrm {(D)} 10\qquad \mathrm {(E)} 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="332" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the two legs of the triangle.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5a9044933664af4b87e2591c8b430b2ecb2e57d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}ab = 3(a+b+c)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf0c88fb011b991ae4432ba6da39c5ec69f5c9af.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=6 \left(a+b+\sqrt {a^2 + b^2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="203" height="32" />.
</p><p>We can complete the square under the root, and we get, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/2/1c284be14320073c4afc45591e320bbe2570c40b.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=6 \left(a+b+\sqrt {(a+b)^2 - 2ab}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="266" height="43" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/1/861f9a393c4d9383eb9de14217d3ea8bdd5add8c.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/6/0162211809d1f1d3e3fe5cf4408b4e138e0dcea1.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=s$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/d/32db862d3fec51889af3895f3b6f20f30b741cab.png" class="latex" alt="$p=6 \left(s+ \sqrt {s^2 - 2p}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="169" height="32" />.
</p><p>After rearranging, squaring both sides, and simplifying, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea9161bcfca4ad741e5cab7bd912e5852ceb3a89.png" class="latex" alt="$p=12s-72$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="15" />.
</p><p><br />
Putting back <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and after factoring using Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick, we've got <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/0/e70cdc133ecbe01e9bee13aa234768fff707dd53.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-12)(b-12)=72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Factoring 72, we get 6 pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e40fa9a2974752dc7393914ad6acca4a7d5eba79.png" class="latex" alt="$(13, 84), (14, 48), (15, 36), (16, 30), (18, 24), (20, 21).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="395" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
And this gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/c/03c057d326bebb0ab1ec6ca924e4533897ddb868.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Alternatively, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/1/7a178f4ccd7841e93202c2523a98ec639d65150d.png" class="latex" alt="$72 = 2^3 \cdot 3^2$" width="87" height="15" />. Then 72 has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dad02a3cf8d0dbe3f250a9f680076c7afd7aed22.png" class="latex" alt="$(3+1)(2+1) = (4)(3) = 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="221" height="18" /> factors. However, half of these are repeats, so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec956e0ab38c40e4aab6db21bffdfd13e2d1719.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{2} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" /> solutions.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We will proceed by using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/9/289668c9ffccf64525a50cf707bd6a55f5edf196.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABC] = r\cdot s$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="107" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the radius of the incircle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semiperimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/4333ff858586d2c1133cc1a9ebe0651ec6c3f488.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(s = \frac{p}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="33" />.
</p><p>We are given <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/a/6fa0b1a3f620e4e16086645fa3e07b02c60c7449.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABC] = 3p = 6s \Rightarrow rs = 6s \Rightarrow r = 6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="294" height="18" />.
</p><p>The incircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> breaks the triangle's sides into segments such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/1/f814604f3a3147f1d0b9a4626fe9f06c9134ee39.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = x + y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f571cc3ef9fbaeb9c5335fa10e9858db4ff255c7.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = x + z$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0eab225c8ae608983e8856f6d96e05108f4bc997.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = y + z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />. Since ABC is a right triangle, one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is equal to its radius, 6. Let's assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/1/781a1eab5324128a6b9b805f38a10a53badd7f3e.png" class="latex" alt="$z = 6$" width="42" height="12" />.
</p><p>The side lengths then become <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/1/f814604f3a3147f1d0b9a4626fe9f06c9134ee39.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = x + y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9ab148319a6bfb1ccd0f8cc3c12f066ebced4b9.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = x + 6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/d/eed0f5fc9dd51b5592ad200939f7341b3bad12c3.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = y + 6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />. Plugging into Pythagorean's theorem:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/08957345d2946ae596329e894b42c18faa2c3d39.png" class="latex" alt="$(x + y)^2 = (x+6)^2 + (y + 6)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="234" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/d/e9df5334a9ca00853025e56e3b0b0cc7282b43fa.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + 2xy + y^2 = x^2 + 12x + 36 + y^2 + 12y + 36$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="370" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c5316ba70b9cf05ef1fd8a0e9cb95d391aea06.png" class="latex" alt="$2xy - 12x - 12y = 72$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="171" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8fdefe7dc64e48b1fe8ca52ba261b8ddb49d3f4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy - 6x - 6y = 36$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="144" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/b/f6bfe7b8a2d02a9f8edabe61c2f5331d641ebd42.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 6)(y - 6) - 36 = 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="193" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adec332663efdbb35a416901844ab6405779a30.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 6)(y - 6) = 72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />
</p><p>We can factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" /> to arrive with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> pairs of solutions: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/1/69110b6668cdd481c9de72d1749a1412e71dea5c.png" class="latex" alt="$(7, 78), (8,42), (9, 30), (10, 24), (12, 18),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="301" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/2/7f2ff5e3bee8e2d1ec60bc20257a105d398f1353.png" class="latex" alt="$(14, 15) \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the two legs of the triangle, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be the hypotenuse.
</p><p>By using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2a05646dae8b780714623a64eb2b46156d4220e.png" class="latex" alt="$Area = \frac{r}{2} (a+b+c)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="163" height="34" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the in-radius, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/b/9fbd527c748cae66c70b9fbca041b89b274d4d31.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3(a+b+c) = \frac{r}{2} (a+b+c)\]" width="212" height="32" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0723dbc9ed03aeb481176d345ab22007bd68c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r=6\]" width="41" height="12" />
</p><p>In right triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/9/ae92c9ee35bcff9794d60aa3946e5dd85a899847.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \frac{a+b-c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="105" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffcd14d654039dcfd540b66f0087a4178fadc79.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+b-c = 12\]" width="111" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97d261005415e0f52bd6509772debb57d8f98e79.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c = a + b - 12\]" width="111" height="14" />
</p><p><br />
By the triangle's area we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/1/c11af1df6927fabe229b0ed07a98448c6416b71a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{ab}{2} = 6 \cdot \frac{a+b+c}{2}\]" width="138" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/5/5e53c8f8fb2bdf036f1edfd36d4e9289e1dd9a27.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab = 6(a+b+c)\]" width="133" height="18" />
</p><p>By substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> in:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/3/7c3c0f8593fc6979caf642b815dc21fd822ebd82.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab = 6(a+b+a + b - 12)\]" width="204" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f6fca34a7847340c9d3b7580208a3441746f4b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab - 12a - 12b + 72 = 0\]" width="188" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/4/384d352bf38a1329964e2ce0faf0ff10737dbbe0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a - 12)(b - 12) = 72\]" width="166" height="18" />
</p><p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/1/7a178f4ccd7841e93202c2523a98ec639d65150d.png" class="latex" alt="$72 = 2^3 \cdot 3^2$" width="87" height="15" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b1080c51e605b229dd2c2d335ad9ce31e220a807.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(3+1)(2+1)}{2} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="148" height="40" /> solutions, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/4/cd49bd8a1832ac84dd46d60f65c3e66ff7e67f24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p><p>~<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/User:Isabelchen">isabelchen</a>
