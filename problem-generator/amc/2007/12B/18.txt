<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be digits with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/6/296eacc72cb179258b4140b7ac9981ec88964fe1.png" class="latex" alt="$a\ne 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />. The three-digit integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" /> lies one third of the way from the square of a positive integer to the square of the next larger integer. The integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da43f2f76250b78d4606efd11b3fa19bf6727d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$acb$" width="25" height="12" /> lies two thirds of the way between the same two squares. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6ebd1e0356cfe09c0d9b5459d6f1542657bb5891.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab2ac48938bc98b6c29efd5dc10cc02016e36895.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 10 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 13 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 16 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 18 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 21$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The difference between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da43f2f76250b78d4606efd11b3fa19bf6727d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$acb$" width="25" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" /> is given by
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/692ee0ee007fce1af63011e850fb30ea417e1a8e.png" class="latex" alt="$(100a + 10c + b) - (100a + 10b + c) = 9(c-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="365" height="18" />
</p><p>The difference between the two squares is three times this amount or
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/e/1deb43ec94fc31fd9a335332010939da64a20993.png" class="latex" alt="$27(c-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />
</p><p>The difference between two consecutive squares is always an odd number, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/1/fc114726036366222ae428560254f3ea294db9c9.png" class="latex" alt="$c-b$" width="38" height="12" /> is odd. We will show that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/1/fc114726036366222ae428560254f3ea294db9c9.png" class="latex" alt="$c-b$" width="38" height="12" /> must be 1. Otherwise we would be looking for two consecutive squares that are at least 81 apart. But already the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/c/eecbf7cf681c7bb20b24a4a8f9380fe9c8dda705.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)^2-x^2 = 27\cdot 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="19" /> solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/5/b858aee871cc6fe6149cf70fbb27f55339ad7556.png" class="latex" alt="$x=40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/1/6212ca78555150d1747270c7f186c1cae3bae46a.png" class="latex" alt="$40^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> has more than three digits.
</p><p>The consecutive squares with common difference <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/1051e7d41ec0b8bd688a872be17538413f4ee97b.png" class="latex" alt="$27$" width="18" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919d9080d869b82e91c713b3e935c2d52013deef.png" class="latex" alt="$13^2=169$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/0/66088e6728b88de29fc7449eb1277adadb20d91d.png" class="latex" alt="$14^2=196$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" />. One third of the way between them is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4bc6999fdc87c37c2ea98fb8e1a54c8a0f2ce7b.png" class="latex" alt="$178$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and two thirds of the way is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d78f7dacc6d1378d13ecf0204600103e905a16c.png" class="latex" alt="$187$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" />.
</p><p>This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/e/3fe4d6ebef2a09e428f2984fb2d677c342dbe1bf.png" class="latex" alt="$b=7$" width="41" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/3/fa39557a4d886b45466417ae28c5b0a3a92ba7ef.png" class="latex" alt="$c=8$" width="40" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/f/faff7103ed16f96986d44a83f90765c77f4fd70a.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c = 16 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>One-third the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c82cf76c497ec62c04f433242f343a4645d2b0c2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/c/00cda1c7e9aa6397fb7a01b6770ccb8e2a8a6e17.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2x^2 + (x+1)^2}{3} = \frac{3x^2+2x+1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="242" height="39" />.
</p><p>Since this must be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/3/0030f5d9039df6d059d0f793590c2e046191f725.png" class="latex" alt="$3x^2+2x+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="16" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/c/fbc1630d29240c6b0d7fdf84094695f0ecb0299a.png" class="latex" alt="$3x^2$" width="25" height="15" /> is always divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f8fc82e121c01034fef87ac28f73e7d4c0b465c.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="13" /> must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, x must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/8/f98ba4bda314627fcae48f239b6fb2fab116b95d.png" class="latex" alt="$10, 13, 16, 19, 22, 25,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="153" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" />. (1, 4, and 7 don't work because their squares are too small)
</p><p>Guessing and checking, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638f55cd5570facc2150ef8519bc5d4244f0232e.png" class="latex" alt="$x=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> works, so the integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" /> is one-third of the way from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/3629b1f1f3b22f1a3c927c7ac0467574394f17ee.png" class="latex" alt="$169$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/f/c5f40cb7585c44d066022164abae4d42af57a926.png" class="latex" alt="$196$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4bc6999fdc87c37c2ea98fb8e1a54c8a0f2ce7b.png" class="latex" alt="$178$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/b/4abf1fbdeebc8921d426d4f7836da903a513ef11.png" class="latex" alt="$1+7+8 = 16.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="117" height="14" />
</p><p>- JN5537
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> be the lesser of the two integers. Then the squares of the integers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/6/ea63478562de10edc65b50b25f1f6d54b792f943.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2+2k+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="16" />, and the distance between them is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f2789508227865dfc89fd46e18ae9b25bff34257.png" class="latex" alt="$2k+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" />. Let this be equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b26c8398882a95a9099c7b9516439dd6b467c65.png" class="latex" alt="$3d$" width="18" height="12" />, so that the one-third of the distance between the squares is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. The numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da43f2f76250b78d4606efd11b3fa19bf6727d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$acb$" width="25" height="12" /> are one-third and two-thirds of the way between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1879a44818d1c5ee8286585d4d4b7e3df1d286.png" class="latex" alt="$(k+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" />. Therefore, the distance between these two numbers is also one-third the distance between the squares, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Setting these equal to each other, we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/f/b9fe17db66f5d208d377f4452ae14cd3eaee703e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2k+1}{3} = 9(c-b)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="138" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a739aba67e19c94a1efad31682308aa6253ecdef.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 2k+1 = 27(c-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Notice that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are digits, their difference is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Also notice that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da43f2f76250b78d4606efd11b3fa19bf6727d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$acb$" width="25" height="12" /> is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/7/4f773d031c2cc283675e9da7ef72d7bd5d1a82db.png" class="latex" alt="$c &gt; b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. Representing this as an inequality, we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/0/b60a15d817d32979382a1db8a6b6a9fabd3493c6.png" class="latex" alt="$27 \le 27(c-b) \le 243$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f2789508227865dfc89fd46e18ae9b25bff34257.png" class="latex" alt="$2k+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" />, we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/c/2ccd9cb03344dc133afec2c842ec988fdb05de40.png" class="latex" alt="$27 \le 2k+1 \le 243$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="143" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/9/b4945e5101b41ffeddb98d9657d4055b502a8886.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 13 \le k \le 121$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="15" />.
</p><p><br />
However, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-digit number, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" /> is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" /> must be at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0a3589706a0df340ebe663e060a8d5448a915c.png" class="latex" alt="$961$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/6/5f669669e3e9848f2b03c6b15cb55fc2a258fcf7.png" class="latex" alt="$31^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/7/7c749b617c3d50a144ac81b89e4aeb9ec235e972.png" class="latex" alt="$k \le 31$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" />. Plugging this back into our inequality, we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/c/fcce615dcf6077bb054bae3d4ed9f7cfacc0a5db.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \le k \le 31$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="93" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/a/91a289b182c5cbf6e522f3a3a76b0a17f7835317.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 27 \le 2k+1 \le63$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="158" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63b0f9e4e1a8e843bf96c8e18af821bfebf4845.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 27 \le 27(c-b) \le 63$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/1846a70be330e3ad73e720f9f604707743806709.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 1 \le (c-b) \le \frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="144" height="37" />.
</p><p><br />
But (c-b) must be an integer, so now we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/f/28f6314ce54a6544d76010ece3fea396e4a43d0d.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \le (c-b) \le 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a78661eed01c1b2e3e8e3ab408b2ee71eff6ca5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 27 \le 27(c-b) \le 54$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/3/7639f76c2f8237dd352e786be3e096a7e32d0f5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 13 \le k \le\frac{53}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="120" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is also an integer, so now we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/e/74e057105b76ab6eb8ed4393fec0f15050a367f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 13 \le k \le 26$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="117" height="15" />

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/c/b4c1c1ce9bbe8b794ebbf58bdf2efcb6adc012f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 27 \le 27(c-b) \le 53$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" />

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/6/da62c58ef0e2fdfb565422b666b636f61058f43e.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow (c-b) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/a/fda47c9d737e0694f18682c9eea8870361d6008f.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 27(c-b) = 27$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="135" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/6/256204f0bfcf0ce1e3c414df0e575730398b7e16.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 2k+1 = 27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b0ed981bc10a7d97a39b9aa74b9b7d0544b834.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow k = 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" />
</p><p><br />
The two squares are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/5/cd54c455a244ae417c0bc931f46d0a3e30b7e79e.png" class="latex" alt="$13^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/0/3109e9835beb0c0d1b81d84e79a1b92dc266b8dd.png" class="latex" alt="$14^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/3629b1f1f3b22f1a3c927c7ac0467574394f17ee.png" class="latex" alt="$169$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/f/c5f40cb7585c44d066022164abae4d42af57a926.png" class="latex" alt="$196$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. A third of the distance between them is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/206f5cbe1a9ecb22d8d5e5d8eb18f584a2aeeee7.png" class="latex" alt="$169 +9 = 178$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/f/55f69956773eb25e263ae5347152541af1053735.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 7 + 8 = 16 \Rightarrow \boxed{\text{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="166" height="23" />.
</p>
