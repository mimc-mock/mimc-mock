<p>How many pairs of positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> are there such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/33501dbf46dacbfb604efa46ac2355f96f5e988e.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{gcd}(a,b)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/8/f38930f7390056f0c5a65aa203710d5c180e6f60.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b} + \frac{14b}{9a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" /> is an integer?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/2/4424219e12056e5ba82c32c55f373bef210d6dba.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)}\ 4\quad\mathrm {(B)}\ 6\quad\mathrm {(C)}\ 9\quad\mathrm {(D)}\ 12\quad\mathrm {(E)}\ \text{infinitely many}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Combining the fraction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f663fc6c123d8b3adb0e38160865031c47ebc994.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9a^2 + 14b^2}{9ab}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="39" /> must be an integer.
</p><p>Since the denominator contains a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b84c8593b109997d798f8f34ee6f1ecedc7e8e.png" class="latex" alt="$9 | 9a^2 + 14b^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 9 | b^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 3 | b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="292" height="19" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/8/9c8b6e1c6e45fb00f1f97a4164f2c721139413e6.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 3n$" width="51" height="12" /> for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we can rewrite the fraction(divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> on both top and bottom) as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/a/0aafd3df58c0236dda45c982ba24a8b31a01baa8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a^2 + 14n^2}{3an}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="39" />
</p><p>Since the denominator now contains a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26b34cdc00642edfec2824ca47949903a2d61840.png" class="latex" alt="$n | a^2 + 14n^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad n | a^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="19" />.
</p><p>But since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/f/a0fef2f5b97ab4f7e69d51c6ac2518c218dc479c.png" class="latex" alt="$1=\gcd(a,b)=\gcd(a,3n)=\gcd(a,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="296" height="18" />, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" /> the original fraction simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d6a01d24cbc4abb433c152ccb6f37e89a1040e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a^2 + 14}{3a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="39" />.
</p><p>For that to be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, and therefore we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/0/550fd932a84d593cc956a7aff3e5968e73a4226d.png" class="latex" alt="$a\in\{1,2,7,14\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" />. Each of these values does indeed yield an integer.
</p><p>Thus there are four solutions: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b6a9f4b07be4cb5a24874bb192da96ca853ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c74fb9c2531de69ae495a9220765cd08a070c92.png" class="latex" alt="$(7,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/0/4e0884f75fdcc19bf7a0602f86c0e2621719af06.png" class="latex" alt="$(14,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f4283cfd441270cb90bfd2cf89a533f3dde81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/a/a4a1dc853eaff7fa81936541e05ed1d7ab8f8b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$u = \frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="33" />. Then the given equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ffb27a73815e3c36f21d954c5807fe169083a0f.png" class="latex" alt="$u + \frac{14}{9u} = \frac{9u^2 + 14}{9u}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="150" height="39" />.
</p><p>Let's set this equal to some value, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/7/21764bf7252e56fe69b5e1bef1310028e6f1c14f.png" class="latex" alt="$k \Rightarrow \frac{9u^2 + 14}{9u} = k$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="143" height="39" />.
</p><p>Clearing the denominator and simplifying, we get a quadratic in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f85d657b2a120ff95fa017958c6c5061fce971d.png" class="latex" alt="$9u^2 - 9ku + 14 = 0 \Rightarrow u = \frac{9k \pm \sqrt{(9k)^2 - 504}}{18}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="41" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> is a rational number. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601d1b26baa9989d112a60febd19786ce1fe460a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(9k)^2 - 504}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="108" height="32" /> is an integer.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/e/e4ecfbe6171d76b2efb197ce727e46b43a9547ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(9k)^2 - 504} = x$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="142" height="32" />. Squaring and rearranging yields:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/f/a7f102057a0889f504b7d5a374d24cb2be1053c2.png" class="latex" alt="$(9k)^2 - x^2 = 504$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="132" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/a/5ca81d9b0b2b8d34338448e2137eba7647e9633a.png" class="latex" alt="$(9k+x)(9k-x) = 504$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="183" height="18" />.
</p><p>In order for both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/9/8f94966c8c25df3316a5e14bcd9866b99c22887e.png" class="latex" alt="$9k + x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/8/b28428dad65bcc7edfcce7d9f7b6f51a4121fe5b.png" class="latex" alt="$9k - x$" width="51" height="12" /> must both be odd or even. (This is easily proven using modular arithmetic.) In the case of this problem, both must be even. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/8/ed8ac03c9a9bca355a3ce53f60bcc25b5736f491.png" class="latex" alt="$9k + x = 2m$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/c/d9c5e206e44156278beed047d51f47c3009483e8.png" class="latex" alt="$9k - x = 2n$" width="95" height="12" />.
</p><p>Then:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/0/a2000ef32262a0ae0abfb2a739a922442d6c663c.png" class="latex" alt="$2m \cdot 2n = 504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/0/9c01ae02560694cd8c46a879e916792b0dbbfff9.png" class="latex" alt="$mn = 126$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" />.
</p><p>Factoring 126, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> pairs of numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/3/5233c206d262b8b35a886401900f1ceee7800aac.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,126), (2,63), (3,42), (6,21), (7,18),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="292" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/c/fbcfa86251c741dfd2fb268c99e85f1b595c746b.png" class="latex" alt="$(9,14)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />.
</p><p>Looking back at our equations for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we can solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/f/b9f04a34c8b558dbe0f82ea63ca65593e5a62074.png" class="latex" alt="$k = \frac{2m + 2n}{18} = \frac{m+n}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="181" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is an integer, there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> that work: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/1637f9de583707df1d29571386e82d2a9689bde5.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,42)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/e/6be71810e56ee3b3a8c9b8ea2335d0e379a453ab.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,21)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />. This means that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer. But looking back at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/91205e81c98c486418902c88d5c396f5355491f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="13" height="12" />, meaning that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> for every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. </p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Rewriting the expression over a common denominator yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f663fc6c123d8b3adb0e38160865031c47ebc994.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9a^2 + 14b^2}{9ab}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="39" />. This expression must be equal to some integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33ff354f978da3272eae41b8627e3abb72f2db9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9a^2 + 14b^2}{9ab} = m \rightarrow 9a^2 + 14b^2 = 9abm$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="302" height="39" />. Taking this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/f/91f7ef7da0dc90830076968200b9da24ebdffaa9.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{a}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/d/96da157c0409b5c70d6389632ae8f758a14db74e.png" class="latex" alt="$14b^2 \equiv 0\pmod{a}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="138" height="19" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/b/bcb04971aa1521dca22b49b27b1c7c026accd676.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a,b)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/9/f39ee155046bec769d6a8ea409be63cde4f18334.png" class="latex" alt="$14 \equiv 0\pmod{a}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="18" />. This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e649a33f7bb64385686796f30b8ea35cfe998a63.png" class="latex" alt="$a|14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34e9c29cf00cf026b132eb7c48983f4150b3cbd7.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1, 2, 7, 14$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="15" />.
</p><p>We can then take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/e/cbe2e96516ce52324c72271158ddb4b3442a7b9a.png" class="latex" alt="$9a^2 + 14b^2 = 9abm \pmod{b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="218" height="19" /> to get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/d/a1d5c6c795e46f16f17f2692d2349541eb3d9a86.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \equiv 0 \pmod{b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/2/2f20ed1428dbb851b7daa62fc08ba6894039fa09.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1, 3, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />.
</p><p>However, taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/2954560702464a5bd7f59dc78b62ab5aa55b3491.png" class="latex" alt="$9a^2 + 14b^2 = 9abm \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="219" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/6/d5669d9ff63705bd1095a3b88a3f389bcfa461b6.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 \equiv 0\pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="19" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot equal 1.
</p><p>Also, note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f0e621ddfd0f3f4728f2161b52587224c273dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 9$" width="40" height="12" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/d/54d96742f944941e55a4256a39a57b56486695e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b}+\frac{14b}{9a} = \frac{a}{9}+\frac{14}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e649a33f7bb64385686796f30b8ea35cfe998a63.png" class="latex" alt="$a|14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/8709d320d666cfc4cf7c1521b2559fffb72f5c7a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{14}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> will be an integer, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/1/3a1ef4f3abd7a21cf7ddb4891ab8efd160b9bf8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> will not be an integer since none of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> are multiples of 9. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot equal 9.
</p><p>Thus, the only possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is 3, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> can be 1, 2, 7, or 14. This yields 4 possible solutions, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f4283cfd441270cb90bfd2cf89a533f3dde81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
