<p>The first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/d/b0db9f93bcba529c93ae151a1ac5c740eec7cca3.png" class="latex" alt="$2007$" width="36" height="12" /> positive integers are each written in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. How many of these base-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> representations are palindromes? (A palindrome is a number that reads the same forward and backward.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/7/da787aba0e82066275fff66867315489a9fb80f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)}\ 100 \qquad \mathrm {(B)}\ 101 \qquad \mathrm {(C)}\ 102 \qquad \mathrm {(D)}\ 103 \qquad \mathrm {(E)}\ 104$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="433" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/e/69e07ef12dbb3793564433c2ec99bfe3ea493754.png" class="latex" alt="$2007_{10} = 2202100_{3}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="144" height="15" />
</p><p>All numbers of six or less digits in base 3 have been written.
</p><p>The form of each palindrome is as follows
</p><p>1 digit  - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />
</p><p>2 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/e/73e64f15931d04c43c468a09ad35a6000c181de9.png" class="latex" alt="$aa$" width="19" height="8" />
</p><p>3 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/6/4562767217eaa6e7acb7b2d04cfc167404e8a282.png" class="latex" alt="$aba$" width="27" height="12" />
</p><p>4 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8ea7e886a23b05912c996d7cdb6d08f7bc7868c.png" class="latex" alt="$abba$" width="35" height="12" />
</p><p>5 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/2/f8211da70cc567d5d14763c89fd27d304af7581a.png" class="latex" alt="$abcba$" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p>6 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/f/06f8a5a64cde808cca6f219a74eea385d9c92866.png" class="latex" alt="$abccba$" width="51" height="12" />
</p><p>Where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> are base 3 digits
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a8181c9d9b8253bd721b620aaabc5b01f4af96.png" class="latex" alt="$a \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />, this gives a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/d/64d051f2068f509e09da8627310707bbdda2be0f.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 2 + 2\cdot 3 + 2\cdot 3 + 2\cdot 3^2 + 2\cdot 3^2 = 52$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="311" height="16" /> palindromes so far.
</p><p>7 digits - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/4/014c83f3134a8c97bd6ca63e071ef8261a930f60.png" class="latex" alt="$abcdcba$" width="60" height="12" />, but not all of the numbers are less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/d/81d9d08edd64340a0d2e9f22243881b516961dc3.png" class="latex" alt="$2202100_{3}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="15" />
</p><p>Case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />
</p><p>All of these numbers are less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/d/4cd49cedcbf92cf1a61ea3ca092a61f216f0c336.png" class="latex" alt="$2202100_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="15" /> giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/a/c4aaecd00c115e0a1f5d807ac00b10f428d07993.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3$" width="15" height="15" /> more palindromes
</p><p>Case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/9/9e9ceda87245f63176b97209db23bd331b4ea3e9.png" class="latex" alt="$b\neq 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="17" />
</p><p>All of these numbers are also small enough, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/0/fd034d8e03f2d4e5812223063fff3ff2e02e3f6e.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 3^2$" width="37" height="15" /> more palindromes
</p><p>Case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80d7a3ca845e6cae14b9db710b64331219ec3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$c=0$" width="40" height="12" />, since any other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> would make the value too large. This leaves the number as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/9/0e962eeae55e21e7accc7ae6113ef6cfd64e1ae2.png" class="latex" alt="$220d022_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="15" />. Checking each value of d, all of the three are small enough, so that gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> more palindromes.
</p><p>Summing our cases there are
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/8/ca8c3342c3689a8e3e0091ce2a948c8a8c9049ab.png" class="latex" alt="$52 + 3^3 + 2\cdot 3^2 + 3 = 100 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="255" height="19" />
</p>
