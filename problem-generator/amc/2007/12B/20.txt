<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Plotting the parallelogram on the coordinate plane, the 4 corners are at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/9/64945ac20f0b79963940375303bccc6816f465ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,c),(0,d),\left(\frac{d-c}{a-b},\frac{ad-bc}{a-b}\right),\left(\frac{c-d}{a-b},\frac{bc-ad}{a-b}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="381" height="43" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/214db40588f910fb995de798fa07daeb674e0826.png" class="latex" alt="$72= 4\cdot 18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa34b110b154dbb1238e5d1090ca1b8fa33d592.png" class="latex" alt="$4(c-d)\left(\frac{c-d}{a-b}\right) = (c+d)\left(\frac{c+d}{a-b}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="283" height="43" /> or that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a7420ce775ed8aaf3707f37384aafdcb9186c1fe.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c-d)=c+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />, which gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/8/bf8131fe1cfd4f1b266ef741f49383972b055337.png" class="latex" alt="$c=3d$" width="50" height="12" /> (consider a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Homothety" title="Homothety">homothety</a>, or dilation, that carries the first parallelogram to the second parallelogram; because the area increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/c/2cc666ca309cab7d697328f67800cc890a5c7810.png" class="latex" alt="$4\times$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="20" height="12" />, it follows that the stretch along the diagonal, or the ratio of side lengths, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df2c9dc6107531e6aaba67c34db01915d7317456.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times$" width="20" height="12" />). The area of the triangular half of the parallelogram on the right side of the y-axis is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/b/50b5f22b75bac31b3b314502ef5a84de933ceec6.png" class="latex" alt="$9 = \frac{1}{2} (c-d)\left(\frac{d-c}{a-b}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="169" height="43" />, so substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/a/08ae5fe79b0088fbc5fe867ce89389524a569f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 3d$" width="50" height="12" />:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/e/51edc5dfccf7d46f4df539eb3d687c0b4d8f4c04.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2} (c-d)\left(\frac{c-d}{a-b}\right) = 9 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad 2d^2 = 9(a-b)\]" width="355" height="43" /></center>
<p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/2/35215e96bad1127c301362a1e786cf63116c8fa6.png" class="latex" alt="$3|d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="18" />, and we verify that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/e/91e7679b01e114773e2822a8fd5615ea83def33c.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 3$" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/0/fb0c85d9d4c94e51b9312aa1117d17efdf9cb29b.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b = 2 \Longrightarrow a = 3, b = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="203" height="16" /> will give us a minimum value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/4/e947add5766b9f26f6dfe73d9425d87664416459.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="14" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/4/6d460ec6324d63fb0eb7a3a06eb61ffeba0541eb.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d = 3 + 1 + 9 + 3 = \boxed{\mathbf{(D)} 16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="313" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The key to this solution is that area is invariant under translation. By suitably shifting the plane, the problem is mapped to the lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e308352ff28cea50d343817d49f0bf50ff9a6e8.png" class="latex" alt="$c,d,(b-a)x+c,(b-a)x+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/8/3681ea8617c4e14fe0b6da0b92e6821cf4695244.png" class="latex" alt="$c,-d,(b-a)x+c,(b-a)x-d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="18" />. Now, the area of the parallelogram contained by is the former is equal to the area of a rectangle with sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/481572680b92ec13cdbe3dd8dfe8f237ce986bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$d-c$" width="39" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/3/5b37912a90aa5d2f7c43f9980bf3177c8e93dd8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{d-c}{b-a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ff4c2fefea6d30ab9b05ced0f5a324cbe79394d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(d-c)^2}{b-a}=18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="39" />, and the area contained by the latter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/5/1852ea1f5868b0a94216e0f4e17222057db4708a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(c+d)^2}{b-a}=72$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="39" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/d/dcde41eb3044c3318efa8cf3a1fa2e24feb9405f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=3c$" width="50" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/c/02c60c151331e235dc6d9df7ca403b6f0c9af39b.png" class="latex" alt="$b-a$" width="39" height="12" /> must be even if the former quantity is to equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/f/04fbd04983c19e00d00461b51b487b4ad8dae2b8.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2=18(b-a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="19" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Putting this all together, the minimal solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b2966422aff71475260f37f019e4de32cd0663.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(3,1,3,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />, so the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/7/e67089c0833553b8f2f53a8ad801c45f2be4ac22.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} 16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="59" height="28" />.
</p>

The area of the larger parallelogram is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/1/6116f2504ea6b6692148adf643a447f23b824e4a.png" class="latex" alt="$b-1*h_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="72" height="15" />, or
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c71ef0dce2bb9c7c20d30ad1e404e90320364e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(c+d) * \frac{c+d}{b-a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/4/044fbfce9c72065fa901be21fcdbe588bcd4129e.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \frac{(c+d)^2}{b-a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="39" />.
</p><p><br />
The areas of the parallelograms are given to us: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" />. Therefore we can set up a ratio:
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/d/3cda1d91a599d6a39f943e15feafa382bf654d68.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{18}{72} = \frac{\frac{(c-d)^2}{b-a}}{\frac{(c+d)^2}{b-a}}$" style="vertical-align: -21px" width="93" height="55" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/1/a4181da5d9d32a63019ab9d5e1ef4a24eee714be.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 18(c+d)^2 = 72(c-d)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/4/ab46b66fbcf4ac2ebec866cd18dee3d6a22b4004.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow (c+d)^2 = 4(c-d)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06bdeba92bbd904f7444dd691d2a07382262bc1d.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow c^2 + 2cd + d^2 = 4c^2 - 8cd + 4d^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="270" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/500c299aa03b38eec8f566bbea72475e0aff4ec5.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow 3c^2 - 10cd +3d^2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="186" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/4/4444cbbc7d239484632f3880ad5acb1250990938.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow (3c-d)(c-3d)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/6778d7e58db9e51babdc115acc4531621ba35aa8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow c=3d, c=\frac{d}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="125" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
We established earlier that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/1/9610f673df65a6ad936ecda11d4aa3444437f286.png" class="latex" alt="$c&gt;d$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/8/bf8131fe1cfd4f1b266ef741f49383972b055337.png" class="latex" alt="$c=3d$" width="50" height="12" />. Plugging this into the intial equations yields
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/d/ffd2ce31830b1c5a7c70e9a8278da50717823c0f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{16d^2}{b-a} = 72$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="85" height="39" />
</p><p><br />
and
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/2/4b28adc817cd9d0563467695d42cdd4da7515578.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4d^2}{b-a} = 18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="85" height="39" />
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we get
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23cf90277e63695d47469d5ff194da0a906f75a7.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 3\sqrt{\frac{b-a}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="104" height="43" />
</p><p><br />
We want the sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. We can now rewrite this
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/8/17834c205ae4943c864515cabc189d38211453e6.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + 12\sqrt{\frac{b-a}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="142" height="43" />
</p><p><br />
We are told that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are all positive integers. Therefore the value under the radical must be a perfect square greater than 0. We can rewrite this
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/2/b62b701aed87343607c0ec1445d755ec602e05fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b-a}{2} = k^2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is some positive integer.
</p><p>Rearranging, we get
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/5/dc502b47e16c9938bbfbec59eec3f0e44ee59eb0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = a + 2k^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="16" />
</p><p>Now we can rewrite the sum as
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33dec55e4ab982ff81b634de031f1476bcea9838.png" class="latex" alt="$a + a + 2k^2 +12k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="16" />.
</p><p><br />
Since both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> must be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the minimum value is
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/f/e9f1c8afe45f7885b851340304fec62a92846388.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 1 + 2(1)^2 + 12(1) = 16 \Rightarrow \boxed{\text{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="261" height="23" />.
</p>
