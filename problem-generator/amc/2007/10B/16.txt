<p>A teacher gave a test to a class in which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the students are juniors and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/870303feff6f491471061a7402eba3e326ba84f2.png" class="latex" alt="$90\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> are seniors. The average score on the test was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e90ce936bbdc9bfbb57310688e750e5c2e8d5ed.png" class="latex" alt="$84.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" /> The juniors all received the same score, and the average score of the seniors was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340c60bb264b4bfe39d369ebbddf0ac0cfdf527c.png" class="latex" alt="$83.$" width="21" height="12" /> What score did each of the juniors receive on the ikd?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08f5abbc0bbb3a7d58849f2af25547466117ecfa.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 85 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 88 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 93 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 94 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 98$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We can assume there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> people in the class. Then there will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> junior and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> seniors. The sum of everyone's scores is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea771a7944b62990722e32f5081e1291847af05e.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \cdot 84 = 840.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" /> Since the average score of the seniors was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/6/3464b14a930b57ded425b36433a53cf9f7126c07.png" class="latex" alt="$83,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> the sum of all the senior's scores is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28e26867198972a865605f331e8a230bb9a82fb1.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \cdot 83 = 747.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="95" height="13" /> The only score that has not been added to that is the junior's score, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f9766b19dcc3cf7c1622f14d7a232af08241e905.png" class="latex" alt="$840 - 747 = \boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 93}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="163" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the average score of the juniors be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" />. The problem states the average score of the seniors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c1ad4a48099624f3bbfc16590f9be2d29351d61.png" class="latex" alt="$83$" width="17" height="12" />. The equation for the average score of the class (juniors and seniors combined) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/3/0538e82d5e69d882775d8594b62d92d2ffa2f096.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{j}{10} + \frac{83 \cdot 9}{10} = 84$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="131" height="38" />. Simplifying this equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/2/b52da35744928bc3d709eb99ad0b14cd6d3d2a2b.png" class="latex" alt="$j = \boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 93}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="96" height="29" />
</p><p>~mobius247
</p>
