<p>Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is inside equilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />. Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> are the feet of the perpendiculars from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/b/fbb0f1d8a43539dbc3355734bafb2bfd855b5f87.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CA}$" width="28" height="15" />, respectively. Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7e9124d05323b7632765d5d9da3cece95300c2.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b96d4cc26e2b9b1d6c0811c525430e63c3659b.png" class="latex" alt="$PR=2$" width="61" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/411e321190286862c90ff5d3c65cbd3c62ce012e.png" class="latex" alt="$PS=3$" width="59" height="12" />, what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad76f57ad6c1bda24f4e49918dbcc052a0916119.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 3\sqrt{3} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 6 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 4\sqrt{3} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="390" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Drawing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/3/be30a7d4898ac6bc1f824ca3f331a7fdb5ed617d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PA}$" width="28" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/0/4d0de57f8b55944ab4532ea225957cdcfff7da09.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PB}$" width="29" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a894a620b157358dab619a863fdea6c368d3d0cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PC}$" width="28" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is split into three smaller triangles. The altitudes of these triangles are given in the problem as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/6/ec6916b50feec24773d374eaada26a95bcabc6fd.png" class="latex" alt="$PR$" width="28" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/5/805452ee372321839953fbd750cd9b23b973a3df.png" class="latex" alt="$PS$" width="26" height="12" />.
</p><p>Summing the areas of each of these triangles and equating it to the area of the entire triangle, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6bacba6cae717b6b1e81eb82d97b264b996dd17c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{s}{2} + \frac{2s}{2} + \frac{3s}{2} = \frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4}\]" width="165" height="41" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the length of a side of the equilateral triangle
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/d/ddd17ba78b8e87e6d688b7af272364fd4811013b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s = \boxed{\mathrm{(D) \ } 4\sqrt{3}}\]" width="111" height="33" />
</p>
<ul><li>Note - This is called <a href="/wiki/index.php/Viviani%27s_Theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Viviani&#39;s Theorem">Viviani's Theorem</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The 60 degree angles suggest constructing 30-60-90 triangles. As such, let the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. Also, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/4/d34d0e3d9114ffe3b6dd37600e05ba31b87b823c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{PQD}=60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" />, let the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/1/f91d788b86016360831215e533c0638b307bc684.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{QD}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="19" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/a/63a7dfd2eff8db1b6ce6e09fcd86e1d670ab6183.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{QEP}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> is 30-60-90, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7e9124d05323b7632765d5d9da3cece95300c2.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a931d8a6620b9043090c27f46d0053a1a0ce7f46.png" class="latex" alt="$QE=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />. Also, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b96d4cc26e2b9b1d6c0811c525430e63c3659b.png" class="latex" alt="$PR=2$" width="61" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/8815f905e685a019987d1d3f44e840ddad55007b.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=2$" width="63" height="12" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/9/2e9a8cfe84d5d0f18180a75ca61e400f441355b3.png" class="latex" alt="$QD=\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="69" height="38" />. Once again, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b1816d77868838da9b7d559c72f2e6fa44d9348a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{QBD}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> is 30-60-90, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/e/30e9c223a15eb458ac958cfe626e6d520eafdc32.png" class="latex" alt="$QB=\frac{5\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="92" height="43" />. Similar reasoning to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/0/0400d2a78f3adac267e7621c65d063b35fe53ab1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AQ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="19" /> and summing the segments yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/6/f869997ab9b49c7299953272e862dfe923b3e189.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(D) 4\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="76" height="35" />
</p>
