<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> ways to choose the first square. The four remaining squares in its row and column and the square you chose exclude nine squares from being chosen next time.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> remaining blocks to be chosen for the second square. The three remaining spaces in its row and column and the square you chose must be excluded from being chosen next time.
</p><p>Finally, the last square has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> remaining choices.
</p><p>The number of ways to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> squares is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/0/460f606d78dbfff6d82628b29d30321aec667118.png" class="latex" alt="$25 \cdot 16 \cdot 9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> but the order in which you chose the squares does not matter as the blocks are indistinguishable, so we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/6859713adc973d3fbe97fafef9ce829757e54b12.png" class="latex" alt="$3!$" width="12" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/1/cc16a325891282648c5127af09cca9adef42b279.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{25 \cdot 16 \cdot 9}{3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1} = 25 \cdot 8 \cdot 3 = 100 \cdot 6 = \boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 600}\]" width="330" height="38" />
</p>

<p>Once we choose our three squares, we will have occupied three separate columns <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59db3616e539878054b9dd007abeb26d5fcde87d.png" class="latex" alt="$(A, B, C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and three separate rows. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/0/b200084dd6ff391d1b1d5ffeb2121e37873f6f71.png" class="latex" alt="${5 \choose 3} \times {5 \choose 3}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="92" height="53" /> ways to choose these rows and columns. 
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to assign the square in column <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to a row, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to assign the square in column <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to one of the remaining two rows, and poor square in column <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> doesn't get to choose. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/b/e1b2d01bd4bc57aff63131d1c4ad46446d534a81.png" class="latex" alt="$:($" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="16" height="18" />
