<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> denote the smallest positive integer that is divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/1288f16f77606bb83db76df0877615ef6e8b149e.png" class="latex" alt="$9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and whose base-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> representation consists of only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />'s and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />'s, with at least one of each. What are the last four digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a28578f91569670ce8f5d1c27b4ea637a09a75a9.png" class="latex" alt="$n?$" width="19" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e899090e32e4888e3a6386d0086d42c6c1ca15bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 4444 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 4494 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 4944 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 9444 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 9944$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="489" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>For a number to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4054552638cf9f29e857a1306bd3ec31df3c19b6.png" class="latex" alt="$4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> the last two digits have to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/1/cb15a16cca90d201094265ee529e9e2cfb113de8.png" class="latex" alt="$4.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> That means the last two digits of this integer must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/1/cb15a16cca90d201094265ee529e9e2cfb113de8.png" class="latex" alt="$4.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>For a number to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/1288f16f77606bb83db76df0877615ef6e8b149e.png" class="latex" alt="$9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> the sum of all the digits must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a1b9d5cec741422bfb37f91db47fd7514f5377.png" class="latex" alt="$9.$" width="12" height="12" /> The only way to make this happen is with nine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />'s. However, we also need one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a1b9d5cec741422bfb37f91db47fd7514f5377.png" class="latex" alt="$9.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>The smallest integer that meets all these conditions is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/3/9e3d9315f52aad60db88b49b77dd9ded12d35cae.png" class="latex" alt="$4444444944$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="13" />. The last four digits are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d33a136bc14c6622fc9884bf58dc97b150a8e66b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 4944}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="81" height="29" />
</p>
