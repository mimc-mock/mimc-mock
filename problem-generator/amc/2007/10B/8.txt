<p>On the trip home from the meeting where this AMC10 was constructed, the Contest Chair noted that his airport parking receipt had digits of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/0/750318af9332f126098284c0f1c245ee98bbd990.png" class="latex" alt="$bbcac,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/2/ba2c5d23ab390f2932f264ea4dd58cbf9202c4d8.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le a &lt; b &lt; c \le 9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="144" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> was the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/201a2eef545643b50b07fed5c09e894bcced5c58.png" class="latex" alt="$c.$" width="11" height="8" /> How many different five-digit numbers satisfy all these properties?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/8/bf8d0a236014bd8cec8b7a4883a3b149b577b7ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 12 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 16 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 18 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 20 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>For the average, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d6265e67657b5886ce257671d45ff9c0282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="16" /> to be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> have to either both be odd or both be even. 
There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/315908d376e842f20449f857fad9c45a38f22a5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5 \times 4}{2 \times 1} = 10$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="86" height="38" /> ways to choose a set of two even numbers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/315908d376e842f20449f857fad9c45a38f22a5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5 \times 4}{2 \times 1} = 10$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="86" height="38" /> ways to choose a set of two odd numbers. 
</p><p>Therefore, the number of five-digit numbers that satisfy these properties is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae22ff56e4dcb2e10086df6cd2f0aed50157f585.png" class="latex" alt="$10+10=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="148" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />: The numbers are separated by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>We this case with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/5/3e59a7801ca66e19a31fb9b26141ca74f631d151.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0, b=1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/7/4f72da4187cb0ff8d0471bef17474df62fe69c94.png" class="latex" alt="$c=2$" width="40" height="12" />. Following this logic, the last set we can get is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4eda9b213ae82891f1c6685952dc6057a84ba1d4.png" class="latex" alt="$a=7, b=8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/c/2bcf069d8a6c65338cedd8d4d727c3dbbc81842b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=9$" width="40" height="12" />. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> sets of numbers in this case.
</p><p><br />
Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />: The numbers are separated by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>This case starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/5/4e5403640ae658c782ad7ca4d217deccc119a17c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0, b=2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/7/4f72da4187cb0ff8d0471bef17474df62fe69c94.png" class="latex" alt="$c=2$" width="40" height="12" />. It ends with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/d/bedf89d1ab89627b69ed30d3e39cdf8ff076b6aa.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5, b=7,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/c/2bcf069d8a6c65338cedd8d4d727c3dbbc81842b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=9$" width="40" height="12" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> sets of numbers in this case.
</p><p><br />
Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />: The numbers start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/417efc231f290796b23dc09246178d7db3198f1c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0, b=3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eb76404ee79952dcc555464b061509299789b09.png" class="latex" alt="$c=6$" width="40" height="12" />. It ends with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/f/c1fe096395857681f3b324f84d87e72affbc9ba5.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3, b=6,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/c/2bcf069d8a6c65338cedd8d4d727c3dbbc81842b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=9$" width="40" height="12" />. This case has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> sets of numbers.
</p><p>It's pretty clear that there's a pattern: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> sets, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> sets, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> sets. The amount of sets per case decreases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so it's obvious Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> sets. The total amount of possible five-digit numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/e/5deb2d3bcd7258b5840ff5b29863cd5c93c07aa8.png" class="latex" alt="$8+6+4+2=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="194" height="30" />.
</p>
