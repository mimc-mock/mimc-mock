<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangle</a> with side lengths in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Ratio" title="Ratio">ratio</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/d/d1d552aca17facd930f91154ef0ea4c917a07b18.png" class="latex" alt="$3&#160;: 4&#160;: 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> is inscribed in a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">circle</a> with <a href="/wiki/index.php/Radius" title="Radius">radius</a> 3. What is the area of the triangle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a4500b2116e7df90987df7886f6cb260c1088d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 8.64\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 12\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 5\pi\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 17.28\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="426" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007_AMC12A-10.png" class="image"><img alt="2007 AMC12A-10.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//4/46/2007_AMC12A-10.png" width="375" height="359" /></a>
</p><p>Since 3-4-5 is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_triple" title="Pythagorean triple">Pythagorean triple</a>, the triangle is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a>. Since the hypotenuse is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Diameter" title="Diameter">diameter</a> of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circumcircle" title="Circumcircle">circumcircle</a>, the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/0/330d4eb9e0825bbb72878fb2e897b048925a178f.png" class="latex" alt="$2r = 6$" width="50" height="12" />. Then the other legs are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/68585bbf0a482ae2447b1c465cba8f3d4c464565.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{24}5=4.8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f8c79df895fd4c6f99d2a971b0152f79754020d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{18}5=3.6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="37" />. The area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a416b1632f89e37e1f8fc6e845cff41e1f2782.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4.8 \cdot 3.6}2 = 8.64\ \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="151" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The hypotenuse of the triangle is a diameter of the circumcircle, so it has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/7/b97ac3d6052b56dbdc7cac9881383a38b5968b5f.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 3 = 6$" width="64" height="12" />. The triangle is similar to a 3-4-5 triangle with the ratio of their side lengths equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4fc4c669518d5da6185c95ea46645895843844d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. The area of a 3-4-5 triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/417837b8f188e9405c70d90eafcab7c9fa111373.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3\cdot 4}{2} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="68" height="37" />.
The square of the ratio of their side lengths is equal to the ratio of their areas. Call the area of the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/011f6bdf2ea1bbdba3b5b3ed5411fa024f767283.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{6}{5}\right)^2 = \frac{A}{6} \Longrightarrow \frac{36}{25} = \frac{A}{6} \Longrightarrow A = \frac{36\cdot6}{25} = 8.64\ \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="401" height="46" />
</p><p>~mobius247
</p>
