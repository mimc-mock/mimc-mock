<p>How many <a href="/wiki/index.php/Ordered_pair" title="Ordered pair">ordered pairs</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> of positive <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integers</a>, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da67e35e9c517fa132e6b7d8b548f3940e80d2f.png" class="latex" alt="$m \ge n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="14" />, have the property that their squares differ by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93ee3a41d9ba3774163cb8dcfafa66c81121d0bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 4 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 6 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 9 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="339" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/d/b9dfb5d5b4fe15db2a54e11cc6df787e8cd77f3f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[m^2 - n^2 = (m+n)(m-n) = 96 = 2^{5} \cdot 3\]" width="319" height="19" /> 
</p><p>For every two factors <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/6/6d6dab9a3216ec447fcfdb7cca0f82c5b2a2eb68.png" class="latex" alt="$xy = 96$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/4/ce4763dd1f2ad5130da33257a8f57c93be5c70b6.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n=x, m-n=y \Longrightarrow m = \frac{x+y}{2}, n = \frac{x-y}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="388" height="35" />. It follows that the number of ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> is given by the number of ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/d/44d75d5c14bbb45367d915999c1b58fed63fc425.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y): xy=96, x &gt; y &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="18" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c525469c267c1e4819f93c83b8910311d1dbb48.png" class="latex" alt="$(5+1)(1+1) = 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="18" /> factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" />, which give us six pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />. However, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46ba6ed097cc88ded349bf99eea89817f135f470.png" class="latex" alt="$m,n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="34" height="11" /> are positive integers, we also need that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec8cf980fb5c70ecba6dbb7be360c5bf90a7a6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}{2}, \frac{x-y}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="35" /> are positive integers, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must have the same <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parity" title="Parity">parity</a>. Thus we exclude the factors <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/2/b42841c8067d5feab661175e6ea86780a7036859.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y) = (1,96)(3,32)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />, and we are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Similarly to the solution above, write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/6/df6859463967b7a6e938bd7666052d03ada5726b.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5\cdot3^1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="17" />. To find the number of distinct factors, add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to both exponents and multiply, which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d0c685d5d16fd64749b78fa5a4e4c36308a87b0.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot2=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> factors. Divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> must be greater than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. We don't need to worry about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> being equal because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" /> is not a perfect square. Finally, subtract the two cases above for the same reason to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Find all of the factor pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/e/23e0a6c67ee8920e23b7ec056a85d90eacad1282.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,96),(2,48),(3,32),(4,24),(6,16),(8,12).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="340" height="18" /> You can eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/b/2fbef3ece53166cf5841dc813a45a0d1fd67cee0.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,96)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> and (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/e/3cee61095261bcd4b1953f228a01a68e5435668b.png" class="latex" alt="$3,32)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> because you cannot have two numbers add to be an even number and have an odd difference at the same time without them being a decimal. You only have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> pairs left, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/8/8383af5599b1323b260a2126817d5f0a5b361c3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\; 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p><p>~HelloWorld21
</p>
