<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The only time when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/e/69e2a22645a022af1c0959d12900f5ece2b56076.png" class="latex" alt="$ad-bc$" width="57" height="12" /> is even is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df74cf2db193657a668ecbe12f59c0630c0fab2.png" class="latex" alt="$ad$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/2/fe207dd510fa3e2012d9747d93de6f9cc71bf3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$bc$" width="16" height="12" /> are of the same <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parity" title="Parity">parity</a>. The chance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df74cf2db193657a668ecbe12f59c0630c0fab2.png" class="latex" alt="$ad$" width="19" height="12" /> being odd is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/1/e2193e1eaf0882230586fa4f5a05107536ac8c46.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 \cdot \frac 12 = \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="75" height="37" />, since the only way to have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df74cf2db193657a668ecbe12f59c0630c0fab2.png" class="latex" alt="$ad$" width="19" height="12" /> be odd is to have both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be odd. As a result, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df74cf2db193657a668ecbe12f59c0630c0fab2.png" class="latex" alt="$ad$" width="19" height="12" /> has a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/1/d21715ef3fb94d12fc089969a648efb480cd5b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> probability of being even. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/2/fe207dd510fa3e2012d9747d93de6f9cc71bf3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$bc$" width="16" height="12" /> also has a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d48fb052d7fa270cfe6d5a0b5ef1f1cb1c2ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of being odd and a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/c/fdcb968f4f3d2e0d78a67fedc92a8d31183b2436.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> chance of being even. Therefore, the probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/e/69e2a22645a022af1c0959d12900f5ece2b56076.png" class="latex" alt="$ad-bc$" width="57" height="12" /> will be even is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06bc05ea90076880cf3e4cdf2dff1b4778c0c2c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 14\right)^2+\left(\frac 34\right)^2=\boxed {\mathrm{(E )} \frac 58\ }$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="198" height="49" />.
</p>
