<p>Yan is somewhere between his home and the stadium. To get to the stadium he can walk directly to the stadium, or else he can walk home and then ride his bicycle to the stadium. He rides 7 times as fast as he walks, and both choices require the same amount of time. What is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Ratio" title="Ratio">ratio</a> of Yan's distance from his home to his distance from the stadium?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36c02fd72511a1c98081e3033ddc52e0b0112a12.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 23\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 34\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 45\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 56\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac 78$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="361" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Simple_Solution">Simple Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> represent the distance from home to the stadium, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> represent the distance from Yan to home. Our goal is to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/feab8bddfcf64f51030a460d8e52a6df0f1507ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{r}{x-r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="33" />. If Yan walks directly to the stadium, then assuming he walks at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, it will take him <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/3/d93a6f70e1a466da7876f0e5970868e48100c2b6.png" class="latex" alt="$x-r$" width="40" height="8" /> units of time. Similarly, if he walks back home it will take him <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/9/ee9879dd00be62db2b25a40db63bd5e55115cfb4.png" class="latex" alt="$r + \frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="33" /> units of time. Because the two times are equal, we can create the following equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51f5ba2d98a3a15987a04c2ca081e3285b95a4b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-r = r + \frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="33" />. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb402eef276f51667ad88a5b70a0859d85fa957.png" class="latex" alt="$x-2r=\frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="33" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/766fe5fd9d7643251c5a2f893412efd3a4476de3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{7}x = 2r$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/f/c9fde3edf6f1608f341baa0924bde34ac37df459.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{r} = \frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />. This minus one is the reciprocal of what we want to find: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/0/770cfa361ffc1b466bf8fcb74b284f165eeadfba.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{3}-1 = \frac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/f/f5f471a80be8e64836eea41e2db9e8252dba001f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="57" height="48" />
</p>
