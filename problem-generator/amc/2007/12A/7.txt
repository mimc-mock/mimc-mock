<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c06de735d79f0b28c30bcbbcf96e67bb1ca42c.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> be five consecutive terms in an arithmetic sequence, and suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/e/3ce606c4782d0356e6d0bf46b6227689a777d6e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d+e=30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="174" height="14" />. Which of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/d/52d9871f0ebe04faa75b50d7055c8a05d08120c2.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> can be found?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/74784916463b94c2c05248c80fe81f06c38d3963.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{(A)} \ a\qquad \textrm{(B)}\ b\qquad \textrm{(C)}\ c\qquad \textrm{(D)}\ d\qquad \textrm{(E)}\ e$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="328" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> be the common difference between the terms.
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17eb578b02b0e257341e003a04ea99b68464123e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c-2f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/1/d0108f0c0362f604d3e1e6ffb9e34f4db70ce7a8.png" class="latex" alt="$b=c-f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="16" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/3437ab942d89f42ffef2cb4cb27634ccfa156417.png" class="latex" alt="$c=c$" width="40" height="8" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe71729db194dcf73a0317f27bbd062cffcf8c9a.png" class="latex" alt="$d=c+f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/2/7728d895da60a7f27e8629dc46abe1dcc7e20fac.png" class="latex" alt="$e=c+2f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" /></li></ul>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/9554e32ca50549a3f8153f10e96f4a4d98e7a0bd.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d+e=5c=30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="215" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eb76404ee79952dcc555464b061509299789b09.png" class="latex" alt="$c=6$" width="40" height="12" />. But we can't find any more variables, because we don't know what <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77dff1dfc319011b04dfe423c8cf864e4a1e93c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{C}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="11" height="12" />.
</p>
