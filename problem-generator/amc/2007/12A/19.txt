<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/b/9ab31a3dc0f6fe4dd1bd934c9c692a04a24c6c79.png" class="latex" alt="$k = [ABC] = \frac 12bh$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="142" height="37" />, we have that the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/2/f42b94f22fc2d2aa53ed214ce7f619ae1b76a30c.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \frac{2k}{b} = \frac{2007 \cdot 2}{223} = 18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="186" height="37" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> lies on the lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/1/d91bea3a96f40870f83374af6cdaa4deac393445.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \pm 18 \quad \mathrm{(1)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/2/a620caf6e84fb373cb6a3d27dcc405553c17e4ef.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = 9\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="18" /> using 45-45-90 triangles, so in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/9/cd96643cd0c1ad5e0733069dc425003d52253b0a.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \frac{2 \cdot 7002}{9\sqrt{2}} = 778\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="173" height="42" />. The slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so the equation of the line is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/d/53da1528b0bfe57d795ab09f8102f20a8c1b2009.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x + b \Longrightarrow b = (380) - (680) = -300 \Longrightarrow y = x - 300$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="448" height="18" />. The point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> lies on one of two <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parallel" title="Parallel">parallel</a> lines that are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ffadd3958e4cb3658e66daad06961d16124f884.png" class="latex" alt="$778\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="18" /> units away from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" />. Now take an arbitrary point on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" /> and draw the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perpendicular" title="Perpendicular">perpendicular</a> to one of the parallel lines; then draw a line straight down from the same arbitrary point. These form a 45-45-90 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/9/009cf3eeb0ff3789cc057632947cadb200ab4663.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle$" width="15" height="13" />, so the straight line down has a length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/0/7b0e08ab840a972f864c73841e2f33a25f8594b7.png" class="latex" alt="$778\sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2} = 1556$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="148" height="18" />. Now we note that the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Y-intercept" title="Y-intercept">y-intercept</a> of the parallel lines is either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f2c8114c7748e6a41e07427fb0b86f33f4d625.png" class="latex" alt="$1556$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> units above or below the y-intercept of line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" />; hence the equation of the parallel lines is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/9/3a97ec0c055a7bd412a6c1217e60207d133225c9.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x - 300 \pm 1556 \Longrightarrow x = y + 300 \pm 1556 \quad \mathrm{(2)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="382" height="18" />.
</p><p>We just need to find the intersections of these two lines and sum up the values of the x-coordinates. Substituting the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/1/f918d8b6eebd872f92f670ed94ce1f9b0280dec9.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(1)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/5/a8521fd0cf61b6327573d5ffb9d893a70b27a73a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(2)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/5/4551d3bf49479a721ee238c82214b48fb54b162f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \pm 18 + 300 \pm 1556 = 4(300) = 1200 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="375" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We are finding the intersection of two pairs of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parallel" title="Parallel">parallel</a> lines, which will form a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parallelogram" title="Parallelogram">parallelogram</a>. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Centroid" title="Centroid">centroid</a> of this parallelogram is just the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" />, which can easily be calculated to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/3/033d00f2237e98e68db687e17e1d674f77807350.png" class="latex" alt="$(300,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />. Now the sum of the x-coordinates is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/e/88e359e85387b69806121e4afaa57a65676748b8.png" class="latex" alt="$4(300) = 1200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" />.
</p>
