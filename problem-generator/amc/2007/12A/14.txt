<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> be distinct integers such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/8/e2849154cef745096a28e05e3e86ea31e6a1856d.png" class="latex" alt="$(6-a)(6-b)(6-c)(6-d)(6-e)=45$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="312" height="18" />
</p><p>What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78e9da7691c4a59208721a2da0f7bbc510d639fe.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/a/c9a377b1847fd3e43825ac624dba81dbb3a388e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 5\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 17\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 25\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 27\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is expressed as a product of five distinct integer factors, the absolute value of the product of any four is at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/e/9de237f06a7d12677f21a901606281b60a6e95ab.png" class="latex" alt="$|(-3)(-1)(1)(3)|=9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" />, so no factor can have an absolute value greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus the factors of the given expression are five of the integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf9331b081181ef7a288dc0a7b79cc306713ce9e.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm 3, \pm 1, \pm 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" />. The product of all six of these is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a77b19f5ef9c43cf2048ae5975ff5d7f5e25468.png" class="latex" alt="$-225=(-5)(45)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" />, so the factors are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce112ab6866730e0513e228bc4dd680e6739c343.png" class="latex" alt="$-3, -1, 1, 3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> The corresponding values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/d/52d9871f0ebe04faa75b50d7055c8a05d08120c2.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2aa8965e142e32176b0c5f22923336e710064c6.png" class="latex" alt="$9, 7, 5, 3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> and their sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b0908e56ba8df4006359ce5874dbc906975933.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{25 (C)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/0/a201e6b51f2b3fd8423529b0eb1004333e7a763d.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 * 5$" width="42" height="15" />. Therefore, the 5 distinct integer factors must have some negative numbers in them. Because there are two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />'s in the prime factorization, one of them must be negative and the other positive. Because there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31b51deaa1a9e903d120a02141556c3e70e348.png" class="latex" alt="$-3$" width="22" height="12" />, there must also be a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> to cancel the negatives out. The 5 distinct integer factors must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c1147644941cf13e1c8471204ff57d57d98c41a.png" class="latex" alt="$-3, 3, 5, -1, 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" />. The corresponding values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/d/52d9871f0ebe04faa75b50d7055c8a05d08120c2.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/74163e1746bfefac382b84013cf3bced18b68386.png" class="latex" alt="$9, 3, 1, 7, 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" />. and their sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b0908e56ba8df4006359ce5874dbc906975933.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{25 (C)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" />
</p>
