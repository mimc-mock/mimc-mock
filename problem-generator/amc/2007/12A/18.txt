<p>The polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/3/9a38ccd7bebcd6e9086e42c1e9dcb6e2d95970c7.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = x^{4} + ax^{3} + bx^{2} + cx + d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="19" /> has real coefficients, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/3/d935758fdc88079e80fb122b82bcdf3975e511d5.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2i) = f(2 + i) = 0.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" /> What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/0/3609992cb62090c9ffa52cc0730cece92f9bfc2c.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c + d?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/6/1c66983d26396563fed3188dca0e655854412bf2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 0 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 4 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 9 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>A fourth degree polynomial has four <a href="/wiki/index.php/Root" class="mw-redirect" title="Root">roots</a>. Since the coefficients are real(meaning that complex roots come in conjugate pairs), the remaining two roots must be the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Complex_conjugate" title="Complex conjugate">complex conjugates</a> of the two given roots. By the factor theorem, our roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7c9aed0bc1a732053db0938c79dbc9248e7c5ef.png" class="latex" alt="$2-i,-2i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" />. Now we work backwards for the polynomial:
</p>
<div style="text-align:center;"><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/2/4a2892d359087ed1923e128f7eb8ca5a29178df5.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-(2+i))(x-(2-i))(x-2i)(x+2i) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="354" height="18" /><br />
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/6/076fbe48577ba10c0d68a0f480ebd552e8f5bf65.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2 - 4x + 5)(x^2 + 4) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="19" /><br />
</p>
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/a/d0a46838b90acb80b3df33c37320d99d5ad1d974.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4 - 4x^3 + 9x^2 - 16x + 20 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="240" height="16" /></div>
<p>Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32d21baa12274267c99eec464d6ccd8549e27b2.png" class="latex" alt="$- 4 + 9 - 16 + 20 = 9\ \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="200" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Just like in Solution 1 we realize that the roots come in conjugate pairs. Which means the roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/f/9bf59023ced3d93343c5b561bb81cd79fec98566.png" class="latex" alt="$2i,i+2,-2i,2-i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="143" height="16" />
So our polynomial is  
</p><p><b>(1)</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/f/5ef29ff13864ab8479edc6e0d3650231c7a04687.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = (x-2i)(x+2i)(x-i-2)(x-2+i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="18" />
</p><p>Looking at the equation of the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/b/bebb998361742138f397bc1629c91c1911ea90dc.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = x^4+ax^3+bx^2+cx+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="19" />. We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/7/e87eb4e245e8c12e256fcde96208ee1e5b1cf175.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d = f(1)-1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="18" />
</p><p>If we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> into equation <b>(1)</b> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/1/de133dd4be3168b8ad05941acf3b003363d042d3.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1) = (1-2i)(1+2i)(-1-i)(-1+i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="310" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Now if we multiply a complex number by its conjugate we get the sum of the squares of its real and imaginary parts. Using this property on the above we multiply  and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53bcb36f74a08d153da1678009318e16cb08abfb.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1) = (1-2i)(1+2i)(-1-i)(-1+i) = (1^2+2^2)(1^2+1^2) = 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="516" height="19" />
So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10df38dcd8c1f517a39b5857f978cd9f3127412a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1) -1 = 10 - 1  = 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/8/e88dc32d72b800739cb3736b1ccaa5c08d18eda3.png" class="latex" alt="$\framebox{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />
</p>
