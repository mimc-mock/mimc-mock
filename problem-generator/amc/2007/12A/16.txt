<p>How many three-digit numbers are composed of three distinct digits such that one digit is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Average" title="Average">average</a> of the other two? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/f/f0f2d5df8de2487beedbcd854b3081b71fb3b03b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 96\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 104\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 112\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 120\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 256$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="423" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can find the number of increasing <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_sequence" title="Arithmetic sequence">arithmetic sequences</a> of length 3 possible from 0 to 9, and then find all the possible permutations of these sequences.
</p>

</td></tr>

<p>This gives us a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/6/ae64dd900275f0e6db4764851eb16499071ccc87.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 4 + 6 + 8 = 20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="145" height="14" /> sequences. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4db3b59940e4f5cd42bbab2b7815c452a0197507.png" class="latex" alt="$3! = 6$" width="47" height="13" /> to permute these, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
</p><p>However, we note that the conditions of the problem require three-digit numbers, and hence our numbers cannot start with zero. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/5/715f3e84a4f9ca515bb0946d5db287f373d25959.png" class="latex" alt="$2! \cdot 4 = 8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" /> numbers which start with zero, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/b/b6b7dac6629488f80ac80e000b4f0c50553e5c2c.png" class="latex" alt="$120 - 8 = 112 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="175" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Observe that, if the smallest and largest digit have the same parity, this uniquely determines the middle digit. If the smallest digit is not zero, then any choice of the smallest and largest digit gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4db3b59940e4f5cd42bbab2b7815c452a0197507.png" class="latex" alt="$3! = 6$" width="47" height="13" /> possible 3-digit numbers; otherwise, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> possible 3-digit numbers. Hence we can do simple casework on whether 0 is in the number or not.
</p><p>Case 1: 0 is not in the number. Then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/8/ab87b3885ddeede48365048af2e36b9c6939fdf0.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{5}{2} + \binom{4}{2} = 16$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="138" height="53" /> ways to choose two nonzero digits of the same parity, and each choice generates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4db3b59940e4f5cd42bbab2b7815c452a0197507.png" class="latex" alt="$3! = 6$" width="47" height="13" /> 3-digit numbers, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9af853a4be65e2af2ef07cf60107b741157e629.png" class="latex" alt="$16 \times 6 = 96$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> numbers.
</p><p>Case 2: 0 is in the number. Then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ways to choose the largest digit (2, 4, 6, or 8), and each choice generates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> 3-digit numbers, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/541f4f0ac5c46d4e572ab6d69e704f8ad1810583.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \times 4 = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> numbers.
</p><p>Thus the total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/0/090df3c982f5350cecb3a07acfb2d999bda649fd.png" class="latex" alt="$96 + 16 = 112 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="175" height="18" />. (by scrabbler94)
</p>

</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
