<p>The zeros of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57e5f08da65a529bc7127ff0ae65de3a9185b8ed.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = x^2-ax+2a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="19" /> are integers. What is the sum of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebcbb9cf1ea65fe561a8906a3baa8a2ca1daa5b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 8 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 16 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 17 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The problem asks us to find the sum of every integer value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> such that the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/6/a06e18ea92bf274f422be61d3dcccaddc56ac291.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - ax + 2a = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="16" /> are both integers.
</p><p>The quadratic formula gives the roots of the quadratic equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/b/cab1861b717741bfc15dfd810df339f4cc6e113b.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{a\pm\sqrt{a^2-8a}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="141" height="40" />
</p><p>As long as the numerator is an even integer, the roots are both integers. But first of all, the radical term in the numerator needs to be an integer; that is, the discriminant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d2918f3b554aa900474257ddd8ca4e12512adf3d.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - 8a$" width="58" height="15" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" />, for some nonnegative integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/1/a1193f31af1a9a8b4fe9009f87abc7163a58cd92.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-8a=k^2$" width="98" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/8/e3877f124a47703352a5478b89a4679cffe8c28b.png" class="latex" alt="$a(a-8)=k^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/f/0bf912771496d4d195c815fd80df7a318db1c84e.png" class="latex" alt="$((a-4)+4)((a-4)-4)=k^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="241" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3830803eede1fd860cf1366c72b6a55c58bcb785.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4)^2-4^2=k^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54e39708309e62c7f34a541ea944fe95d20f196.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4)^2=k^2+4^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="19" />
</p><p>From this last equation, we are given a hint of the Pythagorean theorem. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/4763740fcdc9e4a4ea667304ebd2acffcce72a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$(k,4,|a-4|)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" /> must be a Pythagorean triple unless <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/84574de7797bc7b4e1fc52075f4d265ba60a8bce.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 0$" width="43" height="12" />.
</p><p>In the case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/d/badf0ecd1ef8289387d8462393438c6e2710ff28.png" class="latex" alt="$k=0$" width="43" height="12" />, the equation simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8fea4b9ac1387d3b7b62a2ad1171f47bda39a43.png" class="latex" alt="$|a-4|=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. From this equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/3/86347c45202f5c2c67f1d1834b4394ba373eb63b.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0,8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" />. For both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69cefa6855d2216036df094012f7ad2a7e4e204f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/a/c1a0caf28dae25372408593f2d14ca238035568c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=8$" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f788e9d9af61b9dd135388fd70480ede7db6b4b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a\pm\sqrt{a^2-8a}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="40" /> yields two integers, so these values satisfy the constraints from the original problem statement. (Note: the two zero roots count as "two integers.")
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is a positive integer, then only one Pythagorean triple could match the triple <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/3/35356369c2fb9dd7c66060f0dc8ec65b33eefa83.png" class="latex" alt="$(k,4,|a - 4|)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" /> because the only Pythagorean triple with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> as one of the values is the classic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28eb10edb01b5d1369b1469e15dab327a4ad7b35.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,4,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> triple. Here, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/3359f43f0fd0ed3b672d389374aecd0360921a47.png" class="latex" alt="$k=3$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/5/de596a29f607880f7f74c5e7a05166cb64206ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$|a-4|=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80763fa4fbd962d0a69a395751d98e8ce2406a6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-1,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" />. Again, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f788e9d9af61b9dd135388fd70480ede7db6b4b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a\pm\sqrt{a^2-8a}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="40" /> yields two integers for both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/7/817555099cf3c1ef68bd2e5b1f4bd27d48cbbb6c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/2/792fa2135a12f746ee8d35f224cc514437dd03c8.png" class="latex" alt="$a=9$" width="42" height="12" />, so these two values also satisfy the original constraints.
</p><p>There are a total of four possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/6874319badd6b6efc3b8fdf072bcae1c312c7c5d.png" class="latex" alt="$-1,0,8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Hence, the sum of all of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db5946ec5998020933dc9ef4064e53212c6ff16.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>By the quadratic formula, the roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> can be represented by
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/a/bcaffcb5553fc860cb85d4ee6d43f333317761c4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r=\frac{a\pm\sqrt{a^2-8a}}{2}\]" width="139" height="40" />
For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/a/09a2d90cde71dd9aa1a14b9f4d6995243479b467.png" class="latex" alt="$r\in\mathbb{Z}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="42" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/d/9bdc2ab76a41c168540ac249c2eeb155c2af54c0.png" class="latex" alt="$a\in\mathbb{Z}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="13" />, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/874b08ec9066fe529cb7c1d1b4780dd00c861fd4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{a^2-8a}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="75" height="40" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9afddc3c61aa4e66d003d0c7cc5bd01560d341f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> will have different mantissas (mantissae?). 
</p><p>Now observe the discriminant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a74b21531a17ab1a7be22871fd8d9e0026bc7632.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2-8a}=\sqrt{a(a-8)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="180" height="32" /> and have two cases.
</p><p><br />
<b>Positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /></b>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ed0779438f8eca61c78825911b3102acb7b8251.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78770fd30f793c3d975ad19a41aa49e5cfdb7460.png" class="latex" alt="$a\leq0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/a/43a3e0269cc129de316736297d60845d3bf0f3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$1\geq a \geq7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="14" /> gives imaginary roots. Testing positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> values, quickly see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/0/ad02ddba92975d5c399a998efd3a326eae452a34.png" class="latex" alt="$a\leq9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />, the difference between the closest nonzero factor pairs of perfect squares exceeds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/33541270cbf60df4da7f5fd4f0adadd7cbac2c89.png" class="latex" alt="$8\geq a \geq9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/8/64856da7631481f8d32d5352fda10bb9a8bfdcd3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=8,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" />. Checking both yields an integer.
</p><p><b>Negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /></b>
</p><p>We can instead test with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80298f4be113fcfff5388a047f135b9fe7e8443.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{-a(8-a)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="97" height="32" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/8/df8332a81e1f1b0eb901d3fff44cd1d38a092dbe.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8-a$" width="73" height="12" />, we have our original expression. Thus, for the same reasons, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/d/28d00e3b81d4d3430fa1d29c6034579a1a06a85d.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8,9\implies 8,9=8-a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="199" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/7/817555099cf3c1ef68bd2e5b1f4bd27d48cbbb6c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> (0 does not affect the answer).
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/d/c8d74f93fe6b1a2e65a49d663419158ce5ce3d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$-1+8+9=16\implies\boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="242" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
(Solution by BJHHar)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/b/c6b592af43224842951025690ef6832395fa1832.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-ax+2a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="16" />
</p><p>By Vieta's Formulas, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/b/9bbc36363c2afec2a41122127c041fb43f590068.png" class="latex" alt="$n+m=a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/0874da111f016c37ce6a5b6604303286132851d3.png" class="latex" alt="$mn=2a$" width="69" height="12" />
</p><p>Substituting gets us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/1/3e1d54d30a399eb0ba2260b0f10b59699b0d6cff.png" class="latex" alt="$n+m=\frac{mn}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="33" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/def38920b252e6a3e71450b86508676008e0eb37.png" class="latex" alt="$2n-mn+2m=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />
</p><p>Using Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/4/984b2c9eb111667b5676ba8531995f16049159b8.png" class="latex" alt="$n(2-m)+2m=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a7407e700240dcf8623e55071c85fea6d8a84835.png" class="latex" alt="$-n(2-m)-2m=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b29b2f588446869036874095a5675d9410235f.png" class="latex" alt="$-n(2-m)-2m+4=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37e077df44ec2a1bf85f3d5a7bfcf592bfb7ec73.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-n)(2-m)=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="18" />
</p><p>This means that the values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/d/1fdbb5ad40fa4ad86e28eab529f8a553fc054554.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0),(4,4),(3,6),(1,-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" /> giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/6874319badd6b6efc3b8fdf072bcae1c312c7c5d.png" class="latex" alt="$-1,0,8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Adding these up gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db5946ec5998020933dc9ef4064e53212c6ff16.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The quadratic formula gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b304e9bd6340b86ea36a79649d4e6ed9f794c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = \frac{a \pm \sqrt{a(a-8)}}{2}\]" width="150" height="41" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be an integer, it is necessary (and sufficient!) that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/3/6e32355c601dd01fbc17ad2add7ed462127df8ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a(a-8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> to be a perfect square. So we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/2/54247821bb611fece43136e89b684e10180d3118.png" class="latex" alt="$a(a-8) = b^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="19" />; this is a quadratic in itself and the quadratic formula gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/e/25ef15c74e3024060c12b7efba495bc4fd7e90b1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a = 4 \pm \sqrt{16 + b^2}\]" width="136" height="18" />
</p><p>We want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/a/75aec02a105be84bb64c9abce2a38329dd32b5e5.png" class="latex" alt="$16 + b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="54" height="16" /> to be a perfect square. From smartly trying small values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3baca9c4baa73a1da20b8fd8c3b14df4ba2535c.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 0, b = 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" /> as solutions, which correspond to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/7/247349f2616e60e215adaa20c359eb1451285cab.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -1, 0, 8, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="108" height="16" />. These are the only ones; if we want to make sure then we must hand check up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2ccc9e3c02a4b38f185d5b43a4b7456338e30a6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8$" width="40" height="12" />. Indeed, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/1/811645be7710ca8371ebee9ed6c30bec49ca5ed5.png" class="latex" alt="$b \geq 9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="15" /> we have that the differences between consecutive squares are greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> so we can't have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/b/8eb848c83731d1d7c293b9a0c32c68d72d95689f.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 + 16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="16" /> be a perfect square. So summing our values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/4/ec46d1d5b5102dd68d811214ed5c8869ad2c83fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{16 \textbf{ (C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />. as the answer.
</p><p>Additional note: You can use the quadratic and plug in squares for a (since for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/d/a1d03c3c5b9abf1bdb0c9df06fe5fb11ae13d0e8.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2$" width="14" height="15" /> to be an integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> would have to be some square), and eventually you can notice a limit to get the answer~
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>First of all, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sum of the quadratic's two roots, by Vieta's formulas. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be an integer. Then, we notice that the discriminant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/a/c7ad6c200c430926a1b5abc7e2b4549d12e71030.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-8a$" width="58" height="15" /> must be equal to a perfect square so that the roots are integers. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/a/41ac5c7c728fe647e4ab250e5c736d993a2836dd.png" class="latex" alt="$a(a-8)=b^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="21" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is an integer. 
</p><p>We can complete the square and rearrange to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/7/427cf08e54d961a6e0d930e67d82c244b4ef492d.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4)^2-b^2=16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="144" height="21" />. Let's define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/b/85b196b00400600d0d8299104fcd9bb4c4eaadeb.png" class="latex" alt="$m=a-4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" />, just to make things a little easier to write, so now we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/d/4ddf5271f472f99019e57b865a58bae0254b8cd5.png" class="latex" alt="$(m+b)(m-b)=16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="165" height="20" />. We can now list out the integer factor pairs of 16 and the resulting values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. (Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must both be integers)
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/d/4ddf5271f472f99019e57b865a58bae0254b8cd5.png" class="latex" alt="$(m+b)(m-b)=16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="165" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/9/0e97c84c9b317d86325e04036fa0dce571388cea.png" class="latex" alt="$16*1=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> Doesn't work
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7544bd56dc6b8a01400b964de572d6ccdd9e39fb.png" class="latex" alt="$8*2=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/8/3087bc1a7156694412dea69b59aae5cf2a771f60.png" class="latex" alt="$m=5, b=3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/9/099f1cc4df10b723603df01f246b706f0c9a9cad.png" class="latex" alt="$4*4=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba154df82ee6df15c3cb0d469848537f3c953cf.png" class="latex" alt="$m=4, b=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/8/3d8f4d6ad02292c8fd82ec4059e6506cb33e8018.png" class="latex" alt="$2*8=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563dad5239fa52f869c1ab2898d751199c7c8c7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m=5, b=-3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7542939b083f8968061c5a3a4e075267b6b1336b.png" class="latex" alt="$1*16=16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> Doesn't work
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/0/ea0e611c4a3ce41d5b4032101543fce3350a5337.png" class="latex" alt="$-16*-1=16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="117" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> Doesn't work
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/e/18e5d534fa40b55b49c56d938cf6ca251e38da1e.png" class="latex" alt="$-8*-2=16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/6/e964281e56d51dfaeea734131993b72a9871b6df.png" class="latex" alt="$m=-5, b=-3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/b/fbbebe73cc966a749c543d3ebec626373d2234b9.png" class="latex" alt="$-4*-4=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/0/0900ff5100dee86861b43286b0c32361cf064d0d.png" class="latex" alt="$m=-4, b=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08f2d955c203cbebc9736ace9e53706f9ebd62d2.png" class="latex" alt="$-2*-8=16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/6/e964281e56d51dfaeea734131993b72a9871b6df.png" class="latex" alt="$m=-5, b=-3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/4/044498ba5b085ce62ff3b460cd8adc297c065ec9.png" class="latex" alt="$-1*-16=16$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="117" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> Doesn't work
</p><p><br />
We want the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/2/382ce4ac848f00cc2fcfba86dccd84f63bcb6bc4.png" class="latex" alt="$-5,-4,4,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/9/8f95a8a48e1d7641f85e40533de3705c346fcb66.png" class="latex" alt="$m+4=a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> can equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/6874319badd6b6efc3b8fdf072bcae1c312c7c5d.png" class="latex" alt="$-1,0,8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a1b9d5cec741422bfb37f91db47fd7514f5377.png" class="latex" alt="$9.$" width="12" height="12" /> Adding all of that up gets us our answer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db5946ec5998020933dc9ef4064e53212c6ff16.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
