<p>The ratio of the length to the width of a rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />&#160;: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. If the rectangle has diagonal of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, then the area may be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/c/83c5f596fe052a23524c8d820f9e96816ef5d61e.png" class="latex" alt="$kd^2$" width="25" height="15" /> for some constant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/2/9826dc9039d3673a7fe3a4c37e570f05fc4b39b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{2}{7}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{3}{7}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{12}{25}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{16}{25}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="385" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the rectangle have length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> and width <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />. Then by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> triangles (or the Pythagorean Theorem), we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cff634b3284e49bf72e37aff8347aa9707d618c.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 5x$" width="52" height="12" />, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/2/862fc0b794de55599910bd0aa579882bb5b1082c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \dfrac{d}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. Hence, the area of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/a/50a555b8691c2780816ef81b6b430f574a0a32ff.png" class="latex" alt="$3x \cdot 4x = 12x^2 = \dfrac{12d^2}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="174" height="39" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/023c4b68ca79e34f8d510438269373aced343ec7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\frac{12}{25}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />
</p>
