<p>A circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> passes through both foci of, and exactly four points on, the ellipse with equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/a/fda52ff76ae4eaf2b2e027cb1d9c2894235dae69.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+16y^2=16.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="121" height="18" /> The set of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is an interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/e/1feb8d51c080f531b1fa21f4210fc61f9924d577.png" class="latex" alt="$[a,b).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="41" height="18" /> What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/917cdf8efc9c4864345e3c006cc837fec14e7cc8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/d/d4d565d0c38c142c4e4c7d2adb4d558c5eac018b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5\sqrt{2}+4\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \sqrt{17}+7\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 6\sqrt{2}+3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \sqrt{15}+8\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="580" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can graph the ellipse by seeing that the center is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b86f54134d69cad8793688a431aa907aaf2698.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and finding the ellipse's intercepts. The points where the ellipse intersects the coordinate axes are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/3/e8395c2adeadfa898ad59bfa35754e2f87acfd47.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 1), (0, -1), (4, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/3/70342d768f538dea97717e97479a9cb25bd603a7.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. Recall that the two foci lie on the major axis of the ellipse and are a distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> away from the center of the ellipse, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/8/918db20df17afbc9438a892b9062a2aa656a3857.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = a^2 - b^2$" width="93" height="15" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> being half the length of the major (longer) axis and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> being half the minor (shorter) axis of the ellipse. We have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/a/d4afb6435a3b16fd9efa0f1d40bb47f959f40742.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = 4^2 - 1^2 \implies$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="133" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/d/56da29b1efe75e33260ef06e992523405ebb9a71.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = 15 \implies c = \pm \sqrt{15}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="187" height="18" />. Hence, the coordinates of both of our foci are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/9/299d9b6acc3333bbeabfbe89999f82176ff487ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{15}, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa29c20f74d7926a0c412f8c4e20a807bd9fc601.png" class="latex" alt="$(-\sqrt{15}, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="21" />. In order for a circle to pass through both of these foci, we must have that the center of this circle lies on the y-axis.
</p><p>The minimum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> belongs to the circle whose diameter's endpoints are the foci of this ellipse, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/188a45e380a07c518c3edbff2900ffee78e22b78.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \sqrt{15}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="18" />. The value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is achieved when the circle passes through the foci and only three points on the ellipse, which is possible when the circle touches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/6457ca2dcd40dc21d9aa0db18c66bbac9af4d899.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/4/ba416f638d5172d6343e3abdd1bd664f8f113606.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />. Which point we use does not change what value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is attained, so we use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/4/ba416f638d5172d6343e3abdd1bd664f8f113606.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />. Here, we must find the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec888a16015d32d475ff33a43f72c950f81c4e85.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> such that the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec888a16015d32d475ff33a43f72c950f81c4e85.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> to both foci and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/4/ba416f638d5172d6343e3abdd1bd664f8f113606.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is the same. Now, we have the two following equations. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7bd8451edc43115bf83ed20ea69f5dc0ab9760.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\sqrt{15})^2 + (y)^2 = b^2\]" width="145" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/8/7b81cc09e8c343f24f90b5a6523e4b52f874f0a8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y + 1 = b \implies y = b - 1\]" width="193" height="16" /> Substituting for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/2/73257e4e50f17d6c0aeb9b9cfce94188c9c70255.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[15 + (b - 1)^2 = b^2 \implies -2b + 16 = 0.\]" width="297" height="19" />
</p><p>Solving the above simply yields that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b447277e683dbf98cb0cbcbddf0debfd717e1c0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 8$" width="40" height="12" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/9/5c98e1b7a871c39eab157c69c3bbe0554b09c017.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \sqrt{15} + 8 \textbf{ (D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="21" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As above, we can show that the foci of the ellipse are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/0/d90004e66fee228ef362b821b5144ae153ecf9ec.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{15},0), (-\sqrt{15},0).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="155" height="21" />
</p><p>To obtain the lower bound, note that the smallest circle is when the diameter is on the line segment formed by the two foci. We can check that this indeed passes through four points on the ellipse since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5e9476bfc5404f8dc2d810c0be6c9b1b00fc16.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{15}&gt;1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="20" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/5/f95c84705cf31246afe0e6c85ac0888176ef3735.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\sqrt{15}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="18" />
</p><p>To get the upper bound, note that the circle must go through either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/b/20b6bf5a5265228c9b79b243c66c55ff1bef12af.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,-1).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> WLOG, let let the circle go through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd0ca22e23123ae7f5c26e169721a547d26cca7.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> We know that the circle must go through the foci of the ellipse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/3/b9315693ddab002b23e3d3a38dfb70983cc014bd.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{15},0), (-\sqrt{15},0),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="155" height="21" /> So we can apply power of a point to find the diameter. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> denote the length of the line segment from the origin to the lower point on the circle. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> lies on the diameter. Then by POP, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/b/83b4eccac07968154d499b8a53b2a7604b513b76.png" class="latex" alt="$x \cdot 1 = \sqrt{15} \cdot \sqrt{15},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="20" /> yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e45a63c78330a7020ad39fd035c818528f54ff46.png" class="latex" alt="$x=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />, and so the radius of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/6741c2b7f4ed8425154ca156ce64c32529715eef.png" class="latex" alt="$(15+1)/2=8,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/b/ddbab9929a91d26e20b57182f1163bd9fc596983.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8.$" width="44" height="12" />
Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/e/5ce4c05cfc8a2739a76c999af99396e4090fdc77.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=\sqrt{15}+8 \textbf{ (D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="21" />. 
</p><p>~ ccx09 (Roy Boy Apple Short Long)
