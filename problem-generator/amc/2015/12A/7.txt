<p>Two right circular cylinders have the same volume. The radius of the second cylinder is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> more than the radius of the first. What is the relationship between the heights of the two cylinders?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b16a7a3b06f1cbc53b9857217ba624e8f31c57a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\  \text{The second height is } 10\% \text{ less than the first.} \\ \textbf{(B)}\  \text{The first height is } 10\% \text{ more than the second.}\\ \textbf{(C)}\ \text{The second height is } 21\% \text{ less than the first.} \\ \textbf{(D)}\ \text{The first height is } 21\% \text{ more than the second.}\\ \textbf{(E)}\ \text{The second height is } 80\% \text{ of the first.}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="369" height="97" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the radius of the first cylinder be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adc143c3f887fa7e92fd80d91bb22af51b4abd77.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="10" /> and the radius of the second cylinder be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b4f3f4b698ce35667432041e168f7653533fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$r_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />. Also, let the height of the first cylinder be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/d/32d78aae1741ff43c6f40e2077f1ab67900cca2c.png" class="latex" alt="$h_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> and the height of the second cylinder be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec781a0e6836ea5e030a44927477cfd5560fecb.png" class="latex" alt="$h_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />. We are told <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/41704dff30bf2daea06efcba03fdcd72cb507478.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_2=\frac{11r_1}{10}\]" width="75" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/7/177bac559181a4d8c72c9057a70337eabab64b2f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\pi r_1^2h_1=\pi r_2^2h_2\]" width="113" height="19" /> Substituting the first equation into the second and dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/3/ce3ff9714fedab21b6406945de393a33855a715f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_1^2h_1=\frac{121r_1^2}{100}h_2\implies h_1=\frac{121h_2}{100}.\]" width="267" height="39" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/4/0646f7cddc13e6f3bf3707b8bc43d9bf0b3a3651.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \text{The first height is } 21\% \text{ more than the second.}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="382" height="29" />
</p>
