<p>A regular 15-gon has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ccf4cd60c7bc6b8fa1afc9a42dc811a826d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$L$" width="12" height="12" /> lines of symmetry, and the smallest positive angle for which it has rotational symmetry is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> degrees. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/955b9c3998f3aaf080f0aaa69f49b82cc64ab8c0.png" class="latex" alt="$L+R$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="14" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/7/717d90de50f121bab033886b1eb68ae9bdb1ab8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\; 24 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\; 27 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\; 32 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\; 39 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\; 54$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="392" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>From consideration of a smaller regular polygon with an odd number of sides (e.g. a pentagon), we see that the lines of symmetry go through a vertex of the polygon and bisect the opposite side. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/9942a782a814cb0909f4f053512c7e470c2ee1c1.png" class="latex" alt="$L=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />, the number of sides / vertices. The smallest angle for a rotational symmetry transforms one side into an adjacent side, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9e5eb17b163f6a160464fad8f27f7ca5bc8e6c7.png" class="latex" alt="$R = 360^\circ / 15 = 24^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" />, the number of degrees between adjacent sides. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee68e96162327ed87113f769b2c660ff5441001d.png" class="latex" alt="$L + R = 15 + 24 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} \, 39}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="218" height="28" />.
</p>
