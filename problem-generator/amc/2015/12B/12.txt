<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be three distinct one-digit numbers. What is the maximum value of the sum of the roots of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/4/3644adefa015a4780e5748c0da9842f1113c1a5f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b)+(x-b)(x-c)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="275" height="18" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f9b515f7209a6a8397c4ec22b65920d2a106f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\; 15 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\; 15.5 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\; 16 \qquad\textbf{(D)} 16.5 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\; 17$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="417" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution">Video Solution</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/ba6w1OhXqOQ?t=423">https://youtu.be/ba6w1OhXqOQ?t=423</a>
</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The left-hand side of the equation can be factored as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c78bc08e2449d27aed7c13ec82e6a7cbb57a8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-b)(x-a+x-c) = (x-b)(2x-(a+c))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="360" height="18" />, from which it follows that the roots of the equation are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741720281e465fc7461c32d504f6d54c5bcaff7c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=b$" width="42" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/d/0dd49c8a9f1e4dbfc105a8ee1cd39eb4d90b95ca.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\tfrac{a+c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="60" height="20" />. The sum of the roots is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/4/a4414d3b4c413cf08dcbf1a3bad41cc42a108b4d.png" class="latex" alt="$b + \tfrac{a+c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="55" height="20" />, and the maximum is achieved by choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/c/15cc8804d5482ae978d97e7912aa9c768329ad49.png" class="latex" alt="$b=9$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da5ef6ba24aebcb04d00046fc56d65a7354e022.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a,c\}=\{7,8\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="18" />. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/8/93808b7b3525dc88c39f696b1fae2eda4cba504d.png" class="latex" alt="$9 + \tfrac{7+8}{2} = 9 + 7.5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 16.5}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="243" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Expand the polynomial. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d55a2e4b22c39af43bf1ae56e9714e9e6261a9f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b)+(x-b)(x-c)=x^2-(a+b)x+ab+x^2-(b+c)x+bc=2x^2-(a+2b+c)x+(ab+bc).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="845" height="19" /> 
</p><p>Now, consider a general quadratic equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcc65bb5298c9ac2d711aa905b40c3bf82364d0.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2+bx+c=0.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="16" /> The two solutions to this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/4/c24d41bb4f10c62d76b27486cf8aa36a47dec169.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{-b}{2a}+\dfrac{\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}, \dfrac{-b}{2a}-\dfrac{\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}.\]" width="272" height="40" /> The sum of these roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10ec902f7062667b933d2f9894b5c2bfd9eea4b1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{-b}{a}.\]" width="27" height="37" /> 
</p><p>Therefore, reconsidering the polynomial of the problem, the sum of the roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/8/39806a75adbb86c84bd995a73020aee4c5f15b33.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{a+2b+c}{2}.\]" width="84" height="37" /> Now, to maximize this, it is clear that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/6/1561f024168da4a82d14e80b3f5d22d4e2d3c0aa.png" class="latex" alt="$b=9.$" width="44" height="12" /> Also, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/1/251332e5132b7b06d2918b4872d7b2a0581d0149.png" class="latex" alt="$a=8, c=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> (or vice versa). The reason <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa540ef6360899066bc4fd5b466f10a3bc55a93.png" class="latex" alt="$a,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="11" /> have to equal these values instead of larger values is because each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> is distinct.
</p>
