<p>Cozy the Cat and Dash the Dog are going up a staircase with a certain number of steps.  However, instead of walking up the steps one at a time, both Cozy and Dash jump.  Cozy goes two steps up with each jump (though if necessary, he will just jump the last step).  Dash goes five steps up with each jump (though if necessary, he will just jump the last steps if there are fewer than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> steps left).  Suppose Dash takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> fewer jumps than Cozy to reach the top of the staircase.  Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the sum of all possible numbers of steps this staircase can have.  What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/7/79792405717c52c19b1904a573b5fd41ddd022de.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }9\qquad\textbf{(B) }11\qquad\textbf{(C) }12\qquad\textbf{(D) }13\qquad\textbf{(E) }15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of steps. We have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61ce59740ee02b4ebd7d07e44038a437076d4e24.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lceil \frac{n}{2} \right\rceil - 19 = \left\lceil \frac{n}{5} \right\rceil\]" width="124" height="33" />
</p><p>We will proceed to solve this equation via casework.
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/6/3062818c23279940c089aa53c6f4dc29f758b4c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lceil \frac{n}{2} \right\rceil = \frac{n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="71" height="33" />
</p><p>Our equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/2955acdbf63c3709ad0fd389c09a85588b29de36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{2} - 19 = \frac{n}{5} + \frac{j}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="128" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bc53cb24d28e2c2c029b17794d5bfff2190c65.png" class="latex" alt="$j \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer, we quickly find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db39120014c34e1b098a3090ebd37598244d404.png" class="latex" alt="$j=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/3/1732b5fde3ea9e2af04da5b4036f9ba1c75f6f53.png" class="latex" alt="$j=4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/207ad3f10707bc07c764ae0bc5a4c79fdadb5bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/8/d88323c6da8a0fdfd8edf87ccdf380556537bbaf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=66$" width="52" height="12" />, respectively.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/9/2c9e6c1d22491b61ea0035d5f403fb7fadb912e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lceil \frac{n}{2} \right\rceil = \frac{n}{2}+\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="106" height="37" />
</p><p>Our equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/b/36be35ad6f7432fb8571aadd7f0315969eb761df.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{2} +\frac{1}{2} - 19 = \frac{n}{5} + \frac{j}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="163" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bc53cb24d28e2c2c029b17794d5bfff2190c65.png" class="latex" alt="$j \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer, we quickly find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbd69ff270802989ea1341802afdc8b0642e098e.png" class="latex" alt="$j=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/5/735696f0c2d60b937c23d0eb3f265a7b7e7e00a4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=63$" width="52" height="12" />. Summing up we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcf6090518f8995023f152af4b69eda295ca554.png" class="latex" alt="$63+64+66=193$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />. The sum of the digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1f368a77ceda15d80a4a3e72791b38344731c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know from the problem that Dash goes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> steps further than Cozy per jump (assuming they aren't within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> steps from the top). That means that if Dash takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> fewer jumps than Cozy to get to the top of the staircase, the staircase must be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a58f44011456f6294ac64ec64de80b9e0327cce.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 19=57$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> steps high. </p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" /> steps: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/e/2ae9ab14a0f7e272d3ed9daa42e7371518c85318.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {57/2} \right \rceil = 29$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Cozy, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/c/60c39e13a284370f7d9c81c40c2be8481eade344.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {57/5} \right \rceil = 12$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Dash, giving a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> jumps.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/8/b784ad3135944214ca7ecade87b8dfa3aa54f1f0.png" class="latex" alt="$58$" width="17" height="12" /> steps: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/8/2b85f919aaacfe7a137a7c37213798da580d00c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {58/2} \right \rceil = 29$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Cozy, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/d/75d24c9d595fd5fd0ba34ce42d88bf7db0763088.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {58/5} \right \rceil = 12$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Dash, giving a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> jumps.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/e/afe61e5b80572ad5a200a0d9cd354ce12a8c7800.png" class="latex" alt="$59$" width="17" height="12" /> steps: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/a/80a4e57006b77ddf9857741f1a37c4e74b11e59a.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {59/2} \right \rceil = 30$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Cozy, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/2/f02d01c8ecb244f127bd62ddd58a443f62b1005a.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil {59/5} \right \rceil = 12$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="19" /> jumps for Dash, giving a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> jumps.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Translate the problem into following equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380b2f2c3cffbaf87c5917bd59a539142d842a04.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 5D - \{0 \sim 4\} = 2C - \{0 \sim 1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="272" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/8/918a02c88917e9d02780d212bc11dd48081cf209.png" class="latex" alt="$C = D + 19$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="14" />, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/9002fc8506dd5c41f307c4858efd77ebc48a87be.png" class="latex" alt="$5D - \{0 \sim 4\} = 2D + 38 - \{0 \sim 1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="278" height="18" />
</p><p>i.e.,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45f9c3380eff7a4c4689e4f94fcc0197a3269230.png" class="latex" alt="$3D = 38 + \{0 \sim 4\} - \{0 \sim 1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="18" />
</p><p>We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/10979f90cac4c245e79e64bd7d6187a01db5f8ed.png" class="latex" alt="$D = 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568180461858420d80d8dc263fc1bd6fbcc93727.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1\} - \{0\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9dfedc67d5a9b1818bcd4e96fecea3231ce67bf5.png" class="latex" alt="$\{2\} - \{1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" /> (the dog's last jump has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> steps and the cat's last jump has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> step), which yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/aceb19ac350a8179aa719d524161ffa84bff9947.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/3/70323bd0f8ff191867325badf0632fcf37349adc.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 63$" width="52" height="12" /> respectively.
</p><p>Another solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/7/a57cf940d2baa797e39605dcdabccbe6dca1c988.png" class="latex" alt="$D = 14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/b/d3b1daf2da6cb190b7d0ebaae48bb217adb79825.png" class="latex" alt="$\{4\} - \{0\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />, which yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/a/b6a742edd1183e9bbbb7a09a9998cdc1141afed7.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 66$" width="52" height="12" />.
</p><p>Therefore, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/f/bcf8446655fee94b89ce55b2503a085d5ed0c932.png" class="latex" alt="$63 + 64 + 66 = 193$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />, the digit sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/e/25e40bc2b9bcef49b195e9be1e88febb6eff5fd6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{13}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />.
</p>
