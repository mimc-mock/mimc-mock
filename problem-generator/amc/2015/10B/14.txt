<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be three distinct one-digit numbers. What is the maximum value of the sum of the roots of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/4/3644adefa015a4780e5748c0da9842f1113c1a5f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b)+(x-b)(x-c)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="275" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/6/be6e1709b6ce8ac4c3044dbee7c1a442e241c272.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }15\qquad \textbf{(B) }15.5\qquad \textbf{(C) }16\qquad \textbf{(D) }16.5\qquad \textbf{(E) }17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="427" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Expanding the equation and combining like terms results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/c/95cb427b83c369df381823fd329d4efb22ab5279.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2-(a+2b+c)x+(ab+bc)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="277" height="19" />. By Vieta's formula the sum of the roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/4/3743e38f1afcc5182d2a9da5e198ff8df18800ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{-[-(a+2b+c)]}{2}=\dfrac{a+2b+c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="240" height="38" />. To maximize this expression we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> to be the largest, and from there we can assign the next highest values to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />. So let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/c/15cc8804d5482ae978d97e7912aa9c768329ad49.png" class="latex" alt="$b=9$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/a/c1a0caf28dae25372408593f2d14ca238035568c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=8$" width="42" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/4/df4c636bd4eadfbf8bf1447703d58c79214f3197.png" class="latex" alt="$c=7$" width="41" height="12" />. Then the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/d/bfdcbc5c89a1e59c0ce18b8a52ab7cc1a535e7a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{8+18+7}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)} 16.5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="182" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Factoring out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/4/6e4a10dd00cb5fa2641e79515144dfa871adcd24.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> from the equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/1/131b24c4143f77ee4a2f6a3397cb3a7877e88d71.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-b)(2x-(a+c))=0 \Rightarrow (x-b)\left(x-\frac{a+c}{2}\right)=0$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="418" height="43" />. Therefore the roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/2/f620b55d8a64de8b7669df5fcb658bcc8e2f2cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a+c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="35" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be the larger root to maximize the sum of the roots, letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b28547efd22be1c4a05fa193cd7038fc65d71b.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/9/b29dd0d81ebbb47469065a09a3d5a0e86f14677f.png" class="latex" alt="$8,9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> respectively yields the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/1/2e1bb5f572b5017c4fbac72fc21ba8901a5c3074.png" class="latex" alt="$9+\frac{8+7}{2} = 9+7.5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}~16.5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="257" height="37" />.
</p>
