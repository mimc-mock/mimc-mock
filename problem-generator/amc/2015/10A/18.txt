<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a782ff78da6e832393d617bada1082ed4fd2c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$3E8$" width="32" height="12" /> when converted to hexadecimal (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/9/74988975fbb5f6b14e1c407b23f0ca13bb740c30.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 16^2 + 14 \cdot 16^1 + 8 \cdot 16^0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="195" height="16" />). We will proceed by constructing numbers that consist of only numeric digits in hexadecimal.
</p><p>The first digit could be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/d/ced6b9b2e75857b566f1e516560c4a4d63977299.png" class="latex" alt="$0,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1d1407aac4125d6c453a365eb173d96ebb48e2.png" class="latex" alt="$2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f885f96d2fb7e65f800de4ff71d7dcd0a5d2db5.png" class="latex" alt="$3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and the second two could be any digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/c/6ec816310f047fab01c5571a6cea644423750334.png" class="latex" alt="$0 - 9$" width="40" height="12" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7a8316fecf92eb73c27d1602783dfe6dada563d.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 10 \cdot 10 = 400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="122" height="13" /> combinations. However, this includes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/f/b4f2d33b77872d7db7a20693b7cd11ccf025eab5.png" class="latex" alt="$000,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="31" height="16" /> so this number must be diminished by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdd1b53e5faf7a118222e4b7571d1e9afa0cf56.png" class="latex" alt="$1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />  Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/3/4f39a3242724f70e81d9037b59cca455b4794683.png" class="latex" alt="$399$" width="26" height="12" /> valid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> corresponding to those <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/3/4f39a3242724f70e81d9037b59cca455b4794683.png" class="latex" alt="$399$" width="26" height="12" /> positive integers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> that consist of only numeric digits.  (Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/3/4f39a3242724f70e81d9037b59cca455b4794683.png" class="latex" alt="$399$" width="26" height="12" /> is the least hexadecimal number using only decimal digits before <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a782ff78da6e832393d617bada1082ed4fd2c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$3E8$" width="32" height="12" />.)  Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ff31ad97a8b4722c4742551a6b74c6f7d0e875a.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 9 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="159" height="28" />
