<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/e/5eee8fd0f8dc0946c64fbd05bc502c121977216c.png" class="latex" alt="$( \sqrt{\pi} , a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/6082ce983c636facfaaba5ed379d9ea1ff7d177d.png" class="latex" alt="$( \sqrt{\pi} , b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="19" /> are distinct points on the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/d/1ddbf7cfd621ddd5178601a22f0b50cfa1e96378.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 + x^4 = 2x^2 y + 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="148" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/1/3311eba472bf7dc846f58d649d3756cf443eb108.png" class="latex" alt="$|a-b|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/2/40282d4ee1afb46bf486005c78fce871c0286803.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad\textbf{(B)} \ \frac{\pi}{2} \qquad\textbf{(C)} \ 2 \qquad\textbf{(D)} \ \sqrt{1+\pi} \qquad\textbf{(E)} \ 1 + \sqrt{\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="452" height="33" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since points on the graph make the equation true, substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0529202bae221e2a886dbd92ecd50e87f90452.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="18" /> in to the equation and then solve to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/2/d7252917d7c01b01feb99640c4f01aceb8d6a2b3.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 + \sqrt{\pi}^4 = 2\sqrt{\pi}^2 y + 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="179" height="22" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29f374ffef4a74ae8cf85aeed2441b77e213743f.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 + \pi^2 = 2\pi y + 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="142" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52ee88d873beea5c6a77f6293558d4c39a76d874.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 - 2\pi y + \pi^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="142" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27ab241bf2d48f480b4c0fbd3d0703a863e8d9d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(y-\pi)^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3b2ed610c3c6faf80944d6bba5e39ddd99d0c77.png" class="latex" alt="$y-\pi = \pm 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9ac25d77cfd0e2e79a183c0e6921c714dd841ce.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \pi + 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f8e1fa63ea4132688df6a57ff770b2b6d23a33a.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \pi - 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" />
</p><p>There are only two solutions to the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27ab241bf2d48f480b4c0fbd3d0703a863e8d9d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(y-\pi)^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="19" />, so one of them is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and the other is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. The order does not matter because of the absolute value sign.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/4/2a4a1f64be6488051bd03a999055405eaa11a1f4.png" class="latex" alt="$| (\pi + 1) - (\pi - 1) | = 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />
</p><p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f530eb4e1a2b7f21a225ce5427c1f2707014f560.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>This solution is very closely related to Solution #1 and just simplifies the problem earlier to make it easier.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/d/1ddbf7cfd621ddd5178601a22f0b50cfa1e96378.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 + x^4 = 2x^2 y + 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="148" height="18" /> can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/6/d1685db7d112651870310087b06477ae4b8e990b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-2x^2y+y^2=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="148" height="18" />. Recognizing that this is a binomial square, simplify this to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b3d6e5d9d1f17ce9162c416379525178d1de79.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2-y)^2=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="19" />. This gives us two equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/d/85dc8827e2cac964004c1a64f3e14aa5cd8c69e4.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/6/ca6d390047e3a0533b05a7a2194559b536ebc76f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-y=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="18" />.
</p><p>One of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />'s is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and one is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0529202bae221e2a886dbd92ecd50e87f90452.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76a008f0f83d4812bcbedd0248181bd2d970c89c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\pi+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/c/03ce89f3107c9e5d3f2c31be4ce20548a84bda2a.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\pi-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />. 
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/8/098b5a6d7b8d721a470adbc5ff0232438097a658.png" class="latex" alt="$|a-b|=|(\pi+1)-(\pi-1)|=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="252" height="18" />.
</p><p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f530eb4e1a2b7f21a225ce5427c1f2707014f560.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>This solution is similar to Solution #1 but uses a different way to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> at the end.
</p><p>Just like Solution #1, we arrive at the conclusion that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52ee88d873beea5c6a77f6293558d4c39a76d874.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 - 2\pi y + \pi^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="142" height="18" />.
</p><p>Simplifying we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70ba497d1b41cd09201128e5aba369b593599a80.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 - 2\pi y + \pi^2 -1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="20" />
</p><p>We now can factor this quadratic. We must find two terms that multiply to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/2/552f255d89badf543639ccc7b51cca0fad0a0d89.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi^2 -1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="18" /> and add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />. 
</p><p>These terms are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a94470a0cacc75a853c89b3cfe8a7b7867d1a852.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi+1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/e/7fee4d2af7659f8e3fca20b1014e696bfbe1f1ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Subtracting one from the other, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f530eb4e1a2b7f21a225ce5427c1f2707014f560.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p><p>-DuckDuckGooseGoose
</p>
