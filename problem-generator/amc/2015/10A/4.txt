<p>Pablo, Sofia, and Mia got some candy eggs at a party.  Pablo had three times as many eggs as Sofia, and Sofia had twice as many eggs as Mia.  Pablo decides to give some of his eggs to Sofia and Mia so that all three will have the same number of eggs.  What fraction of his eggs should Pablo give to Sofia?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/0/9d03ac293f4490044413978e640ae683fc754a7a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{12}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{6}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{1}{4}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1}{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="376" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Assign a variable to the number of eggs Mia has, say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />.  Then, because we are given that Sofia has twice the number of eggs Mia has, Sofia has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93d829d38f1f73cd97c8fd926e636e968d0c158.png" class="latex" alt="$2m$" width="24" height="12" /> eggs, and Pablo, having three times the number of eggs as Sofia, has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/a/15a717b58292e0c0ab98767c4fb3ebc3a1a8ee10.png" class="latex" alt="$6m$" width="24" height="12" /> eggs.
</p><p>For them to all have the same number of eggs, they must each have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/4/3343e6b3fd02b22b6a19e3bb83faf9afd49f6f04.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+2m+6m}{3} = 3m$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="163" height="37" /> eggs.  This means Pablo must give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93d829d38f1f73cd97c8fd926e636e968d0c158.png" class="latex" alt="$2m$" width="24" height="12" /> eggs to Mia and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> eggs to Sofia, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/0/6f023b5c7c68eab1669f14de453358ffeb8868ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m}{6m} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{1}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="113" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let Mia have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> egg. Sofia and Pablo would then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> eggs, respectively. If we split the eggs evenly, each would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/b/97b27aba238c77f8733bc3fcdd7efcddc666ec67.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1 + 2 + 6}{3} = \frac{9}{3} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" /> eggs. Since Sofia needs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/4/ce477a82eea926a5190f4c97f71f46c1316acbe4.png" class="latex" alt="$3 - 2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" /> more egg, Pablo must give her <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> of his <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> eggs, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13af2830267e9987076a8a59a930b793ab078ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{1}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />
</p>
