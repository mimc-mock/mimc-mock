<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Note that we can add the two equations to yield the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a894f8fd032c2faff02a49e04044aff2ebdfffd7.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 - 4x - 4y + 8 = x + y + 8.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="272" height="18" />
</p><p>Moving terms gives the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/600142c8d74d9058f605507c2072e90e6bd7ab44.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=5 \left( x + y \right).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="19" />
</p><p>We can also subtract the two equations to yield the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/2/c92fe61f8e2d3b0121039210ffac2b90bc784cea.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - y^2 - 4x +4y = y - x.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="209" height="18" />
</p><p>Moving terms gives the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/e/6fe9ceb98e625f55cd3dd54441e62b078b469ad6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - y^2 = (x + y)(x - y) = 3x - 3y = 3(x - y).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="371" height="19" />
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/6/a16e362e5df6a5a7a6f47773076d893090a20a23.png" class="latex" alt="$x \neq y,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="17" /> we can divide both sides of the equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5c0a0f370ccf9702de26c839b7bffb5f9be88c.png" class="latex" alt="$x - y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" /> to yield the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/b/99b3173dfefa016b0f1fde9b0569649158f13873.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 3.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="16" />
</p><p>Substituting this into the equation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1babbcf2df391e976e9f8f0d3418bc1750197e47.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" /> that we derived earlier gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/b/e6bb9b70649f1aa685fd106d3c60f5ca47d1105e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 = 5 \left( x + y \right) = 5 \left( 3 \right) = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="298" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_2_(Algebraic)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_.28Algebraic.29">Solution 2 (Algebraic)</span></h3>
<p>Subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> from the left hand side of both equations, and use difference of squares to yield the equations
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/e/cfefb12e64e23a3c2cfa6de7e49774b490da3bc6.png" class="latex" alt="$x = y(y-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />  and  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/606a94b9149d534b7e706e2d95218c2fa9b1b0ef.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x(x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" />.
</p><p>It may save some time to find two solutions, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b86f54134d69cad8793688a431aa907aaf2698.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b6a364920ec84c82c57831adffe0e2ad2b9be6.png" class="latex" alt="$(5, 5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, at this point. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/3/2e33fa7c7b2ce1a18e6e0a6d3ef6c08bd1958722.png" class="latex" alt="$x = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" /> in these solutions.
</p><p><br />
Substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/606a94b9149d534b7e706e2d95218c2fa9b1b0ef.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x(x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/e/cfefb12e64e23a3c2cfa6de7e49774b490da3bc6.png" class="latex" alt="$x = y(y-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
This gives the equation
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/c/1ac0ab4290265e725ac568d20d44467e1ae871b1.png" class="latex" alt="$x = x(x-4)(x^2-4x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="19" />
</p><p>which can be simplified to
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/f/e2f691b5959789103700c98cdbf732421566d2b7.png" class="latex" alt="$x(x^3 - 8x^2 +12x + 15) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="215" height="19" />.
</p><p>Knowing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d348bde3e15456e71734dc2c56fc7425c95927f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" /> are solutions is now helpful, as you divide both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/3/cd3cf6bf6152e6a9635548ea401e4bff8dc1b497.png" class="latex" alt="$x(x-5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="64" height="18" />. This can also be done using polynomial division to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" /> as a factor. This gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/c/cbc0396cf0c6dac3cf5bb5bd64652a04906ba4ae.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 3x -3 = 0$" width="123" height="15" />.
</p><p>Because the two equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/e/cfefb12e64e23a3c2cfa6de7e49774b490da3bc6.png" class="latex" alt="$x = y(y-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/606a94b9149d534b7e706e2d95218c2fa9b1b0ef.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x(x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" /> are symmetric, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> values are the roots of the equation, which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/e/e0ed3ed05cb559d9f1387f92589bf1772bd9b454.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{3 + \sqrt{21}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/1/3f1b1b5e9193ddd195d98fe8169bc862ab1fd019.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{3 - \sqrt{21}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="41" />.
</p><p>Squaring these and adding them together gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4fe353406c7f7f04d7b229b7171154cd028a37f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3^2 + 21 + 6\sqrt{21}}{4} + \frac{3^2 + 21 - 6\sqrt{21}}{4} = \frac{2(3^2 +21)}{4} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="470" height="41" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>By graphing the two equations on a piece of graph paper, we can see that the point where they intersect that is not on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> is close to the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55de6ba08733b48ed79c8a9d4894435baa3c0e3b.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> (or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/9/1f9ba91b378b33b75a172e77e70e8396ff92f641.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />). <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/b/c2b15449470b45503b0cf0d0e1daa25b7f8a3f30.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1)^2+4^2=17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="19" />, and the closest answer choice to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/900f3e1644bfe7c012da0d6b1a65efab5024b890.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h4>
<p>This is risky, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> could be a viable answer too. Do not use this method unless you're sure about the answer. In other words, this solution is less reliable than the others, so only use it if you can't do the other methods.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_(When_you_can't_algebraically_manipulate)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.28When_you_can.27t_algebraically_manipulate.29">Solution 4 (When you can't algebraically manipulate)</span></h3>
<p>This, by the way, is a really cheap way of solving the problem but as long as you get an answer, it doesn't matter.
</p><p>Looking at the first given equation, begin searching for solutions. Notice how <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b86f58cb609fa753024f1173510c877971f8e4e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> works but when plugged into the
</p><p>second equation, you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/b/77b43e2f31dc972c9d9524f519c2190aed95eba6.png" class="latex" alt="$8=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. Now, if you decrease the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> value by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23cdcc8ec2f81cbb356069dd41023888c48231bb.png" class="latex" alt="$(2+\sqrt3,-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="21" />, plugging it into 
</p><p>the second equation will yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/6/716f9dc82784b03bf76b24383c948fa882d60bea.png" class="latex" alt="$6+\sqrt3=9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="18" />. Now, notice how the LHS is now less than the RHS as opposed to what it had 
</p><p>been when we plugged <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b86f58cb609fa753024f1173510c877971f8e4e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> into the second given equation. We then conclude that there must be a solution between 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbab939b716843df7cc6fdb3e9a0b91b2fea5db.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" />, so calculating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c71397a41761fa3ae4bc83119c38cd495b29586.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b86f58cb609fa753024f1173510c877971f8e4e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23cdcc8ec2f81cbb356069dd41023888c48231bb.png" class="latex" alt="$(2+\sqrt3,-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="21" /> we obtain
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/f/91f80a2004e0f9b6154e7f1066ef33235e2d9466.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4^2+0^2=16\]" width="96" height="16" /> and
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/4/41437434b241ad0fc90fc82911f098f811fef5bb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2+\sqrt3)^2+(-1)^2=8+4\sqrt3\approx14.9\]" width="287" height="21" />
</p><p>Thus we know the answer to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/900f3e1644bfe7c012da0d6b1a65efab5024b890.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p><p>This is a really bad solution, but it works.&#160;:/
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_5_(Very_in_depth_and_reasoned)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_.28Very_in_depth_and_reasoned.29">Solution 5 (Very in depth and reasoned)</span></h3>
<p>Note: In this solution, I explain my entire thought process and the reason why I did each step, so you can read a more intuitive solution rather than a contrived one.
</p><p>We first notice that each equation has a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, which inspires us to isolate the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> on both equations and equate them to see if we can gather any useful information on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e228943301b048f242377b3b24717037d5996f.png" class="latex" alt="$y.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> 
</p><p>Doing this we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/e/3ae99ec1bb1cef453af707fc1b2d173a4de9697c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-2)^2-y = (y-2)^2- x.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="221" height="21" /> Now you might be tempted to expand, but notice how we have two perfect squares in our equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/8/4089987a9c991bba4233d802131442ff4443263e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-2)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/9/0e968725f1d0d3f85221017736a8e78d3bac1663.png" class="latex" alt="$(y-2)^2,$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="21" /> which motivates us to take advantage of the difference of squares. 
For simplicity, we suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/f/34f27044a2a966a1960b41712c49692c7b11842d.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-2) = a$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="92" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/4/85473e9154d935aed6bf20eb58d8bd9d4fc4401f.png" class="latex" alt="$(y-2) = b.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="20" /> Thus our equation is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe70b6aa2ba646f9b7ed6bc778ca6e9c628737dd.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-y = b^2-x.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="20" /> We manipulate this equation by getting the LHS in a form that lets us do difference of squares, so we can rewrite as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/6/0061d329a116c0de621939d7491c131b86883499.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-b^2 = -(x-y).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="156" height="21" />
</p><p>Now we can finally use our difference of squares to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a877f6754639e9ddb2d12e0f8ff0ccabd2aabe.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a+b) =  -(x-y).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="209" height="20" />
</p><p>Let's now substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> back to get information solely for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e228943301b048f242377b3b24717037d5996f.png" class="latex" alt="$y.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> 
</p><p>Doing this we get, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbeff574c9cfaef857b6e4bd62805680348c10b9.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)(x+y-4) = -(x-y).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="246" height="20" />
</p><p>Now let us divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/643e1765342cf3807fd7d130ee679b71ecd4789b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" /> on both sides and clean up the equation. When we do this, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e7f7212179737b792b812e330d62bda80dac92e.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y = 3.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="17" />
</p><p>We got <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4cc09da10bafaf3f87cc1a29664a4421c5d94e68.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y = 3,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="17" /> which means that this is fact is only of use if we can obtain it in another way, so we add the LHS and the RHS of both equations and equate them to make our fact of essence. 
</p><p>Doing that, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/2/7423d7feec3f0a3def7c3e63be06bd713e1afc30.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y +8 = (x-2)^2+ (y-2)^2.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="253" height="21" /> Also, when we set this equation up we not only do we make our fact useful, but we also can see that after expanding the RHS, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c71397a41761fa3ae4bc83119c38cd495b29586.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" /> on the RHS too, which makes it even a better move that we added both equations because remember our goal is to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/7/b670bfd25d90bf52f7a1ddf63a35367809f5d58c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="18" />
</p><p>Expanding and plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/3/5e3d37be3f12b7d4cf1902010ad004c552ba6bfe.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y = 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="17" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a924d87608c0bd2aa5429a6d2960f10272a46dc.png" class="latex" alt="$11 = x^2+y^2-4(x+y)+8.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="225" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23cc555eb78d8f2aacf9e9fdc0cab5099e875675.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=3,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="17" /> so we can plug that in and we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a0a13878d4e5ef4d3d7554540862b6399d263f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2 = 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="20" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/900f3e1644bfe7c012da0d6b1a65efab5024b890.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" /> after cleaning up some algebra.
</p><p>~triggod
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_6_(Quickest)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6_.28Quickest.29">Solution 6 (Quickest)</span></h3>
<p>Subtract the two equations to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/4/9540ff2122c1c1555d82201e6af702d639b61766.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)= (x+y-4)(y-x) \iff x+y=3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="355" height="20" />. Plugging back into any of the original equations yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/1/bf11e3e25450699f80107ddd9700b045f1a34e50.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-3x=3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="18" />. However, we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/b/87b53fad70d5561f82e8a02d2049c8181fcd2e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2= x^2+(3-x)^2= 2x^2-6x+9$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="311" height="21" />. Then, we can just multiply the first by two and add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/900f3e1644bfe7c012da0d6b1a65efab5024b890.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
