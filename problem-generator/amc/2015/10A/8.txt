<p>Two years ago Pete was three times as old as his cousin Claire. Two years before that, Pete was four times as old as Claire. In how many years will the ratio of their ages be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />&#160;: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/6/2567b2a0221983b124dc8d2ce57b1218acbf53a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2 \qquad\textbf{(B)} \ 4 \qquad\textbf{(C)} \ 5 \qquad\textbf{(D)} \ 6 \qquad\textbf{(E)} \ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>This problem can be converted to a system of equations. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> be Pete's current age and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be Claire's current age. 
</p><p>The first statement can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/0/3e0b65657298fe0f416c5acb074848964952e5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$p-2=3(c-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />. The second statement can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd9af64d338cace23c5e3f2de12c44994bd92a7.png" class="latex" alt="$p-4=4(c-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />
</p><p>To solve the system of equations:
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/3/e231ae848a5659cfeeed78b7d3e85fcccbba3cb3.png" class="latex" alt="$p=3c-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/e/15ed7a714fd4237077697da01c96466d16bfc6fa.png" class="latex" alt="$p=4c-12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c901a59ee42426a395efec6f6df0df0f7477eb.png" class="latex" alt="$3c-4=4c-12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="129" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/3/fa39557a4d886b45466417ae28c5b0a3a92ba7ef.png" class="latex" alt="$c=8$" width="40" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/7/84777cc6eaff85ddf5e9f5a3c4e0588e6f8766d6.png" class="latex" alt="$p=20.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of years until Pete is twice as old as his cousin.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/6/f06979c7352518ba1ceba6aaacfc5453eaffa661.png" class="latex" alt="$20+x=2(8+x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="138" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/f/8cf73e698f7fd89b6153d6e736d69cd8487a8ed8.png" class="latex" alt="$20+x=16+2x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7d65cfa316d639c9267398009812f3eb45fea9.png" class="latex" alt="$x=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/2494711a9f0508844ca4716f548a3252bc93cc6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
