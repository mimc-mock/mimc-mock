<p>Claudia has 12 coins, each of which is a 5-cent coin or a 10-cent coin. There are exactly 17 different values that can be obtained as combinations of one or more of his coins. How many 10-cent coins does Claudia have?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/5/155828867f43df017eeb34cfaacde7f881e7d8b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }3\qquad\textbf{(B) }4\qquad\textbf{(C) }5\qquad\textbf{(D) }6\qquad\textbf{(E) }7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let Claudia have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> 5-cent coins and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af07cfcb7f4b70fa28dfb08fc6f23ff56669afa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( 12 - x \right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> 10-cent coins. It is easily observed that any multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/0/230311493ae732054099b517a7461a101ddeb73f.png" class="latex" alt="$5x + 10(12 - x) = 120 - 5x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="217" height="18" /> inclusive can be obtained by a combination of coins. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/3/cb3bc6af50119d71108dfafec8c5e221c3033762.png" class="latex" alt="$24 - x = 17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="12" /> combinations can be made, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d1e7375e601943b3bb958a24e41da8920d601a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 7$" width="43" height="12" />. But the answer is not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/4/43420adcd0fce9dce3978d53b97a8e0262b58da4.png" class="latex" alt="$7,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> because we are asked for the number of 10-cent coins, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/2/9c2712274dfe620527cb9f845711e9861b02d59f.png" class="latex" alt="$12 - 7 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="129" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the coins are 5-cent and 10-cent, all possible values that can be made will be multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> To have exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> different multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/21415a2eb558eb7c199a0382fcd50cbeda9299fb.png" class="latex" alt="$5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> we will need to make up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/e/7fea07b9bf739f8e420081f31186264b91ced2ce.png" class="latex" alt="$85$" width="17" height="12" /> cents. If all twelve coins were 5-cent coins, we will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> cents possible. Each trade of a 5-cent coin for a 10-cent coin will gain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cents, and as we need to gain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> cents, the answer is 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce935daa182504849cb5ce878344814211351abc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p><p>For small scale substitutions of 5 cent for 10 cent, like in this problem, it is quite fast, however, if the problem is a little bit more complex, knowing that you need 85 cents, explained above, it is also possible to use a system of equations. It would be 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/feef9fa2a66020eae20f4850306fbfcfc511a060.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5x+10y=85\]" width="110" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/d/a5d38bfc59e64867544c7fedd40f4be483be5b00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x+y=12\]" width="83" height="15" />
Solving this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> (5-cent coins) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/917039807758230e4c2af080912ae8560dc7c7ec.png" class="latex" alt="$= 7$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> (10-cent coins) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/4/f24b7a4c7abb64f9820b8fab98c93b556a9c26f2.png" class="latex" alt="$= 5$" width="27" height="12" />, so again the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce935daa182504849cb5ce878344814211351abc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
