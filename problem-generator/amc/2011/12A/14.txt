<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are single-digit positive integers chosen independently and at random. What is the probability that the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> lies above the parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/3/91322d71a8047b18fcfd5404805bfb60edc069e9.png" class="latex" alt="$y=ax^2-bx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/2/f12eb7c304014b84dcd2cb2a6c4ad7821f882991.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{11}{81} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{13}{81} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{5}{27} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{17}{81} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{19}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="413" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> lies above the parabola, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/a/60a3ed3ace8b25d15740d21d631ef8135b6cc285.png" class="latex" alt="$y(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" />. We thus get the inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/0/860766ff5d833f621e77751699877b7e87773827.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;a^3-ba$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="15" />. Solving this for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/6/596c673e8c11a273f0989d9775ef5e276876640a.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;\frac{a^3}{a+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="75" height="40" />. Now note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/2/232ca535c3fe9c6fe55f300a37f65601f0bfb0f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a^3}{a+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="43" height="40" /> constantly increases when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is positive. Then since this expression is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, we can deduce that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> in order for the inequality to hold, since otherwise <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> would be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and not a single-digit integer. The only possibilities for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> are thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/1/a51c461d0679e83325a8e86656e49639d72a5a94.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> for our inequality, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can be any integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/9/0f90993ef5517be4725188bfcc14d704ef52aaea.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;\frac{8}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> for our inequality, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can be any integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340605c5f88cf6864adc5b146c3dffca7a45df22.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3$" width="42" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb6d0fa8c385cdf13f8e800d79dad7e28b01581.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;\frac{27}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="52" height="37" /> for our inequality, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can be any integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Finally, if we total up all the possibilities we see there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> points that satisfy the condition, out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/3/d63f3b079452c5ac922621901f783ded34903f2a.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \times 9 = 81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="13" /> total points. The probability of picking a point that lies above the parabola is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/0/1401e0221fa9ad467ab95cda7a8d473c835ec8b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{19}{81} \rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="74" height="38" />
</p>

~Shreyas S
