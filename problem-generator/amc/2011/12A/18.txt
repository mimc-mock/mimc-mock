<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/2/68269b799b7a534ce3266d4e3c8b73e83f13cb70.png" class="latex" alt="$\left|x+y\right|+\left|x-y\right|=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" />. What is the maximum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/4/f74bdc11951db06cc5c7ee7c520c297376c8c28b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-6x+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebccb94c356d4c6a364d75aad166d4749fcd39ae.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 7 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 8 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Plugging in some values, we see that the  graph of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bf6183c2f00385b53a1e9ddbe0b55e90afddb0.png" class="latex" alt="$|x+y|+|x-y| = 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" /> is a square bounded by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/c/e2cb78b31c6eea2ef509984603219a91867935af.png" class="latex" alt="$x= \pm 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/0/6601418f5acf74318228ed02eb2b99dc8c4db6fd.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \pm 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" />.
</p><p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/c/18c8242033e22ade3df383cd774708693552b877.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 6x + y^2 = (x-3)^2 + y^2 - 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="256" height="19" /> means the square of the distance from a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> minus 9. To maximize that value, we need to choose the point in the feasible region farthest from point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/e/1fe78b99fa2c727dd05f5486e73a376b2e83ce31.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, \pm 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />. Either one, when substituting into the function, yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/114228a01e21efe5394aa489ccef5bb000ccd93d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bf6183c2f00385b53a1e9ddbe0b55e90afddb0.png" class="latex" alt="$|x+y|+|x-y| = 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" /> is dealing with absolute values, the following could be deduced: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/c/f4cdee9e38ea584231849ed1165c61ac7556b513.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)+(x-y)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="18" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/6/5d69b423ce871a06d506a045cfba26dd48a93fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)-(x-y)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/d/84d8d143d7f20a569926f513f6bf077a89347f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$-(x+y)+(x-y)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17eaeb4df713d75a1a56aa362c8a12447b996878.png" class="latex" alt="$-(x+y)-(x-y)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />. Simplifying would give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c3b4834614e5e7e189cb7d621cfb4fb0bd36dd7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbab939b716843df7cc6fdb3e9a0b91b2fea5db.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86c2e8c17cff668023b2f29f2e95e4a0fafa6271.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" />. In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/4/f74bdc11951db06cc5c7ee7c520c297376c8c28b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-6x+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="18" />, we care most about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/1009021fa18e2843323bab9ed4b345dda018cf89.png" class="latex" alt="$-6x,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="16" /> since both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5a3faa3658bcbd8bde8e16cfa5baf019619fc9c.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="15" height="18" /> are non-negative. To maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/7/b6790c9ffc33aa66a7e235079d5619d3ed77236d.png" class="latex" alt="$-6x$" width="33" height="12" />, though, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> would have to be -1. Therefore, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86c2e8c17cff668023b2f29f2e95e4a0fafa6271.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbab939b716843df7cc6fdb3e9a0b91b2fea5db.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c3b4834614e5e7e189cb7d621cfb4fb0bd36dd7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />, the equation evaluates to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/114228a01e21efe5394aa489ccef5bb000ccd93d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
