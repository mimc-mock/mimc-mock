<p>Roy bought a new battery-gasoline hybrid car. On a trip the car ran exclusively on its battery for the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles, then ran exclusively on gasoline for the rest of the trip, using gasoline at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/f/cdf408103cbcf165a24062320d9e6e9f77724988.png" class="latex" alt="$0.02$" width="31" height="12" /> gallons per mile. On the whole trip he averaged <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" /> miles per gallon. How long was the trip in miles?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/d/a0d518388c803c2c161c06935c84611a957ee51a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 140 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 240 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 440 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 640 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 840$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="432" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/1/2e1fe825744bc8c7f1b8b09f44851b2903aff691.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{total miles}}{\text{total gas}}=55$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="122" height="40" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the distance in miles the car traveled during the time it ran on gasoline, then the amount of gas used is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/8906f338888a394b18fb163a67fdd1754ef9c571.png" class="latex" alt="$0.02x$" width="42" height="12" />. The total distance traveled is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/0/19088257fbfe0b303233cb1fe2bf4640dfa8ba69.png" class="latex" alt="$40+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/f/26fad89421453de090ddd751600529719d3ed587.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40+x}{0.02x}=55$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />. Solving this equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/c/73cd160dcd0f2b342aac2c41edbc225b95b16bee.png" class="latex" alt="$x=400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, so the total distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/d/41de023b0f377812c91e851487780b7d0369e7ec.png" class="latex" alt="$400 + 40 = \boxed{440 \ \mathbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="165" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the length of the trip in miles. Roy used no gasoline for the 40 first miles, then used 0.02 gallons of gasoline per mile on the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/994aa1a226bc81efb2c8eb1d02497cc7070a0e9b.png" class="latex" alt="$d - 40$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="16" /> miles, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/1/f6146d4dfb7fbb2ced35dec77d00adecc1f50d32.png" class="latex" alt="$0.02 (d - 40)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="98" height="20" /> gallons. Hence, his average mileage was
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a772dcfc2fb689f01a1999b157ab50bd974810.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{d}{0.02 (d - 40)} = 55.\]" width="144" height="41" />
Multiplying both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/1/f6146d4dfb7fbb2ced35dec77d00adecc1f50d32.png" class="latex" alt="$0.02 (d - 40)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="98" height="20" />, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/c/66c249d90e244a5fc4f853986694d554ca878d6b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[d = 55 \cdot 0.02 \cdot (d - 40) = 1.1d - 44.\]" width="274" height="18" />
Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/5747ecd0fa9467d6b6e2c2da0ef2700bd559081f.png" class="latex" alt="$0.1d = 44$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927b16654fd1efba84026da46c9de24f18a89052.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \boxed{440}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="74" height="25" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/d/abd337e8b85dc471b3cb7c333dea9fd5d610a2f2.png" class="latex" alt="$(C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" />.
</p>
