<p>In 1991 the population of a town was a perfect square. Ten years later, after an increase of 150 people, the population was 9 more than a perfect square. Now, in 2011, with an increase of another 150 people, the population is once again a perfect square. Which of the following is closest to the percent growth of the town's population during this twenty-year period?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc27b0a7772cec8b488734f4691c705d7ab01455.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 42 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 47 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 52\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 57\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 62$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the population of the town in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/0/520099e20f828fb094685e6f5f1b41ceb3340a5c.png" class="latex" alt="$1991$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/1/591f689f950feb4a9cff58ff3166d915f916a405.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="18" />. Let the population in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/7/de7e2da81334aad3aeeb09706fed72248196613b.png" class="latex" alt="$2001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/e/3be74b64ed8618791751b86f36a13d1d609a330a.png" class="latex" alt="$q^2+9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="18" />. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/c/ffce2ba89009cc3e1893857ebc4d4afdc293aedf.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+150=q^2+9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="138" height="18" />. Rearrange this equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/516e22f3e2b2961ba297f8c7b0c2e279fcca8832.png" class="latex" alt="$141=q^2-p^2=(q-p)(q+p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="237" height="19" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> are both positive integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/7/7f7cc22cd4ac9ca3d8f330359dd7ec15e292ecf5.png" class="latex" alt="$q&gt;p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/c/11cd84c523a67b92bb8feea9126ebc51e13f0d4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(q-p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/c/29c069a5af352de389786ff0aaf9593a6e31281d.png" class="latex" alt="$(q+p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> also must be, and thus, they are both factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/80401851927344d980a3cea9388530b9a9f1b065.png" class="latex" alt="$141$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />. We have two choices for pairs of factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/80401851927344d980a3cea9388530b9a9f1b065.png" class="latex" alt="$141$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/80401851927344d980a3cea9388530b9a9f1b065.png" class="latex" alt="$141$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a21383a2b05dbe4ed4ecd095273f0cabc32b78a6.png" class="latex" alt="$47$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. Assuming the former pair, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/c/11cd84c523a67b92bb8feea9126ebc51e13f0d4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(q-p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> must be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/c/29c069a5af352de389786ff0aaf9593a6e31281d.png" class="latex" alt="$(q+p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/f/01feb6471816c8ed1238696d93a6fac2adad11e1.png" class="latex" alt="$q-p=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/1/e21009c9b5ea5baac12ebebcdd3266436e2480cf.png" class="latex" alt="$q+p=141$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="15" />. Solve to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/2/352aadbc81cac2d3e42cc6c2a96263c165d61b79.png" class="latex" alt="$p=70, q=71$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="110" height="16" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/a/deaba5182ef948c6520a2b9773e23c39cfabedd3.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+300$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="18" /> is not a perfect square, this is not the correct pair. Solve for the other pair to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/f/3bfbd467b9cebb67cbff1e4532c21987bc0217cc.png" class="latex" alt="$p=22, q=25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />. This time, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/8/ce83914cb50bd7f6e04ccda624b8f54f3cc7bf0a.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+300=22^2+300=784=28^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="266" height="18" />. This is the correct pair. Now, we find the percent increase from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/c/07ceef2192d1e14a81be0d948a4f6f8074aa79b7.png" class="latex" alt="$22^2=484$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="15" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/2/852421ea55cfe195f445bf7d5c34e470ee5848fd.png" class="latex" alt="$28^2=784$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="15" />. Since the increase is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b899aba26cc33f0026125daebb44a60349c999b6.png" class="latex" alt="$300$" width="26" height="12" />, the percent increase is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/c/5fc1d3cd7e3aa0abe1dc47eb11c6a17c65420bc9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{300}{484}\times100\%\approx\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 62\%}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="198" height="38" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Proceed through the difference of squares for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/516e22f3e2b2961ba297f8c7b0c2e279fcca8832.png" class="latex" alt="$141=q^2-p^2=(q-p)(q+p)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="237" height="19" />
</p><p>However, instead of testing both pairs of factors we take a more certain approach. Here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/2/e2221c84f16a3f75ae07e5023384cc40d9c05357.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2$" width="14" height="15" /> is the population of the town in 2011.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/6/cd6252b95c247c04cdcb59ab39c2656a06ae46b2.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2-p^2=300$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/3/513833dead0993493be0291ef320f3ae0cd9b2d8.png" class="latex" alt="$(r-p)(r+p)=300$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" />
Test through pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> that makes sure <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/4/db4a800e7957c2080e4fea1a3454a1d366b8c018.png" class="latex" alt="$p=22$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/5/7552e42ec1191630db7d18c90e5850ba7db7e3a6.png" class="latex" alt="$p=70$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="16" />. 
Then go through the same routine as demonstrated above to finish this problem. 
</p><p>Note that this approach might take more testing if one is not familiar with finding factors.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Since all the answer choices are around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7f4de02215a8c5319886c13b2e291811346e1c.png" class="latex" alt="$50\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" />, we know the town's starting population must be around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/a/caa9bc3e1ca566d171ec6b80e39ee4cc913b5aa1.png" class="latex" alt="$600$" width="26" height="12" />. We list perfect squares from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8924412a12becf7905729885a46e3e351ec5a86.png" class="latex" alt="$400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b07bd97036c44126d9fe466cc2b593f2a2390fb2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[441, 484, 529,576,625,676,729,784,841,900,961\]" width="379" height="16" />We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/9/3b91c51d372bfcdddb879cc9576495733eaa046c.png" class="latex" alt="$484$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/c/a9c7cc583137cd786583f9df893ce7124f6b20e8.png" class="latex" alt="$784$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> differ by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b899aba26cc33f0026125daebb44a60349c999b6.png" class="latex" alt="$300$" width="26" height="12" />, and can we can confirm that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/9/3b91c51d372bfcdddb879cc9576495733eaa046c.png" class="latex" alt="$484$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" /> is the correct starting number by noting that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/5/df5929545c197448ba126afb58014932835d50ab.png" class="latex" alt="$484+150=634=25^2+9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="209" height="16" />. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/7/a37aafab9a76432b90fab0fb6164ec8ef14c3438.png" class="latex" alt="$784/484-1\approx \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 62\%}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="198" height="29" />.
</p>
