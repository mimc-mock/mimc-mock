<p>Let X and Y be the following sums of arithmetic sequences: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/5/525b936d5ed2de6f87450939c587d6421b133c9f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*}X &amp;=&amp; 10+12+14+\cdots+100,\\ Y &amp;=&amp; 12+14+16+\cdots+102.\end{eqnarray*}" width="259" height="39" />
</p><p>What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/6/49659cb7aa29cc0ed1e7ca802e9037dd62ae6b1f.png" class="latex" alt="$Y - X?$" width="61" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/7/d87dd3ff550e21b204ae29bc075bfa777ac80ca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 92\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 98\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 100\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 102\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 112$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="421" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We see that both sequences have equal numbers of terms, so reformat the sequence to look like: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/516f2a749ff4b6f07dce34b743b20906e35d5a8b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} Y = \ &amp;12 + 14 + \cdots + 100 + 102\\ X = 10 \ + \ &amp;12 +  14 + \cdots + 100\\ \end{align*}" width="284" height="39" />
From here it is obvious that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/b/80b5dd30c641685971ccc4289466179054d75cf6.png" class="latex" alt="$Y - X = 102 - 10 = \boxed{92 \ \mathbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="235" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h3>
<p>Another way to see this is to let the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f65d44832034a8684bc6b52de4719b38af55ea0.png" class="latex" alt="$12+14+16+...+100=x.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="224" height="14" /> So, the sequences become
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/6/056cf14cc08dfcec6b8fdd0056959ca23b47a797.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} X = 10+x \\ Y= x+102 \\ \end{align*}" width="98" height="39" />
</p><p>Like before, the difference between the two sequences is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcf1c65720b691dcdc08d7c93ecb3196ba36ce43.png" class="latex" alt="$Y-X=102-12=92.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="191" height="13" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We see that every number in Y's sequence is two more than every corresponding number in X's sequence. Since there are 46 numbers in each sequence, the difference must be:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70aadaf39344d9338919559332c34408263fbaba.png" class="latex" alt="$46\cdot 2=\boxed{92}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/5/da51920352eb931862c26cd1427e5f4bf0f833f7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} X&amp;=10+12+14+\cdots +100 \\ Y&amp;=X-10+102 = X+92 \\  Y-X &amp;= (X+92)-X \\  Y-X &amp;= X-X+92 \\  Y-X &amp;= 0+92 \\  Y-X &amp;= \boxed{92} \quad \quad \textbf{(A)}\\ \end{align*}" width="268" height="148" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/b/3bbb9e25e5be095e317771a9c5d00877ee9c8012.png" class="latex" alt="$\blacksquare$" width="13" height="12" />
</p><p>- <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4b2b4ed349c1e7a071b605ce366b4b66c81866f.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{herobrine-india}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="12" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>In an actual contest, this would probably take too much time but is nevertheless a solution.
The general formula for computing sums of any arithmetic sequence where x is the number of terms, f is the first term and l is the last term is ((f+l)/2)*x. If one uses that formula for both sequences, they will get 2530 as the sum for X and 2622 as the sum for Y.
Subtracting Y-X, one will get the answer, 92.
- danfan
</p>
