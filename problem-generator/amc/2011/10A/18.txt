Circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> share one point of tangency. Circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> has a point of tangency with the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/2/7f2a482b08ddebc962ed9858ddab94a3db7140e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}.$" width="32" height="15" /> What is the area inside circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> but outside circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d3713b61d71855f44ce52196d5bf838501d09ad5.png" class="latex" alt="$B?$" width="22" height="12" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/4/f84b9e326bd427c8608100174e752c08642c3129.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.1cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(0,0), B=(2,0), C=(1,-1); pair M=(1,0); pair D=(2,-1); dot (A); dot (B); dot (C); dot (D); dot (M);  draw(Circle(A,1)); draw(Circle(B,1)); draw(Circle(C,1));  draw(A--B); draw(M--D); draw(D--B);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,W); label(&quot;$M$&quot;,M,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,SE); [/asy]" width="212" height="158" />
</p><p>The requested area is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> minus the area shared between circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the other intersection of circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p><p>The area shared between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of the regions between arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/d/16d7466b39123bcd705f40246fe2d2b59eb2749a.png" class="latex" alt="$\widehat {MD}$" width="35" height="19" /> and line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/3/1b31782c8439bb79194a830084c6c61a1feb22bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MD}$" width="35" height="15" />, which is (considering the arc on circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />) a quarter of the circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> minus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/1/8418b1ef0e57b381ef7a6380aeda2b78bb76ba55.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MDB$" width="65" height="13" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/2/952af8bd9a6b3237f46739b850d7ebd16790e3a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi r^2}{4}-\frac{bh}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="74" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e8057a4afc7344fb96c6dfb7f74dfb3478c44981.png" class="latex" alt="$b = h = r = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="107" height="13" />
</p><p>(We can assume this because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/4/8642b7c5cd1300d35caeaeb0547bb040af5f8eff.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DBM$" width="62" height="12" /> is 90 degrees, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/4/7441715d996526e8f5fcdee56db17df4636e04bd.png" class="latex" alt="$CDBM$" width="63" height="12" /> is a square, due to the application of the tangent chord theorem at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" />)
</p><p>So the area of the small region is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/7/b879b6e7a19d0ce8fc8ffd8fa6030c34280e552c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="48" height="37" />
</p><p>The requested area is area of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> minus 4 of this area:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/2/fc2db74dc1991db2b0c18eb68d162907a6cefbcb.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi 1^2 - 4(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{1}{2}) = \pi - \pi + 2 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="256" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/9/0b9b68aaa5462a2128fb5d06e10449ebc58b641f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/4825408fc1136554f018d84cef22b540d73f668d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.1cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(0,0), B=(2,0), C=(1,1); pair D=(2,1); pair E=(0,1); pair F = (1, 2); pair M = (1, 0); dot (A); dot (B); dot (C); dot (D); dot (E); dot (F); dot (M);  draw(Circle(A,1)); draw(Circle(B,1)); draw(Circle(C,1));  draw (D--F--E--M--D);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,W); label(&quot;$M$&quot;,M,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,E); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,W); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,N); [/asy]" width="212" height="182" />
</p><p>We can move the area above the part of the circle above the segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> down, and similarly for the other side. Then, we have a square, whose diagonal is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so the area is then just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/d/28dd1eb0b1b5268567783f181e58edf4ec898698.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="94" height="46" />.
</p>
