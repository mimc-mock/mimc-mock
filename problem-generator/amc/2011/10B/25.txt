<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c073f8a05257a19c2dacc368858763bb44fa3747.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> be a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fab676152392a40e2280411f3364ea26be122018.png" class="latex" alt="$2011, 2012,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/7/897470a282b6b85fa5221eacd41369b9a157f491.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/a/9da0180d6c9f9cb378cf15b3765d6ed2598bfefd.png" class="latex" alt="$T_n = \triangle ABC$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/2/a12e13563a6df1a207dad9672dd7b73df2a96faa.png" class="latex" alt="$D, E,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are the points of tangency of the incircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> to the sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/a/5aaa2452d9ce4302e49ed83896ce666bd63e981c.png" class="latex" alt="$AB, BC$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="65" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f24d2b218b0e9ef02fc3dd6846491158adbb440.png" class="latex" alt="$AC,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="31" height="16" /> respectively, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/1/6a1ef39be9a742204eb7347c5d32c9941e98c69d.png" class="latex" alt="$T_{n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="16" /> is a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/a/91ae80de04ce313dba6efc4d1bbfd0881281429d.png" class="latex" alt="$AD, BE,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/b/30be8247e2f804c432204f1a536822abfb617b63.png" class="latex" alt="$CF,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> if it exists. What is the perimeter of the last triangle in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/0/b60c10b4a765d25063e04b8736fa9f9bf879ba22.png" class="latex" alt="$( T_n )$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/6082d98ce926120437c31e06651867fa2d822cbf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1509}{8} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1509}{32} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{1509}{64} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1509}{128} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{1509}{256}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="503" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By constructing the bisectors of each angle and the perpendicular radii of the incircle the triangle consists of 3 kites. 
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:File2011AMC10B25.png" class="image"><img alt="File2011AMC10B25.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/e/e4/File2011AMC10B25.png/260px-File2011AMC10B25.png" width="260" height="238" srcset="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//e/e4/File2011AMC10B25.png 1.5x" /></a></div></div>
<p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/70987852c74ed03e0d7d1d0db9d617405452d80a.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=AF$" width="81" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/e/d0e73db3e75cd8c521bbc7b2ff9536b62a32d05f.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=BE$" width="83" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfb7184668b628bdc29d39c72f10f51bd2bb6ade.png" class="latex" alt="$CE=CF$" width="81" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/6/7760df28f3fa380941757eb80b3396a2188627f5.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = x, BD = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f58306caa4b9f0c8b23d76403dec33ee4dd7843b.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = z$" width="61" height="12" /> gives three equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/5/965e8e5e8bd8c9aa4905bb44811f75eb0144e672.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y = a-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/a/40a47c15c5ac2ccfb5d17cf2265b666977870cff.png" class="latex" alt="$x+z = a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/e/ade26d39eb9af77b9fd3c94e2d11994398afb38f.png" class="latex" alt="$y+z = a+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="15" />
</p><p>(where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/6/3869a9c028169519d8478cb0208d53a0489667d1.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" /> for the first triangle.)
</p><p>Solving gives:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/3/ee392e400671a942b9a1cab4c3b37ae5ee66e14a.png" class="latex" alt="$x= \frac{a}{2} -1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="33" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/b/58b341d53a2db7b942e8d9c4ba7cd1968e624e69.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{a}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="33" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/9/a29b354af5165528ba258350bafb2933ec3481df.png" class="latex" alt="$z = \frac{a}{2}+1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="33" />
</p><p>Subbing in gives that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40db9b6f10ff583906d78a06a76e08e04381bc1a.png" class="latex" alt="$T_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> has sides of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/0/be06ba0478b543b8c4c97f16d1e688d4e9fe9718.png" class="latex" alt="$1005, 1006, 1007$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="125" height="16" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0d8e9c5c70be5e8b812a08ce2566d0db946c608.png" class="latex" alt="$T_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> can easily be derived from this as the sides still differ by 1 hence the above solutions still work (now with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/5984de01a1f436ccc82505aa598a52a3700bacaf.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1006$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />). All additional triangles will differ by one as the solutions above differ by one so this process can be repeated indefinitely until the side lengths no longer form a triangle.
</p><p>Subbing in gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0d8e9c5c70be5e8b812a08ce2566d0db946c608.png" class="latex" alt="$T_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> with sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/a/60ac389ef1f2933688aac2df07271aba080fdaa8.png" class="latex" alt="$502, 503, 504$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="16" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/85746d1f1493a5145f5e46d2a8a07677a21580d8.png" class="latex" alt="$T_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> has sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/f/c6f35998cdd5712c9f5bd9ea35805287b9ab3f93.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{501}{2}, \frac{503}{2}, \frac{505}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" />.

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09ce7108c9cb767007bb49353f9afd60f717b939.png" class="latex" alt="$T_9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> has sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/e/21eed12de15e06a1c75ce9bae4951be9bbe25a11.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{439}{64}, \frac{503}{64}, \frac{567}{64}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="108" height="38" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/9/3a997c8d13100b2b38c175de4a901e2180774424.png" class="latex" alt="$T_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="23" height="15" /> has sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/3/9e3e362547f7d5d438267b7b38da443da9f3230f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{375}{128}, \frac{503}{128}, \frac{631}{128}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="108" height="38" />.

triangle as

We then have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/9/2b9920a8e3ee127d223e3e870d9f159e7e121231.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[503 - 2^{k-3} + 503 &gt; 503 + 2^{k-3}\]" width="239" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/2635979cb9f1f962bc82b741d873a6fcebae4e5e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1006 - 2^{k-3} &gt; 503 + 2^{k-3}\]" width="198" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/f/d6f029700f30ed21a551b2d1f93a36960f925c24.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[503 &gt; 2^{k-2}\]" width="84" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4e819ce0c67d247883a5b6b4eae591c3f155c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9 &gt; k-2\]" width="73" height="13" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Proceeding similarly to the first solution, we have that sides of each triangle are of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/e/82ebeb120b02f74a8c1927635ebbb99bd31e57f0.png" class="latex" alt="$a, a+1, a+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="107" height="15" /> for some number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. Also, note that the perimeter of each triangle is half of the previous one. In order for the triangle to not exist, it must not satisfy the triangle inequality, meaning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/2/52282353ee77ae473abc08fd5e52a10019c647ff.png" class="latex" alt="$a + a + 1 &lt; a+2 \Rightarrow a&lt;1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="208" height="13" />. Then, the perimeter would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/495ba5478f6f26f7fc84f8f79b34444198993edd.png" class="latex" alt="$a + a + 1 + a + 2 = 3a + 3 &lt; 6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="243" height="14" />. So, to have a proper triangle, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/b/d2b01fb09851e2032282dc86ff7a3441dc066f48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3018}{2^{k}} &gt; 6 \Rightarrow 2^k &lt; 503 \Rightarrow 2^{k} \leq 512$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="267" height="37" />. The first triangle to not work would have perimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/08268659ca045800abd96f59b9a82358f702ef20.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3018}{512} = \frac{1509}{256}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="103" height="38" />,  thus the answer is   <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb01216a7b55f46b3a702fa4f0dbcb67b8d95d69.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \frac{1509}{128}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="81" height="48" />.
</p>
