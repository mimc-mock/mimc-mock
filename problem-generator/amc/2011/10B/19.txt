<p>What is the product of all the roots of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/c/4dc3a78877196f453f6485a158c37ac0179def64.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{5 | x | + 8} = \sqrt{x^2 - 16}.\]" width="178" height="32" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e19cccf86811b14d8513e8400b2c232a4422dd61.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -64 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -24 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ -9 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 24 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 576$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="460" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, square both sides, and isolate the absolute value.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3835e5635778d29a1fad8f92fc7677db1224049d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 5|x|+8&amp;=x^2-16\\ 5|x|&amp;=x^2-24\\ |x|&amp;=\frac{x^2-24}{5}. \\ \end{align*}" width="150" height="94" />
Solve for the absolute value and factor.
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/c/aace6d1f15c06cf2cad68c195b6118221e4e62ee.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{x^2-24}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="39" />
</p><p>Multiplying both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> gives us 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f07ba2b1a89474174e953230fe61b76604b0ab1d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5x=x^2-24.\]" width="104" height="15" />
Rearranging and factoring, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aae34ca63c74d3adf77eadf19cbca818e3c2e8e1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} x^2-5x-24 &amp;=0, \\ (x-8)(x+3) &amp;= 0.\\ \end{align*}" width="147" height="44" />
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff48f1943f9d301aebff7d784946b6d04e971497.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{-x^2+24}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="39" />
</p><p>As above, we multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> to find
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/f/cdf5d4795926a3868b4cfa03f73979f12a8c054d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5x=-x^2+24.\]" width="119" height="16" />
Rearranging and factoring gives us
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/5/ae53145c144ea1577622e8025a8966fb22d3ef15.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} x^2+5x-24 &amp;=0, \\ (x+8)(x-3) &amp;= 0. \\ \end{align*}" width="147" height="44" />
</p><p>Combining these cases, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/7/7c7677be7d1140d1dd9fc89a546fba740716cf06.png" class="latex" alt="$x= -8, -3, 3, 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="16" />. Because our first step of squaring is not reversible, however, we need to check for extraneous solutions. Plug each solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> back into the original equation to ensure it works. Whether the number is positive or negative does not matter since the absolute value or square will cancel it out anyways.
Trying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/9/e49364eecc035109fcdbe17b0e528a936b12831e.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|=|3|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/b/60b6e6f88e4471ede7b280deed1ed3213ed2c5c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sqrt{5|3|+8}&amp;=\sqrt{3^2-16}, \\ \sqrt{15+8}&amp;=\sqrt{9-16}, \\ \sqrt{23} &amp;\not= \sqrt{-7}.\\ \end{align*}" width="176" height="89" />
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/0/690ef55a607beaf2389d1ee102df817721bc2392.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 3$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/f/25f98a312772bd7cb40bab7259747832999e949d.png" class="latex" alt="$x= -3$" width="57" height="12" /> are extraneous.
</p><p>Checking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/2/1e2dcf402135eddee39705f714522892b457da97.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|=|8|$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="66" height="20" />, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/5/b756d892526539a12c5fbb899a0c598ba4f391ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sqrt{5|8|+8}&amp;=\sqrt{8^2-16}, \\ \sqrt{40+8}&amp;=\sqrt{64-16}, \\ \sqrt{48}&amp;=\sqrt{48}.\\ \end{align*}" width="178" height="87" />
</p><p>The roots of our original equation are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/922c4f40c55a550006c796efcd63a7828ecfd95f.png" class="latex" alt="$-8$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and product is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/9699cfce7a10709a5500da062eb6384f1bf72551.png" class="latex" alt="$-8 \times 8 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} -64}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="160" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Square both sides, to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/d/acde3b0fcf5d1c23c76a2a1359ce635324b0c27b.png" class="latex" alt="$5|x| + 8 = x^2-16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />. Rearrange to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/90762872788a6ffd050a9f10046c1bf6c498bb73.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 5|x| - 24 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="19" />. Seeing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05f747c1c69944fc048156ea1404184eda914419.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = |x|^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="19" />, substitute to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/5/7c522c5bd93dc44994badcf6d5a759e394af3965.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|^2 - 5|x| - 24 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="153" height="19" />. We see that this is a quadratic in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/49428192d366caf373c0af3c3261687f12b4c883.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="18" height="18" />. Factoring, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad7cb3e78dfc89c8ef4764c57a75ffc9777b94dd.png" class="latex" alt="$(|x|-8)(|x|+3) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/e/deea4659aceca169fdb90cc1cf3b7f147b43c85f.png" class="latex" alt="$|x| = \{8,-3\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />. Since the radicand of the equation can't be negative, the sole solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/9/9d96accd1eba599cf79f90bfd31371e7e5c6ae08.png" class="latex" alt="$|x| = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/922c4f40c55a550006c796efcd63a7828ecfd95f.png" class="latex" alt="$-8$" width="22" height="12" />. The product is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/9699cfce7a10709a5500da062eb6384f1bf72551.png" class="latex" alt="$-8 \times 8 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} -64}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="160" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>First we note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/a/0ca8a46ef6e620e24a7493da730b8f807e53b026.png" class="latex" alt="$x \in (-\infty,-4] \cup [4,\infty]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="176" height="20" />. This will help us later with finding extraneous solutions. 
Next, we have two cases:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/6/3d6afb8a6c618998f75bf9f582f306412d371bc3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{IF } x\leq 4:\]" width="74" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/2/602f9f8cc425c49e35aa9296846f50bccc71de87.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{ } \sqrt{-5x+8} = \sqrt{x^2-16} \implies x^2+5x-24=0 \rightarrow x = -8,3\]" width="462" height="20" />.
We note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is not in the range of possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />'s and thus is not a solution.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d822a323fdbd0ca4bbcfde31f313013c85a1a3d4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{IF } x\geq 4:\]" width="74" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/b/8ebd3bdae0e891dcd918f9112ac2ce644c7622d6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{ } \sqrt{5x+8} = \sqrt{x^2-16} \implies x^2-5x-24=0 \rightarrow x = -3,8\]" width="448" height="20" />.
We again not that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31b51deaa1a9e903d120a02141556c3e70e348.png" class="latex" alt="$-3$" width="22" height="12" /> is an extraneous solution.
</p><p>Thus, we have the two solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/922c4f40c55a550006c796efcd63a7828ecfd95f.png" class="latex" alt="$-8$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. Therefore product is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/2/0e27bb61cf1a283dff786fe51d0592be68e23251.png" class="latex" alt="$-8 \cdot 8 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} -64}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="153" height="30" />.
</p><p>-ConfidentKoala4
</p>
