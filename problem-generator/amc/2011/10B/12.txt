<p>Keiko walks once around a track at exactly the same constant speed every day. The sides of the track are straight, and the ends are semicircles. The track has a width of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> meters, and it takes her <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds longer to walk around the outside edge of the track than around the inside edge. What is Keiko's speed in meters per second?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/5/f655d92c9f5c4d28a941ce8c9078def70a3dc727.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{\pi}{3} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{2\pi}{3} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \pi \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{4\pi}{3} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{5\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="400" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be Keiko's speed in meters per second, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the length of the straight parts of the track, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the radius of the smaller circles, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0afb759a44ae0371e034791f08327974f6737f4.png" class="latex" alt="$b+6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" /> be the radius of the larger circles. The length of the inner edge will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/1/2c1827bfe2c736c56bcdadfc7aa72307c3c2bcbc.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+2b \pi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="14" /> and the length of the outer edge will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/b/d6b7ca65b04e7ffc2d212856a90fc6e01742ee6b.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+2\pi (b+6).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="18" /> Since it takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds longer for Keiko to walk on the outer edge,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2dc6704e40df5f8806a4a4645a8215fb75bf2b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{2a+2b \pi}{s} + 36 &amp;= \frac{2a+2\pi (b+6)}{s}\\ 2a+2b\pi +36s &amp;= 2a+2b\pi +12\pi\\ 36s&amp;=12\pi\\ s&amp;=\boxed{\textbf{(A)} \frac{\pi}{3}} \end{align*}" width="261" height="140" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>It is basically the same as Solution 1 except you can completely disregard the straight edges of the track since it will take Keiko the same time to walk that length for both inner and outer circles. Instead, focus on the circular part. If the diameter of the smaller circle were <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/6/e1630c915bd1b27bfa1f505bf1ac0bb9f5d61f32.png" class="latex" alt="$r,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="11" height="11" /> then the length of the smaller circle would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59df077e771147e3a993e247e4f6fa74bfedba35.png" class="latex" alt="$r \pi$" width="19" height="8" /> and the length of the larger circle would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/c/1ec09550d98bf8e2e85365948597ecd3388b5868.png" class="latex" alt="$(r+12) \pi.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> Since it still takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> seconds longer,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a864b984927ba89a06e64c28f53b7b4b99ef95e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{r \pi}{s} + 36 &amp;= \frac{(r+12)\pi}{s}\\ r \pi + 36s &amp;= r \pi + 12 \pi\\ s&amp;=\boxed{\textbf{(A)} \frac{\pi}{3}} \end{align*}" width="169" height="115" />
</p>
