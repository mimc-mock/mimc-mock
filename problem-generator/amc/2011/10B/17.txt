<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/6/2f6bf7e298a4e3ffb1382c4184d4cbc4905307a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AEB$" width="55" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/b/adb4154a7111a70c199f4bf120ec2ae21be5e8dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE$" width="55" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" /> because they are in the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7a99fdd3cb1bbec54decf5f73c37509a4d855c.png" class="latex" alt="$4&#160;: 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />. When an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Inscribed_angle" title="Inscribed angle">inscribed angle</a> contains the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Diameter" title="Diameter">diameter</a>, the inscribed angle is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_angle" title="Right angle">right angle</a>. Therefore by triangle sum theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/d/c9da7d145f96e7fd324f4e1be77ca5213a5814ef.png" class="latex" alt="$4x+5x+90=180 \longrightarrow x=10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="244" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/74678e9b79545bc2fbf6d524467f4275653e92a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE = 50$" width="97" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/9/c497a217d0bf5df62b6f1a132707f93b2f6cafb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE = \angle BED$" width="137" height="13" /> because they are alternate interior angles and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/e/9bed5136603ff28cfaab79d1a0d4661563d5ba59.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB} \parallel \overline{ED}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="78" height="20" />. Opposite angles in a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Cyclic" title="Cyclic">cyclic</a> quadrilateral are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Supplementary" title="Supplementary">supplementary</a>, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/f/6bfaeb238aa65114fca903d70350cbdb287b1bbd.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BED + \angle BCD = 180$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="188" height="14" />. Use substitution to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/b/0fb93ccf928dd78d4ad2e999e1c899d02463ea98.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE + \angle BCD = 180 \longrightarrow 50 + \angle BCD = 180 \longrightarrow \angle BCD = \boxed{\textbf{(C)} 130}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="564" height="28" />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80a6c3aca7b9063d5d75e662792f0a0edb8a143.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE = \angle BED=50$" width="179" height="13" /> as before. The sum of the interior angles for quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/9/689a440e1bc302569a37a7c9da1885779ee76a4b.png" class="latex" alt="$EBCD$" width="58" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/2/c62a47cbc153efdf4611d8660429ea879be3f192.png" class="latex" alt="$360$" width="26" height="12" />. Denote the center of the circle as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7a0c5ee92b607c3ca1def98c8c132ff0b4c4bb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle PDE = \angle PED = 50$" width="180" height="12" />. Denote <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/a/9aa101eb9f6ad895ec65fb4cca5204bb9b131648.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle PDC = \angle PCD = x$" width="172" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba58824935ff4a6b31c4b14ad01e056ace236036.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle PBC = \angle PCB = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="169" height="16" />. We wish to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/7/21756278a573f005a4a93226b4deeb87f2cc0fd8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BCD = x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="16" />. Our equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb22a43b80b1ef6e59bad2268e75e2aa3a898759.png" class="latex" alt="$(\angle PDE +\angle PED)+(\angle PDC+\angle PCD)+(\angle PBC + \angle PCB) = 360 \longrightarrow 2(50) + 2x +2y = 360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="759" height="18" />. Our final equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030de7dd72fa6b1de21712bbef6209ae934651bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2(x+y)+100 = 360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="18" />. After subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, our answer becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/4/6445799352613878ec18d09a23176fb499ad432a.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=\boxed{\textbf{(C)} 130}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="134" height="28" />
</p>
