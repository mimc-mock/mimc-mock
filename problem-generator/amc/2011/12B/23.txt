<p>A bug travels in the coordinate plane, moving only along the lines that are parallel to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/b/c1bac8091bcf83ea0e346fc7124537a0abd073ac.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (-3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/8/bc8f6f5da7f77fad8f8b2f81797a77860c7e7bbe.png" class="latex" alt="$B = (3, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" />. Consider all possible paths of the bug from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> of length at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. How many points with integer coordinates lie on at least one of these paths?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/5/67590ea5b725083055b2322e61f1d3264428cf25.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 161 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 185 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\  195 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\  227 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\  255$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We declare a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to make up for the extra steps that the bug has to move. If the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> satisfies the property that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/d/42d288ed42d67cce338ebb85c01918624ed4c172.png" class="latex" alt="$|x - 3| + |y + 2| + |x + 3| + |y - 2| \le 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="314" height="18" />, then it is in the desirable range because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/2/182ac2c4b4d79cdf0a395e82f604969d9110f64a.png" class="latex" alt="$|x - 3| + |y + 2|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" /> is the length of the shortest path from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/feebdb3b0f3e50c42cb17c508e1539ad0ec63b35.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6eea57b782f571c147af21d845755cc0bc3457f2.png" class="latex" alt="$|x + 3| + |y - 2|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" /> is the length of the shortest path from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/3/1530ec52ec2f0176349857359faefcf3b58b3572.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/9/149ab491be6458aa24df85528f3f3913e2ddf1ef.png" class="latex" alt="$-3\le x \le 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="15" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/2362c0bc6a60569b51710c4fb2e1799b58a54817.png" class="latex" alt="$-7\le y \le 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="15" /> satisfy the property. there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/b/84bf0790cbc7a1ee8db12635ad0b9811edfe2d90.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \times 7 = 105$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> lattice points here.
</p><p>else let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/5/74535b137b516dd9b0bec962c89703167c7defe2.png" class="latex" alt="$3&lt; x \le 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" /> (and for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b70c2b47faef7541779b346a21c8c14afa39c8.png" class="latex" alt="$-8 \le x &lt; -3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="104" height="15" /> because it is symmetrical) We set 8 as the upper bound for x because the shortest distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/3/1530ec52ec2f0176349857359faefcf3b58b3572.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> added to the shortest distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/feebdb3b0f3e50c42cb17c508e1539ad0ec63b35.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/5/90580e90d5467d696a679228d150ba1cbb41f3a1.png" class="latex" alt="$|x - 3| + |y + 2| + |x + 3| + |y - 2|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="18" />. Since the minimum value for the difference between the y-coordinates is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a06334068417b1781d14e8b28af827907961eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d2ec5f3752f9f0e28b8ae99406cdef837a96bc4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x + 4 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/9/1491acc3a30f31356bcadd1a21dae63399c00d61.png" class="latex" alt="$-2x + 4 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="106" height="14" />. Thus, the upper and lower bounds for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/922c4f40c55a550006c796efcd63a7828ecfd95f.png" class="latex" alt="$-8$" width="22" height="12" />, respectively.
</p><p>Now we test each value for x satisfying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/5/74535b137b516dd9b0bec962c89703167c7defe2.png" class="latex" alt="$3&lt; x \le 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" /> and double the result because of symmetry.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea768618848c1aea8a32490c327911e8ab7243a3.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, the possibles values of y are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/1/031ec5522344128a4118b4209428e0fa44ab4d27.png" class="latex" alt="$|2y| \le 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> lattice points,
</p><p>for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" />, the possibles values of y are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/9/a9969088fb9c0c0875ea0f5f85a0b0dfb2a7811d.png" class="latex" alt="$|2y| \le 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> lattice points,
</p><p>for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea7da1b75cc1a945018db7316c26d618ca11ff9d.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 6$" width="43" height="12" />, the possibles values of y are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/c/44ce01b40964c2abf5721c0e42e4f4732e045e93.png" class="latex" alt="$|2y| \le 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> lattice points,
</p><p>for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d1e7375e601943b3bb958a24e41da8920d601a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 7$" width="43" height="12" />, the possibles values of y are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/1/f91861ef16676e826db5f01e50f9545e8ecfdd82.png" class="latex" alt="$|2y| \le 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> lattice points,
</p><p>for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/62434ae80ca145c20f0fbc7563f5151371ed163c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 8$" width="43" height="12" />, the possibles values of y are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f8c7887c13411afcc934da584f3859144af44ab.png" class="latex" alt="$|2y| \le 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> lattice points,
<br />
</p><p>Hence, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/e/3ee63d8065b5c8367a4e78b5e05fc9044d9be0a9.png" class="latex" alt="$105 + 2 ( 13 + 11 + 9 + 7 + 5) = \boxed{(C) 195}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="317" height="29" /> lattice points.
</p><p>One may also obtain the result by using Pick's Theorem(how?).
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608cdbeb609c98dce8d6082c12bd3081c6f34ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$i = a - \frac{b}{2} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="109" height="39" /> (Suggestion)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>(Anyone mind making a diagram for this)
Notice that the bug is basically moving from A to B (length 10) but going on a detour in the middle. 
</p><p>Specifically the detour would be of length 5 to some point and then going back by retracing its path (also length 5). Just to simplify things we can observe that the coordinates don't matter here and all we need to remember is that A and B are the diagonally opposite vertices of a 4 by 6 rectangle. The bug can start the "detour" from any point on or inside the rectangle. Notice that the bug can go 5 steps in the y direction, 5 steps in the x direction, or anything in between, so the points covered by possible detours from any point would look like a rhombus or square rotated 45 degrees (with centre at a point on or inside the rectangle). Drawing this out we would get an octagon. 
</p><p>Finding the final answer is then easy, for this solution I will slice the octagon into 4 rectangles (2 of which are squares) and 4 isosceles triangles. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/a/3dad45b75d18d29782753cc1cf835c8efd058bef.png" class="latex" alt="$(4+1)\cdot (6+1) = 35$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="166" height="20" /> points on or inside the original triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/b/0cb365d0c918e898a810e19779931fd25ac9c038.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="14" /> points covered by the rectangles above and below the original one and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9eeee466444d6eeab142671bf77bdf8b9e9cd648.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="14" /> points for the squares to the right and left of the original triangle. Lastly each of the four isosceles triangles cover <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/255f46aa0a207721061461f366f6bdd105aecb93.png" class="latex" alt="$4+3+2+1 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="145" height="14" /> points. (Notice that although the length of the detour is 5, the points on the edge of the triangles were already counted). 
</p><p>Adding these up, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a05df099c1ca68d96597222de6367abeaedafdc.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 35 + 2\cdot 25 + 4\cdot 10 = 195 =&gt; \boxed{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="297" height="32" />
</p>
