<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be two-digit positive integers with mean <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />. What is the maximum value of the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52b2cb411bc85412bfd26de9c83279199a4a40a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{33}{7} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{39}{7} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 9 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{99}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="387" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> have a mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db07fcde99953b37c5f8e5621abf6afa9f3e90c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}{2}=60$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="35" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0c8d7be7cf927227fb6186bd64b6755258c3438.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=120$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" />. To maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />, we need to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Since they are both two-digit positive integers, the maximum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> which gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/6/bd662d8cbd050d48a71ec7df8f99c22640527804.png" class="latex" alt="$y=21$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="15" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> cannot be decreased because doing so would increase <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, so this gives the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421bf9b858460fe582b07099cf54fcbc539e48b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{99}{21}=\boxed{\frac{33}{7}\ \textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="114" height="48" />
</p>
