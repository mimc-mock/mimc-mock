<p>A lattice point in an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />-coordinate system is any point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> where both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are integers. The graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d2264e4e7d4e433b38289ec6dd77ddeb0a18f84.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx + 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> passes through no lattice point with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/1/4f17264b2875c57d6725ca2bad50be5250b19866.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; x \leq 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/b/8ab24211e8c6cd92bef80e95ecf7d45d23afcc10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} &lt; m &lt; a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />. What is the maximum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/5/ec5f173f9dafbf807595b2377a8bfa4adc76ffff.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{51}{101} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{50}{99} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{51}{100} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{52}{101} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{13}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="440" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>It is very easy to see that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe54585e0c171194b0e07cc59767d097999658cc.png" class="latex" alt="$+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> in the graph does not impact whether it passes through lattice.
</p><p>We need to make sure that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> cannot be in the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084079bf1d54500f3c59257a7ec3467932b72ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/6/3966fdcb1e67bbabbd3b87cb3ace13fa0bd02ec5.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le b\le 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="15" />, otherwise the graph <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ffab7ab34ab1ae629edd3198607def46afabd1b.png" class="latex" alt="$y= mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> passes through lattice point at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26b35766a1f98740c053f6dd3d88cf9fdbf8b996.png" class="latex" alt="$x = b$" width="42" height="12" />. We only need to worry about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084079bf1d54500f3c59257a7ec3467932b72ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> very close to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/0/910392b69f0d2f81280510a8e36e2f99542500cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+1}{2m+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="58" height="38" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/9/579009300a824241479637a55b6076fa21ffb3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+1}{2m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="49" height="37" /> will be the only case we need to worry about and we want the minimum of those, clearly for  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/6/3966fdcb1e67bbabbd3b87cb3ace13fa0bd02ec5.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le b\le 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="15" />, the smallest is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />, so answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/b/29be17e30c8039a13e7cb834a06a001b9bc8fca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\frac{50}{99} \textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="62" height="48" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Like in the first solution, we note that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe54585e0c171194b0e07cc59767d097999658cc.png" class="latex" alt="$+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> does not affect our answer. Thus, we can ignore it and consider an equivalent problem with the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8cab3b86d4a13c3cc3680c32aaa80cc854689e.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" />.
</p><p>A line with a slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> passes through (among other lattice points) the lattice point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/1/2813504d248553bfdfe4adad6a58b2642fc6bcf9.png" class="latex" alt="$(100,50)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />. As the slope of the line increases from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, the first lattice point it hits is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e4771356d67b2ec6c2aeb83357b00d0db21703.png" class="latex" alt="$(99, 50)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />, the slope of that line being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> . So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />
</p>
