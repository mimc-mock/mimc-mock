<p>Rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/100344f1b264aea2d41a120e851f8d438519f4b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B = 120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="78" height="13" />°. Region <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> consists of all points inside the rhombus that are closer to vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> than any of the other three vertices. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/d/07da12523395b914ae39a5c1c99d0ee182a1a051.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 1 + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="465" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is a point in the rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197a52c0e360f6ffcb9085f553aa851170dee109.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> be the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perpendicular_bisector" title="Perpendicular bisector">perpendicular bisector</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a8f99a7d939dea7a3aab74b34bc58c53c3151b.png" class="latex" alt="$PB &lt; PC$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="13" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is on the same side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197a52c0e360f6ffcb9085f553aa851170dee109.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. The line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197a52c0e360f6ffcb9085f553aa851170dee109.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> divides the plane into two half-planes; let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/f/cef0dfb1f149679c8ce18b922c975ca5e4092365.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="31" height="15" /> be the half-plane containing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. Let us define similarly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/7/967077696335aca336a064e442bf5385cc44216f.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BD},S_{BD}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/4/044acb3ea84fac40cd8936e122cc489b652a8ad2.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BA},S_{BA}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="68" height="16" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/0/0001beed708dde0ce7a88c6e30c57f204c9e94d4.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD \cap S_{BC} \cap S_{BD} \cap S_{BA}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="216" height="15" />. The region turns out to be an irregular pentagon. We can make it easier to find the area of this region by dividing it into four triangles:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e2daaa8586c862ba27d0e7897a318104243855b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(8mm); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(4,0), B=(2,2sqrt(3)), C=(-2,2sqrt(3)), D=(0,0), E=(B+C)/2, F=(B+C+D)/3, G=(A+C)/2, H=(A+B+D)/3, I=(A+B)/2; fill((0,2sqrt(3))--B--(3,sqrt(3))--(2,(2sqrt(3))/3)--(0,(4sqrt(3))/3)--cycle,gray); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(D--(0,2sqrt(3))); draw(D--(3,sqrt(3))); draw(A--C); draw(F--B--H); draw(B--G);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SE);label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NE);label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NW);label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,SW); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,N);label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,SW);label(&quot;$G$&quot;,G,SW);label(&quot;$H$&quot;,H,S);label(&quot;$I$&quot;,I,NE); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(D--C),SW); [/asy]" width="265" height="168" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f372757d55c2d07adc720932c6d6e1396aa389.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCD$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/6/1661ac6f3544a6f06f111d5e09c7072da8bb76e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAD$" width="59" height="13" /> are equilateral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197a52c0e360f6ffcb9085f553aa851170dee109.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/5/4352403bc43f1526e1dedda2c4132c54bfee690e.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BD}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="28" height="15" /> contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/2/a328cf7f1dffc129ac905e4a5aa5e877618c4663.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell_{BA}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="15" /> contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/5/e25a0d7d7b20074128b224e839df2d8f479ac34e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEF \cong \triangle BGF \cong \triangle BGH \cong \triangle BIH$" width="310" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/d/7edf3afba191eb9958ab78c986da35e33e1681b3.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/c/1bca3882d8775b56f38dd6682825cecd4b0f0759.png" class="latex" alt="$EF = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="79" height="41" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/99522386e5bdd18a46622bba455d07407e52c51b.png" class="latex" alt="$[BEF] = \frac{1}{2}\cdot 1 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="153" height="41" />. Multiply this by 4 and it turns out that the pentagon has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31bf5c97700a9fc7de9ac76233e88dfc3d5a7812.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(C)\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="77" height="53" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We follow the steps shown above until we draw pentagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/c/2dcb3332457c098687f924ec2127574c55e7bcf2.png" class="latex" alt="$BIHFE$" width="69" height="12" />. We know that rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> can be divided into equilateral triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/86644a0d74ba9ba18fc2e69713901863ab6c3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CBD$" width="60" height="13" />. Using the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> special right triangle rules, we find the height of the equilateral triangles (and the height of the rhombus) to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />. Therefore, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e455d80f4657b1112ba302a6e2c42e9c81f780ee.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. We now have to take off the areas <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb94e3d043aa1652f6933df7dfc4a760ae51442c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDA$" width="59" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/979c6deb11752ef4146e52440d2955ee0beead13.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CEF$" width="59" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9925cb8e37e6df5baa00866e447e9629bb26cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AIH$" width="55" height="13" /> to get the desired shape. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb94e3d043aa1652f6933df7dfc4a760ae51442c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDA$" width="59" height="13" /> is just half of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c0092b8ccb8c897bd95811bd29aaef6301f29697.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt {3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9925cb8e37e6df5baa00866e447e9629bb26cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AIH$" width="55" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/979c6deb11752ef4146e52440d2955ee0beead13.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CEF$" width="59" height="13" /> are each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1190f645952b8b2b64c6f0c02e8e21f5381f4d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt {3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />, for a total area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/0/5101d13abacc5b5eaf9406349aeb504990bc2e77.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt {3}-\sqrt {3}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}=\boxed{(C)\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="282" height="53" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We split rhombus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> into two equilateral triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ada763e99852a6e44a15d46cce3c3709a944d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BCD$" width="44" height="12" />. In triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" />, the probability that a randomly selected point is closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> than either other point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> (why?). Similarly, in triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ada763e99852a6e44a15d46cce3c3709a944d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BCD$" width="44" height="12" />, the same principle applies. Thus, the area of the region closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b81bea41c718b66bb98b5fed9b0f1c6a18a44c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3} [ABD] + \frac{1}{3} [BCD]$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="155" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ada763e99852a6e44a15d46cce3c3709a944d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BCD$" width="44" height="12" /> are congruent, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/48128e17c956e755a0408b471090586ffc027af4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3} [ABD] + \frac{1}{3} [BCD] = \frac{2}{3} [ABD] = \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{(2)^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = \boxed{\frac{2\sqrt3}{3} = C}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="564" height="53" />, and we are done.
</p>
