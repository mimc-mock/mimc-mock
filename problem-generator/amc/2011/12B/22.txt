<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c073f8a05257a19c2dacc368858763bb44fa3747.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> be a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/e/efe8f67ac18da14c8aa767b8131b0650ac9b9720.png" class="latex" alt="$2011$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/8/578cf6c5b8058f6f8e6369416da6dcaf73f8fc01.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/b/c2b3009a823bfc8f95dbe51e386403f3cb1a221c.png" class="latex" alt="$T_n = \Delta ABC$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="101" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/87082784a051c7525966d9819cc379329a30e030.png" class="latex" alt="$D, E$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are the points of tangency of the incircle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/6/3b6d5d23a7e91fb9f6c248fb4cd43111165540f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta ABC$" width="57" height="13" /> to the sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />, respectively, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/1/6a1ef39be9a742204eb7347c5d32c9941e98c69d.png" class="latex" alt="$T_{n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="16" /> is a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abe2f961bc178cb504fd3e606db4fc2ad4b58cc1.png" class="latex" alt="$AD, BE$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/d/07d29465728e92952374a857beaee91665d8dbd9.png" class="latex" alt="$CF$" width="28" height="12" />, if it exists. What is the perimeter of the last triangle in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/e/24ec3272602fd8aa32b8360df596e388291097ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(T_n\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/2/b628b2b0ce18db50b46194658baa8ab212f00dfe.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1509}{8} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\  \frac{1509}{32} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\  \frac{1509}{64} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\  \frac{1509}{128} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\  \frac{1509}{256}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="503" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Answer: (D)
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25c483bc280f6dc00407da90d4d1503b2c16051.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = c$" width="60" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d78cd4394503dffb3e25f5cd3142ffde2c560fd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = a$" width="62" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/90663d81d23546bf717871a40845d03186a489ff.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = b$" width="60" height="13" />
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55a21c92aa05b1064abf5fd0ed5b7f1c6e21fb5d.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = AF$" width="81" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b873182ba33c7223d013cea04912b4d1fb2e586.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = BD$" width="83" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/c/1bcca880d17eab4d71aa7ed3e19b362ac904413b.png" class="latex" alt="$CF = CE$" width="81" height="12" />
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08fb03ac661b7157a216aa2143a5cb2f95083bc9.png" class="latex" alt="$a = BE + CF$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4af06dfdfd1ff7d7156ba5c5456b321de8e80c0f.png" class="latex" alt="$b = AD + CF$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8fcc29f0bb75ac0ac6222a60b5ca837c9312615.png" class="latex" alt="$c = AD + BE$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />
</p><p>Hence:
</p>
<pre><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/e/e7e8c2f0ab54f607b216f0be87a9cdf6c2f43eef.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = AF = \frac{b + c - a}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="177" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/d/37d6d2195d13bd374b62504e0465bd0e626ce520.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = BD = \frac{a + c - b}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="180" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/e/f0e5163b9227229215060228e9c58d1bc5e9126f.png" class="latex" alt="$CF = CE = \frac{a + b - c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="178" height="37" />
</pre>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db65aadda958b2914535c6ef0e2d20e902a80339.png" class="latex" alt="$a + 1 = b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/1678eefe020842398601d322a0105c8878369450.png" class="latex" alt="$a - 1 = c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c766f9fce6070981e62f805a7cf7294baed4498e.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, I claim that it is true for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, assume for induction that it is true for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, then
</p>
<pre><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1093151d8e2960641d1d39f19889755c362622a3.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = AF = \frac{a}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="117" height="33" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34a13cf094ba5e7e998e782d7c18fc33532c3e05.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = BD = \frac{a - 2}{2} = AD - 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="236" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/f/cbf8ee50379c21fcc12d085b9938d773905ef87a.png" class="latex" alt="$CF = CE = \frac{a + 2}{2} = AD + 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="234" height="37" />
</pre>
<p>Furthermore, the average for the sides is decreased by a factor of 2 each time.
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f4341a1efbe1a890de160de023b4e4b84ef444.png" class="latex" alt="$T_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> is a triangle with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/a/b5abce3ce6c17345df7f10fcfa3aa07a75c96e56.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2012}{2^{n- 1}} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/e/faeaf8c335e9d2c19b4548fa0b3553cc7d276b84.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2012}{2^{n-1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="38" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/1/3510e9af751869fef387aaef85b820380068f240.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2012}{2^{n-1}} + 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />
</p><p>and the perimeter of such <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f4341a1efbe1a890de160de023b4e4b84ef444.png" class="latex" alt="$T_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/3/3f3fa3f6ed1aa9610605614d17faf4366ec5faf7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(3)(2012)}{2^{n-1}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
Now we need to find when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f4341a1efbe1a890de160de023b4e4b84ef444.png" class="latex" alt="$T_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> fails the triangle inequality. So we need to find the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/3/eb318515b932b2f815d3f0b2c07be08b1512e63b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2012}{2^{n-1}} &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" />
</p>
<pre><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/3/eb318515b932b2f815d3f0b2c07be08b1512e63b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2012}{2^{n-1}} &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/2/7b21fd3e59433ac9051d1459779ec0993c38aebd.png" class="latex" alt="$2012 &gt; 2^n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/a/86a435cb6450f07753ec6e06e24ff6f9730930f6.png" class="latex" alt="$n \le 10$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" />
</pre>
<p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/1/fa17013a449147f7545eaf8c6efd44b5b9fa2034.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />, perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/a/5fa067cbaa66e87ea587e2e7e300bc609094d47f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(3)(2012)}{2^{9}} = \frac{1509}{2^7} = \frac{1509}{128}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="205" height="39" />
</p>
