<p>Brian writes down four integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/9/1192c5f717cf0b4bc6e1c268253347f0eab47237.png" class="latex" alt="$w &gt; x &gt; y &gt; z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="114" height="13" /> whose sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b45828f292e161290be6ee62bc4c9df081ee1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. The pairwise positive differences of these numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d3b72c72ca9697a5a9da1ad2d9d6560ecb16e1.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 3, 4, 5, 6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the sum of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/241e238193551b7f8ea31f83ba6c0bcc6ea05bbd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 16 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 31 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 48 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 62 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 93$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/6/8a6afe0dded0c28ff1780d49397609f5cd7f0658.png" class="latex" alt="$y-z=a, x-y=b, w-x=c.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="247" height="16" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f5438bdb7b43b6e6fe0956b8095fda5125bd886b.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z$" width="44" height="8" /> results in the greatest pairwise difference, and thus it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. 
This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/9/9f979a53cc1e8c6a604a3a8d89bfb9230bb08965.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c=9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> must be in the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/d/88d6f4c62fdd2fbe3ea4ba38c778e46b5a8f4f46.png" class="latex" alt="${1,3,4,5,6}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" />.
The only way for 3 numbers in the set to add up to 9 is if they are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/8/97827a34a0f6699ae2b5fd23665cc8eecd056286.png" class="latex" alt="$1,3,5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bbeb7caa1770d1d3cb842655f27e245b08188f.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" /> then must be the remaining two numbers which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.
The ordering of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3d9e22097e20a1249b5332202b24941a4bf9e1f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> must be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/5/7c5e9a2fa63c6727ceb3a308a09115f807daebbe.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6efa3561362f6c9c07f3ab22be0e085c9758da.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,1,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/6/cd667706af76bb07e92861a56809d76ada134b16.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} z + (z + a) + (z + (a + b)) + (z + (a + b + c)) &amp;= 4z + a + (a + b) + 9\\ 4z + a + (a + b) + 9 &amp;= 44\\ \text{if} \hspace{1cm} a &amp;= 3 \\ a + b &amp;= 4\\ 4z &amp;= 44 - 9 - 3 - 4\\ z &amp;= 7\\ w &amp;= 16\\ \end{align*}" width="540" height="163" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/d/5dd02ced6b791b38d32cbd82780e2c884f5beab6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \text{if} \hspace{1cm} a &amp;= 5\\ a + b &amp;= 6\\ 4z &amp;= 44 - 9 - 5 - 6\\ z &amp;= 6\\ w &amp;= 15\\ \end{align*}" width="202" height="112" />
</p><p><br />
The sum of the two w's is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f7262a4fa637d893e155331e48e48fee8379ff7.png" class="latex" alt="$15+16=31$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
