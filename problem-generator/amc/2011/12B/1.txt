<p>What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae1c781c8fdd66c1c103d796b5109d89dc849af1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2+4+6}{1+3+5} - \frac{1+3+5}{2+4+6}?$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="181" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/0/9c0fa3d2f55d5974f9b4f937eee19c829f1f9c6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -1 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{5}{36} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{7}{12} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{147}{60} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{43}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="431" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Add up the numbers in each fraction to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927253cf1083713033a6fadd4716d39ba7c193fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{9} - \frac{9}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="65" height="38" />, which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf570d8c506064b4f2b4abf2aafbd0d460146ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{3} - \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="47" height="38" />. Doing the subtraction yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/e/13e255ecbc8c3a6f98b5924bf1d935c541c4e150.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\frac{7}{12}\   \textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="68" height="48" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Notice that the numerators and denominators of each expression are 3-term arithmetic series. The sum of an arithmetic series is the middle term multiplied with the number of terms. Since each of the arithmetic series have the same number of terms, we can replace the fractions with the middle terms, which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/6/0964fb53afbe0a365861bfae26fa8b8008c72a4c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{3} - \frac{3}{4} = \frac{7}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="93" height="38" />
</p>
