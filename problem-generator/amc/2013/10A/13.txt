<p>How many three-digit numbers are not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, have digits that sum to less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, and have the first digit equal to the third digit?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/b/e5bad9fdcdf5b068b066ad1988d8154f5ec5ba8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 52 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 60  \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 66 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 68 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 70$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We use a casework approach to solve the problem. These three digit numbers are of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/6/6a6be36e143cfe9648096b78ad52f67c1997f721.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{xyx}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="14" />.(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/d/c5d6cf4e9d072fbeba4fb13dffe63fa9ca820854.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{abc}$" width="25" height="16" /> denotes the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5a1ceb95b62c9c34572681c80bee6671a5c9d1.png" class="latex" alt="$100a+10b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="115" height="14" />).  We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/7470aeade5f47b768b503c1547f7e7cbf4540361.png" class="latex" alt="$x\neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/4/5942743972460bb9c9210f39a95b5789dbd3b71b.png" class="latex" alt="$x\neq 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" /> does not yield a three-digit integer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/c/51c80ea5862b45877f08d698a0c893bd2806a2fd.png" class="latex" alt="$x=5$" width="43" height="12" /> yields a number divisible by 5.
</p><p>The second condition is that the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/8/48809f617152f90dc9a255e39e41ba5dee394512.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+y&lt;20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" />.  When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> can be any digit from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/8/3f8ee5eb338bdcce4bc7c52a137df3b0b4333c12.png" class="latex" alt="$2x&lt;10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />.  This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/16437bf8e8f78747ff427711b11a0de9503b31e8.png" class="latex" alt="$10(4) = 40$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="18" /> numbers.  
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd92d02476c3434bf645bf5a3bc249eca83d3c16.png" class="latex" alt="$x=6$" width="43" height="12" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/4/734e0f3249fb83d883de0e1a720a16b301fae13a.png" class="latex" alt="$12+y&lt;20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/65478a5d5cdffa726d7db0942afb6ec5913c4f55.png" class="latex" alt="$y&lt;8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />.  This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> more numbers.  
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/7/5f7f6230166a491e7a471a03829c792496576480.png" class="latex" alt="$x=7$" width="43" height="12" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/7/7b72c5ea9901d864791e4a989fcc40321f2a4cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$14+y&lt;20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53b1aeb9976dea14cd71fe6ae5b911016c775752.png" class="latex" alt="$y&lt;6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />.  This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> more numbers.  
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/f/ccf5d650726be49af3a306c54a2fd7dd1d628b48.png" class="latex" alt="$x=8$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/8/8788b8aa1d97c78259a4632b7e25798d0328b34a.png" class="latex" alt="$16+y&lt;20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/6/d76bfc9cc7d48ac3f4d8445fa0c75ef51c16b9e0.png" class="latex" alt="$y&lt;4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />.  This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> more numbers.  
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d8cdec48abe8293a4169a8afce4fc8a8348e7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=9$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/7/8d7d1063822f773b5976a720519f140169a5aeac.png" class="latex" alt="$18+y&lt;20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/2/2a261b71b48ceea8803ceaba56f60c3e75657df5.png" class="latex" alt="$y&lt;2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />.  This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> more numbers.  
</p><p>Summing, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/0/960b860b9824b158e022b3e9b44464c23770d154.png" class="latex" alt="$40 + 8 + 6 + 4 + 2 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }60}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="232" height="28" />
</p>
