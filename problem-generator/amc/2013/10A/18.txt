<p>Let points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/5/805440e55bd062f398ab4dcf5ba7d3569e19f388.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c681b779b8ccb2780535581efc7dba40bdde867.png" class="latex" alt="$B = (1, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/62502ebeee22e33581e5dbef79ac62d13a0729ed.png" class="latex" alt="$C=(3, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aac6b387a81a0ee0745f9e80a344b6254e25f59.png" class="latex" alt="$D = (4, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />.  Quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is cut into equal area pieces by a line passing through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />.  This line intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/a/06ac471dd6bb4a5bb3bc7ad322deb80623ebe47e.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{p}{q}, \frac{r}{s}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="56" height="43" />, where these fractions are in lowest terms.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/e/2fe4726ba705cea35c5c6479796aa9ac6acd79cd.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q+r+s$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="102" height="14" />?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/f/40f94c5eee50137f03a539804ad377ed24b381bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 54\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 58\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 62\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 70\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 75$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/3/8b3b8e056ad8b3c97066b76d8a5d0c23c0cf43be.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(8cm); pair A, B, C, D, E, EE; A = (0,0); B = (1,2); C = (3,3); D = (4,0); E = (27/8,15/8); EE = (27/8,0); draw(A--B--C--D--A--E); draw(E--EE,linetype(&quot;8 8&quot;)); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); draw(rightanglemark(E,EE,D,4)); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;D&quot;,D,SE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,NE); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(A+D)/2,S); label(&quot;$\frac{27}{8}$&quot;,(A+EE)/2,S); label(&quot;$\frac{15}{8}$&quot;,(E+EE)/2,W); [/asy]" width="378" height="305" /></center>
<p>First, we shall find the area of quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />. This can be done in any of three ways:
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Pick%27s_Theorem" title="Pick&#39;s Theorem">Pick's Theorem</a>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa08a023a9b7850954a34f84a178ae7b0f0f8957.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABCD] = I + \dfrac{B}{2} - 1 = 5 + \dfrac{7}{2} - 1 = \dfrac{15}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="324" height="37" />
</p><p>Splitting: Drop perpendiculars from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to the x-axis to divide the quadrilateral into triangles and trapezoids, and so the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/f/69f44570018378be17649bf670e073b80f1c8765.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 5 + \dfrac{3}{2} = \dfrac{15}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="125" height="37" />
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Shoelace_Theorem" title="Shoelace Theorem">Shoelace Theorem</a>: The area is half of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5b0d41e125ca7e63fdff756d7e1cf1f42ca130.png" class="latex" alt="$|1 \cdot 3 - 2 \cdot 3 - 3 \cdot 4| = 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/5/435257e81e368e688c7565faf67d02ad5ec45a98.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{15}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/d/01d425fd257b26e8f604a40eeb6bcb56995bdef9.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABCD] = \frac{15}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="37" />.  Therefore, each equal piece that the line separates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> into must have an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a776e3c32ba097d70e6c2fa5e6c463f5198b552a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />.
</p><p>Call the point where the line through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />.  We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/b/75b6dc7ed815aeb31c85e08c1ca5b86e3b0ebc67.png" class="latex" alt="$[ADE] = \frac{15}{4} = \frac{bh}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="145" height="38" />.  Furthermore, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efb996ca207df5d210cd86914aed764adb55fcb3.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d592f9a0bd33b27454c6612300587ffe447083.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />.  Thus, solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/e/48ec0cde195fb83f4e08cdd399d747e4b0f4e388.png" class="latex" alt="$2h = \frac{15}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="64" height="38" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2dd8a5d038f939228971f59247704c1dbef178d0.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" />.  This gives that the y coordinate of E is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />.  
</p><p>Line CD can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/e/b6e72c563ac8ae8c29d6960c8ad9e912293be055.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -3x+12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="107" height="16" />, so the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinate of E satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/2/d02bf26810d2b9a49d7569e4f20fd7fb59f8ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8} = -3x + 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="120" height="37" />.  Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f28ccc7e9d33c497d5b8ba2f89dc5a947f78bb27.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{27}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" />.
</p><p>From this, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/9/8999775a2438355ff1cd31263712127cec0e169d.png" class="latex" alt="$E = \left(\frac{27}{8}, \frac{15}{8}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="113" height="43" />.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/3/243197fb4d854becd0b5604ee05beed81ba0bb64.png" class="latex" alt="$27 + 15 + 8 + 8 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }58}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="210" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/e/e0e68784ebc977deaacff37c7fca9bef0fc7cec4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(8cm); pair A, B, C, D, E, F; A = (0,0); B = (1,2); C = (3,3); D = (4,0); E = (27/8,15/8); F = (27/8,0); draw(A--B--C--D--A--E); draw(E--F,linetype(&quot;8 8&quot;)); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); draw(rightanglemark(E,F,D,4)); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;D&quot;,D,SE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,NE); label(&quot;F&quot;,F,S); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,(A+D)/2,S); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(A+F)/2,S); label(&quot;$\frac{15}{8}$&quot;,(E+F)/2,W); [/asy]" width="378" height="295" /></center>
<p>Let the point where the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> be labeled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />.
Following the steps above, you can find that the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />, and from there split the base into two parts, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/2/aa23ee5e80a123218f61d72b7524dd9a7ba5df01.png" class="latex" alt="$4-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the segment from the origin to the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/2/aa23ee5e80a123218f61d72b7524dd9a7ba5df01.png" class="latex" alt="$4-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> is the segment from point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> to point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. Then, by the Pythagorean Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/6/1162c20ba6e443dd61f24009d29a5ff1defa567d.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{27}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" />, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a8749622ed684b0079b3535fdcdb666ba90934.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }58}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
