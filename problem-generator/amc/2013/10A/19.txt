<p>In base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> ends in the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.  In base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, on the other hand, the same number is written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/7465a815870191c6f5d33f9a89723be1df7f6ae1.png" class="latex" alt="$(2676)_{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> and ends in the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.  For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> does the base-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />-representation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> end in the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/6/026b8f9ee756b6298408f2f40c78f3b2fa5025e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 13\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We want the integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/41204484906f5bdbb3e655b6096ac767abdd4e5e.png" class="latex" alt="$2013\equiv 3\pmod{b} \Rightarrow b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" /> is a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e86a49c65006a8f87d1506ca0d270bfe818bd5f0.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 67$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="145" height="13" />, it has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/e/23ef8c3d76ad7887f5f5b98c14a1ca37cccc899d.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+1)(1+1)(1+1)(1+1)=16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="260" height="18" /> factors. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d92ef37930626122612f47d4f44176093e637f.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, as these cannot have the digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> in their base representations, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6ceb33d5120fe738c76f4e2f0a474849048856d8.png" class="latex" alt="$16-3=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="138" height="28" />
</p>
