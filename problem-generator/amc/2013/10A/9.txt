<p>In a recent basketball game, Shenille attempted only three-point shots and two-point shots.  She was successful on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of her three-point shots and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of her two-point shots.  Shenille attempted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> shots.  How many points did she score?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4bb65e611e85322e148aeda058554057b92277e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 12 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 18  \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 24 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 30 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the number of attempted three-point shots be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and the number of attempted two-point shots be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />.  We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/e/82efe55077f267b5c4087fc9149a05360b2b87fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=30$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, and we need to evaluate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/a/41a9b6f9f69b332ae60447e6f04489653793d9b6.png" class="latex" alt="$3(0.2x) + 2(0.3y)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="137" height="20" />, as we know that the three-point shots are worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> points and that she made <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />% of them and that the two-point shots are worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and that she made <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />% of them.
</p><p>Simplifying, we see that this is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/f/cbf99e49da5969be989f54edc0bf3d5a22efe546.png" class="latex" alt="$0.6x + 0.6y = 0.6(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="191" height="18" />.  Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/e/82efe55077f267b5c4087fc9149a05360b2b87fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=30$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f835c200097d09acbc5fe82e88512c63547cb08d.png" class="latex" alt="$0.6(30) = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="144" height="28" />
</p>
