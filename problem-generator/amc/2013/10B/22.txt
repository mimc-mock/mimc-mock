<p>The regular octagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/055dcba798b1424c44c2606d913e0f2edab1c5ad.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDEFGH$" width="118" height="13" /> has its center at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" />.  Each of the vertices and the center are to be associated with one of the digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, with each digit used once, in such a way that the sums of the numbers on the lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/2/332eb19dca4a5c3ff044e6d27cac4a86290b5b1f.png" class="latex" alt="$AJE$" width="39" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/d/05d07c2bec9d829f1a404b025f6dbd368577d91e.png" class="latex" alt="$BJF$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/4/8844e5bd7d730f91dd828be5ab1323eb8c86da62.png" class="latex" alt="$CJG$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/0/4e066877c794b67ba3f844bfc531f07c927e32a8.png" class="latex" alt="$DJH$" width="44" height="12" /> are all equal.  In how many ways can this be done?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/995dbe12c2e940bb7550f6f8fea7a592daa304e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 384 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 576  \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1152 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 1680 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 3456$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="466" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/0/d305e9074b588800bd9056981b0e283a8488bc59.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H,J; A=(20,20(2+sqrt(2))); B=(20(1+sqrt(2)),20(2+sqrt(2))); C=(20(2+sqrt(2)),20(1+sqrt(2))); D=(20(2+sqrt(2)),20); E=(20(1+sqrt(2)),0); F=(20,0); G=(0,20); H=(0,20(1+sqrt(2))); J=(10(2+sqrt(2)),10(2+sqrt(2))); draw(A--B); draw(B--C); draw(C--D); draw(D--E); draw(E--F); draw(F--G); draw(G--H); draw(H--A); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); dot(F); dot(G); dot(H); dot(J); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,NNW); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,NNE); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,ENE); label(&quot;D&quot;,D,ESE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,SSE); label(&quot;F&quot;,F,SSW); label(&quot;G&quot;,G,WSW); label(&quot;H&quot;,H,WNW); label(&quot;J&quot;,J,SE); [/asy]" width="252" height="248" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First of all, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> to preserve symmetry, since the sum of 1 to 9 is 45, and we need the remaining 8 to be divisible by 4 (otherwise we will have uneven sums). So, we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/73106e8577969ba35acc7a5dc066fab12977009f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H,J; A=(20,20(2+sqrt(2))); B=(20(1+sqrt(2)),20(2+sqrt(2))); C=(20(2+sqrt(2)),20(1+sqrt(2))); D=(20(2+sqrt(2)),20); E=(20(1+sqrt(2)),0); F=(20,0); G=(0,20); H=(0,20(1+sqrt(2))); J=(10(2+sqrt(2)),10(2+sqrt(2))); draw(A--B); draw(B--C); draw(C--D); draw(D--E); draw(E--F); draw(F--G); draw(G--H); draw(H--A); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); dot(F); dot(G); dot(H); dot(J); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,NNW); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,NNE); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,ENE); label(&quot;D&quot;,D,ESE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,SSE); label(&quot;F&quot;,F,SSW); label(&quot;G&quot;,G,WSW); label(&quot;H&quot;,H,WNW); label(&quot;J $(1, 5, 9)$&quot;,J,SE); [/asy]" width="252" height="248" />
</p><p>We also notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bdced0dc4a5667480868f4f9d0d039b510724a1.png" class="latex" alt="$A+E = B+F = C+G = D+H$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="279" height="14" />.  
</p><p>WLOG, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1043b3fea003c0423af6a27d7a10590a733e30.png" class="latex" alt="$J = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />.  Thus the pairs of vertices must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/482b0fba62fc7502e8b9fb930374b8f096a3a940.png" class="latex" alt="$4! = 24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" /> ways to assign these to the vertices.  Furthermore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b2ab0ce560a0f76b30b3842c0fe3806043e4d5e.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{4} = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="15" /> ways to switch them (i.e. do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> instead of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />).  
</p><p>Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/0/b8061a4b8921f78584d7258ee1541bea070ddca8.png" class="latex" alt="$16(24) = 384$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" /> ways for each possible J value.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> possible J values that still preserve symmetry: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b2d2edfa163dedc323a5bf081d9915bd3cccca5.png" class="latex" alt="$384(3) = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }1152}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="157" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As in solution 1, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> giving us 3 choices. Additionally <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bdced0dc4a5667480868f4f9d0d039b510724a1.png" class="latex" alt="$A+E = B+F = C+G = D+H$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="279" height="14" />.  This means once we choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> remaining choices. Going clockwise from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> we count, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. Choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> also determines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> which leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, once <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is chosen it also determines  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Once <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is chosen it determines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. Choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> determines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />, exhausting the numbers. Additionally, there are three possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" />. To get the answer we multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a923d20802ced724ef90384e426a5d1207d72b93.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot4\cdot6\cdot8\cdot3=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }1152}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="205" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remark">Remark</span></h2>
<p>Solution 1 and 2 states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e252ef204d5663482ef03a8d1317b59247062c.png" class="latex" alt="$J=1, 5, 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" /> without rigorous analysis. Here it is:
</p>
<pre>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/a/4aacd8ae9033a4d11d7681686c554da4a2580679.png" class="latex" alt="$S=A+J+E=B+J+F=C+J+G=D+J+H$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="454" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c513dbf10244afabd321f03b1a39212488d3f4e7.png" class="latex" alt="$4S=A+B+C+D+E+F+G+H+4J$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="364" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/7/847297ae2ccd9b002662c51d5238658dbdbc37ba.png" class="latex" alt="$4S=45+3J$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="109" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/a/04a6863f2b9313a5933c4ba7c584537fb3954374.png" class="latex" alt="$4S=3(15+J)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="123" height="20" />
</pre>
<p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/f/49fbb56e0d0253282e7b458e94cb81e4d55bab8c.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,3)=1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="76" height="20" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/7/237c04930a749d7e339d13afb69680c1bec61b42.png" class="latex" alt="$4|(15+J)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="82" height="20" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/0/a90e4bea0ba9fe558421116e5c7f66695f7c3177.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \equiv 3 \pmod 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="126" height="20" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/a/4ea79fbcfe2bea090cfc4f53be0789b714ae22a6.png" class="latex" alt="$J \equiv 1 \pmod 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="119" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33d2ecbcc90a40fd9650cf9574afbfeecbe3c193.png" class="latex" alt="$J=1,5,9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />
</p>
<pre>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/4/5a437b35ef80f0b22566dce75afe5b07b281cff0.png" class="latex" alt="$J=1, S=12, A+E=B+F=C+G=D+H=11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="18" />
When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/2/88277dccaa161ba139b888144f33da8939887188.png" class="latex" alt="$J=5, S=15, A+E=B+F=C+G=D+H=10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="18" />
When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/a/7ea68fc6f1be7dfaf98fd5736bc86336ec5853d4.png" class="latex" alt="$J=9, S=18, A+E=B+F=C+G=D+H=9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="430" height="18" />
