<p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34896e6f2bf5b2cdd360b20cb459946ab24871ea.png" class="latex" alt="$a\clubsuit b=a^2b-ab^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="17" />. Which of the following describes the set of points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/0/b90252ae01b7f8d9ab023961309a6dd4f9a4d6c5.png" class="latex" alt="$x\clubsuit y=y\clubsuit x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="17" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/e/3aefdaebb30b5494cb2d13f6b39beeb452347718.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{a finite set of points}\\ \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{one line}\\ \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{two parallel lines}\\ \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{two intersecting lines}\\ \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{three lines}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="96" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/7/5577bf2463608b9106d541090ba3f6ab3b22c357.png" class="latex" alt="$x\clubsuit y = x^2y-xy^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="134" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/2/fc20aeeebffc0ded11e17183c81c9222c6ffe2f1.png" class="latex" alt="$y\clubsuit x = y^2x-yx^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="134" height="18" />. Therefore, we have the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/8/c78120ae79eb93adb4af1792e4b40c916aecd401.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2y-xy^2 = y^2x-yx^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="177" height="18" /> Factoring out a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b21f758603e5c753951d3ff28afdd95fc23bc80f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2y-xy^2 = -(x^2y-xy^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="205" height="19" /> Factoring both sides further, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/b/1cba980fd6b0bbfdf615d2a9129a756d4b39ea68.png" class="latex" alt="$xy(x-y) = -xy(x-y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="189" height="18" />. It follows that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/f/f8fcb7f2e30433467992031f67387beab23f6d64.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="18" />, both sides of the equation equal 0. By this, there are 3 lines (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0110f1bb04c51b2a4d431bf14489fc1b48b37421.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" />) so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/2/2926501dde179e856e67b290cd9f62350f486559.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\text{ three lines}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="119" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Following from the previous solution, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/8/c78120ae79eb93adb4af1792e4b40c916aecd401.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2y-xy^2 = y^2x-yx^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="177" height="18" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/9/059bdd85aa268c48711c09475605d5dda5e6a512.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2y-2xy^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="128" height="18" />. Factoring, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a4659ee1dea94493654fc54718d2209f3df187db.png" class="latex" alt="$2xy(x-y)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" />. Now, the solutions are obviously <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0110f1bb04c51b2a4d431bf14489fc1b48b37421.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" />, which each correspond to a line. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/2/2926501dde179e856e67b290cd9f62350f486559.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\text{ three lines}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="119" height="28" />.
</p>
