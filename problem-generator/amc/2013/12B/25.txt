How many polynomials are in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If we factor into irreducible polynomials (in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/9/3d963b0224d7c8b822ad2df9d699fd043f0013e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathbb{Q}[x]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="32" height="18" />), each factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61c594c97b61f72af93af4e61894b3f16a3bbb30.png" class="latex" alt="$f_i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> has exponent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in the factorization and degree at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e780fda439b2829922a704a60b31c5ed77191.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/2147184808f5c7cddffd9e078b2b412808c41f8c.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ne0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="17" /> come in conjugate pairs with product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/6/cf669d58fbd0410ca860758e53606d06425a4d97.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2+b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="53" height="16" />). Clearly we want the product of constant terms of these polynomials to equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" />; for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b83e4f79900ecad4aa9d694d4f50926b4304e28.png" class="latex" alt="$d\mid 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/e/04ef083bc5520433e19c1db3527f890030078272.png" class="latex" alt="$f(d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> be the number of permitted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61c594c97b61f72af93af4e61894b3f16a3bbb30.png" class="latex" alt="$f_i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> with constant term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. It's easy to compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/a/e4a16b9dffeb3757d936f3a92e8f8f85c2bf6745.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/7/77734cefc22a8de2f1f3634adabf5841f0e1b1ac.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2)=3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/a/4da828050d39f52481688ace3a7c6f7472141cb2.png" class="latex" alt="$f(5)=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c7f1ab89db506437edb0eb9bcb527cd211bb150.png" class="latex" alt="$f(10)=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4af9f73378ba37a558ede2a9a87b9d4b88067580.png" class="latex" alt="$f(25)=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb50c307d4f84fb292f8ecd8772265fec94630fd.png" class="latex" alt="$f(50)=7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, and obviously <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/2/f72ed503ba6a25762cb7747152c8e796f7a73e91.png" class="latex" alt="$f(d) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" /> for negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b83e4f79900ecad4aa9d694d4f50926b4304e28.png" class="latex" alt="$d\mid 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" />.
</p><p>Note that by the distinctness condition, the only constant terms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> that can be repeated are those with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/c/79ced0919266c08a8a1667390375c594393df961.png" class="latex" alt="$d^2\mid 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/3/3634b5ffe6a89808a42ce27f82c214f1db857405.png" class="latex" alt="$f(d)&gt;1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/1178af8fcc5f3f3acb9f151f7dcc2c4eefe88fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/f/2af04862dff77564db101387edce0c903092daff.png" class="latex" alt="$+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="14" />. Also, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/1178af8fcc5f3f3acb9f151f7dcc2c4eefe88fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" />s don't affect the product, so we can simply count the number of polynomials with no constant terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/1178af8fcc5f3f3acb9f151f7dcc2c4eefe88fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> and multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/3/583950b80e8849d0cbd3b71cbf25dc99f7fd3e9f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{f(1)} = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="16" /> at the end.
</p><p>We do casework on the (unique) even constant term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bbaecd3f7b8e0d1643766cf953d9584faec77974.png" class="latex" alt="$d\in\{\pm2,\pm10,\pm50\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" /> in our product. For convenience, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/958a7c7c62aa906f571d6c6db18efaa8a312e8ff.png" class="latex" alt="$F(d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> be the number of ways to get a product of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a47fc38c87952fad45fe6b780d6b437fa0569e6.png" class="latex" alt="$50/d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> without using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/f/7cfbd35086ffefb83637d42166582b43c8a1ff4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> (so only using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/e/8be74735cb0cec0ea1d03ca6fc48c5468f8e0320.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm5,\pm25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />) and recall <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f109246ca8aee3102c3853399aaf2000aa3e314.png" class="latex" alt="$f(-1) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />; then our final answer will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/3/7d327c8b5db23ede094e4d44cdd9dd7507aee1da.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{f(1)}\sum_{d\in\{2,10,50\}}(f(-d)+f(d))(F(-d)+F(d))$" style="vertical-align: -24px" width="352" height="42" />. It's easy to compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/c/80cb0a171bd9fb6eff1b112055a88551deedeac9.png" class="latex" alt="$F(-50)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f99fa5108b6e058b10ef5b15cc51906f37e37a.png" class="latex" alt="$F(50)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/b/7bb067d17d6aa0d7fa6e2b898b5999ab6016bb2e.png" class="latex" alt="$F(-10)=f(5)=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/3/ae3decb3a46a005b0b78118028528b8e79b3db00.png" class="latex" alt="$F(10)=f(-5)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/7/ee7d0b64260f78971c28017d021202d8e2a537e7.png" class="latex" alt="$F(-2)=f(-25)+f(-5)f(5)=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/0/5c00b3c2517aea837ea044280c7241877a349181.png" class="latex" alt="$F(2)=f(25)+\binom{f(5)}{2}=16$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="231" height="53" />, so we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f6a1adffeb1794ddf24f892ccf1162e58dc576.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4 [ (1+3)(6+16) + (1+5)(1+5) + (1+7)(0+1) ] = 4[132] = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }528}\]" width="503" height="28" />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>By Vieta's formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> is the product of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> roots. As the roots are all in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe0b3c32b3838c0b06fd8e2dca022007728b5a1d.png" class="latex" alt="$a + bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" />, there must exist a conjugate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/a/77ac597d092fe9c029e5aec8c1fa43688a8df01e.png" class="latex" alt="$a-bi$" width="45" height="12" /> for each root.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/5/d75bcca9920c6120c150230eabf294723153c36a.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2 + b^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="201" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ee6ea18606d23cfb95ba3e56b639b8c40ef302.png" class="latex" alt="$50 = 2 \cdot 5^2$" width="79" height="15" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/8/7f89a5bb58148b41790e2d7fa3683e34261bf1ee.png" class="latex" alt="$a \neq b \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="17" />, the roots can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/c/8bc507f7ef21329f089497bc38e59cd4a6bd91aa.png" class="latex" alt="$a \pm bi$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/a/b0a029dd01b834b8720aaa7bc00194f85fa185df.png" class="latex" alt="$-a \pm bi$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="62" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/531d5bded2e6254dd77f7dfcae4becc69d9016a4.png" class="latex" alt="$b \pm ai$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="45" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/3/fe308b2cd858ca3eddf263c4c84b6dfdc9846fb4.png" class="latex" alt="$-b \pm ai$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="62" height="16" />, totaling <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> pairs of roots.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/7/397fd7b1f33bdbf6ee27a83d1bcee42d222cb331.png" class="latex" alt="$a = b$" width="42" height="12" />, the roots can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4432c722545282425c4966bf009834bbd96949.png" class="latex" alt="$a \pm ai$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/7/95750171aaebc8ec4d5d865b056949423902a2da.png" class="latex" alt="$-a \pm ai$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="64" height="15" />, totaling <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> pairs of roots.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af40dd462cfac9a98f63c84a474236829d0b3c25.png" class="latex" alt="$a \neq b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/7/be7a58270d4285821716c6093a83541f3ea31dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 0$" width="40" height="12" />, the roots can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b5a3c3658650872cd35872c8e12d6931669384e.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="26" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/c/70c5e84ce14be308a13dbec22edd8b8da7e64caa.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm ai$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="32" height="15" />, totaling <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> pairs of roots.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/8/3c8ce48d8d25d0f440089115dae624dd47b32c51.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 2 \cdot 25 &amp;= (1^2+1^2)5^2 &amp;: 2 \cdot 2 = 4\\ 2 \cdot 25 &amp;= 2 \cdot 5^2 &amp;: 2 \cdot 2 = 4\\ 2 \cdot 25 &amp;= (1^2+1^2) \cdot (3^2+4^2) &amp;: 2 \cdot 4 = 8\\ 2 \cdot 25 &amp;= 2 \cdot (3^2+4^2) &amp;: 2 \cdot 4 = 8 \end{align*}" width="353" height="103" />
