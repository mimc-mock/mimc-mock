<p>Mr. Green measures his rectangular garden by walking two of the sides and finds that it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> steps by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> steps. Each of Mr. Green's steps is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> feet long. Mr. Green expects a half a pound of potatoes per square foot from his garden. How many pounds of potatoes does Mr. Green expect from his garden?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/a/2ca72c965c9c1c466f61d2516c82aecaea50f016.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 600 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 800 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 1000 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 1200 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 1400$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="466" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since each step is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> feet, his garden is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> feet.  Thus, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/a/02a5e8c451696dd309aa60ba9e10998a2537fa53.png" class="latex" alt="$30(40) = 1200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" /> square feet.  Since he is expecting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a pound per square foot, the total amount of potatoes expected is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/6/f268c6ab6455498d12b176ef61bbc4e81cc25862.png" class="latex" alt="$1200 \times \frac{1}{2} =  \boxed{\textbf{(A) }600}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="170" height="37" />
</p>
