<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/1/2812a567a006aa8d7d7dd8f5e8c886a6d79739d9.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b= 2-c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" />. Now, by <a href="/wiki/index.php/Cauchy-Schwarz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cauchy-Schwarz">Cauchy-Schwarz</a>, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e64a8c781c3075289d656dfebea3eb9a72e7d49.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2+b^2) \ge \frac{(2-c)^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="156" height="39" />. Therefore, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/7/bc7575fa29621a3d0d6f0a2460de594f160e931a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2-c)^2}{2}+c^2 \le 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="144" height="39" />. We then find the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> that satisfy equality and find the difference of the roots. This gives the answer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/d/79d070337149a9b186c9eca6ca22448f8d6ef986.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \ \frac{16}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />.
</p><p>Note: We arrived at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e64a8c781c3075289d656dfebea3eb9a72e7d49.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2+b^2) \ge \frac{(2-c)^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="156" height="39" /> as we consider the two sets of numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. From <a href="/wiki/index.php/Cauchy-Schwarz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cauchy-Schwarz">Cauchy-Schwarz</a>, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8eed3c16bca429af93866deb78fc1bfb5b55592.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2+b^2)(1^2+1^2) \ge (2\cdot 1 -c\cdot 1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="269" height="21" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/7/d8769adada7d553cd541f86dadf7d3b95272751e.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = 2-c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" /> which is how we got the second part of the inequality.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>This is similar to the first solution but is far more intuitive. From the given, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/3/85370358acd6558b6161298dd70fae57a41cdee4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a + b = 2 - c\]" width="102" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e924df99f90f668e9e134a1fc6324293dc438e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2 + b^2 = 12 - c^2\]" width="133" height="16" /> This immediately suggests use of the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. By Cauchy, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/6/066668e2daf96b383b80abc2b108d69081b6745b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2\,(a^2 + b^2) \geq (a + b)^2\]" width="164" height="19" /> Substitution of the above results and some algebra yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/c/85cc6e74d1a62145c732fc51d42e220167bae028.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3c^2 - 4c - 20 \leq 0\]" width="136" height="17" /> This quadratic inequality is easily solved, and it is seen that equality holds for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/20626e7285ee7f261eadebf1ed741ae855077906.png" class="latex" alt="$c = -2$" width="54" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1cdf9706f9e72c959cee76d4e8334bdc5f68b31.png" class="latex" alt="$c = \frac{10}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />. 
</p><p>The difference between these two values is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/d/79d070337149a9b186c9eca6ca22448f8d6ef986.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \ \frac{16}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />.
</p>
