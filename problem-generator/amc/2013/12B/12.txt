<p>Cities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are connected by roads <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c7654422e0db3a6a8e99cc2823fc8c857260c990.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{AB}$" width="27" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/5/135aad5785728ce2f6b438850262512f0feb0a55.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{AD}$" width="28" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/c/30c9a491ca2fc917d91751c4f510d36ce1ed8df6.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{AE}$" width="27" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/9/479a324eda6010321c0c1fd4c5c2ddbc648713c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{BC}$" width="28" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/4/3d485f8d97bbecd8570af68d212018ec7355fe29.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{BD}$" width="29" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/4/264b3a56246fbd88d52c4615b105e0fc9422aa43.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{CD}$" width="29" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/1/a61ea3caacf8f100620baf12dc7838e97f0a41cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{DE}$" width="29" height="18" />. How many different routes are there from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> that use each road exactly once? (Such a route will necessarily visit some cities more than once.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cad21e9f9faa2994b8ed3c1a3acfbcc204d0b482.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(10mm); defaultpen(linewidth(1.2pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4; pair A=(1,0), B=(4.24,0), C=(5.24,3.08), D=(2.62,4.98), E=(0,3.08); dot (A); dot (B); dot (C); dot (D); dot (E); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,E); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,N); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,W); guide squiggly(path g, real stepsize, real slope=45) {  real len = arclength(g);  real step = len / round(len / stepsize);  guide squig;  for (real u = 0; u &lt; len; u += step){  real a = arctime(g, u);  real b = arctime(g, u + step / 2);  pair p = point(g, a);  pair q = point(g, b);  pair np = unit( rotate(slope) * dir(g,a));  pair nq = unit( rotate(0 - slope) * dir(g,b));  squig = squig .. p{np} .. q{nq};  }  squig = squig .. point(g, length(g)){unit(rotate(slope)*dir(g,length(g)))};  return squig; } pen pp = defaultpen + 2.718; draw(squiggly(A--B, 4.04, 30), pp); draw(squiggly(A--D, 7.777, 20), pp); draw(squiggly(A--E, 5.050, 15), pp); draw(squiggly(B--C, 5.050, 15), pp); draw(squiggly(B--D, 4.04, 20), pp); draw(squiggly(C--D, 2.718, 20), pp); draw(squiggly(D--E, 2.718, -60), pp); [/asy]" width="278" height="275" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/c/48c5b8caecfbe7585f2272eecf34ccf2eb5b4f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 9 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 12 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 16 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Note that cities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> can be removed when counting paths because if a path goes in to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, there is only one possible path to take out of cities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />.
So the diagram is as follows:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/4/d34c4d1dcb603a21f9aea344170bef5b1ba4ca4d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(10mm); defaultpen(linewidth(1.2pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4; pair A=(1,0), B=(4.24,0), C=(5.24,3.08), D=(2.62,4.98), E=(0,3.08); dot (A); dot (B);  dot (D);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE);  label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,N);  draw(A--B..D..cycle); draw(A--D); draw(B--D); [/asy]" width="265" height="275" />
</p><p>Now we proceed with casework. Remember that there are two ways to travel from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.:
</p><p>Case 1 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c1b610d446fa8f2bb9d3f292bd3b0d9852274ec.png" class="latex" alt="$A \Rightarrow D$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />: From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, if the path returns to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, then the next path must go to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/4/9a4a602a05f79bdafe6bb807a580773b97e3d2a6.png" class="latex" alt="$B\Rightarrow D \Rightarrow B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="101" height="13" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef725655e2c7b2d2af18b4abb1956d496945efff.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 1 \cdot 2 = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="87" height="12" /> possibilities of the path <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/9/0b91f1aac6bfa27eb4ed0b750d9b0aa20347d60f.png" class="latex" alt="$ADABDB$" width="87" height="13" />. If the path goes to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, then the path must continue with either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/1/5c18b1d5dc09f8af752288ea17facde35ef1a7fd.png" class="latex" alt="$BDAB$" width="58" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/1/f8180fa85596aefd715ddf371193d85885c135e9.png" class="latex" alt="$BADB$" width="58" height="13" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/8/698bfb596b54f8b093d7a266f4992195da8f0d9b.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = 8$" width="86" height="12" /> possibilities. So, this case gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a14c6975c1087c1b937fa7d857b1394bfa29990e.png" class="latex" alt="$4+8=12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> different possibilities.
</p><p>Case 2 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/2/4e2bd58155ff97a4978968b3d1bcf5a03863fa78.png" class="latex" alt="$A \Rightarrow B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" />: The path must continue with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/2/d72344e1ab1d40e97f5b57a63f4e92ca1c9eef3d.png" class="latex" alt="$BDADB$" width="73" height="13" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/6/8b69aa4309847d47e4aed15a0b00891106fb1807.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 2 = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="12" /> possibilities for this case. 
</p><p>Putting the two cases together gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/b/f9b60245516d0cb244aff202505860ccbae81232.png" class="latex" alt="$12+4 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} \ 16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="138" height="28" />
</p>
