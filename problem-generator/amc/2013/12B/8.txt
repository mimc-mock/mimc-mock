<p>Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee61fd6e8a1926a802b97fc99b66b8687d8f620.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> has equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dada88b57154978d8911e8b7b997b2674b220438.png" class="latex" alt="$3x - 2y = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> and goes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/4512efd680cb1dc1239dc15ca00434d8af27a971.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (-1, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" />. Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> has equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/4/234b330118777aba0bee513e3ade547ebd70d845.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> and meets line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee61fd6e8a1926a802b97fc99b66b8687d8f620.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2bcc4fbcb6dd28f70ea584900c95e658c7efea.png" class="latex" alt="$l_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> has positive slope, goes through point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, and meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2bcc4fbcb6dd28f70ea584900c95e658c7efea.png" class="latex" alt="$l_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/2/b32b4140435cf3e15a6c6e86d38c1d4018f4162d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{2}{3} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 1 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{4}{3} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="363" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee61fd6e8a1926a802b97fc99b66b8687d8f620.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> has the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/d/84d1b6153ef10835f78920105f1dc9b9be77de96.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3x/2-1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" /> when rearranged. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we find that line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> will meet this line at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, which is point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. We call <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> the base and the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to the line connecting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/b/acbab939b716843df7cc6fdb3e9a0b91b2fea5db.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" />, the height. The altitude has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/2/4923e23f326a8c5088434dee9b13829d493e150c.png" class="latex" alt="$|-2-1|=3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" />, and the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/9/a096640fe7d8811d00d943d70a16347f13903661.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}=3$" width="91" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/1/9210301a4572f21acc80c91ddfca79364c54b66f.png" class="latex" alt="$A={bh}/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2bcc4fbcb6dd28f70ea584900c95e658c7efea.png" class="latex" alt="$l_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> has positive slope, it will meet <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> to the right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, and the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to the right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2bcc4fbcb6dd28f70ea584900c95e658c7efea.png" class="latex" alt="$l_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/e/3ae71a5a172a28d5c8e1be19ee5133cec3eb7d47.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1,-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and thus has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/0/95055584c2c5559f22ea6dc8aeaab8c09037678e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{|1-(-2)|}{|3-(-1)|}=$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="101" height="43" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7d16c07fbac77125dda56c7a8cfc05a3fb0f43.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_-_Shoelace_Theorem">Solution 2 - Shoelace Theorem</span></h2>
<p>We know lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee61fd6e8a1926a802b97fc99b66b8687d8f620.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, so we can solve for that point:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/0/c30f0d411e80dedd0509d3e9c1ca8638386614b0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x-2y=1\]" width="92" height="15" />
Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/4/234b330118777aba0bee513e3ade547ebd70d845.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> we have: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/8/748634a70f2dd94992cdd09d8a29fad569384f2f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x-2(1) = 1\]" width="105" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/7/017e69353a2ea1c51218802fbd1ec90a00b23c05.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x-2=1\]" width="82" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/6/a36539581b6265551b8d9fea7ee19a94e6adbbb4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x=3\]" width="52" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/3/94310ae986fd19878ca259e81cb368df3b450dcb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 1\]" width="42" height="12" />
</p><p>Thus we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33a7cc0fae1ee840d254ddf69453f0092802244.png" class="latex" alt="$B = (1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />. 
</p><p>We know that the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, so by Shoelace Theorem we have: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/2/632d28bdc0a6374164351109466df20e66ba8298.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A = \dfrac{1}{2} |(-2x+y-1) - (-2+x-y)|\]" width="293" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/c/f1c326fffd3cfdef5cfd0b1d986b5509792b30cd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A = \dfrac{1}{2} |-2x+y-1+2-x+y|\]" width="259" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/6/33606fc20e3546728cbf265b18c3c1982022a5ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3 = \dfrac{1}{2} |-2x+y-1+2-x+y|\]" width="254" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a03e5397a03b7ad58c526fb61380045c7deae8a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6 = |-3x+2y+1|.\]" width="160" height="18" />
</p><p>Thus we have two options: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e65a6e4d11bc8193e7d6cc1393a7557f6d9d936.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6 = -3x+2y+1\]" width="137" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/2/3b202cf19495a8b2054aa3cb615e76ac54d829c7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5 = -3x+2y\]" width="106" height="16" />
</p><p>or
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab708e578c30f7e3aaecbaf9fe47ef2010a222d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6 = 3x-2y-1\]" width="123" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/e/e9edfb750f47c29fc796863ef4032deea2d6c2c7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7 = 3x-2y.\]" width="96" height="16" />
</p><p>Now we must just find a point that satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/e/6fec72aad9c9f5bacfdb9018572fe83bdfed34be.png" class="latex" alt="$m_{l_3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="29" height="14" /> is positive. 
</p><p>Doing some guess-and-check yields, from the second equation: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0c1c20bd74ed5b1c691e010047179079d200962.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7 = 3x-2y\]" width="92" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/0/3e06e3060e4c203f7335892e1353255e626f1c1c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7 = 3(3)-2(1)\]" width="117" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/e/b9ec36e7899990925a81c60cb22d5d0791f652a6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7 = 7\]" width="41" height="13" />
</p><p>so a valid point here is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. When calculated, the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2bcc4fbcb6dd28f70ea584900c95e658c7efea.png" class="latex" alt="$l_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> in this situation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7d16c07fbac77125dda56c7a8cfc05a3fb0f43.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
