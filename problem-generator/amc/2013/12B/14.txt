<p>Two non-decreasing sequences of nonnegative integers have different first terms. Each sequence has the property that each term beginning with the third is the sum of the previous two terms, and the seventh term of each sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />. What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/9/ae9d50c353054a169b0a0bb3e7ebbe1f9b50287e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 55 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 89 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 104 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 144 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 273$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="421" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the first two terms of the first sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/4/f742fa0e67547dbeffccff47a951855271de1348.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/8/5e8655384b71dbea96222803bcc8bdfa98299216.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and the first two of the second sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1abf0353f0acd71e6406b4d342160af61fbdce49.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/f/b5f0f8bc6452258649d59d7e7cd4a47d89a192d7.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="15" height="11" />.  Computing the seventh term, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5af0353ef4b95cada76798a4805cb5d80dc85b49.png" class="latex" alt="$5x_{1} + 8x_{2} = 5y_{1} + 8y_{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="172" height="16" />.  Note that this means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/4/f742fa0e67547dbeffccff47a951855271de1348.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1abf0353f0acd71e6406b4d342160af61fbdce49.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="11" /> must have the same value modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.  To minimize, let one of them be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />; WLOG assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/e/07e8f230cce34c9f0411b34e9d9aa21a59f1baaa.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{1} = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />.  Thus, the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1abf0353f0acd71e6406b4d342160af61fbdce49.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="11" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />; and since the sequences are non-decreasing we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4bd4d79440745537fb9bf3c6ff5a9eccb166c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{2} \ge 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="16" />.  To minimize, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d4f143934a689bb89aa4078aab5746b6ef3487.png" class="latex" alt="$y_{2} = 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="16" />.  Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac4e32e0c74a76597e197452622d3e6d5e488cbb.png" class="latex" alt="$5y_{1} + 8y_{2} = 40 + 64 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }104}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="254" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/412787c048e28774dc63fc27db42dc52ca858de7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d9565abaadf04609a2e9941aa2d20b0a299b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" /> be the sequences with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0c38d62a96318b17a9348444743550bb49dff0a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1&lt;b_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="55" height="15" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/7/6d7ed8b3d2c2ebebe407906d1949506a28576c59.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[N=5a_1+8a_2=5b_1+8b_2\]" width="209" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7b0057ea51a8c013cb8d2163ed2c25efb0a7241.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5a_1+8a_2=5(a_1+c)+8(a_2-d)\]" width="261" height="18" /> for some natural numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/2/ba2a3054298b31813cc68b62462d4928ee309e90.png" class="latex" alt="$5c=8d$" width="59" height="12" />. To minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/4/b34d2f04f49849fce3211edf1fe31fbb2f954ddf.png" class="latex" alt="$(c,d)=(8,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a62a54ef71360b15955b138cd5904a62e4bc816.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5a_1+8a_2=5(a_1+8)+8(a_2-5).\]" width="267" height="18" /> To minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/f/69fc4b9ed6d75d026557e7b06c2ed483c6ed0eda.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09d861fc1200508cccb6f57a38eea54ffc054863.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_1,b_1)=(0,0+c)=(0,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="212" height="18" />. Using the same method, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/3/04362bbe3fcd35e0c5673d16c331ea2d314d2aff.png" class="latex" alt="$b_2\ge b_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="53" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/313bcfc209eef173b7836629c5002ba6505ec418.png" class="latex" alt="$b_2\ge8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Thus the minimum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/6/5b655d019ba92e754c96c5e7c99e477dbacd7ecc.png" class="latex" alt="$N=5b_1+8b_2=104\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="202" height="29" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
</p>
