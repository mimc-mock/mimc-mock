<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/d/29d5ab59e32c9ba8cec3c82e666c011227578a62.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{AFB}=\angle{ADB}=90^{\circ}$" width="184" height="13" />, quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/f/96fb98a5a4042e46445a2d6427ae9604508f9efe.png" class="latex" alt="$ABDF$" width="58" height="13" /> is cyclic. </p><p>So, the ratio of the longer leg to the hypotenuse of all three similar triangles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/4/6d4e58b47ac74c9030cfc26984d3606cd8050cf4.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{12}{15} = \tfrac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="54" height="23" />, and the ratio of the shorter leg to the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/74189b2f5baaa13491ec7eb811787637f0b909f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{9}{15} = \tfrac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="50" height="21" />. </p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/6/736d0dcd0b88cfc3d65b102b418bb370140d9495.png" class="latex" alt="$x=DF$" width="64" height="12" />.
