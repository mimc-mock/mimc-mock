<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let pile <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> rocks, and so on.
</p><p>The total weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/8167a876a2484a2b2bc1a4d5d11d9977c838344b.png" class="latex" alt="$44(A + C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />.
</p><p>To get the total weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, we add the weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and subtract the weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/6/7a67273d54b83a1979a7a53a50e231c55d9d19e4.png" class="latex" alt="$44(A + C) + 50B - 40A = 4A + 44C + 50B$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="347" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore, the mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/5/1853cfe5ccbd9f288affea383f633584222d0cc5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4A + 44C + 50B}{B + C}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="135" height="39" />, which is simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c70b26835831f79b77063a768c3d8bebdf5731c7.png" class="latex" alt="$44 + \frac{4A + 6B}{B + C}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="111" height="39" />.
</p><p>We now need to eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> in the numerator.  
Since we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/d/e9d7f2c649d246da75c7a16e7a494988d4b6b5c2.png" class="latex" alt="$40A + 50B = 43(A + B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="192" height="18" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/5/f35041ef7b355c3f975c33d14a1c215835e4b158.png" class="latex" alt="$A = \frac{7}{3}B$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />
</p><p>Substituting,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/e/9ce27c678b96639c7323952dd0b551c261a10a0b.png" class="latex" alt="$44 + \frac{4(\frac{7}{3}B) + 6B}{B + C}=44 + \frac{46}{3}*\frac{B}{B + C}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="296" height="42" />
</p><p>In order to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/184e359136a47eee13622f3a08b656df36533625.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{B}{B + C}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="57" height="41" />, we can minimize the denominator by letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e3435fdaadad9195048f672956b8e6bef8db23.png" class="latex" alt="$C = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> (C must be a positive integer). Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db6a4ae052764c8c16801aef468c612306682f16.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{46}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> must cancel to give an integer, and the only fraction that satisfies both conditions is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/e/91ebb24a217f1c610853328039f92115f79da6a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{45}{46}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />
</p><p>Plugging in, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/4/5942d5969cc3d3f88fbc49067210a2cb2f23ee0b.png" class="latex" alt="$44 + \frac{46}{3} * \frac{45}{46} = 44 + 15 = 59$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="226" height="38" />, which is choice E
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>

We are going to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, subject to the following conditions:

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/1/3d13bda6dc56f5ab2fa4dc412825280640adf56d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7B=3A\]" width="69" height="13" />
