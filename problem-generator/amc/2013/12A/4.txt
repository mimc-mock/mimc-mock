<p>What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/d/49d95c1790482cb4dcf62a2ef5ec3340a39123ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2^{2014}+2^{2012}}{2^{2014}-2^{2012}}?\]" width="105" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/8/d08231e3f5055cbc919e39efc7d6ff5867bf7460.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{5}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2013\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2^{4024}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="428" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23c4d76bfa7879ef51309bb1bd05b99acee3e81f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2^{2014}+2^{2012}}{2^{2014}-2^{2012}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="39" />
</p><p>We can factor a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e806c8d89f7fca19fe8db63a773c76e5a11236c2.png" class="latex" alt="${2^{2012}}$" width="34" height="15" /> out of the numerator and denominator to obtain
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9f7158c67276b3e5531634cb46d8c62dcfc32.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2^{2012}*(2^2+1)}{2^{2012}*(2^2-1)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="118" height="43" />
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e806c8d89f7fca19fe8db63a773c76e5a11236c2.png" class="latex" alt="${2^{2012}}$" width="34" height="15" /> cancels, so we get 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5da065d5d6b5c2f612da3682c103e189ab0d8115.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2^2+1)}{(2^2-1)}=\frac{5}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="101" height="43" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />
</p>
