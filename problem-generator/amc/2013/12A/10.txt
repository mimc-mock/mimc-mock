<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c13e6f961c9bff23cc9fe69c37050986eb4b38.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="10" height="21" /> has the repeating decimal representation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/9/6d95de13dea671bb3b0dcd8edd8182c4db762adb.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{ab} = 0.ababab\cdots,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="156" height="19" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> different digits.  What is the sum of the elements of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b9f8746d47df8f801c0fcc72bf4adf8259a2688.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 11\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 44\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 110\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 143\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 155\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="421" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f28ed97a2bb1868cd14af94e73586146a87f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{11} = 0.\overline{09}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="79" height="37" />.
</p><p>Dividing by 3 gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/7/ac77e50e8b3d15b283ff34cfb02648c3c6989e1e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{33} = 0.\overline{03}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, and dividing by 9 gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/789cd4a49734099afffe8024246cdc07ee594b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{99} = 0.\overline{01}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/5/26562b8f19961292471029d442e704c20906d0a2.png" class="latex" alt="$S = \{11, 33, 99\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/1/fd12635db2710e622faf99f79d41eaaccadee728.png" class="latex" alt="$11 + 33 + 99 = 143$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />
</p><p>The answer must be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f61021f12ab09c5b9c3cfbcd7f6207920ff765b.png" class="latex" alt="$143$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, but cannot be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e46896b395361121e899e0ff08341c136bb443.png" class="latex" alt="$155$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> since no <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/e/bde88f4f9e9b00ab513f737a5152b2b146bedd2d.png" class="latex" alt="$n \le 12$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="14" /> other than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> satisfies the conditions, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f61021f12ab09c5b9c3cfbcd7f6207920ff765b.png" class="latex" alt="$143$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let us begin by working with the condition <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/9/6d95de13dea671bb3b0dcd8edd8182c4db762adb.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{ab} = 0.ababab\cdots,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="156" height="19" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f64aaf408a7b5e227507dc6b366e3a35b81703f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0.ababab\cdots$" width="123" height="12" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae83c02f695d3b2dd35cb261207e3fc12195ba7a.png" class="latex" alt="$100x-x = ab \Rightarrow x = \frac{ab}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="195" height="37" />. In order for this fraction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83dacecc3f852b6d1fc31accf43e249930c0b27f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> must be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" />. Hence the possibilities of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/633e0fa08024885a13d5d5a162d0617d28622ebd.png" class="latex" alt="$1,3,9,11,33,99$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="121" height="16" />. Checking each of these, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/3/99344bf41ef176a52a471f7779c748775628904c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{99} = 0.\overline{01}, \frac{3}{99}=\frac{1}{33} = 0.\overline{03}, \frac{9}{99}=\frac{1}{11} = 0.\overline{09}, \frac{11}{99}=\frac{1}{9} = 0.\overline{1}, \frac{33}{99} =\frac{1}{3}= 0.\overline{3},$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="579" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/b/74b38695681a12f1d7030b44c05b651dadde5091.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{99}{99} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />. So the only values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> that have distinct <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/1/5511748136888aafff9dc3dcd12d7d66762870ca.png" class="latex" alt="$11,33,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="48" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/a/10a5ade64e46d4e7ca316f099dd5e57c9daba44b.png" class="latex" alt="$11+33+99= \boxed{\textbf{(D)} 143}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="191" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Notice that we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/f/8dfaeee47cac2e0b75cf7d680e9f05cc5718adf9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{100}{n}= ab.\overline{ab}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="37" /> 
</p><p>We can subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4b2a9ebeb0eabf84b7915f829e54cf2924cc9f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n}=00.\overline{ab}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/5/2f57df5a15329eb5cb879cfb3323bbda91b1f036.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{99}{n}=ab\]" width="62" height="37" />
</p><p>From this we determine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be a positive factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />
</p><p><br />
The factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/23652685d34609a4186575157eb7af20ce20fa72.png" class="latex" alt="$1,3,9,11,33,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="100" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d89e4f60a889d43ecbbd71272abcec32fa1942d.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1,3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="65" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> however, they yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59d110d5c6fc4687e1c80249f18e3bacbf4f2b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=99,33$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> which doesn't satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> being distinct. 
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dccb0bdeb4680e457036b1230e6a9ae1621b9cd5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=11,33$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/8/c081f3b25f4785ed881af8be87971218852f2499.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=09,03$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1ef24f6cf1d8a2ebbbaba89ee70b68c9947414.png" class="latex" alt="$01$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.  (Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can be zero)
</p><p>The sum of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/a/7ea0e4c3c4e9070d5a86a8fed2ab34f578ffb1db.png" class="latex" alt="$11+33+99=143$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eba5b8dbbe352b9f05ad80f509a88958be45128.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} 143}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="68" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>As in previous solutions, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/6/196ea45e43288e5ebe0e320a01922c7ba08fb757.png" class="latex" alt="$n|99$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/1/901e909dffc64d389dd24006d4f730a0061469dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{ab} = 99/n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="20" />. If we had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abd2c942ae299fd26e18d0ca5d390a7ca4a8c34.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b$" width="42" height="12" />, the decimal would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59123a45e129e35b2b7df3d11ce93d6fd474c66.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{a}$" width="24" height="12" />, which is characterized by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8ea29931235a214b38a8aec73113d1dea712fa1.png" class="latex" alt="$n|9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/d/74d9f9499202a8f988321ea53536c418fb49c1fd.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 9/n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />. So we seek the sum of the factors of 99 that are not also factors of 9.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d987f20ed7cde0f888853d9349659e11f80451f.png" class="latex" alt="$99 = 3^2 \cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="15" />, the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab9f53a818037c25319a099f31ecbc53c702a205.png" class="latex" alt="$(1 + 3 + 9)(1 + 11) - (1 + 3 + 9) = 13(12 - 1) = \textbf{(D)} 143$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="444" height="18" />.
</p>
