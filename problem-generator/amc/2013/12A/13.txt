<p>Let points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/b/1eb1b4badf25fa610365e7a823a70fa3bfa07ffe.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (0,0) , \ B = (1,2), \ C = (3,3),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="267" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/1/131c888579b35136842c6ade87a7f8c62cb23824.png" class="latex" alt="$D = (4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />. Quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is cut into equal area pieces by a line passing through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. This line intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/d/53d4636fbca51bb3e6ebdfd9fc8ffb8ae8c392cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\left (\frac{p}{q}, \frac{r}{s} \right )$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="56" height="43" />, where these fractions are in lowest terms. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/2/f92249165ebc387c1588bc9eee8f77bbd5573253.png" class="latex" alt="$p + q + r + s$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="102" height="14" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If you have graph paper, use Pick's Theorem to quickly and efficiently find the area of the quadrilateral. If not, just find the area by other methods.
</p><p>Pick's Theorem states that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cf26be132ef93923e082ee4153b2cb0ef44a50.png" class="latex" alt="$+$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/6/416d7a2fd742e12560d3d780b1cb600cc67452dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{B}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="17" height="37" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> is the number of lattice points in the interior of the polygon, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is the number of lattice points on the boundary of the polygon.
</p><p>In this case, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cf26be132ef93923e082ee4153b2cb0ef44a50.png" class="latex" alt="$+$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea8d30f111d2f213c3486ec29afd22094d711beb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/0548d22c8d43aa7314709ed34e7c106d4057beb8.png" class="latex" alt="$7.5$" width="22" height="12" />
</p><p>so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a1352d1b32ec30e62432ad8c7ac1a47d1d6ef44.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{A}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="16" height="38" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aa018c85214f33ddd7cb898c49dabc90e25adec.png" class="latex" alt="$3.75$" width="31" height="12" />
</p><p>The bottom half of the quadrilateral makes a triangle with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and half the total area, so we can deduce that the height of the triangle must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> in order for its area to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aa018c85214f33ddd7cb898c49dabc90e25adec.png" class="latex" alt="$3.75$" width="31" height="12" />. This height is the y coordinate of our desired intersection point.
</p><p><br />
Note that segment CD lies on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/a/bfa8fd41c027035a360f634cf359aaf05bb2d340.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -3x + 12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="107" height="16" />. Substituting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> for y, we can find that the x coordinate of our intersection point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fa3c8496009387545863dccb8bd43a123f67e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{27}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />.
</p><p>Therefore the point of intersection is (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fa3c8496009387545863dccb8bd43a123f67e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{27}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />), and our desired result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/907d02e43324033806ab62f84c0add9d7d06b68c.png" class="latex" alt="$27+8+15+8= \boxed{ \text{B}) \ 58}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="201" height="32" />.
</p>
