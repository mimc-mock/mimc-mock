<p>Consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/9/8d96d19c649204cdd9c0ac3758e9555d73f6d7f8.png" class="latex" alt="$A = \log (2013 + \log (2012 + \log (2011 + \log (\cdots + \log (3 + \log 2) \cdots ))))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="537" height="18" />. Which of the following intervals contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/6/106c2fb8d6bd19573d0dd94f6a9ec2c7a7c8cf8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \ (\log 2016, \log 2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/35410473cfbb7dda7fbfb8b549fe2ea4e9c941e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)} \ (\log 2017, \log 2018)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/d/b7dc1c446b8102eaca3632ebe60ae9ce1904a7a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)} \ (\log 2018, \log 2019)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/e/bee0688e7867114726fa6394eda50ef530d35e96.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)} \ (\log 2019, \log 2020)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c82c281ed6cc6d2068b049d376385c3670490041.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)} \ (\log 2020, \log 2021)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="179" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/f/e1fd583416410ac0a70373863e27c239cb9be162.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \log(x + f(x-1))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f503e82227d1f7d6a63ed4db689463d0b2dcaaf3.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2) = \log(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, and from the problem description, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/7/7779635fe30327e23b8bc2184f4706574c6c29ee.png" class="latex" alt="$A = f(2013)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />
</p><p>We can reason out an approximation, by ignoring the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93e72384515cd2cabbfbc86b20e6003fa1991f77.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00e1d3534f6acfbff50fd508d65802ee426d6807.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{0}(x) \approx \log x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" />
</p><p>And a better approximation, by plugging in our first approximation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93e72384515cd2cabbfbc86b20e6003fa1991f77.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> in our original definition for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/633a37541a3bc635c55ac9a308805cb3694e5b16.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x) \approx \log(x + \log(x-1))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="213" height="18" />
</p><p>And an even better approximation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/8/2e833f63eb51e5bc215131cfd8b7d03f73d45af8.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{2}(x) \approx \log(x + \log(x-1 + \log(x-2)))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="314" height="18" />
</p><p>Continuing this pattern, obviously, will eventually terminate at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f39266f89525a95ae7688f8d29a417a1023ca43.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{x-1}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />, in other words our original definition of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />.
</p><p>However, at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/9/8991dcc3fdd9c5a926e18af4a07c1996c043dcef.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, going further than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11ef5766b0426af45297faae19e02b472a57a59b.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> will not distinguish between our answer choices. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/8/ee86b3babfd331e069fe640a5f92277c5deedb21.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012 + \log(2011))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" /> is nearly indistinguishable from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/747a5dc5c5ebbb41aa52e33cd37d7149620410d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />.
</p><p>So we take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11ef5766b0426af45297faae19e02b472a57a59b.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> and plug in.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c761bf2ed343c09f5fbf0cb3d07ccbd825f4c39c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2013) \approx \log(2013 + \log 2012)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="243" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/3/423e44751297b57170ec1e0fe9f51da5f1447f65.png" class="latex" alt="$1000 &lt; 2012 &lt; 10000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="165" height="13" />, we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/0/75019e1b9433ab60e54664285d96984caaf85192.png" class="latex" alt="$3 &lt; \log(2012) &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" />. This gives us our answer range:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/a/deabd22e2ecfc30317784d0239862095b2c88333.png" class="latex" alt="$\log 2016 &lt; \log(2013 + \log 2012) &lt; \log(2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/8/088fd7e500107a7e80eca68ccc795edf9ea263a5.png" class="latex" alt="$(\log 2016, \log 2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a783b06860ef4470d40cef90c0c7f4498b451df2.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\log(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. 
Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/3/d7382a0abccfd4bcc9f109dcd404c4260de42b81.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012+ \cdots)=x-2013$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="213" height="18" />. 
So if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/a/c3a4827804f453a51aa85b2e1c13e9dfd38e261d.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8e5b8365b8026db543cbbda77efcf34cfcd8de4.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012+\log(2011+\cdots))&gt;4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="250" height="18" />. 
So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/c/8cce11a90091631b4de3507f0644362c23eb1696.png" class="latex" alt="$2012+\log(2011+\cdots)&gt;10000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="248" height="18" />. 
Repeating, we then get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/0/7e0814be5c665db29be7144cfe8f66587003bc65.png" class="latex" alt="$2011+\log(2010+\cdots)&gt;10^{7988}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="19" />. 
This is clearly absurd (the RHS continues to grow more than exponentially for each iteration).
So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is not greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. 
So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/1/871695ee434a51fc8a0c7f40499f043bf1dda6df.png" class="latex" alt="$A&lt;\log(2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" />. 
But this leaves only one answer, so we are done.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f503e82227d1f7d6a63ed4db689463d0b2dcaaf3.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2) = \log(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/d/fed31504cc561c2c5f92fb65c9315695de05a3ac.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n) = \log(n+f(n-1)), \text{ for } n &gt; 2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="285" height="18" /> We are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/7/507bb4f14eed821f84fb072d2cb46e80cd90c7f6.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2013)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />. First we show
</p><p><b>Lemma.</b> For any integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b9b7a46559a729577c3ca04dd2d7e66de16b3f.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4c4725864fbe17dc65a60b8a7d2d923821da1f.png" class="latex" alt="$n &lt; 10^k-k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="16" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/0/990e8a2ff203747bd68d7ce8942d9cc72af69c62.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n) &lt; k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Proof.</b> First note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/d/e1d72baf5c184b3fd81129bbad2c81c79953b1ea.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2) &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c566b18cd754b2ccaba3f7094aa4d9c4584f34d6.png" class="latex" alt="$n&lt;10^k-k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="16" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27a0ded0f32c8ab00b2dca85e95af99a1ebcf215.png" class="latex" alt="$n+k&lt;10^k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="17" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/4/3b4bddadad74c1aee36047524fb242ff1c474c9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(n+k)&lt; k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />. Suppose the claim is true for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b9660ae6ab9e6d0c986ff7cbbfc42f98c5ab3afa.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n) = \log(n+f(n-1)) &lt; \log(n + k) &lt; k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="337" height="18" />.  The Lemma is thus proved by induction.
</p><p>Finally, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/e/64ea2183aa7d2cc54f3724e6408e937a8b599f3c.png" class="latex" alt="$2012 &lt; 10^4 - 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="117" height="15" /> so that the Lemma implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/c/06c8b67c8c9e0fdd5cb752d69363dae840c2ad9f.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2012) &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/9792600cd1a0f58acad08fc7855e7db4aa813383.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2013) = \log(2013+f(2012)) &lt; \log(2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="339" height="18" />, which leaves us with only one option <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7ecab82e24e9e1be8a18e48867e1dadf56faa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } (\log 2016, \log 2017)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="195" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f503e82227d1f7d6a63ed4db689463d0b2dcaaf3.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2) = \log(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/d/fed31504cc561c2c5f92fb65c9315695de05a3ac.png" class="latex" alt="$f(n) = \log(n+f(n-1)), \text{ for } n &gt; 2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="285" height="18" /> We start with a simple observation:
</p><p><b>Lemma.</b> For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/9/1b9f4d2caa7fd50dba44bb8c13b061b9cf72f8f6.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y&gt;2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db3c98227093e6a5aa7f642ae85e6e5e3ffef8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(x+\log(y)) &lt; \log(x)+\log(y)=\log(xy)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="339" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Proof.</b> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/9/1b9f4d2caa7fd50dba44bb8c13b061b9cf72f8f6.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y&gt;2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3da95fddf45c1a0d0b3819bf6b5acc8e13c651d.png" class="latex" alt="$xy-x-y = (x-1)(y-1) - 1 &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="283" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/d/6ddef7f4c1c2be51c19b430fa4dfd71341e772da.png" class="latex" alt="$xy - x-\log(y) &gt; xy - x - y &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="263" height="18" />.
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/8/0e833a2f1f3b48074a564a19e2196acc6c63ace2.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(z+\log(x+\log(y))) &lt; \log(x)+\log(y)+\log(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="395" height="18" />, and so on. 
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/2/24202ad600b19e31eb42fa0628851a7adc08e16a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2010) &lt; \log 2 + \log 3 + \cdots + \log 2010 &lt; \log 2010 + \cdots + \log 2010 &lt;  2009\cdot 4 = 8036$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="666" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/82714602dc5d58f10cc107518758fa07ecfd7998.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2011) = \log(2011+f(2010)) &lt; \log(10047) &lt; 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="383" height="18" />. 
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/4/7e48c3139646bd2a23a006dba779f77fd7d7bf94.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2012) = \log(2012+f(2011)) &lt; \log(2017) &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="374" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Finally, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/f/ecf6bb7cbed1725a97944730043d3d91143b7814.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2013) = \log(2013 + f(2012)) &lt; \log(2017)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="339" height="18" />, which leaves us with only option <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_5_(nonrigorous_+_abusing_answer_choices.)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_.28nonrigorous_.2B_abusing_answer_choices..29">Solution 5 (nonrigorous + abusing answer choices.)</span></h3>
<p>Intuitively, you can notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf2679cab6a7ba0a78e354e14e6af697c187c18.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012+\log(2011+\cdots(\log(2)\cdots))&lt;\log(2013+\log(2012+\cdots(\log(2))\cdots))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="625" height="18" />, therefore (by the answer choices) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/b/7fbe251b53bbc4fc94a0a420f4a93d835c7d9e04.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(2012+\log(2011+\cdots(\log(2)\cdots))&lt;\log(2021)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="18" />. We can then say:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38d4771fec9e7ee63c679d1086eb9ab00d37876c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x=\log(2013+\log(2012+\cdots(\log(2))\cdots))\]" width="337" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/1/7e1aa5de62850e57c0f09a30ad9435aaee44c2a4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log(2013)&lt;x&lt;\log(2013+\log(2021))\]" width="300" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/1/711751ba2126ea37114a3f48761218eed9d09f87.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log(2013)&lt;x&lt;\log(2013+4)\]" width="236" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/1/5818fd64c02247a30223aa9098ccca3dd61046d7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log(2013)&lt;x&lt;\log(2017)\]" width="204" height="18" />
</p><p>The only answer choice that is possible given this information is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_6_(super_quick)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6_.28super_quick.29">Solution 6 (super quick)</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc00b0335fad7558140f46eb1ec673654c98a6f3.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \log(x + \log((x-1) + \log((x-2) + \ldots + \log 2 \ldots )))$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="466" height="20" />. From the answer choices, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdc3cd554120a289d8f1c888bc58cd5e2d6950e.png" class="latex" alt="$3 &lt; f(2013) &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="130" height="20" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> grows very slowly, we can assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/c/f0c076ad0802dfe7054f420f094f8cd2c76e5cd0.png" class="latex" alt="$3 &lt; f(2012) &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="130" height="20" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/d/78d069ac46d58e76ee41786f7818de706ccc87e1.png" class="latex" alt="$f(2013) = \log(2013 + f(2012)) \in (\log 2016, \log 2017) \implies \boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="505" height="30" />.
</p><p>~rayfish
