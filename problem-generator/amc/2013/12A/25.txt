<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/7/f4722bd446080194646d6fe6c7be7c2990b3f531.png" class="latex" alt="$f&#160;: \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="16" /> be defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aa6a13c4e9d51462b2a23630e233c74539c3682.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z) = z^2 + iz + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="19" />. How many complex numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> are there such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/055f504c1254f3f83379ae60c3741071bfbd36cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Im}(z) &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" /> and both the real and the imaginary parts of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/301469a1d6b6bd4ddb15b26b27e22c03d5611b2e.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> are integers with absolute value at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/a/eeabb5387abe14975cd9ac5b912b7b774ae802e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \ 399 \qquad \textbf{(B)} \ 401 \qquad \textbf{(C)} \ 413 \qquad \textbf{(D)} \ 431 \qquad \textbf{(E)} \ 441$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d13c28f807c35ff0154e91134286f6b6d0dc95.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z)=z^2+iz+1=c=a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="19" />. We look for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7420a178b0da9a2169a5b4e8b6a21f3bd5c5eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\operatorname{Im}(z)&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> are integers where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/5/465650e71d6acf0ce5a432e776daa99304b7fb70.png" class="latex" alt="$|a|, |b|\leq 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="18" />.
</p><p>First, use the quadratic formula:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/c/2ac380944111a94a8de5d0782bc508d8b6d1c367.png" class="latex" alt="$z = \frac{1}{2} (-i \pm \sqrt{-1-4(1-c)}) = -\frac{i}{2} \pm \sqrt{ -\frac{5}{4} + c }$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="378" height="43" />
</p><p>Generally, consider the imaginary part of a radical of a complex number: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/e/c2e322bd47b066f74264b0d85c39ad653a56080f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{u}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe745c1e3b8deb910b7917e5d67f594fad32dc87.png" class="latex" alt="$u = v+wi = r e^{i\theta}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="138" height="17" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/7/6b74518625040bba73950f85132ca2cc83745f47.png" class="latex" alt="$\operatorname{Im}(\sqrt{u}) = \operatorname{Im}(\pm \sqrt{r} e^{i\theta/2}) = \pm \sqrt{r} \sin(\theta/2) = \pm \sqrt{r}\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos\theta}{2}} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{r-v}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="575" height="44" />.
</p><p>Now let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8180f6d3815ff47791e24c4132bd4e8ee9640868.png" class="latex" alt="$u= -5/4 + c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/4/e54df8643cda0ff34b67cbab65d985afab174992.png" class="latex" alt="$v = -5/4 + a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff50fda2a49821e988cc7eb694f1d592fff680e5.png" class="latex" alt="$w=b$" width="45" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/e/ddecd51a79190a288c5d2913e8489694ee752a45.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\sqrt{v^2 + w^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="18" />.
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7420a178b0da9a2169a5b4e8b6a21f3bd5c5eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\operatorname{Im}(z)&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/c/12ce969bf333f1c3c53702e4f0f0c88bceb8699c.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm \sqrt{\frac{r-v}{2}}&gt;\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="112" height="43" />. The latter is true only when we take the positive sign, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/495946a3d900788664dac69bfd0e13acf37529cf.png" class="latex" alt="$r-v &gt; 1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" />,
</p><p>or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78e8e85fec682244a37356341d024dd28d7822ce.png" class="latex" alt="$v^2 + w^2 &gt; (1/2 + v)^2 = 1/4 + v + v^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="286" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/3/1837806e6f839d3ed6e6f89366ab71919efe7447.png" class="latex" alt="$w^2 &gt; 1/4 + v$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="19" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b6a46461a1da7ead29768a4ed60d6834ad355e.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 &gt; a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="15" />.
</p><p>In other words, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b6a46461a1da7ead29768a4ed60d6834ad355e.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 &gt; a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="15" />, the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/c/03c2c13ed55795729218dc30718436b433fa7086.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z)=a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" /> has unique solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> in the region <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7420a178b0da9a2169a5b4e8b6a21f3bd5c5eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\operatorname{Im}(z)&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />; and when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/c/f3cb44212b04c15fd7ce85081b6a8e0cb36b9b39.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 \leq a-1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="79" height="17" /> there is no solution. Therefore the number of desired solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is the same as the number of ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> such that integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/5/465650e71d6acf0ce5a432e776daa99304b7fb70.png" class="latex" alt="$|a|, |b|\leq 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="18" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f442d8da62d9df20ec3814bc8cf7d74008bb1f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 \geq a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="17" />.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/4/5f4748d892aa7695e9dac5e0a950546c4332a46f.png" class="latex" alt="$a\leq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />, there is no restriction on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6ea7ca11e2776768e672ab9bf3641a911fd6eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$11\cdot 21 = 231$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="99" height="13" /> pairs;
</p><p>when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/5/d25634d7212302efdeb3702974863759bfb74d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$a &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/7/3272c36d504690d45cf89aef1d3c5cfad4e3bee8.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1+4+9+10+10+10+10+10+10+10)=2(84)=168$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="494" height="18" /> pairs.
</p><p>So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/1/811f06f29e2ae27b37be561a052c874213deb6c0.png" class="latex" alt="$231+168=\boxed{399}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="140" height="23" /> in total.
</p>
