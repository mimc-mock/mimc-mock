<p>The angles in a particular triangle are in arithmetic progression, and the side lengths are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/0/ba093ec86ee0e3f1ec47f613cb3e343ec7117d74.png" class="latex" alt="$4,5,x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="16" />. The sum of the possible values of x equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc04c0988983661ce28dfe63bc62dd7054fe93a4.png" class="latex" alt="$a+\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="100" height="21" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3834f0cef2a1f4d8524aa5bc8e3faca298550862.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are positive integers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6ebd1e0356cfe09c0d9b5459d6f1542657bb5891.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/b/06bfa9bd396251568dafe71019b3e0afe2e49c6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 38\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 42\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Because the angles are in an arithmetic progression, and the angles add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />, the second largest angle in the triangle must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d5c45388de5c75af641c046d466de3592ffa98.png" class="latex" alt="$60^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. Also, the side opposite of that angle must be the second longest because of the angle-side relationship. Any of the three sides, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, could be the second longest side of the triangle.
</p><p>The law of cosines can be applied to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in all three cases.
</p><p>When the second longest side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/6/9465cacd4d4df9d863a7341ee82ab2f6ce4c14a1.png" class="latex" alt="$5^2 = 4^2 + x^2 - 2(4)(x)\cos 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="19" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b71097cc2d61ce1f86e984bb04f4fbea1a8321dc.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 4x - 9 = 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="123" height="15" />. By using the quadratic formula, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/4/6946aae5b83570071ea514fb33415c1036872ca4.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac {4 + \sqrt{16 + 36}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="142" height="40" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/7/277126f9f360e116b0783fa5d2ced638d1492c93.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 2 + \sqrt{13}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="18" />.
</p><p>When the second longest side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fca43996a1dc5bc8e3dc0c3a628103021ec4173d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = 5^2 + 4^2 - 40\cos 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="192" height="16" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/625a770ff96d2107b3d252be809917684378476e.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \sqrt{21}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="18" />.
</p><p>When the second longest side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/7/937b192edd494b61bb592f5727ec9db684178ab4.png" class="latex" alt="$4^2 = 5^2 + x^2 - 2(5)(x)\cos 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="19" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/9/d79f8e32de7bc4bba1d0d91729a34284dfdd07c8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 5x + 9 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" />. Using the quadratic formula, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29f04e943e44ecf131255b65955dcca7e687fb77.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac {5 + \sqrt{25 - 36}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="142" height="40" />. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/1140d9d5d98d56ed2cc698afbb9c6e0aa6a56fc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{-11}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="18" /> is not real, therefore the second longest side cannot equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>Adding the two other possibilities gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5acbe2f9e02a06138a1d68acb87dbfbab2e395.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + \sqrt{13} + \sqrt{21}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="18" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/3/e0360c1e104b68c54f1a8ef42d50cf6084a637f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 2, b=13$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="100" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/d/a5db7c4591c4d909481f9b46bd1d1d25688cf728.png" class="latex" alt="$c=21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/f/c4ffd7566f7be7a56881b638c9f767286e98912f.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/5/ec5bdc2c0e59651ee5e6fa74ba318dc73d744feb.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="17" />.
</p>
