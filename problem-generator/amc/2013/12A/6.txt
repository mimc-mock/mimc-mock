<p>In a recent basketball game, Shenille attempted only three-point shots and two-point shots. She was successful on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of her three-point shots and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of her two-point shots. Shenille attempted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> shots. How many points did she score?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37af9f74b30137343b53b7ff710502da5a9a6986.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 24\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the number of 3-point shots attempted be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Since she attempted 30 shots, the number of 2-point shots attempted must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d2351a9ff42ede4103dfc074ebca94aded29d20.png" class="latex" alt="$30 - x$" width="50" height="12" />.
</p><p>Since she was successful on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/2/d129a7b3c0bf9fbd0693afdd426a66d2a0a8c3ae.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, of her 3-pointers, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/0/5f0cf2a41ca6fbaafe3d21b823a9bed30c7272fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />, of her 2-pointers, then her score must be
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/5/075f235fe2ff7c118b66ff738afee7ac1f2e11f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{5}*3x + \frac{3}{10}*2(30-x)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="184" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/e/80eda413be7b188252b48af345aaf12286e3a335.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}*x + \frac{3}{5}(30-x)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="139" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/b/2dbd3a6f1d9489b987c5d36c3724f4b7b53c41af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}(x+30-x)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2d9ab03ba7d15e9a5bcfdbee574769fa5d2fa5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}*30$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="47" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_Solution">Alternative Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since the problem doesn't specify the number of 3-point shots she attempted, it can be assumed that number doesn't matter, so let it be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Then, she must have attempted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> 2-point shots. So, her score must be:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/a/58aaeb5a79d642c7ac42b8235be1f7851d5e8cb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{10}*30*2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="83" height="38" />,which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_note">Additional note</span></h2>
<p>It is also reasonably easy to find all possibilities for the number of two-point and three-point shots she made. Just note that both numbers of successful throws have to be integers. For "<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of her two-point shots" to be an integer we need the number of two-point shots to be divisible by 10. This only leaves four possibilities for the number of two-point shots: 0, 10, 20, or 30. Each of them also works for the three-point shots, and as shown above, for each of them the total number of points scored is the same.
</p>
