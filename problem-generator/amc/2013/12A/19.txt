Moreover <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40e341674d6c973ae0dd53dace5036995f31997b.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BX}$" width="31" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/9/5895bfa441a2027d808cec50fa4da5229e07e89e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CX}$" width="31" height="15" /> have integer lengths. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span id="Solution_1_(Diophantine_PoP)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_.28Diophantine_PoP.29">Solution 1 (Diophantine PoP)</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77ce7565827bb55748707258399412ab0383d8b9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] //Made by samrocksnature size(8cm); pair A,B,C,D,E,X; A=(0,0); B=(-53.4,-67.4); C=(0,-97); D=(0,-86); E=(0,86); X=(-29,-81); draw(circle(A,86)); draw(E--C--B--A--X); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NE); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NE); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,NE); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,X,dir(250)); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^E^^X); [/asy]" width="318" height="378" />
</p><p>Let circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> intersect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> as shown. We apply Power of a Point on point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> with respect to circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/8207b79435a4bd499da7598fb44424c8a8499238.png" class="latex" alt="$A.$" width="17" height="13" /> This yields the diophantine equation 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/5/6955597337bfaf1bbd79dd1d15d2ba9d9227b592.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CX \cdot CB = CD \cdot CE\]" width="168" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/4/dc4add72db43218e0aa56ac52e01618be9434f5a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CX(CX+XB) = (97-86)(97+86)\]" width="296" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/4/0645db9f2cad48e2fafc2d537016066c30a8b241.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CX(CX+XB) = 3 \cdot 11 \cdot 61.\]" width="228" height="18" />
</p><p>Since lengths cannot be negative, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/9/b09b0d7c988db29e9a08901cd69593488a5a9e99.png" class="latex" alt="$CX+XB \ge CX.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="145" height="17" /> This generates the four solution pairs for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/b/b0bc80da9fe0beb4faaef634cc81f7316f6cd707.png" class="latex" alt="$(CX,CX+XB)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="138" height="20" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/c/6ecaeb68a9caf2a965d78622451d2ee3774faa96.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(1,2013) \qquad (3,671) \qquad (11,183) \qquad (33,61).\]" width="353" height="18" />
</p><p>However, by the Triangle Inequality on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/576d27297ff8bebed39327fe4a334b9f91f52027.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACX,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="17" /> we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/4/294d2de3cd8e802e2d99551b68c3033d65ddb4a6.png" class="latex" alt="$CX&gt;13.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="80" height="15" /> This implies that we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/9/3d9004ee1b9554d4e52c82782d16333b0eab9347.png" class="latex" alt="$CX+XB= \boxed{\textbf{(D) }61}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="181" height="30" /> 
</p><p>(Solution by unknown, latex/asy modified majorly by samrocksnature)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd0d1ded44edd12c5c2ea6290b65bde3a31d2eb.png" class="latex" alt="$BX = q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d57a7d3459de8353ddcb527dc4c2038902d3154.png" class="latex" alt="$CX = p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> meet the circle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />.  Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/7/7370a7b3a820622f89dcd80da5b19d135ce9175c.png" class="latex" alt="$AZ = AY = 86$" width="121" height="13" />.  Using the Power of a Point, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f83e04c3156ab3b3d2d1ac7d487638b652f4c53c.png" class="latex" alt="$p(p+q) = 11(183) = 11 * 3 * 61$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="251" height="18" />.  We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/c/65cfdc5838fc3c6d839488e35ee13f03dc88c381.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q&gt;p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="14" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/1792efa42bbaf227ee6321a141bfe7d0d0072e0d.png" class="latex" alt="$p&gt;13$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="16" /> by the triangle inequality on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/2/af253e8b1b795df12f128aad8ff98d491a56fad6.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACX$" width="59" height="13" />.  Thus, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5e142c28844cf10740ca4a7d9715b407c83deff.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = p+q = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }61}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="183" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> represent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/2/73205379672e860eb250c541187600fae3a3e225.png" class="latex" alt="$CX$" width="30" height="12" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> represent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a4b9c32938e96f30b59dcb29f410cd4c2e3982.png" class="latex" alt="$BX$" width="30" height="12" />. Since the circle goes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/d/32d67201d74984f4154c1626066765c65897e0cb.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = AX = 86$" width="124" height="13" />.
Then by Stewart's Theorem,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/e/afe154b646e779e9d2ac9b3ecbe3b7dc1547c895.png" class="latex" alt="$xy(x+y) + 86^2 (x+y) = 97^2 y + 86^2 x.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="301" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/7/957fa992e71cfcdc396697aeca7de7e2ccb9c010.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 y + xy^2 + 86^2 x + 86^2 y = 97^2 y + 86^2 x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="310" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/9/529687b2555be9f749b13c3f315b253698eeb9e4.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + xy + 86^2 = 97^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="156" height="18" />
</p><p>(Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> cannot be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, dividing both sides of the equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is allowed.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec82cd8d508332b558536634b6cd65d7444d5ad.png" class="latex" alt="$x(x+y) = (97+86)(97-86)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="234" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/5/f35141eea0a87f64f3789125ead69c396b347d8d.png" class="latex" alt="$x(x+y) = 2013$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />
</p><p>The prime factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/866b44935f69a5b800547127fe13210bb1e36fbc.png" class="latex" alt="$61$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Obviously, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/1/3b12e98e20610031ffc93c31267bc4689c6c2bfc.png" class="latex" alt="$x &lt; x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="14" />. In addition, by the Triangle Inequality, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/3/c5393cb3cdb1294a26e5901968bf388393de0c35.png" class="latex" alt="$BC &lt; AB + AC$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/9/7f91fb98d0e5e33bf3190d5f6e5b38f5e904f690.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y &lt; 183$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6f3bccae16d5e31266370fced24edc41538d5da.png" class="latex" alt="$33$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/1/2514598e8f3dc38f9a9a05a7aea48ff619e29537.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }61}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />
