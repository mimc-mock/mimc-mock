<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let us label the points on the diagram.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb6fc22d1947c4b1adf1b31092ca955cea458f5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import olympiad; size(200); defaultpen(linewidth(1) + fontsize(10)); pair A = (0,0), B = (1,0), C = (4,0), D = (8,0), K = (0,4), F = (1,4), G = (7.25, 4), H = (8, 4), I = (8,3), J = (5, 7); fill(F--G--I--C--F--cycle, grey); markscalefactor=0.05; draw(A--D--H--K--A^^B--F^^F--C--I--J--F^^rightanglemark(F,J,I)^^rightanglemark(F,B,C)^^anglemark(D,C,I)^^anglemark(B,F,C)^^anglemark(H,I,G)); draw(anglemark(F,C,B)^^anglemark(C,I,D)^^anglemark(I,G,H)); markscalefactor=0.041; draw(anglemark(F,C,B)^^anglemark(C,I,D)^^anglemark(I,G,H)); label(&quot;8&quot;,(4,-.5),S); label(&quot;5&quot;,(3, 5.5),NW); label(&quot;4&quot;,(8.25, 2), E); label(&quot;A&quot;, F, NW); label(&quot;B&quot;, B, S); label(&quot;C&quot;, C, S); label(&quot;D&quot;, D, SE); label(&quot;E&quot;, I, E); label(&quot;F&quot;, H, NE); label(&quot;G&quot;, G, NE); label(&quot;4&quot;, (1,2), E); label(&quot;5&quot;, (2.5,2), SW); label(&quot;3&quot;, (2.5,0), S); label(&quot;4&quot;, (6,0), S); label(&quot;5&quot;, (6,1.5), SE); label(&quot;3&quot;, (8, 1.5), E); label(&quot;1&quot;, (8, 3.5), E); [/asy]" width="335" height="308" />
</p><p>By doing some angle chasing using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/291b6d0f956fd6c89571701ea806380d94e34e55.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACE$" width="55" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a757bf3d05b75eefbb0c237bbd73a6309944d35.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CEG$" width="55" height="12" /> are right angles, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40ee12140150c5d5c43238205a9eca9d8e883fc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC \cong \angle DCE \cong \angle FEG$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="219" height="15" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/b/b9b2d57b8e73f8a8ed82965ed3f89fd699a84705.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB \cong \angle CED \cong \angle EGF$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="219" height="15" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/5/305264491990eb3d06614e81ef9da36ae4f58e54.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC \sim \triangle CDE \sim \triangle EFG$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="229" height="18" />.
</p><p>As we are given a rectangle and a square, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/a/79acca1cedca0d43e9d183383ef794ad7932baa1.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/1/94185900419e82fd8c9e03652017c53deeb065fc.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 5$" width="60" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> right triangle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/f/3bf318adbc664ea1b3a7b469be11de66bce0de67.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 3$" width="61" height="12" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c970cbf5e2e7029089517d5e8efbb8cade6d8a.png" class="latex" alt="$CE$" width="28" height="12" /> is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. So, using the similar triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5b3c9b777daa994886ac518a6988e60c53c22c1.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea807fba10955dec4c8f1d74046b6bae3d760d1c.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = 3$" width="63" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/3/5a3fda9fdc10c6072ad64e84865d0e711382fe4f.png" class="latex" alt="$EF = DF - DE = 4 - 3 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="235" height="16" />. Using the similar triangles again, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/d/4cd0f9f9869b39e79527f9010293adf7bfe5210f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of the corresponding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. So, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/d/92d937c5c9b05aedf0a1f411587e8f438d0752ce.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} [\triangle EFG] &amp;= \left(\frac14\right)^2 \cdot [\triangle ABC] \\ &amp;= \frac{1}{16} \cdot 6 \\ &amp;= \frac38. \end{align*}" width="218" height="133" />
</p><p>Finally, we have 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fc25d6de87038f06371787bebc3b00bdd6e4276.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} [ACEG] &amp;= [ABDF] - [\triangle ABC] - [\triangle CDE] - [\triangle EFG] \\ &amp;= 7 \cdot 4 - \frac12 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 - \frac12 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 - \frac38 \\ &amp;= 28 - 6 - 6 - \frac38 \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(D) }15\dfrac{5}{8}}. \end{align*}" width="431" height="161" />
