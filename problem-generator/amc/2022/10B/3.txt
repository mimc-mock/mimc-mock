<p>How many three-digit positive integers have an odd number of even digits?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/482b4f850c6e1ea042cee85e0906376ad761dead.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }150\qquad\textbf{(B) }250\qquad\textbf{(C) }350\qquad\textbf{(D) }450\qquad\textbf{(E) }550$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="446" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ways for an odd number of even digits: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> even digit or all even digits. 
</p><p><b>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/d/1cdc25220c171bd81d0f2eda5889ed7c75f34e18.png" class="latex" alt="$\bf{1}$" width="9" height="12" /> even digit</b>
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/c/dacb5bc7a12dfba4464008c846b3f636351534b3.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 5 = 25$" width="73" height="12" /> ways to choose the odd digits, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> ways for the even digit, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to order the even digit. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ab06dc5ec99c7631dbb05d4a31079df18f368b.png" class="latex" alt="$25 \cdot 5 \cdot 3 = 375$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="14" />. However, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/b/c1b451240d1dc2d09b5dc2b9fb94b073fdfff5a2.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 5= 25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="76" height="14" /> ways that the hundred's digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and we must subtract this from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/531204a6ace18de0514d0adfd920358e6d0c2e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$375$" width="26" height="12" />, leaving us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/e/9ceffeb3d880c80aa8bb29fe99a7905ca0b2f719.png" class="latex" alt="$350$" width="26" height="12" /> ways.
</p><p><b>Case 2: all even digits</b>
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/0/410e601fb97e2839e8a6b51e27ffcf212ffde45c.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 5 \cdot 5 = 125$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" /> ways to choose the even digits, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/c/dacb5bc7a12dfba4464008c846b3f636351534b3.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 5 = 25$" width="73" height="12" /> ways where the hundred's digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a280a29f830ba96a7e3de4db70dc4c48b04cb576.png" class="latex" alt="$125-25=100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="121" height="15" />.
</p><p>Adding up the cases, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/a/4ba3c4a6d0be1fc389fd9a64586fcef327079f8a.png" class="latex" alt="$100+350=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }450}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="177" height="30" />.
</p><p>~MrThinker
