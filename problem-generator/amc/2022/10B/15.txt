<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74a070eea97d9363f58a257e05e75fc69efa753.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> be the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> term of an arithmetic sequence that has a common difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. The quotient <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/5/03568c99de5ca3c8b879a54c9c3e6a407f4b7ec0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S_{3n}}{S_n}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="29" height="40" /> does not depend on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a171b62f8fa798757e7afa0ca4bbab5c2adeee1.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{20}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" />?

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that the first number of the arithmetic sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. We will try to compute the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bde7059c6b5b207061a12fbd17380fbdff0ea6f.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" />. First, note that the sum of an arithmetic sequence is equal to the number of terms multiplied by the median of the sequence. The median of this sequence is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/9/79994550b649aa465d799fae8c1014ef8a94357b.png" class="latex" alt="$a + n - 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="13" />. Thus, the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bde7059c6b5b207061a12fbd17380fbdff0ea6f.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/1/5c1de602573068d6672763c96041f400a2e6e0e0.png" class="latex" alt="$n(a + n - 1) = n^2 + n(a - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="232" height="21" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaad9bebe5cd0ef8fb0c9936a7094105b3332d56.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{S_{3n}}{S_{n}} = \frac{9n^2 + 3n(a - 1)}{n^2 + n(a - 1)} = 9 - \frac{3n(a-1)}{n^2 + n(a-1)}.\]" width="351" height="44" /> Of course, for this value to be constant, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77dfb3877e9b42918de0afcfb7609ac0a54f849a.png" class="latex" alt="$3n(a-1)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="77" height="20" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> for all values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Finally, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/0/9109a0feb9c12e94646be612d3e6b4143dbf0cb6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{20} = 20^2 = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 400}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="176" height="30" />.
</p><p>~mathboy100
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let's say that our sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/4/6949385de927daa2c0a1ca2865fa033d023d5684.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a, a+2, a+4, a+6, a+8, a+10, \ldots.\]" width="299" height="15" />
Then, since the value of n doesn't matter in the quotient <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/5/03568c99de5ca3c8b879a54c9c3e6a407f4b7ec0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S_{3n}}{S_n}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="29" height="40" />, we can say that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/1/211d4a410ea2f4e0fbb5ba5b4cfd339a58f3b8f1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{S_{3}}{S_1} = \frac{S_{6}}{S_2}.\]" width="71" height="40" />
Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5c4b32a201185302b576cd0c51c958b6d5719c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3a+6}{a}=\frac{6a+30}{2a+2}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="143" height="40" />, from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/a/00a05f13737914ad45f8f09d76035e8148c1b34a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a+2}{a}=\frac{a+5}{a+1}.\]" width="115" height="39" />
Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Now, we proceed similar to Solution 1 and get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/0/9109a0feb9c12e94646be612d3e6b4143dbf0cb6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{20} = 20^2 = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 400}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="176" height="30" />.
</p>
