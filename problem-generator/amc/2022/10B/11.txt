<p>All the high schools in a large school district are involved in a fundraiser selling T-shirts. Which of the choices below is logically equivalent to the statement "No school bigger than Euclid HS sold more T-shirts than Euclid HS"?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/7/e8738bbc91069d2db6a97b6ab9b1e13052ac4606.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> All schools smaller than Euclid HS sold fewer T-shirts than Euclid HS.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/6/506e909041d36bd739c8c959d341fcfbba3e9ad7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> No school that sold more T-shirts than Euclid HS is bigger than Euclid HS.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab3a9c13a5c376b1660f46d00b9007dbae240cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> All schools bigger than Euclid HS sold fewer T-shirts than Euclid HS.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/3787f1b7ce783a57c3a0d02ca4d485ccc950ff0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> All schools that sold fewer T-shirts than Euclid HS are smaller than Euclid HS.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/d/81d05263c2e18459105296c8e3e06c9fddddb735.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E) }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> All schools smaller than Euclid HS sold more T-shirts than Euclid HS.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> denote a school that is bigger than Euclid HS, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> denote a school that sold more T-shirts than Euclid HS.
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/a/caaf20876c9c98b518b8be95efe42fd83b9b26be.png" class="latex" alt="$\neg B$" width="26" height="12" /> denotes a school that is <b>not bigger than</b> Euclid HS, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0e14ddf8575f8770b67feba5c70f42d549b56f.png" class="latex" alt="$\neg M$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="34" height="14" /> denotes a school that <b>did not sell more</b> T-shirts than Euclid HS.
</p><p>Converting everything to conditional statements (if-then form), the given statement becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/6/8563e190d437bf0437071a34b93887bd0861c4b0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[B\implies\neg M.\]" width="98" height="13" />
Its contrapositive is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/44893d6ce3280b1f473ed7dc0c7690c28357f1ae.png" class="latex" alt="$M\implies\neg B,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd9d1003fa0ecb744b1ebc09b28550c6bcf3372.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="43" height="28" />
</p><p>Note that "not bigger than" does not mean "smaller than", and "not selling more" does not mean "selling fewer". There is an equality case. Therefore, none of the other answer choices is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/8/be8cf00a4a04ffb12cfae8ce47f8bf25a6871e0a.png" class="latex" alt="$B\implies\neg M.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" />
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
