<p>The positive difference between a pair of primes is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and the positive difference between the cubes of the two primes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/4/cc4cc34fa4eb94bdfc74be45dd2f4d97a1099199.png" class="latex" alt="$31106$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" />. What is the sum of the digits of the least prime that is greater than those two primes?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/3/583f18f2c5326fe0da7b83576ee8ea2951c91b58.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 10 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 11 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 13 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the two primes be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. We would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/3/9c3af692cc93cdd87ec76bb01f433b98e5be69d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=2$" width="72" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/c/b3c70cda970802dd6f45b8c6f7f0f37f2bb425d5.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{3}-b^{3}=31106$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="18" />. Using difference of cubes, we would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/7/667f3303014c1e593a2c8d287ec933c2e8270229.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a^{2}+ab+b^{2})=31106$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="234" height="21" />. Since we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/b/b1b5f47f19bdc5d77830378aad849b65c108c730.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a^{2}+ab+b^{2})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="19" /> would become <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/7/b67a513e454469a44cfaa0a35744ddfcb60253db.png" class="latex" alt="$2(a^{2}+ab+b^{2})=31106$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="189" height="21" />. Simplifying more, we would get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/313a614aa9b2bb56cf1f5cbe215185fa93aa5226.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{2}+ab+b^{2}=15553$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="166" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Now let's introduce another variable. Instead of using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we can express the primes as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> and b is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Plugging <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> in, we would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/b/84bc3154bc98a95eec162b76b3bcad7f868f0e48.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+2)^{2}+x(x+2)+x^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="194" height="21" />. When we expand the parenthesis, it would become <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/a/54a3d0f7ee97bcf8af3b85d593d98710095e106c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}+4x+4+x^{2}+2x+x^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="215" height="18" />. Then we combine like terms to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/b/81b915a308a1dd9808b46fb1d6123773a5bf86ce.png" class="latex" alt="$3x^{2}+6x+4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="18" /> which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/d/0ed7b46b7d45370f7155c3ef86f7a13fc892cdda.png" class="latex" alt="$15553$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" />. Then we subtract 4 from both sides to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/4/c34bd71b34ce4f6c53d730f7af86d808a2a4765e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x^{2}+6x=15549$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="140" height="18" />. Since all three numbers are divisible by 3, we can divide by 3 to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/e/b2e9b8d2c28151b0dff6bdfacf7e47d880db3005.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}+2x=5183$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="122" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Notice how if we had 1 to both sides, the left side would become a perfect square trinomial: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/48039daa26610335175aedc5ecca51e65b9029bf.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}+2x+1=5184$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="153" height="18" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/d/b5db1a376ce5e2334203f9edb12979fa6f2ffdff.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)^{2}=5184$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="126" height="21" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is too small to be a valid number, the two primes must be odd, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/cccb103143393c0119f5ab61d6d53a852ce70fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1$" width="40" height="13" /> is the number in the middle of them. Conveniently enough, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/a/26a25bea5ccba6c7ffe3a2ba819b182d92ef49ce.png" class="latex" alt="$5184=72^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="17" /> so the two numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5b03f8ead3b3da0bd2cbc1a41d8f01630e638f4.png" class="latex" alt="$73$" width="17" height="12" />. The next prime number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd8b908d15627ea3a118bb38668d865edf722170.png" class="latex" alt="$79$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f4599a15b40d8de3ec8a12ba0956500335f797.png" class="latex" alt="$7+9=16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> so the answer is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/0/cd06f92cbc02074a70521e4ed6b312421988e315.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Trex226
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the two primes be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> being the smaller prime. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce9567c7e977ef5df9565b88b543e3949e91b37f.png" class="latex" alt="$a - b = 2$" width="72" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/76757f5464d4618946e650f4f4dcecf762b7edc9.png" class="latex" alt="$a^3 - b^3 = 31106$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="18" />. Using difference of cubes, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/3/fa356a71f6c7d0c84e570b50136700245878698d.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + ab + b^2 = 15553$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="166" height="18" />. Now, we use the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce9567c7e977ef5df9565b88b543e3949e91b37f.png" class="latex" alt="$a - b = 2$" width="72" height="12" /> to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fcacd314d00a4a0f067f13bef06e8f6dec1c1ffc.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="138" height="18" />. Hence, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/a/dfa4bd261724e63d04a28f218097b5c0b5bd587e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2 + ab + b^2 - (a^2 - 2ab + b^2) = 3ab = 15553 - 4 = 15549\]" width="453" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/6/256cd55aebcd5c473a3f29c546991e7e4ea75513.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab = 5183.\]" width="80" height="13" />
Because we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/40504e40218c9bc8dcf085e0867fbd4c17cb0cf2.png" class="latex" alt="$b = a+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/5/a452b1115aafbd64c2094d9da15db0369b0ae5cf.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = (a+1)^2 - (1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="159" height="21" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5fac295629516e6d39c4c185c202230b7cacac.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)^2 = 5183 + 1 = 5184$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="217" height="21" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/2/1a266f0308852161ec34100b904a701c1991bd8d.png" class="latex" alt="$a+1 = 72$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="15" />. This implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/d/bdd31e97108622bee949dc3f6653edbf22ded9dd.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 71$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="54" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/e/6ae62d7fefea24bb73c0bff2257a918382d4ebe4.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 73$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="14" />, and thus the next biggest prime is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd8b908d15627ea3a118bb38668d865edf722170.png" class="latex" alt="$79$" width="17" height="12" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c44b2e9c2241c77a5277997396fc88a9852d552.png" class="latex" alt="$7 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="130" height="30" />
</p><p>~mathboy100
