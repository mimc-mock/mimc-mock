<p>A four-term sequence is formed by adding each term of a four-term arithmetic sequence of positive integers to the corresponding term of a four-term geometric sequence of positive integers. The first three terms of the resulting four-term sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/d/7fd34ff50f8bba26a08bc96a69e921daa60f5f12.png" class="latex" alt="$91$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What is the fourth term of this sequence?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da4252f8d995c50d6c0569d944566fddd1893f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 190 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 194 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 198 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 202 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 206$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="446" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the arithmetic sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/b/41b9e3ed2a79dc05a08a21dab15e80a944bf297c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,a+d,a+2d,a+3d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="176" height="16" /> and the geometric sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/433f716be42b44b9a6670d6b7e86b2356d11f040.png" class="latex" alt="$b,br,br^2,br^3.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="20" />
</p><p>We are given that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4ff745e3afedd46ee42d9fdfab7e4f8c662b2132.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a+b&amp;=57, \\ a+d+br&amp;=60, \\ a+2d+br^2&amp;=91, \end{align*}" width="142" height="69" />
and we wish to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c73a0f1d4047c97ff3bc1676c453f4a5beac14.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d+br^3.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="103" height="18" />
</p><p>Subtracting the first equation from the second and the second equation from the third, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22a68e52d826ea6f3ce07f90e2cfa48615bf3c5e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} d+b(r-1)&amp;=3, \\ d+br(r-1)&amp;=31. \end{align*}" width="147" height="43" />
Subtract these results, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07fa69c659a238147f3428138ea735b90fd1c9e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b(r-1)^2=28.\]" width="114" height="20" />
</p><p>Note that either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7bfcbe54db6865f55e3f1d84f6775cc84bc6cd.png" class="latex" alt="$b=28$" width="49" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c9996ccaa1ac961155ec7fee5a67094435394022.png" class="latex" alt="$b=7.$" width="44" height="12" /> We proceed with casework:
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/3/1b31f812800209874e29fba3d2a11c49afb4a0c6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=28,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7faafb9892547841db302bcc16d68ba03e1d70.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2,a=29,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/9/319c0588049053a6065fb57e2b2d4ae246803263.png" class="latex" alt="$d=-25.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="16" /> The arithmetic sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/1631176668923790ebcfeef0e2ad5c167c752424.png" class="latex" alt="$29,4,-21,-46,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="17" /> arriving at a contradiction.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/5/f9500800aba6b6b933efe4864c3efc4dd7da8edd.png" class="latex" alt="$b=7,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/d/5adb08ab5f962b7e5256a0de49de67d09504730c.png" class="latex" alt="$r=3,a=50,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/e/94eeac937bb6b6be77c99dac1f0b5d7e9d49f278.png" class="latex" alt="$d=-11.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="16" /> The arithmetic sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/a/8baf2095550bd7775513b22c530c6ef1dc8a8ad5.png" class="latex" alt="$50,39,28,17,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="17" /> and the geometric sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a66d3ec376b19cdefb9b9d346d76bcae324118.png" class="latex" alt="$7,21,63,189.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="17" /> This case is valid.</li></ul>
<p>Therefore, The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13528ac391639118e4bdb86072942e36db3f2bc.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d+br^3=17+189=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 206}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="292" height="30" />
</p><p>~mathboy282 ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Start similarly to Solution 1 and deduce the three equations
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/8/928d07a8a10223073d9a789526e4b677a92cb01e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a+b&amp;=57, \\ a+d+br&amp;=60, \\ a+2d+br^2&amp;=91. \end{align*}" width="142" height="67" />
Then, add the last two equations and take away the first equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/d/44de80aedd97b3eb67b93791354cd24e8770c230.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d+br^2+br-b=94$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="210" height="18" /> We can solve for this in terms of what we want: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eb45ba3896dac2a7bfac166d7aaf7c9d457a2e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d=-br^2-br+b+94$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="224" height="18" />.
</p><p>We're looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adb74cbc3565b80e6831d553a274448f2f2efea.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d+br^3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="18" />. We can substitute our value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/5/c05e574bb2b4ff8d69713613c435fabea7ec0d27.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> in here to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/6/cd667d015a73d0b0356c5523e6238899323eded1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[br^3-br^2-br+b+94=b(r+1)(r-1)(r-1)+94.\]" width="417" height="20" />
Since our sequence only has positive integers we can now check by the answer choices. For each answer choice, we can subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/8/698a90ad1ee1841d9c2a4a80e0a32cbd6b252181.png" class="latex" alt="$94$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> and factor it to see if it has a perfect square factor and at least one other factor and those should differ by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/e/cde2c03870deb1daf64774916aca1e0ed315b41a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{alignat*}{8} \textbf{(A)} \ 190-94&amp;=96&amp;&amp;=2^5\cdot3, \\ \textbf{(B)} \ 194-94&amp;=100&amp;&amp;=2^2\cdot5^2, \\ \textbf{(C)} \ 198-94&amp;=104&amp;&amp;=2^3\cdot13, \\ \textbf{(D)} \ 202-94&amp;=108&amp;&amp;=2^2\cdot3^3, \\ \textbf{(E)} \ 206-94&amp;=112&amp;&amp;=2^4\cdot7. \end{alignat*}" width="228" height="130" />
From this, the only possible answer choices are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e41e3cd48cee2c6866cfbb4386a8e99d3482f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31faa8c684ba5811ac60ea1bd0c855d8ef4d8b16.png" class="latex" alt="$r=3$" width="41" height="12" />. To solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we look back to the given equations above.
</p><p>We are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/0/6a0f31f0ef8012646582dc86b08c3b5f114367a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a+3d+27b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="100" height="16" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> were the answer, then we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> would have to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> would equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Looking at our second equation, if this were the case, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> would also have to be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. However, this contradicts the third equation, as all variables are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, but their sum isn't. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/886bf9fb19e41584786f2ae785229b6c92886938.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 206}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="74" height="30" /> is our answer.
</p>
