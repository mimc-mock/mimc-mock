<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec5740ecfc1939807644a68f8f029be0e8630c0.png" class="latex" alt="$BP=BQ=DR=DS=1-s.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="259" height="18" /> It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/7/4776381a617c17bbeacaf98a7d0da87563b34beb.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BPQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a9321714871dd38d1aad5fbb0e13d76c60b911.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DRS$" width="57" height="13" /> are isosceles right triangles.
</p><p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/b/eabfcd2a5c7bd47d3bc6b16d4a159f4cb8b8df98.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BPQ,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="17" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/6/f86044ac4130b21e4014ab92d04127fdca4943fe.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ=BP\sqrt2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="23" /> or 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b10988bb5f9287bd2e35bdfb531e64731440ca62.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} s &amp;= (1-s)\sqrt2 \\ s &amp;= \sqrt2 - s\sqrt2 \\ \left(\sqrt2+1\right)s &amp;= \sqrt2 \\ s &amp;= \frac{\sqrt2}{\sqrt2 + 1}. \end{align*}" width="189" height="144" />
Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa07139356c10853e011bf157deb6d371ec752f4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s = \frac{\sqrt2}{\sqrt2 + 1}\cdot\frac{\sqrt2 - 1}{\sqrt2 - 1} = \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 2 - \sqrt{2}}.\]" width="294" height="45" />
~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since it is an equilateral convex hexagon, all sides are the same, so we will call the side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb6353211935bf11248eaffbd1c396c46dcc270a.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-x)^2\cdot(1-x)^2 = x^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="184" height="21" />. We can solve this equation which gives us our answer. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5dcd9bca5b34be044ad649ffa0837392091b09.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 1+x^2-2x+1+x^2-2x &amp;= x^2 \\ 2x^2-4x+2 &amp;= x^2 \\ x^2-4x+2 &amp;= 0 \\ \end{align*}" width="243" height="72" />
</p><p>We then use the quadratic formula which gives us:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84e9a62b7bcf1b296e6547d99e3dd97cd4e50636.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} x &amp;= \frac{4\:\pm\sqrt{4^2-4\cdot 1\cdot 2}}{2\cdot 1} \\ &amp;= \frac{4\:\pm\sqrt{8}}{2} \\ &amp;= \frac{4\:\pm2\sqrt{2}}{2} \\ \end{align*}" width="179" height="136" />
</p><p>Then we simplify it by dividing and crossing out 2 which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/8/828d5bed7be2741956ae21c535c816586a8b8a84.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pm{\sqrt2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="21" /> and that gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d0fcd7c03247419cbf2012723d1a84a1db32dcf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }2-{\sqrt2}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="104" height="35" />.
</p><p>~<a href="/wiki/index.php/OrenSH" title="OrenSH">orenbad</a>
