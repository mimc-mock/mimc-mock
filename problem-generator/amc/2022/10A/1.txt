<p>What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/a/7aa51a6ff483afbbf9a1230d0817384db18841f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3+\frac{1}{3+\frac{1}{3+\frac13}}?\]" width="104" height="47" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa67e6e7f9dcb6cdaf3c9dc17d1147a437e7849e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{31}{10}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{49}{15}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{33}{10}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{109}{33}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{15}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="426" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/3/c73cb95f00a723d52e5a01029ad1b686f414e37f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 3+\frac{1}{3+\frac{1}{3+\frac13}} &amp;= 3+\frac{1}{3+\frac{1}{\left(\frac{10}{3}\right)}} \\ &amp;= 3+\frac{1}{3+\frac{3}{10}} \\ &amp;= 3+\frac{1}{\left(\frac{33}{10}\right)} \\ &amp;= 3+\frac{10}{33} \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{109}{33}}. \end{align*}" width="219" height="249" />
~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Continued fractions are expressed as 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/5/2d50b9330c40e3649f2dc73de9af7b1dade3b1db.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \dfrac{[q_0,q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]}{[q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]} \end{align*}" width="131" height="43" />
where
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/6/8f6bc023431782d6b8aff8f4aaf7a5e01e5ec9a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} [q_0,q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]&amp;=q_0[q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]+[q_2,\ldots,q_n]\\ [3]&amp;=3\\ [3,3]&amp;=3(3)+1=10\\ [3,3,3]&amp;=3(10)+3=33\\ [3,3,3,3]&amp;=3(33)+10=109\\ \dfrac{[q_0,q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]}{[q_1,q_2,\ldots,q_n]}&amp;=\dfrac{[3,3,3,3]}{[3,3,3]}\\ &amp;=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{109}{33}} \end{align*}" width="382" height="223" />~lopkiloinm
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>It is well known that for continued fractions of form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/1/de1086b4ddb4b9752a4abbc5bed8ffc465694946.png" class="latex" alt="$n+\frac{1}{n+\cdots}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="93" height="40" />, the denominator <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and numerator <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> are solutions to the Diophantine equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/a/cda7c314c59617760b63a1156081a5eac7741ab4.png" class="latex" alt="$(n^2+4)\left(\frac{y}{2}\right)^2-\left(x-\frac{ny}{2}\right)^2=\pm{1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="266" height="38" />. So for this problem, the denominator <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and numerator <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> are solutions to the Diophantine equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/8/1d8f33423fe238b0a6e76739941a4db2eaf693a8.png" class="latex" alt="$13\left(\frac{y}{2}\right)^2-\left(x-\frac{3y}{2}\right)^2=\pm{1}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="224" height="48" />. That leaves two answers. Since the number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />'s in the continued fraction is odd, we further narrow it down to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/e/b3e52847f58b87379f8b6d931e8faaef6eeaa27d.png" class="latex" alt="$13\left(\frac{y}{2}\right)^2-\left(x-\frac{3y}{2}\right)^2=-1$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="224" height="48" />, which only leaves us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> answer and that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/f/abfba9b040cbf6e0c52fc0890aa9ada58e9432b7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)=(109,33)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="136" height="20" /> which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/2/a6221955b719c643a9d886c03478fb020cca0470.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{109}{33}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="79" height="49" />.
</p><p>~lopkiloinm
</p>
