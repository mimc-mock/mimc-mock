<p>Ted mistakenly wrote <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/4/4446ef7efda16c084ba5c976ee7729f373183908.png" class="latex" alt="$2^m\cdot\sqrt{\frac{1}{4096}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="95" height="45" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/9/5d95b42d9b2ee3a538690f497e19b91b93ed49ec.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot\sqrt[m]{\frac{1}{4096}}.$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="92" height="45" /> What is the sum of all real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> for which these two expressions have the same value?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/4/e14fed811bdf395ed28763cbb895f1db8be2968c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 5 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 6 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 7 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 8 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69c2cbd459c37ee97c02bdebda6d4ce6f683286d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2^m\cdot\sqrt{\frac{1}{4096}} = 2\cdot\sqrt[m]{\frac{1}{4096}}.\]" width="205" height="43" />
Converting everything into powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1d1407aac4125d6c453a365eb173d96ebb48e2.png" class="latex" alt="$2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/7598fbe6ca643fe6ca275252661db555dd392544.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 2^m\cdot(2^{-12})^{\frac12} &amp;= 2\cdot (2^{-12})^{\frac1m} \\ 2^{m-6} &amp;= 2^{1-\frac{12}{m}} \\ m-6 &amp;= 1-\frac{12}{m}. \end{align*}" width="199" height="96" />
We multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, then rearrange as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/7/ba7288683f6d7ca5d6bd5e4ca6374dcd51a462c3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[m^2-7m+12=0.\]" width="147" height="17" />
By Vieta's Formulas, the sum of such values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/0892d2010fb1ad517d3c3509c62b65a0b9e08791.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 7}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="62" height="30" />
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/4/904f27995d33e0d39f43ec959e7cb100516e9396.png" class="latex" alt="$m=3$" width="48" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/500fb9788ed8553a319dd49cb6262faebcacc88b.png" class="latex" alt="$m=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" /> from the quadratic equation above.
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p><p>~KingRavi
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> can only be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f21c6bf272168826f18fdac932fe88dfe83450.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\frac{1}{4096}}=\frac{1}{64}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="106" height="45" />. Testing out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, we see that only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> work. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/a/deac5929cb9928ade8a6ede825419b202ff2e104.png" class="latex" alt="$3+4=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="122" height="30" />.
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
