<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/b/f2b686e282112ee7338ebaf3427727ac8141c10f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="15" height="13" /> be the region in the complex plane consisting of all complex numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> that can be written as the sum of complex numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea31e5c5613f025b5be8aca425d318168491d89.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/29535bc04682b0b477c682ee52dded5983bc41cb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea31e5c5613f025b5be8aca425d318168491d89.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> lies on the segment with endpoints <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/0/2e0822331c14895164ac6d5f8c3bbf1c88659a82.png" class="latex" alt="$4i$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="14" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/29535bc04682b0b477c682ee52dded5983bc41cb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> has magnitude at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. What integer is closest to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/b/f2b686e282112ee7338ebaf3427727ac8141c10f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="15" height="13" />?  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7d0c4782b6519d91646ff86a9bca64e52160d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 13 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 14 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 15 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 16 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 17$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="401" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b5865dd020cde786c1c7723b1e0e1b00df3a402f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(250); import TrigMacros; rr_cartesian_axes(-2,6,-2,6,complexplane=true, usegrid = true); Label f; f.p=fontsize(6);  xaxis(-1,5,Ticks(f, 1.0));  yaxis(-1,5,Ticks(f, 1.0)); dot((3,0)); dot((0,4)); draw((0,4)--(3,0), blue); draw((0.8, 4.6)..(-.6,4.8)..(-.8, 3.4),red); draw((-.8, 3.4)--(2.2, -0.6), red); draw((2.2, -0.6)..(3.6,-0.8)..(3.8,0.6), red); draw((0.8, 4.6)--(3.8,0.6),red); draw((0.8, 4.6)--(-.8, 3.4),red+dashed); draw((2.2, -0.6)--(3.8,0.6),red+ dashed);  draw((3,0)--(3,-1),Arrow); label(&quot;1&quot;,(3,0)--(3,-1),E); draw((0,4)--(-.6,4.8),Arrow); label(&quot;1&quot;,(0,4)--(-.6,4.8),SW); draw((1.5,2)--(2.3,2.6),Arrow); label(&quot;1&quot;,(1.5,2)--(2.3,2.6),SE); [/asy]" width="418" height="408" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is a complex number and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/9/d89ca41a7488a1fa1812ed9802275c78d2c7a2b1.png" class="latex" alt="$z = a + bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="79" height="14" />, then the magnitude (length) of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/2/bc241e5b2c6a386e1177b9d8349a2dead9cb37ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="18" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea31e5c5613f025b5be8aca425d318168491d89.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> has a magnitude of 5. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/29535bc04682b0b477c682ee52dded5983bc41cb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> has a magnitude of at most one, that means for each point on the segment given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea31e5c5613f025b5be8aca425d318168491d89.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />, the bounds of the region <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/b/f2b686e282112ee7338ebaf3427727ac8141c10f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="15" height="13" /> could be at most 1 away. Alone the line, excluding the endpoints, a rectangle with a width of 2 and a length of 5, the magnitude, would be formed. At the endpoints, two semicircles will be formed with a radius of 1 for a total area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec03f16c14aafa2ba31045b9124c26fddbf8859.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi \approx 3$" width="44" height="12" />.
Therefore, the total area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/3/053dd1d59ee03f64f61766350ce7ac25b20ef9b0.png" class="latex" alt="$5(2) + \pi \approx 10 + 3 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 13}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="230" height="30" />.
</p><p>~juicefruit
</p>
