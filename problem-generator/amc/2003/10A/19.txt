<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Semicircle" title="Semicircle">semicircle</a> of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Diameter" title="Diameter">diameter</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> sits at the top of a semicircle of diameter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, as shown. The shaded <a href="/wiki/index.php/Area" title="Area">area</a> inside the smaller semicircle and outside the larger semicircle is called a <i>lune</i>. Determine the area of this lune. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/8/ee832dfa1925af1fe40d6af62243894f0614e222.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(150); defaultpen(fontsize(8)); pair A=(-2,0), B=(2,0); filldraw(Arc((0,sqrt(3)),1,0,180)--cycle,mediumgray); filldraw(Arc((0,0),2,0,180)--cycle,white); draw(2*expi(2*pi/6)--2*expi(4*pi/6));  label(&quot;1&quot;,(0,sqrt(3)),(0,-1)); label(&quot;2&quot;,(0,0),(0,-1)); [/asy]" width="252" height="185" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/e/5ae901a30a3a90c0797cce16c631c5fda5d78d5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{1}{6}\pi-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}-\frac{1}{12}\pi\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}-\frac{1}{24}\pi\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}+\frac{1}{24}\pi\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}+\frac{1}{12}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="705" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7dc6582f0dd4a6d448e272a4a65408d00646000.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(150); defaultpen(fontsize(8)); pair A=(-2,0), B=(2,0); filldraw(Arc((0,sqrt(3)),1,0,180)--cycle,mediumgray); fill(Arc((0,0),2,0,180)--cycle,white); draw(Arc((0,0),2,0,180)--cycle); draw((0,0)--2*expi(2*pi/6)--2*expi(2*pi/6*2)--(0,0));  label(&quot;A&quot;,(0,2),(0,4)); label(&quot;B&quot;,(0,2),(0,-1)); label(&quot;C&quot;,(0,sqrt(3)/2),(0,2)); label(&quot;1&quot;,(-0.5,sqrt(3)/2),(-1,0)); label(&quot;1&quot;,(0.5,sqrt(3)/2),(1,0)); [/asy]" width="252" height="172" />
</p><p><br />
The shaded area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/7/5974998abb6495b7e7cb9c32143687050121fe86.png" class="latex" alt="$[A]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="21" height="18" /> is equal to the area of the smaller semicircle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae1d8ff00ee5641906bcafa9a9ec1e98cb41498a.png" class="latex" alt="$[A+B]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="58" height="18" /> minus the area of a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Sector" title="Sector">sector</a> of the larger circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d77dc2c402c80073c4eb779f64c4b3c5fbc1d9c1.png" class="latex" alt="$[B+C]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="59" height="18" /> plus the area of a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangle</a> formed by two <a href="/wiki/index.php/Radius" title="Radius"> radii</a> of the larger semicircle and the diameter of the smaller semicircle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/b/8eb0d5cccb9df6a659750553ad358f3f7c446956.png" class="latex" alt="$[C]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="22" height="18" />.
</p><p>The area of the smaller semicircle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/1/2b1643c5c18b6fa64bbd187553753154798d61c5.png" class="latex" alt="$[A+B] = \frac{1}{2}\pi\cdot\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{8}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="216" height="46" />. 
</p><p>Since the radius of the larger semicircle is equal to the diameter of the smaller half circle, the triangle is an equilateral triangle and the sector measures <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p>The area of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> sector of the larger semicircle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/8/bc879f9abc0c3162da87a9924837e849fe35b677.png" class="latex" alt="$[B+C] = \frac{60}{360}\pi\cdot\left(\frac{2}{2}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{6}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="235" height="46" />. 
</p><p>The area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/3/1e3b9d2aae4c54ac73fe5f02af335976a89ee9db.png" class="latex" alt="$[C] = \frac{1^{2}\sqrt{3}}{4}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="144" height="41" />.
</p><p>So the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/851075ddcae6c08caa75052db7fd1cbfec7b9ca9.png" class="latex" alt="$[A] = [A+B]-[B+C]+[C] = \left(\frac{1}{8}\pi\right)-\left(\frac{1}{6}\pi\right)+\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\right)=\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}-\frac{1}{24}\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="616" height="57" />. We have thus solved the problem.
</p>
