<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the solutions of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/9/6194e5f19233e921a6f0cbb5de515bbfc0d46c60.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^{2}+3x-5=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="132" height="16" />. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/8/9d8af9b498a9fb57b84e286b1ec324981ae70939.png" class="latex" alt="$(d-1)(e-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/4/834b170c4716766d1f8785b20bac88f8a7ea9150.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } -\frac{5}{2}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 0\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 3\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 5\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="368" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Using factoring: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/9/6194e5f19233e921a6f0cbb5de515bbfc0d46c60.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^{2}+3x-5=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="132" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/8/2e816e31114bfe52cce0bf2dc361ef7d6b0e5da6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2x+5)(x-1)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="153" height="18" />
</p><p>Daddy
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17a14c1b6b680f212dcce7c206bb973956b308f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = -\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> 
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/3/303068584cec18d1ced7af8ff82c262ec91bac48.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43dd3afdc8da794a257ed10f39bcbf01ac0ad8f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(-\frac{5}{2}-1\right)(1-1)=\left(-\frac{7}{2}\right)(0)=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 0}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="323" height="43" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We can use the sum and product of a quadratic (a.k.a Vieta):
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/b/03b879da222f2ecfadef0952c652e80dd87a90a9.png" class="latex" alt="$(d-1)(e-1)=de-(d+e)+1 \implies\text{product}-\text{sum}+1 \implies \dfrac{c}{a}-\left(-\dfrac{b}{a}\right)+1 \implies \dfrac{b+c}{a}+1= \dfrac{5}{-5}+1=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 0}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="901" height="43" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>By inspection, we quickly note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> is a solution to the equation, therefore the answer is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/2/d5274ad034bcd9d74d445c9c7bd86247902d5ef4.png" class="latex" alt="$(d-1)(e-1)=(1-1)(e-1)=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 0}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="319" height="29" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>The form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/8/9d8af9b498a9fb57b84e286b1ec324981ae70939.png" class="latex" alt="$(d-1)(e-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> resembles the factored form for the quadratic, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c77f814c63df809f9828664bb0f381a385ff42a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-d)(x-e)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" /> based on the information given. Note putting in 1 for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in the that quadratic immediately yields the desired expression. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b218861c02a3f6438a9ebb09cb27dfb3fa12f80d.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1)^2+3(1)-5 = \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 0}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="205" height="32" />
</p>
