<p>How many non-<a href="/wiki/index.php/Congruent_(geometry)" title="Congruent (geometry)">congruent</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangles</a> with <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perimeter" title="Perimeter">perimeter</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> have <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integer</a> side lengths? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/d/0ad069be59a1dd259d51051fa77f3b9a9dd3b8bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 1\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 2\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 3\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 4\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle_inequality" class="mw-redirect" title="Triangle inequality">triangle inequality</a>, no side may have a length greater than the semiperimeter, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/2/202ee6e52c56514e96fd7cb769f339f7d6a9eed7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot7=3.5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="82" height="37" />. 
</p><p>Since all sides must be integers, the largest possible length of a side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Therefore, all such triangles must have all sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/6/3a62f5e8b629ef64f45bf2913a0e1f3051d14990.png" class="latex" alt="$2+2+2=6&lt;7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="138" height="14" />, at least one side must have a length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, the remaining two sides have a combined length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/7/fd711fa530450bc7d279233a2d718766536fc0f7.png" class="latex" alt="$7-3=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />. So, the remaining sides must be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Therefore, the number of triangles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/8/fb8184dad25e7323b7229f452a12c5a00db2a0c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="53" height="29" />. 
</p>
