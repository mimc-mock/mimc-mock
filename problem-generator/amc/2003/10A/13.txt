<p>The sum of three numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. The first is four times the sum of the other two. The second is seven times the third. What is the product of all three? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/a/32a154d350c4aeaa17d1d68fcbca91957e0ebc65.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 28\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 40\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 100\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 400\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 800$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="414" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the numbers be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> in that order. The given tells us that 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/3/7a3c7259bf897aaa8d70fe3fc3a0ef46eb9bd955.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*}y&amp;=&amp;7z\\ x&amp;=&amp;4(y+z)=4(7z+z)=4(8z)=32z\\ x+y+z&amp;=&amp;32z+7z+z=40z=20\\ z&amp;=&amp;\frac{20}{40}=\frac{1}{2}\\ y&amp;=&amp;7z=7\cdot\frac{1}{2}=\frac{7}{2}\\ x&amp;=&amp;32z=32\cdot\frac{1}{2}=16 \end{eqnarray*}" width="398" height="195" />
</p><p>Therefore, the product of all three numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/a/8aa65506bd04a85817d2d2cf2dad6f86c337af58.png" class="latex" alt="$xyz=16\cdot\frac{7}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}=28 \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 28}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="258" height="37" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, we can set up the system in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equation" title="Equation">equation</a> form:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fadc3a5f0981faf72eef12e555c48cd64cf770a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*}1x+1y+1z&amp;=&amp;20\\ 1x-4y-4z&amp;=&amp;0\\ 0x+1y-7z&amp;=&amp;0\\ \end{eqnarray*}" width="165" height="66" />
</p><p>Or, in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Matrix" title="Matrix">matrix</a> form
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09c306099469a000a43c917b2df90b8fb89ecd10.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{bmatrix} 1 &amp; 1 &amp; 1 \\ 1 &amp; -4 &amp; -4 \\ 0 &amp; 1 &amp; -7 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \\ \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} 20 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$" style="vertical-align: -27px" width="210" height="64" />
</p><p>To solve this matrix equation, we can rearrange it thus:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b138ec1fcbe221305a4dd1d29b490d1bb4c09b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 &amp; 1 &amp; 1 \\ 1 &amp; -4 &amp; -4 \\ 0 &amp; 1 &amp; -7 \end{bmatrix} ^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 20 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$" style="vertical-align: -27px" width="228" height="67" />
</p><p>Solving this matrix equation by using <a href="/wiki/index.php?title=Inverse_matrices&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Inverse matrices (page does not exist)">inverse matrices</a> and <a href="/wiki/index.php?title=Matrix_multiplication&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Matrix multiplication (page does not exist)">matrix multiplication</a> yields
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a14ef765af83184da001ba68515ea35046f59223.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{7}{2} \\ 16 \\ \end{bmatrix}$" style="vertical-align: -27px" width="95" height="64" />
</p><p>Which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2ff20448834be77dc325234211669d2b44d35aa.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3d0dd078cd22a77879cb9fd52d6450b66322a0b.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/c/7ec4fd697488707ede081f47f633f062efe506e3.png" class="latex" alt="$z = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b89fda2ca20a588e5d0642b379ef6cdfbaaa7882.png" class="latex" alt="$xyz = \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{7}{2}\cdot16 = 28 \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 28}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="258" height="37" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(Simpler)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28Simpler.29">Solution 3 (Simpler)</span></h3>
<p>Let's denote the 3rd number as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, the 2nd as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d8dd5f375b5f3874868d24a8820fe854a68a28.png" class="latex" alt="$7x$" width="19" height="12" />, and the 1st as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f113f19ab069acddad349d417214e9f8a440430.png" class="latex" alt="$4(7x+x)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="77" height="20" /> according to the information given in the problem. We know that all three numbers add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/3/d23ad8f36c7ca203f3a23067d715c81c31ddd713.png" class="latex" alt="$4(8x)+7x+x = 20$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="161" height="20" />. Solving the equation we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2ff20448834be77dc325234211669d2b44d35aa.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. Then substitute this value of x to solve for the other two numbers. Lastly, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> as the product of all three numbers.
</p>
