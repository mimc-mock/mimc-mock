<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6c0d9a03514816a93da4ddcfdd49754d9ecdc4d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle GHC = \angle AHB$" width="139" height="13" /> (Vertical angles are equal).
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/531d673f7fe15093acf12ff2d5f82ef4d7937e3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle F = \angle B$" width="78" height="12" /> (Both are 90 degrees).
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/c/d3c9266d3992ca5170591b3b1f913ada7eb0aed3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BHA = \angle HAD$" width="140" height="13" /> (Alt. Interior Angles are congruent).
</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6eba99487ddba4629417ae013875ea1fa284cc17.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GFA$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ed19718d84ea66bd08cd0ce70a5a9350d58c6c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABH$" width="61" height="13" /> are similar.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/7/f77253e16cecb532de613ecb18ebd0833d018c2b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GCH$" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/912c83ad4002b9a578b4b613d9717020e276cad8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GEA$" width="58" height="13" /> are also similar.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31cd391bebc36897a8c10ea78e5d584980b049.png" class="latex" alt="$DA$" width="29" height="13" /> is 9, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d9456e0361813641f4ce80980839e9038fe8f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$EA$" width="28" height="13" /> must equal 5. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/e/20e8e26d580012b86d8fe55e70e5740908c35af0.png" class="latex" alt="$CH$" width="31" height="12" /> must equal 3. 
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/9229ff62414e83677f61609943cb436b442a2c92.png" class="latex" alt="$GCH$" width="45" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b3d6163256662c5193fef68e3ba0d0114f7480.png" class="latex" alt="$GEA$" width="42" height="13" /> are similar, the ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cd879fc56725b33eb78f2df725ea5e39b46a8f3.png" class="latex" alt="$CH\; =\; 3$" width="73" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7b2c6c565be657443e2cb4415f5139cb5b8896f.png" class="latex" alt="$EA\; =\; 5$" width="71" height="13" />, must also hold true for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/7/0a7a248cfd02095e2a009f811d5732a9bea9d595.png" class="latex" alt="$GH$" width="31" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/6/06695cb2d063998a9cf5c454b72813e6c3d7e735.png" class="latex" alt="$HA$" width="30" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/6/4a6ed872c574861a952e30c24d233d3cd0bb6e65.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{GH}{GA} = \frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/6/06695cb2d063998a9cf5c454b72813e6c3d7e735.png" class="latex" alt="$HA$" width="30" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2297526ede0e6c616802ca72adf69c82518f25.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35ebcd881abc07ce36dce8a81c871f8445a3ea0f.png" class="latex" alt="$GA$" width="28" height="13" />. By Pythagorean theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4240077e05cccbe77f54bc3e7b39014a0e739202.png" class="latex" alt="$(HA)^2\;  =\; (HB)^2\; +\; (BA)^2\;...\;HA=10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="318" height="19" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c9608c73c77e8992639f951cdcac8436860907d2.png" class="latex" alt="$HA\: =\: 10 =\: \frac{2}{5}*(GA)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="180" height="37" />.

</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afdca344ab4ca4d0cd91ae589a0688ae500576bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{GA}{HA}\: =\: \frac{GF}{BA}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="38" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c122dff63004f77f7885a14330299ef5450f75b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{10}\: =\: \frac{GF}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="38" />.
</p><p>Therefore   <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb293d96630393b3accc460336c47f376e0837ad.png" class="latex" alt="$GF= \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="115" height="29" />.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>

</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a rectangle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2ac8344993f7b926952649a2b78c1c888ee6f26.png" class="latex" alt="$GF \perp AF$" width="80" height="13" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/2/f4247a2ef7329a66a7605c157966282952da0abe.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle GFE = \angle CDE = \angle ABC = 90^\circ$" width="265" height="13" />.

Additionally, now note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/b/07b46bb097181ac5df12784ab5f3bb741e63a988.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GAF\sim AHB,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="17" /> which is pretty obvious from insight, but can be proven by AA with extending <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bfc6159e268a0f5d736eca73477c99a13209da9.png" class="latex" alt="$BH$" width="31" height="12" /> to meet <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/8/de88f0fd9024190c2c0216c06b4c36c3c695dcd5.png" class="latex" alt="$GF.$" width="30" height="12" /> From this new pair of similar triangles, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a466a715160180d88e4e6a7f29b42ef8e8b9fc9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x}{8}=\dfrac{y+9}{6}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" /> Therefore, we have by combining those two equations, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/6/2762eb023046cb409678d9f78421e859137695fa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{y+9}{6}=\dfrac{y+4}{4}.\]" width="115" height="38" /> Solving, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/a/c6ae2eeb07d4803104f469cbe2785b3d01198f69.png" class="latex" alt="$y=6,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="16" /> and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/d/ead51c3055797196408c7f883297a276f098369d.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="97" height="29" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Since there are only lines, you can resort to coordinate bashing. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5c3b3b238780ecb8af07163d8379be543fe405.png" class="latex" alt="$FD=k$" width="63" height="12" />. Three lines, line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/857df29da7da4f8eadf2d2481dc811df53738580.png" class="latex" alt="$GF$" width="28" height="12" />, line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e195ed4818e368e25b7cb6e335e24d0613ba50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$GE$" width="28" height="12" />, and line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35ebcd881abc07ce36dce8a81c871f8445a3ea0f.png" class="latex" alt="$GA$" width="28" height="13" />, intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. Our goal is to find the y-coordinate of that intersection point. 
</p><p>Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/857df29da7da4f8eadf2d2481dc811df53738580.png" class="latex" alt="$GF$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p>Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e195ed4818e368e25b7cb6e335e24d0613ba50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$GE$" width="28" height="12" /> passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/5/70565757eea48ed4fc3796fd052d50c8b7e421f7.png" class="latex" alt="$(k+4, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/5/3250fdc204f92a0439c38f85d415675b8820fe99.png" class="latex" alt="$(k, 8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" />. Therefore the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> and the line is 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/8/7d80de3add23c96b984aa13001a60349b885a980.png" class="latex" alt="$y-0=-2(x-k-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="175" height="18" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/e/8fe43e072d473ef00b152f34b45534686f39ea5b.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-2x+2k+8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="139" height="16" />
</p><p>Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35ebcd881abc07ce36dce8a81c871f8445a3ea0f.png" class="latex" alt="$GA$" width="28" height="13" /> passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/1/4c1238dfacd9b0354b118795d3151f4787ec4677.png" class="latex" alt="$(k+9, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/8/b082bda75591f76b007d62c552dff7acacdc1720.png" class="latex" alt="$(k+3, 8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" />. Therefore the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/4/dc4f7a6ab0f90718147f0dd9bd4d5d5ada888778.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> and the line is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/6/e76beccf9a676a8ba42075b2063233641248963c.png" class="latex" alt="$y-0=-\frac{4}{3}(x-k-9)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="179" height="37" /> which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/8/f08926752c461bbe01d6b54bab93d1c2c0df990a.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-\frac{4}{3}x+\frac{4}{3}k+12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="156" height="37" />
</p><p>We solve the system of equations with these three lines. First we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bb867a25aab3d4d336bf7b1780ce3f926b01046.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2k+8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/b/83bdd25fee223453f628eb01ae077ac9bbc2c77a.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{4}{3}k+12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />
</p><p>Next, we solve for k. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/9/a295acb9662ee1bae4bb3aa92a1f42b2cfd83f10.png" class="latex" alt="$k=6$" width="43" height="12" /> Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a0d60b0dde6e681ff17c9c4a01f39047f68eee9.png" class="latex" alt="$y=20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />. The y-coordinate of this intersection point is indeed our answer. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/9/4195c25a21947fbfed871d93370a75a8e30b6d99.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />
~superagh
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_6_(simple_coordinates)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6_.28simple_coordinates.29">Solution 6 (simple coordinates)</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be the origin of our coordinate system. Now line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35ebcd881abc07ce36dce8a81c871f8445a3ea0f.png" class="latex" alt="$GA$" width="28" height="13" /> has equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae26db721ab74b6a6955d2f4996859f6467657e7.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{4}{3}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="37" />. We can use point-slope form to find the equation for line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e195ed4818e368e25b7cb6e335e24d0613ba50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$GE$" width="28" height="12" />. First, we know that its slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" />, and we know that it passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/4/c34ebd5896e99ba7cb847c9a215d572ab7833584.png" class="latex" alt="$E=(-5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" />, so line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e195ed4818e368e25b7cb6e335e24d0613ba50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$GE$" width="28" height="12" /> has equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47f8442db953e9dfc9c0924a1874212a971718.png" class="latex" alt="$-2(x+5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />. Solving for the intersection by letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8ef78096365f7f77659f60afddf27fa4cf45c5e.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{4}{3}x=-2(x+5)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="139" height="37" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/6933ed9cd99b894259239bb056f91d616bf7d015.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" />. Plugging this into our equation for line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35ebcd881abc07ce36dce8a81c871f8445a3ea0f.png" class="latex" alt="$GA$" width="28" height="13" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/785ad5f77773e597df49eb0f1d442a257fd3b92d.png" class="latex" alt="$G=(-15,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb293d96630393b3accc460336c47f376e0837ad.png" class="latex" alt="$GF= \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="115" height="29" />
~chrisdiamond10
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_7_(system_of_equations_through_angle_similarity)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_7_.28system_of_equations_through_angle_similarity.29">Solution 7 (system of equations through angle similarity)</span></h3>
<p>First, using given information, we can find the values of some line segments in the figure. We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89ab3b5e1928564ffbd41f8f825d452c02810b18.png" class="latex" alt="$HA = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" /> (through Pythagorean Theorem), <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa65f79dd7432ce586d80caacbdb155cd2dcdcd7.png" class="latex" alt="$CH = 3$" width="63" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/5/795d4a9bced93c76133a8c3163c939be8ad8a89f.png" class="latex" alt="$EA = 5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="14" />. 
Let Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da41ffb194e726211d856640ece73eaf3b26f5f1.png" class="latex" alt="$FD = x$" width="64" height="12" /> and let Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/8/04867353a8cff9f73f601adf4d52d7ad659376d5.png" class="latex" alt="$FG = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" />. 
We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cd7e2ca7326e61a3e662f96719cbecf07ee37aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle FGE \sim \triangle CDE$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" /> through some angle chasing (they both have a right angle, and they both share angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/8/2e8dd80cb1927f03ef77da74c59e77acf710c815.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CED$" width="56" height="12" />. Using this information, we can write the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd4c79430655986d07823b45ad3400f0d321b2af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{8} = \frac{4+x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="85" height="42" />. Through simplifying this equation, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/9146d4409d5843a0261f9ef7a8863aba645ca0fa.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2x+8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="17" />. Let point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> be the point on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3951902c11a1cd39a091ea15f0f06cbc79095de4.png" class="latex" alt="$FG$" width="28" height="12" /> so that lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/d/a8d37413bae1973358eebff164711f292d16be56.png" class="latex" alt="$CI$" width="24" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3951902c11a1cd39a091ea15f0f06cbc79095de4.png" class="latex" alt="$FG$" width="28" height="12" /> are perpendicular, and we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/1/361968e8c2f02337a8471a14cc82d0f3dbecf6ef.png" class="latex" alt="$GI = 2x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="69" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc22aaf0b63d42bcc63c82dbc6c324443effd81.png" class="latex" alt="$FI =8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" />. Doing some more angle chasing, we can find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/6816874dd5c20ca8654e98a90d69be51bc88234b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GIH \sim \triangle GFA$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="18" />, as they both share <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/c/7bc63a20b323b38f8d79b894b7307eff4e8f3c4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle FGH$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="60" height="14" /> and they both have a right angle.
</p><p>With this information, we can write the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/f/cbf10caa2ad840b3cfd087081201e43e26ef658d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+3}{y-8} = \frac{x+9}{y}.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="123" height="42" /> 
Simplifying this equation we get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d7717cacff13ecd58faf5f09bc897c470e39cf79.png" class="latex" alt="$-8x+6y-72 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="17" />.
Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/9146d4409d5843a0261f9ef7a8863aba645ca0fa.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2x+8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="17" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/9948cf5ef98657858b365168e79c88d5dfc878ef.png" class="latex" alt="$6y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="16" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/c/a9cf542729f59f3becc3c1913ae1e303c1d4265d.png" class="latex" alt="$4x-24 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd92d02476c3434bf645bf5a3bc249eca83d3c16.png" class="latex" alt="$x=6$" width="43" height="12" />.
Lastly, to find the value of y, which is the value of Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3951902c11a1cd39a091ea15f0f06cbc79095de4.png" class="latex" alt="$FG$" width="28" height="12" />, our desired value, we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/9146d4409d5843a0261f9ef7a8863aba645ca0fa.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2x+8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="17" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142d5b68af4284182cb041ef34cef111c4f342f2.png" class="latex" alt="$2(6)+8$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="65" height="20" />, which, finally, we get our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, so therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb293d96630393b3accc460336c47f376e0837ad.png" class="latex" alt="$GF= \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="115" height="29" />
</p><p>~Darth_Cadet
