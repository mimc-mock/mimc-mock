<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the largest integer that is the product of exactly 3 distinct prime numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67f94726da9d0b7e3764214a5dc58428e97b260.png" class="latex" alt="$10d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are single digits. What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48d16706e4c9588c1010d4ef3da351680e88cc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 12\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 15\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 18\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 21\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="388" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since you want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> to be the largest number possible, you will want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67f94726da9d0b7e3764214a5dc58428e97b260.png" class="latex" alt="$10d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> to be as large as possible. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/2/0d2ebc78ee3a2e67ae89407f1312323d25bc80ea.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 7$" width="42" height="12" />.Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> cannot be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/e/f7e4f9ec535a7ce345aac4357e76d1cd14dc4c9e.png" class="latex" alt="$10(7)+5 = 75$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" /> which is not prime. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/6/4a66332dcde07a7f80f49ae1b54b8970bbaf0130.png" class="latex" alt="$e = 3$" width="41" height="12" />.<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bffe5410d2f94ee809e3a0e6de4cf5554dacb9c.png" class="latex" alt="$~~~$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="18" height="2" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/4/5f47d3669ed07fdf12578fa588630552e6f0d88d.png" class="latex" alt="$d \cdot e \cdot (10d+e) = 7 \cdot 3 \cdot 73 = 1533$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="263" height="18" />.
So, the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37ec009c9f28d76c329dfe816a6cd311c3becc6e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+5+3+3=12 \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="237" height="29" /> ~ MathGenius_
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is a single digit prime number, the set of possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/2/372d102bfeb1df0d6036974efaf6a767573c3d68.png" class="latex" alt="$\{2,3,5,7\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> is a single digit prime number and is the units digit of the prime number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67f94726da9d0b7e3764214a5dc58428e97b260.png" class="latex" alt="$10d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />, the set of possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/f/f5f4c90d3098c50e7da6e7ce0b8e408061b80553.png" class="latex" alt="$\{3,7\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Using these values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" />, the set of possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67f94726da9d0b7e3764214a5dc58428e97b260.png" class="latex" alt="$10d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/8/8280551c46b824664d15532e7e9e7cf774a0df10.png" class="latex" alt="$\{23,27,33,37,53,57,73,77\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="219" height="18" />
</p><p>Out of this set, the prime values are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/415a198d93105d83766ecbb41c2a8db74b2711cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\{23,37,53,73\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/7/0b7500c5820272a83c11e834826668491e682471.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot3\cdot23=138$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33dd114b0ca7c7e4d76bda397b3739ac93d76d01.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot7\cdot37=777$" width="114" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/3/4e352c84585d332742e203a29030f2e48f4b3203.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot3\cdot53=795$" width="113" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/6/736ca8c1fc9d44433a091ab64133f9e6936aabea.png" class="latex" alt="$7\cdot3\cdot73=1533$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="122" height="13" /> 
</p><p>The largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/e/0ee5516af65d26d5ae4190acaac8283634d0d5c1.png" class="latex" alt="$1533$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. 
</p><p>So, the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37ec009c9f28d76c329dfe816a6cd311c3becc6e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+5+3+3=12 \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="237" height="29" />
</p>
