<p>The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Polygon" title="Polygon">polygon</a> enclosed by the solid lines in the figure consists of 4 <a href="/wiki/index.php/Congruent" title="Congruent">congruent</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Square_(geometry)" title="Square (geometry)"> squares</a> joined <a href="/wiki/index.php/Edge" title="Edge">edge</a>-to-edge. One more congruent square is attached to an edge at one of the nine positions indicated. How many of the nine resulting polygons can be folded to form a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Cube_(geometry)" title="Cube (geometry)"> cube</a> with one face missing?
