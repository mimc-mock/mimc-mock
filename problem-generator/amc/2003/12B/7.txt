<p>Penniless Pete's piggy bank has no pennies in it, but it has 100 coins, all nickels,dimes, and quarters, whose total value is &#36;8.35. It does not necessarily contain coins of all three types. What is the difference between the largest and smallest number of dimes that could be in the bank?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/b/40b4a975e4ba290bccfa327743bbfd56cf2577f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A) } 0 \qquad \text {(B) } 13 \qquad \text {(C) } 37 \qquad \text {(D) } 64 \qquad \text {(E) } 83$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> is the number of nickels, dimes, and quarters, respectively, we can set up two equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092d5d4a43045893d55007f55221c8ed84eb2059.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(1)\ 5a+10b+25c=835\ \ \ \ (2)\ a+b+c=100\]" width="365" height="18" />
</p><p>Eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> by subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/0/4a01c065de1e2fd037e05035bf977a230832831e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)/5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f6e54958ef445a652c54ff6f69dee4f521ae06.png" class="latex" alt="$b+4c=67$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" />. Of the integer solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10fe40b98e74737c70752abaa29332bc3369bfd8.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> to this equation, the number of dimes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is least in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/4/0640dba14ebb5384e28cdaf2f5340fc96660d740.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,16)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> and greatest in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/4/d542e57d1913d8e744c1395a6f288616dd9e9b26.png" class="latex" alt="$(67,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />, yielding a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/b/57bc95a119ae71817e330365b8867d93e0a68d17.png" class="latex" alt="$67-3=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 64}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="138" height="28" />.
</p>
