<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Set" title="Set">set</a> of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Permutation" title="Permutation">permutations</a> of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Sequence" title="Sequence">sequence</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8480bbc71e3fd12e3f8b749c49c06897ee7bb0.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,4,5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> for which the first term is not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. A permutation is chosen randomly from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Probability" title="Probability">probability</a> that the second term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, in lowest terms, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0c22354f6f8870c3a024305aa3afed00b26bcf5.png" class="latex" alt="$a/b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/5/82518727efcdf5ca8126ab325c9f00ef4d5b5963.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 5 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 6 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 11 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 16 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 19$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="369" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> choices for the first element of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />, and for each of these choices there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da866442e69c040daf44260b4728d37e98de313.png" class="latex" alt="$4!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="13" /> ways to arrange the remaining elements. If the second element must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, then there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> choices for the first element and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/6859713adc973d3fbe97fafef9ce829757e54b12.png" class="latex" alt="$3!$" width="12" height="13" /> ways to arrange the remaining elements. Hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/2016046760b7d0b556c50bda224d532fd516181a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3 \cdot 3!}{4 \cdot 4!} = \frac {18}{96} = \frac{3}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="131" height="38" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37a1a63d5144af2c8077a67f4e2793597c98d66.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=19 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="135" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>There is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/1/d81b2c4e0349cfa874901eb10c0f2d9cf8d0ca74.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> chance that the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is the second term. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the chance that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> will be the second term. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c0714ce5edcd065115fa3a9463f7078e943705e9.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are in similar situations as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, this becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/c/48c76d9e35cb307a8bb2595bd18df90be61e8fce.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {1}{4} + 4x = 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="87" height="37" /> 
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we find it equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a78d7a1c3a1e0194cdee2bdd036868823d4a9e1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {3}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/3/6e39d4ababdf5c70c40871deb0e7cd4665929987.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 16 = 19 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />
</p>

</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
