The centers of the small semicircles divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> into four line segments of equal length, as shown. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/3/6f30c4091f2ff7d5233d20f8213489e5903af3b6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; unitsize(14mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); dashed=linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;); dotfactor=3; pair A=(-2,0), B=(2,0); fill(Arc((0,0),2,0,180)--cycle,mediumgray); fill(Arc((-1,0),1,0,180)--cycle,white); fill(Arc((0,0),1,0,180)--cycle,white); fill(Arc((1,0),1,0,180)--cycle,white); draw(Arc((-1,0),1,60,180)); draw(Arc((0,0),1,0,60),dashed); draw(Arc((0,0),1,60,120)); draw(Arc((0,0),1,120,180),dashed); draw(Arc((1,0),1,0,120)); draw(Arc((0,0),2,0,180)--cycle); dot((0,0)); dot((-1,0)); dot((1,0)); draw((-2,-0.1)--(-2,-0.3),gray); draw((-1,-0.1)--(-1,-0.3),gray); draw((1,-0.1)--(1,-0.3),gray); draw((2,-0.1)--(2,-0.3),gray); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;1&quot;,(-1.5,-0.1),S); label(&quot;2&quot;,(0,-0.1),S); label(&quot;1&quot;,(1.5,-0.1),S); draw((1,0)--(0.5,0.866)); draw((0,0)--(0.5,0.866)); draw((-1,0)--(-0.5,0.866)); draw((0,0)--(-0.5,0.866));[/asy]" width="298" height="155" />
</p><p>By drawing four lines from the intersect of the semicircles to their centers, we have split the white region into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/3/5533bc48602603f16d18c04858cad119c04833c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and two equilateral triangles with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. 
This gives the area of the white region as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4baf9af6cf68c9fb807c314db1326abd8d76c33.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{6}\pi+\frac{2\cdot\sqrt3}{4}=\frac{5}{6}\pi+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="194" height="41" />. 
The area of the shaded region is the area of the white region subtracted from the area of the large semicircle. This is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b462daccfa02c738b4820dc103dd36ca3aed4ee.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi-\left(\frac{5}{6}\pi+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right)=\frac{7}{6}\pi-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="242" height="53" />.
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75ed6896a02669cb8bdcfae8d869ae0e3fbe3bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{7}{6}\pi-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="119" height="53" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h3>
<p>The reason why it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/3/5533bc48602603f16d18c04858cad119c04833c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a circle and why the triangles are equilateral are because, first, the radii are the same and they make up the equilateral triangles.
</p><p>Secondly, the reason it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/3/5533bc48602603f16d18c04858cad119c04833c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a circle is because the middle sector has a degree of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/8/258901c6bc5d64b8fb2c1567f2e4a54aaa3020c4.png" class="latex" alt="$180-2 \cdot 60 = 60$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="131" height="13" /> and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10f36cdab601f9958264a98749e9c7640f75203c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{360}=\frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="37" /> of a circle.
</p><p>The other two have areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/b/c8badce57f5d29abd0ce3b453277943246080172.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{180-60}{360}=\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" /> of a triangle each.
</p><p>Therefore, the total fraction of the circle(since they have the same radii) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/6/8e638f1d6aba1c2c63c166e26698effdf3c6fc81.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{6} + 2 \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{4}{6} = \frac{5}{6}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="184" height="37" />
</p><p>~mathboy282
