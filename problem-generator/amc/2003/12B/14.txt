If we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> be the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/6/24672f8b2f5ff69cefb6864a856dff4739fe44a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EAB,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/0/140780e0cdc7959d758ae0976f1715fcc6ffbfa0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2}{5} = \frac{h-3}{h}\]" width="79" height="37" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can look at this diagram as if it were a coordinate plane with point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. This means that the equation of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/4/1f4bd08912d1cbc11dc45a9b2c1f2305935449e7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" /> and the equation of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bbe91133fdaa92f3e58b137fdb4db178296da61.png" class="latex" alt="$EB$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/1030524fa4a58858a7c9744efeaef49f2d260b8e.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{-3}{2}x+\frac{15}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="114" height="37" />. From this we can set of the follow equation to find the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinate of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4ac3116a22f1f37425cc2781f4d32a183bbb294b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x=\frac{-3}{2}x+\frac{15}{2}\]" width="123" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/a/dca5153dba12a953a9ce8c260b780f2ee3374b56.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6x=-3x+15\]" width="116" height="14" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>At points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> is 5 units from segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7baf95ecafc2d7791788ec79aeeae390d60b119.png" class="latex" alt="$BE$" width="28" height="12" />. At points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />, the segments are 2 units from each other. This means that collectively, the two lines closed the distance between them by 3 units over a height of 3 units. Therefore, to close the next two units of distance, they will have to travel a height of 2 units.
</p><p>Then calculate the area of trapezoid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244d8000587fc8c2939a94b0bfe899fde7363c65.png" class="latex" alt="$FGBA$" width="56" height="13" /> and triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/7/987fcb852317f39696a89e6e52912ba79797dbea.png" class="latex" alt="$EGF$" width="43" height="12" /> separately and add them. The area of the trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/5/88597579c321b26b219aae29694601cbf7852b84.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {2+5}{2}\cdot 3 = \frac {21}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="111" height="37" /> and the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/0/4e04ca52a977a66d032d77e40b57fb79b73f017e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot 2 \cdot 2 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="90" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/e/dae0de59202b7a4afd59fa90ca197d03c3d70c52.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{21}{2}+2=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{25}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="146" height="48" />
</p>
