<p>Several figures can be made by attaching two equilateral triangles to the regular pentagon ABCDE in two of the five positions shown. How many non-congruent figures can be constructed in this way?
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b1113e62996ab9b1c6c83bc4836bbb32070801.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(0.9); real r = 5/dir(54).x, h = 5 tan(54*pi/180); pair A = (5,0), B = A+10*dir(72), C = (0,r+h), E = (-5,0), D = E+10*dir(108); draw(A--B--C--D--E--cycle); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A+(0,-0.5),SSE); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B+(0.5,0),ENE); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C+(0,0.5),N); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D+(-0.5,0),WNW); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,E+(0,-0.5),SW); // real l = 5*sqrt(3); pair ab = (h+l)*dir(72), bc = (h+l)*dir(54); pair AB = (ab.y, h-ab.x), BC = (bc.x,h+bc.y), CD = (-bc.x,h+bc.y), DE = (-ab.y, h-ab.x), EA = (0,-l); draw(A--AB--B^^B--BC--C^^C--CD--D^^D--DE--E^^E--EA--A, dashed); // dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); dot(AB); dot(BC); dot(CD); dot(DE); dot(EA); [/asy]" width="335" height="318" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/b/0eb89b0c6e9882236266b9c5d9b6da4c6890ae6e.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A) } 1 \qquad \text {(B) } 2 \qquad \text {(C) } 3 \qquad \text {(D) } 4 \qquad \text {(E) } 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Place the first triangle. Now, we can place the second triangle either adjacent to the first, or with one side between them, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a167bd0ffda41b60ae5177713726594798a86.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(B) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/c/96c9544caef563e6ec7efdf92aa0071947f7006f.png" class="latex" alt="${5 \choose 2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" /> to realize there are 10 ways to choose 2 different triangles. Then divide by 5 for each vertex of a pentagon to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a167bd0ffda41b60ae5177713726594798a86.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(B) }2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />
</p>
