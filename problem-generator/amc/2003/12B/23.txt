<p>The number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercepts on the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/9/cd92ec9ff8fe3c14c2e1ba8dae7e9c3de95d57a1.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\sin(1/x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" /> in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0ea5f14327f122e8ba062b6096179934fe5560d7.png" class="latex" alt="$(0.0001,0.001)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" /> is closest to
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/e/deed5a5c40f167f304777eb23b1bcc0598c3d5d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 2900 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 3000 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 3100 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 3200 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 3300$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="478" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/c/1bc9eec47d628562a54108d1717ea9298415c317.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sin x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" /> has roots in the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/b/e8bae86d05712ecd180e4dcf0a07c09f5fdd02f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi n$" width="21" height="8" /> for all integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Therefore, we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/4/d840fae76efe39485f1db02818410322bc312163.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{x} = \pi n$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/c/e4cdef24df2dade2bca2e7fd9c23cc9af91cc066.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{10000} \le x \le \frac{1}{1000}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="147" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/2/0c215ce6bec20c064b79213c041a028b8e9720f7.png" class="latex" alt="$1000 \le \frac 1x = \pi n \le 10000$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="190" height="37" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/9/af9ea8d5a3a1fd3858cd75c7040354bd6dfdffa4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10000-1000}{\pi} \approx \boxed{2900} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="234" height="37" /> solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> on this interval. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> belongs to the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0ea5f14327f122e8ba062b6096179934fe5560d7.png" class="latex" alt="$(0.0001,0.001)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58c15aa52bb3e77dd5bf1e3c551a1195fe555f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(1/x)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="67" height="20" />. We see that when we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58c15aa52bb3e77dd5bf1e3c551a1195fe555f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(1/x)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="67" height="20" />, the argument <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dbdda6e85aeb34ed519710c37153a7cdf4163f6.png" class="latex" alt="$(1/x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> is always from the range <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2b023eefa0bc1eb4a3fe52b1bd040b0ddcc93e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1000, 10000)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />. Therefore, the problem simply asks for all the zeros of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081bf0417da2140657aad909a14ca6c3ff8b77d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> values between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2b023eefa0bc1eb4a3fe52b1bd040b0ddcc93e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1000, 10000)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />. We know that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> values of any sine graph is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/0/5605ad148b884cb3983bee7cf8ecd35df70baff7.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> so, we see that values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are any integer value from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/6/0362f8decbd5131e34fd0619f907ecb8be327718.png" class="latex" alt="$320$" width="26" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/3/603a521fa260614aad38c3744c8386dce9d5e0fc.png" class="latex" alt="$3184$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> and therefore gives us an answer of approximately <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/e/6fe07bf94e24989d0669a336db9185b2abf4c252.png" class="latex" alt="$2865$" width="35" height="12" /> which is answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/7/937b338fe3988bcc51959b41d53d874d8a626c77.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(A)} 2900}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />
</p><p>~Jske25
</p>
