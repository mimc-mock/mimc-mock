<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/e/81e111c4b5aae9a3bbf3ec895a7098dded9e94c2.png" class="latex" alt="$\log (xy^3) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/d/a0d55ae30f91340116a7c2a03e257be653a9a0c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\log (x^2y) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="19" />, what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/6/79635a3143ed4cd58d9e302f3216a743dc64d391.png" class="latex" alt="$\log (xy)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/f/80fb322c795f8a6ffdaca9d9cebc50694d9e6609.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ -\frac 12  \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 0  \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 12 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 35  \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="375" height="37" /> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/98660449b8e811b1fa699627e3a98ac124ae10c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} &amp;\log(xy) +2\log y = 1  \\ \log(xy) + \log x = 1 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad &amp;2\log(xy) + 2\log x = 2 \end{align*}" width="391" height="43" />
Summing gives 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/4/88488853af794c07b108c500ea7e9bb74add115a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3\log(xy) + 2\log y + 2\log x = 3 \Longrightarrow 5\log(xy) = 3\]" width="386" height="18" />
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/0/7b04fd4f1f558dfbb6047206f72a6e6f317f25cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\log (xy) = \frac 35 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="149" height="37" />.
</p><p>It is not difficult to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/3/a63dd830772b220118e423e63167cf508df44cde.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 10^{\frac{2}{5}}, y = 10^{\frac{1}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="21" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20fcc78fe3c6905b06d53b3af28d9bd5b61b0150.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(xy)+\log(y^2)=1 \\ \log(xy)+\log(x)=1 \text{ subtracting, } \\ \log(y^2)-\log(x)=0 \\ \log \left(\frac{y^2}{x}\right)=0 \\ \frac{y^2}{x}=10^0 \\ y^2=x \\ \text{substitute and solve: } \log(y^5)=5\log(y)=1 \\ \text{ and we need } 3\log(y) \text{ which is } \frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="330" height="224" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Converting the two equation to exponential form, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/3/273fb71aeab211409963b212f24557284d08514f.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10} xy^3 = 1 \implies 10 = xy^3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="222" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/d/6ad9d1257b0b88f2c1864e0facd3fd2014288c56.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10} x^2y = 1 \implies 10 = x^2y$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="222" height="21" />
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> in the second equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/2/ff2fa37e679c5821abb4224479f00c1f8886d993.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{10}{x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="58" height="38" />.
</p><p>Substituting this into the first equation, we see
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/7/2779a853fda4e5f4bba40f9a212d9ec5be04f65c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1000}{x^5} = 10\]" width="80" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/1/4f159740a07ef683ee184518e84d62e07b6fa3b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x =  \sqrt[5]{100} = 10^{\frac{2}{5}}\]" width="127" height="19" />
Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, wee see it is equal to  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b885430a7fab1e2825d1bd3b344a717504789cc2.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{\frac{1}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="30" height="19" />.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef853b7ead502613714f6749014ae5c0dd4be44.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_{10} xy = \frac{3}{5} \implies \boxed{\frac{3}{5}}\]" width="171" height="48" />
</p><p>~YBSuburbanTea
</p>
