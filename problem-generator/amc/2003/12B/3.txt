<p>Rose fills each of the rectangular regions of her rectangular flower bed with a different type of flower. The lengths, in feet, of the rectangular regions in her flower bed are as shown in the figure. She plants one flower per square foot in each region. Asters cost <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> each, begonias <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d007bb6cae1c4d8e9482ffcc5153d6435926fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$1.50$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" /> each, cannas <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> each, dahlias <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/f/34ff82691719fac3420f37d75784ca32c028d20e.png" class="latex" alt="$2.50$" width="31" height="12" /> each, and Easter lilies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> each. What is the least possible cost, in dollars, for her garden?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/6/53688e514d1c6f1f99b771578717677d9fec99b3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(5mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); draw((6,0)--(0,0)--(0,1)--(6,1)); draw((0,1)--(0,6)--(4,6)--(4,1)); draw((4,6)--(11,6)--(11,3)--(4,3)); draw((11,3)--(11,0)--(6,0)--(6,3)); label(&quot;1&quot;,(0,0.5),W); label(&quot;5&quot;,(0,3.5),W); label(&quot;3&quot;,(11,1.5),E); label(&quot;3&quot;,(11,4.5),E); label(&quot;4&quot;,(2,6),N); label(&quot;7&quot;,(7.5,6),N); label(&quot;6&quot;,(3,0),S); label(&quot;5&quot;,(8.5,0),S);[/asy]" width="288" height="172" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/e/58e3f0725e2b1d0af7fdc9f3b25b88c6e92581d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 108 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 115 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 132 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 144 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 156$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The areas of the five regions from greatest to least are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/1/e215cff0d0f7dd50441fa1bc10aab65d68d6a87a.png" class="latex" alt="$21,20,15,6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>If we want to minimize the cost, we want to maximize the area of the cheapest flower and minimize the area of the most expensive flower. Doing this, the cost is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ec93678e85d6da39a639128d81b1e717515e84f.png" class="latex" alt="$1\cdot21+1.50\cdot20+2\cdot15+2.50\cdot6+3\cdot4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="319" height="14" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3b9e046c83ca25ffa3c15f213a16571b36a47b.png" class="latex" alt="$$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="14" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e2995b7f114dcf18fb809edaba86545ffda72c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 108}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
