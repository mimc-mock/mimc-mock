<p>Given a triangle with side lengths 15, 20, and 25, find the triangle's shortest altitude.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/2/fc28c788e0a8a889676c6f00c2fe3f501a6c50e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 12 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 12.5 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 13 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 15$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="402" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>This is a Pythagorean triple (a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> actually) with legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. The area is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7fc84af83bdfbf33cfa121fe09782052172ff5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(15)(20)}{2}=150$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="119" height="38" />. Now, consider an altitude drawn to any side. Since the area remains constant, the altitude and side to which it is drawn are inversely proportional. To get the smallest altitude, it must be drawn to the hypotenuse. Let the length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />; we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/4/124c5dad5b22573633208350f5f5d591221c29d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x)(25)}{2}=\frac{(15)(20)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="152" height="38" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340b762c8f646e1b2154867073db794692545f76.png" class="latex" alt="$25x=300$" width="79" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is 12. Our answer is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719579edfecf49b3044de7dd96766f4f59eedb8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Heron%27s_formula" class="mw-redirect" title="Heron&#39;s formula">Heron's formula</a>, the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e10e5f3aa7c766cb27c3ad319fa526f71588cd.png" class="latex" alt="$150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, hence the shortest altitude's length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/b/1fbbce63948cfdd1c0f7cc3b488f0852a65753bf.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot\frac{150}{25}=\boxed{12\Rightarrow \textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="166" height="38" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(similarity_rule)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28similarity_rule.29">Solution 3 (similarity rule)</span></h3>
<p>The sides are in the ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, and are therefore a Pythagorean Triple and a part of a right triangle.
We know two altitudes, the legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. All that is left is to check the altitude from the right angle vertex to the hypotenuse. There is a rule that states that the three triangles formed when the altitude is dropped (the big triangle and two smaller ones it gets split into) are similar! We take the triangle with hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. The ratio of sides between that triangle and the bigger one is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/5/445f67e0debd5e4569df74e1a256b3efae6eed61.png" class="latex" alt="$4:5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="13" />. Since the side of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> in the big triangle corresponds with our altitude, we take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/1/ca1743eb49e7f5e54e2c5b16acbdaff0c9e2c4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$15\cdot\frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> to get our answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/3/e23ec5060943f7724dbef463d7ff3859e84c35bc.png" class="latex" alt="${12}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719579edfecf49b3044de7dd96766f4f59eedb8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_(fast_calculation)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.28fast_calculation.29">Solution 4 (fast calculation)</span></h3>
<p>The triangle forms a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> right triangle, so we can first draw a triangle with legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. The two altitudes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> are known, and the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />; therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/38361892e1d18f72ba155604c770b3206564a5e1.png" class="latex" alt="$5x/2 = 6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="20" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/4/38449b446f06537d3af0e63c02462321b8d7e89c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=12/5$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="20" />, which is clearly less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Now scale the triangle back up by multiplying every dimension by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. The smallest altitude is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/8/528ba86d0a8615092e5b0a45fa11dd23ef499441.png" class="latex" alt="$12/5 * 5$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="20" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/3/e23ec5060943f7724dbef463d7ff3859e84c35bc.png" class="latex" alt="${12}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719579edfecf49b3044de7dd96766f4f59eedb8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
