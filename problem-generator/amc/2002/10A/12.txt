<p>Mr. Earl E. Bird gets up every day at 8:00 AM to go to work. If he drives at an average speed of 40 miles per hour, he will be late by 3 minutes. If he drives at an average speed of 60 miles per hour, he will be early by 3 minutes. How many miles per hour does Mr. Bird need to drive to get to work exactly on time?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/b/73b30e2997cc6274bd518650b71c2110fd946c42.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 45 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 48 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 50 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 55 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 58$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the time he needs to get there in be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> and the distance he travels be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. From the given equations, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/8662e42d901ca36dbf9974fc3b554843502eee80.png" class="latex" alt="$d=\left(t+\frac{1}{20}\right)40$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="131" height="43" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/3/583e1410c29a33c1bfcc2c1d461d4a00c1408fe0.png" class="latex" alt="$d=\left(t-\frac{1}{20}\right)60$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="131" height="43" />. Setting the two equal, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/2/2521c322e4e57b8eca47b689c4dbd74bd8a7c5be.png" class="latex" alt="$40t+2=60t-3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="14" /> and we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/8/6b814796c90db145e60fee29fb02385dd803c377.png" class="latex" alt="$t=\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="42" height="37" /> of an hour. Substituting t back in, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/1/8a1547b17e452b32d2ae0e4ef898d680a1872583.png" class="latex" alt="$d=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />. From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1218dbfae7805ea5564ac7ee2a892d0a846653.png" class="latex" alt="$d=rt$" width="48" height="12" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, our answer, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b40daad0af8e923f70fdfbe7fdc7f0b1a992dbe8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }48 }$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="30" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Since either time he arrives at is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> minutes from the desired time, the answer is merely the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Harmonic_mean" title="Harmonic mean">harmonic mean</a> of 40 and 60. The harmonic 
Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33213dfcccd7e01adc7be001acee57bc1c0ffe6.png" class="latex" alt="$t=\frac ds$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" /> and dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/4/e14b51799e3d6281eb91fdf3b0383342027a44eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 2s = \frac 1{40} + \frac 1{60}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="102" height="37" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/9/7f9aceb76601bda33e7df315e49ad0e1155e2132.png" class="latex" alt="$s=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }48}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="99" height="30" />.
</p><p>(Note that this approach would work even if the time by which he is late was different from the time by which he is early in the other case - we would simply take a weighted sum in step two, and hence obtain a weighted harmonic mean in step three.)
</p>
