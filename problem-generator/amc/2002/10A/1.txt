<p>The ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/4/3340ff670e4793345fb68e05a1e3df628c6f20fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10^{2000}+10^{2002}}{10^{2001}+10^{2001}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="115" height="40" /> is closest to which of the following numbers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/7/777271815bafc4a52a5164ecbb15b67e6f4855d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0.1 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 0.2 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 1 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="386" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/4/3340ff670e4793345fb68e05a1e3df628c6f20fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10^{2000}+10^{2002}}{10^{2001}+10^{2001}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="115" height="40" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/f/09fb8355bfeb38ce4e9d2a50e4a854fdc68426f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10^{2000}(1+100)}{10^{2001}(1+1)}=\frac{101}{20}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="176" height="43" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/c/9cc2d70333fb8534812fd2b7f3cecfc558d70d93.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{101}{20}=5.05$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/0/080394b4960521553ed19f85f2e640f7e62c4fa2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 5 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
