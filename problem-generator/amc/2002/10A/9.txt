<p>There are 3 numbers A, B, and C, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/e/b3e7b77423bbddf9a5787077cf1814a9779925a3.png" class="latex" alt="$1001C - 2002A = 4004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="183" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/e/48e559c195df562719b773a903e287446e8f0818.png" class="latex" alt="$1001B + 3003A = 5005$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="183" height="14" />. What is the average of A, B, and C?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/b/32ba813750c886ea9ca372376808a31ff1b1115c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 9 \qquad \textbf{(E) }\text{Not uniquely determined}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="524" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Notice that we don't need to find what <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/f/ccf6d387ce491c0ca100c134021f21f616d75162.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> actually are, just their average. In other words, if we can find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/8/9c8ada654c9fa78d44e7304d40dd67f978b80847.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B+C$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="14" />, we will be done. 
</p><p>Adding up the equations gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/7/bd7c9cc30c42e5c28cc82daf4213511f832e95e4.png" class="latex" alt="$1001(A+B+C)=9009=1001(9)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="284" height="20" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/3/e733c66e31ed787f813159f23d83ed0e2e81ddfd.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B+C=9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="14" /> and the average is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/5/5c59cbf771dbe0ac85e85aaa2909519ecfad61e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9}{3}=3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/a/4faa3b02b5b2deb20b9cae1911c4231bd3342042.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As there are only 2 equations and 3 variables, just set one of the variables to something convenient, like 0. Setting C as 0, we get A is -2, and B is 11 by substitution. By basic arithmetic the average is 3=&gt;B
</p><p>-dragoon
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Start by isolating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> in both of the equations, in order to represent the variables <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> in terms of A. Ending up with the two equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/0/26028f68fc607a2db45f1d1b5e0f9c2a17e61d76.png" class="latex" alt="$C = 2A +4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d4ddefea219613890b9e19092a38a656321a782.png" class="latex" alt="$B = -3A + 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="109" height="16" />, we have to calculate the value of the expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/d/f9d086dade7a8f0e1d121c0f2db930086be6c78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{A+B+C}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="38" />. Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/e/e6e4a2ad19fddde8f1ddc900d6ec191ef4bf2f50.png" class="latex" alt="$2A + 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="56" height="16" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/3/0533d5ba2bd421186c7976c63f09b5df985879c8.png" class="latex" alt="$-3A + 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="16" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, we add them up and end up with a value of 9. Dividing 9 by 3 to compute the average, we get our answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/a/4faa3b02b5b2deb20b9cae1911c4231bd3342042.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Darth_Cadet
</p>
