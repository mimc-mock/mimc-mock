<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/9580b15ecb95e817f4cc6603baaa03ed71254759.png" class="latex" alt="$A,B,C,D,E$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> lie, in that order, on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/5/c65a0b904f8f63efdc2b5abfef3f4f05b2d3f990.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AF}$" width="28" height="15" />, dividing it into five segments, each of length 1. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> is not on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/d/c9daba06f5ec9c5069f24bbeab9c7151ab4f68c2.png" class="latex" alt="$AF$" width="27" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> lies on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/5/3b5df2c499e5b2575bf9c1cb5d3dbabec7c54dc5.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{GD}$" width="30" height="15" />, and point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> lies on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/b/33bfa2720820ef9c158e3de47cbc9e3f905b622a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{GF}$" width="29" height="15" />. The line segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/1/401cf1f2573c35d78a1ce8d7c76e1b748b7e7620.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{HC}, \overline{JE},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f0f6aa257bd96696d97454bb777b1a283069e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AG}$" width="28" height="15" /> are parallel. Find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/e/cce34e1167120abe069006227896d9fd72585e21.png" class="latex" alt="$HC/JE$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86cce01db8b17c5801cd663af256d40a1380f5ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 5/4 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 4/3 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 3/2 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 5/3 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First we can draw an image.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/6/f36a37fb3fee1ec7e6c9eb3326ff0df1b01aedbe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.8 cm);  pair A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, J;  A = (0,0); B = (1,0); C = (2,0); D = (3,0); E = (4,0); F = (5,0); G = (-1.5,4); H = extension(D, G, C, C + G - A); J = extension(F, G, E, E + G - A);  draw(A--F--G--cycle); draw(B--G); draw(C--G); draw(D--G); draw(E--G); draw(C--H); draw(E--J);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, S); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, S); label(&quot;$E$&quot;, E, S); label(&quot;$F$&quot;, F, SE); label(&quot;$G$&quot;, G, NW); label(&quot;$H$&quot;, H, W); label(&quot;$J$&quot;, J, NE); [/asy]" width="292" height="195" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f0f6aa257bd96696d97454bb777b1a283069e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AG}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/f/baf06e5af8cfb43ec7280a9e284f6a338d8b6175.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CH}$" width="31" height="15" /> are parallel, triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/2959f4d76da21a84e4e6fdc2a8402fbd68698902.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GAD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed73c66a56a4eaa67876430ba444a7f26427227.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle HCD$" width="62" height="13" /> are similar. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/c/f6c342940978de878b2c7fbe6c8cd7f2220e7978.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{CH}{AG} = \frac{CD}{AD} = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="128" height="37" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f0f6aa257bd96696d97454bb777b1a283069e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AG}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0d24b907c6febb5b90faabbd0dbaeb6041e69f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{JE}$" width="26" height="15" /> are parallel, triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/0/3d0f4ec670f4492680408aad5326a289cd508ff4.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GAF$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/8/038b0468ae56e253628ea9889e0bf3f3022d1302.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle JEF$" width="56" height="13" /> are similar. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fcaa3636e8ce318ce297c8fb593d94f000668b36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{EJ}{AG} = \frac{EF}{AF} = \frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="124" height="37" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/d/00da3bc67038a43770d563e3228259bb2ecf6f3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{CH}{EJ} = \left(\frac{CH}{AG}\right)\div\left(\frac{EJ}{AG}\right) = \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\div\left(\frac{1}{5}\right) = \boxed{\frac{5}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="374" height="48" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/9/ec9a10b2b320d7be3148c6fbc51b90b21e467eef.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(D) 5/3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="68" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As angle F is clearly congruent to itself, we get from AA similarity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/f/43ff4c9faf5e02af8a50292dea7317faa9700c38.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AGF \sim \triangle EJF$" width="139" height="13" />; hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1a1bb37af5a1396cba01fc22b20d29eaaf9f920.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {AG}{JE} =5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="38" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/f/47f8959842cf85c2646c4e2b3e830bc0fea414e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {AG}{HC} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="38" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/c/d0cdf7d13c10ad5ac9e4ba87361b72b681385b54.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {HC}{JE}=\left(\frac{AG}{JE}\right)\left(\frac{HC}{AG}\right) = \boxed{\frac {5}{3}\Rightarrow \text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="284" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Assume an arbitrary value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b11eccf363d2a68685340dc0b302ccba193de92.png" class="latex" alt="$AG=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" /> WLOG. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f0f6aa257bd96696d97454bb777b1a283069e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AG}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/f/baf06e5af8cfb43ec7280a9e284f6a338d8b6175.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CH}$" width="31" height="15" /> are parallel, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/2959f4d76da21a84e4e6fdc2a8402fbd68698902.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GAD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed73c66a56a4eaa67876430ba444a7f26427227.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle HCD$" width="62" height="13" /> are similar. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b658a0fe2d2aeaf059dcfc6a839c901838e027b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AD}{CD}=3=\frac{GA}{HC}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="128" height="39" /> which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/e/7ce812d04e4d580bbd1b0f4065c0563339ebfbe6.png" class="latex" alt="$HC=5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="14" />. By the same logic, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/8/148abdc662d93b7952af84c94e4a99144c47433e.png" class="latex" alt="$JE=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="61" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63a4e570418535545f1c2a83f0978f2de8d4ce4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{HC}{JE}=\frac{5}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="74" height="39" />.
</p>
