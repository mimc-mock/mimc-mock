<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>It shouldn't be hard to use <a href="/wiki/index.php/Trigonometry" title="Trigonometry">trigonometry</a> to bash this and find the height, but there is a much easier way. Extend <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> to meet at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08fd9a3001f04eb9624f966ea35af9f5984fd7a8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(250); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(0,0), B = (52,0), C=(52-144/13,60/13), D=(25/13,60/13), F=(100/13,240/13); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(D--F--C,dashed); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,W); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,F,N); label(&quot;39&quot;,(C+D)/2,N); label(&quot;52&quot;,(A+B)/2,S); label(&quot;5&quot;,(A+D)/2,E); label(&quot;12&quot;,(B+C)/2,WSW); [/asy]" width="418" height="185" /></center>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/3/5d332902316baefe453ae84fd82954d6ada8708d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB} || \overline{CD}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/a/c6a036f5ba5f0a9be15cad8a8d450cb181110c80.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AEB \sim \triangle DEC$" width="143" height="13" />, with the ratio of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Proportion" title="Proportion">proportionality</a> being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/0/ae0580575c7974f69ee16cbdadf6d40a94fabf81.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {39}{52} = \frac {3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="58" height="38" />. Thus
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/c/9ecaabe64bc78a6c0ddf43771253021e558c1778.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac {CE}{CE + 12} = \frac {3}{4} &amp; \Longrightarrow CE = 36 \\ \frac {DE}{DE + 5} = \frac {3}{4} &amp; \Longrightarrow DE = 15 \end{align*}" width="219" height="84" />
So the sides of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b984c55719cc5f992ee62656d920ac15c78d9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDE$" width="60" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb8c52f3062a3269668a70a21d66ba14204e7da.png" class="latex" alt="$15,36,39$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" />, which we recognize to be a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e80dfc0a2f09378b7e077614d426bef327aee8f3.png" class="latex" alt="$5 - 12 - 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="13" /> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a>. Therefore (we could simplify some of the calculation using that the ratio of areas is equal to the ratio of the sides squared),
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac44872ef92e9370f6abc0ca1ef24d5881ef346e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[ABCD] = [ABE] - [CDE] = \frac {1}{2}\cdot 20 \cdot 48 - \frac {1}{2} \cdot 15 \cdot 36 = \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 210}\]" width="478" height="37" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Draw altitudes from points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/255b3ee5b7acb3f4baca3b52720811aa66cba79e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.2cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(0,0), B = (52,0), C=(52-144/13,60/13), D=(25/13,60/13), E=(52-144/13,0), F=(25/13,0); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(C--E,dashed); draw(D--F,dashed); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,N); label(&quot;\(D&#39;\)&quot;,F,SSE); label(&quot;\(C&#39;\)&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;39&quot;,(C+D)/2,N); label(&quot;52&quot;,(A+B)/2,S); label(&quot;5&quot;,(A+D)/2,W); label(&quot;12&quot;,(B+C)/2,ENE); [/asy]" width="525" height="92" /></center>
<p>Translate the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27a4835d23f08f296a98865dc7255b7d03655689.png" class="latex" alt="$ADD&#39;$" width="48" height="14" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/5/825000b887cf170e8a54ee5dcdafffbdfb959cea.png" class="latex" alt="$DD&#39;$" width="34" height="14" /> coincides with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b7058f8a28ca225136b284b337138a54e39e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$CC&#39;$" width="32" height="14" />. We get the following triangle:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/9/ae9e06d7e09ef1c45cb1eb1354f20938e46480b6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.2cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(0,0), B = (13,0), C=(25/13,60/13), F=(25/13,0); draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(C--F,dashed); label(&quot;\(A&#39;\)&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,N); label(&quot;\(C&#39;\)&quot;,F,SE); label(&quot;5&quot;,(A+C)/2,W); label(&quot;12&quot;,(B+C)/2,ENE); [/asy]" width="162" height="88" /></center>
<p>The length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/c/22cf0fa25e560981109abb13757436b94fadb035.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;B$" width="32" height="14" /> in this triangle is equal to the length of the original <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, minus the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />.
Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/e/2fe4b9e43fa4e12ce6a617b42cb57d97efd4dbef.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;B = 52 - 39 = 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="157" height="14" />.
</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4b7c6fecba4010b78b71bed438ac506d39587f1.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;BC$" width="47" height="14" /> is a well-known <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a604be7f74f466e9c452dab1a5fd92e5da74bddf.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,12,13)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" /> right triangle. Its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/3/54322280d1b10baf6ca8edd14c369866e1f35e0f.png" class="latex" alt="$[A&#39;BC]=\frac{A&#39;C\cdot BC}2 = \frac{5\cdot 12}2 = 30$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="270" height="38" />, and therefore its altitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b7058f8a28ca225136b284b337138a54e39e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$CC&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/e/94e8a9c6a0cc4556e0cf07f422717e08d1688865.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[A&#39;BC]}{A&#39;B} = \frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="106" height="39" />.
</p><p>Now the area of the original trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/a/0ca93e61f77442f741da5cf26a0cbd4c5eb86c7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(AB+CD)\cdot CC&#39;}2 = \frac{91 \cdot 60}{13 \cdot 2} = 7\cdot 30 = \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 210}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="383" height="39" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Draw altitudes from points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/255b3ee5b7acb3f4baca3b52720811aa66cba79e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.2cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(0,0), B = (52,0), C=(52-144/13,60/13), D=(25/13,60/13), E=(52-144/13,0), F=(25/13,0); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(C--E,dashed); draw(D--F,dashed); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,N); label(&quot;\(D&#39;\)&quot;,F,SSE); label(&quot;\(C&#39;\)&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;39&quot;,(C+D)/2,N); label(&quot;52&quot;,(A+B)/2,S); label(&quot;5&quot;,(A+D)/2,W); label(&quot;12&quot;,(B+C)/2,ENE); [/asy]" width="525" height="92" /></center>
<p>Call the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad8019d0fb176ff1ade21d8a9e6d6aa4a1f9563a.png" class="latex" alt="$AD&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/c/5bc856e7f29652b2c928f2c025ed9befbab1873e.png" class="latex" alt="$BC&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, and the height of the trapezoid to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.
By the Pythagorean Theorem, we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/5/8a5e174f37eebd2336bbcabec233ee1e1bdf2561.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z^2 + x^2 = 144\]" width="107" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a04142ba36ef09072723ba5c0ec7e840f3ba2ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y^2 + x^2 = 25\]" width="98" height="18" />
</p><p>Subtracting these two equation yields:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/7/3570d2493e77ace9a387502ad4d1a0a9d354a56f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z^2-y^2=119 \implies (z+y)(z-y)=119\]" width="317" height="19" />
</p><p>We also have: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d4251004edb6d9284503170c0cce3d98b16b01d.png" class="latex" alt="$z+y=52-39=13$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="165" height="16" />.
</p><p>We can substitute the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/955b0030c06d258b4b9a46188afd9c88bb91634c.png" class="latex" alt="$z+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="14" /> into the equation we just obtained, so we now have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/2/1b2ea34d8c8f15fc45d7d39c6286cfdb62f58185.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(13) (z-y)=119 \implies z-y=\frac{119}{13}\]" width="281" height="38" />.
</p><p>We can add the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/955b0030c06d258b4b9a46188afd9c88bb91634c.png" class="latex" alt="$z+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="14" /> and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/1/3f1d516db531c1a66f4f54fec76743404a5c06b8.png" class="latex" alt="$z-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="11" /> equation to find the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, which simplifies down to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e11daf15318eb2664a432383c49e19f02a61268.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{144}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="37" />. Finally, we can plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> and use the Pythagorean theorem to find the height of the trapezoid.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4bcff5389251371e3013c9914d75d23521f3121.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{12^4}{13^2} + x^2 = 12^2 \implies x^2 = \frac{(12^2)(13^2)}{13^2} -\frac{12^4}{13^2}  \implies x^2 = \frac{(12 \cdot 13)^2 - (144)^2}{13^2} \implies x^2 = \frac{(156+144)(156-144)}{13^2} \implies x = \sqrt{\frac{3600}{169}} = \frac{60}{13}\]" width="1030" height="43" />
</p><p>Now that we have the height of the trapezoid, we can multiply this by the median to find our answer.
</p><p>The median of the trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/2/fc2a67c74b75fa1b00861f857e738ca400b59e48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{39+52}{2} = \frac{91}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" />, and multiplying this and the height of the trapezoid gets us:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/0/5200e426505fe8439347aa5bdea83897e4c818b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{60 \cdot 91}{13 \cdot 2} = \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 210}\]" width="147" height="38" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>We construct a line segment parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> from point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f694ab8315c055ae7dd5d9fe5ba57a1a7c97a99.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="19" /> and label the intersection of this segment with line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> as point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/0/710fb5fa3adedd7a16739e543d846747adbe05b5.png" class="latex" alt="$E.$" width="18" height="12" /> Then quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421b4b2fbd1b67330bf1605c6901f73fcefdb5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$AECD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a parallelogram, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/5/a15bb4d09ad85730d936e67ce079180bb315d095.png" class="latex" alt="$CE=5, AE=39,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="144" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc46235a1bd1364f27a04fff1d1a68bbfb610f33.png" class="latex" alt="$EB=13.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" /> Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/2/27282aafa81e06b3343e93b3e9221570c0d14be2.png" class="latex" alt="$EBC$" width="43" height="12" /> is therefore a right triangle, with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a65ac81561b3113532ab571e47ff4dd6579c058b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12 \cdot 5 \cdot 12 = 30.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />
</p><p>By continuing to split <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> into segments of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/1/bf16d3a16d64a9f26214054355b52da40acce300.png" class="latex" alt="$13,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we can connect these vertices in a "zig-zag," creating seven congruent right triangles, each with sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc24a7d9b78eaaa38a1bcea8ba1e2baafe0643c9.png" class="latex" alt="$5,12,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/1/bf16d3a16d64a9f26214054355b52da40acce300.png" class="latex" alt="$13,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> and each with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a8c6bab62d0931b9d43a9658b409b14b8d7c03.png" class="latex" alt="$30.$" width="21" height="12" /> The total area is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec2d4aca560193bb39fcc95bd5208f48a885cf2.png" class="latex" alt="$7 \cdot 30 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)} 210}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="135" height="28" />
</p><p>Alternative: Instead of creating seven congruent right triangles, we can find the height of parallelogram <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421b4b2fbd1b67330bf1605c6901f73fcefdb5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$AECD$" width="57" height="13" /> by drawing an altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" />, creating the new point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. By recognizing that triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31b0f04577d6b6bf91efc86f609edb160cfadb1e.png" class="latex" alt="$DAF$" width="43" height="13" /> is similar to triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/1/ef14ba8fc495f2a5750a5ed394ebf75a28641658.png" class="latex" alt="$BEC$" width="43" height="12" />, we can use properties of similar triangles and find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/a/84aca09a3514adcffa8b19e6038f95106cf6cd10.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = 12 \cdot \frac{5}{13} = \frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="156" height="38" />. Thus, the area of parallelogram <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421b4b2fbd1b67330bf1605c6901f73fcefdb5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$AECD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/5/f15e15d6f209619b1470ffc8f9a4bff31c3716b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{13} \cdot 39 = 180$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="107" height="38" />. Finally, we add the areas of the parallelogram <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421b4b2fbd1b67330bf1605c6901f73fcefdb5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$AECD$" width="57" height="13" /> and the right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/1/ef14ba8fc495f2a5750a5ed394ebf75a28641658.png" class="latex" alt="$BEC$" width="43" height="12" /> together and we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/a/31a8f0166eea34a43e8f0bef07aa6af52735f0f9.png" class="latex" alt="$180 + 30 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)} 210}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="161" height="30" />. ~scarletsyc
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_but_quicker">Solution 2 but quicker</span></h3>
<p>From Solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> we know that the the altitude of the trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/c/7ac94a5f6297a7d6aadbd7ba04e3a0a732298908.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> and the triangle's area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />.
Note that once we remove the triangle we get a rectangle with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/5/e058d401a1b942f106f081ff1fcca8a8b1d42a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$39$" width="17" height="12" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/c/7ac94a5f6297a7d6aadbd7ba04e3a0a732298908.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />.
The numbers multiply nicely to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/8/fe8fd8be56d3d23a1dc218eab655776c33bbccf6.png" class="latex" alt="$180+30=\boxed{(C) 210}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="159" height="29" />
-harsha12345
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quick_Time_Trouble_Solution_5">Quick Time Trouble Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>First note how the answer choices are all integers.
The area of the trapezoid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/b/9fb1f4e1beea2804334b2127c3c9b87136201f46.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{39+52}{2} \cdot h = \frac{91}{2} h$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="145" height="38" />. So h divides 2. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/3/7d3914c5607bf46949390af4e088bb17107565ac.png" class="latex" alt="$2h$" width="19" height="12" />. The area is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b4dc067f9e632f3b0ec28842c84cc2f4835a6e9.png" class="latex" alt="$91x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" />. 
Trying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69271b422e022ea65ff1f288eddfcfa82afd8ab7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2$" width="43" height="12" /> can easily be seen to not work. Those make the only integers possible so now you know x is a fraction. 
Since the area is an integer the denominator of x must divide either 13 or 7 since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/f/23fbf360a9dd912ce80418e843a4776dc54c34b0.png" class="latex" alt="$91 = 13\cdot7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="14" />.
Seeing how <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/2/b92c9b345408bdbab1e4ec0521e9de78392bb1ca.png" class="latex" alt="$39 = 3\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/1/c9189b05f41461a0636221a68837c3293f27ddd9.png" class="latex" alt="$52 = 4\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="14" /> assume that the denominator divides 13. Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/f/23f5acd61732085d1301940ab83e05fbf4f79d8d.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{x}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="54" height="33" /> the area is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0fd07926b28ae4ff8b90a9f1487344e977067c.png" class="latex" alt="$7y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" />.
Note that (A) and (C) are the only multiples of 7. We know that A doesn't work because that would mean <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> which we ruled out. 
So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/0/3e016c30aa0726100db6221fe483fff310c1e24f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} 210}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="67" height="28" />. - megateleportingrobots
</p>
