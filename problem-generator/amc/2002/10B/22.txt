<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2002_12B_AMC-20.png" class="image"><img alt="2002 12B AMC-20.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//5/57/2002_12B_AMC-20.png" width="453" height="187" /></a>
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/d/bcdcffa29ecf82ec37f53d9802ee5fb7b39eb74c.png" class="latex" alt="$OM = x$" width="68" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/c/6bc78fb8119ba4bc1003cf3b58239dfcc14b957b.png" class="latex" alt="$ON = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c94d2aa0f4758455a6a8942881c387074409e294.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle XON, MOY$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="17" /> respectively,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/5/7d5e8b251c14deb1f13ca0546d6670f77b842fd8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (2x)^2 + y^2 &amp;= 19^2\\ x^2 + (2y)^2 &amp;= 22^2\end{align*}" width="128" height="47" />
</p><p>Summing these gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f7f134cc20466b92f0b2a0ae8caff320e1046a1.png" class="latex" alt="$5x^2 + 5y^2 = 845 \Longrightarrow x^2 + y^2 = 169$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="274" height="18" />. 
</p><p>By the Pythagorean Theorem again, we have 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/6/f16445ea2f3d51273e055db6c290c3f43c9d12d2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2x)^2 + (2y)^2 = XY^2 \Longrightarrow XY = \sqrt{4(x^2 + y^2)} = \sqrt{4(169)} = \sqrt{676} = \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 26}\]" width="595" height="32" />
</p><p>Alternatively, we could note that since we found <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/f/fafeb40d05881e6f3cae2644e337548e7f20df18.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 = 169$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="108" height="18" />, segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bdad176347fc5753c956d2e8a8654ad5b03f0bc9.png" class="latex" alt="$MN=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="78" height="13" />. Right triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/a/f4ac0cf3968a203aca43d97d0df5969ccadf68b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MON$" width="66" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29ab3e1eecd4bd8bb9c9d20855ffed7dfc40ffc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle XOY$" width="61" height="13" /> are similar by Leg-Leg with a ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/c/55c84e3322f1ab5b8d7d7eebd071180aa98efeb7.png" class="latex" alt="$XY=2(MN)=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="201" height="29" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/2/4d27d91808d433beff1d107948b796f4e7f56ee0.png" class="latex" alt="$XO=x$" width="65" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/c/03c664e045b4776096b5ef2f3b7b8f2e05eda934.png" class="latex" alt="$YO=y.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" /> Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2066759e07851c69defb411eb4f28beda8ceaf07.png" class="latex" alt="$XY=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="133" height="22" /> 
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/6/036d7d36d4f971a35fd1c7a47de638519616822b.png" class="latex" alt="$XN=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df21f17d1b16567ecf899fda01bd5791d58c614f.png" class="latex" alt="$YM=22,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="80" height="16" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c410046f74f03d31da3f72e8dc8e0702040c760.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[XN^2=19^2=x^2+(\dfrac{y}{2})^2)=\dfrac{x^2}{4}+y^2\]" width="280" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/d/b6d97369c237b3d4e29632c18df6e2aa47573c31.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[YM^2=22^2=(\dfrac{x}{2})^2+y^2=\dfrac{x^2}{4}+y^2.\]" width="279" height="39" />
