<p>Riders on a Ferris wheel travel in a circle in a vertical plane. A particular wheel has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> feet and revolves at the constant rate of one revolution per minute. How many seconds does it take a rider to travel from the bottom of the wheel to a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> vertical feet above the bottom?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/e/ffe9bcccd662d669fa1caa1b31c824140520469b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 5\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 6\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 7.5\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 10\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="374" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37e56fa9375622b7173da2e667258b8e39157603.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.5mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair O=(0,0), A=(0,-20), B=(0,-10), C=(10sqrt(3),-10); real r=20; path ferriswheel=Circle(O,r); draw(ferriswheel); draw(O--A); draw(O--C); draw(B--C); draw(A--C); pair[] ps={A,B,C,O}; dot(ps); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,N); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,W); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SE); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,(O--B),W); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,(A--B),W); label(&quot;$20$&quot;,(O--C),NE);  [/asy]" width="285" height="305" /></center>
<p>We can let this circle represent the ferris wheel with center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/b/56b4c0ef575d9ec02ec52379f2cc6f6fa28acc13.png" class="latex" alt="$O,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> represent the desired point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> feet above the bottom. Draw a diagram like the one above. We find out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/2/f820b345347a6b0b992f9441716d66c2a6bdf5bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle OBC$" width="59" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangle. That means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/d/63df742346090c7cc8b450f6ade24693abca089f.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC = 60^\circ$" width="104" height="13" /> and the ferris wheel has made <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/8/c88bbeb64d076e0cac67d9d137d873fa7e302bc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{360} = \frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="37" /> of a revolution. Therefore, the time it takes to travel that much of a distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/d/72d1dd454cde0ca0f020f352bd30298295058ba9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{6}\text{th}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="28" height="37" /> of a minute, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> seconds. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/4/554548e47195f77344471858fccc17e3ae397c8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D) \ } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="29" />. Alternatively, we could also say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/2/f820b345347a6b0b992f9441716d66c2a6bdf5bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle OBC$" width="59" height="13" /> by SAS, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is 20, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/0/fd0411839678ed7db07e9574d76061d92b39c2d7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AOC$" width="58" height="13" /> is equilateral, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/d/63df742346090c7cc8b450f6ade24693abca089f.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC = 60^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />
</p>
