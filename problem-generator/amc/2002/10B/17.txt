<p>A regular octagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/055dcba798b1424c44c2606d913e0f2edab1c5ad.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDEFGH$" width="118" height="13" /> has sides of length two.  Find the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/b/f7b22de7a6a4a849d8997f3db62b129ea8c74b4e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADG$" width="59" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a59de5cfea6537885df98a7dad9c00a2226539b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 4 + 2\sqrt2 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 6 + \sqrt2\qquad \textbf{(C) } 4 + 3\sqrt2 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 3 + 4\sqrt2 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 8 + \sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="613" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/9/ca90d855ac2876463b79d50db160c0d88e6f0f30.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair[] A = new pair[8]; A[0]=(0,0); for (int i=1; i&lt;8; ++i) A[i] = A[i-1] + 2*dir(45*(i-1)); draw( A[0]--A[1]--A[2]--A[3]--A[4]--A[5]--A[6]--A[7]--cycle ); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A[0],SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,A[1],SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,A[2],SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,A[3],NE); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,A[4],NE); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,A[5],NW); label(&quot;$G$&quot;,A[6],NW); label(&quot;$H$&quot;,A[7],SW); filldraw( A[0]--A[3]--A[6]--cycle, lightgray, black ); pair P = intersectionpoint( A[3]--A[6], A[0]--A[5] ); draw( A[0]--P ); draw( P -- A[5], dashed ); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,NE); draw( A[1]--A[4], dashed ); pair Q = intersectionpoint( A[3]--A[6], A[1]--A[4] ); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,Q,NW); [/asy]" width="278" height="272" />
</p><p>The area of the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/c/35c11c783ddf150fb2c99ce6dd035d42bbaa7e24.png" class="latex" alt="$ADG$" width="43" height="13" /> can be computed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/3/30332448459c106124db07d6eaeb1b6615d843af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{DG \cdot AP}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="38" />. We will now find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/f/eaf9409044fef7f3966027eec40b2cced52eb16c.png" class="latex" alt="$DG$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3bb4eeb347e74e025fd175c22f1f7275af0555e.png" class="latex" alt="$AP$" width="27" height="13" />.
</p><p>Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/e/f3e775199b80d4ef43c1bc7b0a4302325ae4a974.png" class="latex" alt="$PFG$" width="42" height="12" /> is a right isosceles triangle with hypotenuse of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/a/2ba7dd3572fb3ac89291d72d7a0de21e54482e83.png" class="latex" alt="$PG=\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />. 
The same holds for triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/6/4965e3d73b33f66d3c40556be29a9ccdae238e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$QED$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="16" /> and its leg <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/e/dfe3f0862fc765c6a4384b9fd9e0182b086f06a7.png" class="latex" alt="$QD$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" />. The length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42cf1079fd4f9e6ee61b1b7fe39a0cab5a6c8738.png" class="latex" alt="$FE=2$" width="61" height="12" />.
Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7c758ac2851027af3370c40ab176671caed1b53.png" class="latex" alt="$GD = 2 + 2\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/7/3d702a7f949b9babeda91824279fce7d547bc445.png" class="latex" alt="$AP = PD = 2 + \sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/c/35c11c783ddf150fb2c99ce6dd035d42bbaa7e24.png" class="latex" alt="$ADG$" width="43" height="13" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/56272cec170ff636b205ca8f09d27575c74d9a9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{DG \cdot AP}2 = \frac{(2+2\sqrt 2)(2+\sqrt 2)}2 = \frac{8+6\sqrt 2}2 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}=4+3\sqrt 2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="498" height="41" />.
</p>
