<p>For which of the following values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> does the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/a/4daf322db120240e73326c8de6ef29157cc0c296.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x-1}{x-2} = \frac{x-k}{x-6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="115" height="37" /> have no solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The domain over which we solve the equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/f/91fb7c8725435c19dc3d626ebac9754d252dbd5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathbb{R} \setminus \{2,6\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can now cross-multiply to get rid of the fractions, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/2/5c2cba921ad00ab769c4e37e5e15b7546caf847e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-1)(x-6)=(x-k)(x-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="18" />.
</p><p>Simplifying that, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8f501a26def565bd88ecc7369b497d0c97e9301.png" class="latex" alt="$7x-6 = (k+2)x - 2k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" />. Clearly for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5bb215d21ddfbe2ec17a99cb7118ca80e4a2ae.png" class="latex" alt="$k=5$" width="43" height="12" />&#160;we get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70f67ffa6e6e3ccc2701ba71c502d657dcbebd26.png" class="latex" alt="$-6=-10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" />&#160;which is never true. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/b/98bf7c3854bc12eeabff9ea99c27507eaa11113f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{ (E)}\ 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="53" height="29" />
</p><p>For other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, one can solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/5/015453e6f3bdf5a07ba8333c8f1eeca4090b4fde.png" class="latex" alt="$x(5-k) = 6-2k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4b03f6ae48cc6b90cce5df0aa4c6b92b4f8c6c0.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac {6-2k}{5-k}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />. We can easily verify that for none of the other 4 possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is this equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, hence there is a solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in each of the other cases.
</p><p>-Edited by XxHalo711 (typo within the solution)
</p>
