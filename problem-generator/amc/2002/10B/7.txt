<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/7/e77c293a88e8d47d1961dd65677b11b0d937d321.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 + \frac 13 + \frac 17  = \frac {41}{42}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="128" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee13d3960646e8fe1105ba55176b7d41e6907a.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \left(\frac{41}{42} + \frac{1}{n}\right) &lt; \frac {41}{42} + \frac 1n &lt; \frac{41}{42} + \frac 11 &lt; 2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="354" height="43" />
</p><p>From which it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4ff39a30da2a7dc4bec5b18746caba6d750d0346.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{41}{42} + \frac 1n = 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="91" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/3/7d393526f55bac114397f85ee8e894d980bf9a87.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" />.
