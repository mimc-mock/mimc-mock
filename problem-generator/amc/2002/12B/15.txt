<p>How many four-digit numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> have the property that the three-digit number obtained by removing the leftmost digit is one ninth of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/f/4ef6b68ccbab7b0f978e69985f985343b9ec48b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 5 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 6 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 7 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/0/4902f0c30d473213762be56a4e0e0805abf70404.png" class="latex" alt="$N = \overline{abcd} = 1000a + \overline{bcd}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="193" height="17" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/3/b739b9ecae2ed6f2f74f583257e005bfddb2f81b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{N}{9} = \overline{bcd}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/652fd0c73042e9f367174fcb5cdfcc91e63fbd32.png" class="latex" alt="$1000a + \overline{bcd} = 9\overline{bcd} \Longrightarrow 125a = \overline{bcd}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="278" height="17" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/c/66c3b69924c8e1ccb2a9798182a98628b6cbe84c.png" class="latex" alt="$100 \le \overline{bcd} &lt; 1000$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="136" height="18" />, from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3d322e592bf9e23b2c1b1aa08b860b734bcf608.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1, \ldots, 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="15" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> three-digit solutions, and the answer is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since N is a four digit number, assume WLOG that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/8/a38a5e7d6112ef4dbaf97376e51f8a46ff5b579f.png" class="latex" alt="$N = 1000a + 100b + 10c + d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="224" height="14" />, where a is the thousands digit, b is the hundreds digit, c is the tens digit, and d is the ones digit.
Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/412b3168d35add0a6adb3d1fa504a2d2452a70ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{9}N = 100b + 10c + d$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="169" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8ad97c05b66e86d202eabf60ce124f5a3a5b518a.png" class="latex" alt="$N = 900b + 90c + 9d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="14" />
Set these equal to each other:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/2/b72664c9c2d440b53307369eea6d6aaa6ecec06b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1000a + 100b + 10c + d = 900b + 90c + 9d\]" width="330" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2f8a153a5faae42c32c8d0148d02754d6467809.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1000a = 800b + 80c + 8d\]" width="192" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/a/c9a42e8825cd2fc03cafaecc6ebbb3e5c13655bc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1000a = 8(100b + 10c + d)\]" width="205" height="18" />
Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/9/92931f9c79b04aa10ee5cd15493d1bf337b3db6a.png" class="latex" alt="$100b + 10c + d = N - 1000a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="223" height="14" />, thus:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a6fd31a085282a10152f9253eb01d53febf2acf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1000a = 8(N - 1000a)\]" width="174" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c64a6abfacee012c060f51e9a642b78ce719e8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1000a = 8N - 8000a\]" width="162" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/673099001a81a45b956ace6fd3d59d764495b69c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9000a = 8N\]" width="95" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/5/fb5290b31d5c6fbc376e01e35889de01a86ccbae.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[N = 1125a\]" width="86" height="13" />
</p><p>Go back to our first equation, in which we set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/8/a38a5e7d6112ef4dbaf97376e51f8a46ff5b579f.png" class="latex" alt="$N = 1000a + 100b + 10c + d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="224" height="14" />,
Then: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/c/bacb4d586331e9c422106c7686f127b30f7b3fbc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1125a = 1000a + 100b + 10c + d\]" width="252" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c149c7cf80e812d27d60a2b13b8ae3e6a26d7ab6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[125a = 100b + 10c + d\]" width="174" height="14" />
The upper limit for the right hand side (RHS) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> (when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f0e621ddfd0f3f4728f2161b52587224c273dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 9$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa469f5533743cf3ed01982d13681820ffaa3e0.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 9$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e9bff534770db92aae32442b1c3176f818c2aab.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 9$" width="42" height="12" />).
It's easy to prove that for an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> there is only one combination of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/6/d26e7301647e86fde791c482f02a14238d6f7220.png" class="latex" alt="$b, c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> that can make the equation equal. Just think about the RHS as a three digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/4/014ea79aa056a490131df2d39465e11b0e791ec0.png" class="latex" alt="$bcd$" width="25" height="12" />. There's one and only one way to create every three digit number with a certain combination of digits.
Thus, we test for how many as are in the domain set by the RHS. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e642c6427b0376f372c667d190f9a8ba5d7fb6ff.png" class="latex" alt="$125\cdot7 = 875$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> which is the largest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> value, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, giving us the answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/2/422827e0c7a90a3316098a82da4cb858bb09867d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {D) 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="43" height="29" />
</p><p>IronicNinja~
</p>
