<p>Andy's lawn has twice as much area as Beth's lawn and three times as much area as Carlos' lawn. Carlos' lawn mower cuts half as fast as Beth's mower and one third as fast as Andy's mower. If they all start to mow their lawns at the same time, who will finish first?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/6/646f79889a74a1324e002127661ba7658aafe48e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \text{Andy} \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{Beth} \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \text{Carlos} \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \text{Andy\ and \ Carlos\ tie\ for\ first.} \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \text{All\ three\ tie.}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="727" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We say Andy's lawn has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Beth's lawn thus has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876fba774d252c254bd1496f6a44b4db630e55cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" />, and Carlos's lawn has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/f/a3fc48324430ab8e4592260bcaccc655503255f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" />. 
</p><p>We say Andy's lawn mower cuts at a speed of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Carlos's cuts at a speed of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/2/5f28e3e06d85d54db0f44661d2ecd47fa26b6406.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{y}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" />, and Beth's cuts at a speed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/0/ec0422bca0b6cea7529c94f9191454bd38efe773.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2y}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="21" height="37" />.
</p><p>Each person's lawn is cut at a time of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c7b5d505536be9be88375199f14658ec840404e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{area}}{\text{rate}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="33" height="33" />, so Andy's is cut in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" /> time, as is Carlos's. Beth's is cut in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84fcd5353a0cd0ab5544f94d0477c0e5d704a9e5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}\times\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="48" height="41" />, so the first one to finish is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/8/178de5220d1bd0e8dfc53959134203cb85fdb7a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{Beth}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="79" height="29" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, we can set values of their lawns' areas and their owners' speeds. Let the area of Andy's lawn be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> units, Beth's lawn be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> units, and Carlos's lawn be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> units. Let Carlos's mowing area per hour (honestly the time you set won't matter) be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, let Beth's mowing area per hour be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and let Andy's mowing area per hour be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Now, we can easily calculate their time  by dividing their lawns' areas by their respective owners' speeds. Andy's time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/2/942e8bf1bae65e931952a73a0fc771c86b0ff059.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{3} = 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" /> hours, Beth's time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/0/bc0342a4ac8548665d39b0c99f5d75f77e4c5fce.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2} = 1.5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> hours, and Carlos's time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/2/942e8bf1bae65e931952a73a0fc771c86b0ff059.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{3} = 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" /> hours. Our answer is clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/8/178de5220d1bd0e8dfc53959134203cb85fdb7a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{Beth}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="79" height="29" />.
</p><p><br />
~Solution by virjoy2001
</p>
