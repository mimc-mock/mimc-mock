<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> are positive real numbers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> form an increasing arithmetic sequence and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/4/a643bcc8195884f4fc1e16966f6f3d6ad6fe2c27.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> form a geometric sequence, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/cebd1d6d15ed13c1bb9224a0971ee1a738055652.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac ad$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/b/e2bbd73c87d9c8e8e344f739b7ffe6a191484c0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 1{12} \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 16 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 14 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 13 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="370" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>We can let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/8271d4342b38d2ab38bb626ffad8b9dae00b5e2d.png" class="latex" alt="$c=3$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/4/5e4b2503862ce82e4cb89da832f5ccedae370ede.png" class="latex" alt="$d=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/e/97eeb6ae31984ba115d8347522e8730e514016cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{d}=\boxed{\frac{1}{4}}  \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="127" height="48" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/3/7735e0c99a5772e345ffad70b8728d89b4e82455.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> is a geometric sequence, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/f/25f5f831a9a1492b5df52c6c4202bd209e33811f.png" class="latex" alt="$b=ka$" width="51" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/2/f52fe0d88b7987274898cc82423bae4cbec3972d.png" class="latex" alt="$d=k^2a$" width="60" height="15" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e6630318d8cb28de2723180b6d359b972489c9.png" class="latex" alt="$k&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Now, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c06de735d79f0b28c30bcbbcf96e67bb1ca42c.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> is an arithmetic sequence. Its difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/6/4464e62fd7bfb8dc6476bbaae1befd62f0234528.png" class="latex" alt="$b-a=(k-1)a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/1/2711a08eebd757b137eb2846a008a855142ff5e1.png" class="latex" alt="$d=a + 3(k-1)a = (3k-2)a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="238" height="18" />.
</p><p>Comparing the two expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/87499942c3f3ab270745939796cd07c6aeb84367.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2=3k-2$" width="91" height="15" />. The positive solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f681bd84648c174d45eed0037702cae77ed0686.png" class="latex" alt="$k=2$" width="43" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/2/132ba796194ff44224a3d8f44c41c207ed2625e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{d}=\frac{a}{k^2a}=\frac{1}{k^2}=\boxed{\frac{1}{4}} \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="228" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the common difference of the arithmetic progression, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/4/454bcbc31661dc56dcb8824e0a0b0b09e879be63.png" class="latex" alt="$b = a + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="74" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/2/722631ccb964c0326623018859fc9fae8fdb32a1.png" class="latex" alt="$c = a + 2n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/c/5ec1875b996eed6d888268930a4103a5cc52d556.png" class="latex" alt="$d = a + 3n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="14" />. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/2/a22d72fc66fb5191af25650b4fbbc6645baa696a.png" class="latex" alt="$b / a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5ecd3764c78daae2b11aa328a4456b12408b569.png" class="latex" alt="$d / b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />, or
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/0/5d048c478d3ca0b242e47b5621c7d854827cbf34.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a + n}{a} = \frac{a + 3n}{a + n}.\]" width="128" height="39" />
Cross-multiplying, we get 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/6/53646d5adaf792f94b95c1d9f0b0d4148a1b6a59.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2 + 2an + n^2 = a^2 + 3an\]" width="201" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/4/5642d779ac0ea9621db97907b6908c51276813e5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n^2 = an\]" width="62" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/3/f03bdc2df1bf326925e364582a4a26ca2c5483a0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n = a\]" width="44" height="8" />
So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/8216e4dfca6f105e0f367286d7a9828f70ef04be.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{d} = \frac{a}{a + 3n} = \frac{a}{4a} = \boxed{\frac{1}{4}}  \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="254" height="48" />.
</p>
