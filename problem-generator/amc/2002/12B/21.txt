<p>For all positive <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integers</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" />, let 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/4/8d49dd65ded465f1090c6122d420d37cdc37d8b0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} a_n =\left\{ \begin{array}{lr} 11, &amp; \text{if\ }n\ \text{is\ divisible\ by\ }13\ \text{and\ }14;\\ 13, &amp; \text{if\ }n\ \text{is\ divisible\ by\ }14\ \text{and\ }11;\\ 14, &amp; \text{if\ }n\ \text{is\ divisible\ by\ }11\ \text{and\ }13;\\ 0, &amp; \text{otherwise}. \end{array} \right. \end{eqnarray*}" width="321" height="75" />
</p><p>Calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/d/75db55d0047c8be209739f16eb97f02bd33f781a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=1}^{2001} a_n$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="47" height="50" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c121dc6dfa61a09599de04c68f25aee25554d7d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 448 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 486 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 1560 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 2001 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 2002$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="460" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/4/274a4637f71ab9e01081d83e83ddd1c7ff012e20.png" class="latex" alt="$2002 = 11 \cdot 13 \cdot 14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="141" height="13" />, it follows that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/b/90b990ee153a63755b7569f6eef8d2bee13f2d95.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} a_n =\left\{ \begin{array}{lr} 11, &amp; \text{if\ }n=13 \cdot 14 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 10;\\ 13, &amp; \text{if\ }n=14 \cdot 11 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 12;\\ 14, &amp; \text{if\ }n=11 \cdot 13 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 13;\\ \end{array} \right. \end{eqnarray*}" width="369" height="64" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/3/013a48abdee9022ac7e279ceac12632b1ac9669f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=1}^{2001} a_n = 11 \cdot 10 + 13 \cdot 12 + 14 \cdot 13 = 448 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="371" height="50" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/2/292b1fe64a808a082f7a77a25866e73d22d554a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{array}{lr} 11, &amp; \text{if\ }n=13 \cdot 14 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 10;\\ 13, &amp; \text{if\ }n=14 \cdot 11 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 12;\\ 14, &amp; \text{if\ }n=11 \cdot 13 \cdot k, \quad k = 1,2,\cdots 13;\\ \end{array}$" style="vertical-align: -26px" width="322" height="62" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Find the LCMs of the groups of the numbers.
</p><p>Notice that the groups are relatively prime.
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/3/0c358fc6d1a08cb97f1ddb47e4d1e8362d284981.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="37" height="10" />:
</p><p>11 if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a multiple of 182.
</p><p>13 if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a multiple of 154.
</p><p>14 if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a multiple of 143.
</p><p>When do we see ambiguities (for example: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a multiple of 11, 13, and 14)? This is only done when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/d/4ad0d33e455347864fc322b4f0f41cd8f844e150.png" class="latex" alt="$\operatorname{lcm}(11,13,14)=2002$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="176" height="20" />. However, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/8/7982db74fc657a45d2e7519cac2a26a2cead880f.png" class="latex" alt="$n&lt;2002$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" />, this can never happen.
</p><p>So we have 10 multiples of 182 we have to count (1 to 10 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/0/5a05c3ecf5c065ff5ded5723c738c97bd308323b.png" class="latex" alt="$*182$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />), and similarly, 12 multiples of 154, and 13 multiples of 143. The sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/e/a9e19b3d8c042e6df2f7ca0759becac1b8fcf6c7.png" class="latex" alt="$10*11+12*13+13*14=448$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="258" height="15" />. Select <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6eb65962292d994ce2f9a8af63728883f256b18.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="24" />.
</p><p>~hastapasta
</p>
