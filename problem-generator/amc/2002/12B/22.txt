<p>For all <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integers</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/a/79a0762fa67aa3a9484c36995f9da9712690cf88.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = \frac{1}{\log_n 2002}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="117" height="41" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/4/304f1e040ccd68d5dc60d8fc9606e4ee09fb8a72.png" class="latex" alt="$b = a_2 + a_3 + a_4 + a_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="165" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/b/5fbb91320776a260faf7b908f1c1627084f01956.png" class="latex" alt="$c = a_{10} + a_{11} + a_{12} + a_{13} + a_{14}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="238" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/3/953845363a851f083dcb07b81306d0d7a879f838.png" class="latex" alt="$b- c$" width="38" height="12" /> equals
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/2/45206f948cd7bbb6452b1653d7e795667e461a81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ -2 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ -1  \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{1}{2002} \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{1}{1001} \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="452" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Logarithms#Logarithmic_Properties" class="mw-redirect" title="Logarithms">change of base</a> formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/0/720918cfa93fc9a5b9907dadd3df17c3d6436c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = \frac{1}{\frac{\log 2002}{\log n}} = \left(\frac{1}{\log 2002}\right) \log n$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="254" height="48" />. Thus 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/5/cd5e8a06433c143b797a032b03b8a8cceac18a32.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}b- c &amp;= \left(\frac{1}{\log 2002}\right)(\log 2 + \log 3 + \log 4 + \log 5 - \log 10 - \log 11 - \log 12 - \log 13 - \log 14)\\ &amp;= \left(\frac{1}{\log 2002}\right)\left(\log \frac{2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5}{10 \cdot 11 \cdot 12 \cdot 13 \cdot 14}\right)\\  &amp;= \left(\frac{1}{\log 2002}\right) \log 2002^{-1} = -\left(\frac{\log 2002}{\log 2002}\right) = -1 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(B)}\end{align*}" width="713" height="142" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/f/6bfcd35f7fe5fb3e56e6a0243861dbb45e245f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{\log_a b}=\log_b a$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="113" height="41" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/b/d9b886b4fd6ff66ab6eb6b5ca960086cdee1cd3b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=\log_{2002} n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="17" />. Also notice that if we have a log sum, we multiply, and if we have a log product, we divide. Using these properties, we get that the result is the following:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/9/199b6d891f518c6a7a5b91a927f66ce5a7662845.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_{2002}\left(\frac{2*3*4*5}{10*11*12*13*14}=\frac{1}{11*13*14}=\frac{1}{2002}\right)=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}-1}\]" width="520" height="43" />
</p><p>~yofro
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e0e606d8cff59842ef4dfe59d5b3b355c2c2a12.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 = \frac{1}{\log_2 2002}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="117" height="43" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is also equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/1/fa1814784c8faa27549007480b3ebb5e2a14b694.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba170c6bd647ce931c5fe29b18f29957f32ccb5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 = \frac{\log_2 2}{\log_2 2002}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="117" height="44" />. By the change of bases formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/0/4d0b827ddffca99c35976bad11c7c4f48f4254ec.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 = \log_{2002} 2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="19" />. Following the same reasoning, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/33917d98e21f9f94624029f34a7387e72b95118b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 = \log_{2002} 3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/5/33545e8c50c8a829827183182f10418d747e7837.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4 = \log_{2002} 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="19" /> and so on.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311d7b322cb9b87d3679318e60552b5f88169228.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b = \log_{2002} 2 + \log_{2002} 3 + .....+ \log_{2002} 5 = \log_{2002} 5! = \log_{2002} 120\]" width="484" height="17" />
Now solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, we see that it equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc6bcfb1e6e625cdda30b9b7e12576a46abf4bc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{2002} (10\cdot 11 \cdot 12 \cdot 13 \cdot 14)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="210" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/4/b54e7d72385cda0ab092c518c816da504aff3d06.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b-c = \log_{2002} 120 - \log_{2002} 240240 \rightarrow \log_{2002} \frac{1}{2002} = \boxed{-1}\]" width="455" height="37" />
</p><p>~YBSuburbanTea
</p>
