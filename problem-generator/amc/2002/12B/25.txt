<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/7/997ec2a14060425adfd73456f1cec45d46cfeaf7.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = x^2 + 6x + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="19" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> denote the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Set" title="Set">set</a> of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Point" title="Point">points</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coordinate_plane" class="mw-redirect" title="Coordinate plane">coordinate plane</a> such that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc8ac26f16b1cda3bb82ba42bc6622a0feca8c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) + f(y) \le 0 \qquad \text{and} \qquad f(x)-f(y) \le 0\]" width="343" height="18" />
The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> is closest to 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/3/aa3c26406a508e5388f45148199ff9eaf1eb0353.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 21 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 22 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 23 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 24 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 25$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="397" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The first condition gives us that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/8/bb8ffbea5beac4d271409e70b039ab3fd7fc571a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + 6x + 1 + y^2 + 6y + 1 \le 0 \Longrightarrow (x+3)^2 + (y+3)^2 \le 16\]" width="464" height="19" />
</p><p>which is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">circle</a> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/d/34d7ec45ca2534dc60d5b3cdac3098e473713686.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3,-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> with <a href="/wiki/index.php/Radius" title="Radius">radius</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. The second condition gives us that 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcfbc59e8ec63aca25e470670be7d5deb5925eca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + 6x + 1 - y^2 - 6y - 1 \le 0 \Longrightarrow (x^2 - y^2) + 6(x-y) \le 0 \Longrightarrow (x-y)(x+y+6) \le 0\]" width="594" height="19" />
</p><p>Thus either
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/5/e652e0c8f0c71d919631f0f09e73783e50ad2cae.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x - y \ge 0,\quad x+y+6 \le 0\]" width="206" height="16" />
</p><p>or
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/2/ee27e9853791cc1fc519b576ce48f7f479902128.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x - y \le 0,\quad x+y+6 \ge 0\]" width="206" height="16" />
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2002_12B_AMC-25.png" class="image"><img alt="2002 12B AMC-25.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//3/30/2002_12B_AMC-25.png" width="409" height="406" /></a></div></div>
<p>Each of those lines passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/d/34d7ec45ca2534dc60d5b3cdac3098e473713686.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3,-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/f/7cfbd35086ffefb83637d42166582b43c8a1ff4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, as shown above. Therefore, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> is half of the area of the circle, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/c/c6c8befdd964b6c247e15fcd20191ee68066fa31.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} (\pi \cdot 4^2) = 8\pi \approx \boxed{\textbf{(E) }25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="202" height="38" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Similar to Solution 1, we proceed to get the area of the circle satisfying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/e/53e50f2f05e4d573d00894ef086fc63141b66853.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)+f(y) \le 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/3129b1de4fff4c0120a0c752c648d18a64068979.png" class="latex" alt="$16 \pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/63366e6196e46caa5bab21ff5e0584deadf1269a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)-f(y) \le 0 \implies f(x) \le f(y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="267" height="18" />, we have that by symmetry, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/d/92d038a95751a2d4e924b13a6d754084b97e6c37.png" class="latex" alt="$(y,x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> is not, and vice versa. Therefore, the shaded part of the circle above the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> has the same area as the unshaded part below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />, and the unshaded part above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> has the same area as the shaded part below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. This means that exactly half the circle is shaded, allowing us to divide by two to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/1/df115fab95d5d1c54861c5f078a3d7b691c77d02.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{16 \pi }{2} = 8\pi \approx \boxed{\textbf{(E) }25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="167" height="38" />. ~samrocksnature + ddot1
</p>
