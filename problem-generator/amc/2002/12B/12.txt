<p>For how many integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a739c279dd5e08ea3d01c168e1a960497660a058.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac n{20-n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="33" /> the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perfect_square" title="Perfect square">square</a> of an integer?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/8/2a8fba0bf16c9a6d70ea6e6367598a4861e720e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 1 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 2 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 3 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 4 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/8/e38192f76f1acbfb0fde45c065c064f48107aed3.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = \frac{n}{20-n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="33" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/f/dff5a847457c4beaa2c6809fc9d7b26ebcaa398e.png" class="latex" alt="$x \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> (note that the solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/a/36a68e8aa6ec6f48231ad80a4851f84bacf641b3.png" class="latex" alt="$x &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> do not give any additional solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />). Then rewriting, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/9/629c3d6c6014672be5af1f5ce2c0d64107c402ad.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{20x^2}{x^2 + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="86" height="40" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/8/438b58f52f15d4129d0e6432963a59c2540321bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{gcd}(x^2, x^2 + 1) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="19" />, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/4/664ca690c157c78ee252a0a45144c403a673dbcb.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="16" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. Listing the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/e/aee8b3a293ae992f50be22981ede64c2a45c4f0e.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0, 1, 2 , 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" /> are the only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/6/67632ae2dd0da8ca9cb44c6a185838c7b7167156.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" /> solutions (respectively yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/6/956f55243dfad96ba735f2a22d68b2d98d814155.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 0, 10, 16, 18$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="16" />).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/8/79813fe3de65c5970b8c1b6eecc359c0042447ae.png" class="latex" alt="$n&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/0/90037592859847a0d4c684f62aca389c1919c223.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;20$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> the fraction is negative, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/731fff2fc30062dd7135caff108b191df21ea8bf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=20$" width="52" height="12" /> it is not defined, and for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/17464b8d6f6b51eb6f087a9b1b8b31d8a2327143.png" class="latex" alt="$n\in\{1,\dots,9\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="18" /> it is between 0 and 1.
</p><p>Thus we only need to examine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/6/0b6c20ee88145b100d6de0337ce2c9c0155687f0.png" class="latex" alt="$n\in\{10,\dots,19\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="18" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc1b21210db29332b1a7d9ce088fd457f23aeee.png" class="latex" alt="$n=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> we obviously get the squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> respectively.
</p><p>For prime <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> the fraction will not be an integer, as the denominator will not contain the prime in the numerator.
</p><p>This leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c5465530c63878e98ea21ec9f383e5db084f1a9.png" class="latex" alt="$n\in\{12,14,15,16,18\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="173" height="18" />, and a quick substitution shows that out of these only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/7/c874a6ca8456dd226bf716c2edfdd97d1bfd7665.png" class="latex" alt="$n=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/c/9dce59f6afb62825f31819ae31874cca35417bbf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> yield a square. Therefore, there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/6/67632ae2dd0da8ca9cb44c6a185838c7b7167156.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" /> solutions (respectively yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/6/956f55243dfad96ba735f2a22d68b2d98d814155.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 0, 10, 16, 18$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="16" />).
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/5/b0512c8eefc61dacd8a2da37c7def70714e5bbfa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{20-n} = k^2 \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="130" height="33" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/c/e2c7d62172af0e738dd12f47f3f8ad8ef70cd65f.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/e/1ce4098cee7c08b46beee35f922a528630ec6aba.png" class="latex" alt="$20-n &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="84" height="13" />, otherwise <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/158c83df7af85c5e5065a53e368e68c7170fe952.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{20-n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="33" /> will be negative. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/7/bb779977e90809847bd0695738a271f9f4148b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le n \le 19$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/e/9feb430d115aea0e9e86b02cdd2ae225b49642b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0 = \frac{0}{20-(0)} \le \frac{n}{20-n} \le \frac{19}{20-(19)} = 19\]" width="322" height="42" /> Checking all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/c/97c2596d89fcd8895256c85fdf94eb20d2034c98.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le k^2 \le 19$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="17" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> as the possibilities. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de641a309fc5878c26baf699f2382b7d4b02b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="29" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>For all integers x, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> is always a positive integer. So solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/85706c3e74ffcafd9f7149a8ae315fbebc829bd6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{20-n} = 0$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="90" height="36" />, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/7/2573a65d987603a0e3b347d67704111a3bcd4849.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{20-n} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="90" height="36" />, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc1b21210db29332b1a7d9ce088fd457f23aeee.png" class="latex" alt="$n=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. For all values n less than 0 and greater than 20, the value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/158c83df7af85c5e5065a53e368e68c7170fe952.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{20-n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="33" /> is negative, so now try values of n between 10 and 20. Quick substitution finds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> which yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69271b422e022ea65ff1f288eddfcfa82afd8ab7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2$" width="43" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/2/b022dc8ef6d33931fc1ae3d27df3ea0a81b52936.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3$" width="43" height="12" /> respectively. 4 values, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de641a309fc5878c26baf699f2382b7d4b02b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="29" />
</p>
