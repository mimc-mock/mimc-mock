<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a649aee592a9c37d2f9b5d5239f5e06e533130.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286e989082359d570543d0269004ce484008ce68.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 2$" width="60" height="13" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/5/20552f4dac85af4806831750082aae9773f6b706.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  unitsize(4cm);  pair A, B, C, D, M;  A = (1.768,0.935); B = (1.414,0); C = (0,0); D = (1.768,0); M = (0.707,0);  draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(A--D); draw(D--B); draw(A--M);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); label(&quot;$M$&quot;,M,S); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(A+D)/2,E); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(B+D)/2,S); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,(C+M)/2,S); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,(M+B)/2,S); label(&quot;$2a$&quot;,(A+M)/2,SE); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(A+B)/2,SE); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(A+C)/2,NW);  draw(rightanglemark(B,D,A,3));  [/asy]" width="365" height="222" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> extended past <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/c/cdc975a5b35343ca18565b3a7cce9827d332bbbd.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = x$" width="63" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/85788f8dcf5ffb92327bf3a6751433064a5a6450.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />.
Using the Pythagorean Theorem, we obtain the equations
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/4/8349c8156e4b5b1bffc9a5003fc3597575d9a704.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}  x^2 + y^2 = 1 \hspace{0.5cm}(1)\\ x^2 + y^2 + 2ya + a^2 = 4a^2 \hspace{0.5cm}(2)\\ x^2 + y^2 + 4ya + 4a^2 = 4 \hspace{0.5cm}(3) \end{align*}" width="242" height="75" />
</p><p>Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> equation from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b88ea3916e8089c7edbccc3ff91e58e01a6e2682.png" class="latex" alt="$(3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/2/de2d2ff46f69061b7b7bd7f20a74d1c1adbbae08.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 2ya + a^2 = 4a^2 - 1 \hspace{0.5cm}(4)\\ 4ya + 4a^2 = 3 \hspace{0.5cm}(5) \end{align*}" width="194" height="47" />
</p><p>Then, subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/f/ebf9b0d33251531e39f00dcb5dde9f96de857fe4.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times (4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/7/737a366a904e00f936f8728869ad8f2587dab46f.png" class="latex" alt="$(5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> and rearranging, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/3/323737af46a15ea9df7570b0ff9e1743a78b096c.png" class="latex" alt="$10a^2 = 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5ff02f26f2b0fd8323a4a65dfe0e6b6eb6fcd4e.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 2a = \sqrt{2}\Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="187" height="29" />
</p><p>~greenturtle 11/28/2017
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2002_12B_AMC-23.png" class="image"><img alt="2002 12B AMC-23.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//9/98/2002_12B_AMC-23.png" width="278" height="121" /></a>
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the foot of the median from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, and we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2c9e0fdf79fc97acdc927584a47f082da0c13ee.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = BC = 2a$" width="125" height="13" />. Then by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Law_of_Cosines" title="Law of Cosines">Law of Cosines</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/1/9119fa445a3201bfaa4eeb8bab4fb63ba4a15b3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD, \triangle ACD$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="127" height="17" />, we have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/5/825119a3151ab7cbe20f9219217e9e0388e29b20.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 1^2 &amp;= a^2 + (2a)^2 - 2(a)(2a)\cos ADB \\ 2^2 &amp;= a^2 + (2a)^2 - 2(a)(2a)\cos ADC  \end{align*}" width="281" height="47" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/a/e7a835092dad3bd2ae3bf04a6c52cf56931e780e.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos ADC = \cos (180 - ADB) = -\cos ADB$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="339" height="18" />, we can add these two equations and get 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/e/b7ef5bf2a35df8f95e354145fc0ffc141e39ae7a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5 = 10a^2\]" width="66" height="15" />
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/3/5230645e90ec7f6957a44a32a3be2d6421db2d5c.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="60" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/3175e01d90a75b34b5da8687241cbd7bbcbdc2ec.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 2a = \sqrt{2} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="21" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>From <a href="/wiki/index.php/Stewart%27s_Theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Stewart&#39;s Theorem">Stewart's Theorem</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/b/88be3abe86be5ec685cab0597b12cbec10267176.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)(1/2)a(2) + (1)(1/2)a(1) = (a)(a)(a) + (1/2)a(a)(1/2)a.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="463" height="18" /> Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/4/eb4b1da8b0fb1c2abd9c879d640b4fc1a16d330e.png" class="latex" alt="$(5/4)a^3 = (5/2)a \implies (5/4)a^2 = 5/2 \implies a^2 = 2 \implies a = \boxed{\sqrt{2}}.$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="515" height="30" />
- awu2014
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_[Pappus's_Median_Theorem]"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.5BPappus.27s_Median_Theorem.5D">Solution 4 [Pappus's Median Theorem]</span></h3>
<p>There is a theorem in geometry known as Pappus's Median Theorem. It states that if you have triangle ABC, and you draw a median from point A to side BC (label this as Ma), then: (Ma)^2 = (1/4)(2(b^2) + 2(c^2) - (a^2)). Note that b is side AC, c is side AB, and a is side BC. For this specific problem, call where the median from A hits side BC point M. Let MB = MC = x. Then AM = 2x. Now, we can plug into the formula given above: Ma = 2x, b = 2, c = 1, and a = 2x. After some simple algebra, we find x = sqrt(2)/2. Then, BC = a = sqrt(2). 
</p><p>-Flames
</p>
