<p>A point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is randomly selected from the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">rectangular</a> region with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74fb6915c6728fca89c809146694f23ead347f6.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0),(2,0),(2,1),(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="18" />. What is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Probability" title="Probability">probability</a> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is closer to the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Origin" title="Origin">origin</a> than it is to the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/f/1bf8a375ffe0e1e7907d69a3c65ea917f15b9619.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 12 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 23 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 34 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 45 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="357" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2002_12B_AMC-18.png" class="image"><img alt="2002 12B AMC-18.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//c/c1/2002_12B_AMC-18.png" width="406" height="193" /></a>
</p><p><br />
The region containing the points closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> than to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perpendicular_bisector" title="Perpendicular bisector">perpendicular bisector</a> of the segment with endpoints <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/6/a5660fa8be0071663223a7cb9144a2972078866b.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0),(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="18" />. The perpendicular bisector passes through midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/6/a5660fa8be0071663223a7cb9144a2972078866b.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0),(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="18" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c5934f5024094252850a45c33d3a6b18518bde61.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 32, \frac 12\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="57" height="43" />, the center of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Unit_square" title="Unit square">unit square</a> with coordinates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/a/61a11c5fbf5c24dc236b75da7f19b76e1b9ea7d4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0),(2,0),(2,1),(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="18" />. Thus, it cuts the unit square into two equal halves of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3cc0ad966b3e637cd199607cbf0d1befc0e491.png" class="latex" alt="$1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. The total area of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so the area closer to the origin than to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and in the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/451a6518fa5061a8166c3e49f3f92be60a417573.png" class="latex" alt="$2 - \frac 12 = \frac 32$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />. The probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/1/5f16a88bc53818e7c5e0c91c2de47609bfa0abca.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3/2}{2} = \frac 34 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="122" height="39" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Assume that the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is randomly chosen within the rectangle with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. In this case, the region for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to be closer to the origin than to point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> occupies exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the area of the rectangle, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/15788e198a53ced87268aea78fcde68a84de92c0.png" class="latex" alt="$1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="13" /> square units.
</p><p><br />
If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is chosen within the square with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c67f1e6c098104b1cb8f1cdd6bb16f689ac3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6bb1c456d71ee24e7a0ee603d226a7992e5da3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> which has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> square unit, it is for sure closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Now if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> can only be chosen within the rectangle with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c67f1e6c098104b1cb8f1cdd6bb16f689ac3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6bb1c456d71ee24e7a0ee603d226a7992e5da3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, then the square region is removed and the area for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to be closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04c2d1bd65c882efed8513bbbf77022aba66364.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is then decreased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> square unit, left with only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192b4a258ee998961a99523fd0f0a029f9ea194a.png" class="latex" alt="$0.5$" width="22" height="12" /> square unit.
</p><p><br />
Thus the probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f43de4dfa1c99a762cac2f954b50469ebc590007.png" class="latex" alt="$(3.1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/2/cd27c8a8fb52583ec806fd67cac72262850d1de4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{0.5}{2}=\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="63" height="38" /> and that of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is closer to the origin is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/9/c29826428a8261b526c66746f4b59d9ab02b86c5.png" class="latex" alt="$1-\frac{1}{4}=\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="80" height="38" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484f88154fadcf9c781bba19becdae88696b77f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
~ Nafer
</p>
