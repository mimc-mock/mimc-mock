<p>The product of three consecutive positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> times their sum. What is the sum of their <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perfect_square" title="Perfect square">squares</a>?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d7ed05f869b41d88f50c4759a6665dfb7da03c2.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 50 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 77 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 110 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 149 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 194$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="415" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the three consecutive positive integers be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/6/cc6873715a60ff7706da71b8cad30cead78b1ad3.png" class="latex" alt="$a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/b/64be5731bafe97ab8fb5d0f9c52c1c5362f963d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" />. Since the mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, the sum of the integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1cb96a3344461b85f9e6eb91850987abd720a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$3a$" width="18" height="12" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> times the sum is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00ef39094a9c25a9e8d032575c2734b9578c925f.png" class="latex" alt="$24a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="12" />. With this, we now know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/1/b51c0d2d76b45f6e92d0a9239aee6503e9521fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$a(a-1)(a+1)=24a\Rightarrow(a-1)(a+1)=24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="352" height="18" />.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5006ca161ac23e4c284a05b89e72bf8311b7fc7.png" class="latex" alt="$24=4\times6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/53897e4b47f6049266b4bffcef91bc949385b5e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5$" width="42" height="12" />. Hence, the sum of the squares is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/8/6e8b0fcbf4debc463aab6a92313ea573650060fe.png" class="latex" alt="$4^2+5^2+6^2=\boxed{\mathrm{ (B)}\ 77}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="181" height="29" />.
</p>
