<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Convex_polygon" title="Convex polygon">convex</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadrilateral" title="Quadrilateral">quadrilateral</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" /> contains a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> in its interior such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/d/f7d8d2226f3bad723a1a72d46b0acf7d64699be5.png" class="latex" alt="$PA = 24, PB = 32, PC = 28, PD = 45$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="308" height="16" />. Find the perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d743e4ed99802a3c4570b1e5f9c752fe35a4c7eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 4\sqrt{2002} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 2\sqrt{8465} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="295" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b40925f849c32fcd959193fc31fab1d1a98e4eaf.png" class="latex" alt="$(48+\sqrt{2002}) \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 2\sqrt{8633} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 4(36 + \sqrt{113})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/4/68414870d9584f78410cfea4874a58e80bd8a5ce.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[ABCD] = 2002 \le \frac 12 (AC \cdot BD)\]" width="248" height="37" />
(This is true for any convex quadrilateral: split the quadrilateral along <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and then using the triangle area formula to evaluate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/a/63a0c06761ee664f3ab0910bbf3336158cbf1645.png" class="latex" alt="$[ACB]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="50" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/1/fa16d5df87a703864688635578a8f358b31ad6ef.png" class="latex" alt="$[ACD]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="51" height="18" />), with equality only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39facf4ed73f7886f111ff2466878db18e5d8958.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC} \perp \overline{BD}$" width="82" height="15" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle_inequality" class="mw-redirect" title="Triangle inequality">triangle inequality</a>,  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/3/47354ad4fbf58262bd4a168ce26499d05b9aebb4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}AC &amp;\le PA + PC = 52\\ BD &amp;\le PB + PD = 77\end{align*}" width="177" height="40" />
</p><p>with equality if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> lies on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/4373e7b4be983c0315e82e1fda1df66ed34dd7fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BD}$" width="30" height="15" /> respectively. Thus
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/3/763500d2bade003bb04e8fe0bcba8cc118de1a63.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2002 \le \frac{1}{2} AC \cdot BD \le \frac 12 \cdot 52 \cdot 77 = 2002\]" width="304" height="37" />
</p><p>Since we have the equality case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39facf4ed73f7886f111ff2466878db18e5d8958.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC} \perp \overline{BD}$" width="82" height="15" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, as shown below. 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/0/be0cac31118507011fcd067dec64c89d4f9eee03.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(0.6); pair A = (0,0), B = (40,0), C = (25.6 * 52 / 24, 19.2 * 52 / 24), D = (40 - (40-25.6)*77/32,19.2*77/32), P = (25.6,19.2), Q = (25.6, 18.5); pair E=(A+P)/2, F=(B+P)/2, G=(C+P)/2, H=(D+P)/2; draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(A--P--B--P--C--P--D); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,WSW); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,ESE); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,ESE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;\(P\)&quot;,Q,SSW); label(&quot;24&quot;,E,WNW); label(&quot;32&quot;,F,WSW); label(&quot;28&quot;,G,ESE); label(&quot;45&quot;,H,ENE); draw(rightanglemark(C,P,D,50)); [/asy]" width="335" height="275" /></center>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>,  
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a065c8fd0192f46aedd285c32c15ca942464e10.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} AB = \sqrt{PA^2 + PB^2} &amp; = \sqrt{24^2 + 32^2} = 40\\ BC = \sqrt{PB^2 + PC^2} &amp; = \sqrt{32^2 + 28^2} = 4\sqrt{113}\\ CD = \sqrt{PC^2 + PD^2} &amp; = \sqrt{28^2 + 45^2} = 53\\ DA = \sqrt{PD^2 + PA^2} &amp; = \sqrt{45^2 + 24^2} = 51 \end{align*}" width="353" height="108" />
</p><p>The perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/2/9822037ad21e4f2ece1212694d5b0fec0cca986d.png" class="latex" alt="$AB + BC + CD + DA = 4(36 + \sqrt{113}) \Rightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="21" />.
</p>
