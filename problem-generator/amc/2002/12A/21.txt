<p>Consider the sequence of numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/8/2e89f1d752780c25052311294b06148c526a2fb5.png" class="latex" alt="$4,7,1,8,9,7,6,\dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="16" /> For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b9b7a46559a729577c3ca04dd2d7e66de16b3f.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />-th term of the sequence is the units digit of the sum of the two previous terms. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74a070eea97d9363f58a257e05e75fc69efa753.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> denote the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> terms of this sequence. The smallest value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/e/18e57efd4af6c0e358554e7396258d85538143d3.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n&gt;10,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="16" /> is: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/c/3cc490de10aaecf1ee23e0cba96e50f6acc92f8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }1992 \qquad \text{(B) }1999 \qquad \text{(C) }2001 \qquad \text{(D) }2002 \qquad \text{(E) }2004$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="466" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The sequence is infinite. As there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> pairs of digits, sooner or later a pair of consecutive digits will occur for the second time. As each next digit only depends on the previous two, from this point on the sequence will be periodic.
</p><p>(Additionally, as every two consecutive digits uniquely determine the <i>previous</i> one as well, the first pair of digits that will occur twice must be the first pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/129cf570584aaf0e62c4213d318563c196e866d1.png" class="latex" alt="$4,7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="15" />.)
</p><p>Hence it is a good idea to find the period. Writing down more terms of the sequence, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/4/1049243c926e198e5a723b9ad4f30bbbe056255a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4,7,1,8,9,7,6,3,9,2,1,3,4,7,\dots\]" width="259" height="16" />
</p><p>and we found the period. The length of the period is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, and its sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/4/414ec8814d5ca6aa2ab3418ac575e1cf751d56af.png" class="latex" alt="$4+7+\cdots+1+3 = 60$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="188" height="14" />. Hence for each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/d/23dd4a4a61d38fef057984d5b03e7474bc39607f.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{12k} = 60k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="84" height="15" />.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/c/00c8b5c5176d571b78c8adeb6ab60b39f97a6b63.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor 10000/60 \rfloor = 166$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="139" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/d/11d6b304f870f4923b256dc0681308c8c062103c.png" class="latex" alt="$166\cdot 12 = 1992$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="118" height="13" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab2fe3f0686b8ab13b20c67a2ab0742c1f6384af.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{1992} = 60\cdot 166 = 9960$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="181" height="15" />.
The rest can now be computed by hand, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e49884c954044d2efbd97af4d9a3b37732eb04c.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{1998} = 9960+4+7+1+8+9+7= 9996$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="346" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e22cf565622848e807056e5619f5d7dc63ce830.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{1999}=9996 + 6 = 10002$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="199" height="15" />, thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5f6c525be06ce01e21bbd0e7dfbbbbca492efa.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(B)1999}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="77" height="29" />.
</p>
