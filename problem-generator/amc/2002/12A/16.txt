<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>This is not too bad using casework. 
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 3: This happens in only one way <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bb913ad463dcc2916d4135977f0d06f20ad93cd.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and Sergio can choose a number from 4 to 10, inclusive. There are 7 ways that Sergio gets a desirable number here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 4: This once again happens in only one way <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b6a9f4b07be4cb5a24874bb192da96ca853ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Sergio can choose a number from 5 to 10, so 6 ways here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 5: This can happen in two ways <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7c05a83f0a33c3f141fbfb48c803d0e906974e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Sergio can choose a number from 6 to 10, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe05e8a7e0d075708f5a5fbbf0f841551ba38057.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot5=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> ways here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 6: Two ways here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/f/77f46e8ad13e4a3e569bb6c47d1d642243a3119d.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f4d1e83d6eef61f31d78844deb189c54a7abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Sergio can choose a number from 7 to 10, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f2aab8c71f7caee70a3e1cd0db136459243229.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot4=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" /> here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 7: Two ways here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea9d3308a17610dcb38bfd84b443bdd0ea403605.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97fadfe6bdbd1adb164f263b52f2a290723ad45c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Sergio can choose from 8 to 10, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec53f7be728ae20c9dabca93474181a913ea808.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot3=6$" width="64" height="12" /> ways here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 8: Only one way possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/4/564a0113de9b4ac87eeb5fa411e0937162315e29.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />). Sergio chooses 9 or 10, so 2 ways here.
</p><p>Tina gets a sum of 9: Only one way <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc04892c672addda50fb21cd723f160be2443f72.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Sergio must choose 10, so 1 way.
</p><p>In all, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/d/fcd851df91c3cc471029c06ca69a73eafc555569.png" class="latex" alt="$7+6+10+8+6+2+1=40$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="248" height="14" /> ways. Tina chooses two distinct numbers in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee559317aed18491b8093c9dffcc7931e41f1991.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{5}{2}=10$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" /> ways while Sergio chooses a number in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> ways, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b085668dbec5f7ac7ce468298ca36deca25cf5.png" class="latex" alt="$10\cdot 10=100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> ways in all. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1f1d8875587f32ba55d179e16e2b16536c7bb96.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{100}=\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="38" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/3/36385e908426e3a89be2996eec033d15df861f2d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(A)}\frac{2}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="50" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We invoke some symmetry. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> denote Tina's sum, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> denote Sergio's number. Observe that, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37c99072191bc33b741343e05328472c55b19608.png" class="latex" alt="$i = 2, 3, \ldots, 10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="114" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4acffef64b1c46a92481bbe62a341f8ffe5ece15.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Pr}(T=i) = \text{Pr}(T=12-i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="214" height="18" />.
</p><p>If Tina's sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" />, then the probability that Sergio's number is larger than Tina's sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/a/1da6ff8ead6f5ce401d32c7dd70df7568a720655.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10-i}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="49" height="38" />. Thus, the probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f24ff01ce8e35402fec7f992bbec8fa146f333c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P = \text{Pr}(S&gt;T) = \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=i) \times \frac{10-i}{10}\]" width="319" height="50" />
</p><p>Using the symmetry observation, we can also write the above sum as
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f197fc9994b16873f3169a391ef2f1242b263017.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P = \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=12-i) \times \frac{10-i}{10} = \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=i) \times \frac{i-2}{10}\]" width="446" height="50" />
where the last equality follows as we reversed the indices of the sum (by replacing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/5/e1506008537f6da4236d2f32d3584a535738f2b5.png" class="latex" alt="$12-i$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" />). Thus, adding the two equivalent expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/f/7bf12211f7b003811deac24ec1d62aa1764a2c88.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 2P &amp;= \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=i) \times \left(\frac{10-i}{10} + \frac{i-2}{10}\right) \\ &amp;= \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=i) \times \frac{4}{5} \\ &amp;= \frac{4}{5} \sum_{i=2}^{10} \text{Pr}(T=i) \\ &amp;= \frac{4}{5} \end{align*}" width="310" height="215" />
</p><p>Since this represents twice the desired probability, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc0cef2d523584cf7d5d36be13d95b1b34cdb3a0.png" class="latex" alt="$P = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} \frac{2}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="92" height="48" />. -scrabbler94
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We have 5 cases, if Tina choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f55e0a11e29af7baee666c19c147e6f7c1923e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>The number of ways of choosing 2 numbers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/53130d1c08583295cd18d9042920935e82bae549.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" />.
</p>
<hr />
<p>Case 1: Tina chooses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>In this case, since the numbers are distinct, Tina can choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d2979bdee65809caa2c3c11136db79a912e4ad28.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 2), (1, 3), (1, 4),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/994cd14ccf0cd9e6dca73b0673ca4f322ec73d99.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 5).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" />
</p><p>If Tina chooses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> which sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, Sergio only has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fca23217407e3615b4c90fbb9a1fde93d8456e79.png" class="latex" alt="$10-3=7$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> choices.
</p><p>Since the sum of the combined numbers increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> every time for this specific case, Sergio has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> less choice every time.
</p><p>Therefore, the probability of this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d2259cf6932b6e5c3a45dfe617d98c4778d8985.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7+6+5+4}{10 \cdot \binom{5}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="105" height="45" />.
</p>
<hr />
<p>If you do this over and over again you will see that you have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/4/544e24557d589786633ab63e4ce268f1ee1bfed2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(7+6+5+4)+(7+5+4+3)+(6+5+3+2)+(5+4+3+1)+(4+3+2+1)}{10 \cdot \binom{5}{2}} = \frac{80}{100} = \frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="770" height="46" /> probability.
</p><p>But since we overcounted by 2 (e.g. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69273482bff8727194f6407e55ac1bfa94d9c555.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7a25c234892fb8a7738bc31e5ccee3bf9ad5afc.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />) we need to divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e4797950b5e812b65381cbc5fc33ad2db5f57aff.png" class="latex" alt="$2.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d2472f87cc3bc22ce637054ab8942bdf977e8d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{5} \div 2 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} \frac{2}{5}}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="126" height="48" />
</p><p>~mathboy282

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Assume Sergio chooses from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/b/2cba7448f562cfd148ecbeeb3bdd81a9e13fd920.png" class="latex" alt="${2,3,\ldots,10}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />. The probability of Tina getting a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/179631a9be3047af0432ea70d1fe200ff4f4577b.png" class="latex" alt="$6+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" /> and a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/b/a1b2e3749f140d8825e7aa6225b1c0511b6be450.png" class="latex" alt="$6-x$" width="41" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/3/863c54dbe16fb785b4e4578b547435dd48b367cb.png" class="latex" alt="$x \leq 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, are equal due to symmetry. The probability of Sergio choosing numbers higher/lower than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/179631a9be3047af0432ea70d1fe200ff4f4577b.png" class="latex" alt="$6+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" /> is equal to him choosing numbers lower/higher than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/b/a1b2e3749f140d8825e7aa6225b1c0511b6be450.png" class="latex" alt="$6-x$" width="41" height="12" />. Therefore over all of Tina's sums, the probability of Sergio choosing a number higher is equal to the probability of choosing a number lower. 
</p><p>The probability that they get the same value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d3dba22edcee4ff7213635505e90128beebb42e.png" class="latex" alt="$1/9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />, so the probability of Sergio getting a higher number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a37cadbc59f3fff75312977a38aa2db2a2d4ae5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(9-1)/2}{9} = \frac49$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="38" />. 
</p><p>Sergio never wins when choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> so the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/9/049a0bb81a046e19c7d06139c95de8071cea9ab9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac49 \cdot \frac{9}{10} + (0)\frac{1}{10} = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} \frac{2}{5}}.$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="201" height="49" />
</p><p>~zeric
