<p>Several sets of prime numbers, such as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/9/9c970896bd493c2bf552340c55c6997025f65ac6.png" class="latex" alt="$\{7,83,421,659\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" /> use each of the nine nonzero digits exactly once. What is the smallest possible sum such a set of primes could have?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/b/a1bb0f492a95e2cfb8b4fe37e8735ef573b3b697.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }193 \qquad \text{(B) }207 \qquad \text{(C) }225 \qquad \text{(D) }252 \qquad \text{(E) }447$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="421" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Neither of the digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> can be a units digit of a prime. Therefore the sum of the set is at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/4/6146b2e01ac9cdceb3e260e2906cbc450ee479ff.png" class="latex" alt="$40 + 60 + 80 + 1 + 2 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 = 207$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="338" height="14" />.
</p><p>We can indeed create a set of primes with this sum, for example the following sets work: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/c/75c5c7f3721e0f0e81558b2b88852feb753a1a81.png" class="latex" alt="$\{ 41, 67, 89, 2, 3, 5 \}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/2/5422d0b6553871fab09388f644bcf46e6f19aea5.png" class="latex" alt="$\{ 43, 61, 89, 2, 5, 7 \}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/c/d8ce958432b2239ca7384475b81459b03b400fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$207\implies \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="115" height="29" />.
</p>
