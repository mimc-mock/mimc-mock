<p>Find the number of ordered pairs of real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/8/c58deda369560c86483c2b569e21246e7a2cdda9.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+bi)^{2002} = a-bi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="157" height="19" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/291e9a8adb69b87c00a5f82d3178d9516f54bc46.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }1001 \qquad \text{(B) }1002 \qquad \text{(C) }2001 \qquad \text{(D) }2002 \qquad \text{(E) }2004$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="466" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78edde7d8b5252f2e4f8b3ef35b064cbd7e5dfc4.png" class="latex" alt="$s=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="103" height="18" /> be the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e780fda439b2829922a704a60b31c5ed77191.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" />. Then the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea99c70907d685e6f6f74f821812eaa5602af69.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+bi)^{2002}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="19" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/a/cbab48446b2c4319241a6e765056a6cd90e1ce29.png" class="latex" alt="$s^{2002}$" width="34" height="15" />, while the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/a/77ac597d092fe9c029e5aec8c1fa43688a8df01e.png" class="latex" alt="$a-bi$" width="45" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. We get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75baf2c0b6f5e37c2c7e9cd1a522bbfdb6d1138.png" class="latex" alt="$s^{2002}=s$" width="68" height="15" />, hence either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758bb72cac19fa92aa1a6a94b6b69167df026b39.png" class="latex" alt="$s=0$" width="41" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/1/fb16e2177f55574e2538c0d9b240b1ee7d46d767.png" class="latex" alt="$s=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758bb72cac19fa92aa1a6a94b6b69167df026b39.png" class="latex" alt="$s=0$" width="41" height="12" /> we get a single solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4af030c5fbe713412d4f6407e41c9d626884ab1c.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />.
</p><p>Let's now assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/1/fb16e2177f55574e2538c0d9b240b1ee7d46d767.png" class="latex" alt="$s=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. Multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e780fda439b2829922a704a60b31c5ed77191.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" />. The left hand side becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/5/d050150de73bc8d4434696fe186fa97854ebadfc.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+bi)^{2003}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="19" />, the right hand side becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/3/3337d57d9f063332757b578406f6be7a03645f52.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-bi)(a+bi)=a^2 + b^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="19" />. Hence the solutions for this case are precisely all the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b910f4dafca80b54c6e7bedf63b6485eebff0764.png" class="latex" alt="$2003$" width="35" height="12" />rd complex roots of unity, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b910f4dafca80b54c6e7bedf63b6485eebff0764.png" class="latex" alt="$2003$" width="35" height="12" /> of those.
</p><p>The total number of solutions is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/a/36a1aa767ea306d610f0f12612bc552b7bd9030f.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2003 = \boxed{2004}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="140" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As in the other solution, split the problem into when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758bb72cac19fa92aa1a6a94b6b69167df026b39.png" class="latex" alt="$s=0$" width="41" height="12" /> and when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/1/fb16e2177f55574e2538c0d9b240b1ee7d46d767.png" class="latex" alt="$s=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/1/fb16e2177f55574e2538c0d9b240b1ee7d46d767.png" class="latex" alt="$s=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/5/345a92c362675da48f06a2c7368f6635eb99f18f.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="172" height="14" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563f6a0ba1d04671bfd6de850104d051ce105589.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+bi)^{2002}= \cos(2002\theta)+i\sin(2002\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="307" height="19" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/d/49d5454e8b610f8737d4211a889bd58723adc59e.png" class="latex" alt="$=a-bi= \cos\theta-i\sin\theta= \cos(-\theta)+i\sin(-\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="367" height="18" />
</p><p>so we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/d/b7dcb60bdb6e277b51485b0ebb45b112fc1cfb1a.png" class="latex" alt="$2002\theta=-\theta+2\pi k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="144" height="14" /> and hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/3/3f3f053637b0be6fd26893daefd8b9c1d4241fe8.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta=\frac{2\pi k}{2003}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> is restricted to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa914ed4bd30af60a82d993a58fafc8144fdf3a.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,2\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="48" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> can range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" /> inclusive, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/4/954dedcc8ba686cd0d7112bbb008a6bc9f18052a.png" class="latex" alt="$2002-0+1=2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="158" height="14" /> values. Thus the total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/2/b72c22ed2a8c2dc5674eface8b084f94e2e04259.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2003 = \boxed{\textbf{(E)}\  2004}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="173" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e780fda439b2829922a704a60b31c5ed77191.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> for this to be true, as shown in the above solutions. We know this because we are taking magnitude to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b910f4dafca80b54c6e7bedf63b6485eebff0764.png" class="latex" alt="$2003$" width="35" height="12" />rd power, and if the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e780fda439b2829922a704a60b31c5ed77191.png" class="latex" alt="$a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="14" /> is larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, it will increase and if it is smaller than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> it will decrease. However, the magnitude on the RHS is still <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, so this is not possible. Again, only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/0/400365c088499af977542271fc6363b086a63499.png" class="latex" alt="$r=0$" width="41" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1930619a38fceee8e02990ea79026a348193307e.png" class="latex" alt="$r=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> satisfy the equation.
</p><p>Now if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/0/400365c088499af977542271fc6363b086a63499.png" class="latex" alt="$r=0$" width="41" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>However if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1930619a38fceee8e02990ea79026a348193307e.png" class="latex" alt="$r=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />, we then have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/c/3bc56ccf43729e610c0c1214b99850eccc11b65a.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(2002 \theta) = \cos(-\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="172" height="20" />. This has solution of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/5/8656595bf44ec840dac700925abe3bcbccab0b46.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta = 0$" width="42" height="13" />. This would represent the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/8/1d81097e0e0bc7729f84148e991686798d47eb4d.png" class="latex" alt="$1+0i$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="14" />, with conjugate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/f/52fb90379e58ef74f69cc44417fa25aa56a80341.png" class="latex" alt="$1-0i$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" />. This works because the magnitude is the same and the angle is nothing anyways. We multiply angle by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" /> through De Moivre's Theorem and also we do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4ff26b3f049a028b859d12e88fe32c789ce46683.png" class="latex" alt="$-\theta$" width="22" height="13" /> because it is a reflection, angles therefore is negative.
</p><p>We then write:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/b/45b5e457d7ab6abec666c681b2b1b0d4d354c945.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(2002 \theta) = \cos(360-\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="207" height="20" /> which has solution of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/9/8098bffeac059f155421625385fe359139ae96cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta = \frac{360}{2003}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="37" />. 
</p><p>We can also write:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/c/e5cfc44f4b0431dcf4a401414aeff07be6ef82fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(2002 \theta) = \cos(720-\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="207" height="20" /> which has solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/c/ecc0ebadfdb945ab829c9687559f4c103187d5be.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta = \frac{720}{2003}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="37" />.
</p><p>We notice that it is simply headed upwards and the answer is of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/d/a6de48da3af6c3dab0530d51da02ed9bbe3b7be3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{720}{2003} n$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="51" height="37" />, where n is some integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to infinity, inclusive.
</p><p>Well wait, it repeats itself <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/f/2bf6ef1b0443e2f6e7595dfff39d429a74e42bb9.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />, that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/2/c62a47cbc153efdf4611d8660429ea879be3f192.png" class="latex" alt="$360$" width="26" height="12" /> which is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />! Hence we only have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" /> as original solutions, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b910f4dafca80b54c6e7bedf63b6485eebff0764.png" class="latex" alt="$2003$" width="35" height="12" /> solutions.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/a/36a1aa767ea306d610f0f12612bc552b7bd9030f.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2003 = \boxed{2004}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="140" height="23" />.
</p><p><br />
