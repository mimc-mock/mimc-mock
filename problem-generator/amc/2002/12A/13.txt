<p>Two different positive numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> each differ from their reciprocals by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/f/27f4d3eee0b9baea5dbc30e39588e80537d721f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }1 \qquad \text{(B) }2 \qquad \text{(C) }\sqrt 5 \qquad \text{(D) }\sqrt 6 \qquad \text{(E) }3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="360" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Each of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is a solution to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/e/23e3a23c9cda9f1a4ab25f5cda92a560d7e94974.png" class="latex" alt="$\left| x - \frac 1x \right| = 1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="91" height="43" />.
</p><p>Hence it is either a solution to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4eee045bde9d69291fa1ed1ba517d875328a5e22.png" class="latex" alt="$x - \frac 1x = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, or to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/9/2b9c99d6ed0f4f8cf9e869601b1c82f6071a0d7d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 1x - x = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />. Then it must be a solution either to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b13c6b87b616db764c83771e944337285ca2a77.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - x - 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="15" />, or to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b10e9a98751247429fb572343fc381d9cd3052.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + x - 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="114" height="16" />.
</p><p>There are in total four such values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/4/eb4bf0f4c75af2fed649b07514d3876afcc06106.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pm 1 \pm \sqrt 5}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="41" />. 
</p><p>Out of these, two are positive: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/4/a0444335214ba74491163ba0ed6f4db190f449e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-1+\sqrt 5}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10ba702dd4138e9abffe2bbe0acb704702a11fb3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1+\sqrt 5}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="41" />. We can easily check that both of them indeed have the required property, and their sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/7/5d77f562ecfb5ff1f3bb5be3d7bf0230e0393a90.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-1+\sqrt 5}2 + \frac{1+\sqrt 5}2 = \boxed{(C) \sqrt 5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="244" height="41" />.
</p>
