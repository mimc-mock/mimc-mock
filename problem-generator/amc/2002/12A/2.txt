<p>Cindy was asked by her teacher to subtract 3 from a certain number and then divide the result by 9. Instead, she subtracted 9 and then divided the result by 3, giving an answer of 43. What would her answer have been had she worked the problem correctly?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/3/993d79f6c262c2062d52d8f2554b800a410f0363.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 15\qquad \textbf{(B) } 34\qquad \textbf{(C) } 43\qquad \textbf{(D) } 51\qquad \textbf{(E) } 138$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="410" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We work backwards; the number that Cindy started with is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/e/1fe3b3fca1c779d12a71ef45a6173c9b9cbe4552.png" class="latex" alt="$3(43)+9=138$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" />. Now, the correct result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/6/72611fa39c5133e4c5998563b2506347fb9273e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{138-3}{9}=\frac{135}{9}=15$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="160" height="37" />. Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108b3d3f4bf76cf120574a81e8589b8eec356cba.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }15}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="67" height="30" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the number be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. We transform the problem into an equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/e/e8e838d4aaaca883c5dcbbc2b38c72e99ffb7742.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x-9}{3}=43$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />. Solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/6/006d06b740a326eb51899d5fdf75d73ebd5798e8.png" class="latex" alt="$x=138$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Therefore, the correct result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/a/d9afcf8f9cb7bc8c579a68b1d64104bde2ca3122.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{138-3}{9}=\frac{135}{9}=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }15}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="209" height="38" />.
</p>
