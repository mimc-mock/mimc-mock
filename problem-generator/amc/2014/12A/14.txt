<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5ba70df5a2f9630194f32f50010cd2e78318c98d.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b&lt;c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" /> be three integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> is an arithmetic progression and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c330d1138fdde755f13c54e34de11c2d0c4d680.png" class="latex" alt="$a,c,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> is a geometric progression.  What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/f/27fac67c3da012a3bcada3f79bfa5770f11b9dd3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }-2\qquad \textbf{(B) }1\qquad \textbf{(C) }2\qquad \textbf{(D) }4\qquad \textbf{(E) }6\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="375" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/8278c99d5e9f42c042413525178c3c43a5f6390b.png" class="latex" alt="$b-a=c-b$" width="102" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/a/b3a126e9a76abc1dddf2603ae4dcf6a88e943a82.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2b-c$" width="81" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c330d1138fdde755f13c54e34de11c2d0c4d680.png" class="latex" alt="$a,c,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> is geometric, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/c/c5cf17d99e641497950e85c557bfbcfb10cd42bb.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2=ab=(2b-c)b \Rightarrow 2b^2-bc-c^2=(2b+c)(b-c)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="448" height="19" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5ba70df5a2f9630194f32f50010cd2e78318c98d.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b&lt;c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />, we can't have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/d/ebda5306c70a24c574ef0107ed5b5152b5f594cf.png" class="latex" alt="$b=c$" width="40" height="12" /> and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/1/c0171205cc0ad7f4f811ef834bfd7b6af3e5f906.png" class="latex" alt="$c=-2b$" width="63" height="12" />. Then our arithmetic progression is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/4/0d4e83fb611021ac9fa73fb0123aa4c0308fc0c8.png" class="latex" alt="$4b,b,-2b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="16" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/4/4048f34740c4357fc7ae8938e92df8286ed5d0a1.png" class="latex" alt="$4b &lt; b &lt; -2b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/a/beab3b5c143f2b58c18e15cdb470a15386701dab.png" class="latex" alt="$b &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. The smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/1/c0171205cc0ad7f4f811ef834bfd7b6af3e5f906.png" class="latex" alt="$c=-2b$" width="63" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/e/08e49136687e2ed695ec26c96f71c5243ea7b4bc.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2)(-1)=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />.
</p><p>(Solution by AT)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Taking the definition of an arithmetic progression, there must be a common difference between the terms, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/9061c58fcecf12c3bb40bf43291a67efc6299586.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-a) = (c-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />. From this, we can obtain the expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/a/3dab9e38a3c4094dbf8d793bb15db08b274b488c.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 2b-c$" width="81" height="12" />. Again, by taking the definition of a geometric progression, we can obtain the expression, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/d/d8d2e709d34b7619da2e586ded31672af04f76b1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=ar$" width="49" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/0/d40910a5e41a88bc61ca13f6ceaa68c4d2860ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$b=ar^2$" width="56" height="15" />, where r serves as a value for the ratio between two terms in the progression. By substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> in the arithmetic progression expression with the obtained values from the geometric progression, we obtain the equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/d/76d98f859353e1f9924c4eef29fe5935732b3f6b.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2ar^2-ar$" width="108" height="15" /> which can be simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/1/9b19f424d1cecc9c773d8d1c32c5737ea4ba4784.png" class="latex" alt="$(r-1)(2r+1)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1930619a38fceee8e02990ea79026a348193307e.png" class="latex" alt="$r=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fba02d8ac615a83812bbfc81f3de68c13efac077.png" class="latex" alt="$r=-1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" />. Thus, from the geometric progression, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/3/1a351eb298ff1526bb4bc134ed0a57e4a751b6be.png" class="latex" alt="$a=a$" width="43" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/1/c81d39ade559eee4198db4622d2c6b5422ee5887.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1/4a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b585c82563a5199c70dbd9dac783ca07737550d.png" class="latex" alt="$c=-1/2a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" />. Looking at the initial conditions of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5ba70df5a2f9630194f32f50010cd2e78318c98d.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b&lt;c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" /> we can see that the lowest integer value that would satisfy the above expressions is if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/7/4776d545e58865e347c9c5d3991937c1cc143d98.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="12" />, thus making <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/7/4f72da4187cb0ff8d0471bef17474df62fe69c94.png" class="latex" alt="$c=2$" width="40" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" /> 
(Solution by thatuser)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>By the definition of an arithmetic progression, we can label the terms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b83c9c3a8da8388f2c9107e68f8fe59683cd0cdc.png" class="latex" alt="$a+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b89e2225617aed18d1a3bb39906075c99699176.png" class="latex" alt="$a+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />. Now, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b89e2225617aed18d1a3bb39906075c99699176.png" class="latex" alt="$a+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b83c9c3a8da8388f2c9107e68f8fe59683cd0cdc.png" class="latex" alt="$a+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" /> form a geometric progression. Since a geometric ratio has a common ratio between terms, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/729a3e888c4d29ba6b461d8daad7ae2d6040f374.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+2d)/a = (a+d)/a+2d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="222" height="18" />. Cross multiplying, we obtain the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/e/f4ea27139345d3ee7bb3aec8daeefc84925d48f9.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+2d)^2=a(a+d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="19" />. Multiplying it out and cancelling terms, we are left with the quadratic equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c7166f1f3d4d9a953c20235f89710e2cb54b6d1.png" class="latex" alt="$4d^2+3ad=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="16" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/8/f38fa78b71167027dae91e2b24d38171af0ed2fe.png" class="latex" alt="$d=-3a/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791662898215b138613f4dada0a3ce54193d735f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=0$" width="42" height="12" />. Looking at the problem, we know that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> cannot be 0, therefore the arithmetic progression is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6cb11fbd3e78b97d67be1fc9f6b6f93f958c756.png" class="latex" alt="$a, a/4, -a/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />, so we need to find the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52bce5b8ba3e90c6f47c5c114251d1d7312de4e2.png" class="latex" alt="$-a/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22a2a2ba78a899722dbde1c6fefddda78fa19837.png" class="latex" alt="$-a/2&gt;a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />. Looking at our progression, we realize that a must be a multiple of 4 so that every term is an integer. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/9/f59ef1b07d364c2ccf7077ea0d2ddf5fb05a7a33.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="12" />, since that would yield the smallest value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52bce5b8ba3e90c6f47c5c114251d1d7312de4e2.png" class="latex" alt="$-a/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> which satisfies the conditions, we figure out that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />.
(Solution by i8Pie)
</p>
