<p>Five positive consecutive integers starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> have average <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> consecutive integers that start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/e/6fec7c4a4dc839bbb78aa5279521fcb32ab4e1bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ a+3\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ a+4\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ a+5\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ a+6\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ a+7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="508" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Our list is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/2/d2289231163b75dca0daf0f842ee0fba6f289fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,2,3,4,5\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" /> with an average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/7570405e2d4fee5e48684d8394452bb11a63e5ae.png" class="latex" alt="$15\div 5=3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />. Our next set starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/8/aa89af46bb4fcda830729b491fac232f296c0715.png" class="latex" alt="$\{3,4,5,6,7\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" />. Our average is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/4/ad4f9b90cdd4a6ca7ea33fd2a4682e94c7943b58.png" class="latex" alt="$25\div 5=5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />.
</p><p>Therefore, we notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cecb6bbecd2c0c1d613e2547cf0023797994fe99.png" class="latex" alt="$5=1+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> which means that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/7/9c7417af88fc5db87098196fd0c1d5d617f2f84a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ a+4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="86" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a6864d90f09646e0670de52b58eca0e6c0b1d9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b=\frac{a+a+1+a+2+a+3+a+4}{5}\]" width="296" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/3/d0319fc0f9cb638d8026e4c6e9d642c5ea7d3727.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies b =a+2\]" width="110" height="14" />
</p><p>We are asked to find the average of the 5 consecutive integers starting from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. By substitution, this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/5169a0e7b4ba46f5065ee9b32f4356bd2a7b90ef.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a+2+a+3+a+4+a+5+a+6}5=a+4\]" width="360" height="37" />
</p><p>Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/7/9c7417af88fc5db87098196fd0c1d5d617f2f84a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ a+4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="86" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We know from experience that the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> consecutive numbers is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f232863f6c52bf3177e6cc99f1659c9025da91.png" class="latex" alt="$3^\text{rd}$" width="21" height="15" /> one or the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e812ffc8595ea4c89130304a249b52b56bc68449.png" class="latex" alt="$1^\text{st} + 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="54" height="18" />. With the logic, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/2/b9223927ab29544d6991e7d02ee15861a98212e6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=a+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/c/20c57c946bec971779eba827be6068e9eced9c5c.png" class="latex" alt="$b+2=(a+2)+2=\boxed{a+4}$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="228" height="26" />.
</p><p>~MathFun1000
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The list of numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/6/32646af512fe1bb92ba5af59379b5bf598731eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\{a,\ a+1,\ b,\ a+3,\ a+4\right\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="215" height="20" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/2/b9223927ab29544d6991e7d02ee15861a98212e6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=a+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. The new list is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/2/84217532a79fd2cdcfe47067389bcc7a928ad529.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\{a+2,\ a+3,\ a+4,\ a+5,\ a+6\right\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="279" height="20" /> and the average is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/7579faf7a8ac41f96ff5e2f35d61352997d0f4bf.png" class="latex" alt="$a+4 \Longrightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(B) } a+4}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="169" height="30" />.
</p><p>~JH. L
</p>
