<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gallons of milk in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> days. At this rate, how many gallons of milk will <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> cows give in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> days?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3be9b791407bb00c49aea7501d104ec91f3ff81.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{bde}{ac}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{ac}{bde}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{abde}{c}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{bcde}{a}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{abc}{de}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="467" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We need to multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/f/8cf2f872fda858799ad4cbba2f4ca7ddeccd0112.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{d}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="37" /> for the new cows and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/d/61d08b4711d8663836d8a7e013b66870b490d134.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{e}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="10" height="33" /> for the new time, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0fbffbb21dd6a320c5af98c9f02ae9721333bf.png" class="latex" alt="$b\cdot \frac{d}{a}\cdot \frac{e}{c}=\frac{bde}{ac}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/f/24fca6e9c48cc5b8088b3a1660a9eeacfe550505.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } \frac{bde}{ac}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="78" height="50" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63e18205b2a13c9f8d283e834e0bb8d145ba305.png" class="latex" alt="$c=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/6/0c69d1273beeeec5eb86e9cdd3bf326ddc98e0ae.png" class="latex" alt="$d=5$" width="42" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/b/61b88def425b07518734e46dbb0ef3659a60f7af.png" class="latex" alt="$e=6$" width="41" height="12" />. Hence the question becomes "2 cows give 3 gallons of milk in 4 days. How many gallons of milk do 5 cows give in 6 days?"
</p><p>If 2 cows give 3 gallons of milk in 4 days, then 2 cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> gallons of milk in 1 day, so 1 cow gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/a/f8a9f70dda78b486829aaca7adb14e4973b19d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4\cdot2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="33" height="38" /> gallons in 1 day. This means that 5 cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/9/469100e4351c7f3d239bf8e7c1c1d67a83783e3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5\cdot3}{4\cdot2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="33" height="38" /> gallons of milk in 1 day. Finally, we see that 5 cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d1082c2b2b458be69ad2de3123ba689b3dfe7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5\cdot3\cdot6}{4\cdot2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="56" height="38" /> gallons of milk in 6 days. Substituting our values for the variables, this becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/f/16f77c1a35eb7a6e4337a27c719c82294ab78a43.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{dbe}{ac}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="28" height="37" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/8/c9835ecbec2d5b06ca12663c9a7740533499c4b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{bde}{ac}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="76" height="49" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We see that the number of cows is inversely proportional to the number of days and directly proportional to the gallons of milk. So our constant is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/e/0be07c9d4f319dc48388ddb81ba38e87aa1c0e31.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{ac}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="19" height="33" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the answer to the question. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/816352c8eb723908f50c8fa0ac365bcb4a4b5c71.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{de}{g}=\dfrac{ac}{b}\implies gac=bde\implies g=\dfrac{bde}{ac}\implies\boxed{ \textbf{(A)}\ \frac{bde}{ac}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="432" height="49" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>The problem specifics "rate," so it would be wise to first find the rate at which cows produce milk. We can find the rate of work/production by finding the gallons produced by a single cow in a single day. To do this, we divide the amount produced by the number of cows and number of days <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/3/bc326104c3a461a163a7429decb33c6d7b2f40d2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies\text{rate}=\dfrac{b}{ac}\]" width="111" height="37" />
</p><p>Now that we have the gallons produced by a single cow in a single day, we simply multiply that rate by the number of cows and the number of days <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47e7878330df965d3a116bba82631d796ffa71e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies\boxed{\textbf{(A) } \dfrac{bde}{ac}}\]" width="115" height="49" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gallons of milk in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> days, that means that one cow will give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/2/032cb0c5932179b6ceedd7baa2a7b33391437704.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="37" /> gallons of milk in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> days. Also, we want to find the number of gallons of milk will <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> cows give in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> days, so in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/d/61d08b4711d8663836d8a7e013b66870b490d134.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{e}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="10" height="33" /> days <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e601902e89b9efb22938e65c0662c9fef1be520.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{bd}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="19" height="37" /> gallons of milk. Multiplying with the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1218dbfae7805ea5564ac7ee2a892d0a846653.png" class="latex" alt="$d=rt$" width="48" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa3cd35dfe5da3f3924da78f737333011bf7e3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } \dfrac{bde}{ac}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="78" height="50" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6">Solution 6</span></h2>
<p>In the right formula, plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/1/b817c877bfd78e620a2d0c578989c7e4ee19d3bc.png" class="latex" alt="$d=a$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/8/5e8132b76ee2f02d29775ad133e934f6b6c2a37a.png" class="latex" alt="$e=c$" width="40" height="8" /> should simplify to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, as if it doesn't, we'd essentially be saying "<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cows give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gallons of milk in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> days, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cows don't give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gallons of milk in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> days." The only one of the answer choices that simplifies like this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_7">Solution 7</span></h2>
<p>Call the rate at which a single cow produces milk <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. Using the given information we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bdab76b7050616af629549fbc6a3ba08918bec6.png" class="latex" alt="$rac=b$" width="58" height="12" />, and we want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39cbf953875845fbf77fd873fa1ebef5d07d2369.png" class="latex" alt="$rde$" width="26" height="12" />. We solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> in the first equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/c/17c17a5c7111627587d1ec8fe2038c2fd3e8f71d.png" class="latex" alt="$r=b \cdot \tfrac{1}{ac}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="73" height="23" />. Plugging in this value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> into the expression we want, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/9/8e9666725fa8867b879622cd826e8c759d0d47c5.png" class="latex" alt="$rde=\frac{bde}{ac}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p>
