<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f8106d761155c78be9c31cf6666818e88a1013f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAC$" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79b8c0c4f224da467e14fec67208eacb047af42b.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC=40^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/4/6c4b41dd37dcc418b2bd81d72c4d763936a2fc76.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/a/baae3e2b0038d1c14b3b8f3ca091e6f180d0a116.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=6$" width="60" height="13" />.  Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> respectively.  What is the minimum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/9/449ea2f9c45624ffec02784f01d32b9db2a6bf38.png" class="latex" alt="$BE+DE+CD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/5/bf540f0244d09379897113a1a4428f221c1a860a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }6\sqrt 3+3\qquad \textbf{(B) }\dfrac{27}2\qquad \textbf{(C) }8\sqrt 3\qquad \textbf{(D) }14\qquad \textbf{(E) }3\sqrt 3+9\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="510" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70b97eb4ad6e4e7a5a97e2547d9ed6e965960d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> be the reflection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b65e6facfe122c182fe14d503aeace2c52a9739d.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> be the reflection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70b97eb4ad6e4e7a5a97e2547d9ed6e965960d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$C_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />.  Then it is well-known that the quantity <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/9/449ea2f9c45624ffec02784f01d32b9db2a6bf38.png" class="latex" alt="$BE+DE+CD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="14" /> is minimized when it is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/c/f9c599c0300bc798890a013c999a07c4e249a6b8.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2B$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="34" height="15" />.  (Proving this is a simple application of the triangle inequality; for an example of a simpler case, see Heron's Shortest Path Problem.)  As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> lies on both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/3/9c3733442d2a648c9d2af04df38d7c4801730c0f.png" class="latex" alt="$C_2A=C_1A=CA=6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="177" height="15" />.  Furthermore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/8/1c84f4ad5490463ad722b7a3f5ba9c4504e0168e.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CAC_1=2\angle CAB=80^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="198" height="15" /> by the nature of the reflection, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/9/c390a078b2f4f6b049bcac9983e619ef5fe5844a.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C_2AB=\angle C_2AC+\angle CAB=80^\circ+40^\circ=120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="380" height="15" />.  Therefore by the Law of Cosines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/2/a027578a35a1f6405b408f8514f049a12c6b1ed6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[BC_2^2=6^2+10^2-2\cdot 6\cdot 10\cos 120^\circ=196\implies BC_2=\boxed{14\textbf{ (D)}}.\]" width="478" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f8106d761155c78be9c31cf6666818e88a1013f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAC$" width="58" height="13" />, the three lines look like the Chinese character 又. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a79ef38acd8198300ef945366a4c5d7b360f808b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEA$" width="59" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb94e3d043aa1652f6933df7dfc4a760ae51442c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDA$" width="59" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/409f9059cc97f6eb3fee880d797e27ef4dd80fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEA$" width="59" height="13" /> have bases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7baf95ecafc2d7791788ec79aeeae390d60b119.png" class="latex" alt="$BE$" width="28" height="12" /> respectively. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a79ef38acd8198300ef945366a4c5d7b360f808b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEA$" width="59" height="13" /> has the same side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31cd391bebc36897a8c10ea78e5d584980b049.png" class="latex" alt="$DA$" width="29" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb94e3d043aa1652f6933df7dfc4a760ae51442c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDA$" width="59" height="13" /> and the same side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d9456e0361813641f4ce80980839e9038fe8f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$EA$" width="28" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/409f9059cc97f6eb3fee880d797e27ef4dd80fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEA$" width="59" height="13" />. Connect all three triangles with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a79ef38acd8198300ef945366a4c5d7b360f808b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEA$" width="59" height="13" /> in the center and the two triangles sharing one of its sides. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c472ac99d8793d6e7d118e079e4efa27b02144d7.png" class="latex" alt="$\pentagon BACDE$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="85" height="13" /> is formed with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/9/449ea2f9c45624ffec02784f01d32b9db2a6bf38.png" class="latex" alt="$BE+DE+CD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="14" /> forming the base. 
</p><p>Intuitively, the pentagon's base is minimized when all three bottom sides are collinear. This is simply the original <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f8106d761155c78be9c31cf6666818e88a1013f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAC$" width="58" height="13" /> except that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/4337fcd5f89f9bbaeb8ada49f044329dc90dcd8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC =120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="112" height="13" />. (In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a79ef38acd8198300ef945366a4c5d7b360f808b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEA$" width="59" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb94e3d043aa1652f6933df7dfc4a760ae51442c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDA$" width="59" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/409f9059cc97f6eb3fee880d797e27ef4dd80fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEA$" width="59" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02db91aa9b3c20fbbaa53393e1b0b2f9cd0a3673.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A = 40^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />, and the three triangles connect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to form the pentagon). Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/a/5ca219f7d9cbbfb40058dac2bf8ef98da35b7246.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle BAC = 40 * 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="139" height="13" />).
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> in this new triangle is then the minimum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/9/449ea2f9c45624ffec02784f01d32b9db2a6bf38.png" class="latex" alt="$BE+DE+CD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="14" />. Applying law of cosines, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/def4633d3364a83593be764d6f01f62e0a662a4c.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=\sqrt{6^2+10^2-2(6)(10)\cos (120^\circ)}=\sqrt{196}=14 \implies \boxed{14\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="516" height="32" />
</p><p><br />
~bjhhar
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/2/992bb5f4313de15d7b9061d87207385f7af07b81.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(300); defaultpen(linewidth(0.4)+fontsize(10)); pen s = linewidth(0.8)+fontsize(8);  pair A,B,C,D,Ep,Bp,Cp; A = (0,0); B = 10*dir(-110);C = 6*dir(-70); Bp = 10*dir(-30);Cp = 6*dir(-150); D = IP(Cp--Bp, A--B); Ep = IP(Cp--Bp, A--C); draw(A--B--C--A--Cp--Bp--A); draw(Cp--B); draw(C--Bp); draw(C--D); draw(B--Ep);  dot(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, N); dot(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, SW); dot(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, SE); dot(&quot;$B&#39;$&quot;, Bp, E); dot(&quot;$C&#39;$&quot;, Cp, W); dot(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, dir(-70)); dot(&quot;$E$&quot;, Ep, dir(60));  MA(&quot;40^\circ&quot;,Cp,A,D, 1); MA(&quot;40^\circ&quot;,D,A,Ep, 1); MA(&quot;40^\circ&quot;,Ep,A,Bp, 1); label(&quot;$6$&quot;, A--Cp); label(&quot;$10$&quot;, Bp--A); [/asy]" width="502" height="342" />
</p><p>(Diagram by shihan)
Reflect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b798193cb94562989018728e1151587c618655b.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="21" height="16" />. Similarly, reflect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" />. Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/3/48324eed039058dd39949df5f3337956776b91fa.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = B&#39;E$" width="87" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/b/30bafdee48986fe35d47cd17d9741ff7c3132999.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = C&#39;D$" width="88" height="14" />. Thus, the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/6066f14d2bf550b17a35ddf5f3ee78892bba51d7.png" class="latex" alt="$BE + DE + CD = B&#39;E + DE + C&#39;D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="300" height="15" />. This value is minimized when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b798193cb94562989018728e1151587c618655b.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="21" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are collinear. To finish, we use the law of cosines on the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d233be83ec56ac013d078c542367b31a9c585a8.png" class="latex" alt="$AB&#39;C&#39;$" width="51" height="14" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7d79803acf53a5849b2694c387716f738a947b.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;C&#39; = \sqrt{6^2 + 10^2 - 2(6)(10)\cos 120} = 14$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="331" height="32" />
</p>
