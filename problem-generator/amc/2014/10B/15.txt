<p>In rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/a/b5a7ab67eff9c71d0169c879a0775c524dd59c34.png" class="latex" alt="$DC = 2 \cdot CB$" width="104" height="12" /> and points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/7/5f74106a4c831d948ec641e678edc348857426fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{ED}$" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/f/d5fe7e80c69a93c8c39a73a59232e3dd981ffa5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{FD}$" width="30" height="15" /> trisect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28b8d709d3b873ec6db017839b1520919db39db.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC$" width="56" height="13" /> as shown. What is the ratio of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" /> to the area of rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/8/0b8719923caa95ad435933a935115d4f177f5fd2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0, 0)--(0, 1)--(2, 1)--(2, 0)--cycle); draw((0, 0)--(sqrt(3)/3, 1)); draw((0, 0)--(sqrt(3), 1)); label(&quot;A&quot;, (0, 1), N); label(&quot;B&quot;, (2, 1), N); label(&quot;C&quot;, (2, 0), S); label(&quot;D&quot;, (0, 0), S); label(&quot;E&quot;, (sqrt(3)/3, 1), N); label(&quot;F&quot;, (sqrt(3), 1), N); [/asy]" width="252" height="162" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/0/5800d5f6bbfdbab7a09015167ce6136e5800f614.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{\sqrt{6}}{8}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{16}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1}{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="443" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, so that the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/e/d1ee314d4b265b3795d08a3212a0e1697d6657cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{[}ABCD\text{]}=2x^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a rectangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/2/8c20ba53b41b110f72105f6c214e7f879727c346.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC=90^{\circ}$" width="104" height="13" />, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/a/39aa3f012909cce330006887792939c4489a7293.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADE=\angle EDF=\angle FDC=30^{\circ}$" width="267" height="13" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/6/9364f2848153329571245fe91262b2983db3c20f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DAE$" width="59" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> right triangle; this implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/2411b9b7b1833b3e67b12ec8f388198b7192cf3c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DEF=180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}=120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="221" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/5/8b536efe99067a477c05b13ef7cfeb61a4674c90.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EFD=180^{\circ}-(120^{\circ}+30^{\circ})=30^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="284" height="18" />. Now drop the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" />, forming two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangles. 
</p><p>Because the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, from the properties of a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangle the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b845837935ed5bd76cbc986ea6cae8eb45d18986.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="41" /> and the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b4ae8c605f6157695cfb12c3792aeedc104d9b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2x\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="41" />. Thus the altitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b845837935ed5bd76cbc986ea6cae8eb45d18986.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="41" />, and its base is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" />, so its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab2b045bf7f44e9df0da5850a603454e6928fdbe.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(2x)\left(\frac{x\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)=\frac{x^2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="185" height="53" />.
</p><p>To finish, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/0/020ac7c7837aaab0e898393c85b89af05cab96af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{[}\triangle DEF\text{]}}{\text{[}ABCD\text{]}}=\frac{\frac{x^2\sqrt{3}}{3}}{2x^2}=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="237" height="53" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/8/0784975d4b8be997f4dfdeb77135b8ffc032bce6.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/8/c38c67505075cc5b0144b30b6e75fb3abe143da1.png" class="latex" alt="$DC = 2$" width="62" height="12" />. Furthermore, drop an an altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />, which meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28b8d709d3b873ec6db017839b1520919db39db.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC$" width="56" height="13" /> is right and has been trisected, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/6/4c6c4b7a90ff423536309e7050f94b5acd4e96e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DXF$" width="62" height="13" /> are both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangles. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/f/c1f25b0379821f73977f62b9e5d1da5c1ce801c8.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81fe386b23800ca328967e1707daa52209cf3b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$DX = AF = \sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="18" />. Hence, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af455ff1483ea3d456ec5e1ffb8d1eb33ee6e5b0.png" class="latex" alt="$EF = \sqrt{3} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}= \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="187" height="41" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/0/a009564c26bf86933bd2d32bf0c1b854d12a3084.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADE$" width="56" height="13" /> is right, the height and base of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f6f572d974137fa33e72957f374faaa8f58ea32.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />, respectively. Thus, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/c/e5cb6e7c42d6bf0c332b448f0d28fff6c0aaaef1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />, and the area of rectengle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so the ratio beween the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/2/872e3b09425c9659d782119a543dc858aa520a40.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DEF$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/8/d98f2c4b620b20f2deb4c5e9a235660c005a4e91.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="75" height="53" />. Note that we are able to assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd842e36ed64086a882c190267900ce65d1f7017.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/8/c38c67505075cc5b0144b30b6e75fb3abe143da1.png" class="latex" alt="$DC = 2$" width="62" height="12" /> because we were asked to find the ratio between two areas.
</p>
