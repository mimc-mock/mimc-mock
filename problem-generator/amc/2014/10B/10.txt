<p>In the addition shown below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> are distinct digits. How many different values are possible for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/a/43a9d725cbf38b81b4523dbd012ce90bbd022cea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}[t]{r}     ABBCB \\ + \ BCADA \\ \hline     DBDDD \end{array}\]" width="108" height="53" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/8/bc8618c844b0added082fc7f6a284ffdda283049.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 2 \qquad \textbf {(B) } 4 \qquad \textbf {(C) } 7 \qquad \textbf {(D) } 8 \qquad \textbf {(E) } 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Note from the addition of the last digits that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a5a15ed4ef17391abd446c726ecefad5d70edc5.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B=D\text{ or }A+B=D+10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="246" height="14" />. 
From the addition of the frontmost digits, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b831312585cb4ab6697896f86b72accbddbc4b45.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> cannot have a carry, since the answer is still a five-digit number. Also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bf77c6740d00af2866f26718d628a60c667ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> cant have a carry since then for the second column, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2db2dbce80e9bd3677015446f8219625315b3c8.png" class="latex" alt="$C + 1 + D$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="16" /> cant equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. 
Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/cca40b0cad654c5ba05b678aa8eb94dfc972947f.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B=D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />. 
</p><p>Using the second or fourth column, this then implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/7296755ef63523ef0ed2e997caae100445bbb44d.png" class="latex" alt="$C=0$" width="47" height="12" />, so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/8/f18f4d5b268ed6537f2a5d6123091fc8b34dee09.png" class="latex" alt="$B+C=B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/2072bfcb168c0254116c78263126d900d7e7c956.png" class="latex" alt="$C+D=D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" />. 
Note that all of the remaining equalities are now satisfied: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34aa64bf491837f5f32a12c1d517e1b5c0ed9a5e.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B=D, B+C=B,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="192" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/4/4440d49041e17db8339cec8b1e5ec8fdf791c752.png" class="latex" alt="$B+A=D$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />. Since the digits must be distinct, the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c43733c628919124fe2d151e501a45f5af62e59.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />, and the largest possible value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/6/11697470636f9d6d3a4937d0bee9f814d5fdfa71.png" class="latex" alt="$3\le D\le9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="81" height="15" />, so the number of possible values is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f2fb5b6047f99ed1c6294aca81de10d9877a20e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
