<p>Orvin went to the store with just enough money to buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> balloons. When he arrived, he discovered that the store had a special sale on balloons: buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> balloon at the regular price and get a second at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> off the regular price. What is the greatest number of balloons Orvin could buy?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/a/73aad23e4903dce9ab43f829f0448863b6a9336d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 33 \qquad \textbf {(B) } 34 \qquad \textbf {(C) } 36 \qquad \textbf {(D) } 38 \qquad \textbf {(E) } 39$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since he pays <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/3/8031bb3f1dac63198f00d4e91a0459672bd47a07.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> the price for every second balloon, the price for two balloons is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/f/d8f529172a68606a2012c48522c9c3c9decaf96a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{5}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Thus, if he had enough money to buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> balloons before, he now has enough to buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/b/b1b34f1a0276d36681c6741b6d9f2a60557d8647.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \cdot \dfrac{2}{\dfrac{5}{3}} = 30 \cdot \dfrac{6}{5} = \fbox{(C) 36}$" style="vertical-align: -34px" width="200" height="59" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Suppose each balloon costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> dollars. Therefore, Orvin brought <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars. Since every second balloon costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719a9138dfa00997648fb0cbe38aebb4be13f410.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}\cdot3=2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="68" height="37" /> dollars, Orvin gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> balloons for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> dollars. Therefore, Orvin gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/a/26a3b35f08bed26da6aae2124e83f45cce00d0c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2\cdot90}{5}=\fbox{(C) 36}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="130" height="37" />
</p>
