<p>A list of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> positive integers has a mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, a median of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, and a unique mode of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the largest possible value of an integer in the list?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/9/34908107457b2a65dd98c32055145f47502a7328.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 24 \qquad \textbf {(B) } 30 \qquad \textbf {(C) } 31\qquad \textbf {(D) } 33 \qquad \textbf {(E) } 35$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We start off with the fact that the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, so we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97d029e41c8e92c62f112f6ee96f918a6e4e39a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, d, e, 9, f, g, h, i, j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="176" height="16" />, listed in ascending order. Note that the integers do not have to be distinct. 
</p><p>Since the mode is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, we have to have at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> occurrences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> in the list. If there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> occurrences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> in the list, we will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/6/ad6454b039d348692275d8954b45b4757beb74b3.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c, 8, 8, 9, f, g, h, i, j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="177" height="16" />. In this case, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> is the unique mode, the rest of the integers have to be distinct. So we minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef79d78444b851af151245b12b078bad22a4dba6.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,f,g,h,i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="109" height="16" /> in order to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" />. If we let the list be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/291321dc1715c6254e250938c45da3d465fda7a4.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,8,8,9,10,11,12,13,j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="215" height="16" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/f/a6fba89a82add0f79593c177f01ad374ec1cecb5.png" class="latex" alt="$j = 11 \times 10 - (1+2+3+8+8+9+10+11+12+13) = 33$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="497" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Next, consider the case where there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> occurrences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> in the list. Now, we can have two occurrences of another integer in the list. We try <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/f/baf1e3f499e336df03fd358b61ee7dcac30cd205.png" class="latex" alt="$1,1,8,8,8,9,9,10,10,11,j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="206" height="16" />. Following the same process as above, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/5/3055fb44379e7a1c323ded9e4d063d947e2b7f54.png" class="latex" alt="$j = 11 \times 10 - (1+1+8+8+8+9+9+10+10+11) = 35$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="488" height="18" />. As this is the highest choice in the list, we know this is our answer. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/d/70d388cd3cb2ecb8eda5193bfa495a9da6428bee.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }35}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/3/923b383ad8ff4566a7ba7a663e281fe0f365d9aa.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 + \ldots + x_{11} = 110$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="158" height="15" /> let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/4/c34b24889968a7ff3144c5d84189030112c3c53a.png" class="latex" alt="$x_6 = 9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859e90f4f9dde0df798e6ff069359c0d7cc8e923.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 + \ldots + x_5 + x_7 + \ldots + x_{11} = 101$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="282" height="15" />. To maximize the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bc6daa9d6b964201d6cef60cbeb1ac5fd26ead.png" class="latex" alt="$x_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> ranges from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, we let any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> elements be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/8/d08cf2e1210439abad3bc4cec854c7a7970f8731.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,\ldots,7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/2/f326857aaf18fe0202a498315da96945e7e9a051.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 57$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="140" height="15" />. Now we have to let one of above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> values = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e090283e8b4a936647f21a14f1af8e4b422826.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 + x_2 = 49$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="99" height="15" /> now let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/b/91b219d4a43a9735bfd7543436fc3b769d4355cd.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 = 35$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/b/afbaf8aa1d1236d374dcb2f8b5650614ace9af34.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2 = 14$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="14" /> hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/d/70d388cd3cb2ecb8eda5193bfa495a9da6428bee.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }35}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" /> is the answer.
</p>
