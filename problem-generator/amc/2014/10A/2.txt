<p>Roy's cat eats <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a can of cat food every morning and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of a can of cat food every evening. Before feeding his cat on Monday morning, Roy opened a box containing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> cans of cat food. On what day of the week did the cat finish eating all the cat food in the box?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/8/ba8fd5302ec8767c91ff7e5b64709bd5f44531d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{Tuesday}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{Wednesday}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{Thursday}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{Friday}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{Saturday}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="625" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Each day, the cat eats <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/7/db7d1d17724dc13e355d67e0069066bd0180dd36.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac13+\dfrac14=\dfrac7{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="93" height="37" /> of a can of cat food. Therefore, the cat food will last for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/4/924977a2bc32f0109d4987dc4f0d771eec436ef9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{6}{\frac7{12}}=\dfrac{72}7$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="69" height="45" /> days, which is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> days but less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> days.
</p><p><br />
Because the number of days is greater than 10 and less than 11, the cat will finish eating in on the 11th day, which is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> days after Monday, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cd68b9b782d55299ac6ac75a9d0e903abcea237.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \text{Thursday}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="114" height="28" />
</p>
