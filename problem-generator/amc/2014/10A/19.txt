<p>Four cubes with edge lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> are stacked as shown. What is the length of the portion of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/95915f5885ed19f737ac8338c7c0b1d0e23fb73a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{XY}$" width="31" height="15" /> contained in the cube with edge length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/1/e41120e351bda42fada2fe2803e19a131bc30b96.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] dotfactor = 3; size(10cm); dot((0, 10)); label(&quot;$X$&quot;, (0,10),W,fontsize(8pt)); dot((6,2)); label(&quot;$Y$&quot;, (6,2),E,fontsize(8pt)); draw((0, 0)--(0, 10)--(1, 10)--(1, 9)--(2, 9)--(2, 7)--(3, 7)--(3,4)--(4, 4)--(4, 0)--cycle); draw((0,9)--(1, 9)--(1.5, 9.5)--(1.5, 10.5)--(0.5, 10.5)--(0, 10)); draw((1, 10)--(1.5,10.5)); draw((1.5, 10)--(3,10)--(3,8)--(2,7)--(0,7)); draw((2,9)--(3,10)); draw((3,8.5)--(4.5,8.5)--(4.5,5.5)--(3,4)--(0,4)); draw((3,7)--(4.5,8.5)); draw((4.5,6)--(6,6)--(6,2)--(4,0)); draw((4,4)--(6,6)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;, (1,9.5), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;, (2,8), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;, (3,5.5), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;, (4,2), W,fontsize(8pt)); [/asy]" width="308" height="475" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39f22d5c260c68925451cf6ea13efd2c6d6c4db7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{3\sqrt{33}}5\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\sqrt3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \dfrac{2\sqrt{33}}3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 3\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="472" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By Pythagorean Theorem in three dimensions, the distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7774174993ffa29a38cf577ae34d8e0a3a63cb.png" class="latex" alt="$XY$" width="30" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/3/663ca06b65c787bb57267dac72171bd0019c8e87.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{4^2+4^2+10^2}=2\sqrt{33}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="185" height="19" />.
</p><p>Let the length of the segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7774174993ffa29a38cf577ae34d8e0a3a63cb.png" class="latex" alt="$XY$" width="30" height="12" /> that is inside the cube with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. By similar triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/2/512c0dd3c0c70fa4c9bb74739e1303902b1fcebc.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x}{3}=\dfrac{2\sqrt{33}}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="83" height="41" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/9/41937611c31f57d6dc7c7f3f3ef5fa85cce5a148.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }\dfrac{3\sqrt{33}}{5}} skr-$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="168" height="53" />.
</p>
<h1><span id="Solution_2_(3D_Coordinate_Geometry)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_.283D_Coordinate_Geometry.29">Solution 2 (3D Coordinate Geometry)</span></h1>
<p>Let's redraw the diagram, however make a 3D coordinate plane, using D as the origin.(
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/6/d26d45a4b5b55aa7a59dcd355353f8ce6476fd3c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] dotfactor = 3; size(10cm); dot((0, 10)); label(&quot;$X(0,10,0)$&quot;, (0,10),W,fontsize(8pt)); dot((6,2)); label(&quot;$Y(4,0,4)$&quot;, (6,2),E,fontsize(8pt)); draw((0, 0)--(0, 10)--(1, 10)--(1, 9)--(2, 9)--(2, 7)--(3, 7)--(3,4)--(4, 4)--(4, 0)--cycle); draw((0,9)--(1, 9)--(1.5, 9.5)--(1.5, 10.5)--(0.5, 10.5)--(0, 10)); draw((1, 10)--(1.5,10.5)); draw((1.5, 10)--(3,10)--(3,8)--(2,7)--(0,7)); draw((2,9)--(3,10)); draw((3,8.5)--(4.5,8.5)--(4.5,5.5)--(3,4)--(0,4)); draw((3,7)--(4.5,8.5)); draw((4.5,6)--(6,6)--(6,2)--(4,0)); draw((4,4)--(6,6)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;, (1,9.5), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;, (2,8), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;, (3,5.5), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;, (4,2), W,fontsize(8pt)); label(&quot;$D(0,0,0)$&quot;, (0,0), W,fontsize(8pt)); [/asy]" width="398" height="475" />
</p><p>Now we can use the distance formula in 3D, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/249de22dd8b835ab18fcab444f6623b46c513199.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(x_{1}-x_{2})^2+(y_{1}-y_{2})^2+(z_{1}-z_{2})^2}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="296" height="35" /> and plug it in for the distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7774174993ffa29a38cf577ae34d8e0a3a63cb.png" class="latex" alt="$XY$" width="30" height="12" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/5/6e57ce6468d150f4100de86133148c9a11321d98.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(0-4)^2+(10-0)^2+(0-4)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="259" height="35" />
</p><p>We get the answer as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/7588e8129d9e0cea5fea16084cefd0c62ea26a5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{132} = 2\sqrt{33}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="111" height="21" />.
</p><p>Continuing with solution 1, using similar triangles, we get the answer as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/818e14938b28cf15e2c69c9f04bf9e43631e40a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{3\sqrt{33}}5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="83" height="43" />
</p><p>~ghfhgvghj10
</p>
