<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-intercepts, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" />, of two perpendicular lines intersecting at the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/1/3916a3623bb46e7a741c1cadb690c8945143fb1e.png" class="latex" alt="$A(6,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> have a sum of zero. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b89cb1776431d56dcf6668eaeed7e634677d3215.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle APQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="17" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/9582962bdbfc13855e5748f13381043d442e3bb3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 45\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 54\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 60\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/2/f62b92d88e8e60603dc60737bcc8313a783540e0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]//Needs refining (hmm I think it&#39;s fine --bestwillcui1) size(12cm); fill((0,10)--(6,8)--(0,-10)--cycle,rgb(.7,.7,.7)); for(int i=-2;i&lt;=8;i+=1)   draw((i,-12)--(i,12),grey); for(int j=-12;j&lt;=12;j+=1)   draw((-2,j)--(8,j),grey); draw((-3,0)--(9,0),linewidth(1),Arrows); //x-axis draw((0,-13)--(0,13),linewidth(1),Arrows); //y-axis dot((0,0)); dot((6,8)); draw((-2,10.66667)--(8,7.33333),Arrows); draw((7.33333,12)--(-0.66667,-12),Arrows); draw((6,8)--(0,8)); draw((6,8)--(0,0)); draw(rightanglemark((0,10),(6,8),(0,-10),20)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(6,8),NE); label(&quot;$a$&quot;, (0,5),W); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,(0,-5),W); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,(3,4),NW); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,(0,10),SW); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,(0,-10),NW); // wanted to import graph and use xaxis/yaxis but w/e label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(9,0),E); label(&quot;$y$&quot;,(0,13),N); [/asy]" width="275" height="568" />
Note that if the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-intercepts have a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, the distance from the origin to each of the intercepts must be the same. Call this distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. Since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/9/209e49d54c1dc5623e6e8b975aff08cd9b36f668.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle PAQ = 90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="16" />, the length of the median to the midpoint of the hypotenuse is equal to half the length of the hypotenuse. Since the median's length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/a/72a6bd609dd5b3b8ff6594927eee7c9490773064.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{6^2+8^2} = 10$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="112" height="18" />, this means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/b/32b95a5a1bf6f50913e72972a09d742814553958.png" class="latex" alt="$a=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />, and the length of the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/6/bd6076e672c604446c2b9400d913224ffe1b3760.png" class="latex" alt="$2a = 20$" width="60" height="12" />. Since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is the same as the altitude to the hypotenuse, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d8d2be9d7b733395ec426f07aaf3e6a96b8938.png" class="latex" alt="$[APQ] = \dfrac{20 \cdot 6}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} \: 60}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="208" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can let the two lines be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd023992e5d6336d832d358360d7e8825979efe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y=mx+b\]" width="89" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/8/de8379749a4d7e197ddac4fb7647ecd82f0b2bce.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y=-\frac{1}{m}x-b\]" width="108" height="37" /> This is because the lines are perpendicular, hence the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ecf2f6a8e46ee4becd582e7a321282f42b9e6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="37" />, and the sum of the y-intercepts is equal to 0, hence the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/8/3283f4207e2533ada6e968a719123c62dac1c398.png" class="latex" alt="$b, -b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="16" />. 
</p><p>Since both lines contain the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/d/0bd97f1a6f1d76907ee5c4460c2f1fe587beb8d5.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, we can plug this into the two equations to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/8/028c8ed2af10d0d75b1f57ef918f58a4e0864bae.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[8=6m+b\]" width="88" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/6/526142bf91c08d6462a06e94a0c3a50ecbb6ad9d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[8=-6\frac{1}{m}-b\]" width="106" height="37" />
</p><p>Adding the two equations gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/7/ee7f468c7a2f78fdb95d756b21deec502af9d653.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[16=6m+\frac{-6}{m}\]" width="113" height="37" /> Multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/88691e7fef07de9ff889fccf6613f977d231693d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[16m=6m^2-6\]" width="120" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/b/e2b86f7cb5ff49e93eac691c0d498facbe8bb568.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 6m^2-16m-6=0\]" width="191" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/6/e466842623898053d554e3265de8b5104e21d556.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 3m^2-8m-3=0\]" width="182" height="15" /> Factoring gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa1109195c2c029f6278cbfffae553140bebb5a1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(3m+1)(m-3)=0\]" width="163" height="18" /> 
</p><p>Plugging <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/4/904f27995d33e0d39f43ec959e7cb100516e9396.png" class="latex" alt="$m=3$" width="48" height="12" /> into one of our original equations, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/4/034765604cd7e4ffc843954e52a1a8f9dbba7221.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[8=6(3)+b\]" width="95" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/b/edbbd7988d98af284ba5e8e95702d1d4b0a7d645.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies b=8-6(3)=-10\]" width="190" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f826a09308897e7104cbf5e5a1b6d08b3c15db4.png" class="latex" alt="$\bigtriangleup APQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="17" /> has hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/8/748da446e7c3de5dbb90c12096e2806b34a3c7c2.png" class="latex" alt="$2|b|=20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> and the altitude to the hypotenuse is equal to the the x-coordinate of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, or 6, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f826a09308897e7104cbf5e5a1b6d08b3c15db4.png" class="latex" alt="$\bigtriangleup APQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="17" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6bafdfebcba7ec81cb4194533704fece40f26147.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{20\cdot6}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 60}\]" width="131" height="37" />
</p>
