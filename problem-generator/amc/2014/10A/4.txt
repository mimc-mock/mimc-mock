<p>Walking down Jane Street, Ralph passed four houses in a row, each painted a different color. He passed the orange house before the red house, and he passed the blue house before the yellow house. The blue house was not next to the yellow house. How many orderings of the colored houses are possible?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/8/f68a620e8d126d7a4d48fcd5878a055aaa4ab6db.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let's use casework on the yellow house. The yellow house <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/e/63ee5d01213daa3c2b2fda73253a6aa3923df509.png" class="latex" alt="$(\text{Y})$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="28" height="20" /> is either the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f232863f6c52bf3177e6cc99f1659c9025da91.png" class="latex" alt="$3^\text{rd}$" width="21" height="15" /> house or the last house.
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcfb65d98e3e4d7d8592056f225e7e1bddebf4d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Y}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f232863f6c52bf3177e6cc99f1659c9025da91.png" class="latex" alt="$3^\text{rd}$" width="21" height="15" /> house.
</p><p>The only possible arrangement is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/d/8fd50713d1ff79bfbe01acb6e1238158c78d0c90.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{B}-\text{O}-\text{Y}-\text{R}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" />
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcfb65d98e3e4d7d8592056f225e7e1bddebf4d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Y}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> is the last house.
</p><p>There are two possible arrangements:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/0/be08698918e4ebd1d4662872551cee73e57211a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{B}-\text{O}-\text{R}-\text{Y}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/b/fdba0ad652b30ee3d85cc52775dd2babaeccb8a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{O}-\text{B}-\text{R}-\text{Y}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" /> 
</p><p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/1/d410da36bb1fbc91d60a13cf109e1a631207308e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/9/1593463d6fb7eaeb3427e06c069fc425fb4f60d9.png" class="latex" alt="$4!=24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" /> arrangements without restrictions. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/a/c6aec553645b7c4058185f5dbdda692fc805557b.png" class="latex" alt="$3!\cdot2!=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="13" /> arrangements such that the blue house neighboring the yellow house (calculating the arrangments of [<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d73559429233357755249ed1b853f119286c661.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{BY}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="26" height="13" />], <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/9/6497f93a19054873b4fdbf16640b2cd371baeb89.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{O}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e787ae9f9cc165e2da6347db095708a14c3b555c.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />). Hence, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/c/27c8a61d9cb8d818f628f8c321e3ca504bb95555.png" class="latex" alt="$24-12=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="12" /> arrangements with the blue and yellow houses non-adjacent.
</p><p>By symmetry, exactly half of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> arrangements have the blue house before the yellow house, and exactly half of those <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> arrangements have the orange house before the red house, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/1/721d1aabd8f26cd7fede092e97579d5ff34154b3.png" class="latex" alt="$12\cdot\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}= \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="152" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>To start with, the blue house is either the first or second house.
</p><p>If the blue house is the first, then the orange must follow, leading to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> cases: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/b/71b0c3517acd9b7396105972489457fc70ce672f.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{B-O-R-Y}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/1/1217af082e149ed994219c058048edc47221be55.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{B-O-Y-R}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="13" />. 
</p><p>If the blue house is second, then the orange house must be first and the yellow house last, leading to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/7/a876bafbe65f48cc26ca068a75626973bd22018e.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{O-B-R-Y}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="13" />.
Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311b0636396e2eb66e13a6e8fd8f5dbd70ec34e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="57" height="30" />.
</p><p>~MathFun1000
</p>
