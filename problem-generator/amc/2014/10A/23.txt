<p>A rectangular piece of paper whose length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99f089046b752269e77100c333b11d8d72e5895.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> times the width has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. The paper is divided into three equal sections along the opposite lengths, and then a dotted line is drawn from the first divider to the second divider on the opposite side as shown. The paper is then folded flat along this dotted line to create a new shape with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. What is the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340318477c3b9b0805b9fd903c3598e4e9ae43ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{B}{A}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="17" height="37" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/383b46c75e8be868196ec01badfad01411d16223.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(6cm);  real L = 0.05;  pair A = (0,0); pair B = (sqrt(3),0); pair C = (sqrt(3),1); pair D = (0,1);  pair X1 = (sqrt(3)/3,0); pair X2= (2*sqrt(3)/3,0); pair Y1 = (2*sqrt(3)/3,1); pair Y2 = (sqrt(3)/3,1);  dot(X1); dot(Y1);  draw(A--B--C--D--cycle, linewidth(2)); draw(X1--Y1,dashed);  draw(X2--(2*sqrt(3)/3,L)); draw(Y2--(sqrt(3)/3,1-L)); [/asy]" width="285" height="168" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/1/35101a628139bf11bc3563c2878f5c294f43ea1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{3}{5}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{3}{4}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Find the midpoint of the dotted line.  Draw a line perpendicular to it.  From the point this line intersects the top of the paper, draw lines to each endpoint of the dotted line.  These two lines plus the dotted line form a triangle which is the double-layered portion of the folded paper.  WLOG, assume the width of the paper is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and the length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />.  The triangle we want to find has side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/1/b5172fe9283115da437e2f4957c869b079bac57b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a735c286cc07f695f696da46113d28338d994116.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)^{2}+1}=\dfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -24px" width="174" height="64" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a735c286cc07f695f696da46113d28338d994116.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)^{2}+1}=\dfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -24px" width="174" height="64" />.  It is an equilateral triangle with height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/3/623fe474cfa16c0ea1a4bbf4ffeb0d4629e2069b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\cdot\sqrt{3}=1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="41" />, and area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/6/b46b30e39f9b219ebff2e125ddb575e1e61a6b7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}\cdot1}{2}=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="114" height="60" />.  The area of the paper is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/4/ca48e80686018f268d7ce4d5839a4875b8ac2cc3.png" class="latex" alt="$1\cdot\sqrt{3}=\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="95" height="18" />, and the folded paper has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/f/27f21fc7dca721efb06a7db133053fa18aa2a883.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}-\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}=\dfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="134" height="41" />.  The ratio of the area of the folded paper to that of the original paper is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/1/901d10a205c75d3cc5f3a1d4ada3db7ddd7c6cee.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} \: 2:3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="77" height="28" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9ab936eef4c53259854df0f902ee80c3dd8a4d7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]import graph; unitsize(3cm); real L = 0.05; pair A = (0,0); pair B = (sqrt(3),0); pair C = (sqrt(3),1); pair D = (0,1); pair X1 = (sqrt(3)/3,0); pair X2= (2*sqrt(3)/3,0); pair Y1 = (2*sqrt(3)/3,1); pair Y2 = (sqrt(3)/3,1); dot(X1); dot(Y1); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle, linewidth(2)); draw(B--D,dashed); draw(X1--Y1,dashed); draw(Y2--X1--D, dotted); draw(X2--Y1--B, dotted);[/asy]" width="252" height="148" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Our original paper can be divided like this: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c49f52879d3ae4c96a02111a4524bf2ce73119a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; unitsize(3cm); real L = 0.05; pair A = (0,0); pair B = (sqrt(3),0); pair C = (sqrt(3),1); pair D = (0,1); pair X1 = (sqrt(3)/3,0); pair X2= (2*sqrt(3)/3,0); pair Y1 = (2*sqrt(3)/3,1); pair Y2 = (sqrt(3)/3,1); dot(X1); dot(Y1); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle, linewidth(2)); draw(X1--Y1,dashed); draw(Y2--X1--D, dotted); draw(X2--Y1--B, dotted);[/asy]" width="252" height="148" />
After the fold across the dashed line, our paper becomes:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d37847271f1038d0d9ff92b64959fa4196aaa81f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; unitsize(3cm); real L = 0.05; pair A = (0,0); pair D = (0,1); pair X1 = (sqrt(3)/3,0); pair X2 = (sqrt(3)/6,0.5); pair Y1 = (2*sqrt(3)/3,1); pair Y2 = (sqrt(3)/3,1); pair Z1 = (sqrt(3)/2,1.5); dot(X1); dot(Y1); draw(X1--A--D--Z1--Y1, linewidth(2)); draw(X1--D--Y1); draw(X1--Y1, dashed); draw(Y2--X1,dotted); draw(X2--((sqrt(3)/6 + L/sqrt(3)),(0.5+L/2))); draw(Y2--(sqrt(3)/3,1-L));[/asy]" width="168" height="218" />
Since our original sheet of paper has six congruent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangles and and our new one has four, the ratio of the area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/a/17a436fcb26839c1ec781b56be90f3364bbb8bcb.png" class="latex" alt="$B:A$" width="43" height="13" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/0845add929d0cfe5c876146237d89c70e5fc9a90.png" class="latex" alt="$4:6\implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)} \: 2:3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="160" height="28" />
</p>
