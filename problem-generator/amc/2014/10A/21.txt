<p>Positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are such that the graphs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db50fb18d8162fa7ae542c19d4d0bb553bc6b12.png" class="latex" alt="$y=ax+5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/0/dc0f5b90e821897add7f6b6aa4aed4dec34f3404.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3x+b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" /> intersect the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis at the same point. What is the sum of all possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates of these points of intersection?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/b/cabf2c6ac3170c99dfb040eaf80a29846c9335b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ {-20}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ {-18}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ {-15}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ {-12}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ {-8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="455" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> values of the equations should be equal by the problem statement. We have that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/5/9b5974079f962de0b0cdffd7cc3e068e6aa9753a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0 = ax + 5 \implies x = -\dfrac{5}{a}\]" width="194" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/d/05ddc94c34a7ca9558c5927b090cbe03dbd143a6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0 = 3x+b \implies x= -\dfrac{b}{3}\]" width="192" height="37" />
Which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/8/9a816e2e78ddb95d2446fe65f792d4d65d844642.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-\dfrac{5}{a} = -\dfrac{b}{3} \implies ab = 15\]" width="186" height="37" />
The only possible pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> then are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/5/e1537eb1bbd18d22a3c188acbfe04ae298bed0d5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b) = (1,15), (3,5), (5,3), (15, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="266" height="18" />. These pairs give respective <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/4/5145f5141f8ec693122b501e7635d13e275e7884.png" class="latex" alt="$-5, -\dfrac{5}{3}, -1, -\dfrac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="37" /> which have a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/5/0c58e9556b419cfddcd94fb2d1fe47d84c45483f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \: -8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Going off of Solution 1, for the first equation, notice that the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> cannot be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a526e44e36254c196af103613bc724f312b6a209.png" class="latex" alt="$-5$" width="22" height="12" />. We also know for the first equation that the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> have to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> divided by something. Also, for the second equation, the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> can only be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/b/83b80e6c45d8277f7b005f829f5b2aabbd7e227f.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac13,-\frac23,-\frac33, \dots$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="125" height="37" />. Therefore, we see that, the only values common between the two sequences are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/9/4391f97d9abd0ba70eb87b9aa8e6083f2e77b746.png" class="latex" alt="$-1, -5, -\frac13,-\frac53$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="37" />, and adding them up, we get for our answer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/5/0c58e9556b419cfddcd94fb2d1fe47d84c45483f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \: -8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
