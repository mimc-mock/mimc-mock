<p>Nonzero real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/e/0fe3a37f5fe3f8aad2edd000f2343531ee2355bb.png" class="latex" alt="$x &lt; a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/d/01d0054e2461687d07444e337ee9107222d31a53.png" class="latex" alt="$y &lt; b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. How many of the following inequalities must be true?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/7/3979ca0bae618be8e543774fbf7e4483f60948b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(I)}\ x+y &lt; a+b\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa1f624a04f0f4a5bf4e976cfc7780f27a96a125.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(II)}\ x-y &lt; a-b\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca79e6a370455b3ef3b5e4439109768e1a03d8db.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(III)}\ xy &lt; ab\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/a/eba4db5565f310d94d209a8bb35a12a78bc9c0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(IV)}\ \frac{x}{y} &lt; \frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="92" height="36" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3ad07ba45d9a4dcd46fbd971320abb2585f7ce0e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="349" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let us denote <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b66c8a518bad923856228dfe8c2d44f3a8ca09.png" class="latex" alt="$a = x + k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="78" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/a/6da5c768d3e309c1b5337c06bf5ad8df078c5c01.png" class="latex" alt="$k &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/2/952cdef63dd674cdb4e441bd818e562c9a96d8a7.png" class="latex" alt="$b = y + l$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b6929b93575e7fdc389f2cd99c122cad452a7571.png" class="latex" alt="$l &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />. We can write that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/7/cd77a438af192a0e9509583e6f84ff440f50ec3d.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y &lt; x + y + k + l \implies x + y &lt; a + b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="326" height="18" />.
</p><p>It is important to note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> counterexample fully disproves a claim. Let's try substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/3/9e395764b968db4cf5790b3cf4062cf0c9423046.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-3,y=-4,a=1,b=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="225" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a541108812474a0d3781782db95e6ad6579759c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(II)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab2484fb0470e12ca615c9b061a5e921abe2973.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y&lt;a-b \implies -3 - (-4) &lt; 1 - 4  \implies 1 &lt; -3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="411" height="20" />.Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a541108812474a0d3781782db95e6ad6579759c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(II)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> is false.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/3/cc362d4a78df9648fcc75f63332e87bc916b42a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(III)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="17" /> states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/6/7163cd85e54fd9a407a188de702a01e2b13e1e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$xy&lt;ab \implies (-3) \cdot (-4) &lt; 1 \cdot 4 \implies 12 &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="357" height="20" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/3/cc362d4a78df9648fcc75f63332e87bc916b42a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(III)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="17" /> is false.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba5fa1cae0968ba1ff4bce7f4954c2a3cdcc09c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(IV)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="17" /> states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/6/eb6cdb8a6f722bd7613aad3024b5b6c9482027de.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}&lt;\frac{a}{b} \implies \frac{-3}{-4} &lt; \frac{1}{4} \implies 0.75 &lt; 0.25$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="306" height="42" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba5fa1cae0968ba1ff4bce7f4954c2a3cdcc09c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(IV)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="17" /> is false.
</p><p><br />
One of our four inequalities is true, hence, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/2/78239690c6863df7c4c76cb7408bfb720373cb62.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) 1}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />
</p><p>~MathFun1000
</p>
