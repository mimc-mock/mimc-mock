<p>The product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/8/ee8528171177d909de07096d5abfc5ad2db8761b.png" class="latex" alt="$(8)(888\dots8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" />, where the second factor has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> digits, is an integer whose digits have a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/0/020619306452fcc43fe1e5f53290f33dfc7640cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 901\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 911\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 919\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 991\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 999$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can list the first few numbers in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/f/68f4ffb23da95680cb6a401099b3d79351c75d29.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot (8....8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />
</p><p>(Hard problem to do without the multiplication, but you can see the pattern early on)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b1aa55974543867a9f539851a9912e5827b5eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 8 = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/0/c60399f15a71b17fd1bd038cff3d50fd40f894c7.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 88 = 704$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/c/30cae27300afb79ab8e23a76160fca334c861485.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 888 = 7104$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/2/0025578b29e9bff35ffcd1259953929af409ba8c.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 8888 = 71104$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="128" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/2/c22f2c3540a00b50fd0ee228cfb6c7b635ea46d5.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 88888 = 711104$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="146" height="13" />
</p><p>By now it's clear that the numbers will be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/9/0f9dc56a5ff59e63eaefd81bad051d4324767d7a.png" class="latex" alt="$k-2$" width="41" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />'s, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/0/3909f41cdd1f192d375ee677f3fa4f02db09aa90.png" class="latex" alt="$04$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />. We want to make the numbers sum to 1000, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/b/05bda369b473cfa604d0bdf961a65dbc217d9d33.png" class="latex" alt="$7+4+(k-2) = 1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />. Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/1/ea119149b3503387f3d0b390a954706dfc910411.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 991$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />, meaning the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd4003a21b78c8ecdf40aef519713be4d9daa7eb.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" />
</p><p>Another way to proceed is that we know the difference between the sum of the digits of each product and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, so we just do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/5/e85cda7cedb7687ddf4246570672c3bb19793bdf.png" class="latex" alt="$1000-9=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 991}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="168" height="30" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proof_of_Solution_1">Proof of Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Since this solution won't fly on a proof-based competition, here's a proof that it's valid:
</p><p>We will call <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/eddec74883a9e0b19ca1d026d09c179f22ed705c.png" class="latex" alt="$x_k=8(888\dots8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" /> with exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />s. We then rewrite this more formally, as:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f09c81ed3fbc89248e8995f2df9074137aeff4ed.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_k=8\biggr(\sum_{n=0}^{k}8(10)^n\biggr)\]" width="156" height="50" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/263b9b0480eb0f5d817450f95e2f201a941c44c7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=64\biggr(\sum_{n=0}^{k}(10)^n\biggr)\]" width="129" height="50" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/b/3cb61a1ea2bc8d73f7bbd9616149c78989ca8b18.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=64\frac{10^{k+1}-1}{9}\]" width="113" height="39" />
</p><p>Then, finding a recursive formula, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b210ad0b69093f23c8bcc0003452da82aac3d3c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_{k+1}=64\times 10^{k+1}+x_k\]" width="182" height="20" />
</p><p>We will now use induction, Our base case will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f681bd84648c174d45eed0037702cae77ed0686.png" class="latex" alt="$k=2$" width="43" height="12" />. It's easy to see that this becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c688427beb15713e85f6c16cc209ca942c7852c.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2=704$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="15" />. Then, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f05f085f774fc8ab50676b778b86a1f1d1114fc8.png" class="latex" alt="$k+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> case: let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/4/814dee8cccfe63e001fb787714d60ea623ddf766.png" class="latex" alt="$x_k=7111\dots104$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="17" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/9/0f9dc56a5ff59e63eaefd81bad051d4324767d7a.png" class="latex" alt="$k-2$" width="41" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />s. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/1/b51d7258d4dfc431464a77e9f914fa60ff85c18b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{k+1}=64000\dots000+7111\dots104$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="272" height="16" />. Adding these numbers, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e56682c46081b32e4e42a56ed731de72a35201.png" class="latex" alt="$x_{k+1}=71111\dots104$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="16" />. 
</p><p>Summing these digits, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff4d4de699ff0af49673971aaa67d19c5c3ca034.png" class="latex" alt="$4+7+(k-2)=1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="178" height="18" />, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/a/58ad27f44aee76063f1c5f288136261d1618f1d1.png" class="latex" alt="$k=991$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />.
</p>
