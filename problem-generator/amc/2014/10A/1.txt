<p>What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/c/78cc238527a94a4ae05345fb8c6c579760ca5480.png" class="latex" alt="$10\cdot\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+\tfrac{1}{5}+\tfrac{1}{10}\right)^{-1}?$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="156" height="25" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f8ede8bf009ce5a1aebce01cbe863c25b8f262.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{25}{2} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{170}{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 170$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="404" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12e21260e737c4ecc2bf7b4116b1f1e7bcde2b37.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[10\cdot\left(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{5}+\frac{1}{10}\right)^{-1}\]" width="164" height="46" />
Making the denominators equal gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/8/858522583ebacde2c4e851b8fd64c093086a668a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 10\cdot\left(\frac{5}{10}+\frac{2}{10}+\frac{1}{10}\right)^{-1}\]" width="223" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/c/b9c7e18416c20508fb4b4e0822b1c6183699a746.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 10\cdot\left(\frac{5+2+1}{10}\right)^{-1}\]" width="187" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/3/2832b440e25ccb94bfdd37a6c04898ff0c7544ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 10\cdot\left(\frac{8}{10}\right)^{-1}\]" width="134" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/3/6d309b6b45b8f4e35d741ae32ea81647183f0b5e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 10\cdot\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{-1}\]" width="125" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/1/8612999285ee03d02effd49381ab5a5a3aea86a6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 10\cdot\frac{5}{4}\]" width="81" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/0/0100cd85a3f1692e5c483ab0559633686ad33cdd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies \frac{50}{4}\]" width="59" height="38" />
Finally, simplifying gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/b/5fba06f914e8493aeae1d11cbb060a29311f3b7d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{25}{2}}\]" width="106" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/f/bbf8ad5578ed582621b5c63d4182c88e211562c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^{-1}\cdot \left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{10}\right)^{-1}\]" width="210" height="46" />By Distributive Property,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36ced254e97009416e30a918a5873be9d3776858.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{1}{20}+\frac{1}{50}+\frac{1}{100}\right)^{-1}\]" width="158" height="46" />Now, we want to find the least common multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/a/3dae29e5c194989d6a7f722c4949164a4c1f8a61.png" class="latex" alt="$20, 50,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/a/36af3a317653fc5ff424093b4df40c2b4d5a6f5f.png" class="latex" alt="$100,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="31" height="16" /> so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/a/d2ab602940470b5dd03bd09ffa7c057bfd8cfae0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{lcm}(20,50,100)=\text{lcm}(2^2 \cdot 5,2 \cdot 5^2,2^2 \cdot 5^2)=2^2 \cdot 5^2=100\]" width="444" height="20" />Converting everything to a denominator of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/a/36af3a317653fc5ff424093b4df40c2b4d5a6f5f.png" class="latex" alt="$100,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="31" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/3/bb310752666bfc04f6311cae6fd9e37b6f4377b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{5}{100}+\frac{2}{100}+\frac{1}{100}\right)^{-1}=\left(\frac{8}{100}\right)^{-1}=\frac{100}{8}\]" width="332" height="46" />Now, we use Euclidean Algorithm, to find if this fraction is reducible, so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36cf18d63b75b8ad0dd7cbeb2b0b9db88b0217f6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\gcd(100,8)=\gcd(12,8)=\gcd(4,8)=\gcd(4,4)\]" width="368" height="18" />Thus, both the numerator and denominator are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4054552638cf9f29e857a1306bd3ec31df3c19b6.png" class="latex" alt="$4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e616bd29d5fcd3e25ba81f89729a22c1bcfa3283.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{100}{8} \cdot \frac{4}{4}=\frac{100}{4} \cdot \frac{4}{8}=25 \cdot \frac{1}{2}=\boxed{\frac{25}{2}}\]" width="264" height="48" />
</p><p>- kante314
