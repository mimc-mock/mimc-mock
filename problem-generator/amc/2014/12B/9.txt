<p>Convex quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c56ff6dcfd62e4e93c0469964d28b362ccff9c2b.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=3$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a5322c7f896502c208bbb4a354efc8d212620667.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f43e3d27fe9f4d0a322db6e6d2f1f28100e7e5cd.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b196f83c62ce83e5cb39f4d69af59fe42401f4da.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/1/de1ad597f1289a99a95cedd21818dd99f87361e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABC=90^{\circ}$" width="103" height="13" />, as shown. What is the area of the quadrilateral?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed685cbdb58e7beeff868e0123c51cd79633def7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A=(0,0), B=(-3,0), C=(-3,-4), D=(48/5,-36/5); draw(A--B--C--D--A);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SW); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,E); draw(rightanglemark(A,B,C,25)); [/asy]" width="252" height="148" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ef3c2d971cdc20cdba2bba5b41445d13e0bb08.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 58.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="408" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Note that by the pythagorean theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/b/edb20df9f4016a15bd68c2c0286c8b5e540ced58.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=5$" width="60" height="13" />.  Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/1/4f105551f628f107282e0cc6c89063f3c1faece9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CAD$" width="55" height="13" /> is a right angle because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/8/fc8f59495796eb4b2416631af1682f18bfcfbf35.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CAD$" width="59" height="13" /> is a right triangle.  The area of the quadrilateral is the sum of the areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/8/fc8f59495796eb4b2416631af1682f18bfcfbf35.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CAD$" width="59" height="13" /> which is equal to 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/0/d60d5dac5538495750c4b3cbf16b14c243bd6d22.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3\times4}{2} + \frac{5\times12}{2} = 6 + 30 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 36}\]" width="280" height="37" />
</p>
