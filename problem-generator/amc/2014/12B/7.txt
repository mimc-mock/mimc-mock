<p>For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/a/02a003520e3b830bd307b584d7777a3971b673fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{30-n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="33" /> also a positive integer?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/b/5fb08f1c6cfc0feadf543b455cd706558e20a686.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/6/8062f128e803f5747ddd257f47e46fcdc1987b0b.png" class="latex" alt="$n \le 30$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" /> or else <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/6/0a6e01d35d1df97577f5a87837e2b9d6c4de86ff.png" class="latex" alt="$30-n$" width="51" height="12" /> will be negative, resulting in a negative fraction.  We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/d/d7de921059a1c1a3c66ba2bddafb08c582fa335a.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 15$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" /> or else the fraction's denominator will exceed its numerator making the fraction unable to equal a positive integer value.  Substituting all values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> gives us integer values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/6/3c6a5dbdb9d29540236036605b4bed6d334be868.png" class="latex" alt="$n=15, 20, 24, 25, 27, 28, 29$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="210" height="16" />.  Counting them up, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6cecfe394f16d2d6d7bc6844d2d845b2a014f077.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />.  
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b9b859fdba6aba9b9849722ce32a75a7e2dbb82.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{30-n}=m$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="33" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/1/f21b49d4a1d07db0896300cd7c894fdbb50883a6.png" class="latex" alt="$m \in \mathbb{N}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="13" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb6b5fe4e51ce2e84815996b23779135d630045e.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\frac{30m}{m+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="84" height="39" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4bea4f5ee837b0249288558f2fb73a871b264491.png" class="latex" alt="$m+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="13" /> are relatively prime, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/a/24a50ced596c3675a926df1f90f47c5729f80e94.png" class="latex" alt="$m+1|30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />. Our answer is the number of proper divisors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/4/0247ce692cde24e9d6abcbcb823925cd5986a9a6.png" class="latex" alt="$2^13^15^1$" width="47" height="15" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/c/2bcebb2ad8e2985b84ffe45339a596547cbc7072.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+1)(1+1)(1+1)-1 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="275" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span id="Video_Solution_1_(Quick_and_Easy)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution_1_.28Quick_and_Easy.29">Video Solution 1 (Quick and Easy)</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/rN76FKYRjls">https://youtu.be/rN76FKYRjls</a>
</p><p>~Education, the Study of Everything
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa196c781944f2a6128ad10ca916ddca04d7e2f6.png" class="latex" alt="$30-n|n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />. Then, by divisibility rules:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c73c6bbaf45a0c8c5759d25ab53beaf3b65389.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Leftrightarrow 30-n|n+30-n\]" width="163" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/6/f267d661032a685c756479441867af4b1e7b0a39.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Leftrightarrow 30-n|30\]" width="96" height="18" />
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> divisors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be positive, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174c0d42a1809acc82d89a56dfb1fefa6d5766c4.png" class="latex" alt="$30|30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> isn't counted, meaning we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6cecfe394f16d2d6d7bc6844d2d845b2a014f077.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
