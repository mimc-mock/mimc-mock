<p>Find the sum of all the positive solutions of 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/2/2b20aaae1edde833f927a00fe5e9cf44e966b04e.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cos2x \left(\cos2x - \cos{\left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } \right) = \cos4x - 1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="371" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/9/e09240ffccf2c1f83928589bc70f97e05a500d96.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \pi \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 810\pi \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1008\pi \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 1080 \pi \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 1800\pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="494" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Rewrite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/f/c4f937e31cb61aa49be9890a98a7aea67614f830.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{4x} - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/6/3e69ef92981f41b9eb80a583df9ac2359e34c777.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cos^2{2x} - 2$" width="97" height="15" />.  Now let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/501d41a8a188ab8042991bdaf32f88489f8e956e.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \cos{2x}$" width="80" height="12" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84ebb38654cb213eca953ad3b94384b408d1aa35.png" class="latex" alt="$b = \cos{\left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) }$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="141" height="43" />.  We have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/7/8975fcac46468f295fbd0d3333a9455ca2256cac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2a(a - b) = 2a^2 - 2\]" width="153" height="19" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e002e609f83a150ff5ec4544c0fa03ea099cc517.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab = 1\]" width="49" height="13" />.
</p><p>Notice that either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/851b03ce92c3ad8da97daf2785cf4e103c7a8d66.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/35496101e151991fc3213a7472ade63fd75bdf8e.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/6/ce67f0e5287f11dc65e3f81673742d580064d9d1.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />.  For the first case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> only when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/b/27bae8236f6f2a60c7d6b4468864fe89cc53db56.png" class="latex" alt="$x = k\pi$" width="55" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is an integer.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/851b03ce92c3ad8da97daf2785cf4e103c7a8d66.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f66a375b1ff3e469e2bc2238ab9d403c6695236f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2014\pi^2}{k\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="57" height="39" /> is an even multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/6/fa6dc62525fec80a874760c5bcb1c011df7904c5.png" class="latex" alt="$2014 = 2*19*53$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="139" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/0/280c42c583a937e4274ac58e878525b8566bffb9.png" class="latex" alt="$b =1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> only when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is an odd divisor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />.  This gives us these possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/e/61e8a96db482d9ff6a871fefac5c8aea486ed03e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x= \pi, 19\pi, 53\pi, 1007\pi\]" width="174" height="16" />
For the case where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/35496101e151991fc3213a7472ade63fd75bdf8e.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/d/37d275745147cb3de89a06b577eaff5eba272ce6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{2x} = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/6/566d9c107ecc48514e56b81c127d5d6494c0bb63.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{m\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="33" />, where m is odd.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/0/150fb3e3b1401886aa823e115a95ec441133ef28.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2014\pi^2}{\frac{m\pi}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="57" height="45" /> must also be an odd multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> in order for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> to equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/9/30920cd07525eea9aafbb4aa1e1c91c84d9afdf2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4028}{m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="38" height="37" /> must be odd.  We can quickly see that dividing an even number by an odd number will never yield an odd number, so there are no possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, and therefore no cases where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/35496101e151991fc3213a7472ade63fd75bdf8e.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/6/ce67f0e5287f11dc65e3f81673742d580064d9d1.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />. Therefore, the sum of all our possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/0/830c1c88ac182221a4c3439f4527d3450356f47a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\pi + 19\pi + 53\pi + 1007\pi = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 1080 \pi}\]" width="302" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Very similar to the solution above, re-write the expression using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/f/e6f83cfe37b43a59a3eb8ba0d975e8d6d85f8e81.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos 4x = 2 \cos^2 2x - 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="171" height="18" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1e9f2127f1c61e358f479f24b74d363bcda9a3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2\cos2x \left(\cos2x - \cos{\left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } \right) = 2 \cos^2 2x - 2\]" width="390" height="43" />
</p><p>Now, expand the LHS and cancel terms:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/5/375069bf90780dc98b36888cee13ae721e1529c1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2 \cos^2 2x - 2 \cos 2x \cos{\left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } = 2 \cos^2 2x - 2\]" width="384" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/c/2acc99af2129f31d1abbebd69d9c55e98b6e4751.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos 2x \cos{\left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } = 1\]" width="194" height="43" />
</p><p>Now we use product-to-sum identities to get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/3/c23008c4ad5eb5d6e0d5ce098baf8ed474ab7aa8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2} \left(\cos{2x + \left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } + \cos{2x + \left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } \right) = 1\]" width="403" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/5006059b0e866a024c11003cf2d17a8b68d4b919.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos{2x + \left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } + \cos{2x + \left( \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \right) } = 1\]" width="362" height="43" />
</p><p>Notice that for any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a35bd1cc8d3ede8474b4d2a0c6cec47771889e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\max{\cos \theta} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="108" height="14" />. This is achieved when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/5/8656595bf44ec840dac700925abe3bcbccab0b46.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta = 0$" width="42" height="13" />, or equivalently 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9acc8cca66491363cc1e1c5cbd6ff5b15ade0d4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2x - \frac{2014\pi^2}{x} \equiv 0 \mod 2\pi\]" width="212" height="39" />
</p><p>We can cleverly assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/b/d9be3345774f8f8142cb359ef6f776b2c971e86a.png" class="latex" alt="$x=c\pi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="10" /> for some real <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />. Then, we must have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/a/1ca2ca50615b5d0c1c7f3fc2e963a45b3a1864d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2c - \frac{2014}{c} \equiv 0 \mod 2\]" width="180" height="37" />
</p><p>In order for this to be satisfied, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5f7bca0a65dfb1266ccd78d5bf9687437492ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2014}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="42" height="38" /> must be an even integer. Factoring <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d4001de72e4e1d832c9b9e0c6952953802409f8.png" class="latex" alt="$2014 = 2 \cdot 19 \cdot 53$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="14" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> must be a positive odd integer that divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. Our only positive valid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/7318af8584678ee97318c641c4e1c04b04694b80.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 1, 19, 53, 1007$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="17" />. Our answer is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/7/387ebbf94191ea24eb2b214d261f9b89c0661067.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi(1+19+53+1007) = 1080\pi \implies \textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="326" height="20" />. 
</p><p><br />
-FIREDRAGONMATH16
</p>
