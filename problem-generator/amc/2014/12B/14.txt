<p>A rectangular box has a total surface area of 94 square inches. The sum of the lengths of all its edges is 48 inches. What is the sum of the lengths in inches of all of its interior diagonals?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82de85ea2d09abbe0f316708bbb4e7a1f4f627e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 10\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 16\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 40\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="507" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the side lengths of the rectangular box be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/5/32581661cddf01ad30c1e36efe72f99eb0a0adca.png" class="latex" alt="$x, y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="11" />  and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. 
From the information we get 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/8/bc86401a844711e88def93692937422d75fb2fea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4(x+y+z) = 48 \Rightarrow x+y+z = 12\]" width="282" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/7/9e781c0ada050703109cb29ab2e9696f544177b8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2(xy+yz+xz) = 94\]" width="168" height="18" />
</p><p>The sum of all the  lengths of the box's interior diagonals is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea7e1a232129089dd72f040de7f6db3dad14b926.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4 \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\]" width="123" height="22" />
</p><p>Squaring the first expression, we get: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f44629c35e04f8a518d949d8ba7a1e0285bbeb5d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[144 =(x+y+z)^2 =  x^2+y^2+z^2 + 2(xy+yz+xz)\]" width="412" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c681745fbfebd8e6b3f016967ee718f13f5e7303.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[144 =  x^2+y^2+z^2 + 94\]" width="186" height="18" />
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/84312496748f2bd8594ce025b657bf34646b97f2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2+y^2+z^2 = 50\]" width="137" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/5/835d55911dfd8ed143f7905470abcd933e4bde66.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4 \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2} =  \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt 2}\]" width="235" height="33" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc6d9a6172d35127b6cb37ea92192caf7029a54.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> be the side lengths of the rectangular prism. So we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/8/f383c02dc22e02029e2b27c55742389bf83fe414.png" class="latex" alt="$2(ab + ac + bc) = 94$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="162" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/0/1102aadf21b5edafc372c41ad519d77856452b7d.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c= 12$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="114" height="16" />. Note that the interior diagonal is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/3/7636cf5e852fba317aa27fe64443ed53656df350.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="107" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/1/571e6f9e4bafc09441bbf1f1a2d3d9e03df74373.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = (a+b+c)^2 - 2(ab + ac + bc) = 144 - 94 = 50$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="484" height="21" />. So the length of the interior diagonal is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/3/7532deaac4f1f0cec28befbffd3642c1e41b9fba.png" class="latex" alt="$5\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. Since we have 4 of the same interior diagonals that answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55d2d4e49d28398f284f370cf6de5b5f1ec4e69f.png" class="latex" alt="$20\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="18" /> which is D. 
~coolmath_2018
</p>
