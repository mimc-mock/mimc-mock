<p>A set S consists of triangles whose sides have integer lengths less than 5, and no two elements of S are congruent or similar. What is the largest number of elements that S can have? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/7/567797ed33be3423790ac3b609dc91fe47ec8da2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 11\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> to be the set of all integral triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/0/d80414f2f94f70731e91b6e12c4e6934c8fa65de.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b, c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca80e390c2eee07b24d2edc6023def9310a3c76.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge b \ge c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/7/ff759e1c84f47ce7e17f9124a8d420fb6afec69e.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c &gt; a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/7/dc75abe3727f853cd2bca2f352c1e9b2c6b4c5dc.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c &lt; 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" />. Now we enumerate the elements of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2118bc58f41746389def78ee659abf720bd1677.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58cbef51c0b84b10a3cbc99bae0ab1897aff20e.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/7/e97a72de1a41e6ab97eac5cef3f05b12ef3ec38c.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/6/5964ef53ef5b2718c2f2818ded2fca51a9bfacc0.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/9/fa911dcfdaf1d7c8d161016c2cab54eeca9a905e.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 3, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/5/f458f038467e2b9026fdb2b97926b566a9745a38.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/9/37937cd013627c14944d14a1f4f669168c874be5.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 3, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e804b07a6641177cd5bf44a4d7af79b1192d509e.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45de0fb4f26ea0202268618243d20f72d8263949.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 3, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/a/a4a2c54eda84ebad3e26f2070294e034cc4537bd.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 2, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/4/3c4d0f837e9eaf6483102a55535bcc746d3ac3f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 2, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/26388c3ec54e044b5d4001c111e91dd33be2c071.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 2, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e61637ce2d59296380ccb6f26edee8d45c60850.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />
</p><p>It should be clear that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/c/11ceba9d7455309f60178fe82bb6e81e6c01fa83.png" class="latex" alt="$|S|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="20" height="18" /> is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc73f3216cbfb7ceb707c9c143823d7e5e49f659.png" class="latex" alt="$|T|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> minus the larger "duplicates" (e.g. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/4/3c4d0f837e9eaf6483102a55535bcc746d3ac3f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 2, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> is a larger duplicate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e61637ce2d59296380ccb6f26edee8d45c60850.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />). Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc73f3216cbfb7ceb707c9c143823d7e5e49f659.png" class="latex" alt="$|T|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and the number of higher duplicates is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/378a09b380bdddc44bd948b6bb68aed548e0bf66.png" class="latex" alt="$13 - 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/9/629ecb8ecd5f7e0c0f84efe07c0332d2647f54d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p>
