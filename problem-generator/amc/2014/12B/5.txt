<p>Doug constructs a square window using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> equal-size panes of glass, as shown. The ratio of the height to width for each pane is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/8/ab8d490a8b3d87ec6ff3aa702b1567de01037c8d.png" class="latex" alt="$5&#160;: 2$" width="32" height="12" />, and the borders around and between the panes are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> inches wide. In inches, what is the side length of the square window?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4bd527ea5f7e41a6d5541d0fc5b06615b22b69.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] fill((0,0)--(2,0)--(2,26)--(0,26)--cycle,gray); fill((6,0)--(8,0)--(8,26)--(6,26)--cycle,gray); fill((12,0)--(14,0)--(14,26)--(12,26)--cycle,gray); fill((18,0)--(20,0)--(20,26)--(18,26)--cycle,gray); fill((24,0)--(26,0)--(26,26)--(24,26)--cycle,gray); fill((0,0)--(26,0)--(26,2)--(0,2)--cycle,gray); fill((0,12)--(26,12)--(26,14)--(0,14)--cycle,gray); fill((0,24)--(26,24)--(26,26)--(0,26)--cycle,gray); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/9/429d5528310491e7eee11b7bc79185efecff8aec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 26\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 28\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 32\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 34$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the height of the panes equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" />, and let the width of the panes equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" />.  Now notice that the total width of the borders equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and the total height of the borders is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.  We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/1/c31292295a131eb993a821ed21726cc9ea13ebc4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[10 + 4(2x) = 6 + 2(5x)\]" width="178" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969f2a62fcd94063dcee2eff234579ad20ad8c10.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 2\]" width="42" height="12" />
Now, the total side length of the window equals 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/d/5dd5e50ec1c8b28f264628aeddca055d921744a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[10+ 4(2x) = 10 + 16 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 26}\]" width="253" height="28" />
</p>
