<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/2/c92728df69bac90147fdb422ef18ed57f40fb787.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+..+199+200=\frac{(200)(201)}{2}=20100$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="351" height="38" /> numbers in total. Let the median be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. We want to find the median <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/c/8ccc066133aa10fee023edbde69d3985904d2dde.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{k(k+1)}{2}=20100/2,\]" width="159" height="38" />
or
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/d/7ad0ae4f03a0fffc0b86e7b36b3ce0895d9e703b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[k(k+1)=20100.\]" width="137" height="18" />
Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/428305397f26b037becffbf34a767b119a04f383.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{20100} \approx 142$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="18" />. Plugging this value in as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/b/e5bbc3647259e660c3606f8ce0cedfa76a9cff51.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2}(142)(143)=10153.\]" width="167" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e009a1faf20c9d17dad000f3b5f3b44d9f3a9514.png" class="latex" alt="$10153-142&lt;10050$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="163" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/e/5ce8614c1b3993e78519131182767d0a43ea0714.png" class="latex" alt="$142$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e457abc5e55d562a8e426b0d19d8d006c9c06ace.png" class="latex" alt="$152$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />nd and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/5/f152e2688863b88e971932e82c28adec9063a05e.png" class="latex" alt="$153$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />rd numbers, and hence, our desired answer. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/f/0afefb3569f51abfaeb25370231a2f9a8f724045.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{(C) 142}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="67" height="28" />.
</p><p>Note that we can derive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/428305397f26b037becffbf34a767b119a04f383.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{20100} \approx 142$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="18" /> through the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/8/d38c04f868404d0ae2801c0b7dc4c55e84d07898.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{n} = \sqrt{a+b} \approx \sqrt{a} + \frac{b}{2\sqrt{a} + 1},\]" width="248" height="42" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is a perfect square less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. We set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/6/0e6fda9ee736a83793e8fdd874a2885921de7645.png" class="latex" alt="$19600$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e4790acf8c0c7f8a0bb1e84ab5a4e93bdaa17a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a} = 140$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/1/121467f0ecf89e046dd205bef38b128062efdcf8.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 500$" width="58" height="12" />. We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/a/32a841024197d6d0d17b605431dea63ff649f7e6.png" class="latex" alt="$n \approx 140 + \frac{500}{2(140)+1} \approx 142$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="221" height="42" />. ~approximation by ciceronii
</p><p>Note by Fasolinka (use answer choices): Once you know that the answer is in the 140s range by the approximation, it is highly improbable for the answer to be anything but C.
</p>
