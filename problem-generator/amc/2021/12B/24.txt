<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> denote the intersection point of the diagonals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" />. Remark that by symmetry <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> is the midpoint of both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/e/0deaae00f6af4ac1f5882568e7b6667bb334a2ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{PQ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843d282b682b661d2298f2a32e322e364ccac625.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{RS}$" width="26" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/f/f9f35a388a94e0d88925d7a96e161442c897526a.png" class="latex" alt="$XP = XQ = 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a005fc86aba92f22bb252341e075c3b0bc92ad.png" class="latex" alt="$XR = XS = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="116" height="13" />. Now note that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2dd89e92be79d0151c6a203b3d0d754e179692ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle APB = \angle ARB = 90^\circ$" width="183" height="13" />, quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f6e54472913b94e257ce047763e73c9adb27dad.png" class="latex" alt="$ARPB$" width="56" height="13" /> is cyclic, and so
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/4/5e457e2176d3718620628f1f05e29a00f6821b25.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[XR\cdot XA = XP\cdot XB,\]" width="177" height="16" />which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/d/b8d157169d238c71f78f3798a6b2a2b753aad971.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{XA}{XB} = \tfrac{XP}{XR} = \tfrac34$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="110" height="21" />.
</p><p>Thus let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/6/6a6216b67d30a2d821e6cdd9c18c57633cd324c0.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> be such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e3b45ce5b19d9e9eb40e243de55d4ae8adf093b.png" class="latex" alt="$XA = 3x$" width="73" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/3/5432c2266e1310cb2d99a60cc0ccbf1894ba4049.png" class="latex" alt="$XB = 4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />. Then Pythagorean Theorem on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/20168d21322a1ee7807e427c20e28f4dfb609db1.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle APX$" width="59" height="13" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302b741bd54fc24586ead5ea253fbd587b87e872.png" class="latex" alt="$AP = \sqrt{AX^2 - XP^2} = 3\sqrt{x^2-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="263" height="18" />, and so<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d6ab35c60d4c601a70be2ac18760dd868e35771.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[[ABCD] = 2[ABD] = AP\cdot BD = 3\sqrt{x^2-1}\cdot 8x = 24x\sqrt{x^2-1}.\]" width="483" height="21" />Solving this for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/7/1e777506d18d68719d4a3c571af307e3c18fb413.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = \tfrac12 + \tfrac{\sqrt{41}}8$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="101" height="24" />, and so<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e3304ebd189c51dd0bacea140ddf91ce32598c3e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(8x)^2 = 64x^2 = 64\left(\tfrac12 + \tfrac{\sqrt{41}}8\right) = 32 + 8\sqrt{41}.\]" width="338" height="32" />The requested answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74fcffce5e2a28c65542bd0b61ded87470763b46.png" class="latex" alt="$32 + 8 + 41 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} ~81}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="180" height="30" />.
