<p>Two fair dice, each with at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> faces are rolled. On each face of each dice is printed a distinct integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to the number of faces on that die, inclusive. The probability of rolling a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/c/fdcb968f4f3d2e0d78a67fedc92a8d31183b2436.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> of the probability of rolling a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/7/517151f6f181bc7bcbfe164ac5c211c84abc0e92.png" class="latex" alt="$10,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> and the probability of rolling a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e10f7b229549d9afbdef6ae2862bc89bacfe16cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />. What is the least possible number of faces on the two dice combined?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e29a3fd7d7d5839b1499bd199ddb41dee72d77e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }16 \qquad \textbf{(B) }17 \qquad \textbf{(C) }18\qquad \textbf{(D) }19 \qquad \textbf{(E) }20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose the dice have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> faces, and WLOG <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb57e55bf88090389ea5b582cb7d7af7dbee40c.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq{b}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. Since each die has at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> faces, there will always be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. As a result, there must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/b/b9bcf5f308d6a9091e56cbd53cdbf6bf4f057cbd.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{4}{3}\cdot6=8$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="65" height="21" /> ways to sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. There are at most nine distinct ways to get a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, which are possible whenever <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef625d829a1e75f60d98766c438dd52b6d9e066.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b\geq{9}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="16" />. To achieve exactly eight ways, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> faces, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/9/309ac76579ee64befd103ff77f90a9fbfc3529b7.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of ways to obtain a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/8/ac8c7a54763507f1632d1c15c8285d80bcf34121.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{n}{8a}=\tfrac{1}{12}\implies n=\tfrac{2}{3}a$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="164" height="21" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2ccc9e3c02a4b38f185d5b43a4b7456338e30a6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/a/24a56e2a4125972a0eb1e5cecdca3193851610c9.png" class="latex" alt="$n\leq8\implies a\leq{12}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="145" height="15" />. In addition to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/e/86ee62410ee70feaf1ea4cc8f818c35f442c9acc.png" class="latex" alt="$3\mid{a}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" />, we only have to test <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/56260e8a4ec210feae2dbb7217ac43f622b2dfbe.png" class="latex" alt="$a=9,12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="68" height="16" />, of which both work. Taking the smaller one, our answer becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/6/956df7de62a933a3a7c8d70b02f9b7758df84611.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=9+8=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 17}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="192" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Suppose the dice have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> faces, and WLOG <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb57e55bf88090389ea5b582cb7d7af7dbee40c.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq{b}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. Note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/3/4a3cb11eaa39974663470921c653cf3bdc4728c8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" /> since they are both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, there is one way to make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, and incrementing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> by one will add another way. This gives us the probability of making a 12 as 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/f/24f528786b4878213cd19993121dc15823f1ab61.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a+b-11}{ab}=\frac{1}{12}\]" width="127" height="37" />
Cross-multiplying, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/b/25b078f84572ad86fba38472dab171c502397c9b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[12a+12b-132=ab\]" width="165" height="14" />
Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick now gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7de75e7320d7e4d6c5f51d1b0d8750c8581dcd3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a-12)(b-12)=12\]" width="166" height="18" />
This narrows the possibilities down to 3 ordered pairs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c74708cecbdb3c6829482d046a24439f5114f4e.png" class="latex" alt="$(13,24)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/33706bee7292ae456a0b76fbab6728e2da45eaaa.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,10)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a286ce0b75df7a0b70059c3bbe9057e48e17c1bf.png" class="latex" alt="$(8,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.  We can obviously ignore the first pair and test the next two straightforwardly.  The last pair yields the answer:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb624aab3b633b814b8a2997a2441cc6dd968c3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{6}{72}=\frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{9+8-9}{72}\right)\]" width="159" height="43" />
The answer is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/f/41fa9442b0d4a036b82c2c7315efabedfb0e9169.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=8+9=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 17}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="194" height="30" />.
</p><p>~Hyprox1413
</p><p><br />
</p>
