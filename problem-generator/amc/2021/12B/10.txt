<p>Two distinct numbers are selected from the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/9/9d90f234623a632cfe3dc12728d076ca3a31d00d.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,2,3,4,\dots,36,37\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" /> so that the sum of the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7f6c79f2148f0cec592c9517b40ee1b82ef22e.png" class="latex" alt="$35$" width="17" height="12" /> numbers is the product of these two numbers. What is the difference of these two numbers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/9/c09782573923feaeeb698171ed4d19fa465280b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }5 \qquad \textbf{(B) }7 \qquad \textbf{(C) }8\qquad \textbf{(D) }9 \qquad \textbf{(E) }10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="362" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> integers is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64f8070867b03b0dc224db7e741135e07f4502c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/e/03e93164e2d73694e6b320c730bd8e4d53e66d94.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{37(37+1)}{2}=703$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="139" height="40" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/4/fb45faa2bca593d76a69db08784709a4b9fcc9eb.png" class="latex" alt="$703-x-y=xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="16" />
</p><p>Rearranging, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/2/352b94fc258131f828fccd98a6c5275f22f152a6.png" class="latex" alt="$xy+x+y=703$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="16" />. We can factor this equation by <a href="/wiki/index.php/SFFT" class="mw-redirect" title="SFFT">SFFT</a> to get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5b321505a8b8faf5f7875810b708b9df879582.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)(y+1)=704$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" />
</p><p>Looking at the possible divisors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/f/c0fc36007a73940281ed902ed65e36e252fe928a.png" class="latex" alt="$704 = 2^6\cdot11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="17" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" /> are within the constraints of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffa7a666c770027e4fecc9d20cfad8e54ca704a.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; x \leq y \leq 37$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="120" height="16" /> so we try those:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e83224c318950fb4179bad89b07a6da1d328dd1.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)(y+1) = 22\cdot32$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="186" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/5/215b9536ddd05d1752a39c35dcb6176ec17f6380.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1=22, y+1 = 32$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="175" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/a/68af430d38f2a1b1105c1c2f37d8047e9c97d3bc.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 21, y = 31$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />
</p><p>Therefore, the difference <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b9c6fa842b80c3f1072af193f2d8e4de50dfa13.png" class="latex" alt="$y-x=31-21=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="214" height="30" />.
</p><p>~ SoySoy4444
</p><p>~MathFun1000 (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/latex.png" class="latex" alt="$\LaTeX$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="15" /> help)
</p>
