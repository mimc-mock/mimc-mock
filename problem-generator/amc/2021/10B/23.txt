<p>A square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> is colored white except for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> black isosceles right triangular regions with legs of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in each corner of the square and a black diamond with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71df1b44d6caa4b0f798d48ff8ee72da386e4cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> in the center of the square, as shown in the diagram. A circular coin with diameter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is dropped onto the square and lands in a random location where the coin is completely contained within the square. The probability that the coin will cover part of the black region of the square can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/a/f3ad40c6aec01dd2ab973fdb8f68f5beea1af9a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{196}\left(a+b\sqrt{2}+\pi\right)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="154" height="38" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are positive integers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7f338e16788e51acc32f82a10daf271c5a104aa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Made by samrocksnature */ draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,8)--(0,8)--(0,0)); fill((2,0)--(0,2)--(0,0)--cycle, black); fill((6,0)--(8,0)--(8,2)--cycle, black); fill((8,6)--(8,8)--(6,8)--cycle, black); fill((0,6)--(2,8)--(0,8)--cycle, black); fill((4,6)--(2,4)--(4,2)--(6,4)--cycle, black); filldraw(circle((2.6,3.31),0.5),gray); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/7/897498c2b9ea30db565d12aa9ad10ce440b6057c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~64 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~66 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~68 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~70 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diagram">Diagram</span></h2>
<p>Note that the center of the coin can lie anywhere inside a green region, as shown below.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80de1b87bb354410fd58b705ace2bcf031ec62f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Made by MRENTHUSIASM */ draw((0,0)--(8,0)--(8,8)--(0,8)--(0,0)); fill((2,0)--(0,2)--(0,0)--cycle, black); fill((6,0)--(8,0)--(8,2)--cycle, black); fill((8,6)--(8,8)--(6,8)--cycle, black); fill((0,6)--(2,8)--(0,8)--cycle, black); fill((4,6)--(2,4)--(4,2)--(6,4)--cycle, black); draw((0.5,7.5)--(0.5+1+sqrt(2)/2,7.5)--(0.5,7.5-1-sqrt(2)/2)--cycle, green); draw((0.5,0.5)--(0.5+1+sqrt(2)/2,0.5)--(0.5,0.5+1+sqrt(2)/2)--cycle, green); draw((7.5,7.5)--(7.5-1-sqrt(2)/2,7.5)--(7.5,7.5-1-sqrt(2)/2)--cycle, green); draw((7.5,0.5)--(7.5-1-sqrt(2)/2,0.5)--(7.5,0.5+1+sqrt(2)/2)--cycle, green); draw(Arc((4,6),0.5,135,45)--Arc((6,4),0.5,45,-45)--Arc((4,2),0.5,-45,-135)--Arc((2,4),0.5,225,135)--cycle, green); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>To find the probability, we look at the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/9/e698969e1acb79d4e8eef8f49e448c4fc2bc1cb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{success region}}{\text{total possible region}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="148" height="39" />. For the coin to be completely contained within the square, we must have the distance from the center of the coin to a side of the square to be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, as it's the radius of the coin. This implies the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/7/cb703fa4fe262a174e78f7dcaa636571e7b4c076.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{total possible region}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="146" height="15" /> is a square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59e7e5fd78537415d9bcbddad944d64974daa2b6.png" class="latex" alt="$8 - \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2} = 7$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="37" />, with an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Now, we consider cases where needs to land to partially cover a black region.
</p><p><b> Near The Center Square </b>
</p><p>We can have the center of the coin land within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> outside of the center square, or inside of the center square.  So, we have a region with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> emanating from every point on the exterior of the square, forming four quarter circles and four rectangles. The four quarter circles combine to make a full circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, so the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/d/11d3791d908578e9ede8da9a29fefa2429468419.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="13" height="33" />. The area of a rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/f/9cfafcc20e510cbdd14a1230a9211d999b447240.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \sqrt 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of them combine to an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/1/84128fda6939c2dafeda77dbe67654e5c0412312.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. The area of the black square is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/7/2b71d36f48301822d2e56c351555586783858826.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(2\sqrt 2\right)^2 = 8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="37" />. So, for this case, we have a combined total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/3/073147cd940386ebb544af3835d207acefca7994.png" class="latex" alt="$8 + 4\sqrt 2 + \frac{\pi}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="100" height="33" />. Onto the second (and last) case.
</p><p><b> Near A Triangle </b>
</p><p>We can also have the coin land within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> outside of one of the triangles. By symmetry, we can just find the successful region for one of them, then multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Consider the above diagram. We can draw an altitude from the bottom corner of the square to hit the hypotenuse of the green triangle. The length of this when passing through the black region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/b/75bec5f95356b5621c8c473003558d5d8187a4d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />, and when passing through the white region (while being contained in the green triangle) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. However, we have to subtract off when it doesn't pass through the red square. Then, it's the hypotenuse of a small isosceles right triangle with side lengths of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4a7df1100efe353cc612d5d1c289c22d5bf8c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/c/52c0ce4138d5a10a54f5e36773239985c45a3589.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="31" height="41" /> So, the altitude of the green triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/41142ecdf957658b2d3f699200f4610a22390363.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt 2 + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{\sqrt 2}{2} = \frac{\sqrt 2 + 1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="192" height="41" />. Then, recall, the area of an isosceles right triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/9/319c4d6557b81962a2076f2017878709cf698551.png" class="latex" alt="$h^2$" width="16" height="15" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is the altitude from the right angle. So, squaring this, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/f/b5ffe41abbfd9a6fb9f1c46229d1d46521b53a78.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3 + 2\sqrt 2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="41" />. Now, we have to multiply this by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> to account for all of the black triangles, to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/6/2264a62e5f3bac57d0cbada5db042d3f63ad067a.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 2\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="18" /> as the final area for this case. 
</p><p><b> Finishing </b>
</p><p>Then, to have the coin touching a black region, we add up the area of our successful regions, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/163c920f77bc5d11a0d9c0430adad4f268b0dfa6.png" class="latex" alt="$8 + 4\sqrt 2 + \frac{\pi}{4} + 3 + 2\sqrt 2 = 11 + 6\sqrt 2 + \frac{\pi}{4} = \frac{44 + 24\sqrt 2 + \pi}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="466" height="41" />. The total region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, so our probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f7ec579ea5947f2bfb15a12924ea1d18f7994e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{44 + 24\sqrt 2 + \pi}{4}}{49} = \frac{44 + 24\sqrt 2 + \pi}{196}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="223" height="44" />, which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/08983a1b20bb94e6348bad949e96d6b044b4861a.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = 44+24 = 68$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="164" height="14" />. This corresponds to answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e144979cc290cf89bbd813164f6500cb161254a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~68}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p><p>~rocketsri
~ minor edit from jrepsa
~ minor edit from lpieleanu
</p>
