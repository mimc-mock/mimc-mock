<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/5/6b5fdc3950f2fbdbd20edacedd030310d90198d0.png" class="latex" alt="$N = 34 \cdot 34 \cdot 63 \cdot 270$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="161" height="13" />. What is the ratio of the sum of the odd divisors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> to the sum of the even divisors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/d/c3d5266919c06342869393f377d0073a29ac757a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~1&#160;: 16 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~1&#160;: 15 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~1&#160;: 14 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~1&#160;: 8 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~1&#160;: 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="497" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Prime factorize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/30174004e27032c62f900010dd0588a140572690.png" class="latex" alt="$N=2^{3} \cdot 3^{5} \cdot 5\cdot 7\cdot 17^{2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="168" height="15" />. For each odd divisor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, there exist even divisors <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/5/2d5a0e122dae9ee9d730420b31f1c6a8161c688d.png" class="latex" alt="$2n, 4n, 8n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, therefore the ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/2/3e29b79f5b78ec4bec3af650428306a205105124.png" class="latex" alt="$1:(2+4+8)=\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~1&#160;: 14}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="222" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Prime factorizing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, we see <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/30174004e27032c62f900010dd0588a140572690.png" class="latex" alt="$N=2^{3} \cdot 3^{5} \cdot 5\cdot 7\cdot 17^{2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="168" height="15" />. The sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />'s odd divisors are the sum of the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> without <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and the sum of the even divisors is the sum of the odds subtracted by the total sum of divisors. The sum of odd divisors is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/3/d43ad58db14705adb3a80482071db62edeed9039.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a = (1+3+3^2 + 3^3 + 3^4 + 3^5)(1 + 5)(1+7)(1+17+17^2)\]" width="462" height="19" /> and the total sum of divisors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988f4d1d96ecf8b5b835d9022bec978c99e51fa1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(1+2+4+8)(1+3+3^2 + 3^3 + 3^4 + 3^5)(1 + 5)(1+7)(1+17+17^2) = 15a.\]" width="504" height="19" /> Thus, our ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10cb40e0665d665311e990a490d8a6e57c429ab3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{a}{15a-a} = \frac{a}{14a} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~1&#160;: 14}.\]" width="233" height="33" />
</p><p>~JustinLee2017
</p>
