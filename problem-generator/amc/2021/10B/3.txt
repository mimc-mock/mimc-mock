<p>In an after-school program for juniors and seniors, there is a debate team with an equal number of students from each class on the team. Among the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> students in the program, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the juniors and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the seniors are on the debate team. How many juniors are in the program?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7ccf558701fdba7b9b7594ec515988bfb1afe91c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~5 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~6 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~8 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~11 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Say there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" /> juniors and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> seniors in the program. Converting percentages to fractions, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/4/484f4329734f567aa40f24105ad5d41e42eccb53.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{j}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/b/ccbee0118392fc60ca0fcdf07818d4bba75ea5d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{s}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="33" /> are on the debate team, and since an equal number of juniors and seniors are on the debate team, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/9/ba950f5116f0a375d4fc3b1ceeaebee26149e79e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{j}{4} = \frac{s}{10}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="63" height="38" />
</p><p>Cross-multiplying and simplifying we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/2/2f2cb11e8a31d5f25d196311586848a30dea3598.png" class="latex" alt="$5j=2s.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" /> Additionally, since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> students in the program, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/f/16f8a28b9a69a5aee78d2c9d199b6a8a19cee212.png" class="latex" alt="$j+s = 28.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" /> It is now a matter of solving the system of equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/0/e90eca73861ccf271ca585eb49b39608553b1018.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5j=2s\]" width="58" height="16" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/d/f2d9da87d46e3ec7ee67241f9f6c951877716ebb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[j+s=28,\]" width="86" height="16" /> and the solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/8/008562e84c4887d0be141043bfd127f4790c36d6.png" class="latex" alt="$j = 8, s = 20.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="104" height="16" /> Since we want the number of juniors, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/57218434e7e1414f2a6eff3803ced2168d20ead8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\boxed{(C) \text{ } 8}.\]" width="58" height="30" />
