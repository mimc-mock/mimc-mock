<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can set the point on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> where the fold occurs as point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. Then, we can set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/8/7a8b5db9ff00c554cedc81b37d6c23b268ce29f1.png" class="latex" alt="$FD$" width="29" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/d/07d29465728e92952374a857beaee91665d8dbd9.png" class="latex" alt="$CF$" width="28" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/8/b08aa5a28c62ed05af271aee7e5e053a0d4179b9.png" class="latex" alt="$1-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> because of symmetry due to the fold. It can be recognized that this is a right triangle, and solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/d/acde643d9f0e08e1b579af2584cbad1d9af365f1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 = (1-x)^2 \rightarrow x^2 + \frac{1}{9} = x^2 - 2x + 1 \rightarrow x=\frac{4}{9}\]" width="444" height="46" />
</p><p>We know this is a 3-4-5 triangle because the side lengths are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/e/72eede5c72fcfc460d28bda9e8bce80556a5277d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{9}, \frac{4}{9}, \frac{5}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />. We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36cd2051056e3b8f82644856cc72cd0962d8c748.png" class="latex" alt="$EAC&#39;$" width="46" height="14" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/a/53ada683eb855756794b1babcb8edaeaa54ad428.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;DF$" width="48" height="14" /> because angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d68e9c3fbeda3f7c4e21325508a3c773e44aa77.png" class="latex" alt="$EC&#39;F$" width="47" height="14" /> is a right angle. Now, we can use similarity to find out that the perimeter is just the perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/d/7ed95b53f063520ee99b076c927e5bd9bb5c394c.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;DF \times \frac{AC&#39;}{DF}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="109" height="40" />. That's just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d291b78e67bf152c809ebc6f96739865cd927d8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{3} \times \frac{\frac{2}{3}}{\frac{4}{9}} = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} = 2$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="158" height="49" />. Therefore, the final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/d/4cd443038feb18c91eb210d2404882f31f5c37f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)} ~2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let line we're reflecting over be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" />, and let the points where it hits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />, be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, respectively. Notice, to reflect over a line we find the perpendicular passing through the midpoint of the two points (which are the reflected and the original). So, we first find the equation of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" />. The segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b7058f8a28ca225136b284b337138a54e39e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$CC&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/a/48a97a7b4324c376fef4568cc10f8a871c5397c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{0 - 1}{1 - 2/3} = -3$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="109" height="42" />, implying line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> has a slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Also, the midpoint of segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/b/44b7058f8a28ca225136b284b337138a54e39e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$CC&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/6/ad608c8e3d080a2f56cf22745e8175d026694f3c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( \frac{5}{6}, \frac{1}{2} \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="57" height="43" />, so line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> passes through this point. Then, we get the equation of line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/a/60a57a6139df91fc2403195d838b7c30445a3c27.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{1}{3} x + \frac{2}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="37" />. Then, if the point where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is reflected over line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" />, then we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2ae0e30605dc87c83aecb3f588afa7129bee6aa.png" class="latex" alt="$BB&#39;$" width="33" height="14" /> is the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/5/9b5f326b9b07891146527d79cdbecd0fd2779ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -3x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="67" height="16" />. The intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> and segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2ae0e30605dc87c83aecb3f588afa7129bee6aa.png" class="latex" alt="$BB&#39;$" width="33" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/9/839f95b8601e3e66a819de60270a818dbfc1fa21.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( - \frac{1}{15}, \frac{1}{5} \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="80" height="43" />.
