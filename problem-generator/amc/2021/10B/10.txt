<p>An inverted cone with base radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/7/bc7c6c1d0a2250475701d6a0d2df2c93a8e002fa.png" class="latex" alt="$12  \mathrm{cm}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/e/68e9a71f1276d7f86422c8c92b73865cf721f4a3.png" class="latex" alt="$18  \mathrm{cm}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" /> is full of water. The water is poured into a tall cylinder whose horizontal base has radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/b/77b4b89a8c3384feb49876bb7e3e24ac4f867cbb.png" class="latex" alt="$24  \mathrm{cm}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. What is the height in centimeters of the water in the cylinder?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/f/5ffa6e2276ad2d5be15f35d385c0605cf1774e89.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~1.5 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~3 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~4 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~4.5 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="377" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The volume of a cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb8b00aba4e33fc15ce064c3363026ea9b33552.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3} \cdot\pi \cdot r^2 \cdot h$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the base radius and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is the height. The water completely fills up the cone so the volume of the water is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/f/54f0483974111f7a0935bd488a96afbefae8a249.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\cdot18\cdot144\pi = 6\cdot144\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="180" height="37" />.
</p><p>The volume of a cylinder is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/8/a88e3a9ac5584a93316b9d1a926682df733d8833.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi \cdot r^2 \cdot h$" width="63" height="15" /> so the volume of the water in the cylinder would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/0/730563d7c99242f9e7bb9ca865d30b4314f62d49.png" class="latex" alt="$24\cdot24\cdot\pi\cdot h$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="13" />.
</p><p>We can equate these two expressions because the water volume stays the same like this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2f8ae8bb7c14f8cd62274ea9926ea2effc82a70.png" class="latex" alt="$24\cdot24\cdot\pi\cdot h = 6\cdot144\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="181" height="13" />. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/692feec8d7e383a81e7c8a4ba8512b70a07767bb.png" class="latex" alt="$4h = 6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/c/f4ce1d9bf0f74086d98f733e78b4185bfd55f476.png" class="latex" alt="$h=\frac{6}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="46" height="38" />.
</p><p>So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/6/d068281c9a66173f5fe9be713d5815ab7fa5f1ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)} ~1.5}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="76" height="30" />
</p><p>~abhinavg0627
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The water completely fills up the cone. For now, assume the radius of both cone and cylinder are the same. Then the cone has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the volume of the cylinder, and so the height is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Then, from the problem statement, the radius is doubled, meaning the area of the base is quadrupled (since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb0e6e64d73bacef9c8869039bc21581f1884e6.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2 = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="15" />).
</p><p>Therefore, the height is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/6/de68058201dde4d911a6e48b0cb4dde2e5ec7f03.png" class="latex" alt="$18 \div 3 \div 4 = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} ~1.5}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="181" height="30" />
</p><p>~PureSwag
</p>
