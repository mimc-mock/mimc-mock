<p>Grandma has just finished baking a large rectangular pan of brownies. She is planning to make rectangular pieces of equal size and shape, with straight cuts parallel to the sides of the pan. Each cut must be made entirely across the pan. Grandma wants to make the same number of interior pieces as pieces along the perimeter of the pan. What is the greatest possible number of brownies she can produce?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/e/7bee8059c0b73bf82a13d1bd793bbef3f8ffebcd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~24 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~30 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~48 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~60 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~64$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the side lengths of the rectangular pan be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/f/16f3ad35a8c78941fb4515cb9a97a2e0f929143e.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-2)(n-2) = \frac{mn}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="171" height="33" />, since half of the brownie pieces are in the interior. This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/a/64af07b281d51f8618e18db4e3ede7f7196cea24.png" class="latex" alt="$2(m-2)(n-2) = mn \iff mn - 4m - 4n + 8 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="18" />. Adding 8 to both sides and applying <a href="/wiki/index.php/Simon%27s_Favorite_Factoring_Trick" title="Simon&#39;s Favorite Factoring Trick">Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick</a>, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/e/03e0b4287cec7548e9edf9bb0da04bd00ad54904.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-4)(n-4) = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are both positive, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/d/35d0f7458d72773df11f9c2fa871c558db3625b2.png" class="latex" alt="$(m, n) = (5, 12), (6, 8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="169" height="18" /> (up to ordering). By inspection, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/5/ad5db6c80a57196ffbec85904a6820e17433c51a.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 12 = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }60}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="130" height="28" /> maximizes the number of brownies.
</p><p>~ ike.chen
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Obviously, no side of the rectangular pan can have less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> brownies beside it. We let one side of the pan have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> brownies, and let the number of brownies on its adjacent side be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/771dedba573bd8e05d73718b24ffe3bde77f7645.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=2\cdot3(x-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />, and solving yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20fce41465f6ac44f0c78fbacabb9cb5e9682f9a.png" class="latex" alt="$x=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" /> and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/9/2b9cb9ea940ebfc23be2c5ec7fe0bc74390bbf9b.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot12=60$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> brownies in the pan. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e228ac5bee7b9ffb60cacf50f3f2c7c31f8038.png" class="latex" alt="$64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> is the only choice larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, but it cannot be the answer since the only way to fit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e228ac5bee7b9ffb60cacf50f3f2c7c31f8038.png" class="latex" alt="$64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> brownies in a pan without letting a side of it have less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> brownies beside it is by forming a square of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> brownies on each side, which does not meet the requirement. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/f/5ff4f6368b3347765d81d480c2ca801f94fbc258.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }60}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p><p>-SmileKat32
</p>
