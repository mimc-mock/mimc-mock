<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By multiplying the entire equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b419b91958d111b91db52e72a0e8d750e2b23174.png" class="latex" alt="$3-2=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />, all the terms will simplify by difference of squares, and the final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/8/768794f6e0080352db6da4621637ad01a128f3cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~3^{128}-2^{128}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="127" height="31" />.
</p><p>Additionally, we could also multiply the entire equation (we can let it be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />) by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/8/62876ab6a5a1655a34883084ad036b9d1ecd289b.png" class="latex" alt="$2-3=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" />. The terms again simplify by difference of squares. This time, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/3/d7338e5f8c571f7a9acd7a97b472863a20aa847c.png" class="latex" alt="$-x=2^{128}-3^{128} \Rightarrow x=3^{128}-2^{128}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="273" height="15" />. Both solutions yield the same answer.
</p><p>~BakedPotato66

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If you weren't able to come up with the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/5/435e173bf02aed7e3ed9b9cf330feaa33e532eb5.png" class="latex" alt="$(3 - 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> insight, then you could just notice that the answer is divisible by 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/8/27803b32e1de1532968c2bc198dd16b640f3e4b3.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 3 = 5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/762a91c66a0a78c45cabe5004f1c0af0d73eb75a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2 + 3^2 = 13$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="16" />. We can then use Fermat's Little Theorem for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/d/34d607dcab236ee4243d0341f12ddb20e03b0904.png" class="latex" alt="$p = 5, 13$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="16" /> on the answer choices to determine which of the answer choices are divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/8/768794f6e0080352db6da4621637ad01a128f3cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~3^{128}-2^{128}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="127" height="31" />.
</p><p>-mewto

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>After expanding the first few terms, the result after each term appears to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4edded9d1cb60b403721c8cb904a10c98411c988.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2^n-1} + 2^{2^n-2}\cdot{3^1} + 2^{2^n-3}\cdot{3^2} + \cdots + 2^1\cdot{3^{2^n-2}} + 3^{2^n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="432" height="19" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of terms expanded. We can prove this using mathematical induction. The base step is trivial. When expanding another term, all of the previous terms multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f20dc968d9c291b7750fcb40abd898739ea246b.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2^{n-1}}$" width="37" height="18" /> would give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/b/54bf9237f7638103803f3323554ec5de6a99a7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2^n-1} + 2^{2^n-2}\cdot{3^1} + 2^{2^n-3}\cdot{3^2} + \cdots + 2^{2^{n-1}+1}\cdot{3^{2^{n-1}-1}} + 2^{2^{n-1}}\cdot{3^{2^{n-1}}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="536" height="21" />, and all the previous terms multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb30bd6aef7376871a06e6828ff230790eae2bc.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{2^{n-1}}$" width="37" height="18" /> would give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/f/54f166062dd53ed943c2dcbb4c4c4993bdbcb22f.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{2^n-1} + 3^{2^n-2}\cdot{2^1} + 3^{2^n-3}\cdot{2^2} + \cdots + 3^{2^{n-1}+1}\cdot{2^{2^{n-1}-1}} + 3^{2^{n-1}}\cdot{2^{2^{n-1}}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="536" height="21" />. Their sum is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4edded9d1cb60b403721c8cb904a10c98411c988.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2^n-1} + 2^{2^n-2}\cdot{3^1} + 2^{2^n-3}\cdot{3^2} + \cdots + 2^1\cdot{3^{2^n-2}} + 3^{2^n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="432" height="19" />, so the proof is complete. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/538166f2c036b9bf259aa436b68395d71cd2ff7e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3^{2^n}-2^{2^n}}{3-2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="74" height="40" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4edded9d1cb60b403721c8cb904a10c98411c988.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2^n-1} + 2^{2^n-2}\cdot{3^1} + 2^{2^n-3}\cdot{3^2} + \cdots + 2^1\cdot{3^{2^n-2}} + 3^{2^n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="432" height="19" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e2bd6b66f8f667c18e7625afde44411833d50a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3^{2^7}-2^{2^7}}{3-2}=\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~3^{128}-2^{128}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="224" height="42" />.
</p><p>~SmileKat32
