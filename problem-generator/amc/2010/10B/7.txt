<p>A triangle has side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. A rectangle has width <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and area equal to the
area of the triangle. What is the perimeter of this rectangle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd948a55ebc99dc0c50d53881e38bd3a91d468c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 16 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 28 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 32 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The triangle is isosceles. The height of the triangle is therefore given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/7/7c782d87f4442669f66c8ef178a1a850be3c5401.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \sqrt{10^2 - \left( \dfrac{12}{2} \right)^2} =  \sqrt{64} = 8$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="249" height="56" />
</p><p>Now, the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/d/19d6edb2a1e062ebc303c14dd420ae8e9cef3c13.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{bh}{2} = \dfrac{12*8}{2} = \dfrac{96}{2} = 48$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="183" height="37" />
</p><p>We have that the area of the rectangle is the same as the area of the triangle, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. We also have the width of the rectangle: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>The length of the rectangle therefore is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79b70bccbcd9611ba27b75a7fb513d92e1f631e2.png" class="latex" alt="$l = \dfrac{48}{4} = 12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="94" height="38" />
</p><p>The perimeter of the rectangle then becomes:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5e6d85606315d6cabc29c85f14298190caf780d.png" class="latex" alt="$2l + 2w = 2*12 + 2*4 = 32$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="227" height="14" />
</p><p>The answer is:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/e/64ebdd6b165c20f1cfeea18df76cbe0a630823b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 32}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>An alternative way to find the area of the triangle is by using Heron's formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f997468ae90fd9ba0119fe6ce55315d44d775af0.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\sqrt{(s)(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="238" height="32" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semi-perimeter of the triangle (meaning half the perimeter). Here, the semi-perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/41156aa7ecc7ff5951a83cba40a72d7895760a10.png" class="latex" alt="$(10+10+12)/2 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="173" height="18" />. Thus the area equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b6e4e911e6aea92d7ca9831e9f87e6c582a64ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(16)(16-10)(16-10)(16-12)} = \sqrt{16*6*6*4} = 48.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="450" height="32" /> We know that the width of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad2070305770b52e63e2677d0955c03a50c6fb5a.png" class="latex" alt="$48/4 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />, which is the length. The perimeter of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/5/5956805b1dc32890d96b401143bf9e9e797f0b1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2(4+12) =$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/e/64ebdd6b165c20f1cfeea18df76cbe0a630823b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 32}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p><p>(Solution by Flamedragon)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/d/9edbeeb1dadf605a05b53d47308dd8a6667997f2.png" class="latex" alt="$10,10,12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> is essentially <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />   “6,8,10” right triangles stuck together. Hence, the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, and our area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>So, the length of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd12c548ace15da92eeefd9d5c097bcf8d32a58.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{48}{4}=12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="64" height="38" />, and our perimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/c/59cba856ad3ee41a5f3c5937581506961a5f4bb6.png" class="latex" alt="$P=2(4+12)=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 32}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="200" height="28" />
</p>
