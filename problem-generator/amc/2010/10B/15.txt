<p>On a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" />-question multiple choice math contest, students receive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> points for a correct answer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> points for an answer left blank, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> point for an incorrect answer. Jesse’s total score on the contest was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />. What is the maximum number of questions that Jesse could have answered correctly?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/2/2c294c1d6dc9f359635e8fcb7c427e99f1d9e526.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 25 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 27 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 29 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 31 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 33$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the amount of questions Jesse answered correctly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the amount of questions Jesse left blank, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be the amount of questions Jesse answered incorrectly. Since there were <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> questions on the contest, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/5/f35af5fa51e3549e18fc1027728a2e765ec9b280.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c=50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />. Since his total score was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb69547e38fdef24d769e18a44c9682c283c5f7d.png" class="latex" alt="$4a-c=99$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="13" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/7577d330ff802e2599b633d85bbfce1f24165b3e.png" class="latex" alt="$a+c\leq50 \Rightarrow c\leq50-a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="192" height="15" />. We can substitute this inequality into the previous equation to obtain another inequality: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/3/013af0b5e32586267718c28681ca12765f237c17.png" class="latex" alt="$4a-(50-a)\leq99 \Rightarrow 5a\leq149 \Rightarrow a\leq \frac{149}5=29.8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="395" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is an integer, the maximum value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/6/1a6eae616318bd13176bad9c5e3bb0994bd45787.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 29}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>

</p><p>~IceMatrix
