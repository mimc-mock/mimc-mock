<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> be positive integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/8/c48917438fe4244b096d2b4174a675d1635f05f4.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d+e=2010$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="192" height="14" /> and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the largest of the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bbeb7caa1770d1d3cb842655f27e245b08188f.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/5/dd54f8129186e28453c8c19f0c38ca25420a8e9f.png" class="latex" alt="$c+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f8719bd65794b7cadb653bb83b22fe2cc24c8cb0.png" class="latex" alt="$d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" />. What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/6/7e67b119fa6fdbf592498cda62d6a5394e542da7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 670 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 671 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 802 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 803 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 804$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We want to try make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bbeb7caa1770d1d3cb842655f27e245b08188f.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/5/dd54f8129186e28453c8c19f0c38ca25420a8e9f.png" class="latex" alt="$c+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f8719bd65794b7cadb653bb83b22fe2cc24c8cb0.png" class="latex" alt="$d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" /> as close as possible so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" />, the maximum of these, is smallest.
</p><p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/c/6ec5b3fd58f4bcf1d450360db25a0492daad571c.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=670+670+670$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="186" height="14" />. In order to express <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> as a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers, we must split up some of these numbers. There are two ways to do this (while keeping the sum of two numbers as close as possible): <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4cce121060e0e96aedae130b16e474e2d2965229.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=670+1+670+1+668$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="248" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/48265cfb50f279cd9624e038cb3b524f2a3152f9.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=670+1+669+1+669$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="248" height="14" />. We see that in both cases, the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/2/5c2b5b001d120ed78f226ed5d50b1b1c06b490cc.png" class="latex" alt="$671$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59ede54510cf517513917fd4e24cc80e045ac9b2.png" class="latex" alt="$671 \Rightarrow \boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="82" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/3/283da71e258cd47678acef397f40472ab91ea8bb.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b \le M$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/a/dba672c43864c7fb65f959199d2ec275ebeb6cd8.png" class="latex" alt="$d + e \le M$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/7/637df5552de7cd5252a1f443578a8c03103c42b3.png" class="latex" alt="$c &lt; b + c \le M$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="113" height="15" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/501177a58e66386c4ddac17a0451d98fb40b0f56.png" class="latex" alt="$2010 = a + b + c + d + e &lt; 3M$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="245" height="14" />. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5d1fcc4649ea6eb4b097ba553c19dedae5faa0.png" class="latex" alt="$M &gt; 670$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/c/aacae1b8d34bfd59bac9190aaa5c638d0951a565.png" class="latex" alt="$M \ge 671$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="15" />.
</p><p>For the values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/e/8beeed4cd296de20c52c8e966cca6434aad177c1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d,e) = (669,1,670,1,669)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="258" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/0/7805d226d85abf8b00cb9829b41955f2b84ab120.png" class="latex" alt="$M = 671$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, so the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/2/e22fe9583d5df5c630aa8986b32f508a03ecb545.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{671}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="39" height="23" />. The answer is (B).
</p><p>~ math31415926535
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Notice that only the sums of adjacent numbers matter. (For example, a &amp; c could be extremely high, as long as b is relatively low.)
Therefore creating "mountains" and "valleys" is the best way to lower the sum of adjacent numbers. We can do
</p><p><br />
1. (high, low, high, low, high)
</p><p><br />
or
</p><p><br />
2. (low, high, low, high, low)
</p><p><br />
In the extreme case that each "low" = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> will be divided into either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers for cases 1 and 2, respectively. Obviously dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> will yield a lower number, so we consider case 1.
</p><p>Dividing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a4cf3ebb294df5e93003bf9de36ea15cab3b818.png" class="latex" alt="$670$" width="26" height="12" />, or
</p><p><br />
(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/2/8820305b451ac6fe4521ede030db85b24aad168c.png" class="latex" alt="$670, 0, 670, 0, 670$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="16" />)
</p><p><br />
However, all five numbers must be positive, and the closest we can get to this is
</p><p><br />
(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/a/48a26cc848ad2bfa508615d4032149b784050f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$668, 3, 668, 3, 668$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="16" />)
</p><p><br />
The lowest possible sum of two adjacent numbers then becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/2/5c2b5b001d120ed78f226ed5d50b1b1c06b490cc.png" class="latex" alt="$671$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
