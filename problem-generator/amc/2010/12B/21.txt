<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/5/d25634d7212302efdeb3702974863759bfb74d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$a &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> be a polynomial with integer coefficients such that
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/7/6e7f954ffafacc54d24c95c8588c16a0915b340d.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1) = P(3) = P(5) = P(7) = a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="256" height="18" />, and<br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2c924a814a9f39929164ada3bbe535a0d5750d.png" class="latex" alt="$P(2) = P(4) = P(6) = P(8) = -a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="18" />.
</p>
</center>
<p>What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/6/de6a77a0e9b03fd5ae882ae276e0757aad7db588.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 105 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 315 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 945 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 7! \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 8!$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We observe that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/7/6e7f954ffafacc54d24c95c8588c16a0915b340d.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1) = P(3) = P(5) = P(7) = a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="256" height="18" />, if we define a new polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3eeefa367883375994fd2e1f575d088bce9d8b1.png" class="latex" alt="$R(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5f17b4ce84f93302750c9e2983c1c29d15565a1.png" class="latex" alt="$R(x) = P(x) - a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3eeefa367883375994fd2e1f575d088bce9d8b1.png" class="latex" alt="$R(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> has roots when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/c/9cc1155105ffe574ed181abf566d0363ae03e807.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />; namely, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/5/f4531b6a070c13d7ffb75959db43f5876af7e0f7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1,3,5,7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" />.
</p><p>Thus since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3eeefa367883375994fd2e1f575d088bce9d8b1.png" class="latex" alt="$R(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> has roots when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/5/f4531b6a070c13d7ffb75959db43f5876af7e0f7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1,3,5,7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" />, we can factor the product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/4/e44426b4fd9950f00a7c99b69e23947106c4d849.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-1)(x-3)(x-5)(x-7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="221" height="18" /> out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3eeefa367883375994fd2e1f575d088bce9d8b1.png" class="latex" alt="$R(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> to obtain a new polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dfe7c7a0b887097fa2227a230fc915ba2e5477.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/7/407a3db0dea4c3b9a76a4627aa3c4319d8bf3c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-1)(x-3)(x-5)(x-7)(Q(x)) = R(x) = P(x) - a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="434" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then, plugging in values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/6/be67a2e30ef4f1450d57f26dd189988370a7b1b4.png" class="latex" alt="$2,4,6,8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> we get 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9afd67021f0f85e87b25f156824b541f88c0306b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(2)-a=(2-1)(2-3)(2-5)(2-7)Q(2) = -15Q(2) = -2a\]" width="479" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c5b8fac26a68834f50593977429f4698646e2e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(4)-a=(4-1)(4-3)(4-5)(4-7)Q(4) = 9Q(4) = -2a\]" width="476" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/a/d8a09c7042d1dab945a5506a13a985074be1c9e4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(6)-a=(6-1)(6-3)(6-5)(6-7)Q(6) = -15Q(6) = -2a\]" width="479" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/a/88a51f757dc2a65f7aa72e90e745ed6eb67f8ada.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(8)-a=(8-1)(8-3)(8-5)(8-7)Q(8) = 105Q(8) = -2a\]" width="479" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5d5d0b04323f8ee361e635568ff598763eb24ac.png" class="latex" alt="$-2a=-15Q(2)=9Q(4)=-15Q(6)=105Q(8).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="384" height="18" />
Thus, the least value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/2/e82162abea4e08000cf654c1b40b6258e5ebde7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm}(15,9,15,105)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="135" height="18" />.
Solving, we receive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/6/6d60857d2d582089e51f413f701e45aec6ad1bda.png" class="latex" alt="$315$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/2/7c227d867e670d780336d7141219060c070c2fb5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 315}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p><p>To complete the solution, we can let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/3/ae3dfd1b2cca38d91d641bfefdd311deff66fad3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 315$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />, and then try to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dfe7c7a0b887097fa2227a230fc915ba2e5477.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />. We know from the above calculation that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9ac5c94a223459ce84b1eec2784522a29fb8b6c.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(2)=42, Q(4)=-70, Q(6)=42$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="269" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/d/9bd0b511f6650e4eb9fb17cfc07ab731a6079f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(8)=-6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. Then we can let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/3/6d3270a55849b483792282f536c14e807d9beded.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x) = T(x)(x-2)(x-6)+42$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="252" height="18" />, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/7/5070375bd78c7a887852732cf12b16bced14dc9b.png" class="latex" alt="$T(4)=28, T(8)=-4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/6/466a347727d47be4af1dd00fe98af696dca25533.png" class="latex" alt="$T(x)=L(x)(x-8)-4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="186" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e444dc24037d6b5817cfae9e5aeb594d1515ac3.png" class="latex" alt="$L(4)=-8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" />. Therefore, it is possible to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/495b3e8ee8ca21c87565d70b8352825b81af9d5a.png" class="latex" alt="$T(x) = -8(x-8)-4 = -8x + 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="18" />, so the goal is accomplished. As a reference, the polynomial we get is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/0/3209af88e571fba47b68b9d52952a2489cd92412.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(x) = (x-1)(x-3)(x-5)(x-7)((-8x + 60)(x-2)(x-6)+42) + 315\]" width="516" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/5/d0519bc33f88d7578b0bd944572f1a2a01cc5563.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= -8 x^7+252 x^6-3248 x^5+22050 x^4-84392 x^3+179928 x^2-194592 x+80325\]" width="566" height="16" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The evenly-spaced data suggests using <a href="/wiki/index.php/Discrete_derivative" title="Discrete derivative">discrete derivatives</a> to tackle this problem.  First, note that any polynomial of degree <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/c/d0c61a27ea483d0087f7676a6ce184af746f7989.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \ldots + a_n x^n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="289" height="19" /></center>
<p>can also be written as
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7ceb63451cea3c05d040b809249609dc53bcf00.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = b_0 + b_1 (x-1) + b_2 (x-1)(x-2) + \ldots + b_n (x-1)(x-1) \cdots (x-n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="598" height="18" />.</center>
<p>Moreover, the coefficients <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/412787c048e28774dc63fc27db42dc52ca858de7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> are integers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/7/7e767e73c02cab2a344dbdb65f438009eae4ab18.png" class="latex" alt="$i=1, 2, \ldots n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="16" /> iff the coefficients <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d9565abaadf04609a2e9941aa2d20b0a299b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" /> are integers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/7/7e767e73c02cab2a344dbdb65f438009eae4ab18.png" class="latex" alt="$i=1, 2, \ldots n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="16" />.  This latter form is convenient for calculating discrete derivatives of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />.
</p><p>The discrete derivative of a function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is the related function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/f/d9f63c81a35a0be0b118b438e1addda926febf1e.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" /> defined as
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/0/a40ea7880da0e4dceafa910def3c53a7564c293e.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta f(x) = f(x+1) - f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="197" height="18" />.</center>
<p>With this definition, it's easy to see that for any positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> we have
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/2/f225d199590dedb5c4edd3f4e317da31068b9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta [(x-1)(x-2)\cdots(x-k)] = k(x-1)(x-2)\cdots(x-[k-1])$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="490" height="18" />.</center>
<p>This in turn allows us to use successive discrete derivatives evaluated at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> to calculate all of the coefficients <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94d9565abaadf04609a2e9941aa2d20b0a299b8a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="12" height="15" /> using
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/5/5b504d4a6cd17ef0f6456ccf351d4f87075b8757.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)=b_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10f4bedc15ce7fa84437268db010893999897b07.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta P(1) = b_1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/8/9f8c844ed80927e51332aec6290ae409267011fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta^2 P(1) = 2 b_2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3958e984d2597b35da7975f4503cdcf965a777f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ldots$" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/5/0b50bab9c1cf3306d400ebf519085f550f2960d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta^7 P(1) = 7! b_7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="19" />.</center>
