<p>Shelby drives her scooter at a speed of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> miles per hour if it is not raining, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> miles per hour if it is raining. Today she drove in the sun in the morning and in the rain in the evening, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes. How many minutes did she drive in the rain?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/3/d83ea0b0823cbb003c552256dea1eaee9e2986bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 18 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 21 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 27 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the time it is not raining, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the time it is raining, in hours.
</p><p>We have the system: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2cff73a1847d26c435387cbab5d2affc23be4a7.png" class="latex" alt="$30x+20y=16$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="120" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/8/488002242ebc101913d09c8b8ce40f8273ff85b2.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=2/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />
</p><p>Solving gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/1/5210eb29cf6c3f780306da2b78b81907cb1c56d4.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{4}{15}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="55" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/a/2aab3916166e512d45836109c638e42e61d7ae9a.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />
</p><p>We want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> in minutes, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/4/df4b137a653658dd0508b8dd17b4d8afb63bee65.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{5}*60=24 \Rightarrow C$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="133" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the time she drove in the rain. Thus, the number of minutes she did not drive in the rain is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/8/858f83e94f0305c39d9df7371d6f604b8e1a82b4.png" class="latex" alt="$40-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" /> . 
</p><p>Since we are calculating the time in minutes, it is best to convert the speeds in minutes. Thus, the speed per minute when it is not raining is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> miles per minute, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> miles per minute when it is raining. 
Thus, we have the equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/8040ed29b74cabde948ce60ba6e5bf64ffa74bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} * (40-x) + \frac{1}{3} * x = 16$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="202" height="38" /> 
</p><p>Solving, gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> , so the amount of time she drove in the rain is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> minutes.
</p><p>~coolmath2017
</p>
