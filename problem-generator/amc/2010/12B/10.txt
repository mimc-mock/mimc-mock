<p>The average of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/6/4d69bac0c8385bbb6d5af90a5c6e0bd7dc77f3ac.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3,\cdots, 98, 99,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/6/f8601c23e7ea6708da2f181ae49156648df382ae.png" class="latex" alt="$100x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/b/87bdf055d7ae3fec7be12d20921e2363cf512713.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{49}{101} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{50}{101} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \dfrac{1}{2} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \dfrac{51}{101} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \dfrac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="431" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We first sum the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638409f928abb07ee8413357fbd890b23e05ad7a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{99(100)}{2}=99\cdot50$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="136" height="38" />. Then, we know that the sum of the series is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/9/9f940e722cad85c26cb662bbcf2b4d1796a1a9c7.png" class="latex" alt="$99\cdot50+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> terms, so we can divide this sum by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and set it equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/6/f8601c23e7ea6708da2f181ae49156648df382ae.png" class="latex" alt="$100x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/e/dee34095c95d4eb8d0faffde82a81b4071561d9e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{99\cdot50+x}{100}=100x \Rightarrow 99\cdot50=100^2x-x\Rightarrow \frac{99\cdot50}{100^2-1}=x\]" width="458" height="38" />
Using difference of squares:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/3/5d32530090ee83aa2d0b78c9093d51789be3b4f0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x=\frac{99\cdot50}{101\cdot99}=\frac{50}{101}\]" width="150" height="38" />
Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/d/f0d5b8fdfd2bd98c8be0bad569663660c33f3438.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="23" height="23" />.
</p>

</p><p>~IceMatrix
