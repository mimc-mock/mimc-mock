<p>A solid cube has side length 3 inches. A 2-inch by 2-inch square hole is cut into the center of each face. The edges of each cut are parallel to the edges of the cube, and each hole goes all the way through the cube. What is the volume, in cubic inches, of the remaining solid?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/3/443e77961d5932cb9ef01b8d911a5ccb55ca9a59.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 8 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 10 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 12 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Imagine making the cuts one at a time. The first cut removes a box <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e00c42619c12f749a3bb75e5b4afcadc7bafd32c.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2\times 3$" width="71" height="12" />. The second cut removes two boxes, each of dimensions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc5de5cb27afbd247a2fcd9e841b4c6874180f5.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2\times 0.5$" width="85" height="12" />, and the third cut does the same as the second cut, on the last two faces. Hence the total volume of all cuts is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59f7389c3fbb2e4636e89c605442703d4da62426.png" class="latex" alt="$12 + 4 + 4 = 20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />.
</p><p>Therefore the volume of the rest of the cube is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c82eb5dcf2782cbd0fa6df391f9ef104645f4645.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 - 20 = 27 - 20 = \boxed{7\ \textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="219" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We can use <a href="/wiki/index.php/Principle_of_Inclusion-Exclusion" title="Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion">Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion</a> (PIE) to find the final volume of the cube.
</p><p>There are 3 "cuts" through the cube that go from one end to the other. Each of these "cuts" has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae8fdb2e885ba6e56da28228574598968b7ba0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 2 \times 3=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" /> cubic inches. However, we can not just sum their volumes, as
the central <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/c/e1c22f5c1912027b16c00cd431cb20cda62ed955.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2\times 2$" width="71" height="12" /> cube is included in each of these three cuts. To get the correct result, we can take the sum of the volumes of the three cuts, and subtract the volume of the central cube twice.
</p><p>Hence the total volume of the cuts is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/c/66c15a2ac38e6ee451895551e7eb72dba099b6db.png" class="latex" alt="$3(2 \times 2 \times 3) - 2(2\times 2\times 2) = 36 - 16 = 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="336" height="18" />.
</p><p>Therefore the volume of the rest of the cube is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c82eb5dcf2782cbd0fa6df391f9ef104645f4645.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 - 20 = 27 - 20 = \boxed{7\ \textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="219" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>We can visualize the final figure and see a cubic frame. We can find the volume of the figure by adding up the volumes of the edges and corners.
</p><p>Each edge can be seen as a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/1/96189382193e5a3430aadd81400e0df04b65b5f9.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 0.5\times 0.5$" width="99" height="12" /> box, and each corner can be seen as a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/b/43b7c89aee001e4b69ec09f207b4bb598fbe50c8.png" class="latex" alt="$0.5\times 0.5\times 0.5$" width="113" height="12" /> box.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/6/f462a7033eeb1a206f1926296f223a8c5d150e29.png" class="latex" alt="$12\cdot{\frac{1}{2}} + 8\cdot{\frac{1}{8}} = 6+1 = \boxed{7\ \textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="246" height="37" />.
</p>
