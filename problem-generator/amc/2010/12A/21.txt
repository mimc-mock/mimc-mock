<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> values in which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bf65a0e5e2500d1c001daa3e603356bf4bc931.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="265" height="18" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/6/416ae37947d4f62622823098de22e5fcf4a0b875.png" class="latex" alt="$y=bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" /> are the same as the zeros of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2ddb02974f8059914806e9cb0f0bb7d1837ee4c5.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="337" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> zeros and the function is never negative, all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> zeros must be double roots because the function's degree is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Suppose we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the roots of this function, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/a/83a85d9907b4104c6d4cf670f440645e3b206669.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-ux^2+vx-w$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="145" height="16" /> be the cubic polynomial with roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/6/1c69b49db1f012e936a64ac252b8641a8e392756.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}(x-p)(x-q)(x-r) &amp;= x^3-ux^2+vx-w\\ (x-p)^2(x-q)^2(x-r)^2 &amp;= x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c = 0\\ \sqrt{x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c} &amp;= x^3-ux^2+vx-w = 0\end{align*}" width="678" height="74" />
</p><p>In order to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/f/06fbb72fc8f7dbd7d2d5f414ad038678539b9032.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="319" height="18" /> we must first expand out the terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/1/2715ede67a7036c7bad854f06a15d394e6800c1b.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^3-ux^2+vx-w)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="19" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/6/d16c66369fc2eddcf00659452090770548ed7b2f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x^3-ux^2+vx-w)^2\]" width="165" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/0/eb002a07ca7ea06f1283bf510ee1f32a40a4324f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= x^6-2ux^5+(u^2+2v)x^4-(2uv+2w)x^3+(2uw+v^2)x^2-2vwx+w^2\]" width="496" height="19" />
</p><p>[Quick note: Since we don't know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, we really don't even need the last 3 terms of the expansion.]
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/a/3baf78dfd19a18270e11d507b63997111ad9f8be.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}&amp;2u = 10\\ u^2+2v &amp;= 29\\ 2uv+2w &amp;= 4\\ u &amp;= 5\\ v &amp;= 2\\ w &amp;= -8\\ &amp;\sqrt{x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c} = x^3-5x^2+2x+8\end{align*}" width="555" height="171" />
</p><p>All that's left is to find the largest root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1c5b397f295def2d8eafb5cbc6b58950812f008.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-5x^2+2x+8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="16" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/2/cf2369021c156f71636a4ca0af65c44da9efac34.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}&amp;x^3-5x^2+2x+8 = (x-4)(x-2)(x+1)\\ &amp;\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}\end{align*}" width="330" height="55" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> values in which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bf65a0e5e2500d1c001daa3e603356bf4bc931.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="265" height="18" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/6/416ae37947d4f62622823098de22e5fcf4a0b875.png" class="latex" alt="$y=bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" /> are the same as the zeros of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2ddb02974f8059914806e9cb0f0bb7d1837ee4c5.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="337" height="18" />.
We also know that this graph has 3 places tangent to the x-axis, which means that each root has to have a multiplicity of 2.
Let the function be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/f/cffe3af76227000fa8e9025cd121ec4848e17e3f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-p)^2(x-q)^2(x-r)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Applying Vieta's formulas, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/5/00540d831a8294caf5c16d4587d3712b73319354.png" class="latex" alt="$2p+2q+2r = 10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="16" />  or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/4/fd4e810881f140610dea3842e113f0eff01b66f6.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q+r = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" />.
Applying it again, we get, after simplification, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/b/d6b6d4937506b6df93d37c1e99648b9a9d9fcba2.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+q^2+r^2+4pq+4pr+4qr = 29$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="283" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Notice that squaring the first equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/a/58a771f3596dbb09786b8fa35c719206ce5406f4.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+q^2+r^2+2pq+2qr+2pr= 25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="283" height="18" />, which is similar to the second equation.
</p><p>Subtracting this from the second equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/8/a3886a5bb4f3fcbe397937fb10978c491a04b6ad.png" class="latex" alt="$2pq+2pr+2qr = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="158" height="15" />. Now that we have the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/7/b5798edc20aa63959af65f2ee1d0aa7cca9864f4.png" class="latex" alt="$pq+pr+qr$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="14" /> term, we can manpulate the equations to 
yield the sum of squares. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/8201cd54e07d678b96f2732610ecf70040119d4c.png" class="latex" alt="$2(p^2+q^2+r^2+2pq+2qr+2pr)-2pq-2pr-2qr= 25*2-4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="511" height="19" /> or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/b/69beb6bc837f11ceabfa56589e252d6cf1ffc040.png" class="latex" alt="$2p^2+2q^2+2r^2+2pq+2qr+2pr = 46$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="309" height="18" />. We finally reach <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85fcfad87039fb34a2e7af4d48d721564dec4521.png" class="latex" alt="$(p+q)^2+(q+r)^2+(p+r)^2 = 46$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Since the answer choices are integers, we can guess and check squares to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a942cbdbedfc89aa1615f99efbd5877ffc379d13.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(p+q)^2, (q+r)^2, (p+r)^2\} = \{1, 9, 36\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="312" height="19" /> in some order. We can check that this works by adding then and seeing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/5/00540d831a8294caf5c16d4587d3712b73319354.png" class="latex" alt="$2p+2q+2r = 10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="16" />. We just need to take the lowest value in the set, square root it, and subtract the resulting value from 5 to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/0/3502fb5e69be78d9b9580690026667f60fc47147.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p><p>Note: One could also multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/8/a3886a5bb4f3fcbe397937fb10978c491a04b6ad.png" class="latex" alt="$2pq+2pr+2qr = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="158" height="15" /> by 2 and subtract from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/b/d6b6d4937506b6df93d37c1e99648b9a9d9fcba2.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2+q^2+r^2+4pq+4pr+4qr = 29$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="283" height="18" /> to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/b/43b583f1ec8923af18f17715e3fbebc23e57450c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[p^2+q^2+r^2=21\]" width="135" height="18" /> The ordered triple {16,4,1} sums to 21, and the answer choices are all positive integers, therefore the answer is 4. 
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_method:">Alternative method:</span></h4>
<p>After reaching <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/4/fd4e810881f140610dea3842e113f0eff01b66f6.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q+r = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/b/eeb5a8c2acb82e1c21caa4dd8c89e368cc4e59dd.png" class="latex" alt="$pq + qr + rp = 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="15" />, we can algebraically derive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/7/63795f5ae81b5558afc51ffd1b2e8a7a30d38f30.png" class="latex" alt="$pqr$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="11" />.
</p><p>Applying Vieta's formulas on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/48524d3889ec8433cb03669176e738011df245b8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3$" width="16" height="15" /> term yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/2/0a2c485ce2dff855b720b70857da36c6ea9a92bb.png" class="latex" alt="$2p^2q+2pq^2+2q^2r+2qr^2+2r^2p+2rp^2+8pqr = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="408" height="18" />.
</p><p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/7/b87722212830b7e560aaa839f906cce9d343e176.png" class="latex" alt="$(p+q+r)(pq+qr+rp) = p^2q+pq^2+q^2r+qr^2+r^2p+rp^2+3pqr$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="541" height="19" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/8/208fc87b7d29f6da478c25baac279f140aefe5ea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2p^2q+2pq^2+2q^2r+2qr^2+2r^2p+2rp^2+6pqr = 2(p+q+r)(pq+qr+rp) = 20.\]" width="569" height="19" />
</p><p>Subtracting this from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/2/0a2c485ce2dff855b720b70857da36c6ea9a92bb.png" class="latex" alt="$2p^2q+2pq^2+2q^2r+2qr^2+2r^2p+2rp^2+8pqr = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="408" height="18" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/d/ecd0c14f94e374fa8efc43edf4e6be1b99985413.png" class="latex" alt="$2pqr = -16$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/b/18bcfd6412e6a13571b0a3749985f0168f5fba0e.png" class="latex" alt="$pqr = -8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" />, which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> are the roots of the cubic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a60a0feb7021ce8e5fc9ddbbfabcf168a10e6fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3 - 5x^2 + 2x + 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="16" />, and it is not hard to find that these roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. The largest of these values is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/0/3502fb5e69be78d9b9580690026667f60fc47147.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>First, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/8/168e74f4d54ead2f6ac321d8255629749ce86fed.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^6-10x^5+29x^4-4x^3+ax^2-bx-c = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="373" height="20" /> has exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> roots. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/1/8d1306722e26a2af24a6cd13e0f0101b72120efd.png" class="latex" alt="$y = (kx^3+lx^2+mx+n)^2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="246" height="21" />.
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/3/dd3f7a63f57ea6be9b78c7fcd3aeab4a691ae455.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2x^6+2klx^5+(2km+l^2)x^4+2(kn+lm)x^3+ax^2-bx-c = 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="509" height="21" />
</p><p>By matching the coefficients of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> terms, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/f/4df8815ffd2def4759a7809cde63bbaf1f40d349.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2 = 1, 2kl = -10, 2km+l^2 = 29, 2kn+2lm = -4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="402" height="20" />
</p><p>Solving the equations above, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> sets of solutions; first set of which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0dc81754ce4db23546a7f200153337283211fe2.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 1, l = -5, m = 2, n = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="216" height="18" />. Second set of which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa68eb201a047f02c59f535f3d5a95783cfc4ccb.png" class="latex" alt="$k = -1, l = 5, m = -2, n = -8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="245" height="18" />. After squaring both sets, they are the same i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2f04de590563e069949a4738179dc07b129d7c7.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-5x^2+2x+8 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="175" height="18" />.
</p><p>This is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc00cdf36b2f56346ddeaf001f13c22595589975.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-4)(x-2)(x+1) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="203" height="20" />. The largest root is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/3/c5398ac29083f4905f847444f89a4e8d0c015b1d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf{(A) 4}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="58" height="30" />
