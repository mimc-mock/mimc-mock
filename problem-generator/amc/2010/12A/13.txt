<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The image below shows the two curves for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/a/eeae143c5c9dbd376ffa1ffa8bdb93a58e8c9889.png" class="latex" alt="$k=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />. The blue curve is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/0/b806a23f9776fe081ff9a88053326282f59e4c07.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=k^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="18" />, which is clearly a circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, and the red curve is a part of the curve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f8bfc4a69f98f57e935422dd60a7e821b9a634.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=k$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bbace743a2cf4f73b734616d0b3bde5f326531d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(200);  real f(real x) {return 4/x;}; real g1(real x) {return sqrt(4*4-x*x);}; real g2(real x) {return -sqrt(4*4-x*x);}; draw(graph(f,-20./3,-0.6),red); draw(graph(f,0.6,20./3),red); draw(graph(g1,-4,4),blue); draw(graph(g2,-4,4),blue); axes(&quot;$x$&quot;,&quot;$y$&quot;); [/asy]" width="335" height="335" />
</p><p>In the special case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/d/badf0ecd1ef8289387d8462393438c6e2710ff28.png" class="latex" alt="$k=0$" width="43" height="12" /> the blue curve is just the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e722cebe029667c1ffbe7fec74c002ef4eddc3.png" class="latex" alt="$0\cdot 0=0$" width="64" height="12" />, this point is on the red curve as well, hence they intersect. 
</p><p>The case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0b043119262a2beb49176cb4beb5c50ec1915a2.png" class="latex" alt="$k&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> is symmetric to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e6630318d8cb28de2723180b6d359b972489c9.png" class="latex" alt="$k&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />: the blue curve remains the same and the red curve is flipped according to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis. Hence we just need to focus on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e6630318d8cb28de2723180b6d359b972489c9.png" class="latex" alt="$k&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />.
</p><p>Clearly, on the red curve there will always be points arbitrarily far from the origin: for example, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> approaches 0, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> approaches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/7/b671f1bb7e4ee86584347d5d22f1dc8abdb5bef2.png" class="latex" alt="$\infty$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="9" />. Hence the red curve intersects the blue one if and only if it contains a point whose distance from the origin is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p><br />
At this point we can guess that on the red curve the point where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0110f1bb04c51b2a4d431bf14489fc1b48b37421.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" /> is always closest to the origin, and skip the rest of this solution.
</p><p><br />
For an exact solution, fix <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> and consider any point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> on the red curve. Its distance from the origin is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/b/18b7589940b7fe89ad8b7c74f96819443fb3f63e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{ x^2 + (k/x)^2 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="109" height="32" />. To minimize this distance, it is enough to minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/9/c2914b55cd63fb5a2d0aceeefbe4c3936c499a0d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + (k/x)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="19" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_Mean-Geometric_Mean_Inequality" class="mw-redirect" title="Arithmetic Mean-Geometric Mean Inequality">Arithmetic Mean-Geometric Mean Inequality</a> we get that this value is at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8ea7ee8a282949fd853a01537ec7a2b55e56f75.png" class="latex" alt="$2k$" width="18" height="12" />, and that equality holds whenever <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081605f65e25ffefd15e5fb38868cf3c3f0b2223.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = (k/x)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="19" />, i.e., <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/f/b7fccd77d5fc072fb2ec825c4c5958d3375fc138.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\pm\sqrt k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="74" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Now recall that the red curve intersects the blue one if and only if its closest point is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> from the origin. We just computed that the distance between the origin and the closest point on the red curve is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/7/0374f2bf7b18fd0cfe04a4cf8fd633d4ea952e25.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2k}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="34" height="18" />. Therefore, we want to find all positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/5/34592017749664e415d710d5f80938bb6b928b3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2k} &gt; k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="18" />.
</p><p>Clearly the only such integer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed8470bc717a49aafe4556c7e2c2e11ddc7b687.png" class="latex" alt="$k=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, hence the two curves are only disjoint for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed8470bc717a49aafe4556c7e2c2e11ddc7b687.png" class="latex" alt="$k=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/9/db97681e49591a8b330c0dfa9907ee21c2feca2b.png" class="latex" alt="$k=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" />. 
This is a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/5/dc541507a8109c2fec31fc026137e62ee2c9375d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{2\ \textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> values.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>From the graph shown above, we see that there is a specific point closest to the center of the circle. Using some logic, we realize that as long as said furthest point is not inside or on the graph of the circle. This should be enough to conclude that the hyperbola does not intersect the circle. 
</p><p>Therefore, for each value of k, we only need to check said value to determine intersection. Let said point, closest to the circle have coordinates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/4/bc43f63db5b439939251890fbb979ecbd5ef5de4.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, k/x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> derived from the equation. Then, all coordinates that satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cdafa2a2fad4dba429186889b841454febb49b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{ x^2+ (k/x)^2 } \leq k$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="142" height="32" /> intersect the circle.
Squaring, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e86003bed608f0dcee7c91cff4f828310c3ffeb.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+(k/x)^2 \leq k^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="19" />
After multiplying through by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> and rearranging, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/8/1e80867fd0cd1046a80e04b99107dac93b165673.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-x^2k^2+k^2 \leq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="148" height="17" />.
We see this is a quadratic in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> and consider taking the determinant, which tells us that solutions are real when, after factoring:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2211bd2ddbc3ff094ed760688c8e8b880fe590.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2(k^2-4) \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="19" />
We plot this inequality on the number line to find it is satisfied for all values except: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/8/1d84d431f5b6541216a7c475c4af0d2a7b3b7f09.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 0, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" />.
We then eliminate 0 because it is extraneous as both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f71ba6fae7a17cf36f35b44715a9ab362fe293e9.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/5/b95be149fe43e267c27024cbe13fd852cb0b78c9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="18" /> are points which coincide.
Therefore, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/5/dc541507a8109c2fec31fc026137e62ee2c9375d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{2\ \textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> values.
</p>
