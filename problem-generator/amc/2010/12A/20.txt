<p>Arithmetic sequences <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae8179f9e0d5b3bb1863bd0765f3a97157e53560.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a_n\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26ee9081a9419fe093f0b21e99327e124ca7d5fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(b_n\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" /> have integer terms with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/9880d37de25ccd1bda447c87c3becc24822dd469.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=b_1=1&lt;a_2 \le b_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="169" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/d/0fdac46ddc3db135b3b5942f35e4f663330e11a2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n b_n = 2010$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="15" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. What is the largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d73352fdf59e50ee420ceb9362adf1b4c103adc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 8 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 288 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 2009$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae8179f9e0d5b3bb1863bd0765f3a97157e53560.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a_n\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26ee9081a9419fe093f0b21e99327e124ca7d5fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(b_n\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" /> have integer terms with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/2/0326a18185f90726f83091c63e9fe6bba6ef6343.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=b_1=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="89" height="15" />, we can write the terms of each sequence as
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e5779357d90e4bf94b097c02ee89cde8d563e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}&amp;\left(a_n\right) \Rightarrow \{1, x+1, 2x+1, 3x+1, ...\}\\ &amp;\left(b_n\right) \Rightarrow \{1, y+1, 2y+1, 3y+1, ...\}\end{align*}" width="276" height="43" />
</p><p>where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/1/3e172098f92df02657d21e5c4ff164fafc3cc66e.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leq y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />) are the common differences of each, respectively.
</p><p><br />
Since
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/3/00316b46a74880b61c852c28bc895bef87b5c6d5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}a_n &amp;= (n-1)x+1\\ b_n &amp;= (n-1)y+1\end{align*}" width="139" height="43" />
</p><p>it is easy to see that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/8/1e84f4a3f73075d86e1a7dfb93a1257839d0183b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n \equiv b_n \equiv 1 \mod{(n-1)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
Hence, we have to find the largest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/68121183dec509d99c9a8222e6a09709f8567d9c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a_n-1}{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="52" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/5/875634441b380292b324a3501dc9f6ec47387e87.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b_n-1}{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="50" height="38" /> are both integers; equivalently, we want to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b9dfbf8a27cb01bf88dd8e683de7e8b5841130.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a_n-1, b_n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
The prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/4/d541c875e3aec191d6b61e1e1e6dbaf6f5695846.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot{3}\cdot{5}\cdot{67}$" width="84" height="12" />. We list out all the possible pairs that have a product of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, noting that these are the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/a/04a2844915fdf3ddfc5a305bf59a04ba07af91b0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n, b_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> and we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/c/1acca7bed4eb49117acaeed5d78018faab2a8f66.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n \leq b_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/0/da0e897559aa6081021f81f3a1b2cc997eed041f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2,1005), (3, 670), (5,402), (6,335), (10,201),(15,134),(30,67)\]" width="482" height="18" />
</p><p>and soon find that the largest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> for the pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/c/91cfe1336c9cd85b7eadc830fe5703a946076c26.png" class="latex" alt="$(15, 134)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />, and so the largest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/4/1b48cb370d03dab778584e30144f47c4b10e09b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{8\ \textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>As above, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/e/e2e9a493f1d2143fed23e2e51b863a33dca776d3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=(n-1)x+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5c3adb6c68fb342304476fe6d26316b2d38fce.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n=(n-1)y+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="137" height="18" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6454c5f3c62b3ba5d16c37fdd77930512c5fc9.png" class="latex" alt="$1\leq x\leq y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="15" />.
</p><p>Now we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b73037b36dc92c16dfcad1033c2dc3376df99d9.png" class="latex" alt="$2010 = a_n b_n = (n-1)^2xy + (n-1)(x+y) + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="369" height="19" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/b/02bcfd6093307a6bd945c24581c801e125a6fe68.png" class="latex" alt="$2009 = (n-1)( (n-1)xy + x + y )$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="18" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/8/d58fc0dca6f69c4f4b4064add61ce97eaadd0d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$2009 = 7^2 \cdot 41$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="107" height="15" />. And as the second term is greater than the first one, we only have to consider the options <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/0/6c09e04a8657b77737e83f76bfe176e540644119.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1\in\{1,7,41\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="18" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/652902d9eae43fbacd2679da70d5ffb779a3776d.png" class="latex" alt="$n=42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" /> we easily see that for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/5/915f1781caa8b3e0eddafe7a445019f06178da22.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="15" /> the right side is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and for any other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> it is way too large.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4f171162b090bab7ce5dfa1c58715850530ba5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=8$" width="43" height="12" /> we are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a9804c92fcda539f1a4dd6e2f2c7486ac9ecf0c6.png" class="latex" alt="$7xy + x + y = 2009/7 = 7\cdot 41$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="235" height="18" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. We can start looking for the solution by trying the possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" />, and we easily discover that for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/7516e0be8d2011a06af559c086c005eee239d32e.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=21$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="15" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/0/25091609d3e86d0521ca7e07c7afd9218893f18a.png" class="latex" alt="$xy + 3 = 41$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />, which has a suitable solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/b/dab3838ed81bb04b5c7bf61ab124a3d0de102c3c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)=(2,19)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />.
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4f171162b090bab7ce5dfa1c58715850530ba5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=8$" width="43" height="12" /> is the largest possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. (There is no need to check <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/b/6cb7ae00764b56ee2adf59e78a1ffde9685b80db.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2$" width="43" height="12" /> anymore.)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_Thinking">Alternative Thinking</span></h2>
<p>Since
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/0/f602e7fbe61d7c63c4f02aa4bd464bf856716164.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n*b_n = 2010,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="117" height="16" />
</p><p><br />
and
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/6/ff6f9923361d731f58d5cb81de4ebb28ebd9e87a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n \le b_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" />,
</p><p><br />
it follows that
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/a/c0aadc58d74f2acbcfa2ed650736177c627f9193.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n \le \sqrt{2010} \Rightarrow a_n \le 44$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="183" height="19" />.
</p><p><br />
But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ffd01cdc48042ce7c67d8edcd4904809831cfe4.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> are also integers, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> must be a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> smaller than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b45828f292e161290be6ee62bc4c9df081ee1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/e/98ec62db7b03c2bf2668dece1a7c95969fcc05b2.png" class="latex" alt="$2010 = 2*3*5*67$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="157" height="13" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/7/a476eafa915ea0b6502d70c709fe3233883ed028.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n = 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="160" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/a/9ba2f7ca1cb330460e368562e5e5155d02087988.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n = 1005, 670, 402, 335, 201, 134,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="258" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/a/7fa7247f4d29d95459cbf6f238007a1b1090ebea.png" class="latex" alt="$67$" width="18" height="12" />; respectively.
</p><p><br />
Notice that the term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/d/1ddf57a1507686a49e3d290b3542ccac565cd35a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_m$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="10" /> is equivalent to the first term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/1/b1171ce641826e9aca894162f9c15a57f6b46e09.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" /> plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/e/d3e872b1716e55bea35303b2aa0c42770b1da5ec.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> times the common difference for that particular arithmetic sequence. Let the common difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> and the common difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/b/3bbc540ba557955e58c4e6099651014d129f2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$(b_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> (not <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/e/bfeb75fb36b8987924cd73c3c30c75435bd3b4ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="38" height="18" />). Then
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> (the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th term, not the sequence itself) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/4/f44336318c7638ad477f02ce7a0510765923b020.png" class="latex" alt="$=1 + k(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
and
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/9/bb9c6a478446cc5f3724006fd5f858d64aced85f.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n = 1 + i(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
Subtracting one from all the possible values listed above for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/f/8ffd01cdc48042ce7c67d8edcd4904809831cfe4.png" class="latex" alt="$b_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />, we get
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b42d3e6c914a345375ea754eeda296d2f87efe6.png" class="latex" alt="$k(n-1) = 1, 2, 4, 5, 9, 14, 29$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="220" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
and
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5f76a5222932d8b44dd2a08e48443bba230496f.png" class="latex" alt="$i(n-1) = 1004, 669, 401, 334, 200, 133, 66$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="325" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
In order to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we must maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coprime" class="mw-redirect" title="Coprime">coprime</a> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Greatest_common_factor" class="mw-redirect" title="Greatest common factor">GCF</a> of any corresponding pair. Inspecting all of the pairs, we see that the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Greatest_common_factor" class="mw-redirect" title="Greatest common factor">GCF</a> is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> except for the pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70aaad2e57b46d5568ccd3d876a82c7d4a593f2d.png" class="latex" alt="$(14, 133),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> which has a GCF of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. Therefore the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/4/234cb9b07baceaa342affb88068a28ef185a8520.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \Rightarrow \boxed{\text{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="23" />.
</p>
