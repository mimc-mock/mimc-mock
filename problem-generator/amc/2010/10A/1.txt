<p>Mary's top book shelf holds five books with the following widths, in centimeters: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4a7df1100efe353cc612d5d1c289c22d5bf8c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/c/40cba44ffc429fc98e11a1605ced22243b655f52.png" class="latex" alt="$2.5$" width="22" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What is the average book width, in centimeters?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b40fb4be71f2e697c16f18b2da38d674752a929f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 2 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 3 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 4 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>To find the average, we add up the widths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4a7df1100efe353cc612d5d1c289c22d5bf8c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/c/40cba44ffc429fc98e11a1605ced22243b655f52.png" class="latex" alt="$2.5$" width="22" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, to get a total sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. Since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> books, the average book width is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/1/581a3dbd8ea2a232e7aa7dc701fb19f26095dc26.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{20}{5}=4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" /> The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55bdade53f471aab49f63d1a734979cc0c636640.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="27" height="24" />.
</p>
