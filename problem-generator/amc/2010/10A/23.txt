<p>Each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> boxes in a line contains a single red marble, and for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/4/aa4914539356f9cd067409621814f4b7cbfbf4e8.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \le k \le 2010$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="103" height="15" />, the box in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/0/5300af7195084507169b12ba4d5fec382d6513b1.png" class="latex" alt="$k\text{th}$" width="25" height="12" /> position also contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> white marbles. Isabella begins at the first box and successively draws a single marble at random from each box, in order. She stops when she first draws a red marble. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/f/7dfd17dc4d3100462df726e8eb7f9262783429eb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> be the probability that Isabella stops after drawing exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> marbles. What is the smallest value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/8/278248c84b5bd1178f0dcffb2ea38e257eb41a29.png" class="latex" alt="$P(n) &lt; \frac{1}{2010}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="102" height="37" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The probability of drawing a white marble from box <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/5/3453f4764e6f55c65545d8ca1a2b8b9b64685870.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{k}{k + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="43" height="39" />, and the probability of drawing a red marble from box <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/e/bfe0cb68f9e0fcd3a240fa4f37606d494a6ab6f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{k+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="43" height="38" />.
</p><p>To stop after drawing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> marbles, we must draw a white marble from boxes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/f/1ef155f179039d7bcc11608d867ff11e0ca9f900.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, \ldots, n-1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="113" height="15" /> and draw a red marble from box <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd923e41635af40a1abe58f230732f2280b0723a.png" class="latex" alt="$n.$" width="14" height="8" /> Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4afea34f3ad6f2274778795778b2d5dad8ab0e85.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(n) = \left(\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{4} \cdots \frac {n - 1}{n}\right) \cdot \frac{1}{n +1} = \frac{1}{n (n + 1)}.\]" width="386" height="43" />
</p><p>So, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f197e63c23f8b9e32481dff05a8fce2370baa4e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n(n + 1)} &lt; \frac{1}{2010}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="134" height="41" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d33cbc6a9385dacea6bb59599c0246e056e50f55.png" class="latex" alt="$n(n+1) &gt; 2010.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/d/dfd81057d052a6e2d394c00239a5b63ad17a29a1.png" class="latex" alt="$n(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> increases as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> increases, we can simply test values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />; after some trial and error, we get that the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e619ae4e4fd8b30391e0718a08a7632589674c88.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }45}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/c/0cc5e4a750fed3db3864775efe7ee946643b0182.png" class="latex" alt="$45(46) = 2070$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" /> but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/a/4ca0868cb18c502fbb722dc918314869b41feda3.png" class="latex" alt="$44(45) = 1980.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />
</p>
