<p>In a magical swamp there are two species of talking amphibians: toads, whose statements are always true, and frogs, whose statements are always false. Four amphibians, Brian, Chris, LeRoy, and Mike live together in this swamp, and they make the following statements.
</p><p>Brian: "Mike and I are different species."
</p><p>Chris: "LeRoy is a frog."
</p><p>LeRoy: "Chris is a frog."
</p><p>Mike: "Of the four of us, at least two are toads."
</p><p>How many of these amphibians are frogs?
