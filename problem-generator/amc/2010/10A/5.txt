<p>The area of a circle whose circumference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca72da509d0e888f69331dc91184a176fef7dae5.png" class="latex" alt="$24\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da40faccb9c89ca9cfed0f44129b44b014b42639.png" class="latex" alt="$k\pi$" width="20" height="12" />. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/f/0df257abfbb2d44fa2fad2024bc9c62781eb88e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 6 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 12 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 24 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 36 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 144$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="388" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If the circumference of a circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca72da509d0e888f69331dc91184a176fef7dae5.png" class="latex" alt="$24\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" />, the radius would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Since the area of a circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e89ed1c5456915b4e95e0c8a11257ac5ed3d32c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^2$" width="25" height="15" />, the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/1/c6155902d0161aa6d6e6462171864e71c79295f6.png" class="latex" alt="$144\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/2/55206bce678ac0394d97dc04dd72398df2bdf572.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>By definition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> is the ratio of the circumference to the diameter. Since the circumference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca72da509d0e888f69331dc91184a176fef7dae5.png" class="latex" alt="$24\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" />, the diameter must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> and the radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Therefore, by the area of circle formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce9404342242f35ca13e32c0e668929a5e819137.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\pi r^{2}$" width="63" height="15" /> the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/c/21c68d2d3cfe7629eed4fbb6b2dc42baa4be130b.png" class="latex" alt="$12^{2}\pi=144\pi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/7/cd7239cbe257f639bd95444a8304a831d842d0cf.png" class="latex" alt="$k=144 \Longrightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(E)} 144}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="169" height="30" />.
</p>
