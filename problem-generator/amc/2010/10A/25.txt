<p>Jim starts with a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and creates a sequence of numbers. Each successive number is obtained by subtracting the largest possible integer square less than or equal to the current number until zero is reached. For example, if Jim starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/1/f0126a8af0c273138c6f6903efb72142ab6722ff.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 55$" width="52" height="12" />, then his sequence contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers:
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/d/2eda46643f3b6313f65bb202d501f3391552655b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{ccccc} {}&amp;{}&amp;{}&amp;{}&amp;55\\ 55&amp;-&amp;7^2&amp;=&amp;6\\ 6&amp;-&amp;2^2&amp;=&amp;2\\ 2&amp;-&amp;1^2&amp;=&amp;1\\ 1&amp;-&amp;1^2&amp;=&amp;0\\ \end{array}\]" width="146" height="93" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> be the smallest number for which Jim’s sequence has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers. What is the units digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/a/78abc8d260523b3ed009e0b31adf3963a02bff8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 5 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 7 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can find the answer by working backwards. We begin with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/9/c793106f3d7dd7df6976cde908c210374c0228f8.png" class="latex" alt="$1-1^2=0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="80" height="15" /> on the bottom row, then the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> goes to the right of the equal's sign in the row above. We find the smallest value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/12953c1d122581c4df7be9f2f4ad705f324c610e.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1^2=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/586d5accf4aa56d26348098eb6cac397d8cef4ca.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;1^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69271b422e022ea65ff1f288eddfcfa82afd8ab7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2$" width="43" height="12" />.
</p><p>We repeat the same procedure except with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/12953c1d122581c4df7be9f2f4ad705f324c610e.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1^2=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="81" height="15" /> for the next row and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/9/2294bc3943da415bf4b1e92c746993b16c0e669c.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1^2=2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="15" /> for the row after that. However, at the fourth row, we see that solving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a319d4b81e279fcbb40ff9b18d914bdd55d8adf.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1^2=3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="15" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7d65cfa316d639c9267398009812f3eb45fea9.png" class="latex" alt="$x=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, in which case it would be incorrect since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/4/5f4215c50c3e48737000cc4b9230577fda74d20c.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" /> is not the greatest perfect square less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> . So we make it a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/a/32a35631e1e126934ead6d2af161e9d250827bbb.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2$" width="15" height="15" /> and solve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/f/aaf4c7c07365cfc7c2efb42d604b4d55787056e1.png" class="latex" alt="$x-2^2=3$" width="82" height="15" />. We continue on using this same method where we increase the perfect square until <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> can be made bigger than it. When we repeat this until we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> rows, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/c/8acd99dc227327e964c7e0b92b1319c372a9e983.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{ccccc}{}&amp;{}&amp;{}&amp;{}&amp;7223\\ 7223&amp;-&amp;84^{2}&amp;=&amp;167\\ 167&amp;-&amp;12^{2}&amp;=&amp;23\\ 23&amp;-&amp;4^{2}&amp;=&amp;7\\ 7&amp;-&amp;2^{2}&amp;=&amp;3\\ 3&amp;-&amp;1^{2}&amp;=&amp;2\\ 2&amp;-&amp;1^{2}&amp;=&amp;1\\ 1&amp;-&amp;1^{2}&amp;=&amp;0\\ \end{array}\]" width="191" height="153" />
</p><p>Hence the solution is the last digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f34ed6c8bb8e33904c8c5818525d93245c122b.png" class="latex" alt="$7223$" width="35" height="12" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/a/f1a49ee6ad19619363a5ce6088c57cb77e8cf8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p><p>Note: We can go up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa1fc71b09af19812c2d58f881973287e7f6469.png" class="latex" alt="$167$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" />, and then notice the pattern of units digits alternating between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, so we do not need to calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f34ed6c8bb8e33904c8c5818525d93245c122b.png" class="latex" alt="$7223$" width="35" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Notice that to get the previous term, we must add the smallest square number, (let's call it <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/6006bba637eef443381fbaff768fa62d9ff41e62.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2$" width="17" height="15" />) such that the sum is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57efb5c0b218135087ac110b8467426b528d7856.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="19" />. Otherwise, instead of subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/6006bba637eef443381fbaff768fa62d9ff41e62.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2$" width="17" height="15" /> from the previous term, we're subtracting a greater square number. 
</p><p><br />
Remember that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/b/9dbc61b58fbc78e87dc27763a8564150f1b5193b.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)^2 = x^2 + 2x + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="19" />. Recall that to find the previous term, we must add a square number such that it is less than the next square number. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17ecd801c09decac3c098e19f1c5a0e63909562a.png" class="latex" alt="$a + n^2 &lt; (n+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="19" />. For this to be true, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/a/4fa9a52fc9c3cd93ae603a0aec373a4ff0752ae3.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; 2n + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="13" />. What that means is that given a term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we can find the previous term by adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/6006bba637eef443381fbaff768fa62d9ff41e62.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2$" width="17" height="15" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/6/79645afa5908778521ca0707aae1637c8f0733cb.png" class="latex" alt="$n &gt; \frac {a-1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="37" />. 
</p><p><br />
For example, to find the term that precedes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa1fc71b09af19812c2d58f881973287e7f6469.png" class="latex" alt="$167$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" />, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/7/a5791aed2e98b50ac525dfa60f4bb93306caf887.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;166/2 = 83$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="18" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/2/a32f471e0ef880d96dc91af462a673801ce3670c.png" class="latex" alt="$n=84$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="13" /> and the previous term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/5/2c5a5674f6b7f08a6f196b43b17b8e7dc7cf046e.png" class="latex" alt="$167 + 84^2 = 7223$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="135" height="16" />. The last digit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f34ed6c8bb8e33904c8c5818525d93245c122b.png" class="latex" alt="$7223$" width="35" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a425e2e84b6f6ad7d6bf35a074014c8c349757f.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="92" height="28" />
</p>
