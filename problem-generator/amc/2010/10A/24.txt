<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hints_and_Method_of_Attack">Hints and Method of Attack</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> be the result of dividing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ff2c0b95f4812c1f1a8f106e2a09718c79fa04d.png" class="latex" alt="$90!$" width="21" height="13" /> by tens such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. We want to consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/e/cfe684e694cf6d24c7aa35f0fc624f71c5fa2787.png" class="latex" alt="$P \mod 100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="13" />. But because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> is not prime, and because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is obviously divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> (if in doubt, look at the answer choices), we only need to consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/7/0e7841232a6d543d9796ccea18cb6cf74e822123.png" class="latex" alt="$P \mod 25$" width="84" height="12" />.
</p><p>However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> is a very particular number. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/958d536894a7f9c12b20e9e07dd969a88834e508.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \equiv -1 \text{ }(\text{mod }25)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="200" height="20" />, and so is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/7/057f6cf7c6454188f3af37442b231a70ad902edc.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \cdot 7 \cdot 8 \cdot 9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="14" />. How can we group terms to take advantage of this fact?
</p><p>There might be a problem when you cancel out the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />s from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ff2c0b95f4812c1f1a8f106e2a09718c79fa04d.png" class="latex" alt="$90!$" width="21" height="13" />. One method is to cancel out a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> from an existing number along with a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. But this might prove cumbersome, as the grouping method will not be as effective. Instead, take advantage of <i>inverses</i> in modular arithmetic. Just leave the negative powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in a "storage base," and take care of the other terms first. Then, use Fermat's Little Theorem to solve for the power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
