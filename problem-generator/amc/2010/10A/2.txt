<p>Four identical squares and one rectangle are placed together to form one large square as shown. The length of the rectangle is how many times as large as its width? 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/9/d09caec6074d6abf81a6e3a7755b2eecc103bc41.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(8mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt));  draw((0,0)--(4,0)--(4,4)--(0,4)--cycle); draw((0,3)--(0,4)--(1,4)--(1,3)--cycle); draw((1,3)--(1,4)--(2,4)--(2,3)--cycle); draw((2,3)--(2,4)--(3,4)--(3,3)--cycle); draw((3,3)--(3,4)--(4,4)--(4,3)--cycle);  [/asy]" width="155" height="155" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/e/4de27d6131722fa5e19723595186ef1cb45b3538.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \dfrac{5}{4} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \dfrac{4}{3} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \dfrac{3}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 2 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 3$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="354" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the length of the small square be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, intuitively, the length of the big square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" />. It can be seen that the width of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />. Thus, the length of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/2/c3258ea09a55171650a0b7b1bafbb046d83455f8.png" class="latex" alt="$4x/3x = 4/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" /> times as large as the width. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can say the area of one small square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/415dd5722f2b57ce55e47766ccb11a0cdc4eb051.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of the area of the large square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/7/4675e27bb11d6efd975189436e433aaaf1677af2.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="25" height="15" /> so the area of the large square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2cdff2d6a8670b38e569746fb3cdf0dc4f92698.png" class="latex" alt="$16x^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="34" height="15" />, so each side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> so the length of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> and the width of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/e/c0ebafaf0c014017aa471fe94b81c313dcd99206.png" class="latex" alt="$4x-x=3x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="95" height="13" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/1/36107c467418ec8625cf3ca4ddadce0996c687b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{4x}{3x}=\dfrac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />
</p>
