<p>Tyrone had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/239430b0fc1c7fe38f296a8845ed7446198bda7e.png" class="latex" alt="$97$" width="18" height="12" /> marbles and Eric had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> marbles. Tyrone then gave some of his marbles to Eric so that Tyrone ended with twice as many marbles as Eric. How many marbles did Tyrone give to Eric?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/2/f52b32ee2910d40bdfcf1ed0cc6e05402cd6d58f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 13 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 18 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 25 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 29$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of marbles Tyrone gave to Eric. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/f/daf83988dab31daa99c3a3971de7b6e21497c938.png" class="latex" alt="$97-x = 2\cdot(11+x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="18" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba9a27ce55163ac2344d3e6b6e6eee32a57df46.png" class="latex" alt="$75=3x$" width="61" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/7/327ab7cf6343ea0f45cbc5faaf029336b531f0b4.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 25$" width="52" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55bdade53f471aab49f63d1a734979cc0c636640.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="27" height="24" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the number of balls Tyrone and Eric have a specific ratio when Tyrone gives some of his balls to Eric, we can divide the total amount of balls by their respective ratios. Altogether, they have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1ed61c5f59f2680e0c90f4edde3cc352120b38fb.png" class="latex" alt="$97+11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="14" /> balls, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> balls, and it does not change when Tyrone gives some of his to Eric, since none are lost in the process. After Tyrone gives some of his balls away, the ratio between the balls is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b3714d1977e2e6fa2c03536bd04eabab371fd18.png" class="latex" alt="$2:1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="12" />, and added together is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. The two numbers add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and we divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and the outcome is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />. This is the number of balls Eric has, and so doubling that results in the number of balls Tyrone has, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/a/faac2301b6a7a68303da688c3b8e0b9b58ef0ea6.png" class="latex" alt="$97-72$" width="58" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/0/96084ed2e31d3c4af4361a54c5c0389636645d02.png" class="latex" alt="$36-11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />, thus proving our statement true, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/6/3865a3d71198540dcf0fabcb80eb39f5616c4891.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{D=25}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="24" />.
</p>

</p><p>~IceMatrix
