</p><p>Construct the midpoints <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b8288be194d1d584b7b28066c50ac1aeec0dc56e.png" class="latex" alt="$E=(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b0243b287a33cef26d48d4ccca76d873b52cd9c.png" class="latex" alt="$F=(10,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" /> and triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/c/66c94b1fccc85ef63b52e608207ad9a568868a48.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EMF$" width="64" height="13" /> as in the diagram, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is the center of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" />. Also construct points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> as in the diagram so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/3672a48c190c3e7958f06cf311ae1692091c1267.png" class="latex" alt="$BG\parallel PQ$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="76" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/c/5cc11b6537847346f7d19fea4f03c1d87954fb97.png" class="latex" alt="$CH\parallel QR$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="78" height="19" />.
</p><p>Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/4/8d492c418f9d5c181c7e305e5e6163fddd534f36.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AGB\sim\triangle CHD$" width="145" height="13" /> while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> being a square implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/5/1853b3b1c11ef15a2c8e3797f825800bee15d35e.png" class="latex" alt="$GB=CH$" width="84" height="12" />. Furthermore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/4/0e47e3e37564042dd0a9ca1c2600be6dfcefe252.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=6=3\cdot AB$" width="137" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e29f2661a889ec503c08564c72af36ca42bdd1f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CHD$" width="62" height="13" /> is 3 times bigger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f071e95e6267c2a3ebc6be0af5f0d20d01e41c97.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AGB$" width="58" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/0/17000ee06d57b4b9799e3cbce59f634435008654.png" class="latex" alt="$HD=3\cdot GB=3\cdot HC$" width="184" height="12" />. In other words, the longer leg is 3 times the shorter leg in any triangle similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f071e95e6267c2a3ebc6be0af5f0d20d01e41c97.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AGB$" width="58" height="13" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfb064112b6c94470339f6571f69d07afc1c024c.png" class="latex" alt="$K$" width="16" height="12" /> be the foot of the perpendicular from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/b/f5b3d6f91b3cdd41600d568a9060104b39f83235.png" class="latex" alt="$x=EK$" width="65" height="12" />. Triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b6945435f925b8491a7594d3bbddcea42e8530e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EKM$" width="66" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/0/8500d3f2164a294d6dd51c90514023cd6bc71f15.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MKF$" width="66" height="13" />, being similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f071e95e6267c2a3ebc6be0af5f0d20d01e41c97.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AGB$" width="58" height="13" />, also have legs in a 1:3 ratio, therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/d/8bd95431223bdec22bba7ffd3c658b90610b444e.png" class="latex" alt="$MK=3x$" width="79" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/3/983d472d8fd532430d078f83eb7186043dc54b55.png" class="latex" alt="$KF=9x$" width="74" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/c/33c9cef9ab109788f224eaaf219bb097f2c1ade3.png" class="latex" alt="$10x=EF=6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="13" />. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/c/8aceefd0305db63656dc2df364ad67bc2178ee8a.png" class="latex" alt="$EK=0.6$" width="78" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/9/7a98866aa824d66262f378df4ea8131785d7b08c.png" class="latex" alt="$MK=1.8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="13" />, so the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56a4cbbec122eee209368314ec79a24d42855d2a.png" class="latex" alt="$(4+0.6,1.8)=(4.6,1.8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="191" height="18" /> and so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f287e3d6b2a3c9afa935bafc43119ca7f621514a.png" class="latex" alt="$4.6+1.8 = 6.4 =$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bbfb0c6c391f731a0b463b61e9239375c24ff9a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathbf{(C)}\ 32/5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="29" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/816abfee8097b50cee36ca7ef8026c4df03c0c2e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7cm); pair A=(0,0),B=(1,1.5),D=B*dir(-90),C=B+D-A; draw((-4,-2)--(8,-2), Arrows); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); pair AB = extension(A,B,(0,-2),(1,-2)); pair BC = extension(B,C,(0,-2),(1,-2)); pair CD = extension(C,D,(0,-2),(1,-2)); pair DA = extension(D,A,(0,-2),(1,-2)); draw(A--AB--B--BC--C--CD--D--DA--A, dotted); dot(AB^^BC^^CD^^DA);[/asy]" width="332" height="102" />
</p><p>Let the four points be labeled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b0256a78d58b6d5f7a53400b19e7747d2983fa.png" class="latex" alt="$P_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/7/107c7c8e5a64d81ed24331e510b777b7d08345b7.png" class="latex" alt="$P_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/9/9090f2e6bd5da465fea10a8c755d64e9185f61f1.png" class="latex" alt="$P_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/4/d6467cab96a8b0fa5724824ac7d13187e9a84c31.png" class="latex" alt="$P_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" />, respectively. Let the lines that go through each point be labeled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/9/199064ab269e28abf76e7f8f9947f2a4b536da3d.png" class="latex" alt="$L_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce926b8796ad68357b1f99680dac0ae775407bf6.png" class="latex" alt="$L_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" />, respectively. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> go through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b101ae280775cd91fd7bb1d46e315a73a11ff5f.png" class="latex" alt="$SP$" width="26" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/8908a2be2692d4eb840757cd862a1fd76a024398.png" class="latex" alt="$RQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />, respectively, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b101ae280775cd91fd7bb1d46e315a73a11ff5f.png" class="latex" alt="$SP$" width="26" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/8908a2be2692d4eb840757cd862a1fd76a024398.png" class="latex" alt="$RQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" /> are opposite sides of the square, we can say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> are parallel with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/9/199064ab269e28abf76e7f8f9947f2a4b536da3d.png" class="latex" alt="$L_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce926b8796ad68357b1f99680dac0ae775407bf6.png" class="latex" alt="$L_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> have slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ecf2f6a8e46ee4becd582e7a321282f42b9e6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="37" />. Also, note that since square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> lies in the first quadrant, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/3/e13a902eb6a24cf493a2732badd0d151fb4f7672.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> must have a positive slope. Using the point-slope form, we can now find the equations of all four lines: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/7/4876bc1baea8b620c4623d2584e8f6cd25d6f3e0.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1: y = m(x-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac224e37ef46f9501a668a8c6da7a27aeadcfd06.png" class="latex" alt="$L_2: y = m(x-5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/d/90df12b3742092a9e531610109759bb0cac57107.png" class="latex" alt="$L_3: y = -\frac{1}{m}(x-7)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="157" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfb18d1081bfdf6dd671fed096b8449201be136c.png" class="latex" alt="$L_4: y = -\frac{1}{m}(x-13)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="166" height="37" />.
</p><p><br />

Our approach will be to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/b/1eb29f9de3753a59530941141fcb5c7aa3fa2e38.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta x$" width="25" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/a/b4a2530bebabe83e148d01b175c4b29a7e1bcbb8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="24" height="16" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and equate the two to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f22b669e4803ae525cf8120ca251e2eab025872.png" class="latex" alt="$L_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/9/199064ab269e28abf76e7f8f9947f2a4b536da3d.png" class="latex" alt="$L_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> intersect at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. Intersecting the two equations, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-coordinate of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> is found to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b358c7ca0b9cc03eab3884e261114053ebf138f8.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{8m}{m^2 + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="91" height="39" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that the center of the square lies along a line that has an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/31572a833f4041c91695248e85c6f3a17ed948fc.png" class="latex" alt="$x-$" width="23" height="8" />intercept of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b4903429cdb87dfe1142c0105baef4a7919ad5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3+5}{2}=4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="37" />, and also along another line with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/31572a833f4041c91695248e85c6f3a17ed948fc.png" class="latex" alt="$x-$" width="23" height="8" />intercept <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/5/445bef4531d8c5d6313c11d1388eb8f1f54f67b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7+13}{2}=10$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="37" />. Since these 2 lines are parallel to the sides of the square, they are perpendicular (since the sides of a square are). Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> be the slope of the first line. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ecf2f6a8e46ee4becd582e7a321282f42b9e6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="37" /> is the slope of the second line. We may use the point-slope form for the equation of a line to write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/6/b46e74f564eaa42bad7d5ae88e7d8b56b76e2cf6.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1:y=m(x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="132" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/4/0c437be2832c440cc4ea0ca2f6db8cdbe965031c.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2:y=-\frac{1}{m}(x-10)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="159" height="37" />. We easily calculate the intersection of these lines using substitution or elimination to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/b/1fb4db81eff43ab71213deecfd3e00963da1d69c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{4m^2+10}{m^2+1},\frac{6m}{m^2+1}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="166" height="43" /> as the center or the square. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> denote the (acute) angle formed by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee61fd6e8a1926a802b97fc99b66b8687d8f620.png" class="latex" alt="$l_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/31572a833f4041c91695248e85c6f3a17ed948fc.png" class="latex" alt="$x-$" width="23" height="8" />axis. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/e/b4ef80dbdc026bceb78a14593113a3f030b33200.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\theta=m$" width="78" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the side length of the square. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38ed185fa066d89fe6ab8c0bf0b9b8ca4c5e0540.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin\theta=s/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. On the other hand the acute angle formed by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/6/1d69232050ab0ada641e7e2035f6e7c795795b65.png" class="latex" alt="$l_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="11" height="15" /> and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/31572a833f4041c91695248e85c6f3a17ed948fc.png" class="latex" alt="$x-$" width="23" height="8" />axis is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/42880fc831a53dca1ef7e06cdb6b7c848cdc6f8c.png" class="latex" alt="$90-\theta$" width="49" height="13" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/3/a33d7aa8fb506054cd79763da060365165fca1c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos\theta=\sin(90-\theta)=s/6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="197" height="18" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/d/46d7dc1a659c4f4e7693ac0f2266a1b05f14490e.png" class="latex" alt="$m=\tan\theta=3$" width="111" height="13" />. Substituting into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/b/1fb4db81eff43ab71213deecfd3e00963da1d69c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{4m^2+10}{m^2+1},\frac{6m}{m^2+1}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="166" height="43" /> we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/0/4308180c87ec67a72c268b5055d8842cd3f86012.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{23}{5},\frac{9}{5}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="66" height="43" /> so that the sum of the coordinates is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/9/b39a920d79c933e5e0c364cc699af7a9167929e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{32}{5}=6.4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="37" />. Hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/8/0b8c89e855982fd470ad44d61fdbd31f82c4b129.png" class="latex" alt="$\framebox{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="23" height="23" />.
</p>
