<p>Two parabolas have equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f526f7c478299df74c73c277b90621d967b56c1.png" class="latex" alt="$y= x^2 + ax +b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/5/9b5ee5ff6e082f8b985aea208de9a59ba786f332.png" class="latex" alt="$y= x^2 + cx +d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/0/2d02bb2275100261109b043261e702124460a83e.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are integers, each chosen independently by rolling a fair six-sided die. What is the probability that the parabolas will have at least one point in common?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/0/550286a21404244c7e02ccb3e1894435f8a99354.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{25}{36}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{5}{6}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{31}{36}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="382" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Set the two equations equal to each other: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/9/a69ea346be3a60bf0e89884fb60cb092f483bb2b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + ax + b = x^2 + cx + d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="204" height="16" />. Now remove the x squared and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />'s on one side: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/b/fcb69196c885894c90a2d63e458cc211196c3ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$ax-cx=d-b$" width="124" height="12" />. Now factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/8/258be19b1cf5f4daa87ed005045d0e868d7b0270.png" class="latex" alt="$x(a-c)=d-b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cannot equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, then there is always a solution, but if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" />, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> chance, leaving a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/1/5f16fb84a8ee112a4dbb09e361549f8cb2696725.png" class="latex" alt="$1080$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/d/e5d12e688b849bdc599794d49762ec2d6fb3583b.png" class="latex" alt="$1296$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, always having at least one point in common. And if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" />, then the only way for that to work, is if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/7/db7c484eabfd9c6a0df50467667bf3658e63b815.png" class="latex" alt="$d=b$" width="41" height="12" />, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> chance, however, this can occur <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> ways, so a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> chance of this happening. So adding one  thirty sixth to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/f/b7f97d5a3842441a4aea8e3841081b4f25bc47fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1080}{1296}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="38" height="38" />, we get the simplified fraction of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/2/1c253211ed5a85ce23173963862b801e5bad8284.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{31}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />; answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de641a309fc5878c26baf699f2382b7d4b02b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Proceed as above to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/8/258be19b1cf5f4daa87ed005045d0e868d7b0270.png" class="latex" alt="$x(a-c)=d-b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />. The probability that the parabolas have at least 1 point in common is 1 minus the probability that they do not intersect. The equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/8/258be19b1cf5f4daa87ed005045d0e868d7b0270.png" class="latex" alt="$x(a-c)=d-b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" /> has no solution if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a24be171191f46bc2198359cff964ebfee201b.png" class="latex" alt="$d\neq b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="17" />. The probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/b/43ba19be0e30b99142416ebfbe17670437c8453e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> while the probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a24be171191f46bc2198359cff964ebfee201b.png" class="latex" alt="$d\neq b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="17" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/3/5533bc48602603f16d18c04858cad119c04833c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Thus we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/c/79cac78d2a5a9b666875d7b6de755bc67bab5aab.png" class="latex" alt="$1-\left(\frac{1}{6}\right)\left(\frac{5}{6}\right)=\frac{31}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="157" height="43" /> for the probability that the parabolas intersect.
</p>
