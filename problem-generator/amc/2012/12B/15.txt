<p>Jesse cuts a circular disk of radius 12, along 2 radii to form 2 sectors, one with a central angle of 120. He makes two circular cones using each sector to form the lateral surface of each cone. What is the ratio of the volume of the smaller cone to the larger cone?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/9/5691a1ec32689fdef25dce5a8d2535d00594813e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{8}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{4}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{5}}{6}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{\sqrt{5}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="422" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If the original radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, then the circumference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca72da509d0e888f69331dc91184a176fef7dae5.png" class="latex" alt="$24\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" />; since arcs are defined by the central angles, the smaller arc, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> degree angle, is half the size of the larger sector. so the smaller arc is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/b/9fb1c2c9bfe3fc5bca66d58ba9a8cd476ae446d8.png" class="latex" alt="$8\pi$" width="19" height="12" />, and the larger is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/6/a8614a7ac3a38794811c563d39185fa13a00d684.png" class="latex" alt="$16\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" />. Those two arc lengths become the two circumferences of the new cones; so the radius of the smaller cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and the larger cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. Using the Pythagorean theorem, the height of the larger cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4896b3f769896054f21691accd1ed331b99961.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="18" /> and the smaller cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/d/2ad4a58de6859e6c5ca4921b2d801d6bc616bf05.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="18" />, and now for volume just square the radii and multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/3/c53700c7fb5b8947273bbcdaf696719461fcb124.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the height to get the volume of each cone: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49c25f6486c172dce9f6858be0fefb39f3ba316.png" class="latex" alt="$128\cdot\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/3/eb3fc9ce070f31b6ca81bf9b818805e4f2bd5e92.png" class="latex" alt="$256\cdot\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="18" /> [both multiplied by three as ratio come out the same. now divide the volumes by each other to get the final ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/3/5e3c33b22966c788ece10a9b76e47228806cd455.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } \frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="84" height="53" />
</p>
