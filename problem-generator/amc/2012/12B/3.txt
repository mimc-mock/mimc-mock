<p>For a science project, Sammy observed a chipmunk and squirrel stashing acorns in holes. The chipmunk hid 3 acorns in each of the holes it dug. The squirrel hid 4 acorns in each of the holes it dug. They each hid the same number of acorns, although the squirrel needed 4 fewer holes. How many acorns did the chipmunk hide?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a4de86ae315f8a61128216d3d8a15535a159615.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 42\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 54$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of holes that the chipmunk dug, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4be7ccaf818a251c934cb215f4827566c2f7f3e0.png" class="latex" alt="$3x=4(x-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/5/8f555cbc43d4780da281075418071d204f45d253.png" class="latex" alt="$3x=4x-16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/2/1628581d2c8bdf5dfbb9d5fafe360ec1773aaff6.png" class="latex" alt="$x=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. The number of acorns hidden by the chipmunk is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38ca0f5a0bc45e77eeb02374abf34defae4bcfa6.png" class="latex" alt="$3x = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 48}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="108" height="28" />
</p>
