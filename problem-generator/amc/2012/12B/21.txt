</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/5/6a587e896738d07a9ad44d77ea31aa45d0278f85.png" class="latex" alt="$AZ = 29\sqrt{3} ... \framebox{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="133" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>First, we want to angle chase. Set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38ee08ad81fd7f64732d661cadae3486c171aa95.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;YXC$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> degrees. 
</p><p>Now the key idea is that you want to relate the numbers that you have. You know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/0/320662bbfd8c10a834bf06da0d4d1ae08a189985.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB} = 40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="16" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/6/5b6ce2f493e2588a5c152c701fd2ffc3ebdcd4e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{EZ} + \overline{ZF} = 41(\sqrt{3}-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="189" height="21" />. We proceed with the Law of Sines.
</p><p>Call the side length of the square x. Then we are going to set a constant k equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/c/fbcbef31d1315afde8adf6e7c3dc78f9d2f5e642.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sin 120^{\circ}}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="62" height="37" />, and this is consistent for every triangle in the diagram because all the angles of the hexagon are equiangular (and so they are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/4/9b49fcb2f74ccae5ec0e722a8c65b5b9bbd93720.png" class="latex" alt="$120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />). 
</p><p>Then we get the following process:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/a/57a562db5a1110d88bb481c8e9e16c7ed5ac4af6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\sin(90-a)}{40} = k\]" width="122" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/9/d0944a907289affbfefcdba17cff283afee22cfe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos a = 40k\]" width="88" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/f/b2f20d2848a0dca6763b48c0cedaa5476bca401c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\sin(a-30)}{\overline{EZ}} = k\]" width="122" height="40" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/1/3f1d6932ee54ea45d57882b81e459813ef624b3e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin(a-30) = \overline{EZ}\cdot k\]" width="161" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/0/9607341f469f8ada1524972f5f33e84985542b5b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\sin(60-a)}{\overline{ZF}} = k\]" width="122" height="40" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/7/ce7c3c486376b4efaddc847688b5d2fdb9ef9efa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin(60-a) = \overline{ZF}\cdot k\]" width="161" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/b/39bd7252e00c486881ee8b8d0ac5b82bddd55ed7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin(a-30) + \sin(60-a) = k\cdot 41(\sqrt{3}-1)\]" width="328" height="21" />
</p><p>And now expanding using our trig formulas, we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58a14ffd43d5c51f276232ffb56d167bb6ef8f0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\sin a + \cos a)(\frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2} = k\cdot 41(\sqrt{3}-1)\]" width="307" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf90e4feac8699a9d54a1f0c2fc2cf6053789b4f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin a + \cos a = 82k\]" width="145" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59d3811358da6024ede26cdf731fc07f26e51542.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin a = 42k\]" width="86" height="13" />
</p><p>And so now we have a triangle where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc0cbb39e7eb287f1446ead7692b25e7f469ce5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos a = 40k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="88" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10f293faaf6d0951df49514922630f7a7bcbb034.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin a = 42k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" />. Put them in a triangle where the hypotenuse is 1. Then, by the Pythagorean Theorem, we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbbc1de18389d2cc0a3c7b15028fbf85329d7382.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{(40k)^2 + (42k)^2} = 1\]" width="170" height="32" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/2/d22bd6b61cfe9bac92a09d502b26e826708ac485.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3364k^2 = 1\]" width="85" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc1186be01ed889bd6bb306f551a9d7e907e3b4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[k = \frac{1}{58}\]" width="54" height="37" />
</p><p>And since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/c/83c08a80deee0e47fdd1efe7a74969aec194df4b.png" class="latex" alt="$k = \frac{\sin(120^{\circ})}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="38" />, then:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/e/9de729d4c21452634a1487cc9c8d2be657eb826f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\cdot58\]" width="93" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/7463a44a8f2dc18d9790ab6a1fe5a44d031e0690.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = \boxed{29\sqrt{3}}\]" width="88" height="30" />
</p><p>Solution by IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/1/c21879320bb5751de9a8cae84a82cb98c87da2c4.png" class="latex" alt="$EZ = x$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/c/33c14a75756f538b12a80e6525d89906bc95bab0.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle XAB = \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="95" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/a/d3adf9d4c1f3a91f57032b2a241af8046c6a69f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAX = \angle EYZ$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/7/d2740a93f5df5ff3450bca40c70e4c84d0cd2111.png" class="latex" alt="$AX = YZ$" width="82" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/7194d28dd4f66a2d8f0dd3a89fa02d4046c6534c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZEY = \angle XBA$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/7/8671cc8a42c0827a1f8781bc331710c3ef129bdb.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAX \cong \triangle EYZ$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e24afba57b0d24dc12e5eeb38616737a3b08a9.png" class="latex" alt="$AAS$" width="39" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/9/2890ea97467887b89d47f4df721a27ca742aeb21.png" class="latex" alt="$BX = EZ = x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/b/d3bf2d92d0ff2000338fbee2e4ecf227e6078aa9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AXB = 180^\circ - 120^\circ - \alpha = 60^\circ - \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="294" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/d/e4dae505178deed226a468d60dc80d80a1ad625a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{XB}{\sin \angle XAB} = \frac{AX}{\sin \angle ABX} = \frac{AB}{\angle AXB}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="286" height="39" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47a82076682e0ec98017b3f64e81c0337d11bf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{\sin \alpha} = \frac{AX}{\sin 120^\circ} = \frac{40}{\sin (60^\circ - \alpha)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="256" height="44" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/7/4a72f4319d776bab9ec616a2a48db33487069bb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZAF = 120^\circ - 90^\circ - \alpha = 30^\circ - \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="281" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/8861d848bf38471572d5f46f927af884142c5783.png" class="latex" alt="$ZF = 41(\sqrt{3} - 1) - x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="174" height="24" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/6/9960f7e276adb12db4a68fa0e2343e24731089fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{ZF}{\sin \angle ZAF} = \frac{AZ}{ \sin \angle ZFA}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="194" height="39" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/8/7c82501b07ff713f71d25d5ce71bced7c719abe3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{41(\sqrt{3} - 1) - x}{\sin (30^\circ - \alpha)} = \frac{AZ}{ \sin 120^\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="215" height="48" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8ec23b001ede25f0f8e756fe64547815ea1232f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{\sin \alpha} = \frac{41(\sqrt{3} - 1) - x}{\sin (30^\circ - \alpha)}= \frac{40}{\sin(60^\circ - \alpha)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="316" height="48" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/9/6f9429a3a35766c28f3f52487823406857d2afd5.png" class="latex" alt="$40 \cdot \sin \alpha = x(\sin 60^\circ \cos \alpha - \cos 60^\circ \sin \alpha)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="328" height="20" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/e/13e5c713889104dcf105a41b3b1afadc8e95d23a.png" class="latex" alt="$x(\sin 30^\circ \cos \alpha - \cos 30^\circ \sin \alpha) = [41(\sqrt{3} - 1) - x] \sin \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="430" height="24" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p>By simplifying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> we get, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/6/0569f651b2b645f41caf02b909cc187e705c5925.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot x \cdot \cos \alpha - \frac{\sin \alpha}{2} \cdot x = 40 \cdot \sin \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="285" height="43" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b88ea3916e8089c7edbccc3ff91e58e01a6e2682.png" class="latex" alt="$(3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p>By simplifying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> we get, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/4/b241f300e8972cef28c2dd9ccc1e12d94207b80c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\cos \alpha}{2} \cdot x + \frac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \sin \alpha \cdot x = 41(\sqrt{3} - 1)\sin \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="376" height="43" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/4/104992efa4deaa9a2dfeeec9b331ad0bf628f80a.png" class="latex" alt="$(4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p>By <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef96ebd3a11a2b433b4aa9b31a072b4fc04cce6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3} \cdot (4) - (3)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="108" height="24" /> we get, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/4/2e420cb5b22c7378ff7efdd37729dbb1a8de0be8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2\sqrt{3} - 3 +1}{2} \cdot \sin \alpha \cdot x = [41(3-\sqrt{3}) - 40] \sin \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="378" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b752ba9e6faa8ac0ac451c795e7423f990e8dd6.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{3} - 1)x = 83 - 41\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="189" height="24" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea23d4dbd951b8383a04333d3e3475e2b175b6e8.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 21 \sqrt{3} - 20$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="119" height="21" />
</p><p>By the law of cosine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/f/1dff4c7e75b4459cb1a04be2d358fab95dd599a9.png" class="latex" alt="$AX = \sqrt{(21 \sqrt{3} - 20)^2 + 40^2 - 2 \cdot (21 \sqrt{3} - 20) 40 \cdot \cos 120^\circ} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 29 \sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="575" height="37" />
