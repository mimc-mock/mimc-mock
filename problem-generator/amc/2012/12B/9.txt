<p>It takes Clea 60 seconds to walk down an escalator when it is not moving, and 24 seconds when it is moving. How many seconds would it take Clea to ride the escalator down when she is not walking?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80d7c1af573197fcfe67ff334e6bec7a26baae8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 42\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 52$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>She walks at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> units per second to travel a distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/f/a1f68759bd936dcb6b6cfb4234e0a96cf43db62f.png" class="latex" alt="$vt=d$" width="50" height="12" />, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/9/be91a9b821d2d978b86da5e03e4cabe199735471.png" class="latex" alt="$60x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/3170598a9efce12716b6421bedb541f8838590aa.png" class="latex" alt="$24*(x+k)=y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is the speed of the escalator. Setting the two equations equal to each other, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f9d2fa333e9c3ebe5cfa67caf0232ded498b79c.png" class="latex" alt="$60x=24x+24k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="14" />, which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c1607c3af7d20d60fb0f67a893a0fe7f386d4c85.png" class="latex" alt="$k=1.5x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />. Now we divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/15788e198a53ced87268aea78fcde68a84de92c0.png" class="latex" alt="$1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="13" /> because you add the speed of the escalator but remove the walking, leaving the final answer that it takes to ride the escalator alone as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/0302cc646fc04cbb7ea3544dbe62ede815267593.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 40}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We write two equations using distance=rate * time. Let r be the rate she is walking, and e be the speed the escalator moves. WLOG, let the distance of the escalator be 120, as the distance is constant. Thus, our 2 equations are 120=60r and 120=24(r+e). Solving for e, we get e=3. Thus, it will take Clea 120/3 = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/0302cc646fc04cbb7ea3544dbe62ede815267593.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 40}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" /> seconds
</p><p>~coolmath2017
</p>
