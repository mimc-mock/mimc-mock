<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> be the sequence of real numbers defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/b/a0b6ceb87b15ac3c911f9c510607228963083a11.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=0.201,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="86" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/b/3cb4209d568b46fbe97940913d12cfb637b8627f.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=(0.2011)^{a_1},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/f/78fdcf4019c3cd1be24e85cf375113f93c314521.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=(0.20101)^{a_2},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/0/290dc3e820f2bd82a85bbb85201bdc7474a818c1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=(0.201011)^{a_3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="18" />, and in general, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/6/006ecf77f807aa89b66a8e2e9f281e579e7cd062.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_k=\begin{cases}(0.\underbrace{20101\cdots 0101}_{k+2\text{ digits}})^{a_{k-1}}\qquad\text{if }k\text{ is odd,}\\(0.\underbrace{20101\cdots 01011}_{k+2\text{ digits}})^{a_{k-1}}\qquad\text{if }k\text{ is even.}\end{cases}\]" width="353" height="85" />
</p><p>Rearranging the numbers in the sequence  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> in decreasing order produces a new sequence  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/6/8d66d40a30444f8ae9a83825e11bf2b209521f2e.png" class="latex" alt="$\{b_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="19" />.  What is the sum of all integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71d3552ede761f8ad3403c1f57d77eeeb3e036dd.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le k \le 2011$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="15" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/6/896f64b93a5121b3d5662f170924bbc366409bb8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k=b_k?$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/b/feb2bce2dfe6d7808eb889cbf248f36c1cc9418e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 671\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1006\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1341\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2011\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2012$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="475" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, we must understand two important functions: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/b/72b1bc89fda62367b5751b5da84a84427b829b21.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = b^x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/a/9cabde1685a6e1d28759e7155a6ac61afd3e7409.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; b &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />(decreasing exponential function), and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/4213e726791694044572bfd03e8afbc160fc793a.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x) = x^k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="20" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/a/6da5c768d3e309c1b5337c06bf5ad8df078c5c01.png" class="latex" alt="$k &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />(increasing exponential function for positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />). <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is used to establish inequalities when we change the <i>exponent</i> and keep the <i>base</i> constant. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> is used to establish inequalities when we change the <i>base</i> and keep the <i>exponent</i> constant.
</p><p>We will now examine the first few terms.
</p><p>Comparing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/d/eed842f7b67a87d316f9d6dc88db311ac0e9a037.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 = (0.201)^1 &lt; (0.201)^{a_1} &lt; (0.2011)^{a_1} = a_2 &lt; 1 \Rightarrow 0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="562" height="19" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d109f4a2a0b6160d1b9afe90e0acafc5267f19ba.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="124" height="15" />.
</p><p>Comparing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/6/b565fe90952c29c5009d379d2604a7e95b3f448e.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_3 = (0.20101)^{a_2} &lt; (0.20101)^{a_1} &lt; (0.2011)^{a_1} = a_2 &lt; 1 \Rightarrow 0 &lt; a_3 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="605" height="18" />.
</p><p>Comparing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d8573da61f9c2a0a5625697b825f084b4f85f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 = (0.201)^1 &lt; (0.201)^{a_2} &lt; (0.20101)^{a_2} = a_3 &lt; 1 \Rightarrow 0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_3 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="571" height="19" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/e/30e9fdc64275a5f823fde49b269fe9febc089bb0.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_3 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="165" height="15" />.
</p><p>Comparing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b65ebc3f438b176b4ca4a890799f2f72564af.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e28850813a5321d2a8ae3c0510457a7337975ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/1/4913dfe66b117bcceb6268263931df7496256b64.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_3 = (0.20101)^{a_2} &lt; (0.20101)^{a_3} &lt; (0.201011)^{a_3} = a_4 &lt; 1 \Rightarrow 0 &lt; a_3 &lt; a_4 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="624" height="18" />.
</p><p>Comparing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/2/5e28850813a5321d2a8ae3c0510457a7337975ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/e/4aed73017fa1e0bd3828219199326cef32862af5.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_4 = (0.201011)^{a_3} &lt; (0.201011)^{a_1} &lt; (0.2011)^{a_1} = a_2 &lt; 1 \Rightarrow 0 &lt; a_4 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="624" height="18" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/0/240a683079e53be7e74d39903b5ad4377711564e.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_3 &lt; a_4 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="206" height="15" />.
</p><p>Continuing in this manner, it is easy to see a pattern(see Note 1).
</p><p>Therefore, the only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6045dddd3ce9b6ca0c5b71d5818e66b6db41f66a.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k = b_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="15" /> is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/3/883807b888d0d32072e4eb449c6e19a60e429aee.png" class="latex" alt="$2(k-1006) = 2011 - k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="18" />. Solving gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/9944d3f96345f7107f4542272c849cd355f05935.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} 1341}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="76" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
<b>Note 1</b>:
We claim that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/8/408e16253719f6a4c4c7e05c55ee386b567492da.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_3 &lt; ... &lt; a_{2011} &lt; a_{2010} &lt; ... &lt; a_4 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="407" height="15" />.
</p><p>We can use induction to prove this statement. (not necessary for AMC):
</p><p>Base Case: We have already shown the base case above, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d109f4a2a0b6160d1b9afe90e0acafc5267f19ba.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; a_1 &lt; a_2 &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="124" height="15" />.
</p><p>Inductive Step: 
</p><p>Rearranging in decreasing order gives 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26bbe751aa3751ab38263d20f0781943e70ebed4.png" class="latex" alt="$1 &gt; b_1 = a_2 &gt; b_2 = a_4 &gt; ... &gt; b_{1005} = a_{2010} &gt; b_{1006} = a_{2011} &gt; ... &gt; b_{2010} = a_3 &gt; b_{2011} =  a_1 &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="724" height="15" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Start by looking at the first few terms and comparing them to each other. We can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/e/bfe569c0beabb06044562a1c0c763c52f646e6d9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 &lt; a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="12" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/3/143a851be8cbfd4af1b85ec6c58cd16b7bf5f760.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 &lt; a_3 &lt; a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="12" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/f/0af382ebb735e2145f858de892018f67e03a816b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 &lt; a_4 &lt; a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="12" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/6/d3624f9b4d602794693649bb55788f3dc9c64947.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 &lt; a_5 &lt; a_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="12" /> ... 
</p><p>From this, we find the pattern that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/d/8bdaa81850a56e45eeaa3ac2f1346b61a87e21c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{k-1} &lt; a_{k+1} &lt; a_k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="14" />.
</p><p>Examining this relationship, we see that every new number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/a/bea4d576937a04ec200b1037fc8ee33e8f744cb1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> will be between the previous two terms, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/5/6352edc6b969aefe0cbeb900b23a65ae60db8cd0.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{k-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="33" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/6/a366b8b3e317783a60eeca856e2efbe1f5ff7755.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{k-2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="33" height="10" />. Therefore, we can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is the smallest number, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is the largest number, and that all odd terms are less than even terms. Furthermore, we can see that for every odd k, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/d/f5d1e93b15ba08a5e607141e5331123db6bb6076.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k &lt; a_{k+2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="14" />, and for every even k, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/4/284f17bacef4b4a5ea621eab7e9a48a1f9745bd1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k &gt; a_{k+2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="14" />
</p><p>This means that rearranging the terms in descending order will first have all the even terms from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8137a36075138c56ed3372bb72ae789010ff391b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2012$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />, in that order, and then all odd terms from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/2/d32e0f9504d41fb3dfd963835b82c419244a18ac.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2011$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, in that order (so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/0/300aa8dd411175cba15b945e43dc2498f2f9023f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{b_k\}_{k=1}^{2011} = {a_2, a_4, a_6, ... a_{2008}, a_{2010}, a_{2011}, a_{2009}, ... a_5, a_3, a_1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="436" height="19" />).
</p><p>We can clearly see that there will be no solution k where k is even, as the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />th term in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> will appear in the same position in its sequence as the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8ea7ee8a282949fd853a01537ec7a2b55e56f75.png" class="latex" alt="$2k$" width="18" height="12" />th term does in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" />, where k is even. Therefore, we only have to look at the odd terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/a/bea4d576937a04ec200b1037fc8ee33e8f744cb1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, which occur in the latter part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/b/b6bdec4394870e8a713538ce428bea9d41c8775f.png" class="latex" alt="$b_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="15" />.
</p><p>Looking at the back of both sequences, we see that term k in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> progresses backwards in the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/f/f8f4e2de7a3a88832b95879443649595fb95c803.png" class="latex" alt="$2012 - k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="13" />, and that term k in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/6/e361a761d74597fa29719dd014ca09b504e8993f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_k\}_{k=1}^{2011}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="19" /> progresses backwards in the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/7/d9725e28ef5cf646a27665952959be316b1b4826.png" class="latex" alt="$2k - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />. Setting these two expressions equal to each other, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/2/5c2b5b001d120ed78f226ed5d50b1b1c06b490cc.png" class="latex" alt="$671$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
</p><p>However, remember that we started counting from the back of both sequences. So, plugging <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/2/5c2b5b001d120ed78f226ed5d50b1b1c06b490cc.png" class="latex" alt="$671$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> back into either side of the equation from earlier, we get our answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/4/9944d3f96345f7107f4542272c849cd355f05935.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} 1341}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="76" height="28" />.
</p><p>Sorry for the sloppy explanation. It's been two years since I've tried to give a solution to a problem, and this is the first time I've really used \LaTeX. But I think this solution takes a different approach than the one above. 
</p>
