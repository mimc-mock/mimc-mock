<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/2/7e2a5d813154aaa0b2a903e1e42a9cf1d29ada8a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=|2\{x\}-1|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/f/3df2919191b1220773d9b0142dec5c3528fd4c42.png" class="latex" alt="$\{x\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" /> denotes the fractional part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.  The number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the smallest positive integer such that the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0829c5e8bdf5deb5dd048f42edb1d47bbbf0b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[nf(xf(x))=x\]" width="115" height="18" /> has at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> real solutions.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?  <b>Note:</b> the fractional part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is a real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/f/6af59e169ad8775ca3a0870382ca7cd99e0d99fb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\{x\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/8/688897b728f5621c140b3ae0b033307fb216a6a5.png" class="latex" alt="$0\le y&lt;1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/643e1765342cf3807fd7d130ee679b71ecd4789b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" /> is an integer.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/d/cbd19cf538bf7aabdca500c87b488d9421355a71.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 31\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 32\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 62\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 64$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Our goal is to determine how many times the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/b/d3badb13f7cecaa617954a099b1de41d8b076973.png" class="latex" alt="$nf(xf(x))=x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" /> intersects the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. 
</p><p>We begin by analyzing the behavior of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/f/3df2919191b1220773d9b0142dec5c3528fd4c42.png" class="latex" alt="$\{x\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" />. It increases linearly with a slope of one, then when it reaches the next integer, it repeats itself. We can deduce that the function is like a sawtooth wave, with a period of one. We then analyze the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/2/7e2a5d813154aaa0b2a903e1e42a9cf1d29ada8a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=|2\{x\}-1|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="18" />. The slope of the teeth is multiplied by 2 to get 2, and the function is moved one unit downward. The function can then be described as starting at -1, moving upward with a slope of 2 to get to 1, and then repeating itself, still with a period of 1. The absolute value of the function is then taken. This results in all the negative segments becoming flipped in the Y direction. The positive slope starting at -1 of the function ranging from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1bdc7235fb3addb88885159d80367ff041fb52.png" class="latex" alt="$u.5$" width="24" height="12" />, where u is any arbitrary integer, is now a negative slope starting at positive 1.   The function now looks like the letter V repeated within every square in the first row. 
</p><p>It is now that we address the goal of this, which is to determine how many times the function intersects the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. Since there are two line segments per box, the function has two chances to intersect the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> for every integer. If the height of the function is higher than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> for every integer on an interval, then every chance within that interval intersects the line.  
</p><p>Returning to analyzing the function, we note that it is multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and then fed into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is a periodic function, we can model it as multiplying the function's frequency by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" /> chances for every integer, which is then multiplied by 2 once more to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> chances for every integer. The amplitude of this function is initially 1, and then it is multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, to give an amplitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. The function intersects the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> for every chance in the interval of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b1028b1d8d525f1a6676213503750e42e5ace29a.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq x \leq n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="78" height="15" />, since the function is n units high. The function ceases to intersect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/4/0e4719bfdfc165f8e7e05b58e0769180ae94ff3c.png" class="latex" alt="$n &lt; x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="45" height="10" />, since the height of the function is lower than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. 
</p><p>The number of times the function intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> is then therefore equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/061ea7a74409feea65a1fd2614545a8734b71203.png" class="latex" alt="$4+8+12...+4n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="137" height="14" />. We want this sum to be greater than 2012 which occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/3025565ffe4718f62b4e119b2cf941149b5ffd33.png" class="latex" alt="$n=32$" width="52" height="12" /> (C) .
</p>
