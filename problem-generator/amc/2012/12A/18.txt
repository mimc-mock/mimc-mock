<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Inscribe circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> inside triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> so that it meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. Note that angle bisectors of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> are concurrent at the center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />(also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" />) of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/7/5370af657e418f49527aba0013020a2a8d9ea710.png" class="latex" alt="$x=QB$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/3/ab3d8b4495b89063932f5b82777e2b11767614a1.png" class="latex" alt="$y=RC$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f69e8822d1736b6c6689e750becce0775629dd7.png" class="latex" alt="$z=AS$" width="59" height="13" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34a6d4513357f7796665a8d74f15c440cb32dbd9.png" class="latex" alt="$BR=x$" width="63" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/2/682366873566c0e908bd430da894af08a62d9fda.png" class="latex" alt="$SC=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/8/548dcca7291667544f1478de5a8a95e2c57b339a.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ=z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae77b5640fc063dcc7024ea4c8fea6f69ce03561.png" class="latex" alt="$x+z=27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2f1e88cb7d47305cfe18e39880ae12a1ff1b656.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/1/d2159866a273cae2ba1aecdad1287eda896f6490.png" class="latex" alt="$z+y=26$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" />. Subtracting the last 2 equations we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/8/418cb665dce60da031052d5f9557eaf4e6647f17.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="88" height="12" /> and adding this to the first equation we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638f55cd5570facc2150ef8519bc5d4244f0232e.png" class="latex" alt="$x=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. 
</p><p>By Heron's formula for the area of a triangle we have that the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/a/cfa958ebf8603bb8aa107af74e2ea43b88119330.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{39(14)(13)(12)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="133" height="32" />. On the other hand the area is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66993b9b79177ba70ab2ebcd4ab161dbd4680c86.png" class="latex" alt="$(1/2)25r+(1/2)26r+(1/2)27r$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="248" height="18" />.  Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/e/cae36839f956410e96154ee8069030da152d4e61.png" class="latex" alt="$39r=\sqrt{39(14)(13)(12)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="184" height="32" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bbc8d0be59d54e7bf4ddfb7174eae0757b33abf.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2=56$" width="58" height="15" />.
</p><p>Since the radius of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> is perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, we have by the pythagorean theorem <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/d/64d6920e355315bfc2b26e155befba0a218c5000.png" class="latex" alt="$BO^2=BI^2=r^2+x^2=56+169=225$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="315" height="16" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/164aface6e9aa3d69046c5bb606a4b9dd417f805.png" class="latex" alt="$BI=\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="114" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can use mass points and Stewart's to solve this problem. Because we are looking at the Incenter we then label <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> with a mass of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bda00d9a38a0c6bd9aac2512c6d065581882d43c.png" class="latex" alt="$26$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/1051e7d41ec0b8bd688a872be17538413f4ee97b.png" class="latex" alt="$27$" width="18" height="12" />. We also label where the angle bisectors intersect the opposite side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b798193cb94562989018728e1151587c618655b.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="21" height="16" /> correspondingly. It follows then that point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c71f19552a05ee5e2b31e94aff3fb986884bf31.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39;$" width="18" height="14" /> has mass <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1d1611143f5792dc0f61f3b130af88cb790c04.png" class="latex" alt="$52$" width="17" height="12" />. Which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/0/2e0e06feaa3c61ff1386e726362f646529472c84.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BB&#39;}$" width="34" height="15" /> is split into a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b3714d1977e2e6fa2c03536bd04eabab371fd18.png" class="latex" alt="$2:1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="12" /> ratio. We can then use Stewart's to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/0/2e0e06feaa3c61ff1386e726362f646529472c84.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BB&#39;}$" width="34" height="15" />. So we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/5/7751d4cb5008763f028286bdaaa7d8f6450849f9.png" class="latex" alt="$25^2\frac{27}{2} + 27^2\frac{25}{2} = \frac{25 \cdot 26 \cdot 27}{4} + 26\overline{BB&#39;}^2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="307" height="38" />. Solving we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f83dde8706315aaaaf40f3a365833e1bd94332b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BB&#39;} = \frac{45}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />. Plugging it in we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b08e4b75f7f40d81607b089613f9612e5a3aa0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BI} = 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="16" />. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a92034f19baa18c59040e94b8b4be67bfc4e0325.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />
</p><p>-Solution by <b>arowaaron</b>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can use POP(Power of a point) to solve this problem. First, notice that the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/f/2dfef85e2dc001d63ed78ee21aaf8cfc7fd13109.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{39(39 - 27)(39 - 26)(39 - 25)} = 78\sqrt{14}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="330" height="32" />. Therefore, using the formula that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/1/2d1ce49bc3eca615eb7b1cfaf9cb4f647d8a06fd.png" class="latex" alt="$sr = A$" width="55" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semi-perimeter and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the length of the inradius, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/6/8569d1bc5f90f183b6006f08b8741103678c08b9.png" class="latex" alt="$r = 2\sqrt{14}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Draw radii to the three tangents, and let the tangent hitting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c073f8a05257a19c2dacc368858763bb44fa3747.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />, the tangent hitting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40db9b6f10ff583906d78a06a76e08e04381bc1a.png" class="latex" alt="$T_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />, and the tangent hitting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0d8e9c5c70be5e8b812a08ce2566d0db946c608.png" class="latex" alt="$T_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/4/ae4cc3d8e65846116467c28a9bd6c2f66a97e0d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BI = x$" width="58" height="12" />. By the pythagorean theorem, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/f/d3fb1536787fea1e10fc1e549733fb6f8cd8da18.png" class="latex" alt="$BT_1 = \sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="130" height="19" />. By POP, we also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17f808ddafef2e8645e2018be137ce8a05613478.png" class="latex" alt="$BT_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="31" height="15" /> is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/0/810ee0182d6dc101bf300deab576e7477bb9e510.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" />. Because we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b847d386fdb5edd4aa5db4099188d78bd672481c.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 25$" width="70" height="12" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/0/9606358eb2ae94c1032ca05d7458a780549e6299.png" class="latex" alt="$CT_1 = 25 - \sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="170" height="19" />. We can rinse and repeat and find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/0/460ade9163f453c60d37e90016ec05e5fd8f38ab.png" class="latex" alt="$AT_2 = 26 - (25 - \sqrt{x^2 - 56}) = 1 + \sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="21" />. We can find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/3/7d350b6bbea06cbe0d7c6a67c4054542e7d3008b.png" class="latex" alt="$AT_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="30" height="15" /> by essentially coming in from the other way. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4ee915c9855b91c03364aa685038f2544081234a.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 27$" width="70" height="13" />, we also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/d/0cd4d78837080ce3ff8e4be28361e933d101b462.png" class="latex" alt="$AT_3 = 27 - \sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="170" height="19" />. By POP, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/b/d2b0d6f05fd0b69283c9a5281662c5b70987dfda.png" class="latex" alt="$AT_2 = AT_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/61527256318ea71e937b8a25bc00de2103f4bf67.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + \sqrt{x^2 - 56} = 27 - \sqrt{x^2 - 56}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="243" height="18" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/0/5c0db5afb87a96662788899bbeefdf0b5df13de6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^2 - 56} = A$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="111" height="18" />, for simplicity. We can change the equation into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/e/5fef6d1db61cbf2e2d8eeb6035103324f547e039.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + A = 27 - A$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="14" />, which we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/1/3510d0a7853181471a15212154c98ee672ad3018.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^2 - 56} = 13$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="115" height="18" />, which further implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff5a156dfe1bdc788767a466c6355f4c6bead3b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 56 = 169$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="15" />. After simplifying, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/5/2e5c79cf783f843871b91eb95490c92c7e3d2bf9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = 225$" width="68" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/c/32c2e454e6d63380779ea580fb1f5678f4ee5520.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 15}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="100" height="28" />
</p><p>~EricShi1685
