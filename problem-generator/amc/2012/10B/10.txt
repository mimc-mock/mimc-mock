<p>How many ordered pairs of positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/a/71abd51d62c9e3886c038614d6a51556f82c9bb3.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> satisfy the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/3/5e3b4d261e3fa4868a5e3c0ed459e38b952b8b58.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{M}{6}=\frac{6}{N}?$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="79" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a7963579276015fe6bd0337f045c9d028e3d4ed7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Cross-multiplying gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/c/d5c3e4a3afa478ad680884293c67c19c8ca0fcf8.png" class="latex" alt="$MN=36.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="14" /> We write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> as a product of two positive integers:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/5/e55e9c6db5b42335a7772c39cb285a02365c46d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 36 &amp;= 1\cdot36 \\ &amp;= 2\cdot18 \\ &amp;= 3\cdot12 \\ &amp;= 4\cdot9 \\ &amp;= 6\cdot6. \end{align*}" width="81" height="111" />
The products <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efac44cee5fc8c5bd787cbec1673f1a6acafe75b.png" class="latex" alt="$1\cdot36, 2\cdot18, 3\cdot12,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/6439c420869bc9764e05366901d08181fe7996ec.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="14" /> each produce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/2/5d2538faf9fc48e716eb88737324848904f122bc.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N),$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="20" /> as we can switch the order of the factors. The product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/a/10a0fc9cd2a650a60b442c3e62f9185bc4403e87.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot6$" width="30" height="12" /> produces <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/4/bd47639c4d3ed21ad04abda0612bc1ecbdba161f.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="20" /> Together, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/f/2bf87782564ebd3abf4c20c421d4592c6762c037.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot2+1=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 9}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="144" height="30" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/4/bd47639c4d3ed21ad04abda0612bc1ecbdba161f.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="20" />
</p><p>~Rguan (Solution)
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM (Reformatting)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Cross-multiplying gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/c/d5c3e4a3afa478ad680884293c67c19c8ca0fcf8.png" class="latex" alt="$MN=36.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="14" /> From the prime factorization <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c16e5afd9ff5b97f2b78e3321fe8f065575f468a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[36=2^2\cdot3^2,\]" width="92" height="19" /> we conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b0893bb0f2b2881b925f3493aa838d25d220967.png" class="latex" alt="$(2+1)(2+1)=9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="18" /> positive divisors. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/a/52a0c0a33e764fdc62793675f3974a6f8f9c5ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$M,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> and each value generates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/4/bd47639c4d3ed21ad04abda0612bc1ecbdba161f.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="20" /> So, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/0/8d008d8882245f5f2bfca59a6cf940dd1e4c29fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/a/71abd51d62c9e3886c038614d6a51556f82c9bb3.png" class="latex" alt="$(M,N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> in total.
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
