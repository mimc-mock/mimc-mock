<p>Mary divides a circle into 12 sectors. The central angles of these sectors, measured in degrees, are all integers and they form an arithmetic sequence. What is the degree measure of the smallest possible sector angle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/3/c837f10a9932179d69e301612618e0a88d7f6a36.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> be the first term of the arithmetic progression and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6bfb1f0a208187bed5a389462b7e45bb2a04c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{12}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="10" /> be the last term of the arithmetic progression. From the formula of the sum of an arithmetic progression (or arithmetic series), we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/a/d3a6e88f46c2feceb0eccd33c7fce69bf3aac77e.png" class="latex" alt="$12*\frac{a_1+a_{12}}{2}=360$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="156" height="37" />, which leads us to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/1/9b1885e1c9be7327ab3644ab9b5a9189d591658d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + a_{12} = 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="17" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6bfb1f0a208187bed5a389462b7e45bb2a04c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{12}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="10" />, the largest term of the progression, can also be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/3/403e845608bc3b044fa819f113cc1add341e5bf2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1+11d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="17" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is the common difference. Since each angle measure must be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> must also be an integer. We can isolate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> by subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6bfb1f0a208187bed5a389462b7e45bb2a04c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{12}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="10" /> like so: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/9/da9a62c8c8d9b610d796b50fa631c761a5148404.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{12}-a_1=a_1+11d-a_1=11d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="17" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is an integer, the difference between the first and last terms, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e3dd6a74464ce96e7accb778c167a15fe8053b4.png" class="latex" alt="$11d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="29" height="14" />, must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/7/4e76ce9da0f428536412a4f539b127c608760686.png" class="latex" alt="$11.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> Since the total difference must be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, we can start checking multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> for the total difference between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6bfb1f0a208187bed5a389462b7e45bb2a04c9.png" class="latex" alt="$a_{12}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="10" />. We start with the largest multiple, because the maximum difference will result in the minimum value of the first term. If  the difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/7/d07e059b653131aed67c486684a4e380b6901879.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\frac{60-55}{2}=2.5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="153" height="38" />, which is not an integer, nor is it one of the five options given. If the difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b45828f292e161290be6ee62bc4c9df081ee1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/7/71799ffc8396d46ae71b3dc39dfbbd307ae1f7ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\frac{60-44}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="106" height="38" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/0/72018c4224b234db20bdd33a4343170ee495ae69.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p><p>-Solution by Rhiju
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the smallest sector angle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the difference between consecutive sector angles, then we have the angles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a52ef0ae215b7da2adc0758b72a98330c1fbde03.png" class="latex" alt="$a, a+r, a+2r, \cdots. a+11r$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="212" height="15" />. Use the formula for the sum of an arithmetic sequence and set it equal to 360, the number of degrees in a circle.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/e/08e10a9a8484e15a15c178c87575426de948bbeb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{a+a+11r}{2}\cdot 12 &amp;= 360\\ 2a+11r &amp;= 60\\ a &amp;= \frac{60-11r}{2} \end{align*}" width="218" height="106" />
</p><p>All sector angles are integers so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must be a multiple of 2. Plug in even integers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> starting from 2 to minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45ef9cdc7c7d46920eac603663914e0d9261a99b.png" class="latex" alt="$a.$" width="13" height="8" /> We find this value to be 4 and the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/f/6ff6e946e8ffeaf35460957d6c09d85872c088f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60-11(4)}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="165" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Starting with the smallest term, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/f/5ff96fab3a3a69384c1f9aae3c299300bddf34db.png" class="latex" alt="$a - 5x \cdots a, a + x \cdots a + 6x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="216" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sixth term and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the difference. The sum becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/6/37697edb25234fe7ffd0dab75fe8263ebc7a65ab.png" class="latex" alt="$12a + 6x = 360$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="14" /> since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/2/c62a47cbc153efdf4611d8660429ea879be3f192.png" class="latex" alt="$360$" width="26" height="12" /> degrees in the central angle of the circle. The only condition left is that the smallest term in greater than zero. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d77f202da4f5b40b593c468a6ddea362c8f7b649.png" class="latex" alt="$a - 5x &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="84" height="13" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/3/fa34571c4e9803ae208a03bb6bf462408bd467c9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2a + x = 60\]" width="92" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b39616c4f7a2b6d6f404bc6251339957ffeb6fd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 60 - 2a\]" width="93" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5eeac67c598ae08e1eba7dfd1546a278d04cdf9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a - 5(60 - 2a) &gt; 0\]" width="146" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/4157db277d2040a3bba0b7378e1bf2b55445747b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[11a &gt; 300\]" width="77" height="13" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is an integer, it must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" />, and therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/5/83599957120ac9ad3c9d455d9e768b73150cde29.png" class="latex" alt="$a - 5x$" width="51" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/0/72018c4224b234db20bdd33a4343170ee495ae69.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
