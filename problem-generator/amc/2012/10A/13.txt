<p>An <i>iterative average</i> of the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 is computed the following way. Arrange the five numbers in some order. Find the mean of the first two numbers, then find the mean of that with the third number, then the mean of that with the fourth number, and finally the mean of that with the fifth number. What is the difference between the largest and smallest possible values that can be obtained using this procedure?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/3/133c7237aeae5b649a2eb53f64afdd08fc18567f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{31}{16}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{17}{8}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{65}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="387" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The iterative average of any 5 integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/5/5753734e29398cf1e59cb67a78e86180c7499f2d.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d,e$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> is defined as:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/3/d130cad96eb01c77fec131093efc79459847ec04.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\frac{\frac{\frac{a+b} 2+c} 2+d} 2+e} 2=\frac{a+b+2c+4d+8e}{16}\]" width="282" height="54" />
</p><p><br />
Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8480bbc71e3fd12e3f8b749c49c06897ee7bb0.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,4,5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/5/5753734e29398cf1e59cb67a78e86180c7499f2d.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d,e$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />, we see that in order to maximize the fraction,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/3855e463befeecdc97c46ec4186f828daa743d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1,b=2,c=3,d=4,e=5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="241" height="16" />,
</p><p>and in order to minimize the fraction,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/5/425897353a8a9d4981c5ba1c1ffcc6b510905865.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5,b=4,c=3,d=2,e=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="241" height="16" />.
</p><p>After plugging in these values and finding the positive difference of the two fractions, we arrive with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/c/25cca3596677b780719b24d18984a52364c286ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{34}{16} \Rightarrow \frac{17}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="71" height="38" />, which is our answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The minimum and maximum can be achieved with the orders <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/a/44a48616d7b52b8924453836b8da860ddae51f0f.png" class="latex" alt="$5, 4, 3, 2, 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/a/9faa9b63684d102b90b612f4f964224e5437ce30.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4, 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> respectively. We can see this because the iterative average is like a weighted average that gives more weight to later numbers. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/7/7775be0a0562c74aacedec26bbc5df3b2fe42e76.png" class="latex" alt="$5,4,3,2,1 \Rightarrow \frac92,3,2,1 \Rightarrow \frac{15}{4},2,1 \Rightarrow \frac{23}{8},1 \Rightarrow \frac{31}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="372" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/7/357fa3359ecf98e897c89d4bd93cd1e0c8d279e7.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,4,5 \Rightarrow \frac32,3,4,5 \Rightarrow \frac94,4,5 \Rightarrow \frac{25}{8},5 \Rightarrow \frac{65}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="362" height="38" />
</p><p>The difference between the two is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/f/bdf40914bebdc3777cc76f80ca76d059b46b78d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{65}{16}-\frac{31}{16}=\frac{34}{16}=\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{17}{8}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="205" height="48" />.
</p>
