<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be positive integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/c/3cc8cc93008cbed8c1d66f73bc5b60e091a259e7.png" class="latex" alt="$a\ge$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/8/ba8603b617f2d4e7747726ac7ea4a30fe83ae386.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ge$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="26" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59f076db4810c28be400a33f0f9b718ca619a25a.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-b^2-c^2+ab=2011$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="191" height="16" /> and
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/8/c7802a04369d835d65d4f61a570dc67fc0c46cb5.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2+3b^2+3c^2-3ab-2ac-2bc=-1997$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="329" height="16" />.
</p><p>What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58ca2a614f56739a2ac8f6ccd631a7abc6367071.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 249\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 250\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 251\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 252\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 253$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Add the two equations.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd9d43f43c0fa1a89952cdb4e8ff5e85bfeb4d7a.png" class="latex" alt="$2a^2 + 2b^2 + 2c^2 - 2ab - 2ac - 2bc = 14$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="306" height="16" />.
</p><p>Now, this can be rearranged and factored.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/2/b42b35cdc15956ebe7972685f0448cb294567cde.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2 - 2ab + b^2) + (a^2 - 2ac + c^2) + (b^2 - 2bc + c^2) = 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="435" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e907a8e3ee6090f691dfec6d95235312813051.png" class="latex" alt="$(a - b)^2 + (a - c)^2 + (b - c)^2 = 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="268" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are all integers, so the three terms on the left side of the equation must all be perfect squares.
We see that the only is possibility is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ff1ee3a89746999253f175b83695092732ff36.png" class="latex" alt="$14 = 9 + 4 + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/c/a0c6f9aa9aa526d1fd83f173e83650f70727180f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-c)^2 = 9 \Rightarrow a-c = 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="19" />, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/7/567728f76b5bf29f2541d7e02f007e3aafa1abf4.png" class="latex" alt="$a-c$" width="40" height="8" /> is the biggest difference. It is impossible to determine by inspection whether <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2ea7581c87e299152333019e7f838260fcefbe2.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, or whether <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/7/0b7961ec9651359974877e7005fb055cf33639d9.png" class="latex" alt="$b-c = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>We want to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, so take the two cases and solve them each for an expression in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. Our two cases are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2ca55364dc9c85f40625a7848d8b41fdb4070d0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b, c) = (a, a-1, a-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="200" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/0/1505899f682829029eea405f4e42cf3e64590002.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, a-2, a-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" />. Plug these values into one of the original equations to see if we can get an integer for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90fed632dfe07112c3ebf32499663f6632015ae4.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - (a-1)^2 - (a-3)^2 + a(a-1) = 2011$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="332" height="19" />, after some algebra, simplifies to
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/6/006457c43c1985537d5da9addc9a67cf807d81cb.png" class="latex" alt="$7a = 2021$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="78" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/9/e99b9c3842e40e4102ae86b3317f26103c80cdb5.png" class="latex" alt="$2021$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is not an integer. 
</p><p>The other case gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/1/911ff9ca9536b5a04c190a874b3d07585e4717da.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - (a-2)^2 - (a-3)^2 + a(a-2) = 2011$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="332" height="19" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/d/d8d2c79ed1e6eaa031e132c56ab27622b72f6175.png" class="latex" alt="$8a = 2024$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c5624cb7e13bdaaa9874243a116a6d722ba7d460.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 253$" width="60" height="12" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7be7d31566620270833927f0dab6b55567001b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 253}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />.
</p>
