<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Draw the hexagon between the centers of the circles, and compute its area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd9eaf7503b08a9288f607dc9f040dbfddf31b1e.png" class="latex" alt="$(6)(0.5)(2\sqrt{3})=6\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="21" />. Then add the areas of the three sectors outside the hexagon (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />) and subtract the areas of the three sectors inside the hexagon but outside the figure(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />) to get the area enclosed in the curved figure <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc4c898f84ea96df996d2fcda9fa3f8bcc28f41.png" class="latex" alt="$(\pi+6\sqrt{3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="21" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/4/a04defad649f5e80f180b51122b67a7f9559242f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ \pi+6\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="111" height="33" />.
