<p>Externally tangent circles with centers at points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> have radii of lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, respectively. A line externally tangent to both circles intersects ray <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/3/3f301752be8b1fd8bc32ce7a9c5051172352b5e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4.8\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 10.2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 14.4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="418" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/a/c3a9d5ccbf12a08c74fe1ebfe6d2f966349b51f6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3.5mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair A=(0,0), B=(8,0); pair C=(20,0); pair D=(1.25,-0.25sqrt(375)); pair E=(8.75,-0.15sqrt(375)); path a=Circle(A,5); path b=Circle(B,3); draw(a); draw(b); draw(C--D); draw(A--C); draw(A--D); draw(B--E);  pair[] ps={A,B,C,D,E}; dot(ps);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,N); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,N); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,SE); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,SE); label(&quot;$5$&quot;,(A--D),SW); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,(B--E),SW); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,(A--B),N); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(C--B),N);  [/asy]" width="425" height="182" /></center>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the points of tangency on circles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> with line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/ccc55f85ae3c39ddd90ace7764a57bce879351c2.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=8$" width="61" height="13" />. Also, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/e/7ce3e753b80409dd0bc8efe53bef3268617483a1.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=x$" width="63" height="12" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28b8d709d3b873ec6db017839b1520919db39db.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADC$" width="56" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b1cee5c0355428ff8d477ecbf406f0f99bcca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BEC$" width="56" height="12" /> are right angles (a radius is perpendicular to a tangent line at the point of tangency) and both triangles share <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55b591cd4cbc3cd3d8746972dae656279220ce9a.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACD$" width="56" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/643832f0293ccc7386fa685a8090543c81ad9213.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADC \sim \triangle BEC$" width="143" height="13" />. From this we can get a proportion.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08fe9f512c9c8f0c3c4efd46885702d9863f4f90.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BC}{AC}=\frac{BE}{AD} \rightarrow \frac{x}{x+8}=\frac{3}{5} \rightarrow 5x=3x+24 \rightarrow x=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="461" height="39" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
