<p>In a bag of marbles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the marbles are blue and the rest are red. If the number of red marbles is doubled and the number of blue marbles stays the same, what fraction of the marbles will be red?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5738925616c839d4f4fb65f07d173838053796.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{2}{5}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{3}{7}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{4}{7}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{3}{5}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="367" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Assume that there are 5 total marbles in the bag. The actual number does not matter, since all we care about is the ratios, and the only operation performed on the marbles in the bag is doubling.
</p><p>There are 3 blue marbles in the bag and 2 red marbles. If you double the amount of red marbles, there will still be 3 blue marbles but now there will be 4 red marbles. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/2/042933304954135ca2ca571ea8b541ec9f56f043.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{4}{7}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let us say that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> marbles in the bag. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/5/ad56bb341dfd47b5d6e60040d6d7b37e2652cd3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3x}{5x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="21" height="37" /> are blue, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/4/0d4ff0a9dcd87fd72c98682c0c57563c255b9ac6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2x}{5x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="21" height="37" /> are red. When the red marbles are doubled, we now have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6738051db3f3f773bcdf378960b4553e302957a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2*2x}{5x+2x} = \frac{4x}{7x} = \frac{4}{7} \Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="218" height="38" />
</p>
