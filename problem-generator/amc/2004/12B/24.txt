<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/7/fb71d5ba363a314c0343efdd9a19aafe8070c75e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = BC$" width="81" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/4373e7b4be983c0315e82e1fda1df66ed34dd7fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BD}$" width="30" height="15" /> is an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Altitude" title="Altitude">altitude</a>. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is on the extension of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/4/964e41f9f4305229d611f6c703884f582d55b583.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />. The values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/1141e0e19e2f7c726f31e7a2127e366ccc09d21f.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan \angle CBE$" width="85" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e29c29bba6d234a46751df57afeb94662ac2c907.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan \angle DBE$" width="86" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/6/ff630bd1cb6fb35cb8fc6378c6fcb04b3d653419.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan \angle ABE$" width="84" height="13" /> form a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Geometric progression">geometric progression</a>, and the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b7c1e137ad4e9819526d4f2dd2e5151de1a0fb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\cot \angle DBE,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40dd8c4a73a30a499798e6aa9964af4ecd74931c.png" class="latex" alt="$\cot \angle CBE,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/e/b2e68de24bfe54a0f4c2781a0758745306471bd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\cot \angle DBC$" width="84" height="12" /> form an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Arithmetic progression">arithmetic progression</a>. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5ae6e04f0710cb08f0e10ffc47a0f257844910e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(120); defaultpen(0.7); pair A = (0,0), D = (5*2^.5/3,0), C = (10*2^.5/3,0), B = (5*2^.5/3,5*2^.5), E = (13*2^.5/3,0); draw(A--D--C--E--B--C--D--B--cycle); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,N); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,S); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,E,S); [/asy]" width="158" height="202" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77ce14100334e6f91a3e43a8e32b448308bfe988.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 16 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac {50}3 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 10\sqrt{3} \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 8\sqrt{5} \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="430" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/0/b203ea77294a781ab099447ff305cdced1df9318.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha = DBC$" width="80" height="12" />. Then the first condition tells us that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f43efdcb033513acd38738c89f90c9c23fb85ba3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\tan^2 DBE = \tan(DBE - \alpha)\tan(DBE + \alpha) = \frac {\tan^2 DBE - \tan^2 \alpha}{1 - \tan ^2 DBE \tan^2 \alpha},\]" width="527" height="39" />
and multiplying out gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b91cd9a4d21e10b87ea0fa1cda103171a18ac693.png" class="latex" alt="$(\tan^4 DBE - 1) \tan^2 \alpha = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="211" height="19" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/c/44c299b099ec383dd19f7b2dd56e78f9307c0be8.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\alpha \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="17" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/c/30cd5ae9d923c32e88a18a763c877d62bb2094cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan^4 DBE = 1 \Longrightarrow \angle DBE = 45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="260" height="15" />. 
</p><p>The second condition tells us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/b/9ab9540e524e0fe398b07d8fa304a54072bd47f0.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cot (45 - \alpha) = 1 + \cot \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="18" />. Expanding, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b767460b7483cbf73ecb3c2300510a7b60ea87.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + \cot \alpha = 2\left[\frac {\cot \alpha + 1}{\cot \alpha - 1}\right] \Longrightarrow (\cot \alpha - 3)(\cot \alpha + 1) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="440" height="43" />. Evidently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b631f536433aaa9f46e8605f8c6330f6d62ef38.png" class="latex" alt="$\cot \alpha \neq - 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="17" />, so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a060055ae99f128da42264dd87f4f82ce0da96.png" class="latex" alt="$\cot \alpha = 3$" width="71" height="12" />.
</p><p>Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/8/ee84e947d3985aa96e2e9a185a95ead25f83f4c7.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = 5\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/1/661a8dffb89c5559f5e74bf35c3efabd312abb2f.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = \frac {2BD} {\cot \alpha} = \frac {10\sqrt {2}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="164" height="41" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b077d0451ba673b17b24b6f4fe9a43f1bf374f32.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABC] = \frac {1}{2} \cdot 5\sqrt {2} \cdot \frac {10\sqrt {2}}{3} = \frac {50}{3}\ \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="273" height="41" />.
</p>
