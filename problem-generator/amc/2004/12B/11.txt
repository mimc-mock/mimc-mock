<p>All the students in an algebra class took a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />-point test. Five students scored <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, each student scored at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, and the mean score was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d5db6ea360f9b1367e4db85c18474906401e2c0.png" class="latex" alt="$76$" width="17" height="12" />. What is the smallest possible number of students in the class?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/3/b2363beeebcf30eb849740829032325930f19330.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 10 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 11 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 12 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 13 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="388" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the number of students be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/a/5aaffb95eba509c4f82d252b636f1d3348322fac.png" class="latex" alt="$n\geq 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />. Then the sum of their scores is at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/e/5aed16b352300a80058a8b8b420a48af06450149.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 100 + (n-5)\cdot 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="158" height="18" />. At the same time, we need to achieve the mean <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d5db6ea360f9b1367e4db85c18474906401e2c0.png" class="latex" alt="$76$" width="17" height="12" />, which is equivalent to achieving the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/e/14e3e8570896123f0067368b0e7b50dc04f759da.png" class="latex" alt="$76n$" width="28" height="12" />.
</p><p>Hence we get a necessary condition on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />: we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/a/3caea267fda6ddf6f91f71c47c6fec0b9df90feb.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 100 + (n-5)\cdot 60 \leq 76n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="212" height="18" />.
This can be simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/3/043317e85ca4e209145347d6f521644fba7d28e7.png" class="latex" alt="$200 \leq 16n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="80" height="15" />. The smallest integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which this is true is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608f4cbd198d2af6bf20b99fc5e126469e09ec84.png" class="latex" alt="$n=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />.
</p><p>To finish our solution, we now need to find one way how <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> students could have scored on the test. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/9/c69e7dc922368653abc1a0cbe2c3a00c45bc7f93.png" class="latex" alt="$13\cdot 76 = 988$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> points to divide among them. The five <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />s make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" />, hence we must divide the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/6/6369d93b89f4d3a2840aa34fcf324eff704c4ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$488$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> points among the other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> students. This can be done e.g. by giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/866b44935f69a5b800547127fe13210bb1e36fbc.png" class="latex" alt="$61$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> points to each of them. 
</p><p>Hence the smallest possible number of students is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/cca0404e316e5394ca12e225827687d529bc4454.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="29" />.
</p>
