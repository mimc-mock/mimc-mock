<p>A function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbea845ae525c09563fc253c515753423c50962b.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z) = i\overline{z}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/75952e52a3dc061d24146c0df9062a54a5ce5a5e.png" class="latex" alt="$i=\sqrt{-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b8023032c1d94aa923383802ca03df3b4315df.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{z}$" width="9" height="11" /> is the complex conjugate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. How many values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> satisfy both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/7/ac79adbf9c00c9d3488c9cf9b2f0ee9510de9676.png" class="latex" alt="$|z| = 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/4/9f43355c31544cdd37ec99251612acb0a7dc9ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z) = z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/e/a8eb5f691ed381ffacd39e4cb130e6db75b30e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 0 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 1 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 2  \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 4 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/f/d5fdcd6e4bbde5f0a3289c503472267ec35d37e0.png" class="latex" alt="$z = a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="79" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/a/3fad1c419964ceed35955d9af14dd0dc0307271a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{z} = a-bi$" width="79" height="12" />. By definition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/4/814037a724bc0963079ba5e0e6b24b209fa9cfe1.png" class="latex" alt="$z = a+bi = f(z) = i(a-bi) = b+ai$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="297" height="18" />, which implies that all solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/4/9f43355c31544cdd37ec99251612acb0a7dc9ff9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z) = z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> lie on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> on the complex plane. The graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/7/ac79adbf9c00c9d3488c9cf9b2f0ee9510de9676.png" class="latex" alt="$|z| = 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">circle</a> centered at the origin, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/3/8535ab671e44b3ef949b43df5639a2670b46ad01.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" /> intersections.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b245331cc3cccf7ccaf7e2f6a34fa2131b8b33.png" class="latex" alt="$z=a+bi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="79" height="14" />, like above. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/f/5ef5ea4d936cdff3e1c619a31516734497c3c830.png" class="latex" alt="$z = a+bi = i\overline{z} = i(a-bi) = ai+b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="280" height="18" />. We move some terms around to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/4/82428359dd579e006476a5b9fcf5142df2cec8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$bi-b = ai-a$" width="116" height="12" />. We factor: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/b/12b8963f553c911721a6ed3b6000dfca0173b4d6.png" class="latex" alt="$b(i-1) = a(i-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />. We divide out the common factor to see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/1/2510c0ee8fa65393d9a6689e806595cd57234ea2.png" class="latex" alt="$b = a$" width="41" height="12" />. Next we put this into the definition of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/e/43e129f9bd77867d1af2074fd87c28b404cf46a4.png" class="latex" alt="$|z| = a^2 + b^2 = a^2 + a^2 = 2a^2 = 25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="19" />. Finally, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/8/728e77a1880a69b47b398ae495da1fb742f36865.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \pm\sqrt{\frac{25}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="88" height="43" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> has two solutions.
</p>
