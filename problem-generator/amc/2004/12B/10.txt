<p>An annulus is the region between two concentric circles. The concentric circles in the figure have radii <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbd3932fd8be042b3b42f1e4bd56fbd1902b1c3f.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/9/4a903f7c677cbaadb6e72741b95730648ab1d442.png" class="latex" alt="$OX$" width="30" height="12" /> be a radius of the larger circle, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/b/3bb2419be49ff83cbed7db9a2f366858ccabf2e5.png" class="latex" alt="$XZ$" width="30" height="12" /> be tangent to the smaller circle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/f/8afe773cd5709ee6b1f9f84680829802825cfab9.png" class="latex" alt="$OY$" width="28" height="12" /> be the radius of the larger circle that contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/866d92a48eb831ff5c70b73e13313adfdbeb7ce2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=XZ$" width="64" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/a/8ba9c97c3ba09034c27152dc55f71b0534b17e36.png" class="latex" alt="$d=YZ$" width="62" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7ae3f367a4080971e73933e8903ddd7e2ea5aee.png" class="latex" alt="$e=XY$" width="63" height="12" />. What is the area of the annulus? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/a/0ca2ea60f3f8c72bd6e0bb1dd3335aaf69a85ba9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.5cm); defaultpen(0.8); real r1=1.5, r2=2.5; pair O=(0,0); path inner=Circle(O,r1), outer=Circle(O,r2); pair Y=(0,r2), Z=(0,r1), X=intersectionpoint( Z--(Z+(10,0)), outer ); filldraw(outer,lightgray,black); filldraw(inner,white,black); draw(X--O--Y); draw(Y--X--Z); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,SW); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,X,E); label(&quot;$Y$&quot;,Y,N); label(&quot;$Z$&quot;,Z,SW); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,X--Z,N); label(&quot;$b$&quot;,0.25*X,SE); label(&quot;$c$&quot;,O--Z,E); label(&quot;$d$&quot;,Y--Z,W); label(&quot;$e$&quot;,Y*0.65 + X*0.35,SW); defaultpen(0.5); dot(O); dot(X); dot(Z); dot(Y); [/asy]" width="358" height="378" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/e/cdec9c388f68af76ed27a201f7be85c80df158ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \pi a^2 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \pi b^2 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \pi c^2 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \pi d^2 \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \pi e^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="431" height="19" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The area of the large circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07ff2e57aac6f778fd2b55ef4a84e2146100ce21.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi b^2$" width="25" height="15" />, the area of the small one is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/8/d5886c620e20256c8c7affa32f8553c878a02330.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi c^2$" width="25" height="15" />, hence the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/1/b11eb915d77533ba2fa67e0387ecfc80d81f4def.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi(b^2-c^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="19" />.
</p><p>From the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> for the right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/5/985ae45cc9db06e55d850b8f8c045fd834ad202a.png" class="latex" alt="$OXZ$" width="44" height="12" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969a16c3e4948fe6fb474d0a1b5bd5758b5d8fc9.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + c^2 = b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4acf77bc9317800efa3fe0859fdbf21bd0a368c2.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2-c^2=a^2$" width="92" height="15" /> and thus the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/c/52c356cd9aefe57dc7c0a323ce7ac58733b2cac9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(A)\ }\pi a^2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="31" />.
</p>
