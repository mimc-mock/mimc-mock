<p>A truncated cone has horizontal bases with radii <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. A sphere is tangent to the top, bottom, and lateral surface of the truncated cone. What is the radius of the sphere? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a394e5e5873a57c973dc4eac1dc7523a33e730.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 6 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 4\sqrt{5} \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 9 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 10 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 6\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="400" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Consider a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Trapezoid" title="Trapezoid">trapezoid</a> (label it <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> as follows) cross-section of the truncate cone along a diameter of the bases:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79b290d343dc7c6049f7d45f5f1abc749c2af3d2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import olympiad; size(220); defaultpen(0.7); pair A = (0,0), B = (36,0), C = (20,12), D = (16,12), E=(A+B)/2, F=(20+1.6,12-1.2), G = (C+D)/2; draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(circumcircle(E,F,G)); dot(E); dot(F); dot(G); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;\(F\)&quot;,F,NE); label(&quot;\(G\)&quot;,G,N); [/asy]" width="368" height="162" /></center>
<p>Above, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/15702fb3f7b1aeaefff24913baf2a387bdd2d54a.png" class="latex" alt="$E,F,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> are points of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Tangent_(geometry)" title="Tangent (geometry)">tangency</a>. By the Two Tangent Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/6/ac620755efb30ffff0ad4de5c51d4c6f7516a0ec.png" class="latex" alt="$BF = BE = 18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="124" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/6/ca669f780faf25a2a5171adba0031242c5509f4c.png" class="latex" alt="$CF = CG = 2$" width="114" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3df80c6052a2128b30eb8a39a49d8ed45409800.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 20$" width="70" height="12" />. We draw <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> such that it is the foot of the altitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/e/9be32165967b3173aa255274dec44b971d06e9cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{HD}$" width="32" height="15" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />: 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/5/df55cbef93e6c3485f8c0d77176293f0fe2bcb3a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import olympiad; size(220); defaultpen(0.7); pair A = (0,0), B = (36,0), C = (20,12), D = (16,12), E=(A+B)/2, F=(20+1.6,12-1.2), G = (C+D)/2, H=(16,0); pair P=(D+G)/2, Q=(D+H)/2, R=(B+E)/2, T=(A+H)/2, O=(E+G)/2; draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(G--E--H--D); draw(circumcircle(E,F,G)); dot(E);dot(F);dot(G);dot(H);dot(O); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;\(F\)&quot;,F,NE); label(&quot;\(G\)&quot;,G,N); label(&quot;\(H\)&quot;,H,S); label(&quot;\(O\)&quot;,O,NE); label(&quot;\(2\)&quot;,P,N); label(&quot;\(12\)&quot;,Q,W); label(&quot;\(18\)&quot;,R,S); label(&quot;\(16\)&quot;,T,S); label(&quot;\(20\)&quot;,(A+D)/2,NW); label(&quot;\(r\)&quot;,(O+E)/2,SE); [/asy]" width="368" height="162" /></center>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/3/df3b7879ecc2ac8d3fff4c2b6c95853efdf10988.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r = \frac{DH}2 = \frac{\sqrt{20^2 - 16^2}}2 = \boxed{6} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}.\]" width="289" height="41" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Create a trapezoid with inscribed circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> exactly like in Solution #1, and extend lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> from the solution above and label the point at where they meet <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/3/73367b60edea0223325e27a0653d1791c2c510ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\overline{GC}}{\overline{BE}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="31" height="42" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/4/594c8b267069448b58fe77ad60497eb30f763a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c5623cb989571b021c49d06f921178cb1f479a78.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\overline{HG}}{\overline{HE}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="33" height="42" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/4/594c8b267069448b58fe77ad60497eb30f763a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c00bd00d67ac22d491e32aa1127b10f2b034985f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{HG} = x$" width="65" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/a/a5a486bf4029cd5f6c6ed5f120b838bda0147110.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{GE} = 8x$" width="72" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Because these are radii, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/2/cb2ebda64b77795950796ae58e044c37e30cc5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{GO} = \overline{OE} = \overline{OF} = 4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="178" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95a6466cefe33d65b751d6742ad3a226204fafe1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OF} \perp \overline{BH}$" width="84" height="15" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/317a260de7a27524c3a73ae82a3b8f98203dbf92.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OF}^2 + \overline{FH}^2 = \overline{OH}^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="157" height="21" />. Plugging in, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/f/a6fc0f64817256506a984030b8b407bb111f5499.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2 + \overline{FH}^2 = 5x^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="137" height="21" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e766a546ec4cc2b18e2d974406bc9302f3d77c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{FH} = 3x$" width="74" height="15" />.Triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c4561f906f7ef445228f71a1a160ffd99e91b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$OFH$" width="45" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67f1083768a4ebb9aab68d8afc36b5590e2b0b37.png" class="latex" alt="$BEH$" width="45" height="12" /> are similar so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/5/055dddae0d1977307bd6be36f27f3370bb1aaa8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\overline{OF}}{\overline{BE}}  = \frac{\overline{FH}}{\overline{EH}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="91" height="42" /> which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/6/49660c552fe5e59010962917849839b2a2919664.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4x}{18} = \frac{3x}{9x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="69" height="38" />. Solving for x, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8fdb45b0430f931c05a066416c1d0f44c38f43c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 1.5\]" width="56" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/e/b3e9fa6e2bdc737c4e300852022c034c21b65881.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x =  \boxed{6} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}.\]" width="123" height="23" />
</p>
