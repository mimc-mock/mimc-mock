<p>Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a06175902eaedcd48a185fdf05889947555f8c6.png" class="latex" alt="$-4\leq x\leq-2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="104" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/5/7358f15c5d8383814f17c673a9360f86e9213631.png" class="latex" alt="$2\leq y\leq4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="15" />, what is the largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/b/58b855cca26277ed126847e2f8c4b59dcd028fb6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="35" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/0/c50496e1d27d79856bee420bb36d6055a876d5bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } -1 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } -\frac12 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 0 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac12 \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="394" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Rewrite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/3/da37d487151a62d822d57d6b0de290a9ae05f4b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x+y)}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="38" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/5/ee56f08e48d36025c18a211913455902a5a4c5aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}x+\frac{y}x=1+\frac{y}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="119" height="33" />.
</p><p>We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12ac43903c7f75ee456c29ed5670dcd5a9bb2dfd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{y}x&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="47" height="33" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are of opposite sign.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/e/20ea0a64369e97ccb204f677d802feed7836de51.png" class="latex" alt="$1+\frac{y}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="33" /> is maximized when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/c/f8cf63694eaad92b8f4eb7c35a9521b9d78c5bb7.png" class="latex" alt="$|\frac{y}x|$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="22" height="33" /> is minimized, which occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/49428192d366caf373c0af3c3261687f12b4c883.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="18" height="18" /> is the largest and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/6/da61c635016c9cb19b665aef5d4225921056726c.png" class="latex" alt="$|y|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="17" height="18" /> is the smallest.
</p><p>This occurs at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/82031bef057e71a6525c8b5d39ac5092be2f8b52.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/b/63b96a2382611c0eff0e714fccf463cf6607fadc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}x=1-\frac12=\frac12\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="238" height="48" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If the answer choice is valid, then it must satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/3/da37d487151a62d822d57d6b0de290a9ae05f4b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x+y)}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="38" />. We use answer choices from greatest to least since the question asks for the greatest value.
</p><p>Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3626ff02d0ba34a6ae1421f4b5e155992ec4cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="17" />. We see that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/7654427a6d53d0646fa83f10f15480a43ae8370c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x+y)}x = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="92" height="38" /> then
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/6/3b64fb1eda86d3d44c2c280bd6e7ae46142702a0.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> is not in the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3626ff02d0ba34a6ae1421f4b5e155992ec4cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="17" /> is incorrect. 
</p><p>Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/d/87d32bac8c5abe4d6b299d444f24a8055279b232.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="17" />, however, we can find a value that satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/8209c6cfeea8d7be1dd8f8f2e68cb877aca53deb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}{x}=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="82" height="37" /> which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c47b93e716384ce2d910e12867652cd27d9fef28.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, such as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/82031bef057e71a6525c8b5d39ac5092be2f8b52.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" /> is the greatest.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a06175902eaedcd48a185fdf05889947555f8c6.png" class="latex" alt="$-4\leq x\leq-2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="104" height="14" />, we know that the denominator of our given fraction is negative. So to achieve the greatest value possible, our numerator, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> must also be as small as possible.
</p><p>So we pick our smallest value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Now if we if set our value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to its lowest, our expression becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/d/92d1e1c939a740a353672039de35b2bdf5819897.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(-2+2)}{-2} = \frac{0}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="38" /> As we decrease our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> value, we see that our numerator decrease from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and our denominator decrease from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> at the same rate. So decreasing our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> value decreases the overwhelming gap between our denominator and numerator, which gives us an overall bigger number.
</p><p>So we also pick the smallest value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/d/72d85c6959aaabfff6e0104544ff25c1445f3f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$-4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="12" />. We know have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efa9b2c5b7b497b27c46e1d0504d6722cc31fd40.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(-4+2)}{-4} = \frac{-2}{-4} = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="163" height="42" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" /> is our greatest possible value.
</p><p>-JinhoK
</p>
