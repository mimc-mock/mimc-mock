<p>Coin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is flipped three times and coin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is flipped four times.  What is the probability that the number of heads obtained from flipping the two fair coins is the same?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/a/d8a096284c365c007eacff98c81f92ec2d39401a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{29}{128} \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{23}{128} \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac14 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{35}{128} \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="415" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ways that the same number of heads will be obtained; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> heads.
</p><p>The probability of both getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> heads is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/4/ec44afce95da344f3db5c8254bf4abf23e722c4e.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac12\right)^3{3\choose0}\left(\frac12\right)^4{4\choose0}=\frac1{128}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="230" height="53" />
</p><p>The probability of both getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> head is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/827971f642a31e178d0f44780c6c5ae7848baca4.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac12\right)^3{3\choose1}\left(\frac12\right)^4{4\choose1}=\frac{12}{128}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="230" height="53" />
</p><p>The probability of both getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> heads is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/6/3a6e2636afeac705f77e769e9b0f06926b312256.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac12\right)^3{3\choose2}\left(\frac12\right)^4{4\choose2}=\frac{18}{128}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="230" height="53" />
</p><p>The probability of both getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> heads is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/c/a4c9c76a1bc9434fdbe068381b1f27402cf1e722.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac12\right)^3{3\choose3}\left(\frac12\right)^4{4\choose3}=\frac{4}{128}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="230" height="53" />
</p><p>Therefore, the probabiliy of flipping the same number of heads is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/8/788c14b2f7cbb513c85f664eec57da1e4526fd31.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1+12+18+4}{128}=\frac{35}{128}\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{35}{128}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="285" height="48" />
</p>
