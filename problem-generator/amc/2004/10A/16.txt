<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7fe7592026585cc36e4d7c6036f19fd8afdc29.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times 5$" width="40" height="12" /> grid shown contains a collection of squares with sizes from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a8df7312744a199daa5c8008af5b9b4798bfd64.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7fe7592026585cc36e4d7c6036f19fd8afdc29.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times 5$" width="40" height="12" />. How many of these squares contain the black center square?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2004_AMC_10A_problem_16.png" class="image"><img alt="2004 AMC 10A problem 16.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//7/7b/2004_AMC_10A_problem_16.png" width="144" height="134" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4eaf220bb5908f25aff55f0a053c5f37d09fc8.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 12 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 15 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 17 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ }  19\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Since there are five types of squares: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/2639894eb5f648d7dd73dd709611209eb4b1939e.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 1, 2 \times 2, 3 \times 3, 4 \times 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="190" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/a/ddaf84125c1b24d65a28e3a6fa187dab22b17521.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \times 5.$" width="44" height="12" /> We must find how many of each square contain the black shaded square in the center. 
</p><p>If we list them, we get that 
</p>
<ul><li>There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a8df7312744a199daa5c8008af5b9b4798bfd64.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> squares, containing the black square</li>
<li>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/7901356dd65ec4da1b694937cc4ffc663e90d800.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2$" width="40" height="12" /> squares, containing the black square</li>
<li>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b5343f2d9e22816e8de24262565ff2937695b2c.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> squares, containing the black square</li>
<li>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/674dc6717f1938acff63480bd24b1270a30439ff.png" class="latex" alt="$4\times 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> squares, containing the black square</li>
<li>There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7fe7592026585cc36e4d7c6036f19fd8afdc29.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times 5$" width="40" height="12" /> squares, containing the black square</li></ul>
<p>Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/d/8cd560743d8d5d395baabfca641849dce3332f76.png" class="latex" alt="$1+4+9+4+1=19\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 19}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="268" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We use complementary counting. There are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3b32228e9105bf0f3dffd78b0b7310083b693d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times2$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41cc793e4f6d8149b881fa5ac0843dea8f309f24.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> squares that do not contain the black square. Counting, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3b32228e9105bf0f3dffd78b0b7310083b693d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times2$" width="40" height="12" /> squares, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10d5fc1af8dbefb4e206138240c04731cb23e582.png" class="latex" alt="$25-1 = 24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41cc793e4f6d8149b881fa5ac0843dea8f309f24.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> squares that do not contain the black square. That gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/f/e3f67f2143ff7db50b15d6196ea16decff4589a4.png" class="latex" alt="$12+24=36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> squares that don't contain it. There are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/b/88b1c3e69e5fa9a70eb02987237c753594b2d509.png" class="latex" alt="$25+16+9+4+1 = 55$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="194" height="14" /> squares possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/4/d3461aa9f087c5f02d0acd161cb8514a6db74087.png" class="latex" alt="$(25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="18" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41cc793e4f6d8149b881fa5ac0843dea8f309f24.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3b32228e9105bf0f3dffd78b0b7310083b693d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times2$" width="40" height="12" /> squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77d7104f34af16053b63ce7cfa87265d915c6105.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times3$" width="40" height="12" /> squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b104e8c71a3698d4c073f00681bc1bf6412754fc.png" class="latex" alt="$4\times4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> squares and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbf62794748fc97d1bfe454296f10da375126bac.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times5$" width="40" height="12" /> square), therefore there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2482748a99a787b95876fc66d8ccc5b2479f1c27.png" class="latex" alt="$55-36 = 19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> squares that contain the black square, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd9e8916434c4a10a57f03e3cf2f969d8c7e8de8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 19}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="29" />.
</p>
