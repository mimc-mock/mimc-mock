<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/4/4e4050342f16694c6946c030b5d594bdcf7aad34.png" class="latex" alt="$BEF$" width="43" height="12" /> is equilateral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f375e35ba9dfc8e91ebf69ccb65d3bd2bd753999.png" class="latex" alt="$EA=FC$" width="80" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e30e7dec97a3f5e84e0de8a12e3056c6c9a77fc.png" class="latex" alt="$EAB$" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/a/3ca61b798add37d72a65243434f22b0f22cbb0b1.png" class="latex" alt="$FCB$" width="42" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/8/4d8879abf0d7fbb06bc388aa9ab096a5c37c9bb8.png" class="latex" alt="$SAS$" width="37" height="13" /> congruent. Thus, triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/9/2e9e8b2c23e0545ef6de81f0ea578173e9ab7a64.png" class="latex" alt="$DEF$" width="44" height="12" /> is an isosceles right triangle. So we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af0d224662e00a30f3826dc3c123c70d3e06630.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=x$" width="64" height="12" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/2/c920b0f98f3d2b096ffd6483457b577e48047913.png" class="latex" alt="$EF=EB=FB=x\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="194" height="18" />. If we go angle chasing, we find out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/b/56b53162dfd91186e30d3d1425c8bf66d9b64647.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AEB=75^{\circ}$" width="104" height="13" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/d/f2d9b006e36ee190e34dcffd0641970318d7e809.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABE=15^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/6/fd6a17d16a603e6863fb70dd1d48561558db16d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AE}{EB}=\sin{15^{\circ}}=\frac{\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{2}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="206" height="41" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/7/61798af32ae760bbdf7c37d394fafa673cf616e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AE}{x\sqrt{2}}=\frac{\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{2}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="136" height="45" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/3/9036e960d3bc37278a4953f97634a775c42905ab.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=\frac{x(\sqrt{3}-1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="135" height="41" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/1/e41ab8abd73e4878da24d42a9b38cf2b99055d2a.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=\frac{x(\sqrt{3}+1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="135" height="41" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/9/b99b31202c9f900565728bf544d4b608bdd9e9cc.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABE]=\frac{x^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="97" height="39" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b300a7a7522f8fe2d801c3ead97e23d8c9c55646.png" class="latex" alt="$[DEF]=\frac{x^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="39" />. Thus the ratio of the areas is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b5075c652994213397aa0f1e00f91080a3308b8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" />
