<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/0/f00f17f199784afe2bf914b7880b1e5ffc454d6a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(400); defaultpen(fontsize(10)); pair OA=(-1,0),OB=(2/3,8/9),OC=(2/3,-8/9),OD=(0,0),E=(2/3,0); real t = 2.5; pair OA1=(-2+2*t,0),OB1=(4/3+2*t,16/9),OC1=(4/3+2*t,-16/9),OD1=(0+2*t,0),E1=(4/3+2*t,0); draw(Circle(OD,2)); draw(Circle(OA,1)); draw(Circle(OB,8/9)); draw(Circle(OC,8/9)); draw(OA--OB--OC--cycle); draw(OD--OB--OB+(OB-OD)*4/5); draw(OA--E); label(&quot;$O_{A}$&quot;,OA,(-1,0)); label(&quot;$O_{B}$&quot;,OB,(-1,1)); label(&quot;$O_{C}$&quot;,OC,(-1,-1)); label(&quot;$O_{D}$&quot;,OD,(-1,-1)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,(0.5,-1)); label(&quot;$r$&quot;,OB+(OB-OD)*2/5,(-0.5,1)); label(&quot;$r$&quot;,(1*OA+3*OB)/4,(-0.5,1)); dot(OA^^OB^^OC^^OD^^E); draw(OA1--OB1--OC1--cycle); draw(OD1--OB1); draw(OA1--E1); label(&quot;$O_{A}$&quot;,OA1,(-1,0)); label(&quot;$O_{B}$&quot;,OB1,(1,1)); label(&quot;$O_{C}$&quot;,OC1,(1,-1)); label(&quot;$O_{D}$&quot;,OD1,(0,-1)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E1,(1,0)); label(&quot;$1+r$&quot;,(OA1+OB1)/2,(-0.5,1)); label(&quot;$r$&quot;,(E1+OB1)/2,(1,0)); label(&quot;$r$&quot;,(E1+OC1)/2,(1,0)); label(&quot;$2-r$&quot;,(OB1+OD1)/2,(-1,0)); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(OA1+OD1)/2,(0,-1)); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(E1+OD1)/2,(0,-1)); dot(OA1^^OB1^^OC1^^OD1^^E1); [/asy]" width="668" height="315" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/e/d9ec88ae83657205ddb8c4d891fd96a4b7ba07a9.png" class="latex" alt="$O_{i}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> be the center of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/7/097bbaa05e1399e0b4f0f0e3e5aad970123da275.png" class="latex" alt="$i \in \{A,B,C,D\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="18" /> and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the tangent point of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f87e235a36d533fd186185dfe675b1968f54eaf6.png" class="latex" alt="$B,C$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="16" />. Since the radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the diameter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, the radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p><br />
Let the radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f87e235a36d533fd186185dfe675b1968f54eaf6.png" class="latex" alt="$B,C$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="16" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840405360e993282d68a5a00e466f2d9ec275bab.png" class="latex" alt="$O_{D}E = x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" />. If we connect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/5/d850ad41429e2b26f0b9894278713c9ec75fdcc1.png" class="latex" alt="$O_{A},O_{B},O_{C}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" />, we get an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Isosceles_triangle" title="Isosceles triangle">isosceles triangle</a> with lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e10aedf152d879688ef4cd20222709b503450177.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + r, 2r$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Then right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/2/7c26059751cc24c0cc3743e0f07128207c3ed19e.png" class="latex" alt="$O_{D}O_{B}O_{E}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" /> has legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/4/364914591d0965edee0530fecb4a8562c816c8dd.png" class="latex" alt="$r, x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="11" /> and <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hypotenuse" title="Hypotenuse">hypotenuse</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f7c32b2c19d6cf03a408629ec2c975770876847.png" class="latex" alt="$2-r$" width="39" height="12" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/2/742dab5b8085d1fd19189e7a158d749f2e63bd57.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = (2-r)^2 - r^2 \Longrightarrow x = \sqrt{4-4r}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="280" height="20" />. 
</p><p>Also, right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/c/efc4c47a0ed5eda59e249f89cc7199b9a8ea8cc3.png" class="latex" alt="$O_{A}O_{B}O_{E}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" /> has legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/a/58a618506f224b9b347a7dd36a74c66d8e40cffe.png" class="latex" alt="$r, 1+x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="15" />, and hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5af425759ab22795a279637abae525a99be2fe88.png" class="latex" alt="$1+r$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="13" />. Solving, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e8ab199611d33218d324fe38fd3cccea9706bb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} r^2 + (1+\sqrt{4-4r})^2 &amp;=&amp; (1+r)^2\\ 1+4-4r+2\sqrt{4-4r}&amp;=&amp; 2r + 1\\ 1-r &amp;=&amp; \left(\frac{6r-4}{4}\right)^2\\ \frac{9}{4}r^2-2r&amp;=&amp; 0\\ r &amp;=&amp; \frac 89  \end{eqnarray*}" width="307" height="185" /> 
</p><p>So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/9/bc9fdabde597db4f896921d16d00ec2f96faf361.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{8}{9}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="58" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/1/e21e9f6600899f824d87d28a241a4d9bfb5ee11a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(15mm); pair A=(0,1),B=(-8/9,-2/3),C=(8/9,-2/3),D=(0,0), E=(0,-2/3);  draw(Circle(D,2)); draw(Circle(A,1)); draw(Circle(B,8/9)); draw(Circle(C,8/9)); draw(A--B--C--A); draw(B--D--C); draw(A--E);  dot(A);dot(B);dot(C);dot(D);dot(E);  label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;, D,NW); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;, A,N); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;, B,W); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;, C,E); label(&quot;\(E\)&quot;, E,SE); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(-.4,.7)); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(0,0.5),W); label(&quot;\(r\)&quot;, (-.8,-.1)); label(&quot;\(r\)&quot;, (-4/9,-2/3),S); label(&quot;\(h\)&quot;, (0,-1/3), W); [/asy]" width="285" height="285" />
</center>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/c/dfcdc0e89e143b8c5e7b32ac64584a4478906665.png" class="latex" alt="$BD= 2-r$" width="94" height="12" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the center of the larger circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Using the Pythagorean Theorem on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/b/16b4072dec30604cbea5998c12fddd9eacfeb564.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDE$" width="60" height="13" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/7/8174bfb180cf24228728ee78eedb396417c94a03.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} r^2 + h^2 &amp;= (2-r)^2 \\ r^2 + h^2 &amp;= 4 - 4r + r^2 \\ h^2 &amp;= 4 - 4r \\ h &amp;= 2\sqrt{1-r} \end{align*}" width="166" height="102" />
</p><p>Now using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/9/1598bab45a40d37f82c1c831ca6c67965a273d47.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAE$" width="58" height="13" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/4/6b415269b8a7b6a725ffc7e171fda1611f6a15f4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} r^2 + (h+1)^2 &amp;= (r+1)^2 \\ r^2 + h^2 + 2h + 1 &amp;= r^2 + 2r + 1 \\ h^2 + 2h &amp;= 2r \end{align*}" width="239" height="72" />
</p><p>Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/6/ad699d21a6a44ac06da5d742eb338611ee1aed44.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (4-4r) + 4\sqrt{1-r} &amp;= 2r \\ 4\sqrt{1-r} &amp;= 6r - 4 \\ 16-16r &amp;= 36r^2 - 48r + 16 \\ 0 &amp;= 36r^2 - 32r \\ r &amp;= \frac{32}{36} = \frac{8}{9} \Longrightarrow \qquad \textbf{(D)} \end{align*}" width="332" height="149" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can apply <a href="/wiki/index.php/Descartes%27_Circle_Formula" title="Descartes&#39; Circle Formula">Descartes' Circle Formula</a>.
</p><p>The four circles have curvatures <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/c/fcc3e2289e498da01b09cbc8f71dc4063649df7e.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{2}, 1, \frac{1}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b8be31d9e21e92918f42cfaa7b324766d507b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4ff00a85ce24a0ca842fc4bc74ca24e6fb59616a.png" class="latex" alt="$2((-\frac{1}{2})^2+1^2+\frac {1}{r^2}+\frac{1}{r^2})=(-\frac{1}{2}+1+\frac{1}{r}+\frac{1}{r})^2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="369" height="37" />
</p><p>Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/1846d56053c9f61ed7ad97a33fc87733f6213b63.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10}{4}+\frac{4}{r^2}=\frac{1}{4}+\frac{2}{r}+\frac{4}{r^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="178" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/f/a6f582750859876d5ccbc8db6afa4817da0d3296.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{r}=\frac{9}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="49" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/2/e42e322c3c60ab2a3cd9b94ae3ab07e3ccb74866.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac{8}{9} \Longrightarrow \qquad \textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />
</p>
