<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3958e984d2597b35da7975f4503cdcf965a777f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ldots$" width="19" height="2" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3958e984d2597b35da7975f4503cdcf965a777f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ldots$" width="19" height="2" /> be two arithmetic progressions. The set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is the union of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ae760d1ea10eb7a90d22087b3c7e2393d02117.png" class="latex" alt="$2004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> terms of each sequence. How many distinct numbers are in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/4/474634629544e7972de75d21c9e11484677acfaf.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 3722 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 3732 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 3914 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 3924 \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 4007$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="478" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The two sets of terms are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/3/86339252aeb1237faa56ba5bae9dfd64bd57bd10.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\{ 3k+1&#160;: 0\leq k &lt; 2004 \}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="224" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/1/481b395facf20a657d7fdabdfca69fd04c442fe2.png" class="latex" alt="$B=\{ 7l+9&#160;: 0\leq l&lt;2004\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="216" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/b/d7b96ac0a0861c66aa92007711a7279773e0bee8.png" class="latex" alt="$S=A\cup B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="84" height="13" />. We can compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/1/f01e5398ea6e2196944931045e70e18ec9c64508.png" class="latex" alt="$|S|=|A\cup B|=|A|+|B|-|A\cap B|=4008-|A\cap B|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="420" height="18" />. We will now find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/3/9b3b1e84d2dc43e946e2c9fb73404c2503d30b49.png" class="latex" alt="$|A\cap B|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Consider the numbers in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. We want to find out how many of them lie in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. In other words, we need to find out the number of valid values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/2/da2c2d0b17d7dbfb4be227c41a9034ef1a6895ec.png" class="latex" alt="$7l+9\in A$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />.
</p><p>The fact "<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/2/da2c2d0b17d7dbfb4be227c41a9034ef1a6895ec.png" class="latex" alt="$7l+9\in A$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />" can be rewritten as "<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/0/1609e305cfbb20044ea6d766063857d341d38ad8.png" class="latex" alt="$1\leq 7l+9 \leq 3\cdot 2003 + 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="192" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/d/eedfecc87a7ccb260a018a79f047ad9b638544d1.png" class="latex" alt="$7l+9\equiv 1\pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />".
</p><p>The first condition gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad85a8ad851d84c7de999bef047fa69b90784f09.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq l\leq 857$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="15" />, the second one gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/9/fa97ef44e9e917e67ae04fff0f0bcf1e93bb4fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$l\equiv 1\pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus the good values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/5/1b5e9cf9435551c575979e3b59d1179958d3672d.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,4,7,\dots,856\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />, and their count is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/f/a7f5e192c81b98afccd0cd7b761b220fb7b116bc.png" class="latex" alt="$858/3 = 286$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />.
</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/5/635c84b9c5c22193276f70c51428f1f6b6325cab.png" class="latex" alt="$|A\cap B|=286$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/6/616b0d02de19f9e1d17ef0f580851ec8356d2b0a.png" class="latex" alt="$|S|=4008-|A\cap B|=\boxed{(A) 3722}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="264" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can start by finding the first non-distinct term from both sequences. We find that that number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Now, to find every 
</p><p>other non-distinct terms, we can just keep adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25d31faf472ddb4c698a14522fe8125c06f8dd8.png" class="latex" alt="$21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. We know that the last terms of both sequences are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0f47647b73e0aa088fd808e7690b64249ffb0f.png" class="latex" alt="$1+3\cdot 2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> and 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c332426653fdf7acbe06de2401b7b1cb1dc9f911.png" class="latex" alt="$9+7\cdot 2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" />. Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0f47647b73e0aa088fd808e7690b64249ffb0f.png" class="latex" alt="$1+3\cdot 2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> is smaller and that is the last possible common term of both sequences. Now, we can 
</p><p>create the inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/541cdc8d2c91f91f74801443cd8ac28101d89832.png" class="latex" alt="$16+21k \leq 1+3\cdot 2003$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="182" height="15" />. Using the inequality, we find that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/0236ef1144a8417ea4746204c4ddf20b7de1bb30.png" class="latex" alt="$286$" width="26" height="12" /> common terms. There are 4008 
</p><p>terms in total. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/7/057efaec18012147f225e0d2b9d14f926616791c.png" class="latex" alt="$4008-286=\boxed{(A) 3722}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="186" height="29" />
</p><p>~Hithere22702
</p>
