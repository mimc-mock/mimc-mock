<p>A square has sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and a circle centered at one of its vertices has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What is the area of the union of the regions enclosed by the square and the circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/9/f299b8d7843afc3fc78f2ff61ab9a3d33b8a7f53.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)\ }200+25\pi\quad\mathrm{(B)\ }100+75\pi\quad\mathrm{(C)\ }75+100\pi\quad\mathrm{(D)\ }100+100\pi\quad\mathrm{(E)\ }100+125\pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="641" height="18" />
</p>

<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/IGN4XxJIbE0">https://youtu.be/IGN4XxJIbE0</a>
</p><p>~Education, the Study of Everything
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The area of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/d/b2d289bcef129fde7867462fb5618f7a8c6653d1.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{\bigcirc}=100\pi$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="17" />; the area of the square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0ea14ba69c61600c2dc9dfdd8c61b1a9ab0f4f85.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{\square}=100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="15" />.
</p><p>Exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of the circle lies inside the square. Thus the total area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/7/d174c1d86c5da2a57b593979a784a4d549fb72ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac34 S_{\bigcirc}+S_{\square}=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)\ }100+75\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="231" height="38" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/c/9fcea2965eceab15b23e988726a4b3446b01b59f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] Draw(Circle((0,0),10)); Draw((0,0)--(10,0)--(10,10)--(0,10)--(0,0)); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,(5,0),S); label(&quot;$10$&quot;,(0,5),W); dot((0,0)); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p>
