<p>How many two-digit positive integers have at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> as a digit?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/b/fcb9265648016b31c03fafd19cc5e806edc04205.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 10 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 18\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 19 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 20\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Ten numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/4/864780af8d8a5e7650cabd453a6a199b494e42bd.png" class="latex" alt="$(70,71,\dots,79)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> as the tens digit. Nine numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/4/014c8c9e25ef39d56568491cce7c5a2e5c961dfb.png" class="latex" alt="$(17,27,\dots,97)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" /> have it as the ones digit. Number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" /> is in both sets.
</p><p>Thus the result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85fbc2b7f14f21ad65ee7862a1398d344e8b948a.png" class="latex" alt="$10+9-1=18 \Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="146" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0755fa4c94c46543cccd76390b597d6d92229b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We use complementary counting. The complement of having at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> as a digit is having no <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />s as a digit.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> digits to choose from for the first digit and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> digits for the second. This gives a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/6/2c65944d1b77acea02e073974b5f3c8d3a842988.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \times 10 = 90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> two-digit numbers.
</p><p>But since we cannot have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> as a digit, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> first digits and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> second digits to choose from.
</p><p>Thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/4177ce044c58562a5b90850cfb738b4e68798a56.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \times 9 = 72$" width="82" height="12" /> two-digit numbers without a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> as a digit.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> (The total number of two-digit numbers) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/6/306180fb4db874c416387f894dabe9d2614b14d0.png" class="latex" alt="$-  72$" width="31" height="12" /> (The number of two-digit numbers without a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a319c30edbb413373f868d5d50ebaf04ddb31247.png" class="latex" alt="$= 18 \Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0755fa4c94c46543cccd76390b597d6d92229b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
</p>
