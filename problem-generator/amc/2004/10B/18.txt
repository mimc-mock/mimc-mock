What is the ratio of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/6816e3f70d91b2115986ef939cbed51ad6b813b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DBF$" width="60" height="13" /> to that of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/d/6dd17eadc905d042a9f376ccc996ec45099f8fe7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACE$" width="58" height="13" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/263dc6cad183b002bbc16658c481d8935e2373a7.png" class="latex" alt="$x = [DBF]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="18" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/d/6dd17eadc905d042a9f376ccc996ec45099f8fe7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACE$" width="58" height="13" /> is divided into four triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/6/3265f63783f8f764b76e9f3825300b6fe5687e17.png" class="latex" alt="$[ACE] = [BCD] + [ABF] + [DEF] + x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="315" height="18" />.
</p><p>Because of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/8/4d8879abf0d7fbb06bc388aa9ab096a5c37c9bb8.png" class="latex" alt="$SAS$" width="37" height="13" /> triangle area, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/4/ae4d963db8e2c9091db4936279d163124eed4cda.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12 \cdot 12 \cdot 16 = \frac12 \cdot 9 \cdot 4 + \frac12 \cdot 3 \cdot 15 \cdot \sin(\angle A) + \frac12 \cdot 5 \cdot 12 \cdot \sin(\angle E) + x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="520" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/3/bb3983f004e0ce9a1b3f18567676f7e97baace80.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(\angle A) = \frac{16}{20}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/f/9bf65e089b4314c7c6197086b45b0e47f6dffe44.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(\angle E) = \frac{12}{20}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/3/ed35dfca1df357b729b5f3787ff65fa102a459ec.png" class="latex" alt="$96 = 18 + 18 + 18 + x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="173" height="14" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/b/43b7de6d3cc40f71f68294dfd5298ddac82488e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f7896c8dff2f70ba2153cd44766cc4f5507ec7c5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[DBF]}{[ACE]} = \frac{42}{96} = \boxed{\textbf{(E)}\frac 7{16}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="190" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First of all, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/2/cc2fe1a8781d19079f2a119c23037ae8ec2a73f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{CD}{CE} = \frac{EF}{EA} = \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="183" height="38" />, and therefore
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cf56cba51d7c2a7c49ae22f15043ad2fde327a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BC}{AC} = \frac{DE}{CE} = \frac{FA}{EA} = \frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="183" height="38" />.
</p><p>Draw the height from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> as in the picture below:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/a/e3a991ca861b53810c15c17e5a93234eddefa082.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.5cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair C=(0,0), A=(0,12), E=(20,0); draw(A--C--E--cycle); pair B=A + 3*(C-A)/length(C-A); pair D=C + 4*(E-C)/length(E-C); pair F=E + 5*(A-E)/length(A-E); draw(B--D--F--cycle); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,W); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SW); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,SE); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,NE); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,A--B,W); label(&quot;$9$&quot;,0.5*C + 0.5*B,4*W); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,C--D,S); label(&quot;$12$&quot;,D--E,S); label(&quot;$5$&quot;,E--F,NE); label(&quot;$15$&quot;,F--A,NE);  pair G = intersectionpoint(F -- (F-(100,0)), A--C); draw(F--G, dashed); label(&quot;$G$&quot;,G,W); [/asy]" width="532" height="328" />
</p><p>Now consider the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/7/1b7ee1894b9868699832ce22d92598b8f0b8997a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABF$" width="58" height="13" />. Clearly the triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/4/9e4cae83c0fa7a0b33f58a5ec88e27c870c017a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AFG$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/9/f391e223e8b12650009ab6580d0b260c47e39de1.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AEC$" width="58" height="13" /> are similar, as they have all angles equal. Their ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da06e42a678d0cd6cc978f8e6686dff3fcf77a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {AF}{AE} = \frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7f8451967a439796558a4d8afe4c38b45f086ce.png" class="latex" alt="$FG = \frac 34 \cdot CE$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="107" height="38" />. 
Now the area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/3/6a3bd8aa6b2d347e292438e7971ddfdab3a496b3.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{ABF}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/7/1b7ee1894b9868699832ce22d92598b8f0b8997a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABF$" width="58" height="13" /> can be computed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/0/5707677f5a006a97607925fffacc0193c9057fd0.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{ABF} = \frac 12 \cdot AB \cdot FG$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="163" height="37" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/a/23ac138391a8d316e10d2583783eccf72ee1d757.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 \cdot \left( \frac 14 \cdot AC \right) \cdot \left( \frac 34 \cdot EC \right) = \frac 14 \cdot \frac 34 \cdot S_{ACE}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="319" height="43" />.
</p><p>Similarly we can find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dc75b619cd09f044d2b7ad54477d039b604403.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{BCD} = S_{DEF} = \frac 3{16}\cdot S_{ACE}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="213" height="38" /> as well. 
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/4/e54496a3d87b7a0f75d8155bcd929c4a64f0ebba.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{BDF} = S_{ACE} - 3\cdot\left( \frac 3{16} \cdot S_{ACE} \right) = \frac 7{16} \cdot S_{ACE}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="360" height="43" />, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/4/3f4274bcc7c9acd8f9401795bcc0b4f6a005034d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S_{BDF}}{S_{ACE}} = \boxed{\frac 7{16}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="106" height="48" />.
</p>
