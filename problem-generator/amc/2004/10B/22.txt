<p>A triangle with sides of 5, 12, and 13 has both an inscribed and a circumscribed circle. What is the distance between the centers of those circles?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/1/1615c519e1438c3626e7d9a372e0af0522be9d4f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{3\sqrt{5}}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{7}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \sqrt{15} \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{\sqrt{65}}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac{9}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="429" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/e/b3ee7391165284ef992a65e6ec0114c4beb26869.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import geometry;  unitsize(0.6 cm);  pair A, B, C, D, E, F, I, O;  A = (5^2/13,5*12/13); B = (0,0); C = (13,0); I = incenter(A,B,C); D = (I + reflect(B,C)*(I))/2; E = (I + reflect(C,A)*(I))/2; F = (I + reflect(A,B)*(I))/2; O = (B + C)/2;  draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(incircle(A,B,C)); draw(I--D); draw(I--E); draw(I--F); draw(I--O);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, SE); dot(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, S); dot(&quot;$E$&quot;, E, NE); dot(&quot;$F$&quot;, F, NW); dot(&quot;$I$&quot;, I, N); dot(&quot;$O$&quot;, O, S); [/asy]" width="415" height="178" />
This is a right triangle. Pick a coordinate system so that the right angle is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and the other two vertices are at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a08648694721d1ea0dce0949c4d4336b2813a933.png" class="latex" alt="$(12,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e8da5448a5f378d5b8c253cdcd3cb49a90fa49.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>As this is a right triangle, the center of the circumcircle is in the middle of the hypotenuse, at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/8/fc88bc9d238f8aeb2f1174fc9422fc7d125e090c.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,2.5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />.
</p><p>The radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> of the inscribed circle can be computed using the well-known identity <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/a/aea97e45f6cec2cb3cf3540adcc5422e59291045.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{rP}2=S$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is the area of the triangle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> its perimeter. In our case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/b/6ab71bd60653f06a40d595007d4ab6639c1f206c.png" class="latex" alt="$S=\frac{5\cdot 12}{2}=30$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="122" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/9/2b9a197f3454b90971ab737c6854f2dad2b2543c.png" class="latex" alt="$P=5+12+13=30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="170" height="14" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a0736cc24657e4fbe87410091de1c4ebd44e26db.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2$" width="41" height="12" />. As the inscribed circle touches both legs, its center must be at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/c/fac66325c9bff20c3232182943017f13c21338ce.png" class="latex" alt="$(r,r)=(2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" />.
</p><p>The distance of these two points is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f950294ec3b5cdb3ac8ef47d4bd1e834278531.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{ (6-2)^2 + (2.5-2)^2 } = \sqrt{16.25} = \sqrt{\frac{65}4} = \boxed{\frac{\sqrt{65}}2}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="396" height="53" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We directly apply Euler’s Theorem, which states that if the circumcenter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and the incenter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" />, and the inradius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and the circumradius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />, then 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/e/1ee9dc3b52bdd0ea40931dc0ca792540fcb15ebc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[OI^2=R(R-2r)\]" width="135" height="19" />
</p><p>We can see that this is a right triangle, and hence has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />. We then find the inradius with the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/1/1e10db7df3e7fccf99a1ac6de439f3e2cf0723fc.png" class="latex" alt="$A=rs$" width="54" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> denotes semiperimeter. We easily see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/a/d4a0c4f278dacc3a3519b89e9240ce12c203af9e.png" class="latex" alt="$s=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a0736cc24657e4fbe87410091de1c4ebd44e26db.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2$" width="41" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We now find the circumradius with the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33295a45f64955c07396031ff1f68c2b6d861d5.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\frac{abc}{4R}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="65" height="38" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3eac4492a6e647f8c6b2ebc501c2f2998804a506.png" class="latex" alt="$R=\frac{13}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="37" />. 
</p><p>Substituting all of this back into our formula gives:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/a/faafcf2ce22effea97ba9366498d8dfe6840fb13.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[OI^2= \frac{65}{4}\]" width="75" height="38" />
So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/b/afb044702711604a530ba684f2ab17986b23ce83.png" class="latex" alt="$OI=\frac{\sqrt{65}}{2}\implies \boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="162" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/f/c1fc6bc723b7023b9df7692c8a82f38c970177d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(15cm); draw((0,0)--(0,5), linewidth(2));  draw((0,0)--(12,0), linewidth(2));  draw((12,0)--(0,5), linewidth(2));  draw((2,0)--(2,2), linewidth(2));  draw((2,2)--(2.770565628817799,3.8455976546592505), linewidth(2));  draw((2,2)--(6.023716614191289,2.4901180774202962), linewidth(2));  draw((2,2)--(0,5), linewidth(2));  draw((2,2)--(12,0), linewidth(2));  draw((0,2)--(2,2), linewidth(2));  label(&quot;$A$&quot;, (0.14164244785738467,0.25966489738837517), NE);  label(&quot;$B$&quot;, (0.14164244785738467,5.311734560831129), NE);  label(&quot;$C$&quot;, (12.120449134133493,0.3232129434694161), NE);  label(&quot;$D$&quot;, (0.14164244785738467,2.324976395022205), NE);  label(&quot;$E$&quot;, (2.111631876369636,0.3232129434694161), NE);  label(&quot;$F$&quot;, (2.9059824523826405,4.167869731372392), NE);  label(&quot;$G$&quot;, (6.146932802515699,2.801586740630012), NE);  label(&quot;$I$&quot;, (2.1, 2.1), NE); [/asy]" width="712" height="325" />
Construct <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff29028b2432b4e2cf3d924ac82114813de066d.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=5$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/8/bb890e788c451fed032c8b221192c5ac43790206.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50fde934b870fb9c1fc6a9092cc49004288c218e.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. Since this is a pythagorean triple, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/7/7e7d66b94c2e62ed66e5724a264cd4db51c25034.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{A}=90$" width="68" height="13" />. By a property of circumcircles and right triangles, the circumcenter, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />, lies on the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/1/771d39baf6df6832464783b2a6e13bb96904228f.png" class="latex" alt="$BG=\frac{13}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="77" height="38" />. Turning to the incircle, we find that the inradius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, using the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/1/1e10db7df3e7fccf99a1ac6de439f3e2cf0723fc.png" class="latex" alt="$A=rs$" width="54" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is the area of the triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the inradius, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semiperimeter. We then denote the incenter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" />, along with the points of tangency <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/2/2529bdd3f1a29b84cfed4a7ef6b954ae6e587204.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{IDA}=\angle{IEA}=90$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="170" height="14" /> by a property of tangency, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52ecad7705a595864f5f27bd9d3d462462f15dfe.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{EID}=90$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="14" />, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4af2767def915f417415971a5c251d5bd14c6c8a.png" class="latex" alt="$IDAE$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="14" /> is a square. Then, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/1/d9115c574b959a69102fb24a0cdae3f84d6d5bac.png" class="latex" alt="$IE=2$" width="56" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/2/b627ea151c19351c2c70f7bf6a856beaed5adeb2.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=2$" width="62" height="13" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff29028b2432b4e2cf3d924ac82114813de066d.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=5$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f7d95c4cd037f3050ac44ab28c2e112bb989f3.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=3$" width="63" height="12" />, and because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/9/929ee9a147ed7215b0cd1801ec79faa1093f96d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{BID}\cong\triangle{BIF}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="136" height="18" /> by HL, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/5/f455798a2bbffa29a08f5fe2846a57aa122c23a1.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=BF=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="14" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/1/981d5415b5e4efd94ca9ce27fedc1330e50c80b6.png" class="latex" alt="$FG=\frac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/6/a9609630beee11fe13d6ddce9adf3d8d7417491a.png" class="latex" alt="$IF=2$" width="56" height="12" />, pythagorean theorem gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/5/5c53e4efee4c99cfd874b3ff679819bac2e397f4.png" class="latex" alt="$IG=\boxed{\frac{\sqrt{65}}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="98" height="54" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>A triangle with sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc24a7d9b78eaaa38a1bcea8ba1e2baafe0643c9.png" class="latex" alt="$5,12,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> must be right. Let the right angle be at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9f88f6ffb2e7b890daad253b82020ebbd9cdd8.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=5,AC=12,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="147" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50fde934b870fb9c1fc6a9092cc49004288c218e.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. The circumcenter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> must be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bbd6df4f15406a8cc73a2df90c0b1864310a2ba.png" class="latex" alt="$BO=CO=\frac{13}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="135" height="38" /> Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f4a11d6090f9eac0ce2488df6384dad1263ea.png" class="latex" alt="$I$" width="9" height="12" /> be the incenter and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the point where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> is tangent to the incircle. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93dc0c6b2cec287dc0b9dfcc3f7ace142652c38.png" class="latex" alt="$ID \perp DO, \triangle IDO$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" /> is a right triangle. Therefore, to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33f85e8c5408e12d1e17e232001b1d0613ab674a.png" class="latex" alt="$IO$" width="23" height="12" />, it suffices to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0bfbf26e6ab98a461a5cb0964c6778a20122f07.png" class="latex" alt="$ID$" width="24" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff1d53900652da5f2d40ef5427512642e85c0b7.png" class="latex" alt="$DO$" width="29" height="12" />. The area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is equal to the semiperimeter times the inradius, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. This allows us to set up an equation involving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/f/2ffa9bb94f1e603d923625573218fd5bbe632fb9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{5 \cdot 12}{2}= r \cdot \frac{5+12+13}{2}.\]" width="186" height="38" /> Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a0736cc24657e4fbe87410091de1c4ebd44e26db.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2$" width="41" height="12" />. Now it only remains to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff1d53900652da5f2d40ef5427512642e85c0b7.png" class="latex" alt="$DO$" width="29" height="12" />. To start, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/5/185527b85b06f6f7af65084573bdd01b7bf68e31.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=s-b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the semiperimeter and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />. Simplifing, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b170b21ad591d43e88ae1824208d7abf55a2409b.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=3&lt;\frac{13}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="112" height="38" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is on the same side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/1/b31b5d9541280db7e345dee1e12cd07436672b56.png" class="latex" alt="$DO=BO-BD=\frac{13}{2}-3=\frac{7}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="258" height="38" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/4515cc27ba1c830dc196320c021efe0e92d713ca.png" class="latex" alt="$IO=\sqrt{(\frac{7}{2})^2+2^2}=\sqrt{\frac{65}{4}}=\boxed{(D)\frac{\sqrt{65}}{2}}.$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="313" height="54" />
</p>
