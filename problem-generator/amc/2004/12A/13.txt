<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Set" title="Set">set</a> of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Point" title="Point">points</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coordinate_plane" class="mw-redirect" title="Coordinate plane">coordinate plane</a>, where each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> may be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/4/004a37661613b63882b9f43a64dca05fb1feb2d2.png" class="latex" alt="$- 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. How many distinct <a href="/wiki/index.php/Line" title="Line">lines</a> pass through at least two members of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/7/f47dd747c3a28fac76075533dc7e48590567e4ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)}\ 8 \qquad \text {(B)}\ 20 \qquad \text {(C)}\ 24 \qquad \text {(D)}\ 27\qquad \text {(E)}\ 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2004_AMC12A-13.png" class="image"><img alt="2004 AMC12A-13.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//f/f1/2004_AMC12A-13.png" width="274" height="275" /></a></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let's count them by cases:
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2004_AMC12A-13b.png" class="image"><img alt="2004 AMC12A-13b.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//4/4d/2004_AMC12A-13b.png" width="932" height="252" /></a></div></div>
<ul><li><b>Case 1</b>: The line is horizontal or vertical, clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/b/d6b0375ea5d39656d7f86f115ebfbed8c55fccb7.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 2 = 6$" width="64" height="12" />.</li>
<li><b>Case 2</b>: The line has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/f/7cfbd35086ffefb83637d42166582b43c8a1ff4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> additional ones one unit above or below those. These total <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.</li>
<li><b>Case 3</b>: The only remaining lines pass through two points, a vertex and a non-vertex point on the opposite side. Thus we have each vertex pairing up with two points on the two opposites sides, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e7a1f54e5eb742563df52e8d0f8a4ee122b4d03.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 2 = 8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" /> lines.</li></ul>
<p>These add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9a501d8b114e1c14b4554f31ba557e8a7c6f4d7.png" class="latex" alt="$6+6+8=20\ \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/a/09a5a10dc7fdac73be4b61960e9cd52d5eeafe34.png" class="latex" alt="${9 \choose 2} = 36$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" /> ways to pick two points, but we've clearly overcounted all of the lines which pass through three points. In fact, each line which passes through three points will have been counted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/9/219c461b1263a35860ae36426f27dd8234771b9a.png" class="latex" alt="${3 \choose 2} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="70" height="53" /> times, so we have to subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> for each of these lines. Quick counting yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> horizontal, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> vertical, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> diagonal lines, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/4/ce4a5185c309518274c045f6669451459ad6b949.png" class="latex" alt="$36 - 2(3+3+2) = 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" /> distinct lines.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>First consider how many lines go through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/6155e28d15217ab6e8ea537771fdce74d647ed6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and hit two points in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. You can see that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> such lines. Now, we cross out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/6155e28d15217ab6e8ea537771fdce74d647ed6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and make sure to never consider consider lines that go through it anymore (As doing so would be double counting). Repeat for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2671c3350cb05610366963fe0cb7fb94293b3e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, making sure not to count the vertical line as it goes through the crossed out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/6155e28d15217ab6e8ea537771fdce74d647ed6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />. Then cross out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/2/ae2671c3350cb05610366963fe0cb7fb94293b3e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. Repeat for the rest, and count <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> lines in total.
</p>
