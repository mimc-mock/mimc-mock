<p>For how many ordered pairs of positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/a/dbad3805de93760ebdc38c2c51b2d34a07f56d74.png" class="latex" alt="$x + 2y = 100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="102" height="16" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db775ef60fe9c65f846dd93bf8d9dafb61d2f44.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)} 33 \qquad \text {(B)} 49 \qquad \text {(C)} 50 \qquad \text {(D)} 99 \qquad \text {(E)}100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Every integer value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> leads to an integer solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must be positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/1/04145f1af381dbfe213e557b3337822bc8a80279.png" class="latex" alt="$y\geq 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />
</p><p>Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/5/6f5ba84656ad6bb2d4f62838bd8387e2d414ac23.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{100-x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must be positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/3/1e39e0d68dfbdb7af6e057358f4b406fb5fc13bf.png" class="latex" alt="$y &lt; 50$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/c/5bc2dd6d517a3e06e2d63badcb26c0ba9353e22a.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \leq y &lt; 50$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />
This leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> values for y, which mean there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> solutions to the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/e/41e1a33aa98a1c90339a098b97ad62b20f590fb8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cab3f0704de3232f601b03d02a45d46103c172.png" class="latex" alt="$2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" /> must each be positive integers, then we can say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is at least 1 and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cab3f0704de3232f601b03d02a45d46103c172.png" class="latex" alt="$2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" /> is at least 1. From there, we want to find out how many ways there are to distribute the other 98 ones (the smallest positive integer addends of 100). 98 identical objects can be distributed to two distinct bins in 99 ways (think stars and bars), yet this 99 is an overcount. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must be an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cab3f0704de3232f601b03d02a45d46103c172.png" class="latex" alt="$2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" /> must be even; thus only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84e72208cd73e879e897f24268aad71f8b90a59d.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{99}{2} \right\rfloor = \boxed{ 49 \implies B}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="161" height="43" /> ways exist to distribute these ones.
</p>
