<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Plane" title="Plane">plane</a> contains points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a649aee592a9c37d2f9b5d5239f5e06e533130.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Union" title="Union">union</a> of all disks of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in the plane that cover <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/d/27d93713b2c8dc66868e447f12a3f3690fa43145.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 2\pi + \sqrt3 \qquad \textbf {(B) } \frac {8\pi}{3} \qquad \textbf {(C) } 3\pi - \frac {\sqrt3}{2} \qquad \textbf {(D) } \frac {10\pi}{3} - \sqrt3 \qquad \textbf {(E) }4\pi - 2\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="625" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35cef40efa03fb5dbaf36f6512271569051c0f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A=(-.5,0), B=(.5,0), C=(0,3**(.5)/2), D=(0,-3**(.5)/2);  draw(arc(A,2,-60,60),blue); draw(arc(B,2,120,240),blue); draw(circle(C,1),red);  draw(A--(.5,3^.5)); draw(B--(-.5,3^.5)); draw(A--(.5,-3^.5)); draw(B--(-.5,-3^.5)); draw(A--B);  dot(A);dot(B);dot(C);dot(D);  label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(0,0),N); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+D/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+C/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+D/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+C/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+3D/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+3C/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+3D/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+3C/2,E);  label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,W); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,E); [/asy]" width="212" height="252" /></center>
<p>As the red circles move about segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, they cover the area we are looking for.
On the left side, the circle must move around pivoted on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.
On the right side, the circle must move pivoted on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />
However, at the top and bottom, the circle must lie on both A and B, giving us our upper and lower bounds.
</p><p>This egg-like shape is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/6/736dfffedb1372a1342c41b76b561f24e133e9fd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A=(-.5,0), B=(.5,0), C=(0,3**(.5)/2), D=(0,-3**(.5)/2);  draw(arc(A,2,-60,60),blue); draw(arc(B,2,120,240),blue); draw(arc(C,1,60,120),red); draw(arc(D,1,-120,-60),red);  draw(A--(.5,3^.5)); draw(B--(-.5,3^.5)); draw(A--(.5,-3^.5)); draw(B--(-.5,-3^.5)); draw(A--B);  dot(A);dot(B);dot(C);dot(D); label(&quot;\(A\)&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;\(B\)&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;\(C\)&quot;,C,W); label(&quot;\(D\)&quot;,D,E);  label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,(0,0),N); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+D/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+C/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+D/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+C/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+3D/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,A/2+3C/2,W); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+3D/2,E); label(&quot;\(1\)&quot;,B/2+3C/2,E); [/asy]" width="202" height="252" /></center>
<p>The area of the region can be found by dividing it into several sectors, namely
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/7/807594c4f0b1dc1e4a857cabc341db3b94386c71.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} A &amp;= 2(\mathrm{Blue\ Sector}) + 2(\mathrm{Red\ Sector}) - 2(\mathrm{Equilateral\ Triangle}) \\ A &amp;= 2\left(\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} \cdot \pi (2)^2\right) + 2\left(\frac{60^\circ}{360^\circ} \cdot \pi (1)^2\right) - 2\left(\frac{(1)^2\sqrt{3}}{4}\right) \\ A &amp;= \frac{8\pi}{3} + \frac{\pi}{3} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \\ A &amp;= 3\pi - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \Longrightarrow \textbf {(C)}\end{align*}" width="486" height="175" />
</p>
