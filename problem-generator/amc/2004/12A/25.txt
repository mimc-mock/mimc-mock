<p>For each integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d034b51294ef53e016b9d7fefd837a865e52d2e.png" class="latex" alt="$n\geq 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="14" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> denote the base-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/94955dabf51a58a99d620be595fe8f76c1fed7e4.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{133}_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="17" />. The product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/c/32c613b71010035e4172dfe1fb4b952af14972d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4a_5\cdots a_{99}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="10" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e9e139a14911866cbc0ed0733fafc93ed9828c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {m}{n!}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="18" height="33" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are positive integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is as small as possible. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/3/8537979f216242f8c3042329a94920aa7e8d38f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)} 98 \qquad \text {(B)} 101 \qquad \text {(C)} 132\qquad \text {(D)} 798\qquad \text {(E)}962$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="384" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>This is an infinite <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_series" class="mw-redirect" title="Geometric series">geometric series</a> with common ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/c/16cbf94dd75d7e1d5f7bab6ff540b085826ad77c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{x^3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> and initial term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/515bb31b17af15c0c6fc74dbf81b7065e37291ea.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{-1} + 3x^{-2} + 3x^{-3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="146" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e0695b2f3eb4e2240b38d0863b274d651134df3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_x = \left(\frac{1}{x} + \frac{3}{x^2} + \frac{3}{x^3}\right)\left(\frac{1}{1-\frac{1}{x^3}}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="249" height="45" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3bb744f9930e9fdb9c8a884cf0b76376fcdba15.png" class="latex" alt="$= \frac{x^2 + 3x + 3}{x^3} \cdot \frac{x^3}{x^3 - 1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="178" height="39" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/f/52f7a33bbaf653a2718b0fafde37e2fef8904404.png" class="latex" alt="$= \frac{x^2 + 3x + 3}{x^3 - 1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="112" height="39" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27a3cd93be79b63df77dd900cbe6a4d794bb8895.png" class="latex" alt="$= \frac{(x+1)^3 - 1}{x(x^3 - 1)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="116" height="43" />.
</p><p>Alternatively, we could have used the algebraic manipulation for repeating decimals, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/2/f023e74460b3c730d118447967e7f6d1fa6e661b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a_x &amp;= \frac{1}{x}+\frac{3}{x^2}+\frac{3}{x^3}+\frac{1}{x^4}+\frac{3}{x^5}+\frac{3}{x^6}+\cdots \\ a_x \cdot x^3 &amp;= x^2+3x+3+a_x\\ a_x(x^3-1) &amp;= x^2+3x+3\\ a_x &amp;= \frac{x^2+3x+3}{x^3-1}=\frac{(x+1)^3-1}{x(x^3-1)} \end{align*}" width="381" height="142" />
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Telescoping" class="mw-redirect" title="Telescoping">Telescoping</a>, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/0/580618e7c0cd904ca791d38be470ebde43342988.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a_4a_5\cdots a_{99}&amp;= \frac{(5^3-1)(6^3-1)\cdots (100^3-1)}{4 \cdot 5 \cdot 6 \cdot \cdots \cdot 99 \cdot (4^3-1)(5^3-1)\cdots(99^3-1)}\\ a_4a_5\cdots a_{99}&amp;= \frac{999999}{4 \cdot 5 \cdot 6 \cdot \cdots \cdot 99 \cdot 63}=\frac{13 \cdot 37 \cdot 33 \cdot 6}{99!}\end{align*}" width="466" height="87" />
</p><p>Some factors cancel, (after all, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7e40ee2ffe8b5f8ec0898d2da93d56515ecca2.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \cdot 37 \cdot 33 \cdot 6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="102" height="13" /> isn't one of the answer choices)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/8/52810daca693273a5f69bfa46b11f70a730759a9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{13 \cdot 37 \cdot 33 \cdot 6}{99!}=\frac{13 \cdot 37 \cdot 2}{98!}\]" width="203" height="37" />
</p><p>Since the only factor in the numerator that goes into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/73119f04d6ae5f6eb4afc8c9c946a5744a11be50.png" class="latex" alt="$98$" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is minimized. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/114b8acae2d762b64c1f81dcdd23919140d1fd56.png" class="latex" alt="$13 \cdot 37 \cdot 2=962 \Rightarrow \text {(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="17" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/1/5515d34ff47fe7730ad2872fdaf7bd36f2fe557d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0.\overline{133}_n = \frac{n^2+3n+3}{n^3-1},\]" width="174" height="39" />by geometric series.
Thus, we're aiming to find the value of<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e270e2d083dd4dce4e9acc3f793d437362f5d0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\prod_{k=4}^{99} \frac{k^2+3k+3}{k^3 - 1}.\]" width="124" height="50" />Expanding the product out, this is equivalent to
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/b/fdbc97466d7e7c98aeedc597122d6c3d3dc7cf06.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{4^2+3(4)+3}{4^3 - 1} \cdot \frac{5^2+3(5)+3}{5^3 - 1} \cdot \frac{6^2+3(6)+3}{6^3 - 1} \cdot ... \cdot \frac{99^2+3(99)+3}{99^3 - 1}.\]" width="480" height="39" />Note that the numerator of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />th fraction and the denominator of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/b/64be5731bafe97ab8fb5d0f9c52c1c5362f963d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" />th fraction for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f25b2323b1014854bd54d77a7cf1b1f666b4f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \leq a \leq 95$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="84" height="15" /> cancel out to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83dde6bf635bb1c7aac5cad1328d5240203e0113.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{a+3},$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="49" height="38" /> since<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/c/9cce8bcb11a2f7ba2512db00a5e1ec243533553e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{k^2 + 3k + 3}{(k+1)^3 - 1} = \frac{k^2 + 3k + 3}{k^3 + 3k^2 + 3k} = \frac{1}{k},\]" width="274" height="43" />by the binomial theorem on the the denominator of the aforementioned. Since this forms a telescoping series, our product is now equivalent to<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/f/79fe4bc33aedfb1a1f5cb5e0705d5f4466cac7e1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{99^2 + 3(99) + 3}{4^3 - 1} \cdot \prod_{k=4}^{98} \frac{1}{k},\]" width="181" height="50" />which, after simplification gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/b/beb78d55e18c0beb1ddf122af3a71f04d6c9de82.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{98!} \cdot \frac{10101}{63} = \frac{962}{98!},$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="149" height="37" /> giving an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/789c2e0453f480ccf4bc112f2d27e2eee5fb4acd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{962}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="42" height="23" />
</p><p>-fidgetboss_4000
</p>
