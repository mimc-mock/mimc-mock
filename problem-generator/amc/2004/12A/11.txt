<p>The average value of all the pennies, nickels, dimes, and quarters in Paula's purse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> cents. If she had one more quarter, the average value would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25d31faf472ddb4c698a14522fe8125c06f8dd8.png" class="latex" alt="$21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> cents. How many dimes does she have in her purse?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/ace0946eff7bae58a53420f105bd10ea56ef9907.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)}\ 0 \qquad \text {(B)}\ 1 \qquad \text {(C)}\ 2 \qquad \text {(D)}\ 3\qquad \text {(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the total value, in cents, of the coins Paula has originally be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f23bf124b6b2b2a993eb313c72e678664ac74a.png" class="latex" alt="$v$" width="9" height="8" />, and the number of coins she has be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/d/0ddbbfc473048a315de0a29d0c8cdc2fc890c4e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{v}{n}=20\Longrightarrow v=20n$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="159" height="33" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/e/3ee3ad79b2c42b424f2173490070af6036f34d32.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{v+25}{n+1}=21$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="95" height="39" />. Substituting yields: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bde48bbffee8f2b1430bf734ec132bf120d93e.png" class="latex" alt="$20n+25=21(n+1),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39cf3e35a4981583288d2a7c4b34989916fb7360.png" class="latex" alt="$n=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/b/27b77351dcc33d1d2530afd7f775e006d62dd8be.png" class="latex" alt="$v = 80.$" width="55" height="12" />  Then, we see that the only way Paula can satisfy this rule is if she had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> quarters and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> nickel in her purse. Thus, she has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/c/0ec361c7e9aeb3c86487803857531536c3380334.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 0}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" /> dimes.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>If the new coin was worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> cents, adding it would not change the mean. The additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cents raise the mean by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, thus the new number of coins must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Therefore there were <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins worth a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/6/276c160bca059e8b8a097deb72d2108192170b75.png" class="latex" alt="$4\times20=80$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> cents. As in the previous solution, we conclude that the only way to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea5fcc6d66685ee21f722efcd228eb44d38f262.png" class="latex" alt="$80$" width="17" height="12" /> cents using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/5/8e5d9f6953af989fc080f598b3c29705e27903a2.png" class="latex" alt="$25+25+25+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="129" height="14" />. Thus, having three quarters, one nickel, and no dimes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/7/c17838d77eefebbceaab087da260cb5100aabf73.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 0}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="57" height="29" />
</p>
