<p>A company sells peanut butter in cylindrical jars.  Marketing research suggests that using wider jars will increase sales.  If the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Diameter" title="Diameter">diameter</a> of the jars is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> without altering the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Volume" title="Volume">volume</a>, by what percent must the height be decreased?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cfb8566d509d929e51f6c91955f4d2dd463d5c.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 10 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 25 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 36 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 50 \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>When the diameter is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" />, it is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/f/92f7fbfd399817ffc78c526f5668e3315ab3d9e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{5}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />, so the area of the base is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/0/540494f89b773134a7b159ce0f7300b26e04da77.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\dfrac54\right)^2=\dfrac{25}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="91" height="46" />.
</p><p>To keep the volume the same, the height must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/7/f1754305c236f5e31c285bd6a17d6fe4b1ce3a10.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{\frac{25}{16}}=\dfrac{16}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="66" height="44" /> of the original height, which is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/9/219d19a2cd739683cc264acbc4340e000f0bfd5c.png" class="latex" alt="$36\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> reduction. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/c/1cc9458b77c3899bd1ec0830ea3f7a3e10e7bc8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 36}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />
</p>
