<p>A game is played with tokens according to the following rule.  In each round, the player with the most tokens gives one token to each of the other players and also places one token in the discard pile.  The game ends when some player runs out of tokens.  Players <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> tokens, respectively.  How many rounds will there be in the game?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/e/6aecdd53c8980dfe4b2f57c756f8559d138d1b05.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 36 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 37 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 38 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 39 \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 40$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>We look at a set of three rounds, where the players begin with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/cccb103143393c0119f5ab61d6d53a852ce70fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1$" width="40" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4baf6d4b1cfa69b68b3204b5e2eba0c5755fe20.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> tokens.
After three rounds, there will be a net loss of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> token per player (they receive two tokens and lose three). Therefore, after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> rounds -- or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> three-round sets, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/6/5f642cbbd390b4882363ab6b5c2906dbf6be6cc8.png" class="latex" alt="$A,B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> tokens, respectively.  After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> more round, player <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> will give away <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> tokens, leaving them empty-handed, and thus the game will end. We then have there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/4/b541ff4bc07ee89a27c98da3172173ec71a76e71.png" class="latex" alt="$36+1=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 37}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="136" height="29" /> rounds until the game ends.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let's bash a few rounds. The amounts are for players <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/8202c680beb60049930968ddb62b65f7cf079118.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, respectively.
</p><p>First round: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec8c176c8bbb4c57ada93e8bf2e171d838bbbd3f.png" class="latex" alt="$15,14,13$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> (given)
Second round: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a08707aa9254e32bfc242706d978bb18b485018f.png" class="latex" alt="$12,15,14$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" />
Third round: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/a/38aa5f2d4001fa9a4806bc24d2a7d2638f4c252a.png" class="latex" alt="$13,12,15$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" />
Fourth round: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/7566a88f41e5440cb0859d03b1149456f731c1c7.png" class="latex" alt="$14,13,12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" />
</p><p>We see that after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> rounds are played, we have the exact same scenario as the first round but with one token less per player. So, the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/1/c31265c834a6ad48036c5801f601d5fde337b517.png" class="latex" alt="$1,4,7,10...$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" /> where each of the next members are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> greater than the previous one corresponds with the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/9/9e9714d513d6572390f633d21bc6b594fdb1fd9c.png" class="latex" alt="$15,14,13,12...$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="110" height="16" /> where the first sequence represents the round and the second sequence represents the number of tokens player <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> has. But we note that once player <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> reaches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> coins, the game will end on his next turn as he must give away all his coins. Therefore, we want the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/3/2c3d6243148e8395894e7e0fc1081dc8e9cc3a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$15-3+1=13$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />th number in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/1/c31265c834a6ad48036c5801f601d5fde337b517.png" class="latex" alt="$1,4,7,10...$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/b/6abd080fdc8ff2c30993cc5c0f18eceeb4d27999.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 37}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
</p><p>Solution by franzliszt
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Looking at a set of five rounds, you'll see <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> fewer tokens than in the beginning. Looking at four more rounds, you'll notice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> has the same amount of tokens, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, compared to round five. If you keep doing this process, you'll see a pattern: Every four rounds, the amount of tokens <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> has either decreased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> or stayed the same compared to the previous four rounds. For example, in round nine, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> tokens, in round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> tokens, and in round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> tokens, etc. Using this weird pattern, you can find out that in round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> should have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> tokens, but since they would have given them away in that round, the game would end on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/b/6abd080fdc8ff2c30993cc5c0f18eceeb4d27999.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 37}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
</p><p>This strategy is a very slow one, requiring you to list out about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> rounds.
</p>
