<p>Camilla had twice as many blueberry jelly beans as cherry jelly beans. After eating 10 pieces of each kind, she now has three times as many blueberry jelly beans as cherry jelly beans. How many blueberry jelly beans did she originally have?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/751cca8d6cbbf8b32c3ea521b7d9f6b288e4063d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 20\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 40\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Denote the number of blueberry and cherry jelly beans as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> respectively. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/4934ae4c40d6f0fc684aeed2d9d5491586706a40.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 2c$" width="49" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/8/138d02aaf27f0d51f1c42a6857fe2f074c5b3370.png" class="latex" alt="$b-10 = 3(c-10)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="18" />. Substituting, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/d/d4da1b005a98ef5b0d2a497047306f845e708f48.png" class="latex" alt="$2c-10 = 3c-30$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="138" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/7/407f553fe0e13d106e11399b9865e0a4c9a7be97.png" class="latex" alt="$c=20$" width="49" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/6/896581592b1ebaffbc135092104d3679bba3e04c.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 40}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="97" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>From the problem, we see that 10 less than one of the answer choices must be a multiple of 3 and positive. The only answer choice satisfying this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efa97cde8cf03dda74625cbcd3055eff4ace936e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 40}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />. We can check that 40 blueberry and 20 cherry jelly beans indeed does work.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that the number of jellybeans minus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/7/6379c48cba58821b5fc27f5394d099539056edee.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 10 = 20$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and positive. Therefore, the number of jellybeans is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/6/f06cc069e89ba00917d239f101eb9ada9808a43c.png" class="latex" alt="$&gt;20$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. The only answer choice satisfying this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efa97cde8cf03dda74625cbcd3055eff4ace936e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 40}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
