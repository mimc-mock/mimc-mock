<p>Mary thought of a positive two-digit number. She multiplied it by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and added <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Then she switched the digits of the result, obtaining a number between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" />, inclusive. What was Mary's number? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/3/8339bfd778ccd475ef5e201a99364b29e5003095.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 11\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 13\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 14\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let her <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />-digit number be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> makes it a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, meaning that the sum of its digits is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Adding on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> increases the sum of the digits by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/2/3724c72b495927cdf30766977d7e1b80c28324bb.png" class="latex" alt="$1+1 = 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="15" /> (we can ignore numbers such as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/7/4d79fb19ef93982da62a012db4a7a9d36d9115fc.png" class="latex" alt="$39+11=50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" />) and reversing the digits keeps the sum of the digits the same; this means that the resulting number must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> more than a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. There are two such numbers between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea9b58be20255fc7a367ccf62d1b60384f0b624e.png" class="latex" alt="$74.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> Now that we have narrowed down the choices, we can simply test the answers to see which one will provide a two-digit number when the steps are reversed:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa9fa1f5612d7d7d238f3a9161cf72451c2a8a36.png" class="latex" alt="$71,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="15" /> we reverse the digits, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55d9112910335dfe813b84b8d9db66a08dcf5a2f.png" class="latex" alt="$17.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/e/1de3ed5fb7fa9b1fa73395d65c43e5bc81cd9d61.png" class="latex" alt="$6.$" width="12" height="12" /> We can already see that dividing this by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> will not be a two-digit number, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> does not meet our requirements.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
Therefore, the answer must be the reversed steps applied to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea9b58be20255fc7a367ccf62d1b60384f0b624e.png" class="latex" alt="$74.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> We have the following:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abe8c018d835cba8b6691f4e981db34eb837a94c.png" class="latex" alt="$74\rightarrow47\rightarrow36\rightarrow12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="157" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/9/219de30318b3d8dadb300c7b7f6e349e34d523b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\bold{(B)} 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="59" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Working backwards, we reverse the digits of each number from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/c/38c4bc29a5a0d6756b52b0277d9cd07e756225c8.png" class="latex" alt="$71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />~<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> and subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> from each, so we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/4/f1470bf84cc79a1ad9170b8ff6a076ee889b0b71.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6, 16, 26, 36, 46\]" width="113" height="16" />
The only numbers from this list that are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />. We divide both by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is not a two-digit number, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719579edfecf49b3044de7dd96766f4f59eedb8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>You can just plug in the numbers to see which one works. When you get to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, you multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a21383a2b05dbe4ed4ecd095273f0cabc32b78a6.png" class="latex" alt="$47$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. When you reverse the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a21383a2b05dbe4ed4ecd095273f0cabc32b78a6.png" class="latex" alt="$47$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/1/90100e7ec57be3145070551137e9c47224adb4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$74$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, which is within the given range. Thus, the answer is  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719579edfecf49b3044de7dd96766f4f59eedb8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
