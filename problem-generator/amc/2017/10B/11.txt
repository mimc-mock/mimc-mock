<p>At Typico High School, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/7/fd7edd3842c40dbc1e54e522917a6e07b43c92e2.png" class="latex" alt="$60\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the students like dancing, and the rest dislike it. Of those who like dancing, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/7/bb7e03ccc8985ba9d029ea4add6e3e15ab9a953a.png" class="latex" alt="$80\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> say that they like it, and the rest say that they dislike it. Of those who dislike dancing, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/870303feff6f491471061a7402eba3e326ba84f2.png" class="latex" alt="$90\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> say that they dislike it, and the rest say that they like it. What fraction of students who say they dislike dancing actually like it?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/c/d9cf7ef3caeb80bf4554aed5a7ac90b1ef6ed57b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10\%\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 12\%\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 20\%\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 25\%\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 33\frac{1}{3}\%$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="483" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/9/ad97a432193998cdfc0a95b74c4020b22bc0b5ef.png" class="latex" alt="$60\% \cdot 20\% = 12\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="14" /> of the people that claim that they like dancing say they dislike it, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/8/4b8209b610be49c76532d526f581a4c1d38c448a.png" class="latex" alt="$40\% \cdot 90\% = 36\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="14" /> of the people that claim that they dislike dancing actually dislike it. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/3/fe3f2b9d074019cf706990b3050c7145dc07ca19.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12\%}{12\%+36\%} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 25\%}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="198" height="40" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Assume WLOG that there are 100 people. Then 60 of them like dancing, 40 dislike dancing. Of the ones that like dancing, 48 say they like dancing and 12 say they dislike it. Of the ones who dislike dancing, 36 say they dislike dancing and 4 say they like it. We want the ratio of students like it but say they dislike it to the total amount of students that say they dislike it. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e6dd5c955df6012fb7ad6d8b211f17d1619938.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{12+36}=\frac{12}{48}=\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="148" height="40" />. We choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/8/1a83bbf71b192741ca183ddaee6f5850edee9601.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 25\%}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="82" height="31" />
</p>
