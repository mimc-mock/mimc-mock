<p>Call a positive integer <b>monotonous</b> if it is a one-digit number or its digits, when read from left to right, form either a strictly increasing or a strictly decreasing sequence. For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/4/1b434575dc6aa6c68d975b7fb9201bef7fca7b13.png" class="latex" alt="$23578$" width="44" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/f/a5fea9c440f25f8613ea5441834196f44d9348b9.png" class="latex" alt="$987620$" width="53" height="12" /> are monotonous, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40d924a5d0ade21a3824a7f0343f8beae70ef96e.png" class="latex" alt="$88$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/9/92971c235de6400abd188d667b4b95716dea3e4d.png" class="latex" alt="$7434$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/8/088010e79a13e9876ce87dcc6bd4eb5ae4e40729.png" class="latex" alt="$23557$" width="45" height="12" /> are not. How many monotonous positive integers are there?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/2/c624a9f24c95546edef40e766769b914a9fbe977.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1024\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1524\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1533\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 1536\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2048$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Case 1: monotonous numbers with digits in ascending order
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/9/0498b83f705df82b0fad58755670cf060b1c8e35.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=1}^{9} \binom{9}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="70" height="55" /> ways to choose n digits from the digits 1 to 9. For each of these ways, we can generate exactly one monotonous number by ordering the chosen digits in ascending order. Note that 0 is not included since it will always be a leading digit and that is not allowed. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/2/84241c448cbf497968ba86136beecf766431a3ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\emptyset$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="16" /> (the empty set) isn't included because it doesn't generate a number. The sum is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/c/c9cf1627ca7c846505645e03cf9fde1a4358b802.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=0}^{9} \binom{9}{n} -\binom{9}{0} = 2^9 - 1 = 511.$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="258" height="55" />
</p><p>Case 2: monotonous numbers with digits in descending order
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/e/2dea4a4aa4dfcee63390715574d49f68cba82240.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=1}^{10} \binom{10}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="77" height="55" /> ways to choose n digits from the digits 0 to 9. For each of these ways, we can generate exactly one monotonous number by ordering the chosen digits in descending order. Note that 0 is included since we are allowed to end numbers with zeros. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/2/84241c448cbf497968ba86136beecf766431a3ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\emptyset$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="8" height="16" /> (the empty set) still isn't included because it doesn't generate a number. The sum is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/10877bb19bfe95a50c1c878c60042c341f4cc756.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=0}^{10} \binom{10}{n} -\binom{10}{0} = 2^{10} - 1 = 1023.$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="290" height="55" /> We discard the number 0 since it is not positive. Thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/9/d19f6c66057ecac4ed06919ad4718379419937d6.png" class="latex" alt="$1022$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> here. 
</p><p>Since the 1-digit numbers 1 to 9 satisfy both case 1 and case 2, we have overcounted by 9. Thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/d/fbd13c5260241c25858d926bb9bf4c96ed30d0c4.png" class="latex" alt="$511+1022-9=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }  1524}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="226" height="30" /> monotonous numbers.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Like Solution 1, divide the problem into an increasing and decreasing case:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/3/d534b942086dae501cbdce030206adb87567bb07.png" class="latex" alt="$\bullet$" style="vertical-align: 1px" width="8" height="7" /> Case 1: Monotonous numbers with digits in ascending order.
</p><p>Arrange the digits 1 through 9 in increasing order, and exclude 0 because a positive integer cannot begin with 0.
</p><p>To get a monotonous number, we can either include or exclude each of the remaining 9 digits, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cf2f75e211d8030dc8a4d4d8304752565b5e403.png" class="latex" alt="$2^9 = 512$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="15" /> ways to do this. However, we cannot exclude every digit at once, so we subtract 1 to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/a/52af322aba3854f6330221cad8dfbebb820c6f52.png" class="latex" alt="$512-1=511$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> monotonous numbers for this case.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/3/d534b942086dae501cbdce030206adb87567bb07.png" class="latex" alt="$\bullet$" style="vertical-align: 1px" width="8" height="7" /> Case 2: Monotonous numbers with digits in descending order.
</p><p>This time, we arrange all 10 digits in decreasing order and repeat the process to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/8/cf856775e3de1168a9a4f29bc68d3651d0bf6051.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{10} = 1024$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="15" /> ways to include or exclude each digit. We cannot exclude every digit at once, and we cannot include only 0, so we subtract 2 to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4f942a8028167fd24032059f227ed76fc3505e.png" class="latex" alt="$1024-2=1022$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="127" height="13" /> monotonous numbers for this case.
</p><p>At this point, we have counted all of the single-digit monotonous numbers twice, so we must subtract 9 from our total.
</p><p>Thus our final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f8fa083a17aaa4fec1aebe240143b36834c7401.png" class="latex" alt="$511+1022-9 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 1524}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="224" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Unlike the first two solutions, we can do our casework based off of whether or not the number contains a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>If it does, then we know the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> must be the last digit in the number (and hence, the number has to be decreasing). Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> is not positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> is not monotonous. So, we need to pick at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> number in the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33a34a0af5d3cf7bc716e26858e228975bcc51f.png" class="latex" alt="$[1, 9].$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="39" height="18" /> After choosing our numbers, there will be just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> way to arrange them so that the overall number is monotonous.
</p><p>In total, each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> digits can either be in the monotonous number or not, yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cf2f75e211d8030dc8a4d4d8304752565b5e403.png" class="latex" alt="$2^9 = 512$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="15" /> total solutions. However, we said earlier that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot be by itself so we have to subtract out the case in which we picked none of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/3856d888046107ef12e2b33c2065737e0b2602d3.png" class="latex" alt="$1-9$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. So, this case gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/6/06681161db752e0642652340b0832dcdaecefde0.png" class="latex" alt="$511$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. 
</p><p><br />
Onto the second case, if there are no <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />s, then the number can either be arranged in ascending order or in descending order. So, for each selection of the digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/d/58d951490213efb4dcf862ee54de5c6f3f46d7ef.png" class="latex" alt="$1- 9$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions. This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/19551d27ea0f7c03247ca968a11b2bd29865f143.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2 \cdot (2^9 - 1) = 2 \cdot 511 = 1022\]" width="217" height="19" /> possibilities. Note that we subtracted out the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> because we cannot choose none of the numbers. 
</p><p>However, realize that if we pick just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> digit, then there is only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> arrangement. We cannot put a single digit in both ascending and descending order. So, we must subtract out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> from there (because there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> possible ways to select one number and for each case, we overcounted by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />). 
</p><p>All in all, that gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/e/aeeaa6084f175cc3c76e7a398640e6268f40f0d5.png" class="latex" alt="$511 + 1022 - 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)  } 1524}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="226" height="30" /> monotonous numbers.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of digits of a monotonous number. Notice for an increasing monotonous number with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/317096889b84df0b72bf69ceb4c82208305f1b0f.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, we can obtain 2 more monotonous numbers that are decreasing by reversing its digits and adding a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to the end of the reversed digits. Whenever <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> digits are chosen, the order is fixed. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/e/92e926b02fd124589a6ae74e418a72720a5574ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{9}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="35" height="53" /> ways to obtain an increasing monotonous number with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> digits. So, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/9/7c92c61059f41c48e9d95b5b3cd4094044b944d6.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot \sum_{n=2}^{9} \binom{9}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="92" height="55" /> monotonous numbers when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/317096889b84df0b72bf69ceb4c82208305f1b0f.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />. When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, there is no reverse but we could add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to the end, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/1/151bde0758d8a9f77211cfcd1e8b2aaa3da1d54c.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \binom{9}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="61" height="55" /> monotonous numbers.
</p><p>The answer is:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b8feeb95401dedda21e7b7f0d4266bb42c841d.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot \sum_{n=2}^{9} \binom{9}{n} + 2 \cdot \binom{9}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="173" height="55" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f5817bc9b73129982f9ae0b3f1413cfe86d41b33.png" class="latex" alt="$=3\cdot \sum_{n=1}^{9} \binom{9}{n}  -  \binom{9}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="170" height="55" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/6/a6639791477b01739ddeb62686f50dbb17a49e23.png" class="latex" alt="$=3\cdot \left( \sum_{n=0}^{9} \binom{9}{n} - \binom{9}{0} \right) -  \binom{9}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="258" height="55" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2574014f4fa3d5e20a5c3ba0c5efb70f05b633.png" class="latex" alt="$= 3 \cdot (2^9-1) - 9$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="137" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/3/f033a9324f200ec5cf5dbd61b90a3a5a2efc7557.png" class="latex" alt="$=\boxed{\textbf{(B)  } 1524}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="103" height="30" />
</p><p>~<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/User:Isabelchen">isabelchen</a>
</p>
