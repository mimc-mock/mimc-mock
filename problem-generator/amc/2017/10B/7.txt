<p>Samia set off on her bicycle to visit her friend, traveling at an average speed of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> kilometers per hour. When she had gone half the distance to her friend's house, a tire went flat, and she walked the rest of the way at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> kilometers per hour. In all it took her <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b45828f292e161290be6ee62bc4c9df081ee1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> minutes to reach her friend's house. In kilometers rounded to the nearest tenth, how far did Samia walk?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/a/5fa0cc14450154c38f9c267ac9e615adb528af45.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2.0\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2.2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2.8\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 3.4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 4.4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="419" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let's call the distance that Samia had to travel in total as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" />, so that we can avoid fractions. We know that the length of the bike ride and how far she walked are equal, so they are both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/f/bff2503349c04193e2e860350bbef7e096929e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="21" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
She bikes at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> kph, so she travels the distance she bikes in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/d/64d8669d2c454ee9d2650969955aac5ff4412f59.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{17}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="33" /> hours. She walks at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> kph, so she travels the distance she walks in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/e/2ce02ca58255dfe23311218ac9a9ea5268e5bc4e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> hours.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
The total time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/8/8680815b1b96bbdc20a1f9eca057d5784bcbdeb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{17}+\frac{x}{5} = \frac{22x}{85}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="37" />. This is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/7294d001f56c635b1aa3621ba38e0c7ff1321218.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{44}{60} = \frac{11}{15}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="37" /> of an hour. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/4/1047df3d263df2f4101416c6bbd2350f5b3fe49f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{22x}{85} = \frac{11}{15}\]" width="75" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abea6c21f569b9e8506517f21781841f11a2cf64.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2x}{85} = \frac{1}{15}\]" width="66" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/d/7ddd1cf0135652196e90aa16b630babae2eb6214.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[30x = 85\]" width="70" height="12" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608bd4ededb3cc5b4bdfb775e9ac91ab4633320a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6x = 17\]" width="61" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/3/d9360642a7e793f542984bb113dba97831767793.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = \frac{17}{6}\]" width="54" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the distance of how far Samia traveled by both walking and biking, and we want to know how far Samia walked to the nearest tenth, we have that Samia walked about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1ede7d6992df1c11d9d1bce95de42a5dad364d80.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\bold{(C)} 2.8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="29" />.
