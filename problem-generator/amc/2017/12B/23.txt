<p>The graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/e/97eded22ccc3e54189a8f157c4e81f36fe034a26.png" class="latex" alt="$y=f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> is a polynomial of degree <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, contains points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/49417b70a6415912a26eb6ff6d69202ed6f0cbf3.png" class="latex" alt="$A(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/b/cbb31389480c8e62f2ffa839797ed130bcb1f415.png" class="latex" alt="$B(3,9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1c28c9fccab20cbb1bbb892bf47844517bbe25.png" class="latex" alt="$C(4,16)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />. Lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> intersect the graph again at points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />, respectively, and the sum of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> is 24. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/f/fbff8c2d6247813a08aab2e4c24e40178d8e1bcd.png" class="latex" alt="$f(0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/d/b1df74d0c389ebbda73603adf0068959454cd335.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\quad {-2} \qquad \qquad \textbf{(B)}\quad 0 \qquad\qquad \textbf{(C)}\quad 2 \qquad\qquad \textbf{(D)}\quad \dfrac{24}5 \qquad\qquad\textbf{(E)}\quad 8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="564" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, we can define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/0/560a757116d4672cfb2b6260c5871fbd5e8e5ae5.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = a(x-2)(x-3)(x-4) +x^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="275" height="19" />, which contains points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Now we find that lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> are defined by the equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/4/6b4d7331f0290770f6f5a817a54f48e512689b87.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 5x - 6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/8/be82b3f212433231911c54d9fc2953ce14958edf.png" class="latex" alt="$y= 6x-8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/c/66cc6860143b96a870b58d486a6eeadcf65e87b9.png" class="latex" alt="$y=7x-12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="15" /> respectively. Since we want to find the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates of the intersections of these lines and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we set each of them to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, and synthetically divide by the solutions we already know exist (eg. if we were looking at line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, we would synthetically divide by the solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69271b422e022ea65ff1f288eddfcfa82afd8ab7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/2/b022dc8ef6d33931fc1ae3d27df3ea0a81b52936.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3$" width="43" height="12" />, because we already know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> intersects the graph at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, which have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />). After completing this process on all three lines, we get that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/f/d5fe6c6fa0562009c8a1a821a181c9ba7b504426.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4a-1}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/4/aa44a11a37ded35af9c9f8b68abe5f2620693937.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3a-1}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/2/b72df57ef3954b639b4a1bb04b3ac66ec8db23ae.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2a-1}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" /> respectively. Adding these together, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/6/456433e9800ca7cde48d1ed0a13e4b1114d836de.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9a-3}{a} = 24$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" /> which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/e/3ee10ce12345eb94c2205abc5d0e64b7355de0da.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -\frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />. Substituting this back into the original equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59e628bfe53a1356fff61b851cda0dc000776739.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = -\frac{1}{5}(x-2)(x-3)(x-4) + x^2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="293" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba125bad293f5ccf10fd35410d2a717607a42b1.png" class="latex" alt="$f(0) = -\frac{1}{5}(-2)(-3)(-4) + 0 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\frac{24}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="316" height="48" />
</p><p>Solution by vedadehhc
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/c/52c753db7b5834deef1856a9308efb2b35686a9c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{No need to find the equations for the lines, really.}}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="430" height="26" /> First of all, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/0/560a757116d4672cfb2b6260c5871fbd5e8e5ae5.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = a(x-2)(x-3)(x-4) +x^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="275" height="19" />. Let's say the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/6/416ae37947d4f62622823098de22e5fcf4a0b875.png" class="latex" alt="$y=bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="82" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/ccada11db7b2b90693e2fac4f887a57fce6f96bf.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinate of the third intersection, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/ccada11db7b2b90693e2fac4f887a57fce6f96bf.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> are the three roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/04571db68a22dab0fa09678dcdc42238b9adf289.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) - bx-c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" />. The values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> have no effect on the sum of the 3 roots, because the coefficient of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> term is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e467ff84851b69938fc78114dec3c2631b31f6.png" class="latex" alt="$-9a+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="14" />. So we have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/8/0685d1e843414378603594ff29704116028df317.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{9a-1}{a} = 2 + 3 + x_1= 3 + 4 + x_2 = 2 + 4 + x_3\]" width="364" height="37" />
Adding all three equations up, we get 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5ee00bed488cc87f7f3ff6b62e3a8341b8687bb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3\left(\frac{9a-1}{a}\right) = 18 + x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 18 + 24\]" width="337" height="43" />
Solving this equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/e/3ee10ce12345eb94c2205abc5d0e64b7355de0da.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -\frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" />. We finish as Solution 1 does.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709bc1b00a6f5abf67b335c2305576a4d9191408.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\frac{24}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="63" height="48" />.
