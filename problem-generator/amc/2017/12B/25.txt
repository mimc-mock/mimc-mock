<p>A set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> people participate in an online video basketball tournament. Each person may be a member of any number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-player teams, but no two teams may have exactly the same <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> members. The site statistics show a curious fact: The average, over all subsets of size <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> of the set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> participants, of the number of complete teams whose members are among those <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> people is equal to the reciprocal of the average, over all subsets of size <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> of the set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> participants, of the number of complete teams whose members are among those <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> people. How many values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/1/ad1013614d7d2bda4b3e829b8294b338713acbfc.png" class="latex" alt="$9\leq n\leq 2017$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="104" height="15" />, can be the number of participants?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b8466e5baae20e8ed08d7d4a7887327258820ccd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 477 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 482 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 487 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 557 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 562$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let there be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> teams. For each team, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/6/5c6f9d289dd1f66c220a93ab22073ebf60d7c5f0.png" class="latex" alt="${n-5\choose 4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="66" height="53" /> different subsets of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> players including that full team, so the total number of team-(group of 9) pairs is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/a/dea29aebd3e741317d1da6d6d5e0d039c865b66c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T{n-5\choose 4}.\]" width="87" height="53" />
</p><p>Thus, the expected value of the number of full teams in a random set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> players is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/3/473fe9b2ab47679ee71a8d44dd0f224bf8a8d2d3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{T{n-5\choose 4}}{{n\choose 9}}.\]" width="60" height="48" />
</p><p>Similarly, the expected value of the number of full teams in a random set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> players is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/b/7bb2aa4cfe97d1e2b11de995288c1cb51ad54fb1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{T{n-5\choose 3}}{{n\choose 8}}.\]" width="60" height="48" />
</p><p>The condition is thus equivalent to the existence of a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> such that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e11179484370cc6d51c3464865a025e00b72cb72.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{T{n-5\choose 4}}{{n\choose 9}}\frac{T{n-5\choose 3}}{{n\choose 8}} = 1.\]" width="152" height="48" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59fde910a2530687da424bd1f4bfde3bde37b66f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T^2\frac{(n-5)!(n-5)!8!9!(n-8)!(n-9)!}{n!n!(n-8)!(n-9)!3!4!} = 1\]" width="331" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1cfec8977bb6d01c6a6576afb77a0a77fd91f18.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T^2 = \big((n)(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)\big)^2 \frac{3!4!}{8!9!}\]" width="349" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/3/b53d5744d9e02f1bdad30ba0f1af2b4082f6a644.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T^2 = \big((n)(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)\big)^2 \frac{144}{7!7!8\cdot8\cdot9}\]" width="403" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/5/fa5ea4bb436228ff0dd76d5fe38fd75453c0fc46.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T^2 = \big((n)(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)\big)^2 \frac{1}{4\cdot7!7!}\]" width="372" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc16b7255557e77c2cb36057a28665397194727.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[T = \frac{(n)(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)}{2^5\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7}\]" width="290" height="38" />
</p><p>Note that this is always less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a4623444434eaafbefa35ac6ccb1b58f091ec802.png" class="latex" alt="${n\choose 5}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="35" height="53" />, so as long as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> is integral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a possibility. Thus, we have that this is equivalent to 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5c769adf9021769328a6400141bc1d703873a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2^5\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7\big|(n)(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4).\]" width="350" height="22" />
</p><p>It is obvious that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> divides the RHS, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> does iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/c/65c04b47a4e567ed9b3f404ee2cc795a054ecdfd.png" class="latex" alt="$n\equiv 0,1,2,3,4\mod 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="175" height="16" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/9/819068968ed60608a91d0c3921d6296964660e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2$" width="15" height="15" /> divides it iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a98d13f3534624ef34f07c7566e1d2e9179fd4e4.png" class="latex" alt="$n\not\equiv 5,8\mod 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="17" />. One can also bash out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> divides it in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> out of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" /> possible residues <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/1/9916bedba721704fd1d6ce40dbb5f6b04104832f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mod 32$" width="70" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/4/0248c7f64643258eab2b109de25f5ea16d09ce63.png" class="latex" alt="$2016 = 7*9*32$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="14" /> so by using all numbers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />, inclusive, it is clear that each possible residue <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/3/a13b0c46d494922fa8192bb2fdae8ef683de9fb4.png" class="latex" alt="$\mod 7,9,32$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" /> is reached an equal number of times, so the total number of working <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> in that range is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/9/d99d72952d2a8c65769e10e2e73d161e05f1c025.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 7\cdot 16 = 560$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />. However, we must subtract the number of "working" <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8eb398721adbeb9140ee7f3f938669a79d82212.png" class="latex" alt="$2\leq n\leq 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/118cd2ad2cdf55b4235b4d64375e8d4926cf2371.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 557}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="75" height="28" />.
</p><p>Alternatively, it is enough to approximate by finding the floor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/d/a3d0e57ee0fad2c9e9cfc970dcf3229ad2318000.png" class="latex" alt="$2017 \cdot \frac57 \cdot \frac79 \cdot \frac12 - 3$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="148" height="38" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/118cd2ad2cdf55b4235b4d64375e8d4926cf2371.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 557}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="75" height="28" />.
</p>
