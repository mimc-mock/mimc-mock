<p>Kymbrea's comic book collection currently has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> comic books in it, and she is adding to her collection at the rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> comic books per month. LaShawn's collection currently has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> comic books in it, and he is adding to his collection at the rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> comic books per month. After how many months will LaShawn's collection have twice as many comic books as Kymbrea's?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dad3be62d3a7b2e5cd53bc4d388448387175e93e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 20\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 25$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Kymbrea has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> comic books initially and every month, she adds two. This can be represented as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/1/721e2579f7eb506cbe94bb55bfc1ab88ff4f8351.png" class="latex" alt="$30 + 2x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" /> where x is the number of months elapsed. LaShawn's collection, similarly, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/c/52cd5386e1ba757ebde2f69f0f830eff4cf8e94a.png" class="latex" alt="$10 + 6x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" />. To find when LaShawn will have twice the number of comic books as Kymbrea, we solve for x with the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/7/bc76a040faa9c07aed35917a49a21fa888ccf01f.png" class="latex" alt="$2(2x + 30) = 6x + 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="18" /> and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/9/6f946afe804fd82db976ead7f0b7a2ba6b81b1de.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 25}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="98" height="28" />.
</p>
