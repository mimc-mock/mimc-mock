<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/c/bac163d0905411f6ffcb4d0f3cf4003a4a7d6f58.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]  /* Geogebra to Asymptote conversion, documentation at artofproblemsolving.com/Wiki, go to User:Azjps/geogebra */ import graph; size(8.865514650638614cm);  real labelscalefactor = 0.5; /* changes label-to-point distance */ pen dps = linewidth(0.7) + fontsize(10); defaultpen(dps); /* default pen style */  pen dotstyle = black; /* point style */  real xmin = -6.36927122464312, xmax = 11.361758076634109, ymin = -3.789601803155515, ymax = 7.420015026296013;  /* image dimensions */   draw((-2.,0.)--(0.6486486486486486,1.8918918918918919)--(2.,0.)--cycle);   /* draw figures */ draw(circle((0.,0.), 2.));  draw((-2.,0.)--(5.,5.));  draw((5.,5.)--(5.,0.));  draw((5.,0.)--(-2.,0.));  draw((-2.,0.)--(0.6486486486486486,1.8918918918918919));  draw((0.6486486486486486,1.8918918918918919)--(2.,0.));  draw((2.,0.)--(-2.,0.));  draw((2.,0.)--(5.,5.));  draw((0.,0.)--(5.,5.));   /* dots and labels */ dot((0.,0.),dotstyle);  label(&quot;$O$&quot;, (-0.10330578512396349,-0.39365890308038826), NE * labelscalefactor);  dot((-2.,0.),dotstyle);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;, (-2.2370398196844437,-0.42371149511645134), NE * labelscalefactor);  dot((2.,0.),dotstyle);  label(&quot;$B$&quot;, (2.045454545454548,-0.36360631104432517), NE * labelscalefactor);  dot((5.,0.),dotstyle);  label(&quot;$D$&quot;, (4.900450788880542,-0.42371149511645134), NE * labelscalefactor);  dot((5.,5.),dotstyle);  label(&quot;$E$&quot;, (5.06574004507889,5.15104432757325), NE * labelscalefactor);  dot((0.6486486486486486,1.8918918918918919),linewidth(3.pt) + dotstyle);  label(&quot;$C$&quot;, (0.48271975957926694,2.100706235912847), NE * labelscalefactor);  clip((xmin,ymin)--(xmin,ymax)--(xmax,ymax)--(xmax,ymin)--cycle);   /* end of picture */ [/asy]" width="438" height="428" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> be the center of the circle. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc6863df7c804512ed37550df24c3c74ec372e18.png" class="latex" alt="$EC + CA = EA = \sqrt{AD^2 + DE^2} = \sqrt{(2+2+3)^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{74}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="499" height="32" />. However, by Power of a Point, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10d61f5dcecb0881fb0149d790e5afa022495d2a.png" class="latex" alt="$(EC)(EC + CA) = EO^2 - R^2 = (2+3)^2 + 5^2 - 2^2 = 25 + 25 - 4 = 46 \implies EC = \frac{46}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="698" height="42" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b794ec6fd9179a28db8556fc6cae100d3fdf89aa.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = \sqrt{74} - \frac{46}{\sqrt{74}} = \frac{28}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="205" height="42" />. Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/f/d7f734ab039022f1d47214b98675b71e1a07461d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = \sqrt{AB^2 - AC^2} = \sqrt{4^2 - \frac{28^2}{74}} = \sqrt{\frac{16 \cdot 74 - 28^2}{74}} = \sqrt{\frac{1184 - 784}{74}} = \frac{20}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="607" height="46" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c284f4efd1e65e369c2eff7c074b55f3974c6309.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB = 90^{\circ}, [ABC] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot BC \cdot AC = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{20}{\sqrt{74}} \cdot \frac{28}{\sqrt{74}} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{140}{37}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="527" height="48" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2:_Similar_triangles_with_Pythagorean">Solution 2: Similar triangles with Pythagorean</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> is the diameter of the circle, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0728bebb477a2636300c56851b6a6f9e382c1b83.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB$" width="55" height="13" /> is a right angle, and therefore by AA similarity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac0058b20924ee6a2b2d270cf4babc0062a2d55d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACB \sim \triangle ADE$" width="142" height="13" />.
</p><p>Because of this, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09db56460ddb57045257c491b9e6e5447c71f958.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AC}{AD} = \frac{AB}{AE} \Longrightarrow \frac{AC}{2+2+3} = \frac{2+2}{\sqrt{7^2 + 5^2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="301" height="42" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/495ccf41bb0c350e534652f3d30e5d0aa39f6542.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = \frac{28}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="88" height="42" />.
</p><p>Likewise, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/b/22b00f2f4b3ca1d01440438bcc5ad380f2bfb059.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BC}{ED} = \frac{AB}{AE} \Longrightarrow \frac{BC}{5} = \frac{4}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="223" height="42" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/c/cfc3ef6be3f2d01defa5c4b71380407ecfcb4206.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = \frac{20}{\sqrt{74}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="89" height="42" />.
</p><p>Thus the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa9086be2299344dd3d0090d5fae3c88b9eb666a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{28}{\sqrt{74}} \cdot \frac{20}{\sqrt{74}} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{140}{37}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="299" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2b:_Area_shortcut">Solution 2b: Area shortcut</span></h2>
<p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec8f8cb0ae09e32f94672f87644de131030cc872.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{74}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, the ratio of the sides is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/4/8142c9744d77d00f48fe415a9d975ff2751ad79b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{74}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="35" height="41" />, meaning the ratio of the areas is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/c/fcc92ac2ea722da3299c301edac660483b2d669b.png" class="latex" alt="${(\frac{\sqrt{74}}{4})}^2 \implies \frac{74}{16} \implies \frac{37}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="200" height="45" />. We then have the proportion <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d53d4eeb806e3d9c801c70c63def6340603e898.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{5*7}{2}}{[ABC]}=\frac{37}{8} \implies 37*[ABC]=140 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{140}{37}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="419" height="48" />
</p>
