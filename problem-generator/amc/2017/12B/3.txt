<p>Supposed that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are nonzero real numbers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e8aaaf637a1c7f04003b1010755d48c6dfa15bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3x+y}{x-3y}=-2$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="102" height="41" />. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70fc3c7d38463c31fe0b506ffe2275c0cf59e372.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+3y}{3x-y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="53" height="41" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee656e5a312c047132ae4636767d630b1a290eef.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -3\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -1\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Rearranging, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5e8c3d002b6352d6d9a626dcf155e12099f89a0.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+y=-2x+6y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="149" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/b/eab47dda8a725a40b0c1be9b21473fd909df796d.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=5y\implies x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="155" height="16" />.
Substituting, we can convert the second equation into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/4/7747ecb70933102194eb756fe484589f11576ad2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+3x}{3x-x}=\frac{4x}{2x}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="184" height="37" />.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>More step-by-step explanation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e8aaaf637a1c7f04003b1010755d48c6dfa15bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3x+y}{x-3y}=-2$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="102" height="41" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/1/301533c5902717a1216159e448a0929422dcc9c4.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+y=-2\left(x-3y\right)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="169" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5e8c3d002b6352d6d9a626dcf155e12099f89a0.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+y=-2x+6y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="149" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/8/648360d34ba49da6ce21605bac84b8228ca236e2.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=5y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="64" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0110f1bb04c51b2a4d431bf14489fc1b48b37421.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/9/db9da993d1518b47ab5518f994d623f4f7d017ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+3y}{3x-y}=\frac{1+3\left(1\right)}{3\left(1\right)-1}=\frac{4}{2}=2$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="223" height="45" />.
</p><p>We choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/6/0767eab43354a52b47e69e32e893cbfe28f817dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Substituting each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, we see that the given equation holds true, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/3/8d38cf6181bd309f5af68c961fe74d75a348e43b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3(1)+1}{1-3(1)} = -2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="114" height="43" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c141a51247a583b350430376f1b44faec4ed9dc5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+3y}{3x-y}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="135" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0459893b6e7c554d5a0d3ba8a3ce7ecb3cc0b8.png" class="latex" alt="$y=ax$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="11" />. The first equation converts into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/8/9685744e3f63732ff176c14d86d4a08f5648d776.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(3+a)x}{(1-3a)x}=-2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="125" height="43" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/0/a10e5d9e8b39baaabe686f455dd3829fad1f739c.png" class="latex" alt="$3+a=-2(1-3a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="151" height="18" />. After a bit of algebra we found out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0110f1bb04c51b2a4d431bf14489fc1b48b37421.png" class="latex" alt="$x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" />. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> into the second equation it becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc019a1c78ddb73224399eac11ab13bd497d3b82.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4x}{2x}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="37" /> - mathleticguyyy
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c3b4834614e5e7e189cb7d621cfb4fb0bd36dd7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />. So the desired result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Select <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55bdade53f471aab49f63d1a734979cc0c636640.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="27" height="24" />.
</p><p>~hastapasta
</p>
