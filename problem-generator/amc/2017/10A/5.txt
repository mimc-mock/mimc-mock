<p>The sum of two nonzero real numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times their product. What is the sum of the reciprocals of the two numbers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/1/571c1026e49506d70e6e271bdb9920253b289b54.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the two real numbers be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/4/b6400c6fe3f1ed70e5afd387a9686cdc3fa09531.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="11" />. We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b9912f427d0dfacd861d2641c7b4eeaea8ea46a.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=4xy,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="15" /> and dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4b2ccfe02561ef15a393ba4e97058998006e2a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{xy}+\frac{y}{xy}=4.$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="107" height="36" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/1/ea1dadbf53587b5968e46e59c6f61f87125eeed4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{y}+\frac{1}{x}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 4}.\]" width="131" height="40" />
</p><p>Note: we can easily verify that this is the correct answer; for example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/e/5fe5e71c7d8317d4177fa56b61211bcca007e682.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="57" height="43" /> works, and the sum of their reciprocals is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Instead of using algebra, another approach at this problem would be to notice the fact that one of the nonzero numbers has to be a fraction.
See for yourself. And by looking into fractions, we immediately see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> would fit the rule. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/1/c614e06fc27d91fdb119a3ea4a8a05bce31942b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} 4}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="52" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Notice that from the information given above, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/8/d08c1d599ed5c382ab7827339699084d0ead73e5.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=4xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="15" />
</p><p>Because the sum of the reciprocals of two numbers is just the sum of the two numbers over the product of the two numbers or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/b/3abd2341c3a6904f4b8f972cf00c466568ef1190.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+y}{xy}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="44" height="39" /> 
</p><p>We can solve this by substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/8/898ebd560e26a7b4e81a23951a3d3a678e15aa56.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y\implies 4xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="120" height="15" />.
</p><p>Our answer is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45d5c09e0ebaed53108dba7ea9b8b3a6fcd86265.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4xy}{xy}\implies4$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="91" height="40" />.
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/c/88cfe513eb8c018f4f918d63b4f244a861f9558a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
