<p>For certain real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/b/19bd1aae4cf00cac12042acf2f2dd99c6f179c35.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[g(x) = x^3 + ax^2 + x + 10\]" width="198" height="19" />has three distinct roots, and each root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> is also a root of the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/0/220c07851848a30837a8227c41b3ef91db1d210b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) = x^4 + x^3 + bx^2 + 100x + c.\]" width="258" height="19" />What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/b/edb4fe9204dff2534c4d88c2ec2920d0c945acf9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -9009 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -8008 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ -7007 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ -6006 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ -5005$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="596" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> must have four roots, three of which are roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />. Using the fact that every polynomial has a unique factorization into its roots, and since the leading coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> are the same, we know that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/f/cef8065ed96307ff245b089c65c5644731f99b1c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x)=g(x)(x+r)\]" width="147" height="18" />
</p><p>where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/3/5c36cb756fc96f99e47342a2f2b5692a9d075d24.png" class="latex" alt="$-r\in\mathbb{R}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="16" /> is the fourth root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />. 
(Using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/d/6ed5170be9330295e202ff38fcd2f6940bb74224.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+r) = (x- (-r))$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="165" height="20" /> instead of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/a/2ca978367e1d4c3db5b249834c61b050bd565aee.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> makes the following computations less messy.)
Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> and expanding, we find that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e2dca5b8d5857c1bff2f0a4840fbb68079d3f0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}f(x)&amp;=(x^3+ax^2+x+10)(x+r)\\ &amp;=x^4+(a+r)x^3+(1+ar)x^2+(10+r)x+10r.\end{align*}" width="423" height="47" />
</p><p>Comparing coefficients with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we see that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcf83b8bcba63e8e81617b11013d02ae86d35d30.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a+r&amp;=1\\ 1+ar&amp;=b\\ 10+r&amp;=100\\ 10r&amp;=c.\\ \end{align*}" width="98" height="87" />
</p><p><br />
Let's solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8b8490a47ba3063da2731316f5d65dc5cf731ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/f/cbf40bef9b3779f1480e1c3dc5e4ad3c3447fda3.png" class="latex" alt="$10+r=100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2dd360fab4000307c24a7d11fc856df6c4993aa7.png" class="latex" alt="$r=90$" width="50" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/7/ab79f90dba668dd44322d8095ebd5c4b6b990655.png" class="latex" alt="$c=(10)(90)=900$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="150" height="20" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/b/e0b94d0a15e330443f77a07f4e400b0cf907fa3f.png" class="latex" alt="$a+r=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6acf6bf3890300d25b8ee4e202331df2cc7dc96d.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-89$" width="65" height="12" />. 
</p><p>(Solution 1.1 picks up here.)
</p><p>Then, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c925820c10ef982e4668b1f587cd189be9f77e8.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1+ar$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="84" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/9/af96ab40162870521d2b83905b7f51042f7ee98c.png" class="latex" alt="$b=-8009$" width="81" height="12" />. Thus, we know that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/6/4b6e6a3e84dcd94578ffe9707db30435bb9863f7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x)=x^4+x^3-8009x^2+100x+900.\]" width="306" height="19" />
</p><p>Taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />, we find that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/2/772c47579187e04618d9d68b626fa1dfd4a3be22.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} f(1)&amp;=1^4+1^3-8009(1)^2+100(1)+900\\ &amp;=1+1-8009+100+900\\ &amp;=\boxed{\bold{(C)}\, -7007}.\\ \end{align*}" width="323" height="82" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.1">Solution 1.1</span></h2>
<p>A faster ending to Solution 1 is as follows.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/2/d32f5afb9b9a72adc69b5e1bb3094f26ce96d597.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} f(1)&amp;=(1+r)(1^3+a\cdot1^2+1+10)\\ &amp;=(91)(-77)\\ &amp;= (7)(13)(11)(-7) = (1001)(-7) \\ &amp;=\boxed{\bold{(C)}\, -7007}.\\ \end{align*}" width="293" height="107" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We notice that the constant term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/1/691b8ed9474ce2bd48093b5c06c0d9cd831108de.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and the constant term in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/0/ab055ab5e0ac80304d5b866e5a97bf321b5623ba.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)=10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> can be factored as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4bfd31d8ab739bfca7d94a1e1a027e21f0c55c6.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x) \cdot (x- r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" /> (where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the unshared root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we see that using the constant term, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/5/ef5eabf14fff0426e580f62d72ca74cfca6080a0.png" class="latex" alt="$-10 \cdot r = c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" /> and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/0/b000c798d5ccb487d16a57bcf316846db1390055.png" class="latex" alt="$r = -\frac{c}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="33" />.
Now we once again write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> out in factored form: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/2/8621c57d7b4b36645732b7b948daae3d29cb5fb2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) = g(x)\cdot (x-r) = (x^3+ax^2+x+10)(x+\frac{c}{10})\]" width="408" height="33" />.
</p><p>We can expand the expression on the right-hand side to get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/4/6946dc61d93d525203bd587efed71194ee536f11.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) = x^4+(a+\frac{c}{10})x^3+(1+\frac{ac}{10})x^2+(10+\frac{c}{10})x+c\]" width="432" height="33" />
</p><p>Now we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/b/58b5ae4b8dd833824a9df6325cd8eb9c064ea9f7.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = x^4+(a+\frac{c}{10})x^3+(1+\frac{ac}{10})x^2+(10+\frac{c}{10})x+c=x^4+x^3+bx^2+100x+c$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="653" height="33" />.
</p><p>Simply looking at the coefficients for each corresponding term (knowing that they must be equal), we have the equations:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b7e9a1b113f57ff49a437e3162423bf0d449e2d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[10+\frac{c}{10}=100 \Rightarrow c=900\]" width="199" height="32" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7d36b7333fa6bdfba466eda80f6bac22500ca6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+\frac{c}{10} = 1, c=900 \Rightarrow a + 90 =1 \Rightarrow a= -89\]" width="359" height="33" />
</p><p>and finally,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/d/e3d97475208571690c60b1272de21b0c4f8158c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1+\frac{ac}{10} = b = 1+\frac{-89 \cdot 900}{10} = b = -8009\]" width="322" height="38" />.
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> is the sum of its coefficients, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/5/1a55b0f6310fc798ab6f42d8cada798206be770d.png" class="latex" alt="$1+1+b+100+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="150" height="14" />. We substitute the values we obtained for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> into this expression to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38dcc22edd07bd69a8591484db231d4e8ba4abc1.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1) = 1 + 1 + (-8009) + 100 + 900 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\,-7007}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="411" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/9/cd9dbe4d2011a8a31941aa0f368b469dc4870785.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1,r_2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/e/5ae5f496b8ddda88b5cbbc06ccb02bee0580fd0b.png" class="latex" alt="$r_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> be the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7ce82c4f62f7e439d80be7f44293f527af4619b.png" class="latex" alt="$r_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> be the additional root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />. Then from Vieta's formulas on the quadratic term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> and the cubic term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we obtain the following:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/7/287939bc3ca37062cb608f542dee956afffc9ffb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} r_1+r_2+r_3&amp;=-a \\  r_1+r_2+r_3+r_4&amp;=-1 \end{align*}" width="176" height="38" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/890f0ac87062c599dc6785f3f8cf5f39e45b65b5.png" class="latex" alt="$r_4=a-1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="79" height="14" />.
</p><p>Now applying Vieta's formulas on the constant term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />, the linear term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />, and the linear term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we obtain:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/f/faf064ed931087fb696151f68dca7ba1d6cd02e9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} r_1r_2r_3  &amp; = -10\\ r_1r_2+r_2r_3+r_3r_1 &amp;= 1\\  r_1r_2r_3+r_2r_3r_4+r_3r_4r_1+r_4r_1r_2  &amp; = -100\\ \end{align*}" width="318" height="65" />
</p><p>Substituting for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/c/84caf895c301c3ddf9021b473852bc2139af85a1.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1r_2r_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="45" height="10" /> in the bottom equation and factoring the remainder of the expression, we obtain:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/a/fda276e0e758f1f9e105835c6198ca6456e07787.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-10+(r_1r_2+r_2r_3+r_3r_1)r_4=-10+r_4=-100\]" width="380" height="18" />
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/4/954cf93eabf4a9d214e6906761275451585fc2cd.png" class="latex" alt="$r_4=-90$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="71" height="15" />. But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/890f0ac87062c599dc6785f3f8cf5f39e45b65b5.png" class="latex" alt="$r_4=a-1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="79" height="14" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6acf6bf3890300d25b8ee4e202331df2cc7dc96d.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-89$" width="65" height="12" />
</p><p>Now we can factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> as
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/8/d68b87814a7afd422069c8313f70050ca317855a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x)=(x-r_4)g(x)=(x+90)g(x)\]" width="277" height="18" />
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/8/c588f9574ad158056eac0be9fdde4c77d89bf0b0.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)=91g(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> and
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/8/fa8b3d81208cffe40ef991700c914f2fbb29dd5a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[g(1)=1^3-89\cdot 1^2+1+10=-77\]" width="271" height="19" />
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/e/cfe5f0d60e7338a438fa3eb87166948a65b5b971.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)=91\cdot(-77)=\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\,-7007}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="262" height="28" />.
</p>
