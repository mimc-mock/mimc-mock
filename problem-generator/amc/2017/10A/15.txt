<p>Chloe chooses a real number uniformly at random from the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10eba1aa7b7e8ac41f2ca92850bdca6ca8d5c4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, 2017]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" />. Independently, Laurent chooses a real number uniformly at random from the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/409b3cbc7fc05da96f487c86b345dbae7ee98d6e.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, 4034]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" />. What is the probability that Laurent's number is greater than Chloe's number? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/6/8d6cd048b05a3cdced9bcf4aedd38d2a2d55257a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{\textbf{(A)} \ }\frac{1}{2}\qquad \mathrm{\textbf{(B)} \ } \frac{2}{3}\qquad \mathrm{\textbf{(C)} \ } \frac{3}{4}\qquad \mathrm{\textbf{(D)} \ } \frac{5}{6}\qquad \mathrm{\textbf{(E)} \ }\frac{7}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Denote "winning" to mean "picking a greater number".
There is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance that Laurent chooses a number in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/c/fdce99d940d567c0241a5dca0a21a8a77f631351.png" class="latex" alt="$[2017, 4034]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="88" height="18" />. In this case, Chloé cannot possibly win, since the maximum number she can pick is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. Otherwise, if Laurent picks a number in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10eba1aa7b7e8ac41f2ca92850bdca6ca8d5c4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, 2017]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" />, with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, then the two people are symmetric, and each has a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of winning. Then, the total probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/0/0109173849e58d7a03e702fb313ae30ac8cd792d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\times1 + \frac{1}{2}\times\frac{1}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{3}{4}}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="201" height="48" />
</p><p>~Small grammar mistake corrected by virjoy2001 (missing period), small error corrected by Terribleteeth
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can use geometric probability to solve this.
Suppose a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> lies in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />-plane. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be Chloe's number and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be Laurent's number. Then obviously we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/b/d5b7ad4c34a3f2485fb4fd2342d49e605df553fc.png" class="latex" alt="$y&gt;x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="13" />, which basically gives us a region above a line. We know that Chloe's number is in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/2/2923eaa5fb158c9fb73b5c3184f250517c9325da.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,2017]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" /> and Laurent's number is in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/c/e4ce3d7d085bbe0844278017306c1118e35e3d6c.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,4034]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" />, so we can create a rectangle in the plane, whose length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> and whose width is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/d/e4dc4a9c18f1cf47f733180e6bcf1343294f2dbd.png" class="latex" alt="$4034$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />. Drawing it out and dividing into 4 congruent triangles, we see that Laurent's winning area is 3 triangles and Chloe's  is 1 triangle. \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{3}{4}}$.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Scale down by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> to get that Chloe picks from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e861e10e1c19918756b9c8b7717684593c63aeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" /> and Laurent picks from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/9076ffb2b9c051f1b6f07bf18066581d57216258.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />. There are an infinite number of cases for the number that Chloe picks, but they are all centered around the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192b4a258ee998961a99523fd0f0a029f9ea194a.png" class="latex" alt="$0.5$" width="22" height="12" />. Therefore, Laurent has a winning range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/c/0bc91b5f2465d6e0ace1864e3040920f6a73f41f.png" class="latex" alt="$[X, 2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="20" />, where the average value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192b4a258ee998961a99523fd0f0a029f9ea194a.png" class="latex" alt="$0.5$" width="22" height="12" />. Thus the average winning length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/1/c315f1502e700dcdff46ff73fc1b67fecda74f9b.png" class="latex" alt="$2-0.5=1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="101" height="13" /> out of a total length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/6/546c3db0c590a838768ffa590bf72349364f0395.png" class="latex" alt="$2-0=2$" width="73" height="12" />. Therefore, the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dbae2a00c57303c8ac7fd4756f1becc71a43a11.png" class="latex" alt="$1.5/2=15/20=\boxed{\frac{3}{4} \space \text{(C)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="188" height="48" />
</p>
