<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be a set of points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> in the coordinate plane such that two of the three quantities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/8/d787bc1b0b196767e59b8d24223b0af982c72d6c.png" class="latex" alt="$3,~x+2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="68" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> are equal and the third of the three quantities is no greater than this common value. Which of the following is a correct description for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/7/4d753026173a63cfed0ea918be191cc3b451677e.png" class="latex" alt="$S?$" width="20" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bdaad160362ac5b27b59e33ce2e47288b2ae3420.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{a single point} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{two intersecting lines} \\\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{three lines whose pairwise intersections are three distinct points} \\\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{a triangle} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{three rays with a common endpoint}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="498" height="57" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If the two equal values are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/11477c8fa7f2eff67baf776483e7817986c2b3e3.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4\le 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is the common value. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a2a9064ba64d35fcfcf7252a768c7c29edb1ed.png" class="latex" alt="$y \le 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />. Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/a/a7a2a9064ba64d35fcfcf7252a768c7c29edb1ed.png" class="latex" alt="$y \le 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" /> is part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. This is a ray with an endpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Similar to the process above, we assume that the two equal values are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" />. Solving the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/6/8f61279c0249ddd75948828ef746744b27f6551b.png" class="latex" alt="$3=y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c37261466c0ff756401c32f293c33e111f4712.png" class="latex" alt="$y=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/c/ecc103e198800ce1ee24566788be3375bd414f98.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2\le 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" /> because 3 is the common value. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2c3495f829e4f037d2f031a4edab72c455aefae.png" class="latex" alt="$x\le1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />. Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c37261466c0ff756401c32f293c33e111f4712.png" class="latex" alt="$y=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/9/479e1f98be1f18cac99683c6195e920bd53c3889.png" class="latex" alt="$x\le 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" /> is also part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. This is another ray with the same endpoint as the above ray: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> are the two equal values, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/7479ee2548c328ed198d967b9c5f9ecf7044331d.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2=y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="15" />. Solving the equation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a9348cab3888ef34d6c3597fda8a0a96a670e4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x+6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />. Also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/5/d15f94aa6ea3a693597d2f4cc9538a18bd5884f8.png" class="latex" alt="$3\le y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> is one way to express the common value. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/0/470d18cbb91e07d26e3e4398079eafccb2229b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$y\ge 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />. We also know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a606fc35f009da61916f16a3b1240831cc229aa3.png" class="latex" alt="$3\le x+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd70eb3335de97b1d0b61733183aba667317380.png" class="latex" alt="$x\ge 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />.Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a9348cab3888ef34d6c3597fda8a0a96a670e4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x+6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/0/470d18cbb91e07d26e3e4398079eafccb2229b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$y\ge 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" /> is part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> like the other two rays. The lowest possible value that can be achieved is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is made up of three rays with common endpoint <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/3/303b006c380d6030e369a6cf3c9ed733de58800d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }\text{three rays with a common endpoint}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="305" height="28" />
</p>
