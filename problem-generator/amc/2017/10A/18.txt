<p>Amelia has a coin that lands heads with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, and Blaine has a coin that lands on heads with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2297526ede0e6c616802ca72adf69c82518f25.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Amelia and Blaine alternately toss their coins until someone gets a head; the first one to get a head wins. All coin tosses are independent. Amelia goes first. The probability that Amelia wins is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/4/7347a1d5586e82c832f61ea799d89945ed2837fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{p}{q}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="11" height="36" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> are relatively prime positive integers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0fd869078478f66f2c1e7b9b44c80bf0bfb34e.png" class="latex" alt="$q-p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/9959d47fffb760b6cf89894df252b99436e4d8f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> be the probability Amelia wins. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c705e83cfc097fe6b13c3cbb8d5f394b6aea63ff.png" class="latex" alt="$P = \text{chance she wins on her first turn} + \text{chance she gets to her turn again}\cdot P$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="567" height="15" />, since if she gets to her turn again, she is back where she started with probability of winning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. The chance she wins on her first turn is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. The chance she makes it to her turn again is a combination of her failing to win the first turn - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and Blaine failing to win - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Multiplying gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2297526ede0e6c616802ca72adf69c82518f25.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Thus,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a086f7f990b659b2c3ff6f2ccc7957acdf020bc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{5}P\]" width="100" height="37" />
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d6d22a6f0e7c51bb3a0e28de7d95fcbfa047312.png" class="latex" alt="$P = \frac{5}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/b/9eb96d35823339c5040ee15da2ddaf69983eddc0.png" class="latex" alt="$9-5=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> be the probability Amelia wins. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c5661a42ca5d4a758598d229ad42bc7a4e5f0054.png" class="latex" alt="$P = \text{chance she wins on her first turn} + \text{chance she gets to her second turn}\cdot \frac{1}{3} + \text{chance she gets to her third turn}\cdot \frac{1}{3} ...$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="876" height="37" /> This can be represented by an infinite geometric series: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bbd77f6a3b85fd5453e8cf9a09caaa0deae6542.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P=\frac{\frac{1}{3}}{1-\frac{2}{3}\cdot \frac{3}{5}} = \frac{\frac{1}{3}}{1-\frac{2}{5}} = \frac{\frac{1}{3}}{\frac{3}{5}} = \frac{1}{3}\cdot \frac{5}{3} = \frac{5}{9}.\]" width="321" height="47" /> 
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d6d22a6f0e7c51bb3a0e28de7d95fcbfa047312.png" class="latex" alt="$P = \frac{5}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/1/44158592b51323b7ee31f8212e6d1f3b28a98b70.png" class="latex" alt="$9-5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 4}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="123" height="28" />
</p><p>Solution by ktong
</p><p>~minor LaTeX edit by virjoy2001
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can solve this by using 'casework,' the cases being:
Case 1: Amelia wins on her first turn.
Case 2 Amelia wins on her second turn.
and so on.
</p><p>The probability of her winning on her first turn is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e6261a9b57684fff897400a639b5fe3b2d282c00.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. The probability of all the other cases is determined by the probability that Amelia and Blaine all lose until Amelia's turn on which she is supposed to win. So, the total probability of Amelia winning is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/d/6dd3aac0ef564cc62e876837dc600d3ebbd5a918.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{1}{3}+\left(\dfrac{2}{3}\cdot\dfrac{3}{5}\right)\cdot\dfrac{1}{3}+\left(\dfrac{2}{3}\cdot\dfrac{3}{5}\right)^2\cdot\dfrac{1}{3}+\cdots.\]" width="299" height="46" />
Factoring out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e6261a9b57684fff897400a639b5fe3b2d282c00.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> we get a geometric series:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/effc694b3ac680f3ef387c09e1c0506f75e0b1c4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{1}{3}\left(1+\dfrac{2}{5}+\left(\dfrac{2}{5}\right)^2+\cdots\right) = \dfrac{1}{3}\cdot\dfrac{1}{3/5} = \boxed{\dfrac59}.\]" width="335" height="53" />
</p><p>Extracting the desired result, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/8/478d01f2c8ca727b666edf6a0b8d42d6e335a91c.png" class="latex" alt="$9-5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} \ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="122" height="30" />.
</p><p>-ConfidentKoala4
</p>
