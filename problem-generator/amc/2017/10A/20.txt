<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/6/0965bf432b818b46740e3cd4d042899628c7ad0e.png" class="latex" alt="$S(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> equal the sum of the digits of positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/4/02474038a7a0bace6997fbad80cbade79e0557a4.png" class="latex" alt="$S(1507) = 13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" />. For a particular positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/26204ebf156e73a07fea0cb94965810f874e0f3d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(n) = 1274$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />. Which of the following could be the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/6/e964a2172d005c1230f8830bea0e78dbb447b779.png" class="latex" alt="$S(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/1/d610f8a4da3999d9e8b039e9b74e99b85bed9d08.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 1239\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 1265$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/56136a65cf16f51622ca95f76a18fe8cf2612c84.png" class="latex" alt="$n \equiv S(n) \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />. This can be seen from the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/5/9b56bcddd0d8592d23d04ee4a546690250d43c67.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{k=0}^{n}10^{k}a_k \equiv \sum_{k=0}^{n}a_k \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="214" height="48" />. Thus, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/26204ebf156e73a07fea0cb94965810f874e0f3d.png" class="latex" alt="$S(n) = 1274$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0610b5c2f360cf15454ab564cb0fe766b57f4284.png" class="latex" alt="$n \equiv 5 \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3b952e28f22e20a2c7333cff5d0d3b28c2ff211.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1 \equiv S(n+1) \equiv 6 \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="239" height="18" />. The only answer choice that satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/5/1e5052d2baa3134fee9d100a71f6c49e9de0f959.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1 \equiv 6 \pmod{9}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="150" height="20" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/7/c97d59afd633b88f296e4917ac68ce035b2197e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} 1239}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="79" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>One divisibility rule that we can use for this problem is that a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> will always have its digits sum to a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. We can find out that the least number of digits the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> has is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/e/5ce8614c1b3993e78519131182767d0a43ea0714.png" class="latex" alt="$142$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/80401851927344d980a3cea9388530b9a9f1b065.png" class="latex" alt="$141$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />'s and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, assuming the rule above. No matter what arrangement or different digits we use, the divisibility rule stays the same. To make the problem simpler, we can just use the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/80401851927344d980a3cea9388530b9a9f1b065.png" class="latex" alt="$141$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />'s and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. By randomly mixing the digits up, we are likely to get: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" />...<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/c/27cfe345c35bf466315171ae77cd126773c6a358.png" class="latex" alt="$9995999$" width="62" height="12" />...<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" />. By adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to this number, we get: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/450352c42d8f7a2b16abf3323453aa1ab13cbe0d.png" class="latex" alt="$9999$" width="35" height="12" />...<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/38560d6cb66a44c783d493ebf75f5cfa6bf3a410.png" class="latex" alt="$9996000$" width="62" height="12" />...<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/4/e34ada25a413c699ddb5f831106d14ab926a7243.png" class="latex" alt="$0000$" width="35" height="12" />.
Knowing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e3efd9b14723a92c2ae891fe27780d5f8e2b215.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, we can subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> from every available choice, and see if the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> afterwards.
After subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> from every number, we can conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/645227b6f40ddcee5670286fba3925ad27bca15e.png" class="latex" alt="$1233$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> (originally <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/179c1a815c7919a1c68e9d2f92e619ea9c416080.png" class="latex" alt="$1239$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />) is the only number divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. 
So our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/2/b724d354724dad1a462c09020e310ba0f4db2ffe.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 1239}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> can be viewed as having some unique digits in the front, following by a certain number of nines. We can then evaluate each potential answer choice.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is correct, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be some number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/6/786f4c78f0056d6ecb57183902bbc17bd48399ae.png" class="latex" alt="$99999999...9$" width="96" height="12" />, because when we add one to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/6/786f4c78f0056d6ecb57183902bbc17bd48399ae.png" class="latex" alt="$99999999...9$" width="96" height="12" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/e/3bea7a758d6480c731214379631129b993191cf6.png" class="latex" alt="$10000000...00$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" />. Thus, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is the correct answer, then the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f875489dc47dc3f1d780620a5b99b4803ebf81a.png" class="latex" alt="$9x=1274$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" /> must have an integer solution (i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5e64ba09d81cfe56e269ab704e517be6f941bae.png" class="latex" alt="$1274$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="12" /> must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />). But since it does not, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is not the correct answer.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is correct, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be some number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c47e04dc2a55b7ca8f5a4421889ce18421eb1008.png" class="latex" alt="$29999999...9$" width="96" height="12" />, because when we add one to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c47e04dc2a55b7ca8f5a4421889ce18421eb1008.png" class="latex" alt="$29999999...9$" width="96" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/d/b3dd82293136b639264fe14f12f2b6b19666989e.png" class="latex" alt="$30000000...00$" width="105" height="12" />. Thus, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is the correct answer, then the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/7/3b7a88a3bf92af16f502d7cfecbb4bf217cc60b5.png" class="latex" alt="$2+9x=1274$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" /> must have an integer solution. But since it does not, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is not the correct answer.
</p><p>Based on what we have done for evaluating the previous two answer choices, we can create an equation we can use to evaluate the final three possibilities. Notice that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/cccd5ea1e6abd8e03b6248e4ce84d8f56c948e5a.png" class="latex" alt="$S(n+1)=N$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be a number whose initial digits sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a256c70ad4c46ec1127c5be68f8bb3075e9ced31.png" class="latex" alt="$N-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="13" />, and whose other, terminating digits, are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, we can evaluate the three final possibilities by seeing if the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e2067d76be3e072e9920371d2f335209beb05f9a.png" class="latex" alt="$(N-1)+9x=1274$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" /> has an integer solution.
</p><p>The equation does not have an integer solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/2/c420ce6df6299255fc314c589195d40a8e20882f.png" class="latex" alt="$N=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is not correct. However, the equation does have an integer solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/f/75f6771b27c41272b04820e765061df3894f850f.png" class="latex" alt="$N=1239$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7d65cfa316d639c9267398009812f3eb45fea9.png" class="latex" alt="$x=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />), so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3dc5e272ed0b8a7757f80be68ae9b223e60f1d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} ~1239}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="85" height="30" /> is the answer.
</p>
