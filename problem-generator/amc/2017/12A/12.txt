<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> horses, named Horse 1, Horse 2, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3958e984d2597b35da7975f4503cdcf965a777f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ldots$" width="19" height="2" />, Horse 10. They get their names from how many minutes it takes them to run one lap around a circular race track: Horse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> runs one lap in exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> minutes. At time 0 all the horses are together at the starting point on the track. The horses start running in the same direction, and they keep running around the circular track at their constant speeds. The least time <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/8/f383a25cbaf4e6cdd01c91d221acbe0da42cf772.png" class="latex" alt="$S &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="45" height="13" />, in minutes, at which all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> horses will again simultaneously be at the starting point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/0/1c0c6ed646f2006f8d5b3db898c3822dbcd8df61.png" class="latex" alt="$S = 2520$" width="72" height="12" />. Let  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/f/e6f39f6a9bfb48b021b65d4485f850a0c71e7233.png" class="latex" alt="$T&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> be the least time, in minutes, such that at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> of the horses are again at the starting point. What is the sum of the digits of  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/8/f68a620e8d126d7a4d48fcd5878a055aaa4ab6db.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We know that Horse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> will be at the starting point after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> minutes if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf56c5bf1f0747569a75062049d3f80b81a384bb.png" class="latex" alt="$k|n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />. Thus, we are looking for the smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb560252fa3957fcda446c8534e5b0f3efe2c4f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,2,\cdots,10\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" /> divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> has at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> positive integer divisors.
</p><p>We quickly see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is the smallest number with at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> positive integer divisors and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/a/91a1926789b9b31c50c218bc86640658e5de67c9.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,4,6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> are each numbers of horses. Thus, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/1/d410da36bb1fbc91d60a13cf109e1a631207308e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>In order for at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> horses to finish simultaneously, the current time needs to have at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> divisors. Thus the number must have a form of either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4eeda7a7a8630f80b8b345d1bc47e9fdfc43c597.png" class="latex" alt="$p^4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/e/c2ef75f986f9cea3da6e94c7288b14ac653e1788.png" class="latex" alt="$p^2*q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="18" />, which have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> factors respectively. The smallest number of the first form is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and the smallest number of the second form is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b40dc5c54a0dac797341eefc0e3befa7caa8d74f.png" class="latex" alt="$12.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" />  Thus, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/1/d410da36bb1fbc91d60a13cf109e1a631207308e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />.
</p>
