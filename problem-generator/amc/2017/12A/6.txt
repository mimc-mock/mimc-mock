<p>Joy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> thin rods, one each of every integer length from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78ed721adc9a4cb545c4949f7b24fcc62ad98a79.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \text{ cm}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/5/665d4ca6471d70415a61cbec47f7886c3abaa582.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \text{ cm}$" width="44" height="12" />. She places the rods with lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94c9cc980f0d47c1d95d6dc57372f375410c0ee5.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \text{ cm}$" width="35" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/1/9610aefefe393225dc6aa67ebd1184cb0796ba39.png" class="latex" alt="$7 \text{ cm}$" width="35" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/8/098f1d4ead503bd95b33474d00a69ec3052d8338.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \text{cm}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" /> on a table. She then wants to choose a fourth rod that she can put with these three to form a quadrilateral with positive area. How many of the remaining rods can she choose as the fourth rod?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/d/a4d1b55c0b487630b326d59375f9dc45f03fc6c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 16 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 17 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 18 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 19  \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The quadrilateral cannot be a straight line. Thus, the fourth side must be longer than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0ad62d177e42a99a0e697e5f64f102ed5cd430.png" class="latex" alt="$15 - (3 + 7) = 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="18" /> and shorter than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a47ab8a5e4cf424eba1b9b437350de4d79dd8ab.png" class="latex" alt="$15 + 3 + 7 = 25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />. This means Joy can use the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> possible integer rod lengths that fall into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/3/b93d84871b0a3d11e3bfa6cac25df7ac7edc0b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$[6, 24]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="43" height="18" />. However, she has already used the rods of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> cm and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> cm so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/3/eb31c48611ee89ca67a3b48cd02708e48c0999de.png" class="latex" alt="$19 - 2 = 17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />
</p>
