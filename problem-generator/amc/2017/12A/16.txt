<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Connect the centers of the tangent circles! (call the center of the large circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />)

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aa17dd98346292d2b958a6c0bade6db3bdc4e9a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(5cm); draw((-1,0)--(2,0)); pair P = (9/7,12/7); pair A = (-1,0); pair C = (0,0); pair B = (2,0); dot((-1,0)); dot((2,0)); dot((0,0)); dot(P); draw((-1,0)--P); draw((2,0)--P); draw((0,0)--P); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,N); [/asy]" width="238" height="155" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70fea2ae9485b17e61d311dafb21bfc14b702b42.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7.5cm); draw(arc((0,0),3,0,180)); draw(arc((2,0),1,0,180)); draw(arc((-1,0),2,0,180)); draw((-3,0)--(3,0)); pair P = (9/7,12/7); pair A = (-1,0); pair C = (0,0); pair B = (2,0); draw(circle(P,6/7)); dot((-1,0)); dot((2,0)); dot((0,0)); dot(P); draw((-1,0)--P); draw((-2.45,12/7)--P); draw((2.45,12/7)--P); draw((2,0)--P); draw((0,0)--(9/5,12/5)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,N); [/asy]" width="355" height="202" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be the center of the largest semicircle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/5/c05d30ec932ed282fda908cb5da6aa2ef54f5fd7.png" class="latex" alt="$\circ P$" width="23" height="12" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/5769bd8c4884b0688e98d67f254a7e3e2fa9f11f.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/2/d22bcf76d8352a6ab3325ebb98f8c1c12eecc97f.png" class="latex" alt="$CB = 2$" width="61" height="12" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/c/e3c119941f3635e12c2b9c93f36c8882fffaa77b.png" class="latex" alt="$AP = r + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" />,  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/3/ed334394658a5d6f7719ed02b70e2f7fc40f13e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BP = r + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="14" />, and  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/b/04b18ce9d2fb2e9881017f1d81b683d10a8e6390.png" class="latex" alt="$CP = 3 - r$" width="92" height="12" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568b94f1efbe48cb0f22ce851f2389e24dd6c5b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta ACP$" width="57" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/2/02215887bfc4c2c40bf45260b88198bd1e62ec43.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta CBP$" width="58" height="13" /> are bounded by the same two parallel lines, so these triangles have the same heights. Because the bases of these two triangles (that have the same heights) differ by a factor of 2, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/2/02215887bfc4c2c40bf45260b88198bd1e62ec43.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta CBP$" width="58" height="13" /> must be twice that of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568b94f1efbe48cb0f22ce851f2389e24dd6c5b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta ACP$" width="57" height="13" />, since the area of a triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a833fcd4afa201ebce950b849bec8eef6d8cd2ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}  \text{Base} \cdot \text{Height}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" />.
</p><p>Again, we don't need to look at the circle and the semicircles anymore; just focus on the triangles.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/f/2eff75026871378b0c0a67a5b6e216735c9178a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7.5cm); pair A = (-1,0); pair C = (0,0); pair B = (2,0); draw((-1,0)--(2,0)); pair P = (9/7,12/7); dot((-1,0)); dot((2,0)); dot((0,0)); dot(P); draw((-1,0)--P); draw((2,0)--P); draw((0,0)--P); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,N); [/asy]" width="355" height="222" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36c7f3f828cf12ab9cafeffad282468a95219600.png" class="latex" alt="$A_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> equal to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568b94f1efbe48cb0f22ce851f2389e24dd6c5b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta ACP$" width="57" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/a/e3a8ae66ded7f33f9385a37c9cadfb82720013d8.png" class="latex" alt="$A_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> equal to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/2/02215887bfc4c2c40bf45260b88198bd1e62ec43.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta CBP$" width="58" height="13" />.

<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/a/38a5264844702a97c443c282284a92204c275d28.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}\]" width="184" height="32" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, or the semiperimeter, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/a/a8a89d18bba1b98335766cb9c78d7a958fb128d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a+b+c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="72" height="37" />
</p><p>The semiperimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/e/7ae90d71491f591ab5a16d19ee7629e8226ec9ce.png" class="latex" alt="$s_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568b94f1efbe48cb0f22ce851f2389e24dd6c5b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta ACP$" width="57" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2ddcd6bf8946efb92eaa10751b517424798c9af3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{[(r + 2) + (3 - r) + 1]}{2} = \frac{6}{2} = 3\]" width="244" height="38" />
Use Heron's Formula to obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/9/4394200d34aa18d82b5e489037da3808517a7eaf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A_1 = \sqrt{3(2)(3-2-r)(3-3+r)} = \sqrt{6r(1-r)} = \sqrt{6r-6r^2}\]" width="484" height="32" />

<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/6/e7665b47b608ebc8262cd50e301247f70fe77b17.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A_2 = \sqrt{3(3-2)(3-1-r)(3-3+r)} = \sqrt{3(2r-r^2)} = \sqrt{6r-3r^2}\]" width="523" height="32" />
</p><p><br />
