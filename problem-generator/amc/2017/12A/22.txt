<p>A square is drawn in the Cartesian coordinate plane with vertices at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/0/5506b58320deb5004bf2ed2f1a5e3e672d6e326e.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d77f6629a03324c8d886ed383a47e8f090b71de6.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/f/4dfc8ed4ef6b2f3ec098e1f25ded824009c3db4e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6e85b4d24c9bf37db6589e193f50dbba5ccdf8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />. A particle starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Every second it moves with equal probability to one of the eight lattice points (points with integer coordinates) closest to its current position, independently of its previous moves. In other words, the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/3/6136193afb3f1957d9feaa1b626ccc1e01eed6f8.png" class="latex" alt="$1/8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> that the particle will move from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> to each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/a/a4ad0bcb15c2b5c0bd1f5009772733672341ae7e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/23920e5481ba8a3ba28e9c447cf3084ed6ebdac0.png" class="latex" alt="$(x + 1, y + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/c/87c68e46d0751179bf4b194982e5a155a852748f.png" class="latex" alt="$(x + 1, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/b/9ebbd158dd38ea285688ac3025249efd7414f7f2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x + 1, y - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa159683250c458e150a51bb6ff90e60f479c288.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/9/d39dce75e6c818edee3521442ec2d12fe43cdad3.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 1, y - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/7/067c4d0d7d7a4fc0e14967b27667b3cae70479d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 1, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/b/35bf752f50ffa8b5617dec60eac00c131f8ebf3e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 1, y + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />. The particle will eventually hit the square for the first time, either at one of the 4 corners of the square or at one of the 12 lattice points in the interior of one of the sides of the square. The probability that it will hit at a corner rather than at an interior point of a side is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/c/83ce0c4966858ed8ef848e596e2bd7e8f694dc81.png" class="latex" alt="$m/n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are relatively prime positive integers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53bf1afcb274a3bb84338109fffec8a1f1c02008.png" class="latex" alt="$m + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/6/0b61194d076ba86a9ebe63e3503a742fc4c611aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 4 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 5 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 7 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 15 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 39$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="371" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/8/1b8dc2630e8490f348066e04e679aa87396af6eb.png" class="latex" alt="$c, e,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> be the probability of reaching a corner before an edge when starting at an "inside corner" (e.g. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31fa6b94e5a84b15c9a080cd52edc749580d90c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />), an "inside edge" (e.g. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/754e946d1a160f767f0f61cbd9460401c2d1c17f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />), and the middle respectively.
</p><p>Starting in the middle, there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/620d70ac15c246f05b2b41d6ff647e0d412532a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to an inside edge and a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/620d70ac15c246f05b2b41d6ff647e0d412532a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to an inside corner, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09c7e9598e237027f630d0902905a6fcedb167b6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[m = \frac{1}{2}e + \frac{1}{2}c.\]" width="108" height="37" />
</p><p>Starting at an inside edge, there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/33980afecb9562ff64caf9965adfd2045f949de2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to another inside edge, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/33980afecb9562ff64caf9965adfd2045f949de2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to an inside corner, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db9e227e8177d26de452c2a2faeb6d45cc53d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving into the middle, and a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/639534d18207fde5d468bcf2bb90aa2d7ad517ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of reaching an outside edge and stopping. Therefore,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36c32a22682574cd18cf923d0046bdc21c06402b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[e = \frac{1}{4}e + \frac{1}{4}c + \frac{1}{8}m + \frac{3}{8}\cdot 0 = \frac{1}{4}e + \frac{1}{4}c + \frac{1}{8}m.\]" width="349" height="37" />
</p><p>Starting at an inside corner, there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/33980afecb9562ff64caf9965adfd2045f949de2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to an inside edge, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db9e227e8177d26de452c2a2faeb6d45cc53d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving into the middle, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/620d70ac15c246f05b2b41d6ff647e0d412532a0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of moving to an outside edge and stopping, and finally a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db9e227e8177d26de452c2a2faeb6d45cc53d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance of reaching that elusive outside corner. This gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/8/4e89561af79eaabd497d3e7788882fbd93cee24f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c = \frac{1}{4}e + \frac{1}{8}m + \frac{1}{2}0 + \frac{1}{8}\cdot 1 = \frac{1}{4}e + \frac{1}{8}m + \frac{1}{8}.\]" width="342" height="37" />
</p><p>Solving this system of equations gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c7320d977b41ce17e21dd42138d560e104569ad.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[m = \frac{4}{35},\]" width="66" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/3/763d1d62d2d1de18ef658dc60ed70c272bf43963.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[e = \frac{1}{14},\]" width="58" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/f/1df8ce7dea94a05220817b43cf3de8181554f7b2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c = \frac{11}{70}.\]" width="57" height="37" />
</p><p>Since the particle starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac02b61e084dddf32f5295ba51d90a70daad5e38.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="44" height="18" /> it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> we are looking for, so the final answer is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d5c13f9717ccd15993bf112dfa2e0d835d67cfd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4 + 35 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }39}.\]" width="140" height="28" />
</p>
