<p>The infinite product
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/1/351d2e676280300007e4b9e7dd3296d6779dd7c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt[3]{10} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots\]" width="204" height="43" />
evaluates to a real number. What is that number?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/58669832b19a56c4b8fda00b4c2151cba3f003f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }\sqrt{10}\qquad\textbf{(B) }\sqrt[3]{100}\qquad\textbf{(C) }\sqrt[4]{1000}\qquad\textbf{(D) }10\qquad\textbf{(E) }10\sqrt[3]{10}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="509" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/4/9a414a76d3daf242ab82d4069a47d6a9b39428da.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="36" height="21" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/c/31c73a0eb5e08ac326da475ab8c938db8fd6a02b.png" class="latex" alt="$10 ^ \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="30" height="19" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/7/e8797d24c656db81f5590b6dd1cd96299a42f87c.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}} = (10 ^ \frac{1}{3}) ^ \frac{1}{3} = 10 ^ \frac{1}{3^2}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="191" height="35" />.
</p><p>By continuing this, we get the form
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7b4649da8dec74937e51bedbcdbcac63e018874.png" class="latex" alt="$10 ^ \frac{1}{3} \cdot 10 ^ \frac{1}{3^2} \cdot 10 ^ \frac{1}{3^3} ...$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="142" height="19" />
</p><p>which is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/c/1dcaf1004e866dc1953ea9844c2e9249ec6fabb7.png" class="latex" alt="$10 ^ {\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{3^3} ...}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="95" height="19" />.
</p><p>Using the formula for an infinite geometric series <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/a/4aac549850e9534c27b2a6eabccc5406e5a975ac.png" class="latex" alt="$S = \frac{a}{1-r}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="79" height="33" />, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/7663543bc36ea96cdb91146ad9beaa65d0c132da.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{3^3} ... = \frac{\frac{1}{3}}{1-\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="223" height="49" />
</p><p>Thus, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/4/b14cdb5345229967a0cfb42a349141ab3b465b43.png" class="latex" alt="$10 ^ \frac{1}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }\sqrt{10}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="135" height="35" />.
</p><p>- phuang1024
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can write this infinite product as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ccf4cd60c7bc6b8fa1afc9a42dc811a826d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$L$" width="12" height="12" /> (we know from the answer choices that the product must converge):
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/d/ebd4f6b0d54dfa39b6afe11fc4f71300ec7d3c6c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[L = \sqrt[3]{10} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots\]" width="242" height="43" />
</p><p>If we raise everything to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/0/c30809dd5584e7d5e6a2b4f5d0a31ce53f7611b8.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{rd}$" width="22" height="15" /> power, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/e/0eeff502018f3c83c979f22bc580b94a44a298bc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[L^3 =  10 \, \cdot \, \sqrt[3]{10} \, \cdot \, \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}} \ldots = 10L \implies L^3 - 10L = 0 \implies L \in \{0, \pm \sqrt{10}\}\]" width="562" height="32" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ccf4cd60c7bc6b8fa1afc9a42dc811a826d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$L$" width="12" height="12" /> is positive (it is an infinite product of positive numbers), it must be that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/0/eb0297e2f95692ac25ec4443074d090bcf623535.png" class="latex" alt="$L = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }\sqrt{10}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="119" height="35" />.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>~ Oxymoronic15
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Move the first term inside the second radical. We get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/47245f249f7aa3aa5e0eafff41eead4a9db3bf17.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt[3]{10} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots = \sqrt[3]{10\sqrt[3]{10}} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots\]" width="407" height="43" />
</p><p>Do this for the third radical as well.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/4/9042d406a3ef766679a1a442d8fa8f1696abca65.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt[3]{10\sqrt[3]{10}} \cdot \sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots = \sqrt[3]{10\sqrt[3]{10}\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}} \ldots = \sqrt[3]{10\sqrt[3]{10\sqrt[3]{10\ldots}}}\]" width="495" height="43" />
</p><p>It is clear what the pattern is. Setting the answer as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/e/ede78a06018011f48c24a76284afce482ebc6c47.png" class="latex" alt="$P,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="16" /> we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/b/93bc6df8c7ece436cd92a22dcc2ffb47bb78bacf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P = \sqrt[3]{10P}\]" width="86" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/b/3cb841409b9c0c8443adcabc6d88c9ee7396fccd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P = \boxed{\sqrt{10}}\]" width="83" height="30" />
</p><p>~kxiang
</p>
