<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/a/7da81cb3cee239abc137061d65f85d90d269ca50.png" class="latex" alt="$T_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> be the transformation of the coordinate plane that first rotates the plane <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> degrees counterclockwise around the origin and then reflects the plane across the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis. What is the least positive
integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that performing the sequence of transformations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/3/51343c35ba784a44081df5a49dd789daee56b933.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1, T_2, T_3, \cdots, T_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="132" height="16" /> returns the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a306093bbd9e0699c65dba033c9483cd379dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> back to itself?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/3/6836652325e249304c7d1c5a55e876b029aa040a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 359 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 360 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 719 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 720 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 721$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="446" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/2/ed2eba0a14b01383dfcca6d77e7f2439fbcfc439.png" class="latex" alt="$P=(r,\theta)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" /> be a point in polar coordinates, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> is in degrees.
</p><p>Rotating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/5/dc5472cfb23a4dd89620226202da72696017349a.png" class="latex" alt="$k^{\circ}$" width="16" height="13" /> counterclockwise around the origin gives the transformation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cd436fb42c259da3f8c0dc8d89a408be48de0ee.png" class="latex" alt="$(r,\theta)\rightarrow(r,\theta+k^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="153" height="20" /> Reflecting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> across the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis gives the transformation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/c/3cca70c9ddbcf08f4d241fa3f9ad573e3421b510.png" class="latex" alt="$(r,\theta)\rightarrow(r,180^{\circ}-\theta).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="170" height="20" /> Note that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/e/80e0251cc137c7929106fe857b58850103b35221.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} T_k(P)&amp;=(r,180^{\circ}-\theta-k^{\circ}), \\ T_{k+1}(T_k(P)) &amp;= (r,\theta -1^{\circ}). \end{align*}" width="261" height="43" />
We start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/9/e792ab230469da7b4ee7156f671fea05813b3116.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0^{\circ})$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="50" height="20" /> in polar coordinates. For the sequence of transformations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/c/6dc0032b417ec5a725e9accf70093e5920393fce.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1, T_2, T_3, \cdots, T_k,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" /> it follows that
</p>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/8/5884698f4005c39113fc7a18a11f6590cc34a0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff55fbbb2ad55200caec40757d5eb470e3a07a5a.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,179^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/7/e17c048195ae609f16acde3b733d4116b7fe5b8e.png" class="latex" alt="$T_2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/6/5e64908a336cda454bf55b2d7539586ac7af10b5.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-1^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7c24f011b7eb7b01984d63f53722d02ae46994b.png" class="latex" alt="$T_3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7fdd35ae2c16f47063545bac9305ee525b88059.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,178^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/2/80277db755c34eccf1de7192004a5eae680bf559.png" class="latex" alt="$T_4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf218d6d50906fe039671c7217c33295d10ceb5f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-2^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/a/00a86993fccae5ceb65197d4471058cd39e4158e.png" class="latex" alt="$T_5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6533c0a49c8ee4d8caeea5f9a4507264fa3fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,177^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46bfe178e5cd53484c23ecea763b9deb72d9c65c.png" class="latex" alt="$T_6,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="16" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/6/b26147e95e74b928b338aec8deab8395ff605a1b.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-3^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>...</li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f21e0e147e27a1f240cd27d5915535c20839826.png" class="latex" alt="$T_{2k-1},$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="49" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/9/22908d4fe3a2ce6777a72030dac390acc72c7975.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,180^{\circ}-k^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="113" height="20" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/d/92d19e3468f4c53751eaa90636b11248b4b58e80.png" class="latex" alt="$T_{2k},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/a/65a9b503e6b86d151b3f78b0dc18ae15356cf83c.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-k^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="70" height="20" /></li></ul>
<p>The least such positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e5839ad97e6771efbdf1f2737716119e566eba10.png" class="latex" alt="$180.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="34" height="14" /> Therefore, the least such positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/f/34fc759071c97f2fa93c1fde4168dfde6134b82b.png" class="latex" alt="$2k-1=\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 359}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="156" height="30" />
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that since we're reflecting across the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis, if the point ever makes it to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9b83617178c137c55232eea1ca6791131d94eca.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> then it will flip back to the original point. Note that after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c073f8a05257a19c2dacc368858763bb44fa3747.png" class="latex" alt="$T_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> the point will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> degree clockwise from the negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. Applying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40db9b6f10ff583906d78a06a76e08e04381bc1a.png" class="latex" alt="$T_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> will rotate it to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> degree counterclockwise from the negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis, and then flip it so that it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> degree clockwise from the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. Therefore, after every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> transformations, the point rotates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> degree clockwise. To rotate it so that it will rotate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b578e21439895ecf647228d2d1089fc5c7d59e5.png" class="latex" alt="$179$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> degrees clockwise will require <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/9/069eb9aa1abcd8002b5c178555bff69a1da2da87.png" class="latex" alt="$179 \cdot 2 = 358$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> transformations. Then finally on the last transformation, it will rotate on to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9b83617178c137c55232eea1ca6791131d94eca.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> and then flip back to it's original position. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd31551309453d9b543a8e9e1ea0f596add1f538.png" class="latex" alt="$358+1 = 359 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 359}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="210" height="30" />.
</p><p>~KingRavi
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/5/d555a2fb62bf338fd13e9d9ced457fe49932324e.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> be the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/6/676b65da88921a77dddb0c8ca988da2cbaa6e5c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(\cos n^{\circ}, \sin n^{\circ}).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="119" height="20" />
</p><p>Starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" />, the sequence goes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/9/e396b2b59daa8b59d7055d27ea6c3ab972f754c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A_{0}\rightarrow A_{179}\rightarrow A_{359}\rightarrow A_{178}\rightarrow A_{358}\rightarrow A_{177}\rightarrow A_{357}\rightarrow\cdots.\]" width="455" height="16" />
</p><p>We see that it takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> turns to downgrade the point by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/9/f7954fdecc7372bce92ddd7d4045f1942a6eeff8.png" class="latex" alt="$1^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="15" height="13" />. Since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />th point in the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5ed85742cef6f00d4ed79aba64c8f7145273ed2.png" class="latex" alt="$A_{177}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="17" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/1/10191c42d0f9619703484f6409d7b88dedf83069.png" class="latex" alt="$5+2(177)=\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 359}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="187" height="30" />
