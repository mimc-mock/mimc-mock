<p>How many three-digit positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/d/58d98ba0214a50d7d64ca253149208096064772c.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{a} \ \underline{b} \ \underline{c}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> are there whose nonzero digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b28547efd22be1c4a05fa193cd7038fc65d71b.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> satisfy
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/4/9e448c87f48492e9f1518a6989dee06242cab99f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0.\overline{\underline{a}~\underline{b}~\underline{c}} = \frac{1}{3} (0.\overline{a} + 0.\overline{b} + 0.\overline{c})?\]" width="221" height="37" />
(The bar indicates repetition, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0a175898592a6b763aac47c236314e5cc76d0.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{\underline{a}~\underline{b}~\underline{c}}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="22" /> is the infinite repeating decimal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/1588aaa4bdc902a1ff333d41541847c8094b4d8b.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\underline{a}~\underline{b}~\underline{c}~\underline{a}~\underline{b}~\underline{c}~\cdots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="18" />)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/6/d16b9b030f8acde25e666be01a8e637e00c32527.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 9 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 10 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 11 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 13 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 14$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="392" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We rewrite the given equation, then rearrange:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/574485f1e449aad173c26ce9e276a358de6a3900.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{100a+10b+c}{999} &amp;= \frac13\left(\frac a9 + \frac b9 + \frac c9\right) \\ 100a+10b+c &amp;= 37a + 37b + 37c \\ 63a &amp;= 27b+36c \\ 7a &amp;= 3b+4c. \end{align*}" width="265" height="115" />
Now, this problem is equivalent to counting the ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3d9e22097e20a1249b5332202b24941a4bf9e1f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> that satisfies the equation.
</p><p>Clearly, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19ab2712d5be82676ba4bcfef68ef44dbf251593.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)=(1,1,1),(2,2,2),\ldots,(9,9,9)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="303" height="20" /> are solutions to this equation.
</p><p>The expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/a/84afc4c65c21e035d649f26cb942959bf2d95d2d.png" class="latex" alt="$3b+4c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="58" height="16" /> has the same value when:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> decreases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93032f1ab8b685d84a767a2fde0d9006a464b5e4.png" class="latex" alt="$3.$" width="12" height="12" /></li></ul>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> decreases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93032f1ab8b685d84a767a2fde0d9006a464b5e4.png" class="latex" alt="$3.$" width="12" height="12" /></li></ul>
<p>We find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> more solutions from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> solutions above: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1d8053d1bc2a5ed63bec2ab96159bc20305bdb.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)=(4,8,1),(5,1,8),(5,9,2),(6,2,9).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="341" height="20" /> Note that all solutions are symmetric about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f2193c286d812dcf1dd9f45ed1e8d4b84f13cb.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)=(5,5,5).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="144" height="20" />
</p><p>Together, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/a/faa6d7091d0e1036875520662bad98a814f8cc9e.png" class="latex" alt="$9+4=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 13}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="132" height="30" /> ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/e/e3e4ce6135f44658120400b12106ad7ced4cd72f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="62" height="20" />
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
