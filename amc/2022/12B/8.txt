<p>What is the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/8/b08d4f9c466fcdf14dadf8773712ed69b072c229.png" class="latex" alt="$y^4+1=x^4+2y^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="20" /> in the coordinate plane?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/7/8371dc4146ed640c799a81d09d7a70eb070416a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{two intersecting parabolas} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \text{two nonintersecting parabolas} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \text{two intersecting circles} \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="777" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c92a45613191ec2382d225e7c95de1c5204b38.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}\ \text{a circle and a hyperbola} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \text{a circle and two parabolas}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="469" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since the equation has even powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919521ced5acdd82f6aedc7e8d82569df0d585b5.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39;=y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/2/712139c426886f3e9b0eb764d8f39e9968a9435f.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39; = x^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="17" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/0/c90097967368ab53c7f00b60b2b91a92affb621a.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39;^2 + 1 = x&#39;^2 + 2y&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="20" />. Rearranging gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/4/964a7416073c1ccacec726f65a22ec21b129e18a.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39;^2 - 2y&#39; + 1 = x&#39;^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="20" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/8/4c8972a37af5b2e198ef7755442115abe7c124d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(y&#39;-1)^2=x&#39;^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="115" height="21" />. There are two cases: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec15e50dbe5e28f22afae8fb0e43df5f964554a.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; \leq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="19" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/c/efc1c07db9137662ef1310c2621c1c527a3b16b4.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="19" />.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec15e50dbe5e28f22afae8fb0e43df5f964554a.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; \leq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="19" />, taking the square root of both sides gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/e/21ebb8c47fe3935671c48f141cf2d9a443167666.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - y&#39; = x&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="19" />, and rearranging gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/5/db538980d918c60b43c9e898ff9b17482a56922b.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39; + y&#39; = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="19" />. Substituting back in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/e/f7ee1db867c31c4213dd5e70a6a751aefb9ee75b.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39;=x^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919521ced5acdd82f6aedc7e8d82569df0d585b5.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39;=y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="18" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/d/f1dd4184b561ea747be652fc3dee7fc5e1c5103f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="18" />, the equation for a circle.
</p><p>Similarly, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/c/efc1c07db9137662ef1310c2621c1c527a3b16b4.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="19" />, we take the square root of both sides to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/2/5322c36077d9937f35fc53ff5e6bafd86cffc756.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; - 1 = x&#39;$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="19" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/0/f209ba05b3381912beb86ba37979f53724a77d5d.png" class="latex" alt="$y&#39; - x&#39; = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="19" />, which is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/8/a88ec66b44e6decb2f0f9757d27fa50ceb4a5c7c.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2 - x^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="20" />, a hyperbola.
</p><p>Hence, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/0/dd0028efe79cc3b0dae1757851d2f55e2cb816ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \text{a circle and a hyperbola}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="224" height="30" />.
</p><p>~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Bxiao31415" title="User:Bxiao31415">Bxiao31415</a>
</p>
