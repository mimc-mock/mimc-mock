<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are positive real numbers such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/f/5ef8c5bc88c35ef680a20c247fbc74f7e8742720.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^y=2^{64}\text{ and }(\log_2{x})^{\log_2{y}}=2^{7}.\]" width="240" height="20" />
What is the greatest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/9/269176d287ca1ea71b8ce347a1248109dd332fa2.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2{y}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="19" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/31170ede509d9ab1a5877daa401379305938d854.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }3 \qquad \textbf{(B) }4 \qquad \textbf{(C) }3+\sqrt{2} \qquad \textbf{(D) }4+\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf{(E) }7$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="448" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Take the base-two logarithm of both equations to get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/4/8f47a4b368e4f0415f96769e9598dec209b2f8f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y\log_2 x = 64\quad\text{and}\quad (\log_2 y)(\log_2\log_2 x) = 7.\]" width="345" height="18" />
Now taking the base-two logarithm of the first equation again yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/c/06c0a34836171b89dbc9a02b784e9f120723f0c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_2 y + \log_2\log_2 x = 6.\]" width="180" height="17" />
It follows that the real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/d/01d8dfda8c9faca3c27c6d8420e0b9fae65e39b8.png" class="latex" alt="$r:=\log_2 y$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="83" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/6/d962dbc52baf988371e2613d72f5fd2cd6426999.png" class="latex" alt="$s:=\log_2\log_2 x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="118" height="19" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/b/9dbdeb94548ed339f9e6b7f603dd17868bda50d6.png" class="latex" alt="$r+s=6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4fe513b58d2e453868dbfe5c5d2987f216c7c0.png" class="latex" alt="$rs = 7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="14" />. Solving this system yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/3/953110c6ca9748e7dc665b6fd0de7a159b57ba92.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\{\log_2 y,\log_2\log_2 x\}\in\{3-\sqrt 2, 3 + \sqrt 2\}.\]" width="310" height="21" />
Thus the largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/6/cd6d7ae2be65d02a6145ccceb31a906e5e2c37a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2 y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/3/743fa42853fdda48873c00cebca8d89d900100e3.png" class="latex" alt="$3+\sqrt 2 \implies \boxed{\textbf {(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="147" height="30" />.
</p><p>cr. djmathman
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/6/086c679d70adb4f8d2a8f230ca6e32123d4adb02.png" class="latex" alt="$x^y=2^{64} \Rightarrow y\log_2{x}=64 \Rightarrow \log_2{x}=\dfrac{64}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="314" height="42" />.
</p><p>Substitution into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/b/7bb1e6db2f38c35e2705ee2ea4a2a36f3a4c55d8.png" class="latex" alt="$(\log_2{x})^{\log_2{y}}=2^{7}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="135" height="22" /> yields
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/3/1138c11f60a9b1224fc25de5e761d8a1b541108d.png" class="latex" alt="$(\dfrac{64}{y})^{\log_2{y}}=2^{7} \Rightarrow \log_2{y}\log_2{\dfrac{64}{y}}=7 \Rightarrow \log_2{y}(6-\log_2{y})=7 \Rightarrow \log^2_2{y}-6\log_2{y}+7=0$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="686" height="42" />.
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/9/269176d287ca1ea71b8ce347a1248109dd332fa2.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2{y}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="19" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/8/b687997295a04dfb55d9d766a021fe33a8c2523f.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2{y}=3-\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="125" height="24" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/0/650626df9ebacae4851eece8f6f410186b820615.png" class="latex" alt="$3+\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="56" height="18" />, and we take the greater value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/2/f327348d6a04fc101d2cc99a76ed0f3c5fa99b7f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\boldsymbol{(\textbf{C})3+\sqrt{2}}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="107" height="36" />.
</p><p>~4SunnyH
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/2/512403b3734fcd98faa423b39edfb6994660add9.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 2^a, y = 2^b.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="21" /> We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/8/a4892fe380c43e30fde01fb0a7bb7358ae99f49a.png" class="latex" alt="$(2^a)^{2^b} = 2^{64} \Rightarrow 2^{a\cdot 2^b} = 2^{64} \Rightarrow a\cdot 2^b = 64,$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="318" height="25" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/e/53eb9d70d5f2c48aafe5ba26a2e93b0109841121.png" class="latex" alt="$a^b = 128$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="17" />.
</p><p>Then, from eq 1, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/f/b4fe9862a01df68d08c319c8be6e087ddc80522f.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 64\cdot 2^{-b},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="21" /> and substituting in to eq 2, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/f/1bf6bac519bda05e56ad20d49e77e7dff5a515aa.png" class="latex" alt="$(64\cdot 2^{-b})^b = 64^b\cdot 2^{-b^2} = 2^{6b}\cdot 2^{-b^2} = 2^{6b-b^2} = 2^{7}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="382" height="24" /> Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/1/a418bc71e84a926c90558ff3b9212606ae490094.png" class="latex" alt="$6b-b^2 = 7.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="95" height="18" />
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> using the quadratic formula gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/2/332cd7b3429188b545ab6f9aa240b1bc19219357.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 3 \pm \sqrt{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="21" /> Since we are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/9/269176d287ca1ea71b8ce347a1248109dd332fa2.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2{y}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="19" /> which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d6265e67657b5886ce257671d45ff9c0282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="16" /> we put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/a/0ca496e05b2a6499a7ce7dd769ecfadb66386a17.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} \ 3+\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="103" height="35" /> as our answer.
</p><p>~sirswagger21
</p>
