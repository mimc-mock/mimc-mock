<p>Camila writes down five positive integers. The unique mode of these integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> greater than their median, and the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> greater than their arithmetic mean. What is the least possible value for the mode?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/270954708129715c77d9ded6342e425b3a034860.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 7 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 9 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 11 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 13$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="369" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the median. It follows that the two largest integers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/8/77830fb144c7845189e6f24e94f10a55a118b698.png" class="latex" alt="$M+2.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="58" height="16" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the two smallest integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/9/4390726363479c0cbb8ddc106faf7021e2067668.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="45" height="13" /> The sorted list is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/0/9a0ec4d708d73a1c4bb1a86ddef45f82d1310e0c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a,b,M,M+2,M+2.\]" width="173" height="16" />
Since the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> greater than their arithmetic mean, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/9/6a957e98eae470803e49269d7f10fdb33be89c83.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a+b+M+(M+2)+(M+2)}{5}+2=M,$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="341" height="40" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0088148fb4116cbdabacd5e4e1c77d86cfd961.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+b+14=2M.\]" width="135" height="14" />
Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> must be even. We minimize this sum so that the arithmetic mean, the median, and the unique mode are minimized. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/d/42da0ebdd0e73fef720b015a11b6f1d9cf65825d.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34819452270cc13bc43752f5450c86628ebfa0c2.png" class="latex" alt="$M=9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75edcc3ddc98d17ef2586318a863cb4c1eed7f38.png" class="latex" alt="$M+2=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 11}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="147" height="30" />
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can also easily test all the answer choices. 
</p><p>For answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/3/cb3db983b534373488fba3fdfaf7b692194bb301.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="17" /> the mode is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/21415a2eb558eb7c199a0382fcd50cbeda9299fb.png" class="latex" alt="$5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f885f96d2fb7e65f800de4ff71d7dcd0a5d2db5.png" class="latex" alt="$3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and the arithmetic mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdd1b53e5faf7a118222e4b7571d1e9afa0cf56.png" class="latex" alt="$1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> However, we can quickly see this doesn't work, as there are five integers, and they can't have an arithmetic mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> while having a mode of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> 
</p><p>Trying answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/d/a5d19752c57bc1e73b61c9b9940635a708e80943.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="17" /> the mode is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/4/43420adcd0fce9dce3978d53b97a8e0262b58da4.png" class="latex" alt="$7,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/21415a2eb558eb7c199a0382fcd50cbeda9299fb.png" class="latex" alt="$5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and the arithmetic mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93032f1ab8b685d84a767a2fde0d9006a464b5e4.png" class="latex" alt="$3.$" width="12" height="12" /> From the arithmetic mean, we know that all the numbers have to sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1119733ed51d0c818ac7464686341a08905d3c.png" class="latex" alt="$15.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" /> We know three of the numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/7/d5755f7e8bcaa131d9563b33b43f1a33ecbb6043.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{\hspace{3mm}},\underline{\hspace{3mm}},5,7,7.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="17" /> This exceeds the sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1119733ed51d0c818ac7464686341a08905d3c.png" class="latex" alt="$15.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" />
</p><p>Now we try answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/7/f07759fa176d8edab201426b075c105d704ff275.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="17" /> The mode is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/1288f16f77606bb83db76df0877615ef6e8b149e.png" class="latex" alt="$9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/4/43420adcd0fce9dce3978d53b97a8e0262b58da4.png" class="latex" alt="$7,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> and the arithmetic mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> From the arithmetic mean, we know that the list sums to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bc7bd42e6cb90851b324c8f2955a8d3021b6cb.png" class="latex" alt="$25.$" width="21" height="12" /> Three of the numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/c/68cb4317de1fc3d11c166d2f9934483c17889c28.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{\hspace{3mm}},\underline{\hspace{3mm}},7,9,9,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="17" /> which is exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bc7bd42e6cb90851b324c8f2955a8d3021b6cb.png" class="latex" alt="$25.$" width="21" height="12" /> However, our list needs positive integers, so this won't work.
</p><p>Since we were really close on answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/0/3e0177d15168f7b1af172ddb1ebd4ae8d56b1aea.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="17" /> we can intuitively feel that the answer is probably going to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4feb78447beef6c93631b0912fb24e7936b6dad3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="17" /> We can confirm this by creating a list that satisfies the problem and choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a5bc6c8333917a47cc07b0b531705727e8ccea0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}: 1,3,9,11,11.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="147" height="18" />
</p><p>So, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/3/f93c53715a169a74ac90775a47000d8031b60110.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 11}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="71" height="30" />
</p>
