<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> be the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/1/b013563174cfbf4d48d26388b12a9c3ce62d6021.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}+kx+36.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="18" /> By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_Formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta&#39;s Formulas">Vieta's Formulas</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/7/bd7d7644a488bb357f8568c595607d9ab01e6aff.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q=-k$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/3/783c1e058669ce9057c0b977bb6b567ac4378fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$pq=36.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="17" />
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> must be distinct factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/9/7997886baa31e286c806e30d7c27c5ffe64ce2a4.png" class="latex" alt="$36.$" width="21" height="12" /> The possibilities of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/8/278acbe0bc88dfb0707019572e8a0529f4381e28.png" class="latex" alt="$\{p,q\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/9/9a995e75c8baf10fff9deffb17fa9f4cdd457393.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\pm\{1,36\},\pm\{2,18\},\pm\{3,12\},\pm\{4,9\}.\]" width="288" height="18" />
Each unordered pair gives a unique value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d48e0968a64d782fe75bca620ce749938b7d0bb.png" class="latex" alt="$k.$" width="13" height="12" /> Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/6/ea6849aecee476ae66ffcecf8513dd162124719c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="57" height="30" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99a6aaebc17474bd09ea71d84d4e769a0662b55.png" class="latex" alt="$k,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="16" /> namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/6/7c6e4bcce4da2ea815e168cf038a8a9bc9916793.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm37,\pm20,\pm15,\pm13.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> must be an integer. Using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_formula" title="Quadratic formula">quadratic formula</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87ec79c636ca398ab891f35d4bdc3e05b5d565ba.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{-k \pm \sqrt{k^2-144}}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="174" height="43" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50ff9540650f0f95f80da2f6692fc8ee413e2eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$144$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="12" /> evenly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/c/6dc771678ef0e5a75c69b4bd2558e81f06d14f07.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2-144$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="18" /> have the same parity, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/c/6dc771678ef0e5a75c69b4bd2558e81f06d14f07.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2-144$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="69" height="18" /> is a perfect square.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/c/f4c844e82633ff1cc1b5c3a016bffe8f2ada7fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2-144=n^2.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="116" height="18" /> Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/31532981eb29c6d8c9179a74c477a84562a83f70.png" class="latex" alt="$(k+n)(k-n)=144.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="173" height="20" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is an integer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50ff9540650f0f95f80da2f6692fc8ee413e2eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$144$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="12" /> is even, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/c/72c9bea668f84e83795d27b6eb8d4c49e8f4c093.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b5856a822c658802de6e2b870bf8c06f9e83aeda.png" class="latex" alt="$k-n$" width="43" height="12" /> must both be even. Assuming that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is positive, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/c/72c9bea668f84e83795d27b6eb8d4c49e8f4c093.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="14" />, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/5/21504d30a3a6cd46c1dbefdb1f94c1dc49cfa788.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 4, 8, 6, 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="17" />, which will give distinct positive values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/a/23a1e85d8c0a4ca5adb70b46e70ea997278417e7.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n=12$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="87" height="16" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/7/6373da91b884fc080ae4c877efd7642dfddb36dc.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n=k-n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="112" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02349576cee613512cdf301a365f06c0760acab5.png" class="latex" alt="$n=0$" width="43" height="12" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> identical integer roots. Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> distinct positive values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d48e0968a64d782fe75bca620ce749938b7d0bb.png" class="latex" alt="$k.$" width="13" height="12" /> Multiplying that by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to take the negative values into account, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e05d7eaa3a913eaf169a9daed13f93d88500a92.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot2=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="113" height="30" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
