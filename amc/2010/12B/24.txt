<p>The set of real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> for which 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/d/f9d51dcb945e183da3554b60e54e9f18e92536e0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{1}{x-2009}+\dfrac{1}{x-2010}+\dfrac{1}{x-2011}\ge1\]" width="293" height="37" />
</p><p>is the union of intervals of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/5/4258e901bad3a205164869d2af2fc939671c9e46.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;x\le b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" />. What is the sum of the lengths of these intervals?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/6/4c6075fb1998fa3f5dfd6c2e86f0cb71cab0f346.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{1003}{335} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{1004}{335} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \dfrac{403}{134} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \dfrac{202}{67}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="454" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Because the right side of the inequality is a horizontal line, the left side can be translated horizontally by any value and the intervals will remain the same. For simplicity of calculation, we will find the intervals where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/4/324910843f199b5977e90e0a097a86d713c7c1e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x-1}\ge1\]" width="180" height="38" />
We shall say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f57aa683f951fe4e7f1a5f101e2d35a0127953e6.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x-1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="208" height="38" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> has three vertical asymptotes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/f/aff897c3a386a71417122ababee7125f5878a811.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\{-1,0,1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />. As the sum of decreasing hyperbolas, the function is decreasing at all intervals. Values immediately to the left of each asymptote approach negative infinity, and values immediately to the right of each asymptote approach positive infinity. In addition, the function has a horizontal asymptote at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. The function intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> at some point from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86c2e8c17cff668023b2f29f2e95e4a0fafa6271.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, and at some point from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, and at some point to the right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. The intervals where the function is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> are between the points where the function equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and the vertical asymptotes.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> are values of x where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/4/224ae5ff2e22575ba5a41f26a68c2c5fdc0d502d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, then the sum of the lengths of the intervals is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/3/fe35547ef462faf68a229632b4bf7a1a601ae9af.png" class="latex" alt="$(p-(-1))+(q-0)+(r-1)=p+q+r$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/7/ee708104d86af6b6b8ed65c2e151d9e270bb9073.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x-1}=1\]" width="180" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eefc271d737d4992a71d9f3510f76ed6ef2da97d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies x(x-1)+(x-1)(x+1)+x(x+1)=x(x-1)(x+1)\]" width="471" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dceb886f833655131c1a823ebc38182f71cc178d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies x^3-3x^2-x+1=0\]" width="202" height="16" />
</p><p>And now our job is simply to find the sum of the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/476ff99207b3fe03aee5fb11339b59bde0c786a1.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-3x^2-x+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="16" />.
Using <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>, we find this to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/6/89687cd0176d13ffe9803a01cac203a3fbaeba0f.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="49" height="24" />.
</p><p><i>NOTE'</i>: For the AMC, one may note that the transformed inequality should not yield solutions that involve big numbers like 67 or 134, and immediately choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As in the first solution, note that the expression can be translated into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/4/324910843f199b5977e90e0a097a86d713c7c1e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x-1}\ge1\]" width="180" height="38" /> without affecting the interval lengths.
</p><p>This simplifies into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb712003910d5156742641c1f1ffc4a18de12a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{-x^3+3x^2+x-1}{(x)(x+1)(x-1)}\ge0,\]" width="184" height="43" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7f7f7d20662f7dbf1dc2faa8a2da802e035e324.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-x^3+3x^2+x-1\ge0.\]" width="180" height="17" />
Each interval is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/c/29cc50ab9ec85a77c47a1c17b3d20a871b149fbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, a), (0, b), (1, c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfc4326ebc5c149ac6525cac81769380ba13fd77.png" class="latex" alt="$-x^3+3x^2+x-1=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="178" height="16" />
so the total length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6ebd1e0356cfe09c0d9b5459d6f1542657bb5891.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />, which is the sum of the roots, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/1/a4167a37efaddc6ceec10462260cabd36341ca8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that all of the answers but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> have weird factors of 2010, but 2010 is a random number (set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/1/3a14b00d2078cf09fcde4098aff3fa7c759742ab.png" class="latex" alt="$x&#39;=x-2010$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="110" height="17" />). So therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef8f46d35099cb6a032e6bc4af224432be78897c.png" class="latex" alt="$\fbox{C(3)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="45" height="28" />
</p>
