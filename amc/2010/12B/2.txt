<p>A big <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ccf4cd60c7bc6b8fa1afc9a42dc811a826d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$L$" width="12" height="12" /> is formed as shown. What is its area?
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/2/122a4290575eda1b63a0b302c47384ced6bb393e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(4mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt));  draw((0,0)--(5,0)--(5,2)--(2,2)--(2,8)--(0,8)--cycle); label(&quot;8&quot;,(0,4),W); label(&quot;5&quot;,(5/2,0),S); label(&quot;2&quot;,(5,1),E); label(&quot;2&quot;,(1,8),N); [/asy]" width="132" height="195" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/b/38b18ed553eb360bacc2afdd32cf79cfff36a3d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 22 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 26 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 28 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 30$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We find the area of the big rectangle to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/0/740ef6e0d64563c4a74168709993fd2759913d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \times 5 = 40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />, and the area of the smaller rectangle to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/63870fb36f67e9ef13cc2e70f392a733a2c953cf.png" class="latex" alt="$(8 - 2) \times (5 - 2) = 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="173" height="18" />. The answer is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/1/281a4deb9e86dcec7aa5fa195078c46a571720ef.png" class="latex" alt="$40 - 18 = 22$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/5/565ab5e3715db52b9176b04dd9991b71f4e93a86.png" class="latex" alt="$(A)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
