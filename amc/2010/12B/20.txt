<p>A geometric sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e848f9188a9fd706d99ab6513a7d949c48a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82fd25d7548dfa831a4133fc927ed7a66500cf28.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\sin x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/0/f800cf6893f0827d2a3d5372d0229438cc65e948.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=\cos x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="78" height="10" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c63f6de716a41cb0b10c644f4d96a19a3ae64fe.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3= \tan x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="80" height="14" /> for some real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. For what value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> does <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35bbbe898fc95d012b6a08532516a8485c6d92d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n=1+\cos x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="111" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/e/ccefe446ac26b1d2ff26fd59aaaea8ce281b5a83.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 7 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By the defintion of a geometric sequence, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7e140e61e7f2c02a7dee7f2e29951b0de5b737.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^2x=\sin x \tan x$" width="147" height="15" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa13e9b66ffef6a37a9be3fa57e0a606a5e9454d.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan x=\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="104" height="37" />, we can rewrite this as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/1/ca1c361e1368dbc1c5a12bb4f290621fac88cde5.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^3x=\sin^2x$" width="112" height="15" />. 
</p><p>The common ratio of the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd83a266176775fae7bf2f4851ea819387da970e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="40" height="33" />, so we can write
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aabb1dd85adb650024782c4a00d25af69ce9d08.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_1= \sin x\]" width="76" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/c/43ca1cff036216ab2bfd7f46fbdb3e5c9fd953cd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_2= \cos x\]" width="78" height="11" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/3/b2393d9ecc7f124d48e1f02f9b4e73260bff9d5a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_3= \frac{\cos^2x}{\sin x}\]" width="88" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192747714a5472598036838a6af4ca461a7fb764.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_4=\frac{\cos^3x}{\sin^2x}=1\]" width="122" height="40" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/3/203672b722b8e4a64a0f72c6e2271938abe92355.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_5=\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\]" width="80" height="32" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/0/6105eae8dce61679696242306072701b1a158834.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_6=\frac{\cos^2x}{\sin^2x}\]" width="88" height="40" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9af8c8f10513413e48d001ab1bc43bac716fb4f9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_7=\frac{\cos^3x}{\sin^3x}=\frac{1}{\sin x}\]" width="152" height="40" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67fb2736c525445714ed24588d8b56676298dc00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_8=\frac{\cos x}{\sin^2 x}=\frac{1}{\cos^2 x}\]" width="159" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f324cfd4df5fa892a1833b2b02d9291073b1a4d.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^3x=\sin^2x=1-\cos^2x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="213" height="16" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/6/c5638f9de83638d59f75341c42b16ec4a171b25f.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^3x+\cos^2x=1 \implies \cos^2x(\cos x+1)=1 \implies \cos x+1=\frac{1}{\cos^2 x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="547" height="37" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/a/7ead075983f8dbfa721f00c28be967778b4a6677.png" class="latex" alt="$a_8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> , making our answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/f/8afb5aed613a30f7d0dce32866e464fc9181b32e.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \Rightarrow \boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="63" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Notice that the common ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/a/ada2c0a60be10804ac1bca9b527e5979e66a520d.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac{\cos(x)}{\sin(x)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="84" height="43" />; multiplying it to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/9588b40a54c466065cc319ee67b576ed8acc11ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan(x)=\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="126" height="43" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe0c40815344128c5f5388d25a985a6f16f668e4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />. Then, working backwards we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/e/e1e0d718d188a331bc44a7dd69d60eadc898927c.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=\frac{1}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2fc05b934387d161a4867774f18ba10fb448350.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=\frac{1}{r^2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7eafc6d17912c704ffae773acccd07dda1b8c1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\frac{1}{r^3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" />. Now notice that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/f/e9f28a0b480bde17dc5f20a933ed7da92802d66e.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=\sin(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/7/6477cc49cd88ec6c6294cf93575b17aed2a9e3dc.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=\cos(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />, we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45e1d434d7048340cddca16498439644d72e64d4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1^2+a_2^2=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="19" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/e/53e1af7c7f68b5aabe486ac2e7e15b66d2299d45.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{r^6}+\frac{1}{r^4}=\frac{r^2+1}{r^6}=1\implies r^2+1=r^6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="307" height="39" />. Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/2/e2221c84f16a3f75ae07e5023384cc40d9c05357.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2$" width="14" height="15" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/e/b6e2155a7ff636048fc6e3c63470e3c01d4703e1.png" class="latex" alt="$1+\frac{1}{r^2}=r^4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="37" />, which the left side is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2f54ffd339957d807ce07961aae1f97f0169b51.png" class="latex" alt="$1+\cos(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />; we see as well that the right hand side is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/a/7ead075983f8dbfa721f00c28be967778b4a6677.png" class="latex" alt="$a_8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> given <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/0/fe0c40815344128c5f5388d25a985a6f16f668e4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/2/55206bce678ac0394d97dc04dd72398df2bdf572.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />. - mathleticguyyy
</p>
