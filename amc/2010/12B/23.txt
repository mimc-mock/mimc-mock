What is the sum of the minimum values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dfe7c7a0b887097fa2227a230fc915ba2e5477.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/a/cfadc880207aa66a17a0df0ffdd621b33e0a7a59.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = (x - a)^2 - b, Q(x) = (x - c)^2 - d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="322" height="19" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> has roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/daddc78438b8f76915392b97a8c5476dea8fa22c.png" class="latex" alt="$a\pm \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="18" />, so that the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a391549fa05320091d96f9251e4ac2e31376eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$P(Q(x))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> are the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/a/daa2daf364a02d51620bcf08c46bf6ffd892b11b.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x) = a + \sqrt {b}, a - \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="22" />. For each individual equation, the sum of the roots will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f6072f36e4577a20084d5e518bf7e2f2068ab3d.png" class="latex" alt="$2c$" width="17" height="12" /> (symmetry or Vieta's). Thus, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/4/cb46b9165607510d1bda306eb6712823563c265e.png" class="latex" alt="$4c = - 23 - 21 - 17 - 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="194" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/7475f1416ba09ea138c0b514d31052a558b7928d.png" class="latex" alt="$c = - 19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" />. Doing something similar for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/b/19b49f668f73e5181e04854534005c8c416115e2.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(P(x))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/3/243d9be138d829c8d64223816be7573e7c16f181.png" class="latex" alt="$a = - 54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" />.
We now have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/6/106e3e22b2719600a3dba8c91138d1aaa15bc935.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = (x + 54)^2 - b, Q(x) = (x + 19)^2 - d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="340" height="19" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> is monic, the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/0/1402b0056a23b2d51ace1c77925a182fb0af3cae.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x) = a + \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="22" /> are "farther" from the axis of symmetry than the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4ccb1d29bf960b86982f919aa374fac93006c88b.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x) = a - \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="22" />. Thus, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/5/715c3299730503275810f7235e3a90c90d752b1e.png" class="latex" alt="$Q( - 23) = - 54 + \sqrt {b}, Q( -21) =- 54 - \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="333" height="22" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50d91cbf7455cebdffddfd306219d007d5534a89.png" class="latex" alt="$16 - d = - 54 + \sqrt {b}, 4 - d = - 54 - \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="302" height="21" />. Adding these gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40ea101855b1c028ca63c2ce0955fc31c02f1355.png" class="latex" alt="$20 - 2d = - 108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="123" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e63123c570d078498dd734fc7996c8fe98b0c1.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />. Plugging this into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/4/e245f1b52c09d571affb154a8915dd539948dc5f.png" class="latex" alt="$16 - d = - 54 + \sqrt {b}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="152" height="19" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/e/13eae6c93267da55037d62be2372e475d032d750.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 36$" width="49" height="12" />.
The minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e7dff418736af20bf61bd56bf7a20fd7d0a674.png" class="latex" alt="$- b$" width="22" height="12" />, and the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dfe7c7a0b887097fa2227a230fc915ba2e5477.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/164f45abd55983a8e87fcb0ee029f3a9a0d31ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$- d$" width="23" height="12" />. Thus, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/b/c2bc7e167c04604697f9f663be24512bbb3c6be8.png" class="latex" alt="$- (b + d) = - 100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="132" height="18" />, or answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
