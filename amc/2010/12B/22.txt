<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> be a cyclic quadrilateral. The side lengths of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> are distinct integers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/e/50ebd842390a502751f429e3fbcc529e4f68816d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC\cdot CD=AB\cdot DA$" width="167" height="13" />. What is the largest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/4/5442f300e3e2fd6d6516d361b218c3c01b19369c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \sqrt{\dfrac{325}{2}} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \sqrt{185} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \sqrt{\dfrac{389}{2}} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \sqrt{\dfrac{425}{2}} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \sqrt{\dfrac{533}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="542" height="43" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/0/f80e67527934e0557de487e6fdc23ea439b269b1.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = a$" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/d/a7d5f81f182d0b2c07569dfc7ce37b85d901cabf.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = b$" width="61" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/c/d9c125ae5c4c453e15e1cca88cbcf5135549137f.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = c$" width="62" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/2/da2762ed3957f65b3d18cfb2dbd39bfc921d44ab.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = d$" width="63" height="13" />.  We see that by the Law of Cosines on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/86644a0d74ba9ba18fc2e69713901863ab6c3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CBD$" width="60" height="13" />, we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/5/4b5749dfc23fa4e4ac3971c679cedd6879558dc5.png" class="latex" alt="$BD^2 = a^2 + d^2 - 2ad\cos{\angle BAD}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="255" height="16" />.  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/9/0f9e2c1fd170f992e811de9f6a4ae97bcbee83ad.png" class="latex" alt="$BD^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos{\angle BCD}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="249" height="16" />.
</p><p>We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1ea99f7d8c51a0673f220b40db1fdcb812408c.png" class="latex" alt="$ad = bc$" width="59" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a cyclic quadrilateral. As a property of cyclic quadrilaterals, opposite angles are supplementary so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/8/a38ad0a8b45f8fedb2acf4c5973bc3abc4f4f263.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAD = 180 - \angle BCD$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="187" height="13" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/2/a627037f9ba193fdc1c63afa38a9d9a98d5cff7a.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{\angle BAD} = -\cos{\angle BCD}$" width="209" height="13" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/7/9e7076e3d013ee54a6fec0d3f36154f50c8ef407.png" class="latex" alt="$2ad\cos{\angle BAD} = -2bc\cos{\angle BCD}$" width="265" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Adding, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/2/7d2ea7a955e22a411510b848c9bee3ca561d8aa3.png" class="latex" alt="$2BD^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="201" height="16" />.
</p><p>We now look at the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1ea99f7d8c51a0673f220b40db1fdcb812408c.png" class="latex" alt="$ad = bc$" width="59" height="12" />.  Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75d29a3a31438e162d1b3050d3656c4863b4a2d.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" />.  Then, we must have either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />.  Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6c122a5f4ffcb58a92ffc845df880b199f6571c.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 7$" width="41" height="12" />.  We let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/5/5d5cea31c66ebe6e26b3a41f6c64316f0f618b09.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 6$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/e/2eeb57269daee143ec45dcedeadf422f2cf9b271.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" />.  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/3/793e6bfc94c650283ec9e18156dc60e0142f11d5.png" class="latex" alt="$2BD^2 = 196 + 49 + 36 + 144 = 425$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="279" height="16" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40eda3b32803a0e4260c393662025208d23c0b7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \sqrt{\frac{425}{2}}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="90" height="55" />.
</p>
