<p>Every high school in the city of Euclid sent a team of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> students to a math contest. Each participant in the contest received a different score. Andrea's score was the median among all students, and hers was the highest score on her team. Andrea's teammates Beth and Carla placed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" /><sup>th</sup> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e228ac5bee7b9ffb60cacf50f3f2c7c31f8038.png" class="latex" alt="$64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /><sup>th</sup>, respectively. How many schools are in the city?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/f/02fd604bd7f271ff4e8412e5d292d68770452dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 22 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 23 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 25 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 26$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> schools. This means that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" /> people. Because no one's score was the same as another person's score, that means that there could only have been <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> median score. This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is an odd number. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> cannot be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" />, because there wouldn't be a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e228ac5bee7b9ffb60cacf50f3f2c7c31f8038.png" class="latex" alt="$64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />th place if x was. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> cannot be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> either, because that would tie Andrea and Beth or Andrea's place would be worse than Beth's. Thus, the only possible answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/6/a86f41056bdd1a0c6d66a0905c2a1c1b9b2c5d32.png" class="latex" alt="$23 \Rightarrow \boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be Andrea's place. We know that she was the highest on her team, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/9/349c70db7121b7a4bc7552a22ced7c9acbe7de17.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; 37$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the median, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/6/cc6873715a60ff7706da71b8cad30cead78b1ad3.png" class="latex" alt="$a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> to the left and right of the median, so the total number of people is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f1daa9ab402c89fd76285ac43ae812ff2e5b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$2a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" /> and the number of schools is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab3441858e47560ccd3b12300af80d1b8d15e4f.png" class="latex" alt="$(2a-1)/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />. This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/0/b20df674018637c43520d7f5789f4a020d90a670.png" class="latex" alt="$2a-1 \equiv 0 \pmod{3} \implies a \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="318" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Also, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f1daa9ab402c89fd76285ac43ae812ff2e5b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$2a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" /> is the rank of the last-place person, and one of Andrea's teammates already got 64th place, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/b/50b53d56cd2af918734408a5b4aa59edc4dd1d88.png" class="latex" alt="$2a-1 &gt; 64 \implies a \ge 33$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="193" height="15" />.
</p><p>Putting it all together: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53b7e700aafcd657916d8bd617cdd4d1b60bc6c7.png" class="latex" alt="$33 \le a &lt; 37$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fc95d74d277f19e2896fd4ad9cbeb11dad43830.png" class="latex" alt="$a \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" />, so clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52b7bf59e10f7d19db6e57fb7df7dbb6ea711eb4.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 35$" width="51" height="12" />, and the number of schools as we got before is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab73e62089c294642fd002e01020a8b813e7ad4.png" class="latex" alt="$(2a-1)/3 = 69/3 = \boxed{23}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="197" height="23" />.
</p><p>~adihaya
</p>
