<p>The solution of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48de8e61c1c6065c2944d909d7343bd8c40a41cb.png" class="latex" alt="$7^{x+7} = 8^x$" width="74" height="15" /> can be expressed in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/b/41bf9e256ab9b3dc393cc5d4434fd349a26eb204.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \log_b 7^7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="19" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/a/97aed20a1943ea7b60c42d6ff01ce1e9e328d13e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{7}{15} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{7}{8} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{8}{7} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{15}{8} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{15}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="394" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>This problem is quickly solved with knowledge of the laws of exponents and logarithms.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa23781e0e01f1f8fdbe183ef0893c6290e8121c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 7^{x+7} &amp;= 8^x \\  7^x*7^7 &amp;= 8^x \\  \left(\frac{8}{7}\right)^x &amp;= 7^7 \\  x &amp;= \log_{8/7}7^7 \end{align*}" width="137" height="128" />
</p><p>Since we are looking for the base of the logarithm, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/8/65822f94f334a55d3479eff98db488ca0fa8c79c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{8}{7}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
