<p>A 16-step path is to go from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/f/cef2b7d99e90cfb5dc7207ff32b7d3d28849b83c.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/7/9e74a8a996f28bdc445eb3beaf3c3f14594eac27.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> with each step increasing either the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinate or the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-coordinate by 1. How many such paths stay outside or on the boundary of the square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/9/0990994305c433d76ef6c22a3203dcfa9378b7fc.png" class="latex" alt="$-2 \le x \le 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/3/b63a9e77f2f7af0d80a4bc97d9c3ef4f8ba15156.png" class="latex" alt="$-2 \le y \le 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="15" /> at each step?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/d/c6dade1d73d3bab8db4f4d8e44b25b8e399b6fa2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 92 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 144 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 1568 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 1698 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 12,800$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="474" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Each path must go through either the second or the fourth quadrant.
Each path that goes through the second quadrant must pass through exactly one of the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/c/1ec2a301764c44423433f83cfe2409191abe5a52.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d34dd031355c8cf6d3ebe2c053be908f787b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a95882ef79b538a727f5eb550c3a9810a7b28674.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />.
</p><p>There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> path of the first kind, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e86c6cad701918f0476d33d0f32febc721a34214.png" class="latex" alt="${8\choose 1}^2=64$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="87" height="56" /> paths of the second kind, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/87668bcb852770bc653a734f2f448e2d6f66a08e.png" class="latex" alt="${8\choose 2}^2=28^2=784$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="146" height="56" /> paths of the third type. 
Each path that goes through the fourth quadrant must pass through exactly one of the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c59b8dea83a413956d5bcff2e9815bb50de17772.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/c/4bc569256e9eab30daf47718764e0705248d35e0.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/1/211f55c01222ffbccc389f0d79159ad07ad77d48.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />.
Again, there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> path of the first kind, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e86c6cad701918f0476d33d0f32febc721a34214.png" class="latex" alt="${8\choose 1}^2=64$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="87" height="56" /> paths of the second kind, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/87668bcb852770bc653a734f2f448e2d6f66a08e.png" class="latex" alt="${8\choose 2}^2=28^2=784$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="146" height="56" /> paths of the third type. 
</p><p>Hence the total number of paths is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/8/c582d75a7a3a9e2929ab8818358f1737ae9c69d5.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1+64+784) = \boxed{1698}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="194" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We will use the concept of complimentary counting. If you go in the square you have to go out by these labeled points(click the link) <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://imgur.com/VysX4P0">https://imgur.com/VysX4P0</a> and go out through the borders because if you didnt, you would touch another point in one of those points in the set of points in the link(Call it <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />). There is symmetry about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />, so we only have to consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/1/9712bb3277f0f8dd79aa13badf57fea2ef194392.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-1), (1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> can go on the boundary in two ways, so we can only consider one case and multiply it by two. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a306093bbd9e0699c65dba033c9483cd379dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/0/350caf4c4803973cd5992382cc5cac99f9bf45f0.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> we can just multiply by two. So we count paths from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/c/1ec2a301764c44423433f83cfe2409191abe5a52.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> to each of these points, and then multiply that by the number of ways to get from the point one unit right of that to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/7/9e74a8a996f28bdc445eb3beaf3c3f14594eac27.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and all in all, we get the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420dcbd7d349606e42ebffa1f31c0c1a02e231f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{16}{8}-2\left[\dbinom{8}{3}\dbinom{7}{2}+\dbinom{9}{4}\dbinom{6}{2}+\dbinom{10}{5}\dbinom{5}{2}\right]=1698$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="438" height="53" />, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" />
-vsamc
Note: Sorry if this was rushed.
</p>
<h2><span id="Further_Explanation(for_Solution_1)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_Explanation.28for_Solution_1.29">Further Explanation(for Solution 1)</span></h2>
<p>As stated in the solution, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> points along the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f08809818fd50ee3fc13182a3112bc2bd28dccb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="11" /> that constitute a sort of "boundary". Once the ant reaches one of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> points, it is exactly halfway to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/a/79ad99fd4eff7c80182e09559fe59f36b5844e2a.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Also notice that the ant will only cross one of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> points during any one of its paths. Therefore we can divide the problem into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> cases, focusing on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> quadrant; then multiplying the sum by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to get the total (because there is symmetry).
</p><p><br />
For the sake of this explanation, we will focus on the fourth quadrant (it really doesn't matter which quadrant because, again the layout is symmetrical) The three cases are when the ant crosses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/3/183e6e48a966e3a8065c99bb62f62a218aece7b8.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, -4), (3, -3),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="121" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6e85b4d24c9bf37db6589e193f50dbba5ccdf8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />.
</p><p><br />
For each of the cases, notice that the path the ant takes can be expressed as a sequence of steps, such as:
</p><p><br />
right, right, up, right,..., etc.
</p><p><br />
Also notice that there are always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> steps per sequence (if there were more or less steps, the ant would be breaking the conditions given in the problem). This means we can figure out the number of ways to get to a point based on the particular sequence of steps that denote each path. For example, there is only one way for the ant to pass through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/2/2926e8cc4fdba2000654489160614f6c10b70dff.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, -4);$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" /> it MUST keep traveling right for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> steps. This seems fairly obvious; however, notice that this is equivalent to
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/8/318ecf4130c6d00ae45f1c8289b780efd0d9509a.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{0}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" />
</p><p><br />
Now we consider the number of ways to get from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/1/bd1e9b948a205bdf8e165e12fc2ad9cc3ae98d23.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, -4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/a/79ad99fd4eff7c80182e09559fe59f36b5844e2a.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. by symmetry, there is only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> such way. So the number of paths containing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/1/bd1e9b948a205bdf8e165e12fc2ad9cc3ae98d23.png" class="latex" alt="$(4, -4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/9/3a9249b180ebbae4f6e434f3a8de7b56f8979901.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="20" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><br />
Moving on to the next case, we see that the ant MUST travel right exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> times and up exactly once. So each sequence of this type will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> "right"s and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> "up". So, the total number of paths that go through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/f/40f78d2f8fb70e5f497df71f3853ba62453e622e.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is equivalent to the number of ways to arrange <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> "up" into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> spots. This is
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/9/b590cac26a1aa2a2874be1cd0334277ff5784bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{1} = 8$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="70" height="53" />
</p><p><br />
Similarly to the first case, we square this value to account for the second half of the journey: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/a/15ab925f4c11a0035ece4b71b1e5644fd6496d3e.png" class="latex" alt="$8^2 = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="15" />.
</p><p><br />
Finally, for the third case (ant passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6e85b4d24c9bf37db6589e193f50dbba5ccdf8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />) the ant must travel right exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> times and up exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> times. This is equivalent to the number of ways to arrange <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> "ups" in a sequence of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> movements, or
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/c/9ec107ded919f2f36a5c3861201d6db41aa85736.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{2} = 28$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" />
</p><p><br />
Again, we square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/7/407c7bfcc0be773985dfbf75feaaf67af2089057.png" class="latex" alt="$28^2 = 784$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="15" />. Adding up all of these cases we get
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/b/20b27ab9b9466133d46bd074b14333249080dc2e.png" class="latex" alt="$1+64+784 = 849$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />
</p><p><br />
paths through the fourth quadrant. Doubling this number to account for the paths through the second quadrant, we have
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/d/41d2e9f666f171dd3840fbb01515500c03a1aa80.png" class="latex" alt="$849*2=1698 \Rightarrow \boxed{\text{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="166" height="23" />.
</p><p><br />
For all the notation geeks out there, the solution can be expressed as such:
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed68653a638ce265b0a25226c1974601f97491f7.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sum_{k=0}^2 \binom{8}{k}^2$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="85" height="56" />
</p>
