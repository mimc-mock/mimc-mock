<p>The polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/2/f42a2b30021659b86770ec8c61db2ab77ccf97e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-ax^2+bx-2010$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="16" /> has three positive integer roots. What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63d9c0163a9081f260935ee5648be991d14fd0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 78 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 88 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 98 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 108 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 118$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_Formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta&#39;s Formulas">Vieta's Formulas</a>, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sum of the three roots of the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/2/f42a2b30021659b86770ec8c61db2ab77ccf97e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-ax^2+bx-2010$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="16" />. Again Vieta's Formulas tell us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is the product of the three integer roots. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> factors into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/8/278fafe6ccac91c961dcb75f9d495f8276d861f9.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot3\cdot5\cdot67$" width="84" height="12" />. But, since the polynomial has only three roots, two of the four prime factors must be multiplied so that we are left with three roots. To minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> should be multiplied, which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/b/c0ba45eb0bdc4c66ede392757b87e4ae78ffb9ad.png" class="latex" alt="$6+5+67=78$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b467c3f83b480dc18e9f04b4f3a0eb5977b2156.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)78}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" />.
~JimPickens
</p><p><br />
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Note:
</p><p>If you are feeling unconfident about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/d/76d15d808807bfa561e72dc8111649ed0a204ebe.png" class="latex" alt="$78$" width="17" height="12" />, you can try to expand the expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019dde90ecdcf7b146a13739f318e457505a96e5.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-5)(x-6)(x-67)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="178" height="20" /> which has roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/a/7fa7247f4d29d95459cbf6f238007a1b1090ebea.png" class="latex" alt="$67$" width="18" height="12" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/5/1a5b8fe93007fd7edf400815a3741bd81916fdd0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x-5)(x-6)(x-67)=(x^{2}-11x+30)(x-67)\]" width="385" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7e4c1138c302577e6faed2305472838737ef42.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=x\left(x^{2}-11x+30\right)-67\left(x^{2}-11x+30\right)=x^{3}-11x^{2}+30x-67x^{2}+737x-2010=x^{3}-78x^{2}+767x-2010\]" width="798" height="21" />
</p><p>As we can see, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae2d62a0c99f2806b52815579a9c5f1c5fa0783.png" class="latex" alt="$a=78$" width="51" height="12" />, and since this is the least answer choice, we can be confident that the right option is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/8/96879521df429e0652687b0edf3a9913aaef0f29.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 78}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="67" height="30" />.
</p><p>~JH. L
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We can expand <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/7/267cf79115f5569ae982f50f7c4374c77749ce54.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+a)(x+b)(x+c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87eacf049f87e1d2cd996a64779fcede1fb23995.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2+ax+bx+ab)(x+c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/4/1f43272098eeff58f4a4d83522a5453d9f414214.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2+ax+bx+ab)(x+c)=x^3+abx+acx+bcx+abx^2+acx^2+bcx^2+abc=x^3+x^2(a+b+c)+x(ab+ac+bc)+abc$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="973" height="19" />
</p><p>We do not care about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1871cea7f1c897cb1984dd51c14defa5863b6e.png" class="latex" alt="$+bx$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> in this case, because we are only looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />.  We know that the constant term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/2/15285e1b33a29a0556a4190c0a13ebebd3a92bd9.png" class="latex" alt="$-2010=-(2\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 67)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="186" height="18" />
We are trying to minimize a, such that we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/e/fde44abf296ec6273d993c853c7d3a7aa5c01c96.png" class="latex" alt="$-ax^2$" width="40" height="15" />
Since we have three positive solutions, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/c/73c4905426f0eeedc33ae16591c11df78ce18b20.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b)(x-c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" /> as our factors.  We have to combine two of the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/f/0ef66f9ebebcd563f3976f66a7b3d16cd946dbea.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 67$" width="84" height="12" />, and then sum up the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> resulting factors.  Since we are minimizing, we choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to combine together.  We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/8/ed82f0baab63afa9615b7cffe71cf7f74ec83aaa.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-6)(x-5)(x-67)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="175" height="18" /> which gives us a coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce524ea1aeb4dbc1d80c905d8dcf0e7afe691c90.png" class="latex" alt="$-6-5-67=-78$" width="150" height="12" />
Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/5/355a0f774cc1e716a6ea3615f94eb785d4cc97f5.png" class="latex" alt="$-a=-78$" width="79" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/236684ae1a2fbb0396f2e5a79d21297accbc4c35.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}78}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="93" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We want the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/2/f42a2b30021659b86770ec8c61db2ab77ccf97e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3-ax^2+bx-2010$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="16" /> to have POSITIVE integer roots. That means we want to factor it in to the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/5/d15959c036b1e927dd07a300c92a97b417e120b6.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b)(x-c).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="172" height="20" /> We therefore want the prime factorization for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. The prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/1/4f1f3a16076cc1d7d9fc1763bf88198546e84b6c.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 67$" width="84" height="12" />. We want the smallest difference of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> roots since by <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sum of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> roots.
</p><p>We proceed to factorize it in to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019dde90ecdcf7b146a13739f318e457505a96e5.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-5)(x-6)(x-67)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="178" height="20" />. Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/114f497daf57fed23517b8e5178c8715903f3f13.png" class="latex" alt="$5+6+67$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="83" height="15" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b467c3f83b480dc18e9f04b4f3a0eb5977b2156.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)78}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Arcticturn
</p><p><br />
Suggestion for the author: The variables <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are already defined as coefficients of the cubic polynomial. So, consider using different variables when describing how we want to factor this polynomial in the second sentence. Thanks!
</p><p>~Jwarner
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span></h2>
<p>We can check <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/4/c248b75b8c7bc0eabca8294dc52132b061446841.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 6 \cdot 67$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="64" height="14" /> has the smallest possible sum because of the following reasons:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/f/a0f8bd547ff43c3e012d26f860ea0195734c8cc2.png" class="latex" alt="$1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="14" height="18" />: We don't want to multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/a/7fa7247f4d29d95459cbf6f238007a1b1090ebea.png" class="latex" alt="$67$" width="18" height="12" /> by anything since that would make the sum of the roots too big.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/9/af95213021525f8505a2a32b93a30b9cba9280d4.png" class="latex" alt="$2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="14" height="18" />: The smallest number should multiply since that would make the numbers optimally small. Therefore, we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> times <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_Formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta&#39;s Formulas">Vieta's Formulas</a>
