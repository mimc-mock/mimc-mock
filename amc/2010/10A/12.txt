<p>Logan is constructing a scaled model of his town. The city's water tower stands 40 meters high, and the top portion is a sphere that holds 100,000 liters of water. Logan's miniature water tower holds 0.1 liters. How tall, in meters, should Logan make his tower?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/9/de9746a5fddaf3faad195eddcb7c4fb15eccf662.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0.04 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{0.4}{\pi} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 0.4 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{4}{\pi} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="410" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The water tower holds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/d/4dd7385f1af0244416ebdea8cb35307bb53f58f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{100000}{0.1} = 1000000$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="145" height="38" /> times more water than Logan's miniature. The volume of a sphere is: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/1/c214dc015dce30ea587d263eb5899e6f55491760.png" class="latex" alt="$V=\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="37" />. Since we are comparing the heights (m), we should compare the radii (m) to find the ratio. Since, the radius is cubed, Logan should make his tower <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/5/7059c25b41a49aadf0f433430eb36aeb9ce89dc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{1000000} = 100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="18" /> times shorter than the actual tower. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/67154ae7586c53871ca9362f272ec627691da968.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{100} = \boxed{0.4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="90" height="38" /> meters high, or choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" />.
</p><p>Note: The fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/5/2a547e6b9c8827a049eb89b17338b48eb68f42d1.png" class="latex" alt="$1\text{ L}=1000\text{ cm}^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="118" height="15" /> doesn't matter since only the ratios are important.
</p>
