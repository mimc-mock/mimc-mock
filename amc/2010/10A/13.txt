<p>Angelina drove at an average rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea5fcc6d66685ee21f722efcd228eb44d38f262.png" class="latex" alt="$80$" width="17" height="12" /> kmh and then stopped <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes for gas. After the stop, she drove at an average rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> kmh. Altogether she drove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" /> km in a total trip time of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> hours including the stop. Which equation could be used to solve for the time <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> in hours that she drove before her stop?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/8/0187ebf903af816c830f075d0b39e5fad2ac7828.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 80t+100\left(\frac{8}{3}-t\right)=250 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 80t=250 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 100t=250 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 90t=250 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 80\left(\frac{8}{3}-t\right)+100t=250$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="934" height="43" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> because she drove at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea5fcc6d66685ee21f722efcd228eb44d38f262.png" class="latex" alt="$80$" width="17" height="12" /> kmh for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> hours (the amount of time before the stop), and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> kmh for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/9/7b99a48cc084784eba58040490a868641b774869.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8}{3}-t$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="41" height="37" /> because she wasn't driving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> hours. Multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> gives the total distance, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" /> km. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/493e479cb8b73a1f86ab2c80756e56e0b3432e6d.png" class="latex" alt="$80t+100\left(\frac{8}{3}-t\right)=250$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="196" height="43" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70432e471002b1c1f363f2d085b0f3dff8d930a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />
</p>
