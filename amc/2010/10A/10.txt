<p>Marvin had a birthday on Tuesday, May 27 in the leap year <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc1744a459ad135efd3fd45fca7285cae133717.png" class="latex" alt="$2008$" width="35" height="12" />. In what year will his birthday next fall on a Saturday?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc67f14d7470ff05c6cf4c69afc9fd0be11d8cf6.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 2011 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 2012 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 2013 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 2015 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 2017$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="478" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0cd82367e63fe9f487758f5656e94818f4d4b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="29" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988ab73be88b10c8cbf520db5e7cb4b3c6531963.png" class="latex" alt="$365$" width="26" height="12" /> days in a non-leap year. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> days in a week. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/1/49158a298bb0ec42da3eacf331e2acf4df98f52a.png" class="latex" alt="$365 = 52 \cdot 7 + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" /> (or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/a/6daba05d8944282f2d9362a4e513d07dcbcadecb.png" class="latex" alt="$365\equiv 1 \pmod{ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />), the same date (after February) moves "forward" one day in the subsequent year, if that year is not a leap year.
</p><p>For example:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/8/c582adc968af49a28a24b376decf5f28d4e0db16.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/08$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Tue
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b5789b63907e1221f45e6a4b135e8d4f689b36.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/09$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Wed
</p><p>However, a leap year has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/c/72c7f26c7f5056ac93c5472b3e66e25efec0160c.png" class="latex" alt="$366$" width="26" height="12" /> days, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/6/b1605d8b522fd83873148342f972e62ea50f2ee8.png" class="latex" alt="$366 = 52 \cdot 7 + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" /> . So the same date (after February) moves "forward" <b>two</b> days in the subsequent year, if that year is a leap year.
</p><p>For example:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/0/e503fd6ad74a6ccbe44ce9ece4b284a121dc383b.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Fri
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/8/b680087685c89c0f6abd30349470e7ec52d32be7.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Sun
</p><p>You can keep counting forward to find that the first time this date falls on a Saturday is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5617f6d8def6056c5921630439d64b56cfa519ab.png" class="latex" alt="$2017$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bd427f1c69bc02c5abb2b3be2ddb479ae9cbc51.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Mon
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/f/d4fbd89de32f83f30fb59f895f335ddf20943c4e.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />	Tue
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/4/634b372e09b79959984e90528e0e91bb2bd8ade8.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Wed
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/5/21500815c9bac1b63e4e8827dbce014854b1beb9.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />	Fri
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/1/971969d9d394d7b2ea545e37f3154ed2f023451f.png" class="latex" alt="$5/27/17$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />	Sat
</p>
