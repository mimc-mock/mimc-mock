<p>A palindrome between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/b/50b19142d92b7b4f1a115c4ec91766582e2b8faf.png" class="latex" alt="$10,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" /> is chosen at random. What is the probability that it is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/5/2e57575255ac069fd864e439317e89918e9300bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{1}{10} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{1}{9} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \dfrac{1}{7} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \dfrac{1}{6} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \dfrac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="376" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>View the palindrome as some number with form (decimal representation):
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c16f33263cb11b99bf84785adb3ea6f999f151b1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 \cdot 10^3 + a_2 \cdot 10^2 + a_1 \cdot 10 + a_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="242" height="17" />. But because the number is a palindrome, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/2/73232647103061a76898d9ae8dd76e5069a07507.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3 = a_0, a_2 = a_1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="11" />. Recombining this yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/0/7e0c69a1e50828fa8ded857b59f657d49d859fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$1001a_3 + 110a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" />. 1001 is divisible by 7, which means that as long as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/0/a10773861bd71038f323459aaed5c9a721ffa1ca.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />, the palindrome will be divisible by 7. This yields 9 palindromes out of 90 (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/a/0fa6989d476911a243af663b3cc6a411aef94ba4.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \cdot 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" />) possibilities for palindromes. However, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/4/714977400bf2fa7c8e5bbd9b624f143aa958bb3b.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 = 7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="14" />, then this gives another case in which the palindrome is divisible by 7. This adds another 9 palindromes to the list, bringing our total to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/e/8eeb90c28bd8b15108b2e6228c5a9653815e3cf5.png" class="latex" alt="$18/90 = \boxed {\frac{1}{5} } = \boxed {E}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="144" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span id="Addendum_(Alternate)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Addendum_.28Alternate.29">Addendum (Alternate)</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/9/d1980c505da997fcec6b0f7fa62d955f7d98651b.png" class="latex" alt="$7\mid 1001a^3+110b^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/7/c8729a5aebe9d81f9ba6d3b7c619b84d5c351ad6.png" class="latex" alt="$1001 \equiv 0 \pmod 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" />. Knowing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> does not factor (pun intended) into the problem, note 110's prime factorization and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/8232637fb6e589dd178edc3439d219a09b4d626d.png" class="latex" alt="$7\mid b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />. There are only 10 possible digits for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, 0 through 9, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/8232637fb6e589dd178edc3439d219a09b4d626d.png" class="latex" alt="$7\mid b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> only holds if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/f/86f2b5601fc80fd005949cba7eb54a1242114311.png" class="latex" alt="$b=0, 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="16" />. This is 2 of the 10 digits, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec5b878a1931b938e783ec85d95e665951d53ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{10}=\boxed{\textbf{E)}\frac{1}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="91" height="48" />
</p><p>~BJHHar
</p>
