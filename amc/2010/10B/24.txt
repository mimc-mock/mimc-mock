<p>A high school basketball game between the Raiders and Wildcats was tied at the end of the first quarter. The number of points scored by the Raiders in each of the four quarters formed an increasing geometric sequence, and the number of points scored by the Wildcats in each of the four quarters formed an increasing arithmetic sequence. At the end of the fourth quarter, the Raiders had won by one point. Neither team scored more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> points. What was the total number of points scored by the two teams in the first half?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/f/dffa004064f7845bde7be865ab7ada80dc81737d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 31 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 32 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 33 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 34$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcf5e6cb9ed47c5d0a6e9477317bc1811aa7ac48.png" class="latex" alt="$a,ar,ar^{2},ar^{3}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="18" /> be the quarterly scores for the Raiders. We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/b/a2b4e485bf802fab7cae595eefd961523b68ffc3.png" class="latex" alt="$r &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> because the sequence is said to be increasing. We also know that each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/6743d8c9e59eab2fa3fc4375fc41c40ee415f272.png" class="latex" alt="$a, ar, ar^2, ar^3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="18" /> is an integer. We start by showing that <b><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must also be an integer.</b>
</p><p>Suppose not, and say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/0/250d24f2929b4a6e755fd6708ad8140122f7c8a3.png" class="latex" alt="$r = m/n$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/8/d88297269ec48be7a8acf554cd82c8e230a092f6.png" class="latex" alt="$m&gt;n&gt;1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8edce2e5c4fc23d616b9921bcb7cb84471f209c.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(m,n)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="18" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/3/29324dcd0e9c8cd6ad29c38e31e77b6e9e2699e7.png" class="latex" alt="$n, n^2, n^3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="18" /> must all divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/3/a23b9706a939e27fb070d7d62e2168d30c4bf612.png" class="latex" alt="$a=n^3k$" width="61" height="15" /> for some integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d73b1e96d12fa408b6af5b0b5da0603af01e0de.png" class="latex" alt="$S_R = n^3k + n^2mk + nm^2k + m^3k &lt; 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="314" height="17" /> and we see that even if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5ed8470bc717a49aafe4556c7e2c2e11ddc7b687.png" class="latex" alt="$k=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/b/6cb7ae00764b56ee2adf59e78a1ffde9685b80db.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2$" width="43" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/493ba0de4c253fda20a6744d8838a46c7f0de1a9.png" class="latex" alt="$m &lt; 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" />, which means that the only option for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/3/303c50240589de9b2d35a7c5bcef866b9e3d3d11.png" class="latex" alt="$r=3/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />. A quick check shows that even this doesn't work. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> must be an integer.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/2/782d92f989e875d33c26f4a5f71515c556e3df0b.png" class="latex" alt="$a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="176" height="16" /> be the quarterly scores for the Wildcats. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/6/346bc9312bb70339634fb06e7439dc0b26b6d73c.png" class="latex" alt="$S_W = a+(a+d) + (a+2d)+(a+3d) = 4a+6d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="395" height="18" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/e/3ee54374a57b398b0cd2a63d9163aed4990b074e.png" class="latex" alt="$S_R = a+ar+ar^2+ar^3 = a(1+r)(1+r^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="340" height="19" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4b29ac2cd8fcfff9b658b9df85e8cc58dfbcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$S_R&lt;100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" /> implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/7/f47c36f42c17eee0bc0aaeb0b7d35e18dbc80ca8.png" class="latex" alt="$r&lt;5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/a/52adb821a1eef82db3ee31f1fc4c79d4fffb8716.png" class="latex" alt="$r\in \{2, 3, 4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" />. The Raiders win by one point, so<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/def59369d56fef84625dc54e288f26479eefa613.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a(1+r)(1+r^2) = 4a+6d+1.\]" width="243" height="19" />
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17fcc101cc2bd83642d07d7e8a9b38f698d08a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$r=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5ac3ac1b402bad0c89f1764d90b1366286fbf34.png" class="latex" alt="$85a = 4a+6d+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="141" height="14" /> which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/0/f00443d1ffc7dbe7d35313ededfc091130ddb96f.png" class="latex" alt="$3(27a-2d) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" />, which is not possible with the given conditions.</li>
<li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31faa8c684ba5811ac60ea1bd0c855d8ef4d8b16.png" class="latex" alt="$r=3$" width="41" height="12" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/312aa0d22d8f5dc9b952edff216bbf95796fe191.png" class="latex" alt="$40a = 4a+6d+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="141" height="14" /> which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/9/f2942bab7c4e75e44e6ec2e6dda26e78162cd706.png" class="latex" alt="$6(6a-d) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" />, which is also not possible with the given conditions.</li>
<li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a0736cc24657e4fbe87410091de1c4ebd44e26db.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2$" width="41" height="12" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c863dd06649e0e6f38ba4ea6175651358ddba0e3.png" class="latex" alt="$15a = 4a+6d+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="141" height="14" /> which means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a783c67cf3c87ae07f47e20dbe5e98623b63cb2e.png" class="latex" alt="$11a-6d = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="101" height="13" />. Reducing modulo 6 we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/9/da9f55ce264fb58c65c617455cd90c0ceee0bbb5.png" class="latex" alt="$a \equiv 5\pmod{6}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/0/6905f3bb75fb473e096d82d1c28fabaecf1036a6.png" class="latex" alt="$15a&lt;100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="78" height="13" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/3/6f3b7684b5c967732c8db5c7828a5765d40b43f2.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;7$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/53897e4b47f6049266b4bffcef91bc949385b5e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5$" width="42" height="12" />. It then follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/1/89101f005d6d15db3de71be6289394d82d802cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$d=9$" width="42" height="12" />.</li></ul>
<p>Then the quarterly scores for the Raiders are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc78e1ab9a8f7e48c233c4f7c4f8432ce76b975c.png" class="latex" alt="$5, 10, 20, 40$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="16" />, and those for the Wildcats are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/0/050561fe018526a1e7e8c6687725776a29c0d37a.png" class="latex" alt="$5, 14, 23, 32$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="16" />. Also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/b/89b15a636cde12dbc61e7a88bf40b271a227e593.png" class="latex" alt="$S_R = 75 = S_W + 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="145" height="15" />. The total number of points scored by the two teams in the first half is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/5/a355f9ce9655ce7af4e681300b17e2f3c2da2a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$5+10+5+14=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 34}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="208" height="28" />.
</p><p>Note if you don't realize while taking the test that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> might not be an integer: since an answer is achieved through casework on the integer value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and since there is only one right answer, the proof of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> being an integer can be skipped on the test (it takes up time).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcf5e6cb9ed47c5d0a6e9477317bc1811aa7ac48.png" class="latex" alt="$a,ar,ar^{2},ar^{3}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="18" /> be the quarterly scores for the Raiders. We know that the Raiders and Wildcats both scored the same number of points in the first quarter so let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/b/41b9e3ed2a79dc05a08a21dab15e80a944bf297c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,a+d,a+2d,a+3d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="176" height="16" /> be the quarterly scores for the Wildcats. The sum of the Raiders scores is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e78f8155f511df5c31f538d4bd4664cb37d51908.png" class="latex" alt="$a(1+r+r^{2}+r^{3})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="19" /> and the sum of the Wildcats scores is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/4/294383fee1afb4ca63732f3fb7aa2b5eab2a7f39.png" class="latex" alt="$4a+6d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" />. Now we can narrow our search for the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/e/86e1bb289e27492225b3df9d4e9191c1fddcb736.png" class="latex" alt="$a,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. Because points are always measured in positive integers, we can conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are positive integers. We can also conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is a positive integer by writing down the equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e8d6af3ad87d23fd98dc3f2722228d5e7b3184.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a(1+r+r^{2}+r^{3})=4a+6d+1\]" width="254" height="19" />
</p><p>Now we can start trying out some values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. We try <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a0736cc24657e4fbe87410091de1c4ebd44e26db.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2$" width="41" height="12" />, which gives
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/f/bdffb9953d5181c4cc9786948632a5cdd985f014.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[15a=4a+6d+1\]" width="140" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/6/8d6e7395caef82372aa154ce4a1207267df2c206.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[11a=6d+1\]" width="99" height="14" />
</p><p>We need the smallest multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> (to satisfy the &lt;100 condition) that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/e/2be8ab5bbc2978dd34eda4d9226063c857cb03b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\equiv 1 \pmod{6}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="18" />. We see that this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" />, and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/53897e4b47f6049266b4bffcef91bc949385b5e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/1/89101f005d6d15db3de71be6289394d82d802cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$d=9$" width="42" height="12" />.
</p><p>So the Raiders' first two scores were <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and the Wildcats' first two scores were <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/f/ddfe96a3b478220e49cf566fdd3b90a1748ebac9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5+10+5+14=34 \longrightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(E)}}\]" width="241" height="28" />
</p>
