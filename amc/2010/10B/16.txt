<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The radius of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/e/b6e9c71815e9f4b30d3f2250dc24825594fc8d4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="83" height="43" />. Half the diagonal of the square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/d/4dde834f51c722c6c16c98333c2841e95b00fff3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{1^2+1^2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \sqrt{\frac12}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="182" height="43" />. We can see that the circle passes outside the square, but the square is NOT completely contained in the circle. Therefore the picture will look something like this:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/f/bafc98be6b00d03d2e10fefe63f66799919da8b2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(5cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=3;  real r=sqrt(1/3); pair O=(0,0); pair W=(0.5,0.5), X=(0.5,-0.5), Y=(-0.5,-0.5), Z=(-0.5,0.5); pair A=(-sqrt(1/12),0.5), B=(sqrt(1/12),0.5); pair V=(0,0.5); path outer=Circle(O,r); draw(outer); draw(W--X--Y--Z--cycle); draw(O--A); draw(O--B); draw(V--O);  pair[] ps={A,B,V,O}; dot(ps);  label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,SW); label(&quot;$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$&quot;,O--B,SE); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NE); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,V,NW); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,B--V,S); label(&quot;$\frac12$&quot;,O--V,W); [/asy]" width="275" height="275" /></center>
<p>Then we proceed to find: 4 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/1/8f1fb66751f2ee2626c75303485dba473c39a2f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\cdot$" style="vertical-align: 3px" width="3" height="2" /> (area of sector marked off by the two radii - area of the triangle with sides on the square and the two radii).
</p><p>First we realize that the radius perpendicular to the side of the square between the two radii marking off the sector splits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> in half. Let this half-length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/1/e7159af81e4e22aa478bacdccdce716e4994683d.png" class="latex" alt="$OX=\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="66" height="37" /> because it is half the sidelength of the square. Because this is a right triangle, we can use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45ef9cdc7c7d46920eac603663914e0d9261a99b.png" class="latex" alt="$a.$" width="13" height="8" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/a/08a0272869daf76694cd06cdef2cdda39af67411.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2+\left( \frac12 \right) ^2 = \left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \right) ^2\]" width="172" height="56" />
</p><p>Solving, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244309edc05b47be2c4cc3838ad8e9b212502484.png" class="latex" alt="$a= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/f/4df45fe32db28184c0ee7b0e90a84ddb8200c57d.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="41" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/790c352b45da75b75183dfb736e55436825232b8.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=AO=BO$" width="133" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/4/e44c01c0ed926b86414a0fbedc2fe1d2eaa9b871.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AOB$" width="58" height="13" /> is an equilateral triangle and the central angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d5c45388de5c75af641c046d466de3592ffa98.png" class="latex" alt="$60^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. Therefore the sector has an area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c71696c52d5855ad1b3c1abcfce3cd4564cfa45.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi \left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \right) ^2 \left( \frac{60}{360} \right) = \frac{\pi}{18}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="183" height="56" />.
</p><p>Now we turn to the triangle. Since it is equilateral, we can use the formula for the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Area_of_an_equilateral_triangle" title="Area of an equilateral triangle">area of an equilateral triangle</a> which is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/f/77fa99c7ea5fbce379ca7bfe1f65e3ac3218e423.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = \frac{\frac13 \sqrt{3}}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{12}\]" width="156" height="42" />
