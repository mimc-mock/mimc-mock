<p>At the beginning of the school year, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7f4de02215a8c5319886c13b2e291811346e1c.png" class="latex" alt="$50\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of all students in Mr. Well's class answered "Yes" to the question "Do you love math", and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7f4de02215a8c5319886c13b2e291811346e1c.png" class="latex" alt="$50\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> answered "No." At the end of the school year, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282fdef75f579c92565cd7e0734d83ccfd38bfdc.png" class="latex" alt="$70\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> answered "Yes" and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e5939a7bdd847ec86582225cd653c240b4abce.png" class="latex" alt="$30\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> answered "No." Altogether, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de1a36d69a7028480a87b712a7141c651398a06.png" class="latex" alt="$x\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="25" height="14" /> of the students gave a different answer at the beginning and end of the school year. What is the difference between the maximum and the minimum possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/0/d800f15dc6c96845e10c8a6e8af60e120cf6885a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 20 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 40 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 60 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Clearly, the minimum possible value would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/8/19875fbd0020a5f15bbef4be8b1444aca5a3f54e.png" class="latex" alt="$70 - 50 = 20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="115" height="14" />. The maximum possible value would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/7/58787ac243a246d488517095c9422d0df757d3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$30 + 50 = 80\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="115" height="15" />. The difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/2/1e242a883f508f3587f9d513831f2130cd632df9.png" class="latex" alt="$80 - 20 = \boxed{60}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="114" height="25" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b4066cd24c53cd4053f840d210f974f46e1d913.png" class="latex" alt="$(D)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="18" />.
</p>
