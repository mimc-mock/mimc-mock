<p>Positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are randomly and independently selected with replacement from the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/8/62838b951e5c42023b54b5b6aab7778e88c3523f.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1, 2, 3,\dots, 2010\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="137" height="18" />. What is the probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/6/bf6fd8a539280cecbe00bae8a42d59ce9340b279.png" class="latex" alt="$abc + ab + a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="96" height="14" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/1/611e44813e12699950bdf87cd92b9def406dbbf1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac{1}{3} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{29}{81} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \dfrac{31}{81} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \dfrac{11}{27} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \dfrac{13}{27}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="404" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We group this into groups of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/5/935972d2a68fb66c5d0d657f2af80a175d2a0e26.png" class="latex" alt="$3|2010$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" />. This means that every residue class mod 3 has an equal probability.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aabe4e974b14ba4a8d96e60f0f2692c9d351938.png" class="latex" alt="$3|a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="18" />, we are done. There is a probability of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> that that happens.
</p><p>Otherwise, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/4/e04faa2470dffe06d7c1c9f24a75d1557494c617.png" class="latex" alt="$3|bc+b+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" />, which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/e/39e79e623e09a98085178e818b2b2c3e28915e30.png" class="latex" alt="$b(c+1) \equiv 2\pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="18" />. So either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/0/d602e48894f740c6b18b927d143632cd7c677267.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b \equiv 1 \pmod{3}, c \equiv 1 \pmod{3}\]" width="253" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f3bfbde99b5d9adcd70b1baa3141a7b2d4d3e0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b \equiv 2 \pmod {3}, c \equiv 0 \pmod 3\]" width="253" height="18" /> which will lead to the property being true. There is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/6/bd69512b5f5f4443ef16a385f1ac05b143cd39e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\cdot\frac{1}{3}=\frac{1}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="75" height="37" /> chance for each bundle of cases to be true. Thus, the total for the cases is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/1/041a0b37ca609642fb34c7c855b9d67ed498a560.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. But we have to multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> because this only happens with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance. So the total is actually <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89f8914ddec472f71365684baa2221159248682b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{27}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />.
</p><p>The grand total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/b/cdbc13df778bd61f5004a303c301f10b345310e3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{3} + \frac{4}{27} = \boxed{\text{(E) }\frac{13}{27}.}\]" width="148" height="48" />
</p>
