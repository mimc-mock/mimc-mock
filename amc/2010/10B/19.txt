<p>A circle with center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4721a39060a6d72daa278cc3d62a5ca459b3ea.png" class="latex" alt="$156\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />. Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is equilateral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> is a chord on the circle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/e/92e51b1662dd50636adb80d951997fc59fa583fa.png" class="latex" alt="$OA = 4\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />, and point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> is outside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />. What is the side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/1/3811fccad02a654a0b8d7fc33e3d44ae36f58289.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 4\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 12 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="415" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The formula for the area of a circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e89ed1c5456915b4e95e0c8a11257ac5ed3d32c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^2$" width="25" height="15" /> so the radius of this circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/e/24e4048c3483c17570042651b3f157849942f594.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{156}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="18" />
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/5/825d2a55ac3a22a3f22765736462297154c5ff3b.png" class="latex" alt="$OA=4\sqrt{3} &lt; \sqrt{156}, A$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="174" height="20" /> must be in the interior of circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c79ddff6b45c6ad853d03d6231fdf0fba5ededb.png" class="latex" alt="$O.$" width="18" height="12" />
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/f/03fe10542785e49de90d325a8bb86d13462ffd6a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(11pt)); dotfactor=3;  real r=sqrt(156); pair A=(0,sqrt(48)), B=(-3,sqrt(147)), C=(3,sqrt(147)); pair O=(0,0); pair X=(0,7sqrt(3)); path outer=Circle(O,r); draw(outer); draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(O--X); draw(O--B);  pair[] ps={A,B,C,O,X}; dot(ps);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SE); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,S); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,X,N); label(&quot;$s$&quot;,A--C,SE); label(&quot;$\frac{s}{2}$&quot;,B--X,N); label(&quot;$\frac{s\sqrt{3}}{2}$&quot;,A--X,NE); label(&quot;$\sqrt{156}$&quot;,O--B,SW); label(&quot;$4\sqrt{3}$&quot;,A--O,E); [/asy]" width="358" height="378" /></center>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be the unknown value, the sidelength of the triangle, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> be the point on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/620ba336cf9c7a8c7298923c5201c6f9c59e2970.png" class="latex" alt="$OX \perp BC.$" width="86" height="12" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is equilateral, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/b/4cb819d89c4cec8d49a75538de1ab1904f9c4375.png" class="latex" alt="$BX=\frac{s}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="33" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/c/84ce4dc80ff50ead1d48bbbfaaade620cb0efc0d.png" class="latex" alt="$AX=\frac{s\sqrt{3}}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="94" height="41" /> We are given <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/4/8e4f10ca7798b3119ce0607e1b1ba868123fc75d.png" class="latex" alt="$AO=4\sqrt{3}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="18" /> Use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> and solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09d9c01a214955f96b174391be96b26abf545cac.png" class="latex" alt="$s.$" width="12" height="8" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/8/468dc818299e27b2804ce95f974515e503a4c540.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (\sqrt{156})^2 &amp;= \left(\frac{s}{2}\right)^2 + \left( \frac{s\sqrt{3}}{2} + 4\sqrt{3} \right)^2\\ 156 &amp;= \frac14s^2 + \frac34s^2 + 12s + 48\\ 0 &amp;= s^2 + 12s - 108\\ 0 &amp;= (s-6)(s+18)\\ s &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 6} \end{align*}" width="277" height="187" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can use the same diagram as Solution 1 and label the side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. Using congruent triangles, namely the two triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6af3c7e2f2403f2fa92d2d1d20861cb9354e0e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BOA$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/2/0e2d97829369bc06782c17988a22533979294d85.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle COA$" width="58" height="13" />, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608d1168ae98cc9b020ce9b26d5c4e962629d8b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAO = \angle CAO \implies \angle BAO = \frac{360-60}{2} = 150$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="386" height="37" />. From this, we can use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Law_of_Cosines" title="Law of Cosines">Law of Cosines</a>, to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/1/39138561e4aec938a4f5b4594408d632bc8101d7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s^2 + (4 \sqrt{3})^2 - 2 \times s \times 4 \sqrt{3} \times - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = (2 \sqrt{39})^2\]" width="361" height="41" /> Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/1/f41e76300e27bf883a4b5193a96636fcfffc8f97.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s^2 + 12s + 48 = 156 \implies s^2 + 12s - 108 = 0\]" width="353" height="16" /> We can factor this to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/6/a6656760af5ff81b27a2ebe5e02d278bba7f0b98.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x-6)(x+18)\]" width="117" height="18" /> Lengths must be non-negative, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/8/ac8a9cb7dadbb8ebe7303b5b5e8214059fbb7efd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />
~hansenhe
</p>
