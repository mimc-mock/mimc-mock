<p>Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are distinct nonzero real numbers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/02342d06806b85e72feaac3f8bd964c8fb287baf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\tfrac{2}{x} = y + \tfrac{2}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="109" height="23" />, what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/8/2987c7cfe5a02f985818a451cc041f14c8a607a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{4}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{2}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 4\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="357" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1116bf04a0080cbdb485eed39c744716079f3c.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\tfrac{2}{x}= y+\tfrac{2}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="109" height="23" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c58076da60877943813920a227ed36b6a911d24.png" class="latex" alt="$x\not=y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" />, we may assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/34323e4c3544e3e4dba6ecbe6d82e00faaa0fca2.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{2}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="46" height="40" /> and/or, equivalently, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33bf4b1920ea89631271d845fa91c32fe487941.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{2}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />.
</p><p>Cross multiply in either equation, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4ccfb3fe8a8b7bfb7bace3e932d0a18a3a659f59.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d662ddb01064fc70caf47984c2ae346e7c733dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1116bf04a0080cbdb485eed39c744716079f3c.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\tfrac{2}{x}= y+\tfrac{2}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -8px" width="109" height="23" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f71309b285d57119917c021ed8dc1f3c3e9e63d5.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y+\frac{2}{x}-\frac{2}{y} = 0$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="147" height="40" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/c/afc2931facfc960fd9d17d3515a6a60750ef78c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)+2(\frac{y-x}{xy}) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="180" height="36" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/f/2bf4978c07b3950268956355d1fb3c1ff40a6fe7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)(1-\frac{2}{xy})=0$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="158" height="40" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c58076da60877943813920a227ed36b6a911d24.png" class="latex" alt="$x\not=y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8401130db9dae14b494c64cdd03a5fc688f7a273.png" class="latex" alt="$1 = \frac{2}{xy}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="55" height="40" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/7/9779311aab441e4a55cba407f9bec73868f0030a.png" class="latex" alt="$xy = 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/8/898b48dd1064cf3808d54f36ca5772ad81211911.png" class="latex" alt="$A = x + \frac{2}{x} = y + \frac{2}{y}.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="161" height="42" /> Consider the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/531f76440557c2264bbf85191233815ac42e4bc3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[u + \frac{2}{u} = A.\]" width="87" height="37" /> Reorganizing, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/8/b0847d15dd2bad446e686191b5192bd385c174fb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[u^2 - Au + 2 = 0.\]" width="131" height="17" /> Notice that there can be at most two distinct values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/d/9ad99798ec4c38e165cf517cb9e02b1c9e824103.png" class="latex" alt="$u$" width="10" height="8" /> which satisfy this equation, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are two distinct possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/d/ccd3c78be1c29e0097baa8b6322ee1ff82b9d86f.png" class="latex" alt="$u.$" width="14" height="8" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are roots of this quadratic, and by Vieta’s formulas we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> thereby must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/a/0ba6f5aab268739c63b4c45afb8c209432dcb1e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />
</p><p>~ Professor-Mom
</p>
