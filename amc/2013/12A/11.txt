<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is equilateral with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/4/4e4e71e9c0786b12fb9b4b5c67364147ed6e402f.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />. Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" /> are on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> such that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/4/8f450f6af9c000449bff7dc1801fdd77978f6e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{FG}$" width="29" height="15" /> are parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />. Furthermore, triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70422100db8de33f8e5e0ab5062c5bb6a5ac969d.png" class="latex" alt="$ADE$" width="43" height="13" /> and trapezoids <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/6/dd662e635fd79eea74cb296a005ebba3a8b8b04c.png" class="latex" alt="$DFGE$" width="58" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/4/e04ae5998ceeb9284f13f1c85244d9f7ec17546f.png" class="latex" alt="$FBCG$" width="57" height="12" /> all have the same perimeter. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bd84151050a3ca360d8e91940655ccc7e3b3ecb.png" class="latex" alt="$DE+FG$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="80" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/e/d2eb992c9eae1b2af8bec0d92f03bde1e1e8b647.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(180); pen dps = linewidth(0.7) + fontsize(10); defaultpen(dps); real s=1/2,m=5/6,l=1; pair A=origin,B=(l,0),C=rotate(60)*l,D=(s,0),E=rotate(60)*s,F=m,G=rotate(60)*m; draw(A--B--C--cycle^^D--E^^F--G); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^E^^F^^G); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,N); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,NW); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,S); label(&quot;$G$&quot;,G,NW); [/asy]" width="302" height="265" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/b/74b84e2ae84146cdef9a15de8ea37c5e18cc2142.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }1\qquad \textbf{(B) }\dfrac{3}{2}\qquad \textbf{(C) }\dfrac{21}{13}\qquad \textbf{(D) }\dfrac{13}{8}\qquad \textbf{(E) }\dfrac{5}{3}\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="386" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/c/cdc975a5b35343ca18565b3a7cce9827d332bbbd.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = x$" width="63" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/2/532cd91c2b8e759b1113a49eb21e9287f9abc572.png" class="latex" alt="$AG = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" />. We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/9/06959451895c1e8fcd1701908a6ee8f3427f5225.png" class="latex" alt="$DE + FG$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="80" height="14" />, which is nothing but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" />.
</p><p>Based on the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70422100db8de33f8e5e0ab5062c5bb6a5ac969d.png" class="latex" alt="$ADE$" width="43" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c16df895dad08efcc67a9a5d66f8eb08a7c37e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$DEFG$" width="58" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/8/9f8e2832b6f8af9c1b0d0312f03976d64797816c.png" class="latex" alt="$BCFG$" width="57" height="12" /> have the same perimeters, we can say the following:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/5/f559c4aafb9198fc9168722894b7490f666f854a.png" class="latex" alt="$3x = x + 2(y-x) + y = y + 2(1-y) + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="324" height="18" />
</p><p>Simplifying, we can find that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/8814ab02b72fa2edf86fb1bcb31a6a0258fd86b0.png" class="latex" alt="$3x = 3y-x = 3-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="159" height="16" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/8/5c847b80b94041590f5cabc90651e9a9328d0774.png" class="latex" alt="$3-y = 3x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/9/f7971148a0c4aac03901275f7cd73d852c482c30.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 3-3x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />.
</p><p>After substitution, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/3/203360bc8ba976b36389a075b3cc85808cca891a.png" class="latex" alt="$9-10x = 3x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/b/71bc239a45eb6fbc5d4b9e9f197307e8c2db1aaf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />.
</p><p>Again substituting, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/a/14aeefc3d3435fc2484af58aa32401c9ce8683f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/9/8d9f4c45075600fc423c342d499fedf81c763c09.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{21}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />
</p>
