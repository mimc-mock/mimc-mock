<p>Three distinct segments are chosen at random among the segments whose end-points are the vertices of a regular 12-gon. What is the probability that the lengths of these three segments are the three side lengths of a triangle with positive area?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/917ac7b4c4abfb5dcbfeb67a98d1c7750a98a332.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \ \frac{553}{715} \qquad \textbf{(B)} \ \frac{443}{572} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \ \frac{111}{143} \qquad \textbf{(D)} \ \frac{81}{104} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \ \frac{223}{286}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="458" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> is the answer. We calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/d/5fdd66831cbbb8238ab62234975610d1682bbb03.png" class="latex" alt="$1-p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="15" />.
</p><p>Assume that the circumradius of the 12-gon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and the 6 different lengths are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/087594ea26fa196b8e61bc20a36228270dc695f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\cdots$" style="vertical-align: 3px" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ace040475607683853fcfd4960a8f7fa6678c7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, in increasing order. Then
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/b/2db751b2ae00207df0235b8910c57863c37539d1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k = 2\sin ( \frac{k\pi}{12} )$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="38" />. 
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282347bc076812f54025bb07a70a9b7629637a87.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1=(\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{2})/2 \approx 0.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="191" height="21" />, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7c4c84cd04bf09b23af76a4714f60d649e3e1e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="14" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04b3c77b28127f102faa7d02a8d482250455ab6.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3=\sqrt{2}\approx 1.4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="113" height="19" />, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/c/f1c2269fb6c38a8a91f2e4e154428ef26b5c67f8.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4=\sqrt{3}\approx 1.7$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="113" height="19" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/7/577b6797d0336b088c616bc231abdb417637fbaa.png" class="latex" alt="$a_5=(\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{2})/2 = a_1 + a_3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="223" height="21" />,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33f6fbc2e7626aeb56f0ce68dc4bc69108b5fc35.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="14" />.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Now, Consider the following inequalities:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b71690229e3b7c3449280bd248f4e069f42bfdd5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_3&gt;2a_1 &gt; a_2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="111" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f97caf3509f99c3ee8a68e890bccb4627df6996f.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4&gt; a_1 + a_2&gt;a_3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="15" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27f146d38a744038c987b7216fde8f1f84c2039.png" class="latex" alt="$a_4&lt;a_1 + a_3=a_5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/751c212fdb5c388da472ec2cdae8b2c867ea0caa.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + a_4 &gt; a_6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="99" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/7/56785739f1e0924f0447f0c4a534175f64ba7fda.png" class="latex" alt="$2a_2 = 2 = a_6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="17" />. Thus any two segments with at least one them longer than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> have a sum greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ace040475607683853fcfd4960a8f7fa6678c7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />.
</p><p>Therefore, all triples (in increasing order) that can't be the side lengths of a triangle are the following. Note that x-y-z means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/0/d6007536a8100d586122f9293e497270437d8177.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_x, a_y, a_z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="18" />:
</p>
<pre>1-1-3, 1-1-4, 1-1-5, 1-1-6,
1-2-4, 1-2-5, 1-2-6,
1-3-5, 1-3-6,
2-2-6
</pre>
<p>Note that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> segments of each length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e97a8af68fbc8e357d3ee0eba452022b06c1875.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/087594ea26fa196b8e61bc20a36228270dc695f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\cdots$" style="vertical-align: 3px" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/5/0d5d34d49a7eb6afd4874d7bdaf23946dbf63cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$a_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, respectively, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> segments of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ace040475607683853fcfd4960a8f7fa6678c7.png" class="latex" alt="$a_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d40f3652c1962e98ce050a88c04ed37997c67df.png" class="latex" alt="$66$" width="17" height="12" /> segments in total.
</p><p>In the above list there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> triples of the type a-a-b without <i>6</i>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> triples of a-a-6 where a is not <i>6</i>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> triples of a-b-c without <i>6</i>, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> triples of a-b-6 where a, b are not <i>6</i>. So,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/4/664789c0327055b6f37b531076cf626d92f7dda1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1-p = \frac{1}{66\cdot 65\cdot 64} ( 3\cdot 3 \cdot 12\cdot 11\cdot 12 + 2\cdot 3 \cdot 12\cdot 11\cdot 6 + 3\cdot 6\cdot 12^3 + 2\cdot 6 \cdot 12^2 \cdot 6)\]" width="493" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/8/5a8e9ca2efa46c7a5930b5d482527ad3449f372c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \frac{1}{66\cdot 65\cdot 64} (12^2 (99+33) + 12^3(18+6)) = \frac{1}{66\cdot 65\cdot 64} (12^3 \cdot 35) = \frac{63}{286}\]" width="528" height="38" />
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c76874eb6e23bf26e7b1c6a81c8eb2dea47ee465.png" class="latex" alt="$p = 223/286$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />.
</p>
