<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of coins.  After the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/b/3cb9fb3b35f43cc27e4e63da0093b956154335a2.png" class="latex" alt="$k^{\text{th}}$" width="22" height="15" /> pirate takes his share, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/3/71393c49a8262157738e7e922f3203a6dd9f29ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12-k}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="52" height="38" /> of the original amount is left.  Thus, we know that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff5757ac54e4e2692ee53670749a4ae5fcb01cb.png" class="latex" alt="$x \cdot \frac{11}{12} \cdot \frac{10}{12} \cdot \frac{9}{12} \cdot \frac{8}{12} \cdot \frac{7}{12} \cdot \frac{6}{12} \cdot \frac{5}{12} \cdot \frac{4}{12} \cdot \frac{3}{12} \cdot \frac{2}{12} \cdot \frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="397" height="38" /> must be an integer.  Simplifying, we get
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/d/c2d5371240aaf8ecaf4b54d93a13f366bf0cea2f.png" class="latex" alt="$x \cdot \frac{11}{12} \cdot \frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{1}{2}  \cdot \frac{7}{12} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{5}{12} \cdot \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="307" height="38" />.  Now, the minimal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the denominator of this fraction multiplied out, obviously.  We mentioned before that this product must be an integer.  Specifically, it is an integer and it is the amount that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/1/b0131bb8233ad52e2df4bd15144c08adadcc2e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$12^{\text{th}}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="16" /> pirate receives, as he receives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/f/98f08ac722cbfd8e8725448172f1c431d82a209d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{12} = 1 =$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="74" height="37" /> all of what is remaining.  
</p><p>Thus, we know the denominator is canceled out, so the number of gold coins received is going to be the product of the numerators, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37a52209351e9e092bb89a883ac9c3dd50673b89.png" class="latex" alt="$11 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 \cdot 5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }1925}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="193" height="28" />.
