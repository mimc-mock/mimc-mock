<p>Six spheres of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> are positioned so that their centers are at the vertices of a regular hexagon of side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. The six spheres are internally tangent to a larger sphere whose center is the center of the hexagon.  An eighth sphere is externally tangent to the six smaller spheres and internally tangent to the larger sphere.  What is the radius of this eighth sphere?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab249ee68f1734ddb4b2a5111c4ae79ae1dbc9fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \sqrt2\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{3}{2}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{5}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \sqrt3\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="387" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Set up an isosceles triangle between the center of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />th sphere and two opposite ends of the hexagon. Then set up another triangle between the point of tangency of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />th and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />th spheres, and the points of tangency between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />th sphere and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of the original spheres on opposite sides of the hexagon. Express each side length of the triangles in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> (the radius of sphere <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />) and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> (the height of the first triangle). You can then use Pythagorean Theorem to set up two equations for the two triangles, and find the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2ee86e509ee7209fefbe985fcb2be982fc59c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+r)^2=2^2+h^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="141" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/6/2f68d29cc1a79b78dbbd9a4059dba781349f6d8c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3\sqrt{2})^2=3^2+(h+r)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="179" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/2/dd20aa84def8ac60dc4fd74cb96fc8e1b9ddada4.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{3}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="92" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We have a regular hexagon with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and six spheres on each vertex with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> that are internally tangent, therefore, drawing radii to the tangent points would create this regular hexagon.
</p><p>Imagine a 2D overhead view.  There is a larger sphere which the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> spheres are internally tangent to, with the center in the center of the hexagon. To find the radius of the larger sphere we must first, either by prior knowledge or by deducing from the angle sum that the hexagon can be split into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> equilateral triangles from its vertices, that the radius is two more than the small radius, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.  
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/e/f0eaac35e01f296e1c7a8e6637a80fbb859454e8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw(circle((0.5,0.866025404),0.5)); draw(circle((-0.5,0.866025404),0.5)); draw(circle((1,0),0.5)); draw(circle((-1,0),0.5)); draw(circle((0.5,-0.866025404),0.5)); draw(circle((-0.5,-0.866025404),0.5)); draw(circle((0,0),1.5));  draw((-0.5,0.866025404)--(0.5,0.866025404)); draw((-1,0)--(1,0)); draw((-0.5,-0.866025404)--(0.5,-0.866025404));  draw((-1,0)--(-0.5,0.866025404)); draw((-0.5,-0.866025404)--(0.5,0.866025404)); draw((0.5,-0.866025404)--(1,0));  draw((0.5,0.866025404)--(1,0)); draw((-0.5,0.866025404)--(0.5,-0.866025404)); draw((-1,0)--(-0.5,-0.866025404)); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p>diagram made by erics118
</p><p><br />
Now imagine the figure in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> dimensions.  The 8th sphere must be sitting atop of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> spheres, which is the only possibility for it to be tangent to all the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> small spheres externally and the larger sphere internally. The ring of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> small spheres is symmetrical and the 8th sphere will be resting atop it with its center aligned with the diameter of the large sphere.
</p><p>We can now create a right triangle with the legs being the line from a vertex of the hexagon to the center of the hexagon and the line from the center of the 7th sphere to the center of the 8th sphere.  Let the radius of our 8th sphere be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />.  As previously mentioned, the distance from the center of the hexagon to one of its vertices is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.  The distance between the centers will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/a/d4ae590064faef31147228c583a5927a187add52.png" class="latex" alt="$3-r$" width="39" height="12" />.  The hypotenuse will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/0/080bdd4b53cf3c5d997f0ee91d272b451f0827d6.png" class="latex" alt="$r+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="13" />.  
</p><p>We now have a right triangle.  Applying the Pythagorean Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/2/58267d5a4bd900312b00b3349c16ab0b0d22239d.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2+(3-r)^2=(1+r)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="19" />.  Expanding and solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d81dbd1d672bca5fb802a6f910fc837d13c4c85.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac{12}{8}=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\frac{3}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="132" height="48" />.
</p>
