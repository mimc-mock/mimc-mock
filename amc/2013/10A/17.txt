<p>Daphne is visited periodically by her three best friends: Alice, Beatrix, and Claire.  Alice visits every third day, Beatrix visits every fourth day, and Claire visits every fifth day.  All three friends visited Daphne yesterday.  How many days of the next <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988ab73be88b10c8cbf520db5e7cb4b3c6531963.png" class="latex" alt="$365$" width="26" height="12" />-day period will exactly two friends visit her?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/5/5958ccea63684a358326fb641c59716e2e17a76f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 54\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 60\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 66\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988ab73be88b10c8cbf520db5e7cb4b3c6531963.png" class="latex" alt="$365$" width="26" height="12" />-day time period can be split up into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />-day time periods, because after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> days, all three of them visit again (Least common multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />).
You can find how many times each pair of visitors can meet by finding the LCM of their visiting days and dividing that number by 60.
Remember to subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, because you do not wish to count the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />th day, when all three visit.
</p><p>A and B visit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/6/7f6a0cf8af6bad558e7dfbc29a62ad7319b06d53.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{3 \cdot 4}-1 = 4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="100" height="38" /> times.
</p><p>A and C visit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/7/047cacb87c42f6da553440d40d63c0bdcf5286e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{3 \cdot 5}-1 = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="99" height="37" /> times.
</p><p>B and C visit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/8/4e854c29dd79a08b87ded51764b7e91fb46a2ab2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{4 \cdot 5}-1 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="99" height="38" /> times.
</p><p><br />
This is a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> visits per <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> day period.
Therefore, the total number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />-person visits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/c/a6c9a8e07fc1cab9ee20fc5b09dbc33823f3730d.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \cdot 6 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }54}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />.
</p>
<ul><li>Note: We do not have to worry about the numbers over 360: (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f224fb3a29cdf115a51f0dc39d14bd5522299cb.png" class="latex" alt="$361,362,363,364,365$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="168" height="16" />) having 2 factors. This is because we can rewrite</li></ul>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/2/c62e37ca2ee5084ca5e3afba37e70c86b27af003.png" class="latex" alt="$(361,362,363,364,365) \Rightarrow (361,362, 3\cdot 121, 4 \cdot 91, 5 \cdot 73)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="18" />. We note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76ad7692f5d86be790209a56a68c2a3488235054.png" class="latex" alt="$121$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> is not further divisible by 4 or 5, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/d/7fd34ff50f8bba26a08bc96a69e921daa60f5f12.png" class="latex" alt="$91$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is not further divisible by 3 or 5, 73 is not further divisible by 3 or 4. Therefore, none of the numbers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/2071802952fb5ba735fdbfb8ed3b2040ee9e49a6.png" class="latex" alt="$361-365$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" /> have 2 factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f6bde4dc7fce438a6685ab174f8926aa25b0ae5.png" class="latex" alt="$3,4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, so we can conclude that the answer is indeed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f490937c0b9f1b956a4460fb79a65072f3681f6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{54}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>From the information above, we get that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302aece6a5a47865d42ff4cd9b5f79a565b056af.png" class="latex" alt="$A=3x$" width="57" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/c/d8c3ebf8b05db817de08b98f0153b815fcbc27c7.png" class="latex" alt="$B=4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/b/c6b78b2f10e82ab950cdfbd98ae59f9d30d4e278.png" class="latex" alt="$C=5x$" width="57" height="12" />
</p><p>Now, we want the days in which exactly two of these people meet up
</p><p>The three pairs are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb0c75d9abe3e693ba0bb677cecbb923cefd81af.png" class="latex" alt="$(A,B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/c/f1c8b247a2087e207417435b6f42ea04a0b9cf94.png" class="latex" alt="$(B,C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/6/b0655ad32e6d1a40429a867397c94a1826876577.png" class="latex" alt="$(A,C)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />.
</p><p>Notice that we are trying to find the LCM of each pair.
</p><p>Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/8/568e09acedab23e0928cc0108e21ce2a4f3e7537.png" class="latex" alt="$LCM(A,B)=12x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e61b51ab50c0300a10c9d33ada6fdd3e8e88b1be.png" class="latex" alt="$LCM(B,C)=20x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/e/dde1166b9ee53c0dac0df033c4cbe708b6fd583e.png" class="latex" alt="$LCM(A,C)=15x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" />
</p><p>Notice that we want to eliminate when all these friends meet up. By doing this, we will find the LCM of the three letters.
</p><p>Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/c/dac0dbd8c4ce9bba47d3d37c46adef7c6192295b.png" class="latex" alt="$LCM(A,B,C)=60x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="171" height="18" />
</p><p>Now, we add all of the days up(including overcount).
</p><p>We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/f/f4fd0a2570621edf17d3b31d138d6be1b17d6240.png" class="latex" alt="$30+18+24=72$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="14" />.
Now, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4ea16445cd22eb75af16f8b2d6b0941b267588.png" class="latex" alt="$60(6)=360$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" />, we have to subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> days from every pair.
Hence, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0cb1461d5f547ac3a06c7b047f84820b575ddd4.png" class="latex" alt="$72-18=\boxed{54}\implies\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="188" height="24" />.
</p>
