<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd0d1ded44edd12c5c2ea6290b65bde3a31d2eb.png" class="latex" alt="$BX = q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d57a7d3459de8353ddcb527dc4c2038902d3154.png" class="latex" alt="$CX = p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> meets the circle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/a/baaf047f3064e842eee50196f41003a1a0174ab9.png" class="latex" alt="$BX.$" width="33" height="12" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d82e88b3efdcfe5340faa5a70a547e07bbaf9d87.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABX$" width="60" height="13" /> is isosceles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/4/6c4a588d1cae136c1316f1ced8d840269d7bd24d.png" class="latex" alt="$AX=AB=86,EB=EX,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="220" height="16" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/6/a366cbdcabf8f0f92a22d30d3b1c075a028a2c02.png" class="latex" alt="$EC+EB=EC+EX.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="189" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/4/fa486ff7d6445cedcfe8570f22a269c48079e773.png" class="latex" alt="$(EC+EX)(EC-EX)=EC^2-EX^2=(97^2-AE^2)-(86^2-AE^2)=97^2-86^2=2013$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="712" height="19" /> by the Pythagorean Theorem. Only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/e/24e174ac585a9db3bb309aeb07373d3e52cba3a3.png" class="latex" alt="$EC+EX=\boxed{(D)~61}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="171" height="29" /> is a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/6/b36bbec3504f3ba15a25e71b144d7865864b9820.png" class="latex" alt="$97&gt;EC+EX&gt;EC-EX=\frac{2013}{EC+EX}.$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="344" height="39" />
</p><p>~dolphin7
