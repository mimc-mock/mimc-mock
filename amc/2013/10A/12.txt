<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/c/0bcdd2686190ec06b7e4306dae071e7f7a2671bb.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=AC=28$" width="122" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/4483542fa46123b1e899eda06f3de671df25bd07.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=20$" width="70" height="12" />.  Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/9/8394d8e0d9795555d3255c4fc621558d2236b766.png" class="latex" alt="$D,E,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are on sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />, respectively, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0f6a692f19a65fe60fe5745826792f3fd1d57e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{EF}$" width="29" height="15" /> are parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, respectively.  What is the perimeter of parallelogram <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dad61e2885c538a9eedc7969b6639097f2e8e292.png" class="latex" alt="$ADEF$" width="57" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/4/ec45f0635e288a5e5da73a19319f4eb7045b5eab.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(180); pen dps = linewidth(0.7) + fontsize(10); defaultpen(dps); real r=5/7; pair A=(10,sqrt(28^2-100)),B=origin,C=(20,0),D=(A.x*r,A.y*r); pair bottom=(C.x+(D.x-A.x),C.y+(D.y-A.y)); pair E=extension(D,bottom,B,C); pair top=(E.x+D.x,E.y+D.y); pair F=extension(E,top,A,C); draw(A--B--C--cycle^^D--E--F); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^E^^F); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,W); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,dir(0)); [/asy]" width="242" height="302" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/4/0f47fd7632c4aa5f86c477b38f41487c4ad409e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }48\qquad \textbf{(B) }52\qquad \textbf{(C) }56\qquad \textbf{(D) }60\qquad \textbf{(E) }72\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0f6a692f19a65fe60fe5745826792f3fd1d57e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{EF}$" width="29" height="15" /> are parallel to the sides of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, the internal triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/b/16b4072dec30604cbea5998c12fddd9eacfeb564.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDE$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/3/aa343fe20d5dd55341a82bddf61aafed77cce728.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EFC$" width="59" height="13" /> are similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, and are therefore also isosceles triangles.
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/b/9cb7942d975dd9071bb3214413eaf654249622c7.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = DE$" width="84" height="12" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac0978ab73fea09ae8988561db6f67f8158479d5.png" class="latex" alt="$AD + DE = AD + DB = AB = 28$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="281" height="14" />.
</p><p>The opposite sides of parallelograms are equal (you can prove this fact simply by drawing the diagonal of the parallelogram and proving that the two resulting triangles are congruent by SSS), so the perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/d/f0da02739872ce7e2e9b72ba2c09a023fda13da0.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times (AD + DE) = 56\implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="259" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/8/808010bb840f51aac5f554e3e4a7ba5bd454e44b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=0$" width="62" height="13" />, by fakesolving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/9/2c9e192562f4114e326543506b52b5acddf54e58.png" class="latex" alt="$56\implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="108" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Drawing the diagram with a ruler and compass (and scaling back by x4), we can draw approximate parallel lines. This yields about 14, but we need to multiply by 4 to get 56, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dbbac62a58811f44fda1df541fe2d2b7f43bc64.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="29" />.
</p>
