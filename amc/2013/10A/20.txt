<p>A unit square is rotated <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb649f063149ebc97d3ee58c4fd38d4c440846.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> about its center.  What is the area of the region swept out by the interior of the square?  
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a526ea0cc4d682dfaf74d89d529694101ee19274.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 - \frac{\sqrt2}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2 - \sqrt2 + \frac{\pi}{4}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt2}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 1 + \frac{\sqrt2}{4} + \frac{\pi}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="704" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, we need to see what this looks like.  Below is a diagram.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/e/2cef856b58cfff2a7283d5219d0dfdaa03042386.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)); path square=shift((-.5,-.5))*unitsquare,square2=rotate(45)*square; fill(square^^square2,grey); for(int i=0;i&lt;=3;i=i+1) { path arcrot=arc(origin,sqrt(2)/2,45+90*i,90*(i+1)); draw(arcrot); fill(arcrot--(0,0)--cycle,grey); draw(arc(origin,sqrt(2)/2+1/8,50+90*i,90*(i+1)-10),EndArrow); } draw(square^^square2);[/asy]" width="332" height="332" />
</p><p>For this square with side length 1, the distance from center to vertex is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/0/00079a78a7c4e993aab3c7c44b27dd15fdfc65ad.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="41" />, hence the area is composed of a semicircle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times a parallelogram (or a kite with diagonals of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/d/9dda7c09052b6309a09a6eba298dbf11fef2635d.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{2}-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adf7f8555dba46db252c3a1de44f597a9171be9b.png" class="latex" alt="$r \text{ or} \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="41" />) with height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/c/62c394b13f6cf0ad73a826d0f1e208b07603e356.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2(1+\sqrt{2})}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="81" height="47" />. That is to say, the total area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/3/943543e1c6803688bc1000cb36d152826bd722a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \pi \left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2 + 4 \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4(1+\sqrt{2})} = \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 2 - \sqrt{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="369" height="56" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/184aa4bab0f1d089a1cc659b41a0c3fae9aa9e7a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150);defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)); path square=shift((-.5,-.5))*unitsquare,square2=rotate(45)*square;//fill(square^^square2,grey); for(int i=0;i&lt;=3;i=i+1){path arcrot=arc(origin,sqrt(2)/2,45+90*i,90*(i+1));draw(arcrot); fill(arcrot--(0,0)--cycle,grey);} //draw(arc(origin,sqrt(2)/2+1/8,50+90*i,90*(i+1)-10),EndArrow);} draw(square^^square2); //draw((-.5,.5)--(.5,-.5)^^(0,sqrt(.5))--(0,-sqrt(.5)),dotted);draw((.5,.5)--(-.5,-.5),dotted); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
(To turn each dart-shaped piece into a parallelogram, cut along the dashed line and flip over one half.)
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/5/4f5c5a5cf3a60dd0e7cdc32bb0caf1b6d792df89.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150,Aspect);real r=sqrt(2);real b=2-2/r; draw((0,0)--(-1,1)--(b-1,1)--(0,r)--cycle);draw((0,1)--(b-1,1)--(b/2-1,1-b/2));draw((0,0)--(b-1,1),dashed); fill((2,0)--(b+1,1)--(b+2,0)--cycle,lightgray);draw((.5,.5)--(1,.5),EndArrow); draw((2,0)--(1,1)--(b+1,1)--(b+2,0)--(2,0)^^(b+1,1)--(b/2+1,1-b/2)^^(2,0)--(2+b/2,b/2)); draw((2,0)--(b+1,1),dashed); [/asy]" width="252" height="102" />
Alternatively, you can move the dart-shaped piece to the other side and make a kite.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/a/49a54e3a86d6718092983262eb1a1ceb745723aa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(75,Aspect);real r=sqrt(2);real b=2-2/r; draw((r-1,1)--(b-1,1)); draw((0,0)--(b-1,1)--(0,r)--(r-1,1)--cycle); draw((0,r)--(0,0),dashed); [/asy]" width="75" height="125" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/b/39baf46e49824d8bc53a4b853563984045ce96b7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)); path square=shift((-.5,-.5))*unitsquare,square2=rotate(45)*square; for(int i=0;i&lt;=6;i=i+1) { path arcrot=arc(origin,sqrt(2)/2,45+270*i,270*(i+1)); draw(arcrot); } draw(square^^square2);[/asy]" width="335" height="335" />
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/c/d0cad456843171c2445debebc39ca51772eae46a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(high res image; no labels)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="221" height="17" /></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:AMC_10A_2013_20.jpg" class="image"><img alt="AMC 10A 2013 20.jpg" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//1/1e/AMC_10A_2013_20.jpg" width="348" height="352" /></a>
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> be the center of the square and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/6/0964c9cf406ef8e5ec8beaa6a92125f2d8f90460.png" class="latex" alt="$OB$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" />. The desired area consists of the unit square, plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> regions congruent to the region bounded by arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> triangular regions congruent to right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ada763e99852a6e44a15d46cce3c3709a944d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BCD$" width="44" height="12" />. The area of the region bounded by arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/633ba3810c6fd945c9e692e43f5463071afcd870.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{Area of Circle}-\text{Area of Square}}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="234" height="37" />. Since the circle has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/8/ed89feeb1e08299fe3bc0efb5ab3fc0a121eb031.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{\sqrt {2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="26" height="41" />, the area of the region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/f/cffc6a62370892570161f8426292ca3b8245efe6.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{\pi}{2}-1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="48" height="52" />, so 4 times the area of that region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/d/30df88050262c0f965308e383d98a3f8e6e3d6c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\pi}{4}-\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="48" height="37" />. Now we find the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f372757d55c2d07adc720932c6d6e1396aa389.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCD$" width="60" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/9/0a9137a9d694ec13e06932a0e089f80f90fc8b96.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=BO-OC=\dfrac{\sqrt {2}}{2}-\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="219" height="41" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f372757d55c2d07adc720932c6d6e1396aa389.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCD$" width="60" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> right triangle, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f372757d55c2d07adc720932c6d6e1396aa389.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCD$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/c/3bc517687fa61f1f933381d087d20d4462cb142c.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{BC^2}{2}=\dfrac {\left (\dfrac {\sqrt {2}}{2}-\dfrac{1}{2} \right)^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="166" height="76" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f372757d55c2d07adc720932c6d6e1396aa389.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCD$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/c/50cb9ec43176097de8b35103058857a09cb556d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{3}{2}-\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" />. Finally, the area of the whole region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9a1670d714f0a8d8931b622585085c9c57dd78.png" class="latex" alt="$1+ \left(\dfrac {3}{2}-\sqrt {2} \right) + \left(\dfrac{\pi}{4}-\dfrac{1}{2} \right)=\dfrac{\pi}{4}+2-\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="333" height="43" />, which we can rewrite as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/7/54788a6493d42d371a391ac7cd59de6e2ca1df92.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }    2 - \sqrt{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="139" height="44" />.
</p>
