<p>A triangle with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/2/0429617bc1afa148bd90f2912e2e3f0a85c98ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$(6, 5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93079e5856b4a3cb0d7a4ee15b26bb7af626828c.png" class="latex" alt="$(8, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f095623550b16f9b2f89155178fb409d00e97f72.png" class="latex" alt="$(9, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is reflected about the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/f/ccf5d650726be49af3a306c54a2fd7dd1d628b48.png" class="latex" alt="$x=8$" width="43" height="12" /> to create a second triangle.  What is the area of the union of the two triangles?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/b/7bbbedc16502f642a5a109c3c830202f3b58f4d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 9 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{28}{3} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 10 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{31}{3} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{32}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="396" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/2/0429617bc1afa148bd90f2912e2e3f0a85c98ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$(6, 5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, B be at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93079e5856b4a3cb0d7a4ee15b26bb7af626828c.png" class="latex" alt="$(8, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f095623550b16f9b2f89155178fb409d00e97f72.png" class="latex" alt="$(9, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.  Reflecting over the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/f/ccf5d650726be49af3a306c54a2fd7dd1d628b48.png" class="latex" alt="$x=8$" width="43" height="12" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/f/fbf96d9cf02eca9273efc2dc0746a75c82522e04.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39; = D =  (10,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba66ac46f7105f9e32fee88515b18d758f58f38.png" class="latex" alt="$B&#39; = B$" width="57" height="14" /> (as the x-coordinate of B is 8), and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/8/e08ce8bebb4f951d63e1c69523203f11ff367a6e.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39; = E =  (7, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" />.  Line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> can be represented as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/a/bdab128dde4efd23508d481263eaffa46276e559.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-4x+29$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="107" height="16" />, so we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />.  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/4/f34a65277762945bba6c3c514a62b91d5b905cd6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A = (6, 5), B = (8, -3), C = (9, 1), D = (10, 5), E = (7, 1), F = (8, 7/3); draw(A--B--C--cycle^^D--E--B--cycle); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^E^^F); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,S); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NE); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,W); label(&quot;$F$&quot;, F, N);   [/asy]" width="152" height="252" />
</p><p><br />
We see that if we connect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, we get a line of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> (between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/2/0429617bc1afa148bd90f2912e2e3f0a85c98ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$(6, 5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/7/b77cbd6cca73e46d9414dc49c7caa765a4937e4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(10,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />).  The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/295b419ddec1c883ebdf67aed80832eb31b67fa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{bh}{2} = \frac{4(8)}{2} = 16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="124" height="38" />.
</p><p>Now, let the point of intersection between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />.  If we can just find the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e728a789f2ca839593526b82229b72fde61ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADF$" width="59" height="13" /> and subtract it from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, we are done.
</p><p>We realize that because the diagram is symmetric over <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/62434ae80ca145c20f0fbc7563f5151371ed163c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 8$" width="43" height="12" />, the intersection of lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> should intersect at an x-coordinate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.  We know that the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19a748a3b3bdbf55ecebfa52c128107d232c1e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5-1}{10-7} = \frac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="89" height="38" />.  Thus, we can represent the line going through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d300ae2e2b00b50021923c70ffb7a4f8960360.png" class="latex" alt="$y - 1=\frac{4}{3}(x - 7)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="132" height="37" />.  Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/62434ae80ca145c20f0fbc7563f5151371ed163c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 8$" width="43" height="12" />, we find that the y-coordinate of F is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/f/e1f2bce73877ab24e4abe8ae41191c7620c66c1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.  Thus, the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e728a789f2ca839593526b82229b72fde61ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADF$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/7625bd5ad2ad042101c7240339217ab6fa57e284.png" class="latex" alt="$5 - \frac{7}{3} = \frac{8}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />.  Using the formula for the area of a triangle, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e728a789f2ca839593526b82229b72fde61ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADF$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/d/f9d8f06fd9af82fe3d8c913f99bbf749621b13bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{16}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />.  
</p><p>To get our final answer, we must subtract this from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/5/1753208c88db0afccf6341a5a19315c8707b0188.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABD] - [ADF] = 16 - \frac{16}{3} = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }\frac{32}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="308" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, realize that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" />. Connect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />. Let the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. Connect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2ef2fa9310285ea0bc63937e3bf83c99168a04.png" class="latex" alt="$BG$" width="28" height="12" /> is a median of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />. 
</p><p><br />
Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> is isosceles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2ef2fa9310285ea0bc63937e3bf83c99168a04.png" class="latex" alt="$BG$" width="28" height="12" /> is also an altitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />. We know the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2ef2fa9310285ea0bc63937e3bf83c99168a04.png" class="latex" alt="$BG$" width="28" height="12" /> from the given coordinates. The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/295b419ddec1c883ebdf67aed80832eb31b67fa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{bh}{2} = \frac{4(8)}{2} = 16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="124" height="38" />. 
</p><p><br />
Let the intesection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2ef2fa9310285ea0bc63937e3bf83c99168a04.png" class="latex" alt="$BG$" width="28" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> is the centroid of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />. Therefore, it splits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/2/3f2ef2fa9310285ea0bc63937e3bf83c99168a04.png" class="latex" alt="$BG$" width="28" height="12" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/c/03c2139057eeab8ebb65b2792f00f58a982e1111.png" class="latex" alt="$BF={2 \over 3}(BG)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="108" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108028ef9fe7ccd38a130c1b5d70289e96816212.png" class="latex" alt="$FG={1\over 3}(BG)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" />. The area of quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/a/b4a308ff92324b36878a4afc075cd3f216bd237b.png" class="latex" alt="$ABDF = 16\cdot {2 \over 3} = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }\frac{32}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="218" height="48" />
</p><p>~Zeric Hang
</p>
