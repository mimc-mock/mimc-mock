<p>Tom, Dorothy, and Sammy went on a vacation and agreed to split the costs evenly.  During their trip Tom paid $105, Dorothy paid $125, and Sammy paid $175.  In order to share costs equally, Tom gave Sammy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> dollars, and Dorothy gave Sammy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> dollars.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/236c8b639a26e618cb78c2befb89f78fc61694cc.png" class="latex" alt="$t-d$" width="38" height="12" />?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67f6e43038efd8cf47d657684adf291a269b6e9e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 15\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 20\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 25\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 35$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The total amount paid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad2c7713b229fe4ad79e02c243fb6403b7cfc94a.png" class="latex" alt="$105 + 125 + 175 = 405$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="177" height="14" />.  To get how much each should have paid, we do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/4/e3447a926899a8184e6b8cf463f3d8cb4a8cce0f.png" class="latex" alt="$405/3 = 135$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus, we know that Tom needs to give Sammy 30 dollars, and Dorothy 10 dollars.  This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/6/b167dbb4b6e79063985a05bed57a8b7b904d0246.png" class="latex" alt="$t-d = 30 - 10 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="210" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The difference in the money that Tom paid and Dorothy paid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />.  In order for them both to have paid the same amount, Tom must pay <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> more than Dorothy.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/4/3b43f083b7ebfa3369ff6f538cdd81f184838712.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{{(B)20}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The meaning of sharing costs equally is meaning that, after the vacation, they are equally dividing the money in a way such that, each person would have the same amount left. As each person spends an amount of money, greater than 100, let it be that they all had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> dollars to spend. This means that after the vacation we want the amount of money, they currently have. After the trip, Tom would've <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars, Dorothy would've <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars, and Sammy had <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars. This gives us a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff4756033ea3270bfc9395b29639f570488d9aab.png" class="latex" alt="$95+75+25=195$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="152" height="15" /> dollars. 
</p><p>We want to equally split this money, as that is what happens after splitting the cost equally. This means that we want Dorothy, Tom, and Sammy to each have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars. We know that Tom gave Sammy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> dollars meaning that we want to split this money first. As Tom gives money to no one else, we want him to reach <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars in this trade, meaning that as Tom has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars and Sammy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars, we can do a trade of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0722c452ffbd2ce78ac00cfd13f628514bab13d7.png" class="latex" alt="$t=30$" width="48" height="12" />. After this trade, we get that Tom has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars, Sammy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars, and Dorothy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars.
</p><p>Next trade is where Dorothy gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> dollars to Sammy. Dorothy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars and Sammy has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars. As both of these don't have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars and this is the last trade, we need to make sure both have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> dollars at the end. This is possible if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/f/48f49c25bebad7fde170b47bef3a86c188ed2c0b.png" class="latex" alt="$d=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />
</p><p>We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/7/5770d4e2c88b6aa7efbb29d4c6512eb32f25096d.png" class="latex" alt="$t-d=30-10=\boxed{B) 20}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="198" height="32" />
</p><p>~Batmanstark
</p>
