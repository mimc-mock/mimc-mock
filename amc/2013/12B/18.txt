<p>Barbara and Jenna play the following game, in which they take turns. A number of coins lie on a table. When it is Barbara’s turn, she must remove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins, unless only one coin remains, in which case she loses her turn. When it is Jenna’s turn, she must remove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> coins. A coin flip determines who goes first. Whoever removes the last coin wins the game. Assume both players use their best strategy. Who will win when the game starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins and when the game starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> Barbara will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins and Jenna will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b7193f66028e13f331d53ff5e6942c4b05587465.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> Jenna will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins, and whoever goes first will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> Barbara will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins, and whoever goes second will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> Jenna will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins, and Barbara will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e41e3cd48cee2c6866cfbb4386a8e99d3482f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> Whoever goes first will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins, and whoever goes second will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/7/16794ed8d576bcdfc1b48ac2155ce53ff11aff25.png" class="latex" alt="$2014$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> coins.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We split into 2 cases: 2013 coins, and 2014 coins.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/69953aa35251649de262c6ad07270422f885186c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{2013 coins:}$" width="90" height="11" />
Notice that when there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> coins left, whoever moves first loses, as they must leave an amount of coins the other person can take. If Jenna goes first, she can take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> coins. Then, whenever Barbara takes coins, Jenna will take the amount that makes the total coins taken in that round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. (For instance, if Barbara takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins, Jenna will take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />). Eventually, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/d/00d2ee275ff6c74071b55b71b85c0c51c7cb6136.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=0 (\text{mod }5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" /> it will be Barbara's move with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> coins remaining, so she will lose. If Barbara goes first, on each round, Jenna will take the amount of coins that makes the total coins taken on that round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb25f28978c0539332ea25da84da1b1e4847c1b3.png" class="latex" alt="$2013=3 (\text{mod }5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />, it will be Barbara's move with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> coins remaining, so she will have to take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> coins, allowing Jenna to take the last coin. Therefore, Jenna will win with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/286f2b30957c22e1aac02c07980ec93d3979e8f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{2014 coins:}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="12" />
If Jenna moves first, she will take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> coin, leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> coins, and she wins as shown above. If Barbara moves first, she can take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins, leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054090e60a5dcb8bc33213620b3af9278f9a3e12.png" class="latex" alt="$2010$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. After every move by Jenna, Barbara will then take the number of coins that makes the total taken in that round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d37cba839ab8c189ae42abafa5cbfc1a09b952de.png" class="latex" alt="$2010=0\text{(mod }5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />, it will be Jenna's turn with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> coins left, so 
Barbara will win. In this case, whoever moves first wins.
</p><p>Based on this, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />
</p>
