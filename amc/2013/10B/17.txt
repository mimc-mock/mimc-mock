<p>Alex has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> red tokens and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> blue tokens. There is a booth where Alex can give two red tokens and receive in return a silver token and a blue token, and another booth where Alex can give three blue tokens and receive in return a silver token and a red token. Alex continues to exchange tokens until no more exchanges are possible. How many silver tokens will Alex have at the end?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/9/4a90f65f4c6514755474bfe618a47372951d5c32.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 62 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 82 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 83 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 102 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 103$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If Alex goes to the red booth 3 times, then goes to the blue booth once, Alex can exchange 6 red tokens for 4 silver tokens and one red token. Similarly, if Alex goes to the blue booth 2 times, then goes to the red booth once, Alex can exchange 6 blue tokens for 3 silver tokens and one blue token. Let's call the first combination Combo 1, and the second combination Combo 2.
</p><p>In other words, Alex can exchange 5 red tokens for 4 silver tokens as long as he has at least 6 red tokens, and Alex can exchange 5 blue tokens for 3 silver tokens as long as he has at least 6 blue tokens. Hence after performing 14 Combo 1's and 14 Combo 2's, we end up with 5 red, 5 blue, and 98 silver tokens.
</p><p>Finally, Alex can visit the blue booth once, then do Combo 1, then visit the blue booth once more to end up with 1 red token, 2 blue tokens, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/1/7816785c5b2dbf3fcaa3a98a3f81396f13940267.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 103}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" /> silver tokens, at which point it is clear he cannot use the booths anymore.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can approach this problem by assuming he goes to the red booth first. You start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7b390b64bae4f1f73d20b573b161afcc8b0a36b.png" class="latex" alt="$75 \text{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/2/042895507f6b80bb6dc73d54b4c849a84a49153e.png" class="latex" alt="$75 \text{B}$" width="29" height="12" /> and at the end of the first booth, you will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f5885550711778ee5540661a93a54c935d99d71b.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \text{R}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/3/f335518d2fc4b75feb7615b74c6d792625e8f518.png" class="latex" alt="$112 \text{B}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="38" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/d/c1dac2b9bf14715b9ef0342f8ce67c68adf2cab9.png" class="latex" alt="$37 \text{S}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> denote the number of visits to the first booth and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> denote the number of visits to the second booth. Then we can describe the quantities of his red and blue coins as follows:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244246c3a8d32a704111feffd84c68ff62b82760.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[R(x,y)=-2x+y+75\]" width="185" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95ac7ef3a9431d4268c06c4aa31e707e2257968f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[B(x,y)=x-3y+75\]" width="171" height="18" />
There are no legal exchanges when he has fewer than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> red coins and fewer than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> blue coins, namely when he has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> red coin and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> blue coins. We can then create a system of equations:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/e/49e2b05aeeca41a480424920fb94c4a2349ce569.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1=-2x+y+75\]" width="137" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/e/16e8f82d83f165ea83ace08805ceec4612b697d4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2=x-3y+75\]" width="124" height="16" />
Solving yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/0/f30c82b463a5c680a411c9e784f7ad3fdaff8c42.png" class="latex" alt="$x=59$" width="52" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/2/9d24dd975f11aab0263ab3f1b60fb6745c971b58.png" class="latex" alt="$y=44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" />. Since he gains one silver coin per visit to each booth, he has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/4371e84ef7f04120055aef9513aaa861a93ac418.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=44+59=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}103}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="215" height="28" /> silver coins in total.
