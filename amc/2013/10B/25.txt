<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Observe that the maximum possible value of the sum of the last two digits of the base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> number and the base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/9/e19972237942c070796e0a6b5368c48235f9482d.png" class="latex" alt="$44+55=99$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" />. 
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/1/931ea3a0be00a17b70b1f47bb7cf7ce4bfe7f082.png" class="latex" alt="$N \equiv a \pmod {25}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/4/25456cac90496487f0c56657e6af284de6936e47.png" class="latex" alt="$N \equiv b \pmod {36}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="18" />. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/9/fb9bc58d7852806df4e1316dba4fb19d57ac7af7.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; \frac{25}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd4334a70c3b007829f728da6d9cf02f647269b3.png" class="latex" alt="$2N \equiv 2a \pmod {25}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" /> and if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/6/7a6c746e0c406739b66dedc3de1369fcf1a1da51.png" class="latex" alt="$a &gt; \frac{25}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/c/afc15b0a24be37abfe83a8a6c6f868b6fb9dd038.png" class="latex" alt="$2N \equiv 2a - 25 \pmod {25}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="188" height="18" />.
</p><p>Using the same logic for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f5eb8f00d270ebb56fcbc13f3de20da65b7bf6d.png" class="latex" alt="$b &lt; 18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc026e3e3301c33fb31dd08b059a89643e2ee46.png" class="latex" alt="$2N \equiv 2b \pmod {36}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" />, and in the other case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/2/8a2f3062f71a264b041e9b4e96d075cd3d4fc840.png" class="latex" alt="$2N \equiv 2b - 36 \pmod {36}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="187" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can do four cases:
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/2/0f21249f1bfd412f1dbe69b68204d3cc9a6bd489.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = 2a - 25 + 2b - 36 \implies a + b = 61$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="333" height="14" />.

We find the possible ordered pairs to be: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/c/efc5e4189edfa8f4cae5d06e1d721726877956e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(13, 12), (14, 11), (15, 10), ..., (24, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> ordered pairs.
</p><p>Case 3: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/6/7c681f621c8ad7f1d4fa429354772c2f3f140ab8.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = 2a + 2b - 36 \implies a + b = 36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="293" height="14" />.
</p><p>Note that in this case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9f45055ef1e8fb6be49213bf30f939b440df6a.png" class="latex" alt="$b \geq 18$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="49" height="15" /> must hold, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/9/de9d3259544c3bdf48b1acb07120fccb34640686.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" /> must hold.
We find the possible ordered pairs to be: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/d/20d494caaa773c9c98d0796d4159bb6e5874d282.png" class="latex" alt="$(24, 12), (25, 11), (26, 10), ..., (35, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="18" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> ordered pairs.
</p><p>Case 4: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/0195255e2d1f3e32ca9250c460ae357c0efdde64.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = 2a + 2b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />.
</p><p>Trivially no solutions except <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c443f6c46d02d305ff2d61384adee7b6dae8b113.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b) = (0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />, which matches the solution in Case 1, which makes this an overcount.
</p><p>By CRT, each solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28e24c43456d95b5ab8d4f2f8f85a644e98b7ed3.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> corresponds exactly one positive integer in a set of exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa4737e10f0aeb54521180b06962a47c1d086d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{lcm} (25, 36) = 900$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="135" height="18" /> consecutive positive integers, and since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" /> positive integers between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" />, our induction is complete, and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/3/1e3dae334660d83c5c255fd6e0ed538a7fe7568b.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 12 + 12 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }25}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="177" height="28" />.
</p><p>~ fidgetboss_4000
</p><p><br />
