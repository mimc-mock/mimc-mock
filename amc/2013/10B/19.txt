<p>The real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/222dc10d4328ed2db5f8951cb11e6556c27e67ae.png" class="latex" alt="$c,b,a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> form an arithmetic sequence with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/b/b1b205dca73cdd85b03f2ba43f0eda90ce949b5d.png" class="latex" alt="$a \geq b \geq c \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="106" height="15" />. The quadratic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71d13ac0e00358ad3fa8b0d6db3217b8a10cc7d2.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="16" /> has exactly one root. What is this root?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/c/44c239cbaf19a7e8fb1b469b457a7795265d706a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -7-4\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -2-\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ -1\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ -2+\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ -7+4\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="665" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>It is given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/1/98172d1b54b563033d26dc22c407dad4d4802be5.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2+bx+c=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="16" /> has 1 real root, so the discriminant is zero, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f776091da02aa8d3c44dbd0d8d7e6129a134ae.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Because a, b, c are in arithmetic progression, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/8278c99d5e9f42c042413525178c3c43a5f6390b.png" class="latex" alt="$b-a=c-b$" width="102" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/6743ab1f9a41330e25ae02c95b953982c43ba851.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac {a+c} {2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="74" height="35" />.
</p><p>We need to find the unique root, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/5/36588f5e8fdfe7c968c3359574a711cd30c7f488.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac {b} {2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="37" /> (discriminant is 0).
</p><p>From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f776091da02aa8d3c44dbd0d8d7e6129a134ae.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />, we can get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bd54effeddd55bae4835b857e35133815026abb.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac {b} {2a} =-\frac {2c} {b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="94" height="37" />.
</p><p>Ignoring the negatives(for now), we have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0cfcfd0b6bf8a239b6363fbaa7b7c1625e6608e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {2c} {b} = \frac {2c} {\frac {a+c} {2}} = \frac {4c} {a+c} = \frac {1} {\frac {1} {\frac {4c} {a+c}}} = \frac {1} {\frac {a+c} {4c}} = \frac {1} {\frac {a} {4c} + \frac {1} {4} }$" style="vertical-align: -24px" width="332" height="49" />.
</p><p>Fortunately, finding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/d/89d9a62542baf027131eb0c3868c5985183d05ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {a} {c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> is not very hard.
</p><p>Plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/6743ab1f9a41330e25ae02c95b953982c43ba851.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac {a+c} {2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="74" height="35" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f776091da02aa8d3c44dbd0d8d7e6129a134ae.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/9/35901c55e457d3460450abfa0c8b89178bdfd7a8.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2+2ac+c^2=16ac$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="163" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/0/0b0d178903e434f515f1c65553c1a3058be4df33.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2-14ac+c^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="145" height="16" />, and dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b693026c70424da68ed4c52d3d0d35d9548ee4e.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2$" width="14" height="15" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/3/1138e19dc3a1f9ab0b3b843de18d76996fa03831.png" class="latex" alt="$(\frac {a} {c} ) ^2-14( \frac {a} {c} ) +1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="167" height="33" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d7d7c7fd880c065f2e1898e527c137ddfa22bef.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {a} {c} = \frac {14 \pm \sqrt {192} } {2} = 7 \pm 4 \sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="214" height="41" />.
</p><p>But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/5/145cde36c0e0418c6ea577c6b67c2c7e96607261.png" class="latex" alt="$7-4\sqrt {3} &lt;1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="18" />, violating the assumption that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/3/88357334572fff6f341375240c9b999b594102e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/3/1233eebe2909008b7a23c80374b77736fcb9432c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {a} {c} = 7 +4\sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="33" />.
</p><p>Plugging this in, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/4/5548a4e53a26b87bbf081e36f717a4f8a4987be7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {1} {\frac {a} {4c} + \frac {1} {4} } = \frac {1} {2+ \sqrt {3} } = 2- \sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="217" height="43" />.
</p><p>But we need the negative of this, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/e/8bee0e46406290910ca0a630148d26fb1c7a5f17.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf{(D)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="44" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Note that we can divide the polynomial by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to make the leading coefficient 1 since dividing does not change the roots or the fact that the coefficients are in an arithmetic sequence. Also, we know that there is exactly one root so this equation must be of the form 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/6083d91dc5b1ee6da0ddc789e79a244e781518bd.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-r)^2 = x^2 - 2rx + r^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="192" height="19" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab915a040dcce4bfe94f0431fd0bb0213e761671.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \ge -2r \ge r^2 \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="138" height="17" />.
We now use the fact that the coefficients are in an arithmetic sequence. Note that in any arithmetic sequence, the average is equal to the median. Thus,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/4/ab4dc746f0706c232fe761536201213aebf5b0ea.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2 + 1 = -4r$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/a/46afb4c2fd9e1f384b95e4f80ee7c749f5f2de09.png" class="latex" alt="$r = -2 \pm \sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/e/07e64d1ff4580a04d61f1316d0071347b78ee03b.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \ge r^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="17" />, we easily see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/3/113a0737a9ef90586213e7dd7bbeb5fe90a5a667.png" class="latex" alt="$|r|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="17" height="18" /> has to be between 1 and 0. Thus, we can eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/9/7a902059212d3f1e709def651d711c5ab9a7ab7b.png" class="latex" alt="$-2 - \sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="18" /> and are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a50a120560546a4baf834084e303374a25c4651.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} -2 + \sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="119" height="33" /> as the answer.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/1/98172d1b54b563033d26dc22c407dad4d4802be5.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2+bx+c=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="16" /> has only 1 real root, we know that the discriminant must equal 0, or that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f776091da02aa8d3c44dbd0d8d7e6129a134ae.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />. Because the discriminant equals 0, we have that the root of the quadratic is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/2/7528dc9dda75a00def012213f479bae1de9ea80c.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac {-b} {2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="57" height="37" />. We are also given that the coefficients of the quadratic are in arithmetic progression, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/4/3146f9c4d83b2b0f04e6d2e17bae57b9aa5b6a52.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge b \ge c \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="106" height="15" />. Letting the arbitrary difference equal variable <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e6e592a43022ef5fbf3de3b1312a62b6a7d834c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/d/9dde789ddeaa3bc7605544be1dea0efa9b3c397b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=b-d$" width="71" height="12" />. Plugging those two equations into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/f/71f776091da02aa8d3c44dbd0d8d7e6129a134ae.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/4/61427533c818923ed3610afb630e08379b3d3e93.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4(b^2-d^2)=4b^2-4d^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="212" height="19" /> which yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/c/d8c89c3ba540b877aaf302a617ffd134d1ec2732.png" class="latex" alt="$3b^2=4d^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="15" />. Isolating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8ce0c9f273e4c3c7dd313101b02b49aaf6fc21c0.png" class="latex" alt="$d=\frac {b \sqrt{3}} {2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="68" height="41" />. Substituting that in for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e6e592a43022ef5fbf3de3b1312a62b6a7d834c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/2/71204b1dd5e6e2085ff7f271f5d03344339cd5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b+\frac {b \sqrt{3}} {2}=b(1+\frac {\sqrt{3}} {2})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="204" height="41" />. Once again, substituting that in for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/2/7528dc9dda75a00def012213f479bae1de9ea80c.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac {-b} {2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="57" height="37" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fab13d3d8638279e0e2ac37f80a5a3078756eaba.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac {-b} {2b(1+\frac {\sqrt{3}} {2})}=\frac {-1} {2+\sqrt {3}}=-2+\sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="299" height="47" />. The answer is: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/e/8bee0e46406290910ca0a630148d26fb1c7a5f17.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf{(D)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="44" height="28" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/9/079b46a82b303d029cb546b6a9f075efb37a9d72.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=b-c$" width="102" height="12" />
Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/8/bb81480621cb40261584d9e80d59e87d4694b623.png" class="latex" alt="$2b=a+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />.
</p><p>And we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/4/b144c925ed88ba51c70aa70c913e4f61efb3422e.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2-4ac=0\implies b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="213" height="15" />.
Squaring the first expression, and dividing by 4, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b8912935a41e67a340af2050252e12ff774a5227.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=\frac{a^2+2ac+c^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="145" height="39" />.
</p><p>Setting the two equations equal, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/8/c18ee7449eac9c208145fcde9bfd5577b02324dc.png" class="latex" alt="$4ac=\frac{a^2+2ac+c^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="156" height="39" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/2/c022c37dfdb73c5d048e0e17b8d580e6658c0c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\implies 16ac=a^2+2ac+c^2\implies 14ac=a^2+c^2\implies a^2-14ac+c^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="566" height="16" />.
</p><p>Dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b693026c70424da68ed4c52d3d0d35d9548ee4e.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2$" width="14" height="15" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/8/5189435aa9f50e1f58cc5fcfdebf37a911910015.png" class="latex" alt="$(\frac{a}{c})^2-14(\frac{a}{c})+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="167" height="33" />. 
</p><p>Setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/1/641627f8a7b1aa7c2aba7f0959505d58718ffee3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{c}=x,$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="33" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/2/e8204ce06b45cb8af8f048916e5230c927e8db60.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-14x+1=0.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="16" /> 
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/65426259e2b608116116985e70bcd156d5f2f116.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{a}{c}=7 \pm 4\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="137" height="33" /> We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/3/88357334572fff6f341375240c9b999b594102e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d3785d39dfc1391e721eb871b864a4909434b38.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{c}=7+4\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="33" />.
</p><p>By Vieta's, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/e/d9e18669c9709731fb76d563e91c2573873f78ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{c}{a}=r^2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="33" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> equals the double root of the quadratic.
So, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/4/fa405f376bc0895f840bd6195a38f235151fa094.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{c}{a}=\frac{1}{7 + 4\sqrt3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="105" height="41" />. After rationalizing the denominator, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/5/475f18b3927c4c2cba3656304b4df019413a6034.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{c}{a} = 7- 4\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="33" />.
</p><p>Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/4/374aae608a84e620700f504c5be876cde19fe394.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2=7-4\sqrt3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="105" height="18" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/6/28626cf09d059e6de8f99d5c2d8f7b00ae332278.png" class="latex" alt="$-2+\sqrt3=\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="121" height="24" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>The unique root is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/5/36588f5e8fdfe7c968c3359574a711cd30c7f488.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac {b} {2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="37" /> as discriminant has to be 0.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/b/cdbf8978ffce9c3dad00a7b028ddd97f36326894.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=b-c \implies c=2b-a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="234" height="16" />.
</p><p>Also discriminant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/e/40e0ad91ae7e8016dda8f1a0ef856bc2d2cfb570.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2-4ac=0 \implies b^2=4ac$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="215" height="18" />. Plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/7/dd7de479325b06262e1b0fc49e35583cac8709df.png" class="latex" alt="$c=2b-a$" width="80" height="12" /> from above, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/e/d4e16eacfa672a188c24ec6c36c50aa369736075.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2=4a(2b-a) \implies b^2-8ab+4a^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="318" height="21" />. Divide both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/9698c992334be77221be8c8c819909eca34fde9b.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2$" width="15" height="15" />,  we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/6/ea6e3887c932bc2b31b5efe88fbc7be556b048de.png" class="latex" alt="$(\frac{b}{a})^2-8(\frac{b}{a})+4=0$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="161" height="39" />.
</p><p>Solve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/2/032cb0c5932179b6ceedd7baa2a7b33391437704.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="37" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/42453f37367b31cac683d1a45f15597dafb6964a.png" class="latex" alt="$4\pm2\sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="66" height="21" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/7/18786649350a0a7e257fb57a375eb1093d51a225.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/0/520641ba09e9422e34b7a1fdbf2c7829e4a85ab2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{a}=4-2\sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="104" height="39" />. 
</p><p>Therefore the unique root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/3/b03dab7e801abd548a7be20dc71faed21c292c15.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{b}{2a} = -2+\sqrt3=\boxed{D}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="184" height="39" />.
</p>
