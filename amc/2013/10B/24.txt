<p>A positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <i>nice</i> if there is a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> with exactly four positive divisors (including <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />) such that the sum of the four divisors is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. How many numbers in the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/2/a12a0641f7f1101d86c6d6f149626c91f0e0ec99.png" class="latex" alt="$\{ 2010,2011,2012,\dotsc,2019 \}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="218" height="18" /> are nice?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/c/d2c2682118562721ddf0cd6419973b673dc4af92.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>A positive integer with only four positive divisors has its prime factorization in the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e789167f554110898d8a401bfaaf447ce2b65c.png" class="latex" alt="$a \cdot b$" width="30" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are both prime positive integers or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/1/a913c6d975b086fcae264ef46a8906bd203204a9.png" class="latex" alt="$c^3$" width="14" height="15" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is a prime. One can easily deduce that none of the numbers are even near a cube so the second case is not possible. We now look at the case of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e789167f554110898d8a401bfaaf447ce2b65c.png" class="latex" alt="$a \cdot b$" width="30" height="12" />. The four factors of this number would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" />.  The sum of these would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/1/1c1bcd280bdc51abcdc13b6802ea0b4c14845143.png" class="latex" alt="$ab+a+b+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="14" />, which can be factored into the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae1ab5a9e4812b64bf188877c0a46d893f2dbac5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)(b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" />. Easily we can see that now we can take cases again. 
</p><p>Case 1: Either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is 2. 
</p><p>If this is true then we have to have that one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2bddf56169e3d800f7a71d5d35d3196b35e3c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a4de8b7e90f04b8788906a6749731784b182ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="18" /> is odd and that one is 3. The other is still even.  So we have that in this case the only numbers that work are even multiples of 3 which are 2010 and 2016. So we just have to check if either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/2/4b20a264a9794563d9ffdf3181785ba9e959024f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2016}{3} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />  or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/d/23d37abe0aed9cc2fd3d3a7db3f8893728233bf7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2010}{3} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" /> is a prime. We see that in this case none of them work.
</p><p>Case 2: Both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are odd primes. 
</p><p>This implies that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2bddf56169e3d800f7a71d5d35d3196b35e3c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a4de8b7e90f04b8788906a6749731784b182ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="18" /> are even which implies that in this case the number must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. This leaves only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/a/69add693327f2808bf0e2c482176ecf5c7283e8f.png" class="latex" alt="$2012={4}\cdot{503}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> so either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2bddf56169e3d800f7a71d5d35d3196b35e3c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a4de8b7e90f04b8788906a6749731784b182ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="18" /> both have a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or one has a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. If it was the first case, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> will equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.  That means that either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2bddf56169e3d800f7a71d5d35d3196b35e3c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a4de8b7e90f04b8788906a6749731784b182ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="18" /> has a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.  That means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/6/f563574744d6a2d6761415d8edfe3b33182be38d.png" class="latex" alt="$502$" width="26" height="12" /> which isn't a prime, so 2012 does not work.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/5/005532708ef80e2573eeb54119290f28edc54f69.png" class="latex" alt="$2016 = 4 \cdot 504$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="110" height="13" /> so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/c/bacbb211891184187764245df4d6e05fc7c75d37.png" class="latex" alt="$(503 + 1)(3 + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />. 503 and 3 are both odd primes, so 2016 is a solution. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/b/97b74ee8cccf0c3cfc83d191b525a0059233f3d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shortcut">Shortcut</span></h3>
<p>After deducing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> and case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is impossible, and since there is no option for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is obviously a solution and the answer is (A) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>-mathboy282
</p>
