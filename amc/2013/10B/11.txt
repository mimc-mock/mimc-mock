<p>Real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> satisfy the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/0/3c0e67c8e69737d0c44d4ba87e832a10fca55b96.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=10x-6y-34$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="191" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/a/48a8be93c14100adb30eb5a36dac882a2e08f4ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 1 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 2 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 3 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 6 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If we move every term dependent on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to the LHS, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a93e1fa56f2bdaf8c68e2a1e48517fc77a1bc39e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 10x + y^2 + 6y = -34$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="205" height="18" />. Adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/3/d037f1aa313d30fe7ade693f1b672ed2a68880fa.png" class="latex" alt="$34$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> to both sides, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aaeb166b06d69a3ccef8d4ef0e1b47436592c486.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 10x + y^2 + 6y + 34 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="222" height="18" />. We can split the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/3/d037f1aa313d30fe7ade693f1b672ed2a68880fa.png" class="latex" alt="$34$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/a/86aec043f217f5be9b05e9e868858daa650afdf2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 5)^2 + (y + 3)^2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="19" />. Notice this is a circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, which only contains one point. So, the only point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/b/e0b7ad42dd93799f2e806a8edc40fedc21c01bd9.png" class="latex" alt="$(5, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, so the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b6916f4178fea248636a04383391a117f9abbe74.png" class="latex" alt="$5 + (-3) = 2 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="191" height="28" />. ~ asdf334
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If we move every term including <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to the LHS, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/1/dd18bde5b66627a6df1efd5dc46ed9e3700c99e4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 - 10x + y^2 + 6y = -34.\]" width="207" height="18" />  We can complete the square to find that this equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/a/b5a35b78809aaa8554858d204ba654a09ff65f46.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x - 5)^2 + (y + 3)^2 = 0.\]" width="183" height="19" />  Since the square of any real number is nonnegative, we know that the sum is greater than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />.  Equality holds when the value inside the parhentheses is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />.  We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/5/ac52edccd81507a34589475cb65faa21d649d07f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x,y) = (5,-3)\]" width="117" height="18" /> and the sum we are looking for is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/7/7973ca732d2cb205e20ea299a2f6bfd784a9cd2a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5+(-3)=2 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(B)}}.\]" width="194" height="28" />  - Honestly
</p>
