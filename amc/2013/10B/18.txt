<p>The number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> has the property that its units digit is the sum of its other digits, that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/2/dc2a5cd5b5ce932ba71c524f3ebf54cd8b785007.png" class="latex" alt="$2+0+1=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="104" height="14" />. How many integers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> but greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> have this property?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f19b516f5d3328b0fd5c67a9b6b3e7e6d5bfc974.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 33\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 34\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 45\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 46\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 58$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.1">Solution 1.1</span></h2>
<p>We take cases on the thousands digit, which must be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />:
If the number is of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/e/fcec8c91414cd405852592c7c4c65202055210d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{1bcd},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="19" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/3/b935c15651d822aa87116a2c254e1a1ff73de233.png" class="latex" alt="$b, c, d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> are digits, then we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/5/20583680fac40f5b38126addfdf83d513b3a5e87.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 1 + b + c.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="106" height="14" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/d/58d93a5c29f9535017640d26d8a5e438147e6128.png" class="latex" alt="$d \le 9,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="46" height="16" /> we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/d/3edac19473f6bdecf3e9ed67b369e20fcde28ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$b + c \le 9 - 1 = 8.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="15" /> By casework on the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we find that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd86c4d5032615a7343ca69e72e7b79eab0ae019.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 2 + \dots + 9 = 45$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="157" height="14" /> possible pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/348dfab6e448efb00583951e03ab7ac6087c4a86.png" class="latex" alt="$(b, c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, and each pair uniquely determines the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> numbers with the given property.
</p><p>If the number is of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/881e00f0e0287453323bfae18a06b78ea0b94f00.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{2bcd},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="19" /> then it must be one of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/b/fdb0290bba3d9c6bc61462ec4136b9f127c72eff.png" class="latex" alt="$2000, 2001, \dots, 2012.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="160" height="16" /> Checking all these numbers, we find that only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e470b7ce7a2f4d0c1ae064d2874ca377693897.png" class="latex" alt="$2002$" width="35" height="12" /> has the given property.
Therefore, the number of numbers with the property is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71dfef644521c6138b9979342d4fe426d66fc0e9.png" class="latex" alt="$45 + 1 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}46}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="135" height="30" /> .
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.2">Solution 1.2</span></h2>
<p>This solution picks up from finding that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39f11b5550862f7ddd8cfa0ffdabd523171f5c22.png" class="latex" alt="$b + c \le 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="17" /> in solution 1.1. Instead of using casework to find all possible pairs, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/348dfab6e448efb00583951e03ab7ac6087c4a86.png" class="latex" alt="$(b, c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, let's introduce a dummy variable, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. Let us now have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac3931e4f39930ac1b95cfa79b9c75cd18a92fd3.png" class="latex" alt="$b + c + z = 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="105" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/4/c643ae9cc5a7e77dc2a727cc4502e322e482a734.png" class="latex" alt="$b, c, z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> are all nonnegative. 
</p><p>We may now use stars and bars to distribute units between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/9/8c9b1452aab72a3897a7921688449e11bf3dff28.png" class="latex" alt="$b, c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="24" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. Any units that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is given will essentially be discarded - this is how we get the 'less than' in the 'less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />' relation we found earlier.
</p><p>Using two dividers, we find that the number of distributions is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/362633fac4ce5f9319e85b16bca5886ccc94c26c.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{10}{2},$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="53" height="55" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. We proceed from here as above.
</p>
