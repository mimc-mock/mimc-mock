<p>Jo and Blair take turns counting from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to one more than the last number said by the other person. Jo starts by saying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b19c547299a7e2865e9f5e86cac562ad0dd19a9.png" class="latex" alt="$``1&quot;$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="12" />, so Blair follows by saying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe96d07d63c88bdc11d1777a9dd6a241f9a10a30.png" class="latex" alt="$``1, 2&quot;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />. Jo then says <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d4dea176b4993fa4154e7968183c2ee4b577206.png" class="latex" alt="$``1, 2, 3&quot;$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" />, and so on. What is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/241bf43d042307108e47540e16589e6d05898591.png" class="latex" alt="$53^{\text{rd}}$" width="30" height="15" /> number said?<br />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/b/0bb4b4403d47a7a85b5938ce917dbffae014e080.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We notice that the number of numbers said is incremented by one each time; that is, Jo says one number, then Blair says two numbers, then Jo says three numbers, etc.  Thus, after nine "turns", <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/0/430146f579a6c81774e8b4b6c2822ca27e955559.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8+9=45$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="301" height="14" /> numbers have been said.  In the tenth turn, the eighth number will be the 53rd number said, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/a/9fa75655386d600f3bc2d0ee970bad628331ad74.png" class="latex" alt="$53-45=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />.  Since we are starting from 1 every turn, the 53rd number said will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/129c1c4cbf3d35dbd549e42c49762617b1b73022.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Faster_Solution">Faster Solution</span></h2>
<p>We notice that the number of numbers said is incremented by one each time; that is, Jo says one number, then Blair says two numbers, then Jo says three numbers, etc.  We notice that the number of numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/0/d00ef46bab9ecf296a4156b9c6f4bc2146fb85be.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 2 + 3 + 4 ...$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="120" height="15" /> every time we finish a "turn" we notice the sum of these would be the largest number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64f8070867b03b0dc224db7e741135e07f4502c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" /> under 53, we can easily see that if we double this it's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/1/69109cf486a851d765ce9cf112332a30939905e8.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2 + n \simeq 106$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="105" height="18" />, and we immediately note that 10 is too high, but 9 is perfect, meaning that at 9, 45 numbers have been said so far, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/b/05bbab64c4f34dc54835b7649e762bad908fcf01.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9(9+1)}{2} = 45$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="112" height="40" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0dcbe535538af0801a34e009ab02a0dcf7480a.png" class="latex" alt="$53 - 45 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="139" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b8945a2aaa3228362b3976be341ad72f51f56efe.png" class="latex" alt="$T(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> denote the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th triangle number. Then, observe that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b8945a2aaa3228362b3976be341ad72f51f56efe.png" class="latex" alt="$T(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />th number said is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. It follows that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79030abeb688469b5ffc2e1c4e37fee225a529e.png" class="latex" alt="$55$" width="17" height="12" />th number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> (as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/f/b7f8b19c35fd194978511bfa618a8521b6b9b021.png" class="latex" alt="$55 = T(10)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="90" height="20" />). Thus, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba318f7385187c693cb1859d9b4b1b587e649d9.png" class="latex" alt="$53$" width="17" height="12" />rd number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/2/2326a4ec693fc25d97ef7a8a229b1c63f324a0a9.png" class="latex" alt="$10 - 2 = 8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="15" />, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/f/6bfc0829f3f29bbf35d0a861644e00cfef0f50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Mathavi
</p>
