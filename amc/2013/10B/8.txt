<p>Ray's car averages <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles per gallon of gasoline, and Tom's car averages <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles per gallon of gasoline. Ray and Tom each drive the same number of miles. What is the cars' combined rate of miles per gallon of gasoline?<br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/5/795ce54846fd1ff7d694bc0ee35bbf722724a0f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 16 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 25 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 40$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let Ray and Tom drive 40 miles. Ray's car would require <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/5/a450581e7aa1bed81fa2be62b0bacbd19f786887.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{40}=1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="54" height="38" /> gallon of gas and Tom's car would require <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/6/1169fc1bcdf2e20647151102d37a13add62c31e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{10}=4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="55" height="38" /> gallons of gas. They would have driven a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/24496421d4ebd59162987f40e0eb30928df6d9ab.png" class="latex" alt="$40+40=80$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> miles, on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/a/84af484a5bcdd0f7963c3bb46e9d70c663d0ffde.png" class="latex" alt="$1+4=5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> gallons of gas, for a combined rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/c/5bc1ca62d35ffc9ee8746cebf886479c2cfac535.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{80}{5}=$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="40" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b1b569fe813886086234b950813b0c4cd76d367.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Taking the harmonic mean of the two rates, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/6/9b6db99b489317a9225179391b994358e160d481.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{40^{-1} + 10^{-1}}{2}\right)^{-1} = \frac{2}{\frac{1}{40}+\frac{1}{10}} = \frac{2}{\frac{5}{40}} = \frac{2}{\frac{1}{8}} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }16}.\]" width="400" height="48" />
</p><p>-Solution by Joeya
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let the number of miles that Ray and Tom each drive be denoted by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. Thus the number of gallons Ray's car uses can be represented by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c582e2fbcf0e26dc1d605aef42e22a53ef047dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m}{40}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="24" height="34" /> and the number of gallons that Tom's car uses can likewise be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/5/8a573d0043231081fc044b5aa8a5ba0ab667eef4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="33" />. Thus the total amount of gallons used by both cars can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2f699c5fe582f969be79fe639322bbe2962ccc1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m}{40} + \frac{m}{10} = \frac{m}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="113" height="34" />. The total distance that both drive is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93d829d38f1f73cd97c8fd926e636e968d0c158.png" class="latex" alt="$2m$" width="24" height="12" /> so the total miles per gallon can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/0/8f046e0dd35330708da32e72413d0048ac8b42c2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2m}{\frac{m}{8}} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="119" height="45" />
</p><p>~AnkitAMC
</p>
