<p>The graph of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is shown below. How many solutions does the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f282851d0f4f858d95740aaa34eecf5804f034d9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(f(x))=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" /> have? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/e/d8ea42d74287fb95665640e991c92aa37ba2c70f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)+linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair P1=(-7,-4), P2=(-2,6), P3=(0,0), P4=(1,6), P5=(5,-6); real[] xticks={-7,-6,-5,-4,-3,-2,-1,1,2,3,4,5,6}; real[] yticks={-6,-5,-4,-3,-2,-1,1,2,3,4,5,6};  draw(P1--P2--P3--P4--P5);  dot(&quot;(-7, -4)&quot;,P1); dot(&quot;(-2, 6)&quot;,P2,LeftSide); dot(&quot;(1, 6)&quot;,P4); dot(&quot;(5, -6)&quot;,P5);  xaxis(&quot;$x$&quot;,-7.5,7,Ticks(xticks),EndArrow(6)); yaxis(&quot;$y$&quot;,-6.5,7,Ticks(yticks),EndArrow(6)); [/asy]" width="335" height="305" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/d/87d2673f4c0ee1e1541c3746b260c7dfe13978c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }2 \qquad \text{(B) }4 \qquad \text{(C) }5 \qquad \text{(D) }6 \qquad \text{(E) }7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>First of all, note that the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/117ad60483f9bf8b2eab1bbe25006ecd064baa09.png" class="latex" alt="$f(t)=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="64" height="18" /> has two solutions: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec99c15b19905543282aa82b91d7b36b930654c.png" class="latex" alt="$t=-2$" width="53" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/2/cd2a4ec9cf64d68580d5ef68252432965551b464.png" class="latex" alt="$t=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Given an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c6bf3c17114c63ae009e88d7162670bee3f7489.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=t$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" />. Obviously, to have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f282851d0f4f858d95740aaa34eecf5804f034d9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(f(x))=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />, we need to have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/117ad60483f9bf8b2eab1bbe25006ecd064baa09.png" class="latex" alt="$f(t)=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="64" height="18" />, and we already know when that happens. In other words, the solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f282851d0f4f858d95740aaa34eecf5804f034d9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(f(x))=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" /> are precisely the solutions to (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/b/66b52d07828a18008054e2764195487583559e7b.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=-2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/4/224ae5ff2e22575ba5a41f26a68c2c5fdc0d502d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />).
</p><p>Without actually computing the exact values, it is obvious from the graph that the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/b/66b52d07828a18008054e2764195487583559e7b.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=-2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" /> has two and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/4/224ae5ff2e22575ba5a41f26a68c2c5fdc0d502d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> has four different solutions, giving us a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/b/bdb09162ef11ce04fc1d54cb6b03464c134785b9.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4=\boxed{(D)6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="115" height="29" /> solutions.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/3/653468fb472ce591f7429c348a1f2b68deca3c12.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)+linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair P1=(-7,-4), P2=(-2,6), P3=(0,0), P4=(1,6), P5=(5,-6); real[] xticks={-7,-6,-5,-4,-3,-2,-1,1,2,3,4,5,6}; real[] yticks={-6,-5,-4,-3,-2,-1,1,2,3,4,5,6};  path graph = P1--P2--P3--P4--P5; path line1 = (-7,1)--(6,1); path line2 = (-7,-2)--(6,-2);  draw(graph); draw(line1, red); draw(line2, red);  dot(&quot;(-7, -4)&quot;,P1); dot(&quot;(-2, 6)&quot;,P2,LeftSide); dot(&quot;(1, 6)&quot;,P4); dot(&quot;(5, -6)&quot;,P5); dot(intersectionpoints(graph,line1),red); dot(intersectionpoints(graph,line2),red);  xaxis(&quot;$x$&quot;,-7.5,7,Ticks(xticks),EndArrow(6)); yaxis(&quot;$y$&quot;,-6.5,7,Ticks(yticks),EndArrow(6)); [/asy]" width="335" height="305" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
