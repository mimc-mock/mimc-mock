<p>Betsy designed a flag using blue triangles, small white squares, and a red center square, as shown. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> be the total area of the blue triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10cb764f88509fb1c8012366993fdbee98f31bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$W$" width="19" height="12" /> the total area of the white squares, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> the area of the red square. Which of the following is correct?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/b/a0b90bdab50e7b1984850bd644f9291ddc2de34e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); fill((-4,-4)--(-4,4)--(4,4)--(4,-4)--cycle,blue); fill((-2,-2)--(-2,2)--(2,2)--(2,-2)--cycle,red); path onewhite=(-3,3)--(-2,4)--(-1,3)--(-2,2)--(-3,3)--(-1,3)--(0,4)--(1,3)--(0,2)--(-1,3)--(1,3)--(2,4)--(3,3)--(2,2)--(1,3)--cycle; path divider=(-2,2)--(-3,3)--cycle; fill(onewhite,white); fill(rotate(90)*onewhite,white); fill(rotate(180)*onewhite,white); fill(rotate(270)*onewhite,white); [/asy]" width="115" height="115" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/f/06f35c64faf66283af11573567cc58b0ea474786.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ B = W \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ W = P \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ B = P \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 3B = 2P \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 2P = W$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="614" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The blue that's touching the center red square makes up 8 triangles, or 4 squares. Each of the corners is 1 square and each of the edges is 1, totaling 12 squares. There are 12 white squares, thus we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/9174bb74a076500b464606638df6bbaa3035f25a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B=W\Rightarrow \text{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="124" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Cut the pattern into 16 square regions and use symmetry.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/c/e2c65e1a95d3f58215e53f1192048a8ec706c1e8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3mm); fill((-4,-4)--(-4,4)--(4,4)--(4,-4)--cycle,blue); fill((-2,-2)--(-2,2)--(2,2)--(2,-2)--cycle,red); path onewhite=(-3,3)--(-2,4)--(-1,3)--(-2,2)--(-3,3)--(-1,3)--(0,4)--(1,3)--(0,2)--(-1,3)--(1,3)--(2,4)--(3,3)--(2,2)--(1,3)--cycle; path divider=(-2,2)--(-3,3)--cycle; fill(onewhite,white); fill(rotate(90)*onewhite,white); fill(rotate(180)*onewhite,white); fill(rotate(270)*onewhite,white); draw((-4,-2)--(4,-2),black); draw((-4,0)--(4,0),black); draw((-4,2)--(4,2),black); draw((-2,-4)--(-2,4),black); draw((0,-4)--(0,4),black); draw((2,-4)--(2,4),black); [/asy]" width="115" height="115" />
</p><p>This clearly shows <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/6/376c13ff851ef50f664da4e71a575660004a270c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }B=W}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="107" height="30" />.
</p>
