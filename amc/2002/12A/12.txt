<p>Both roots of the quadratic equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/e/3de54feaaf5afe2e45ca84e0678b12f2e54e2789.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 63x + k = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="16" /> are prime numbers. The number of possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/9/0e9130bb8b3b8e70f07c286c75f04cc45e959efa.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 0 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 1 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 2 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 4 \qquad \text{(E) more than 4}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="410" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Consider a general quadratic with the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and the roots being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. It can be factored as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a5c1d8ab857f150325f70d4a8fbe1e845c3b89a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-r)(x-s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" /> which is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/8/c08489137c32459203902bc10e7ed731a75164d1.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-(r+s)x+rs$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="19" />. Thus, the sum of the roots is the negative of the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and the product is the constant term. (In general, this leads to <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_Formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta&#39;s Formulas">Vieta's Formulas</a>).
</p><p>We now have that the sum of the two roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/8/848ec5b5f695afbacb864efead154761481780c0.png" class="latex" alt="$63$" width="17" height="12" /> while the product is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. Since both roots are primes, one must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, otherwise the sum would be even. That means the other root is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/6/866b44935f69a5b800547127fe13210bb1e36fbc.png" class="latex" alt="$61$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and the product must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/8/7a8948c71337e6ef8aac086dd7444c757c379b53.png" class="latex" alt="$122$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />. Hence, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/4/2b47d1aa4d55970eca58860fc6504338a692af94.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(B)}\ 1 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="51" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the quadratic be factored as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a5c1d8ab857f150325f70d4a8fbe1e845c3b89a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-r)(x-s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e6497fa29c1922e788054cdd9ac0c142f2affd87.png" class="latex" alt="$-r-s=-63$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="112" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/225f6874d80269f851e7b0a993d481812ab203cb.png" class="latex" alt="$rs=k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="53" height="14" />. We know that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> are prime, and all primes but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> are odd. The sum of two odd numbers is also always even, but in this case the sum of two primes is odd, which means that one of the primes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. This leaves only one possible value for the other root and one possible value for their product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>By Vieta's you have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f78cca460ad1c36974bce73c317a853717e1571e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+b=63\]" width="81" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7c29e60814388974641c8bc4c3abc8ff4561422.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab=k\]" width="50" height="12" />
Since we know odd + even = odd, we must have either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and the other equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efa46b2cad04f03d823193655d77f9fb809d077.png" class="latex" alt="$63-2=61.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="99" height="15" /> Both of these are prime so they satisfy the restraints. Thus there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/3547351abf03ab0b6cbda7b0b905b2e4f6220e1e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" /> solution.
</p>
