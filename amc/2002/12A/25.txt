<p>The nonzero coefficients of a polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> with real coefficients are all replaced by their mean to form a polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" />. Which of the following could be a graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2f8a88218090325aa39267786c0e541f04f06f2.png" class="latex" alt="$y = P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/d/b0d204c88bde258b322e885671618465be00f6bf.png" class="latex" alt="$y = Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> over the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/7/ed7c97c198b9fbfd98ef9176439a931258d0a08a.png" class="latex" alt="$-4\leq x \leq 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="91" height="14" />?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2002AMC12A25.png" class="image"><img alt="2002AMC12A25.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//4/40/2002AMC12A25.png" width="648" height="286" /></a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The sum of the coefficients of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> and of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> will be equal, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/b/21b506d2a70002afcfe97badb6bbf174540b3692.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1) = Q(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />. The only answer choice with an intersection between the two graphs at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/3/34310f2f36ed6d724838edb08788ee62acb33386.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> is <b>(B)</b>. (The polynomials in the graph are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17af7b8ca670024306e8b288931ca209c884344.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x) = 2x^4-3x^2-3x-4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="212" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/b/2ebc124b1f6220ab57858270ebe7b8658e737ef1.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x) = -2x^4-2x^2-2x-2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="226" height="19" />.)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know every coefficient is equal, so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/a/f0ae3ff6573749ac6fc327fbe3501e557836b832.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^n + ... + ax + a = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="173" height="14" /> which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/f/82f0d70c26bed163f145747e45bdcce074b008dc.png" class="latex" alt="$x^n + ... + x + 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="153" height="14" />. We see apparently that x cannot be positive, for it would yield a number greater than zero for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9dfe7c7a0b887097fa2227a230fc915ba2e5477.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />. We look at the zeros of the answer choices. A, C, D, and E have a positive zero, which eliminates them. B is the answer.
</p>
