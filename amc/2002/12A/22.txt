<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is a right triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0728bebb477a2636300c56851b6a6f9e382c1b83.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB$" width="55" height="13" /> as its right angle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/d/44d2515ad214ae211c32742bce44b0b655a53380.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle ABC = 60^\circ$" width="119" height="13" /> , and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/b/b6b5b6b078188523fb69a22aff2fbd2b3df42b14.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> be randomly chosen inside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> , and extend <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4bb1db3d066da48df7576e30ef3831ec64cecc7.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BP}$" width="29" height="15" /> to meet <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. What is the probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5bafeabcb6375a0814c5618872e5c4d682b8bca3.png" class="latex" alt="$BD &gt; 5\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="18" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/1/031229b02b99c3f8d5235b9e281cbab7abe4250b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import math;  unitsize(4mm);  defaultpen(fontsize(8pt)+linewidth(0.7));  dotfactor=4;   pair A=(10,0);  pair C=(0,0);  pair B=(0,10.0/sqrt(3));  pair P=(2,2);  pair D=extension(A,C,B,P);   draw(A--C--B--cycle);  draw(B--D);  dot(P);  label(&quot;A&quot;,A,S);  label(&quot;D&quot;,D,S);  label(&quot;C&quot;,C,S);  label(&quot;P&quot;,P,NE);  label(&quot;B&quot;,B,N);[/asy]" width="205" height="142" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/e/33e1947d2b438ac853db86421dcbf072b2790ca5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{2-\sqrt2}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{3}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{3-\sqrt3}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1}{2}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{5-\sqrt5}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="507" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e156331c13aae7e86644da6fa391b4389b826d9.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=5$" width="61" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/4/8f44cae473c077dc48b426077f305104eeeee650.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=5\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />. Choose a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/3545551b3d62c4e23d5df7352a55602d0aa9adcd.png" class="latex" alt="$P&#39;$" width="17" height="14" /> and get a corresponding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/3/b239e47c75fa1adfb0e7d7fb6bf219fcc53f86d9.png" class="latex" alt="$D&#39;$" width="19" height="14" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/1/15179a7947b82f8946e49364fdb3ede404b7daf7.png" class="latex" alt="$BD&#39;= 5\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/4/76452d71626e286103ed983ca11680436d77a8aa.png" class="latex" alt="$CD&#39;=5$" width="67" height="14" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5bafeabcb6375a0814c5618872e5c4d682b8bca3.png" class="latex" alt="$BD &gt; 5\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="18" /> we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/1/dd1bbdd39a2d70026c1b8ae80f8b9b4b1d6edd19.png" class="latex" alt="$CD&gt;5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, creating an isosceles right triangle with hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651f5b345fe4324b59ecd5f0e5e079dec2ccd2ac.png" class="latex" alt="$5\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> . Thus the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> may only lie in the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f3deb39b6805de0f0dadfa9ababfe8cc26415f.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD&#39;$" width="47" height="14" />. The probability of it doing so is the ratio of areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f3deb39b6805de0f0dadfa9ababfe8cc26415f.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD&#39;$" width="47" height="14" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />, or equivalently, the ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad8019d0fb176ff1ade21d8a9e6d6aa4a1f9563a.png" class="latex" alt="$AD&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> because the triangles have identical altitudes when taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad8019d0fb176ff1ade21d8a9e6d6aa4a1f9563a.png" class="latex" alt="$AD&#39;$" width="32" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> as bases. This ratio is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/6/d460381f29fb0f19215b2afd0662d5c71a319b2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AC-CD&#39;}{AC}=1-\frac{CD&#39;}{AC}=1-\frac{5}{5\sqrt{3}}=1-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}= \frac{3-\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="441" height="45" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f58d2efaaa17d28cec9dd927d78708a63b43e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
