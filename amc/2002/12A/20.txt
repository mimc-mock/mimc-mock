<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are digits, not both nine and not both zero, and the repeating decimal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/91492cd23008d5e1eab0dedb2a0035b55193a856.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{ab}$" width="31" height="16" /> is expressed as a fraction in lowest terms. How many different denominators are possible?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/d/13d36a98f9649818c07052b127e6e13d6472f8ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) }3 \qquad \text{(B) }4 \qquad \text{(C) }5 \qquad \text{(D) }8 \qquad \text{(E) }9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The repeating decimal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/91492cd23008d5e1eab0dedb2a0035b55193a856.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{ab}$" width="31" height="16" /> is equal to 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e79d4e1c4a5a5a280b24504155a203de95908f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{10a+b}{100} + \frac{10a+b}{10000} + \cdots = (10a+b)\cdot\left(\frac 1{10^2} + \frac 1{10^4} + \cdots \right) =  (10a+b) \cdot \frac 1{99} = \frac{10a+b}{99}\]" width="618" height="43" />
</p><p>When expressed in lowest terms, the denominator of this fraction will always be a divisor of the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/3/df35c18943ca27bd932076ba6b73348e6ded2a67.png" class="latex" alt="$99 = 3\cdot 3\cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />. This gives us the possibilities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7a3d0f97507392052a1063860502f74e50c703e.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,3,9,11,33,99\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are not both nine and not both zero, the denominator <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> can not be achieved, leaving us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/9/9f99d7a86080f615a9bdd5c63cfaa86d950157ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(C)5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="49" height="29" /> possible denominators.
</p><p>(The other ones are achieved e.g. for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6f3bccae16d5e31266370fced24edc41538d5da.png" class="latex" alt="$33$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, respectively.)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Another way to convert the decimal into a fraction (simplifying, I guess?). We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/0/8707e53b3d4768c851c5a79f6de7e4e1c9b50ff5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[100(0.\overline{ab}) = ab.\overline{ab}\]" width="135" height="20" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/c/c4c34df03e28538a13094fec999535943fe7c461.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[99(0.\overline{ab}) = 100(0.\overline{ab}) - 0.\overline{ab} = ab.\overline{ab} - 0.\overline{ab} = ab\]" width="374" height="20" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0dba88361c68a63ea9cfe47d53c29c700459dd47.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0.\overline{ab} = \frac{ab}{99}\]" width="75" height="37" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3834f0cef2a1f4d8524aa5bc8e3faca298550862.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> are digits. Continuing in the same way by looking at the factors of 99, we have 5 different possibilities for the denomenator. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dbbac62a58811f44fda1df541fe2d2b7f43bc64.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="29" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
~ edit by SpeedCuber7
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59e1a6002570c1f53da9534fcd01708bc14a8c19.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{99}=0.\overline{01}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5af9dc5fd8e9a3d6b9f9f85d260e2508a4d49d28.png" class="latex" alt="$0.\overline{ab} = \frac{ab}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />. From here, we wish to find the number of factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. However, notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is not a possible denominator, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/a/caacd4b605b68264753d35ef2795a3e10fa28cb6.png" class="latex" alt="$6-1=\boxed{5}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="85" height="23" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f1406b65f9f2e6a365b7356da731780b42cceb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\]" />
~AopsUser101
</p>
