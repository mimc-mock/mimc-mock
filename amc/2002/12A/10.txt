<p>Sarah places four ounces of coffee into an eight-ounce cup and four ounces of cream into a second cup of the same size. She then pours half the coffee from the first cup to the second and, after stirring thoroughly, pours half the liquid in the second cup back to the first. What fraction of the liquid in the first cup is now cream?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/3118bc0476f8f5ece0736498708ed4a8743be59a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{1}{4}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac13\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac38\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac25\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="361" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We will simulate the process in steps.
</p><p>In the beginning, we have:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ounces of coffee in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ounces of cream in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /></li></ul>
<p>In the first step we pour <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70ab628b092245509ad2b3603ae1da3748f81191.png" class="latex" alt="$4/2=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> ounces of coffee from cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, getting:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ounces of coffee in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ounces of coffee and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ounces of cream in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /></li></ul>
<p>In the second step we pour <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/f/04f002914bff24e34305b970216e39271d72ccd9.png" class="latex" alt="$2/2=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" /> ounce of coffee and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70ab628b092245509ad2b3603ae1da3748f81191.png" class="latex" alt="$4/2=2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> ounces of cream from cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, getting:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/0/c40d25bf3b35c55768d241a0121ad6dc12cd2865.png" class="latex" alt="$2+1=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> ounces of coffee and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b2b528b36956c0d22e2feb422c3b18c4efd67.png" class="latex" alt="$0+2=2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> ounces of cream in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /></li>
<li>the rest in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /></li></ul>
<p>Hence at the end we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/d/efd511c1545dc62444789b5e1f7ec0c0fec0b442.png" class="latex" alt="$3+2=5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> ounces of liquid in cup <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and out of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ounces is cream. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3ddab0b7a397f83b7934adad25265e44ad2ff0f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D) } \frac 25}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="56" height="48" />.
</p>
