<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a9874ea56cf8fec1bd246793224fbcfaad17ab3f.png" class="latex" alt="$A,B,C$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> lie on a line, in that order, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9a427387ff6212e24b2c3bf3ce692b2e62d50a7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = CD$" width="82" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/f/d5fb59fff6b6968adfa1e4b3d06a609a2a8af375.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is not on the line, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/2/102f733a99ce9d33fabdd5e5ba7ad1187a5565bd.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = CE = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="124" height="13" />. The perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/1/18167d788fd698322ba516cf8bfdba2c931fa17d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AED$" width="59" height="13" /> is twice the perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97dd577dd14751b09771a1c83d880f5142b86181.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEC$" width="59" height="13" />. Find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/0/970138dce96b3f6aea2cf911e99c04d13efd7c70.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 15/2 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 8 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 17/2 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 9 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 19/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="411" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>First, we draw an altitude to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />. Let it intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97dd577dd14751b09771a1c83d880f5142b86181.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEC$" width="59" height="13" /> is isosceles, we immediately get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/2/6d2f7d331088511a83b2c800453f3bd86ee9a0b6.png" class="latex" alt="$MB=MC=6$" width="125" height="12" />, so the altitude is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. Now, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/6/716b45f37eb66b0c165d0afcc2b8f2b596323c5e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=CD=x$" width="116" height="13" />. Using the Pythagorean Theorem on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e15c6bc4d2a7e91a9fb5b77871d857aec0efd65.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EMA$" width="64" height="13" />, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/677fcc00cebc0f29e58a5829993c6e4ff71e2fba.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=\sqrt{x^2+12x+100}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="186" height="18" />. From symmetry, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/f/65f56db1894644727c2ccb738ec3ecb95aa385a2.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=\sqrt{x^2+12x+100}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="188" height="18" /> as well. Now, we use the fact that the perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/1/18167d788fd698322ba516cf8bfdba2c931fa17d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AED$" width="59" height="13" /> is twice the perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97dd577dd14751b09771a1c83d880f5142b86181.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEC$" width="59" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b79ebfcff1a86b654fef6c643e2b188609e7c04.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.25 cm);  pair A, B, C, D, E, M;  A = (0,0); B = (9,0); C = (21,0); D = (30,0); E = (15,-8); M = (15,0);  draw(A--D--E--cycle); draw(B--E); draw(M--E); draw(C--E);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, N); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, N); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, N); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, N); label(&quot;$E$&quot;, E, S); label(&quot;$M$&quot;, M, N); [/asy]" width="375" height="142" />
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/f/23f25cc6c4e5af10ac42e1f9e6fec7d0c2ddb1ac.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{x^2+12x+100}+2x+12=2(32)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="288" height="21" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/0/5806709b2f5a7417059b93c3e19e403c696b8109.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^2+12x+100}=26-x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="208" height="18" />. Squaring both sides, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a4d505eea4d1e0c93f8e6037002a2fca4a55edb.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+12x+100=676-52x+x^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="259" height="16" /> which nicely rearranges into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/e/b0e1fe06a2c576cfbe4101f4fecee249b175be90.png" class="latex" alt="$64x=576\rightarrow{x=9}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="151" height="13" />. Hence, AB is 9 so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Simpler_Solution_2">Simpler Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/f/b3f6f7cd16a47f33bebcf3fc361eeb808b630504.png" class="latex" alt="$BC.$" width="31" height="12" /> Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/2/6d2f7d331088511a83b2c800453f3bd86ee9a0b6.png" class="latex" alt="$MB=MC=6$" width="125" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97dd577dd14751b09771a1c83d880f5142b86181.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEC$" width="59" height="13" /> is isosceles. By the Pythagorean triple <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0514b5c78bcf23d88fc697a05a66250517dc5519.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,8,10)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> the altitude is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb13a1ea7b9fd6892ebe9599c3587aa68288c873.png" class="latex" alt="$8.$" width="12" height="12" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/d/39de39884ea5612d1f0520e299a01fefd9daf2f3.png" class="latex" alt="$(8,15,17)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" /> is the only primitive Pythagorean triple with leg <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a9fb4c5b23667e57f28d844ad99715f18ba3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> we test <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd49bc521eb85bea9c002a0546e75dddd2cd6ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=DE=17,AM=DM=15.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="271" height="16" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/4/ba496014cbed53522ce712d6a5d79c0c429efa16.png" class="latex" alt="$2(10+10+12)=(17+17+2\cdot 15)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="280" height="18" /> this works, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/1/5f185a7edea1ca0474f38a209b56d4827e354c23.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=15-6=\boxed{\text{(D)}\ 9}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="181" height="28" />
</p><p>~dolphin7
</p>
