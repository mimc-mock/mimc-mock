<p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is appended to a list of integers, the mean is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is appended to the enlarged list, the mean of the enlarged list is decreased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. How many integers were in the original list?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/6/c3666861b0032bb3da464d793c0af8565580ce7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 4\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 5\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 6\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 7\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the sum of the integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the number of elements in the list. Then we get the equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/7/13758eb801ff369042822ec486ab0de9f7725c44.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x+15}{y+1}=\dfrac{x}{y}+2$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="124" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/4/7345e64f0c0cc1a5177695fd32b446b901f2d3b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x+15+1}{y+1+1}=\dfrac{x+16}{y+2}=\frac{x}{y}+2-1=\frac{x}{y}+1$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="335" height="41" />. With a lot of algebra, the solution is found to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec854b60955576cc8c49aa5485d921d9bbd40853.png" class="latex" alt="$y= \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="89" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the original number of elements in the set and we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> be the original average of the terms of the original list. Then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/0/150c08478d63c3d2f5cc0de4222ea9183673d7ef.png" class="latex" alt="$mn$" width="26" height="8" /> is the sum of all the elements of the list. So we have two equations: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/b/66b21d1fbe8f4cc78d1eb0fcc195630b089938aa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[mn+15=(m+2)(n+1)=mn+m+2n+2\]" width="371" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af4fbe178484847c58081846dad4c976910bd24d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[mn+16=(m+1)(n+2)=mn+2m+n+2.\]" width="375" height="18" />Simplifying both equations and we get,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/1/5f1613bc612071685ebd46c8791690ac0b36dfb6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[13=m+2n\]" width="98" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/e/85e9e11be3059a8465cea31baf18907be815086f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[14=2m+n\]" width="99" height="13" />
Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/3/5033797fbefe9b61e4c92b8213b3ff5769967f06.png" class="latex" alt="$m=5$" width="48" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/c/bec2ab894939f03ef01baf0885254b20664a723c.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="85" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Warning: This solution will rarely ever work in any other case. However, seeing that you can so easily plug and chug in problem 25 it is funny to see this.
</p><p>Plug and chug random numbers with the answer choices, starting with the choice of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> numbers. You see that if you have 4 5s and you add 15 to the set, the resulting mean will be 7; we can verify this with math
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/a/50a7de715e5f84e8a3554c87d5e927175af30d9a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{5+5+5+5+15}{5}=7\]" width="178" height="37" />
adding in 1 to the set you result in the mean to be 6.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/4/34441527a8923a2e71f54494f79e108743910c9c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{5+5+5+5+15+1}{6}=6\]" width="209" height="37" />
Thus we conclude that 4 is the correct choice or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
Video solution by Canadamath
<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hYq8GgJ0it4">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hYq8GgJ0it4</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the sum of the numbers. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of integers, and the mean be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. We have the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/8/928cb601b7c2bc2915ab32ac3c69375d926ed90d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S}{x} = y$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="52" height="39" />. We also have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/d/0ddfc34e629990ffc92f34116a0b01b030577160.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S+15}{x+1} = y+2$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="124" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/5/745b37c026c77bb1aedf270c3bf917a363b20496.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{S+16}{x+2} = y+1$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="124" height="41" />. From the first equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/874b5af170f1b9a8adba8547f4376abdfa8c7ead.png" class="latex" alt="$S = xy$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />. We can multiply both sides by the denominators of the second and third equations to yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/08258574e25e970da5f0f4f733d574d031823184.png" class="latex" alt="$S+15 = xy+2x+y+2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/a/17a85e17eda0c6313c8f77575b74699c0b77201f.png" class="latex" alt="$S+16 = xy+x+2y+2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/4/874b5af170f1b9a8adba8547f4376abdfa8c7ead.png" class="latex" alt="$S = xy$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />, we can substitute that into the equations and subtract by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75dc3e97225dc21c7d28abdf5eeadf47aefb9bb.png" class="latex" alt="$15 = 2x+y+2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="17" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d77421c96b642fd90f646c35329a96285fce017.png" class="latex" alt="$13 = 2x+y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="17" />. We also get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e05c2160cb4085de9a95776f1e4dcb24cd79d5.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2y = 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="17" />, and adding the two equations up gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/6/93650b6da600abbf052df0db5180efe550ad3190.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+3y = 27$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="17" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/0/000974c6e9c4e72e97025d27549243cc132ef935.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y = 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="17" />. Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/0/3b0102a096450868a1e6f9f4086a9ba189f1c393.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/d/39dcb40b2af25069625238b2166afc1a94e532d6.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+y=13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="17" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea768618848c1aea8a32490c327911e8ab7243a3.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/9/889f56ce08684be3b2ab9914892de17686ae9594.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. 
</p><p>We can check that this is the correct answer by having <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47edbb95a8e4ec8f3254d552dd5c90d65ace9b87.png" class="latex" alt="$4, 9, 6,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> as our numbers. The sum is 20, and the mean is 5. Adding 15 gives that the sum is 35, and the number of integers is 5, so the mean is 2, an increase of 2. Adding 1 to the integers gives that the sum is 36, and the number of numbers is 6, for a mean of 6, a decrease of 1.
</p><p>We can also prove that since the sum is 20, the number of integers is 4, and the mean is 5, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/5/2a5e5a53c6544153820f71518fb58278421ccc99.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{20}{4} = 5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="55" height="38" />, which is true. The second equation gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/0/f80be05c5abfd935f37bdec21867fe160ceb3c31.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{35}{5} = 7 = 5+2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="122" height="38" />, which satisfies the condition. We also have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/3/bc36360a745117338452b6747c278fd4115e7bd3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{36}{6} = 6 = 7-1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="122" height="38" />, which also satisfies the condition.
</p><p>Therefore, since we correctly solved it and checked our work multiple ways, we can be confident that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" />.
</p>
