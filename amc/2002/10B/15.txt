<p>The positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/3/2234a4fad9968f2e6a70981c44d65222d3df15df.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B, A-B,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b831312585cb4ab6697896f86b72accbddbc4b45.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> are all prime numbers. The sum of these four primes is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/7/1279c3decdb0198f652e3856c4ad462a06997970.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \mathrm{even} \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \mathrm{divisible\ by\ }3 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \mathrm{divisible\ by\ }5 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \mathrm{divisible\ by\ }7 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \mathrm{prime}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="692" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/d/d6d5a5848db0c94aa754588536b1b218cb81200a.png" class="latex" alt="$A-B$" width="50" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b831312585cb4ab6697896f86b72accbddbc4b45.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> must have the same <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parity" title="Parity">parity</a>, and since there is only one even prime number, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/d/d6d5a5848db0c94aa754588536b1b218cb81200a.png" class="latex" alt="$A-B$" width="50" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b831312585cb4ab6697896f86b72accbddbc4b45.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> are both odd. Thus one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/f/bef747a48a2a8d43c4841e12c89f2de2502a672e.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="16" /> is odd and the other even. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/6/456d871dc0edb7025eb151497a00bb3cdf4e366d.png" class="latex" alt="$A+B &gt; A &gt; A-B &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="196" height="14" />, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> (as a prime greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />) is odd. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed45766ae06d282b582b4b49d4289dcc81331314.png" class="latex" alt="$B = 2$" width="47" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/e/cbea4cf794ae9ce2d727b26f3a3c4246e8e47601.png" class="latex" alt="$A-2, A, A+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="16" /> are consecutive odd primes. At least one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/e/cbea4cf794ae9ce2d727b26f3a3c4246e8e47601.png" class="latex" alt="$A-2, A, A+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="16" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, from which it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/2/5d2c2236d016f37c0b87b01d4991f187c58e8154.png" class="latex" alt="$A-2 = 3$" width="77" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/a/14a7b7cfba975b9ef5efff26ae652dd4b2d9ef5d.png" class="latex" alt="$A = 5$" width="46" height="13" />. The sum of these numbers is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, a prime, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/6/f06a4001910549904e9688da5a96dd125a7a49c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(E)}\ \text{prime}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="85" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>In order for both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5e589efce2b099b83a8437a41f75acdce0c8e31.png" class="latex" alt="$A - B$" width="50" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bf77c6740d00af2866f26718d628a60c667ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> to be prime, one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/f/bef747a48a2a8d43c4841e12c89f2de2502a672e.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="16" /> must be 2, or else both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5e589efce2b099b83a8437a41f75acdce0c8e31.png" class="latex" alt="$A - B$" width="50" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bf77c6740d00af2866f26718d628a60c667ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> would be even numbers. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/c/e1c13d77e2b5ede10a622c2229043191de82ce17.png" class="latex" alt="$A = 2$" width="46" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/a/4daec1b65b2c7204eba605d45b19b072005b0218.png" class="latex" alt="$A &lt; B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/2/12264f41330c32dcd66cd377031cbdb5f48ea9a3.png" class="latex" alt="$A - B &lt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="83" height="13" />, which is not possible. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed45766ae06d282b582b4b49d4289dcc81331314.png" class="latex" alt="$B = 2$" width="47" height="12" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is prime and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/263cea2ba72385dfcb2f0a867ab2015643b8cfca.png" class="latex" alt="$A &gt; A - B &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="121" height="13" />, we can infer that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/43774152564421c2e7784fb38e533693aa22b400.png" class="latex" alt="$A &gt; 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/78525cbaa7d3414f24edec96d599421309313c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$6n \pm 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" /> for some natural number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. 
</p><p>However in either case, one of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5e589efce2b099b83a8437a41f75acdce0c8e31.png" class="latex" alt="$A - B$" width="50" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bf77c6740d00af2866f26718d628a60c667ef2.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/5/975b181b463919f2ce32ac1b617d7e261f8a98ce.png" class="latex" alt="$6n \pm 3 = 3(2n \pm 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="18" /> which is a multiple of 3. Therefore the only possibility that works is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/e/1aef9950a3d23f38e0044c835a680795e1021141.png" class="latex" alt="$A - B = 3$" width="83" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/9/009d79e62fcb86d45c7d973d181b0c3b8d45bb69.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A + B + (A - B) + (A + B) = 5 + 2 + 3 + 7 = 17\]" width="393" height="18" />
</p><p>Which is a prime number. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0cd82367e63fe9f487758f5656e94818f4d4b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="29" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
