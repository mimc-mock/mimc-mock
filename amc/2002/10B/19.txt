<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cad681354a2a529a1828a56b2b5629faec3cd128.png" class="latex" alt="$\{a_n\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> is an arithmetic sequence with
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e868a80b5058466bd743a2b7b66cb8c2f25c7447.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_{100}=100 \text{ and } a_{101}+a_{102}+\cdots+a_{200}=200.\]" width="474" height="15" />
What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/7/4471ab1dcdf239493f98c420d1332770a8c5379d.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2 - a_1&#160;?$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="64" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db00a18277aeddbada3a073ecea33f0b18d589b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 0.0001\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 0.001\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 0.01\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 0.1\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="452" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We should realize that the two equations are 100 terms apart, so by subtracting the two equations in a form like...
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/b/49b1ec02bb866acc3c0e21a35a5c0c450d2bf287.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_{101} - a_1) + (a_{102} - a_2) +... + (a_{200} - a_{100}) = 200-100 = 100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="498" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
</p><p>...we get the value of the common difference of every hundred terms one hundred times. So we have to divide the answer by one hundred to get ... 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6ef5ce271a74140d35e68a8376d500398e7e5e44.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{100}{100} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="63" height="38" />
</p><p>...the common difference of every hundred terms. Then we have to simply divide the answer by hundred again to find the common difference between one term, therefore...
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/a/65a0431374c411530339b8b434fe51798537173e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{100} =\boxed{(\text{C})0.01}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="125" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Adding the two given equations together gives 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5fbb0770eb72eb03c34bd1b3d1b1419e452145.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1+a_2+...+a_{200}=300$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="197" height="15" />.
</p><p>Now, let the common difference be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/2/722e578acb9416ec8800a0c8e8d250a59e739149.png" class="latex" alt="$a_2-a_1=d$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="15" />, so we merely need to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> to get the answer. The formula for an arithmetic sum is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/9/9a944e4389fe6ca6269bd4b36ca2da8a4c1c9d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{2}(2a_1+d(n-1))$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="141" height="33" />,
</p><p>where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> is the first term, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of terms, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is the common difference. Now we use this formula to find a closed form for the first given equation and the sum of the given equations. For the first equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3a84af200a7370473abe7c5c58f6784a64a222.png" class="latex" alt="$n=100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Therefore, we have 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/472101f4a307ebf60201046c14561bc172955c46.png" class="latex" alt="$50(2a_1+99d)=100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" />,
</p><p>or 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/3/df33ae85b0c4e5ef3aca9d7d8f3a515ac212d960.png" class="latex" alt="$2a_1+99d=2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="109" height="15" />.          *<b>(1)</b>
</p><p>For the sum of the equations (shown at the beginning of the solution) we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09c6fafd49ef2da76f81efd0029f1e6ff5a6afb3.png" class="latex" alt="$n=200$" width="61" height="12" />, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142fc2eb386241a04d1553335641854177212b61.png" class="latex" alt="$100(2a_1+199d)=300$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" />
</p><p>or 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/3/c23898822fd126e35b4a3be90ecd3fb7d8c6f33d.png" class="latex" alt="$2a_1+199d=3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="118" height="15" />          *<b>(2)</b>
</p><p>Now we have a system of equations in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />.
Subtracting <b>(1)</b> from <b>(2)</b> eliminates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/4/e04c9a1b8c90b65cf4f47d588d60916ae57f9fab.png" class="latex" alt="$100d=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/0/1e0885c0841d87e5b7837c4383c1f6121fcbe0ce.png" class="latex" alt="$d=a_2-a_1=\frac{1}{100}=\boxed{(\text{C}) .01}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="231" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Subtracting the 2 given equations yields
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/3/dc3cb44b97258e8a0c380559a29e003fa782cc21.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_{101}-a_1)+(a_{102}-a_2)+(a_{103}-a_3)+...+(a_{200}-a_{100})=100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="503" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
Now express each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1a2b6b7c193f9ba2b2134a59f1da5addcfbc98.png" class="latex" alt="$a_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> in terms of first term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and common difference <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> between consecutive terms
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/6/406db64e2935b1d32c99131c7aedca646f262683.png" class="latex" alt="$((a_1+100x)-(a_1))+((a_1+101x)-(a_1+x))+((a_1+102x)-(a_1+2x))+...+((a_1+199x)-(a_1+99x))=100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="914" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
Simplifying and canceling <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab39d761413804680d26d972381f028001562f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> terms gives
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/6/656b673a6ff2950f7f91bbb553966b1fa54a94af.png" class="latex" alt="$100x+100x+100x+...+100x=100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="305" height="14" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/b/72ba9664409a389d28a8ca77b66375f2bd9b1fc9.png" class="latex" alt="$100x\times100=100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="138" height="13" /> 
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/7/4d7a23ce820ea2e52703c61e285bf6405c9b4cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$100x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/1/3b18f9584ce06a9c5cd9cae5a9e8a32ef1a0dac7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0.01=\boxed{(\text{C})0.01}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="161" height="29" />
</p>
