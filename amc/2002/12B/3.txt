<p>For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e401dcfd066660187824a483c4625ce4905c2e41.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2 - 3n + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="16" /> a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Prime" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime">prime</a> number?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/7/5a704203a7b5598744c0e3c55c2995b7fcec5b0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \text{none} \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{one} \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \text{two} \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \text{more\ than\ two,\ but\ finitely\ many} \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \text{infinitely\ many}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="733" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Factoring, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709829c03830ade4a292082d0a741acbe49bc904.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2 - 3n + 2 = (n-2)(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="227" height="19" />. Either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9b02e32cfba0db0f9038a8d077596720f62e91.png" class="latex" alt="$n-2$" width="41" height="12" /> is odd, and the other is even.  Their product must yield an even number.  The only prime that is even is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, which is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> is not a positive number, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/f/7efba8d8079289db6e71786896985ea693868105.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{one}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="69" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Considering parity, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e401dcfd066660187824a483c4625ce4905c2e41.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2 - 3n + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="16" /> is always even.  The only even prime is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4ac4a29feb2a98306789bca28e8b53192c821d07.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2-3n=0$" width="93" height="15" /> whence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/5/50542a37dc4f9ac84c6626de257f16bb6476340e.png" class="latex" alt="$n=3\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{one}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="141" height="29" />.
</p>

<p>Usage of the Prime Number 2
Note that two is the only even prime number. 
So, in order for this expression to result in a prime number, the result must be 2.
You can factor the quadratic equation and eventually see that 3 is the only number satisfying this condition.
Hence, the answer is B.
</p>
