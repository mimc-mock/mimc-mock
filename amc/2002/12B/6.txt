<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are nonzero real numbers, and that the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Equation" title="Equation">equation</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/0/2d02669809d1590446ef6fae4d98f02810eff0e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + ax + b = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" /> has solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Then the pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/8/4b863434b13287e6ada996e05f3e6d88b60c0ef4.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ (-2,1) \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ (-1,2) \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ (1,-2) \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ (2,-1) \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ (4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="554" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/5/d85283a3cbd2025ed9d6f447cc0ffaa84e67f3b1.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-a)(x-b) = x^2 - (a+b)x + ab = x^2 + ax + b = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="426" height="19" />, it follows by comparing <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coefficient" title="Coefficient">coefficients</a> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/5/6e59084e5929624dd2d1022b4e5dc623716b76e1.png" class="latex" alt="$-a - b = a$" width="87" height="12" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e327018efeacbe7c350a25542799a5b882f43eb.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = b$" width="49" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is nonzero, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9ad3feb71e591545cd5252bfb9297571022e614.png" class="latex" alt="$-1 - b = 1 \Longrightarrow b = -2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="180" height="13" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/765b2a7d7b54d237d291e6d30a0b3a33067c4a57.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b) = \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ (1,-2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="163" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Another method is to use <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>. The sum of the solutions to this polynomial is equal to the opposite of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coefficient, since the leading coefficient is 1; in other words, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/e/1ceadb6686c87d2d95455a35083b934d65edb899.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = -a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="14" /> and the product of the solutions is equal to the constant term (i.e, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/8/db8cd8078dfc4a8747d37c579f15ec738deb0894.png" class="latex" alt="$a*b = b$" width="67" height="12" />). Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is nonzero, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and therefore (from the first equation), <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/e/dae985590d05f950d8e5dbd4c0d53a862e3cd46d.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -2a = -2$" width="111" height="12" />. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/765b2a7d7b54d237d291e6d30a0b3a33067c4a57.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b) = \boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ (1,-2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="163" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are solutions to the given equation, we can write the two equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/2/5a2a99b4693a57623947c887f42d1295268ad3b1.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + a^2 + b = 2a^2 + b = 0,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="204" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9b6aca3331d5d5dfea8f84c0f47f5b3baaa6bcd.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 + ab + b = 0.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="16" /> From the first equation, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d66a864eef755ae85f30785eb4d0e0b2ce578628.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -2a^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="18" /> and substituting this in our second equation, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a772895eea1091dae76553a2a9f979d9abdd32e7.png" class="latex" alt="$4a^4 - 2a^3 - 2a^2 = 0,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="160" height="18" /> and solving this gives us the solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/5/ab5c5310a9cf09c1138f1feea6c8b64b3da12225.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -\tfrac{1}{2},$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="62" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/9/b5969c2cfb1ffadf981fbbddcf796c8eb0037546.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 0$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/7/387f00ce72cc81499ce4379e754effa4b2902a78.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="12" /> We discard the first two solutions, as the first one doesnt show up in the answer choices and we are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is nonzero. The only valid solution is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/d/20d4dce7024a2b85d82127fa561093229d51a09b.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="15" /> which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d879e7bc10ca9947a5e4368a63874fc7be6fe0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = -2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/d/15d1f26577d4901c615d8d1430f9b7f7cd4c67b2.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b) = \boxed{\text{(C) } (1, -2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="160" height="29" />
</p>
