<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/3/9a3e0783ce6020c08264d8726cd2aad65a54d6da.png" class="latex" alt="$v, w, x, y,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> be the degree measures of the five angles of a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pentagon" title="Pentagon">pentagon</a>. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7cc0bd129e4893a52e5c9e104da6adcfe53d768f.png" class="latex" alt="$v &lt; w &lt; x &lt; y &lt; z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="148" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/3/9a3e0783ce6020c08264d8726cd2aad65a54d6da.png" class="latex" alt="$v, w, x, y,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> form an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_sequence" title="Arithmetic sequence">arithmetic sequence</a>. Find the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/b/f5b401963a4093c86e0d9a92ed69fcf532b7df44.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 72 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 84 \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 90 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 108 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 120$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="405" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The sum of the degree measures of the angles of a pentagon (as a pentagon can be split into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d74e44f371ba70b00a50c760e76c0431e43fad1f.png" class="latex" alt="$5- 2 = 3$" width="73" height="12" /> triangles) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/7/f6765446f2a8fe37b02af0810b932257fa3b80bf.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 180 = 540^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="13" />. If we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/3/3d3d663bed77bc8eea7a8456f6e367abd1b4c021.png" class="latex" alt="$v = x - 2d, w = x - d, y = x + d, z = x+2d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="349" height="16" />, it follows that 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/0/2a03fa9f8fc2b8f56860f65f81e93bfc3b933f99.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x-2d)+(x-d)+x+(x+d)+(x+2d) = 5x = 540 \Longrightarrow x = 108 \ \mathrm{(D)}\]" width="503" height="18" />
</p><p>Note that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the middle term of an arithmetic sequence with an odd number of terms, it is simply the average of the sequence.
</p><p>You can always assume the values are the same so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fba28c04e483fbd4aba3d4d55a8ec6c1605c36bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{540}{5}=109$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f23bf124b6b2b2a993eb313c72e678664ac74a.png" class="latex" alt="$v$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f23bf124b6b2b2a993eb313c72e678664ac74a.png" class="latex" alt="$v$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/d/56dafe0a41bf3241f83c1580256cdd2c4a2f0be2.png" class="latex" alt="$v + d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f9787f8b3532b9135461abda9a4069d7de20b2c9.png" class="latex" alt="$v+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/4/224c3bc78058da14fc650fc7c5456222435c1e53.png" class="latex" alt="$v+3d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054579a91e261811e28311e00965656f34ad8596.png" class="latex" alt="$v+4d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, respectively. Then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/9/b39bdd7d2dc26e54954af8dbd765bf22fb080ab0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[v + w + x + y + z = 5v + 10d = 180^{\circ} (5 - 2) = 540^{\circ}\]" width="404" height="18" />
Dividing the equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/d/fedaf1b53101b80f4a59cff8d7b99979fd9bc9c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[v + 2d = x = 108^{\circ} \mathrm {(D)}\]" width="170" height="18" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
</p>
