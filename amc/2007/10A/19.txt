<p>A paint brush is swept along both diagonals of a square to produce the symmetric painted area, as shown. Half the area of the square is painted. What is the ratio of the side length of the square to the brush width?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/4/5240fbdb071637f49d1f5606cb9dc40dfbef075f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(3 cm); real w = (sqrt(2) - 1)/2; filldraw((0,0)--(w*sqrt(2)/2,0)--(0.5,0.5 - w*sqrt(2)/2)--(1 - w*sqrt(2)/2,0)--(1,0)--(1,w*sqrt(2)/2)--(0.5 + w*sqrt(2)/2,0.5)--(1,1 - w*sqrt(2)/2)--(1,1)--(1 - w*sqrt(2)/2,1)--(0.5,0.5 + w*sqrt(2)/2)--(w*sqrt(2)/2,1)--(0,1)--(0,1 - w*sqrt(2)/2)--(0.5 - w*sqrt(2)/2,0.5)--(0,w*sqrt(2)/2)--cycle,gray(0.7)); draw((0,0)--(1,0)--(1,1)--(0,1)--cycle);  [/asy]" width="145" height="145" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/f/d9fdc491438f65a6ae41b27820bfd2f582b5bca5.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 2\sqrt {2} + 1 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 3\sqrt {2}\qquad \text{(C)}\ 2\sqrt {2} + 2 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 3\sqrt {2} + 1 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 3\sqrt {2} + 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="576" height="21" />                                         
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Without_loss_of_generality" title="Without loss of generality">Without loss of generality</a>, let the side length of the square be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> unit. The area of the painted area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/d/c8ddd412de3ac72144ea9a21077e9350b7a961c5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the area of the larger square, so the total unpainted area is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.
Each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> unpainted triangles has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbbd5cce2a8a39cc8bc13cea4217cbc4e52f2128.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. It can be observed that these triangles are isosceles right triangles, so let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the side length of one of the smaller triangles:
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/e/e4eb7735c344fc0ab3325b70fe612e1c26de2ab0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a^2}2 = \frac{1}8 \rightarrow a= \frac{1}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="132" height="39" />
</p>
</center>
<p>The hypotenuse of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41cf6aa0dbb37cacfa007dd4be61f4ab243a27ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />. The corners of the painted area are also isosceles right triangles with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3b3dd6df2f84c37faa787a58481abc95548f734.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1-\frac{\sqrt{2}}2}2 = \frac{1}2-\frac{\sqrt2}4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="143" height="44" />. Its hypotenuse is equal to the width of the paint, and is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c146db63f52269a6a14e376e11f43485f0725a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}2-\frac{1}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="62" height="41" />. The answer we are looking for is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/a/54aeb1fca4c32b4d7001ba1f2bc09ea964401f15.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{\frac{\sqrt{2}}2-\frac{1}2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="56" height="46" />. Multiply the numerator and the denominator by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/1/6916ede6e333f0a9f671841fd958355fd1ecbe4e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}2+\frac{1}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="62" height="41" /> to simplify, and you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/7/94767b2de34006ad0ee18b951de8420df8f12346.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}+\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{2}{4}-\frac{1}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="56" height="50" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/3/f430b47fa931ff8254cc52bf04931c4572d00b70.png" class="latex" alt="$4\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="99" height="53" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/2/372a1efd63f95f2b55865e48d79195330cfe714e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{2\sqrt{2}+2} \rightarrow C$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="118" height="30" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Again, have the length of the square equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and let the width of each individual stripe be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Note that you can split each stripe into one rectangle and two isosceles right triangles at the corners. Then the area of each stripe is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/e/0de479d20f667e7bb988ef92db4c7eb9ce12a473.png" class="latex" alt="$n(\sqrt{2}-\frac{n}{2})=\sqrt{2}n-\frac{n^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="188" height="39" />. The area covered by the two total stripes is twice the area of one stripe, minus the area in the intersection of the stripes, which is a square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. This area is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. So:
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0f0bc72fd7380267bb6e8d870a1d7e4da6dc4a.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt2n-2n^2=\frac{1}{2}\rightarrow-2n^2+2\sqrt2n-\frac{1}{2}=0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="335" height="37" />.
</p>
</center>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_formula" title="Quadratic formula">quadratic formula</a>, 
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c6376595dee3f8ba463a32dcecc685ece5576a35.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\frac{-2\sqrt{2}\pm\sqrt{(2\sqrt{2})^2-4(-2)(-\frac{1}{2})}}{2(-2)}\rightarrow n=\frac{-2\sqrt{2}\pm 2}{-4}\rightarrow n=\frac{\sqrt{2}\pm 1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="556" height="56" />
</p>
</center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/9/d6919c75ffefde4ba70ea8206b8769f573cf64c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="41" /> is greater than the length of the square, so it is impossible for that to be the brush width. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b1b8083c756a613e297ed929e355123e82e22d7.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="93" height="41" />. We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83dacecc3f852b6d1fc31accf43e249930c0b27f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b19098ece2b97bc61512eb7d80fca239bef9020.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}-1}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="97" height="41" />. Multiply the numerator and the denominator by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc749b0e7d47fb1f19fa539c653c3cdf3a15bd2c.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="54" height="18" />, 
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/9/4395efd85ea30c4758354a5469dbdeee73986c0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{n}=\frac{2(\sqrt{2}+1)}{(\sqrt{2}-1)(\sqrt{2}+1)}=\boxed{2\sqrt{2}+2}\rightarrow C$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="325" height="47" />
</p>
</center>
