<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are positive <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integers</a> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/f/7cf6f8fa48b22def853a427ff1d67020201621dd.png" class="latex" alt="$75m = n^{3}$" width="75" height="15" />. What is the minimum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53bf1afcb274a3bb84338109fffec8a1f1c02008.png" class="latex" alt="$m + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/f/a7f3ee7e10ebd4f754902f39f43e6afc3b960cb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 15 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 30 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 50 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 60 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 5700$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/b/2cb19b964390f57a444060ef1077b55a50fadfe9.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 5^2m$" width="54" height="15" /> must be a perfect cube, so each power of a prime in the factorization for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/b/2cb19b964390f57a444060ef1077b55a50fadfe9.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 5^2m$" width="54" height="15" /> must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf9cd891fca223c463dd1f35ef5ead2c85744804.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 \cdot 5 = 45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="80" height="15" />, which makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/2/8b219ec60fc52fffb3a087cd962705121a553544.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \sqrt[3]{3^3 \cdot 5^3} = 15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="18" />. The minimum possible value for the sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/3/753f6667acf70ab315dfc8dc1d0958986e1415b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(D)60}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="32" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, we need to prime factorize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/2/68209f3c4dff7c50a30e66fc09f8c6b579f394fb.png" class="latex" alt="$5^2 \cdot 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="17" />. We need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4ac513592bd877eb807a53d88deefaedab637f4a.png" class="latex" alt="$75m$" width="33" height="12" /> to be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/4/9848f0c3cdc3f23cd80d86189319937f709fc8bc.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3y^3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="18" />. Therefore, the smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/2/4a29c8656e2c115aed2fe9156b165d6f702f7811.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \cdot 3^2$" width="37" height="15" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> = 45, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd60b595daae109ea23e0ed38840822692e12e9.png" class="latex" alt="$5^3 \cdot 3^3 = 15^3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="17" />, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/a/5ea3bf55e379b09acf36eb56ef1f5230ac726d96.png" class="latex" alt="$45 + 15$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="15" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad8bf9e78f944bb79daf11abfc84e33adc5f71e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {(D)60}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="61" height="32" />
</p><p>~Arcticturn
</p>
