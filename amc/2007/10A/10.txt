<p>The Dunbar family consists of a mother, a father, and some children. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Average" title="Average">average</a> age of the members of the family is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, the father is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> years old, and the average age of the mother and children is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. How many children are in the family?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/9/c0904a1807339bc66742fe6716ebc810957bf718.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ 2 \qquad \text{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \text{(C)}\ 4 \qquad \text{(D)}\ 5 \qquad \text{(E)}\ 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="330" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of children. Then the total ages of the family is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e84a35b21bf6cde90ebfd6266d2d32515a6af8b.png" class="latex" alt="$48 + 16(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" />, and the total number of people in the family is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f6e3acd481bacb78f324773254902e3a08d848f.png" class="latex" alt="$n+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" />. So
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/e/1be138d16be316ae6222625ea479d7d88a1e76f9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[20 = \frac{48 + 16(n+1)}{n+2} \Longrightarrow 20n + 40 = 16n + 64 \Longrightarrow n = 6\ \mathrm{(E)}.\]" width="478" height="40" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of the children and the mom. The father, who is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, plus the sum of the ages of the kids and mom divided by the number of kids and mom plus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> (for the dad) = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. This is because the average age of the entire family is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f06afca5b285202e10a200dc2a833602490ab73.png" class="latex" alt="$20.$" width="21" height="12" />
This statement, written as an equation, is: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/5/1f5ea4671c0efbfd1eaf18e0e06d5bc08cb110ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{48+16x}{x+1}=20\]" width="113" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/9/b49dbecea7024b644d54a85d520ef9a3caa83f04.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[48+16x=20x+20\]" width="161" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/47299abfba9d2940ad9ee3ace4ef92ed1df3ca89.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x=28\]" width="60" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/d/bddb3fa7a8c67bd2de27054bd3cf5f8fa14a9fc3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x=7\]" width="43" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> people - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> mom = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> children.
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/2/55206bce678ac0394d97dc04dd72398df2bdf572.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> be the Mom's age.
</p><p>Let the number of children be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and their average age be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Their age totaled up is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />. 
</p><p>We have the following two equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693235b77d1f848d71d109fa5c473c56f22b9bd9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+48+xy}{2+x}=20$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="144" height="39" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9eacfe1d6a2a11904072f3572295a55f993188d0.png" class="latex" alt="$m+48+xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" /> is the family's total age and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/b/77b265186f7b5c1c76da9c1bd809a6d0095a7ac4.png" class="latex" alt="$2+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> is the total number of people in the family.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693235b77d1f848d71d109fa5c473c56f22b9bd9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+48+xy}{2+x}=20$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="144" height="39" />   
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80dedbca4c85d06a8c71172c096220007d414f75.png" class="latex" alt="$m+48+xy=40+20x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="194" height="17" />
</p><p>The next equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f28142c597d714f76003c25ddf8d42cc204ea592.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+xy}{1+x}=16$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="104" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/e/46e24d9865538f2ed50bbcb9d42ed00124971702.png" class="latex" alt="$m+xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="14" /> is the total age of the Mom and the children, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/378eec6c0e5607fb907c0c9073d11273c8d69fa5.png" class="latex" alt="$1+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> is the number of children along with the Mom.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/8/f28142c597d714f76003c25ddf8d42cc204ea592.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+xy}{1+x}=16$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="104" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54314b5840a89cf890820d5c38a66a44a97c5b2.png" class="latex" alt="$m+xy=16+16x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="16" />.
</p><p>We know the value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/e/46e24d9865538f2ed50bbcb9d42ed00124971702.png" class="latex" alt="$m+xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="14" />, so we substitute the value back in the first equation.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80dedbca4c85d06a8c71172c096220007d414f75.png" class="latex" alt="$m+48+xy=40+20x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="194" height="17" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/75691774a6385f3cf67dfd2966716faaf6f4579a.png" class="latex" alt="$(16+16x)+48=40+20x$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="219" height="20" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd92d02476c3434bf645bf5a3bc249eca83d3c16.png" class="latex" alt="$x=6$" width="43" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Earlier, we set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be the number of children. Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/d/ffd8349ea6d510ab127edb77431f2197ed95a2bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(E)}  6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="45" height="28" /> children.
</p>
