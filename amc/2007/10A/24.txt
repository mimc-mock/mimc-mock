<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">Circles</a> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> each have radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, as shown. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Midpoint" title="Midpoint">midpoint</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32f809bf232cc37239a39fb8c3845d726826717b.png" class="latex" alt="$OA = 2\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />. Segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20f8f2f1a3ddc6b42fae25a0637e85c747875180.png" class="latex" alt="$OC$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/5/46569d6dd3fa86f698827e8de15b0480e1733d11.png" class="latex" alt="$OD$" width="29" height="12" /> are tangent to the circles centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, respectively, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> is a common <a href="/wiki/index.php/Tangent_(geometry)" title="Tangent (geometry)">tangent</a>. What is the area of the shaded region <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1a8a9904c342b20543bd5fc1c261f05c1c00646.png" class="latex" alt="$ECODF$" width="72" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/3/803ab8f735a6bc15c04e579b999095ac8d71e780.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(5cm); pair A=(-2*sqrt(2),0), B = (2*sqrt(2),0), C = A+2*dir(45), D = B+2*dir(135), E = A+2*dir(90), F = B+2*dir(90);  fill((0,0)--C--E--F--D--cycle,gray(0.6)); unfill(circle(A,2)); unfill(circle(B,2)); draw(circle(A,2)); draw(circle(B,2)); draw(E--F); draw(C--(0,0)--D); draw(A+2*dir(300)--A--B--B+2*dir(240));  dot((0,0)); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); dot(E); dot(F);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,dir(180)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,dir(0)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,dir(240)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,dir(300)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,dir(90)); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,dir(90)); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,(0,0),dir(270)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,A+dir(300),dir(210)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,B+dir(240),dir(330)); [/asy]" width="238" height="122" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/b/59be50cd9944d3ef2f44e9e05bebcea18b26995f.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ \frac {8\sqrt {2}}{3} \qquad \text{(B)}\ 8\sqrt {2} - 4 - \pi \qquad \text{(C)}\ 4\sqrt {2} \qquad \text{(D)}\ 4\sqrt {2} + \frac {\pi}{8} \qquad \text{(E)}\ 8\sqrt {2} - 2 - \frac {\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="624" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The area we are trying to find is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5031f4ee3ffc439e09e93d2ddcf51fe42d3668.png" class="latex" alt="$ABFE-(\overarc{AEC}+\triangle{ACO}+\triangle{BDO}+\overarc{BFD})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="349" height="28" />.
Obviously, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd30a7c3e92b118c95ec4393d823887313c0ddf.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{EF}\parallel\overline{AB}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="76" height="20" />.  Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/5009e5bfc2d8dd48da9c1fbd33a9e7a10dcb8309.png" class="latex" alt="$ABFE$" width="56" height="13" /> is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">rectangle</a>, and so its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/12989d0274675ec9e2fb759a45781ef54aafac39.png" class="latex" alt="$b\times{h}=2\times{(AO+OB)}=2\times{2(2\sqrt{2})}=8\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="359" height="21" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/1/0019a9c6ae9bef5eda9166daa34ea37465190dd1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OC}$" width="29" height="15" /> is tangent to circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/4/034c66c174efe21f030136f37940957c4582ce92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ACO}$" width="57" height="13" /> is a right triangle.  We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/4/cf4a775f6ea170301574adfadf6e96a1110c4bc4.png" class="latex" alt="$AO=2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/d/53d084de05e313a40923ec33ee357544b1b93874.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=2$" width="60" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/4/034c66c174efe21f030136f37940957c4582ce92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ACO}$" width="57" height="13" /> is an isosceles right triangle, and has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a758ef3b5007db39fe64715408203590f26885.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CO}$" width="29" height="15" /> with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.  The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/b/c8b895a8f7f9cea430f50343e44e64c6388a5c8c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ACO}=\frac{1}{2}bh=2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="147" height="37" />.  By symmetry, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/b/16b9b9ce8f59fba029eb02472092ee79836d2d60.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ACO}\cong\triangle{BDO}$" width="142" height="13" />, and so the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76c39d58140304fafee8af14888b1ed3378ad58d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{BDO}$" width="60" height="13" /> is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7e3a73cd9daf435ded4d986b7e4bc7bfbbbc13d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{AEC}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="24" /> (or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/4/6649bfd4cfcbe322a189a3ac27d80011c95142f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{BFD}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="24" />, for that matter) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db9e227e8177d26de452c2a2faeb6d45cc53d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> the area of its circle since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/a/d3a25d33565610efd849549775e634d486771d08.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{OAC}$" width="54" height="13" /> is 45 degrees and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/6/946f5e5c5cbec36a592651afd62441fb942cb572.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{OAE}$" width="54" height="13" /> forms a right <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangle</a>.  Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7e3a73cd9daf435ded4d986b7e4bc7bfbbbc13d.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{AEC}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="24" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/4/6649bfd4cfcbe322a189a3ac27d80011c95142f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{BFD}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="24" /> both have an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/e/aeebb746f8c0d3dac5219c3635617032430afee9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" />.
</p><p>Plugging all of these areas back into the original equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/6/0160328f4ba3d9a505f7b5d9f9a5289c4a69a593.png" class="latex" alt="$8\sqrt{2}-(\frac{\pi}{2}+2+2+\frac{\pi}{2})=8\sqrt{2}-(4+\pi)=\boxed{8\sqrt{2}-4-\pi}\ \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="485" height="36" />.
</p>
