<p>A star-<a href="/wiki/index.php/Polygon" title="Polygon">polygon</a> is drawn on a clock face by drawing a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Chord" title="Chord">chord</a> from each number to the fifth number counted clockwise from that number. That is, chords are drawn from 12 to 5, from 5 to 10, from 10 to 3, and so on, ending back at 12. What is the degree measure of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Angle" title="Angle">angle</a> at each <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vertex" title="Vertex">vertex</a> in the star polygon?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/f/88fb1683ee2e65a70c17d28be14cae3cd6f0abeb.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 20\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 24\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 30\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 36\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007_AMC12A-8.png" class="image"><img alt="2007 AMC12A-8.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//e/e8/2007_AMC12A-8.png" width="364" height="367" /></a>
</p><p>We look at the angle between 12, 5, and 10. It subtends <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/337176718520f1f13185f020dff6ffc997f9f1f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the circle, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees (or you can see that the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arc" title="Arc">arc</a> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcfeb8cb09b8af3dae2cf162c738f90021a90849.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 23$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_angle" title="Right angle">right angle</a>). Thus, the angle at each vertex is an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Inscribed_angle" title="Inscribed angle">inscribed angle</a> subtending <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees, making the answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/4/9347604b04c95da68badea4e8a47670b2f9eeec1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 1260 = 30^{\circ} \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />
</p>
