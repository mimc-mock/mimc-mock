<p>Yan is somewhere between his home and the stadium. To get to the stadium he can walk directly to the stadium, or else he can walk home and then ride his bicycle to the stadium. He rides 7 times as fast as he walks, and both choices require the same amount of time. What is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Ratio" title="Ratio">ratio</a> of Yan's distance from his home to his distance from the stadium?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/c/36c02fd72511a1c98081e3033ddc52e0b0112a12.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 23\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 34\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 45\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 56\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac 78$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="361" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Simple_Solution">Simple Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> represent the distance from home to the stadium, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> represent the distance from Yan to home. Our goal is to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/feab8bddfcf64f51030a460d8e52a6df0f1507ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{r}{x-r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="33" />. If Yan walks directly to the stadium, then assuming he walks at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, it will take him <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/3/d93a6f70e1a466da7876f0e5970868e48100c2b6.png" class="latex" alt="$x-r$" width="40" height="8" /> units of time. Similarly, if he walks back home it will take him <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/9/ee9879dd00be62db2b25a40db63bd5e55115cfb4.png" class="latex" alt="$r + \frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="33" /> units of time. Because the two times are equal, we can create the following equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51f5ba2d98a3a15987a04c2ca081e3285b95a4b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-r = r + \frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="33" />. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb402eef276f51667ad88a5b70a0859d85fa957.png" class="latex" alt="$x-2r=\frac{x}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="33" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/766fe5fd9d7643251c5a2f893412efd3a4476de3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{7}x = 2r$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/f/c9fde3edf6f1608f341baa0924bde34ac37df459.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{r} = \frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />. This minus one is the reciprocal of what we want to find: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/0/770cfa361ffc1b466bf8fcb74b284f165eeadfba.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{3}-1 = \frac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/f/f5f471a80be8e64836eea41e2db9e8252dba001f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="57" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the distance from Yan's initial position to the stadium be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and the distance from Yan's initial position to home be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. We are trying to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a1af41fd022f227591233ea61563f7b81b2ba5.png" class="latex" alt="$b/a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />, and we have the following identity given by the problem:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/a/61a80f0b3ab196e98139bf72dae3f9a8de7aeb64.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}a &amp;= b + \frac{a+b}{7}\\ \frac{6a}{7} &amp;= \frac{8b}{7} \Longrightarrow 6a = 8b\end{align*}" width="165" height="81" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ecf4a42a023a0baf12836c66852609c155277c8.png" class="latex" alt="$b/a = 6/8 = 3/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/9/b9956c8f4da76c8411602695771da0dcab523a3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="44" height="38" />
</p><p>Note: The identity can be given from an application of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5c1fe955f73bb046a117727b6a9ac041abb5fb.png" class="latex" alt="$t=\frac dr$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Another way of solving this problem is by setting the distance between Yan's home and the stadium, thus filling in one variable.
Let us set the distance between the two places to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/476aade8218d6bc01fb522e1537356d84ae67fca.png" class="latex" alt="$42z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> is a random measurement (cause life, why not?). The time going to his home then riding his bike, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> times faster, to the stadium is equal to the time it takes for him to walk to the stadium. So the equation would be:
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff51845797a05fd68c53c81a189bc899dc2537b9.png" class="latex" alt="$x=$" width="29" height="8" /> the distance from Yan's position to his home. 
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5a36d7260bbd42d64baa0681ccf0696ccb7ffdc.png" class="latex" alt="$42z=$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="12" /> the distance from Yan's home to the stadium.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/47697c38af3f93dbc5fbb1baa15a4f52b06b2a49.png" class="latex" alt="$42z-x=x+\frac{42z}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/8/cc8a804efd4eff701925770187521e41342bd8e3.png" class="latex" alt="$42z-x=x+6z$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/8/038dd8f931a62d276cbe0218168de25e30d0e063.png" class="latex" alt="$36z=2x$" width="70" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/d/a6de43045b26c11a8be66622e7c72f6611e7336b.png" class="latex" alt="$x=18z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />
</p><p>But we're still not done with the question. We know that Yan is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/9/7e9f03bcdb5d9feefdcadfddfa43dfe163dc9c32.png" class="latex" alt="$18z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> from his home, and is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/b/2abc711a096a4808d62dc8939ddf13c9315bc917.png" class="latex" alt="$42z-18z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/f/f6f7535a34c89f72428d158d9e08fac84a9a20b7.png" class="latex" alt="$24z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> from the stadium.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c4790f4c3bec3de9474d0d2a5df993660cd104bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{18z}{24z}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" />  , the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />'s cancel out, and we are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />.
Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/9/b9956c8f4da76c8411602695771da0dcab523a3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="44" height="38" />
</p><p>~ProGameXD
</p><p>~minor edits by mobius247
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Assume that the distance from home to the stadium is 1, and the distance from Yan to home is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Also assume that the speed of walking is 1, so the speed of biking is 7. Thus
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030cbdb0599449653d340619ffb6e8a68c30cc49.png" class="latex" alt="$1-b=b+\frac{1}{7} \Longrightarrow b=\frac{3}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="189" height="37" />.
We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e09fdf28c8ff41a66de4c551a240c6f1f533f53.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{1-b}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="41" height="38" /> which is equal to
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/3/2a3215d92b2feed42dfab2d140cdc2c3b013d4bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{7} \div \frac{4}{7} = \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="116" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c599a225aea8ebea938f3b2bad0d6ce3ff57e1c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(7,0)); dot((0,0)); dot((3,0)); dot((6,0)); dot((7,0)); label(&quot;$H$&quot;,(0,0),S); label(&quot;$Y$&quot;,(3,0),S); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,(6,0),S); label(&quot;$S$&quot;,(7,0),S); [/asy]" width="252" height="28" />
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> represent Yan's home, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> represent the stadium, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> represent Yan's current position. If Yan walks directly to the stadium, he will reach Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> the same time he will reach <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> if he is walking home. 
Since he bikes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> times as fast as he walks and the time is the same, the distance from his home to the stadium must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> times the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to the stadium. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10e2664b2499c60c8c87b48c3a7ffc332c7beb55.png" class="latex" alt="$PS=x$" width="60" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/5/db59a2494d0604429526d65a515aad59ad257df0.png" class="latex" alt="$HS=7x$" width="72" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/d/84dc9d0c13c8b7384d2718c7b25c7a656b5e16c9.png" class="latex" alt="$HP=6x$" width="74" height="12" />. Since Y is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/7/1e7f6dbeabde59e76efe5fba9f0621591c655d3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{HP}$" width="31" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9a5887488c6258e640c770cc6d25db2babeea8b.png" class="latex" alt="$HY=YP=3x$" width="127" height="12" />. Therefore, the ratio of Yan's distance from his home to his distance from the stadium is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/930ad05674665d613fdbed93f9bcbf2e17ff5604.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{YH}{YS}=\frac{3x}{4x}=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="164" height="48" />
</p>
