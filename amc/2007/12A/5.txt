<p>Last year Mr. Jon Q. Public received an inheritance. He paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> in federal taxes on the inheritance, and paid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of what he had left in state taxes. He paid a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/4/ca44b22bb4638a0aa46f917124b298bb35174b69.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar10500$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="53" height="14" /> for both taxes. How many dollars was his inheritance?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/3/5331d95d70e29e086b0f43b9d6d5e1dfc980a338.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A})\ 30000 \qquad (\mathrm {B})\ 32500 \qquad(\mathrm {C})\ 35000 \qquad(\mathrm {D})\ 37500 \qquad(\mathrm {E})\ 40000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="523" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>After paying his taxes, he has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/2013f009cd62ac56314b722b7c79c43c5cf0a251.png" class="latex" alt="$0.8*0.9=0.72$" width="119" height="12" /> of his earnings left. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a21486a3981b45e4d1b2419d6a1bd74ca6e62d.png" class="latex" alt="$10500$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/9/849d8f99d4e24e1604390e40c53015faa5286747.png" class="latex" alt="$0.28$" width="31" height="12" /> of his income, he got a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d1897f43daaf6d9826d4bd39648a46478c934cff.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10500}{0.28}=37500\  \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="151" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let his total inheritance be a number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The amount of tax he has to pay at first can be represented as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/a/71aafb205b7de5c6d44ada13e4db83676d17c372.png" class="latex" alt="$0.2x$" width="33" height="12" />. The amount of money he has left over after this expense can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/a/17a2fb4bcc6090bd04da6e4dbe232e0374589212.png" class="latex" alt="$0.8x$" width="33" height="12" />. He then has to pay <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> tax on this remaining amount, which can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/f/35f0d0423aa8088856c4f6d051a8c75c6cb30f78.png" class="latex" alt="$0.1(0.8x) = 0.08x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="137" height="18" />. The combined expense of these taxes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a21486a3981b45e4d1b2419d6a1bd74ca6e62d.png" class="latex" alt="$10500$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/9/2c9c44f3b45a66a0a479b87b5b99855754e4dbf1.png" class="latex" alt="$0.2x + 0.08x = 10500 \Longrightarrow 0.28x = 10500$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="319" height="14" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5bab308fd704e9609080d2486e804eff5bbf0e3.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{10500}{0.28}\ = 37500  \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="185" height="37" />. <a href="/wiki/index.php?title=User:Ankitamc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="User:Ankitamc (page does not exist)">Ankitamc</a> (<a href="/wiki/index.php?title=User_talk:Ankitamc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="User talk:Ankitamc (page does not exist)">talk</a>) 13:32, 17 January 2021 (EST)AnkitAmc.
~edited by mobius247
</p>
