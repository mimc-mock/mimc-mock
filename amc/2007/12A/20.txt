<p>Corners are sliced off a unit cube so that the six faces each become regular octagons. What is the total volume of the removed tetrahedra?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/9/1b95e738fb8304f684119dbb721d08b62d109c76.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{5\sqrt{2}-7}{3}\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{10-7\sqrt{2}}{3}\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{3-2\sqrt{2}}{3}\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{8\sqrt{2}-11}{3}\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac{6-4\sqrt{2}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="657" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png" class="image"><img alt="2007 AMC 12A Problem 20.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/e/e3/2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png/150px-2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png" width="150" height="156" srcset="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/e/e3/2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png/225px-2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png 1.5x, https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/e/e3/2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png/300px-2007_AMC_12A_Problem_20.png 2x" /></a>
</p><p>Since the sides of a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Regular_polygon" title="Regular polygon">regular polygon</a> are equal in length, we can call each side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Examine one edge of the unit cube: each contains two slanted diagonal edges of an octagon and one straight edge. The diagonal edges form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4fe048f5c1382916a77470eb42a56f76de35b789.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90 \triangle$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="14" /> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangles</a>, making the distance on the edge of the cube <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/4/ce4186006fe77ab75269bf5ab14332e75c56970c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="26" height="37" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a4fedf05e5a15adab9982f28b37897ec17e36c.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot \frac{x}{\sqrt{2}} + x = 1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="115" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a0a300109f47ae15e25fdda04ba22ece4f350ce.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} + 1} \cdot \left(\frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{\sqrt{2} - 1}\right) = \sqrt{2} - 1$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="273" height="53" />. 
</p><p>Each of the cut off corners is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pyramid" title="Pyramid">pyramid</a>, whose volume can be calculated by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/2/a22ce7c05dd2053f3b673fd9044ebaa873d58ce8.png" class="latex" alt="$V = \frac 13 Bh$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />. Use the base as one of the three <a href="/wiki/index.php?title=Congruency_(geometry)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Congruency (geometry) (page does not exist)">congruent</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Isosceles_triangle" title="Isosceles triangle">isosceles triangles</a>, with the height being one of the edges of the pyramid that sits on the edges of the cube. The height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/8/078d702c8c14d3e782b87b1208c96f2178c7ebbe.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac x{\sqrt{2}} = 1 - \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="110" height="41" />. The base is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4fe048f5c1382916a77470eb42a56f76de35b789.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90 \triangle$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="14" /> with leg of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/1/971dc40edb08c40e312197c118e10939411cb371.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="58" height="41" />, making its area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/2/132e699b2574003cba6df19b6105c120cf6061ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12\left(1 - \frac 1{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = \frac{3 - 2\sqrt{2}}4$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="200" height="46" />. Plugging this in, we get that the volume of one of the tetrahedra is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/6/0660ad9dc1ec95808f68ff87244e4ffa34f59240.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 13 \left(1 - \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\left(\frac{3 - 2\sqrt{2}}4\right) = \frac{10 - 7\sqrt{2}}{24}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="302" height="53" />. Since there are 8 removed corners, we get an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/a/41ac82578fd2a3e0b8047e21e987154946c1fb3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10 - 7\sqrt{2}}{3} \Longrightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="141" height="41" />
</p>
