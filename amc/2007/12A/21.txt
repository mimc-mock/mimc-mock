<p>The sum of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Root" class="mw-redirect" title="Root">zeros</a>, the product of the zeros, and the sum of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coefficient" title="Coefficient">coefficients</a> of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Function" title="Function">function</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/3/bf34a339362e0ff0d2d7ffd1f49e4af029a7ca4a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=ax^{2}+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="157" height="19" /> are equal. Their common value must also be which of the following? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/4/964b00c9f7e6439b7bc27f1f9dd708b28c0f91bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{(A)}\ \textrm{the\ coefficient\ of\ }x^{2}~~~ \textrm{(B)}\ \textrm{the\ coefficient\ of\ }x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="355" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/f/2af53481be050d1b950fed002213742b0f009313.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{(C)}\ \textrm{the\ y-intercept\ of\ the\ graph\ of\ }y=f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="324" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4ce1ecfe49c439b5938bf9d752be7a18c2f815be.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{(D)}\ \textrm{one\ of\ the\ x-intercepts\ of\ the\ graph\ of\ }y=f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="381" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbe750773d618584a11917f253e97d77c50e6a5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textrm{(E)}\ \textrm{the\ mean\ of\ the\ x-intercepts\ of\ the\ graph\ of\ }y=f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="422" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>, the sum of the roots of a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_equation" title="Quadratic equation">quadratic equation</a> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/a/69adcf50f5152711464f6d19c156782cb894ae3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {-b}a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="24" height="37" />,  the product of the zeros is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd38c0b006565bd66f7cdf9f9a3cbfa955673f52.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac ca$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" />, and the sum of the coefficients is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/d/2cdfeae33c1e09de6207d446beb72396c04be481.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />. Setting equal the first two tells us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/b/8ab386776004a2e966c99eb2b1b2e69f31239af9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {-b}{a} = \frac ca \Rightarrow b = -c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="146" height="37" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/3/c03697e404927ba1f9df0d81727302bd6c356413.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = a + b - b = a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="197" height="14" />, so the common value is also equal to the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/a/d5aaea5f5a0c59fbe5728346845f4340e2dfe32e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 \Longrightarrow \fbox{\textrm{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="81" height="23" />. 
</p><p>To disprove the others, note that:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/3/0630a19ec36e5e78a5a38b29446b59f240a3f75d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{B}$" width="12" height="12" />: then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/4/5d49f94f216301e2069778d62824d2acb8282c5b.png" class="latex" alt="$b = \frac {-b}a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="56" height="37" />, which is not necessarily true.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/7/5875c5dc39c062d1166ed1e25f1258033fc0d9b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{C}$" width="12" height="13" />: the y-intercept is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/e/c2e81179b6de9066673e2aab733f36d0ff90a581.png" class="latex" alt="$c = \frac ca$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="33" />, not necessarily true.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/d/f0d45378143e9d318692101f621a56b88327d722.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{D}$" width="13" height="12" />: an x-intercept of the graph is a root of the polynomial, but this excludes the other root.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/7/a07e655cd29678d48f0577dc2f5c5d1dda102c12.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{E}$" width="12" height="12" />: the mean of the x-intercepts will be the sum of the roots of the quadratic divided by 2.</li></ul>
