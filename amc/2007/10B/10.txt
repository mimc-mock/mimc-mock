<p>Two points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> are in a plane. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of all points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> in the plane for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdd1b53e5faf7a118222e4b7571d1e9afa0cf56.png" class="latex" alt="$1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> Which of the following describes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/7/4d753026173a63cfed0ea918be191cc3b451677e.png" class="latex" alt="$S?$" width="20" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe74e4a21010a01b0d89d05808fa721bf3351fed.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \text{two parallel lines} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \text{a parabola} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \text{a circle} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \text{a line segment} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \text{two points}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="738" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> be the length of the altitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919b9f77a6985e760286799909be5bfe0152f40e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC.$" width="61" height="13" /> Since segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> is the base of the triangle and cannot change, the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/f/6ffe619487b7618d571860c7320df3f6d66fc805.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(BC)(h)=1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22a13af4a02b62333954b95dafccffe9f5526f30.png" class="latex" alt="$h=\frac{2}{BC}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="71" height="37" /> Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> consists of two lines parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> and is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/1/1c1ad4840999a0c451718e9495dce925b748a8a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{BC}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="31" height="37" /> units away from it. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbde511d99e0b6bf37158e9dd7976fa2430d9c6e.png" class="latex" alt="$\longrightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A) \ } \text{two parallel lines}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="202" height="29" />
</p>
