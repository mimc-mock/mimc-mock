<p>All sides of the convex pentagon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a96240c51d61ba3733d9f5c3020fa92ec4136.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDE$" width="72" height="13" /> are of equal length, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/f/7ef11006736d9bb341a1b52c8e7a3778c38cd37f.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A= \angle B = 90^\circ.$" width="132" height="13" /> What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/1/e910d0ce39399cf0da968c29f0b8c03a00702652.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle E?$" width="35" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/4/ad4bad945d50306497c0d504dd702738cf2dc034.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 90 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 108 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 120 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 144 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 150$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="434" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center>
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/a/b1adac4a39fceaa691b5d5346eee30cd5e1094fc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1.5cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); pair A=(0,2), B=(0,0), C=(2,0), D=(2+sqrt(3),1), E=(2,2); draw(A--B--C--D--E--cycle); draw(E--C,gray); draw(rightanglemark(B,A,E)); draw(rightanglemark(A,B,C)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,E); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,NE); [/asy]" width="312" height="178" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a953c9242ed8faa37ed5ac0b7e10d893a6dfe17e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = EC$" width="81" height="13" /> because they are opposite sides of a square. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f57c93c3ea32056e8f5cab7ae02e9225a9cc0d1f.png" class="latex" alt="$ED = DC = AB$" width="136" height="13" /> because all sides of the convex pentagon are of equal length. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/9/249aa48167a0ec4808fb235a2796eaffab883dba.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCE$" width="56" height="13" /> is a square and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d6098ce09f6f2b36bb447d30083315c8c0ca9c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CED$" width="60" height="13" /> is an equilateral triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/0/440597a9110750e37d8e07ed4129756f8879dcce.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AEC = 90$" width="97" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/2/da24e0efc6bd48572d7b911236c32f9e3ed78288.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CED = 60.$" width="103" height="12" /> Use angle addition:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7676dfaafe286eed98677436adb6d221a5797049.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle E = \angle AEC + \angle CED = 90 + 60 = \boxed{\textbf{(E)} 150}\]" width="359" height="28" />
</p>
