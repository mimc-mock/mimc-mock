<p>A player chooses one of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. After the choice has been made, two regular four-sided (tetrahedral) dice are rolled, with the sides of the dice numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/1/cb15a16cca90d201094265ee529e9e2cfb113de8.png" class="latex" alt="$4.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" /> If the number chosen appears on the bottom of exactly one die after it has been rolled, then the player wins <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar. If the number chosen appears on the bottom of both of the dice, then the player wins <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> dollars. If the number chosen does not appear on the bottom of either of the dice, the player loses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar. What is the expected return to the player, in dollars, for one roll of the dice?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/1/3217106e68d41d5242e630f89a34aa9615a19b88.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } -\frac{1}{8} \qquad\textbf{(B) } -\frac{1}{16} \qquad\textbf{(C) } 0 \qquad\textbf{(D) } \frac{1}{16} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="430" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/e/1be5867f704e37c6578ec1148398c21821a9567e.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 3 \cdot 1 = 6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" /> ways for your number to show up once, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/f/76fe31c8abe611e194e05cc4edbb9378573eb602.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \cdot 1 = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="12" /> way for your number to show up twice, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a08f8b84240fbf97ba60819f475b53b1a2295b9e.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 3 = 9$" width="64" height="12" /> ways for your number to not show up at all. Think of this as a set of sixteen numbers with six <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />'s, one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and nine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />'s. The average of this set is the expected return to the player.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/b/75be7467f74e920f61e20d6784fe11fd29f6872a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{6(1)+1(2)-9(1)}{16} = \frac{6+2-9}{16} = \boxed{\mathrm{(B) \ } -\frac{1}{16}}\]" width="356" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We approach this through casework. We have a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/4/a64e32a43ec15d3fe0bde0077b68dffbf025506c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{3}{4} \cdot 2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="61" height="38" /> chance of earning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar. We have a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/4/444c68dd724bfd929ea4674c357a930fa639f343.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="37" height="37" /> chance of earning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> dollars, and a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/5/d05c630587c9c3cb17ed17a64252008d9efe8d91.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="37" height="38" /> chance of losing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar. Thus, our final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/8/1a8be87f732e6861828ca10823c38503a99d3f63.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{8} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{16} \cdot 2 + \frac{9}{16} \cdot -1 = \boxed{\mathrm{(B) \ } -\frac{1}{16}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="295" height="48" />  -SuperJJ
</p>
