<p>Right <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/9220fbf9b857057ce8ebf126a73345075914fca6.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=3, BC=4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="136" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/e/3de655228c3d678063f24bb8b8b4acea69c06e7a.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=5.$" width="64" height="13" /> Square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b19f8dbfc953dc92e24445d3a1b26f5fd120eff.png" class="latex" alt="$XYZW$" width="64" height="12" /> is inscribed in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/e/66e3c8aa5c0ff967ac6e36ae99795caf4117b7ee.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}, W$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="19" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f694ab8315c055ae7dd5d9fe5ba57a1a7c97a99.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a54a3be7e759a747245725fab3ded3d9e2e2540.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}.$" width="32" height="15" /> What is the side length of the square?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/9/0392ede8ff49cd688062dd92d84ea7abac0569a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{3}{2} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \frac{60}{37} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{12}{7} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \frac{23}{13} \qquad\textbf{(E)} 2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="389" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2007AMC10B21.png" class="image"><img alt="2007AMC10B21.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//d/da/2007AMC10B21.png" width="347" height="208" /></a></div></div>
<p>There are many similar triangles in the diagram, but we will only use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/3/473e185ef7fd5cdcfabeac6d4bf7ee967c9a0da3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle WBZ \sim \triangle ABC.$" width="150" height="13" /> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the sidelength of the square, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb9f00d4cda9d17ac0047e45340885204b2f828.png" class="latex" alt="$h-s$" width="40" height="12" /> is the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b71133f5874e8f7d9ecac40f95ba5724a68724f6.png" class="latex" alt="$WZ.$" width="36" height="12" /> By similar triangles,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/0/6504f4a1af2a0b102bffb2e23bcc2126b906a866.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{h-s}{s}&amp;=\frac{h}{5}\\ 5(h-s)&amp;=hs\\ 5h-5s&amp;=hs\\ 5h&amp;=s(h+5)\\ s&amp;=\frac{5h}{h+5} \end{align*}" width="151" height="159" />
</p><p>Find the length of the altitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919b9f77a6985e760286799909be5bfe0152f40e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC.$" width="61" height="13" /> Since it is a right triangle, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4db0dbc4cac70485f532703143d1b0c995e43b86.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(3)(4) = 6.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />
</p><p>The area can also be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/378343a4ce08678cddcd727c7deb74f634a3497f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(5)(h),$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28e855ca6250b8e88f422f008b4b7d3004c8027f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}h=6 \longrightarrow h=2.4.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="157" height="37" />
</p><p>Substitute back into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09d9c01a214955f96b174391be96b26abf545cac.png" class="latex" alt="$s.$" width="12" height="8" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/2/2428823e03c93fa3658d6a943a086222219dd6eb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s=\frac{5h}{h+5} = \frac{12}{7.4} = \boxed{\mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{60}{37}}\]" width="220" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" /> be the side length of the inscribed square. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/d/2ad6d92d74708cbe89ca4498e213a4baf9d6bd97.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ZYC \sim \triangle WBZ \sim \triangle ABC$" width="230" height="13" />.
</p><p>Then we can setup the following ratios:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/0/7204a7763a44bff02492d4aeabcdd6ccccb0a685.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{CZ}{l} = \frac{5}{3} \rightarrow CZ = \frac{5}{3}l\]" width="166" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/9/0295445dd41ed387652802a841dc47f2f39d511d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{ZB}{l} = \frac{4}{5} \rightarrow ZB = \frac{4}{5}l\]" width="167" height="37" />
</p><p>But then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/9/0e9e434cb5d8af8f591079117d2ddd69447f94ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{3}l+\frac{4}{5}l = CZ+ZB = CB = 4 \longrightarrow \frac{37}{15}l=4 \longrightarrow l = \frac{60}{37} \Longrightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\frac{60}{37}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="542" height="48" />
</p>
