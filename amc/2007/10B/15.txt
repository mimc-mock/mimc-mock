<p>The angles of quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96eccef7588e23271a0d263c87e3cc997c726c06.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A=2 \angle B=3 \angle C=4 \angle D.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="213" height="13" /> What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/a/78ac988be3830a77b0fa5078a20f28a509eb0939.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> rounded to the nearest whole number?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/812d49d03e5f27d38366c60334433e4d4119883e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 125 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 144 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 153 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 173 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 180$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb6d9aa13776acda22b9a6d94e65077438a4625.png" class="latex" alt="$360^\circ.$" width="38" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/b/beb6f3e3ca45085449c65f3480bc70219a99345c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 360 &amp;= \angle A + \angle B + \angle C + \angle D\\ &amp;= \angle A + \frac{1}{2}A + \frac{1}{3}A + \frac{1}{4}A\\ &amp;= \frac{12}{12}A + \frac{6}{12}A + \frac{4}{12}A + \frac{3}{12}A\\ &amp;= \frac{25}{12}A \end{align*}" width="261" height="149" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/8/288a357b357408d940cbe49d2de74f1f15a2fc78.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle A = 360 \cdot \frac{12}{25} = 172.8 \approx \boxed{\mathrm{(D) \ } 173}\]" width="275" height="37" />
</p>
