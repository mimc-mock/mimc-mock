<p>Two distinct lines pass through the center of three concentric circles of radii 3, 2, and 1. The area of the shaded region in the diagram is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/d/cbdd6533c2882af567cedd2174829ff136094530.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> of the area of the unshaded region. What is the radian measure of the acute angle formed by the two lines? (Note: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> radians is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb1b41850ab676e4d1cf15c69b1609af4befbc5.png" class="latex" alt="$180$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> degrees.)
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9afaa1f9ed8ef23f107f3e17a274aa0a17aeb3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(85); fill((-30,0)..(-24,18)--(0,0)--(-24,-18)..cycle,gray(0.7)); fill((30,0)..(24,18)--(0,0)--(24,-18)..cycle,gray(0.7)); fill((-20,0)..(0,20)--(0,-20)..cycle,white); fill((20,0)..(0,20)--(0,-20)..cycle,white); fill((0,20)..(-16,12)--(0,0)--(16,12)..cycle,gray(0.7)); fill((0,-20)..(-16,-12)--(0,0)--(16,-12)..cycle,gray(0.7)); fill((0,10)..(-10,0)--(10,0)..cycle,white); fill((0,-10)..(-10,0)--(10,0)..cycle,white); fill((-10,0)..(-8,6)--(0,0)--(-8,-6)..cycle,gray(0.7)); fill((10,0)..(8,6)--(0,0)--(8,-6)..cycle,gray(0.7)); draw(Circle((0,0),10),linewidth(0.7)); draw(Circle((0,0),20),linewidth(0.7)); draw(Circle((0,0),30),linewidth(0.7)); draw((-28,-21)--(28,21),linewidth(0.7)); draw((-28,21)--(28,-21),linewidth(0.7));[/asy]" width="142" height="142" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/f/1df956a70f1ecade85c32aa0066c86f1e185beea.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{\pi}{8} \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{\pi}{7} \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac{\pi}{6} \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{\pi}{5} \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac{\pi}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="370" height="33" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the area of the shaded region be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />, the area of the unshaded region be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/b/e2bbebb3bd73f1ae5c64098ab0244f739abf7ca4.png" class="latex" alt="$U$" width="14" height="12" />, and the acute angle that is formed by the two lines be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" />. We can set up two equations between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/b/e2bbebb3bd73f1ae5c64098ab0244f739abf7ca4.png" class="latex" alt="$U$" width="14" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/4/3e44785cc8aae3adf6be4ee99857eeb3435e11b6.png" class="latex" alt="$S+U=9\pi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/3/113c122232a1da59c862a1efd02a5cb552f70066.png" class="latex" alt="$S=\dfrac{8}{13}U$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="72" height="38" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4d137c4d99eaa5b2d67fbc6cd754b0f51ab1a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{21}{13}U=9\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="80" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/f/96f54ecb8d87e257c9ac556391f08baea6a2c9a6.png" class="latex" alt="$U=\dfrac{39\pi}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="70" height="37" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/206d36902ac866bf1dbfbfb89918322b71d39b8d.png" class="latex" alt="$S=\dfrac{8}{13}\cdot \dfrac{39\pi}{7}=\dfrac{24\pi}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="160" height="38" />.
</p><p>Now we can make a formula for the area of the shaded region in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/e/ece80f6b5630e09d3829014f41c5b7948f866379.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2\theta}{2\pi} \cdot \pi +\dfrac{2(\pi-\theta)}{2\pi} \cdot (4\pi-\pi)+\dfrac{2\theta}{2\pi}(9\pi-4\pi)=\theta +3\pi-3\theta+5\theta=3\theta+3\pi=\dfrac{24\pi}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="640" height="38" />
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/1/171167538f988273e44a2178d63880d4416f47f2.png" class="latex" alt="$3\theta=\dfrac{3\pi}{7}\Rightarrow \theta=\dfrac{\pi}{7}\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{\pi}{7}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="230" height="44" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As mentioned in Solution #1, we can make an equation for the area of the shaded region in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659ee0f6d4069d2d3f020c986608fb94a85784f8.png" class="latex" alt="$\implies\dfrac{2\theta}{2\pi} \cdot \pi +\dfrac{2(\pi-\theta)}{2\pi} \cdot (4\pi-\pi)+\dfrac{2\theta}{2\pi}(9\pi-4\pi)=\theta +3\pi-3\theta+5\theta=3\theta+3\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="628" height="38" />.
</p><p>So, the shaded region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a19d5d9c7796bfa25b566b6d79e3cc62ea99a923.png" class="latex" alt="$3\theta+3\pi$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="60" height="14" />. This means that the unshaded region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/0/080ddb0a93c63663aebe4f1d636defbe6f397f96.png" class="latex" alt="$9\pi-(3\theta+3\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" />.
</p><p>Also, the shaded region is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/d/cbdd6533c2882af567cedd2174829ff136094530.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> of the unshaded region. Hence, we can now make an equation and solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" />.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa0dc82c5491d83fba64e802b51ae8bbf919658e.png" class="latex" alt="$3\theta+3\pi=\frac{8}{13}(9\pi-(3\theta+3\pi)\implies 39\theta+39\pi=8(6\pi-3\theta)\implies 39\theta+39\pi=48\pi-24\theta$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="697" height="38" />.
</p><p>Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/8/4c84bec6f976f4fe77fa07081417cb6fabe0a249.png" class="latex" alt="$63\theta=9\pi\implies \theta=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{\pi}{7}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="213" height="44" />
</p>
