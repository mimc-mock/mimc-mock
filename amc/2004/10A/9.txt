<p>In the overlapping <a href="/wiki/index.php/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangles</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245ff420d808b2e09a77194551ff12dbfda05a19.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABE}$" width="58" height="13" /> sharing common <a href="/wiki/index.php/Edge" title="Edge"> side</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/8/068cc77328b74134c841e0f31b4fa2428d394477.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{EAB}$" width="55" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/0/0d089768b09c8f192985935b4efd1d34ab6094ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{ABC}$" width="55" height="13" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_angle" title="Right angle">right angles</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b44beb78e8be18179bfb7fe6b3424e7fb1aef32.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/4/2a4826138756e48059393331c30916b2b06430cf.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=6$" width="61" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/8/8982a7982c7df8159c047c172a2b90e531ee3865.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=8$" width="61" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f32db217d7630882c3441fa8821cd70a6958c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BE}$" width="29" height="15" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. What is the difference between the areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/a/acad5629f89ba033c79d7d59d7c0605d2a5cac48.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ADE}$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/8/738998ea6564224ed451aa403ba88be4a5cd47fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{BDC}$" width="60" height="13" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/d/37d48968371e6d998d5b4c40c47c99005f414ab9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); defaultpen(linewidth(0.4)); //Variable Declarations pair A, B, C, D, E;  //Variable Definitions A=(0, 0); B=(4, 0); C=(4, 6); E=(0, 8); D=extension(A,C,B,E);  //Initial Diagram draw(A--B--C--A--E--B); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,3N); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,NW);  //Side labels label(&quot;$4$&quot;,A--B,S); label(&quot;$8$&quot;,A--E,W); label(&quot;$6$&quot;,B--C,ENE); [/asy]" width="152" height="252" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/e/1eec0d175928e5a1f77fa50f166f575625ad9fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm {(A)}\ 2 \qquad \mathrm {(B)}\ 4 \qquad \mathrm {(C)}\ 5 \qquad \mathrm {(D)}\ 8 \qquad \mathrm {(E)}\ 9 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d77ee06209244e5b26cc40287c2ff258c1dad5a6.png" class="latex" alt="$AE \perp AB$" width="80" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/9/be9cd94d70a9017b5e82095dca9d79f7430317f7.png" class="latex" alt="$BC \perp AB$" width="80" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f6d7514d189ebae6d583ba746c831c20e7e57ff.png" class="latex" alt="$AE \parallel BC$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="76" height="19" />. By alternate interior angles and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/a/38a32b1f092cd9dd7de4eccec461822ac0c5e76c.png" class="latex" alt="$AA\sim$" width="46" height="13" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a4722fcc2b4681bf03f14047dc5e4b6b55d532.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE \sim \triangle CDB$" width="144" height="13" />, with side length ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/3/7b33bd495b7492f95385da7afb197cd34638cdfa.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Their heights also have the same ratio, and since the two heights add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/6521428d61f191fd653cae82d3ce10b5304e4a32.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{ADE} = 4 \cdot \frac{4}{7} = \frac{16}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="147" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/6250ca9a39dbb313a10949d977f583524ceb1572.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{CDB} = 3 \cdot \frac 47 = \frac {12}7$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="147" height="37" />. Subtracting the areas, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/3/00324b074de310d8cb3c8c6efca6904b43a95032.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \cdot 8 \cdot \frac {16}7 - \frac 12 \cdot 6 \cdot \frac{12}7 = 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="196" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/9/1a9376a323ba22e23f0934e2eca624d5e7c32bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="53" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/3/f631f7a53d834de52b718ed6b496689e9658eadf.png" class="latex" alt="$[X]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="18" /> represent the area of figure <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf0f7f89e567669b122e8c013b4ca11e37dd631.png" class="latex" alt="$[\triangle BEA]=[\triangle ABD]+[\triangle ADE]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="253" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/6/086fd4bca85db5d287e525a878f3d9038f61c369.png" class="latex" alt="$[\triangle BCA]=[\triangle ABD]+[\triangle BDC]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="254" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/2/e122ce5abe06381e17e2a1c78bd7f21bb086618f.png" class="latex" alt="$[\triangle ADE]-[\triangle BDC]=[\triangle BEA]-[\triangle BCA]=\frac{1}{2}\times8\times4-\frac{1}{2}\times6\times4= 16-12=4\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="743" height="37" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(coordbash)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28coordbash.29">Solution 3 (coordbash)</span></h3>
<p>Put figure <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a96240c51d61ba3733d9f5c3020fa92ec4136.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDE$" width="72" height="13" /> on a graph. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> goes from (0, 0) to (4, 6) and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f32db217d7630882c3441fa8821cd70a6958c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BE}$" width="29" height="15" /> goes from (4, 0) to (0, 8). <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> is on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/2/782f5ce1864af21821f8311b7f9145ae10e0a4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 1.5x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="67" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f32db217d7630882c3441fa8821cd70a6958c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BE}$" width="29" height="15" /> is on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04cddcba43112b0a49b7e10209fbdcf3149da2b5.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -2x + 8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" />. Finding intersection between these points,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45f7a890e91cad17c8b19cf92cd0062e30dd3604.png" class="latex" alt="$1.5x = -2x + 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="14" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd377a5c95e2bbfbb414b81c25dbce50b7fa06b.png" class="latex" alt="$3.5x = 8$" width="66" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/c/c8c001bc84d380077acf3c707e023bd89823c33f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 8 \times \frac{2}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/2/4e2099770d2b536f7cc6f1df2dbdb24355592223.png" class="latex" alt="$= \frac{16}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="39" height="37" />
</p><p>This gives us the x-coordinate of D. 
So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/a/60a55b66cc723625ed1733eba83737d56ec2682f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{16}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> is the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" />, then area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> is 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/e/5ee101bc7bff99b1eb518e5a4c87c311157aee86.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{16}{7} \times 8 \times \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c15db2bc0753257ca4a0d99595b10941495c13a.png" class="latex" alt="$= \frac{64}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="39" height="37" />
</p><p>Now, the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/7/1a7a0327109ede54bf3abf9eb325bb58a503afc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDC$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/9/64959529658d302a4dd50385f57ecdd5dab88b29.png" class="latex" alt="$4-\frac{16}{7} = \frac{12}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="37" />
And the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/7/1a7a0327109ede54bf3abf9eb325bb58a503afc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDC$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77dbe07df0390a573b9358c1ec39362fb7ba816a.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \times \frac{12}{7} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{36}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="133" height="37" />
</p><p>This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d262b06b05b0c270f1f2b4529ec24363cded8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{64}{7} - \frac{36}{7} = 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="100" height="37" />
</p><p>Therefore, the difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>We want to figure out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5e8ea2c390e32ee0d8ee8b99300ff14c909dc53.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle ADE) - Area(\triangle BDC)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="254" height="20" />.
Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/9/1598bab45a40d37f82c1c831ca6c67965a273d47.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BAE$" width="58" height="13" /> "intersect" and form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/8/e08af8f3acc34996e3cba566dc970b7ed496ba4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADB$" width="59" height="13" />.
</p><p>This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/7/c77bbf0049c6f8dc21c291ace3f321c3a96830d8.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle BAE) - Area(\triangle ABC) = Area(\triangle ADE) - Area(\triangle BDC)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="527" height="20" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/b/28bf45b80b5612ed3286fcb605cf2d963f6b7f07.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle ADB)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="116" height="20" /> cancels out, which can be seen easily in the diagram.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/e/d2ead6cabec1104822c8058bd946ccd8dbfc67b0.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle BAE) = 0.5 * 4 * 8 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="258" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/9/6f9cf732a5c9aa47a577961e65706841581c663b.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle ABC) = 0.5 * 4 * 16 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="267" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/f/14fea38481322700aa53eedf2e97b50c9ebf6dee.png" class="latex" alt="$Area(\triangle BDC) - Area(\triangle ADE) = 16 - 12 =\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="414" height="32" />
</p>
