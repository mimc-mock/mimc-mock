<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/4820e9c378ef42e0fe36c7d37d2bb816d542a300.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); defaultpen(fontsize(10)); pair A=(0,0), B=(2,0), C=(2,2), D=(0,2), E=(0,1/2), F=E+(C-E)/abs(C-E)/2; draw(A--B--C--D--cycle);draw(C--E); draw(Arc((1,0),1,0,180));draw((A+B)/2--F); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,(-1,-1)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,( 1,-1)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,( 1, 1)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,(-1, 1)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,(-1, 0)); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(A+E)/2,(-1, 0)); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(E+F)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(F+C)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(D+C)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(B+C)/2,( 1, 0)); label(&quot;$2-x$&quot;,(D+E)/2,(-1, 0)); [/asy]" width="252" height="222" />
Let the point of tangency be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Two_Tangent_Theorem" title="Two Tangent Theorem">Two Tangent Theorem</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/e/d2e9a4a1312f28628f8b96901d9496593a8a9b6d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = FC = 2$" width="114" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/7/267fb8ac2c5952065c03e8a451c4d2bf6b39f36e.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = EF = x$" width="115" height="13" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c5109cbfd9f2213ae5e2ac433af1d8262e77cc0e.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = 2-x$" width="95" height="12" />. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b984c55719cc5f992ee62656d920ac15c78d9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDE$" width="60" height="13" /> yields
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/8/ac85581edb8a0845caa297836bde528b05a51a66.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} DE^2 + CD^2 &amp;= CE^2\\ (2-x)^2 + 2^2 &amp;= (2+x)^2\\ x^2 - 4x + 8 &amp;= x^2 + 4x + 4\\ x &amp;= \frac{1}{2}\end{align*}" width="215" height="119" />
</p><p>Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/f/8ef9b7c30269b1b1043526d463153ec4863215f3.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = FC + x = \frac{5}{2} \Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{5}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="238" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Call the point of tangency point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/a/03a1d58c360bbc87c34aa7849f2e19c3447de15a.png" class="latex" alt="$CF=2$" width="61" height="12" /> by Tangent Theorem. Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb619026816a95f06de8d2032defdd74f37a468c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EGF=\frac{180-2\cdot\angle CGF}{2}=90-\angle CGF$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="331" height="37" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/d/11dff015e5f197be99d05ceb64f9106e3bfbd783.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EGF=\angle FCG$" width="135" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/f/41ffbae67cf60b2497b71ecf8c6da0266554dfcd.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan EGF=\tan FCG=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="208" height="38" />. Solving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/2/c5245e4b8f093644487df1e99fa0ce725bafc699.png" class="latex" alt="$EF=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />. Adding, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2004_AMC12A-18.png" class="image"><img alt="2004 AMC12A-18.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//e/ed/2004_AMC12A-18.png" width="550" height="358" /></a>
</p><p>Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f3740ccd672d2775c7e1ffcf8d397e37200319cd.png" class="latex" alt="$EA = EF = BG$" width="134" height="13" />. Thus, the sides of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/b/c7bc69328ef74607767b467b2a851f202438edec.png" class="latex" alt="$CDE$" width="44" height="12" /> are in arithmetic progression. Thus it is <a href="/wiki/index.php/Similar_triangles" class="mw-redirect" title="Similar triangles">similar</a> to the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc928c5117c0680e27803f980cf21fd83f0fc51.png" class="latex" alt="$3 - 4 - 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/8/c38c67505075cc5b0144b30b6e75fb3abe143da1.png" class="latex" alt="$DC = 2$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/8/108a54ed45c3e97ed7cfe2a6628a787fde318300.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = \frac{5}{2} \Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{5}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="152" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a945dc4c5d8e4d141e7fedc0828d0c0dbb79d5cc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(150); defaultpen(fontsize(10)); pair A=(0,0), B=(2,0), C=(2,2), D=(0,2), E=(0,1/2), F=E+(C-E)/abs(C-E)/2, G=(1,0); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle);draw(C--E); draw(Arc((1,0),1,0,180));draw((A+B)/2--F); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,(-1,-1)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,( 1,-1)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,( 1, 1)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,(-1, 1)); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,(-1, 0)); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(A+E)/2,(-1, 0)); label(&quot;$x$&quot;,(E+F)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(F+C)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(D+C)/2,( 0, 1)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,(B+C)/2,( 1, 0)); label(&quot;$2-x$&quot;,(D+E)/2,(-1, 0)); label(&quot;$G$&quot;,G,(0,-1)); dot(G); draw(G--C); label(&quot;$\sqrt{5}$&quot;,(G+C)/2,(-1,0)); [/asy]" width="252" height="222" />
</p><p>Let us call the midpoint of side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. Since the semicircle has radius 1, we can do the Pythagorean theorem on sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/b/61b53baf18405aa6ce6072c0ad38b866de6f5901.png" class="latex" alt="$GB, BC, GC$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" />. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da820830c0f30fe7a0fe09417ad03ad2cab326b.png" class="latex" alt="$GC=\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />. We then know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/a/03a1d58c360bbc87c34aa7849f2e19c3447de15a.png" class="latex" alt="$CF=2$" width="61" height="12" /> by Pythagorean theorem. Then by connecting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/927d4689159fbeb80f7b468ca9ae96b48e926312.png" class="latex" alt="$EG$" width="28" height="12" />, we get similar triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/a/6ca8e1ed0a0d34754cdd985dfc1d6683a066da7a.png" class="latex" alt="$EFG$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/9/7c96381ff2a2459e8fcf8c4d512dfb092c52334c.png" class="latex" alt="$GFC$" width="42" height="12" />. Solving the ratios, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a14649b556d4c316856b101be1b84a92084b195b.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f3e3e4ac1fa529bbe9581979190dd626d72029f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2} \Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{5}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="99" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Using the diagram as drawn in Solution 4, let the total area of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> be divided into the triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/8/5b8451bee5a68657a8f8d5c57de669c2de023c04.png" class="latex" alt="$DCE$" width="44" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/4/e2430dc69d6ac24f18117676e99de7f7af2a1b38.png" class="latex" alt="$EAG$" width="42" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/d/52d0b96b1ee923624fdda30eeb4bb6952f90d97a.png" class="latex" alt="$CGB$" width="43" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c9994edc1b1c9b6d3e67c618d63673925842e099.png" class="latex" alt="$EGC$" width="43" height="12" />. Let x be the length of AE. Thus, the area of each triangle can be determined as follows: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/d/3cd6f26e09e9e078fb0f0ff507f4aed26963d73e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[DCE = \frac{DC\cdot{DE}}{2} = \frac{2\cdot(2-x)}{2} = 2-x\]" width="317" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/c/1acfb9e9d6c8ae36e80d5310a8fec0d8a203ed72.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[EAG= \frac{AE\cdot{AG}}{2} = \frac{1\cdot{x}}{2} = \frac{x}{2}\]" width="236" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/4/3848485e72f2da8a85314106bed19ba998208854.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CGB = \frac{GB\cdot{CB}}{2} = \frac{1\cdot(2)}{2} = 1\]" width="248" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/f/e9f87c190a73787b6b6ce5550da4a4f170b3c80e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[EGC= \frac{EG\cdot{GC}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{5x^2 + 5}}{2}\]" width="241" height="40" /> (the length of CE is calculated with the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, lines GE and 
CE are perpendicular by definition of tangent)
</p><p>Adding up the areas and equating to the area of the total square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b4c05d8353cc1bc7f48d93a0f377f29b71820d6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2 \cdot 2=4)\]" width="75" height="18" />, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a3955e39c71c94e47977dc39fc2dae3d126fb96c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = \frac{1}{2}\]" width="45" height="37" />
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/4/a944bc8d18c0cdc2905ae7e4e3694413ba7a1cd4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[CE = 2 + \frac{1}{2} = 5/2\]" width="147" height="37" />.
