<p>Three circles of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> are externally tangent to each other and internally tangent to a larger circle. What is the radius of the large circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/f/7dfe6128d93d11ca58bb6fb5b947d91d747001c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{2 + \sqrt{6}}{3} \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 2 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac{2 + 3\sqrt{2}}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{3 + 2\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \frac{3 + \sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="561" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The situation is shown in the picture below. The radius we seek is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/7/1d7543c4e22ef5e113ce982fee2f419153d717a9.png" class="latex" alt="$SD = AD + AS$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="129" height="14" />. Clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd842e36ed64086a882c190267900ce65d1f7017.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. The point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is the center of the equilateral triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/8/0f861cdd52e65580668061b0eaf3c023960029f1.png" class="latex" alt="$AS$" width="25" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/b/38bd36e353df831303c0de895a9ec614cf3e7202.png" class="latex" alt="$2/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> of the altitude of this triangle. We get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94da2629f2bab6c8568172b863637c792608ef72.png" class="latex" alt="$AS = \frac23 \cdot \sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="37" />. Therefore the radius we seek is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/c/e3c0e1ffca5584f4d2588cfbe65bc33de4b444ec.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + \frac23 \cdot \sqrt 3 = \boxed{\mathrm{(D)\ }\frac{3+2\sqrt{3}}3}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="220" height="53" />.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/6/056b92c537632c2d904effcbd77cab0e9d61be72.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); pair A=(0,0), B=dir(0)*2, C=dir(60)*2; draw(circle(A,1)); draw(circle(B,1)); draw(circle(C,1)); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); draw(A--B--C--cycle);  pair D=A+dir(210), E=B+dir(-30), F=C+dir(90); draw(circumcircle(D,E,F)); dot(D); dot(E); dot(F);  pair S=(A+B+C)/3; dot(S);  draw(S--D); draw(S--E); draw(S--F);  label(&quot;$S$&quot;,S,S); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,ENE); [/asy]" width="408" height="412" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Using <a href="/wiki/index.php/Descartes%27_Circle_Formula" title="Descartes&#39; Circle Formula">Descartes' Circle Formula</a>, we can assign curvatures to all the circles: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5028215beac6db9339110bb79ff23669544430.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> (b/c the bigger circle is externally tangent to all the other circles, the radius of the bigger circle is negative). Then, we can solve:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/c/dec8242e8275295d108dc91d0fa76eb026c5b38b.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1^2+1^2+1^2+(-\frac{1}{r})^2) = (1+1+1-\frac{1}{r})^2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="339" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/4/a446b043698ca0bde483f403172768acadfcf0f8.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \boxed{\mathrm{(D)\ }\frac{3+2\sqrt{3}}3}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="147" height="53" />
</p>
