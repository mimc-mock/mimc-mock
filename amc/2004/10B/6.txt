<p>Which of the following numbers is a perfect square?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/3/be304be7ca92a9b1859e34a10b27a8f83f379222.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 98! \cdot 99! \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 98! \cdot 100! \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 99! \cdot 100! \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 99! \cdot 101! \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 100! \cdot 101!$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="638" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/0/ff06c43a1fea23ceef30b8f477e0df554af5e550.png" class="latex" alt="$n! = n\cdot (n-1)!$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="18" />, we can write:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/6/4c6a7be94573285f6b307d0d2c6ed71c6aab7771.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align} A&amp;=98! \cdot (99\cdot 98!) = 99 \cdot (98!)^2 = 11\cdot3^2\cdot(98!)^2 \\ B&amp;=100 \cdot 99 \cdot (98!)^2 = 11\cdot10^2\cdot3^2\cdot( 98!)^2 \\ C&amp;=100\cdot (99!)^2 = 10^2\cdot (99!)^2\\ D&amp;=101\cdot 100\cdot (99!)^2 = 101 \cdot 10^2 \cdot (99!)^2\\ E&amp; =101\cdot (100!)^2 \end{align}" width="425" height="130" />
</p><p>We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/d/3cdcea95e05ffe882f15dc7800925d0720627c27.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 99! \cdot 100!}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="113" height="29" /> is a square, and because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> are primes, none of the other four choices are squares.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd629034145b95656ef371c2f5abe03bd3df687.png" class="latex" alt="$99! \cdot 99!$" width="58" height="13" /> is a perfect square (obviously). Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef8573e266358e2e60508d81fba091e34120f5c1.png" class="latex" alt="$100 = 10^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="15" />. Thus, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea94e4bcc1fe32d3c88103e712c1e5fd0b38b686.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(99!)^2 \cdot 10^2 = 99! \cdot 100!\]" width="173" height="19" /> 
is a perfect square, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/d/3cdcea95e05ffe882f15dc7800925d0720627c27.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C) \ } 99! \cdot 100!}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="113" height="29" />.
</p>
