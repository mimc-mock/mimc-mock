<p>Two eight-sided dice each have faces numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. When the dice are rolled, each face has an equal probability of appearing on the top. What is the probability that the product of the two top numbers is greater than their sum?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7c660e7ffcb9aabe9b7532b51ce9bad2b969b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)\ }\frac{1}{2}\qquad\mathrm{(B)\ }\frac{47}{64}\qquad\mathrm{(C)\ }\frac{3}{4}\qquad\mathrm{(D)\ }\frac{55}{64}\qquad\mathrm{(E)\ }\frac{7}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="379" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/c/4dcd2b0a5da460aec1f544ecc88c175ddd1c8ba6.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times n = n &lt; 1 + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="143" height="13" />, hence if at least one of the numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the sum is larger. There <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> such possibilities.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/0/9c02bbf1d3fd616e7203353ad2d6d27f284cddd3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2 = 2+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="104" height="13" />.
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b9b7a46559a729577c3ca04dd2d7e66de16b3f.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> we already have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a3416e7cd2dd6f08414f51f5a9f0ea5f6be2021.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times n = n + n &gt; 2 + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="176" height="13" />, hence all other cases are good.
</p><p>Out of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/1970593e80703dfe31ef799575bae2ee9819ced1.png" class="latex" alt="$8\times 8$" width="40" height="12" /> possible cases, we find that in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/b/2abe0177095752b98d423414e471f595e938fea2.png" class="latex" alt="$15+1=16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" /> the sum is greater than or equal to the product, hence in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31b5ee6b4ce1f9264ed2b3787c33fb7821597ffb.png" class="latex" alt="$64-16=48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> cases the sum is smaller, satisfying the condition. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/e/fbef201d027845ecad621947e9a5f58fdbb976f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{48}{64}=\boxed{\mathrm{(C)\ }\frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="104" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the two rolls be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. 
</p><p>From the restriction: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/6/ff60c124fab5ec6139579a245c5e9f034b8799ab.png" class="latex" alt="$mn &gt; m + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/d/bbd5c7948ea29a841c7caef4b597ce3bf84e7c63.png" class="latex" alt="$mn - m - n &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="131" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/3/9d3b3f7f9dc7f1fcf75f03111109b44c467124b7.png" class="latex" alt="$mn - m - n + 1 &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/8862475efc61d1fd5686bab24771a99bf90a349a.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96eec32de93b032b6d531a03aa5b346156b62a75.png" class="latex" alt="$m-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> are non-negative integers between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, either  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95ac1737d39c40b9701736d5395dae469bd09767.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/8/dc83554e08028d416a8b4eb60df8a9be2f13d85e.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/8862475efc61d1fd5686bab24771a99bf90a349a.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95ac1737d39c40b9701736d5395dae469bd09767.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4b5800f5ad040d308609190ef18a9e0ca08a9b0.png" class="latex" alt="$m=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />. 
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4b5800f5ad040d308609190ef18a9e0ca08a9b0.png" class="latex" alt="$m=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pairs with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pair with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/b/a4b5800f5ad040d308609190ef18a9e0ca08a9b0.png" class="latex" alt="$m=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />. So, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd40dd563dee8cb012306c4cbabf69e1887cfdb6.png" class="latex" alt="$8+8-1 = 15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95ac1737d39c40b9701736d5395dae469bd09767.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/8/dc83554e08028d416a8b4eb60df8a9be2f13d85e.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8e8e07d4f7737c14605f5416a695915b2f97c2c.png" class="latex" alt="$m-1=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/0/c60ae6084525015b65167c039a0a3babba53e302.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="12" /> or equivalently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/3/6f3eaf8047a3f5bebfc5c43e27123a15a44d7540.png" class="latex" alt="$m=2$" width="48" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/b/6cb7ae00764b56ee2adf59e78a1ffde9685b80db.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2$" width="43" height="12" />. This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> ordered pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57e6523f425ff3d81ea3cd39d32269c92ca6d0c2.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n) = (2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" />. 
</p><p>So, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/b/2abe0177095752b98d423414e471f595e938fea2.png" class="latex" alt="$15+1=16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/1/1a1e7270aeaf17b2b1a27314ea48f744aacb505f.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Since there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/2/38268a86911b029b797eb3974dfe71e64e446430.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot8 = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/9/da96ae6fd92607752dead4758ad4dab9aaf9de41.png" class="latex" alt="$64-16 = 48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> ordered pairs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/6/8862475efc61d1fd5686bab24771a99bf90a349a.png" class="latex" alt="$(m-1)(n-1) &gt; 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Thus, the desired probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/e/fbef201d027845ecad621947e9a5f58fdbb976f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{48}{64}=\boxed{\mathrm{(C)\ }\frac{3}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="104" height="48" />.
</p>
