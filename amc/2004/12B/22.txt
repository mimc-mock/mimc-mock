<p>The square
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/9/2b91968b87cc7e340fb458ae8e44a75714a7622d.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|} \hline 50 &amp; \textit{b} &amp; \textit{c} \\ \hline \textit{d} &amp; \textit{e} &amp; \textit{f} \\ \hline \textit{g} &amp; \textit{h} &amp; 2 \\ \hline \end{tabular}$" style="vertical-align: -26px" width="96" height="62" /></center>
<p>is a multiplicative magic square. That is, the product of the numbers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same. If all the entries are positive integers, what is the sum of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/8/868f9baee23f9da98426d54a762dec37be624df2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 10 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 25 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 35 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 62 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 136$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>All the unknown entries can be expressed in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />.
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/6/c36ba6f9a25a62195c8edeac770d79070162c336.png" class="latex" alt="$100e = beh = ceg = def$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="189" height="16" />, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/195d586913f9ee340322597ef0c5674a104453bb.png" class="latex" alt="$h = \frac{100}{b}, g = \frac{100}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="137" height="37" />,
and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/8/a08295e7d632c6c8e509a2bd3631c49b23aff70d.png" class="latex" alt="$f = \frac{100}{d}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" />. Comparing rows <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> then gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d37c20b780752ae904eb16aa292f4adef51a9068.png" class="latex" alt="$50bc = 2 \cdot \frac{100}{b} \cdot \frac{100}{c}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="154" height="37" />,
from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/d/a0d8374b7197a99f9415889047d1ddc688705c24.png" class="latex" alt="$c = \frac{20}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />.
Comparing columns <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab97df9393382c7f2153d86adb72805f521e3967.png" class="latex" alt="$50d \cdot \frac{100}{c}= 2c \cdot \frac{100}{d}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="156" height="37" />,
from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/674b3c27ce7718400b3ee4607e09518e82187019.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \frac{c}{5} = \frac{4}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="82" height="37" />.
Finally, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd99f8ec82d80c65dc20bc75e4bb83a9d771e764.png" class="latex" alt="$f = 25b, g = 5b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3d4ed0ff144f61790e320dc78a734e954b4fa31.png" class="latex" alt="$e = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" />. All the entries are positive integers
if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/c/eac9e0f69cc18ea58ba6dd8607e32a127b6890a5.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1, 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. The corresponding values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/1/bd109f5a07306aa4f9e52931a3edd9ef8c76261c.png" class="latex" alt="$5, 10,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" /> and
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />, and their sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9b870bc94e453eef061e1006af660b3d1e92f29.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathbf{(C)}35}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="59" height="29" />.
</p><p>Credit to Solution B goes to <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://billingswest.billings.k12.mt.us/math/AMC%201012/AMC%2012%20work%20sheets/2004%20AMC%2012B%20ws-15.pdf">http://billingswest.billings.k12.mt.us/math/AMC%201012/AMC%2012%20work%20sheets/2004%20AMC%2012B%20ws-15.pdf</a>, a page with a play-by-play explanation of the solutions to this test's problems.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know because this is a multiplicative magic square that each of the following are equal to each other:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f878f038805f46be88e05a93acf23cde48f39c9b.png" class="latex" alt="$100e=ceg=50dg=beh=2cf=50bc=def=2gh$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="413" height="16" />
</p><p>From this we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/7/a2782c5fed621ec441e3efe72684477ed59acc97.png" class="latex" alt="$50dg=2hg$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="16" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab977cf3bf508a0f194df2e1385a34aea9e60adc.png" class="latex" alt="$h=25d$" width="62" height="12" />.
Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/f/1ef33734cddb11cdd49c8c5388750148910f556b.png" class="latex" alt="$beh=be(25d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/687e40964969a72cd355dafbe29776c87a85d7fa.png" class="latex" alt="$be(25d)=100e$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="18" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7c5c5b8940114da1f4e270a6b9bf347103cd7ec.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac{4}{d}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" />
From this we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0723873e03b47c2e333e26a23c6df06cd0fce43c.png" class="latex" alt="$50bc=(50)(\frac{4}{d})(c)=50dg$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="201" height="37" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f204c228ecab09f8417c584f438c636876cc67a.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\frac{d^2g}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="60" height="39" />.
Now we know from the very beginning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/914b4c3f2801efbf10383e895fe54c3dd301f156.png" class="latex" alt="$100e=ceg$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/27019b7396ff196e3e4f366c298fc918c855a838.png" class="latex" alt="$100=cg$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="68" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/d/37d8536f4df06252d9d2d30bf7f816ac6d0b076b.png" class="latex" alt="$100=\frac{d^2g}{4}(g)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="103" height="39" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/9/7a95f782559a733b736059a2aae82b357f6b95c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{d^2g^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="36" height="39" />. Rearranging the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97db732abc5f16aa46b518b9a60df3f67e88579c.png" class="latex" alt="$100=\frac{d^2g^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="87" height="39" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/1/d5161c7f6b4fb088fe202500dc9616a221474830.png" class="latex" alt="$(d^2)(g^2)=400$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="19" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e7682f42a636918423c2b104fa5aa85b40b97d.png" class="latex" alt="$dg=20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> due to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> both being positive. Now that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/e/56e7682f42a636918423c2b104fa5aa85b40b97d.png" class="latex" alt="$dg=20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> we find all pairs of positive integers that multiply to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. There is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626c99110560adfbf2b1345a94d88075484fa364.png" class="latex" alt="$(d,g)= (20,1);(10,2);(5,4);(4,5);(2,10);(1,20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="382" height="18" />. Now we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7c5c5b8940114da1f4e270a6b9bf347103cd7ec.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac{4}{d}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> and b has to be a positive integer. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> can only be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> can only be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />,or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b163f9438f098a069cd442382020ad848532a49.png" class="latex" alt="$20+10+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7f6c79f2148f0cec592c9517b40ee1b82ef22e.png" class="latex" alt="$35$" width="17" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9b870bc94e453eef061e1006af660b3d1e92f29.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathbf{(C)}35}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="59" height="29" />.
</p>
