<p>The graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34831b9eadcb62e10bad479e226ccd953c0dc5bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2 + xy + 3y^2 - 11x - 20y + 40 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="291" height="18" /> is an ellipse in the first quadrant of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />-plane. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the maximum and minimum values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/9/3f9d06b1ffe0694bd2be52b3bb95a8733098e367.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac yx$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> over all points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> on the ellipse. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/d/9dd5e63df32eca344900b89f4d5d561b67a98913.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 3 \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \sqrt{10} \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 72 \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 92 \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 2\sqrt{14}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="408" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2004_12B_AMC-21.png" class="image"><img alt="2004 12B AMC-21.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//3/36/2004_12B_AMC-21.png" width="203" height="264" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/9/3f9d06b1ffe0694bd2be52b3bb95a8733098e367.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac yx$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> represents the slope of a line passing through the origin. It follows that since a line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8cab3b86d4a13c3cc3680c32aaa80cc854689e.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> intersects the ellipse at either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/f/c3f41feba960f5daf82e943291e40d85d0379a79.png" class="latex" alt="$0, 1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> points, the minimum and maximum are given when the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8cab3b86d4a13c3cc3680c32aaa80cc854689e.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> is a tangent, with only one point of intersection. Substituting,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/1/7810f02a1d842903268ef4e400f6355a7262fa0b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2x^2 + x(mx) + 3(mx)^2 - 11x - 20(mx) + 40 = 0\]" width="383" height="19" />
Rearranging by the degree of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db7670b17d2532db53dee1c1c5e8c1cc7a786c5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(3m^2 + m + 2)x^2 - (20m + 11)x + 40 = 0\]" width="327" height="19" />
Since the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/1/0d1fe0afdea68630af2bda225ce5f2978b5ba0be.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> is tangent to the ellipse, we want the discriminant, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b41331a8f9d71b05e9dae9769abf4b648dce70.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(20m+11)^2 - 4\cdot 40 \cdot (3m^2 + m + 2) = -80m^2 + 280m - 199\]" width="477" height="19" />
to be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. We want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, which is the sum of the roots of the above quadratic. By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%E2%80%99s_formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta’s formulas">Vieta’s formulas</a>, that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/8/5f8ad526281a8417d9dcc3501a392d4d2b225701.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{280}{80} = \frac{7}{2} \Rightarrow \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="122" height="37" />.
</p>
