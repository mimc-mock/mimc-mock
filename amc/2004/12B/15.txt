<p>The two digits in Jack's age are the same as the digits in Bill's age, but in reverse order. In five years Jack will be twice as old as Bill will be then. What is the difference in their current ages?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a28c5effd9fda698b67efcdff9a1759fd5a7e330.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 9 \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 18 \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 27 \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 36\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 45$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="378" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If Jack's current age is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/e/a5e8d95dc7748dd5c11312c321785208f430ae21.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{ab}=10a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="17" />, then Bill's current age is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/1/e511fce1987f103c5cc8d2b595cca7e0f7acab44.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{ba}=10b+a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="17" />.
</p><p>In five years, Jack's age will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/6/a16b5b179db5fdb7f5f30bc25a94344147ee8f28.png" class="latex" alt="$10a+b+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="14" /> and Bill's age will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31b180f8438ab5a1e01288acba785945d81fdc56.png" class="latex" alt="$10b+a+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="14" />. 
</p><p>We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b719a986dae2689b7cc83d9932a7e0c01d2a3ab4.png" class="latex" alt="$10a+b+5=2(10b+a+5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="224" height="18" />. 
</p><p><br />
Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/8/c18327375419f8ebdc90056820b9b68058d7d8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$8a=19b+5 \Rightarrow a=\dfrac{19b+5}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="222" height="37" />. 
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf590e9abb33aa9776ddc386b1ba6ee7b43fa413.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340605c5f88cf6864adc5b146c3dffca7a45df22.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3$" width="42" height="12" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" /> the value <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3b046447229594aac0f43b92d55f4322b98b9f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{19b+5}8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" /> is not an integer, and for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6abec3a69863b7137579b586c175c92efafd1d5.png" class="latex" alt="$b\geq 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus the only solution is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/c/28c149933e245c140475c79ab8b42f1a5f5af545.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)=(3,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />, and the difference in ages is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b54f1d475d9bf00c0258de86bd599f2b2f8863.png" class="latex" alt="$31-13=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)\ }18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="145" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Age difference does not change in time. Thus in five years Bill's age will be equal to their age difference.
</p><p>The age difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e564b5147959124e441a6cdfa628831c883be9cf.png" class="latex" alt="$(10a+b)-(10b+a)=9(a-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="252" height="18" />, hence it is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus Bill's current age modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>Thus Bill's age is in the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/0/510b74c982b2297e1bebb539536deafcbec7000c.png" class="latex" alt="$\{13,22,31,40,49,58,67,76,85,94\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="18" />.
</p><p>As Jack is older, we only need to consider the cases where the tens digit of Bill's age is smaller than the ones digit. This leaves us with the options <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b4afde67c5617e84dcf7b596bdac19631d42ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\{13,49,58,67\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Checking each of them, we see that only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> works, and gives the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b54f1d475d9bf00c0258de86bd599f2b2f8863.png" class="latex" alt="$31-13=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)\ }18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="145" height="29" />.
</p>
