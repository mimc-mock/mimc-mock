<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are on the parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/0/b50ba49988458e285a4ebda604c769d6808e7582.png" class="latex" alt="$y=4x^2+7x-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="132" height="18" />, and the origin is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. What is the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/7/e67a8872f3dad306c3eb22e15d0fbbf50603025b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 2\sqrt5 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 5+\frac{\sqrt2}{2} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 5+\sqrt2 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 7 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 5\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="488" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff4de5aff3a578973a7ac3aa9f84b9ea69e7510c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x_A,y_A)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> lies on the parabola, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0945a3e0ba78fb474f6218b89d8e0ddee96aeca8.png" class="latex" alt="$y_A=4x_A^2+7x_A-1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="157" height="19" />.
As the origin is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/9/b49c4a8fdd43165dd3d978e209c358db77117a2d.png" class="latex" alt="$(-x_A,-y_A)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" />.
We need to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18fc18a66926b39254d09908f38cf0ab8c24ddb7.png" class="latex" alt="$x_A$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="10" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> will lie on the parabola as well. In other words, we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1155044d4cab5631a8ce2db6dcebc68e6127ff.png" class="latex" alt="$-y_A = 4(-x_A)^2 + 7(-x_A) - 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="235" height="19" />.
</p><p>Substituting for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/4/9c427e33bd38e90e34efb7c65e3efeef770253c7.png" class="latex" alt="$y_A$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="11" />, we get: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b27522eee065a08811a11420ee0fcebf9b16524.png" class="latex" alt="$-4x_A^2 - 7x_A + 1 = 4(-x_A)^2 + 7(-x_A) - 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="329" height="19" />.
</p><p>This simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/7/477c3d3402ed8e1f3bd450cb7339cc4be74423a7.png" class="latex" alt="$8x_A^2 - 2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="19" />, which solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39c89646128b63c348740f570b6595ee2ec5dba3.png" class="latex" alt="$x_A = \pm 1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />. Both roots lead to the same pair of points: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69c4a98ec52cd27c71fe63e6c9e5be533dd44bc0.png" class="latex" alt="$(1/2,7/2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/2/1027c2cd1c8439d1e1e139870fe9449feca750b9.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1/2,-7/2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />. Their distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90a4ed0685230cbd7bcda27514a672ae77ea20af.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{ 1^2 + 7^2 } = \sqrt{50} = \boxed{5\sqrt2}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="197" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternate_Solution">Alternate Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/c/33c987e7c50f087b4e5d1b36c0d42819bf205144.png" class="latex" alt="$(x_A, y_A)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/1/961bab0b7b753fab09edff3d881ee1dae5e1a521.png" class="latex" alt="$(x_B, y_B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />, respectively. Since the median of the points lies on the origin, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/3/1b3cbca8ebcaabc9e28b944fb1d59e23acad790b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_A + x_B = y_A + y_B = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="184" height="16" /> and expanding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/5/3b5d8bf233ac75a71f528393fa2e4ed2941b2487.png" class="latex" alt="$y_A + y_B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="14" />, we find:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/2/332d505f905b0df56bda847ab6cb771e9a8ed25f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x_A^2 + 7x_A - 1 + 4x_B^2 + 7x_B - 1 = 0\]" width="284" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/d/b0d8ec99843741914f691865bf38b51cdde05739.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4(x_A^2 + x_B^2) + 7(x_A + x_B) = 2\]" width="231" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5da12f63c06b93e5c90bbcb57627c12a34280ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_A^2 + x_B^2 = \frac{1}{2}.\]" width="105" height="37" />
</p><p>It also follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/5/de57ba922f38a32b480863389e4f8ffccd8751c6.png" class="latex" alt="$(x_A + x_B)^2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="19" />. Expanding this, we find:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/8/588f3eb919a7f639c0c37137ee37b6a2892b7b00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_A^2 + 2x_A x_B + x_B^2 = 0\]" width="172" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12e864113eedb4a4d33977aa8cb2cfefa220e57a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2} + 2x_A x_B = 0\]" width="118" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/7/b374b51e27ff7539c33dcac9f9c0620ccb0573e5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_A x_B = -\frac{1}{4}.\]" width="97" height="37" />
</p><p>To find the distance between the points, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/d/61d18d273d6c872e571f76cf53777232d5c36c79.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(x_A - x_B)^2 + (y_A - y_B)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="211" height="32" /> must be found. Expanding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/b/0ab1c48f356a6fba5425864c0919ab1a82b09c88.png" class="latex" alt="$y_A - y_B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="11" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dadbe8da4462f5b9d9a4b745d6a625682cfe1df0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y_A - y_B = 4x_A^2 + 7x_A - 1 - 4x_B^2 - 7x_B + 1\]" width="336" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/c/dbcc4502200a4616a57fd956c4037550c25bbe0a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= 4(x_A^2 - x_B^2) + 7(x_A - x_B)\]" width="215" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6e6eb17e122fef3e50b985d3f341fd87e7d489f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= 4(x_A + x_B)(x_A - x_B) + 7(x_A - x_B)\]" width="295" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/0/390303e0446e8287104471c28a97b3f7d47b5544.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= 7(x_A - x_B)\]" width="105" height="18" />
we find the distance to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/d/5cdbd214d6e43d4283321e636d94dc3c31c403ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{50(x_A - x_B)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="123" height="32" />. Expanding this yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/4/5f478a8767a9ba372d193ba50ff33df6a60a4775.png" class="latex" alt="$5\sqrt{2(x_A^2 + x_B^2 - 2x_A x_B)} = \boxed{5\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="259" height="33" />.
</p>
