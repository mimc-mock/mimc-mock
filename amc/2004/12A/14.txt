<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Sequence" title="Sequence">sequence</a> of three real numbers forms an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Arithmetic_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Arithmetic progression">arithmetic progression</a> with a first term of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is added to the second term and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> is added to the third term, the three resulting numbers form a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_progression" class="mw-redirect" title="Geometric progression">geometric progression</a>. What is the smallest possible value for the third term in the geometric progression?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/8/cb86d0721d883d9bb19b0f9a1e74435361789efe.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)}\ 1 \qquad \text {(B)}\ 4 \qquad \text {(C)}\ 36 \qquad \text {(D)}\ 49 \qquad \text {(E)}\ 81$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the common difference. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/2/8a2f750a1c4123ecde172bab3385f88cfe74c743.png" class="latex" alt="$9+d+2=11+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="146" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/8/67835723b1f7f983b6ce6a1ffd898ce49a05c2d0.png" class="latex" alt="$9+2d+20=29+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="173" height="14" /> are the terms of the geometric progression. Since the middle term is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_mean" title="Geometric mean">geometric mean</a> of the other two terms, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/0/9d0d1f8758c79498feb83bb61ffc89eca9d5bae9.png" class="latex" alt="$(11+d)^2 = 9(2d+29)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="19" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/d/4fd62f4c6157b5be124dc929aa754b0a843b9302.png" class="latex" alt="$\Longrightarrow d^2 + 4d - 140$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="141" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/f/8afdc48c9df6eaaf29644fc446f1b65e17cc1f85.png" class="latex" alt="$= (d+14)(d-10) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="18" />. The smallest possible value occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e802a83d8454950c98d3668eb654770cd1a21511.png" class="latex" alt="$d = -14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="13" />, and the third term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/7/7e7e996d8a41568f4c6b892b8a2d630145847cc7.png" class="latex" alt="$2(-14) + 29 = 1\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="211" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the common difference and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the common ratio. Then the arithmetic sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c76e585885f3bf5af886f622d20e61d0290acfc6.png" class="latex" alt="$9+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/c/9bcfb5fbd2fc1db88ed5433fad9f5c6607cb211d.png" class="latex" alt="$9+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />. The geometric sequence (when expressed in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />) has the terms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/3/4a374ff0273b35409faec5b1b91249ad390b04f0.png" class="latex" alt="$11+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b0724a4f8543ea69e35f5dc3cf54c0e59132179.png" class="latex" alt="$29+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" />.  Thus, we get the following equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/6596a078060297c7d71636ff933328f46e10576e.png" class="latex" alt="$9r=11+d\Rightarrow d=9r-11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="211" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/c/72c6a0dce4ff599b79f867ca52b6ea98c2c009cc.png" class="latex" alt="$9r^2=29+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="108" height="16" />
</p><p>Plugging in the first equation into the second, our equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/6/5e60dbe1e1a8571f52b443d5f2f9e0ebde497c2f.png" class="latex" alt="$9r^2=29+18r-22\Longrightarrow9r^2-18r-7=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="335" height="16" />. By the quadratic formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> can either be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/4/b44eecfc043d7b666561dbe643e5fc3b9fc80db6.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/f/e1f2bce73877ab24e4abe8ae41191c7620c66c1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.  If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/4/b44eecfc043d7b666561dbe643e5fc3b9fc80db6.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" />, the third term (of the geometric sequence) would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/f/e1f2bce73877ab24e4abe8ae41191c7620c66c1b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, the third term would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.  Clearly the minimum possible value for the third term of the geometric sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/070ebb795e7bad2abc593cb0ef40257f77c2a53c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let the three numbers be, in increasing order, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/6/3c65e8e12d6e772147b3167e4a0a83dc7862c480.png" class="latex" alt="$9,y,z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="17" />
</p><p>Hence, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/4/96414051687521e05997e73b17462f8e5dd0d3c4.png" class="latex" alt="$9-y=y-z\implies 9+z=2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="238" height="16" />.
</p><p>Also, from the second part of information given, we get that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/b/ddb9993c1d4e1b8392af5f3e8093b7389433c955.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9}{y+2}=\frac{y+2}{z+20}\implies 9(z+20)=(y+2)^2\implies y=3(\sqrt{z+20})-2$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="534" height="41" />
</p><p>Plugging back in...
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/5/e05fb7d8ef4315da4899c8df3170355be77cf3b5.png" class="latex" alt="$9+z=6(\sqrt{z+20})-4\implies (9+z)^2=36(z+20)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="400" height="20" />
</p><p>Simplifying, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3de1860c133d25c1bb3ee4267a814d80764a7c1.png" class="latex" alt="$z^2-10z-551=0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="148" height="15" />
</p><p>Applying the quadratic formula, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/d/c0df648a3f30e981f2123841036cc79aa71f9abd.png" class="latex" alt="$z=\frac{10\pm \sqrt{2304}}{2}\implies \frac{10\pm48}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="240" height="41" />
</p><p>Obviously, in order to minimize the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, we have to subtract. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/e/c1e6afb7e7bbd60d9c6ee7d3f391bd15e058d0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$z=-19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="13" />
</p><p>However, the problem asks for the minimum value of the third term in a geometric progression.
</p><p>Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/4/ef4717d8c93165bfed1800b517bd62ccdbe35d79.png" class="latex" alt="$-19+20=\boxed{1} \implies \boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="192" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let the arithmetic sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/4/bb4c9550155b55cb41d4d011742e15e1d58e9097.png" class="latex" alt="$9,9+x,9+2x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="117" height="16" /> and let the geometric sequence be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/8/bc8c4003c84edb822294f1ea3d38efa1e8e96e67.png" class="latex" alt="$9,11+x,29+2x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="16" />. Now, we just try all the solutions. If the last term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/2/0221e69f48f3a1a1182922b05ea5eee07352f4a6.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="12" />. This gives the geometric sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/4/eb4e4b48c555392b5564d5796740365b30194e46.png" class="latex" alt="$9,-3,1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" /> which indeed works. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/c/a2c8a95f70c0fc882e09542c63c36835273896c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1} \implies \boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="95" height="24" />
</p><p>Solution by franzliszt
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>The terms of the arithmetic progression are 9, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c76e585885f3bf5af886f622d20e61d0290acfc6.png" class="latex" alt="$9+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/c/9bcfb5fbd2fc1db88ed5433fad9f5c6607cb211d.png" class="latex" alt="$9+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" /> for some real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. The terms of the geometric progression are 9, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/3/4a374ff0273b35409faec5b1b91249ad390b04f0.png" class="latex" alt="$11+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b0724a4f8543ea69e35f5dc3cf54c0e59132179.png" class="latex" alt="$29+2d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" />. Therefore<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/317aa7a89bbbe5310b038f8be6659371fad344c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(11+d)^{2} = 9(29+2d) \quad\text{so}\quad d^{2}+4d-140 = 0.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="370" height="19" />Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/f/48f49c25bebad7fde170b47bef3a86c188ed2c0b.png" class="latex" alt="$d=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/5/f5571178b257f8eb1cbd24c226f2d236b2d1cf98.png" class="latex" alt="$d=-14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="13" />. The corresponding geometric progressions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/7/177edf16f349ab054858fbcb8a7f1e53e79c2dee.png" class="latex" alt="$9, 21, 49$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/d/ecd3c626cb84787dc859e382826d44c61ea9fa6c.png" class="latex" alt="$9, -3, 1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="16" /> so the smallest possible value for the third term of the geometric progression is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/c/a2c8a95f70c0fc882e09542c63c36835273896c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1} \implies \boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="95" height="24" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_6(Partially_Brute_Force)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6.28Partially_Brute_Force.29">Solution 6(Partially Brute Force)</span></h3>

Then you will find that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6eb65962292d994ce2f9a8af63728883f256b18.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="24" />.
