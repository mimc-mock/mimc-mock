<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Polynomial" title="Polynomial">polynomial</a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/3/be39b3d4524a3a3ebd6afcdbbf78f21186dfa863.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(x) = c_{2004}x^{2004} + c_{2003}x^{2003} + ... + c_1x + c_0\]" width="352" height="19" />
</p><p>has <a href="/wiki/index.php/Real" class="mw-redirect" title="Real">real</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coefficient" title="Coefficient">coefficients</a> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/e/a6ee7e962df3af21105dd1fa9af734da84f5a11e.png" class="latex" alt="$c_{2004}\not = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ae760d1ea10eb7a90d22087b3c7e2393d02117.png" class="latex" alt="$2004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> distinct complex <a href="/wiki/index.php/Zero" title="Zero">zeroes</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/a/21a9c0f8b68352372c3cb690d2728b2ada3ce739.png" class="latex" alt="$z_k = a_k + b_ki$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/7/2b7266538fa53ab7a514de3b1cea423670509ea8.png" class="latex" alt="$1\leq k\leq 2004$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="103" height="15" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/a/bea4d576937a04ec200b1037fc8ee33e8f744cb1.png" class="latex" alt="$a_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/b/b6bdec4394870e8a713538ce428bea9d41c8775f.png" class="latex" alt="$b_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="15" /> real, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c65211d9ab6c9bd1af0f71a4194c6395c9791e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 = b_1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="89" height="15" />, and
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/8/088ed1cd3d91f1acfc5dcedf987cb2799891cd91.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{a_k} = \sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{b_k}.\]" width="120" height="50" />
</p><p>Which of the following quantities can be a non zero number?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a903f18f1cc78cfc467b1a380e198def56cec2.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)} c_0 \qquad \text {(B)} c_{2003} \qquad \text {(C)} b_2b_3...b_{2004} \qquad \text {(D)} \sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{a_k} \qquad \text {(E)}\sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{c_k}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="486" height="50" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We have to evaluate the answer choices and use process of elimination:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f4283cfd441270cb90bfd2cf89a533f3dde81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />: We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c65211d9ab6c9bd1af0f71a4194c6395c9791e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 = b_1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="89" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/78566a764f1fae77bd2d8419ce4697581f5115a1.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />. If one of the roots is zero, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ed77e1f41105bf2e59e41673c4dc500eb58cf58.png" class="latex" alt="$P(0) = c_0 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />: By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e633ad2a17f081c517b33c4f4a4a1b6864b81df.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{c_{2003}}{c_{2004}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="51" height="35" /> is the sum of all of the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />. Since that is real, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d822d7f62a3778d63ce32ea610ee7dd08d646379.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{b_k}=0=\sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{a_k}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="149" height="50" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bc2d67753bc47cec47654b809eb962339ba0f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{c_{2003}}{c_{2004}}=0$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="72" height="35" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e962de373f5c9a03e720b589cad1ec641478f5.png" class="latex" alt="$c_{2003}=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="15" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484f88154fadcf9c781bba19becdae88696b77f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />: All of the coefficients are real. For sake of contradiction suppose none of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cdef8b7e5038334e5abfcc7689243fa6fc1b6a.png" class="latex" alt="$b_{2\ldots 2004}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" /> are zero. Then for each complex root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f756c0f47c2c000ff11cc29f13381232ff401aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, its <a href="/wiki/index.php/Complex_conjugate" title="Complex conjugate">complex conjugate</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b1766ea42e0ae098ac7a98b6291fb41c425ca6b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{z_k} = a_k - b_k i$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="17" /> is also a root. So the roots should pair up, but we have an odd number of imaginary roots! (Remember that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32f25f30e2bf419190d9774c0dd676b8d91f4b36.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" />.) This gives us the contradiction, and therefore the product is equal to zero.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />: We are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efa92cb40ff17c0d81038a2b0623ba1d2df5049d.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{a_k} = \sum_{k = 1}^{2004}{b_k}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="116" height="50" />. Since the coefficients are real, it follows that if a root is complex, its conjugate is also a root; and the sum of the imaginary parts of complex conjugates is zero. Hence the RHS is zero.</li></ul>
<p>There is, however, no reason to believe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea2b8f6b62721fc3e02ca622cd6da092a31bbc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="24" /> should be zero (in fact, that quantity is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/7/797a20647c81f3259c5069556cab51c94bf4b752.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" />, and there is no evidence that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />).
</p>
