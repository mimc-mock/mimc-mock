<p>Select numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> independently and at random, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be their sum. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/f/bef747a48a2a8d43c4841e12c89f2de2502a672e.png" class="latex" alt="$A, B$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be the results when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3834f0cef2a1f4d8524aa5bc8e3faca298550862.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, respectively, are rounded to the nearest integer. What is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Probability" title="Probability">probability</a> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/3/1d3d99747227e632f73e535a91acaac5526825ed.png" class="latex" alt="$A + B = C$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/0/b2073745bd8e74d06c71a90b3b0cfffd9e832e6a.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)}\ \frac14 \qquad \text {(B)}\ \frac13 \qquad \text {(C)}\ \frac12 \qquad \text {(D)}\ \frac23 \qquad \text {(E)}\ \frac34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="349" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Casework" title="Casework">Casework</a>:
</p>
<ol><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe74e7ca51b767bc13e0e0cd53e58e5800de01b4.png" class="latex" alt="$0 + 0 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. The probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/8/a689cea3a462529e80c535f6a136415467a822a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/4/7f48e067b6ed915c30d5f09181e7c54ae3912d18.png" class="latex" alt="$b &lt; \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/7/62702114499f41b4dd5f0858d29ce49e7112cf6e.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac 12\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="82" height="46" />. Notice that the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> ranges from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> with a symmetric distribution across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e86c22d83ea01eb477116a1414a9adb33f5cbc4b.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=c=\frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" />, and we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/e/f5e3bc115262648447eaae2cd7df5314b8bb82cf.png" class="latex" alt="$c &lt; \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" />. Thus the chance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/c/70cdd6f508ad2154563a4f188b4689b03778f97c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{4}}2 = \frac 18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="40" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff4783cf49551c2d97df6a2f98006089c5938fa3.png" class="latex" alt="$0 + 1 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. The probability that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/b/84b1f7185e790c58926a7ea111524a1388799932.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f99a057e418b97d4c51600ff198b1b6bbdc0a488.png" class="latex" alt="$b &gt; \frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d48fb052d7fa270cfe6d5a0b5ef1f1cb1c2ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />, but now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/9/139e38b2333e6d2728e543237c387e57e768888c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} &lt; a+b = c &lt; \frac 32$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="145" height="37" />, which makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e3435fdaadad9195048f672956b8e6bef8db23.png" class="latex" alt="$C = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" /> automatically. Hence the chance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d48fb052d7fa270cfe6d5a0b5ef1f1cb1c2ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/586e64c2b6aebf903e473aa7f46f730d232ab6b3.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 0 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. This is the same as the previous case.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/9/349b6b8a00581fb06095c20113d8fa49f3f5cf9c.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 1 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="13" />. We recognize that this is equivalent to the first case.</li></ol>
<p>Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/7/997497fba04318b01f1a6a17dafaaf08dd70e985.png" class="latex" alt="$2\left(\frac 18 + \frac 14 \right) = \frac 34 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="177" height="43" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Use areas to deal with this continuous probability problem. Set up a unit square with values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> on x-axis and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> on y-axis.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/e/efea22aeb207eeb0b1544fb0ab814ca5a1752ba0.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b &lt; 1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> then this will work because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/6/036eaaa5ac4842d013f88736c4e5df9eacd01e9f.png" class="latex" alt="$A = B = C = 0$" width="124" height="13" />. Similarly if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/c/5acbf5e7f35784b085597153f77ee3c46c541bea.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b &gt; 3/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> then this will work because in order for this to happen, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are each greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3cc0ad966b3e637cd199607cbf0d1befc0e491.png" class="latex" alt="$1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> making <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/917f20abc977fee360d76a224e6e8529f03e2a79.png" class="latex" alt="$A = B = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="85" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/d/13d38e76d7d5fcfc00ea546b2219585c058823e9.png" class="latex" alt="$C = 2$" width="47" height="12" />. Each of these triangles in the unit square has area of 1/8.
</p><p>The only case left is when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/e/77e3435fdaadad9195048f672956b8e6bef8db23.png" class="latex" alt="$C = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="13" />. Then each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> must be 1 and 0, in any order. These cut off squares of area 1/2 from the upper left and lower right corners of the unit square.
</p><p>Then the area producing the desired result is 3/4. Since the area of the unit square is 1, the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/1/d21715ef3fb94d12fc089969a648efb480cd5b6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 34$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />.
</p>
