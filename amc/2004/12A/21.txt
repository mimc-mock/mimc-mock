<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b3eb2e21a67f8b12ae0c6fc9b815e7ab085d199.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n = 0}^{\infty}{\cos^{2n}}\theta = 5$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="110" height="48" />, what is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/4/13404ffc5c317889f69dbe8ca491cc4b8fc73e7d.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{2\theta}$" width="45" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/8/f6819fd867759c26c70103f42eeef0b4865d70ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A)} \frac15 \qquad \text {(B)} \frac25 \qquad \text {(C)} \frac {\sqrt5}{5}\qquad \text {(D)} \frac35 \qquad \text {(E)}\frac45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="336" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>This is an infinite <a href="/wiki/index.php/Geometric_series" class="mw-redirect" title="Geometric series">geometric series</a>, which sums to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/d/5cd41d77f15bfbcc48f2b63865e2b861290fa85a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\cos^0 \theta}{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = 5 \Longrightarrow 1 = 5 - 5\cos^2 \theta \Longrightarrow \cos^2 \theta = \frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="391" height="39" />. Using the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70f406203af967c7559f9b61552a4ac9872d5bf2.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos 2\theta = 2\cos^2 \theta - 1 = 2\left(\frac 45\right) - 1 = \frac 35 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="356" height="43" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12ec554fccc37c1deb6cf7079a0c91039098e697.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sum_{n = 0}^{\infty}{\cos^{2n}}\theta = \cos^{0}\theta + \cos^{2}\theta + \cos^{4}\theta + ... = 5\]" width="347" height="48" />
</p><p>Multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/c/94c5add9a5ddb86c2398bcb4622bc347f344ec37.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos^{2}\theta$" width="43" height="15" /> to get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/9490d6446fce03624c048ad7195270634def7715.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos^{2}\theta + \cos^{4}\theta + \cos^{6}\theta + ... = 5*\cos^{2}\theta\]" width="307" height="16" />
</p><p>Subtracting the two equations, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/b/5eb6bf302469c6990405ea0ca6c34934a34185d9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos^{0}\theta=5-5*\cos^{2}\theta\]" width="168" height="15" />
</p><p>After simplification, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/4/1940f4021be5ba65e4441f70416118e4e02b732a.png" class="latex" alt="$cos^{2}\theta=\frac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="37" />. Using the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70f406203af967c7559f9b61552a4ac9872d5bf2.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos 2\theta = 2\cos^2 \theta - 1 = 2\left(\frac 45\right) - 1 = \frac 35 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="356" height="43" />.
</p>
