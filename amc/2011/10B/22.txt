<p>A pyramid has a square base with sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and has lateral faces that are equilateral triangles. A cube is placed within the pyramid so that one face is on the base of the pyramid and its opposite face has all its edges on the lateral faces of the pyramid. What is the volume of this cube?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac27c2b91c8aa7184a343c2e7fdf5732243242d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5\sqrt{2} - 7 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 7 - 4\sqrt{3} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{27} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{9} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="525" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>It is often easier to first draw a diagram for such a problem.
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2011AMC10B22.png" class="image"><img alt="2011AMC10B22.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//c/ce/2011AMC10B22.png" width="260" height="181" /></a></div></div>
<p>Sometimes, it may also be easier to think of the problem in 2D. Take a cross section of the pyramid through the apex and two points from the base that are opposite to each other. Place it in two dimensions.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/5/215607fef86013352901dcb40b988542f35becbf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(35mm); defaultpen(linewidth(2pt)+fontsize(10pt)); pair A=(0,0), B=(sqrt(2),0), C=(0.5sqrt(2),0.5sqrt(2)); pair W=(sqrt(2)-1,0), X=(1,0), Y=(1,sqrt(2)-1), Z=(sqrt(2)-1,sqrt(2)-1); draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(W--X--Y--Z--cycle,red); [/asy]" width="238" height="122" />
</center>
<p>The dimensions of this triangle are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/2/a8278a1bb48ff3531325b96aed3a86516f2a698a.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> because the sidelengths of the pyramid are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and the base of the triangle is the diagonal of the pyramid's base. This is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> triangle. Also, we can let the dimensions of the rectangle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/9/6798d54ee20abfb2ce2cd64969ac98c1836282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$s\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> because the longer length was the diagonal of the cube's base and the shorter length was a side of the cube.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/6/c360ad03631afaf0bd9850333d36ecfc30161c02.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(35mm); defaultpen(linewidth(2pt)+fontsize(12pt)); pair A=(0,0), B=(sqrt(2),0), C=(0.5sqrt(2),0.5sqrt(2)); pair W=(sqrt(2)-1,0), X=(1,0), Y=(1,sqrt(2)-1), Z=(sqrt(2)-1,sqrt(2)-1); draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(W--X--Y--Z--cycle,red); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(A--C),NW); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(B--C),NE); label(&quot;$\sqrt{2}$&quot;,(A--B),S);  label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(W--Z),E,red);  label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(X--Y),W,red);  label(&quot;$s\sqrt{2}$&quot;,(W--X),N,red); [/asy]" width="238" height="148" />
</center>
<p>The two triangles on the right and left of the rectangle are also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> triangles because the rectangle is perpendicular to the base, and they share a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8adb649f063149ebc97d3ee58c4fd38d4c440846.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> angle with the larger triangle. Therefore, the legs of the right triangles can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09d9c01a214955f96b174391be96b26abf545cac.png" class="latex" alt="$s.$" width="12" height="8" />
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/d/d0dbfd874b642e9b3edf55e50e44d677f4eed432.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(35mm); defaultpen(linewidth(2pt)+fontsize(12pt)); pair A=(0,0), B=(sqrt(2),0), C=(0.5sqrt(2),0.5sqrt(2)); pair W=(sqrt(2)-1,0), X=(1,0), Y=(1,sqrt(2)-1), Z=(sqrt(2)-1,sqrt(2)-1); draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(W--X--Y--Z--cycle,red); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(A--C),NW); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,(B--C),NE); label(&quot;$\sqrt{2}$&quot;,(A--B),S); label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(W--Z),E,red);  label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(X--Y),W,red);  label(&quot;$s\sqrt{2}$&quot;,(W--X),N,red); label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(A--W),N); label(&quot;$s$&quot;,(X--B),N);  [/asy]" width="238" height="148" />
</center>
<p>Now we can just use segment addition to find the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09d9c01a214955f96b174391be96b26abf545cac.png" class="latex" alt="$s.$" width="12" height="8" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741b40bf46711a835adac9f9d3258c9c4171bb21.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt{2}=s+s\sqrt{2}+s=2s+s\sqrt{2}=(2+\sqrt{2})s\]" width="340" height="21" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18f6b6c3290938ff60834f8795c20bbe60b8fa0a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2+\sqrt{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}+1}=\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2-1}=\sqrt{2}-1\]" width="338" height="45" />
</p><p>The volume of the cube is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9df15c496c1b07ca09e80f474f7caeac24a025d2.png" class="latex" alt="$s^3 = (\sqrt{2}-1)^3 = (\sqrt{2}-1)(3-2\sqrt{2}) = 3\sqrt{2}-3-4+2\sqrt{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(A)} 5\sqrt{2}-7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="595" height="33" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be the slant height and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the distance from one side of the base to the point right under the apex. Then using the Pythagorean Theorem, the altitude from the apex to the base is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/5/4358c4e4d1204f34d1e1986f476b5d35fa669265.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a=\sqrt{s^2-d^2}=\sqrt{\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2-\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2}=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\]" width="330" height="63" />
Notice that the smaller pyramid on top of the cube is similar to the larger pyramid. Thus, letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the edge length of the cube, then the altitude of the smaller pyramid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f07397ea94c92e570df06ea147468b9ee75f2e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot{\dfrac{x}{1}}=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}x$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="120" height="43" />.
</p><p>Since the altitude of the larger pyramid is the sum of the edge length of the cube and the altitude of the smaller pyramid, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd995e3f8d9a4bb77ca48c60ba30023f98304076.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}=x+\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}x\]" width="121" height="41" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c76bca694227457fa0288624308ce9532f5e3a6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x=\sqrt{2}-1\]" width="88" height="18" />
</p><p>Finally, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/8/4d8bfec64a11af0da4adbe2329a3eeaad0f7a283.png" class="latex" alt="$V_{cube}=x^3=(\sqrt{2}-1)^3=\boxed{\textbf{(A)} 5\sqrt{2}-7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="310" height="33" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
