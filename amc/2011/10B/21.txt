<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>

</p><p>The smallest difference, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> must be directly between two integers. This also means the differences directly between the other two should add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb13a1ea7b9fd6892ebe9599c3587aa68288c873.png" class="latex" alt="$8.$" width="12" height="12" /> The only remaining differences that would make this possible are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> However, those two differences can't be right next to each other because they would make a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a9fb4c5b23667e57f28d844ad99715f18ba3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> which isn't given as a possibility in the problem. This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> must be the difference between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/3/fc3be04db73828946870d3ccc6d122395a4bacf6.png" class="latex" alt="$x.$" width="14" height="8" /> We can express the possible configurations as the lines.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, like Solution 1, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/5/c05b837b3f52caf254b4e85022238b26e2c40e42.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z=9 \ \text{(1)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="17" />, because no two numbers could have a larger difference. Next, we find the sum of all the differences; since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> is in the positive part of a difference 3 times, and has no differences where it contributes as the negative part, the sum of the differences includes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/e/0ce3fe965a1d829cbdd8c35441b11687e90a7ce8.png" class="latex" alt="$3w$" width="22" height="12" />. Continuing in this way, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb562ead08862aa5f73f847f7a7198f11d5aba32.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3w+x-y-3z=28 \ \text{(2)}\]" width="195" height="17" />. Now, we can subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/2/a82a5485863606aa5dffa6e29a451286ebd2aa38.png" class="latex" alt="$3w-3z=27$" width="105" height="12" /> from (2) to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/3/4e33d96a14a8edd332e8d596b7624d88e95955fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=1 \ \text{(3)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="17" />. Also, adding (2) with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/7/627b0389dfce75a504d763dc730b45add9a9d960.png" class="latex" alt="$w+x+y+z=44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="15" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/9/479e4d7ef5a0a0f06a7a671103bfff3132727c26.png" class="latex" alt="$4w+2x-2z=72$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="146" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66630a6b6318933f4819826ac49050248d0c27c7.png" class="latex" alt="$2w+x-z=36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="128" height="14" />. Subtracting (1) from this gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b48c238bf6012a7207de9b4ab8b1356e19fcc3e.png" class="latex" alt="$w+x=27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="13" />. Since we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f5438bdb7b43b6e6fe0956b8095fda5125bd886b.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z$" width="44" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/643e1765342cf3807fd7d130ee679b71ecd4789b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/e/02e2ad0c5e7a55f91f3bbc7f781b8c4c97cb5266.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(w-z)+(x-y)=(w-y)+(x-z)=9+1=10\]" width="404" height="18" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee18632400da79ebf7a59c16b0de29d4a4640e09.png" class="latex" alt="$w-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33d7af37b081eb59a5a444341be7872f5977813d.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z$" width="41" height="8" /> must be 4 and 6, in some order. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/e/7de259f18b1f184568578dab7db54895dde7ee06.png" class="latex" alt="$w-y=6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="78" height="16" />, then subtracting this from (3) gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/e/fde51e6f7247504851ef086f95f4d0e712f153bf.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-y)-(x-y)=6-1=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="235" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/3/623e52308a5d6fc13c11823ad40c3e03d0a626b6.png" class="latex" alt="$w-x=5$" width="79" height="12" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/c/b8c690417c2b620e631682d60fc53cba5237ddab.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-x)+(w+x)=2w=27+5=32$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="305" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/c/07c8b3ba314c2746b20b6ab7bfad432130dc07f8.png" class="latex" alt="$w=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> can also equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>Now if you are in a rush, you most likely would have answered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/e/7be68d2bc53613586941511f1ed59624bdb233fe.png" class="latex" alt="$16+15=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }31}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="147" height="28" />. But we do have to check if these work. In fact, they do, giving solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/c/bcc8fcbaa4afe8267cc2dec4cb5b2c3a215a3d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$(w,x,y,z)=(16, 11, 10, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="205" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/671758856840451bb36429248a56a8dec1b204a1.png" class="latex" alt="$(w,x,y,z)=(15, 12, 11, 6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="205" height="18" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/c/17c14504e1bc49a6743257ca1f1fc36f3018d384.png" class="latex" alt="$w - x = a$" width="80" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/3/0735f5e95fd248c3a5dfc23677bfc133fcea7c97.png" class="latex" alt="$w - y = b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/6245814935c023b035ccba7159045a799fa10a52.png" class="latex" alt="$w - z = c$" width="77" height="8" />. As above, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa469f5533743cf3ed01982d13681820ffaa3e0.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 9$" width="40" height="12" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/2/7c286a6b2750b9c5c514b55b7f52660b1f66c21f.png" class="latex" alt="$a &lt; b &lt; c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />. 
So, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/e/3ae23847f7acd77470847dacfdc6ac885e5c6a3a.png" class="latex" alt="$w + x + y + z = w + (w - a) + (w - b) + (w - 9) = 4w - a - b - 9 = 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="572" height="18" />. This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/87681124c7391cf2b60fbbfd539ffd0ed657da94.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + 9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Testing values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/c/8ac2e908c17b25339806926169f94d36526e55e4.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, b, c) = (1, 6, 9), (3, 4, 9),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/d/2edf69914f498c6c18ad2cb7f6c9d41c3d3ab120.png" class="latex" alt="$(5, 6, 9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> all satisfy this relation. The corresponding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/7/48774f3f189de86deae7c27d0fbb27eecfc8081b.png" class="latex" alt="$(w, x, y, z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> sets are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63cfb6a9111bdd52e00a8fcfd0a25574e88beeb4.png" class="latex" alt="$(15, 14, 9, 6), (15, 12, 11, 6),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/2/cf26e1f4190f457c581f6d0941d4c5d06772e906.png" class="latex" alt="$(16, 11, 10, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="18" />. The first set does not satisfy the given conditions, but the other two do. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/193ceb2096dd2ad7ea04093754fdfc5007a596a3.png" class="latex" alt="$w = 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/d/87d7e123e0655c8b88f55959038975da7b9a8e2a.png" class="latex" alt="$w = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" /> are both possible solutions so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/e/7be68d2bc53613586941511f1ed59624bdb233fe.png" class="latex" alt="$16+15=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }31}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="147" height="28" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>From the problem, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/7/627b0389dfce75a504d763dc730b45add9a9d960.png" class="latex" alt="$w+x+y+z=44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="15" />. Since it is said that the pairwise positive differences between numbers are 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, and we can figure that the pairwise positive differences are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72fcddb37f6f04fd8fb93a3982dba150f7eb82db.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/6/0268399fded47ac18a817aefa1ab426f497acde5.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c3d59eb8091b6150d809ddc0f69ab3f1ec18414.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/d/40d4d32572cbcecb17e77eaac1354e9b3ffb757e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c331396276647696f2c332ca8d0122a4d2d2c5c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/6/7e616454e277cfc2572c7b7da9b9529ac59852df.png" class="latex" alt="$(y-z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, the sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c6f77b9d846eb7b59855e894775148ce1bdffd2.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-x), (w-y), (w-z), (x-y), (x-z), (y-z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="18" /> is equal to the sum of 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/20130aa16cfd36fe1b54fe264f926d3ca4aa067a.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-x)+(w-y)+(w-z)+(x-y)+(x-z)+(y-z)=28$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="499" height="18" />. Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421ddf6818a56347b2eab1e8e9776c8aab9e5bc2.png" class="latex" alt="$3w-3z+x-y=28$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="169" height="16" />. Adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/7/627b0389dfce75a504d763dc730b45add9a9d960.png" class="latex" alt="$w+x+y+z=44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/20130aa16cfd36fe1b54fe264f926d3ca4aa067a.png" class="latex" alt="$(w-x)+(w-y)+(w-z)+(x-y)+(x-z)+(y-z)=28$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="499" height="18" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/9/479e4d7ef5a0a0f06a7a671103bfff3132727c26.png" class="latex" alt="$4w+2x-2z=72$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="146" height="13" />, and simplifying we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66630a6b6318933f4819826ac49050248d0c27c7.png" class="latex" alt="$2w+x-z=36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="128" height="14" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33d7af37b081eb59a5a444341be7872f5977813d.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z$" width="41" height="8" /> is one of our positive differences, we can start guessing values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />, and if the equation simplifies to one of our numerical positive differences, that value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> should work. We can start at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/1/4614b5ba43679c4b7c0eab49a04e97db65cb695c.png" class="latex" alt="$w=18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" /> and keep going down, because our sum has to be positive. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/1/4614b5ba43679c4b7c0eab49a04e97db65cb695c.png" class="latex" alt="$w=18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d668d09c69fc88aff6a664a4062b775d0a1bb582.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=0$" width="74" height="12" />, which is not one of our sums. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/9/df999ac983b45a6cc8ecb7f332138d30a8247653.png" class="latex" alt="$w=17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/0/c103db85bdc9c8923ad206bef84ecc1449185eff.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=2$" width="74" height="12" />,which is not one of our sums. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/c/07c8b3ba314c2746b20b6ab7bfad432130dc07f8.png" class="latex" alt="$w=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/e/04e30efaa5f6fecf9218c7f37c382debdab5026d.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="12" />, which is one of our sums, so 16 works. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/e/7ce41e03dfb2395456a4a5d3139a1431378f414e.png" class="latex" alt="$w=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c9e74b20f661a51534d983eca1e0ba8af9a0697.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=6$" width="74" height="12" />, which is one of our sums, so 15 works. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55b3a77a0bc86eb65aec4e2d86a73d66a2e92a7.png" class="latex" alt="$w=14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/8/ad84e111d6a2632f61ecb6396db72f9de9850206.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z=8$" width="74" height="12" />, which is not one of our sums. If we keep going, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33d7af37b081eb59a5a444341be7872f5977813d.png" class="latex" alt="$x-z$" width="41" height="8" /> will soon exceed 10 and exceed all our sums, so any value below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55b3a77a0bc86eb65aec4e2d86a73d66a2e92a7.png" class="latex" alt="$w=14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="12" /> will not work. Therefore, our only solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> are 15 and 16, which means our sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/e/80e4c569db8826f87ba3f5a5587af9372634bc82.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }31}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />. You can check that 15 and 16 work by forming a string of 4 numbers as shown above.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Because we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/e/90ee78a28be204fe79265160878ea69e0e525658.png" class="latex" alt="$w&gt;x&gt;y&gt;z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="114" height="13" /> and that the positive differences are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/766283627a28a40a860f5d6e085d50cef0ce838c.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="94" height="16" />, we can immediately come to the conclusion that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c079994f8f25da526fff967bdb36e32145c35eb9.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z= 9$" width="77" height="12" /> (because w is the largest integer and z is the smallest integer, so their difference must be the greatest). With this we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> equations, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e200ac346d024c2368a25c4b7015cffbfd56bb32.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+z+w=44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="15" /> (from the problem), <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/f/13fdc9b0086f1ef30a6bea84520791faf150d833.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z=9$" width="77" height="12" /> and because we can add up all the possible possible differences (as shown in the previous solutions), we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/a/43a4e7dd1c1d42614e45fafe27cc281ac8e868d0.png" class="latex" alt="$3w+x-y-3z=28$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="169" height="16" />. With these equations, we eventually manipulate these equations by doing the first equation minus 3 times the second to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09de60f7648059000bf21b368a5ae9dd1b10e079.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="15" />. We can also add the first and third equation and subtract the second equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b48c238bf6012a7207de9b4ab8b1356e19fcc3e.png" class="latex" alt="$w+x=27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="88" height="13" /> thus we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/8/0583817dcd492141aa5a4a7643d91265439227dc.png" class="latex" alt="$w-x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="14" /> can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/c/56c5439905133cc7d994805d8faa49d24caef061.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 4, 5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> (note: 1 and 9 are not possible because we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/d/09de60f7648059000bf21b368a5ae9dd1b10e079.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/f/13fdc9b0086f1ef30a6bea84520791faf150d833.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z=9$" width="77" height="12" /> and the remaining differences can only be taken by 1 pair, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/8/0583817dcd492141aa5a4a7643d91265439227dc.png" class="latex" alt="$w-x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="14" /> cannot be equal to 1 or 9). However, in order to get an integer value for x and z, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f5438bdb7b43b6e6fe0956b8095fda5125bd886b.png" class="latex" alt="$w-z$" width="44" height="8" /> can only be equal to 3 and 5. Thus, by solving these, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/2/4128b9142a139ec794de7ef9a19e13544f66e64b.png" class="latex" alt="$w= 15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/c/07c8b3ba314c2746b20b6ab7bfad432130dc07f8.png" class="latex" alt="$w=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/a/ddaa147522d108e9a41bdfa42ecbe98bc3af1e9e.png" class="latex" alt="$15+16=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }31}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="149" height="30" />.
</p>
