<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/3/7637fe044dd52a13b24fee4cdc9f8d5f274d9a8e.png" class="latex" alt="$@$" width="13" height="13" /> denote the "averaged with" operation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/7/4e768d2fee3b5f8c30d5bbc6b77646148ae0b7a1.png" class="latex" alt="$a @ b = \frac{a+b}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="37" />. Which of the following distributive laws hold for all numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/5/0b56d32444d8efd65eb0cb6c01f8ac72c32587e2.png" class="latex" alt="$x, y,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />? <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12dc1d88f7d35e87ea9ea35f7dad4d9c92214d24.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{I. x @ (y + z) = (x @ y) + (x @ z)}\]" width="256" height="17" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/7/28722502a9a6fb1ef5bc3f366283389f0ab6c39b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{II. x + (y @ z) = (x + y) @ (x + z)}\]" width="262" height="17" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/b/92ba5c4645149e2c062620bbbf23b70a70b02479.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{III. x @ (y @ z) = (x @ y) @ (x @ z)}\]" width="268" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/9144bd2e58f81f0f48411b55ba10607957f33845.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{I only} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{II only} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{III only} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{I and III only} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{II and III only}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="655" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Simplify each operation and see which ones hold true.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/9/2d912c0803493e63cfa74c9cf60c70c0f563a84c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \text{I.} \qquad x @ (y + z) &amp;= (x @ y) + (x @ z)\\ \frac{x+y+z}{2} &amp;= \frac{x+y}{2} + \frac{x+z}{2}\\ \frac{x+y+z}{2} &amp;\not= \frac{2x+y+z}{2} \end{align*}" width="264" height="104" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb0e237c5266c23e5c63f1cf7c254df9d92620d4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \text{II.} \qquad x + (y @ z) &amp;= (x + y) @ (x + z)\\ x+ \frac{y+z}{2} &amp;= \frac{2x+y+z}{2}\\ \frac{2x+y+z}{2} &amp;= \frac{2x+y+z}{2} \end{align*}" width="279" height="106" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/5/e15acff11b04c19edbe6caef8d682afcd2f2d8ab.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \text{III.} \qquad x @ (y @ z) &amp;= (x @ y) @ (x @ z)\\ x @ \frac{y+z}{2} &amp;= \frac{x+y}{2} @ \frac{x+z}{2}\\ \frac{2x+y+z}{4} &amp;= \frac{2x+y+z}{4} \end{align*}" width="261" height="105" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/e/9be161a4db3de48d64f2c63131e5b585ff287dea.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \text{II and III only}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="144" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Alternatively, substitute arbitrary values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/b/64b0b15f6b595da31c740d814f0dad87134b9b0a.png" class="latex" alt="$x\neq y\neq z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="17" />. For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/a/40ac4b6f5ab516ac6fe7132ed2fe7a1d77ca02dd.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/b/47bce20bbdaf69387adff6c5d223526a36b91a72.png" class="latex" alt="$z=3$" width="42" height="12" /> give that only II and III work. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/e/9be161a4db3de48d64f2c63131e5b585ff287dea.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \text{II and III only}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="144" height="28" />
</p>
