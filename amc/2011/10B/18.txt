Use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Base_Angle_Theorem" title="Base Angle Theorem">Base Angle Theorem</a> to show <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/8/f985fafb339c9a5114b72ac014a8a08b652ccd2b.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DC} \cong \overline{MC}$" width="88" height="15" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">rectangle</a>, so it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/a/e1a22b89860eeca087b61047f5dd196b9d26b25b.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MC} = 6$" width="66" height="15" />. We notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/a/0faed260933ee64e9300b5750492d63184fc7687.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BMC$" width="64" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangle, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/e/7fe901fa76fd9bb89f8239f57abb4d8bf9eb81f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BMC = 30^{\circ}$" width="109" height="13" />.
