<p>A lattice point in an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />-coordinate system is any point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> where both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are integers. The graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/f/7dfb1b8f564901dd14ad70a5e2c937ea9d8c5428.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx +2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> passes through no lattice point with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/c/f6c8a55111e91443ac56a007fee9cadeab1774f0.png" class="latex" alt="$0 &lt; x \le 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/b/8ab24211e8c6cd92bef80e95ecf7d45d23afcc10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} &lt; m &lt; a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />. What is the maximum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/e/d3e328f743d2fef01d1e41b4012f4e4d416daefc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{51}{101} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{50}{99} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{51}{100} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{52}{101} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{13}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="440" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1bd9e9a2a82d0af53d34252ca77ddb9f5b0b76c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> to not pass through any lattice points with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/f/83f6bce98091845a86f93d5471a93240e5b5e38f.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x\leq 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" /> is the same as saying that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb6fcd5f085db28aae1117dc2e9276d9a987c5cf.png" class="latex" alt="$mx\notin\mathbb Z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/d/87d80c513bb7186f22ba4c6ce94539d667338a61.png" class="latex" alt="$x\in\{1,2,\dots,100\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="18" />, or in other words, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is not expressible as a ratio of positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/b/70be6995b09baf8d0976d9f17955aa88d98dd8fe.png" class="latex" alt="$s/t$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="23" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/f/c2f50c33941635f92564e7e6f61e297a8d08fa24.png" class="latex" alt="$t\leq 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="15" />. Hence the maximum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the first real number after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3cc0ad966b3e637cd199607cbf0d1befc0e491.png" class="latex" alt="$1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> that is so expressible.
</p><p>For each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e62c84c1d5ee8e435ad5bdf2dcf2d8d9d9ecaca3.png" class="latex" alt="$d=2,\dots,100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="109" height="16" />, the smallest multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f473e84d04440b4f694f931eec597fa6baa85e.png" class="latex" alt="$1/d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" /> which exceeds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3cc0ad966b3e637cd199607cbf0d1befc0e491.png" class="latex" alt="$1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d885338618a73595d668a9eb70e227fda5a829.png" class="latex" alt="$1,\frac23,\frac34,\frac35,\dots,\frac{50}{98},\frac{50}{99},\frac{51}{100}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="203" height="38" /> respectively, and the smallest of these is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/5/8e5c7ec89bd9e66292341e5ff6bdea14daabf3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\frac{50}{99}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="62" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We see that for the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1bd9e9a2a82d0af53d34252ca77ddb9f5b0b76c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> to not pass through any lattice points, the denominator of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> must be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, or else it would be canceled by some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d46b7fb3a219ebe7d2bc57de37798c8f830ec8c.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x\le100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" /> which would make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> an integer. By using common denominators, we find that the order of the fractions from smallest to largest is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/2369a7eadc93f664e0fe8aced85c26049d25eb01.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A), (B), (C), (D), (E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="17" />. We can see that when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f8d830b1e730bed49a5717da164d9a079536824.png" class="latex" alt="$m=\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> could be an integer, so therefore any fraction greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> would not work, as substituting our fraction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> would produce an integer for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. So now we are left with only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d75d57e066a182bb360075f72a27bad12f01ed0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{51}{101}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />. But since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2dd3326072e9f8e830356092a2da940625f3882d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{51}{101}=\frac{5049}{9999}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="94" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4eeef6b6cbf431dd38afdd8bbcb6f39555997f90.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}=\frac{5050}{9999}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="85" height="37" />, we can be absolutely certain that there isn't a number between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d75d57e066a182bb360075f72a27bad12f01ed0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{51}{101}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> that can reduce to a fraction whose denominator is less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. Since we are looking for the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we take the larger of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d75d57e066a182bb360075f72a27bad12f01ed0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{51}{101}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699b40301620472a97d6073256ff855fbd199130.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/5/8e5c7ec89bd9e66292341e5ff6bdea14daabf3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\frac{50}{99}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="62" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We want to find the smallest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> such that there will be an integral solution to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1bd9e9a2a82d0af53d34252ca77ddb9f5b0b76c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d46b7fb3a219ebe7d2bc57de37798c8f830ec8c.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x\le100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />. We first test A, but since the denominator has a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must be a nonzero multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb6fb7a3ff722532798b45607025254679e7b2.png" class="latex" alt="$101$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, but it then will be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. We then test B. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/5/755360e627531b196f8e870de39975e01f5ebbde.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{50}{99}x+2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="37" /> yields the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/3/39326f6845950ec5c0ded5da67e6242fde7eb340.png" class="latex" alt="$(99,52)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> which satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d46b7fb3a219ebe7d2bc57de37798c8f830ec8c.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x\le100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />. Checking the answer choices, we know that the largest possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/765f16b77312acd9f599cccdcd0459e2001f6cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}\implies\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="111" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Notice that for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70f7c0c752fadd1373f58dcec4fc1211c1a4f49b.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{1}{2}x+2=\frac{50}{100}x+2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="185" height="38" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0915cc1e4227db0c107c7feceaba36941f940c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=99$" width="52" height="12" /> is one of the integral values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> such that the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/d/abd8f6fa8b19ffc095185ff102c5695b00686015.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{100}x$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="41" height="38" /> is the closest to its next integral value.
</p><p><br />
Thus the maximum value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> when the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/8/fc8d13524370f19d69a17666a2a0396c4b347791.png" class="latex" alt="$y=99m+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" /> goes through its next lattice point, which occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/f/cdf4ece0e68e600849230ea77982d6ace4fdc77d.png" class="latex" alt="$m=\frac{b}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><br />
Finding the common denominator, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/d/b2de1e4e330ee675e67c3dfe649acbe84a4ea042.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{50}{100}=\frac{4950}{9900}, \frac{b}{99}=\frac{100b}{9900}\]" width="187" height="38" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/7/ea77deca157e5a7031617781221f872c54fc89d2.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />, the smallest value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/5/e854d3552d32b7f713b4ce323a5973199dbd0f26.png" class="latex" alt="$100b&gt;4950$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="95" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/9/1f95c60fbb990939840b5552bc3efd0b6a94df6b.png" class="latex" alt="$b=50$" width="49" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Thus the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/8/ba8078b9c3c26552c260cf0d37becbeb73fb9ad1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{99}.\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="66" height="37" />
