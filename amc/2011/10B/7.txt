<p>The sum of two angles of a triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4fc4c669518d5da6185c95ea46645895843844d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a right angle, and one of these two angles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/3/e73393067e6d6e401a5354accc7a730c7a2977aa.png" class="latex" alt="$30^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> larger than the other. What is the degree measure of the largest angle in the triangle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/0847bac2d53dee206c23623c037c4edee9e09158.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 69 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 72 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 90 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 102 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 108$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="412" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The sum of two angles in a triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/f/e4fc4c669518d5da6185c95ea46645895843844d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a right angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/a/e6af7cf7ba0ba7ff8053357796cf8c67af5fd233.png" class="latex" alt="$\longrightarrow \frac{6}{5} \times 90 = 108$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="138" height="37" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the measure of the first angle, then the measure of the second angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/1/5313d44901ff7f466d3054cec2d1740241b5dca9.png" class="latex" alt="$x+30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/b/18bf4d3e6772d54562d7ac37e5725731ac30c340.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x + x + 30 = 108 \longrightarrow 2x = 78 \longrightarrow x = 39\]" width="327" height="14" />
</p><p>Now we know the measure of two angles are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61c1de7374c98f545caa2c4cb7633eeb627171a8.png" class="latex" alt="$39^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/43376841be714bd279d36f178ddb35655497aa5b.png" class="latex" alt="$69^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. By the Triangle Sum Theorem, the sum of all angles in a triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5ab8f36b3d6a85590cc8b2629daa58e3559df11f.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" /> so the final angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/b/24b827998def193fbf2d8ec73227dc09a0a27e63.png" class="latex" alt="$72^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. Therefore, the largest angle in the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e967ab0e1030c0d9bd25d6542660c11e0872580.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(B) \ } 72}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />
</p>
