<p>The circular base of a hemisphere of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> rests on the base of a square pyramid of height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. The hemisphere is tangent to the other four faces of the pyramid. What is the edge-length of the base of the pyramid?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/4/794b804e8ea5c3f8c1a08f50ef63d6fdf5f6f91b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3\sqrt{2} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{13}{3} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 4\sqrt{2} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{13}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="422" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/3/f13a96240c51d61ba3733d9f5c3020fa92ec4136.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCDE$" width="72" height="13" /> be the pyramid with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> as the square base. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> be the center of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> and the midpoint of side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> respectively. Lastly, let the hemisphere be tangent to the triangular face <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd85454c0ce4000c0e63b4a1c4a12ef6f8b56e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$ABE$" width="42" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p><p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/0/d9053237c11f93476d03e5e8384171c727b8784a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EOM$" width="64" height="13" /> has a right angle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />. Since the hemisphere is tangent to the triangular face <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd85454c0ce4000c0e63b4a1c4a12ef6f8b56e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$ABE$" width="42" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/1/2110e5c2b850f0153ec6fd5bb97df45f3fe988d7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EPO$" width="55" height="12" /> is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/0/d9053237c11f93476d03e5e8384171c727b8784a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EOM$" width="64" height="13" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4ce92facedcf972df801b9718ab05594f6c366.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EPO$" width="58" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/4/e049bc5b3169976b9d2be4e4fbae3fd408dcc5f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{OM}{2} = \frac{6}{EP}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="93" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47ec7e39c018daa87c3269ef81769c5ff70e3d21.png" class="latex" alt="$OM = \frac{6}{EP} \times 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="121" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/3/5c325e4d89cf25b85e3a844054a60cecc26c4caf.png" class="latex" alt="$OM = \frac{6}{\sqrt{6^2 - 2^2}} \times 2 = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="224" height="45" />
</p><p>The length of the square base is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/f/4cf3d4d75bd2eec6832a97a7247b4c7d16a2a79a.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{2} = 3\sqrt{2} \rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="180" height="41" />
</p>
