<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/6/5d613511e25cafc7f98eb5e1feccc94affd5bbff.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x)=\sqrt{1-x}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="20" />, and for integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/2/72287b09043ed45b372a517d764e12d07aff8071.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="14" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/3/7e31555587da25661bc77c8be737f130b54ef54e.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{n}(x)=f_{n-1}(\sqrt{n^2 - x})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="21" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is the largest value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340d9d103363a5ec9fe13554fdcf0ee7a0bec229.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="16" /> is nonempty, the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/a/64a6524f3ee6103c559888db97a9d041689fadae.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{N}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="20" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52bae999d8320de9922bb4da965c7d6eb93f7ed1.png" class="latex" alt="$[c]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="16" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659ddd16bf2d477bd439f02e928dbdae9702becf.png" class="latex" alt="$N+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/9/6b9de5ad1832d1c845686be1a2500988e3f3e6be.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -226 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ -144 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ -20 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 20 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 144$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="488" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/6/5d613511e25cafc7f98eb5e1feccc94affd5bbff.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x)=\sqrt{1-x}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="20" /> is defined when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/652beb4a0edefea57900b60ed2ae963e3a18d5f7.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leq1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/3/4533f0f767c7fd6b4ecb2745c2749e1b019fd1f6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f_{2}(x)=f_{1}\left(\sqrt{4-x}\right)=\sqrt{1-\sqrt{4-x}}\]" width="292" height="36" /> 
</p><p>Applying the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11ef5766b0426af45297faae19e02b472a57a59b.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{1}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> and the fact that square roots must be positive, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/2/052626cf901fe29f62812ac54856eac6f59de47c.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq\sqrt{4-x}\leq1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="122" height="18" />. Simplifying, the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/3/723d8c0f4846a293f09776078bbbdb232ba27087.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{2}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/c/eecd531e67f2b4013c432ed2df7bf49ef37180ea.png" class="latex" alt="$3\leq x\leq4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Repeat this process for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/b/deb367968b6b5ec6902c5984b6adc6f4f3f88091.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{3}(x)=\sqrt{1-\sqrt{4-\sqrt{9-x}}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="220" height="43" /> to get a domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/9/f7975175dfae086cbeed0a61b9ae3a2fa34690a8.png" class="latex" alt="$-7\leq x\leq0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="15" />.  
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/b/22b1ae5a0af1c60e326e5bb463e1b4efa48a1271.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{4}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" />, since square roots must be nonnegative, we can see that the negative values of the previous domain will not work, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a60ea2c68278f789621218d6bdd138c53f954868.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{16-x}=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="18" />. Thus we now arrive at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> being the only number in the of domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/d/80d6c17847d4361966abbb0b8cf48b01a8ac7b8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f_4 x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" /> that defines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. However, since we are looking for the largest value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> for which the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340d9d103363a5ec9fe13554fdcf0ee7a0bec229.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="16" /> is nonempty, we must continue checking until we arrive at a domain that is empty. 
</p><p>We continue with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2eb9cd4b60277b9a794e8801d1e34daa9c4070f.png" class="latex" alt="$f_{5}(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> to get a domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/5/0c5f7c52f936a98c82bf25a2c31a2ba6076bcb69.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{25-x}=16 \implies x=-231$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="236" height="21" />. Since square roots cannot be negative, this is the last nonempty domain. We add to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/e/15eb623e362b5a094db84cdb70547e6cce92fbcb.png" class="latex" alt="$5-231=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ -226}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="179" height="28" />.
</p>
