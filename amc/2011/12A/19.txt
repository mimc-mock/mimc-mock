<p>At a competition with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> players, the number of players given elite status is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/1/991c3b67d00fc673db8784d4966800335412e39b.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{1+\lfloor \log_{2} (N-1) \rfloor}-N$" width="140" height="16" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> players are given elite status. What is the sum of the two smallest possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f9c90b1ab38adeff4a0fc7383389855ea8628a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 38 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 90 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 154 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 406 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 1024$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="430" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We start with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/2/d729dca844eb227a00f360ebe117700fa254563e.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{1+\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor}-N = 19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="182" height="17" />. After rearranging, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/6/d1604a10850e56f9c904239da474fd0632f0e673.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor = \log_{2} \left(\frac{N+19}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="251" height="43" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/f/8bf1204204b8bb320cd4ca6fb1227958f790cbb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="19" /> is a positive integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d3572194cc4b642881fc1248655cbd26a3a8894.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{N+19}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" /> must be in the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/f/0af69f8a85e05779edaa1f0bec74bbbbcf8a06b6.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{m}$" width="20" height="12" /> for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. From this fact, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/c/c8c4811e2880c75566fe033062993280d93c8ac9.png" class="latex" alt="$N=2^{m+1}-19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="119" height="15" />.
</p><p>If we now check integer values of N that satisfy this condition, starting from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/1/521172502112174b59a989c68c5aa79da7d61073.png" class="latex" alt="$N=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, we quickly see that the first values that work for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af4c37ff1a89a2667cb70c3e6e731b7040a36bfa.png" class="latex" alt="$109$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, that is, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/e/16e6995920e7a9cba4d82eec7488350b014b0644.png" class="latex" alt="$2^6-19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/3/f731a67068e7ea89a8fa0f4c37d80fd102543fd7.png" class="latex" alt="$2^7 -19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="15" />, giving values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, respectively. Adding up these two values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62df7253fa4098a0240fffc3817ae522e2a4fe4e.png" class="latex" alt="$45 + 109 = 154 \rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="173" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We examine the value that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/c/cbc8f74ae6720b860dc5e823af4c694fa4f529f6.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{1+\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor}$" width="98" height="16" /> takes over various intervals. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/f/8bf1204204b8bb320cd4ca6fb1227958f790cbb2.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="106" height="19" /> means it changes on each multiple of 2, like so:
</p><p>2 --&gt; 1
</p><p>3 - 4 --&gt; 2
</p><p>5 - 8 --&gt; 3
</p><p>9 - 16 --&gt; 4
</p><p>From this, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/7/2977b01f96926a0c923c23249c45456de661fc1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{1+\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor} - N$" width="140" height="16" /> is the difference between the next power of 2 above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/8/8d842df568cfefa47fad3db748c89bcda41a4682.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{\lfloor\log_{2}(N-1)\rfloor}$" width="82" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />. We are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> such that this difference is 19. The first two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> that satisfy this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/e/88e9fedf7265abd0647a6dea5824440e5c2d70da.png" class="latex" alt="$45 = 64-19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3eac6a73d600c55b6202c6a677dae79f83c54acc.png" class="latex" alt="$109=128-19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="118" height="13" /> for a final answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62df7253fa4098a0240fffc3817ae522e2a4fe4e.png" class="latex" alt="$45 + 109 = 154 \rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="173" height="23" />
</p><p><br />
