<p>A power boat and a raft both left dock <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> on a river and headed downstream. The raft drifted at the speed of the river current. The power boat maintained a constant speed with respect to the river. The power boat reached dock <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> downriver, then immediately turned and traveled back upriver. It eventually met the raft on the river 9 hours after leaving dock <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/8207b79435a4bd499da7598fb44424c8a8499238.png" class="latex" alt="$A.$" width="17" height="13" /> How many hours did it take the power boat to go from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/8/ca8dc4ecac26eb79b07199314b5c0a2a33cb477d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3.5 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\  4 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 4.5 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="377" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>WLOG let the speed of the river be 0. This is allowed because the problem never states that the speed of the current has to have a magnitude greater than 0. In this case, when the powerboat travels from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, the raft remains at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. Thus the trip from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> takes the same time as the trip from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to the raft. Since these times are equal and sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> hours, the trip from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> must take half this time, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/b/0fbe18900c777778ad24c84cb3d99df7032b4fd3.png" class="latex" alt="$4.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="13" /> hours. The answer is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cfb0d12967a18f3020a605b42d458edc87eea8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="23" />.
</p><p>Remark: This is equivalent to viewing the problem from the raft's perspective.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>What's important in this problem is to consider everything in terms of the power boat and the raft, since that is how the problem is given to us. Think of the blue arrow as the power boat and the red arrow as the raft in the following three diagrams, which represent different time intervals of the problem.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/f/3ef87a63974e838a67e23aba253c78bfb4889283.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(8cm,8cm); pair A, B; A=(-3,4); B=(3,4); draw(A--B); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,S); arrow((-2.5,6.4),dir(180),blue); arrow((-2.5,6.2),dir(180),red); pair A, B; A=(-3,5); B=(3,5); draw(A--B); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,S); arrow((2.6,5.4),dir(360),blue); arrow((-0.5,5.2),dir(180),red); pair A, B; A=(-3,6); B=(3,6); draw(A--B); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,S); arrow((.3,4.4),dir(360),blue); arrow((1.1,4.2),dir(180),red);[/asy]" width="378" height="165" />
</p><p>Thinking about the distance covered as their distances with respect to each other, they are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> distance apart in the first diagram when they haven't started to move yet, some distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> apart in the second diagram when the power boat reaches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, and again <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> distance apart in the third diagram when they meet. Therefore, with respect to each other, the boat and the raft cover a distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> on the way there, and again cover a distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> on when drawing closer. This makes sense, because from the 1st diagram to the second, the raft moves in the same direction as the boat, while from the 2nd to the 3rd, the boat and raft move in opposite directions.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> denote the speed of the power boat (only the power boat, not factoring in current) and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the speed of the raft, which, as given by the problem, is also equal to the speed of the current. Thus, from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, the boat travels at a velocity of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/75745b7864aaad2e91e9a33c15e27692b12d5ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$b+r$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" />, and on the way back, travels at a velocity of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b931a2552a9876cc4674bfed23ce13e4475871.png" class="latex" alt="$-(b-r)=r-b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />, since the current aids the boat on the way there, and goes against the boat on the way back. With respect to the raft then, the boat's velocity from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/e/dae2848cf1391a2d7478490da4f5afa4a05271fc.png" class="latex" alt="$(r+b)-r=b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" />, and on the way back it becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/8/7887941ac6814e4e626b7acb4491a1208cc6a582.png" class="latex" alt="$(r-b)-r=-b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />. Since the boat's velocities with respect to the raft are exact opposites, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa30e2beb1820f6b39f6251023709c8165cefc6.png" class="latex" alt="$-b$" width="22" height="12" />, we therefore know that the boat and raft travel apart from each other at the same rate that they travel toward each other.
</p><p>From this, we have that the boat travels a distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> at rate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> with respect to the raft both on the way to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and on the way back. Thus, using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/f/24f22cf8922f4a5bc8377c87aeff9f2017fcf26f.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{distance}{speed}=time$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="133" height="41" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/d/4ad8a3a205115576dd4b3fb34bc7b11d1275b36a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2d}{b}=9\text{ hours}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="100" height="37" />, and to see how long it took to travel half the distance, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/1/7e1a4d3b7841272615f1176a9af2b4e37d99161e.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{d}{b}=4.5\text{ hours}\implies\boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="182" height="37" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> be the time it takes the power boat to go from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> in hours, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the speed of the river current (and thus also the raft), and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> to be the speed of the power boat with respect to the river. 
</p><p>Using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/3/953f29a2b6ad8f2c5d0a2c619a084c4d6896600e.png" class="latex" alt="$d = rt$" width="48" height="12" />, the raft covers a distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/5987d6d522364696d9b9fd59a5d72cf6c86a9cdd.png" class="latex" alt="$9r$" width="17" height="12" />, the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/3/9e31ca22adcb65ebadc7cca8937c59986d3d140f.png" class="latex" alt="$(p + r)t$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" />, and the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to where the raft and power boat met up is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f092a5d4e2ae719a7cb8e01d34cf44e646ea0a1d.png" class="latex" alt="$(9 - t)(p - r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ba7e4e5cc586e8658681e915ec8f5da262e1e.png" class="latex" alt="$9r + (9 - t)(p - r) = (p + r)t$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="230" height="18" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab988c4d54d1ffe23cfe7af8d9e719fdd6060220.png" class="latex" alt="$t = 4.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="13" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cfb0d12967a18f3020a605b42d458edc87eea8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="23" />.
</p>
