<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> be the second smallest positive integer that is divisible by every positive integer less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/b/80b8baf686a05f9e90813cb4adc33ae41213db8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> must be divisible by every positive integer less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/7/d87c4f20da2e1950da5c2d2b8fcebab09cb12b1b.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4, 5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Each number that is divisible by each of these is is a multiple of their least common multiple. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/d/13d80a26e067a91b0f6607a802dd6123e4c4fcd7.png" class="latex" alt="$LCM(1,2,3,4,5,6)=60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="197" height="18" />, so each number divisible by these is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />. The smallest multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> is clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, so the second smallest multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/9/d994ac6816732e38036acb22c1590134da24bba9.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times60=120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />. Therefore, the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/7/d57b5b60125e1aa4292d3623765b3b4882ef23eb.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+0=\boxed{3\ \textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="151" height="28" />
</p>
