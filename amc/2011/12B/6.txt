<p>Two tangents to a circle are drawn from a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />.  The points of contact <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> divide the circle into arcs with lengths in the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/5/bf5aca1df088f2be27fdbbb5edabf5830f9e8d30.png" class="latex" alt="$2&#160;: 3$" width="32" height="12" />.  What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/2/2e2c830e136e667485791216de260e10db2c2195.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{BAC}$" width="55" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a50c3620b0e711af9b1109f70cc91e93420739.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 36 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 48 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>In order to solve this problem, use of the tangent-tangent intersection theorem (Angle of intersection between two tangents dividing a circle into arc length A and arc length B = 1/2 (Arc A° - Arc B°).
</p><p>In order to utilize this theorem, the degree measures of the arcs must be found.  First, set A (Arc length A) equal to 3d, and B (Arc length B) equal to 2d.  
</p><p>Setting 3d+2d = 360° will find d = 72°, and so therefore Arc length A in degrees will equal 216° and arc length B will equal 144°.
</p><p>Finally, simply plug the two arc lengths into the tangent-tangent intersection theorem, and the answer:
</p><p>1/2 (216°-144°) = 1/2 (72°) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/a/d9a18b42f0725032165f42a50afe32c6aa6e6543.png" class="latex" alt="$=\boxed{36 \textbf{(C)}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="81" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let arc A be 3x and arc B be 2x. Then the angle formed by the tangents is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/1/c01526ba774fcf7bdd5d33998e6e99b3ad76d09d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3x-2x}2 = \frac {x} 2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="37" /> by the arc length formula. Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/3/f93317a3b7bd61772102933c6c8e1cccfe9edf6d.png" class="latex" alt="$3x + 2x = 360$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/5/065e750353f8ab2ddf3a7d3e65bea603b37cf065.png" class="latex" alt="$x= 72.$" width="56" height="12" /> Hence the angle formed by the tangents is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/2/5928f355e859dccf9aab80cf90d911f628e30153.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{36 \textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let the center of the circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />. The radii extending from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> to the points of tangency <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> are both perpendicular to lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/027f8ef2fd0146fdc23ec890970a365cd7c7a2ad.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABO = \angle ACO = 90^{\circ}$" width="183" height="13" />, and quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/3/4d3270671538661b06c32401184cbccef9e942cc.png" class="latex" alt="$ABOC$" width="56" height="13" /> is cyclic, since the opposite angles add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Since the ratio of the arc lengths is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/5/bf5aca1df088f2be27fdbbb5edabf5830f9e8d30.png" class="latex" alt="$2&#160;: 3$" width="32" height="12" />, the shorter arc (the arc cut off by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4fff693c9a4aff817b05b712cd2376cd1673c08d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC$" width="56" height="12" />) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a031119d386df3fe72a2f47df29000577a0e46e.png" class="latex" alt="$144^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4fff693c9a4aff817b05b712cd2376cd1673c08d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC$" width="56" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/5862947b38a495ec9be80e5b9c7582de6cf0d269.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC$" width="55" height="13" /> must add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> since quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/3/4d3270671538661b06c32401184cbccef9e942cc.png" class="latex" alt="$ABOC$" width="56" height="13" /> is cyclic, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/4/53443e919425de9bccec50cd7980a7dab97c67c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC = 180 - 144 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 36}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="245" height="28" />.
</p><p>-Solution by Joeya
</p>
