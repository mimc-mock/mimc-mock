<p>A dart board is a regular octagon divided into regions as shown below. Suppose that a dart thrown at the board is equally likely to land anywhere on the board. What is the probability that the dart lands within the center square?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/7/7274a387184a2ce0dc35e8ecaac8b5da8b052e4c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(10mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(10pt)); dotfactor=4; pair A=(0,1), B=(1,0), C=(1+sqrt(2),0), D=(2+sqrt(2),1), E=(2+sqrt(2),1+sqrt(2)), F=(1+sqrt(2),2+sqrt(2)), G=(1,2+sqrt(2)), H=(0,1+sqrt(2)); draw(A--B--C--D--E--F--G--H--cycle); draw(A--D); draw(B--G); draw(C--F); draw(E--H);[/asy]" width="165" height="165" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/d/2ade6be4cca41feef541fb8c6d49c15b24d7af23.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{2} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{4} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2 - \sqrt{2}}{2} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 2 - \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="520" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let's assume that the side length of the octagon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The area of the center square is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />. The triangles are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> triangles, with a side length ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/7/d27c68a9f98a38e4278f21c7dcff055bae89bee3.png" class="latex" alt="$1:1:\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="18" />. The area of each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> identical triangles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/6/796135758772a1d32e2acded6523507c02160d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\dfrac{x}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2\times\dfrac{1}{2}=\dfrac{x^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="141" height="46" />, so the total area of all of the triangles is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />. Now, we must find the area of all of the 4 identical rectangles. One of the side lengths is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and the other side length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/8/da8cca075be22ea721a26f51f9e2ad4017488b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x}{\sqrt{2}}=\dfrac{x\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="89" height="45" />, so the area of all of the rectangles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5d1da5f86018fda415830d027db836ee40745d.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="18" />. The ratio of the area of the square to the area of the octagon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fa79f045880aa455f88e0876e71c9624d5ec481.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{x^2}{2x^2+2x^2\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="102" height="43" />. Cancelling <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> from the fraction, the ratio becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/1/ab1c8f1e090f56d72cc080237d0eadc2202c1cef.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt2+2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="67" height="41" />. Multiplying the numerator and the denominator each by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/6/8e6c077ebacdad03aa08a1e9a00ef5dca9b8be96.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{2}-2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="64" height="18" /> will cancel out the radical, so the fraction is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/e/0eeafa7c713e376996786f23aba2362b7a0914a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt2+2}\times\dfrac{2\sqrt{2}-2}{2\sqrt{2}-2}=\dfrac{2\sqrt{2}-2}{4}=\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ \dfrac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="381" height="53" />
</p>
