<p>A pyramid has a square base with side of length 1 and has lateral faces that are equilateral triangles. A cube is placed within the pyramid so that one face is on the base of the pyramid and its opposite face has all its edges on the lateral faces of the pyramid. What is the volume of this cube?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/47660cfaa8d40ad1c0cc0ae6442cda82abc8e61b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5\sqrt{2} - 7 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 7 - 4\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{27} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{9} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="525" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can use the Pythagorean Theorem to split one of the triangular faces into two 30-60-90 triangles with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/c/c3c585df8aa9fc1223e10f9ca9e80e8c2c8485af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}, 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="29" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/9/4e9191a5007067cff8f6d73f4d3419e08238f00b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />.
</p><p>Next, take a cross-section of the pyramid, forming a triangle with the top of the pyramid and the midpoints of two opposite sides of the square base.
</p><p>This triangle is isosceles with a base of 1 and two sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/9/4e9191a5007067cff8f6d73f4d3419e08238f00b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />.
</p><p>The height of this triangle will equal the height of the pyramid. To find this height, split the triangle into two right triangles, with sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/4/ea482385d1d726a107500b3a8de761c6a6cbd905.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="48" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/9/4e9191a5007067cff8f6d73f4d3419e08238f00b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />.
</p><p>The cube, touching all four triangular faces, will form a similar pyramid which sits on top of the cube. If the cube has side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, the small pyramid has height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/c/88cc6d4aac3d9a7cfe2dd796471b38d85668e516.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="37" height="41" /> (because the pyramids are similar).
</p><p>Thus, the height of the cube plus the height of the smaller pyramid equals the height of the larger pyramid.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/e/01e9d3e574f5c61d1281cee0d908b3c3ab7a1f87.png" class="latex" alt="$x +\frac{x\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="122" height="41" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/1030b0d210420b5bf06e1ba91494fb234267d58e.png" class="latex" alt="$x\left(1+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \right) =\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="152" height="53" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7c49338c4e85796a0e8d611c8caf8a27eaadf28.png" class="latex" alt="$x\left(2+\sqrt{2}\right) = \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="139" height="32" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/4/834d6181b1ace831b35c4a3a5c0d9e1ad8107433.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2+\sqrt{2}}  \cdot \frac{2-\sqrt{2}}{2-\sqrt{2}} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}-2}{4-2} = \sqrt{2}-1 =$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="358" height="45" />side length of cube.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/b/57b4fd017580f77b04f4ec0cc2d5aca483ec4537.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\sqrt{2}-1\right)^3 = (\sqrt{2})^3 + 3(\sqrt{2})^2(-1) + 3(\sqrt{2})(-1)^2 + (-1)^3 = 2\sqrt{2} - 6 +3\sqrt{2} - 1 =\textbf{(A)} 5\sqrt{2} - 7$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="744" height="35" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the side length of the square be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. Let the top of the pyramid be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and the square base be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/a/65af420a6e2b903f5295dd8f65f321545f69e44d.png" class="latex" alt="$BCDE$" width="58" height="12" />. Then, let the smaller cube meet edge <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and edge <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />. Let the closest vertice of the cube to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> on the base of the pyramid be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />.
Consider diagonal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c970cbf5e2e7029089517d5e8efbb8cade6d8a.png" class="latex" alt="$CE$" width="28" height="12" />. It has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />. Since the diagonal of the smaller cube's base is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/9/6798d54ee20abfb2ce2cd64969ac98c1836282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$s\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />, note that the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/0876d1c14c57e7b5a0bd94d502b4dfa6809f199b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{2} - s\sqrt{2}}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="90" height="43" /> Also, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/d/9fdae50c9da5ac4614e447fa0f6b2e81e352361c.png" class="latex" alt="$AG = s$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="63" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/3/ff39a68f9856d8bc25688633301be9ce9fedea0b.png" class="latex" alt="$CG = 1-s$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="16" />. We can now use the Pythagorean Theorem on triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/4/1845024d637349dbc0c2899d452db2dcbf5c3f21.png" class="latex" alt="$CHG$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" />, with right angle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e28ce12d49d39f160d5a0ef54077fc98e4b9d2b.png" class="latex" alt="$G$" width="14" height="12" />, to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/6/5e6662f33ba4ea326e705c9fd1d7e82cac25e60a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s^2 + (\dfrac{\sqrt{2} - s\sqrt{2}}{2})^2 = (1-s)^2\]" width="226" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/1976af1c4db60f244ec5b81736135c14ea1c7784.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\rightarrow s = \sqrt{2} - 1 \rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(A)}}.\]" width="183" height="28" />
</p>
