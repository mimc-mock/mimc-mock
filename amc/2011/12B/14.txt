<p>A segment through the focus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> of a parabola with vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d58aa29201da09d8e620f8698e3a37547f6b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$V$" width="14" height="12" /> is perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbf1ff1d126cff774cb5ed2d1828dad9f0314383.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{FV}$" width="29" height="15" /> and intersects the parabola in points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/0/da03c3b71a583ab89a5af0ae235720b22ba9444d.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos\left(\angle AVB\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/5/02526ba7c8faa380c98e10dae857160480a529fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -\frac{3\sqrt{5}}{7} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ -\frac{2\sqrt{5}}{5} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ -\frac{4}{5} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ -\frac{3}{5} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ -\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="527" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution  1</span></h2>
<p>Name the directrix of the parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" />. Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/1/641f4830aa7c7ef8726773f21c918d2f9bbaa281.png" class="latex" alt="$d(X,k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="18" /> to be the distance between a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> and a line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>Now we remember the geometric definition of a parabola: given any line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" /> (called the directrix) and any point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> (called the focus), the <i>parabola</i> corresponding to the given directrix and focus is the locus of the points that are equidistant from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/d/ecdec0fc7942b0311ff63cee1f790b31fb0a9c7f.png" class="latex" alt="$FV=d(V,l)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="18" />. Let this distance be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Now note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/98658bb2678db0c4ee813b8c0102f316bb932262.png" class="latex" alt="$d(F,l)=2d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/7/307403bf9b9adf3ac57910e79a2e81bc49b92e85.png" class="latex" alt="$d(A,l)=d(B,l)=2d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0bdae99cf880682579231415a6c6d8f831e733e.png" class="latex" alt="$AF=BF=2d$" width="124" height="13" />. We now use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> on triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17f5c1af4539d92a06cb7f855edd5f59939c9707.png" class="latex" alt="$AFV$" width="42" height="13" />; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/20415205b5c4d6c045f8d02d5861cdbe3a4d3426.png" class="latex" alt="$AV=\sqrt{AF^2+FV^2}=d\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="220" height="19" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/c/3cccf870209984611ebf5e89c89eff2cdbd5770e.png" class="latex" alt="$BV=d\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="18" />. We now use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Law_of_Cosines" title="Law of Cosines">Law of Cosines</a>:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843b573a2ccca06d638b0cd4fec78aa552ae339a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AB^2=AV^2+VB^2-2AV\cdot VB\cos{\angle AVB}\Rightarrow 16d^2=10d^2-10d^2\cos{\angle AVB}\]" width="575" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0abcf6e76912af95d1a054db86198580cc5c92.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Rightarrow \cos{\angle AVB}=-\frac{3}{5}\]" width="155" height="37" />
</p><p>This shows that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693c4044f8526a8179956e8ac7a3f2f9d61e2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>WLOG we can assume that the parabola is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/f/8ef862bdd16c2d5356e11735b8c766c402f5e10c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="18" />. Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/9/3d98b64e59f933c3320e7dd82fe4ddb849bc06a9.png" class="latex" alt="$V = (0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/1/2110353300c055d71c47ffbc2d8c1df167e09a52.png" class="latex" alt="$F = (0,\frac{1}{4})$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="81" height="37" />. Also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/f/b0f0dc2a943f9a27586bf0fb9355885ff635dc9b.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{4})$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="99" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/0/6507c048d4a6376ccbe7b8e315d71e660a86305f.png" class="latex" alt="$B = (\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{4})$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a649aee592a9c37d2f9b5d5239f5e06e533130.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/4/5a4dfb455b01fe38a5f0c9e98ce6b34cec725a1c.png" class="latex" alt="$AV = VB = \sqrt{(\frac{1}{2})^2+(\frac{1}{4})^2} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="266" height="43" /> by the pythagorean theorem.
</p><p>Now using the law of cosines on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a1c3668f547d2e91b02fda40dd15da432f87172.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AVB$" width="58" height="13" /> we have: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/2/d4294d75fffb68032489e7ad4bf189a96f646ef9.png" class="latex" alt="$AB^2 = 2AV^2-2AV^2\cos(\angle AVB) = 2AV^2(1-\cos(\angle AVB))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="476" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/8/6783516c150d4d52c1759ca5225e0a70f7c4d3fb.png" class="latex" alt="$1 = \frac{5}{8} (1-\cos(\angle AVB))$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="184" height="37" />
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/6/3e6514992527daa813ed416ff723d96c7a71c108.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos(\angle AVB) = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} -\frac{3}{5}}.\]" width="197" height="48" />
</p><p>(solution by mihirb)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>After assuming that the parabola is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />,find the points A and B, which are +/- 1,2,1/4.Now treat them as vectors,take the dot product,then find the magnitudes and multiply them.A well known definition of the dot product says that the quotient of the two is the cosine of the angle between them.This will give you D.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>As we know, an expression for the equation of a parabola is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/5/c8508cb145d16b28e47a603c78e1286d05e9a4d8.png" class="latex" alt="$y-k=\frac{1}{4a}(x-h)^2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="152" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169720b6d97c7c69f8f8a740ff74dee5dbc96419.png" class="latex" alt="$(h,k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> is the vertex and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the distance from the focus to the vertex, here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d58aa29201da09d8e620f8698e3a37547f6b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$V$" width="14" height="12" />. The length of the latus rectum, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> here, is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d68655485b913597ec573661c0763b9a37e3972.png" class="latex" alt="$|4a|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/2/882cbe010e92dbad043afebf23c6d46840744266.png" class="latex" alt="$AF=BF=2a$" width="124" height="13" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. Then we can use right Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/c/56c3d6aa1806d73eb7ff1b9f93153e8765442cc7.png" class="latex" alt="$VAF$" width="42" height="13" /> to figure out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8fa1008bef199f9e4c79840edd0fdef316922e60.png" class="latex" alt="$VA=\frac{a\sqrt{5}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="89" height="41" />. Now we can use the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/8/168990c1c6d08d5534022bbcf8ee67723b9eed7d.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{\angle AVB}=\cos{2 \angle AVF}$" width="199" height="13" /> and use the double angle formula. This results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22e9a7b047dab8ae77d54a7a44e5a58b15f66a0c.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{2 \angle AVF}=2{\cos}^2{\angle AVF}-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="242" height="15" />. We can find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/6/8a60eacb4c9764c5a8e04cec50dcb0cb6eee9f84.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{\angle AVF}$" width="82" height="13" /> from right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/6/e469ed444426da566352072c44376c88dbcad6b1.png" class="latex" alt="$AVF$" width="42" height="13" /> using the Pythagorean Theorem, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/b/1ebcfae00aba32d4d6ee5b4ef53bdad268822acc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="26" height="37" />. Evaulating the expression, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/5/ea5c2cfe47a132c979ecf83833624cfb11b6d958.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{2 \angle AVF}=\frac{-3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="141" height="37" />.
</p><p>-Indefintense
</p>
