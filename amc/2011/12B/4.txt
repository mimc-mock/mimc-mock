<p>In multiplying two positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, Ron reversed the digits of the two-digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />. His erroneous product was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a141918ea2696454645ae9d426e11ef432dcdc50.png" class="latex" alt="$161.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="13" /> What is the correct value of the product of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/a/c4aaa78a0e23075ae3c86cc71856c75d72925d5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 116 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 161 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 204 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 214 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 224$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Taking the prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8403aaee7e752f7fadd7a9beb7ba81262bd41a26.png" class="latex" alt="$161$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> reveals that it is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/90604f5ad990860da03b23ea8679fe0b9013f47c.png" class="latex" alt="$23*7.$" width="48" height="12" /> Therefore, the only ways to represent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8403aaee7e752f7fadd7a9beb7ba81262bd41a26.png" class="latex" alt="$161$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> as a product of two positive integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d3780818ac27d9aeda27eb0a3a93fce7cb429e6.png" class="latex" alt="$161*1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/90604f5ad990860da03b23ea8679fe0b9013f47c.png" class="latex" alt="$23*7.$" width="48" height="12" /> Because neither <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8403aaee7e752f7fadd7a9beb7ba81262bd41a26.png" class="latex" alt="$161$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> nor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is a two-digit number, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17ce6fcb2994d4241d780dd5311d828db133411.png" class="latex" alt="$7.$" width="12" height="12" /> Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> is a two-digit number, we know that a, with its two digits reversed, gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/9/7d9803b67b315db7dfce1c1ce717cfaaa8ec4d54.png" class="latex" alt="$23.$" width="21" height="12" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/0/2e07a77fbd1dfd66033443e1917e56cc7cd142b2.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 32$" width="51" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bbfa1f272b20123aa84aa230975d60d0f36f685.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 7.$" width="44" height="12" /> Multiplying our two correct values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> yields
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b15af41adbaac2f450d801429ad90374b7824b7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a*b = 32*7 =\]" width="121" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/6/2565fe98537a4d80d87df8cc8475c4e2d9b00ab6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \boxed{224 \textbf{(E)}}\]" width="84" height="28" />
</p>
