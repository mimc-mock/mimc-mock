<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/0/b808e260d341f190e2080beaa349f1f7bba735c5.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)=\log_{10}\left(\frac{x}{10}\right)=\log_{10}\left({x}\right) - 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="261" height="34" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8ce7bdb2bbd189df6b4f4604c7516fcf39bab667.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{1}(x)=g(f(x))\text{ = }g(10^{10x}=\log_{10}\left({10^{10x}}\right){ - 1 = 10x - 1}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="436" height="22" />
</p><p>Proof by induction that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/d/7ad7d21b936cfbfbeb9364366974d12b2652eb3c.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{n}(x)\text{ = }10^n x - (1 + 10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{n-1})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="341" height="19" />:
</p><p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/2/5121ef08cd68c0c0ff2c84566093ea03702c9c6d.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{1}(x)=10x - 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />
</p><p>Assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53b06cd01df9c57797e532df89787689643f7448.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{n}(x)=10^n x - (1 + 10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{n-1})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="19" /> is true for n:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b65beafdbc573de32fba46940e6969122aa0df.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} h_{n+1}(x)&amp;= h_{1}(h_{n}(x))\\ &amp;=10 h_{n}(x) - 1\\ &amp;=10 (10^n x   - (1 + 10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{n-1})) - 1\\ &amp;= 10^{n+1} x - (10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{n}) - 1\\ &amp;= 10^{n+1} x - (1 + 10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{(n+1)-1}) \end{align*}" width="422" height="127" />
</p><p>Therefore, if it is true for n, then it is true for n+1; since it is also true for n = 1, it is true for all positive integers n.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/8/d78b2be433c30d189e4d9a1f550d0772c1dc2d94.png" class="latex" alt="$h_{2011}(1) = 10^{2011}\times 1{ - }(1 + 10 + 10^2 + ... + 10^{2010})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="391" height="19" />, which is the 2011-digit number 8888...8889
</p><p>The sum of the digits is 8 times 2010 plus 9, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/e/b5e7c029728fcb1e1ea88a359398f79971230bb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{16089\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="85" height="28" />
</p>
