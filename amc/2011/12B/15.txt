<p>How many positive two-digit integers are factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/4/e847a2a1840b6aaf0cbd3023cd258f3d90485c65.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{24}-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="15" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/7/e3730d8603269f1bc39b8c5f2e65c221c3899ebd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 8 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 10 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 12 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution_by_OmegaLearn">Video Solution by OmegaLearn</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/mgEZOXgIZXs?t=770">https://youtu.be/mgEZOXgIZXs?t=770</a>
</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>From repeated application of difference of squares:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/c/eac80d46f80e757eba3c3885a52f5e20f5f06d0f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{24}-1 = (2^{12} + 1)(2^{6} + 1)(2^{3} + 1)(2^{3} - 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="331" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/c/11c0888911944735a3f199a8c76560ed2bb48129.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{24}-1 = (2^{12} + 1) * 65 * 9 * 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="235" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/5627325a3ddcc7701ac2856dacff9d57f651639a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{24}-1 = (2^{12} +1) * 5 * 13 * 3^2 * 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="269" height="19" />
</p><p>Applying sum of cubes: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/7/557d2cf8cee1b7b7b766413c56c151d8c3cba244.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{12}+1 = (2^4 + 1)(2^8 - 2^4 + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/e/31eec7921553a9627886e6b298d289a4559605fd.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{12}+1 = 17 * 241$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="16" />
</p><p><br />
A quick check shows <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/b/0eb5ac9cb75be8bacde6b2f88e1c2d33aa8e292a.png" class="latex" alt="$241$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> is prime.  Thus, the only factors to be concerned about are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/7/3478ee45cd6b35e6081e1ddd713330d7d689a031.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 * 5 * 7 * 13 * 17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="139" height="15" />, since multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/b/0eb5ac9cb75be8bacde6b2f88e1c2d33aa8e292a.png" class="latex" alt="$241$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> will make any factor too large.
</p><p>Multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> will give a two digit factor; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> itself will also work.  The next smallest factor, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, gives a three digit number.  Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> factors which are multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />.
</p><p>Multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17fd2cd9f6e99b1cb8a62451cc12bbf2afe22b0c.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> will also give a two digit factor, as well as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> itself.  Higher numbers will not work, giving an additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> factors.
</p><p>Multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/0/2f0131e1154f216fa51f0ead1787f055d82e4c72.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/9/819068968ed60608a91d0c3921d6296964660e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2$" width="15" height="15" /> for a two digit factor.  There are no more factors to check, as all factors which include <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> are already counted.  Thus, there are an additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> factors.
</p><p>Multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/9/819068968ed60608a91d0c3921d6296964660e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2$" width="15" height="15" /> for a two digit factor.  All higher factors have been counted already, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> more factors.
</p><p>Thus, the total number of factors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/a/eca9264a1630a84d736d5381a3e032dfd03d6a9c.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 4 + 3 + 2 = \boxed{12    \textbf{ (D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="193" height="28" />
</p>
