<p>The arithmetic mean of two distinct positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is a two-digit integer. The geometric mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is obtained by reversing the digits of the arithmetic mean. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/0/6c0a86663edb0dccee099a47d48b1b19a4ee6cb9.png" class="latex" alt="$|x - y|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/1/7113ac762caf217fd361fbfb21c730f16b918fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 24 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 48 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 54 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 66 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 70$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Answer: (D)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/2/342e669c5f982921510eed5990165058204fe3c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x + y}{2} = 10 a+b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="128" height="35" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/7/21706f6d7bd91de80b7907e39e93716c6a52ffb0.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le a\le 9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e00f24846ce303d725c578b544695249bddd1e.png" class="latex" alt="$0\le b\le 9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f5dfb99b9325e1a558580a99ca460757e4b7eb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{xy} = 10 b+a$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="117" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/9/489ea4548ff8636043c689167fc5bb8bc829a9a9.png" class="latex" alt="$100 a^2 + 20 ab + b^2 = \frac{x^2 + 2xy + y^2}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="274" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/8/0085dd68b349b5c40cc868bee99b36fa2425061b.png" class="latex" alt="$xy = 100b^2 + 20ab + a^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="182" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/7/de78275ca54933bcd4e3b2c8821fbd1ddc7dcb76.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x^2 + 2xy + y^2}{4} - xy = \frac{x^2 - 2xy + y^2}{4} = \left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)^2 = 99 a^2 - 99 b^2 = 99(a^2 - b^2)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="620" height="46" />
</p><p><br />
<br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/0/6f0ea020cf911fd729950f156f8092c4e00885e8.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y| = 2\sqrt{99(a^2 - b^2)}=6\sqrt{11(a^2 - b^2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="328" height="32" />
</p><p>Note that in order for x-y to be integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/5/af55ef3efb75660e1eed740749565b7a8e42e9b6.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2 - b^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="19" /> has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8fb94bc1d984fa0e967f0b77d4db5e754ed122c.png" class="latex" alt="$11n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" /> for some perfect square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c766f9fce6070981e62f805a7cf7294baed4498e.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c766f9fce6070981e62f805a7cf7294baed4498e.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/b/61b303c12fc9d4db885f2c06a6933c5ff100bb27.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y| = 66$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="18" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/1/5218af40079081e694d8b82eeac2d368ef6ea76a.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/a/b5afd2cfc4ae679d9c5369dc9f52e0accc198907.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y| = 132$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />. In AMC, we are done. Otherwise, we need to show that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/d/42d1a8a23f35e20b35017fa741618ed859022905.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 -b^2 = 44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="15" /> is impossible.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/4/26480ca4b9eb7af3e430e124d4bfe5d2d6681b4e.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a+b) = 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> -&gt; <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2ea7581c87e299152333019e7f838260fcefbe2.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/9/689ff6d70f0963c7d5592caee0fa05ff9768dcef.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = 44$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> respectively. And since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/6/ea6df4d5b93ac3cfadfffd1d26d1cda4a121c4ec.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b \le 18$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="81" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/b/eab0940a9cbb5562898f5e0a9049f31e0516f4e0.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = 11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/6/a06c23e5af123b80b7c95fa562313cac7c4cd3a8.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, but there is no integer solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Short_Cut">Short Cut</span></h3>
<p>We can arrive at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b3808440d238bbf65c6f84db700ae3d8e631d3a.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y| = 6\sqrt{11(a^2 - b^2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="190" height="32" /> using the method above. Because we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/2/e526fbac5ee18e20a7293e9cb9c317989b31988a.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" /> is an integer, it must be a multiple of 6 and 11. Hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/e/68e540df2be5d0f86905d68f2b28ba1f8fcc6f67.png" class="latex" alt="$66.$" width="21" height="12" />
</p><p>In addition:
Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8fb94bc1d984fa0e967f0b77d4db5e754ed122c.png" class="latex" alt="$11n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c766f9fce6070981e62f805a7cf7294baed4498e.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> may be obtained with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/c/eac964e50ab7e4904f600a0a12e157a994617fe4.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 6$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add0bf97955f4eedafea7db58ac66eb4a130baa2.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 5$" width="40" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/5/65542e7e189df28226e40f039153e3439ccace0c.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - b^2 = 36 - 25 = 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="178" height="15" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sidenote">Sidenote</span></h2>
<p>It is easy to see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/a/5ba37a4f7fe90fe6a04c3d35c232b7e6bea9feb0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)=(6,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" /> is the only solution. This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/c/2acbb28f1ba53b87f9e59ae65c78097f113a4170.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)=(98,32)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" />. Their arithmetic mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> and their geometric mean is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/9/6392be3c4d257afbc7ed9283bdb8ba630f4ef7e1.png" class="latex" alt="$56$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/7/4a7cb720e9fc1a250b277e8cd88d2225a4f57a4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)/2 = 10a + b$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="158" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/4/194c493b95b417f9f6d1fe135daec0baa567d96f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{xy} = 10b + a$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="119" height="21" />. By AM-GM we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/7/18786649350a0a7e257fb57a375eb1093d51a225.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. Squaring and multiplying by 4 on the first equation we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/e/29eed4fd215324d23a044116cb15f3c929f4c2f5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 + 2xy = 400a^2 + 4b^2 + 80ab$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="283" height="20" />. Squaring and multiplying the second equation by 4 we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/0/390b2a5a36bc52ecf995bbe51981a312eea4debc.png" class="latex" alt="$4xy = 400b^2 + 4a^2 + 80ab$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="20" />. Subtracting we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/e/0ae62ee3c9e588d40daeceb616fd0f2fa864dbde.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)^2 = 396(a^2 - b^2)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="186" height="21" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/9/1c93dbdc11469e48b455f6b1171908b9cb5acea5.png" class="latex" alt="$396 = 2^2 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="128" height="15" />. So to make it a perfect square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/2/3721f47a8780121bf279d325fc6608c1856bd656.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - b^2 = 11$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="99" height="18" />. By quick difference of squares we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/c/eac964e50ab7e4904f600a0a12e157a994617fe4.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 6$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add0bf97955f4eedafea7db58ac66eb4a130baa2.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 5$" width="40" height="12" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/e/b7e05cb23011a37aa28294d54b4332698d4b506a.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 2 \cdot 11 = 66$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="14" />. 
~coolmath_2018
</p>
