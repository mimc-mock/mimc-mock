<p>How many even integers are there between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/4/b640caf462417d59208b590f634dd8de89b8a96f.png" class="latex" alt="$700$" width="26" height="12" /> whose digits are all different and come from the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/2/992ec0ec2e295a6586364ffc36fa06a912b9a3b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\{1,2,5,7,8,9\right\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/8/4a8a56b91e96eeee60b195962ce16e1698430520.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\,12 \qquad\text{(B)}\,20 \qquad\text{(C)}\,72 \qquad\text{(D)}\,120 \qquad\text{(E)}\,200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="381" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We split up into cases of the hundreds digits being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. If the hundred digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, then the units digits must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> in order for the number to be even and then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> remaining choices (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/d/eadef98569e0e025058f253f4b21b45a0db0f15f.png" class="latex" alt="$1,5,7,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />) for the tens digit, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f75755f1002b3094dafbaf5dbf36f3f7e0e7ff7.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 4 \times 1=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="12" /> possibilities. Similarly, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/d/5fdecf5e0365330c3fccb549174b99c885326cad.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 2 \times 4=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" /> possibilities for the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> case, giving a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/6/976879ec56eee0803120cd48c88d07a1383d3f0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{4+8=12 \ \mathbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="130" height="29" /> possibilities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We see that the last digit of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-digit number must be even to have an even number. Therefore, the last digit must either be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />-the last digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. We must have the hundreds digit to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and the tens digit to be any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/8/1685514246f3e5338ca865bba3f00b17d7384e4e.png" class="latex" alt="${1,7,8,9}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, thus obtaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> numbers total.
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />-the last digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. We now can have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> to be the hundreds digit, and any choice still gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> choices for the tens digit. Therefore, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/5/555b7c97419362f97938014e19847a9a4e221a9f.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 4=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" /> numbers. 
</p><p>Adding up our cases, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/b/36bef311a867a0e291c980b29ab7f65912653bc0.png" class="latex" alt="$4+8=\boxed{\text{(A)}12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" /> numbers.
</p>
