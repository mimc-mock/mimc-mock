<p>Square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> has one vertex on each side of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />.  Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/4/134506cee7411dac7e90772b27d282fd1915d985.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=7\cdot EB$" width="103" height="13" />.  What is the ratio of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/4/834025d32475e378c5cbf1d75b4cb573326d2e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\,\frac{49}{64}     \qquad\text{(B)}\,\frac{25}{32}     \qquad\text{(C)}\,\frac78 \qquad\text{(D)}\,\frac{5\sqrt{2}}{8}   \qquad\text{(E)}\,\frac{\sqrt{14}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="403" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/c/b0c8833fa39551750316971e9c5369fc03cbe2c6.png" class="latex" alt="$8s$" width="17" height="12" /> be the length of the sides of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />, then the length of one of the sides of square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/3/de3b9fbed47518829782e0009904585727a374be.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(7s)^2+s^2}=\sqrt{50s^2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="169" height="32" />, and hence the ratio in the areas is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/2/1a2ade7982353a6164a36a3a0fe29d357b47bad0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{50s^2}^2}{(8s)^2}=\frac{50}{64} = \boxed{\frac{25}{32} \ \mathbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="200" height="50" />.
</p>
