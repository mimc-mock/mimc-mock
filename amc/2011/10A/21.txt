<p>Two counterfeit coins of equal weight are mixed with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> identical genuine coins. The weight of each of the counterfeit coins is different from the weight of each of the genuine coins. A pair of coins is selected at random without replacement from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> coins. A second pair is selected at random without replacement from the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> coins. The combined weight of the first pair is equal to the combined weight of the second pair. What is the probability that all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> selected coins are genuine?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/c/f5c10b21992ae4d8872fa4efcecb3fd19dc3c4fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{7}{11}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{9}{13}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{11}{15}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{15}{19}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{15}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="413" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that we are trying to find the conditional probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/3/3a3e47074c0dddcabc310f990a658af01115fce4.png" class="latex" alt="$P(A \vert B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="165" height="43" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> coins being genuine and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is the sum of the weight of the coins being equal. The only possibilities for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are (g is abbreviated for genuine and c is abbreviated for counterfeit, and the pairs are the first and last two in the quadruple) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/d/19d8ace8e82852d227ddb373d8d12f6fcde60b4d.png" class="latex" alt="$(g,g,g,g),(g,c,g,c),(g,c,c,g),(c,g,g,c),(c,g,c,g)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="397" height="18" />. We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/6/706277135716c6703c66dc5565e018a1580a51ad.png" class="latex" alt="$A \cap B$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="13" /> happens with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/0/b807a117b0a06cb2dee14634393fefc04ad61dbb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8}{10} \times \frac{7}{9} \times \frac{6}{8} \times \frac{5}{7} = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="164" height="38" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> happens with probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/b/92b948b513657af6eae65fac75d76dceed5aff01.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}+4 \times \left( \frac{8}{10} \times \frac{2}{9} \times \frac{7}{8}\times \frac{1}{7}\right) = \frac{19}{45}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="265" height="43" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/4/2b41667dcbfe38a340c82317696e8d3c0c053a50.png" class="latex" alt="$P(A \vert B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{\frac{1}{3}}{\frac{19}{45}}=\boxed{\frac{15}{19} \ \mathbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="306" height="49" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If we pick <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> indistinguishable real coins from the set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> real coins, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/0/f80fede77083e08f8c00baaa6b6651cae05eab37.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" /> ways to pick the coins.  If we then place the coins in four distinguishable slots on the scale, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da866442e69c040daf44260b4728d37e98de313.png" class="latex" alt="$4!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="13" /> ways to arrange them, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15f55d78e2958af6bc67daab0bc35ee00750c89a.png" class="latex" alt="$4!\cdot \binom{8}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="60" height="53" /> ways to choose and place <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> real coins.  This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/d/2ed7bff80e0f4fa8df7ba094f5316671d8774824.png" class="latex" alt="$1680$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> desirable combinations.
</p><p>If we pick <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> real coins and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> fake coins, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/411211a0efe1a87366eb1f0bca74fb2802753903.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{2}\binom{2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="70" height="53" /> ways to choose the coins.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> choices for the first slot on the left side of the scale.  Whichever type of coin is placed in that first slot, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> choices for the second slot on the left side of the scale, since it must be of the opposite type of coin.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> choices for the first slot on the right side of the scale, and only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> choice for the last slot on the right side.
</p><p>Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/6/b4638d989ba46b60fd6cffea04b96b13ea0f0864.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot 2\cdot 2\cdot 1 = 16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="117" height="13" /> ways to arrange the coins, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/f/66f5c16a6d31cce00d634fe14deb7314bb2f8c4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{8}{2}\binom{2}{2} = 28$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="116" height="53" /> sets of possible coins, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/9/b4939d02a7889c7f344787aedc447503e4f197b2.png" class="latex" alt="$16\cdot 28 = 448$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> combinations that are legal, yet undesirable.
</p><p>The overall probability is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/4/30478cbd1bb7361feff98c0e811f7fcfc314bb04.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1680}{1680 + 448} = \boxed{\frac{15}{19} \ \mathbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="183" height="48" />.
</p><p>Note that in this solution, all four slots on the scale are deemed to be distinguishable.  In essence, this "overcounts" all numbers by a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, since you can switch the coins on the left side, you can switch the coins on the right side, or you can switch sides of the scale.  But since all numbers are increased 8-fold, it will cancel out when calculating the probability.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Place the two coins from the first pair in a line followed by the two coins from the second pair followed by the six left-over coins.  We can do that in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/a/b4a36d045406715079df9fc3e9053bd327aa03cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{10}{2} = 45$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="88" height="53" /> different ways.
</p><p>We need to exclude those cases where the weight shows a difference.  There are two cases where a pair has both counterfeit coins and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/8/0184f95f67c47eed7bf84cbfe2a6defb63f2fb0f.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="13" /> cases where one counterfeit coin is chosen and one is in the left-over.  That leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/e/28e2c858d300ee4d02116fe54201bf539a328f1d.png" class="latex" alt="$45-2-24=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="131" height="13" /> cases.
</p><p>Of these, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/e/dbe36ab48eb2448f8b279bfb5c60f071208dbf11.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{6}{2}=15$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" /> cases has both counterfeit coins in the left-over.  The probability of having chosen four genuine coins therefore is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/8/88895a9eab2994f8d2a4407cc904d951af45c78d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\frac{15}{19} \ \mathbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, allow for all the coins to be distinguishable.
We split this up into cases. Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> being the weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> genuine coins together and Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> being the weight of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> genuine coin and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> counterfeit coin.
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />: All Genuine coins chosen.
This happens in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/9/129dac25f541c8d9524ec314d712bd53dab549ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\binom{8}{2}\cdot\binom{6}{2}}{2}=210$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="41" /> ways
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />: Genuine coin and Counterfeit coin both chosen.
This happens in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/9/8c9dcef05297fea305d76bd1b0eb97319bbd2a71.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot7=56$" width="73" height="12" /> ways.
</p><p>Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/8/88895a9eab2994f8d2a4407cc904d951af45c78d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\frac{15}{19} \ \mathbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />.
</p>
