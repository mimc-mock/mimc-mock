<p>Seven students count from 1 to 1000 as follows:
</p><p>Alice says all the numbers, except she skips the middle number in each consecutive group of three numbers. That is, Alice says 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9, . . ., 997, 999, 1000.
</p><p>Barbara says all of the numbers that Alice doesn't say, except she also skips the middle number in each consecutive group of three numbers.
</p><p>Candice says all of the numbers that neither Alice nor Barbara says, except she also skips the middle number in each consecutive group of three numbers.
</p><p>Debbie, Eliza, and Fatima say all of the numbers that none of the students with the first names beginning before theirs in the alphabet say, except each also skips the middle number in each of her consecutive groups of three numbers.
</p><p>Finally, George says the only number that no one else says.
</p><p>What number does George say?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/2/8920630c3afdd46de5fdac3d771769ffe9042df7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 37\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 242\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 365\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 728\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 998$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="430" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First look at the numbers Alice says. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/c/79c9a5bb2a4062fb9535c9de974c54b89aba9c99.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9 \cdots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="117" height="16" /> skipping every number that is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/9/14993088c90e11f704b76e68891cac3a83f0389a.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />. Thus, Barbara says those numbers EXCEPT every second - being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/2/062645807c2390febd32f01020d4419621eb2275.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 3^1 \equiv 5 \pmod{3^2=9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="192" height="19" />. So Barbara skips every number congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/0/320fd61a5704824ad96a46764d2ee9d136d4e230.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \pmod 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />. We continue and see: 
</p><p>Alice skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/9/14993088c90e11f704b76e68891cac3a83f0389a.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \pmod 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />, Barbara skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/0/320fd61a5704824ad96a46764d2ee9d136d4e230.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \pmod 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />, Candice skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/2/f72b020c86dcbddbff0056be47af758740d84f9d.png" class="latex" alt="$14 \pmod {27}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />, Debbie skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/a/3fa51dbe2affab5c2d481f7fe4db0aeb62d659ca.png" class="latex" alt="$41 \pmod {81}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="18" />, Eliza skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/d/9ed7034fb3ba4e6be262b69c0041e741b37b7251.png" class="latex" alt="$122 \pmod {243}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" />, and Fatima skips <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/3/133d651fc99bca8646cf58ea5d3edc12500ff553.png" class="latex" alt="$365 \pmod {729}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since the only number congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/3/133d651fc99bca8646cf58ea5d3edc12500ff553.png" class="latex" alt="$365 \pmod {729}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" /> and less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/2/a7211efdf41d2bc909e3d0549b2ab5cba0356ca5.png" class="latex" alt="$1,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/8/988ab73be88b10c8cbf520db5e7cb4b3c6531963.png" class="latex" alt="$365$" width="26" height="12" />, the correct answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85fe11bf0663d2377c32a8b0a1c728d8bd460afd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{365\ \mathbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>After Alice says all her numbers, the numbers not mentioned yet are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/5/c254a439f1b7c61678f6c93b65a1e804cb43a022.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Alice: } 2,5,8,11,14,17,\cdots,998.\]" width="244" height="16" />After Barbara says all her numbers, the numbers that haven't been said yet are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/9/5398b79586eb3a31db84fcb7401958793bc617ef.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Barbara: } 5,14,23,32,41,50,\cdots,995.\]" width="284" height="16" />After Candice, the list is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c70eae04acd2e5039b7f3dd4d2f8082f4232fd49.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Candice: } 14,41,68,\cdots,986.\]" width="214" height="16" />Notice how each list is an arithmetic sequence where the common ratio is thrice the common ratio of the previous list and the first term is the second term of the previous list. Now that the pattern is clear, we construct the rest of the lists: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/f/94f813642162b02d347eec913ee07568faaf9fa6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Debbie: } 41,122,203,\cdots,959\]" width="220" height="16" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cec87f92101e13fd4c468c7b7d4b709564697123.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Eliza: } 122,365,608,878\]" width="180" height="16" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5fcf7b4b0cb7db4e8d4e2d32e86da8d151e08c3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\text{Fatima: } 365\]" width="89" height="13" />
</p><p>Thus, George says <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/9/4c90e2c2dd383b488d045b341bdabc8cf0241524.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 365}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Notice that Alice has skipped the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/e/68eabcc9922cdddf961b463f94a8b64d0fecbd42.png" class="latex" alt="$3n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/7/0c71dca8822a567176a78d67d8327e83a47ccfbd.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1,2,3,...,333$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="136" height="16" />.
Namely, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/674e3d30f059c1abd0e9efdfdeabb6a5f5917a0a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3\cdot1-1,3\cdot2-1,3\cdot3-1,...,3\cdot333-1\]" width="314" height="16" />
Thus the numbers that Barbara skips are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/e/49ed09af990c89a4bd31a46fec217927c1ab01b4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3\cdot2-1,3\cdot5-1,3\cdot8-1,...\]" width="225" height="16" />
or in a more general expression, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/4/91414dcd27e1e7bf2d11a9f27488db1bb19eef50.png" class="latex" alt="$3(3n-1)-1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/8/e88831557651694d152b65db1ff4b5fd2d5e237b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1,2,3,...$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="100" height="16" />.
Namely, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/f/f4fe7662f52581f068a0b6bff96a799094169d58.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3\cdot(3\cdot1-1)-1,3\cdot(3\cdot2-1)-1,3\cdot(3\cdot3-1)-1,...\]" width="428" height="18" />
Repeating the pattern until George, we have the first number he says,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/6/796d7f9cb0796bd989119b1c57d66c12ab5d9c00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3(3(3(3(3(3\cdot1-1)-1)-1)-1)-1)-1= \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 365}\]" width="433" height="28" />
In addition, note that the second number George says exceeds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />.
</p>
