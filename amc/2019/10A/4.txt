<p>A box contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> red balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> green balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> yellow balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> blue balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> white balls, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> black balls. What is the minimum number of balls that must be drawn from the box without replacement to guarantee that at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> balls of a single color will be drawn?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/e/a4ebe2e29ae11332275cf5ea7a7ccff1f6f73d62.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 75 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 76 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 79 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 84 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 91$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="397" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We try to find the worst case scenario where we can find the maximum number of balls that can be drawn while getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/2/e6209f2299b7c65dcc3658e8838fed28608f1ef6.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> of each color by applying the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pigeonhole_principle" class="mw-redirect" title="Pigeonhole principle">pigeonhole principle</a> and through this we get a perfect guarantee. 
Namely, we can draw up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> red balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> green balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> yellow balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> blue balls, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> white balls, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> black balls, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> balls, without drawing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> balls of any one color. Drawing one more ball guarantees that we will get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> balls of one color — either red, green, or yellow. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/b/99bd85b011860022d8ea82ffced6e5bc65232d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$75 + 1 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 76}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="138" height="28" />.
</p>
