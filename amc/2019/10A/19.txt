<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Grouping the first and last terms and two middle terms gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/4/ff41d06a0394a796e1cb07aa460c25f9aed7bac7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2+5x+4)(x^2+5x+6)+2019$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="267" height="19" />, which can be simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9b4214e4fad59069eb4d7b09da14cbf62a5a6e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2+5x+5)^2-1+2019$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="19" />. Noting that squares are nonnegative, and verifying that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb63e6748a6efd487b9add5b2b9de7fdbe150a39.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+5x+5=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" /> for some real <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c16aa87629fc4d7eb3af83eb24cc6ed75065a72c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 2018}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="83" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/0/1e00c082ea4101184ffffd540eeb8a4c5acf6696.png" class="latex" alt="$a=x+\tfrac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="75" height="21" />. Then the expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/7/4f780f2fc457f3ee899d25ee4de3d4d50dc16966.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)(x+2)(x+3)(x+4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="221" height="18" /> becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/0/ad07595de90df617bcc661491b8e1c33cd62f0f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a-\tfrac{3}{2}\right)\left(a-\tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(a+\tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(a+\tfrac{3}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="241" height="22" />. 
</p><p>We can now use the difference of two squares to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/d/e3dbff21dfae43481821282f812d18ae59b36d59.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a^2-\tfrac{9}{4}\right)\left(a^2-\tfrac{1}{4}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="132" height="22" />, and expand this to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/e/c4eeebb172804d10a60665e194c1740ac06d047c.png" class="latex" alt="$a^4-\tfrac{5}{2}a^2+\tfrac{9}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="104" height="21" />.
</p><p>Refactor this by completing the square to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/a/1cab56123c73768d4520f0197e8eb390a15b90a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a^2-\tfrac{5}{4}\right)^2-1$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="104" height="25" />, which has a minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />. The answer is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/6/406210b881ab9728d91ed633e64a4a7d9d0e6123.png" class="latex" alt="$2019-1=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }2018}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="174" height="28" />.
</p>
