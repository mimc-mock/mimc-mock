<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1c2532c45ec05a88a74ea36b442cbd49851637.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(40);draw((-1,0)--(1,0)--(0,2.75)--cycle);draw(circumcircle((-1,0),(0,0),(0,2.75)));label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(1,0),SE);label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(0,2.75),N);label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(-1,0),SW);label(&quot;$E$&quot;,(0,0),S);label(&quot;$D$&quot;,(0.77,0.64),E);draw((0,0)--(0,2.75));draw((-1,0)--(0.77,0.64));[/asy]" width="222" height="228" />
</p><p>Drawing it out, we see <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5529f83cca44961d418dc5a92baf90a667913c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDC$" width="57" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b1cee5c0355428ff8d477ecbf406f0f99bcca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BEC$" width="56" height="12" /> are right angles, as they are inscribed in a semicircle. Using the fact that it is an isosceles triangle, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/95921cbed071c6395df4cf40392e741e64e23db7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ABC=70^{\circ}$" width="103" height="13" />. We can find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/a/38a11225fa05960ad3f7f45b8b3708a52f697044.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ECB=20^{\circ}$" width="104" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/3/de39f2db1a60f108043791114b23d14f2beeafa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DBC=50^{\circ}$" width="105" height="13" /> by the triangle angle sum on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/7/587303f5573fed1a511c759c6b096556daed47c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ECB$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/e/fee181f0e279c48906d10847f1986f902dc84efe.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DBC$" width="60" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/3/c13f8b45e2c4c03e9f08e49dbb458eac1a67adc9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle BDC+\angle DCB+\angle DBC=180^{\circ}\implies90^{\circ}+40^{\circ}+\angle DBC=180^{\circ}\implies\angle DBC=50^{\circ}\]" width="660" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/7/ff7ba7cfa8f12cbf77d145917999d5f041d69be7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle BEC+\angle EBC+\angle ECB=180^{\circ}\implies90^{\circ}+70^{\circ}+\angle ECB=180^{\circ}\implies\angle ECB=20^{\circ}\]" width="654" height="14" />
</p><p>Then, we take triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/d/efdd26b7cbfad505c1adbdfccc28d3ab1eb91872.png" class="latex" alt="$BFC$" width="43" height="12" />, and find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/9/579caf91d35f223d9d75e469aa51df9a4add6a89.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BFC=180^{\circ}-50^{\circ}-20^{\circ}=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 110^{\circ}}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="321" height="29" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Alternatively, we could have used similar triangles. We start similarly to Solution 1.
</p><p>Drawing it out, we see <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5529f83cca44961d418dc5a92baf90a667913c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDC$" width="57" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b1cee5c0355428ff8d477ecbf406f0f99bcca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BEC$" width="56" height="12" /> are right angles, as they are inscribed in a semicircle. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e646207dd9c9f02f9d86ca5957424705df5ce4f9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle BDA = 180^{\circ} - \angle BDC = 180^{\circ} - 90^{\circ} = 90^{\circ}.\]" width="356" height="13" />
</p><p>So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/d/9eded3d602f290553c15fc7e3ceee2b956186008.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BEF \sim BDA$" width="127" height="13" /> by AA Similarity, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/6/936ca8ba13931ce893f57c7ff6a632ab73edb3b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EBF = \angle DBA$" width="137" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/ffffc035f57649565fc830ad0db406a09603f430.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BEC = 90^{\circ} = \angle BDA$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="189" height="15" />. 
Thus, we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/f/13fe88ad0aa1bb15f5eb90b72aec4cbc66925fc0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle EFB = \angle DAB = \angle CAB = 70^{\circ}.\]" width="270" height="13" />
</p><p>Finally, we deduce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2fa26ffdb5647c3bb11330e44ff47f321c41045.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\angle BFC = 180^{\circ} - \angle EFB = 180^{\circ} - 70^{\circ} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 110^{\circ}}.\]" width="410" height="29" />
</p>
