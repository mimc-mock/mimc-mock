<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let's first work out the slope-intercept form of all three lines:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/8/ff811a70e36e6b6f5be8a11a0162dd987fd51b26.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)=(2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="105" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/d/68d3046ad3a93ec75bc68a1242e81c1f17bf3599.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{x}{2} + b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="33" /> implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/b/e7bedbc91ee3be6f268636bccd83367567f8acb9.png" class="latex" alt="$2=\frac{2}{2} +b=1+b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="140" height="37" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf590e9abb33aa9776ddc386b1ba6ee7b43fa413.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1261eef3a40ffc3c46fa8b5a6e95894472b2aa.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2x + c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="15" /> implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/a/3aa6642c893ed4a73976980996c919b7ecb1eede.png" class="latex" alt="$2= 2 \cdot 2+c=4+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="158" height="13" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/5/52566cda76e94a472e3d567b038aff381ea43375.png" class="latex" alt="$c=-2$" width="54" height="12" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/3/6631d7f1b7fd1489eb9406d34ae9566aa2a796db.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" /> implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/9/5692f7cbcbd5ab2ab9a4d5e60319152f275aca19.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x+10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" />. Thus the lines are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/4/73495636fd3674e97c4e36a4a0da969f9c18502b.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{x}{2} +1, y=2x-2,$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="176" height="33" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/9/5692f7cbcbd5ab2ab9a4d5e60319152f275aca19.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x+10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" />. 
Now we find the intersection points between each of the lines with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/9/5692f7cbcbd5ab2ab9a4d5e60319152f275aca19.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x+10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="98" height="16" />, which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/d/07d9cf67e797afa7e029e4b13c4c758197f8cfd6.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/5/0c5d8e12531fe9f78ed85df73d0591e676489292.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Using the distance formula and then the Pythagorean Theorem, we see that we have an isosceles triangle with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71df1b44d6caa4b0f798d48ff8ee72da386e4cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/5/005458b4333cedea7a3aa771f6620c976597f9af.png" class="latex" alt="$3\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />, whose area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/4/394e31b6e19ba6e9c14f340669915c86d068a16a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
