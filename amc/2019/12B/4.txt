<p>There is a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/d/98dc20d6f1386364161a4508d2ff25986eed1fd0.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)! + (n+2)! = n! \cdot 440$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="225" height="18" />. What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d346d6b6bffb3d088857498848276d580c012e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }3\qquad\textbf{(B) }8\qquad\textbf{(C) }10\qquad\textbf{(D) }11\qquad\textbf{(E) }12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="380" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/4/e94a76dd637eee784abfc844bcc187bc69d72464.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}&amp; (n+1)n! + (n+2)(n+1)n! = 440 \cdot n! \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;n![n+1 + (n+2)(n+1)] = 440 \cdot n! \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;n + 1 + n^2 + 3n + 2 = 440 \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;n^2 + 4n - 437 = 0\end{split}\]" width="325" height="95" />
</p><p>Solving by the quadratic formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/2/a023ee925fa8d3fd93a37f46364df404ee8d89d4.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{-4\pm \sqrt{16+437\cdot4}}{2} = \frac{-4\pm 42}{2} = \frac{38}{2} = 19$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="369" height="40" /> (since clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d5b948fd2e8a82be18042d192710914b5e819.png" class="latex" alt="$n \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />). The answer is therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0bfc50fc2473f35c63580ea31e43dd3aa38806.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="129" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f344e7d8b14a7886fc7fb72da53cd0a3271dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$n!$" width="14" height="13" /> gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95acebc265a9f6c6779a1c3e16f867517e875b6a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(n+1)+(n+2)(n+1)=440 \Rightarrow n^2+4n-437=0 \Rightarrow (n-19)(n+23)=0.\]" width="544" height="19" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is non-negative, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba21440957dd537f17834562b567ea84248f4c0.png" class="latex" alt="$n=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0bfc50fc2473f35c63580ea31e43dd3aa38806.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="129" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f344e7d8b14a7886fc7fb72da53cd0a3271dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$n!$" width="14" height="13" /> as before gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/2/a42d89bee1ecf65da91202c91ee88012635736cf.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)+(n+1)(n+2)=440$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="242" height="18" />. Now factor out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/c/99cb3f3a8b3bbfc702c5863d16602a06cded535d.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/d/ded98c42b6ff670c17e4156389c227f01a4c5942.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+3)=440$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />. By considering the prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/b/b7bac8d158ad07b42ad9889011a7e4e67851a82d.png" class="latex" alt="$440$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, a bit of experimentation gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/f/e6f46a9e0ab409c6c256eec4b5016ec9ca874cfe.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1=20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/4/6344e40f77ba5178ab64e65c57ad7b5c153262e7.png" class="latex" alt="$n+3=22$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba21440957dd537f17834562b567ea84248f4c0.png" class="latex" alt="$n=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0bfc50fc2473f35c63580ea31e43dd3aa38806.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="129" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/3/1632e151890c4d4661838e345bb94afd164db4bb.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)! + (n+2)! = (n+1)n! + (n+2)(n+1)n! = 440 \cdot n!$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="475" height="20" />, the result can be factored into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/0/f7066b873664571ed8d9ba5112824cdb5e922750.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+3)n!=440 \cdot n!$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="211" height="20" /> and divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f344e7d8b14a7886fc7fb72da53cd0a3271dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$n!$" width="14" height="13" /> on both sides to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/d/ded98c42b6ff670c17e4156389c227f01a4c5942.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+3)=440$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />. From there, it is easier to complete the square with the quadratic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/3/ea3e70c62aaab0dca2d6b289775e76c8bc2e9456.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)(n+3) = n^2 + 4n + 3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="231" height="21" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/f/6afca704f98098fd57ce728de78ff178ffd18cc1.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2+4n+4=441 \Rightarrow (n+2)^2=441$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="289" height="21" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/7/b8764197f5186bd985a80b8a20831f9fc7bcd100.png" class="latex" alt="$n=19,-23$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="17" />, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/4/0247db3d702b5ce6be98f50771750a2723cddcea.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba21440957dd537f17834562b567ea84248f4c0.png" class="latex" alt="$n=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/0/cc0bfc50fc2473f35c63580ea31e43dd3aa38806.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="129" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Randomlygenerated
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Rewrite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/0/66069cde13f52c05f55e9fd9f6800234e5b1395c.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)! + (n+2)! = 440 \cdot n!$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="227" height="20" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/f/53ffe565e3cda7d1d91484034b9ff844623d9c21.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+1)n! + (n+2)(n+1)n! = 440 \cdot n!.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="311" height="20" /> Factoring out the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f344e7d8b14a7886fc7fb72da53cd0a3271dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$n!$" width="14" height="13" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/5/3158908ccc7ad52781e8beb9a5ac5aba03b0a640.png" class="latex" alt="$n!(n + 1 + (n+1)(n+2)) = 440 \cdot n!.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="295" height="20" /> Expand this to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/e/5fe47928f33609c4a7b640ebc8bf6e8aa281f23b.png" class="latex" alt="$n!(n^2 + 4n + 3) = 440 \cdot n!.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="209" height="21" /> Factor this and divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/f/63f344e7d8b14a7886fc7fb72da53cd0a3271dcc.png" class="latex" alt="$n!$" width="14" height="13" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/9/4f9f17168605b8b64d32902c914598efc84e1b29.png" class="latex" alt="$(n + 1)(n + 3) = 440.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="171" height="20" /> If we take the prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/b/b7bac8d158ad07b42ad9889011a7e4e67851a82d.png" class="latex" alt="$440$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> we see that it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/d/90dd29d92ab3d3c7ca7144a0edc100b7baf7f9ae.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3 * 5 * 11.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="17" /> Intuitively, we can find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/606e393c8e92a0a1769a15a6cac5e91426b0d528.png" class="latex" alt="$n + 1 = 20$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/0/c605f3d3a24c35950c348857b7e5d9a16ca9a0f4.png" class="latex" alt="$n + 3 = 22.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="92" height="15" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/a/c5a43ed1f0530f16bbbc786ba1f9e8fd14fbffd9.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 19.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="60" height="14" /> Since the problem asks for the sum of the didgits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, we finally calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/3/6639552b29a3b33bcd048300a8dde74e0e84590f.png" class="latex" alt="$1 + 9 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="15" /> and get answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95a37e2c613248cd21b3266be23fe9b0b6e1ac11.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="30" />.
</p><p>~pnacham
