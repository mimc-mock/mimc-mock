<p>Right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6ce2cd123db24a7b8373999aea7a98927e3b3c81.png" class="latex" alt="$ACD$" width="43" height="13" /> with right angle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is constructed outwards on the hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> of isosceles right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> with leg length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, as shown, so that the two triangles have equal perimeters. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/a/63acf06cac58c422a4223211c11219c8cc1ec820.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(2\angle BAD)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="18" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Firstly, note by the Pythagorean Theorem in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/a/53af4dc7f903af34395fa663d80b9a03ca61c9e0.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />. Now, the equal perimeter condition means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2ddba06212173155d8d32e87a8adf154c8acf271.png" class="latex" alt="$BC + BA = 2 = CD + DA$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="218" height="14" />, since side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is common to both triangles and thus can be discounted. This relationship, in combination with the Pythagorean Theorem in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7a55113a84ee4c04eb66f66d08310c32533ca2.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACD$" width="59" height="13" />, gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/f/bcf46ff8a32ca1ac6c6c0331804664cfca4e5888.png" class="latex" alt="$AC^2+CD^2=\left(\sqrt{2}\right)^2+\left(2-DA\right)^2=DA^2$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="335" height="35" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/6/ef6b6a295a2018f29f3fdf0f659c100ac085a916.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 4 - 4DA + DA^2 = DA^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="219" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/f/02f13fa5248756f5c219405e8a0f2a83f45cb3cf.png" class="latex" alt="$DA = \frac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/81671d2a0e680f74e52d80adb5db9824ae34d589.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />.
</p><p>Next, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e56cf0545f23438edb02db8f0353effc64ae850.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC = 45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/0/9a09b2a71113597e4185c02821b938b3ad234e85.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\left(\angle BAC\right)} = \cos{\left(\angle BAC\right)} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="267" height="41" />. Using the lengths found above, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8ec080d44e081956ef394a1f317f3a91cbca113.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\left(\angle CAD\right)} = \frac{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)} = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="186" height="48" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/2/7527551e8c7d711fb6a7fd8eb8b01dcc857e15f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos{\left(\angle CAD\right)} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)} = \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="213" height="48" />.
</p><p>Thus, by the addition formulae for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/b/6fb300dacfcde1b57ad070409262b1743e0cd966.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5e108fdc4bf4fb6151cb922992f23bc6c35985.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos$" width="24" height="8" />, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/d/2fdf3addefe605adb6e5ed59823c1d9fec0df695.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}\sin{\left(\angle BAD\right)}&amp;=\sin{\left(\angle BAC + \angle CAD\right)}\\&amp;=\sin{\left(\angle BAC\right)}\cos{\left(\angle CAD\right)}+\cos{\left(\angle BAC\right)}\sin{\left(\angle CAD\right)}\\&amp;=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cdot\frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cdot\frac{1}{3} \\ &amp;= \frac{2 \sqrt{2} + 1}{3 \sqrt{2}}\end{split}\]" width="532" height="149" />
and
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/e/65ecfb234bb7493ec8c221d140fecc64e1564ca5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}\cos{\left(\angle BAD\right)}&amp;=\cos{\left(\angle BAC + \angle CAD\right)}\\&amp;=\cos{\left(\angle BAC\right)}\cos{\left(\angle CAD\right)}-\sin{\left(\angle BAC\right)}\sin{\left(\angle CAD\right)}\\&amp;=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cdot\frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cdot\frac{1}{3} \\ &amp;= \frac{2 \sqrt{2} - 1}{3 \sqrt{2}}\end{split}\]" width="534" height="149" />
</p><p>Hence, by the double angle formula for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/b/6fb300dacfcde1b57ad070409262b1743e0cd966.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin$" width="22" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/8/1d81a7e0ae3c479bf3e0b0d311aed31cb607411a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\left(2\angle BAD\right)} = 2\sin{\left(\angle BAD\right)}\cos{\left(\angle BAD\right)} = \frac{2(8-1)}{18} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } \frac{7}{9}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="516" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_2_(coordinate_geometry)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2_.28coordinate_geometry.29">Solution 2 (coordinate geometry)</span></h3>
<p>We use the Pythagorean Theorem, as in Solution 1, to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/5/e9547bfbb1098b5315066c2fafc344410c06faef.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=\frac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/e/9ae43094cc83d154c740f67e412d52c26bc18471.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />. Now notice that the angle between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> and the vertical (i.e. the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> &#8211; to see this, drop a perpendicular from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d66492998689e8303534c9e4f3ba1fa650fd41f0.png" class="latex" alt="$BA$" width="28" height="13" /> which meets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d66492998689e8303534c9e4f3ba1fa650fd41f0.png" class="latex" alt="$BA$" width="28" height="13" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and use the fact that the angle sum of quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3cdfaa8309bb90dcd2109efbb63d5ac09ec026.png" class="latex" alt="$CBED$" width="58" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/b/96bd59024f5cb49b070ca9b0f8906499940c75e7.png" class="latex" alt="$360^{\circ}$" width="33" height="13" />. Anyway, this implies that the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and the length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> has coordinates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/0/4f0d082fb8f629441a1c66f275d01605716ae9d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(0+\frac{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\sqrt{2}}, 1+\frac{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\sqrt{2}}\right) = \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}, 1+\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="321" height="53" />. 
</p><p>Thus we have the lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/5/85541b02c219dea43716bfb377df35c21678e139.png" class="latex" alt="$DE=1+\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="121" height="41" /> (it is just the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-coordinate) and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/b/23b06adae2cefc2e162c78b0e5971e0a0e2d257b.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = 1-\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="119" height="41" />. By simple trigonometry in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/6/9364f2848153329571245fe91262b2983db3c20f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DAE$" width="59" height="13" />, we now find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/4/9840a4756050c95e3de4cb4524444a44bc4189b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin{\left(\angle BAD\right)} = \frac{\left(1+\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)} = \frac{\left(2+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)}{3} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}+1}{3\sqrt{2}}\]" width="398" height="59" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/5/205097b914a3c38a283f9f25cbdd47e4603d1f11.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos{\left(\angle BAD\right)} = \frac{\left(1-\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}}\right)}{\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)} = \frac{\left(2-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)}{3} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}-1}{3\sqrt{2}}\]" width="400" height="59" /> just as before. We can then use the double angle formula (as in Solution 1) to deduce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a7979a9baee61e046691edda2457821583361dc1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\left(2\angle BAD\right)} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } \frac{7}{9}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="190" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(easier_finish_to_Solution_1)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28easier_finish_to_Solution_1.29">Solution 3 (easier finish to Solution 1)</span></h3>
<p>Again, use Pythagorean Theorem to find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/5/e9547bfbb1098b5315066c2fafc344410c06faef.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=\frac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/e/9ae43094cc83d154c740f67e412d52c26bc18471.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/7/727c60a33d0a969e044a8a2191912840ce82c4c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DAC=\theta$" width="89" height="13" />. Note that we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/5/dd588f35e5d0d80204a299c11aaa929102b1debb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin{(90+2\theta)}=\cos{2\theta}\]" width="166" height="18" />
which is easy to compute: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/7/8179264932fcd7b3160f522aee95c483c794ec89.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos{\theta}=\frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3}\implies \cos{2\theta}=2(\frac{8}{9})-1= \boxed{\textbf{(D) } \frac{7}{9}}\]" width="369" height="48" />
</p>
