<p>How many ways are there to paint each of the integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c14b72ddb47c1a5a508eadcb1b62b31669155c59.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 3, \dots, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> either red, green, or blue so that each number has a different color from each of its proper divisors?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/9/6a9edbc60df4d61f5ed4e3444a7c7e1957051cd8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 144\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 216\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 256\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 384\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 432$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> can be painted with no restrictions because the set of integers does not contain a multiple or proper factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. There are 3 ways to paint each, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/3/ee3b8d2ddaba13ea4facb9248e92f4994d3da66a.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{9}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> ways to paint both. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is the most restrictive number. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/a/93ae69b24f3b73911be31d1522a164f53c2712dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{3}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> ways to paint <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, but without loss of generality, let it be painted red. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> cannot be the same color as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef3359e13c64b45a3591d2e5534d39d10401a7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> ways to paint <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, which automatically determines the color for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> cannot be painted red, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef3359e13c64b45a3591d2e5534d39d10401a7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> ways to paint <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, but WLOG, let it be painted blue. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef3359e13c64b45a3591d2e5534d39d10401a7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> choices for the color for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, which is either red or green in this case. Lastly, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/f/eef3359e13c64b45a3591d2e5534d39d10401a7c.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="15" /> ways to choose the color for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/0/d601d4f7a118a22c39792fa65366138d67ebe820.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }432}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="217" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We note that the primes can be colored any of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> colors since they don't have any proper divisors other than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, which is not in the list. Furthermore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> is the only number in the list that has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> distinct prime factors (namely, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />), so we do casework on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p><b>Case 1</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> are the same color
</p><p>In this case, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> primes to choose the color for (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />). Afterwards, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> have two possible colors, which will determine the color of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c006155041352ca4eefb70d803f3c6d1ee8dae46.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3\cdot 2^3=216$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="15" /> possibilities here. 
</p><p><b>Case 2</b>: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> are different colors
</p><p>In this case, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> primes to color. Without loss of generality, we'll color the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> first, then the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> color choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/c/21c5141b46f6a0bffda60305cf8b51786ab4f857.png" class="latex" alt="$2,5,7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> color choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. This will determine the color of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> as well. After that, we only need to choose the color for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, which each have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> choices. Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c006155041352ca4eefb70d803f3c6d1ee8dae46.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3\cdot 2^3=216$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="15" /> possibilities here as well. 
</p><p>Adding up gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5daf91cb1be7b51dee188694239c2fba708f6555.png" class="latex" alt="$216+216=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }432}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="173" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/d/d7d59b9ed8d9fb13113f63abd77fd45aa99914c7.png" class="latex" alt="$2,4,8$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> require different colors each, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to color these.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> can be any color, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b5343f2d9e22816e8de24262565ff2937695b2c.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> ways to color these.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> can have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> colors once <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is colored, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> also has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> colors following <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, which leaves another <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>All together: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/6/5e6e5553cc4373da732356082f9366c898a412a2.png" class="latex" alt="$6\times 3 \times 3 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 432 \Rightarrow \boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="271" height="24" />.
</p>
