<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/0/26077fe0cd0e9b7374589fcd2e9a2db9f24df7a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_2{x} = \log_y{16}=k$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="155" height="19" />, so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/1/a411b0864d2f88348df2af5c8e439f63210c0b97.png" class="latex" alt="$2^k=x$" width="51" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/b/83baa37c5830d781ff4d9e52fa61d06f6c9dc3f1.png" class="latex" alt="$y^k=16 \implies y=2^{\frac{4}{k}}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="161" height="21" />. Then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb00531d0f1e47de891c5427cd2b3a093a63024d.png" class="latex" alt="$(2^k)(2^{\frac{4}{k}})=2^{k+\frac{4}{k}}=2^6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="22" />.
</p><p>We therefore have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/c/8ccaaf9be36bb015dc8bf49f298d9c605e83617b.png" class="latex" alt="$k+\frac{4}{k}=6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, and deduce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/8/9580bb55b19ad880ecd69896e26f78305b0205b7.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2-6k+4=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" />. The solutions to this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/b/23bd6f45ea3d0a0672188d342810d63ac1f5c173.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 3 \pm \sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="18" />. 
</p><p>To solve the problem, we now find 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/e/46ed77e3d87b6098d7519d0212977a30c112d3f5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (\log_2\tfrac{x}{y})^2&amp;=(\log_2 x - \log_2 y)^2\\ &amp;=(k-\tfrac{4}{k})^2=(3 \pm \sqrt{5} - \tfrac{4}{3 \pm \sqrt{5}})^2 \\ &amp;= (3 \pm \sqrt{5} - [3 \mp \sqrt{5}])^2\\ &amp;= (3 \pm \sqrt{5} - 3 \pm \sqrt{5})^2\\ &amp;=(\pm 2\sqrt{5})^2 \\ &amp;= \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 20}. \\ \end{align*}" width="315" height="181" />
