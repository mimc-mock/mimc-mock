<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The triangle is placed on the sphere so that its three sides are tangent to the sphere. The cross-section of the sphere created by the plane of the triangle is also the incircle of the triangle. To find the inradius, use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/9/3496e81e4edbc431e2d4301d39e4b6b6792b7b3c.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{area} = \text{inradius} \cdot \text{semiperimeter}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="228" height="15" />. The area of the triangle can be found by drawing an altitude from the vertex between sides with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to the midpoint of the side with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. The Pythagorean triple <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> allows us easily to determine that the base is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> and the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. The formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db6bcdd5f7a4428b59f79599ad2d4f3c328ab2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {\text{base} \cdot \text{height}} {2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="37" /> can also be used to find the area of the triangle as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, while the semiperimeter is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/f/5fff78de6df7ee2ca09ecafce23c71824d8fb7bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {15 + 15 + 24} {2} = 27$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="145" height="37" />. After plugging into the equation, we thus get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5d7de1ade01506c90308340b3f78208fa43aef.png" class="latex" alt="$108 = \text{inradius} \cdot 27$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="141" height="13" />, so the inradius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Now, let the distance between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and the triangle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Choose a point on the incircle and denote it by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. The distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a762ac8b4e23ffdd778ad6d5a8b97df8c7ce01.png" class="latex" alt="$OA$" width="28" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, because it is just the radius of the sphere. The distance from point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to the center of the incircle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, because it is the radius of the incircle. By using the Pythagorean Theorem, we thus find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/c/bcca90264a9cebf5f6da440213714421d11a4d18.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \sqrt{6^2-4^2}=\sqrt{20} = \boxed{\textbf {(D) } 2 \sqrt {5}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="267" height="33" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Drop an altitude on the isosceles triangle. Let the resulting 3-4-5 right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/1/841477a87e41ca9c72a3c11eaabb6cf25ccd4977.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/816beb1dc6e9feb897721c321fa3344f60539b2f.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. By special triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c356cf0e82b342a8157ca8e2811447a5c25a639.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=9$" width="60" height="13" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the circle's radius. Let the circle's center be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the closest point on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />.
</p><p><br />
Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/1/071872eef2f0f07d4682e805535a8a3c67a438f0.png" class="latex" alt="$OD=r$" width="62" height="12" />. Obviously, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/3/86336c38e566ee9aed996bcb23b696b87afed22b.png" class="latex" alt="$ODBC$" width="58" height="12" /> is a kite. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db62bc9b34001cb8f1cce81203bcae4de5fb914a.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=DB=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="125" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/6/c3680935482609933399a720e8e50067a53f3f81.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=15-DB=3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="157" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/0/eb0536c13339e858238e952282d18f19b18e7c84.png" class="latex" alt="$AO=AC-r=9-r$" width="174" height="13" />. By Pythagoras, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/d/9cdf147484e6d387b8a4a2217b1e691d775303bc.png" class="latex" alt="$AD^2+OD^2=AO^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/c/9ec4edf3d31f3bfe709e2906550dce189637d781.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2+r^2=(9-r)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="19" />. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/2/e2221c84f16a3f75ae07e5023384cc40d9c05357.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2$" width="14" height="15" /> terms cancel out, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17fcc101cc2bd83642d07d7e8a9b38f698d08a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$r=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. 
</p><p><br />
As before, using Pythagoras again, the distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/0/700efb2a0b57030c0a4d5aff75b9e95db764bfd1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{6^2-4^2}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}2\sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="169" height="33" />
</p><p>(Solution by BJHHar)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>As in Solution 1, we note that by the Pythagorean Theorem, the height of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, and that the three sides of the triangle are tangent to the sphere, so the circle in the cross-section of the sphere is the incenter of the triangle. 
</p><p>Recall that the inradius is the intersection of the angle bisectors. To find the inradius of the incircle, we use the Angle Bisector Theorem.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/c/bdcf44c11c7c70c94800daa2f74b4b0a4fd15efc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(12,9)--(24,0)--cycle); dot((0,0)); dot((12,9)); dot((24,0)); dot((12,0)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,0),SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(12,9),N); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(24,0),SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,(12,-1/2),S); label(&quot;$I$&quot;,(12,4),SE);  draw((12,9)--(12,0), dashed); draw(circle((12,4),4)); draw((0,0)--(216/13,216/39)); dot((12,4)); label(&quot;$15$&quot;, (6,9/2),NW); label(&quot;$12$&quot;, (6,-1),S);  [/asy]" width="252" height="132" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/1/d7186b14a754a376e6a4e18cc72f642306cfd06f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split}&amp;\frac{AB}{BI}=\frac{AD}{DI} \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;\frac{15}{BI}=\frac{12}{DI} \\ \Rightarrow \ &amp;\frac{BI}{5}=\frac{DI}{4}\end{split}\]" width="111" height="125" />
Since we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08f556a367a704941ee3ae3b150c9132ea0956ba.png" class="latex" alt="$BI+DI$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" /> (the height) is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/1/d512ad2a21a5cd7fef5f6a6b590b89ec2a10289a.png" class="latex" alt="$DI$" width="25" height="12" /> (the inradius) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
From here, the problem can be solved in the same way as in Solution 1. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c582afe95b08bee9b196728872b11e01baec878.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf {(D) } 2 \sqrt{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="81" height="33" />.
</p>
