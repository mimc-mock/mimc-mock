<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions_1">Solutions 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/6/606f98896e6d8bf627a50089790657bf62dbdd06.png" class="latex" alt="$z = \mathrm{cis }(45^{\circ})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" />.
</p><p>Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/e/d2e2cd20ac20699ca6df87a3d9cbfa653e9e4f94.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{k} = z^{k + 8}$" width="74" height="15" /> for all positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> because of De Moivre's Theorem. Therefore, we want to look at the exponents of each term modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142830e75065cde7f2bda475790280312859d70b.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2, 5^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/f/d7ff7fb175cc5bcf2b3253037e5e8b0cd466f64e.png" class="latex" alt="$9^2$" width="15" height="15" /> are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/6/546b1d3e14b1967b249b9ab8d55c7efa425d084c.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \pmod{8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/4/d640945ab1cb9a23af186602d24c466608f922ec.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2, 6^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db61fd75a1a8bb6fa2b202b6b73daf9b4c9838d0.png" class="latex" alt="$10^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/2/622925d8c08b46cb1dd51db02633acc547cdaeac.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \pmod{8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afadb8f600462150f509cb512db79b96f076680.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2, 7^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c932acac3f8af148abfd610f23ef8d45a84b8071.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/6/546b1d3e14b1967b249b9ab8d55c7efa425d084c.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \pmod{8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/f/7dfb93bb043c71db28c6fa8152cf5361e1d042d0.png" class="latex" alt="$4^2, 8^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/a/5ca7ee3ad271a2c92ed9665f008c2831f5770407.png" class="latex" alt="$12^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" /> are all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/2/4e2918d9e4f3311ef03630c8a463e3d50a7761e1.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \pmod{8}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6f85fe79395c24066c44878be651d9c545e2f48.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{1^2} = z^{5^2} = z^{9^2} = \mathrm{cis }(45^{\circ})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="201" height="23" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/1/4d17404a1b74f465cdfd2337d08a838c94b7313b.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{2^2} = z^{6^2} = z^{10^2} = \mathrm{cis }(180^{\circ}) = -1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="264" height="23" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>It is well known that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/f/01f7aac3e57f997317789dcd4368f8a7b07ebc24.png" class="latex" alt="$|z|=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="51" height="18" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/1/6c132e93fd9e58937f8795d818e216649e652922.png" class="latex" alt="$\bar{z}=\frac{1}{z}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />. Therefore, we have that the desired expression is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b763e127c4049d35a9f4aca3944fa8115a243b3d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(z^1+z^4+z^9+...+z^{144}\right)\left(\bar{z}^1+\bar{z}^4+\bar{z}^9+...+\bar{z}^{144}\right)\]" width="398" height="22" /> We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/a/99af6b74d65e429c07455dfd8fcd31a2e7b83073.png" class="latex" alt="$z=e^{\frac{i\pi}{4}}$" width="56" height="17" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d02a319ea652d594ea4d4c76241b20c40fddf6e.png" class="latex" alt="$\bar{z}=e^{\frac{i7\pi}{4}}$" width="61" height="18" />. Then, by De Moivre's Theorem, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/b/fcb9722d92c8d55ccf625ff32f9755f4f5dd0e7b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(e^{\frac{i\pi}{4}}+e^{i\pi}+...+e^{2i\pi}\right)\left(e^{\frac{i7\pi}{4}}+e^{i7\pi}+...+e^{2i\pi}\right)\]" width="367" height="32" /> which can easily be computed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/e/c9eb973aa01d2a44ff9f20aaaa8c2d64387b7545.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{36}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />.
</p>
