<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> with integer side lengths, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/9/429806b5abc99c08d6ac09add0f8de0e57ccc790.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos A = \frac{11}{16}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39c48d319d634a1112d0fe3a34998d444ee481bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos B = \frac{7}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/f/f0f883377ebfdcd8e073d9eb6bc87b3cd8eac4ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos C = -\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="91" height="37" />. What is the least possible perimeter for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/0/290cb569a77038975aa298f392f799fbd7224efb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 9 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 12 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 23 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 27 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Notice that by the Law of Sines, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f20d4d5ff3edfce7abc9a9325a0091180749191.png" class="latex" alt="$a:b:c = \sin{A}:\sin{B}:\sin{C}$" width="228" height="13" />, so let's flip all the cosines using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/d/8ddf93c10344f9e2cb9cc98a8cb15db20a09b568.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin^{2}{x} + \cos^{2}{x} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="143" height="17" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/1/f41470508df2ceb87f97c7e939589024bceb16e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{x}$" width="35" height="12" /> is positive for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2cc9617c842e1e7642c9e9ab0fb0652953c500.png" class="latex" alt="$0^{\circ} &lt; x &lt; 180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="108" height="13" />, so we're good there).
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/1/e31bc1f43392d735a84f162d005b83755a6cb112.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin A=\frac{3\sqrt{15}}{16}, \qquad \sin B= \frac{\sqrt{15}}{8}, \qquad \text{and} \qquad\sin C=\frac{\sqrt{15}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="456" height="41" />
</p><p>These are in the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/103884da0ee9c6833a50952ede1026f42fce4ac3.png" class="latex" alt="$3:2:4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />, so our minimal triangle has side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/0/bd092b73eb02ad34cd3d767d4f347db11e312ebf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" /> is our answer.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0728bebb477a2636300c56851b6a6f9e382c1b83.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB$" width="55" height="13" /> is obtuse since its cosine is negative, so we let the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/5/c95137ae4be6a2067be5fad65da3e9f19e86ce70.png" class="latex" alt="$AH=11x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/d/d6db03c1a5ba26cd5c43a8eab83ad14fbab1cd53.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=16x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="80" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/b/f8b7fcc76a1ef39e7560d7479a1dd4e4b278c94f.png" class="latex" alt="$BH=7y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e7bffec0677cc5ccddbc1fa01618d79996c58dd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=8y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="16" />. By the Pythagorean Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/5/bd5348ffa8534a6e5fbd03e58963376b8f0d98e9.png" class="latex" alt="$CH=\sqrt{256x^2-121x^2}=3x\sqrt{15}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="260" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/e/91e25ed6c3fab16ac3e5d2f8f955edb6b8f215e3.png" class="latex" alt="$CH=\sqrt{64y^2-49y^2}=y\sqrt{15}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="233" height="22" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/4/1f4bd08912d1cbc11dc45a9b2c1f2305935449e7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" />. The sides of the triangle are then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/8/7a84756a03ce2796f241a7a945d7d865376aac03.png" class="latex" alt="$16x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/57449524ceb29e458f7487038108a4c1085c6c05.png" class="latex" alt="$11x+7(3x)=32x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/3/523b367da5ff9f1a5c21286cb0e035d209b71206.png" class="latex" alt="$24x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" />, so for some integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/6/3c6ceb41c87e81b2b7dea9dd4d85dc10f4200cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$16x=a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/7/277eb7ca3b7d2a391bade63ca00987a0699f9717.png" class="latex" alt="$24x=b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are minimal. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/4/1f4a42bdd3dc4d70add01026b97ef0acd81110ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{16}=\frac{b}{24}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="38" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/7/b17079b89b00e0b8c94d5bd24452453768f092f1.png" class="latex" alt="$3a=2b$" width="60" height="12" />. Thus the smallest possible positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> that satisfy this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/1/431ef9780f93ffb12986278c91f8f5921299dc9d.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. The sides of the triangle are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/0/bd092b73eb02ad34cd3d767d4f347db11e312ebf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" /> is our answer.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Using the law of cosines, we get the following equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/d/35d4013946eae185a89225f089db548f75d547d6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c^2=a^2+b^2+\frac{ab}{2}\]" width="135" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/c/27c198d772e88e74981fd4b9b61c9dfb7071f43c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b^2=a^2+c^2-\frac{7ac}{4}\]" width="145" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/0/980193d53e1d01409fe5f6383bb79e4937375b27.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2=b^2+c^2-\frac{11bc}{8}\]" width="151" height="37" />
</p><p>Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/6/ff65a6b0eb820bc1d94eaa81a7ad5c478eb23ef9.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2+c^2-\frac{7ac}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="106" height="37" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/d/a1d03c3c5b9abf1bdb0c9df06fe5fb11ae13d0e8.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2$" width="14" height="15" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/7/1d79eccff439beaa6bf80a082ab7e76a2952d246.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2=b^2+c^2-\frac{11bc}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="152" height="37" /> and simplifying, we get the following: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e88650b5486753d54595897d09a262484a4abd4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[14a+11b=16c\]" width="124" height="14" />
</p><p>Note that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc6d9a6172d35127b6cb37ea92192caf7029a54.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> are integers, we can solve this for integers. By some trial and error, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb58d242230a30f38f433de6a6a9f3cc6c139904.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c) = (3,2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="135" height="18" />. Checking to see that this fits the triangle inequality, we find out that this indeed works. Hence, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/e/80e2cf966c8a5c1d53e845111af6c1dbff270b0d.png" class="latex" alt="$3+2+4 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="152" height="28" />.
</p><p>~hiker
</p>
