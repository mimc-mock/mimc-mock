<p>Raashan, Sylvia, and Ted play the following game. Each starts with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />. A bell rings every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> seconds, at which time each of the players who currently have money simultaneously chooses one of the other two players independently and at random and gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> to that player. What is the probability that after the bell has rung <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f49e1f42131f302f2b4a45393cdc3fed5be2655c.png" class="latex" alt="$2019$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> times, each player will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />? (For example, Raashan and Ted may each decide to give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> to Sylvia, and Sylvia may decide to give her dollar to Ted, at which point Raashan will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/5/de5552aa8609c6804707c747d7b7aba076e1f740.png" class="latex" alt="$$0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />, Sylvia will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6e8cd66adab965db3a961cd262a4ba54e9a132.png" class="latex" alt="$$2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />, and Ted will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />, and that is the end of the first round of play. In the second round Rashaan has no money to give, but Sylvia and Ted might choose each other to give their <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> to, and the holdings will be the same at the end of the second round.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcf9869fee8823592a3f22fac8eb0896aad4751c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{1}{7} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \frac{1}{4} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{1}{3} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \frac{1}{2} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="371" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>On the first turn, each player starts off with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />. Each turn after that, there are only two possibilities: either everyone stays at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" />, which we will write as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />, or the distribution of money becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9df236a1a60e9290437e0c827817a2b1667c589d.png" class="latex" alt="$$2-$1-$0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="14" /> in some order, which we write as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/e/e0ebec07be4f92d99c9f7cea8ed403bca78a8590.png" class="latex" alt="$(3-0-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> cannot be achieved since either(1)the person cannot give money to himself or (2)there are a maximum of 2 dollars being distributed and the person has nothing to start with). We will consider these two states separately.
</p><p>In the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state, each person has two choices for whom to give their dollar to, meaning there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/4/57421b8557f6aa4384653806bfc7a5b80faf557a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3=8$" width="49" height="15" /> possible ways that the money can be rearranged. Note that there are only two ways that we can reach <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> again: 
</p><p>1. Raashan gives his money to Sylvia, who gives her money to Ted, who gives his money to Raashan.
</p><p>2. Raashan gives his money to Ted, who gives his money to Sylvia, who gives her money to Raashan.
</p><p>Thus, the probability of staying in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />, while the probability of going to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" /> (we can check that the 6 other possibilities lead to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />)
</p><p><br />
In the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state, we will label the person with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6e8cd66adab965db3a961cd262a4ba54e9a132.png" class="latex" alt="$$2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> as person A, the person with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> as person B, and the person with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/5/de5552aa8609c6804707c747d7b7aba076e1f740.png" class="latex" alt="$$0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> as person C. Person A has two options for whom to give money to, and person B has 2 options for whom to give money to, meaning there are total <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b824aee97b45806f24a637ddf896c9a52968eafe.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 2 = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="12" /> ways the money can be redistributed. The only way that the distribution can return to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> is if A gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> to B, and B gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/3/6739c8a3ef02d0f557bfbe01c9c92e562ff9a7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$$1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> to C. We check the other possibilities to find that they all lead back to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" />. Thus, the probability of going to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />, while the probability of staying in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/6/2068815bd6355203a34982e49c60b548f542d4f8.png" class="latex" alt="$(2-1-0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/1/e1150a17bf1b733cb666be84d920c71fc96bb3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />.
</p><p>No matter which state we are in, the probability of going to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />. This means that, after the bell rings 2018 times, regardless of what state the money distribution is in, there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> probability of going to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d5bee7f709fea3f887831511a0d4b904f3f70e.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-1-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="18" /> state after the 2019th bell ring. Thus, our answer is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/b/54bcfa89985334796ddfe01df5bd291f4370f5cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } \frac{1}{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
