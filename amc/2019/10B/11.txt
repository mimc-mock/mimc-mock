<p>Two jars each contain the same number of marbles, and every marble is either blue or green. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> green marbles in all. How many more blue marbles are in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> than in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Call the number of marbles in each jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> (because the problem specifies that they each contain the same number). Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26c41275e58f4e06d320e2deca4e758f1ad6179e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="33" /> is the number of green marbles in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca29cd57f340578db4d88c36920cbda3ad8b1e30.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> is the number of green marbles in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/1/191badeeb3bd0bab0bdf89b9371fdcd325dc7eea.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{9}+\frac{x}{10}=\frac{19x}{90}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="38" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/1/8b16114c96a6f7897877855c757af18c02bc0d6d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{19x}{90}=95$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="74" height="37" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85fcf5d7ea99a5cec1dbeb9319d9ff06b01cf32a.png" class="latex" alt="$x=450$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> marbles in each jar. 
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/9/799acf2caafda136525c1dae3607c17664261cee.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9x}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="21" height="38" /> is the number of blue marbles in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/b/2dbdc6ec89e165da1374b16d7b2bb59b500ed488.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{8x}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="21" height="37" /> is the number of blue marbles in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/f/4efbdae8b3e9d34ecbdce07bd4226512dd0cf3d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{9x}{10}-\frac{8x}{9}=\frac{x}{90} = 5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="148" height="38" /> more marbles in Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> than Jar <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. This means the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/8/79812fd9a09e7c6ae74dd56c36106638edc816aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
