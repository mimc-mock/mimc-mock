<p>All lines with equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/4/cb41f8995a5b0d9d6af2cb51ab1f427f2f477f79.png" class="latex" alt="$ax+by=c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> form an arithmetic progression pass through a common point. What are the coordinates of that point?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If all lines satisfy the condition, then we can just plug in values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> that form an arithmetic progression. Let's use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/8271d4342b38d2ab38bb626ffad8b9dae00b5e2d.png" class="latex" alt="$c=3$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/f/24f7150d029fa128fa37d225f668174382d6219b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=5$" width="40" height="12" />. Then the two lines we get are: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/3/9c3cc3de86dae8a6d15929d4bfc1cee1c4cc3bf8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x+2y=3\]" width="83" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/b/52bbca35323da434d502013ab88b64b0f41c48c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x+3y=5\]" width="83" height="16" />
Use elimination to deduce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d6268c8f2a2c9fee56a2090135a47741eb6487.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y = 2\]" width="42" height="15" /> and plug this into one of the previous line equations. We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/d/cddaecac8d8386d5b0db0ac822dd0d446fb57314.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x+4 = 3 \Rightarrow x=-1\]" width="159" height="14" /> Thus the common point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/207948d2a69617a7c089d5af027feda4127e0225.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } (-1,2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="102" height="29" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> form an arithmetic progression, so if the common difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we can say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/7/c87b7cb0d6d38cbb16702d2cdd5a88f4017c3b8e.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c = a, a+d, a+2d.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="186" height="16" /> Now we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67f8993d18fc2ec46824d72093c2bbb7f93d3183.png" class="latex" alt="$ax+ (a+d)y = a+2d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="182" height="18" />, and expanding gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/5/c0501f673f100d829ff861adff29e8b5b610c89f.png" class="latex" alt="$ax + ay + dy = a + 2d.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="180" height="16" /> Factoring gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/f/2ef13f3cf76c5969977ffa32330fbf148d56ff9d.png" class="latex" alt="$a(x+y-1)+d(y-2) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="216" height="18" />. Since this must always be true (regardless of the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />), we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/60177344a89790b3c242f653038a0dbfbd2c227b.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y-1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/e/2ce7188c53b847814fc9c25cd17babe3b65c6a6c.png" class="latex" alt="$y-2 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/e/4de6b655086178160a21cdc9d441aeb756ad8c71.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y = -1, 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="15" /> and the common point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/207948d2a69617a7c089d5af027feda4127e0225.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } (-1,2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="102" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We use process of elimination. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/7/5178df2ddd498a2b2eaa255413e1fda543664f70.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{B}$" width="12" height="11" /> doesn't necessarily work because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/4/e3494065b237daa79bb6ecedafbf0bb28d5812bd.png" class="latex" alt="$b = c$" width="40" height="12" /> isn't always true.  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/b/e6b7bf615076d5679afdfca42811b3ab9e5c56ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{C, D, E}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="14" /> also doesn't necessarily work because the x-value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, but the y-value is an integer. So by process of elimination, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/6/3868c3ba18863a76fa66027e991a7e4e411232a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } (-1, 2)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="102" height="29" /> is our answer.    ~Baolan
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>We know that in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/f/c9fb4aacd55efaa8c9ee5749b4b75aa7067ee43e.png" class="latex" alt="$ax + by = c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are in an arithmetic progression. We can simplify any arithmetic progression to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>For example, the progression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> by going back by one value. We can then divide all 3 numbers by 2 which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Now, we substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> respectively. This gives us
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/d/b8db1c24a6c4d0ade195b8b098cfe96a64267735.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a73e2712c33a157207f1d7eed363c74f4bed2534.png" class="latex" alt="$-x = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="12" /> which can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/4/8d4c38a419ae56f13a202216dd45a1978bc91a54.png" class="latex" alt="$x = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" />. The only point of intersection is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/3/923c8e80d8fee58e27cf524644fdc8b05e464db0.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. So, our answer is
