</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1929e41961587ee09272908be642ced7896db86.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="367" height="17" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We will consider this number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/0/ba0cc99138be1d5e4b6e7d9566a9f23156d4fc5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod\ 5$" width="51" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/9/4b91d0b8c57d3c840c20978ca2486f547a8e750c.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod\ 9$" width="51" height="12" />. By looking at the last digit, it is obvious that the number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/9/609bb076263762ad7eaa51b95931b5eee9035176.png" class="latex" alt="$\equiv 4\bmod\ 5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="87" height="13" />. To calculate the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/9/4b91d0b8c57d3c840c20978ca2486f547a8e750c.png" class="latex" alt="$\bmod\ 9$" width="51" height="12" />, note that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/d/85d2840185e459a74b0b33b2f38da35ccf8231f6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[123456\cdots 4344 \equiv 1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8+9+(1+0)+(1+1)+\cdots+(4+3)+(4+4) \equiv 1+2+\cdots+44 \bmod\ 9,\]" width="799" height="18" />
</p><p>so it is equivalent to
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93e7ccf6d2d377c1101b951cac39ba47a59cbf7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{44\cdot 45}{2} = 22\cdot 45 \equiv 0\bmod\ 9.\]" width="221" height="37" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the remainder when this number is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/0/52065ed07e55dc58cbebf74fd5249c2bf9c4e707.png" class="latex" alt="$x\equiv 0 \pmod {9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f8e7d455a79615febbf1a96b248eb8fa600e443.png" class="latex" alt="$x\equiv 4 \pmod {5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="18" />, so by the Chinese remainder theorem, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/4/b143cc3af39929c82aeddd3898a8052f4e8cc9af.png" class="latex" alt="$9(-1)\equiv 1 \pmod{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/6/0d6096504ab3381e6c54b69e9b6a566916301170.png" class="latex" alt="$x\equiv 5(0)+9(-1)(4) \pmod {5\cdot 9}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="251" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/a/cda5e4f754102864d1b664d1f568864096e94913.png" class="latex" alt="$x\equiv -36 \equiv 9 \pmod {45}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="180" height="18" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/8/ba8f68d5d328d6ced2499e0bfeb62b320ec85c67.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf {(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We know that this number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> because the sum of the digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/6/496208bfed993b16f902b0edc179b34b89804160.png" class="latex" alt="$270$" width="26" height="12" />, which is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. If we subtracted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> from the integer we would get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa1041f154ba92a3b93341bb812d981f9faaed86.png" class="latex" alt="$1234 \cdots 4335$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="99" height="13" />, which is also divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Thus the remainder is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/9/0b9b68aaa5462a2128fb5d06e10449ebc58b641f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="23" />.
</p>
