<p>A coin is biased in such a way that on each toss the probability of heads is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and the probability of tails is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. The outcomes of the tosses are independent. A player has the choice of playing Game A or Game B. In Game A she tosses the coin three times and wins if all three outcomes are the same. In Game B she tosses the coin four times and wins if both the outcomes of the first and second tosses are the same and the outcomes of the third and fourth tosses are the same. How do the chances of winning Game A compare to the chances of winning Game B?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> The probability of winning Game A is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a659788d7a349117b319e617b859e6798553b5f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> less than the probability of winning Game B. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b7193f66028e13f331d53ff5e6942c4b05587465.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> The probability of winning Game A is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d58cb58ae13e97ab59c5f7197763361eea81e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> less than the probability of winning Game B. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> The probabilities are the same.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> The probability of winning Game A is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d58cb58ae13e97ab59c5f7197763361eea81e2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> greater than the probability of winning Game B.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e41e3cd48cee2c6866cfbb4386a8e99d3482f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> The probability of winning Game A is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a659788d7a349117b319e617b859e6798553b5f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> greater than the probability of winning Game B.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The probability of winning Game A is the sum of the probabilities of getting three tails and getting three heads which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c124da197e59b6a903bfaff9bcdd7f2db42080b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 + \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 = \frac{8}{27} + \frac{1}{27} = \frac{9}{27} = \frac{27}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="297" height="46" />. The probability of winning Game B is the sum of the probabilities of getting two heads and getting two tails squared. This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac38fd490d159b5f4c37274ea963c2c41fa38285.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 \right)^2 = \left(\frac{5}{9}\right)^2 = \frac{25}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="267" height="56" />. The probability of winning Game A is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a74e94af8d3c5eb3ed58b159de5021a458c07ca0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{27}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> and the probability of winning Game B is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/5/e25ef0c17701f3e69d283e4f00426392494c62cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{81}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693c4044f8526a8179956e8ac7a3f2f9d61e2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />
</p><p>Solution by: vedadehhc
</p>
