<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>

Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3b33ddb8bf6aca742593c6b02be26f0489fa42b.png" class="latex" alt="$BF=\frac{x}{x^2+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="104" height="34" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/6/1b62fa0957fa5dc29b155b46e5f143db8782920a.png" class="latex" alt="$CF=\frac{x^3}{x^2+1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="104" height="40" />. Let the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/b/6dbe073e978e0e1fd7fab0405f8fb4602797382e.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BFC \sim \triangle ABC$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" />. However, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> is a trapezoid, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/9/009cf3eeb0ff3789cc057632947cadb200ab4663.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle$" width="15" height="13" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89a3f0e3dd12e6a4cc523abc05db1a2bce99298d.png" class="latex" alt="$BCF$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/7/3e728a789f2ca839593526b82229b72fde61ec6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADF$" width="59" height="13" /> share the same area, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/9/009cf3eeb0ff3789cc057632947cadb200ab4663.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle$" width="15" height="13" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/91275aa1f85be8f39ea3ae3cef68f10100fc664b.png" class="latex" alt="$BCE$" width="43" height="12" /> is the reflection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/9/009cf3eeb0ff3789cc057632947cadb200ab4663.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle$" width="15" height="13" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89a3f0e3dd12e6a4cc523abc05db1a2bce99298d.png" class="latex" alt="$BCF$" width="43" height="12" /> over the perpendicular bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/d/c1d2e19a4a14e740d1d2b7b53b268e4d579d8619.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />. We use the linear equations of the diagonals, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/8/1e8f7dea008135f46d651326bf7e285e821bff5f.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -ax + 1, y = \frac1a x$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="167" height="38" />, to find the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/2/60210eaf10801fb8aa31aaf4e6f5d6e45aaa73c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-ax+1 = \frac1ax \Longrightarrow x = \frac{1}{a+\frac1a} = \frac{a}{a^2+1}\]" width="307" height="43" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/9/5c9a0701a3c96b8f8c545c702f2ed3b0c8f3638f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y = \frac1ax = \frac{1}{a^2+1}\]" width="132" height="38" />
The y-coordinate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/a/5ea70c6538023d944954a5a760fdd35941305309.png" class="latex" alt="$1-\frac{1}{a^2+1} = \frac{a^2}{a^2+1}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="162" height="42" />
The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/3/423d2cdb9f74980709bf26fc4bb3cb0304964a8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BCE$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/4/5a4c041ea02a31ca8b0140cb00848c1f0f69728a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12 \frac{a}{a^2+1}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="67" height="40" />. We apply shoelace theorem to solve for the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" />. The coordinates of the triangle are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa08a42957f48e3edbe9e4b5d5a25460c0c8453b.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(\frac{a}{a^2+1}, \frac{a^2}{a^2+1}), (a,1), (\frac1a, 0)\}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="249" height="42" />, so the area is 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/e/88e89075264d5ca204c82d65491688af381242e9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac12 |\frac{a^3}{a^2+1} + \frac1a - \frac{a}{a^2+1} - \frac{a}{a^2+1}| = \frac12 |\frac{a^3-2a}{a^2+1} + \frac1a|\]" width="401" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc173e9625323baf6814c7d9b183caebe67a22e5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= \frac12 |\frac{a^4-2a^2}{a(a^2+1)} + \frac{a^2+1}{a(a^2+1)}| = \frac12 \frac{a^4-a^2+1}{a(a^2+1)}\]" width="343" height="44" />
Finally, we use the property that the ratio of areas equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/6/b7616a230c77f0d9a577e5bca01aa06f1c35c457.png" class="latex" alt="$17$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/8/928bb25be3d6759304ecf6ffc5b47bad457d9d31.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{\frac12}{\frac12} \frac{\frac{a^4-a^2+1}{a(a^2+1)}}{\frac{a}{a^2+1}} = 17 \Rightarrow \frac{a^4-a^2+1}{a^2} = 17 \Rightarrow a^4-18a^2+1 = 0\]" width="446" height="54" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/7/d27babb825602f9fe06301e5f2f071ea245d03df.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2 = 9+4\sqrt{5} = (2+\sqrt{5})^2 \Longrightarrow a = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 2+\sqrt{5}}\]" width="383" height="33" />
</p><p>~Zeric
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span></h2>
<p>1) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/6/2a6847786e6937f02f810e74a056f4c8f289a931.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{17}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> is the most relevant answer choice because it shares numbers with the givens of the problem.
</p><p>2) It's a very good guess to replace finding the area of triangle AED with the area of the triangle DAF, where F is the projection of D onto AB(then find the closest answer choice).
