<p>An ice-cream novelty item consists of a cup in the shape of a 4-inch-tall frustum of a right circular cone, with a 2-inch-diameter base at the bottom and a 4-inch-diameter base at the top, packed solid with ice cream, together with a solid cone of ice cream of height 4 inches, whose base, at the bottom, is the top base of the frustum. What is the total volume of the ice cream, in cubic inches?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/c/f8ca8947495f088b86c571a93b4849b5682eee15.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8\pi \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{28\pi}{3} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 12\pi \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 14\pi \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{44\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="447" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The top cone has radius 2 and height 4 so it has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876f98a1b600b61f85b320905d9bb4b90302d4b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{3} \pi (2)^2 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="37" />. 
</p><p>The frustum is made up by taking away a small cone of radius 1, height 4 from a large cone of radius 2, height 8, so it has volume
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/c/cac15e455b5a9152064c54d79fc8b06b6104d55c.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{3} \pi (2)^2 \times 8 - \dfrac{1}{3} \pi (1)^2 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="194" height="37" />.
</p><p>Adding, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f87798828f54d88e6350bb36290065d781159046.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{3} \pi (16+32-4) = \boxed{ \textbf{E} \ \dfrac{44\pi}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="215" height="48" />. 
</p><p>Solution by: SilverLion 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Find the volume of the cone with the method in Solution 1. The volume of the frustrum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/9/ff9b539d98b176e965990782eaa41f6f94a10713.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi\int_{0}^{4} \left(-\frac{1}{4}x+2\right)^2 dx=\frac{28\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="223" height="46" />
</p><p>Adding, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/6/c063cb05828714fb83964a58b2e899accc33a4f3.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{44\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="31" height="37" />.
</p><p>Solution by IdentityChaos2020
</p>
