<p>The functions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081bf0417da2140657aad909a14ca6c3ff8b77d5.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c938e3f9dea789595c6290070f7975f45bc4300a.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> are periodic with least period <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />. What is the least period of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/6/6461b51571bec4ca17a6ab0ca0105cf872c2dfd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(\sin(x))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/f/0df0543a40af59ee9f611d3b47cda294e53f379a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{\pi}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \pi\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2\pi \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\pi \qquad\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="363" height="33" /> The function is not periodic.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Start by noting that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/2/0c2fa440df4c6dc36c257fb9ae25e0b3063d34ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(-x)=\cos(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="18" />. Then realize that under this function, the negative sine values yield the same as their positive value, so take the absolute value of the sine function to get the new period. This has period <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a558b37c441e1b7ac7c5ff6a848f199ac53ba75.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="29" />.
</p>
