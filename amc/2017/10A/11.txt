<p>The region consisting of all points in three-dimensional space within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> units of line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f099af0894a766e83c9a8af00567a186ddfa3886.png" class="latex" alt="$216\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />. What is the length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/9/e498a0d7159579955adab10c9bb4d3ac3178ccc0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textit{AB}$" width="25" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/3/6e3de5a494bb888a56d3378211e1a3d3df958aa3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 20\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>In order to solve this problem, we must first visualize what the region looks like. We know that, in a three dimensional space, the region consisting of all points within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> units of a point would be a sphere with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.  However, we need to find the region containing all points within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> units of a segment.  It can be seen that our region is a cylinder with two hemispheres/endcaps on either end.  We know the volume of our region, so we set up the following equation (the volume of our cylinder + the volume of our two hemispheres will equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/6/5f6e9b84e67186f910a8832ac4e267ce1357198e.png" class="latex" alt="$216 \pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />):
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b1061db52cd865c8e7abc7c1bc24215edc8a59e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4 \pi }{3} \cdot 3^3+9 \pi x=216 \pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="168" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is equal to the length of our line segment.
</p><p>Solving, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f786bc655d428a33a947dc31c2199a68bdecef50.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="99" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Because this is just a cylinder and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hemispheres ("half spheres"), and the radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, the volume of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hemispheres is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3b125cd191b8590da58de3e7f4e260ee4bf35d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4(3^3)\pi}{3} = 36 \pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="39" />. Since we also know that the volume of this whole thing is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/6/5f6e9b84e67186f910a8832ac4e267ce1357198e.png" class="latex" alt="$216 \pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />, we do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/3/213e43c7799f240afc9358f2e1ae8a646bd7707c.png" class="latex" alt="$216-36$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/9/4d9683610d5ffafa4c0edeb89972aef25f02cb1a.png" class="latex" alt="$180 \pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" /> as the volume of the cylinder. Thus the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/9/4d9683610d5ffafa4c0edeb89972aef25f02cb1a.png" class="latex" alt="$180 \pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" /> divided by the area of the base, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/1/e917ce8beb9c5f920fa0607c1516212cc73651b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{180 \pi}{9\pi}=20$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="37" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e32b4b921717482e7a5d49e7f17c3887c2f8b3b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="68" height="28" />
</p><p>~Minor edit by virjoy2001
</p>
