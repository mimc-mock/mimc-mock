<p>Sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> of equilateral triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> are tangent to a circle at points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> respectively. What fraction of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> lies outside the circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/0/d5035002690b704d11cf97b8ae0328335236fb33.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{4\sqrt{3}\pi}{27}-\frac{1}{3}\qquad \textbf{(B) } \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}-\frac{\pi}{8}\qquad \textbf{(C) } \frac{1}{2} \qquad \textbf{(D) } \sqrt{3}-\frac{2\sqrt{3}\pi}{9}\qquad \textbf{(E) } \frac{4}{3}-\frac{4\sqrt{3}\pi}{27}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="646" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/e/0be4ef4bdb9bff2cbb4272529b2272423c5bc1ea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] real sqrt3 = 1.73205080757; draw(Circle((4, 4), 4)); draw((4-2*sqrt3,6)--(4,4)--(4+2*sqrt3,6)--(4-2*sqrt3,6)--(4,12)--(4+2*sqrt3,6)); label(&quot;A&quot;, (4, 12.4)); label(&quot;B&quot;, (-.3, 6.3)); label(&quot;C&quot;, (8.3, 6.3)); label(&quot;O&quot;, (4, 3.4)); [/asy]" width="188" height="252" />
Let the radius of the circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, and let its center be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> are tangent to circle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/f/9cfe4f90ab8a84d6f935d0d48bb97b9de5a3addc.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle OBA = \angle OCA = 90^{\circ}$" width="183" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/6/336358a364ce49b354744d52dd99f5b0b69a48b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC = 120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />. Therefore, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/b/3fb5b6369d95e2d66ab4cd9acd69168b7a605c2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OB}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/1/0019a9c6ae9bef5eda9166daa34ea37465190dd1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OC}$" width="29" height="15" /> are equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, then (pick your favorite method) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/4/404b352cc79d7c4021b1d2ec658028f57f2dd37f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC} = r\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />. The area of the equilateral triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/b/28bd3a55b898fbd1bfc0bead3c2d5a5a2e2d450d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(r\sqrt{3})^2 \sqrt{3}}4 = \frac{3r^2 \sqrt{3}}4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="159" height="41" />, and the area of the sector we are subtracting from it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/c/f0cc1a9e360615acb520d5646393c1bdf8555246.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 13 \pi r^2 - \frac 12 \cdot r\sqrt{3} \cdot \frac{r}2 = \frac{\pi r^2}3 -\frac{r^2 \sqrt{3}}4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="272" height="43" />. The area outside of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f834393bf2eada4176abb6b5d188b45581dab5bc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3r^2 \sqrt{3}}4-\left(\frac{\pi r^2}3 -\frac{r^2 \sqrt{3}}4\right) = r^2 \sqrt{3} - \frac{\pi r^2}3$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="318" height="53" />. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/8/5b8363dc4e99d80da70629508f5bddc1ed8c7995.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{r^2 \sqrt{3} - \frac{\pi r^2}3}{\frac{3r^2 \sqrt{3}}4} = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } \frac 43 - \frac{4\sqrt 3 \pi}{27}}\]" width="243" height="57" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h3>
<p>The sector angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/1/2918f7d23f9ee8c04baabad86100d68023ec03f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B$" width="27" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4056d41f42aa8a00adca0ff5d1209b6bb0e92d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C$" width="27" height="12" /> are both 90 degrees meaning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/d/1dde9f02e08d6c37316c548e0b973d180785084e.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B + \angle C = 180^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa99a3a7bbbb020ffd44715e8c401955536590d4.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCO$" width="56" height="13" /> is cyclic. Thus, the angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/6/dd6a703784f3c791f8b600cc21d00011f0052314.png" class="latex" alt="$180-60=120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="129" height="16" />
</p><p>~mathboy282
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da4672fb20ee6555d7a1d12c315d17ecaf76ce72.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] real sqrt3 = 1.73205080757; draw(Circle((4, 4), 4)); draw((4-2*sqrt3,6)--(4,4)--(4+2*sqrt3,6)--(4-2*sqrt3,6)--(4,12)--(4+2*sqrt3,6)); draw((4, 6)--(4, 4)); label(&quot;A&quot;, (4, 12.4)); label(&quot;B&quot;, (-.3, 6.3)); label(&quot;C&quot;, (8.3, 6.3)); label(&quot;O&quot;, (4, 3.4)); label(&quot;D&quot;, (4, 6.6)); [/asy]" width="188" height="252" />
(same diagram as Solution 1)
</p><p>Without the Loss of Generality, let the side length of the triangle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Then, the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/b/b9b30d7166f38d8f18e66dfd6e9473880efaca2e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="26" height="41" />. We are looking for the area of the portion inside the triangle but outside the circle divided by the area of the triangle. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6ac02955449b522bbf5d8ee76600ec0e98933d8f.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle ABO = 90^{\circ}=m\angle ACO = 90^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="269" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/a/15ad16bab66ddb8073defa8faf7bd1567545f4e4.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle ABC = m\angle ACB = 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="219" height="15" />, we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/5151a9bc40115f26fc6872bdcc80f31bdb3b17e3.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle OBC=m\angle OCB=30^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="220" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b211fddd2d8732d36f6eaae69e48205861c64118.png" class="latex" alt="$m\angle BOC = 120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="129" height="13" />. Drop an angle bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />, call the point of intersection <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />. By SAS congruence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/070d27727410442aec609fbb9a62c93b21ac80e7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BDO \cong \triangle CDO$" width="144" height="13" />, by CPCTC (Congruent Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/4/46485e002514e96497ce7c0d14cfe5f5693b7fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$BD \cong DC$" width="84" height="12" /> and they both measure <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. By 30-60-90 triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef79ee583dcf850f82fb6d38628fb477d61509ed.png" class="latex" alt="$OC = BO = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="136" height="43" />. The area of the sector bounded by arc BC is one-third the area of circle O, whose area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc67fb9badfeec8991f538bbfe0bcd87722aa925.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)^{2}\pi=\frac{1}{3}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="128" height="58" />. Therefore, the area of the sector bounded by arc BC is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/4092395bc0dc17c7139edbdd54b6f5efb2938c87.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}\cdot\frac{1}{3}\pi=\frac{\pi}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="91" height="38" />. 
</p><p>We are nearly there. By 30-60-90 triangle, we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/9/af9d13ba838b2260aee00b7ef454aa85866f9565.png" class="latex" alt="$DO = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="84" height="43" />, so the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/41752e67cd033c961edd7532c9376bc82296d255.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BOC$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/5/69581a4ef0a4d1ef3fa9aaf28b78b10aae54c23d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}}{2}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="102" height="46" />. The area of the region inside both the triangle and circle is the area of the sector bounded by arc BC minus the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/41752e67cd033c961edd7532c9376bc82296d255.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle BOC$" width="59" height="13" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/8/4080286f3dd5c246c8a10632944868b815295039.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{9}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="64" height="41" />. The area of the region outside of the circle but inside the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/c/bcc13d436e91b25b6f2732bb4868709730dc57e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}-\left(\frac{\pi}{9}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{12}\right)=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}-\frac{\pi}{9}+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{12}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}-\frac{\pi}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="376" height="55" /> and the ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/d/56d77054207c13167d13cbc37b10f2508de84cc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}-\frac{\pi}{9}}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}}=\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}-\frac{\pi}{9}\right)\cdot\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{4\sqrt{3}}{3\sqrt{3}}-\frac{4\pi}{9\sqrt{3}}=\frac{4}{3}-\frac{4\sqrt{3}\pi}{27} \Longrightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(E) } \frac 43 - \frac{4\sqrt 3 \pi}{27}}$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="618" height="58" />.
</p><p>~JH. L
</p>
