<p>How many triangles with positive area have all their vertices at points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/7/887919dfbc86eebc29e0373f98f97dbf23a0ae23.png" class="latex" alt="$(i,j)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> in the coordinate plane, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" /> are integers between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, inclusive?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/e/09efaed5bdb608ed7b8713a65ff72ff4b18740ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2128 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2148 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2160 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2200 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 2300$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can solve this by finding all the combinations, then subtracting the ones that are on the same line. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> points in all, from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/b/e3b140eff6338916ca72f19c0b695e806cb666de.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/0/050f473ac3ad407e01a8279d74ce3f500aac1776.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{25}3$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="42" height="53" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/8/0c86ba640a2261399d566ad0240093208e98c328.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25\cdot 24\cdot 23}{3\cdot 2 \cdot 1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="83" height="38" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/9494d181f910775b4d0e7cb5e4797a375f7d9416.png" class="latex" alt="$2300$" width="35" height="12" />.
Now we count the ones that are on the same line. We see that any three points chosen from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/f/77f46e8ad13e4a3e569bb6c47d1d642243a3119d.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> would be on the same line, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e32383027a66cc62d94dae0636cf9c7057ff8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom53$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> rows, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> columns, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> long diagonals, so that results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
We can also count the ones with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> on a diagonal. That is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd4ca78219c80d2b362e15c23c16cdd44e2f360.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom43$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" />, which is 4, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of those diagonals, so that results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
We can count the ones with only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> on a diagonal, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> diagonals like that, so that results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />.
We can also count the ones with a slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/c/bdc960c165b21ba43f7a90caa2893d40409d6944.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf9affa4df6a5590079c4a35470c692005d2d397.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> points in each. Note that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> such lines, for each slope, present in the grid. In total, this results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />.
Finally, we subtract all the ones in a line from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/9494d181f910775b4d0e7cb5e4797a375f7d9416.png" class="latex" alt="$2300$" width="35" height="12" />, so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/7/2f791e8b541fa858579239e2560bdae998b91ff5.png" class="latex" alt="$2300-120-16-4-12=\boxed{(\textbf{B})\ 2148}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="305" height="32" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b99c53667f3625e490831cbe690d6d400d6936.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \times 5 = 25$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="15" /> total points in all. So, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/1/c21e999adceb09dbdc303c77d92f3bc3580a1c44.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{25}3 = 2300$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="109" height="55" /> ways to choose the three vertices for the triangle. However, there are some cases where they 3 points chosen results in a straight line. 
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/5/db55ca5656db768873d7bde67f4c80be41c55ff5.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \times 10 = 100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> cases where the 3 points chosen make up a vertical or horizontal line.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/a/35aaa4fcbca94ea306cb3115aa67f20f4049556e.png" class="latex" alt="$2\left(1+\dbinom{4}3+\dbinom{5}3+\dbinom{4}3+1\right)=40$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="303" height="55" /> cases where the 3 points all land on the diagonals of the square.
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/8/af85e67238f3076d072a408df3202a98b7c8462d.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \times 4=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> ways where the 3 points make the a slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a7748d3d38355a68b51bddc29d7647230728287e.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" />.
</p><p>Hence, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/8/90832618623ce6740b0f49dd1163736609443f15.png" class="latex" alt="$100+40+12=152$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="161" height="15" /> cases where the chosen 3 points make a line. The answer would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/6/fd63c102693be826ee41464a5059d93eb0c049f2.png" class="latex" alt="$2300-152=\boxed{(\textbf{B})\ 2148}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="193" height="32" />
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
