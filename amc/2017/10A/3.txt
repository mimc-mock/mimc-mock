<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Finding the area of the shaded walkway can be achieved by computing the total area of Tamara's garden and then subtracting the combined area of her six flower beds.
</p><p>Since the width of Tamara's garden contains three margins, the total width is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/4/c647b236e40d8db29b8f1829704376c37df1414f.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 6+3\cdot 1 = 15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="126" height="14" /> feet.
</p><p>Similarly, the height of Tamara's garden is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/e/ffe99fcd1197b910eb55feb56f65e1ca1f11818e.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 2+4\cdot 1 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="126" height="14" /> feet.
</p><p>Therefore, the total area of the garden is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/1/2213c31419bb53b9ce775b9e33a08b13b0614384.png" class="latex" alt="$15\cdot 10 =150$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> square feet.
</p><p>Finally, since the six flower beds each have an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/4515127cbaee7d56f6f47e01b3fb8157ff762aff.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 6 = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> square feet, the area we seek is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/e/6ae69451d974c3ec69133eb0085067e13dfa5af4.png" class="latex" alt="$150 - 6\cdot 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="13" />, and our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/1/34181285142665cda173d169783f8b72baccb0a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 78}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
