<p>At a gathering of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> people, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> people who all know each other and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> people who know no one. People who know each other hug, and people who do not know each other shake hands. How many handshakes occur within the group?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/e/36e424a1eb98c87745165200b02aadfa250f930e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 240\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 245\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 290\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 480\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 490$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Each one of the ten people has to shake hands with all the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> other people they don’t know. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/e/0cea9ba70919637b54c77f3d1eff6186a6b9dff6.png" class="latex" alt="$10\cdot20 = 200$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />. From there, we calculate how many handshakes occurred between the people who don’t know each other. This is simply counting how many ways to choose two people to shake hands from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/a/b4a36d045406715079df9fc3e9053bd327aa03cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{10}{2} = 45$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="88" height="53" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/7/397b6d46baef2d21bfad692feea07c465f34f83e.png" class="latex" alt="$200 + 45 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 245}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="165" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can also use complementary counting. First of all, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/8/cc869ce9208ceeb1029e92d59ce2125dd6b6746b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{30}{2}=435$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="97" height="53" /> handshakes or hugs occur. Then, if we can find the number of hugs, then we can subtract it from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/4/7248d1be121a9c19af3ea4affd50a0de0f812615.png" class="latex" alt="$435$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to find the handshakes. Hugs only happen between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> people who know each other, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/4/974aace4eaf1d600b403a0d9653d33201f453c99.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{20}{2}=190$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="97" height="53" /> hugs. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/0/9a043b25eccf0ccd30458cc9057cc3b57083c034.png" class="latex" alt="$435-190= \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 245}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="174" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can focus on how many handshakes the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> people who don't know anybody get. 
</p><p>The first person gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/5/0657f5cd42256c0a39d45b265235e2a939625515.png" class="latex" alt="$29$" width="17" height="12" /> handshakes with other people not him/herself, the second person gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> handshakes with other people not him/herself and not the first person, ..., and the tenth receives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> handshakes with other people not him/herself and not the first, second, ..., ninth person. We can write this as the sum of an arithmetic sequence:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/3/c2338b9bc0c35ec48ce0a7efa8b47d60054b47f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10(20+29)}{2}\implies 5(49)\implies 245.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="266" height="38" />
Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/7/347c4bdb36fa9242f056a400bdf1dc7140bd9ea4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 245}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>First, we can find out the number of handshakes that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> people who don't know anybody share with the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> other people. This is simply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/d/28d598b2d75ac7ef215a1382c5b0ec73abd72ae9.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \cdot 20 = 200$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />. Next, we need to find out the number of handshakes that are shared within the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> people who don't know anybody. Here, we can use the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/4/53404ad4af12519f95b03c74a1c7af0d609baa8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n(n-1)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of people being counted. The reason we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/d/c9d3407c16ac230a65d2dbb640f0d46dfe4e1807.png" class="latex" alt="$n(n-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> counts the case where the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72fa122f2311c7317db8be229ab220bc039f6b96.png" class="latex" alt="$1^{st}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="20" height="15" /> person shakes hands with the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/e/6bef53b7de0e501a06b7678f8acb69bc70d7937b.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{nd}$" width="24" height="15" /> person <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/c/3cc65598b6db7bea293318d452d448c8f905736c.png" class="latex" alt="$and$" width="30" height="12" /> the case where the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/e/6bef53b7de0e501a06b7678f8acb69bc70d7937b.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{nd}$" width="24" height="15" /> shakes hands with the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72fa122f2311c7317db8be229ab220bc039f6b96.png" class="latex" alt="$1^{st}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="20" height="15" /> (and these 2 cases are the same). Thus, plugging <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc1b21210db29332b1a7d9ce088fd457f23aeee.png" class="latex" alt="$n=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/d/60d6da227f0f73579a1c24b12f6c3be75465af59.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{10 \cdot 9}{2} \implies 45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="111" height="37" />. Adding up the 2 cases gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/1/251d2fbd8e74bfb96269207f84334ac41defd37d.png" class="latex" alt="$200+45=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 245}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="165" height="28" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
