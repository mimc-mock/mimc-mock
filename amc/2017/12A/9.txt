<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> in the coordinate plane such that two of the three quantities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> are equal and the third of the three quantities is no greater than the common value. Which of the following is a correct description of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/f/f6f55e588fdb046e3143690387d2da0946e89ccc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{a single point} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{two intersecting lines} \\ \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{three lines whose pairwise intersections are three distinct points} \\ \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{a triangle}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{three rays with a common point}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="498" height="57" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If the two equal values are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f054225c72654c42d26a5ceb6539de98d2c1df62.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4\leqslant 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is the common value. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/3/cc3b49209665a56a586a9e7db9ff9da84f79a3ae.png" class="latex" alt="$y\leqslant 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />. Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e02a35db152495396584bfeae1d0599cd753.png" class="latex" alt="$x=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/3/cc3b49209665a56a586a9e7db9ff9da84f79a3ae.png" class="latex" alt="$y\leqslant 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> is part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. This is a ray with an endpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Similar to the process above, we assume that the two equal values are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" />. Solving the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/6/8f61279c0249ddd75948828ef746744b27f6551b.png" class="latex" alt="$3=y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c37261466c0ff756401c32f293c33e111f4712.png" class="latex" alt="$y=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/4/5245e3f91b5242d288401bbb8ab3e07c0de2c493.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2\leqslant 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is the common value. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/1/521100c9d9eefa86813f30591f4df489f4d8cfad.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leqslant 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />. Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c37261466c0ff756401c32f293c33e111f4712.png" class="latex" alt="$y=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="15" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/1/521100c9d9eefa86813f30591f4df489f4d8cfad.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leqslant 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" /> is also part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. This is another ray with the same endpoint as the above ray: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. (Note that the only answer choice which has rays in it is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />.)
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8ab0ec5027c055e1b34dc345b1d8532b611bce.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> are the two equal values, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/7479ee2548c328ed198d967b9c5f9ecf7044331d.png" class="latex" alt="$x+2=y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="15" />. Solving the equation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a9348cab3888ef34d6c3597fda8a0a96a670e4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x+6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />. Also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380f888c8b4b00dc9b5f90a9791fc3f7381cd9dc.png" class="latex" alt="$3\leqslant y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d9adb49a9b5e64f4532221a8022cb2c9f4bda0.png" class="latex" alt="$y-4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="15" /> is one way to express the common value (using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/4/be4e0c9fe53284fc1fc418baf62e3a7253237ce7.png" class="latex" alt="$x-2$" width="41" height="12" /> as the common value works as well). Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba751ec0ded6dd819aa45153dff0dcf888724e2.png" class="latex" alt="$y\geqslant 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" />. Therefore the portion of the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a9348cab3888ef34d6c3597fda8a0a96a670e4b7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x+6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba751ec0ded6dd819aa45153dff0dcf888724e2.png" class="latex" alt="$y\geqslant 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> is part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> like the other two rays. The lowest possible value that can be achieved is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is made up of three rays with common endpoint <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74feea56c7173ed444f78792b01daa892d219659.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/2/55206bce678ac0394d97dc04dd72398df2bdf572.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{E}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p><p>Solution by TheMathematicsTiger7
