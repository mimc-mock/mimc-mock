<p>Jerry and Silvia wanted to go from the southwest corner of a square field to the northeast corner. Jerry walked due east and then due north to reach the goal, but Silvia headed northeast and reached the goal walking in a straight line. Which of the following is closest to how much shorter Silvia's trip was, compared to Jerry's trip?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/6/fa68a4b7e2d86f099aaca2178fe6d67da01bf07d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30\%\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 40\%\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 50\%\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 60\%\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 70\%$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="469" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" /> represent how far Jerry walked, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> represent how far Sylvia walked. Since the field is a square, and Jerry walked two sides of it, while Silvia walked the diagonal, we can simply define the side of the square field to be one, and find the distances they walked. Since Jerry walked two sides, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/e/d3e7f7f7537494ac1190cfe8ce31fe5964add90e.png" class="latex" alt="$j = 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />
Since Silvia walked the diagonal, she walked the hypotenuse of a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> triangle with leg length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Thus,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/8/8c813b371cd66fd4d704817271be9e9e214412b1.png" class="latex" alt="$s = \sqrt{2} = 1.414...$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="18" />
We can then take 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/c/d6c6c120a5d13fe3a75d8b9a80889e7287d5cf18.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{j-s}{j} \approx \frac{2 - 1.4}{2} = 0.3 = 30\%$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="230" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/7/7273e5e4b456f8111bd2fe05d1975bf2780d963c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{ \textbf{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="23" />.
</p>
