<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose Laurent's number is in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76a259806417a270b1859d1dd2db61fb0090d670.png" class="latex" alt="$[ 0, 2017 ]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="61" height="18" />. Then, by symmetry, the probability of Laurent's number being greater is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4a7df1100efe353cc612d5d1c289c22d5bf8c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Next, suppose Laurent's number is in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22eb67bd191cf44619936c4b6abe568adb550602.png" class="latex" alt="$[ 2017, 4034 ]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="88" height="18" />. Then Laurent's number will be greater with a probability of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Since each case is equally likely, the probability of Laurent's number being greater is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f12d3976ce9e22c85b2f630eab2f9e7de03af79.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1 + \frac{1}{2}}{2} = \dfrac{3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="81" height="41" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f58d2efaaa17d28cec9dd927d78708a63b43e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
