<p>A square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is inscribed in a right triangle with sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> so that one vertex of the square coincides with the right-angle vertex of the triangle. A square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is inscribed in another right triangle with sides of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> so that one side of the square lies on the hypotenuse of the triangle. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15ffa7dd3945e571925afd048250eca35fb671aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{12}{13} \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{35}{37} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 1 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{37}{35} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{13}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="400" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Analyze the first right triangle.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2d8d4056c8f797b4635c3aa467fd8f36bb9281c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C; pair D, e, F; A = (0,0); B = (4,0); C = (0,3);  D = (0, 12/7); e = (12/7 , 12/7); F = (12/7, 0);  draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(D--e--F);  label(&quot;$x$&quot;, D/2, W); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, N); label(&quot;$D$&quot;, D, W); label(&quot;$E$&quot;, e, NE); label(&quot;$F$&quot;, F, S); [/asy]" width="252" height="198" />
</p><p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/a/ffa8d493eebaf7cccb91cb38faf5e8603c2b36cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle FBE$" width="59" height="13" /> are similar, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/a/7aabbdd33c8976adf688d8c5efc9b4f7476dba6d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{BF}{FE} = \frac{AB}{AC}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="38" />. This can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/a/7baea4362d71aff2b243e467018804f46cc2d71f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4-x}{x}=\frac{4}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="81" height="37" />. Solving, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e58a64f6c40f0b01342db16e6e944c641ac2fa13.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{12}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="55" height="37" />.
</p><p>Now we analyze the second triangle.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc0a7730ccae9580fa62d17f8130c558ceec4bc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C; pair q, R, S, T; A = (0,0); B = (4,0); C = (0,3);  q = (1.297, 0); R = (2.27 , 1.297); S = (0.973, 2.27); T  = (0, 0.973);  draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(q--R--S--T--cycle);  label(&quot;$y$&quot;, (q+R)/2, NW); label(&quot;$A&#39;$&quot;, A, SW); label(&quot;$B&#39;$&quot;, B, SE); label(&quot;$C&#39;$&quot;, C, N); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;, (q-(0,0.3))); label(&quot;$R$&quot;, R, NE); label(&quot;$S$&quot;, S, NE); label(&quot;$T$&quot;, T, W); [/asy]" width="252" height="198" />
</p><p>Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/a/73a764854d1073fac0a788e874db580d732c7c89.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A&#39;B&#39;C&#39;$" width="71" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/2/5f287563f368176de746a39a18b5bd9f67a4ae2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle RB&#39;Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="17" /> are similar, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/e/eaeb75a8123b0e761ebde2ad2690ef5e7c1e7981.png" class="latex" alt="$RB&#39; = \frac{4}{3}y$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/9/319cd04f03e14b5157aac45c2f88dda996f66dd4.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;S = \frac{3}{4}y$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="77" height="38" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/c/e8c8841c95a5d0a7a1e2d8b9ab309aefb1eabda1.png" class="latex" alt="$C&#39;B&#39; = C&#39;S + SR + RB&#39; = \frac{4}{3}y + y + \frac{3}{4}y = 5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="354" height="38" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c9448b5df6dc8deff1d35c0dddd4c87416474a37.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{60}{37}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/8/858641939a3e97747e0b2217e5913272b153f3bd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y} = \frac{37}{35} \implies \boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="138" height="42" />.
</p>
