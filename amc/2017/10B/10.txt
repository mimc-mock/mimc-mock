<p>The lines with equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/c/35ca41022e485a424927db463ad5729dd76b9d6b.png" class="latex" alt="$ax-2y=c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2eca6785fbeb75e71933f0e4bd054dfc627f3439.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+by=-c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" /> are perpendicular and intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/b/47b4589cb030ed8e74597d3f9c18204bcbcae33f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, -5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d7be443cd1c1c7d4e616935b43f4ff419d638b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -13\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -8\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="411" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Writing each equation in slope-intercept form, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18f4de2b40445373c075a8febc3b5b2de99cd2dc.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{a}{2}x-\frac{1}{2}c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/4/e24f4d977ea85c37bb3537623827a1adcf452906.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-\frac{2}{b}x-\frac{c}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="37" />. We observe the slope of each equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9afddc3c61aa4e66d003d0c7cc5bd01560d341f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/222bb4505c896b5c221fb57ff9a3edadb7e5f651.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{2}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" />, respectively. Because the slope of a line perpendicular to a line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ecf2f6a8e46ee4becd582e7a321282f42b9e6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{m}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="37" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/0/210473b3040c10efdc38f91ff0cfa204ee634fce.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{2}=-\frac{1}{-\frac{2}{b}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="79" height="43" /> because it is given that the two lines are perpendicular. This equation simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abd2c942ae299fd26e18d0ca5d390a7ca4a8c34.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b$" width="42" height="12" />.
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/b/47b4589cb030ed8e74597d3f9c18204bcbcae33f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, -5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is a solution of both equations, we deduce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7ec2fd41a50f59c5787b8903de7e0426f85c827.png" class="latex" alt="$a \times 1-2 \times -5=c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="150" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/6/46604d9624cbaa0b5caccf791f2fe7f26aa058a1.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 1+b \times -5=-c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="14" />. Because we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abd2c942ae299fd26e18d0ca5d390a7ca4a8c34.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b$" width="42" height="12" />, the equations reduce to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf05456b6a0dcee0a228edef0514793d3120c96f.png" class="latex" alt="$a+10=c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a98d80b28d4922640aa10cfc4bc66f1a150cb149.png" class="latex" alt="$2-5a=-c$" width="96" height="12" />. Solving this system of equations, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/9/a09994c3d82cb6dc802f67c7167f66a35708da85.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="95" height="28" />
</p>
