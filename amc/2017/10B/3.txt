<p>Real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> satisfy the inequalities
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/128206b7583760e7ef76e802861294c334f7ca2b.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x&lt;1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/8/fb8298a37872109d5a896c8e5d0d4e653895b563.png" class="latex" alt="$-1&lt;y&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/1/2a198e0d6ced768b18f5058c07a05b2675a3b692.png" class="latex" alt="$1&lt;z&lt;2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="12" />.
Which of the following numbers is necessarily positive?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/7/8d7e9fd14c66475a555c79637b186142de439948.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ y+x^2\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ y+xz\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ y+y^2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ y+2y^2\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ y+z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="552" height="19" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59d04c99b71a480dbfc87512c2c75a45798b83f3.png" class="latex" alt="$y+z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="14" /> must be positive because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/2/db219c3f0681629000863a63f8b64746c0a102c7.png" class="latex" alt="$|z|&gt;|y|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e5969eb59d0d2c2dc0e44a2cfcf6459ff7cc10.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } y+z}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="86" height="28" />.
</p><p>The other choices:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> grows closer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> decreases and thus becomes less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b7193f66028e13f331d53ff5e6942c4b05587465.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> can be as small as possible (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/e/02e28efa00c4f2c66d1aa381c0cb1f8e0ef8d6e4.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />), so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/d/2dd70347c7940f63ff9417e383548564fd92aff7.png" class="latex" alt="$xz$" width="19" height="8" /> grows close to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> approaches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> For all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/8/fb8298a37872109d5a896c8e5d0d4e653895b563.png" class="latex" alt="$-1&lt;y&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/4/294fbeefa29a1d1e254ede9f10b211e9679a7d62.png" class="latex" alt="$|y|&gt;|y^2|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, and thus it is always negative.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> The same logic as above, but when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/d/49d53358c9b98707c0f81a1873111659fc319e88.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{1}{2}&lt;y&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="94" height="37" /> this time.
</p>
