<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/5/52503385edca4db0f227ae98c9ac42205b28420a.png" class="latex" alt="$N=123456789101112\dots4344$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="240" height="13" /> be the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd8b908d15627ea3a118bb38668d865edf722170.png" class="latex" alt="$79$" width="17" height="12" />-digit number that is formed by writing the integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b45828f292e161290be6ee62bc4c9df081ee1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> in order, one after the other. What is the remainder when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1929e41961587ee09272908be642ced7896db86.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 44$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="367" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We only need to find the remainders of N when divided by 5 and 9 to determine the answer.
By inspection, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c441a9cd2148d28626c82d200731db6ff84aee06.png" class="latex" alt="$N \equiv 4 \text{ (mod 5)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="17" />.
The remainder when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/8/d084a6545289c869d6a27851a2dc21b62cb2f9de.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+4+ \cdots +1+0+1+1 +1+2 +\cdots + 4+3+4+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="503" height="14" />, but since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/a/caa90f5312e130b7091692ce9bb9f04f7918ed4d.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \equiv 1 \text{ (mod 9)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="17" />, we can also write this as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/4/8547beb45ee8db0be6880569ebec074f36fddca0.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3 +\cdots +10+11+12+ \cdots 43 + 44 = \frac{44 \cdot 45}2 = 22 \cdot 45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="492" height="37" />, which has a remainder of 0 mod 9. Solving these modular congruence using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Chinese_Remainder_Theorem" title="Chinese Remainder Theorem">Chinese Remainder Theorem</a> we get the remainder to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/6/45631acc3fceaf75f264c9f83ca05f38d0c35eb5.png" class="latex" alt="$9 \pmod{45}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d52c42c934344fefe4d51b4221fd7acaf0f7e6a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_Ending_to_Solution_1">Alternative Ending to Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Once we find our 2 modular congruences, we can narrow our options down to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/1/1716e238f553f7dabd85afad448d78d505bc0ce1.png" class="latex" alt="${C}$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/9/b29f5f47e0eda4a96ade3c908bda26c59faa83fa.png" class="latex" alt="${D}$" width="15" height="12" /> because the remainder when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> should be a multiple of 9 by our modular congruence that states <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> has a remainder of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> when divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Also, our other modular congruence states that the remainder when divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> should have a remainder of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> when divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Out of options <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d52c42c934344fefe4d51b4221fd7acaf0f7e6a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> satisfies that the remainder when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/e/5eee9393438304d48b26aea020a0580c48f24765.png" class="latex" alt="$45 \equiv 4 \text{ (mod 5)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="17" />.
</p>
