<p>Last year Isabella took <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> math tests and received <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> different scores, each an integer between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/d/7fd34ff50f8bba26a08bc96a69e921daa60f5f12.png" class="latex" alt="$91$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, inclusive. After each test she noticed that the average of her test scores was an integer. Her score on the seventh test was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" />. What was her score on the sixth test?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b968b4ecef089acdaaa41d7c83fc20a06907ff06.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 92\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 94\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 96\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 98\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the sum of the scores of Isabella's first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> tests be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. Since the mean of her first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> scores is an integer, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/d/cedcd9197913ad8da87335b1f80b8e9be8f02a95.png" class="latex" alt="$S + 95 \equiv 0 \text{ (mod 7)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="17" />, or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb697947c348991b9e60e1d7e8056e65bd45343.png" class="latex" alt="$S \equiv3 \text{ (mod 7)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="17" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/b/c8bda4b147f6e3afa84b3d8c4ed62111e3eb53bf.png" class="latex" alt="$S \equiv 0 \text{ (mod 6)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="17" />, so by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/CRT" class="mw-redirect" title="CRT">CRT</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/4/0e40c4005a543ec74ea7fd1f66d75711461b65ec.png" class="latex" alt="$S \equiv 24 \text{ (mod 42)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="17" />. We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d75bf208b9a4df8048d5a7a264f8bf71ad92406.png" class="latex" alt="$91 \cdot 6 \leq S \leq 100 \cdot 6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="150" height="15" />, so by inspection, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1f14f04c8c36db9dda3bf740f8a26120c6d86b6.png" class="latex" alt="$S = 570$" width="63" height="12" />. However, we also have that the mean of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> test scores must be an integer, so the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> test scores must be an multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, which implies that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />th test score is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b1754e12e76c5dd70f75384d97f256ab9570a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 100}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, we find the largest sum of scores which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/5/ae5a800c4b08b036360a4fae6347bccd3d32b6f1.png" class="latex" alt="$100+99+98+97+96+95+94$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="269" height="14" /> which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/a/70a4bca78a4430b16fa910d6e802dcb78bdd1636.png" class="latex" alt="$7(97)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" />. Then we find the smallest sum of scores which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/6/506533509dcd0ea0f3b3187e84510a995b115248.png" class="latex" alt="$91+92+93+94+95+96+97$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="260" height="14" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aaece72770ce631417215bfb3ba657f0d80e4a25.png" class="latex" alt="$7(94)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" />. So the possible sums for the 7 test scores so that they provide an integer average are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/1/9b1eb585bae225ed0aa6166c365776c00a818556.png" class="latex" alt="$7(97), 7(96), 7(95)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="138" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/e/aaece72770ce631417215bfb3ba657f0d80e4a25.png" class="latex" alt="$7(94)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="18" /> which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55db3bc836588bda0bfa3817d64425df23f942d1.png" class="latex" alt="$679, 672, 665,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/0/70043dfc8b7ee5952b43781f9d80559c36e967da.png" class="latex" alt="$658$" width="26" height="12" /> respectively.  Now in order to get the sum of the first 6 tests, we subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> from each sum producing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/f/1af475da73c696184d8cf8b029a94c22dfcc0f82.png" class="latex" alt="$584, 577, 570,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/d/d5d2ab17089fa7519fe595bdad25e3187bab6dbb.png" class="latex" alt="$563$" width="26" height="12" />. Notice only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/8/0e8d9a7268ee310e5bd269e4c07400848af284e5.png" class="latex" alt="$570$" width="26" height="12" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> so, therefore, the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> tests is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/8/0e8d9a7268ee310e5bd269e4c07400848af284e5.png" class="latex" alt="$570$" width="26" height="12" />. We need to find her score on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/e/85e3218d569b20eb9c4e1b86c362a029f84fe4f1.png" class="latex" alt="$6th$" width="25" height="12" /> test so we have to find which number will give us a number divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> when subtracted from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca7099251ea564c320e42fa291fbe5db8e1b7097.png" class="latex" alt="$570.$" width="30" height="12" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/e/dfecd48604f4d56df72d59786de714456b3d3c47.png" class="latex" alt="$7th$" width="25" height="12" /> test score and all test scores are distinct that only leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b1754e12e76c5dd70f75384d97f256ab9570a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 100}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Since all of the scores are from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/1/8c157d950cb5d33bf61525fd6a40cea509547b9e.png" class="latex" alt="$91 - 100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, we can subtract 90 from all of the scores. Since the last score was a 95, the sum of the scores from the first six tests must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e813f2430b76402d67161c6f735da374f9c87797.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \pmod 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/8/3b84dcfb387ea00db647ba9c49f05e692d2e0725.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \pmod 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />. Trying out a few cases, the only solution possible is 30 (this is from adding numbers 1-10). The sixth test score must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89ac24490820bb6563d5bb9c7f0c35756c02e5a8.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \pmod 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/d/3ed623c080a2e797b99e85121efe8e03970b76c6.png" class="latex" alt="$30\equiv 0\pmod5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="18" />. The only possible test scores are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/1/0510fa96a9909623c5966ad29945fabc84c704a4.png" class="latex" alt="$95$" width="17" height="12" /> is already used, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22a605af6f00da6c5c16d54b8070a2b30e023fdd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}100}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="66" height="28" />.
</p>
