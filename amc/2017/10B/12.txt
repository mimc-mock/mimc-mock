<p>Elmer's new car gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7f4de02215a8c5319886c13b2e291811346e1c.png" class="latex" alt="$50\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> percent better fuel efficiency, measured in kilometers per liter, than his old car. However, his new car uses diesel fuel, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> more expensive per liter than the gasoline his old car used. By what percent will Elmer save money if he uses his new car instead of his old car for a long trip?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/e/92e85ab4190fa11108dd3d03d3d463fb71eed374.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 20\% \qquad \textbf{(B) } 26\tfrac23\% \qquad \textbf{(C) } 27\tfrac79\% \qquad \textbf{(D) } 33\tfrac13\% \qquad \textbf{(E) } 66\tfrac23\%$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="516" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that his old car runs at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> km per liter. Then his new car runs at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/c/62cd9a7ade53f614d2755cd9addb5918530237d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="23" height="37" /> km per liter, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> km per <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of a liter. Let the cost of the old car's fuel be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, so the trip in the old car takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/3/ca31f3cf0b7dc567790fe150e8c4b5b9b4633341.png" class="latex" alt="$xc$" width="18" height="8" /> dollars, while the trip in the new car takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/8/5b8b6e0730fc21f2d51c49414e3a77309d81bda9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}\cdot\frac{6}{5}xc = \frac{4}{5}xc$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="113" height="37" />. He saves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/f/f9fd563ae56d8de64836321237e117d09453093d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{5}xc}{xc} = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 20\%}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="137" height="40" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Because they do not give you a given amount of distance, we'll just make that distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" /> miles. Then, we find that the new car will use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/9/5a9f8749a617de11ba2f6d8328b6253bf285625f.png" class="latex" alt="$2*1.2=2.4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="12" />. The old car will use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/9792a0cb18396a80357f196a68308e98e080d5c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(3-2.4)/3=.6/3=20/100=  \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 20\%}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="326" height="29" />.
</p><p>-Lcz
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>You can find that the ratio of fuel used by the old car and the new car for the same amount of distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e2b27d57dc758888b40975d899e302dc8a726e.png" class="latex" alt="$3&#160;: 2$" width="32" height="12" />, and the ratio between the fuel price of these two cars is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/b/95b63028cf879ac5b75d9f638fcaf811cc690344.png" class="latex" alt="$5&#160;: 6$" width="32" height="12" />. Therefore, by multiplying these two ratios, we get that the costs of using these two cars is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/e/21e9509e047a47a0dcffc35e8049a518e22ed0ad.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[15&#160;: 12 = 5&#160;: 4\]" width="107" height="13" />So the percentage of money saved is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/9/7f925779f088e6b8eb2e00269e454c0bf56b0148.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - \frac{4}{5} = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 20\%}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="148" height="37" />.
</p><p>-Quadraticfunctions
(edited by mydad)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Assume WLOG that Elmer's old car's range is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> miles. So, Elmer's new car's range is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/3/5e33a6f6d0515df490ce9061077d31942948aeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$100 \times 1.5 = 150$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="15" /> miles. Also, assume that the gas Elmer's old car uses is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/f/1af88c1534bab510152766fb60851db0c3a5af52.png" class="latex" alt="$$10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="26" height="14" />, which means that diesel will cost <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71d5892881022a75bb9b28d73782e5c4dcc76242.png" class="latex" alt="$$12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="26" height="14" />. Now we can deduce that Elmer's old car uses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8f2a738be90299eebd056e0d968250fade0e1b.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \div 100 = $0.10$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="135" height="17" /> per mile, and Elmer's new car uses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45d578679a0827af16769e6a3202861ee55b88b3.png" class="latex" alt="$12 \div 150 = $0.08$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="135" height="17" /> per mile. Therefore, Elmer's new car saves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/b/61b8e0f307b8593bf465b2ac98afc7827e22a34d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }20\%}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="82" height="31" /> more money than his old car.
</p><p>~MrThinker
</p>
