<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" /> is an equilateral triangle of side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, with the property that there is a unique point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> inside the triangle such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/2/a72da52bb3713e08113d197bdbb42f939655b3b8.png" class="latex" alt="$AP=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57e064eb6fbfb570814a3c1cad5d0a69d59cff20.png" class="latex" alt="$BP=\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c97e5602eaa2bc1f354354c1969ce5e39ebee5.png" class="latex" alt="$CP=2$" width="61" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/6/5f63f4d2de605f49b7319b55473e3f4164e3b15d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 1+\sqrt{2} \qquad \textbf{(B) } \sqrt{7} \qquad \textbf{(C) } \frac{8}{3} \qquad \textbf{(D) } \sqrt{5+\sqrt{5}} \qquad \textbf{(E) } 2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="508" height="38" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<h3><span id="Solution_1(a)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.28a.29">Solution 1(a)</span></h3>
<p>We begin by rotating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/a/84aac376f28a2fe48e02a6a3023fbe272550ea47.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ APB}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" /> counterclockwise by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/d/08d5c45388de5c75af641c046d466de3592ffa98.png" class="latex" alt="$60^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/a/b0a1b949a9aa1b7555c907c20b74aa42b8b3f25e.png" class="latex" alt="$P\mapsto Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/5/005a77522909000e26f6890d3115fa94e3d5f003.png" class="latex" alt="$B\mapsto C$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />. We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/538ccdb7bc0b0ffdc26976885c347407dbddbec8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ APQ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" /> is equilateral with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, meaning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/9/f09b7c979de7a288ce565c9ad912bcc29292b6ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle APQ = 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />. We also see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/a/85aadc3982602f720205c013502de0e7da613bce.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{CPQ}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="17" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> right triangle, meaning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/f/67fc74e201268560014f9a0cd91dadb2669180b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CPQ= 60^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="18" />. Thus, by adding the two together, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/5/f052993f4342596619acb0404df32bd91e2f7c58.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle APC = 120^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="112" height="13" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/2/e527d8fe3e531f30770a6d41ad075c4f5b2fcb5c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); pen p = fontsize(10pt)+gray+0.5; pen q = fontsize(13pt); pair A,B,C,D,P,Q; real s=sqrt(7); B=origin; A=s*dir(60); C=s*right; P=IP(CR(A,1),CR(C,2)); Q=rotate(60,A)*P; draw(A--B--C--A, black+0.8); draw(A--P--B^^P--C^^A--Q--C, p); draw(P--Q, p+dashed); //draw(A--A+C--C, p); label(&quot;$A$&quot;, A, up, q);  label(&quot;$B$&quot;, B, 0.5*(B-P), q); label(&quot;$C$&quot;, C, 0.5*(C-P), q);   label(&quot;$P$&quot;, P, dir(180), q);   label(&quot;$Q$&quot;, Q, 0.25*(Q-B), q);  label(&quot;$\sqrt{3}$&quot;,B--P, right, p);  label(&quot;$2$&quot;,C--P, 2*left, p);  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,A--P, 1.5**dir(-10), p);   label(&quot;$1$&quot;, A--Q, dir(250), p);  label(&quot;$1$&quot;,P--Q, down, p);  label(&quot;$\sqrt{3}$&quot;,C--Q, right, p);  [/asy]" width="335" height="295" />
We can now use the law of cosines as following:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/3/cd3ec80c420bbe9820ef9f9202a82dd1261ec223.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} s^2 &amp;= (AP)^2 + (CP)^2 - 2\cdot AP\cdot CP\cdot \cos{\angle{APC}} \\ &amp;= 1 + 4 - 2\cdot 1\cdot 2\cdot \cos{120^{\circ}} \\ &amp;= 5 - 4\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) \\ &amp;= 7, \end{align*}" width="370" height="120" />
giving us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/a/3aa22411dfa1afb63f031e693201cd508a965807.png" class="latex" alt="$s = \boxed{ \sqrt{7} \ \textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="104" height="35" />.

<h3><span id="Solution_1(b)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.28b.29">Solution 1(b)</span></h3>
