<p>There are integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/1/421dbe5ac249bea2c9d25145a7eb9b73644c5c61.png" class="latex" alt="$c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="11" /> each greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> such that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/959a043a438414196edd9f9fb8e63acdfe06aa2c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sqrt[a]{N\sqrt[b]{N\sqrt[c]{N}}} = \sqrt[36]{N^{25}}\]" width="176" height="43" />
</p><p>for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/0/75093ec72bd05203ad4940148066480cf941e4d5.png" class="latex" alt="$N \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="17" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/f/cff4535f4252c03431c79fd1638208e478a32971.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 3 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 4 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 5 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d033b4bc2ebe602b317e2fa722f46469bb8190a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[a]{N\sqrt[b]{N\sqrt[c]{N}}}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="103" height="43" /> can be simplified to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8dec15fffc64f81a050bf4364c5b4ad32b05bd45.png" class="latex" alt="$N^{\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{ab}+\frac{1}{abc}}.$" width="88" height="18" />
</p><p>The equation is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/e/04ea2d2c526a8f4575f86e6c514b10371fc26fd8.png" class="latex" alt="$N^{\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{ab}+\frac{1}{abc}}=N^{\frac{25}{36}}$" width="139" height="18" /> which implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/9/4e90a24f61debbf0756f50c5e1a5243c955ae055.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{ab}+\frac{1}{abc}=\frac{25}{36}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="158" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/4/96425aaa4fcf97e6c4a665d13b286792b7b00b65.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{36}&gt;\frac{7}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="38" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/3/a2370a3e7c4737d9b84d5546ce132105975dc476.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" /> is the result when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/d/c2d7845e8b1533219e4352f35231cbed058dc5ae.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />  
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> will make the fraction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> which is close to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4ce4b2225811a6de476ea7c3ba911b6869d4c6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />. 
</p><p>Finally, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, the fraction becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4ce4b2225811a6de476ea7c3ba911b6869d4c6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{25}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" />. In this case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> work, which means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b45c225c54959c63e75513fc939aae9ec563c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" />~lopkiloinm
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As above, notice that you get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/9/4e90a24f61debbf0756f50c5e1a5243c955ae055.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{ab}+\frac{1}{abc}=\frac{25}{36}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="158" height="37" /> 
</p><p>Now, combine the fractions to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/a/17ade63af6ae076ba74e075c52016a70a4d27db7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{bc+c+1}{abc}=\frac{25}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />.
</p><p>Let us assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fba3e441f9fc0f0fa492bc8cfe917df73009d88d.png" class="latex" alt="$bc+c+1=25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19ac33506155f21d746d1fd7a9fecd91c453c99c.png" class="latex" alt="$abc=36$" width="67" height="12" /> as this is the most convenient. (EDIT: This used to say WLOG but that is inaccurate)
</p><p>From the first equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/3/8d3686b40fd9596408150c332ea424115fb365fb.png" class="latex" alt="$c(b+1)=24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />. Note also that from the second equation, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> must be factors of 36.
</p><p>After listing out the factors of 36 and utilising trial and error, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eb76404ee79952dcc555464b061509299789b09.png" class="latex" alt="$c=6$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" /> works, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55afdab16c8c49089c4d9ceeb8d3c8cf1f088be6.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2$" width="42" height="12" />. So our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b45c225c54959c63e75513fc939aae9ec563c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 3.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" />
</p><p>~Silverdragon
</p><p>Edits by ~Snore, ~Swaggergotcha
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Collapsed, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/b/55b801a8cda28fae4e7ba36812a678d84caf35a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[a]{N\sqrt[b]{N\sqrt[c]{N}}} = \sqrt[abc]{N^{bc+c+1}}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="216" height="43" />. Comparing this to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/0/c203875ea549e53462f87b17ca5d42447f9267ff.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[36]{N^{25}}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="18" />, observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/a/fba3e441f9fc0f0fa492bc8cfe917df73009d88d.png" class="latex" alt="$bc+c+1=25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19ac33506155f21d746d1fd7a9fecd91c453c99c.png" class="latex" alt="$abc=36$" width="67" height="12" />. The first can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/3/8d3686b40fd9596408150c332ea424115fb365fb.png" class="latex" alt="$c(b+1)=24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="18" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/91927ed6d1093e6ce84e3aa8446121218cb17b71.png" class="latex" alt="$b+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" /> has to factor into 24 while 1 less than that also must factor into 36. The prime factorizations are as follows <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/9/af9f2e8d6e1cd0323a44787a9c0ca81167241a91.png" class="latex" alt="$36=2^2 3^2$" width="74" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/7/2370b59d37ad22ec0cfb89057038092b744c380d.png" class="latex" alt="$24=2^33$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="15" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/158580a5973c10826f63f422a96bee8ca7ffd8b3.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\boxed{\textbf{B)}3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="74" height="28" />, as only 4 and 3 factor into 36 and 24 while being 1 apart.
</p><p>~~BJHHar
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/b/42b0a873689cb2d3bc35c55378cedf5602d5a754.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[a]{N\sqrt[b]{N\sqrt[c]{N}}} = \sqrt[36]{N^{25}}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="179" height="43" /> can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/8517e12a650789da99d7b97225c2b2a1915e2676.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{\frac{1}{c}+1}{b}+1}{a} = \frac{25}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="47" />. The trick is to focus on the properties of the denominators and numerators. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be either be a factor of 36, because in any other situation the denominator of the final expression is preserved, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is forced to be 36, which is not an answer choice. Next, realize that the numerator of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/2/6125110c2e9ead20d0925f08ff4d71f2d57a5e60.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{c}+1}{b}+1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="79" height="42" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />, and even more importantly, that the denominator must be large enough so that when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is subtracted from it, it will form a fraction less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. The only number large enough to do this will also being a fraction of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> is 18. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is 2. Moving onward, we are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/f/f8f01107b26a3a1c6fae5b8df0ea38a9aadab0a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{c}+1}{b} = \frac{7}{18}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="94" height="42" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, just plug in values from the answer choices to find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/b/4eb76404ee79952dcc555464b061509299789b09.png" class="latex" alt="$c=6$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/626beed3a7eee094ce4b6cf79a49d81669f72d99.png" class="latex" alt="$b=3$" width="40" height="12" />.
</p><p>~jackshi2006
