<p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d7701e9469f26e22c106341791344b28f3196443.png" class="latex" alt="$-10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/9/e395ecfa661165ea0d06fc0114eb7c24f075ead7.png" class="latex" alt="$14,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="15" /> inclusive, can be arranged to form a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-by-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> square in which the sum of the numbers in each row, the sum of the numbers in each column, and the sum of the numbers along each of the main diagonals are all the same. What is the value of this common sum?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/7567d9da241f4573816f70f4fe7167d1fd903f93.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }2 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 5\qquad\textbf{(C) } 10\qquad\textbf{(D) } 25\qquad\textbf{(E) } 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="380" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Without loss of generality, consider the five rows in the square. Each row must have the same sum of numbers, meaning that the sum of all the numbers in the square divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is the total value per row. The sum of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/9/cc97a414da20dbc661d32d60472aa7aaff0bbd86.png" class="latex" alt="$-10+-9+...+14=11+12+13+14=50$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="363" height="15" />, and the common sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/3/8e3846b65c25c8d69c9143aabb500064532c96af.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{50}{5}=\boxed{\text{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="106" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Take the sum of the middle 5 values of the set (they will turn out to be the mean of each row). We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/1/6d103ff29de4cec8d47b20fff89ddd997379bc3c.png" class="latex" alt="$0 + 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="223" height="28" /> as our answer.
~Baolan
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Taking the average of the first and last terms, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d7701e9469f26e22c106341791344b28f3196443.png" class="latex" alt="$-10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />, we have that the mean of the set is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. There are 5 values in each row, column or diagonal, so the value of the common sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/f/faf66240ecba6fcb8c48d56281ea7bb1933fa46e.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot2$" width="30" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/f/b5f6002355beb793dc42eb5090d34ffef7aceda6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />. 
~Arctic_Bunny, edited by KINGLOGIC
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Let us consider the horizontal rows.  Since there are five of them, each with constant sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we can add up the 25 numbers in 5 rows for a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" />.  Since the sum of the 25 numbers used is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/3541f7c3e3c0ab5edcb7ab9f9895449e70f9978d.png" class="latex" alt="$-10-9-8-\cdots{}+12+13+14+15=11+12+13+14+15=50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="549" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac354f37aa0664e7292e65c231d994425dacd3df.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=50$" width="61" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/6/076a6145b447a11b81b6a0c32393c781656f7870.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="99" height="28" />. 
~cw357
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>The mean of the set of numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/6/166b64245de54cabb15fa6a9bc1ab4407835d7d0.png" class="latex" alt="$(14-10) \div 2 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="139" height="20" />. The numbers around it must be equal (i.e. if the mean of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a98cc52a4ceef5de20a62e2be2e7d5bb5bf77df.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4=1+5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="107" height="15" />.)
One row of the square would be 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/3/de3091fc4d9d5f46c0717e31bb904441d5d96769.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\square  \square  2  \square  \square\]" width="63" height="12" />
</p><p>Adding the numbers would be 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/8/bb80231ddaf31a166523ada64db3e0709900ee32.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0, 1, 2, 3, 4\]" width="77" height="15" />
</p><p>with a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bd710c9221212dba3bf44ccb7d478426ac3ac8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {\textbf {(C) }10}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="30" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
