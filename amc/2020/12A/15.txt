<p>In the complex plane, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be the set of solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d181b7e15cf72fb7b7e379e9933fd4c1fea59610.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8=0$" width="81" height="15" /> and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> be the set of solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/3/3b301ae63cf51e2749b5d4aee69a211277213062.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8z^{2}-8z+64=0.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="182" height="16" /> What is the greatest distance between a point of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and a point of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d3713b61d71855f44ce52196d5bf838501d09ad5.png" class="latex" alt="$B?$" width="22" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a74551e6499fddc46f8734b089908d00ad45207b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf{(B) } 6 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 9 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 2\sqrt{21} \qquad \textbf{(E) } 9+\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="458" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We solve each equation separately:
</p>
<ol style="margin-left: 1.5em;">
  <li>We solve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d181b7e15cf72fb7b7e379e9933fd4c1fea59610.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8=0$" width="81" height="15" /> by De Moivre's Theorem.<p>
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/a/27a62307d1822b4d97ea04707fa44c90dd300528.png" class="latex" alt="$z=r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)=r\operatorname{cis}\theta,$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="234" height="20" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/0/3b082fa500291bf2be24d7655214c4802c3e237f.png" class="latex" alt="$r\geq0,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="46" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> is the argument of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b448bd981311c4251d88961af1b0a2bb5fd0000.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq\theta&lt;2\pi.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="90" height="15" /> </p><p>
We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/3/d63e79f796093f49b3fbb06a85ea78050324e1fc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z^3=r^3\operatorname{cis}(3\theta)=8(1),\]" width="172" height="20" /> from which
</p><ul style="list-style-type:square;">
  <li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/6/da6a29b2f435ba5105072e475d40029d3b1eaa42.png" class="latex" alt="$r^3=8,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="20" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/1/dc17db83da4930ca92c89529b0c111e8a8ac8e56.png" class="latex" alt="$r=2.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8ad4139ea4a44843d95ac3910071e9b4da68d0a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\begin{cases} \begin{aligned} \cos(3\theta) &amp;= 1 \\ \sin(3\theta) &amp;= 0 \end{aligned}, \end{cases}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="129" height="47" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b93a309555fa30b127d91eb7663bfc86a04523.png" class="latex" alt="$3\theta=0,2\pi,4\pi,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821bd99e8d000868495dddf0f3fe167dcbc450f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta=0,\frac{2\pi}{3},\frac{4\pi}{3}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="114" height="38" /> </li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p></ul>
The set of solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d181b7e15cf72fb7b7e379e9933fd4c1fea59610.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8=0$" width="81" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/c/08c84939d829e603618ff15ae8bd48d9e444f9cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boldsymbol{A=\left\{2,-1+\sqrt{3}i,-1-\sqrt{3}i\right\}}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="278" height="34" /> In the complex plane, the solutions form the vertices of an equilateral triangle whose circumcircle has center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e4797950b5e812b65381cbc5fc33ad2db5f57aff.png" class="latex" alt="$2.$" width="12" height="12" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li>We solve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4afd2ff2e261e77baa7cabfbc24314b818227970.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8z^{2}-8z+64=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="178" height="16" /> by factoring by grouping.</li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p><p>We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/b/1cb696ae6b5338042033a7d6359766220b853067.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} z^2(z-8)-8(z-8)&amp;=0 \\ \bigl(z^2-8\bigr)(z-8)&amp;=0. \end{align*}" width="193" height="49" />
The set of solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/f/4afd2ff2e261e77baa7cabfbc24314b818227970.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8z^{2}-8z+64=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="178" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/4/304a2f24e7e70663c8e37ddb00310f023d9003ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\boldsymbol{B=\left\{2\sqrt{2},-2\sqrt{2},8\right\}}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="197" height="34" />
</p>
</ol>
<p>In the graph below, the points in set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> are shown in red, and the points in set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are shown in blue. The greatest distance between a point of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and a point of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is the distance between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/d/eadae860a6f541ab8db5944ea1b7d8bbca4506d1.png" class="latex" alt="$-1\pm\sqrt{3}i$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/a/12a9fb4c5b23667e57f28d844ad99715f18ba3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> as shown in the dashed line segments.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/4/444e00e5a4bba363ceec79dc3b496fc3f1a846a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Made by MRENTHUSIASM */ size(200);   int xMin = -10; int xMax = 10; int yMin = -10; int yMax = 10; int numRays = 24;  //Draws a polar grid that goes out to a number of circles  //equal to big, with numRays specifying the number of rays:  void polarGrid(int big, int numRays)  {   for (int i = 1; i &lt; big+1; ++i)   {     draw(Circle((0,0),i), gray+linewidth(0.4));   }   for(int i=0;i&lt;numRays;++i)    draw(rotate(i*360/numRays)*((-big,0)--(big,0)), gray+linewidth(0.4)); }  //Draws the horizontal gridlines void horizontalLines() {   for (int i = yMin+1; i &lt; yMax; ++i)   {     draw((xMin,i)--(xMax,i), mediumgray+linewidth(0.4));   } }  //Draws the vertical gridlines void verticalLines() {   for (int i = xMin+1; i &lt; xMax; ++i)   {     draw((i,yMin)--(i,yMax), mediumgray+linewidth(0.4));   } }  horizontalLines(); verticalLines(); polarGrid(xMax,numRays); draw((xMin,0)--(xMax,0),black+linewidth(1.5),EndArrow(5)); draw((0,yMin)--(0,yMax),black+linewidth(1.5),EndArrow(5)); label(&quot;Re&quot;,(xMax,0),(2,0)); label(&quot;Im&quot;,(0,yMax),(0,2));  //The n such that we&#39;re taking the nth roots of unity multiplied by 2. int n = 3;  pair A[]; for(int i = 0; i &lt;= n-1; i+=1) {   A[i] = rotate(360*i/n)*(2,0); }  draw(Circle((0,0),2),red); draw(A[1]--(8,0),dashed); draw(A[2]--(8,0),dashed);  for(int i = 0; i&lt; n; ++i) dot(A[i],red+linewidth(4.5));   dot((2*sqrt(2),0),blue+linewidth(4.5)); dot((-2*sqrt(2),0),blue+linewidth(4.5)); dot((8,0),blue+linewidth(4.5)); [/asy]" width="335" height="325" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Alternatively, we can solve <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d181b7e15cf72fb7b7e379e9933fd4c1fea59610.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8=0$" width="81" height="15" /> by the difference of cubes: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/c/24ce584dc1d1105f8057ed0ac1bc522b7da857c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(z-2)\left(z^2+2z+4\right)=0.\]" width="198" height="21" />
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/2/212cd710cc92eb9b7877d9530ee150fe6439d9fc.png" class="latex" alt="$z-2=0,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="81" height="17" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/e/76e9faa79a74fd520dfd4dcc4df4eef623338e25.png" class="latex" alt="$z=2.$" width="46" height="12" /></li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/b/f2bea243a40ead49ca93e753d6a6de3c25046fb3.png" class="latex" alt="$z^2+2z+4=0,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="20" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/4/e7432ab4354e118e7f7218b8aca726e2b979a130.png" class="latex" alt="$z=-1\pm\sqrt{3}i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="111" height="21" /> by either completing the square or the quadratic formula.</li></ul>
<p>The set of solutions to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d181b7e15cf72fb7b7e379e9933fd4c1fea59610.png" class="latex" alt="$z^{3}-8=0$" width="81" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/a/39a64ceda8d1ffc03226b2dfaa38fc4a2dc6fa66.png" class="latex" alt="$A=\left\{2,-1+\sqrt{3}i,-1-\sqrt{3}i\right\}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="249" height="34" />
