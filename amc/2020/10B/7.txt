<p>How many positive even multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/9/509a9369d5c55fd98dc99cb2938fb44f0101f90a.png" class="latex" alt="$2020$" width="35" height="12" /> are perfect squares?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09c50bbb3572b7e8844da7beef02f584c7faff59.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 8 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\  9 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 10 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Any even multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, so we need to find multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> that are perfect squares and less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/9/509a9369d5c55fd98dc99cb2938fb44f0101f90a.png" class="latex" alt="$2020$" width="35" height="12" />. Any solution that we want will be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/4/9d4e6bc69851b329ae75d4468346d088ef167e01.png" class="latex" alt="$(6n)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="19" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a positive integer. The smallest possible value is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, and the largest is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7f0f830b5b25ea929198068e407e8c9f62f56c4.png" class="latex" alt="$n=7$" width="44" height="12" /> (where the expression equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0cea478aa2d0f27d9f7e5e6ab83cf0ba59abe61.png" class="latex" alt="$1764$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />). Therefore, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/b/7dbf74dc64cf0f4fb1e277e03bf74c5a8dff3880.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" /> possible numbers.-PCChess
</p>
