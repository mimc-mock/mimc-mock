<p>For each real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/d/00d3083d1692bad42166a441dc3293eda390788a.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \leq a \leq 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" />, let numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be chosen independently at random from the intervals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/7/ce7d05d4da0d50abf77ee166ae0c6474bee5a1cf.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, a]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/1/ab178d831a786b92cb4c9ddc2d33578223036f98.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, 1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />, respectively, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2ac37e8ff9aeea3ac5eaf42779864032eb645c9.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> be the probability that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/d/4dda018877a7b217408e61c488287f3bb9d31582.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin^2{(\pi x)} + \sin^2{(\pi y)} &gt; 1\]" width="190" height="20" />
What is the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc203907a2faff74b09a5108a894bd0f6642bb0.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a)?$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/2/7229dbda36c22f3c7a4aad5e76338000284c8e11.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{7}{12} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2 - \sqrt{2} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{1+\sqrt{2}}{4} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{5}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="512" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let's start first by manipulating the given inequality.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/8/cb8efe9949fa008c546e52b562015960b29b76f7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin^{2}{(\pi x)}+\sin^{2}{(\pi y)}&gt;1\]" width="190" height="20" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3e20e036a736e493cf3d41f17f106a79f6ebc86.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin^{2}{(\pi x)}&gt;1-\sin^{2}{(\pi y)}=\cos^{2}{(\pi y)}\]" width="283" height="20" />
</p><p>Let's consider the boundary cases: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/5/17557408fa78ab42e49c5f64481891d3ffac07b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{(\pi x)}=\pm \cos{(\pi y)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="166" height="20" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c93ef44e6b1df372b5c977cd09b9ef7f5d1ae6ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin{(\pi x)}=\pm \cos{(\pi y)}=\sin{(\tfrac 12 {\pi}\pm \pi y)}\]" width="290" height="21" />
</p><p>Solving the first case gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b25d7cd56a6d0c43ef832495aab8ab53a5f7e05.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y=\tfrac{1}{2}-x \quad \textrm{and} \quad  y=x-\tfrac{1}{2}.\]" width="217" height="21" /> Solving the second case gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/8/e2834ef3bae5c13dd5fb8845ce37722f9b42436e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y=x+\tfrac{1}{2}\quad \textrm{and} \quad y=\tfrac{3}{2}-x.\]" width="217" height="21" /> If we graph these equations in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6aa56acfcb95ab5be4a1c4210c59219c425167b.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,1]\times[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="93" height="18" />, we get a rhombus shape. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43d922c21d526814bbd9d43648760262218cf640.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] defaultpen(fontsize(9)+0.8); size(200); pen p=fontsize(10); pair A,B,C,D,A1,A2,B1,B2,C1,C2,D1,D2,I,L; A=MP(&quot;(0,0)&quot;,origin,down+left,p); B=MP(&quot;(1,0)&quot;,right,down+right,p); C=MP(&quot;(1,1)&quot;,right+up,up+right,p);  D=MP(&quot;(0,1)&quot;,up,up+left,p); A1=MP(&quot;&quot;,extension(A,B,(0.5,0),(0,0.5)),2*down,p); dot(A1); A2=MP(&quot;&quot;,extension(A,D,(0.5,0),(0,0.5)),2*left,p); dot(A2); B1=MP(&quot;&quot;,extension(B,C,(0.5,0),(0,-0.5)),2*right,p); dot(B1); B2=MP(&quot;&quot;,extension(C,D,(0.5,1),(0,0.5)),2*up,p); dot(B2); real a=0.7; draw(A1--B1--B2--A2--cycle, gray+0.6); draw(a*right--a*right+up, royalblue); draw(A1--B2, royalblue+dashed); draw(A--B--C--D--A, black+1.2); dot(&quot;$(a,0)$&quot;,(a,0),down); dot(&quot;$(a,1)$&quot;,(a,1),up); [/asy]" width="335" height="295" />
Testing points in each section tells us that the inside of the rhombus satisfies the inequality in the problem statement.
</p><p>From the region graph, notice that in order to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2ac37e8ff9aeea3ac5eaf42779864032eb645c9.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/75629ef9d465220e558e8dc7b8ec12fe3c437fe9.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq\tfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="46" height="23" />. We can solve the rest with geometric probability.
</p><p>Instead of maximizing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2ac37e8ff9aeea3ac5eaf42779864032eb645c9.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, we minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32e020ca867d2caec0fe8b51b6d12d0c92642b44.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(a)=1-P(a)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="133" height="20" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/0/fd06d465665181cb95333f18ab4ca802d5ffb815.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(a)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="40" height="20" /> consists of two squares (each broken into two triangles), one of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/4/c84a414e9655bf25b41aebc8c9f7bcb651acf2fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> and another of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/7/f47c6533644032aea376b5f4d4d1a2add73eda1a.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-\tfrac 12)^2$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="66" height="23" />. To calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/0/fd06d465665181cb95333f18ab4ca802d5ffb815.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(a)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="40" height="20" />, we divide this area by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/1/081895533609872b95d9f66fc1fc82b496c8a642.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[Q(a) = \frac{1}{a}\left(\frac{1}{4}+(a-\tfrac 12)^2\right) = \frac{1}{a}\left(\frac{1}{2}+a^2-a\right)= a+\frac 1{2a}-1.\]" width="468" height="43" />
</p><p>By AM-GM, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/5/795338420a95ff516a71c53cddb71aeb247ddab0.png" class="latex" alt="$a+\frac{1}{2a}\geq 2\sqrt{\frac{a}{2a}}=\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="177" height="43" />, which we can achieve by setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/0/4601a5c2ee9625a8c361176435d3df8737534056.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="41" />.
</p><p>Therefore, the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2ac37e8ff9aeea3ac5eaf42779864032eb645c9.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7ff90d5dedb7340c9f9f5d3296e56fd4fd373e.png" class="latex" alt="$1-\min(Q(a))$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="116" height="20" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/c/4ec55f508a8cdcfc542892243d97536aa8aca33e.png" class="latex" alt="$1-(\sqrt{2}-1) =\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 2 - \sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="228" height="35" />
</p>
