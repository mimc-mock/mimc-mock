<p>A single bench section at a school event can hold either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> adults or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> children. When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> bench sections are connected end to end, an equal number of adults and children seated together will occupy all the bench space. What is the least possible positive integer value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/c/85cf31acc4d19289fd14d58e39c17cff9fe2b0e4.png" class="latex" alt="$N?$" width="24" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/3/d932ac55b336812b1b396ba7cd1c6b8865a1bbf9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 9 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 18 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 27 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 36 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 77$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The least common multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" />. Therefore, there must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" /> adults and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" /> children. The total number of benches is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/3801ae3699a24290ae6b5e38de35c29a85ee74c5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{77}{7}+\frac{77}{11}=11+7=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }18}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="231" height="38" />.~taarunganesh
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> denote how many adults there are. Since the number of adults is equal to the number of children we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/c/74c7133876b46ccafc1db1c3fecec5a669d42da5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{7}+\frac{x}{11}=N$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="99" height="33" />. Simplifying we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/9/3c9ded2d2c586f6fc7af41703a18f3352fc6f31d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{18x}{77} = N$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" />
Since both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> have to be positive integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> has to equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/6/196ad9aac61d9072daf2a321b8d08e711a1cd024.png" class="latex" alt="$N=\boxed{\textbf{(B) }18}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="107" height="30" /> is our final answer.
</p>
