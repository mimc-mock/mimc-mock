Except for the bottom cube, the bottom face of each cube lies completely on top of the cube below it. What is the total surface area of the tower (including the bottom) in square units?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The volume of each cube follows the pattern of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/a/4ba37f50c674bcbae995ed1d9dfd4beb5e53c765.png" class="latex" alt="$n^3$" width="17" height="15" />, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>We see that the total surface area can be comprised of three parts: the sides of the cubes, the tops of the cubes, and the bottom of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/7/2f7b1a314264c2f145e4f3f4cbcafe72b6300773.png" class="latex" alt="$7\times 7\times 7$" width="71" height="12" /> cube (which is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/c/90cc27affd59f059f5a536bdf40e6d366ae23f22.png" class="latex" alt="$7 \times 7 = 49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" />). The sides areas can be measured as the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a19cb9d636b7ebb0787a8b46bb1453c0bd1f96f6.png" class="latex" alt="$4\sum_{n=1}^{7} n^2$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="61" height="55" />, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/1/98166ad8c04d7db41c3aa4233142974fa5baf614.png" class="latex" alt="$560$" width="26" height="12" />. Structurally, if we examine the tower from the top, we see that it really just forms a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/2/d82a7109054f6068dbb1152f48bd109090f2c73d.png" class="latex" alt="$7\times 7$" width="40" height="12" /> square of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Therefore, we can say that the total surface area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/6/78620586356066a3b2dafaeded0cfebc747252d2.png" class="latex" alt="$560 + 49 + 49 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }658}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="206" height="28" />.
Alternatively, for the area of the tops, we could have found the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/2/7423cee53cc44085e65e1c39172fcbc9d51b7687.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{n=2}^{7}((n)^{2}-(n-1)^{2})$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="160" height="55" />, giving us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> as well.
</p><p>~ciceronii
</p><p>Note: The area on top and bottom are 49 because the largest area is 49, and the other cubes are "inscribed" in it.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>It can quickly be seen that the side lengths of the cubes are the integers from 1 to 7 inclusive.
</p><p>First, we will calculate the total surface area of the cubes, ignoring overlap. This value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/7/c57398744908a93244b3e0cae3a0a84415ef0f20.png" class="latex" alt="$6 ( 1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 7^2 ) = 6\sum_{n=1}^{7} n^2 = 6 \left( \frac{7(7 + 1)(2 \cdot 7 + 1)}{6} \right) = 7 \cdot 8 \cdot 15 = 840$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="588" height="50" />. Then, we need to subtract out the overlapped parts of the cubes. Between each consecutive pair of cubes, one of the smaller cube's faces is completely covered, along with an equal area of one of the larger cube's faces. The total area of the overlapped parts of the cubes is thus equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/2/1b2f02eb40c104e4af8d82e54f76c26a6b19bdb2.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sum_{n=1}^{6} n^2 = 182$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="110" height="50" />. Subtracting the overlapped surface area from the total surface area, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec1b88a6daa3b1d70e72e397347dcf45e69db04.png" class="latex" alt="$840 - 182 = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }658}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="174" height="28" />. ~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
</p>
