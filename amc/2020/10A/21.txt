</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/0/1f0c4f3bca87f068f670003a9ea8a35e95d10f14.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 117 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 136 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 137 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 273 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 306$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/f/83f8bf4e1fb1c938fbaeed31f39e90e3576aa35f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{17}$" width="22" height="15" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. 
Then, the given equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/8/fe8dd549209f65c644879fa7b9c7bbe823d195e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x^{17}+1}{x+1}=x^{16}-x^{15}+x^{14}...-x^1+x^0$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="299" height="42" />.
Now consider only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/5164e3b94d2632bb96f82406190aa92c3f99a0c5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{16}-x^{15}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" />. This equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/6/62690c729d40159fa14ad7b7ad22086d3d680794.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{15}(x-1)=x^{15} \cdot (2^{17}-1)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="213" height="21" />.
Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/8/bb8690849cabfb92e4d5d57fe8ef9b4d874ad321.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{17}-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="15" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/5/0b54a384b72ab79db26afcd79b8c40cb6b6bd1e1.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{16}+2^{15}+...+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="136" height="16" />, since the sum of a geometric sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/d/b7df843c51dbdc5528ad79fb55342cef23191a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x^n-1}{x-1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="53" height="37" />.
Thus, we can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/6/5164e3b94d2632bb96f82406190aa92c3f99a0c5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{16}-x^{15}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" /> forms the sum of 17 different powers of 2. 
Applying the same method to each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/b/ccbdca20cb146a7750925269932fc217e97204d8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{14}-x^{13}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/e/4aed6795332df7a0e2113d61701973fdf862a019.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{12}-x^{11}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" />, ... , <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58c4389a64bab6a6eda613266010da737f54b5fb.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}-x^{1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="18" />, we can see that each of the pairs forms the sum of 17 different powers of 2. This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47e060e084c783f907984394fce1b8a3920fff08.png" class="latex" alt="$17 \cdot 8=136$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="14" />.
But we must count also the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/a/eda9cb06647e9f40c563a46b475f4ae23decad20.png" class="latex" alt="$x^0$" width="16" height="15" /> term. 
Thus, Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/4/ee46295f8d43a2c52c0c0387b5ca44209de29598.png" class="latex" alt="$136+1=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 137}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="157" height="28" />.
