<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> fails. Except for the special case of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d96ecc15129c5631ba4a2c6578e91e3d13e3baf.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/3/d131cf36cbb8624b185d23a4d99d4eedb9e95563.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor - \left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor\]" width="126" height="43" />
equals either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. If it equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, this implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a246fa08053b8e03a80b7a33e75cd5e9508bcb.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="209" height="43" />, so their sum is clearly a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, so this will always fail. If it equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the sum of the three floor terms is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/263249fe69e46cf9d62cfc419c2d40c5cb9d0a53.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor \pm 1$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="97" height="45" />, so it is never a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, we are looking for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/c/5dca5fca8ec867b96b11a41db1a9188d9e3f30a4.png" class="latex" alt="$n \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" /> such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5af8b32ff72072447cd8238c92b3399ddd9cc353.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor - \left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor = 1.\]" width="168" height="43" />
This implies that either
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/0/2308471101c54f0c15728b885fe38648ec8dce80.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor,\]" width="162" height="43" />
or
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/0/860b8b6c5877dff1334c41e7a21bdbb4d6cfa17e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor.\]" width="171" height="43" />
</p><p>Let's analyze the first equation of these two. This equation is equivalent to the statement that there is a positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> such that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/6/5566c5b62083d605f78c80a30b42fde19717f40c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{998}{n} &lt; a \leq \frac{999}{n} \implies 998 &lt; an \leq 999 \implies an = 999 \implies a = \frac{999}{n} \implies n | 999.*\]" width="633" height="37" />
Analogously, the second equation implies that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/8/0f8a220f5443bcd989f6af775c83fdbc1f3174b5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n | 1000.\]" width="55" height="18" />
So our only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> that satisfy this condition are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/c/5dca5fca8ec867b96b11a41db1a9188d9e3f30a4.png" class="latex" alt="$n \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="17" /> that divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. Using the method to find the number of divisors of a number, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> divisors and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> divisors. Their only common factor is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/2/492eada7fa5c165efc9a441970fa8007fc831316.png" class="latex" alt="$8+16-1 = 23$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="125" height="15" /> positive integers that divide either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. Since the integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is a special case and does not count, we must subtract this from our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" />, so our final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/5/cc598c3dbd0028ba29364106c37e0d89a9db6561.png" class="latex" alt="$23-1 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 22}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="146" height="30" />
</p>
<pre>*While this observation may seem strange, it is actually "trivial by intuition" to go straight from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/6/706566098a4f64e5245bb028b31f8529122705c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="155" height="43" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/f/aef07e432eb13364c405a612453a04e90c4c37d5.png" class="latex" alt="$n | 999$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="20" />. In fact, "trivial by intuition" is basically a good summary of the solution to this entire problem.
</pre>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>First, we notice the following lemma:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/e/e0e98d9393c406a69e25bef89a98f75b6ec9a135.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{Lemma}$" width="62" height="12" />: For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/c/14c079526febdbbea91033f17de32202fef5a843.png" class="latex" alt="$N, n \in \mathbb{N}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd0d2d2d873bf04291129e191990c6d63ce8a35.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor + 1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="168" height="43" /> if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/28265bb3b7e920903f6b74b94c8380ae21e64efe.png" class="latex" alt="$n \mid N$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" />; and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/0/06067a0d9e1bfdec521af21cd407ffa0b8866c8f.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="134" height="43" />  if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/b/1ab4572b1a9a046b266f1f62557eb19bc462abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$n \nmid N.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="45" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fead881ca5083ee04f9c3aa9c976c87234e6b41a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{Proof}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="12" />: Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/082e606dbb93b3844c97c945507ef24a7aff2fb2.png" class="latex" alt="$A = kn + r$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/a/61a41adf74448538b6cef6ba4978e400da73f147.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \leq r &lt; n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="15" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/28265bb3b7e920903f6b74b94c8380ae21e64efe.png" class="latex" alt="$n \mid N$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/5/545933c25a1802d9dbcfc24ab4c013d07ef4ccbc.png" class="latex" alt="$r = 0$" width="41" height="12" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/5/5f54511b2ed2b0269fe69474f8b013048fbbd70a.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = k$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="75" height="43" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/6/6466bf1f5db0a056f634c78064eaf886817bb0e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{(k-1)n+n-1}{n} \right\rfloor = k-1 + \left\lfloor \frac{n-1}{n} \right\rfloor = k-1$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="471" height="43" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b078b214b3c97ee469a68955a44a5c5ebeb116b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor + 1.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="173" height="43" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/c/15cabc2dff704f8beb74493337c9a314b9a6e6bc.png" class="latex" alt="$n \nmid N$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/7/9972c64430999877edb3c23ad48ed4bdc9e43a06.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \leq r &lt; n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="77" height="14" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/5/5f54511b2ed2b0269fe69474f8b013048fbbd70a.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = k$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="75" height="43" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9d40560d5e2fe7a4381b9bc7ef17ed5dd76a76.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor = k + \left\lfloor \frac{r-1}{n} \right\rfloor = k$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="227" height="43" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/d/21de4679313f3df30382f8e21b71cbaebbbf01b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{N}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{N-1}{n} \right\rfloor.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="144" height="43" />
</p><p>From the lemma and the given equation, we have four possible cases:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/9/0493b1d6dfba5bc057d4e727447320afc0c13e11.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor - 1 \qquad (1)\]" width="327" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/8/0685b2bcbefc4c6b6d84068e23cdde1629f95242.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor \qquad (2)\]" width="330" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/c/c4c1d07e2591aa2072dea43084ed74e3f6b0c2a4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lfloor \frac{998}{n} \right\rfloor + 1 = \left\lfloor \frac{999}{n} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{n} \right\rfloor \qquad (3)\]" width="298" height="43" />
