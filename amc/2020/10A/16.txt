<p>A point is chosen at random within the square in the coordinate plane whose vertices are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/1/991b6524d35671aebeadd858e4882f22f54f8be3.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0), (2020, 0), (2020, 2020),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="223" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/2/852ebc1cfa640b88428b37a2020e2e13962cf723.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 2020)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" />. The probability that the point is within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> units of a lattice point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/8/218c6b3e76211f744168e77b8ad9822ccce27d1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" />. (A point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> is a lattice point if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are both integers.) What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> to the nearest tenth<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/8/4a8960d40d67144344fa86ca431cc1d6491690c5.png" class="latex" alt="$?$" width="8" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/d/4adad9ba95bc6136403bc3a22d95bfcb835c517d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 0.3 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 0.4 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 0.5 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 0.6 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 0.7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="424" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Diagram">Diagram</span></h4>

</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h4>
<p>We consider an individual one-by-one block.
</p><p>If we draw a quarter of a circle from each corner (where the lattice points are located), each with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, the area covered by the circles should be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192b4a258ee998961a99523fd0f0a029f9ea194a.png" class="latex" alt="$0.5$" width="22" height="12" />. Because of this, and the fact that there are four circles, we write
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/b/cbb35441d138812d3ccf626d960126c5eca570b7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4 \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \pi d^2 = \frac{1}{2}\]" width="111" height="37" />
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/f/c5fe8ff3aef63f775d375f4948c8559be3b7df24.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="71" height="41" />, where with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec03f16c14aafa2ba31045b9124c26fddbf8859.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi \approx 3$" width="44" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/b/a8b9e808ca68d334fc501f281d824dbfd9fd884d.png" class="latex" alt="$d \approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} \approx \dfrac{1}{2.5} = \dfrac{10}{25} = \dfrac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="199" height="43" />, and from here, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/c/bcc5a8fe0f21aed42f99fe5e2837f2daefd33fa1.png" class="latex" alt="$d \approx 0.4 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0.4}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="179" height="28" /> 
</p><p>~Crypthes
</p><p>~ Minor Edits by BakedPotato66
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/36267c5f4ee67ae71ac26988998233a17818f69b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{Note:}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="45" height="12" /> To be more rigorous, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43c30e47265a4f054766e8145efaca9a3f8e5bc.png" class="latex" alt="$d&lt;0.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" /> since if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/1/801be21ba553507a20e5dba333bb8dc9febb36f6.png" class="latex" alt="$d\geq0.5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="56" height="15" /> then clearly the probability is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. This would make sure the above solution works, as if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/1/801be21ba553507a20e5dba333bb8dc9febb36f6.png" class="latex" alt="$d\geq0.5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="56" height="15" /> there is overlap with the quartercircles. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/5/8b5d72d1cc7f823de9c657979bd8570941acccf3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{- Emathmaster}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="125" height="12" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>As in the previous solution, we obtain the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94ba454e7f55306586810bd6ccd566430cf0f6ea.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \pi d^2 = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="112" height="37" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d0da1037a72c0cd8b92aa193684a3da036a031f.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi d^2 = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2ca003a7da0de4994b4733e203b74ff52d42553.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi$" width="10" height="8" /> is slightly more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/1/3c11014b80c74e6ecbdf01fa2ae54f96096a3ad8.png" class="latex" alt="$d^2$" width="15" height="15" /> is slightly less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/0/1a04ef40d07fdca5ad1975ee4d47273c69145533.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{0.5}{3} = 0.1\bar{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="83" height="37" />. We notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/3/e03825b8776f421ca639b01f95fe2604789a2857.png" class="latex" alt="$0.1\bar{6}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="15" /> is slightly more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b189c6613f1f4367a330b065178883cd9a27330.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4^2 = 0.16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is roughly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/a/c2a7c4fa59124abfdb106a12f68993212f47ed4c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0.4}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" /> ~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(Estimating)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28Estimating.29">Solution 3 (Estimating)</span></h3>
<p>As above, we find that we need to estimate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/f/c5fe8ff3aef63f775d375f4948c8559be3b7df24.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="71" height="41" />. 
</p><p>Note that we can approximate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76c3ed35ca37825d15a0dfcef5210068ee0fe232.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi \approx 6.28318 \approx 6.25$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="159" height="13" /> and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/d/98de859ffcf1b9da0765bf3c4aeccc7ab939798a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="37" height="41" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/f/e5fb783ac8453ad6c3f6de6e4d09bf2663436e3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{6.25}}=\frac{1}{2.5}=0.4$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="169" height="41" />.
</p><p>And so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/9/be9a6e814c089b0f1a9635a1554bffe7954df101.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0.4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="69" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Silverdragon
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_(Estimating_but_a_bit_different)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.28Estimating_but_a_bit_different.29">Solution 4 (Estimating but a bit different)</span></h3>
<p>We only need to figure out the probability for a unit square, as it will scale up to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/c/0bc367de7a579de279a76a0c35ce6026eb648c93.png" class="latex" alt="$2020\times 2020$" width="94" height="12" /> square. Since we want to find the probability that a point inside a unit square that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> units away from a lattice point (a corner of the square) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, we can find which answer will come the closest to covering <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f960094315d60883495f9b74148e17487ee9584.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the area. 
</p><p>Since the closest is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/2/532f79402cd2d051c7bde5d1c1a6ecce07e3bc2b.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="13" /> which turns out to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33f8ba7f9d39083da5a8a6f5f109c6dc5812fb3b.png" class="latex" alt="$(0.4)^2\times \pi = 0.16 \times \pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="167" height="19" /> which is about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/538d45000cfaf666f5331465c624f56bdd76c881.png" class="latex" alt="$0.502$" width="40" height="12" />, we find that the answer rounded to the nearest tenth is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/2/532f79402cd2d051c7bde5d1c1a6ecce07e3bc2b.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />.
</p><p>~RuiyangWu
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_5_(Estimating_but_differently_again)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_.28Estimating_but_differently_again.29">Solution 5 (Estimating but differently again)</span></h3>
<p>As per the above diagram, realize that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f5118ffbeb5832ae01e980adf562b775f0e1695.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi d^2 = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/9/cd98df041224ee2c9500706f048195e5f02ece38.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{2})(\sqrt{\pi})}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="114" height="43" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/2/e92f24663c554eb5ee77a5b7b329d01ce4268cf1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4 = \frac{7}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0529202bae221e2a886dbd92ecd50e87f90452.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="26" height="18" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/f/86fbd1e334b15413571578a3e743a26ab4f29efb.png" class="latex" alt="$1.7$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17eb283a2aa80592f03fa76756f961cf2ddf7b79.png" class="latex" alt="$1.8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/d/74d195d428502c9dd67529777397887a5bc5bf0f.png" class="latex" alt="$((1.7)^2 = 2.89$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/0/81077e4568e3195f281060c4b3036cb493c7e947.png" class="latex" alt="$(1.8)^2 = 3.24)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="19" />, so we can say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/b/1dbf8503ed4b506003c1d384d67a0e59fce0a18a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\pi} \approx 1.75 = \frac{7}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="118" height="37" />.
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/aba6bb6e162e95690cafe6b8a796c9c130777ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$d \approx \frac{1}{(\frac{7}{5})(\frac{7}{4})} = \frac{1}{\frac{49}{20}} = \frac{20}{49}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="177" height="44" />. This is slightly above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/9/be9a6e814c089b0f1a9635a1554bffe7954df101.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0.4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="69" height="28" />, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/0/37011939d82ee2651072470a3f5c0e7ce25d8e58.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{20}{49} \approx \frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="58" height="38" />.
</p><p>-Solution by Joeya
