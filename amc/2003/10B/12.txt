<p>Al, Betty, and Clare split <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/e/0be1b81812ebaf299adc3886e7c95f8858ea42e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 1000$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="44" height="14" /> among them to be invested in different ways. Each begins with a different amount. At the end of one year, they have a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/9/ff927f1ca468888056b00db6fef84c4cc490a26c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 1500$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="44" height="14" /> dollars. Betty and Clare have both doubled their money, whereas Al has managed to lose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc6a1da7f18069a90481296d18af7ca979fe3854.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="35" height="14" /> dollars. What was Al's original portion?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758892c510bd28c51a2d1b53b3251efd85dd09b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \textdollar 250 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \textdollar 350 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \textdollar 400 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \textdollar 450 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \textdollar 500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For this problem, we will have to write a three-variable equation, but not necessarily solve it. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> represent the original portions of Al, Betty, and Clare, respectively. At the end of one year, they each have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/3/a432a824ee1efc54c195ebd8734cdd369d6c3783.png" class="latex" alt="$a-100, 2b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="88" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f6072f36e4577a20084d5e518bf7e2f2068ab3d.png" class="latex" alt="$2c$" width="17" height="12" />. From this, we can write two equations, marked by (1) and (2). 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/758a113c4680a0bbc3ce28fefd61a2e47a8db399.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a+b+c=1000\]" width="128" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/b/47b435cba1e52e1776a9001017791304b42638e4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(1). \text{ }2a+2b+2c=2000\]" width="189" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae8ff2820c248dd9515df1709037eccc86be5e26.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a-100+2b+2c=1500\]" width="196" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/6/126041f6e69ade4b098b1a4a752abd8081da1690.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(2). \text{ }a+2b+2c=1600\]" width="179" height="18" />
</p><p>(Equations (1) and (2) are derived from each equation above them.)
</p><p>Since all we need to find is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/8/7c8acfd7d5ee559262593701b8dbd02e43ad96e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> subtract the second equation from the first equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/405dc9bb3ae58c5907d95cbe669a3209a9a2fbf2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=400.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="64" height="13" />
</p><p>Al's original portion was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76cfdb7c2ebef66ecb7468d39fd96634743ebaf5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \textdollar 400}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="82" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Suppose the total amount of money Betty and Clare has is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64ffaf285a405a29915217084cbf12af74f9a2f1.png" class="latex" alt="$1000-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="13" /> and Al has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> dollars. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a6d85c00f166fbc76edb13159076eacf9d8d9a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-100)+2(1000-x)=1500$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="251" height="20" />, so Al has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76cfdb7c2ebef66ecb7468d39fd96634743ebaf5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \textdollar 400}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="82" height="29" /> dollars.
</p><p>~Mathkiddie
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Suppose if Al had not lost <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/751f8354c64df7687207c0df786c41cc883ab3cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="35" height="14" />. The total amount would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec56278960ccdf32f5cb6aab2153c03119dafd3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 1600$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="16" />. As he has not gained any amount. So, Betty and Clare have collectively gained <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc2aab121b3f6d205cb10dd2d5da035a83e7f69.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 600$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="38" height="16" /> and as they have doubled their collective fortune,
they must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc2aab121b3f6d205cb10dd2d5da035a83e7f69.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 600$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="38" height="16" /> with them at the beginning. This leaves <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76cfdb7c2ebef66ecb7468d39fd96634743ebaf5.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ \textdollar 400}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="82" height="29" /> for Al.
~Anshulb
</p>
