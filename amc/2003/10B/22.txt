<p>A clock chimes once at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes past each hour and chimes on the hour according to the hour. For example, at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/f/56f3ba6222e1ce7182d3b032fa31b97c32307e12.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \text{PM}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="12" /> there is one chime and at noon and midnight there are twelve chimes. Starting at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/7/4d770fb42d8dd44e091a511ecc7eff805ffdd6b7.png" class="latex" alt="$11:15 \text{AM}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da6d2fd138f3a091012c56f54963985f899cc62.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{February 26, 2003},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="135" height="15" /> on what date will the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/2/d92bd23848268583d05014ad003a6aa7e40481d0.png" class="latex" alt="$2003^{\text{rd}}$" width="48" height="15" /> chime occur?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/8/008a983a8ce21eba32039a1de33f511321803548.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \text{March 8} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \text{March 9} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \text{March 10} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \text{March 20} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \text{March 21}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="631" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>First, find how many chimes will have already happened before midnight (the beginning of the day) of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/0/dc0ab7c246e9d01baccdd38c5882b5d55e107ec6.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{February 27, 2003}.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="134" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> half-hours have passed, and the number of chimes according to the hour is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33d4b43536e8237c758552fb3edfcfff8cd18d3.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots+12.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="159" height="14" /> The total number of chimes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/0/d20f293d84173554e689cfe8462096b964769879.png" class="latex" alt="$13+78=91.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="15" />
</p><p>Every day, there will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> half-hours and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bb32e4e31d3a26f3852e3b7310144d46813390a.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1+2+3+\cdots+12)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" /> chimes according to the arrow, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/4/27491f9bec8a062b1e77d62dd39df7875e4fce17.png" class="latex" alt="$24+156=180$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="118" height="14" /> total chimes.
</p><p>On <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/5/5057dafc5b520d553ccfaaa38437f82a941e5c98.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{February 26},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> the number of chimes that still need to occur is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/c/a2c2f656b40e24dd4dfce5ccde5417312ed8940a.png" class="latex" alt="$2003-91=1912.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="140" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea1382fb93badd9010179511157dfa3cb8d33c4.png" class="latex" alt="$1912 \div 180=10 \text{R}112.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="171" height="13" /> Rounding up, it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> days past <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/5/5057dafc5b520d553ccfaaa38437f82a941e5c98.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{February 26},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/b/e5b63299709c1a913a1ebe7fa50d83e323370b8d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) \ } \text{March 9}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="113" height="28" />
</p>
