<p>The first four terms in an arithmetic sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/3/643e1765342cf3807fd7d130ee679b71ecd4789b.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />, in that order. What is the fifth term? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/1/c014255fe627d7cc95083f6bb6a360f3f95519b6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ -\frac{15}{8}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ -\frac{6}{5}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 0\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{27}{20}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{123}{40}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="444" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The difference between consecutive terms is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/2/962064d8b7ad50de4d4ed4a7e416d4735aa33af3.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)-(x+y)=-2y.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="18" /> Therefore we can also express the third and fourth terms as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/d/90dc5734a1f1634f502f1edef4c58a32a729f5a9.png" class="latex" alt="$x-3y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d7fa576a222beed25273e0d50392e2f1ddfc396.png" class="latex" alt="$x-5y.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="16" /> Then we can set them equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" /> because they are the same thing.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/2/c82bd1e7f2934c3e57e62eb6a3358bf2540281c4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} xy&amp;=x-3y\\ xy-x&amp;=-3y\\ x(y-1)&amp;=-3y\\ x&amp;=\frac{-3y}{y-1} \end{align*}" width="139" height="115" />
</p><p>Substitute into our other equation.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/4/7447ff4042273b628b683c0be7a6adb782094cc4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{x}{y}=x-5y\]" width="87" height="36" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/f/eafd224e1b0406671c0ea78a89f70250ab4d9598.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{-3}{y-1}=\frac{-3y}{y-1}-5y\]" width="152" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/2/582e642d7ac3fe394341c104bb678404cf1937d6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-3=-3y-5y(y-1)\]" width="173" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fc65b027cacf27699496e8f7a46167eb301ea72.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0=5y^2-2y-3\]" width="130" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/1/2715b828ebf0e900ba0d1a4a4409393b2d5c9683.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0=(5y+3)(y-1)\]" width="150" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/f/2bf85034e5c754150e8e99435ec811627279e560.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[y=-\frac35, 1\]" width="76" height="37" />
</p><p>But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> cannot be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> because then the first term would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/cccb103143393c0119f5ab61d6d53a852ce70fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1$" width="40" height="13" /> and the second term <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4baf6d4b1cfa69b68b3204b5e2eba0c5755fe20.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> while the last two terms would be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/3/fc3be04db73828946870d3ccc6d122395a4bacf6.png" class="latex" alt="$x.$" width="14" height="8" /> Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/7/e77c6b44e7fd1f17c8fca2b615714cfe05fe26c1.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-\frac35.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" /> Substituting the value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> into any of the equations, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a9522918d9a7fea8f82c1f05a6e83e590261f9d.png" class="latex" alt="$x=-\frac98.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="65" height="37" /> Finally,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/e/55e30872e72c0aa052d66c068d2af833a675a508.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{x}{y}-2y=\frac{9\cdot 5}{8\cdot 3}+\frac{6}{5}=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{123}{40}}\]" width="248" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Because this is an arithmetic sequence, we conclude from the first two terms that the common difference is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6efdf74a0d002b82228e7dd182b0b39d2985cf9.png" class="latex" alt="$-2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="15" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/a/35a522134032a54e9bb0c4c01eab81fe500ec100.png" class="latex" alt="$xy = x-3y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="97" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dadc64f272335ec822777c99ec2a8e424b450da2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}=x-5y$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="89" height="36" />. If we multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />, we see: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/7/f97f219818d31474ba3653c55f1322cb50956c7a.png" class="latex" alt="$xy\cdot\frac{x}{y} = (x-3y)(x-5y) = x^2 - 8xy + 15y^2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="355" height="38" />
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/645b6d363cfdb5bc42798504fce81dc25c05fc08.png" class="latex" alt="$xy\cdot\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="49" height="38" />, by basic multiplication, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />, we have 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/8/9e8d0d1990f89f1dfa521424151d17de24eff867.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = x^2 - 8xy + 15y^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/3/e63a350c9d52b107928b2b21d346f9b92d659a97.png" class="latex" alt="$8xy = 15y^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/c/6ec72ef74f5175074cb2f4b4583b61a872b29d9b.png" class="latex" alt="$8x = 15y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/48521e590d51efcbedfacc39e471c34866b393dc.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{15y}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />
</p><p>Now that we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we substitute in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/7/68783f8ea6614c5e6d7012edc72d898e6c9b52b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15y}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="34" height="38" /> in for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" /> (the fourth term). This leaves us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/b/84b014dab307414317aa503091817facf665085a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{15y}{8}}{y} = \frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="77" height="46" />. 
</p><p>Recall that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" /> can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/5/8b5e064a59bf8781bd05292cfd91942ec7f9f03f.png" class="latex" alt="$x - 5y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="16" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d8813daef179dc5b9fe5e08b730f659d7fc8c7e.png" class="latex" alt="$x - 5y = \frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="100" height="38" />. Substitute in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c6c840d3b2f21ec688bca3ba8850a8c77990b48.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> in for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and we see:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/746c736a35d512fe97f5003f0701d8cb81d1c5a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15y}{8} - 5y = \frac{15}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="121" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/9/e490cc81cde00aa79992c32ac94994eb42d0b518.png" class="latex" alt="$15y - 40y = 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="17" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/b/11b3556561ad475a3824e1fff34628837185b2ad.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{15}{-25} = \frac{-3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="123" height="40" />
</p><p>Aha! This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26cab3f0704de3232f601b03d02a45d46103c172.png" class="latex" alt="$2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" />, the common difference, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/5/b959727b797e8351e8a6b44525904a8d818e04b2.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot y = 2\cdot \frac{-3}{5} = \frac{-6}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="159" height="38" />. Now, all we need to do is find the fifth term, which is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/6/e968cee1b694963fd2aa6a2680a5ca1ebd04120d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}-2y$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="54" height="36" />. We can substitute known values to solve: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/7/1f70f157b3e7dca95dc687fe3375bda2a21d4a39.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}-2y = \frac{15}{8} - \frac{-6}{5} = \frac{15}{8} + \frac{6}{5} = \frac{75 + 48}{40} = \boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{123}{40}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="421" height="49" />.
</p><p>~SXWang
</p>
