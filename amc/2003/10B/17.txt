<p>An ice cream cone consists of a sphere of vanilla ice cream and a right circular cone that has the same diameter as the sphere. If the ice cream melts, it will exactly fill the cone. Assume that the melted ice cream occupies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/2/e126f0d41c904d37ba2c332347a24b75d4ac3066.png" class="latex" alt="$75\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the volume of the frozen ice cream. What is the ratio of the cone's height to its radius? (Note: a cone with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/9/5b99c996a0b4e8d903e88f244d1e592d246099e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^2 h / 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="19" /> and a sphere with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/6/3067d8d93f0faeefd7efa96475f45f95de630225.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \pi r^3 / 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="19" />.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cad3f9b4c9a6e16461e11699a801c6ef3742b587.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2:1 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 3:1 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 4:1 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 16:3 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 6:1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="482" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the radius of both the cone and the sphere of ice cream, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> be the height of the cone. The cone can hold a maximum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/0/ba0770ebce80c7a3ecf73a49db518dfed6b0111f.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^2h/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="19" /> melted ice cream. The volume of the frozen ice cream is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bfba5ef938691d531f54e356f6dd3b795f1f090.png" class="latex" alt="$4\pi r^3/3.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="19" /> The volume of the melted ice cream is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b582c9d24f700d771f3612571cf29dbe7503fd25.png" class="latex" alt="$75 \%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of that, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/5/e755b17489f1bd0e5682e766942c6100be7eeef0.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^3.$" width="30" height="15" /> Since the melted ice cream fills the cone exactly,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1935127b747f9503985ba20a2152ebe193c4714a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}\pi r^3&amp;=\pi r^2h/3\\ r&amp;=h/3\\ 3&amp;=h/r\end{align*}" width="106" height="69" />
</p><p>Then the ratio of the cone's height to its radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/4/484694c50807744c1a528927a4409451fe41537f.png" class="latex" alt="$h:r=\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 3:1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="137" height="28" />
</p>
