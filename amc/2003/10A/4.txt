<p>It takes Anna <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes to walk uphill <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> km from her home to school, but it takes her only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes to walk from school to her home along the same route. What is her average speed, in km/hr, for the round trip? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/9/ec9f28dbd021e69ad5cf0c95a8964a45f39ac638.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 3\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 3.125\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 3.5\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 4\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 4.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="402" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since she walked <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> km to school and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> km back home, her total distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/3/cf30c7f8ce33da1bccad605bf72d60949211ceda.png" class="latex" alt="$1+1=2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="13" /> km. 
</p><p>Since she spent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes walking to school and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes walking back home, her total time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/20454386010a9cd188603873acbc5ef81df24c83.png" class="latex" alt="$30+10=40$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> minutes = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a65695526ddf9994a7ea435f39dac1db1c06855b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{60}=\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="37" /> hours. 
</p><p>Therefore her average speed in km/hr is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a0ecf887c3edafdac55c8fb37af751476ac0c80.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{\frac{2}{3}}=\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 3}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="93" height="43" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The average speed of two speeds that travel the same distance is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Harmonic_mean" title="Harmonic mean">harmonic mean</a> of the speeds, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/6/916c86eccb8dd850ef8ac5e5b7e084cc53b88363.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2}{\dfrac{1}{x}+\dfrac{1}{y}}=\dfrac{2xy}{x+y}$" style="vertical-align: -38px" width="123" height="62" /> (for speeds <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />). Mary's speed going to school is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10ef3470bdb7f56dab7814d655b7e3539bd650d0.png" class="latex" alt="$2\,\text{km/hr}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="17" />, and her speed coming back is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/8/92895fc4b370a52b4bfd1c5b5a18f3eb7fc83b62.png" class="latex" alt="$6\,\text{km/hr}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="17" />. Plugging the numbers in, we get that the average speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51d191b5a32f5d609060e21db62942b34ccfb1dc.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2\times 6\times 2}{6+2}=\dfrac{24}{8}=\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ 3}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="200" height="39" />.
</p>
