<p>Simplify 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/2/cf2756b7e9bb76b2adf76f1709cc765535b27602.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="112" height="53" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/1/2e12822d4d418ef37b7ca03cff39d5b6e8123508.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \sqrt{x}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \sqrt[3]{x^{2}}\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \sqrt[27]{x^{2}}\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \sqrt[54]{x}\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \sqrt[81]{x^{80}}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="473" height="22" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/5/5b532d915bdc855d96cfa266d7d3bb9a168fd803.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}=\sqrt[3]{(\sqrt{x})^{2}\cdot\sqrt{x}}=\sqrt[3]{(\sqrt{x})^{3}}=\sqrt{x}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="322" height="33" />. 
</p><p>Therefore: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/6/7a6a1831eb969273f9c00ce0ca3ba2e52d2b5c72.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}}}=\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}}=\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}= \sqrt{x} \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\ \sqrt{x}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="446" height="53" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/9/279577b98d2f0150723f2f0515956c7644b64f25.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}} = \sqrt[3]{x\cdot(x^\frac{1}{2})} = \sqrt[3]{x^\frac{3}{2}} = x^\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="261" height="33" /> We plug this into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/2/cf2756b7e9bb76b2adf76f1709cc765535b27602.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt{x}}}}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="112" height="53" /> and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/a/06a99fffd0d716670d45d3670d720ce5a2759b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\sqrt[3]{x\cdot(x^\frac{1}{2})}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="106" height="43" />. Again, we substitute and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/1/1b1adbd26fb38c3122623b411b4ac95ec22a10d0.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt[3]{x\cdot(x^\frac{1}{2})}$" style="vertical-align: -9px" width="77" height="32" />. We substitute one more time and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/7/1776fe56fd8aca62634dae2e4c3810f6efc04159.png" class="latex" alt="$x^\frac{1}{2} = \boxed{(A) \sqrt{x}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="110" height="31" />.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(Lame??)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28Lame.3F.3F.29">Solution 3 (Lame??)</span></h3>
<p>WLOG, plug in some random square number like <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and the output you get after simplifying the expression will always be the square root of the number, so the answer is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/1/551a3120d6f0aaf730bc5c423202ae8e0b5a6353.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A) \sqrt{x}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="33" />.
</p><p>~Darth_Cadet
</p>
