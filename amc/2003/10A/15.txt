<p>What is the probability that an integer in the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/9/dd94689d38e94553d4ed2fdb0d1b85da9ce5d61b.png" class="latex" alt="$\{1,2,3,...,100\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/4/bf4a2208f63dfb97f706b17e2680bd0630b84ebc.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{1}{6}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ }  \frac{33}{100}\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ }  \frac{17}{50}\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ }  \frac{1}{2}\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ }  \frac{18}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="397" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> integers in the set. 
</p><p>Since every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbdd606f9a293cef748b15755d8d0991765f38bb.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{\text{nd}}$" width="23" height="15" /> integer is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/7/be76fa1b0dcf282f229fba4056dbf169542e62b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\frac{100}{2}\rfloor=50$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" /> integers divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in the set. 
</p><p>To be divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, a number must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5a1e48d6bc4232597bddbe30c1d721fe9334b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)=6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac214aa69544fa61cb17bcac19266dd563c93ece.png" class="latex" alt="$6^{\text{th}}$" width="21" height="15" /> integer is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a192a1859e92af1c607c4b733d27ce20875c49e9.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\frac{100}{6}\rfloor=16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" /> integers divisible by both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> in the set. 
</p><p>So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/9/889f078f96691d70d9db7df00e70fe04a3475798.png" class="latex" alt="$50-16=34$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="101" height="13" /> integers in this set that are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and not divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, the desired probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/f/83f56d6ea734a71f64a2663f9e36fa45d7c22075.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{34}{100}=\frac{17}{50}\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{17}{50}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="172" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution_by_WhyMath">Video Solution by WhyMath</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/UfzS5griBic">https://youtu.be/UfzS5griBic</a>
</p><p>~savannahsolver
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span></h2>
<p>Due to the wording of the question, it may be taken as "Find the probability that an integer in said set is divisible by 2 and not 3 EXISTS". One example would be 2, which is not a multiple of 3, thus the probability is 1. But because 1 is not an option, we can assume that it was not meant like that.
</p>
