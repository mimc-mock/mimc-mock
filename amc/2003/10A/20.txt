<p>A base-10 three digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is selected at random. Which of the following is closest to the probability that the base-9 representation and the base-11 representation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> are both three-digit numerals? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/8/2283f4ca9bb17750a79a32972bebeffda473d96e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 0.3\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 0.4\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 0.5\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 0.6\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 0.7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="413" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If we explore a similar problem:
Which positive intergers have 3 digits in base 10? 
The smallest one ranges from 100-999, or 10^2 --&gt; 10^3-1
Therefore, 
The smallest base-11 number that has 3 digits in base-10 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/c/0cced4e83845a4c7dd49d7e40578e3095ed7b069.png" class="latex" alt="$100_{11}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="39" height="15" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50deb257710f1417912f03b5fe07267b55bc3a2b.png" class="latex" alt="$121_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="39" height="14" />. because 11^2 
</p><p>The largest number in base-9 that has 3 digits in base-10 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c535f319ccd21db2aba8756e3a9076909d01319.png" class="latex" alt="$8\cdot9^2+8\cdot9^1+8\cdot9^0=888_{9}=728_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="283" height="17" />
Alternatively, you can do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/e/3ae4b72d5f36ec942c0e3a7236dc2c1dab02f7ec.png" class="latex" alt="$9^3-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="15" /> 
</p><p>Hence, all numbers that will have 3 digits in base-9, 10, and 11 will be between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/4/d547712c1dbcaa2d14f9df34f3d4530cfe9358e5.png" class="latex" alt="$728_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="39" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50deb257710f1417912f03b5fe07267b55bc3a2b.png" class="latex" alt="$121_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="39" height="14" />, thus the total amount of numbers that will have 3 digits in base-9, 10, and 11 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/746adb6e4214a9c87b954fbe01fab4b2444ae843.png" class="latex" alt="$728-121+1=608$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="159" height="14" />
</p><p>There are 900 possible 3 digit numbers in base 10, because it is 9 possibilities for the hundreds digit, 10 for the tens digit, and 10 fo the units digits, so its 9x10x10 which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/5/b25c24f02fac1cb00970c2be385f7f5cfb38c4c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{608}{900}\approx .675 \approx \boxed{0.7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="147" height="37" />
</p><p>~CharmaineMa07292010
</p>
