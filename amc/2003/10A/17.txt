<p>The number of inches in the perimeter of an equilateral triangle equals the number of square inches in the area of  its circumscribed circle. What is the radius, in inches, of the circle? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/a/7aaab18006c1840073707efe926150e89d0f64a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{\pi}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ }  \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{\pi}\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \sqrt{3}\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{6}{\pi}\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } \sqrt{3}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="445" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be the length of a side of the equilateral triangle and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> be the radius of the circle. 
</p><p>In a circle with a radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, the side of an inscribed equilateral triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/f/3bfca334c3f61ae13cdcb7160ac48e7916ca895f.png" class="latex" alt="$r\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. 
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/8/ab8d7351898b676de5d68280aeeaa6de2342f726.png" class="latex" alt="$s=r\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />. 
</p><p>The perimeter of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174e2b53b848cd9922b8b2010a1c15534df93c96.png" class="latex" alt="$3s=3r\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="18" />
</p><p>The area of the circle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/e/88e1ac9b59dc367092dad4ca13e35ab3442551ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^{2}$" width="25" height="15" />
</p><p>So: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/3/0339350f95227b101706e9fae6fca0b0c3aee966.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r^{2} = 3r\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="94" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/0/fc0ca079806c1c7a50dbc1c061e2b69bf84ccb56.png" class="latex" alt="$\pi r=3\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/f/d2f3c0f440633ed0c7cdabffd3c2d470bd7e55ec.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{\pi} \Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{\pi}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="180" height="53" />
</p>
