<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>When a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-digit number is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> digits become the quotient, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, and the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> digits become the remainder, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> can be any integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> inclusive, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> can be any integer from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> inclusive. 
</p><p>For each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/e/eaed782d24999db75d0f9c1135c53df2692d99ce.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot10\cdot10=900$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="122" height="13" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, there are at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/6/e060b19f919ceeb7dc0093a9199fa9df6ab5a046.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{100}{11} \right\rfloor = 9$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="84" height="43" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce04a7d147d142fb4def4f61f8c4596eb67e1c5.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Since there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> "extra" possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> that is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/c/edc20bccf186adffa7225f53d842ad2a27c63266.png" class="latex" alt="$0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />, each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/9/0597aea89941058d9acf9bf570b94bf66ec2a4b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{900}{11} \right\rfloor = 81$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="93" height="43" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> that are congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/c/edc20bccf186adffa7225f53d842ad2a27c63266.png" class="latex" alt="$0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> more possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce04a7d147d142fb4def4f61f8c4596eb67e1c5.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />. Another way to think about it is the number of possible values of q when r, the remainder, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. In this case, q itself has to be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/5/8759cd7deb078f52a5fe1d3d905f781019df609e.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lfloor \frac{999}{11} = 90 \right\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="96" height="45" />. Then we'll need to subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> since we only want multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/b/f9bc3dbd34e70a16734692ca46f9e28669aaee6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(90-9=81)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="108" height="20" />
</p><p>Therefore, the number of possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce04a7d147d142fb4def4f61f8c4596eb67e1c5.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/a/cdaf2f5fd21fb2b7c325970006d1d45539b753ec.png" class="latex" alt="$900\cdot9+81\cdot1=8181 \Rightarrow B$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="215" height="14" />.
</p><p>~ Minor Edit by PlainOldNumberTheory

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02ddb39d8380d59b3013c8af79b25ba99b1871cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{abcde}$" width="43" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> are digits. Therefore,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/3/2e38d9b3bca4da426cd77315cb4e43f580c6df85.png" class="latex" alt="$q=\overline{abc}=100a+10b+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="198" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/e/0fe77d6e2461b1e187796edba9facf5ef1f5678b.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\overline{de}=10d+e$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="17" />
</p><p>We now take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/6514ccf74189ccac2062ed24ad3ab12444555779.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r\bmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/3/09349b812be49b42195e008864affe4c51da92fe.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r=100a+10b+c+10d+e\equiv a-b+c-d+e\equiv 0\bmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="511" height="16" />
</p><p>The divisor trick for 11 is as follows:
</p><p>"Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/b/e4b96d788a2bc687ff8ce53b7a0e1882f6374fcb.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\overline{a_1a_2a_3\cdots a_x}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="131" height="13" /> be an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> digit integer. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/7/3579fbe8fc6d8600ea5d0dddeee3c75d8bcbd515.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1-a_2+a_3-\cdots +(-1)^{x-1} a_x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="19" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is also divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />."
</p><p>Therefore, the five digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is divisible by 11. The 5-digit multiples of 11 range from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/f/bff3c07a4fe4ad8ac52d6159625282d9d5c63579.png" class="latex" alt="$910\cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/d/ced81c5fdaa81632a34c3e7a07c40464c3c4d670.png" class="latex" alt="$9090\cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/f/9ef486f3edc51a41a8db0adbdaa92db90523564c.png" class="latex" alt="$8181\Rightarrow \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> divisors of 11 between those inclusive.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h4>
<p>The part labeled "divisor trick" actually follows from the same observation we made in the previous step: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a0b9beef10fb793dd6f40f7ed03fbff888a7650.png" class="latex" alt="$10\equiv (-1)\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="159" height="18" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/7/6574bdf20467efe1ac81420f22a4d23433038381.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2k}\equiv 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/f/26f3659a3f1f07efffbf2c5f1cb63c0274b1bb98.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2k+1}\equiv (-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="20" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. 
For a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/c/dac6fd23d243ac88de33fa701dd723ecbb157031.png" class="latex" alt="$5-$" width="22" height="12" />digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02ddb39d8380d59b3013c8af79b25ba99b1871cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{abcde}$" width="43" height="16" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/c/cac6bec139bcb64fab8f2beacef214364821fd7e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{abcde}\equiv a\cdot 1 + b\cdot(-1) + c\cdot 1 + d\cdot(-1) + e\cdot 1 = a-b+c-d+e$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="523" height="20" />, as claimed.
</p><p>Also note that in the "divisor trick" we actually want to assign the signs backwards - if we make sure that the last sign is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cf26be132ef93923e082ee4153b2cb0ef44a50.png" class="latex" alt="$+$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="12" />, the result will have the same remainder modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> as the original number.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> is a quotient and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is a remainder when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. So we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/6/026a3ece9998d3be7c8a17d296abe0bc380aa68e.png" class="latex" alt="$n=100q+r$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" />. Since we are counting choices where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/a/31a6cd957da325a37d10bbf4c8078775c574306d.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="14" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/4/e74f309a3da514a99447f1f26ff7b9522606d090.png" class="latex" alt="$n=99q+q+r=99q+11k$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="225" height="16" /> for some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the sum of two multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and would thus itself be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Then we can count all the five digit multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> as in Solution 2. (This solution is essentally the same as Solution 2, but it does not necessarily involve mods and so could potentially be faster.)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Defining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> in terms of floor functions and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> would yield: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/6/a96418b2292892965f903c259b84765b69991d75.png" class="latex" alt="$q=\left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="85" height="35" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/0/b9007842bf2041ee11c96c22978d9239414ac49c.png" class="latex" alt="$r=n-100 \left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="148" height="35" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce04a7d147d142fb4def4f61f8c4596eb67e1c5.png" class="latex" alt="$q+r \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/6854c49d2fa6da2019db98ed34b120d7d576f42b.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor + n-100 \left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="302" height="35" />. Simplifying gets us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/1/c913a984b1ad16bcff049804f1d45fb17dd94c75.png" class="latex" alt="$n-99 \left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor\equiv 0\pmod{11} \rightarrow n \equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="375" height="35" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/8/af8445eee432c51e45894519478817be9985c80d.png" class="latex" alt="$99 \left \lfloor \frac{n}{100} \right \rfloor\equiv 0\pmod{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="188" height="35" /> is always true, since floor function always yields an integer, and 99 is divisble by 11 w/o any remainder). After we come to this conclusion, it becomes easy to solve the rest of the problem (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1bd3724acbd1afdf11ed15ba6666047c7374c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lfloor \frac{n}{99999} \right \rfloor - \left \lfloor \frac{n}{10000} \right \rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="160" height="35" />). 
~hw21
</p>
