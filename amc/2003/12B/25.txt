<p>Three points are chosen randomly and independently on a circle. What is the probability that all three pairwise distance between the points are less than the radius of the circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/9/4196598fdb3512f21510e199ee0eccf448b9a351.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \dfrac{1}{36} \qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \dfrac{1}{24} \qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \dfrac{1}{18} \qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \dfrac{1}{12} \qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \dfrac{1}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="397" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The first point is placed anywhere on the circle, because it doesn't matter where it is chosen.
</p><p>The next point must lie within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees of arc on either side, a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> degrees possible, giving a total <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> chance. The last point must lie within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees  of both points. 
</p><p>The minimum area of freedom we have to place the third point is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees arc(if the first two are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees apart), with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/b/43ba19be0e30b99142416ebfbe17670437c8453e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> probability.
The maximum amount of freedom we have to place the third point is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaa4de95cc6371d125d5cf773cdd9f9913dd1d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$120$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> degree arc(if the first two are the same point), with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> probability.  
</p><p>As the second point moves farther away from the first point, up to a maximum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> degrees, the probability changes linearly (every degree it moves, adds one degree to where the third could be).
</p><p>Therefore, we can average probabilities at each end to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />, the average probability we can place the third point based on a varying second point.
</p><p>Therefore the total probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/5/d15aa1b39c30be4aee90b680c963f4c4876f3c81.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times\frac{1}{3}\times\frac{1}{4}=\frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="124" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We will use geometric probability.
</p><p>The first point can be anywhere. Each point must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/2/2d2d0dc818ffa40c824ec87c9ad9c51fa86f458d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" /> or less away from each other.
</p><p>Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the amount of radians away the second point is from the first. We limit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35e10ec8f56c704d87c8b856a08c455e2f91ee3.png" class="latex" alt="$[-\pi, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="52" height="18" />. Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the amount of radians away the third point is from the first. We limit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to be in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/5/b35e10ec8f56c704d87c8b856a08c455e2f91ee3.png" class="latex" alt="$[-\pi, \pi]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="52" height="18" />. Now, we can deduct that: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/164c1dbd7f6ba85f75f4b6d1775aaf669763a5ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|x| \le \frac{\pi}{3},\]" width="62" height="32" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f615f298010fc1b0b0e17c107c3f54c2178291a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|y| \le \frac{\pi}{3},\]" width="61" height="32" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/1/e81400ac61882d564705038c1f162db0f86d2d3a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|x-y| \le \frac{\pi}{3}.\]" width="93" height="32" /> We now begin plotting these on the coordinate grid. 
</p><p>First note that the area of the points that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> can be (ignoring the conditions) is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4ce7f72a7794404c06729cd9760afef958c66ef6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2\pi)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="19" /> (remember what we restricted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to). 
</p><p>Now, we can graph the equations we deduced on the coordinate grid. That should look like this: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/b/d5ba1fd6c5072aa1ef45750d2bb1a01c0d2fac45.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] fill((40, 0)--(40, 40)--(0, 40)--(-40, 0)--(-40, -40)--(0, -40)--cycle, green); draw((-50, 0)--(50, 0)); draw((0, -50)--(0, 50)); draw((-50, -10)--(10, 50),red); draw((-10, -50)--(50, 10),red); draw((-40, 40)--(40, 40),red); draw((-40, -40)--(40, -40),red); draw((40, 40)--(40, -40),red); draw((-40, 40)--(-40, -40),red); label(&quot;$\frac{\pi}{3}$&quot;, (40, 0), SE); label(&quot;$\frac{\pi}{3}$&quot;, (-40, 0), SW); label(&quot;$\frac{\pi}{3}$&quot;, (0, 40), NE); label(&quot;$\frac{\pi}{3}$&quot;, (0, -40), NW); [/asy]" width="248" height="252" />
The area of the shaded region can be calculated in many ways. Eventually, you will find that the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/73118ed4ec298a4d01509bc2387594d659418c8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\pi^2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="39" />. 
</p><p>Thus, the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6acb91e13bf3111027038d355cd355259e455720.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{\pi^2}{3}}{(2\pi)^2} = \frac{1}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="90" height="48" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" />.
</p><p>~superagh
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/a/b8a6df161faa4e3b6fd047c69ac61c6be6aae994.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom32 = 3$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="72" height="55" /> different ways to choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> points out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. The probability that an angle between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> points is smaller than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/b/6ab295f9eb9c03fde394578d5cbec60710ab9eb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac16$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="78" height="39" />. The probability that the third point is inside the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> angle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/b/6ab295f9eb9c03fde394578d5cbec60710ab9eb1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac16$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="78" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/1/ca1b5848558d1b9818957cf4bafbc04723b93f95.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot \frac16 \cdot \frac16 = \boxed{\text{(D)} \frac{1}{12} }$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="147" height="49" />
</p><p>~<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/User:Isabelchen">isabelchen</a>
</p>
