<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Consider the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fc742e57cf5fc67693f8df6262cde7a96b0ef98.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=|x-a|+|x-b|+|x-c|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="255" height="18" />.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/9/db9a42be377d9781da67c3347a3d3a1ddc69e5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$x&lt;a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="10" />, the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31b51deaa1a9e903d120a02141556c3e70e348.png" class="latex" alt="$-3$" width="22" height="12" />.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea200ce05e8129b1169b9e90372236816ee2219a.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;x&lt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" />, the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a92fd4b010b17c5ce65c84909e9a54e986e3f7c4.png" class="latex" alt="$b&lt;x&lt;c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />, the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/a/59ab5c98496a83ed16e5b8ccc07d73beaae93fcb.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="10" />, the slope is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741720281e465fc7461c32d504f6d54c5bcaff7c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=b$" width="42" height="12" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b7a7453263a8fb2e47b318afc336a9db8935621.png" class="latex" alt="$y=|b-a|+|b-b|+|b-c|=c-a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="287" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/0/b70e6301769eaad173a5d094159ec7fee880b649.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,c-a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> is a point on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />. In fact, it is the minimum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> considering the slope of lines to the left and right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/0/b70e6301769eaad173a5d094159ec7fee880b649.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,c-a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />. Thus, graphing this will produce a figure that looks like a cup:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa926b12d0a7c803d530009612b24aae9cbe456c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(100); draw((0,6)--(3,0)); xaxis(0,8.5); yaxis(0,10); real f(real x) { return -3(x-2)+5; } real f2(real x) { return -1(x-2)+5; } real f3(real x) { return 1(x-4)+3; } real f4(real x) { return 3(x-7)+6; } draw(graph(f,0,2)); draw(graph(f2,2,4)); draw(graph(f3,4,7)); draw(graph(f4,7,8.5)); draw((2,-0.25)--(2,0.25)); label(&quot;a&quot;,(2,0),N); draw((4,-0.25)--(4,0.25)); label(&quot;b&quot;,(4,0),N); draw((7,-0.25)--(7,0.25)); label(&quot;c&quot;,(7,0),N); dot((2,5)); label(&quot;P&quot;,(1.9,5.2),E); [/asy]" width="128" height="168" />
From the graph, it is clear that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5daffd02eb1112367cdeee413853763c4a093106.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+y=2003$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" /> have one intersection point if and only if they intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed47ef35959c171399e8f18eed4b517223823c04.png" class="latex" alt="$x=a$" width="44" height="8" />. Since the line where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea200ce05e8129b1169b9e90372236816ee2219a.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;x&lt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" /> has slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />, the positive difference in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-coordinates from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed47ef35959c171399e8f18eed4b517223823c04.png" class="latex" alt="$x=a$" width="44" height="8" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741720281e465fc7461c32d504f6d54c5bcaff7c.png" class="latex" alt="$x=b$" width="42" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/c/02c60c151331e235dc6d9df7ca403b6f0c9af39b.png" class="latex" alt="$b-a$" width="39" height="12" />. Together with the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/0/b70e6301769eaad173a5d094159ec7fee880b649.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,c-a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> is on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/0/02060b20d1316a52b062f9de731f53c458e54b7e.png" class="latex" alt="$P=(a,c-a+b-a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. Since this point is on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed47ef35959c171399e8f18eed4b517223823c04.png" class="latex" alt="$x=a$" width="44" height="8" />, the only intersection point with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5daffd02eb1112367cdeee413853763c4a093106.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+y=2003$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/1/e41a7b74dee93566aa6911bfa3c64c586e9cebc0.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot a+(b+c-2a)=2003 \implies b+c=2003$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="354" height="18" />. This is indeed a solution as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/e/8becb83d37f49e4f7485ec5a53d66d441b6a4068.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" /> puts <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a77b269dab2cab9fac4604af5272ea43009b648d.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2003-2x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />, and thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/f/c3fd47e6a3e448ac2c22260fd32aef92052be914.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 1002}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="81" height="29" />.
</p><p>This indeed works for the two right segments of slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. We already know that the minimum is achieved between slopes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31b51deaa1a9e903d120a02141556c3e70e348.png" class="latex" alt="$-3$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/9/1f9563360f04d9b959c980b16bef6f5a9be58615.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c=2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="14" />:

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Step_1:_Finding_some_promising_bound">Step 1: Finding some promising bound</span></h3>
<p>Does the system have a solution where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/7/12789c7998bd4728e0105e048101cc230435907d.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leq a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="14" />? 
</p><p>For such a solution we would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/3/b337cc6cb6a1b6d3f3289897ed4c0c7fb35151e9.png" class="latex" alt="$y=(a-x)+(b-x)+(c-x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="242" height="18" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/7042c723c849257d8c09888bde6861d1d8664fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$2x+(a+b+c-3x)=2003$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="227" height="18" />, which solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/6/4865be686dbcd2ce1db1982692358e4d6d7fa3d3.png" class="latex" alt="$x=a+b+c-2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="14" />.
If we want to avoid this solution, we need to have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/f/65f61cc6a1236f3fecd0ebd5dd964268c3519ee9.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c-2003&gt;a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="14" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/4/a34f13e9b258824cdce703e9e309af540b28f6d3.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c&gt;2003$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="14" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/5/5051d880af458f8cc7426d804e7a64ab5d73bb1a.png" class="latex" alt="$c\geq 1003$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="67" height="15" />. 
In other words, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/681a3aae9f39d8f112761fdac24f0b1c9faf7c3e.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1003$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, there will always be one solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/7/12789c7998bd4728e0105e048101cc230435907d.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leq a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="14" />. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Step_2:_Showing_one_solution">Step 2: Showing one solution</span></h3>
<p>We will now find out whether there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/681a3aae9f39d8f112761fdac24f0b1c9faf7c3e.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1003$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> for which (and some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />) the system has only one solution. We already know of one such solution, so we need to make sure that no other solution appears.
</p><p>Obviously, there are three more theoretically possible solutions: one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28d39d206246ca7566d0b820e2edbaf10b03954.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a,b\right]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" />, one in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2bd1fa23107f95ab25b8af850d1a9cbe06fb4f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(b,c\right]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />, and one in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/2/6f2f0195c6a11fde5c8936f582e24ba1a43ec931.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(c,\infty\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" />.
The first case solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/3/3f3ee64fce8c2f871ed428dce9abb5c1a1c46a6e.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2003+a-b-c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="163" height="14" />, the second to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/40591530da10ae8caabf5f03af6b5cefd7a0e09b.png" class="latex" alt="$3x=2003+a+b-c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="172" height="14" />, and the third to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67ce166b17a1d831a6698a393a6eb47c246cda89.png" class="latex" alt="$5x=2003+a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="172" height="14" />.
We need to make sure that the following three conditions hold:

<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/3/1a3a8d88298ad9c9e82c0cfc0eb062850bb3e8bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2003+a+b-c}3 = \frac{2002+a}3 \leq \frac{2002+1000}3 &lt; 1001 = b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="444" height="37" /></li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fcab9ac4078d2611b0ae859e5c1be70ba3ab58c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2003+a+b+c}5 = \frac{4006+a}5 \leq \frac{4006+1006}5 &lt; 1002 = a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="446" height="37" /></li></ol>
<p>Hence for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45e533ef700405599bc93e10d0835e25c78b6ff8.png" class="latex" alt="$c=1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/f/40f37787765da37aa2a891a87836737d928a2ae7.png" class="latex" alt="$b=1001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> and any valid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> the system has exactly one solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/a/5fa50c4b84159ae00e8bd2a4e548d715deaff231.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)=(a,2003-2a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Step_3:_Proving_the_optimality_of_our_solution">Step 3: Proving the optimality of our solution</span></h3>
<p>We will now show that for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/56334c2b30fd67a232c785e26bd8f4ac97d9e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> the system always has a solution such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/c/49cc074e4377ef012b7ae84a6b96debec486e2ae.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="10" />. This will mean that the system has at least two solutions, and thus the solution with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45e533ef700405599bc93e10d0835e25c78b6ff8.png" class="latex" alt="$c=1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> is optimal.
</p>
<ol><li>As we are looking for a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/56334c2b30fd67a232c785e26bd8f4ac97d9e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/4/ab4764c902e50c1aff98fae8a305c58a2467c90c.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c\leq 2001$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="97" height="15" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9d7f924f53d729bee984a79047687cdb4e5b50.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a-b-c &gt; a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="162" height="14" />. To make sure that the value falls outside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28d39d206246ca7566d0b820e2edbaf10b03954.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(a,b\right]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" />, we need to make it larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/b/f9b0aca3860ca9c54d4bb9b14c6be54083641945.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a-b-c &gt; b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="160" height="14" />, or equivalently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea20c99856f5c91a5c8d26045f6460e51e517ab2.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a &gt; 2b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />.</li>
<li>The condition we just derived, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea20c99856f5c91a5c8d26045f6460e51e517ab2.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a &gt; 2b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />, can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/c/0bcb59b4b55afd3c3c378cef7947301f4fc7f39e.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a+b &gt; 3b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="169" height="14" />, then as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a009ecae18525ec4832029b467d7a1f9f077187.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a+b-c &gt; 3b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="169" height="14" />, which becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/0/58002d9ca16e67f6fc8b3bd5f850585156450efd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2003+a+b-c}3 &gt; b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="164" height="37" />. Thus to make sure that the second value falls outside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2bd1fa23107f95ab25b8af850d1a9cbe06fb4f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(b,c\right]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />, we need to make it larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />. The inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/b/22baffbc816204d4136f6306c20b0084cf09fb09.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2003+a+b-c}3 &gt; c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="164" height="37" /> simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/1/f01332350020fd2d685f719c3a8a5e62a8adcd30.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a+b &gt; 4c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />.</li>
<li>To avoid the last solution, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/a/afa85b663fe5a0ad7d3cff5f99c8465e35e7e8c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2003+a+b+c}5\leq c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="164" height="37" />, which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5ea6db4de7297ab0caed65f492f0b8c7fe72dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$2003+a+b \leq 4c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="15" />.</li></ol>
<p>The last two inequalities contradict each other, thus there are no <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/b/a5b29b358e825defa3e11b7d903d43ec31e5909a.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> that would satisfy both of them.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusion">Conclusion</span></h3>
<p>We just showed that whenever <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/56334c2b30fd67a232c785e26bd8f4ac97d9e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, the system has at least two different solutions: one with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/7/12789c7998bd4728e0105e048101cc230435907d.png" class="latex" alt="$x\leq a$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="44" height="14" /> and one with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/c/49cc074e4377ef012b7ae84a6b96debec486e2ae.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="10" />. 
</p><p>We also showed that for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/e/45e533ef700405599bc93e10d0835e25c78b6ff8.png" class="latex" alt="$c=1002$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" /> there are some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> for which the system has exactly one solution. 
</p><p>Hence the optimal value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/f/c3fd47e6a3e448ac2c22260fd32aef92052be914.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}\ 1002}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="81" height="29" />.
</p>
