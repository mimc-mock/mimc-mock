<p>What is the largest integer that is a divisor of
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/9/c694dd7f6739034188fe758646f1fd28508df784.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(n+1)(n+3)(n+5)(n+7)(n+9)\]" width="278" height="18" />
</p><p>for all positive even integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/b/02b90914276aa817ebcdcc763787a2348f9a93b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\text {(A) } 3 \qquad \text {(B) } 5 \qquad \text {(C) } 11 \qquad \text {(D) } 15 \qquad \text {(E) } 165$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For all consecutive odd integers, one of every five is a multiple of 5 and one of every three is a multiple of 3. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/7/c1764cd0624246966ee9a84f13d3e1d2e205bfde.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot 5 = 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, so  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/8/30848fe2906a75e423084a915226d0b4e3105252.png" class="latex" alt="${\boxed{\textbf{(D)15}}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" /> is the correct answer.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We'll just test all the answer choices.
</p><p>Note that for any 3 consecutive odd integers, there will be exactly one multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93032f1ab8b685d84a767a2fde0d9006a464b5e4.png" class="latex" alt="$3.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>Let's list all possibilities of 3 consecutive odd integers. (multiple of 3, not multiple of 3, not multiple of 3), (not multiple of 3, multiple of 3, not multiple of 3) and (not multiple of 3, not multiple of 3, multiple of 3)
</p><p>To support this further, list the first few  consecutive lists of 3 consecutive odd integers.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/d/abd9fe15d4b334b5a4c6f12415480f2d2a4855e2.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 3, 5), (3, 5, 7), (5, 7, 9), (7, 9, 11), (11, 13, 15), \ldots$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="384" height="18" />
</p><p>So if the first two consecutive odd numbers aren't multiples of three, the last one must be a multiple of three.
</p><p>Therefore for five consecutive odd integers, there must be at least one multiple of three.
</p><p>In the same fashion as we did above, note that for any 5 consecutive integers, there will also be exactly one multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>Therefore, for any 5 consecutive odd integers, there must be exactly one multiple of five.
</p><p>We can skip 7 since none of the answer choices are a multiple of 7.
</p><p>Now we try <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/7/4e76ce9da0f428536412a4f539b127c608760686.png" class="latex" alt="$11.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /> 11 doesn't work since as we see the first set of 5 consecutive odd integers doesn't fit, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/2/7f263dc1f4b7d6274719e0592e3b9501cba5285f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 3, 5, 7, 9).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" />
</p><p>Since any 5 consecutive integers is divisible both by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, it also must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/8/30848fe2906a75e423084a915226d0b4e3105252.png" class="latex" alt="${\boxed{\textbf{(D)15}}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" /> and no higher since we saw that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> does not work and that there is no answer choice that is multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />.
</p><p>~mathboy282
</p>
