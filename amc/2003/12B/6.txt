<p>The second and fourth terms of a geometric sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Which of the following is a possible first term?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f9922c06a32ecd2479568060e245858446cc6180.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } -\sqrt{3}  \qquad\textbf{(B) } -\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad\textbf{(C) } -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \sqrt{3} \qquad\textbf{(E) } 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="497" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the first term be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and the common ratio be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />. Therefore, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/d/d6da05e8becb6457c46753fab5b6c95676cb5de6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ar=2\ \ (1) \qquad \text{and} \qquad ar^3=6\ \ (2)\]" width="270" height="19" />
</p><p>Dividing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> eliminates the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, yielding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/6/616b9e90ab61c407d0eb624b8d9bb616659f1b6f.png" class="latex" alt="$r^2=3$" width="49" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/0/5a03242fde0a17ff38e0a70594afc1a0863af2b0.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\pm\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="18" />.
</p><p>Now, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a796f649d15c3f70c05ed2ffcd17c6a3ec5bfdde.png" class="latex" alt="$ar=2$" width="51" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/e/1be550f35afc30ac0c8519b247bd13b70e024491.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{2}{r}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/d/a0d7d66cea92bebe0efc6fb54bb75d34f794be4e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{2}{\pm\sqrt{3}}=\pm\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="150" height="45" />.
</p><p>We therefore see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5cb9b6dab641e23c26dfa87c265c1566d2102e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ -\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="105" height="53" /> is a possible first term.
</p>
