<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be positive integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/a/b1ad0600d41a9a3e97e3090c1a968b0d7061bd1c.png" class="latex" alt="$7x^5 = 11y^{13}.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="18" /> The minimum possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> has a prime factorization <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/2/e92e2a8a963bc360325f725f413371f39cb4d42f.png" class="latex" alt="$a^cb^d.$" width="35" height="15" /> What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/0/3609992cb62090c9ffa52cc0730cece92f9bfc2c.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c + d?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/f/dffa004064f7845bde7be865ab7ada80dc81737d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 31 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 32 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 33 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 34$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/6/8e66232b7b99b87eb7fba2a1e008500fa5080572.png" class="latex" alt="$a^cb^d$" width="31" height="15" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/f/c4f149e2e1037736a529afff3c561433b1700e20.png" class="latex" alt="$7(a^{5c}b^{5d}) = 11y^{13}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="20" />. Divide both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, and it follows that:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/a/04a82189f3bc1d68d5c3b2583f51cc876eaa92a5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a^{5c}b^{5d}) = \frac{11y^{13}}{7}.\]" width="131" height="39" />
</p><p>Note that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> are prime, the minimum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must involve factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> only. Thus, we try to look for the lowest power <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/0/4c03e0e8160bbe09869cd8f73822fa7cf49907a0.png" class="latex" alt="$13p + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" />, so that we can take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/4/a740d388e787e68a150776177c7c6f5d671b5bee.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{13p + 1}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="15" /> to the fifth root. Similarly, we want to look for the lowest power <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5a827304a28703ca3302712b272d78bb8b38f7.png" class="latex" alt="$13n - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="18" />. Again, this allows us to take the fifth root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/8/498a802193180eb54fad3e7541e343eb94d3c77c.png" class="latex" alt="$7^{13n - 1}$" width="46" height="15" />. Obviously, we want to add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/6/296478033d772ec3935af329b343f3ad4e511b08.png" class="latex" alt="$13p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" /> and subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/8/c4839f403cf9a56c011d5614790387513495806c.png" class="latex" alt="$13n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="13" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/4/744eb481ff621f9a485f59e323491c008ecdcdee.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{13p}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f4fba9652a6fc1fc7aa7f3640ab6281848cec6.png" class="latex" alt="$7^{13n}$" width="30" height="15" /> are multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, respectively. With these conditions satisfied, we can simply multiply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7d418af1e7d4be708233e55d7858d61c4adc9a.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{p}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/0/f6044b56da95e4a71e200bd1056b74a78f363741.png" class="latex" alt="$7^{n}$" width="17" height="12" /> and substitute this quantity into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> to attain our answer.
</p><p><br />
We can simply look for suitable values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. We find that the lowest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, in this case, would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/0/4e0f702a878e6833d877844c7a3aebcd7c099152.png" class="latex" alt="$13(3) + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="18" />. Moreover, the lowest <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> should be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/6/e662de33cfe31ca54377dbe436f85f1ba388c32f.png" class="latex" alt="$13(2) - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="18" />. Hence, we can substitute the quantity <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/1/191350b7b641f7258b0cacacf6cf8a93ef29050c.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{3} \cdot 7^{2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="15" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Doing so gets us:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/2/cc2b7218ac2b462b1546c8c468d6ced0e1360e65.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a^{5c}b^{5d}) = \frac{11(11^{3} \cdot 7^{2})^{13}}{7} = 11^{40} \cdot 7^{25}.\]" width="284" height="39" />
</p><p>Taking the fifth root of both sides, we are left with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/1/fa1a9e5d0701bc45c217586b4765b97b6e875881.png" class="latex" alt="$a^cb^d = 11^{8} \cdot 7^{5}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="110" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/5/c2536d42bf44c329ffdd924de4b7886f7e914a77.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c + d = 11 + 7 + 8 + 5 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 31}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="326" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>A simpler way to tackle this problem without all that modding is to keep the equation as:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/7/bd76ecec3357c1ba4e6d9253c33419991c050d87.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7*a^{5b}c^{5d} = 11y^{13}\]" width="135" height="19" />
</p><p>As stated above, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> must be the factors 7 and 11 in order to keep <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> at a minimum. Moving all the non-y terms to the left hand side of the equation, we end up with:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/9/d69ca8a60413ef574c9313f253ab372b275aa9cd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7^{5b+1}11^{5d-1}=y^{13}\]" width="134" height="19" />
</p><p>The above equation means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must also contain only the factors 7 and 11 (again, in order to keep <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> at a minimum), so we end up with:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/0/920af943350ad715daba0b1176ee28d9a7387cc9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[7^{5b+1}11^{5d-1}=7^{13h}11^{13j}\]" width="179" height="16" />
</p><p>(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" /> are arbitrary variables placed in order to show that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> could have more than just one 7 or one 11 as factors)
</p><p>Since 7 and 11 are prime, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/5/34504a2845a673dbe5a500b9d7fb3ca5d50bd3a6.png" class="latex" alt="$5b+1=13h$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/8/d7805743e88918bd7c362f6dc84b8e1d61a34328.png" class="latex" alt="$5d-1=13j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="99" height="16" />. The smallest positive combinations that would work are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/9/529341ca980faa0014cc67bf64e324276e54c410.png" class="latex" alt="$b=5,h=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/0/a60cce7f1988d74d8ffd4fbd45775628c4cb8322.png" class="latex" alt="$d=8,j=3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/e/86e308a1897fbf9a1caebf6187760d716a27937a.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d=7+5+11+8=31$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="279" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67ceb75ed8a49bc9f99586422d471572208a8c55.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 31}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" /> is correct.
</p>
