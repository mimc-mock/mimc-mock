<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/8/5a8d20deb2ce4deb409db1b050e2d84c4c53c467.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)= \sqrt{ax^2+bx}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="21" />.  For how many <a href="/wiki/index.php/Real_number" title="Real number"> real</a> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is there at least one <a href="/wiki/index.php/Positive_number" title="Positive number"> positive</a> value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> for which the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Domain" title="Domain">domain</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> and the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Range" title="Range">range</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> are the same <a href="/wiki/index.php/Set" title="Set">set</a>?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/7/6172dab49ed06062107c326c335ba29c2ee26c53.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ 0 } \qquad \mathrm{(B) \ 1 } \qquad \mathrm{(C) \ 2 } \qquad \mathrm{(D) \ 3 } \qquad \mathrm{(E) \ \mathrm{infinitely \ many} }$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="453" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/addeebbf92b07c8481473b882f212fce0062a1a9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sqrt{x(ax+b)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="152" height="32" /> has a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Codomain" title="Codomain">codomain</a> of all non-negative numbers, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5c5819fbbafdc2a8a27e9acc4493277b2ab35f.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />. Since the domain and the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> are the same, it follows that the domain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> also satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/3/6831c542e2a17d2b51b0923510b2dd07c48a975a.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le x$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />.
</p><p>The function has two zeroes at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/3/7e37b18a28ab69688f19e179644bbeb9a869539a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0, \frac{-b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />, which must be part of the domain. Since the domain and the range are the same set, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/6/3c6ab647b465ee283403c5992e8071b6621e40a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="24" height="37" /> is in the codomain of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/d/d8dfe7e7f42a1392886a058cf023142428008ab8.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le \frac{-b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="57" height="37" />. This implies that one (but not both) of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" /> is non-positive. The problem states that there is at least one positive value of b that works, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be non-positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is non-negative, and the domain of the function occurs when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/f/53ff389cad698f0082891a16be5d078665db611e.png" class="latex" alt="$x(ax+b) &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />, or 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/8/048dba23cb79813ba2028192a7ccc50fbe521655.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le x \le \frac{-b}{a}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="37" /></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Completing_the_square" title="Completing the square">Completing the square</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8ff39310e96009d197538549c43162fb749c7f0.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x) = \sqrt{a\left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 - \frac{b^2}{4a}} \le \sqrt{\frac{-b^2}{4a}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="299" height="53" /> by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Trivial_Inequality" title="Trivial Inequality">Trivial Inequality</a> (remember that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/28227ec51149979b3f7246ba50102650a65801d8.png" class="latex" alt="$a \le 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />). Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is continuous and assumes this maximal value at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/7/527d7a384f8238a6203175e598d5ff7cf0a7f435.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{-b}{2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="37" />, it follows that the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b434ee413a6b27f6d7e0664398aae02a6f8d6c3.png" class="latex" alt="$0 \le f(x) \le \sqrt{\frac{-b^2}{4a}}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="147" height="43" /></center>
<p>As the domain and the range are the same, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/1/3914e540e340609fe6b899083fbec789e203e568.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-b}{a} = \sqrt{\frac{-b^2}{4a}} = \frac{b}{2\sqrt{-a}} \Longrightarrow a(a+4) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="314" height="46" /> (we can divide through by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> since it is given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is positive). Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/7582c5a6c18e789df04430ce563cbff2c9d78994.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 0, -4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" />, which both we can verify work, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/3/9b32acfcdd2f4bb76f3034350dd1e67726655d58.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/8/028d59f5f255262894b49646f92f67941d202454.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />, then squaring both sides of the given equation and subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/762b48c9dabf0dbafca8359fda4f04bea25e3311.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2$" width="26" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/3/473630c2e90f9f51fb86de9d40a1a2a5b436a608.png" class="latex" alt="$bx$" width="18" height="12" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e161bfec8b130d9adfa112d074c358b6db71c1b.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2-ax^2-bx=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="140" height="18" />. Completing the square, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/b/eab229688e13611086b4b8f9e840e4dd530de37e.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2-\frac{y^2}{a}=\frac{b^2}{4a^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="173" height="39" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/4/a34a53a91f16c862705e5e466f965f7cf5a6ff71.png" class="latex" alt="$y\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />. Divide out by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/b/87b329bd218129c87a4b27ac976aa1b5c22c445a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b^2}{4a^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="28" height="39" /> to put the equation in the standard form of an ellipse or hyperbola (depending on the sign of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />) to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/5002db83791e7c2cd48d03d4f428e34d8164ff10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2}{(\frac{b}{2a})^2}-\frac{y^2}{(\frac{b}{2\sqrt {\pm a}})^2}=1$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="195" height="51" />.
</p><p>Before continuing, it is important to note that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7e0b8ce4a517e8f66c4e0ee60585aa6875aa697.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=\sqrt{ax^2+bx}=\sqrt{ax(x+\frac{b}{a})}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="265" height="43" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> has roots 0 and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b848e82a6f7ef8135636b57ab2da5825a55b61.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="37" />. Now, we can use the function we deduced to figure out some of its properties when:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/2/4c28242522f23e896074dc89612847f8317911d7.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />: A semi-hyperbola above or on the x-axis. Therefore, no positive value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> allows the domain and range to be the same set because the range will always be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/6/a86f243b13e6ed9b3b2934d2dd9024dc39cdc6b8.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, \infty)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="18" /> and the domain will always be undefined on some finite range between some value and zero.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69cefa6855d2216036df094012f7ad2a7e4e204f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0$" width="42" height="12" />: We must refer back to the original function; this results in a horizontal semiparabola in the first quadrant, which satisfies that the domain and range of the function are equal. Specifically, both sets are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43de392dda90010f3068357206fe4d17768b7fba.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, \infty )$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="45" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69cefa6855d2216036df094012f7ad2a7e4e204f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0$" width="42" height="12" /> is the only case where this happens.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef8362e1f78725beb1de44c30360f0a10d0d3c82.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />: A semi-ellipse in quadrant one. Since its roots are 0 and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b848e82a6f7ef8135636b57ab2da5825a55b61.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="37" />, its domain must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/9/cf9cf7ad21f3a3271e986abc4f353a34c397cb00.png" class="latex" alt="$[0, -\frac{b}{a}]$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="37" />. To make its domain and range equal, the maximum value of the ellipse must then be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/b/d1b848e82a6f7ef8135636b57ab2da5825a55b61.png" class="latex" alt="$-\frac{b}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="37" />. But we have another expression for the maximum value of the ellipse, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee131cb4acd9980d94b98301a8329f745eec9ada.png" class="latex" alt="$0+\frac{b}{2\sqrt {\pm a}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="81" height="42" />. Setting these two expressions equal to each other will find us the final value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> that satisfies the question.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c12e019fa9afaffea8a7f35f7d73a2d75e9e6c54.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{-a}=\frac{b}{2\sqrt {\pm a}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="102" height="42" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/a/29a4aa797a0f95c818aa9a87d469e33a30403799.png" class="latex" alt="$-a=2\sqrt {\pm a}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="96" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/7/fe7638efa38b349a006411a6377e242e0f4877fd.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2=\pm 4a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38e703ec46179bc4606100c00bc5f600f85ff403.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0,\pm 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="16" />
</p><p>We already knew 0 was a solution from earlier, so -4 is our only new solution (we already ruled out any positive value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> as a solution, so 4 does not work). Thus there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aaf5247758bc51756fed99ddb30a8c45c5757cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {2\implies C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="84" height="24" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> that make the domain and range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c96dd6ec1dc4ad7520fbdc78fcdbec9edd068d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="34" height="18" /> the same set.
</p>
