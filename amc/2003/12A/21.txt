<p>The graph of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Polynomial" title="Polynomial">polynomial</a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/a/85a8f024c29a92d2b5ebd8359ba595c26a38a65a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(x) = x^5 + ax^4 + bx^3 + cx^2 + dx + e\]" width="298" height="19" />
</p><p>has five distinct <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercepts, one of which is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Which of the following <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coefficient" title="Coefficient">coefficients</a> cannot be zero?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d08a41598126cc87481fe15618e05ad95f58a94.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)}\ a \qquad \text{(B)}\ b \qquad \text{(C)}\ c \qquad \text{(D)}\ d \qquad \text{(E)}\ e$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="328" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the roots be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/f/0bf505e9e80b1b167ced7296132efcef0fb41a4e.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1=0, r_2, r_3, r_4, r_5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="142" height="16" />. According to <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas"> Viète's formulae</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/8/7586b04cdefd7127ac468d3c01e6d0c926f6ce57.png" class="latex" alt="$d=r_1r_2r_3r_4 + r_1r_2r_3r_5 + r_1r_2r_4r_5 + r_1r_3r_4r_5 + r_2r_3r_4r_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="434" height="15" />. The first four terms contain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/0/0b0649d7828ff86087991e8374c0ecfbe359c968.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1=0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" /> and are therefore zero, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31b960ad3ab0649287887a4d67b1d46be4754d01.png" class="latex" alt="$d=r_2r_3r_4r_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="94" height="15" />. This is a product of four non-zero numbers, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> must be non-zero <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/6/5561da2815c8c34ec9821146aa9fe66097b361f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\Longrightarrow \mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Clearly, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is an intercept, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3a59bb1293ee3f6dec19de4019a7178874219ae.png" class="latex" alt="$e$" width="8" height="8" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. But if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> would divide the polynomial, which means it would have a double root at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, which is impossible, since all five roots are distinct.
</p>
