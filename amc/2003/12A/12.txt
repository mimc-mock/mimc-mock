<p>Sally has five red cards numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and four blue cards numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. She stacks the cards so that the colors alternate and so that the number on each red card divides evenly into the number on each neighboring blue card. What is the sum of the numbers on the middle three cards? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/5/6b59635958fa2386b9bc8fef4b34dac8023c0ac1.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 8\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 9\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 10\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 11\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="369" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/f/1ef7ab23d6fc7d336379d457f66aa6ed57d72ec9.png" class="latex" alt="$R_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/d/bbdaac3abe08d4a0bd3761cadf3804c9b24eac9c.png" class="latex" alt="$B_j$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="19" height="17" /> designate the red card numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> and the blue card numbered <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/2/8122aa89ea6e80784c6513d22787ad86e36ad0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$j$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="16" />, respectively. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/8/bd8ad0256d4e1c350b9b78b0f148d572dca17cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$B_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> is the only blue card that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d7e56978275cd4ab49c7729521b3a577ce737dc.png" class="latex" alt="$R_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> evenly divides, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/7/0d7e56978275cd4ab49c7729521b3a577ce737dc.png" class="latex" alt="$R_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be at one end of the stack and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/8/bd8ad0256d4e1c350b9b78b0f148d572dca17cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$B_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be the card next to it. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbbeb5834997d74dbc44264f04660c2bf84077.png" class="latex" alt="$R_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> is the only other red card that evenly divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/8/bd8ad0256d4e1c350b9b78b0f148d572dca17cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$B_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbbeb5834997d74dbc44264f04660c2bf84077.png" class="latex" alt="$R_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> must be the other card next to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/8/bd8ad0256d4e1c350b9b78b0f148d572dca17cd2.png" class="latex" alt="$B_5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c4c84a311424215d4f1f09027795a1d2fd91d4.png" class="latex" alt="$B_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> is the only blue card that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/e/a9e8e03f8c7eb6e0d080c73c4860636d6cb8dd7d.png" class="latex" alt="$R_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> evenly divides, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/e/a9e8e03f8c7eb6e0d080c73c4860636d6cb8dd7d.png" class="latex" alt="$R_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be at one end of the stack and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c4c84a311424215d4f1f09027795a1d2fd91d4.png" class="latex" alt="$B_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be the card next to it. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5f6500d904b18c0eaf34da49a31bd3fb72d7f8.png" class="latex" alt="$R_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> is the only other red card that evenly divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c4c84a311424215d4f1f09027795a1d2fd91d4.png" class="latex" alt="$B_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5f6500d904b18c0eaf34da49a31bd3fb72d7f8.png" class="latex" alt="$R_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be the other card next to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42c4c84a311424215d4f1f09027795a1d2fd91d4.png" class="latex" alt="$B_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5f6500d904b18c0eaf34da49a31bd3fb72d7f8.png" class="latex" alt="$R_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> doesn't evenly divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/8/c48424701a2c36b4d4d6956d8c41e2798d8b32a8.png" class="latex" alt="$B_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/8/c48424701a2c36b4d4d6956d8c41e2798d8b32a8.png" class="latex" alt="$B_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be next to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbbeb5834997d74dbc44264f04660c2bf84077.png" class="latex" alt="$R_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b4197a348eae154101dbe18c35b97741837481f7.png" class="latex" alt="$B_6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be next to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/5/ca5f6500d904b18c0eaf34da49a31bd3fb72d7f8.png" class="latex" alt="$R_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/9/049680fc475590e3302c08973c2416e40d650d69.png" class="latex" alt="$R_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> must be in the middle. 
</p><p>This yields the following arrangement from top to bottom: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/9/0694907279a15ea45ef59bccd3b2fc972044fccc.png" class="latex" alt="$\{R_5,B_5,R_1,B_3,R_3,B_6,R_2,B_4,R_4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="273" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore, the sum of the numbers on the middle three cards is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f332fab93e09a6c695b071d73ff39bd3660f0a7.png" class="latex" alt="$3+3+6=\boxed{\mathrm{(E)}\ 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="158" height="29" />.
</p>
