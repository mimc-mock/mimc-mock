<p>A parabola with equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/c/84cea4f7bfc5284747e737af0d1a25e64afdd47e.png" class="latex" alt="$y=ax^2+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="132" height="18" /> is reflected about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. The parabola and its reflection are translated horizontally five units in opposite directions to become the graphs of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/e/97eded22ccc3e54189a8f157c4e81f36fe034a26.png" class="latex" alt="$y=f(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b07623f87e4369fb18ed7ca64337e70c2ee0e5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />, respectively. Which of the following describes the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/1/371024d4b7409f7aaef2de35eedd47c5316307d3.png" class="latex" alt="$y=(f+g)(x)?$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/3/c23f7a51942ac7d8dd3b264c6e156b6e794b5cec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{a parabola tangent to the }x\text{-axis}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="270" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/f/d6f1aacdc49dd17f96016984849802706a521e2e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}\ \text{a parabola not tangent to the }x\text{-axis}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{a horizontal line}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/3/a93776dc460898adfbe80572e43da7eb6cd3cc02.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}\ \text{a non-horizontal line}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{the graph of a cubic function}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="462" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If we take the parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/746895408579e2b016bc2ee476721bee6ff5117f.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2 + bx + c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="16" /> and reflect it over the x - axis, we have the parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bbe8b3d4a3a8fd0bbcdda73706f284a9c11941a.png" class="latex" alt="$-ax^2 - bx - c$" width="112" height="15" />. Without loss of generality, let us say that the parabola is translated 5 units to the left, and the reflection to the right. Then:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a4eefc5db0058df263cc2b30715e44bcdd9913.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} f(x) = a(x+5)^2 + b(x+5) + c = ax^2 + (10a+b)x + 25a + 5b + c \\  g(x)  = -a(x-5)^2 - b(x-5) - c = -ax^2 + 10ax -bx - 25a + 5b - c \end{align*}" width="547" height="47" /> 
</p><p>Adding them up produces: 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e567cbaa26060ddeebc50b547872d554ba9ba83f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (f + g)(x) = ax^2 + (10a+b)x + 25a + 5b + c - ax^2 + 10ax -bx - 25a + 5b - c = 20ax + 10b \end{align*}" width="735" height="19" /> 
</p><p>This is a line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/1/681a2bdbe3bc8753cf58db4cffb9e0563a5d7edf.png" class="latex" alt="$20a$" width="27" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cannot be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> (because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/746895408579e2b016bc2ee476721bee6ff5117f.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2 + bx + c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="16" /> would be a line) we end up with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638fdd3ff46de49b77f3f8bf8bd07de8a1951191.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)} \text{ a non-horizontal line }}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="202" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2:_less_computation">Solution 2: less computation</span></h2>
<p>WLOG let the parabola be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/f/8ef862bdd16c2d5356e11735b8c766c402f5e10c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="18" />, and the reflected parabola is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab27a40e2b41908c693c1f69bcd5cfa04e3ea9a7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="18" />. We can also assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/248146c0dd9a04f5f7c7c130a5fd4108deaa57f1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x) = (x+5)^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25\]" width="255" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/1/ff19b0b1afe1b23a2f745434d4384278233d7a69.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[g(x) = -(x-5)^2 = -x^2 + 10x - 25.\]" width="286" height="19" /> Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/9/b8923b792dd06eee2d789704e72df43820054403.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(f+g)(x) = x^2 + 10x + 25 - x^2 + 10x - 25 = 20x,\]" width="401" height="19" /> which is a non-horizontal line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d51641e75bca1251764e9e30cbb0f038e58ebb3.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{D}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="49" height="23" />.
</p><p>-MP8148
</p>
