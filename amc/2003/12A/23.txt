<p>How many perfect squares are divisors of the product <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18fba907d4f901c083e61f03152d8c846c495739.png" class="latex" alt="$1! \cdot 2! \cdot 3! \cdot \hdots \cdot 9!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="128" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7673d0f0de83d9a1351a0f2bdf66578a2195352d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 504\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 672\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 864\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 936\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 1008$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="448" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We want to find the number of perfect square factors in the product of all the factorials of numbers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a8c4507655b839f4f624f81e3b12ca3b6dfe2a7.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - 9$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />. We can write this out and take out the factorials, and then find a prime factorization of the entire product. We can also find this prime factorization by finding the number of times each factor is repeated in each factorial. This comes out to be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/a/5ea5a6a10a10b3d80a1960823cbfd05a00b5af48.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{30} \cdot 3^{13} \cdot 5^5 \cdot 7^3$" width="117" height="15" />. To find the amount of perfect square factors, we realize that each exponent in the prime factorization must be even: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/4/8b44aed3bbfca463b48300e6c2462b3a35e68aa7.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{15} \cdot 3^{6}\cdot 5^2 \cdot 7^1$" width="110" height="15" />. To find the total number of possibilities, we add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to each exponent and multiply them all together. This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/0/d00c37f69d3901429df466d499540b63253346a9.png" class="latex" alt="$16 \cdot 7 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 = 672$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="135" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/5/c85fa79f94f23a676629b9742ac5fc3c3773a144.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>( Just Explanation Of 1 )
</p><p>We can easily find that factorials upto 9 in product lead to prime factorization <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/c/53c3c50502c75f87675985d8a89d92d0f397d345.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{30}$" width="21" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e4bb5c0261c8d726157108cdd95cf6eb7bb6d46.png" class="latex" alt="$*$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/1/fe1d889d1c902300bc09bec1ea22846a66bc6907.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{13}$" width="21" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e4bb5c0261c8d726157108cdd95cf6eb7bb6d46.png" class="latex" alt="$*$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e1baff6050db5d3e78ff320d0358d74adf9b153.png" class="latex" alt="$5^5$" width="15" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/4/1e4bb5c0261c8d726157108cdd95cf6eb7bb6d46.png" class="latex" alt="$*$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd99228146175c61e5cca6be965d17c918d45cec.png" class="latex" alt="$7^3$" width="15" height="15" /> So number of pairs possible = { <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/6/d461a03c629720cfd53171c56b9318dd10351ecf.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{0}$" width="15" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e782613426a53e4e1b68656d409a03bfe3dad499.png" class="latex" alt="$3^{0}$" width="15" height="15" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/9/d198e115ee5481f6012df3f623690b4bcab5f869.png" class="latex" alt="$,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="4" height="5" /> <img </p><p>Which leads to resulting number of pairs = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/6/766ed5a841725c8dbb3c590958409d5fc68ed126.png" class="latex" alt="$16*7*3*2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="96" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/2/3a21eedf8987958dc8173f6c266f7c4dafde16b3.png" class="latex" alt="$672$" width="26" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/5/c85fa79f94f23a676629b9742ac5fc3c3773a144.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="62" height="29" />.
