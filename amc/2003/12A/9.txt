<p>A set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> of points in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />-plane is symmetric about the origin, both coordinate axes, and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />, what is the smallest number of points in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/2/1f2cd6ba32fc5929b00180c94476da65f12f600a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 1\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } 2\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 4\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 8\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ } 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="351" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/9/8790f46883a296366f038323844601dd2cde9ba6.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is also, and quickly we see that every point of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/5/23521f6eca785daff8420a51175e9b597c465c22.png" class="latex" alt="$(\pm 2, \pm 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/c/87c6aac1c25085142987ed60f67a0a940c66b9af.png" class="latex" alt="$(\pm 3, \pm 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> must be in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. Now note that these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> points satisfy all of the symmetry conditions. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/2/7321d4a0b2f2cbdac92d9fab2d4e4f6ca3316370.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 8}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="29" />.
</p>
