<p>A triathlete competes in a triathlon in which the swimming, biking, and running segments are all of the same length. The triathlete swims at a rate of 3 kilometers per hour, bikes at a rate of 20 kilometers per hour, and runs at a rate of 10 kilometers per hour. Which of the following is closest to the triathlete's average speed, in kilometers per hour, for the entire race?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/6/916759d671c104ef36f735bac64ec84cdcda456f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 3\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ 4\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 5\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ 6\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be the length of one segment of the race.
</p><p>Average speed is total distance divided by total time. The total distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b26c8398882a95a9099c7b9516439dd6b467c65.png" class="latex" alt="$3d$" width="18" height="12" />, and the total time is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a979d18618c8846d27f7b62f31bbbad906cfbf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{d}{3}+\frac{d}{20}+\frac{d}{10}=\frac{29d}{60}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="156" height="38" />.
</p><p>Thus, the average speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/451a1d54c1c71e315bd2b4f3b1c01c1b55fd3a23.png" class="latex" alt="$3d\div\left(\frac{29d}{60}\right)=\frac{180}{29}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="152" height="43" />. This is closest to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the three segments are all the same length, the triathlete's average speed is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Harmonic_mean" title="Harmonic mean">harmonic mean</a> of the three given rates. Therefore, the average speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/3/27301fb8f762ab0e29ede7c8d7e2e9c2538e9eaf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3}{\frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{20}+\frac{1}{10}}=\frac{3}{\frac{29}{60}}=\frac{180}{29}\approx6\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\ 6}\]" width="308" height="44" />.
</p>
