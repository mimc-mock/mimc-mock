<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a> has <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perimeter" title="Perimeter">perimeter</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" /> and area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />. What is the length of its <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hypotenuse" title="Hypotenuse">hypotenuse</a>?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d8773b514469fed2cb062892112fe0b13dd515a.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{57}{4}\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{59}{4}\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{61}{4}\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{63}{4}\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \frac{65}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="406" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let the legs of the triangle have lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9bdf3e496517e8f8742f81dd774be1f93cf6e8c.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />. Then, by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, the length of the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/729acb7391552a1f8d893217944a9d7004a5b32b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="18" />, and the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/c/06c6e11d9cb46fffbc59dbe09bfb1629f799231b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 ab$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="30" height="37" />. So we have the two equations
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/0/590e0375a717ca40cb135ab5c622719465e9abb0.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+\sqrt{a^2+b^2} = 32 \\\\ \frac{1}{2}ab = 20$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="173" height="75" />
</p>
</center>
<p>Re-arranging the first equation and squaring, 
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29f223f26ebcdf7a2b8eeb0d40497bc16a87dde6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2+b^2} = 32-(a+b)\\\\ a^2 + b^2 = 32^2 - 64(a+b) + (a+b)^2\\\\ a^2 + b^2 + 64(a+b) = a^2 + b^2 + 2ab + 32^2\\\\ a+b = \frac{2ab+32^2}{64}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="322" height="157" />
</p>
</center>
<p>From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/3/d8302cf6a912695e3be4d4d4a7e58c2cc708f49d.png" class="latex" alt="$2ab = 80$" width="68" height="12" />, so
</p>
<center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cfbf4145c3db882de61026050f28490e208533.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = \frac{80 + 32^2}{64} = \frac{69}{4}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="184" height="39" />
</p>
</center>
<p>The length of the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d939a921e23326bd3dadb0d19e2b8ae481c90d8.png" class="latex" alt="$p - a - b = 32 - \frac{69}{4} = \frac{59}{4}\boxed{\ \mathrm{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="249" height="38" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>From the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f6a8dfe6d1a7764e3d737e70a7baa2408be22e0.png" class="latex" alt="$A = rs$" width="54" height="13" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is the area of a triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is its <a href="/wiki/index.php/Inradius" title="Inradius">inradius</a>, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Semiperimeter" title="Semiperimeter">semiperimeter</a>, we can find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/7/f57fec775e82058486b5bff164b31eadd5210f2e.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \frac{20}{32/2} = \frac{5}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="109" height="42" />. In a right triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32f1c9411224bdf2102ad07631d1aa456cb2d8f1.png" class="latex" alt="$r = s - h$" width="73" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is the hypotenuse, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b23cd9568ef41019e89cda1137ccc53fd945eee.png" class="latex" alt="$h = 16 - \frac{5}{4} = \frac{59}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="133" height="38" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p><p><b>Note:</b> If the reader is unfamiliar with the inradius being equal to the semiperimeter minus the hypotenuse for a right triangle, it should not be too difficult to prove the relationship for themselves.~mobius247
</p><p><b>Why <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/5/f95980d303d0edaa9e6231e93fff470cbb32dcd8.png" class="latex" alt="$r=s-h$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="16" />.</b> Draw a right triangle and inscribe a circle. Connect the center of the circle with tangency points; there are 3, one from each side of the triangle. Now, connect the center with each vertice and you will see 3 pairs of congruent triangles by HL (Hypotenuse-Leg Congruence). From these congruent triangles, mark equal lengths and you will find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/5/f95980d303d0edaa9e6231e93fff470cbb32dcd8.png" class="latex" alt="$r=s-h$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="16" />. 
</p><p>~BakedPotato66

<p>From the problem, we know that

<p>Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> from both sides of the first equation and squaring both sides, we get
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/1/8f13a9ea7ec9417ab1fab36f5ae1d9ecb5ef4179.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} (a+b)^2 &amp;= (32 - c)^2\\ a^2 + b^2 + 2ab &amp;= 32^2 + c^2 - 64c.\\ \end{align*}" width="242" height="44" /></center>
<p>Now we substitute in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/b/7fbd4264fbec874b1c14992abd59ea22d4daf604.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" /> as well as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/3/d8302cf6a912695e3be4d4d4a7e58c2cc708f49d.png" class="latex" alt="$2ab = 80$" width="68" height="12" /> into the equation to get

<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/1/aa184abe19e53f4bb2e8712ad5b4e6a2e0aaf82d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 80 &amp;= 1024 - 64c\\ c &amp;= \frac{944}{64}. \end{align*}" width="126" height="62" /></center>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the legs of the triangle and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> the hypotenuse.
</p><p>Since the area is 20, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9af5d070d26e6a7b8f4979f4708bb2a3a439bd3c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}ab = 20 =&gt; ab=40$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="170" height="37" />.
</p><p>Since the perimeter is 32, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/a/94afa4cf7b5fd2b704f36e8972614ac74d62c225.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 32$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />.
</p><p>The Pythagorean Theorem gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/9/3a9f829217fefe4b2d99d2c54d8890862f243a4a.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = a^2 + b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" />. 
</p><p>This gives us three equations with three variables:
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/3/ab34477dc4e0b22ad043a9d2ae5228ade3611d17.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = 40 \\ a + b + c = 32 \\ c^2 = a^2 + b^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="54" /></center>
<p>Rewrite equation 3 as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1fae9d1b0de7f9faa9b55a9d6b34d8dccafbc567.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = (a+b)^2 - 2ab$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="19" />.
Substitute in equations 1 and 2 to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e229cd83aa450ea5ef75bb64aba804e29da10a.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = (32-c)^2 - 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="149" height="19" />.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb0955d038919472bbefb6e672b0b6c7192fb561.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2 = (32-c)^2 - 80 \\\\ c^2 = 1024 - 64c + c^2 - 80 \\\\ 64c = 944 \\\\ c = \frac{944}{64} = \frac{236}{16} = \frac{59}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="201" height="153" />. </center>
<p>The answer is choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/313048c3149436615f6cd06b7f1bec83420d6a37.png" class="latex" alt="$(B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be the sides of the triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> as the hypotenuse.
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/4/7c4116032a6b4e0e84172123eab46a2197a407b5.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c =32$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />.
</p><p>According to the Pythagorean Theorem, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/b/7fbd4264fbec874b1c14992abd59ea22d4daf604.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" />.
</p><p>We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/823a2ba9087ffa4a3453fb54c57f45e1cea26421.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = 40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="13" />, since the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>We substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c86e802b02490888501a6d8ef3e6836615e2129a.png" class="latex" alt="$2ab$" width="26" height="12" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/b/7fbd4264fbec874b1c14992abd59ea22d4daf604.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/7/f87a8cae76ece70831f5fa1953e2bd9542801b6a.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)^2 = c^2 + 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="19" />.
</p><p>Moving the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b693026c70424da68ed4c52d3d0d35d9548ee4e.png" class="latex" alt="$c^2$" width="14" height="15" /> to the left, we again rewrite to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbff11fec9a940f6a81a11dbdccd0b4a5adf9a2b.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b+c)(a+b-c) = 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="209" height="18" />.
</p><p>We substitute our value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6ebd1e0356cfe09c0d9b5459d6f1542657bb5891.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" /> twice into our equation and subtract to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/7/487f55ab05585946e71bc4fa589cb24bb79318f3.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \frac{69}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="84" height="38" />.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6">Solution 6</span></h3>
<p>Let the legs be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/3/3834f0cef2a1f4d8524aa5bc8e3faca298550862.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />. Then the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/c/e4c1ecaa6b366c304792733023306ddb1ce59363.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="18" />
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/823a2ba9087ffa4a3453fb54c57f45e1cea26421.png" class="latex" alt="$ab = 40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c6419aac5c931278d49d34bab302d452419fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} = 32$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="173" height="18" />.

We want the hypotenuse which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/0/c10c89fd1f3e2dffa9a0952779ebe051396c8918.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2 + b^2} = \sqrt{ (a+b)^2 - 2ab}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="225" height="32" />

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_7">Solution 7</span></h3>
<p>Let the sides be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/acc6d9a6172d35127b6cb37ea92192caf7029a54.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are the legs and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is the hypotenuse.
</p><p>Since the perimeter is 32, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/6/6e63a61993ce7231f1843088cdd10df6f62c3972.png" class="latex" alt="$(1) \phantom{a} a+b+c=32$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since the area is 20 and the legs are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, we have that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/2/522cf461ff53385204821b860f44428c9a54d28c.png" class="latex" alt="$(2) \phantom{a} \frac{a \cdot b}{2}=20$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="37" />.
</p><p>By the Pythagorean Theorem, we have that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/d/b8d8a1007133a038b736f960be3e85ec9d6e79d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(3) \phantom{a} a^2+b^2=c^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="19" />.
</p><p>Since we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, we will equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/a/0fafafcb8dbe78fec70b78cd2a2f4a0222372f2e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> be in the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/201a2eef545643b50b07fed5c09e894bcced5c58.png" class="latex" alt="$c.$" width="11" height="8" />
</p><p>Equation 1 can be turned into
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/f/f6f3e44e794d08e1083cdbf05feb738a0d964494.png" class="latex" alt="$(4) \phantom{a} a+b=32-c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="18" />.
</p><p>Equation 2 can be simplified into
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/9/4b9930702a800ee05d9fb7d4c5ec1f1375dfe980.png" class="latex" alt="$(5) \phantom{a} ab=40.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="96" height="18" />
</p><p>Equation 3 is already simplified.
</p><p>Onto the calculating process.
</p>
<hr />
<p>Squaring the 1st equation we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/2/d720cf984c4b698072ce10cc0ba5b49add6a3453.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b+c)^2=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="19" />
</p><p>Expanding and grouping, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c397610cd6446c0905b56fc8f829fbe23d96d80.png" class="latex" alt="$(a^2+b^2+c^2)+2(ab+ac+bc)=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="300" height="19" />
</p><p>By equation 3 and substituting we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/a/54a3fd6f6c1012ab55d767cb8ca56916220320f6.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c^2+ab+ac+bc)=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="209" height="19" />
</p><p>By equation 5 and substituting we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/0/3305aee23029440a82945a38932eda7240e96168.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c^2+40+ac+bc)=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="210" height="19" />
</p><p>Note that we can factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> out in the inner expression, and we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/7/4a7e861ccdcd151537ec6f5f799a3bc018b29bdc.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c^2+40+c(a+b))=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="216" height="19" />
</p><p>By equation 4 and substituting, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/5/ea54252bb59e61434434a470f39205f048ab8de8.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c^2+40+c(32-c))=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="225" height="19" />
</p><p>Expanding, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67cf4e547062821fa30e6212733e64d8219db192.png" class="latex" alt="$2(c^2+40+32c-c^2)=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="218" height="19" />
</p><p>Simplifying, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/b/dab6186f7185722792533732bfd6b25adb6b667b.png" class="latex" alt="$2(40+32c)=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="19" />
</p><p>Expanding again, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/b/30b5a2113f37b22a228aceb15f893030699e4b4d.png" class="latex" alt="$80+64c=32^2.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="16" />
</p><p>Dividing both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> gets us
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f3a42914be862e8406e1d8fd3b62e6be6d8879.png" class="latex" alt="$4c+5=64$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />
</p><p>Calculating gets us
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/9/fd97eace25cdf0b729ec53d82ee6dfbd5d1a9e8b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\mathrm{(B) \frac{59}{4}}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="93" height="48" />.
</p><p>~mathboy282
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_8">Solution 8</span></h3>
<p>This solution is very similar to Solution 1, except instead of subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> from both sides in the first part, we subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/729acb7391552a1f8d893217944a9d7004a5b32b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="18" /> from both sides, which gets us:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf97430213a6f0cfe4cb72ccebd4cde9b93ddb99.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a+b&amp;=32-\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\\ (a+b)^2&amp;=(32-\sqrt{a^2+b^2})^2\\ a^2+2ab+b^2&amp;=32^2-2\cdot32\cdot\sqrt{a^2+b^2}+a^2+b^2\\ 2ab&amp;=32^2-2\cdot32\cdot\sqrt{a^2+b^2}.\\ \end{align*}" width="373" height="107" />
</p><p>We know that 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/6/f56e93045388f379add529e51bbe043ce67175e4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2}ab=20\]" width="70" height="37" />
so 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c12bda4001994a59b5e42c460e8a445b9496df3b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab=40.\]" width="62" height="12" />
</p><p>We can then substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" /> to get us:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f329961a3398794018508e60d682e33c862429d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 80&amp;=32^2-2\cdot32\cdot\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\\ 2\cdot32\cdot\sqrt{a^2+b^2}&amp;=32^2-80\\ 64\cdot\sqrt{a^2+b^2}&amp;=32^2-80\\ \sqrt{a^2+b^2}&amp;=\frac{32^2-80}{64}\\ \end{align*}" width="318" height="127" />
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/9/729acb7391552a1f8d893217944a9d7004a5b32b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="18" /> is the hypotenuse, so we only have to solve the right-hand side now.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2fb8a2580237dd765bffdf206106ffe5137418d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \sqrt{a^2+b^2}&amp;=\frac{32^2-80}{64}\\ &amp;=\frac{32^2}{64}-\frac{80}{64}\\ &amp;=\frac{32\cdot32}{32\cdot2}-\frac{32\cdot\frac{5}{2}}{32\cdot2}\\ &amp;=\frac{32}{2}-\frac{\frac{5}{2}}{2}\\ &amp;=16-\frac{5}{4}\\ &amp;=\frac{64}{4}-\frac{5}{4}\\ &amp;=\boxed{\mathrm{(B)} \frac{59}{4}}\\ \end{align*}" width="213" height="319" />
</p><p>~zlrara01
</p>
