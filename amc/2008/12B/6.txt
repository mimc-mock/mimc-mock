<p>Postman Pete has a pedometer to count his steps. The pedometer records up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/a/86a2f56dd0e57cb4603f97bc19530bf036969f2a.png" class="latex" alt="$99999$" width="44" height="12" /> steps, then flips over to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9a15d522e9299ff10f38a48009273ffad9264e2.png" class="latex" alt="$00000$" width="44" height="12" /> on the next step. Pete plans to determine his mileage for a year. On January <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> Pete sets the pedometer to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9a15d522e9299ff10f38a48009273ffad9264e2.png" class="latex" alt="$00000$" width="44" height="12" />. During the year, the pedometer flips from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/a/86a2f56dd0e57cb4603f97bc19530bf036969f2a.png" class="latex" alt="$99999$" width="44" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9a15d522e9299ff10f38a48009273ffad9264e2.png" class="latex" alt="$00000$" width="44" height="12" /> forty-four times. On December <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/6/296fac336caaa4265aa24b6ecbc408a348ad7e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$31$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> the pedometer reads <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/c/cfc063af4024a9f7c2d461b3b01c7678fab588db.png" class="latex" alt="$50000$" width="44" height="12" />. Pete takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa858c71f0659dbdb750259e38da3ead33c96b4.png" class="latex" alt="$1800$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> steps per mile. Which of the following is closest to the number of miles Pete walked during the year?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/6/246a5bfe165af4d40be33f5cb2ddff766149c04e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2500 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3000 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 3500 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 4000 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 4500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="484" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Every time the pedometer flips, Pete has walked <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/4/d545bb45d1f0199e4785046df6c11481c9164de4.png" class="latex" alt="$100,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" /> steps. Therefore, Pete has walked a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/a/ceaa61df512479d6b241cf35706552770d566407.png" class="latex" alt="$100,000 \cdot 44 + 50,000 = 4,450,000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="273" height="16" /> steps, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/0/080162bf0b4a8f7da8c14481f5d0048e6f0d50dc.png" class="latex" alt="$4,450,000/1,800 = 2472.2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="18" /> miles, which is the closest to the answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6eb65962292d994ce2f9a8af63728883f256b18.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="24" />.
</p>
