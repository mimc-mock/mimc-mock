<p>A function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/d/39de37a7701c491668310aaadb5169b1ec27a5b9.png" class="latex" alt="$f(z) = (4 + i) z^2 + \alpha z + \gamma$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="19" /> for all complex numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/f/10f32377ac67d94f764f12a15ea987e88c85d3e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha$" width="11" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> are complex numbers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/d/0dd1c90fd1a2f6486fbdccc868c760c0b162a640.png" class="latex" alt="$i^2 = - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/e/74efea4ce02738f0765b2b9d578ceb427631eadc.png" class="latex" alt="$f(i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="30" height="18" /> are both real. What is the smallest possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/f/1ef87f16f9ef4d6f152fad51ab6f61971d12a34e.png" class="latex" alt="$| \alpha | + |\gamma |$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/b/96b5520d7e33de4e667732c1c580dfc7a66e76e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \; 1 \qquad \textbf{(B)} \; \sqrt {2} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \; 2 \qquad \textbf{(D)} \; 2 \sqrt {2} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \; 4 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="386" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1:">Solution 1:</span></h2>
<p>We need only concern ourselves with the imaginary portions of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/e/74efea4ce02738f0765b2b9d578ceb427631eadc.png" class="latex" alt="$f(i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="30" height="18" /> (both of which must be 0). These are:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/9/069fa3c9028eae58fefcc12bb6caf1d7ab6744c0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \Im(f(1)) &amp; = i+i\Im(\alpha)+i\Im(\gamma) \\ \Im(f(i)) &amp; = -i+i\Re(\alpha)+i\Im(\gamma) \end{align*}" width="237" height="43" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/3/4e38aeb9e33b8f8a1ba58842c04aeee203443a13.png" class="latex" alt="$p=\Im(\gamma)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/0/f30f7ce8f5ccebbf26c915f5863ff655be25b129.png" class="latex" alt="$q=\Re{(\gamma)},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" /> then we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/2/aa29861d12180241dbb933d83204817785178262.png" class="latex" alt="$\Im(\alpha)=-p-1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c689e74f87c10437d4adcc03d7d355621a85834d.png" class="latex" alt="$\Re(\alpha)=1-p.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" /> Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11ef7cae82a4f33f113baedb0472cdd897070286.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|\alpha|+|\gamma|=\sqrt{(1-p)^2+(-1-p)^2}+\sqrt{q^2+p^2}=\sqrt{2p^2+2}+\sqrt{p^2+q^2},\]" width="534" height="32" /> which reaches its minimum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/b/75bec5f95356b5621c8c473003558d5d8187a4d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/c/0bc2f876be1816c656fab9dc5b6d6b3cb2f10ff7.png" class="latex" alt="$p=q=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="16" /> by the Trivial Inequality. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed6e8243647f9655d194f393c78c4765b7d45dee.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed B.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="29" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2:">Solution 2:</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/f/e8fde65fa6c575a27a2c5890aec64f939096496a.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)=4+i+\alpha+\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="162" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/2/1b232b8fbe9177f41835f368040bcb396c50401c.png" class="latex" alt="$f(i)=-4-i+\alpha \cdot i +\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="193" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb5b19bf7575fdc3655bb5767980f09dbb747a8d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/e/74efea4ce02738f0765b2b9d578ceb427631eadc.png" class="latex" alt="$f(i)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="30" height="18" /> are both real we get, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/9/0b9be1d6ebced83e6254403573be09115d96bae2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\alpha+\gamma=-i\]" width="87" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/7/c07af11d839913d5d4f792fe56235d930ae491b2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\alpha \cdot i+\gamma=i\]" width="93" height="16" />
</p><p>Solving, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/118e8481cc935db07b968579aa15a48031535e22.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha=1-i$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/66981fa3920210c6ad8dbe5e968783d5dd7520c3.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> can be anything, to minimize the value we set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/6/3d6dc714776522a81d7277e57165e167a83199db.png" class="latex" alt="$\gamma=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" />, so then the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/787e99dbeb1c405bcd322eff2abfc01e87d8d6c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{1^2+1^2}=\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="119" height="19" />. Thus, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p><p>By: Quaratinium
</p>
