<p>A basketball player made <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> baskets during a game. Each basket was worth either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> points. How many different numbers could represent the total points scored by the player?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/3/42378a91350e833fafb2a76b7df85d17a25fe36e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 2 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>If the basketball player makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> three-point shots and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c445a843f7b9a5ae5e089d62c7a3d22d55a9bee6.png" class="latex" alt="$5-x$" width="41" height="12" /> two-point shots, he scores <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/5/2c5b5586e75953b490e31e9030e911027bd52de3.png" class="latex" alt="$3x+2(5-x)=10+x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="18" /> points. Clearly every value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> yields a different number of total points. Since he can make any number of three-point shots between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> inclusive, the number of different point totals is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45fd367d8f8aeca902a7577e49025815206e6f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \Rightarrow E$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Stars and bars can also be utilized to solve this problem. Since we need to decide what number of 2's and 3's are scored, and there are a total of 5 shots. It can be written like such: _ _ _ | _ _. Solving this, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/f/45fd367d8f8aeca902a7577e49025815206e6f4b.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \Rightarrow E$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />.
</p>
