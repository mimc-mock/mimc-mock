<p>A bakery owner turns on his doughnut machine at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/8/bf8d88a6cfd8c1b6ae7fcdab7a3d9b98136f1122.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{8:30}\ {\small\text{AM}}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="12" />. At <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/a/30a3b3a8b0ad2c96d2767fc116b03d930b934db9.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{11:10}\ {\small\text{AM}}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="12" /> the machine has completed one third of the day's job. At what time will the doughnut machine complete the job?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/f/3ef148899b30592b70054b8b9ccc37c704a956f1.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \text{1:50}\ {\small\text{PM}}\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \text{3:00}\ {\small\text{PM}}\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \text{3:30}\ {\small\text{PM}}\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \text{4:30}\ {\small\text{PM}}\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ \text{5:50}\ {\small\text{PM}}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="594" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The machine completes one-third of the job in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/6/e06fd3f6d128e56096d38c4e42e671f4a6b30fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{11:10}-\text{8:30}=\text{2:40}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="143" height="12" /> hours. Thus, the entire job is completed in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e24a0041547641389c79e9c49cd6066b055977f.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot(\text{2:40})=\text{8:00}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" /> hours.
</p><p>Since the machine was started at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/e/14e2e32d2a2758f58a5ba05f921e703eb5e0159a.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{8:30 AM}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="12" />, the job will be finished <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> hours later, at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bb881405ea47037c708fdeab84038854dad98ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{4:30 PM}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p><p>Note: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/9/749e5a619278a8bf88617f6e9ce4527b03054acb.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{2:40}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="29" height="12" /> means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hours and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> minutes. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> multiplied by this time interval is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> hours.
</p>
