<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>This sequence can also be expressed using matrix multiplication as follows: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/4/8145c3c2d40b98971d259f7fefb379aaf5fea0fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\left[ \begin{array}{c} a_{n+1} \\ b_{n+1} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} \sqrt{3} &amp; -1 \\ 1 &amp; \sqrt{3} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} a_{n} \\ b_{n} \end{array} \right] = 2 \left[ \begin{array}{cc} \cos 30^\circ &amp; -\sin 30^\circ \\ \sin 30^\circ &amp; \ \cos 30^\circ \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} a_{n} \\ b_{n} \end{array} \right]$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="515" height="43" />. 
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/49329d3a32f52ad960c2396d11bb9906793df7d0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_{n+1} , b_{n+1})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> is formed by rotating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/f/3dfa275d1b489f988d3e46fddc8a992b02438e05.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_n , b_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> counter-clockwise about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919bf5d461970fc8d905d63704ef763c08594184.png" class="latex" alt="$30^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> and dilating the point's position with respect to the origin by a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>So, starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/8/5a83ddcdda3f888e11d8b786ea13f4e093930d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_{100},b_{100})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> and performing the above operations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> times in reverse yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd5eaa48b2c8e2511c5463aa85f6dacc5b9c409.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_1,b_1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Rotating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f4d1e83d6eef61f31d78844deb189c54a7abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> clockwise by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/8/0f80cee9f534e9817e788783274ead0f4e34d677.png" class="latex" alt="$99 \cdot 30^\circ \equiv 90^\circ$" width="105" height="13" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/3/043b3c9c08690a58e011eb238316a58295360bed.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />. A dilation by a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/0/e301b2f9e794a850cab75d3ce3fc56d53c2c384b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2^{99}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> yields the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/2/e425203dfa45b4aea0761d8250b1b3f55d2267df.png" class="latex" alt="$(a_1,b_1) = \left(\frac{4}{2^{99}}, -\frac{2}{2^{99}} \right) = \left(\frac{1}{2^{97}}, -\frac{1}{2^{98}} \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="305" height="43" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/d/cdd28743082dfb41dbda2c2ffca311e6f42e4e04.png" class="latex" alt="$a_1 + b_1 = \frac{1}{2^{97}} - \frac{1}{2^{98}} = \frac{1}{2^{98}} \Rightarrow D$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="250" height="37" />.
</p>
