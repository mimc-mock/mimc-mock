<p>What is the area of the region defined by the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Inequality" title="Inequality">inequality</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/7/0b732c7132b14e1d2127007081a9af5e4c41c2e0.png" class="latex" alt="$|3x-18|+|2y+7|\le3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="185" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/c/3fc0ec7234aa0ba2530edf248c3b29b2cee131af.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 3\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{7}{2}\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ 4\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{9}{2}\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="350" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Area is invariant under translation, so after translating left <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and up <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/9/2e91fa856328e9040eeb941655c40732ee86f0f7.png" class="latex" alt="$7/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" /> units, we have the inequality
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a19f2f8dd7ca0806a76ba3bfc99a47c9ab449dbb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|3x| + |2y|\leq 3\]" width="111" height="18" />
</p><p>which forms a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Rhombus" title="Rhombus">diamond</a> centered at the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Origin" title="Origin">origin</a> and vertices at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/a/4caf927860c15ba28049dc7e26fe77f56b6ec52f.png" class="latex" alt="$(\pm 1, 0), (0, \pm 1.5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />. Thus the diagonals are of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Using the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76893a5624c5af893e34ea0994fd0e475046b29.png" class="latex" alt="$A = \frac 12 d_1 d_2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="83" height="37" />, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e201db83df44f96ae855439a7a89a6de5ef3e238.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(2)(3) = 3 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="147" height="37" />.
</p>
