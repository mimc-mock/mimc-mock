<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Function" title="Function">function</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> has <a href="/wiki/index.php/Domain" title="Domain">domain</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/7/9076ffb2b9c051f1b6f07bf18066581d57216258.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" /> and <a href="/wiki/index.php/Range" title="Range">range</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/6/e861e10e1c19918756b9c8b7717684593c63aeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="34" height="18" />. (The notation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ecbd1ba3da8f2adef66a63f2ab32c47e63fa734.png" class="latex" alt="$[a,b]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="33" height="18" /> denotes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/c/eec59c5bea4db239785d4cca7142fdf2c8b1689b.png" class="latex" alt="$\{x:a \le x \le b \}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" />.) What are the domain and range, respectively, of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> defined by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/7/657a26dfd4167650c15ab3522868ee748fa7eb3b.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)=1-f(x+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="154" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/8/78886ac42b5d0e223a915fbc087f11ad5bb80640.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ [-1,1],[-1,0]\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ [-1,1],[0,1]\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ [0,2],[-1,0]\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ [1,3],[-1,0]\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ [1,3],[0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="771" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" /> is defined if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b964c6561f466b7f03b558c173d127b41f7f3778.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> is defined. Thus the domain is all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8b6624181948180d27595212f0b42c0aceed048.png" class="latex" alt="$x| x + 1 \in [0,2] \rightarrow x \in [ - 1,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="225" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/9/629de8878872a37847132bd922824fc8b712810d.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x + 1) \in [0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="123" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/9/cc930a8e58693cb8571f0405363867f1f5d76983.png" class="latex" alt="$- f(x + 1) \in [ - 1,0]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="151" height="18" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/3/8834c52dfda63c5f6ffb6a790b72dadf548aa29b.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x) = 1 - f(x + 1) \in [0,1]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="212" height="18" /> is the range of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659a6a2610357101d77dbae8eb9bf0b3100c08fd.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/0/1f0db379b8d21a2b1747508605654327f5c0a7e9.png" class="latex" alt="$[- 1,1],[0,1] \longrightarrow \boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="160" height="24" />.
</p>
