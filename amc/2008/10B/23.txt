<p>A rectangular floor measures <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> feet, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are positive integers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/18850c5a6f67139956c8c1e35094c0a864ecb8ca.png" class="latex" alt="$b &gt; a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />. An artist paints a rectangle on the floor with the sides of the rectangle parallel to the floor. The unpainted part of the floor forms a border of width <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> foot around the painted rectangle and occupies half the area of the whole floor. How many possibilities are there for the ordered pair <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/9959d47fffb760b6cf89894df252b99436e4d8f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Because the unpainted part of the floor covers half the area, then the painted rectangle covers half the area as well. Since the border width is 1 foot, the dimensions of the rectangle are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61c1dd3bbde066f5334db006cebf1c06cbe03807.png" class="latex" alt="$a-2$" width="40" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/2/ac2315e2ea63caf8a4b7e55c1363c55715803472.png" class="latex" alt="$b-2$" width="38" height="12" />. With this information we can make the equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/d/acd3a3c5ea6b851c03f4fa5f8bda9a411a904fdd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} ab &amp;=&amp; 2\left((a-2)(b-2)\right) \\ ab &amp;=&amp; 2ab - 4a - 4b + 8 \\ ab - 4a - 4b + 8 &amp;=&amp; 0  \end{eqnarray*}" width="313" height="65" />
Applying <a href="/wiki/index.php/Simon%27s_Favorite_Factoring_Trick" title="Simon&#39;s Favorite Factoring Trick">Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick</a>, we get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9df47b9e32588f9cfce3dacd23da7cc9313c8656.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*}ab - 4a - 4b + 16 &amp;=&amp; 8 \\ (a-4)(b-4) &amp;=&amp; 8 \end{eqnarray*}" width="193" height="42" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/8/18850c5a6f67139956c8c1e35094c0a864ecb8ca.png" class="latex" alt="$b &gt; a$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, then we have the possibilities <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea813496bc0f9d776e2bd90ef65031e7b94a2ea5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4) = 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="87" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/4/e648a0aedd5dc11b2257e49d85ed937685dde4bd.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-4) = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/2/462a59b3600aeddbc8084b61fbac286338e94da6.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4) = 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b96aad980355edeb5064129abb0e4b4896832344.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-4) = 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />. This allows for 2 possibilities: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/2/372b122f51cad9f701b1d946b28c2f07f55c18de.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,12)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/d/0bd97f1a6f1d76907ee5c4460c2f1fe587beb8d5.png" class="latex" alt="$(6,8)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/813dfcfc8cacde52072f9c998b67c6f7158e0bfe.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) 2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />
</p>
