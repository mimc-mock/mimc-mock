<p>Points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> are on a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8e7d324ea465aef56f3f3e708d966da9db8e90e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=6$" width="61" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Midpoint" title="Midpoint">midpoint</a> of the minor arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. What is the length of the line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/e/c0e28752d93e4394719fd81fa0a030a81f7d3f3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \sqrt{10}\qquad\mathrm{(B)}\ \frac{7}{2}\qquad\mathrm{(C)}\ \sqrt{14}\qquad\mathrm{(D)}\ \sqrt{15}\qquad\mathrm{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="419" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the center of the circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/1/0019a9c6ae9bef5eda9166daa34ea37465190dd1.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{OC}$" width="29" height="15" /> (then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />). <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/6/bd6f8e841ed27ce2071718832e98b6d1e38c4d2e.png" class="latex" alt="$OA=OB=5$" width="114" height="13" />, since they are both radii of the circle.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/3/dd3aa670574ebe99dcdc49faa66af5ec3c509a15.png" class="latex" alt="$OD = \sqrt{OA^2 - DA^2} = 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="197" height="18" />, and by subtraction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f779926ba104b640634edc11457011f8bba9cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=OC - OD = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="167" height="13" />.
</p><p>Using the Pythagorean Theorem again, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/3/e23643ee15cf0d5dd521a08a185cad79958435bd.png" class="latex" alt="$AC= \sqrt{AD^2 + CD^2} = \sqrt{3^2+1^2}=\sqrt{10} \Longrightarrow \textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="383" height="21" />.
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72fbe8b90fcde66500984b26ba2b38b0f7765119.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pen d = linewidth(0.7); pathpen = d; pointpen = black; pen f = fontsize(9); path p = CR((0,0),5); pair O = (0,0), A=(5,0), B = IP(p,CR(A,6)), C = IP(p,CR(A,3)), D=IP(A--B,O--C); D(p); D(MP(&quot;A&quot;,A,E)--D(MP(&quot;O&quot;,O))--MP(&quot;B&quot;,B,NE)--cycle); D(A--MP(&quot;C&quot;,C,ENE),dashed+d); D(O--C,dashed+d); D(rightanglemark(O,D(MP(&quot;D&quot;,D,W)),A)); MP(&quot;5&quot;,(A+O)/2); MP(&quot;3&quot;,(A+D)/2,SW); [/asy]" width="668" height="652" /></center>
