<p>A cylindrical tank with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> feet and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> feet is lying on its side. The tank is filled with water to a depth of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> feet. What is the volume of water, in cubic feet?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a62b57c568cffc0fb93acec0563804defc7d308.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 24\pi - 36 \sqrt {2} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 24\pi - 24 \sqrt {3} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 36\pi - 36 \sqrt {3} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 36\pi - 24 \sqrt {2} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 48\pi - 36 \sqrt {3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="766" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span id="Video_Solution(Based_on_Solution_1)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution.28Based_on_Solution_1.29">Video Solution(Based on Solution 1)</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9RRrumPRK4">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9RRrumPRK4</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Any vertical cross-section of the tank parallel with its base looks as follows:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a702ddd98af1b8d46b21db963afe5d1ae0d985.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.8cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair s=(0,0), bottom=(0,-4), mid=(0,-2); pair x[]=intersectionpoints( (-10,-2)--(10,-2), circle(s,4) ); fill( arc(s,x[0],x[1]) -- cycle, lightgray ); draw( circle(s,4) ); dot(s); draw( s -- bottom ); label( &quot;$2$&quot;, (mid+bottom)/2, E ); draw ( s -- x[0] -- x[1] -- s ); label( &quot;$4$&quot;, (s+x[0])/2, NW ); label( &quot;$4$&quot;, (s+x[1])/2, NE ); label( &quot;$A$&quot;, s, N ); label( &quot;$B$&quot;, x[0], W ); label( &quot;$C$&quot;, x[1], E ); label( &quot;$D$&quot;, mid, NW ); label( &quot;$E$&quot;, bottom, S ); [/asy]" width="312" height="328" />
</p><p>The volume of water can be computed as the height of the tank times the area of the shaded part.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> be the size of the smaller angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/2/f6250647b3666c03d1363b3a401f37c1d9db3c5f.png" class="latex" alt="$DAC$" width="43" height="13" />. We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bda1e0012223e45a8f715966f79cbd65f93944a.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos\theta = \frac{AD}{AC}=\frac 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="129" height="38" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/2/8d25be641607d3ca0d4632143cd3507a0c384b16.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta=60^\circ$" width="57" height="13" />.
</p><p>The figure is symmetrical, so the angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/9/459b39e026037707282c95dfdbfd6dc64c855a16.png" class="latex" alt="$CAB$" width="42" height="13" /> has size <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/3/9a3b3a6ac9ce35e81e994a4bb0262344d345d2ae.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot 60^\circ = 120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" />. Hence the shaded part consists of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/a/b8abd5f84144827235aa7c1926fdd1090ce59c76.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac 13$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="74" height="37" /> of the circle, minus the area of the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />.
</p><p>Using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> we can compute that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/9/2795ab70cffa329497c67a1f5424b8231218d4fd.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=\sqrt{AC^2-AD^2}=\sqrt{16-4}=2\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="310" height="19" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/9/c39ac0f06b9847b380a95b581e394034ea2ebd5b.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=4\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="18" />, and the area of the triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/6/2f67850bb5cd690dc2f5ac506731154d45854e8a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {2 \cdot 4\sqrt 3} 2 = 4\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="117" height="41" />.
</p><p>The area of the shaded part is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/7/017ee37972b97e3452c3a06c13940f9088d6a575.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4^2\pi}3 - 4\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="39" />, and the volume of water is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/5/9e582fa2dca848a8b34fb98eb82fac713a7255b7.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot\left(\frac{4^2\pi}3 - 4\sqrt 3\right) = \boxed{48\pi - 36\sqrt 3}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="265" height="43" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a5bb2aa78132b4b716cfde4a9136c884d94c753.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{E}$" width="11" height="11" />.
</p><p>Alternatively, we can solve for DC and see it is congruent to a 30 60 90 triangle by SSS ~Williamgolly
</p>
