<p>Four distinct points are arranged on a plane so that the segments connecting them have lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. What is the ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/2/3627370e7bc10161933d1986d635c4dadd7fe5a8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \sqrt{5}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \pi$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="381" height="21" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>When you see that there are lengths a and 2a, one could think of 30-60-90 triangles. Since all of the other's lengths are a, you could think that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32ebcd4f68e948f835299ae0f5ac59f0b174c826.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\sqrt{3}a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />.
Drawing the points out, it is possible to have a diagram where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32ebcd4f68e948f835299ae0f5ac59f0b174c826.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\sqrt{3}a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />. It turns out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/8/7c8acfd7d5ee559262593701b8dbd02e43ad96e3.png" class="latex" alt="$a,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/a/1ea65f8b35707fe994bb8a25490b36bdd01e5d3e.png" class="latex" alt="$2a,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> could be the lengths of a 30-60-90 triangle, and the other 3 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/a/baaa579ecb56662a750219a56a30528f0cab29f2.png" class="latex" alt="$a\text{&#39;s}$" width="20" height="11" /> can be the lengths of an equilateral triangle formed from connecting the dots.
So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32ebcd4f68e948f835299ae0f5ac59f0b174c826.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\sqrt{3}a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae1a2f41d402ddfce3983da3ba70c0d1b251a2dc.png" class="latex" alt="$b:a= \boxed{\textbf{(A)} \: \sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="126" height="33" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c169ac2474df3176c192ec03c2214eff85ecce50.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]draw((0, 0)--(1/2, sqrt(3)/2)--(1, 0)--cycle); draw((1/2, sqrt(3)/2)--(2, 0)--(1,0)); label(&quot;$a$&quot;, (0, 0)--(1, 0), S); label(&quot;$a$&quot;, (1, 0)--(2, 0), S); label(&quot;$a$&quot;, (0, 0)--(1/2, sqrt(3)/2), NW); label(&quot;$a$&quot;, (1, 0)--(1/2, sqrt(3)/2), NE); label(&quot;$b=\sqrt{3}a$&quot;, (1/2, sqrt(3)/2)--(2, 0), NE); [/asy]" width="252" height="128" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>For any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> non-collinear points with the given requirement, notice that there must be a triangle with side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" />, which is not possible as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/0/8108e2bcfba487db56d209624844fc797123b213.png" class="latex" alt="$a+a=2a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="13" />. Thus at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> points must be collinear. 
</p><p>If all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> points are collinear, then there would only be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> lines of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, which wouldn't work.
</p><p>If exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> points are collinear, the only possibility that works is when a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4639838d98ea82f9cf1ed539e607c70a3f0520.png" class="latex" alt="$30^{\circ}-90^{\circ}-60^{\circ}$" width="120" height="13" /> triangle is formed.
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/e/32ebcd4f68e948f835299ae0f5ac59f0b174c826.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\sqrt{3}a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="66" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/2/402fb23a67f2f9b67cfd93f3f728e16d5ab6128a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{a}=\boxed{\mathrm{(A)}\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="101" height="37" />
</p><p>~ Nafer
