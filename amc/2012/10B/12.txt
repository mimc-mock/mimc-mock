<p>Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is due east of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is due north of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. The distance between points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/1/111767ba8fce0a85eaefe250e524c60588aadbc9.png" class="latex" alt="$10\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="43" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3d9348f066c43453a7cc8e2ccaed4948d2d0d95.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> meters due north of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. The distance <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> is between which two integers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/8/4684c0b1d6f411e3a20568604fe86b0f57f9f2cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30\ \text{and}\ 31 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 31\ \text{and}\ 32 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 32\ \text{and}\ 33 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 33\ \text{and}\ 34 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 34\ \text{and}\ 35$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="674" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/3/ec398827b66ac43e40e090ab5d0282314660eadd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(4); pair A=(0,0); label (&quot;A&quot;,(0,0),W); pair B=(10,0); label (&quot;B&quot;,(10,0),E); pair C=(10,10); label (&quot;C&quot;,(10,10),E); pair D=(10,30); label (&quot;D&quot;,(10,30),E); dot(A); dot(B); dot(C); dot(D); draw(A--B); draw(A--C); draw(A--D); draw(C--D); draw(B--C); [/asy]" width="115" height="222" />
If point B is due east of point A and point C is due north of point B, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7cc1a19a004754af5a3459c905ca7b927f04b0b4.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CBA$" width="55" height="13" /> is a right angle. And if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3d9348f066c43453a7cc8e2ccaed4948d2d0d95.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/9/e09d223fe3a393a817bc4d1b325f60453a236abe.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CBA$" width="58" height="13" /> is a 45-45-90 triangle. Thus, the lengths of sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e3a4ec0df571e719bd3f1d7e00bf71fc55ba2b9.png" class="latex" alt="$CB$" width="28" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d66492998689e8303534c9e4f3ba1fa650fd41f0.png" class="latex" alt="$BA$" width="28" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> are in the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/8/d388c134d20acfc03ab6b8970ed2c333604a3978.png" class="latex" alt="$1:1:\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e3a4ec0df571e719bd3f1d7e00bf71fc55ba2b9.png" class="latex" alt="$CB$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/2/b928098f8efa361f4c0313e3a766890e9c8df993.png" class="latex" alt="$10 \sqrt 2 \div \sqrt 2 = 10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/9/d89d9c6e34af80070cc62b22b31eb98a6867cf72.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DBA$" width="60" height="13" /> is clearly a right triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> on the side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e525b776c49bbe7d0ae66cfc7ab8f58f40624e.png" class="latex" alt="$DC$" width="29" height="12" /> is 20, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/9/3f9371655650fa27e7ccb6e5861007a5850ef830.png" class="latex" alt="$DB = DC + CB = 20 + 10 = 30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="260" height="14" />. 
</p><p>By the Pythagorean Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af4a64e0dc663457a540d352d5e815d2d1955b43.png" class="latex" alt="$DA = \sqrt {DB^2 + BA^2} = \sqrt {30^2 + 10 ^2} = \sqrt {1000}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="353" height="19" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/a/aaaf93d6f589f7f63bdbe8da0314dd180738540f.png" class="latex" alt="$31^2 = 961$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/a/f8a85557b34a44f4ca4feb3dfb565ede731cff63.png" class="latex" alt="$32^2 = 1024$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="15" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/5/42568adda464e3f869f36e87b7edf63debc7c2b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt {1000}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="18" /> must be between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/6/296fac336caaa4265aa24b6ecbc408a348ad7e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$31$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/d/86d5aa162c7537ea0463bb8efcec69113e93cdbd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
