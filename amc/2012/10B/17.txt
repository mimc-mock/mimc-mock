<p>Jesse cuts a circular paper disk of radius 12 along two radii to form two sectors, the smaller having a central angle of 120 degrees. He makes two circular cones, using each sector to form the lateral surface of a cone. What is the ratio of the volume of the smaller cone to that of the larger?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59fe4cad279f9ab6ceb23db4c54e2a56c912de9c.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A)} \frac{1}{8} \qquad \text{(B)} \frac{1}{4} \qquad \text{(C)} \frac{\sqrt{10}}{10} \qquad \text{(D)} \frac{\sqrt{5}}{6} \qquad \text{(E)} \frac{\sqrt{5}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="376" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let's find the volume of the smaller cone first. We know that the circumference of the paper disk is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca72da509d0e888f69331dc91184a176fef7dae5.png" class="latex" alt="$24\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="28" height="12" />, so the circumference of the smaller cone would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/7/717f10c8be94d37b48a1b9e38677ad02957ba90d.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{120}{360} \times 24\pi = 8\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />. This means that the radius of the smaller cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Since the radius of the paper disk is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, the slant height of the smaller cone would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. By the Pythagorean Theorem, the height of the cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df2ac941e02de0cafe92b319f1046348c1e57d0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{12^2-4^2}=8\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="137" height="19" />. Thus, the volume of the smaller cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/415c06e5bd4e3a5c92107f84d8c4252cfd0ab65d.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{3} \times 4^2\pi \times 8\sqrt{2}=\dfrac{128\sqrt{2}}{3}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="208" height="41" />.
</p><p>Now, we need to find the volume of the larger cone. Using the same reason as above, we get that the radius is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and the slant height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. By the Pythagorean Theorem again, the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/7/d1768a4753badeaf5cea89030367958ead46a571.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{12^2-8^2}=4\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="137" height="19" />. Thus, the volume of the larger cone is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b96b7fa346d44a96fecfa3387dafc7e665cf109.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{3} \times 8^2\pi \times 4\sqrt{5} = \dfrac{256\sqrt{5}}{3}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="208" height="41" />.
</p><p>The question asked for the ratio of the volume of the smaller cone to the larger cone. We need to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/8/bd8290fead31e944490375ca3ec4db527cf84877.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{128\sqrt{2}}{3}\pi}{\dfrac{256\sqrt{5}}{3}\pi}=\dfrac{\sqrt{10}}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -39px" width="130" height="87" /> after simplifying, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/c/1acc2d8106aba563c4db2d35b9127e61ecde0238.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(C)  \frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="77" height="53" /> .
</p>
<ul><li>A side note</li></ul>
<p>We can first simplify the volume ratio: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/4/154f95f2c3f713e901cab92c31512b4ecb74b0cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{(r_1)^2 \cdot h_1}{(r_2)^2 \cdot h_2}.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="122" height="43" /> Now we can find the GENERAL formulas for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> based on the original circle radius and angle to cut out, then we can substitute the appropriate numbers, which gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/2/792daa030c96693198f143f9176bf888b7ca25cd.png" class="latex" alt="$C.$" width="17" height="12" />
</p>
