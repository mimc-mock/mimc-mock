<p>Suppose that one of every 500 people in a certain population has a particular disease, which displays no symptoms. A blood test is available for screening for this disease. For a person who has this disease, the test always turns out positive. For a person who does not have the disease, however, there is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/0/0b04a3bd51b4c6a454c3aae545a1dbc6a5bff05d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="23" height="14" /> false positive rate--in other words, for such people, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/6/30696f72e40dac44ba3d77958f23e54518505ca9.png" class="latex" alt="$98\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> of the time the test will turn out negative, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/0/0b04a3bd51b4c6a454c3aae545a1dbc6a5bff05d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="23" height="14" /> of the time the test will turn out positive and will incorrectly indicate that the person has the disease. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> be the probability that a person who is chosen at random from this population and gets a positive test result actually has the disease. Which of the following is closest to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/6/02635f12f403b891c26538073d75aeefe31ef18b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{98}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{9}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{1}{11}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{49}{99}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{98}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="404" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>This question can be solved by considering all the possibilities:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" /> people will have the disease and will be tested positive for the disease.
</p><p>Out of the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/0/6500248a8e3334fcfb20b237b0e343980327ad17.png" class="latex" alt="$499$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> people, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/0/0b04a3bd51b4c6a454c3aae545a1dbc6a5bff05d.png" class="latex" alt="$2\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="23" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55da1aee425075edfe336d0d8f368383690b13ee.png" class="latex" alt="$9.98$" width="31" height="12" /> people, will be tested positive for the disease incorrectly.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> can be found by taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/0/a00fb1b078eb46ad08064e412de4310c78078ea8.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{1+9.98}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="66" height="38" />, which is closest to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/f/d9f5c714f4589ca841e79b0ae10a0ffa2eae7f37.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{1+10}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="51" height="38" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22afcbc99797f29eaa84c1dbf30bf0670acc30d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />,or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>This question can also be solved by using a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/2/6325a27e1b67d110bb8f0aafbed2e1c3e2d60992.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{probability1}{probability2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="101" height="43" /> solution. 
</p><p>If the test is positive there are two cases: (1) The person was true-positive (2) The person was false-positive
</p><p>For the first case, the probability of the person being true-positive is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/b/82b7bf02eac53496952c04e63a95129968725abd.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{500}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="29" height="37" />.
</p><p>For the second case, the probability of the person being false-positive is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4ce52a82d7eddf6f56f171a455275bf0cf30f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{499}{500}\cdot\dfrac{1}{50}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />
</p><p>The probability of the test being positive is the sum of these values, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/3/aa33af020cccf7df745109354dcae093123428c2.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{549}{25000}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="51" height="38" />
</p><p>The final probability will be in the form: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/8/7b8c14535d1b06256ed23155206f22359f89b597.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{P(true positive)}{P(positive)}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="132" height="45" />.
</p><p>Plugging in the values we get: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/0/4a0fedc436eae7a04d6b66acee8e3b97e682f86e.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{1}{500}}{\dfrac{549}{25000}}=\dfrac{50}{549}$" style="vertical-align: -35px" width="111" height="80" /> This fraction is clearly closest to  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/a/22afcbc99797f29eaa84c1dbf30bf0670acc30d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{11}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />
</p>
