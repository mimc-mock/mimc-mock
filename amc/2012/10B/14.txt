<p>Two equilateral triangles are contained in square whose side length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/8/cd8259912929d3140ef16477ddfb545a4331623b.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. The bases of these triangles are the opposite side of the square, and their intersection is a rhombus. What is the area of the rhombus?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/a/39ac23b1e03bac20b278c15ad58c757275af6353.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } \frac{3}{2} \qquad \text{(B) } \sqrt 3 \qquad \text{(C) } 2\sqrt 2 -   1  \qquad \text{(D) } 8\sqrt 3 - 12 \qquad \text{(E)}  \frac{4\sqrt 3}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="491" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/4/1b491bea6ff8007acc151d99ff17f5f0ab905857.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(8cm); pair A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, BF, AF; A = (0,0); B = (1,0); C = (1,1); D = (0,1); E = (1/2,0); H = (1/2,1); G = (1/2,1/2^(1/2)); F = (1/2,1-(1/2^(1/2))); AF = (3^(1/2)/2,1/2); BF = (1-3^(1/2)/2,1/2); draw(A--B--C--D--A--AF--D); draw(C--BF--B); draw(H--E,linetype(&quot;8 8&quot;)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,S); label(&quot;$H$&quot;,H,N); label(&quot;$G$&quot;,G+1/20,E); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F-1/20,W); [/asy]" width="378" height="378" /></center>
<p>Observe that the rhombus is made up of two congruent equilateral triangles with side length equal to GF. Since AE has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> and triangle AEF is a 30-60-90 triangle, it follows that EF has length 1. By symmetry, HG also has length 1. Thus GF has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/4093e34760fbae62e6c2d0969b4003b95bf863d4.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{3} - 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="64" height="18" />. The formula for the area of an equilateral triangle of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/f/92fe9758cda7434b713a2a906f0931971f4aff4a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="42" height="41" />. It follows that the area of the rhombus is:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/7/c976fc9c047e0bb93d79512651d7f314e3b6ed73.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(2\sqrt{3}-2)^2 = \boxed{\mathbf{(D)} 8\sqrt{3} - 12}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="288" height="41" />
</p>
