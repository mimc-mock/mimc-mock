<p>A fruit salad consists of blueberries, raspberries, grapes, and cherries.  The fruit salad has a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/1/a11fabd799fd8a2b00222b3895f3cff5e032066a.png" class="latex" alt="$280$" width="26" height="12" /> pieces of fruit.  There are twice as many raspberries as blueberries, three times as many grapes as cherries, and four times as many cherries as raspberries.  How many cherries are there in the fruit salad?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/8/2c8b6b178aafdfe83464e9e4fe5bf30675d729f0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 25\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 64\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 96$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>So let the number of blueberries be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d6265e67657b5886ce257671d45ff9c0282eb.png" class="latex" alt="$b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="12" height="16" /> the number of raspberries be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/6/e1630c915bd1b27bfa1f505bf1ac0bb9f5d61f32.png" class="latex" alt="$r,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="11" height="11" /> the number of grapes be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/b/1db81d75ebc2fb7a1598b08fcb48f05cc9e73202.png" class="latex" alt="$g,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="11" /> and finally the number of cherries be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/201a2eef545643b50b07fed5c09e894bcced5c58.png" class="latex" alt="$c.$" width="11" height="8" />
</p><p>Observe that since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/1/a11fabd799fd8a2b00222b3895f3cff5e032066a.png" class="latex" alt="$280$" width="26" height="12" /> pieces of fruit,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38e13b54d1ccd5c52ea7d45d283ef7ccae426a5f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b+r+g+c=280.\]" width="154" height="16" />
</p><p>Since there are twice as many raspberries as blueberries,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3ae4880cd0b104efc38649cb19fa55186cdbfe4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2b=r.\]" width="52" height="12" />
</p><p>The fact that there are three times as many grapes as cherries implies,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/e/c8e7d9bc5ee6212edbe561fa4c33a34cf9680e68.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3c=g.\]" width="53" height="16" />
</p><p>Because there are four times as many cherries as raspberries, we deduce the following:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/c/54cea1f40d6de1b653d3e91349ea7525cf0ce26d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4r=c.\]" width="53" height="12" />
</p><p>Note that we are looking for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/201a2eef545643b50b07fed5c09e894bcced5c58.png" class="latex" alt="$c.$" width="11" height="8" />  So, we try to rewrite all of the other variables in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/1/201a2eef545643b50b07fed5c09e894bcced5c58.png" class="latex" alt="$c.$" width="11" height="8" />
The third equation gives us the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> already.  We divide the fourth equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> to get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2ad3030554513c3a68ac57698760762e0351313.png" class="latex" alt="$r=\frac{c}{4}.$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="49" height="33" />  Finally, substituting this value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> into the first equation provides us with the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8ce64acd2da7fa88b0ca2ac6703138bf3a1d7e8f.png" class="latex" alt="$b=\frac{c}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="33" /> and substituting yields:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/7/b276900e7b6be663c4d777224475d99e610ac30d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{c}{4}+\frac{c}{8}+3c+c=280\]" width="166" height="33" />
Multiply this equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> to get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/2/a822820bc1a07f28661af5fdf879635cca47b4c7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2c+c+24c+8c=8\cdot 280,\]" width="211" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/7/ed7ee4bf44aee65b1ea574ffcb64ddf72e73ccc4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[35c=8\cdot 280,\]" width="103" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb07c565cbc4e84c339061fc43debdf38c94d00b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c=64.\]" width="53" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/d/dadc8e71363a80c53a137421994b9e02de6eeabd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\boxed{D}\]" width="27" height="24" />
</p>
