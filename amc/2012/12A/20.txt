<p>Consider the polynomial
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/d/33ddd0db31c6fb3476fca1a836a076c90c2c0841.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(x)=\prod_{k=0}^{10}(x^{2^k}+2^k)=(x+1)(x^2+2)(x^4+4)\cdots (x^{1024}+1024)\]" width="477" height="50" />
</p><p>The coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c169299278d7ec9dcdd079973f674e84e9e7f33d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2012}$" width="36" height="15" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/5/b752b796e9f32efcbd969c24a631e9e5f45f787e.png" class="latex" alt="$2^a$" width="16" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/0/ff00a33605701fb3b8adff21802c46d01211a2ed.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\textbf{(A)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 24\]" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Every term in the expansion of the product is formed by taking one term from each factor and multiplying them all together. Therefore, we pick a power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> or a power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> from each factor.
</p><p>Every number, including <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, has a unique representation by the sum of powers of two, and that representation can be found by converting a number to its binary form. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50db0424ba4cf7f357d8cfa026480cb132bf1ec4.png" class="latex" alt="$2012 = 11111011100_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="166" height="15" />, meaning <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/6/a265686ee7d0994e043661b8051c50ca46111e7e.png" class="latex" alt="$2012 = 1024 + 512 + 256 + 128 + 64 + 16 + 8 + 4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="389" height="14" />. 
</p><p>Thus, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c169299278d7ec9dcdd079973f674e84e9e7f33d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2012}$" width="36" height="15" /> term was made by multiplying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/b/33b8a2e0f36e41b880812d47763714264756b52d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{1024}$" width="36" height="15" /> from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f35990da086831e688eaacb72cf38039ce5744.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^{1024} + 1024)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="19" /> factor, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef8b342d8cdffb84209786c2aeae7ab84ecf2863.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{512}$" width="29" height="15" /> from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/e/2cef08947ab29fe0f1d56db65d7e7ed0fafd8866.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^{512} + 512)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="19" /> factor, and so on. The only numbers not used are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Thus, from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a95797a3b32a81904d8e61a69bdf0a22bc969a5c.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^{32} + 32), (x^2+2), (x+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="212" height="19" /> factors, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f4b8590f8aa9f9894b3891dd5dede0e354fa04e.png" class="latex" alt="$32$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> were chosen as opposed to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea03733594f86db4b8eb8b9f173c8dd8316e771.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{32}, x^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. 
</p><p>Thus, the coefficient of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c169299278d7ec9dcdd079973f674e84e9e7f33d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2012}$" width="36" height="15" /> term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/c/a1cc99a2686e980f8ae23bdb010b509bc2847b87.png" class="latex" alt="$32 \times 2 \times 1 = 64 = 2^6$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="162" height="15" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/b/deb5dc62496b971af98e9dbc7f93a18748182409.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \rightarrow \boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="63" height="24" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The degree of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/f/6efaaf74e576202fad965134bc49c6a34aac4c30.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/e/a2e135863579055b00ad42d85cd01780db48abc8.png" class="latex" alt="$1024+512+256+\cdots+1=2047$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="270" height="14" />. We want to find the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c169299278d7ec9dcdd079973f674e84e9e7f33d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2012}$" width="36" height="15" />, so we need to omit the powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> that add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/ffffa26d3e3fef29dbca8be26ee7d5592dd91610.png" class="latex" alt="$2047-2012=35$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="139" height="15" />. We find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/d/ecd073a4643ab0ffc15b937c86b31c9a10bcf825.png" class="latex" alt="$35=2^0+2^1+2^5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="135" height="16" />. From here, we know that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/6/cb687ac79e5be545eb4bbb74017301ec6eedbb20.png" class="latex" alt="$2^0\cdot2^1\cdot2^5=2^6$" width="115" height="15" />. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d40e35ca05443330aaad07dba9fa2e9b3aa56a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(B)\:6.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="29" />
</p>
