<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2012_AMC-12B-19.jpg" class="image"><img alt="2012 AMC-12B-19.jpg" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//6/68/2012_AMC-12B-19.jpg" width="360" height="297" /></a>
</p><p>Observe the diagram above. Each dot represents one of the six vertices of the regular octahedron. Three dots have been placed exactly x units from the (0,0,0) corner of the unit cube. The other three dots have been placed exactly x units from the (1,1,1) corner of the unit cube. A red square has been drawn connecting four of the dots to provide perspective regarding the shape of the octahedron. Observe that the three dots that are near (0,0,0) are each (x)(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />) from each other. The same is true for the three dots that are near (1,1,1). There is a unique x for which the rectangle drawn in red becomes a square. This will occur when the distance from (x,0,0) to (1,1-x, 1) is (x)(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />).
</p><p><br />
Using the distance formula we find the distance between the two points to be: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f66faad57ea6f23f69acd800d99dc7f85774abc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{{(1-x)^2} + {(1-x)^2} + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="198" height="32" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/e/dfe4e05b0dbf5e3382dde9473ef6db5ba0f735a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2x^2 - 4x +3}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="115" height="18" />. Equating this to (x)(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />) and squaring both sides, we have the equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/7/0777f6046ee39df82ba1089bf29de6963f9c313e.png" class="latex" alt="$2{x^2} - 4x + 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="16" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a143bd9d24232eb93dff39ad6cf5b01989efbb8c.png" class="latex" alt="$2{x^2}$" width="25" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/4/854dc16912bd11d99fd8365af728080e126ccbde.png" class="latex" alt="$-4x + 3 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="97" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/def33ddb26b3f0fdc46bf5806766e71a0bf02c66.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3} {4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="38" />.
</p><p><br />
Since the length of each side is (x)(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />), we have a final result of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/a/efa31327df85715f4e2a2106132654b93d41b677.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3 \sqrt{2}}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="35" height="41" />. Thus, Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/9/d79e0e8ce41b5c08729443bcc258d13ca061ff35.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" /> is correct.
</p><p><br />
(If someone can draw a better diagram with the points labeled P1,P2, etc., I would appreciate it).

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Standard 3D geometry, no coordinates. 
</p><p><br />
Let the tip of the octahedron on side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/4/7e4d19045bc8fab9ee08f56b0130f1e772624eb1.png" class="latex" alt="$P_1P_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="36" height="15" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/0/3101a8fb302472d167884ef2296671d5ded79da5.png" class="latex" alt="$K_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" /> and the opposite vertex be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/7/2a7a629a3ef8f8f46f7850ebdeac02d4b52bad2b.png" class="latex" alt="$K_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="21" height="15" />. Our key is to examine the trapezoid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/2/562e788232e2ef698309e265cb655c5246b8ce99.png" class="latex" alt="$P_1K_1K_2P_3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="17" />. 
</p><p><br />
Let the side length of the octahedron be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/6594904a94b7aa60fc4f78bb753183a96219ca62.png" class="latex" alt="$P_1K_1 = \frac{s}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="96" height="39" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/1/4713024461d3d123000168f3149052ecbc8c413a.png" class="latex" alt="$P_3K_2 = 1 - \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="128" height="43" />. Then, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/5/d355c396bf674a9f3124fb032a9efefdc092eb93.png" class="latex" alt="$P_1P_3 = \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="22" />. Finally, we want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba575fce83d891b4bf9f7d6e0e5c176b539bed1d.png" class="latex" alt="$K_1K_2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="17" />, which is just double the height of half the octahedron. We can use Pythagorean Theorem to find that height as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/b/21bb75656e06a9730ce6220119675ab302bfa6f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}s$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="21" />. Now, we use the Pythagorean Theorem on the trapezoid. We get
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/8/8d8adcc7c79a6d438fa07b959fb664510792a87c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\sqrt{2})^2 + (2\sqrt{2}-1)^2 = (s\sqrt{2})^2\]" width="230" height="21" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/9/df9b310c98ab2ff1eee8e9b4949e48b17d51708d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{4}.\]" width="73" height="41" />
