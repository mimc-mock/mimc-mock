<p>Consider all polynomials of a complex variable, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96aa7a1c2fc6ff201b88e0e1e48d836c8b439653.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)=4z^4+az^3+bz^2+cz+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="254" height="19" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8b8490a47ba3063da2731316f5d65dc5cf731ed.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/1/d41603faa533968a57aeedb02ff15976c4219380.png" class="latex" alt="$0\le d\le c\le b\le a\le 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="174" height="15" />, and the polynomial has a zero <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/2/ed2c6d195111c926da4299235b07508d65ab0cf3.png" class="latex" alt="$|z_0|=1.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" /> What is the sum of all values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/7/797a20647c81f3259c5069556cab51c94bf4b752.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> over all the polynomials with these properties?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> has integer coefficients, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> must be algebraic. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is algebraic and lies on the unit circle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> must be a root of unity (Comment: this is not true. See this link: <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/4323/are-all-algebraic-integers-with-absolute-value-1-roots-of-unity">[1]</a>). Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> has degree 4, it seems reasonable (and we will assume this only temporarily) that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> must be a 2nd, 3rd, or 4th root of unity. These are among the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/e/16e771b382cc2859914e1c3553bb04568a5d8ed9.png" class="latex" alt="$\{\pm1,\pm i,(-1\pm i\sqrt{3})/2\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="185" height="21" />. Since complex roots of polynomials come in conjugate pairs, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> has one (or more) of the following factors: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/3/b53a269eb5b759335e974de25a423ff64dd4123d.png" class="latex" alt="$z+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/e/50e0412f1e4c5bde18c5a76f43bd0c5256c8fb2a.png" class="latex" alt="$z-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/d/8fdcbae166da009fa0b34e377a4b33f471488a70.png" class="latex" alt="$z^2+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/8/7484c757ceb0e609f062314c849d48479348f122.png" class="latex" alt="$z^2+z+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="16" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd3d68e77a39e1ea97e7009f70e12622fa76835.png" class="latex" alt="$z=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af06d810b09098b10f2334ce178062a956f5404.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c+d+4=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="14" />; a contradiction since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> are non-negative. On the other hand, suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/9/ae9ee3a66b769c07dfa546f0a322c2a4053a46cf.png" class="latex" alt="$z=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd6b8273a3b803782b2f085f95ff7e2c87f0ae6.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+c)-(b+d)=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />. This implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/6/9e633b34ac10547dd7bc426689f73eebe0e35a87.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=8,7,6,5,4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="141" height="16" /> while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/b/31bc5ff5500bf75bc9101b53375ecaa68745298d.png" class="latex" alt="$b+d=4,3,2,1,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="141" height="16" /> correspondingly. After listing cases, the only such valid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d74754791ffdc64e18c4c4d2e60712ccaa26d1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$4,4,4,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/d/dcdcf9de95e563e290518e8ff521965409dd9e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4,3,3,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/2/79220dd538cba7f613acac99d0bcadbe14eca4a4.png" class="latex" alt="$4,2,2,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/2/c22f99af2410bbdacec1ca8a2a6becb424e466ea.png" class="latex" alt="$4,1,1,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/4/f0407cde112fe5bd3efa029e1501339a70b66d37.png" class="latex" alt="$4,0,0,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />. 
</p><p>Now suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/0/510a35f00aa1141b54cf02891c4fb92c37f28f24.png" class="latex" alt="$z=i$" width="39" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/2/122cd2993acfabc05d2d75961c27dd58a965f13d.png" class="latex" alt="$4=(a-c)i+(b-d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" /> whereupon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec20c892f502d4c43a3ca677b852d54d01bbdfe.png" class="latex" alt="$b-d=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />. But then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/6/566b9136144a62fac113ecbc8065793a74123238.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b=c$" width="74" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e179491182efb896336c6e12e790f55ee4dce4.png" class="latex" alt="$d=a-4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />. This gives only the cases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d74754791ffdc64e18c4c4d2e60712ccaa26d1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$4,4,4,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, which we have already counted in a previous case.
</p><p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/0/3508f4f7e48fe81774a1289ec16b80de9e4767f9.png" class="latex" alt="$z=-i$" width="53" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/c/9bc168665ac4d9d8784f5e6d9b15e2afd6c2f49f.png" class="latex" alt="$4=i(c-a)+(b-d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" /> so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba3c2d04f325d92dc551bd038d165efacf07362.png" class="latex" alt="$b=4+d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. This only gives rise to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d74754791ffdc64e18c4c4d2e60712ccaa26d1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$4,4,4,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> which we have previously counted. 
</p><p>Finally suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/8/7484c757ceb0e609f062314c849d48479348f122.png" class="latex" alt="$z^2+z+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="16" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" />. Using polynomial division ((or that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/c/11ce6380524a246b8a433a324fb40b273ef15c89.png" class="latex" alt="$z^3=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="15" /> to make the same deductions) we ultimately obtain that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/5/a85902a4ba1e008cb835180d42e7eacc13147e47.png" class="latex" alt="$b=4+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" />. This can only happen if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb60e0857a2c0a2c47acfbe81781032feb1f7150.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b83a6327a89e13681f3db0a3baa38f406ef2b75.png" class="latex" alt="$4,4,0,0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />. 
</p><p>Hence we've the polynomials
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/4/ce436d86eb88e27b564d6310dd17d853826fe76c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3+4x^2+4x\]" width="166" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/8/2981003b1c06a870ff70bd010dde58ae75af9398.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3+3x^2+3x\]" width="166" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/0/d4089891ae8fcb695bddbde677e710e3aa784ea2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3+2x^2+2x\]" width="166" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/c/00c4e979ced32e39cac4abc52525026425e5929b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3+x^2+x\]" width="148" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/6/d96075757032a62e65b5195a49871ee42df76ea0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3\]" width="74" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/0/04061c14fa08d007436855acb5fbba671f521c84.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4x^4+4x^3+4x^2\]" width="123" height="16" />
However, by inspection <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/3/4e3ce2f3dce0efaa8d5621ab108ee4ce2ef52c80.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^4+4x^3+4x^2+4x+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="198" height="16" /> has roots on the unit circle, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/d/eadf6fd25dbdbad01baa506f8ddc9ba1697ce93d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1=(x^5-1)/(x-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="312" height="19" /> which brings the sum to 92 (choice B).  Note that this polynomial has a 5th root of unity as a root. We will show that we were \textit{almost} correct in our initial assumption; that is that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is at most a 5th root of unity, and that the last polynomial we obtained is the last polynomial with the given properties. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> in an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th root of unity where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2c57d1f5101d7fe31db3c0b42f2d45f5ca0fc81.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is not a 3rd or 4th root of unity. (Note that 1st and 2nd roots of unity are themselves 4th roots of unity). If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is prime, then \textit{every} <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th root of unity except 1 must satisfy our polynomial, but since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2c57d1f5101d7fe31db3c0b42f2d45f5ca0fc81.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> and the degree of our polynomial is 4, this is impossible. Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is composite. If it has a prime factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> greater than 5 then again every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />th root of unity must satisfy our polynomial and we arrive at the same contradiction. Therefore suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is divisible only by 2,3,or 5. Since by hypothesis <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is not a 2nd or 3rd root of unity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> must be a 5th root of unity. Since 5 is prime, every 5th root of unity except 1 must satisfy our polynomial. That is, the other 4 complex 5th roots of unity must satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/b/dcbb26516017f4866edcb2c574b84d2bb03d1b2d.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z_0)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />. But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/1/ca123cc5c6a625e54e896c17decff0490cd2e4e3.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^5-1)/(x-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="19" /> has exactly all 5th roots of unity excluding 1, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c74829975cfcaac3253f6375ef297f1e196a235.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^5-1)/(x-1)=x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="313" height="19" />. Thus this must divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c30dad2ab32626109cd44d5f3ea90f70bb4db74.png" class="latex" alt="$P(x)=4(x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="19" />. This completes the proof.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/60161910a74182ec0546f1bd70bef522f2fb8a42.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0\in \mathbb{R}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a748d24e07be896ad52818cd72cadf39cc6711.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> does not work because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/c/a3caf6e375d1da980d5a62a94e45b232f4d001ef.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)\geq 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" />. Assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/1/c4112876d82b4ba31a8712111b527b80d0aac2ca.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=-1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="62" height="14" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbd7f4bd071a930c29d9278460e8eff393ce48a.png" class="latex" alt="$0=P(-1)=4-a+b-c+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="242" height="18" />,  we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/d/dfd31d0f035a2f2d57fb347ccbd3342b530089f4.png" class="latex" alt="$4+b+d=a+c\leq 4+b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="198" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791662898215b138613f4dada0a3ce54193d735f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=0$" width="42" height="12" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> has to be true since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/6/2e6f73869b8d3e064a1500ac1c1883f490914ce8.png" class="latex" alt="$4+b=a+c \leq a+b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="167" height="15" />. Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/6/1468cb6539f70f1c086d58dc438beecb3fbb2573.png" class="latex" alt="$4+b=4+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/d/ebda5306c70a24c574ef0107ed5b5152b5f594cf.png" class="latex" alt="$b=c$" width="40" height="12" />, therefore the only possible choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/e/faedb9d30dc3763d92cbcacf2cc17e2bf64f3e8b.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/d/9edc11ab8ee8fe800be704cc08d437e181c2cbd3.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,t,t,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />. In these cases, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbd331375e27b63691e9cfeab7e1d7384370791b.png" class="latex" alt="$P(-1)=4-4+t-t+0=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="238" height="18" />. The sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/7/797a20647c81f3259c5069556cab51c94bf4b752.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" /> over these cases is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/2/ea2fd0c12a3398367ece0485ad23f6f38dd41fc4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum_{t=0}^{4} (4+4+t+t) = 40+20=60$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="262" height="50" />.
</p><p>Second, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/6/8568eb6e472878c6d76ff8b223e24bee8e12e4a0.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0\in \mathbb{C} \backslash \mathbb{R}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/f/06fba2f73cc4f4e476eeb525953399f8a7107615.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=x_0+iy_0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" /> for some real <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/4/d2495c742487d19e3a395980a638378008b2f2ff.png" class="latex" alt="$x_0, y_0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/4/a041d4960e70cdcd480d8d153cd3690139e18ad3.png" class="latex" alt="$|x_0|&lt;1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" />. By conjugate roots theorem we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/f/ecf33c6480b43bddda583cebfc97c2fea92066c7.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z_0)=P(z_0^{*})=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/b/fdbf35cc51b793e91a12267f42eb5ac7ccda2e4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(z-z_0)(z-z_0^{*}) = (z^2 - 2x_0*z + 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="284" height="19" /> is a factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e048a9db04ffc89eec3e0d78777919f12df3aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, and we may assume that 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/d/7bdcbbf2c06056f2aac58c77abdfac130b26d428.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(z) = (z^2-2x_0 z + 1)(4z^2 + pz + d)\]" width="292" height="19" />
</p><p>for some real <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />. Expanding this polynomial and comparing the coefficients, we have the following equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/0/f10c1860eead869420ede5a6c8f3cc22b31d4c0c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[p-8x_0 = a\]" width="93" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/b/91b62584d650f5bceb5c2a732f086d21f6ec918b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[d+4-2px_0 = b\]" width="130" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/4/e14f063d0f0e5b57ef96166a8ca04717bd31d3dd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[p-2dx_0 = c\]" width="101" height="16" />
</p><p>From the first and the third we may deduce that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/b/46b407279afdcb502611eba2a0aba02292fd3062.png" class="latex" alt="$2x_0 = \frac{a-c}{d-4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="94" height="33" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a42a3b62a1bd4df696e9c386c1fda4a538726b2.png" class="latex" alt="$p=\frac{da-4c}{d-4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="95" height="38" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/6/2b6f7fc6ec086e31eeac864dec797ad341c5ed05.png" class="latex" alt="$d\neq 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" /> (we will consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/4/5e4b2503862ce82e4cb89da832f5ccedae370ede.png" class="latex" alt="$d=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> by the end). Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/e/20eb7ba455e601f728490b47239691a415f1f6e6.png" class="latex" alt="$k=2px_0=\frac{(a-c)(da-4c)}{(4-d)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="223" height="43" />. From the second equation, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/b/91b21bd2c350af84c2298f7444624f1f5c5fe299.png" class="latex" alt="$k=d+4-b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="105" height="14" /> is non-negative.
</p><p>Consider the following cases:
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f717c40ef1d78faf6efbaef5c9e6f804d544faf3.png" class="latex" alt="$a=c$" width="42" height="8" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/d/badf0ecd1ef8289387d8462393438c6e2710ff28.png" class="latex" alt="$k=0$" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df077015405674244193fea2bbccaed957698dff.png" class="latex" alt="$b=d+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/f/5ef8c9523252e9688910ce641c54e17e5918036c.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b=c=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="107" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791662898215b138613f4dada0a3ce54193d735f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=0$" width="42" height="12" />. However, this has already been found (i.e. the form of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/d/9edc11ab8ee8fe800be704cc08d437e181c2cbd3.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,t,t,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />).
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/3/3734724744205dbad381ba2a2b5688f6f26586c3.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;c\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="15" />. Then since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/f/23fa365b4a0751d7292384305fbca7fb7329316e.png" class="latex" alt="$k\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/785ac3f7ea5c585e44a4b9c8aceee4bc529074c6.png" class="latex" alt="$da-4c\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="91" height="15" />. However, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cbb4f372eee36dde7be644530f92f0b038f7241.png" class="latex" alt="$da \leq 4c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="15" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d1897954891d6e9ae85807b6eb66b96bdd33591.png" class="latex" alt="$da-4c=0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />. This is true only when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/0/990cd6c8e8c463ed230d494a63de80c165e6fe60.png" class="latex" alt="$d=c$" width="41" height="12" />. Also, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/d/badf0ecd1ef8289387d8462393438c6e2710ff28.png" class="latex" alt="$k=0$" width="43" height="12" /> again. In this case, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df077015405674244193fea2bbccaed957698dff.png" class="latex" alt="$b=d+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/4/a44ba09df6e83a901db8b0a2144e81765cac1482.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a21de17cc73a28697bdd5a86268a6ae59429d6e4.png" class="latex" alt="$c=d=0$" width="74" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/f/59ffceed6b93662690bed35b90559c999f6d9225.png" class="latex" alt="$x_0=-1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e048a9db04ffc89eec3e0d78777919f12df3aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> has a root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/4/d744c4722c1ccb5f9a06899648e43f7043b5c7cd.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=e^{i2\pi/3}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="81" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/c/43ca1c1cc3fa4b9ae8101eacd15454b6daed9a14.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)=12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />.
</p><p>Last case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/4/5e4b2503862ce82e4cb89da832f5ccedae370ede.png" class="latex" alt="$d=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/7/a8779d1f2cac24b5ab745eef57eaead281cccf67.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b=c=d=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="140" height="13" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e048a9db04ffc89eec3e0d78777919f12df3aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" /> has a root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d4ae122af2aff519de8ed4ccbfe756ea947413a.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=e^{i2\pi/5}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="81" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d424e9317b46fb90d49bf9252e8110ceea3fec8.png" class="latex" alt="$P(1)=20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" />.
</p><p>Therefore the desired sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/9/be97201baaf1fd37766cb0eeb407544b1b660e3a.png" class="latex" alt="$60+12+20=92 ...\framebox{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="180" height="23" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>First, notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/d/6bd3d68e77a39e1ea97e7009f70e12622fa76835.png" class="latex" alt="$z=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> cannot be a root of the polynomial because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/3/9c392a6d0276ab3eb609e42785b5c74dde3671c5.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c,d \geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" />. Multiplying the polynomial by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c63470ad34c18869d3dc0a0bbd4c53100fd6ab3f.png" class="latex" alt="$(z-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48df63eb107bf7de26931e4b32481442849d03e4.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)(z-1) = 4z^5-(4-a)z^4-(a-b)z^3-(b-c)z^2-(c-d)z-d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="537" height="19" />, so for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/d/edda2d5834c424a73605a2679307eb95a03081e6.png" class="latex" alt="$z \neq 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="17" /> to be a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e048a9db04ffc89eec3e0d78777919f12df3aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$P(z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/1/781c54c225a291af7a81c26ef2bbbaef07f928bd.png" class="latex" alt="$4z^5 = (4-a)z^4+(a-b)z^3+(b-c)z^2+(c-d)z+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="422" height="19" />. Now we consider the root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b82758bbd1e38da673baa1ebf0fdb7c673eb5cc.png" class="latex" alt="$|z_0|=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/2/aa2f001255fbfea3befd5bb55ae9f8b7d6127f61.png" class="latex" alt="$|4z_0^5| = 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, so the right hand side must have absolute value 4. By the triangle inequality, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/3/3a34584e64f07574984d60b4b7307d93411a9d45.png" class="latex" alt="$|(4-a)z_0^4+(a-b)z_0^3+(b-c)z_0^2+(c-d)z_0+d|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="19" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/2/f62d08457a6d0499f11c8f9a526d96f6f4e8bf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$\leq |(4-a)z_0^4|+|(a-b)z_0^3| + |(b-c)z_0^2| + |(c-d)z_0|+d$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="438" height="19" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/b/a2b48989d982065596172cb668be98607ff2b7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$= (4-a)+(a-b)+(b-c)+(c-d)+d=4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="365" height="18" />, with equality if and only if each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebcb4ea0d4674d211b5ea972e31f3e1e7ae06a74.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/3/4b347ed58eb0b02cef8c89caac446949246e6465.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56af83f233531aa4fe7864a7bf7cc4fa181b85ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef96332f4b1c312c127294259efe8a3b02f353ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/5/4554e72b4be9c5f8a2971bc0d1452c3c8ae6e563.png" class="latex" alt="$(c-d)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is either zero or in the same direction as all the others when looked at as vectors in the complex plane. 
</p><p>We can now divide into two cases: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/3/2a3aec8655fb9d75b591e32533ddd7ec4c222909.png" class="latex" alt="$d \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791662898215b138613f4dada0a3ce54193d735f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=0$" width="42" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/3/2a3aec8655fb9d75b591e32533ddd7ec4c222909.png" class="latex" alt="$d \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebcb4ea0d4674d211b5ea972e31f3e1e7ae06a74.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="19" /> must be real by the previous argument, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is a fifth root of unity. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/3/4b347ed58eb0b02cef8c89caac446949246e6465.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56af83f233531aa4fe7864a7bf7cc4fa181b85ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef96332f4b1c312c127294259efe8a3b02f353ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/5/4554e72b4be9c5f8a2971bc0d1452c3c8ae6e563.png" class="latex" alt="$(c-d)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> must all be zero because if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is a fifth root of unity, none of these can be real numbers with positive absolute value. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/d/c4d6c17fef7c3d0a099d86cb13c69c5ef21fc480.png" class="latex" alt="$b=a$" width="41" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/3/7d3b5237083d71c73e70a0a1d8bd3942e625c95e.png" class="latex" alt="$c=b$" width="40" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/0/990cd6c8e8c463ed230d494a63de80c165e6fe60.png" class="latex" alt="$d=c$" width="41" height="12" />, leading to the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a95a78ffaf9ac8c9a2148699f74741de48427cd0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(4,4,4,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. Just to be sure, we can easily verify that this solution leads to the six complex numbers under question being in the same direction.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/1/791662898215b138613f4dada0a3ce54193d735f.png" class="latex" alt="$d=0$" width="42" height="12" />, then each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/c/ebcb4ea0d4674d211b5ea972e31f3e1e7ae06a74.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/3/4b347ed58eb0b02cef8c89caac446949246e6465.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56af83f233531aa4fe7864a7bf7cc4fa181b85ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef96332f4b1c312c127294259efe8a3b02f353ba.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b848b15dcf8e4788d05fa93b1161c964609a3378.png" class="latex" alt="$cz_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="22" height="10" /> must either be zero or in the same direction as all the others, so each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e5b6a8343086b99fcade71597ff895c8ed73.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f8cceb7b800022c0650dda060fe35ca84f9fbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/472c157433a1453ea68c9d866a84b2b0d4b0a41e.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb947c7a717c5915628e2f06eebdbf11d8fc589.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> must either be zero or in the same direction as all the others. We can divide this into two cases: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/5/3a5061ac0ea7f3b0c9eb6387005ae6c28eac6bfe.png" class="latex" alt="$c \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80d7a3ca845e6cae14b9db710b64331219ec3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$c=0$" width="40" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/5/3a5061ac0ea7f3b0c9eb6387005ae6c28eac6bfe.png" class="latex" alt="$c \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="17" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb07e5b6a8343086b99fcade71597ff895c8ed73.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="19" /> must be real. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is a fourth root of unity. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is not a second root of unity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f8cceb7b800022c0650dda060fe35ca84f9fbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/472c157433a1453ea68c9d866a84b2b0d4b0a41e.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb947c7a717c5915628e2f06eebdbf11d8fc589.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" /> must be zero, implying that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/9/5a9b14fc7225af608ef91d590bcdbceddb620491.png" class="latex" alt="$b=a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/c/88cd1608ab3ab30d4cd7905b33fae2a5d10ee57a.png" class="latex" alt="$c=b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, leading to the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/7575c2892a19211e77ecffcc7a74953fa6a5da4c.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(4,4,4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> is also a second root of unity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f8cceb7b800022c0650dda060fe35ca84f9fbf.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/b/bbb947c7a717c5915628e2f06eebdbf11d8fc589.png" class="latex" alt="$(b-c)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" /> must be zero but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/472c157433a1453ea68c9d866a84b2b0d4b0a41e.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="19" /> can be anything. This implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/d/ebda5306c70a24c574ef0107ed5b5152b5f594cf.png" class="latex" alt="$b=c$" width="40" height="12" /> with no other restrictions, leading to the new solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/7/3d7ef4a1bb1c0a8c7737ca2ab769577b0d19ed6d.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d) = (4,3,3,0), (4,2,2,0), (4,1,1,0), (4,0,0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="419" height="18" />. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80d7a3ca845e6cae14b9db710b64331219ec3f7.png" class="latex" alt="$c=0$" width="40" height="12" />, then we can similarly show that each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e4044472b9b615c698bef3b6eaa70dcd650cb0d4.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bcfc09035a319a7298548ff120446ec2dcf491.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/9/589f7535653b18e736e500197732f9cae5d8ece2.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be zero or in the same direction as all the others. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/9/d09016ae7d4bb0ccd869b43b3da544e9bacc08e0.png" class="latex" alt="$b \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="17" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> must be a third root of unity, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bcfc09035a319a7298548ff120446ec2dcf491.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/9/589f7535653b18e736e500197732f9cae5d8ece2.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="18" /> must be zero, implying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/4/a44ba09df6e83a901db8b0a2144e81765cac1482.png" class="latex" alt="$a=b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" />, leading to the new solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/b/63baf8a0d7fdd7401468b9f68fb9a93ce2877b95.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(4,4,0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc43f0ea59e862d7db01586894e9eea730d427b7.png" class="latex" alt="$b=0$" width="40" height="12" />, then we can similarly show that each of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/a/cfaecf96f64482cb29dccae1f0c8654008f4e56d.png" class="latex" alt="$4z_0^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="19" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/7/057327307e832e036ec613580f3b76ab1057c7c1.png" class="latex" alt="$(4-a)z_0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be zero or in the same direction as the others. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b82758bbd1e38da673baa1ebf0fdb7c673eb5cc.png" class="latex" alt="$|z_0|=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f98b3f405113241659d8c8db2ea3480346db4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, but we have already counted the solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9f520a0ca156a6b7bd439192f8a59ef961db2b.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(4,0,0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="170" height="18" />. 
</p><p>Then, the complete list of solutions is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/2/772d80d4c76f89e89e23a328e133758864538bd4.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c,d)=(4,4,4,4),(4,4,4,0),(4,3,3,0),(4,2,2,0),(4,1,1,0),(4,0,0,0),(4,4,0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="667" height="18" />, leading to a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/4/b747eb0d52775812383b49b32f0b8e241ccce362.png" class="latex" alt="$\framebox{B}=92$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="65" height="23" />.
</p>
