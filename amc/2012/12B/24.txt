<p>Define the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37a9e7fca70e2dce829d902af2088735306bc1a3.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="16" /> on the positive integers by setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/1/ec1eb2ed0f9ecf4e9f4b8d9133e844a9ad61e422.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(1)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" /> and if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d7f3218529101d80487c70782e4c50d33a80d99.png" class="latex" alt="$n=p_1^{e_1}p_2^{e_2}\cdots p_k^{e_k}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="17" /> is the prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/2/65262bf247c4a3c6114a8223d249dc9702619d3e.png" class="latex" alt="$n&gt;1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0944e39eeee13aeb77856674043dd44dde818cc7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f_1(n)=(p_1+1)^{e_1-1}(p_2+1)^{e_2-1}\cdots (p_k+1)^{e_k-1}.\]" width="375" height="19" />
For every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/b/fbb96b520628a521d3593972c35e0ece69fc0264.png" class="latex" alt="$m\ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="14" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/8/ac8a7da41de658686beaf6710dc96fdd3bb8136f.png" class="latex" alt="$f_m(n)=f_1(f_{m-1}(n))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />. For how many <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />s in the range <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/b/aabc2b42dd6e54e681b08d04c2f808ac979691ad.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le N\le 400$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="100" height="15" /> is the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09cb5798f12ea6f83e29024261bd5be37454268e.png" class="latex" alt="$(f_1(N),f_2(N),f_3(N),\dots )$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="18" /> unbounded?
</p><p><b>Note:</b> A sequence of positive numbers is unbounded if for every integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, there is a member of the sequence greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/e/8eec68a816c4e904315b3653d2c0bc3ee47a33b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 15\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 17\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 19$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First of all, notice that for any odd prime <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, the largest prime that divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/5/5d58d0cd6161c8c4be4d6284f59ec576295093e1.png" class="latex" alt="$p+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="15" /> is no larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b584d289789a99d94513085dee7a1b8ed09cf23d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{p+1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" />, therefore eventually the factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/b/febeaad4002d6c8a54ef3a81c7122d9e1de98751.png" class="latex" alt="$f_k(N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> does not contain any prime larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Also, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f176291f5d905a192d5b69a6baeb86edaa9826e.png" class="latex" alt="$f_2(2^m) = f_1(3^{m-1})=2^{2m-4}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="214" height="19" />, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/500fb9788ed8553a319dd49cb6262faebcacc88b.png" class="latex" alt="$m=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" /> it stays the same but when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/6/ca6b09d1fd6b64944831e5db761cce62f467c1a6.png" class="latex" alt="$m\geq 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="48" height="15" /> it grows indefinitely. Therefore any number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> that is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> or any number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/b/febeaad4002d6c8a54ef3a81c7122d9e1de98751.png" class="latex" alt="$f_k(N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> makes the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09cb5798f12ea6f83e29024261bd5be37454268e.png" class="latex" alt="$(f_1(N),f_2(N),f_3(N),\dots )$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="18" /> unbounded. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8924412a12becf7905729885a46e3e351ec5a86.png" class="latex" alt="$400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/9/0295ac901ef30beadc58b0832f09ab2754445eb0.png" class="latex" alt="$2^4 5^2=400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="84" height="15" /> also works: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/e/25ef7563ea3bfd01560abc4ab7e4f54ca05906dd.png" class="latex" alt="$f_2(2^4 5^2) = f_1(3^4 \cdot 2) = 2^6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="19" />.
</p><p>Now let's look at the other cases. Any first power of prime in a prime factorization will not contribute the unboundedness because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/a/55a2093943e022dfb46c170ca41b6feaac54163a.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(p^1)=(p+1)^0=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="19" />. At least a square of prime is to contribute. So we test primes that are less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da6b090ef4d83b77996941aa41af800e65a0e6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{400}=20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="84" height="18" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/5/6e51ccb39a9e5a5612d02e714fb40cb65009ce6c.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(3^4)=4^3=2^6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="19" /> works, therefore any number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/9/d59f66308c66d52e876036dcc5dad1df0f426872.png" class="latex" alt="$\leq 400$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="45" height="15" /> that are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/2259c263bde7b68aadfeca3df73046a41c953212.png" class="latex" alt="$3^4$" width="15" height="15" /> works: there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> of them.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/0/0907f528a4b827f8011765c77ee496b556414180.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 \cdot Q^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="18" /> could also work if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c52e98ff6e755fe0a4161e44dbbc9e40af1a54b.png" class="latex" alt="$Q^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="20" height="18" /> satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/1/d6180ba132056c1975ab92fdf287fdbf7b271a17.png" class="latex" alt="$2~|~f_1(Q^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="19" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/1/cc1c53a6a5e7634f83d4399fbc4c172b66c50224.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 \cdot 5^2 &gt; 400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="15" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/f/51feff76217209fc68c8fa3d1476f91b88d2c96a.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(5^3)=6^2 = 2^2 3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="143" height="19" /> does not work.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b2087a7ae153b2e3cc68646a6ce02396a1a5b35.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(7^3)=8^2=2^6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="19" /> works. There are no other multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd99228146175c61e5cca6be965d17c918d45cec.png" class="latex" alt="$7^3$" width="15" height="15" /> within <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8924412a12becf7905729885a46e3e351ec5a86.png" class="latex" alt="$400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/2/cd26f6ed947516cb83c7f36d96d3f5ffcf1b1f1c.png" class="latex" alt="$7^2 \cdot Q^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="50" height="18" /> could also work if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4b17c7ba5b7fb015a0d7dfce5d0abf6da87b085.png" class="latex" alt="$4~|~f_1(Q^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="19" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e43322916858e5d55515c58ced4ea6ac8634d8b2.png" class="latex" alt="$7^2 \cdot 3^2 &gt; 400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="97" height="15" /> already.
</p><p>For number that are only divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/1/7c1da776d2efb1372d8b59dc67445e66032df6a5.png" class="latex" alt="$p=11, 13, 17, 19$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="16" />, they don't work because none of these primes are such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/5/5d58d0cd6161c8c4be4d6284f59ec576295093e1.png" class="latex" alt="$p+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="15" /> could be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> nor a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/2259c263bde7b68aadfeca3df73046a41c953212.png" class="latex" alt="$3^4$" width="15" height="15" />.
</p><p>In conclusion, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/118966c9f7d233b604535ba0899b5ab5bd8bf3cd.png" class="latex" alt="$12+1+4+1=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="14" /> number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />'s ... <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/8/e88dc32d72b800739cb3736b1ccaa5c08d18eda3.png" class="latex" alt="$\framebox{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Say a number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/6/f16700204caa4c9bd5c83330ada97c3973e40c83.png" class="latex" alt="$\textit{boring}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="17" /> if the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/8/7d839ba77d9e978a7316e1b959e368c1ba2f4447.png" class="latex" alt="$\{f_k(n)\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" /> is bounded; otherwise <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a1aeed48a0f48c6468f6dee3c7c1a4355cb3afc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textit{interesting}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="16" />. 
</p><p>It is clear that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is interesting iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/d/5ad4f4eb592b637ee8fa1689f05bbe61ee165522.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> is. For a prime, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/b/3fbd0f43c3310725b19c3595485ac1bd6bcecfab.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(p)=1$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="75" height="20" />. Thus, for a prime, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/4508393135451dc6c41b01f36072dd16674586bc.png" class="latex" alt="$p_k$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="11" />, in the prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />; if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/9/e7955c79570dc894b0d6f3e6aa753b0f2b273c0d.png" class="latex" alt="$e_k=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="17" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/a/40a51b3904095d9b9a8881ee643fe64b58b33aae.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(n)=f_1(n/p_k)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="135" height="20" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is interesting iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/1/eb1c79281b418523b2a8926cc2da8c09bb729008.png" class="latex" alt="$n/p_k$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="39" height="20" /> is. Continuing in this manner, we can divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> by all such primes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/0/4508393135451dc6c41b01f36072dd16674586bc.png" class="latex" alt="$p_k$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="17" height="11" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/9/e7955c79570dc894b0d6f3e6aa753b0f2b273c0d.png" class="latex" alt="$e_k=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="17" />; and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is interesting iff each of these resulting numbers are. Finally we will end up with a number  whose prime factorization  contains only exponents <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/e/d7e6acf1d2b06a7e8f0528d1100794942f83ab7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="14" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of such numbers. It suffices to find all interesting numbers in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />; all other interesting numbers will be multiples of these. Note that  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/d/a7df7d8ca4b61fd1b734d8393af0615ae085eb22.png" class="latex" alt="$f_1(n)\mid f_1(kn)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="110" height="20" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e64abd678bb3af3c10991074c5da8e32565401.png" class="latex" alt="$k\in\mathbb{N}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="45" height="13" />; so by induction a multiple of an interesting number is also interesting.
</p><p>The set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> contains either powers of primes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/b/60b6fa26a01acdcbf07e020eaec4a36e61f2a3e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\le 19$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="36" height="15" />, or a product of two such powers.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/5/db5760e48d718c09e509e8b29eaeb1bc5c4d3af1.png" class="latex" alt="$f_2(2^a)=f_1(3^{a-1}) = 2^{2(a-2)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="214" height="23" />; since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/7/8a7ca06b2f1251c57bb5e3d7b00623061ffabd66.png" class="latex" alt="$2(a-2)&gt; a$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="100" height="20" /> iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/3/ae3636aa22d567d870b702ad4cb8ed048c20a3c5.png" class="latex" alt="$a\ge 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" /> and its multiples are interesting; there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> such.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/4/f94d498410e99cf7630034041113ea7cd7c04263.png" class="latex" alt="$f_2(3^b)=f_1(2^{2(b-1)}) = 3^{2b-3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="216" height="23" />; since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/3/3736f5e16ca2e8909b8a552c7463f88c4bab3f06.png" class="latex" alt="$2b-3&gt; b$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="82" height="16" /> iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192706533237d1990fa3586a52902817c8bbc49e.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ge 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/2259c263bde7b68aadfeca3df73046a41c953212.png" class="latex" alt="$3^4$" width="15" height="15" /> and its multiples are interesting; there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> such.
</p><p>Among the remaining prime powers only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd877059e55571d6c570a4e0cf5be2a132ad5ac9.png" class="latex" alt="$7^3 \textbf{ is interesting}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="20" /> but it has no other multiples in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc65380cdaae5be142d6d77f4f384b9dd2ad1a02.png" class="latex" alt="$[400]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="35" height="18" />.
</p><p>We are now left with numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/3/cd3f0c16090f5284a274ddf20268392f7b62efed.png" class="latex" alt="$n\in S$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="47" height="15" /> that are products of two prime powers, i.e <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1fadbf40596fad1b25f76f9285656948aa2b92.png" class="latex" alt="$n=p^aq^b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="21" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/b/d0b8fc2eb92cfa6b6296b6ec7ccbf1ad43b86462.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />.  
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f63e2e472145ebd5831ae8e4b92bfd1ff5a5b4b.png" class="latex" alt="$f_2(2^a3^b)= 2^{2(a-2)}3^{2b-3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="180" height="23" />, so a number of this form is interesting iff <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/3/ae3636aa22d567d870b702ad4cb8ed048c20a3c5.png" class="latex" alt="$a\ge 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/2/192706533237d1990fa3586a52902817c8bbc49e.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ge 4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />; they are already counted above.
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/b/86ba66ed990330b15d00579f595a03d2589443b4.png" class="latex" alt="$5^2\mid n$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="44" height="21" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/4/f24f1fa2642eb0901e7670c7028fc3f8e7d519d1.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2^2\cdot 5^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="17" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/8/e883f419ae288e2d93dba733c6f8f065863fef8a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3\cdot 5^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="17" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/5/5f53b55e998999c934627faf1249e7458aab51b2.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2\cdot 5^2$" width="44" height="15" /> (all boring) or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/7/9c7d2bae559c6dd7a9a568e7a92723f9e177d930.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2^4\cdot 5^2=400$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="135" height="17" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/91739f186581726817766770ab5200e774049a85.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{which is interesting}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="164" height="17" />, but has no other multiples. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/c/73c9ec37b92ed4142d660181015a83bea3c10be9.png" class="latex" alt="$7^2\mid n$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="44" height="21" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/c/aec9e496898cf3fbd93d50ee0941b95786a33328.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2\cdot 7^2$" width="44" height="15" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/4/8c4cf6f495696523c205cd6f687213da321aa8f0.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3\cdot 7^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="17" /> and both are boring. 
</p><p>No larger prime can divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> because even <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/9/1f968c255937c8bf373bcd10fd6371d62a35bf3c.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2\cdot 2^2 &gt; 400$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="109" height="18" />. 
</p><p>We have found all the interesting numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/7/5b7f45c960c681827d5d03d7b3c30811d3bfd77f.png" class="latex" alt="$2^5$" width="15" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/5/2259c263bde7b68aadfeca3df73046a41c953212.png" class="latex" alt="$3^4$" width="15" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/3/59316aa5f495b1345db9e596f787460976b79550.png" class="latex" alt="$2^4\cdot 5^2$" width="44" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd99228146175c61e5cca6be965d17c918d45cec.png" class="latex" alt="$7^3$" width="15" height="15" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/c/bcc5c529035e391809316b340833f41dd8190a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$12+4+1+1= 18$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="156" height="15" /> ... <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/8/e88dc32d72b800739cb3736b1ccaa5c08d18eda3.png" class="latex" alt="$\framebox{D}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="24" height="23" />.
</p>
