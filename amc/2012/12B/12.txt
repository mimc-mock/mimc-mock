<p>How many sequences of zeros and ones of length 20 have all the zeros consecutive, or all the ones consecutive, or both?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/e/07eaac1a14c48d1df239ea7e5f97dc3715af5078.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 190\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 192\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 211\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 380\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 382$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/49454eb64bb98859bbacb97830ffda186b480ef5.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{20}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="42" height="53" /> selections; however, we count these twice, therefore
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/677c9520a1bc9d6565cc0dedad91beb79c90b4d5.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot\binom{20}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 380}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="167" height="53" />. The wording of the question implies D, not E.
</p><p>However, MAA decided to accept both D and E.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Consider the 20 term sequence of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />'s and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />'s.  Keeping all other terms 1, a sequence of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e6630318d8cb28de2723180b6d359b972489c9.png" class="latex" alt="$k&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> consecutive 0's can be placed in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/f/22f38c49916736d81484a5aff48b163406c8c78f.png" class="latex" alt="$21-k$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" /> locations. That is, there are 20 strings with 1 zero, 19 strings with 2 consecutive zeros, 18 strings with 3 consecutive zeros, ..., 1 string with 20 consecutive zeros. Hence there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee8fed683ddc5aec714faa9befea22cf8fdaf96.png" class="latex" alt="$20+19+\cdots+1=\binom{21}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="200" height="53" /> strings with consecutive zeros. The same argument shows there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/5/f554fa80a926c7a2a92a35b080ac3bd50669afdf.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{21}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="42" height="53" /> strings with consecutive 1's. This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/2/9424e41e5d004fc37c62315d4803877a665822ed.png" class="latex" alt="$2\binom{21}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="51" height="53" /> strings in all. However, we have counted twice those strings in which all the 1's and all the 0's are consecutive. These are the cases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/0/7809ebf9f0d6edb94472e95f4691d13df0c385ab.png" class="latex" alt="$01111...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/3/b6303ae2b9522d787b193a3a0f77134fbf75339c.png" class="latex" alt="$00111...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/7/1d7e3797452cc7f425eace66fd22a850c4511b84.png" class="latex" alt="$000111...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, ..., <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b83e5e70548e33a160e9c5797214e3af7f19ec47.png" class="latex" alt="$000...0001$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="77" height="13" /> (of which there are 19) as well as the cases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/0/b909b1836f1e70360f47c2de76dc2f1486121765.png" class="latex" alt="$10000...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/2/ef2dbcb2d6d0f8a501d9065ef48c9d4cefa8ff34.png" class="latex" alt="$11000...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/a/8ca5191c61288840ce0d04eb698f208ebc0d25fd.png" class="latex" alt="$111000...$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="67" height="13" />, ..., <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/e/97e86e684c391cf1f7df997d9b9cd072cf54f585.png" class="latex" alt="$111...110$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="13" /> (of which there are 19 as well). This yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608ba19170bdae33b5cb135f6b83c4f2745dfd2c.png" class="latex" alt="$2\binom{21}{2}-2\cdot19=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 382}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="214" height="53" />
</p>
