<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> at a right angle, (this can be proved by noticing that the slopes of the two lines are negative reciprocals of each other) so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/5/2e511a9c9ae68ff2929b87efa8323f24033e15a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC \sim \triangle BED$" width="143" height="13" />. The hypotenuse of right triangle BED is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7c102e6d465673a7871e64ea35e2c43eeabc419.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{1^2+2^2}=\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="119" height="19" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/5/0150847710c03762e544bcc9613888d3bb87c8ff.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{AC}{BC}=\frac{BD}{ED} \Rightarrow \frac{AC}{BC} = \frac21 \Rightarrow AC=2BC\]" width="306" height="38" />
