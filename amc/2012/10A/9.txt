<p>A pair of six-sided dice are labeled so that one die has only even numbers (two each of 2, 4, and 6), and the other die has only odd numbers (two of each 1, 3, and 5). The pair of dice is rolled. What is the probability that the sum of the numbers on the tops of the two dice is 7?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1aadb05de22a36916774168f491dc3267e596c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{6}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{5}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{1}{4}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1}{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The total number of combinations when rolling two dice is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/1/eb19e12505c3121320b941210f02895ef79d771e.png" class="latex" alt="$6*6 = 36$" width="77" height="12" />.
</p><p>There are three ways that a sum of 7 can be rolled. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/8/4886e28554d678d6758dfa9e44bc9a333a62f0da.png" class="latex" alt="$2+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/7/4a70bf42cf1bcc9a7d115821cf7fc9963f598e04.png" class="latex" alt="$4+3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/d/e8d7b2dcbe33a3d1fe1038f15af89915fdf2b5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$6+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" />. There are two 2's on one die and two 5's on the other, so there are a total of 4 ways to roll the combination of 2 and 5. There are two 4's on one die and two 3's on the other, so there are a total of 4 ways to roll the combination of 4 and 3. There are two 6's on one die and two 1's on the other, so there are a total of 4 ways to roll the combination of 6 and 1. Add <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/6/e76aa701423194b95400465650c5c3f2011e53e7.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 4 + 4 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="13" />.
</p><p>Thus, our probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e78a4802c70400e4eb172cb75f9db2b411b33608.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{12}{36} = \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="37" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/6512ff23dcac00933d9cc088bac2431b59d1f59e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac13}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Assume we roll the die with only evens first. For whatever value rolled, there are exactly 2 faces on the odd die that makes the sum 7. The odd die has 6 faces, so our probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/6512ff23dcac00933d9cc088bac2431b59d1f59e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac13}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />.
</p>
