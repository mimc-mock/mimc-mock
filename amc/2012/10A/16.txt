<p>Three runners start running simultaneously from the same point on a 500-meter circular track. They each run clockwise around the course maintaining constant speeds of 4.4, 4.8, and 5.0 meters per second. The runners stop once they are all together again somewhere on the circular course. How many seconds do the runners run?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/d/0bd8e3137a571d98d3bc33bf12cb7e2069589115.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1,000\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1,250\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2,500\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 5,000\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 10,000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="534" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First consider the first two runners. The faster runner will lap the slower runner exactly once, or run 500 meters farther. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the time these runners run in seconds.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/5/b057e99777ad260717093ce35127f5e1e84a8cde.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4.8x-4.4x=500 \Rightarrow x=1250\]" width="238" height="13" />
</p><p>Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/0/18086e3acf585d6f2390669c1f228df03115e0ef.png" class="latex" alt="$4.4(1250)=5500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" /> is a multiple of 500, it turns out they just meet back at the start line.
</p><p>Now we must find a time that is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83d25f65c98ed0d9b4a0e798000d70071685d98b.png" class="latex" alt="$1250$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> and results in the 5.0 m/s runner to end up on the start line. Every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83d25f65c98ed0d9b4a0e798000d70071685d98b.png" class="latex" alt="$1250$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> seconds, that fastest runner goes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/0/1402848799d901703246ee5a8cbefb21b852c546.png" class="latex" alt="$5.0(1250)=6250$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="133" height="18" /> meters. In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/5/1451b234235def7b948a89eabcddc90b9ed2d267.png" class="latex" alt="$2(1250)=2500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" /> seconds, he goes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43cb538514409635844bef2613ea6c3795b1b3e.png" class="latex" alt="$5.0(2500)=12500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="18" /> meters. Therefore the runners run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5edb0a9d9a57b01e5f0219c1f46425bda2eae7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 2,500}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="90" height="28" /> seconds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Working backwards from the answers starting with the smallest answer, if they had run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> seconds, they would have run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/7/5671c8ea23689273bc527846add3c7eefab590b6.png" class="latex" alt="$4400, 4800, 5000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="16" /> meters, respectively.  The first two runners have a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8924412a12becf7905729885a46e3e351ec5a86.png" class="latex" alt="$400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> meters, which is not a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" /> (one lap), so they are not in the same place.
</p><p>If they had run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/d/83d25f65c98ed0d9b4a0e798000d70071685d98b.png" class="latex" alt="$1250$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> seconds, the runners would have run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/8/b78ffe1fac0e376174b5fe48582139a9f02f6b41.png" class="latex" alt="$5500, 6000, 6250$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="16" /> meters, respectively.  The last two runners have a difference of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/8/448b3c8f2596d1b93b4b3ef199f8b3241ced9da2.png" class="latex" alt="$250$" width="26" height="12" /> meters, which is not a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" />.
</p><p>If they had run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/3/5b32623c3d21608acb59f855590c5b920018645a.png" class="latex" alt="$2500$" width="35" height="12" /> seconds, the runners would have run <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3b4f895fd6936625d7aeede8a1331ae812da1ec.png" class="latex" alt="$11000, 12000, 12500$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="151" height="16" /> meters, respectively.  The distance separating each pair of runners is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/5/3d5edb0a9d9a57b01e5f0219c1f46425bda2eae7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 2,500}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="90" height="28" /> seconds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> be the time run in seconds, then the difference in meters run between the three runners is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25aa96ea8f7ef3111c890f6ef534fc6431d2b52c.png" class="latex" alt="$0.2t, 0.4t, 0.6t$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" />. For them to be at the same location all of them need to be multiples of 500. It is now easy to see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/3/c834db6c44ecf180be074bb3b3a3ea8e0181c7fa.png" class="latex" alt="$0.2t=500, 0.4t=1000, 0.6t=1500$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="277" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/d/dcd8a2f9bac72695ba0f02fb0abc7ed04b564b1a.png" class="latex" alt="$t=\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 2,500}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="121" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds, respectively the runners would've ran <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/5/5f51e9348e4e22aad6ef125a009554a71f3ad6ec.png" class="latex" alt="$4.4t, 4.8t,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/480e4c21aa2b804be8421a064873f531785d924f.png" class="latex" alt="$5t$" width="15" height="12" /> meters. Their current positions on the track are these values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/a/cfa150a93dbe575ac09a299a372aa23d9e02e7c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{500}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />. We're trying to find the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6e10bca629ada4c11046cbf337384896ea55f4d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4.4t \equiv 4.8t \equiv 5t \pmod{500}\]" width="221" height="18" /> Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/4/6e4ce24d8e15dfef72046bd490c7f210195122fb.png" class="latex" alt="$4.4t$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="29" height="12" /> on all sides, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/5/8e5dc889834aafcf025e1f2e01050490841ea017.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0 \equiv 0.4t \equiv 0.6t \pmod{500}\]" width="215" height="18" /> Now, we must find a value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> such that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054d1bdf2ade9c62020c240ad247d92aecbf2e23.png" class="latex" alt="$0.6t$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/d/bede9d5781f342efa37925010a5ed7ad1b6caa9e.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4t$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="29" height="13" /> are simultaneously multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" />.
</p><p>Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/d/bede9d5781f342efa37925010a5ed7ad1b6caa9e.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4t$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="29" height="13" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/5/265414d0b6da3a0e4b2ac66764ebec4be91136fc.png" class="latex" alt="$t=1250$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="13" />, but this does not work for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054d1bdf2ade9c62020c240ad247d92aecbf2e23.png" class="latex" alt="$0.6t$" width="29" height="12" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aaff8bc92f20b4eba4e69d07c4fcc53f6f99e64.png" class="latex" alt="$750$" width="26" height="12" /> isn't a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/9/a39b14023c98c943554d150561aacc188c4ce26d.png" class="latex" alt="$500$" width="26" height="12" />). Plugging in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/9/2c91ccdc5c8880cd4410dabdb6996f119caa803c.png" class="latex" alt="$0.4t=1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="13" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/a/7da6522faaa13076bd26201e1cb83c0f73efeca9.png" class="latex" alt="$t=2500$" width="66" height="12" />, and this does work for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/4/054d1bdf2ade9c62020c240ad247d92aecbf2e23.png" class="latex" alt="$0.6t$" width="29" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/a/7da6522faaa13076bd26201e1cb83c0f73efeca9.png" class="latex" alt="$t=2500$" width="66" height="12" /> and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/c/4ac423cd592400d73de4c599076e2c0c830e20e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C) } 2500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="17" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<ul><li>Note: Modular Arithmetic works only for integral values, so my usage of decimals is technically incorrect but the intuition leads to the right answer</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Similar to the solution above, but is much quicker and does not involve trial and error. This uses decimal mod arithmetic, which can be justified by intuition...
After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds, respectively the runners would've ran <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/5/5f51e9348e4e22aad6ef125a009554a71f3ad6ec.png" class="latex" alt="$4.4t, 4.8t,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/0/480e4c21aa2b804be8421a064873f531785d924f.png" class="latex" alt="$5t$" width="15" height="12" /> meters. These three values are congruent <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/a/cfa150a93dbe575ac09a299a372aa23d9e02e7c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod{500}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />, so 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6e10bca629ada4c11046cbf337384896ea55f4d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4.4t \equiv 4.8t \equiv 5t \pmod{500}\]" width="221" height="18" />. Subtract 4.4t from all three sides to get 0, 0.4t, and 0.6t are congruent. Now all we need to find is a value of t for which 0.4t and 0.6t are congruent mod 500. Subtract 0.4t from both sides to get 0.2t and 0 are congruent mod 500, or that 0.2t=t/5 is a multiple of 500. Let t=500k, so we want 100k to be a multiple of 500, or k to be a multiple of 5. Therefore, the smallest value of t is when k=5, and when t=500k=500(5)=<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/9/7c9326067ccad67300d018e3de6551b30f1345d8.png" class="latex" alt="$2500 \space (\text{C})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />
</p><p>- Solution by mathchampion1
</p>
