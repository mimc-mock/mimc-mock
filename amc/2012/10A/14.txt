<p>Chubby makes nonstandard checkerboards that have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/6/296fac336caaa4265aa24b6ecbc408a348ad7e0b.png" class="latex" alt="$31$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> squares on each side.  The checkerboards have a black square in every corner and alternate red and black squares along every row and column.  How many black squares are there on such a checkerboard?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/2/3321bd867435343d4609d5a67f31582051a5ae5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 480 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 481 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 482 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 483 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 484$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="440" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>There are 15 rows with 15 black tiles, and 16 rows with 16 black tiles, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/a/e6a7ec21efc5a28ea5a4b918600cebd8ce61d6d9.png" class="latex" alt="$15^2+16^2 =225+256= \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 481}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="271" height="28" />
</p><p>Note: When solving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/d/bade9c1fe9e69f976dbef4cf4458c5671671ecf5.png" class="latex" alt="$16^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />+<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/6/5962bd17aa962087061b2201d2e0a8026ebd5cea.png" class="latex" alt="$15^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />, you only need to calculate the units digit.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We build the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/8/548e2efae35789acbb32e297acc17e7ac0ad947e.png" class="latex" alt="$31 \times 31$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> checkerboard starting with a board of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/a/68ac5f036c91d2384577c1ac04612b08abb481b3.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \times 30$" width="58" height="12" /> that is exactly half black.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/b/91bc020f9815a9a48e144ddb4ef48d5e43297d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \cdot 30$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> black tiles in this region.
</p><p>Add to this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/a/68ac5f036c91d2384577c1ac04612b08abb481b3.png" class="latex" alt="$30 \times 30$" width="58" height="12" /> checkerboard a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/b/04b537396c15430d63cb19fb5bc349a96e713d09.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 30$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> strip on the bottom that has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> black tiles.
</p><p>Add to this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/c/e2c4786c7ab2977f8c82ef4caf5cbef0b5ad5d74.png" class="latex" alt="$31 \times 30$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> checkerboard a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/0/d801e0cfb00b1167f34ef43b7131cd88d29ac520.png" class="latex" alt="$31 \times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="13" /> strip on the right that has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/e/f0e4fe246e6c6322be6dde23ca8a681339088223.png" class="latex" alt="$15 + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="14" /> black tiles.
</p><p>In total, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/e/dfef1fd5f5ba00828728e40d3657beadad0c560b.png" class="latex" alt="$15 \cdot 30 + 15 + 15 + 1 = 481$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="212" height="14" /> tiles, giving an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/e/94ebbcee4425943359f924c2b7a67f7dfdaa8cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 481}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>For every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/7/4272db7fd3b90867ad13324fcb38f8ef0dd3a838.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 31$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" /> strip, there are 31 black tiles and 31 white tiles. There are 15 such strips in total and another <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/d/13dea87b9abdd4f769fcc317d5b3074a9e25f512.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \times 31$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" /> strip having 16 black titles.
</p><p>In total, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/d/35d6427619cb0048f98131bc902d42aab0dd9dea.png" class="latex" alt="$31\times 15+16=481$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="152" height="15" /> black tiles.
</p><p>~Bran_Quin
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Drawing smaller scale sketches, we notice that the odd columns of an <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/d/c3d38f82f48ef9e81c04d49354293305b0067afc.png" class="latex" alt="$n \times n$" width="43" height="9" /> (where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is odd) board have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/1/711fb96d5f47414b7f68114c27aa58e388ead3fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lceil \frac{n}{2} \right \rceil$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="30" height="33" /> black tiles, while the even columns have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/9/529ed29729a2b2403d8c85740261c03bbc9ccb6b.png" class="latex" alt="$\left \lfloor \frac{n}{2} \right \rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="30" height="33" /> black tiles. In our case, we have a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/8/548e2efae35789acbb32e297acc17e7ac0ad947e.png" class="latex" alt="$31 \times 31$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> board. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> odd columns, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> even columns, so the number of black tiles in total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/4/a14bb36fe075745656da53cdcd00981af10b5458.png" class="latex" alt="$16^2 + 15^2 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 481}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="172" height="30" />.
</p><p>~kn07
</p>
