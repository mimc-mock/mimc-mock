<p>The sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> positive odd integers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/7/f67ff4304a3602b8aac31124d7ce9b035d1b89e2.png" class="latex" alt="$212$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" /> more than the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> positive even integers. What is the sum of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/f/00fd1ec35b8f4f0ca941240ef76579cf1f545e85.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 255\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 256\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 257\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 258\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 259$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> odd integers is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/5/0f521656db779d024e6aff0f9a3771ac28d2c3db.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2$" width="22" height="15" />. The sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> even integers is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/d/dfd81057d052a6e2d394c00239a5b63ad17a29a1.png" class="latex" alt="$n(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a2563bec9175c3a2ddf8edccd2c9d9f1ebfaedd3.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2 = n^2 + n + 212$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="148" height="16" />. Since we want to solve for n, rearrange as a quadratic equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/a/c6a1ee802ecab9dbc30ed0d9a3f32802696870a9.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2 + n + (212 - m^2) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="193" height="19" />.
</p><p><br />
Use the quadratic formula: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7da58be493f34cb34dd9ff541c38f0e8244eb8.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{1 - 4(212 - m^2)}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="228" height="41" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is clearly an integer, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/5/8852bfd84922454e7f8fa4f12c06a486021de09c.png" class="latex" alt="$1 - 4(212 - m^2) = 4m^2 - 847$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="233" height="19" /> must be not only a perfect square, but also an odd perfect square for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> to be an integer.
</p><p><br />
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/9/9c94c5441fc42f22e4582ce99d85f764bbe6e0f3.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \sqrt{4m^2 - 847}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="132" height="18" />; note that this means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/100649ceba71a1d67c50e47c5219669d90303bdf.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{-1 + x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="93" height="37" />. It can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcf556f418c90b511bf9224bcc5d2f9adfde5ac8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = 4m^2 - 847$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="124" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/0/5605a9ceba0507672b5b20e73b8b8476ffec87e7.png" class="latex" alt="$4m^2 - x^2 = 847$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="124" height="15" />. Factoring the left side by using the difference of squares, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2af53ba4d741dea71d281d621a1eb8becf6cc7b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2m + x)(2m - x) = 847 = 7\cdot11^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="265" height="19" />.
</p><p><br />
Our goal is to find possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, then use the equation above to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. The difference between the factors is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/d/78df98f01750b59ac63f97168e8ef07a4b3f5edd.png" class="latex" alt="$(2m + x) - (2m - x) = 2m + x - 2m + x = 2x.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="375" height="18" /> We have three pairs of factors, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/8/fb885bb0696be97b49252e83937aceea8a8f7ad3.png" class="latex" alt="$847\cdot1, 121\cdot 7,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="114" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/7/b07cb267ce29d67cb78b88148aebfc5e7745f81b.png" class="latex" alt="$77\cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="14" />. The differences between these factors are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/b/87b1d84dd533d395221aed4b41265312062192c7.png" class="latex" alt="$846$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/9/819ef52beb1d611606a86bbca20416a336cf13bb.png" class="latex" alt="$114$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d40f3652c1962e98ce050a88c04ed37997c67df.png" class="latex" alt="$66$" width="17" height="12" /> - those are all possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/c/2ec701493fa72dda5d6747fe14f30e7b1c2fb6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2x$" width="19" height="12" />. Thus the possibilities for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aacd879f0eb861824ec08071b3940c3163cb9ec.png" class="latex" alt="$423$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6f3bccae16d5e31266370fced24edc41538d5da.png" class="latex" alt="$33$" width="17" height="12" />. 
</p><p>Now plug in these values into the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/0/100649ceba71a1d67c50e47c5219669d90303bdf.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{-1 + x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="93" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> can equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/5/7651217ecd64cc60cbd7805a091340edeef83281.png" class="latex" alt="$211$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8403b10472b04d97a53e5030736d439c146b5d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 255}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Edits by BakedPotato66
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As above, start off by noting that the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> odd integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/3/2f30eb15c3a3a5dd56441d6c08aa2892f80bebf3.png" class="latex" alt="$= m^2$" width="41" height="15" /> and the sum of the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> even integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/2/9e2d48b48fcdb08f83359cb8d3a9fcf010b230ee.png" class="latex" alt="$= n(n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="18" />. Clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b0b58b81ad8d1281aaf0c31d8bf23d6e26cf24e.png" class="latex" alt="$m &gt; n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="10" />, so let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/8/a9800afbab6a9706967f0c1e71e391aa28217a0e.png" class="latex" alt="$m = n + a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is some positive integer. We have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28173ebc3efdd6cd1b16bccc7996782a8be4431.png" class="latex" alt="$(n+a)^2 = n(n+1) + 212$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="204" height="19" />.
Expanding, grouping like terms and factoring, we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/e/1decc2bee44bf37290256784e56377e9ee152cbe.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{(212 - a^2)}{(2a - 1)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="118" height="43" />.
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> are both positive integers, so we need only check values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/b/45b561186a84a65f8f21cea4bdc41a98303b8265.png" class="latex" alt="$14^2 = 196 &lt; 212 &lt; 15^2 = 225$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="229" height="15" />). Plugging in, the only values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> that give integral solutions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6041a3a231da612ca35a5df872f100ca7e279926.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. These gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c94b082daa6988fbde9ac332a7f103fb0b7133a9.png" class="latex" alt="$211, 28,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, respectively. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04cbc95c3a2e99cc84ccd3e5c4a9001226b30d43.png" class="latex" alt="$211 + 28 + 16 = 255$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="159" height="14" />. Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8403b10472b04d97a53e5030736d439c146b5d8e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 255}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Using the closed forms for the sums, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/6/646297a03296bfaa91d4c0cdc0040a69c213eedf.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2=n(n+1)+212$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="19" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db11b02d0eacb3a23528776408c3ff514458251.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2=n^2+n+212$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="148" height="16" />. We would like to factor this equation, but the current expressions don't allow for this. So we multiply both sides by 4 to let us complete the square. Our equation is now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/0/5009ca95d9ca4a64b5d1c33e5dcb8ec761e69961.png" class="latex" alt="$4m^2=4n^2+4n+848$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="175" height="16" />. Complete the square on the right hand side: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/a/64a72adfded1c03967d948903f4330315609fcfd.png" class="latex" alt="$4m^2=(4n^2+4n+1)+848-1=(2n+1)^2+847$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="399" height="19" />. Move over the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/d/d4d73182c7df3488b6ef911ad8b073d9c0e73982.png" class="latex" alt="$(2n+1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" /> and factor to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/2/f226cc732aa71163c2ca8d0e884c323fdd918010.png" class="latex" alt="$(2m-2n-1)(2m+2n+1)=847=7\cdot11\cdot11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="372" height="18" />. The second factor is clearly greater than the first, and the only possible factor pairs are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/3/243b4d4cfae7a622868eaf66e2ba4555d47690f8.png" class="latex" alt="$847$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="27" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76ad7692f5d86be790209a56a68c2a3488235054.png" class="latex" alt="$121$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af4b3f8897671e2879b10badf471e5deec8952e.png" class="latex" alt="$77$" width="18" height="12" />. In each of these cases, solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and we find the solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/48291d7e7264bf18608077086448ae6b60fee079.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)=(212,211), (32,28), (22,16)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="281" height="18" />. The sum of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/4/404a87a27386978e3e0470c20becb0580abad20e.png" class="latex" alt="$211+28+16=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 255}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="206" height="28" />.
</p>
