<p>A year is a leap year if and only if the year number is divisible by 400 (such as 2000) or is divisible by 4 but not 100 (such as 2012).  The 200th anniversary of the birth of novelist Charles Dickens was celebrated on February 7, 2012, a Tuesday.  On what day of the week was Dickens born?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/3/ed37611cc15dc4910978f7649b0f147f0bf77367.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{Friday}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{Saturday}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{Sunday}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{Monday}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{Tuesday}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="587" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>In this solution we refer to moving to the left as decreasing the year or date number and moving to the right as  increasing the year or date number. Every non-leap year we move to the right results in moving one day to the right because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/5/5459ff4c1c759d42d3ae03ae437da66b0998fc42.png" class="latex" alt="$365\equiv 1\pmod 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />.  Every leap year we move to the right results in moving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> days to the right since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/3/42373d05b32e269f63f6fa9abd47c3108081d34a.png" class="latex" alt="$366\equiv 2\pmod 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" />. A leap year is usually every four years, so 200 years would have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf5eca5564bf1a03666df2e7ed5a72af5d4cc740.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{200}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="29" height="38" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> leap years, but the problem says that 1900 does not count as a leap year.
</p><p>Therefore there would be 151 regular years and 49 leap years, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df0a401ea3197efc2220805bea1b15aed619b3d.png" class="latex" alt="$1(151)+2(49)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="112" height="18" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/7/8f70a88401805d41dd703dc15c65ff8880289e86.png" class="latex" alt="$249$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> days back.  Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/c/b8c220a15bcbb87e5e1910bb635b6cfb3edd5968.png" class="latex" alt="$249 \equiv 4\ (\text{mod}\ 7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" />, four days back from Tuesday would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c00f5a7587ce2ee018577d5509f333f896ee6940.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ \text{Friday}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="93" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since they say that February <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />th, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/8/f48f95ec89b6c50220ecbbbb6130586135db8ff6.png" class="latex" alt="$2012$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> is the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" />th anniversary of Charles dickens birthday, that means that the birth of Charles Dickens is on February <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />th, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/0/1b09c6befdbb9f056cbd25146b93547033aaaebc.png" class="latex" alt="$1812$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. We then see that there is a leap year on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/759fdc29ba6d8259afa6b473cc8dacfa4086fbe6.png" class="latex" alt="$1812, 1816, ...., 2008$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="155" height="17" /> but we must excluse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/1/cb188438cd378732afbc6cc5d4845e38792bd7fb.png" class="latex" alt="$1900$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> which equates to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c8e6bf4d57cd6f2bb0d7d43e00f0f442f53b84.png" class="latex" alt="$49$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> leap years. So, the amount of days we have to go back is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/0/690374600847fe6b85660fe19d879d3892edb1d8.png" class="latex" alt="$200(365) + 49$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="111" height="20" /> days which in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd857d6a0ef1f81bc60305f76c5c39f1194b40d9.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{mod }7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="14" /> gives us 4. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> days back from tuesday is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/0/c00f5a7587ce2ee018577d5509f333f896ee6940.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ \text{Friday}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="93" height="28" />.
</p>
