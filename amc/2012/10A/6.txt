<p>The product of two positive numbers is 9. The reciprocal of one of these numbers is 4 times the reciprocal of the other number. What is the sum of the two numbers?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/4/d845a6293555ff25411b727306dbc36e6111e506.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{10}{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{20}{3}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{15}{2}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 8$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="388" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the two numbers equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. From the information given in the problem, two equations can be written:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/4/f0457561f9f1b01411cfbe364647dd0eac1b1ccb.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c86dd577c0eae94254c0707bb909c9f742af2ad9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{x}=4 \left( \frac{1}{y} \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="87" height="43" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c8e5841ffebe81e4f0a4ca06c1500abf637dc9.png" class="latex" alt="$4x=y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" />
</p><p>Replacing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> in the equation,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab7ab1cff4fed46c3ebb0cf7b06d3ba86db40b3.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2=9$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="15" />
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/6/b469dcc2e054456ab4c6d9a8c8b5403a61178241.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> would then be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/8/96833e93d4751aed0ef00575b813c588e9645200.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \times$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="20" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/0/a90dec76e5e58db01806005f31c9be903a11c194.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2}=6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />
</p><p>The sum would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/d/0adfad30accea7db26a323c1116bffc3b6193bf0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{2}+6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="37" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/0/5f0eb0750a713be53278f8fcd44d18edcb0cafa7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{15}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
