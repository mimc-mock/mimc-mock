<p>A <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a832b76c4f4a4ec13ac1b557da4fed6006e64d00.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> rectangle and a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f3f04882ae10c114ebe301c72ee68990d97c48e.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> rectangle are contained within a square without overlapping at any point, and the sides of the square are parallel to the sides of the two given rectangles. What is the smallest possible area of the square? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/9/669ade051dc75933c164c9d0579381fc055e224c.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 16\qquad \textbf{(B) } 25\qquad \textbf{(C) } 36\qquad \textbf{(D) } 49\qquad \textbf{(E) } 64$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="401" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>By placing the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a832b76c4f4a4ec13ac1b557da4fed6006e64d00.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> rectangle adjacent to the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f3f04882ae10c114ebe301c72ee68990d97c48e.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> rectangle with the 3 side of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/3/a832b76c4f4a4ec13ac1b557da4fed6006e64d00.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> rectangle next to the 4 side of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/3/9f3f04882ae10c114ebe301c72ee68990d97c48e.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> rectangle, we get a figure that can be completely enclosed in a square with a side length of 5. The area of this square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/3/3b31bdf57c202bda35b77d630124399deab12b96.png" class="latex" alt="$5^2 = 25$" width="58" height="15" />.
</p><p>Since placing the two rectangles inside a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62d4eeb6a966219df4ac79cfc9981ec095e6be0b.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \times 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> square must result in overlap, the smallest possible area of the square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/3/dc3d885331dfd1c95d782aac3adccec7dd046fb7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }25}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
