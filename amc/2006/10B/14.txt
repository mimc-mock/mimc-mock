<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the roots of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f2787cc2c741509cc364d73b58f86c788da59c21.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-mx+2=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="130" height="16" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/0/e505372ca41c86f6672a6801b75efd23db2a3098.png" class="latex" alt="$a+\frac1b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/295ea0f852c92e630bf5e2af8d760929420f6954.png" class="latex" alt="$b+\frac1a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" /> are the roots of the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac36e406af02bf6555ee54ff421e92be152afb9f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-px+q=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="18" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/1/641446adf4ff407ab449e0d92666584f79d980cc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{5}{2}\qquad \textbf{(B) } \frac{7}{2}\qquad \textbf{(C) } 4\qquad \textbf{(D) } \frac{9}{2}\qquad \textbf{(E) } 8$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution">Video Solution</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/3dfbWzOfJAI?t=457">https://youtu.be/3dfbWzOfJAI?t=457</a>
</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_equation" title="Quadratic equation">quadratic equation</a> of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/2/48240238b0a7255fd48482f895e8cb2149ca9b8d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + bx + c = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="16" />, the product of the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Root" class="mw-redirect" title="Root">roots</a> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> (<a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_Formulas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieta&#39;s Formulas">Vieta's Formulas</a>).
</p><p>Using this property, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18fbcfabf473b776f1b72c23aab537ae5993b60f.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=2$" width="50" height="12" /> and
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/2/f12377ddf1a26499dd19910d8dd73deff546a8c3.png" class="latex" alt="$q = (a+\frac{1}{b})\cdot(b+\frac{1}{a}) = \frac{ab+1}{b} \cdot \frac{ab+1}{a}  = \frac{(ab+1)^2}{ab} = \frac{(2+1)^2}{2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }\frac{9}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="581" height="49" />.
</p>
<ul><li>Notice the fact that we never actually found the roots.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Assume <a href="/wiki/index.php/Without_loss_of_generality" title="Without loss of generality">without loss of generality</a> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/4/904f27995d33e0d39f43ec959e7cb100516e9396.png" class="latex" alt="$m=3$" width="48" height="12" />. We can factor the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d51dced775e273dc9699181601dd8b18b78545c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-3x+2=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="16" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/3/903719e118778a59337eda2f412e50160c6acd81.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-1)(x-2)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />. Using these values, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/0/de0c244682a2c9137d52533c86a9b89bfe8805ce.png" class="latex" alt="$a+\frac1b=\frac32$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="80" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/8/8c833d8ca6b692e229bc900677c1a8bf57910efa.png" class="latex" alt="$b+\frac1a=3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />. By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> is the product of the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/3/ac36e406af02bf6555ee54ff421e92be152afb9f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-px+q=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="18" />, which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/0/e505372ca41c86f6672a6801b75efd23db2a3098.png" class="latex" alt="$a+\frac1b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/295ea0f852c92e630bf5e2af8d760929420f6954.png" class="latex" alt="$b+\frac1a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="37" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1ac9be832a49c5519646ae0009983b9e6014024.png" class="latex" alt="$q=\left(a+\frac1b\right)\left(b+\frac1a\right)=\frac32\cdot3=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }\frac92}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="323" height="49" />.
</p>
