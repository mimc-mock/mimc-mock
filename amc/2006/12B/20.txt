<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be chosen at random from the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. What is the probability that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/9954af0afc226930c792cbc2e0668ef1fc1f7872.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor - \lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor = 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="198" height="19" />?
Here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/1/441416e660d25335f27e55334ac2a06e51541c97.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="23" height="19" /> denotes the greatest integer that is less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/6/086c22a6e12fd8a862c2c553dba0670d085718ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac 18 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac 3{20} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac 16 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac 15  \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac 14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="370" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> be an arbitrary integer. For which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> do we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9ab95f789c6b50ebe31b2e923db5973738d88c2.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor = \lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor = k$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="201" height="19" />?
</p><p>The equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/d/e4d2e3a674af7e0f0f24b6da1a238137faf57d35.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor = k$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="101" height="19" /> can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/9/5295da19eb2aa649b3708d9385b3d595e67a7050.png" class="latex" alt="$10^k \leq x &lt; 10^{k+1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="18" />. The second one gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/d/90d2b032d1babb94ef22f86ebf596b67fdf720f7.png" class="latex" alt="$10^k \leq 4x &lt; 10^{k+1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="135" height="18" />. Combining these, we get that both hold at the same time if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/5/1f53cc25e2458f5bf8f797866075ca1216f7f94d.png" class="latex" alt="$10^k \leq x &lt; \frac{10^{k+1}}4$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="130" height="40" />.
</p><p>Hence for each integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> we get an interval of values for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/5/9954af0afc226930c792cbc2e0668ef1fc1f7872.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor - \lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor = 0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="198" height="19" />. These intervals are obviously pairwise disjoint.
</p><p>For any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/f/23fa365b4a0751d7292384305fbca7fb7329316e.png" class="latex" alt="$k\geq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> the corresponding interval is disjoint with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, so it does not contribute to our answer. On the other hand, for any <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0b043119262a2beb49176cb4beb5c50ec1915a2.png" class="latex" alt="$k&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> the entire interval is inside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/1/be11a56a55425d6c6b69f70f5fa98eab95e8ad18.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Hence our answer is the sum of the lengths of the intervals for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0b043119262a2beb49176cb4beb5c50ec1915a2.png" class="latex" alt="$k&lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />.
</p><p>For a fixed <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> the length of the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/7/c47f27f00a17b2434ddf1a50a456d596a97cbd0e.png" class="latex" alt="$\left[ 10^k, \frac{10^{k+1}}4 \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="100" height="44" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/3/4d35635182849bae8e118fc4b5205c8650697cd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 32\cdot 10^k$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="51" height="37" />. 
</p><p>This means that our result is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/3/66362d70547dfaf83aff31571d3591cd0ed58ff1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 32 \left( 10^{-1} + 10^{-2} + \cdots \right) = \frac 32 \cdot \frac 19 = \boxed{\frac 16}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="285" height="48" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The largest value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad23c112010fbf57d3d35bb956c54b44a1d64c4e.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{0}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ecdf579f047118eed51c59652bb79fff813df60.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; 10^{-1}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="15" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fce809a49cf02d76de2cd32309546c0498e1918.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="19" /> doesn't fulfill the condition unless <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/6/8365ca24fc34a05a85b5b3c32d9049b4f96e81b1.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{-2} \leq x &lt; 0.25 * 10^{-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="178" height="17" />. The same holds when you get smaller, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/5/fb55554c1b2f63a8a8c22789bdc07fa54bfbea83.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0.25 * 10^{n}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="110" height="13" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/e/47e4f873107bffa06aff7bf154f25daac593cecc.png" class="latex" alt="$n \leq 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> is the lowest value such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> becomes a higher power of 10.
</p><p>Recognize that this is a geometric sequence. The probability of choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> such that  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fce809a49cf02d76de2cd32309546c0498e1918.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5d9273cd203ec290c30286a608c73e501ad347.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="19" /> both equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/472449a81b0591e71269e557b418e59fc8fc64fa.png" class="latex" alt="$(9/10)* (15/90) =15/100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="205" height="18" />, because there is a 90 percent chance of choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ecdf579f047118eed51c59652bb79fff813df60.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; 10^{-1}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="68" height="15" />, and only values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa0a382ce3d6bb1bb75ce59ed25bc26724c57450.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{-1}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="34" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/a/2ca53097709d5ea8add7dae667d3b3a9dc709cfc.png" class="latex" alt="$0.25*10^{0}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="15" /> work in this case. Then, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fce809a49cf02d76de2cd32309546c0498e1918.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}4x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5d9273cd203ec290c30286a608c73e501ad347.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\log_{10}x\rfloor$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="64" height="19" /> both equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" />, you have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/3/c9379131e057c61783373c44e7dd5e89603b0f3b.png" class="latex" alt="$(1/10) * ((9/10) *(15/90))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="18" />. This is a geometric series with ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/a/74a3d67ed68c56765f7050e4a060eb6de5470dd2.png" class="latex" alt="$1/10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="35" height="18" />. Using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/5/4f5cb4536d71b01c01a8d04d369fbdb47b2f9224.png" class="latex" alt="$a/(1-r)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> for the sum of an infinite geometric sequence, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/8/4c85e1bc316449ee44c3cc453d2ead00508489a0.png" class="latex" alt="$(15/100)/(1-(1/10)) =  \boxed{\frac 16}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="222" height="48" />.
</p><p>Solution by Halt_CatchFire
</p>
