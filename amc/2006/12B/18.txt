<p>An object in the plane moves from one lattice point to another. At each step, the object may move one unit to the right, one unit to the left, one unit up, or one unit down. If the object starts at the origin and takes a ten-step path, how many different points could be the final point?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/2/282d359388900f278287611464de8863d1e57f4e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 120 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 121 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 221 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 230 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 231$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="432" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the starting point be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> steps we can only be in locations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/7/887d3639cad3387678a271b50d862d3ff35b64ef.png" class="latex" alt="$|x|+|y|\leq 10$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="104" height="18" />. Additionally, each step changes the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parity" title="Parity">parity</a> of exactly one coordinate. Hence after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> steps we can only be in locations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> is even. It can easily be shown that each location that satisfies these two conditions is indeed reachable.
</p><p>Once we pick <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/c/eec582558d20aeb364ca85d50d2081953e4094af.png" class="latex" alt="$x\in\{-10,\dots,10\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c683755fa589f0c5d8835fecc4b6cdb52d9c208.png" class="latex" alt="$11-|x|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" /> valid choices for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, giving a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/b/fdb534909b1e8856e9c04d450936eca1f5a2d9bf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{121}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="39" height="23" /> possible positions.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> moves results in a lot of possible endpoints, so we try small cases first.
</p><p>If the object only makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> move, it is obvious that there are only 4 possible points that the object can move to.
If the object makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> moves, it can move to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/5/2a5f89d3f7133140df636f513744cf04af597309.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/f/31fa6b94e5a84b15c9a080cd52edc749580d90c4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/7/e575424cbb00924687d2a4e7e2aa3b1bbcfec030.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/f/e6f3bf596b5b5551059a9d28fbbcc0cd576d4838.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/b/cdb1e50df224b89e360bdec6ca6a0adaba1c3aab.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/6155e28d15217ab6e8ea537771fdce74d647ed6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/4/264de7a1a8b9a40142b9e852f2a93a87473e3049.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> as well as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b86f54134d69cad8793688a431aa907aaf2698.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> moves.
If the object makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> moves, it can end up at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9a6559764cd20f0ff10edd3cd446afd3fffba0d.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7a25c234892fb8a7738bc31e5ccee3bf9ad5afc.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69273482bff8727194f6407e55ac1bfa94d9c555.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a6a917716191facf6d17308cdb8119392b77732.png" class="latex" alt="$(3, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f87e62007337ad2c663ec7e0a1902ced7bfc811.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/9/8f94bd48579891ed9e55f65b047ddba7ec1bd1e3.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/a/00a4ab2b2bd612a4b55653c920e81060a4c29831.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/7/7b76f33741877a3689a474af44d81b23003db379.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, -2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/b/9ab1fd911f0a673fb11d55f18649034d8a85fefb.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, -1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/a/00a4ab2b2bd612a4b55653c920e81060a4c29831.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, -3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> etc. for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> moves.
</p><p>At this point we can guess that for n moves, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/128328cd49de5eec06e04d25ae0a4fea53cffe83.png" class="latex" alt="$(n + 1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="19" /> different endpoints. Thus, for 10 moves, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/3/cf307af5793b1820789a10c54f8240188778f66e.png" class="latex" alt="$11^2 = 121$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="15" /> endpoints, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p>
