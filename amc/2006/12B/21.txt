<p>Rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/2/d1216dd3855e1bb88b6136b4ce49c58b57747417.png" class="latex" alt="$2006$" width="35" height="12" />.  An ellipse with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d0f141a4950a55a46eed31ee659daf8cf909d8.png" class="latex" alt="$2006\pi$" width="46" height="12" /> passes through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and has foci at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />.  What is the perimeter of the rectangle? (The area of an ellipse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/0/6801049254e97b2fe75bfc4410f1184945956dec.png" class="latex" alt="$ab\pi$" width="28" height="12" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" /> are the lengths of the axes.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/5/955c5e4f2621cea32b853cde718f8b0c1ceec38b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac {16\sqrt {2006}}{\pi} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac {1003}4 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 8\sqrt {1003} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 6\sqrt {2006} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ \frac {32\sqrt {1003}}\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="604" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/5/905e36368530c426cb71e1619e8af231adb4aa63.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(7cm);  real l=10, w=7, ang=asin(w/sqrt(l*l+w*w))*180/pi;  draw((-l,-w)--(l,-w)--(l,w)--(-l,w)--cycle); draw(rotate(ang)*ellipse((0,0),2*l+2*w,l*w*2/sqrt(l^2+w^2)));  draw(rotate(ang)*((0,0)--(2l+2w,0)),red); draw(rotate(ang+90)*((0,0)--(l*w*2/sqrt(l^2+w^2),0)),red);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-l,w),NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(-l,-w),SW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(l,-w),SE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,(l,w),SE); // Made by chezbgone2 [/asy]" width="332" height="252" />
</p><p>Let the rectangle have side lengths <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b25f8e64b484493fda944d25cad453423041fe6.png" class="latex" alt="$l$" width="5" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />.  Let the axis of the ellipse on which the foci lie have length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" />, and let the other axis have length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" />.  We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/c/64ca5cb3ac715ad0642585f852b24b5a23c6ba4f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[lw=ab=2006\]" width="120" height="12" />
From the definition of an ellipse, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/d/57d53aba4f416592458489e17d4b197f52740fe2.png" class="latex" alt="$l+w=2a\Longrightarrow \frac{l+w}{2}=a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="203" height="37" />.  Also, the diagonal of the rectangle has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/b/e1bedadf1cd5b9bcf61cff42b419af42bf8d1013.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{l^2+w^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="71" height="18" />.  Comparing the lengths of the axes and the distance from the foci to the center, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/d/55db6690aa38e6203cda73957eec187e9696b385.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2=\frac{l^2+w^2}{4}+b^2\Longrightarrow \frac{l^2+2lw+w^2}{4}=\frac{l^2+w^2}{4}+b^2\Longrightarrow \frac{lw}{2}=b^2\Longrightarrow b=\sqrt{1003}\]" width="620" height="39" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/9/db991caab869bfd9d2165e1e111372f3949475e4.png" class="latex" alt="$ab=2006$" width="77" height="12" />, we now know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/c/facc0957ababc8aa66fcb117136bf5dc7760ebae.png" class="latex" alt="$a\sqrt{1003}=2006\Longrightarrow a=2\sqrt{1003}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="255" height="18" /> and because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/c/56ca08e76b650cf19d057268b9940b1814b41681.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{l+w}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="37" />, or one-fourth of the rectangle's perimeter, we multiply by four to get an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20f78360c5a006a24301e55fd2ccd41a2250b5cf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{8\sqrt{1003}}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="72" height="30" />.
</p>
