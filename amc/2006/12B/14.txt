<p>Elmo makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> sandwiches for a fundraiser. For each sandwich he uses <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> globs of peanut butter at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> cents per glob and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> blobs of jam at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> cents per glob. The cost of the peanut butter and jam to make all the sandwiches is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/1/a5109425aeb0bee10fcf6d613ab7614e840a4a0b.png" class="latex" alt="$2.53$" width="31" height="12" />. Assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> are all positive integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/9/0c9c0479fbd8aad814e20f8d875602b38a115c4d.png" class="latex" alt="$N&gt;1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />. What is the cost of the jam Elmo uses to make the sandwiches? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/7/5c7d096273541a144fcca6304517a6a8c417913f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 1.05 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 1.25 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 1.45 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 1.65 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 1.85$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="458" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>From the given, we know that 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/4/6644fb3b6c6efa3d02278a72a140dfead0f3aede.png" class="latex" alt="$253=N(4B+5J)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="147" height="18" /> 
(The numbers are in cents)
</p><p>since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/3806c0e9287df04377bf99f6c938a80234633bb0.png" class="latex" alt="$253=11\cdot23$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is an integer, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7812e61b6f26d0d994d2a80bef28c7a2ef2745.png" class="latex" alt="$4B+5J=11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="108" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" />. It is easily deduced that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> is impossible to make with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> integers, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/c/9dcaff3b5a28c5bc81229c7e0cb1216ad4bd7296.png" class="latex" alt="$N=11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/b/f0b91d254cadc578c6d7da2f0333fff14f59d98f.png" class="latex" alt="$4B+5J=23$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="108" height="14" />. Then, it can be guessed and checked quite simply that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/1/3417d76d7ccdf011b0760de58dd37e42a924bdf7.png" class="latex" alt="$B=2$" width="47" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf9fa150888856bbc5c32a1c159404113d588134.png" class="latex" alt="$J=3$" width="44" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f60aad34482612b8f25aa6f25e5c92c71ff10b4.png" class="latex" alt="$4B+5J=4(2)+5(3)=23$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="220" height="18" />. The problem asks for the total cost of jam, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c72265281ce70c766f98d1e42904f8496825170.png" class="latex" alt="$N(5J)=11(15)=165$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="177" height="18" /> cents, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/c/cdccd49ee1c1c2ca47727a5d900a6ad6c752300a.png" class="latex" alt="$1.65\implies\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="108" height="18" />
</p>
