<p>Given a finite sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/5/595f49ae5d398ac1a14cd13844b8a508bdfa9eaf.png" class="latex" alt="$S=(a_1,a_2,\ldots ,a_n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="150" height="18" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> real numbers, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/6/496761119df26cc13f4b05a2e99254eff491d695.png" class="latex" alt="$A(S)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> be the sequence 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/2/4325f16692282dfef51a4fe932b8a7e7d06f6a94.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{a_1+a_2}{2},\frac{a_2+a_3}{2},\ldots ,\frac{a_{n-1}+a_n}{2}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="272" height="43" />
</p><p>of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> real numbers. Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/2/0629e1540d0f6b6832668f7a21899205a274fd65.png" class="latex" alt="$A^1(S)=A(S)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="19" /> and, for each integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d609764da0dea9c1ef12e2a493a59f90af03b12.png" class="latex" alt="$2\le m\le n-1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="114" height="14" />, define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/d/86d50b8b0e9ca347e49131a9199f60b44b0132d5.png" class="latex" alt="$A^m(S)=A(A^{m-1}(S))$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="19" />. Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/e/02e28efa00c4f2c66d1aa381c0cb1f8e0ef8d6e4.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e7889d3d5dfbbbe1d4b67b5c7563dfb7a7a85f6b.png" class="latex" alt="$S=(1,x,x^2,\ldots ,x^{100})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="19" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/c/3dcb723ecbfe28a363cc7ed75830807bffb0986b.png" class="latex" alt="$A^{100}(S)=(1/2^{50})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="19" />, then what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/e/64e35d4a55651b87cedef1994b9d9750a41c3c7e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 1-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \sqrt{2}-1\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } \frac{1}{2}\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } 2-\sqrt{2}\qquad \mathrm{(E) \ }  \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="507" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/1/8614efa469574473ea38eae7b192f0144fe9a703.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A^1(S)=\left(\frac{1+x}{2},\frac{x+x^2}{2},...,\frac{x^{99}+x^{100}}{2}\right)\]" width="315" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/2/9b2fdff313f1b6438eda71cb4253fba50b5ea9cf.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[A^2(S)=\left(\frac{1+2x+x^2}{2^2},\frac{x+2x^2+x^3}{2^2},...,\frac{x^{98}+2x^{99}+x^{100}}{2^2}\right)\]" width="469" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e00a877ac56692fbcb156eab6cdea47340edd594.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\Rightarrow A^2(S)=\left(\frac{(x+1)^2}{2^2},\frac{x(x+1)^2}{2^2},...,\frac{x^{98}(x+1)^2}{2^2}\right)\]" width="392" height="43" />
</p><p>In general, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/4/5144ebd6a0420c6c12e1de1f1724951faafd812e.png" class="latex" alt="$A^n(S)=\left(\frac{(x+1)^n}{2^n},\frac{x(x+1)^n}{2^n},...,\frac{x^{100-n}(x+1)^n}{2^n}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="402" height="43" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/0/6a06abae962f696022be6f80d9f857a627c53126.png" class="latex" alt="$A^n(s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/f/fdfe87522486e4e67c34523af8e6302d6c25e1d1.png" class="latex" alt="$101-n$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" /> terms. Specifically, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b86f9dd2ca2f2f2f90a4f1906f53a67c258b961c.png" class="latex" alt="$A^{100}(S)=\frac{(x+1)^{100}}{2^{100}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="164" height="39" /> To find x, we need only solve the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaafc2a9f0d5f76cdfa07357c305a20e1fa2ab81.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(x+1)^{100}}{2^{100}}=\frac{1}{2^{50}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="130" height="39" />. Algebra yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/f/abf6a55c8cf6cbe65c3fbe265f40ffcc27a61374.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\sqrt{2}-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="18" />.
</p>
