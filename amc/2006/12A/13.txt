<p>The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vertex" title="Vertex">vertices</a> of a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/1/af1a2cb4ff1720f169ddde1726c1a5a1ab816ae0.png" class="latex" alt="$3-4-5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a> are the centers of three mutually externally tangent <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">circles</a>, as shown. What is the sum of the areas of the three circles?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:Original_2006_12A_13.png" class="image"><img alt="Original 2006 12A 13.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//1/18/Original_2006_12A_13.png" width="477" height="310" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdb7828f9b5375af03c58a5221b78253d595c2c.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A) \ } 12\pi\qquad \mathrm{(B) \ } \frac{25\pi}{2}\qquad \mathrm{(C) \ } 13\pi\qquad \mathrm{(D) \ } \frac{27\pi}{2}\qquad\mathrm{(E) \ }  14\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="450" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the radius of the smallest circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/0/1900b71723057339142feef3152cbfe3d588f3c3.png" class="latex" alt="$r_A$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" />, the radius of the second largest circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/a/15a421b8519a788db422b9150f43792fd6dc4c49.png" class="latex" alt="$r_B$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="10" />, and the radius of the largest circle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/2/ab2dc239c4e0eae5c9bb4fa82937f6b76b3a4fe7.png" class="latex" alt="$r_C$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="10" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/2/d32fc5e73b196d772754dd22491fcd35a84bb5f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_A + r_B = 3\]" width="93" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/c/19c0750788a3a435260e2fd673e16c9ac408bd4f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_A + r_C = 4\]" width="93" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/0/5b0ea74a9c0df59da5cb769e4a8d0415e3c427ea.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_ B + r_C = 5\]" width="93" height="15" />
</p><p>Adding up all these equations and then dividing both sides by 2, we get,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/1/7d1cefc3fb4362fcd5dbae413e070ba9c5bc3a71.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_A + r_B + r_C = 6\]" width="134" height="15" />
</p><p>Then, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/c/bdce8b27aad966cc6135f5df01b677cb1d602633.png" class="latex" alt="$r_A = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/b/b0b39ef5749e4c6f4677969d9be79ee6f5054cfc.png" class="latex" alt="$r_B = 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="14" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a65da53275d5d0e12f9c2ec827371038404462bd.png" class="latex" alt="$r_C = 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="52" height="15" /> Then we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/a/86af4175a0ccc276e21b081fc336d6358980a579.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2 \pi + 2^2 \pi + 3^2 \pi = 14 \pi \iff\mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="259" height="19" />
</p>
