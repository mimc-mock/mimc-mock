<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>One possibility is to use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Coordinate_plane" class="mw-redirect" title="Coordinate plane">coordinate plane</a>, setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> at the origin. Point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/f/77fbd20b92946dbb65e0eda34946cd86a9ec293b.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59fff9655c08dd200cfe8b8da28198b25941244.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(s + \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}},\ s + \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="154" height="43" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4dba3a2b89599010096ed143bca2d1b9f07b1f7b.png" class="latex" alt="$B, D$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="38" height="16" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Collinear" title="Collinear">collinear</a> and contain a diagonal of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> results in
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/1/d71803837ddd2739fca2601339696a8bda7a977c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AF^2 + EF^2 = AE^2\]" width="151" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/d/5fdeb0d443078117c60e8fd7a8bc11fac819921d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r^2  + \left(\sqrt{9 + 5\sqrt{2}}\right)^2  = \left(\left(s + \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}\right) - 0\right)^2 + \left(\left(s + \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}\right) - s\right)^2\]" width="482" height="46" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/3/5035fb6846965c9f72d4bab118a4348594b3539d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r^2  + 9 + 5\sqrt{2} = s^2 + rs\sqrt{2} + \frac{r^2}{2} + \frac{r^2}{2}\]" width="289" height="39" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/c/d4c51cc1cc2c9c1a9f1289b458925ff8757c55b9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9 + 5\sqrt{2} = s^2 + rs\sqrt{2}\]" width="168" height="18" />
</p><p>This implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/f/9dff47f805ff3ffa7c2b8f8d98335e9c90bc3e4c.png" class="latex" alt="$rs = 5$" width="50" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/1/f016d931ad7a089f823ee0ebbf733abbc6f4a222.png" class="latex" alt="$s^2 = 9$" width="48" height="15" />; dividing gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/2/7b29c1552c256e981dc5b982aae6a8b77d085b55.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{r}{s} = \frac{5}{9} \Rightarrow B$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="92" height="37" />.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>First note that angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a053a2fd6ac8ef0ab8f8530fc73eb38e6ff59de9.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AFE$" width="54" height="13" /> is right since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/5/c65a0b904f8f63efdc2b5abfef3f4f05b2d3f990.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AF}$" width="28" height="15" /> is tangent to the circle. Using the Pythagorean Theorem on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/f/e8f2628c7bd8aba6794daadad02ce8f46d94c15c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AFE$" width="58" height="13" />, then, we see
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/c/25c4083d528c34ad1d6367510a4e14faccac95f1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AE^2 = 9 + 5\sqrt{2} + r^2.\]" width="166" height="18" />
</p><p>But it can also be seen that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/c/b5cfb9531c01457c8b5de849f536708340dacd99.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BDA = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" />. Therefore, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> lies on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f32db217d7630882c3441fa8821cd70a6958c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BE}$" width="29" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/a/32a167e8b700f7704beaecb7c2845e7d30e6bf09.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADE = 135^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />. Using the Law of Cosines on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" />, we see
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/6/8161db3710ab9fa2497d6e595aa9b77859ca1577.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AE^2 = s^2 + r^2 - 2sr\cos(135^\circ)\]" width="237" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/a/eca29b32ce59ee360a954cac585dbdfeee4c25b9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AE^2 = s^2 + r^2 - 2sr\left(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\]" width="230" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/40563b86e4b5862175b46ba601c2357078403ecc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AE^2 = s^2 + r^2 + \sqrt{2}sr\]" width="176" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/b/9ab7aedd4738e7284f627b50809732dacad3f1c6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9 + 5\sqrt{2} + r^2 = s^2 + r^2 + \sqrt{2}sr\]" width="244" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/b/ceb83cd257547b0822adade5376c7986adb35db7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9 + 5\sqrt{2} = s^2 + \sqrt{2}sr.\]" width="170" height="18" />
</p><p>Thus, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> are rational, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/1/f016d931ad7a089f823ee0ebbf733abbc6f4a222.png" class="latex" alt="$s^2 = 9$" width="48" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/7/5d7a60015a787964c2dedb656ed8097102608005.png" class="latex" alt="$sr = 5$" width="50" height="12" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76720064e1b87e0398cffaa492648ca5ef307a8.png" class="latex" alt="$s = 3$" width="41" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/08988a46ab759d05f0a6a803be7ef5e0730373a5.png" class="latex" alt="$r = \frac{5}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/4/be49f6698c1390e4faf950eecdcea634b44dff10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{r}{s} = \frac{5}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="48" height="37" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>(Similar to Solution 1)
First, draw line AE and mark a point Z that is equidistant from E and D so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/c/15c5f55e21488b10de4e2b7799191d4ac6bd90d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{DZE} = 90^\circ$" width="105" height="13" /> and that line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/a/b5ac02dee6530bc6c04958fb5c85ade5142d4279.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AZ}$" width="27" height="15" /> includes point D. Since DE is equal to the radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b889af79bfeebef3608c8d32397b140934ef1802.png" class="latex" alt="$DZ=EZ=\frac{r}{\sqrt2}=\frac{r\sqrt2}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="199" height="45" />
</p><p>Note that triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/f/e8f2628c7bd8aba6794daadad02ce8f46d94c15c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AFE$" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9614cf308afc05856826cf9db2e7bda7ad71a1.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AZE$" width="57" height="13" /> share the same hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/7/577acfc9c2249b274eb453cea7b0723737e4a11f.png" class="latex" alt="$(AE)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />, meaning that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/6/f663e70afe26fee9429f9c29f2033a144f67731b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AZ^2+EZ^2=AF^2+EF^2\]" width="209" height="16" />
Plugging in our values we have:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/b/50bbebe7331fc1695fbcbfda0189aefbdb789b84.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(s+\frac{r\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{r\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2=\left(\sqrt{9+5\sqrt{2}}\right)^2+r^2\]" width="371" height="56" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/5/ef5e6ee171a750a306c118b67f27c3cf96e32dda.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s^2+sr\sqrt{2}+\frac{r^2}{2}+\frac{r^2}{2}=9+5\sqrt{2}+r^2\]" width="290" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/4/5640b3c27003030cc9bc0dd605220ee494b14993.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s^2+sr\sqrt{2}=9+5\sqrt{2}\]" width="168" height="18" />
By logic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/5/4955f631984fec9540e6bcb3c372e92c60d2da94.png" class="latex" alt="$s=3$" width="41" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/6/42632d2ef57866c887f7166ef80ce757f41af0f8.png" class="latex" alt="$sr=5 \implies r=5/3.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/d/2fd90516b67d76cd1517bd707a493956ba5cda39.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{5}{3}}{3}=\frac{5}{9}=\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="40" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_-_Alcumus">Solution 4 - Alcumus</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5bd04e23c8346bd1d0008b62aea1761ae09247f.png" class="latex" alt="$B=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51db4d0c619e77e499120400d0b946dacec6923.png" class="latex" alt="$C=(s,0)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="80" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/f/ccfb096b7da0632a2e489fcf47a5b58a9096cdf8.png" class="latex" alt="$A=(0,s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/1/b9149945dc46694f85f5cd3a83dcdb6e2a630fc3.png" class="latex" alt="$D=(s,s)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f60d8abe1c9a76d516cef00549359459afe9f2f.png" class="latex" alt="$E=\left(s+\frac{r}{\sqrt{2}},s+\frac{r}{\sqrt{2}} \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="187" height="43" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> are rational, it follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/9/d89c0b50ac47f4c57bb62dd8eff06ffb3378671e.png" class="latex" alt="$s^2=9$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/d/14d993acb4511620652b175aa4fbb4663b662cb8.png" class="latex" alt="$rs=5$" width="50" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1fae4c27efb1ce2d6b1516092507b0317c0f8729.png" class="latex" alt="$r/s = \boxed{5/9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="89" height="29" />.
</p><p>OR
</p><p>Extend <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> past <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to meet the circle at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0943d0e14a57f9a68f146c1feee289602d802db6.png" class="latex" alt="$G \ne D$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="17" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is collinear with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/8040720a89c961637d47eeb0fade626cc0965cc7.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EDG = 45^\circ.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="114" height="15" /> Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/b/9bb488f8c33bc32d2bb981edffec0fb5145b0849.png" class="latex" alt="$ED = EG,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="16" /> which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/8/ed8679511f143eb7a8709a2e914d4370349d789c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle EGD = 45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="105" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/255d1f32521c27edfd48bc7f60fda22ddf59ad3d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle EDG$" width="60" height="13" /> is an isosceles right triangle. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/4/4d4e9add11bd19f91a7f28b9bb14eb4eb0c24835.png" class="latex" alt="$DG = r\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="18" />. By the Power of a Point Theorem,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/8/f689ecaf79cad4b3cc8d1186ef784d3b0cf849fc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 9+5\sqrt{2} &amp;= AF^2 \\ &amp;= AD\cdot AG\\ &amp; = AD\cdot \left(AD+DG\right) \\ &amp;= s\left(s+r\sqrt{2}\right) \\ &amp;= s^2+rs\sqrt{2}.\end{align*}" width="225" height="137" /> As in the first solution, we conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/3/a1388266dc29607fd1cb2656d79bf09bfda697a5.png" class="latex" alt="$r/s=\boxed{5/9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="89" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_-_Answer_Choices">Solution 5 - Answer Choices</span></h3>
<p>(Last minute solution) We roughly measure the distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> and the distance of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is clearly less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, we can eliminate answer choices (D) and (E). Next, if we compare the distances, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> seems to be just a little more than half of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, thus eliminating answer choice (A). <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/5/b55ca7a0aa88ab7d58f4fc035317fdac39b17861.png" class="latex" alt="$r$" width="8" height="8" /> is only a little bit bigger than half of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, so we can reasonably assume that their ratio is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. That leaves us with answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/5/3f58d2efaaa17d28cec9dd927d78708a63b43e24.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{C}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" /> , or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7cee4c1bc083c3979f7ac19ac9f8646a3d0a29e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />.
</p>
