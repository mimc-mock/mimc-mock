A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Common_internal_tangent_line" title="Common internal tangent line"> common internal tangent</a> intersects the circles at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, respectively. Lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/8/5c884057c1218625814fc38808eb0f395aac6928.png" class="latex" alt="$AE=5$" width="61" height="13" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2006_AMC12A-16a.png" class="image"><img alt="2006 AMC12A-16a.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//d/d2/2006_AMC12A-16a.png" width="559" height="312" /></a>
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/0/410914bb0ff2a9961ffd2cf13393f5ed8d9fefae.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AED$" width="56" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/b/f1b1cee5c0355428ff8d477ecbf406f0f99bcca8.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BEC$" width="56" height="12" /> are vertical angles so they are congruent, as are angles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/0/a009564c26bf86933bd2d32bf0c1b854d12a3084.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ADE$" width="56" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/1/7d1d270eb208e23b0fd5d26568bc8e28503a699c.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BCE$" width="56" height="12" /> (both are right angles because the radius and <a href="/wiki/index.php/Tangent_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tangent line">tangent line</a> at a point on a circle are always perpendicular). Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/7/887249ad200c996e533aef3f0c4708747c590baf.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACE \sim \triangle BDE$" width="143" height="13" />.
By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, line segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/1/ff1ddf6aa610ac904a125091618f4d20600389e0.png" class="latex" alt="$DE = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" />.  The sides are proportional, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e1048c73a38104b0c10336e908627be3f450cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{DE}{AD} = \frac{CE}{BC} \Rightarrow \frac{4}{3} = \frac{CE}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="188" height="37" />. This makes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/c/42cf9731256157a7e7d856e5e7825753ee45b8e8.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = \frac{32}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/1/b813b36466b7655b3dc50fe41e22e96982fed8f6.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = CE + DE = 4 + \frac{32}{3} = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }\frac{44}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="307" height="49" />.
</p>
