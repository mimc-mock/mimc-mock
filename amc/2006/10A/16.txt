<p>A circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is <a href="/wiki/index.php/Tangent" title="Tangent">tangent</a> to a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. The sides of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> are tangent to the circles as shown, and the sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />  and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> are congruent. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/a/f4a115a9c8dc91a741d0149edb037b3cb07f6b01.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pointpen = black; real t=2^0.5; D((0,0)--(4*t,0)--(2*t,8)--cycle); D(CR((2*t,2),2)); D(CR((2*t,5),1)); D(&#39;B&#39;, (0,0),SW); D(&#39;C&#39;,(4*t,0), SE); D(&#39;A&#39;, (2*t, 8), N); D((2*t,2)--(2*t,4)); D((2*t,5)--(2*t,6)); MP(&#39;2&#39;, (2*t,3), W); MP(&#39;1&#39;,(2*t, 5.5), W);[/asy]" width="252" height="335" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/f/4ef2b62896d8fc15e2d6333dc07f44443676fbd7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{35}{2}\qquad\textbf{(B) } 15\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(C) } \frac{64}{3}\qquad\textbf{(D) } 16\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(E) } 24\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="490" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the centers of the smaller and larger circles be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/1/3216e8846eb44f1055c322fb5bd30169d5d62cec.png" class="latex" alt="$O_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/e/5ee48e5d3968014ef1e27c31e56d2f7ea5f8cb1d.png" class="latex" alt="$O_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="20" height="15" /> , respectively.
Let their tangent points to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />, respectively.
We can then draw the following diagram:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7dd38d7d5efd1fe119ba93263f1f91a5d1d40fb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pointpen = black; pointfontpen = fontsize(10); real t=2^0.5; D((0,0)--(4*t,0)--(2*t,8)--cycle);  D(CR(D((2*t,2)),2)); D(CR(D((2*t,5)),1)); D(&#39;B&#39;, (0,0),SW); D(&#39;C&#39;,(4*t,0), SE); D(&#39;A&#39;, (2*t, 8), N); pair A = foot((2*t,2),(2*t,8),(4*t,0)), B = foot((2*t,5),(2*t,8),(4*t,0)); D((2*t,0)--(2*t,8)); D((2*t,2)--D(A)); D((2*t,5)--D(B)); D(rightanglemark((2*t,2),A,(4*t,0))); D(rightanglemark((2*t,5),B,(4*t,0))); MP(&#39;2&#39;, (2*t,3), W); MP(&#39;1&#39;,(2*t, 5.5), W); MP(&quot;F&quot;,(2*t,0)); MP(&quot;O_1&quot;,(2*t,5),W); MP(&quot;O_2&quot;,(2*t,2),W); MP(&quot;D&quot;,B,NE); MP(&quot;E&quot;,A,NE); [/asy]" width="248" height="335" />
</p><p>We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/f/32fc6f779b0e8adb2293eb9a7d45b88e899c53dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADO_1 \sim \triangle AEO_2 \sim \triangle AFC$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="239" height="16" />. Using the first pair of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Similar_triangles" class="mw-redirect" title="Similar triangles">similar triangles</a>, we write the proportion:
</p>
<div style="text-align:center;"><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/a/10ada06cd47acf9909068e7654b0ddc0944a8f67.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AO_1}{AO_2} = \frac{DO_1}{EO_2} \Longrightarrow \frac{AO_1}{AO_1 + 3} = \frac{1}{2} \Longrightarrow AO_1 = 3$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="358" height="40" /></div>
<p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/6/0a6f8df26d8788dc53740f3194e3dddd68447c31.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = \sqrt{3^2-1^2} = \sqrt{8}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="173" height="19" />.
</p><p>Now using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/5/80526aa7b1b45ef92b994846479159f4bb9ee065.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADO_1 \sim \triangle AFC$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="149" height="16" />,
</p>
<div style="text-align:center;"><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/1/091c4ac75473dc96bea1372a641fa96643f04862.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AD}{AF} = \frac{DO_1}{FC} \Longrightarrow \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{8} = \frac{1}{FC} \Longrightarrow FC = 2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="357" height="41" /></div>
<p>Hence, the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/d/dcd6a252bcb196578125be40211d95e06cb54e54.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2}\cdot AF \cdot BC = \frac{1}{2}\cdot AF \cdot (2\cdot CF) = AF \cdot CF = 8\left(2\sqrt{2}\right) = \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 16\sqrt{2}}\]" width="501" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7dd38d7d5efd1fe119ba93263f1f91a5d1d40fb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(200); pathpen = linewidth(0.7); pointpen = black; pointfontpen = fontsize(10); real t=2^0.5; D((0,0)--(4*t,0)--(2*t,8)--cycle);  D(CR(D((2*t,2)),2)); D(CR(D((2*t,5)),1)); D(&#39;B&#39;, (0,0),SW); D(&#39;C&#39;,(4*t,0), SE); D(&#39;A&#39;, (2*t, 8), N); pair A = foot((2*t,2),(2*t,8),(4*t,0)), B = foot((2*t,5),(2*t,8),(4*t,0)); D((2*t,0)--(2*t,8)); D((2*t,2)--D(A)); D((2*t,5)--D(B)); D(rightanglemark((2*t,2),A,(4*t,0))); D(rightanglemark((2*t,5),B,(4*t,0))); MP(&#39;2&#39;, (2*t,3), W); MP(&#39;1&#39;,(2*t, 5.5), W); MP(&quot;F&quot;,(2*t,0)); MP(&quot;O_1&quot;,(2*t,5),W); MP(&quot;O_2&quot;,(2*t,2),W); MP(&quot;D&quot;,B,NE); MP(&quot;E&quot;,A,NE); [/asy]" width="248" height="335" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/1/ac168761e3eddc1b3ae1d5786fd1aecdecb95d24.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{A O_1 D} \sim \triangle{A O_2 E},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="160" height="17" /> we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/e/ffe5f49ff41927f15b960fd201dba764e76cf957.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{A O_1}{A O_2} = \frac{O_1 D}{O_2 E} = \frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="140" height="40" />.
</p><p>Since we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/c/33cccc92e4c6f69f25637e742ed81ab8af7f30bd.png" class="latex" alt="$O_1 O_2 = 1 + 2 = 3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="144" height="16" /> the total length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/9/899e11a9dc4e364083145ad5eb87be8cf6a0f803.png" class="latex" alt="$A O_2 = 2 \cdot 3 = 6.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="127" height="15" />
</p><p>We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/4/c84a34c023078cc068ba531b409c780e8dba6f33.png" class="latex" alt="$O_2 F = 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/f/14f0baab5817bb749a1d1cb6e83e73add3f612a1.png" class="latex" alt="$A F = A O_2 + O_2 F = 6 + 2 = 8.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="246" height="15" />
</p><p>Also, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/1/4310eee9b620e4646cf61e6c7d7516944da2f4c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABF} \sim \triangle{A E O_2},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="152" height="17" /> we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/e/ffefde884d74dc20d1e7f3f2509eb611d6470c5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AC}{A O_2} = \frac{FC}{O_2 E}.$" style="vertical-align: -15px" width="106" height="40" />
</p><p>Since we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/2/7422d34fdaedc435941b019ccf5e2752f229ce11.png" class="latex" alt="$A O_2 = 6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="67" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/e/aeeabe73062d87290a941ecf5713b472c4525730.png" class="latex" alt="$O_2 E = 2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="73" height="16" /> we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/b/81bae3b81ef46413ae9905f8fbfe044984061532.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AC}{6} = \frac{FC}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="92" height="38" />
</p><p>This equation simplified gets us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/5/6f580c5478a47658be329d8fe86a88fbcd1b0d11.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 3 \cdot FC.$" width="105" height="13" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78e10f4a272e514fcc1fd3a792fee66e3be7ace7.png" class="latex" alt="$FC = a$" width="62" height="12" />
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/b/2eb5b2cd7a1ddfa3ae1c753633ffa6a077d14f20.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{AFC},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="17" /> we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/a/1eac99539a4d1b63bd594eb17b90cd8c912bf3ce.png" class="latex" alt="$AF^2 + FC^2 = AC^2.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="156" height="16" />
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/1/2f11fd78e70b9f9143c3d617e4faf959966a46c3.png" class="latex" alt="$AF = 8$" width="60" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/e/78e10f4a272e514fcc1fd3a792fee66e3be7ace7.png" class="latex" alt="$FC = a$" width="62" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/d/e4d447bf8bb778325fb63c76a9b9fac523174013.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 3a$" width="70" height="13" /> so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/3/0c38c9598abe15fff342b72531367a88df40f962.png" class="latex" alt="$8^2 + a^2 = (3a)^2.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="124" height="19" />
</p><p>Simplifying, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/8/0e8d3dd88b3f5fba4d263c96deeb69832faf45ed.png" class="latex" alt="$64 = 8a^2 \Rightarrow a^2 = 8 \Rightarrow a = \sqrt{8} = 2 \sqrt{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="294" height="18" />
</p><p>Recall that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/515bb0d545fb6989ea589a9002136e7e91a003f8.png" class="latex" alt="$FC=a$" width="62" height="12" />.
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/0/f7063b6ff14723e04667035cb0db64b697f09ea4.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 2 \cdot FC = 2 \cdot 2 \sqrt{2} = 4 \sqrt{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="245" height="18" />
</p><p>Since the height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/4/df4558749dac946baa16514b8f039577ad5d1533.png" class="latex" alt="$AF = 8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="65" height="16" /> we have the area equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/e/42efc04be5a19b21e39187075fa3caa71c2847c1.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4 \sqrt{2} \cdot 8}{2}=16\sqrt{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="129" height="41" />
</p><p>Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/0270cb4e2f7f4b008824f32c7f9639c1c949669f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }16 \sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="91" height="35" />.
</p><p>~mathboy282
</p>
