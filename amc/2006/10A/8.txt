<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Parabola" title="Parabola">parabola</a> with equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/6/ba697e110eb547ec3222ea358ed4c1060ac37887.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^2+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="18" /> passes through the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563948106c3332662355412547d99acab218c16f.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/6/106e80c5637d34b5569709227e8087d276176724.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2\qquad \textbf{(B) } 5\qquad \textbf{(C) } 7\qquad \textbf{(D) } 10\qquad \textbf{(E) } 11$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="373" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Substitute the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563948106c3332662355412547d99acab218c16f.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> into the given equation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />.
</p><p>Then we get a system of two equations:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/7/d67b03119d650354b6246295ef681a132f900812.png" class="latex" alt="$3=4+2b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3e6cb793fac9cbd617cdd94014b30c46f2d25c3.png" class="latex" alt="$3=16+4b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="14" />
</p><p>Subtracting the first equation from the second we have:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/d/cfd88c6c30f2befeb00ac6e768b0058cb844874b.png" class="latex" alt="$0=12+2b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66d2f9f4f35bd078f1caef463342467875d525a5.png" class="latex" alt="$b=-6$" width="54" height="12" />
</p><p>Then using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66d2f9f4f35bd078f1caef463342467875d525a5.png" class="latex" alt="$b=-6$" width="54" height="12" /> in the first equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/6/af6e5ec1c50c7276a2b39a34d5b566f1a309d00b.png" class="latex" alt="$0=1+-12+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="127" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7671f0e1833b4154e7e02ef9412ab7d9a61e44e1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="97" height="30" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.1">Solution 1.1</span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, notice that since the equation is that of a conic parabola, the vertex is likely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/9/8790f46883a296366f038323844601dd2cde9ba6.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Thus, the form of the equation of the parabola is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4ed524279279f436e49f40d20c823e0394a17319.png" class="latex" alt="$y - 2 = (x - 3)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="19" />. Expanding this out, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7671f0e1833b4154e7e02ef9412ab7d9a61e44e1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="97" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The points given have the same <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-value, so the vertex lies on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90f1f22be9cd8dc7b5b0d8aa40f5f0648164f20a.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{2+4}{2}=3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />.
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-coordinate of the vertex is also equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/5/b250a7d94be19932d73b6f79e32ec07dbb15e390.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-b}{2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="24" height="37" />, so set this equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/2/502e059d1a020b368aee3a99c5669d88331d321f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/a/0daeb25b17a4a869fba32f953619e199b0560500.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{-b}{2a}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/1/341886afc6f3872da3ecc782d809b8bdd84e8124.png" class="latex" alt="$3=\frac{-b}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="57" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/a/faad98146290527b2bade699d0aab6b9f22a3c77.png" class="latex" alt="$6=-b$" width="55" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66d2f9f4f35bd078f1caef463342467875d525a5.png" class="latex" alt="$b=-6$" width="54" height="12" />
</p><p>Now the equation is of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/9/4f9c58a92d77e017f54f182d4b84609c5c4c6bde.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^2-6x+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="18" />.  Now plug in the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/9/4f9c58a92d77e017f54f182d4b84609c5c4c6bde.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x^2-6x+c$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="123" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/8/cf88f4bdfd6e82ac6da19217f9c2db2532e0522d.png" class="latex" alt="$3=2^2-6(2)+c$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="19" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/1/fe138cb4436e503159afc5f86fcab209d7d33a92.png" class="latex" alt="$3=4-12+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/7/9271809b39c53f0254b2c8f756ff395c0fd14277.png" class="latex" alt="$3=-8+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="87" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7671f0e1833b4154e7e02ef9412ab7d9a61e44e1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="97" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Substituting y into the two equations, we get:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/7/b974e7c92ca541a3b3aa954a1858094da7634c68.png" class="latex" alt="$3=x^2+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="16" />
</p><p>Which can be written as:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/e/bee7c465e6bef014c7285ce04bc38d5f3c7410a5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+bx+c-3=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="152" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> are the solutions to the quadratic. Thus:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/4/e54fd9d6bbf82d018a1dc16c9b77c8bccda9c929.png" class="latex" alt="$c-3=4\times2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/4/13462c45c56a1410b5e6b821a5681c8ea1be0695.png" class="latex" alt="$c-3=8$" width="72" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/7/7671f0e1833b4154e7e02ef9412ab7d9a61e44e1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="97" height="30" />.
</p>
