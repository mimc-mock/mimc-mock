<p>Odell and Kershaw run for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes on a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Circle" title="Circle">circular</a> track. Odell runs clockwise at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37ae2c4d47fa61a16c7b3f3dbf7181b3e15dba2d.png" class="latex" alt="$250 m/min$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="20" /> and uses the inner lane with a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> meters. Kershaw runs counterclockwise at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/8/9389714b776b5d2e4bad766af3ead76f1da0b713.png" class="latex" alt="$300 m/min$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="20" /> and uses the outer lane with a radius of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> meters, starting on the same radial line as Odell. How many times after the start do they pass each other? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/5/d954470119b9baa743d28f3f1e28d7c4d1fb2cfc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 29\qquad\textbf{(B) } 42\qquad\textbf{(C) } 45\qquad\textbf{(D) } 47\qquad\textbf{(E) } 50\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="434" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1ed90d93483a84bc0ef2a6c44c0257bb56aecc19.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((5,0){up}..{left}(0,5),red); draw((-5,0){up}..{right}(0,5),red); draw((5,0){down}..{left}(0,-5),red); draw((-5,0){down}..{right}(0,-5),red); draw((6,0){up}..{left}(0,6),blue); draw((-6,0){up}..{right}(0,6),blue); draw((6,0){down}..{left}(0,-6),blue); draw((-6,0){down}..{right}(0,-6),blue); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" /></center>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/3/953f29a2b6ad8f2c5d0a2c619a084c4d6896600e.png" class="latex" alt="$d = rt$" width="48" height="12" />, we note that Odell runs one lap in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/5/a35315c23009cf3ac042bee5ec6d8d5863f72d06.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2 \cdot 50\pi}{250} = \frac{2\pi}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="37" /> minutes, while Kershaw also runs one lap in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/7316b9b5971a16f2acffa7277d410e5e39b30330.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2 \cdot 60\pi}{300} = \frac{2\pi}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="37" /> minutes. They take the same amount of time to run a lap, and since they are running in opposite directions they will meet exactly twice per lap (once at the starting point, the other at the half-way point). Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/9/5690ab5ef44cae4fd9b7c8dd685289806ec2da21.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30}{\frac{2\pi}{5}} \approx 23.8$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="78" height="44" /> laps run by both, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/4/82406e7791e09731561535fd66c450c1e3a6d439.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor 2\cdot 23.8\rfloor = 23 \cdot 2 + 1 =\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 47}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="258" height="30" /> meeting points.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We first find the amount of minutes, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, until Odell and Kershaw's next meeting. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the angle in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Radians" class="mw-redirect" title="Radians">radians</a> between their starting point and the point where they first meet, measured counterclockwise. 
</p><p>Since Kershaw has traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b1875da75f32d3109831ada151c3a2765f28e53f.png" class="latex" alt="$300k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" /> meters at this point and the circumference of his track is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/3/9334736d526939044c2b6f42310f39af87d97469.png" class="latex" alt="$120\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/f/22f80ec6763f999b9402ac94892bb2b957373a14.png" class="latex" alt="$a=\frac{300k}{120\pi}\cdot 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="111" height="39" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/d/1bd93ae6aa9c636abcc349dbbce56f6aa3127bd7.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi-a=\frac{250k}{100\pi}\cdot{2\pi}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="154" height="39" /> since Odell has traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/d/5eda7389e49fd3a7120ef1fada782fa7750c799d.png" class="latex" alt="$250k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="39" height="14" /> meters in the opposite direction and the circumference of his track is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/3/b034b5cee39a45fc8e259a06f15941013320075f.png" class="latex" alt="$100\pi$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="37" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> in the second equation, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/433a5d12314a559abfb2401d43c04dac77aa7363.png" class="latex" alt="$a=2\pi-\frac{250k}{100\pi}\cdot 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="154" height="39" />. Then, from the first equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/0/0e0b44ff42ef9239b86839b98ba7415c3c358cf2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{300k}{120\pi}\cdot 2\pi=2\pi-\frac{250k}{100\pi}\cdot 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="219" height="39" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/c/c8c395e414b913d32240d167eed42665463af4f9.png" class="latex" alt="$k=\frac{\pi}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="51" height="34" />. After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> minutes, they are back at the same position, except rotated, so they will meet again in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> minutes. So the total amount of meetings is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/5/66563ccd0a727ae147ed96a76549d20d8139f5b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\lfloor\frac{30}{k}\rfloor=\lfloor\frac{150}{\pi}\rfloor=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }47}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="201" height="38" />.
</p><p>~apsid
</p>
