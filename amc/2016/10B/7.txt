<p>The ratio of the measures of two acute angles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/0/ad0a4bc0ad5d6415575678e779682fb207069c5b.png" class="latex" alt="$5:4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />, and the complement of one of these two angles is twice as large as the complement of the other. What is the sum of the degree measures of the two angles?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/1/1413d92141324ac3232d118be4e1533d6d5befc2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 75\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 90\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 135\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 150\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 270$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="421" height="17" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can set up a system of equations where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are the two acute angles. WLOG, assume that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/262a080d0d87e973b817945967a512346d8bfc49.png" class="latex" alt="$&lt;$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="13" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> in order for the complement of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be greater than the complement of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/3/cf35c47933e3a4ca4cc7cbe772c8eac6da774965.png" class="latex" alt="$4y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add22d9bda21e86390bc74fa4dde17730a442da7.png" class="latex" alt="$-$" style="vertical-align: 4px" width="13" height="1" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/a/e4a13b162a331ab567d4fcefe066a34f349e6bf6.png" class="latex" alt="$(90$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="24" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add22d9bda21e86390bc74fa4dde17730a442da7.png" class="latex" alt="$-$" style="vertical-align: 4px" width="13" height="1" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/e/f7e157113df4f31f54bbf87bd7c693edbd40f1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="15" height="18" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> in the first equation and substituting into the second equation yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/6/37605e261d79ab5f53fd1c8add55b71c9584dbfc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{split} 90 - x &amp; = 2 (90 - 1.25x) \\ 1.5x &amp; = 90 \\ x &amp; = 60 \end{split}\]" width="179" height="63" />
</p><p>Substituting this <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> value back into the first equation yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" />, leaving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cf26be132ef93923e082ee4153b2cb0ef44a50.png" class="latex" alt="$+$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/4/414da89c5cbe83120d734b32f0077b98684aa15f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 135}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p><p>(Solution by akaashp11)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We let the measures be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87e9f9eb8f39820b3b8cd2c4b06e10d90e0d3b44.png" class="latex" alt="$5x$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" /> giving us the ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/0/ad0a4bc0ad5d6415575678e779682fb207069c5b.png" class="latex" alt="$5:4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />. We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/c/2ccbf1b7ca9297986577a7fa0f07e7642c1f7238.png" class="latex" alt="$90-4x&gt;90-5x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="143" height="13" /> since this inequality gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/e/02e28efa00c4f2c66d1aa381c0cb1f8e0ef8d6e4.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" />, which is true since the measures of angles are never negative. We also know the bigger complement is twice the smaller, so 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6eb41acdd9e544c6f63ace29b8d7c893472aaa73.png" class="latex" alt="$90-4x=2(90-5x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/0/dc06c69f0969acde4ca9f84cf38b1a287b932ac9.png" class="latex" alt="$90-4x=180-10x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="161" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/e/0eed227a6427a636a4d7a1e07de82d87fc7a3ff7.png" class="latex" alt="$6x=90$" width="61" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e45a63c78330a7020ad39fd035c818528f54ff46.png" class="latex" alt="$x=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />
</p><p>Therefore, the angles are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, which sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/4/414da89c5cbe83120d734b32f0077b98684aa15f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 135}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="28" />.
</p>
