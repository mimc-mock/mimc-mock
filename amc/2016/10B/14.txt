<p>How many squares whose sides are parallel to the axes and whose vertices have coordinates that are integers lie entirely within the region bounded by the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b1f9c240b401bf8f668591af84f9b8f5b5c5b15.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\pi x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="11" />, the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244f7416b4021f7c60b19f40a907ef805fe2c404.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-0.1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/67143068e3926fe54383526c26c911aa39ff783f.png" class="latex" alt="$x=5.1?$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/6/4d6d3e41dfccd2f58fc8f35da664df72faad0775.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 41 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 45 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 50 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 57$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The region is a right triangle which contains the following lattice points:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/8/c88de68d1330a46b639d08ee346c8a4febe3c88d.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0); (1,0)\rightarrow(1,3); (2,0)\rightarrow(2,6); (3,0)\rightarrow(3,9); (4,0)\rightarrow(4,12); (5,0)\rightarrow(5,15)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="646" height="20" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/7/ca7eccad68b556a67ec2fba01fc71e5cccf2d1d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy]size(10cm); for(int i=0;i&lt;6;++i)for(int j=0;j&lt;=3*i;++j)dot((i,j)); draw((0,-1)--(0,16),EndArrow);draw((-1,0)--(6,0),EndArrow); draw((-.5,-pi/2)--(5.2,5.2*pi),gray);draw((-1,-.1)--(6,-.1)^^(5.1,-1)--(5.1,16),gray); [/asy]" width="188" height="475" />
</p><p>Squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a8df7312744a199daa5c8008af5b9b4798bfd64.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" />:
Suppose that the top-right corner is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/1/fa16b113ae2a44158cb7bc668e09000d290eda41.png" class="latex" alt="$2\le x\le 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" />. Then to include all other corners, we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/4/e9451d7eadc3ddbf9801fe2f8a2e2f6a2b330e1f.png" class="latex" alt="$1\le y\le 3(x-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />.
This produces <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/e/ebe7b603ff67283d293e1287b0c61e4e0addda7e.png" class="latex" alt="$3+6+9+12=30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="14" /> squares.
</p><p>Squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/7901356dd65ec4da1b694937cc4ffc663e90d800.png" class="latex" alt="$2\times 2$" width="40" height="12" />:
Here <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/a/3da719d5ee0e898b61fb95b08c82604e1ae95a80.png" class="latex" alt="$3\le x\le 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="76" height="15" />. To include all other corners, we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/2/002d2e1331b2d03cd33d137317c4b7a3264659c5.png" class="latex" alt="$2\le y\le 3(x-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />.
This produces <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/e/92eab0ca02f681db282b401e43c432c66fbff9f7.png" class="latex" alt="$2+5+8=15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" /> squares.
</p><p>Squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b5343f2d9e22816e8de24262565ff2937695b2c.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times 3$" width="40" height="12" />:
Similarly, this produces <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> squares.
</p><p>No other squares will fit in the region. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/c/48c6fe97d4e047ce99b64a78e4c4fe48a6349678.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }50}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The vertical line is just to the right of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" />, the horizontal line is just under <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a06334068417b1781d14e8b28af827907961eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, and the sloped line will always be above the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />.
This means they will always miss being on a coordinate with integer coordinates so you just have to count the number of squares to the left, above, and under these lines. After counting the number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/4/ad47a5c082096bf54f236a7d9bf4505c46dbcb4f.png" class="latex" alt="$1\cdot1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/8/638ee7316e2377ea24df5d33afe4ead7a45fee2e.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot2$" width="30" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/9/ff9fc44cf13887cfa02c6e660c2c69e1c6e6505e.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot3$" width="30" height="12" /> squares and getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> respectively, and we end up with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/d/06dc80c9f28f8faa70c1439fe99087626d74f5ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 50}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p><p>Solution by Wwang
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The endpoint lattice points are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/7/b4769887371b00929a1675c1c12815587d7ee68d.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,3), (2,6), (3,9), (4,12), (5,15).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="256" height="18" /> Now we split this problem into cases.
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/4/8c48f75b5eb43530c84af32fbcfbd6dda062921a.png" class="latex" alt="$1:$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> Square has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/d/6cdd1b53e5faf7a118222e4b7571d1e9afa0cf56.png" class="latex" alt="$1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinates must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/b/1bb913ad463dcc2916d4135977f0d06f20ad93cd.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876b66789c31005ef23e41081d8f7c10137faaa5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and so on to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/a/bfabbb531c2b249507fb05072d1771f1436e3c0e.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,5).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> The idea is that you start at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c3b4834614e5e7e189cb7d621cfb4fb0bd36dd7.png" class="latex" alt="$y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="15" /> and add at the endpoint, namely <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/b/2eb512f9d62958a19d0d8db91bf6a3c1c187d82c.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="46" height="16" /> The number ends up being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/7/af7971fdf110d39ea11adb856b429b8ff41a4348.png" class="latex" alt="$3+6+9+12 = 30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="14" /> squares for this case.
</p><p><br />
Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/1/ff1be62d9bb7a4e1e12b6ecef0d2f43c133e9c32.png" class="latex" alt="$2:$" width="17" height="12" /> Square has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e4797950b5e812b65381cbc5fc33ad2db5f57aff.png" class="latex" alt="$2.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinates must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/b/a3b6a9f4b07be4cb5a24874bb192da96ca853ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97f4d1e83d6eef61f31d78844deb189c54a7abc3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/e/b4e20228ad3968e49ee0aaf1bd4399402c6ecfeb.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,15)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> and so now it starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/8/e48a3203219c3462de0dc49e047bdca04be24edf.png" class="latex" alt="$y=2.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="46" height="15" /> It ends up being <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b58fce2817dd684f8b24546b5e83ecbe5dd86829.png" class="latex" alt="$2+5+8 = 15.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="117" height="14" />
</p><p>Case <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/6/366695dbee156daed8bd946e4c00df56286df7e6.png" class="latex" alt="$3:$" width="17" height="12" /> Square has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/0/93032f1ab8b685d84a767a2fde0d9006a464b5e4.png" class="latex" alt="$3.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coordinates must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/c/e7c05a83f0a33c3f141fbfb48c803d0e906974e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/9/ea9d3308a17610dcb38bfd84b443bdd0ea403605.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> so there is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/a/1aab8f6083ae971ebe82e6fd379cb52248d8c534.png" class="latex" alt="$1+4 = 5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> squares for this case.
</p><p>Answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/2/8b283f23a46e9adeba694484004a4d53f61f14d4.png" class="latex" alt="$30+15+5 = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 50}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="179" height="28" />.
</p>
