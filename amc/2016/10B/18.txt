<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/3/ff35bddba45a1a680b7ed74c1347a411fc2a9c0a.png" class="latex" alt="$345=3\cdot 5\cdot 23$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />.
</p><p>Suppose we take an odd number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> of consecutive integers, with the median as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0e61647f7f44b877e66e4f048fc6e848e7f6f6.png" class="latex" alt="$mk=345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="76" height="13" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05fb76ee2a149144ec5474fe396b4ae74152dc37.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac12k&lt;m$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="60" height="21" />.
Looking at the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c72d8d34783d3db7b917c953ca22225f513823.png" class="latex" alt="$345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/7198e25f6a2fb001dac0d33f8aa79f35c7de8398.png" class="latex" alt="$3,5,15,23$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="78" height="16" /> with medians as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/9/999bd4a29982209088c71cb05dabf9ffd64be5c1.png" class="latex" alt="$115,69,23,15$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="105" height="16" /> respectively.
</p><p>Suppose instead we take an even number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8ea7ee8a282949fd853a01537ec7a2b55e56f75.png" class="latex" alt="$2k$" width="18" height="12" /> of consecutive integers, with median being the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/e/4bea4f5ee837b0249288558f2fb73a871b264491.png" class="latex" alt="$m+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="46" height="13" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/2/5a24d60aeb830512c16869d95b8ab0fea73d0094.png" class="latex" alt="$k(2m+1)=345$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="131" height="18" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb107d78bbf8d6d9260f9c97c4413d1d787839d6.png" class="latex" alt="$k\le m$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="50" height="15" />.
Looking again at the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c72d8d34783d3db7b917c953ca22225f513823.png" class="latex" alt="$345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/8/97827a34a0f6699ae2b5fd23665cc8eecd056286.png" class="latex" alt="$1,3,5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> with medians <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/5/4f5fde636d19a9b53abfcf3f4929a4f2e5fefd69.png" class="latex" alt="$(172,173),(57,58),(34,35)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="208" height="18" /> respectively.
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/b/1db8297beb63746f6ff2048545ae337e3b0962ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We need to find consecutive numbers (an arithmetic sequence that increases by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />) that sums to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c72d8d34783d3db7b917c953ca22225f513823.png" class="latex" alt="$345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. This calls for the sum of an arithmetic sequence given that the first term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, the last term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> elements, which is: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f53cf328e08bf502e78c91b5f9a8bd5e7e29f658.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {n \cdot (k+g)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="82" height="38" />.
</p><p>We look for sequences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> consecutive numbers starting at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> and ending at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/9/769df4aeb46799be3d2b5b0f7de9dd2730f9d51a.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. We can now substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/9/769df4aeb46799be3d2b5b0f7de9dd2730f9d51a.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. Now we substitute our new value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f53cf328e08bf502e78c91b5f9a8bd5e7e29f658.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {n \cdot (k+g)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="82" height="38" /> to get that the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/6206664cd7e799bd7c43ea52dd11b9374d4ca02f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {n \cdot (k+k+n-1)}{2} = 345$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="199" height="38" />.
</p><p>This simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/e/c7eb49b7959432ae00ab1090f9e064aab0b514a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {n \cdot (2k+n-1)}{2} = 345$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="176" height="38" />. This gives a nice equation. We multiply out the 2 to get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/2/75261dc9042ee0c6393aebbadece6780f4c211ef.png" class="latex" alt="$n \cdot (2k+n-1)=690$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="18" />. This leaves us with 2 integers that multiply to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd2065782067101d1311681df1de20f0e1a7af9.png" class="latex" alt="$690$" width="26" height="12" /> which leads us to think of factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd2065782067101d1311681df1de20f0e1a7af9.png" class="latex" alt="$690$" width="26" height="12" />. We know the factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/d/5bd2065782067101d1311681df1de20f0e1a7af9.png" class="latex" alt="$690$" width="26" height="12" /> are:  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/0/2209015cd2ab1990adf3ab485721173623e32541.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,5,6,10,15,23,30,46,69,115,138,230,345,690$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="411" height="16" />. So through inspection (checking), we see that only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/6/ed6fbfb69662bc3183ac8828e10a7eed255b80b2.png" class="latex" alt="$2,3,5,6,10,15$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="112" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> work. This gives us the answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/b/1db8297beb63746f6ff2048545ae337e3b0962ed.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }7}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" /> ways.
</p><p>~~jk23541

An alternate way to finish.
</p><p>Let 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e787ef4775d22cb112ed54d2abdb402f11195eed.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 2k+n-1 &amp;=\frac{690}{k} \\ n &amp;= k \\ \end{align*}" width="136" height="58" />

Fortunately, regardless of the parity of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/b/9fb1b1ac45af3f10166e2f9302a25daa7630f952.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{690}{k} - k$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="37" /> is odd. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2.1">Solution 2.1</span></h2>
