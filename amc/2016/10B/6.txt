<p>Laura added two three-digit positive integers. All six digits in these numbers are different. Laura's sum is a three-digit number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />. What is the smallest possible value for the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/6/12607aa7862b7509ddd863aae637eae03f050f7d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 15\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the two three-digit numbers she added be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/0842496d1b101d3dd0453641cd2570ee00100050.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=S$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="76" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/0/710e3e4f18d7c0270ab719a00adc61fa12363b1e.png" class="latex" alt="$a&lt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />. The hundreds digits of these numbers must be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/0/9d0f110af579231e09bc55d05ee56dc6d96533bc.png" class="latex" alt="$a\ge 100$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/b/73ba9c17c9c21f240ad46ce41e0d2f0cdaf57b5f.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ge 200$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="58" height="15" />.
</p><p>Say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/f/0cf1ec0f7ffbc19a35f412829a18170424de6af2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=100+p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/9/0c9e4fbda1e2336657b8ab9048ac5d21359b261e.png" class="latex" alt="$b=200+q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="16" />; then we just need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/3/a638b390f94ef2a961e674b7baad3ec094e6f626.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q=100$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="16" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> having different digits which aren't <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.There are many solutions, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/c/54cfc82a82f113a7432ca68f71338c383bbcb44b.png" class="latex" alt="$p=3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/1/ab1efcc183895a91a1a50a5a1937aed1e74617b7.png" class="latex" alt="$q=97$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" /> give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/a/6fa1ab94b6a4189ff28b19555abc56cf86d54f5f.png" class="latex" alt="$103+297=400$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="127" height="14" /> which proves that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/0/5f03028c2292cd337ee145bd8db85ebe2afe59d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" /> is attainable.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>For this problem, to find the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-digit integer with the smallest sum of digits, one should make the units and tens digit add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. To do that, we need to make sure the digits are all distinct. For the units digit, we can have a variety of digits that work. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> works best for the top number which makes the bottom digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. The tens digits need to add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> because of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> that needs to be carried from the addition of the units digits. We see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> work the best as we can't use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Finally, we use <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> for our hundreds place digits.
</p><p>Adding the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/0/9f08cfac0b129db1aca0aa61e8b822793c35d8ca.png" class="latex" alt="$257$" width="27" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/6/6f61021f12ab09c5b9c3cfbcd7f6207920ff765b.png" class="latex" alt="$143$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/9/e8924412a12becf7905729885a46e3e351ec5a86.png" class="latex" alt="$400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> which means our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/0/5f03028c2292cd337ee145bd8db85ebe2afe59d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" />.
</p>
