<p>Each vertex of a cube is to be labeled with an integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, with each integer being used once, in such a way that the sum of the four numbers on the vertices of a face is the same for each face. Arrangements that can be obtained from each other through rotations of the cube are considered to be the same. How many different arrangements are possible?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/5/ec5c1b46ef2d103ab0aef26add269f5a279eac0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 1\qquad\textbf{(B) } 3\qquad\textbf{(C) }6 \qquad\textbf{(D) }12 \qquad\textbf{(E) }24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="371" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Note that the sum of the numbers on each face must be 18, because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/2/0726ffbf60c844a02f0fc72e5452e60abffb3ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1+2+\cdots+8}{2}=18$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="161" height="37" />. 
</p><p>So now consider the opposite edges (two edges which are parallel but not on same face of the cube);
they must have the same sum value too.
Now think about the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />. If they are not on the same edge, they must be endpoints of opposite edges, and we should have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/0/dd04f8356bcfcb5ca235d9ac4f0a468fb7a29406.png" class="latex" alt="$1+X=8+Y$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="117" height="14" />. However, this scenario would yield no solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/3/3235479e4378c6449ae51f19405a4d1d17206ba9.png" class="latex" alt="$[7,2]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="38" height="20" />, which is a contradiction. (Try drawing out the cube if it doesn't make sense to you.)
</p><p>The points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> are therefore on the same side and all edges parallel must also sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Now we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> parallel sides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/d/84da668a22978b1c3140a4bb52db978790219ed7.png" class="latex" alt="$1-8, 2-7, 3-6, 4-5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="185" height="16" />.
Thinking about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> endpoints, we realize they need to sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
It is easy to notice only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/1/b51376213e65601476d054df7dfd2e555456bf34.png" class="latex" alt="$1-7-6-4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/c/a9ce4dd22e58e81d323c527da722001cb28a2124.png" class="latex" alt="$8-2-3-5$" width="102" height="12" /> would work.
</p><p>So if we fix one direction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/2/ec26b55371e40c22ec236d1d49af53ccc12e0c34.png" class="latex" alt="$1-8 ($" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/2/402cbca624b538b3bb90fe300adf24ebb18742bd.png" class="latex" alt="$8-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> all other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> parallel sides must lay in one particular direction. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/c/dfcc63b33c041ee6b0c1f3552b6c412fa4372d39.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-8,7-2,6-3,4-5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/6/336a2545a1bf9080947f5dd306b506b0901973c7.png" class="latex" alt="$(8-1,2-7,3-6,5-4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="18" />
</p><p>Now, the problem is the same as arranging <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> points in a two-dimensional square, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/f/defec4d1d0c2fcf975736678b1d318565cdace5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4!}{4}=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }6.}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="103" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Again, all faces sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/e/65e766a44591081fc5b1ee0d9664d0c425551243.png" class="latex" alt="$18.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" /> If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/c/accc80fdf164cef264f56a82b6f9f6add320fe05.png" class="latex" alt="$x,y,z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> are the vertices next to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, then the remaining vertices are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/9/1d9c69211d95919254b6ad89453dfad665dcd09b.png" class="latex" alt="$17-x-y, 17-y-z, 17-x-z, x+y+z-16.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="388" height="16" /> Now it remains to test possibilities. Note that we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3b461fab7951603bedddde56d998907213b6b62.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+z&gt;17.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="15" /> Without loss of generality, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/7/9874198275dc8eda8fbdb45bb1b48b0d88c9154b.png" class="latex" alt="$x&lt;y&lt;z.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="13" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/c/28c5f8b771d712536bf0010e019f734ab6ea3c0b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3,7,8\text{: Does not work.}\]" width="164" height="16" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/4/654cdf311aa0f4ed66d73e2ed803146491126e4b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4,6,8\text{: Works.}\]" width="104" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019864be311252cad53c64f3c9d67d699f73c0fe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4,7,8\text{: Works.}\]" width="104" height="16" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/6/196850ae6ae751b249215ad84ce9a78525097539.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5,6,7\text{: Does not work.}\]" width="164" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/2/9f274715ba4a0028889669e2425b2494f162e1fa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5,6,8\text{: Does not work.}\]" width="164" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/c/b4c10774707b2c0a428a20011f86af96d8a13625.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5,7,8\text{: Does not work.}\]" width="164" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/d/05dfb8c3a9da1e26eda7ec6974264442f45858c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[6,7,8\text{: Works.}\]" width="104" height="16" />
</p><p>Keeping in mind that a) solutions that can be obtained by rotating each other count as one solution and b) a cyclic sequence is always asymmetrical, we can see that there are two solutions (one with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/42004458f04b89e29f293d1ce7e517b2f5a9b6ca.png" class="latex" alt="$[x, y, z]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="53" height="18" /> and one with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169a84e132dfd1c272c62b4b5e211cb22247b056.png" class="latex" alt="$[z, y, x]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="53" height="18" />) for each combination of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" /> from above. So, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380ae15a1c6af5be7795bdca4e2e23c97373a7ff.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }6.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="116" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We know the sum of each face is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/e/65e766a44591081fc5b1ee0d9664d0c425551243.png" class="latex" alt="$18.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" /> If we look at an edge of the cube whose numbers sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, it must be possible to achieve the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/c/4dc4237db7753f85826031bfafb9711742e67e28.png" class="latex" alt="$18-x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="50" height="13" /> in two distinct ways, looking at the two faces which contain the edge. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> were on the same edge, it is possible to achieve the desired sum only with the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> since the values must be distinct. Similarly, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> were on the same edge, the only way to get the sum is with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> are not on the same edge as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, or in other words they are diagonally across from it on the same face, or on the other end of the cube.
</p><p>Now we look at three cases, each yielding two solutions which are reflections of each other:
</p><p>1) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> are diagonally opposite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> on the same face.
</p><p>2) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> is diagonally across the cube from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is diagonally across from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> on the same face.
</p><p>3) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> is diagonally across the cube from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />, while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> is diagonally across from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> on the same face.
</p><p>This means the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/0/380ae15a1c6af5be7795bdca4e2e23c97373a7ff.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }6.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="116" height="28" />
</p>
