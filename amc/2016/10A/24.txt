<p>A quadrilateral is inscribed in a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/9/979d6af8d9033d413caa42744b891d57d377f885.png" class="latex" alt="$200\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="52" height="18" />. Three of the sides of this quadrilateral have length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" />. What is the length of the fourth side?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/0/5308bc47eb5c1a570f8871c41b008a9169f92326.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }200\qquad \textbf{(B) }200\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(C) }200\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(D) }300\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(E) } 500$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="516" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82d9f44723a6583a6a305aba1f5bef358287f7ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(250); defaultpen(linewidth(0.4)); //Variable Declarations real RADIUS; pair A, B, C, D, E, F, O; RADIUS=3;  //Variable Definitions A=RADIUS*dir(148.414); B=RADIUS*dir(109.471); C=RADIUS*dir(70.529); D=RADIUS*dir(31.586); O=(0,0);  //Path Definitions path quad= A -- B -- C -- D -- cycle;  //Initial Diagram draw(Circle(O, RADIUS), linewidth(0.8)); draw(quad, linewidth(0.8)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,NE); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,ENE); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,S); label(&quot;$\theta$&quot;,O,3N);  //Radii draw(O--A); draw(O--B); draw(O--C); draw(O--D);  //Construction E=extension(B,O,A,D);  label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,NE);  F=extension(C,O,A,D);  label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,NE);   //Angle marks draw(anglemark(C,O,B));  [/asy]" width="412" height="418" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/5/bc56862b1fdb71204894da278e42ef75de73a40b.png" class="latex" alt="$AD$" width="28" height="13" /> intersect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/6/0964c9cf406ef8e5ec8beaa6a92125f2d8f90460.png" class="latex" alt="$OB$" width="28" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20f8f2f1a3ddc6b42fae25a0637e85c747875180.png" class="latex" alt="$OC$" width="28" height="12" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/9/f2920fa2bc40ed68149a854f8b32c09f619b0d70.png" class="latex" alt="$F.$" width="15" height="12" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/a/6da0d28871914a8f788739877d6a51c902231f93.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{AB}= \overarc{BC}= \overarc{CD}=\theta$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="154" height="21" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/2/de2077e14a0e0f013aa9c7d6bd75d15f4f814787.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{BAD}=\frac{1}{2} \cdot \overarc{BCD}=\theta=\angle{AOB}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="256" height="37" />
</p><p><br />
From there, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec8721474fddb588774c3dbf442c7980e8763279.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{OAB} \sim \triangle{ABE}$" width="141" height="13" />, thus:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/5/9d5c179a1a3df807728e8f6e40ba6ef8173edca9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{OA}{AB} = \frac{AB}{BE} = \frac{OB}{AE}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="145" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82d591c3074650a5eaad2d407da2f01e87a4896c.png" class="latex" alt="$OA = OB$" width="80" height="13" /> because they are both radii of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/4/9443443afeeacd51a3c7daf8a9808b15d7a82a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\odot{O}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="27" height="14" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/b/ffb4d9c7b4d59d8df17bcd2189c2b7ab106474e6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{OA}{AB} = \frac{OB}{AE}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="38" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/1/751109d5a88337cfee32e6584d8f224d5a53d195.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = AE$" width="80" height="13" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/6250eb24f3adf07da18c12f6bdd127fafa464509.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = DF$" width="83" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/8/138774092f141d5914fa05a465b254f55ca9dc83.png" class="latex" alt="$OE = 100\sqrt{2} = \frac{OB}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="160" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/e/5fec7b389a3e3c5a8c39ed8b4cf8f1c315e7a5cb.png" class="latex" alt="$EF=\frac{BC}{2}=100$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="136" height="37" /> , so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b79548acdc12a5c9a6e813402fcdf4259cb15fac.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=AE + EF + FD = 200 + 100 + 200 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 500}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="432" height="28" />
</p>
