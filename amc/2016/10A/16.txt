<p>A triangle with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/b/6bb9f070def9537e447d722b27815b9c2865cb55.png" class="latex" alt="$A(0, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/8/d68517b209448e01f8003a8348dc6dc2b64f60cf.png" class="latex" alt="$B(-3, 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/a/1dabcb4821a72c9e62a73d42a146447fb9d28419.png" class="latex" alt="$C(-3, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> is reflected about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis, then the image <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/a/73a764854d1073fac0a788e874db580d732c7c89.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A&#39;B&#39;C&#39;$" width="71" height="14" /> is rotated counterclockwise about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> to produce <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90f8fdc9c5931ac709a5637d045227015d696453.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A&#39;&#39;B&#39;&#39;C&#39;&#39;$" width="83" height="14" />. Which of the following transformations will return <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90f8fdc9c5931ac709a5637d045227015d696453.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle A&#39;&#39;B&#39;&#39;C&#39;&#39;$" width="83" height="14" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> counterclockwise rotation about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b7193f66028e13f331d53ff5e6942c4b05587465.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> clockwise rotation about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> reflection about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> reflection about the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/0/a40fee4603b4536f856dde720ee499468ace443f.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e41e3cd48cee2c6866cfbb4386a8e99d3482f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> reflection about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Consider a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adfd9ae8a3fac031d3b8b470a52a709a23d4d6d2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />. Reflecting it about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis will map it to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/6/03602d0a82eee3f6d96218824ebcb97ce4bda89a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x, -y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, and rotating it counterclockwise about the origin by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> will map it to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/c/14ca8bb93e4658fc4e854f6ebbe9b6c1eabfc185.png" class="latex" alt="$(y, x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" />. The operation that undoes this is a reflection about the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/0/a40fee4603b4536f856dde720ee499468ace443f.png" class="latex" alt="$y = x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/693c4044f8526a8179956e8ac7a3f2f9d61e2c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p>
