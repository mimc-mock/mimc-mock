<p>How many ways are there to write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> as the sum of twos and threes, ignoring order? (For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/8/be81295cf7c8a2639ee838d80e4598720eff3a55.png" class="latex" alt="$1008\cdot 2 + 0\cdot 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd9b01aca4c73ab0d5108eb9da8e0028b10c1b6.png" class="latex" alt="$402\cdot 2 + 404\cdot 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="120" height="14" /> are two such ways.)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/b/69bf8113b867f356b187d6583372de1fb2d36f9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 236\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 336\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 337\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 403\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 672$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="439" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The amount of twos in our sum ranges from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/3/f233b4bd39c27b96921ff522ca6f43eff8813674.png" class="latex" alt="$1008$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, with differences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/b/d8b484b925ef7b4404e4e046604d5740a64a994a.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 3 = \operatorname{lcm}(2, 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="122" height="18" />.
</p><p>The possible amount of twos is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/3/b33110cac5f69ce35da890d157cd047012c14887.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1008 - 0}{3} + 1 \Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(C)} 337}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="199" height="37" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>You can also see that you can rewrite the word problem into an equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/4/ad448c942032df6fe4b8dd232912bbe6afc8cfed.png" class="latex" alt="$2x + 3y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="59" height="16" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. Therefore the question is just how many multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> subtracted from 2016 will be an even number. We can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/5/2853fb210940d6b5bd64d0c158b7f9c543a4ca8f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 1008$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/0/7a06334068417b1781d14e8b28af827907961eb3.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> all the way to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d348bde3e15456e71734dc2c56fc7425c95927f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 0$" width="43" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/1/81106193688c6e25d31db36fea527b29b09fac21.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 672$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" /> works, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> being incremented by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />'s.Therefore, between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/2/3a21eedf8987958dc8173f6c266f7c4dafde16b3.png" class="latex" alt="$672$" width="26" height="12" />, the number of multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/5/d7505fadf58ae315fc9e3f767b58363a8e1431b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}337}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="67" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can utilize the stars-and-bars distribution technique to solve this problem. 
We have 2 "buckets" in which we will distribute parts of our total sum, 2016. By doing this, we know we will have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/f/02f48d656c95a0c50a0c188852ca85d38dd10495.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{2016-1}{2-1}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="91" height="53" /> "total" answers. We want every third x and second y, so we divide our previous total by 6, which will result in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/8/b180294f1301d3b26ae0c26405bb51d2c3991877.png" class="latex" alt="$2015/6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. We have to round down to the nearest integer, and we have to add 2 because we did not consider the 2 solutions involving x or y being 0.
So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e1216000e2c0669cd69a95151f83438f90e024a.png" class="latex" alt="$2015/6\implies335\implies335+2=\boxed{\textbf{(C)}337}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="331" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Another idea that one might get is to try and figure out the nature of how many ways there are to write for any even number X as the sum of twos and threes. We might be able to spot a pattern and apply it to a larger number such as 2016. Let's try the first six evens from zero: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12. There is 1 way for 2, 1 way for 4, 2 ways for 6, 2 ways for 8, 2 ways for 10, and 3 ways for 12. Notice that the number of ways goes up every time an even number is divisible by 3. Notice also that 2016 is divisible by 3. This means if we can find a pattern for every even number that is divisible by 3, we can find the answer for 2016. The first three even numbers that are divisible by 3 from zero are 6, 12, and 18. There are 2 ways for 6, 3 ways for 12, and 4 ways for 18 (5 for 24, 6 for 30, and 7 for 36). The pattern here is that the number of ways for any even number X that is divisible by 36 is X/6 + 1. It turns out this pattern holds for every X number. So, take 2016 and divide it by 6 and add 1 which gives 337. 
</p><p>-- danfanLOL
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/4/4e406543e16d25beb7efdbbe947456dadef292d5.png" class="latex" alt="$2016 = 6 \cdot 336$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="14" />. In other words, we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> as the sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad2d0937b35e43b1d504a7197abe9c3560a3a856.png" class="latex" alt="$336$" width="26" height="12" /> sixes. 
</p><p>In turn, we can express each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> as either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/5/87551e90d62ab66dca2899eb9d26ca4d1f25c069.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 2 + 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/1/ea14be4f38c278ed2f53dca6802949bb63f10ab6.png" class="latex" alt="$3 + 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />.
</p><p>Therefore, we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45d56c1f8e40ee3e4cc96fcb10e06ed3c389d30d.png" class="latex" alt="$n (2 + 2 + 2) + (2016 - n) (3 + 3)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="259" height="20" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is an integer between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/7/4b781fdf1cd172b567be38e2ffa6f20f0b8390c0.png" class="latex" alt="$2016$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, inclusive. Since each value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> corresponds to a unique way to write the sum, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/1/021955c1f2e434b0724eed722ed79338fc141f40.png" class="latex" alt="$2016 + 1 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}337}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="161" height="30" />
</p><p>~jd9
</p>
