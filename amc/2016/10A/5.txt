<p>A rectangular box has integer side lengths in the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/d/6fd9fbf202c5de95ce83bf5e1bdaeb0d374b7d72.png" class="latex" alt="$1: 3: 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />. Which of the following could be the volume of the box?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/070d147c2d04864c90be0c63134c111cf4485a71.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 56\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 64\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 96\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 144$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the smallest side length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. Then the volume is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/27038f06f89487dbd4bac164e3bed017dadc1bb6.png" class="latex" alt="$x \cdot 3x \cdot 4x =12x^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="134" height="15" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/2/69271b422e022ea65ff1f288eddfcfa82afd8ab7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2$" width="43" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04cc2b78d3d9e7d1d1534308d2292f82d6c1f1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$12x^3 = 96 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(D) } 96.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="198" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As seen in the first solution, we end up with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/7/6b7fce1b591d9a9150b1f977d59f766c9b5290df.png" class="latex" alt="$12x^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="34" height="15" />. Taking the answer choices and dividing by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1a8af08b2ea45399e120629de2356c54589da47.png" class="latex" alt="$(A) 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="36" height="18" />,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/0/140a53679f07dc651c7b1d0a04580644cce3b1d5.png" class="latex" alt="$(B) 4 \frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="49" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/1/0212d552cabefeaaf22930654e98d5b137d899df.png" class="latex" alt="$(C) 5 \frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="49" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/5/9255836ab51b06c5b34a75fb95b842c76667e18c.png" class="latex" alt="$(D) 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/6/1b676f531bc5c3356c599fa0d618a765851241d1.png" class="latex" alt="$(E) 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> and the final answer has to equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/48524d3889ec8433cb03669176e738011df245b8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3$" width="16" height="15" />. The only answer choice that works is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b4066cd24c53cd4053f840d210f974f46e1d913.png" class="latex" alt="$(D)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="18" />.
</p>
