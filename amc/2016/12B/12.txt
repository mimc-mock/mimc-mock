<p>All the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f6e1ebffbc1cb0eec51440bbe0ddd5c03ba6b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="146" height="16" /> are written in a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77d7104f34af16053b63ce7cfa87265d915c6105.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times3$" width="40" height="12" /> array of squares, one number in each square, in such a way that if two numbers are consecutive then they occupy squares that share an edge. The numbers in the four corners add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What is the number in the center?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/7/a97af68b0e27ddcc9bb656fba006759ed49696f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 6\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Solution by Mlux:
Draw a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77d7104f34af16053b63ce7cfa87265d915c6105.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times3$" width="40" height="12" /> matrix. Notice that no adjacent numbers could be in the corners since two consecutive numbers must share an edge. Now find 4 nonconsecutive numbers that add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Trying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/f/e3f9e44d7f658fa5f994265939788f085f4587c6.png" class="latex" alt="$1+3+5+9 = 18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="145" height="14" /> works. Place each odd number in the corner in a clockwise order. Then fill in the spaces. There has to be a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. There is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />.  The final grid should similar to this.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/262a82d366c81f043b7c12d5eac1ec87f190eaa9.png" class="latex" alt="$\newline 1, 2,  3\newline 8,  7, 4\newline 9,  6,  5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="56" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff7b4a26287a8502b582b45d04c404bf54f26ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="17" /> is in the middle.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If we color the square like a chessboard, since the numbers alternate between even and odd, and there are five odd numbers and four even numbers, the odd numbers must be in the corners/center, while the even numbers must be on the edges.  Since the odd numbers add up to 25, and the numbers in the corners add up to 18, the number in the center must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/f/f3f6d1175d973aea4f0b82b9ecd8258ae4244d19.png" class="latex" alt="$25 - 18 = 7$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="92" height="13" />.
</p>
