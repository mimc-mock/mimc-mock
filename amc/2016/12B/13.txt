<p>Alice and Bob live <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> miles apart. One day Alice looks due north from her house and sees an airplane. At the same time Bob looks due west from his house and sees the same airplane. The angle of elevation of the airplane is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919bf5d461970fc8d905d63704ef763c08594184.png" class="latex" alt="$30^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> from Alice's position and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/f/29fa0a9949f018e121b2093216dd5db13d3abef0.png" class="latex" alt="$60^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> from Bob's position. Which of the following is closest to the airplane's altitude, in miles?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/4/da490a7c86a0c2a3c842a424e0bfb069e9600b12.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3.5 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 4 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 4.5 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 5 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 5.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="391" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let's set the altitude = z, distance from Alice to airplane's ground position (point right below airplane)=y and distance from Bob to airplane's ground position=x
</p><p>From Alice's point of view, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/1/3b1942e1c2e3bf402a1ec7fd4e128d02be39f150.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan(\theta)=\frac{z}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="85" height="36" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/0/2c05d0dd5148e5725cf0ef3b72aefb6c98b159b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan{30}=\frac{\sin{30}}{\cos{30}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="172" height="42" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/9/1b9dece3d43ae9440306c054950c7280314edab0.png" class="latex" alt="$y=z*\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="20" />
</p><p>From Bob's point of view, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c14b95b538c175bfc4b522e3d06195560594a8b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan(\theta)=\frac{z}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="33" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/9/6198e03577c63d5c453f2294a1ec346427174ba8.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan{60}=\frac{\sin{60}}{\cos{60}}=\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="170" height="37" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/d/66d94fe99330887954d53c66e1fb7e0c292ba324.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{z}{\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="61" height="37" />
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> + <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5a3faa3658bcbd8bde8e16cfa5baf019619fc9c.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="15" height="18" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db61fd75a1a8bb6fa2b202b6b73daf9b4c9838d0.png" class="latex" alt="$10^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="15" />
</p><p>Solving the equation (by plugging in x and y), we get z=<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/d/7edef3e943c3e7f25309279e268b0b120036bbbe.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{30}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> = about 5.5.  
</p><p>So, answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7c89b39a1621ea0c8b65f454443cec3dab6730a.png" class="latex" alt="$E) 5.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="44" height="18" />
</p><p>solution by sudeepnarala
