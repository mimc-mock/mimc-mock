<p>How many squares whose sides are parallel to the axes and whose vertices have coordinates that are integers lie entirely within the region bounded by the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b1f9c240b401bf8f668591af84f9b8f5b5c5b15.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\pi x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="11" />, the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244f7416b4021f7c60b19f40a907ef805fe2c404.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-0.1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/67143068e3926fe54383526c26c911aa39ff783f.png" class="latex" alt="$x=5.1?$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="65" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/6/4d6d3e41dfccd2f58fc8f35da664df72faad0775.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 30 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 41 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 45 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 50 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 57$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Solution by e_power_pi_times_i
Revised by Kinglogic
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc2430047d924cb59d8f9799986b35cea9a979a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] Label l;  l.p=fontsize(8);  xaxis(-1,8,Ticks(l, 1.0));  yaxis(-1,16,Ticks(l, 1.0)); real f(real x)  {  return x * pi; }  D(graph(f,-1/pi,5.1)); D((5.1,-1)--(5.1,16)); D((-1,-0.1)--(8,-0.1)); for(int x = 0; x &lt; 5.1; ++x) { 	for(int y = 0; y &lt; 16; ++y) {     	if(x * pi &gt; y) {         	D((x,y));         }     } } [/asy]" width="362" height="668" />
(red shows lattice points within the triangle)
</p><p>If we draw a picture showing the triangle, we see that it would be easier to count the squares vertically and not horizontally. The upper bound is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/2/ec2caeecd83f1c0a672b0486a7d073690e922c44.png" class="latex" alt="$(y=5.1*\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" />, and the limit for the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> squares. First we count the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/7/3479f881022c09ba65fc1aefed8f2727bd8ab524.png" class="latex" alt="$1*1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="34" height="12" /> squares. In the back row, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb20f349ab0d80aa796d210a4120c7306eaa9f6.png" class="latex" alt="$y=4*\pi$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="15" /> generates squares from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/6/b86f58cb609fa753024f1173510c877971f8e4e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/c/18cfd6877cef16ce9d3db9f0fbd8f0fafce8b825.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,4\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />, and continuing on we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> in the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b1f9c240b401bf8f668591af84f9b8f5b5c5b15.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\pi x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="11" />. So there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/a/18a5be789373d211cdded1a7a2a9514e59b15171.png" class="latex" alt="$12+9+6+3 = 30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="14" /> squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> in the figure. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/acec99fcada4c4016744a341d66ceabd94559094.png" class="latex" alt="$2*2$" width="35" height="12" /> squares, each square takes up <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> units left and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> units up. Squares can also overlap. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/acec99fcada4c4016744a341d66ceabd94559094.png" class="latex" alt="$2*2$" width="35" height="12" /> squares, the back row stretches from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1c18e336ea72166c85eb8b2765d6b6439f12c06.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,3\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> box. Repeating the process, the next row stretches from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c67f1e6c098104b1cb8f1cdd6bb16f689ac3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d10385bc6ead3c2e16a9bb52574d3ffd102c342d.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,2\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> squares. Continuing and adding up in the end, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/6/2e66256373728b0ce17dfd8054f6638ea3094c5a.png" class="latex" alt="$8+5+2=15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" /> squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> in the figure. Squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> in the back row start at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c67f1e6c098104b1cb8f1cdd6bb16f689ac3e6.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and end at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d10385bc6ead3c2e16a9bb52574d3ffd102c342d.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,2\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" />, so there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> such squares in the back row. As the front row starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/a/25a306093bbd9e0699c65dba033c9483cd379dc4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and ends at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/5/9b5004c9d7fa3ea8f87627b1133e60a7e6c2207b.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="41" height="18" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/1/8d1784175ccab2b055588d760e77de61bb46da93.png" class="latex" alt="$4+1=5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. As squares with length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> would not fit in the triangle, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/2315fbfb3be0ae25f8b5081f2f7a2bab8a5c8d1b.png" class="latex" alt="$30+15+5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="14" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/3/613df725df4a77306478fe7ee93b7506485ac933.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{D)}\ 50}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="57" height="28" />.
</p>
