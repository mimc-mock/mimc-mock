<p>A quadrilateral has vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d681b85deed0dc65b5d2134f50636c3d11e9825.png" class="latex" alt="$P(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/c/06c258bab7f94d8dafe5ffde264ced3d8fe58613.png" class="latex" alt="$Q(b,a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/3/71337ecb4cb7cb2f904e3dd6cd3bf6f8f510f37a.png" class="latex" alt="$R(-a, -b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/1/021a34ca04dfa9dfbebb8f5baf413213430c2875.png" class="latex" alt="$S(-b, -a)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/a/faaa8e0ee4c7cc10efb7957393ffcba8ba661bca.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;b&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="13" />. The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/5/e959a00a2f6293338bc0b40855387d018ceb8f16.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 5 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 6 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 12  \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 13$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Note that the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/4/d44a2004c1c2191dde0db29fce6a2099b1eac476.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a-b}{b-a}=-1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="90" height="37" /> and the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/5/805452ee372321839953fbd750cd9b23b973a3df.png" class="latex" alt="$PS$" width="26" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba577dae55e81003ab047fc20899abe38b2eed10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b+a}{a+b}=1$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="76" height="39" />.  Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/3/7f3e0dbc1fe65033b301efa0727741f4f2d92bba.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ\perp PS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="16" /> and we can similarly prove that the other angles are right angles.  This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> is a rectangle. By distance formula we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/d/2bd0e512da2855cd839c27f1f68108d488fba4f0.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)^2+(b-a)^2*2*(a+b)^2 = 256$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="300" height="19" />. Simplifying we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/7/be781dd7666af1f323089f4f81b824f2c2b6efd8.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a+b) = 8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="18" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> have to be a factor of 8. The only way for them to be factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> and remain integers is if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d9f0e25daf4bc643a5e9e6da4e2a95b388fa65.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = 4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/a/01a705eb828d061884fcbab0e8701883bcb544f8.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b = 2$" width="72" height="12" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/0/3502fb5e69be78d9b9580690026667f60fc47147.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p><p>Solution by I_Dont_Do_Math
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Solution by e_power_pi_times_i
</p><p><br />
By the Shoelace Theorem, the area of the quadrilateral is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/8/e3877dc5539da12dad223e1dff196664fcb444b5.png" class="latex" alt="$2a^2 - 2b^2$" width="71" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/0/2d0e0512fa97c0fbf145bd93a56185b22430f508.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - b^2 = 8$" width="87" height="15" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are integers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/b/33b3c27171aba2140505212ce5b912115d1cdd69.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 3$" width="42" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/851b03ce92c3ad8da97daf2785cf4e103c7a8d66.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/3/9e3e4cdc0ce6e5a449d71585213c7d53d9a1108c.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \boxed{\textbf{(A)}\ 4}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="119" height="28" />.
</p>
