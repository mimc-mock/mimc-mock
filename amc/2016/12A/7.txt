<p>Which of these describes the graph of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db0fe4e5601f81191792b0657fa475e578139f7.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2(x+y+1)=y^2(x+y+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="232" height="19" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/5/89510af51aebd65c8eaa91c163e2310d9c335352.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{two parallel lines}\\ \textbf{(B)}\ \text{two intersecting lines}\\ \textbf{(C)}\ \text{three lines that all pass through a common point}\\ \textbf{(D)}\ \text{three lines that do not all pass through a common point}\\ \textbf{(E)}\ \text{a line and a parabola}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="96" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/b/3db0fe4e5601f81191792b0657fa475e578139f7.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2(x+y+1)=y^2(x+y+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="232" height="19" /> tells us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0a0855de5852bc700ca7019c0acb8ef802f76e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2=y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> . <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0a0855de5852bc700ca7019c0acb8ef802f76e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2=y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="18" /> generates two lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f08809818fd50ee3fc13182a3112bc2bd28dccb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="11" /> . <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> is another straight line. The only intersection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f08809818fd50ee3fc13182a3112bc2bd28dccb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="11" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> , which is not on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" /> . Therefore, the graph is three lines that do not have a common intersection, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a575fdd96afeecf3eb879682a7631c3e3b54647.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; \text{three lines that do not all pass through a common point}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="455" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/c/57c19728b3bea9e7150e49308ef156354c3ca3d2.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1\neq0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="17" />, then dividing both sides of the equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/8/e9838a1225fd687a07f61b9a10323935f80d7c35.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="15" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/0/9b0a0855de5852bc700ca7019c0acb8ef802f76e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2=y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="18" />. Rearranging and factoring, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/a/ada7569ce67015059a82ab5614b7e090db27afb9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-y^2=(x+y)(x-y)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="226" height="19" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" />, then the equation is satisfied. Thus either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51b625d9c281c479f6fe888c3c485ab22f50aea.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cecad80cb6ce8f3ac0c0eca745fc3dce7cca9afa.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" />. These equations can be rearranged into the lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f08809818fd50ee3fc13182a3112bc2bd28dccb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="11" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/a/ffa1aa9dfd04bd921d4c5e1befde25c6e9191cad.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x-1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="88" height="15" />, respectively. Since these three lines are distinct, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a575fdd96afeecf3eb879682a7631c3e3b54647.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; \text{three lines that do not all pass through a common point}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="455" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c164ba37037f40278225156f39054e10b9a8aeae.png" class="latex" alt="$y^2(x+y+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="103" height="19" /> on both sides of the equation to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/4/7140504f7ef6cb072520045428d81a04a527758b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2(x+y+1)-y^2(x+y+1)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="265" height="19" />. Factoring <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/8/e9838a1225fd687a07f61b9a10323935f80d7c35.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="15" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/4/fb4f1b418b910ffa3d83a48fce079d876f38f1d3.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y+1)(x^2-y^2)=(x+y+1)(x+y)(x-y)=0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="416" height="19" />, so either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/c/37cf301fccde205c03900cbe252706900f389908.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+1=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/1/c51b625d9c281c479f6fe888c3c485ab22f50aea.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cecad80cb6ce8f3ac0c0eca745fc3dce7cca9afa.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />. Continue on with the second half of solution 2.
</p>
