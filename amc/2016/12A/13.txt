<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> be a positive multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. One red ball and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> green balls are arranged in a line in random order. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/a/74a3c6a3a3cae21e33f051e2e292b0bc344d576f.png" class="latex" alt="$P(N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> be the probability that at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/6/eb6bb9c8e61f79290bbbdcef2f63c301c806d087.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> of the green balls are on the same side of the red ball. Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/4/8a4f1beff51de4233eea027e49748b2a1e972179.png" class="latex" alt="$P(5)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/a/74a3c6a3a3cae21e33f051e2e292b0bc344d576f.png" class="latex" alt="$P(N)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="43" height="18" /> approaches <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/c/d7c7ebcf985f8b908aac4b0401ab8ea1a455bbcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> grows large. What is the sum of the digits of the least value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/9/d99c439de1d27f4f436e55628feeba7bc5a6a777.png" class="latex" alt="$P(N) &lt; \tfrac{321}{400}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="91" height="21" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a25cdc92a872f59916556284b4849143dd4dde1f.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 12 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 14 \qquad \textbf{(C) }16 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 18 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/b/38bd7f6cc4fc68cfb5505393ce201b90a1ff8e04.png" class="latex" alt="$n = \frac{N}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="54" height="37" />. Then, consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> blocks of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> green balls in a line, along with the red ball. Shuffling the line is equivalent to choosing one of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/f/e0fcf01bef88eb69ac4d0a5b94a85a7ad21b913e.png" class="latex" alt="$N + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="47" height="14" /> positions between the green balls to insert the red ball. Less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/4/ac40ce1434cda0943c408cc185f5976212d95801.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> of the green balls will be on the same side of the red ball if the red ball is inserted inside the middle block of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> balls, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/e/d0e80c6b6a84ad4015ea436a915435d69edbdd96.png" class="latex" alt="$n - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> positions where this happens. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/5/615e88ecdc81e21a37175068dce372a373027129.png" class="latex" alt="$P(N) = 1 - \frac{n - 1}{N + 1} = \frac{4n + 2}{5n + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="230" height="38" />, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c3bfc0bdd6af48fe144d39a9a29bb75655fb686.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[P(N) = \frac{4n + 2}{5n + 1} &lt; \frac{321}{400}.\]" width="183" height="39" />
</p><p>Multiplying both sides of the inequality by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/e/2fea93c2fc2dbc4b53723a1cd16b66b477e23981.png" class="latex" alt="$400(5n+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="91" height="18" />, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/e/3be1158d81465d6b9a207cdbd0dcfb6980d840a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[400(4n+2)&lt;321(5n+1),\]" width="213" height="18" />
</p><p>and by the distributive property,
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f86bd6ae6fe29f6ba4b675f086f5254b680b670.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1600n+800&lt;1605n+321.\]" width="219" height="14" />
</p><p>Subtracting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/6/0366ecd97630670a8eca5812b5f00d348b5aa327.png" class="latex" alt="$1600n+321$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="95" height="14" /> on both sides of the inequality gives us
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/1/451a4ecd2a1e89a16924845398a0ec1934b8d1d2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[479&lt;5n.\]" width="74" height="13" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/c/23c20396b7c0c15215845de3e0d231228b1607e0.png" class="latex" alt="$N=5n&gt;479$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="111" height="13" />, so the least possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/5019ae5ab5a63c85111154e82ebcc78eedcdbe86.png" class="latex" alt="$480$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. The sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/5019ae5ab5a63c85111154e82ebcc78eedcdbe86.png" class="latex" alt="$480$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea8ef3edbec9490d6f8dd4923a786993a5996661.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
