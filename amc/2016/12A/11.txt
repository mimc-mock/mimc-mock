<p>Each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> students in a certain summer camp can either sing, dance, or act. Some students have more than one talent, but no student has all three talents. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> students who cannot sing, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> students who cannot dance, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/5/0657f5cd42256c0a39d45b265235e2a939625515.png" class="latex" alt="$29$" width="17" height="12" /> students who cannot act. How many students have two of these talents? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/8/998dccbde566fa492bfab8cd1b98e639613df868.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 25\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 49\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 64$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" /> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the number of students that can only sing, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can only dance, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> can only act.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" /> be the number of students that can sing and dance, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90fe8263dd262c6d4d42e5e955afefd6adc0917c.png" class="latex" alt="$ac$" width="17" height="8" /> can sing and act, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/2/fe207dd510fa3e2012d9747d93de6f9cc71bf3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$bc$" width="16" height="12" /> can dance and act.
</p><p>From the information given in the problem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/3/003352758558ef0adc0942a12a834aa1e9f7a87b.png" class="latex" alt="$a + ab + b = 29, b + bc + c = 42,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="252" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/d/a9d3578efcbbe3227ccd6365fd64eb82d9681ccc.png" class="latex" alt="$a + ac + c = 65$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="14" />.
</p><p>Adding these equations together, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb6b402119ac9d1cd4c98d520cc3c25d6f0901ee.png" class="latex" alt="$2(a + b + c) + ab + bc + ac = 136$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="261" height="18" />.
</p><p>Since there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> students, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/5/b15ac2eb88a12cc3dd921447ce24c420defa6115.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c + ab + bc + ac = 100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="237" height="14" />.
</p><p>Subtracting these equations, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/f/c4ffd7566f7be7a56881b638c9f767286e98912f.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />.
</p><p>Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/b/c5bbbdc5cfb2479c525db6b53c633b7b96a4a876.png" class="latex" alt="$ab + bc + ac = 100 - (a + b + c) = 100 - 36 = \boxed{\textbf{(E)}\; 64}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="431" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>An easier way to solve the problem:
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/0/ed0d6c36fdad9c2874053be46c7e4120f2edcf47.png" class="latex" alt="$42$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> students cannot sing, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/2/582a48232fbd71e2d99d08f0e5a5d693e3f82da9.png" class="latex" alt="$100-42=58$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> students who can.
</p><p>Similarly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> students cannot dance, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/d/f6d4f338cd68bce480708700c9074531bf90672c.png" class="latex" alt="$100-65=35$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> students who can.
</p><p>And <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/5/0657f5cd42256c0a39d45b265235e2a939625515.png" class="latex" alt="$29$" width="17" height="12" /> students cannot act, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1160d9910476b43d37c171d4ce1556ec6ca724.png" class="latex" alt="$100-29=71$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="109" height="13" /> students who can.
</p><p>Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/3/443a3d032bb780e5fa461e4ecc38833befe6d62c.png" class="latex" alt="$58+35+71=164$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="150" height="14" /> students in all ignoring the overlaps between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> talent categories.
There are no students who have all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> talents, nor those who have none <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/4/3b4348e74b87553c026874630c862d1b35c59b0b.png" class="latex" alt="$(0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />, so only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> talents are viable.
</p><p>Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/c/f2cac74a754e113a7b8b3ad46774480907de1ea7.png" class="latex" alt="$164-100=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }64}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="164" height="28" /> students who have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> talents.
</p>
