<p>The mean, median, and mode of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> data values <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/9/9890cf93e5ac17f6c4cfed3fa7a00cf9152ae34b.png" class="latex" alt="$60, 100, x, 40, 50, 200, 90$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="185" height="16" /> are all equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/a/d0aca6baa162cfb74364f302225c0b10ce0b27e0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 50 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 60 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 75 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 90 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="403" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the mean,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db6cdf1e2ce5c40061c734f8cc44d1faede06150.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} x&amp;=\frac{60+100+x+40+50+200+90}{7}\\ &amp;=\frac{540+x}{7}. \end{align*}" width="307" height="80" />
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/5/9852a1483ce904f4adef62321dd6ed5002f8504c.png" class="latex" alt="$7x=540+x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="103" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76c643792d2a5fb493fe7c6ff6a9bf9eb79f3bfd.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }90}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="103" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must be the median so it must equal either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" />. You can see that the mean is also <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and by intuition <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> should be the greater one. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76c643792d2a5fb493fe7c6ff6a9bf9eb79f3bfd.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\textbf{(D) }90}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="103" height="28" />
~bjc
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Check">Check</span></h2>
<p>Order the list: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/5/945841734d86499e02222d732d142ace376f43ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\{40,50,60,90,100,200\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="184" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> must be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> because it is both the median and the mode of the set. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> is correct.
</p>
