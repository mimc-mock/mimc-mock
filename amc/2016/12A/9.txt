<p>The five small shaded squares inside this unit square are congruent and have disjoint interiors. The midpoint of each side of the middle square coincides with one of the vertices of the other four small squares as shown. The common side length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1980bd428d0929c997feee7e2b306d7bc133b31.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{a-\sqrt{2}}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="37" height="24" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are positive integers. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/2/822d7c29853b8e549e65edc3bf9cf6445b799d11.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] real x=.369; draw((0,0)--(0,1)--(1,1)--(1,0)--cycle); filldraw((0,0)--(0,x)--(x,x)--(x,0)--cycle, gray); filldraw((0,1)--(0,1-x)--(x,1-x)--(x,1)--cycle, gray); filldraw((1,1)--(1,1-x)--(1-x,1-x)--(1-x,1)--cycle, gray); filldraw((1,0)--(1,x)--(1-x,x)--(1-x,0)--cycle, gray); filldraw((.5,.5-x*sqrt(2)/2)--(.5+x*sqrt(2)/2,.5)--(.5,.5+x*sqrt(2)/2)--(.5-x*sqrt(2)/2,.5)--cycle, gray); [/asy]" width="252" height="252" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/f/3af1025237725763f525a2be317da1aefcf3c13e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 10\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> be the side length of the small squares.
</p><p>The diagonal of the big square can be written in two ways: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/6/9b691e3c889e0d3171d94eeab9b701a1fd4241f2.png" class="latex" alt="$s \sqrt{2} + s + s \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="119" height="18" />.
</p><p>Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/8/14828390d171aff49b874df3043acbfd92458418.png" class="latex" alt="$s = \frac{4 - \sqrt{2}}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="41" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/f/7bfdeb2086daf5ca1d16cc9d279a8e910d391a0c.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 7 \Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 11}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="132" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The diagonal of the small square can be written in two ways: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/49348060a83bb2a69d0a7a4197a652a68964daeb.png" class="latex" alt="$s \sqrt(2)$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="48" height="32" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/a/34ae92c4a52480beebcd069945ce8aeda9940cc9.png" class="latex" alt="$2*(1-2s).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" /> Equating and simplifying gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/8/14828390d171aff49b874df3043acbfd92458418.png" class="latex" alt="$s = \frac{4 - \sqrt{2}}{7}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="41" />. Hence our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/4/a547ac594f1e7cb36d974d4cd058b8e41e543fe8.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 7 \Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 11}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="135" height="28" />
</p>
