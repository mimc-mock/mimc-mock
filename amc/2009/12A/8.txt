<p>Four congruent rectangles are placed as shown. The area of the outer square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times that of the inner square. What is the ratio of the length of the longer side of each rectangle to the length of its shorter side?
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/8/4f8d08abd119d663ad6630516ebc1b3bd784727e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(6mm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt));  path p=(1,1)--(-2,1)--(-2,2)--(1,2); draw(p); draw(rotate(90)*p); draw(rotate(180)*p); draw(rotate(270)*p); [/asy]" width="115" height="115" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/e/b0ef7036644005690598351b266e10e22af17d9d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \sqrt {10} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 2 + \sqrt2 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 2\sqrt3 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="444" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/70432e471002b1c1f363f2d085b0f3dff8d930a9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />
The area of the outer square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> times that of the inner square.
Therefore the side of the outer square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb58d5528cef6e633dd5c25b21abe2ad719d50be.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt 4 = 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="57" height="18" /> times that of the inner square.
</p><p>Then the shorter side of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/8/8f88d403085fe6600059002646348a5f267266d1.png" class="latex" alt="$1/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" /> of the side of the outer square, and the longer side of the rectangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/c/2bcddca5092a7ec886294a0561a3f69b682f12d0.png" class="latex" alt="$3/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" /> of the side of the outer square, hence their ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/433d742232f7bf3512c11eb996630ff5ead3641f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the side length of the smaller square be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and let the smaller side of the rectangles be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Since the larger square's area is four times larger than the smaller square's, the larger square's side length is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0e772a775ebe0553fb169c0040f5f6c71795a3.png" class="latex" alt="$2y+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="15" />, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/4/b849be3e10b1c9b4bd2ceeace68892730ce6f06f.png" class="latex" alt="$y=1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" />. Then, the longer side of the rectangles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f7c0c7dfafc48da66f90769b0ecd2ef11e73c14.png" class="latex" alt="$3/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/b/1dbcbfe635cb8baff4a0bedaa2e63dcb8819ccb6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{3}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}}=\boxed{3}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="59" height="47" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let the longer side length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and the shorter side be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />.
</p><p>We have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/0/df0349aa17d6b6dad810cc622f9a83a13fb8e188.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+a)^2=4(x-a)^2\implies x+a=2(x-a)\implies x+a=2x-2a\implies x=3a \implies \frac{x}{a}=3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="718" height="33" />
</p><p>Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/1/d51a30e91f336214879e9c22472b581a18bc292e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{A}\implies 3$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="83" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>WLOG, let the shorter side of the rectangle be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and the longer side be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> . Thus, the area of the larger square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/5/c55fda3438132a15bb248d6aa1896ff7c715996d.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+x)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="19" /> . The area of the smaller square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/e/06edf63382db19bdc136afe2e37aac1567b19572.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-1)^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="65" height="21" /> . Thus, we have the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/b/96b23853c357f24767683cb64ca57d52679a0734.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+x)^2 = 4 (x-2)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="158" height="19" /> . Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> .
</p><p>~coolmath2017
</p>
