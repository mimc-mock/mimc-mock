<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/3/d33cc80fa8f093e155c5be46d2e5d9da3d7e1ef5.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABC]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="50" height="18" /> denote the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/4208f1e3cdfa6ec09fff237f03162a9dc1cb3bf7.png" class="latex" alt="$[AED] = [BEC]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="129" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/3/a6327ac15115d722fa437eae535e151003a62da2.png" class="latex" alt="$[ABD] = [AED] + [AEB] = [BEC] + [AEB] = [ABC]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="435" height="18" />. Since triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> share a base, they also have the same height and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/0/ff03773bf62d2b7df427afea6bdf56be158a29c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}||\overline{CD}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/a/e9a1c34227ef6445fec96b2938a3e9235f76cce9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{AEB}\sim\triangle{CED}$" width="143" height="13" /> with a ratio of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/b/57b18233f30f00818b2a9e5c229c65b079c153e3.png" class="latex" alt="$3: 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/2/3b2c55a3c2dcb93bb535f5ee3e4e508605542b72.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = \frac {3}{7}\times AC$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="115" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/3/e93f9496af97a727ea693e801595e5bf58fe2dbd.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = \frac {3}{7}\times 14 = 6\ \boxed{\textbf{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="183" height="37" />.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Using the sine area formula on triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1bf55dee68ba4123ca736e3b4b2121ffebc98a.png" class="latex" alt="$AED$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/1/ef14ba8fc495f2a5750a5ed394ebf75a28641658.png" class="latex" alt="$BEC$" width="43" height="12" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/d/90d4f9deb354750aef95d3fccc87e584744999cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AED = \angle BEC$" width="136" height="13" />, we see that 
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e8d0633df9bf333912e3d0c13884bc95b2114e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(AE)(ED) = (BE)(EC)\quad \Longrightarrow\quad \frac {AE}{EC} = \frac {BE}{ED}.\]" width="362" height="37" /></center>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/c/54cc460072fa91546382d5111aa37612fa697cac.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle AEB = \angle DEC$" width="136" height="13" />, triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/c/9ece55f9a0c8c69d74514764c04b2a52b1cf734e.png" class="latex" alt="$AEB$" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7a9191aefcf2eb237d1999486083ed78f6d515f.png" class="latex" alt="$DEC$" width="44" height="12" /> are similar. Their ratio is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/2/7c2ac72305bf8537853f1927d2838834cedf4985.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {AB}{CD} = \frac {3}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="69" height="38" />.  Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/1/331074daa090cd0c3e22906601320f286953138e.png" class="latex" alt="$AE + EC = 14$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="14" />, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/b/96ba878c7a2530c6f9a7b78bc307993075c8301f.png" class="latex" alt="$EC = 8$" width="61" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/f/76fc69ef9fbbc254418bf4ab8a929f32109bfe9b.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = 6\ \textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="17" />.
