<p>The first three terms of an arithmetic sequence are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/1/76142730ad27f345c66d8623ad85fb9feba6a90e.png" class="latex" alt="$2x - 3$" width="50" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/6/d365ff0dfa746638951885d074192e44da8320c6.png" class="latex" alt="$5x - 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/e/5ae961150ab2011a5139c435f8a8f01a03f1efb4.png" class="latex" alt="$3x + 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> respectively. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th term of the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/7/85727a3ea4f5d7ba25bc591590b0e085bca557b1.png" class="latex" alt="$2009$" width="35" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/c/abc0b57389fa1f1d7ffa9d363c45210534ef3ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 255 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 502 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 1004 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 1506 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 8037$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="467" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>As this is an arithmetic sequence, the difference must be constant: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/2/7d259fafe93dbb17a21cf2b73ed9ecb15b3c6613.png" class="latex" alt="$(5x-11) - (2x-3) = (3x+1) - (5x-11)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="345" height="18" />. This solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/7/2e7d65cfa316d639c9267398009812f3eb45fea9.png" class="latex" alt="$x=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />. The first three terms then are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. In general, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />th term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/7/267123a0d0ccc46d0c1972827d115d757041d5c5.png" class="latex" alt="$1+4n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="51" height="13" />. Solving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/b/e1bd7186744b21a36036b731c250fafbbe27cfe4.png" class="latex" alt="$1+4n=2009$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="111" height="14" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/4/50429736b91911ab9a75f1f17d7854e5bf2ae38f.png" class="latex" alt="$n=\boxed{502}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="74" height="23" />.
</p>
