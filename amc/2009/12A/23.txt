<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea8ac43e7941f88d8b25a44e8f402a22b1617cac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph; size(250);Label k; k.p=fontsize(6); int ymax = 400, ymin = -400; real rt = 175+150*2^.5; real f(real x){return 1/400*(x-125)*(x+rt);} real g(real x){return -f(100-x);} xaxis(-600,600,Ticks(k, 5),Arrows(6));yaxis(ymin,ymax,Ticks(k, 5),Arrows(6));  draw(graph(f,-450,300),red+linewidth(0.8),Arrows(6));draw(graph(g,-200,550),blue+linewidth(0.8),Arrows(6)); draw((50,ymax)--(50,ymin),linetype(&quot;4 4&quot;),Arrows(4));dot((-rt,0));dot((100+rt,0));dot((-25,0));dot((125,0)); [/asy]" width="418" height="275" /></center>
<p>The two quadratics are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" /> rotations of each other about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/e/24e7cc57eac46b6cf0b6eb4bc07b393ff5e4ee22.png" class="latex" alt="$(50,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="48" height="18" />. Since we are only dealing with differences of roots, we can translate them to be symmetric about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/2/b024a706cdf9e0ea65c3c403d852a1f73fe15e82.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3 = - x_2 = 75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="116" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/9/cd9f45d3383fc2ee4f9777835d12068852e90eb1.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4 = - x_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="72" height="10" />. Say our translated versions of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />, respectively, so that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/5/ac5ae62c8260538431f7ec0b237eb571bfe4c7fb.png" class="latex" alt="$p(x) = - q( - x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="18" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/f/adf58bc6622905a518adb538fda7ee46d13d0fef.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3 = 75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" /> be a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/1/5e1a76f08084f1e1ac0906d5b2f53313f0efa4bb.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2 = - 75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" /> a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> by symmetry. Note that since they each contain each other's vertex, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/ccada11db7b2b90693e2fac4f887a57fce6f96bf.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7d010bbff66a0c41e43310a51efbaa6bf63396.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee60922c5fc5ee1941df04f838cdae71fbc29d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420543d9a4f93ed4180b45b3149b663a8aa7674d.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> must be roots of alternating polynomials, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/a/ccada11db7b2b90693e2fac4f887a57fce6f96bf.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> is a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/0/420543d9a4f93ed4180b45b3149b663a8aa7674d.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" /> a root of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/3/7c3ca73017503cd793f3965def892b14a2331811.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[p(x) = a(x - 75)(x - x_1)\]" width="196" height="18" /></center>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/6/e66be70cc10a674a7ab821d066ba50eca4afe25f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[q(x) = - a(x + 75)(x + x_1)\]" width="209" height="18" /></center>
<p>The vertex of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/5/2751d79d3bbfb34440d68c685fe6ba7414951749.png" class="latex" alt="$p(x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> is half the sum of its roots, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/3/fb3d3452233b645c1162f419b39763c9634bba3f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac {75 + x_1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" />. We are told that the vertex of one quadratic lies on the other, so
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/c/1bca712494ac3ccd8bd454be08ab880255e2d26b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} p\left(\frac {75 + x_1}{2}\right) &amp; = &amp; a\left(\frac {75 - x_1}{2}\right)\left(\frac { - 75 + x_1}{2}\right) \\ &amp; = &amp; - \frac {a}{4}(x_1 - 75)^2 \\ - \frac {a}{4}(x_1 - 75)^2 &amp; = &amp; q\left(\frac {75 + x_1}{2}\right) \\ &amp; = &amp; - a\left(\frac {x_1 + 225}{2}\right)\left(\frac {3x_1 + 75}{2}\right) \\ &amp; = &amp; - \frac {a}{4}(x_1 + 225)(3x_1 + 75) \end{eqnarray*}" width="374" height="220" /></center>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/9/7d9177e051a7e0278f11a5fa54738ef8f016026f.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1 = 75u$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="15" /> and divide through by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/6/7e689d1278c41b1d53a67ea277b93078316bf09a.png" class="latex" alt="$75^2$" width="24" height="15" />, since it will drastically simplify computations. We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72f14a1a226ff27116518251cc1778b190f6d4be.png" class="latex" alt="$u &lt; - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/f/33fe0f469dfe27187ab7fe37a3c39204bee71171.png" class="latex" alt="$(u - 1)^2 = (3u + 1)(u + 3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="206" height="19" />, or
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/9/369c373ca02974656cb1caba542ca177c3397fdc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{eqnarray*} 0 &amp; = &amp; (3u + 1)(u + 3) - (u - 1)^2 \\ &amp; = &amp; 3u^2 + 10u + 3 - (u^2 - 2u + 1) \\ &amp; = &amp; 2u^2 + 12u + 2 \\ &amp; = &amp; u^2 + 6u + 1 \end{eqnarray*}" width="290" height="91" /></center>
<p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/c/4bc1f226c36733bef67e9f85a3fc8eea30bdbb96.png" class="latex" alt="$u = \frac { - 6\pm\sqrt {32}}{2} = - 3\pm2\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="221" height="41" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/f/72f14a1a226ff27116518251cc1778b190f6d4be.png" class="latex" alt="$u &lt; - 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/3/1d34092843ba45cccedfd28c9a215387bf37f9ee.png" class="latex" alt="$u = - 3 - 2\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="114" height="18" />. 
</p><p>The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/8/7c8ed069aa5b614ed2621b4e3b4ba46b7a065142.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4 - x_1 = (-x_1) - x_1 = - 150u = 450 + 300\sqrt {2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="370" height="21" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/1/c21121f54b5973ab53db89d2a2598b90c6cbdca0.png" class="latex" alt="$450 + 300 + 2 = 752\ \mathbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="193" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Note">Note</span></h2>
<p>Actually it is not necessary to solve any quadratic equations, if one utilizes the two facts about the quadratic <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/d/71d13ac0e00358ad3fa8b0d6db3217b8a10cc7d2.png" class="latex" alt="$ax^2+bx+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="16" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/2/4c28242522f23e896074dc89612847f8317911d7.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />) that (i) the difference of the two quadratic roots equals to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/2/83204d217ebefd686d219c24f6431c8550d66c2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\Delta}/a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="49" height="22" />, and (ii) that the minimum value of a quadratic equals to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c046f28f978dc4a969e148f3ecb125f65714a5b9.png" class="latex" alt="$-\Delta/4a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5e45f290267432d6b5202adb894a75bf362b1e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\Delta=b^2-4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="15" />. Here is a possible adjustment to the solution:
</p><p>Without loss of generality we may "shift" <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/9/df93ae0e9ebaf28a0afa5a27f3475c7f84baea9d.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="11" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> units to the left, then the differences of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/b/67bc6daa9d6b964201d6cef60cbeb1ac5fd26ead.png" class="latex" alt="$x_i$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> remain the same, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee60922c5fc5ee1941df04f838cdae71fbc29d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7d010bbff66a0c41e43310a51efbaa6bf63396.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> are symmetrical about <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/9/8b942ef0caca91281337b6b47f4ddf30191a04b3.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2=-75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="73" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/a/fcaba46227370adf1400d28919f2df70499c0d7e.png" class="latex" alt="$x_3=75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" />. The relationship of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />,<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311cabda3a9b09f0dde217303ca9d1cd9201dcf6.png" class="latex" alt="$g$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/f/a9f36fa37c08605cc75a954079f0abfcd696f22f.png" class="latex" alt="$g(x)=-f(-x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" />. So we may write:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/3/aa31c2cd9705860eeedf5556825cbd4842ba6c07.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[f(x)=a(x-b)^2 + m\]" width="168" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/2/1c27413d3d521825e3e36cff0d5e403c59bd38c7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[g(x)=-a(x+b)^2 - m\]" width="181" height="19" />
</p><p>Again without loss of generality, we can assume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/2/4c28242522f23e896074dc89612847f8317911d7.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/a/91a5b8b0bb9d8e27be1b824f08fd6df60ba1d9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$b&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> (Short argument is needed here instead of the lazy "wlog"). Also, the vertex of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/cec7fb8124dcebb66a59d7150ccb0ef4ff9fa8a3.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,m)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="44" height="18" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/c/6fcc28b3ab193566ee553c7536da8837b37abc5b.png" class="latex" alt="$m=g(b)=-4ab^2-m$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="19" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/922dc8c147691fb2d70ac0405d2f05bf9ae68c9c.png" class="latex" alt="$m=-2ab^2 &lt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="121" height="15" />.
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/c/7bc19d54b91045a003e06d8d8a0ee72a7faa275b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_2,x_4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="11" /> are roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/2/bb2c93730dbb48558bb3c4738c956c4e8f816437.png" class="latex" alt="$f$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="16" />, we have the following relationship of the roots:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/6/f1613a57e12afa83ace9ac3e95b4c180e244de61.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_2 + x_4 = 2b\]" width="98" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/a/0eaea17d72806181717d045e948df0edb0803740.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x_4 - x_2 = \sqrt{\Delta}/a = \sqrt{-4am}/a = 2\sqrt{-m/a} = 2\sqrt{2b^2} = 2\sqrt{2}b = \sqrt{2} (x_2+x_4)\]" width="578" height="32" />
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/8/a18a246cd3608a549c684db4ec809cb431875876.png" class="latex" alt="$(\sqrt{2}-1)x_4 = 75(1+\sqrt{2})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="198" height="21" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/e/1de2748f43ebbb09ae564f1bee03de40b733754b.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4 = 75(1+\sqrt{2})^2 = 75(3+2\sqrt{2})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="257" height="21" />.
</p><p>Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/9/d49ac4502bb20a838d7fa6696e7695ecfce1800a.png" class="latex" alt="$x_4-x_1=2x_4=450+300\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="234" height="19" />.
</p>
