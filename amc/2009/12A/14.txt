<p>A triangle has vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/4/2249ef41e3aabf1d1e65de53e8c1fd4f76759a6e.png" class="latex" alt="$(6m,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb8cab3b86d4a13c3cc3680c32aaa80cc854689e.png" class="latex" alt="$y = mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> divides the triangle into two triangles of equal area. What is the sum of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a928b43ca7d1b13cdcac3ba059580bdf0c9be9d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{A} - \!\frac {1}{3} \qquad \textbf{(B)} - \!\frac {1}{6} \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac {1}{6} \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac {1}{3} \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac {1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="380" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let's label the three points as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e56c2d75970eccaf7b305f042636bcebdb2a1cb0.png" class="latex" alt="$A=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/6/1a614885d3185761a5744a0de9ebd12a58201426.png" class="latex" alt="$B=(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/f/12f30549ba35b32cf668596dc204dc001ee23e80.png" class="latex" alt="$C=(6m,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />.
</p><p>Clearly, whenever the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/1/0d1fe0afdea68630af2bda225ce5f2978b5ba0be.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> intersects the inside of the triangle, it will intersect the side <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the point of intersection. 
</p><p>The triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/e/6ce2cd123db24a7b8373999aea7a98927e3b3c81.png" class="latex" alt="$ACD$" width="43" height="13" /> have the same height, which is the distance between the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />.
Hence they have equal areas if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />.
</p><p>The midpoint of the segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> has coordinates <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/1/591e74d0bac255900624ff32a05708e6d4194830.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( \frac{6m+1}2, \frac 12 \right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="104" height="43" />. This point lies on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/1/0d1fe0afdea68630af2bda225ce5f2978b5ba0be.png" class="latex" alt="$y=mx$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="11" /> if and only if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/c/e3c2a0324042d2ffee44a4a1b4bf6c1159ab9f35.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 = m \cdot \frac{6m+1}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="125" height="37" />. This simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/4/844c79c8ec1d9cdba8e416fbf615a8e9b5ffb91c.png" class="latex" alt="$6m^2 + m - 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="16" />. Using Vieta's formulas, we find that the sum of the roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/5/1959e8791326371c179908c681dfc6bb26ccd4db.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} - \!\frac {1}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="72" height="48" />.
</p><p><br />
For illustration, below are pictures of the situation for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/4/0a499235a34b7f1551248489c227099d9936aff9.png" class="latex" alt="$m=1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/5/7a56cd0d239005a50cc947396700c6f0e3acd75f.png" class="latex" alt="$m=0.5$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b1bb8db193a3415ae209659079707b418c9c740.png" class="latex" alt="$m=1/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="66" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/6/70674cda448c1bb51efbedaeec565c0d9000bdc9.png" class="latex" alt="$m=-1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/e/eee0289fb1f74e7bf8b303d3db34f731e6f637d5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1cm); defaultpen(0.8); real m=1.5; draw( (0,0)--(1,1)--(6*m,0)--cycle ); draw( ((-0.5)*(1,m)) -- ((1.5)*(1,m)), dashed ); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,0),NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(1,1),NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(6*m,0),E); [/asy]" width="475" height="145" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/0/920597b66dc7afdf08e499aa29a879582a966bfd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(0.8); real m=0.5; draw( (0,0)--(1,1)--(6*m,0)--cycle ); draw( ((-1)*(1,m)) -- (3*(1,m)), dashed ); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,0),NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(1,1),N); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(6*m,0),E); pair D = intersectionpoint( (1,1)--(6*m,0), ((-1)*(1,m)) -- (3*(1,m)) ); dot(D); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); [/asy]" width="405" height="192" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/b/28bd630603b59402c8d7a30b20ac1fa955b987a4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(0.8); real m=1/3; draw( (0,0)--(1,1)--(6*m,0)--cycle ); draw( ((-1)*(1,m)) -- (3*(1,m)), dashed ); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,0),NW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(1,1),N); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(6*m,0),E); pair D = intersectionpoint( (1,1)--(6*m,0), ((-1)*(1,m)) -- (3*(1,m)) ); dot(D); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); [/asy]" width="382" height="152" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/9/7a90b71d893fb87d66d3bab05651a8ecfd69317a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(0.8); real m=-1/2; draw( (0,0)--(1,1)--(6*m,0)--cycle ); draw( ((-2)*(1,m)) -- (1*(1,m)), dashed ); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(0,0),S); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(1,1),NE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(6*m,0),W); pair D = intersectionpoint( (1,1)--(6*m,0), ((-1)*(1,m)) -- (3*(1,m)) ); dot(D); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,S); [/asy]" width="425" height="165" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The line must pass through the triangle's centroid, since the line divides the triangle in half. The coordinates of the centroid are found by averaging those of the vertices. The slope of the line from the origin through the centroid is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/b/42b436b59d8d15978dfde0a7739f7b1c2b3bc942.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1+6m}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="41" height="47" />, which is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/b/beb0c8b06def7670778e70b6215560b8039ec6cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1+6m}{3}}=m$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="83" height="47" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/d/16d6c311738a8150f6574a05d46221371cb831a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}=m\left(\frac{1+6m}{3}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="139" height="43" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b90768a98de11b52b630adca61b25654e78dd1f.png" class="latex" alt="$1=m(1+6m)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="118" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/2/0e238e395c37202c0a30e764bd2ee7a784ac5882.png" class="latex" alt="$6m^2+m-1=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="134" height="16" />
</p><p>Using Vieta's Formulas, the sum of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/b/25bbcae7adb42d6352e9b035cb9ef226a31c5829.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\; -\frac{1}{6}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="80" height="48" />
</p>
