<p>How many positive integers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee927e1332358c96c62c277441c907c4f51057f.png" class="latex" alt="$1000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> times the sum of their digits?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/7/1872586a859364d0de64e5a341082bbe4d5b1510.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 0 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 1 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 2 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The sum of the digits is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/d/19d6399b71b30f4b9073c8a8d10e4a69db7c08aa.png" class="latex" alt="$9+9+9=27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="114" height="14" />. Therefore the number is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/4/6343ae11f7d4980254c30eea36fc6f1a1183f0c2.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot 27 = 162$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />. Out of the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/1/911bd1cd1243d0fb147bd1300b1e2f867a86f1eb.png" class="latex" alt="$162$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> the one with the largest sum of digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" />, and the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/e/29e0a7dd842377240046d537f17ab0137270ce07.png" class="latex" alt="$9+9=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />. Hence the sum of digits will be at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>Also, each number with this property is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, therefore it is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, and thus also its sum of digits is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.
</p><p>We only have six possibilities left for the sum of the digits: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, but since the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, the digits can only add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. This leads to the integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/d/94db15b430439ad5b3c80bfacfef0c6499ede2ee.png" class="latex" alt="$72$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> being possibilities. We can check to see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/3547351abf03ab0b6cbda7b0b905b2e4f6220e1e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" /> solution: the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> is the only solution that satisfies the conditions in the problem.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>We can write each integer between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/0/0705411d92671faf1fe5602cfef8f353c7f0f83d.png" class="latex" alt="$999$" width="26" height="12" /> inclusive as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/d/c1daa8c30e11d92002c4a3e4c990d7d8b2abf8fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{abc}=100a+10b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="17" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/7197daa43d44b172780291b4fe51b49c34d13cfb.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b,c\in\{0,1,\dots,9\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb122460c32546fcfd0b1f51acd37ba61722c631.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" />.
The sum of digits of this number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/b/6ebd1e0356cfe09c0d9b5459d6f1542657bb5891.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="70" height="14" />, hence we get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/7/4675f7d6f9ddc8b283dccc1db1f1a4d190d3de55.png" class="latex" alt="$100a+10b+c = 6(a+b+c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="18" />. This simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/c/80cb0d51c46999211be9fccced5a6b839a2b0703.png" class="latex" alt="$94a + 4b - 5c = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="14" />. Clearly for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/2/4c28242522f23e896074dc89612847f8317911d7.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" /> there are no solutions, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69cefa6855d2216036df094012f7ad2a7e4e204f.png" class="latex" alt="$a=0$" width="42" height="12" /> and we get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/b/dabbaf90a275f2112f3ebeb52240fa0354642a98.png" class="latex" alt="$4b=5c$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" />. This obviously has only one valid solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/4/df4b7fb942277923cb7a2c1514f9dd85316f23b0.png" class="latex" alt="$(b,c)=(5,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" />, hence the only solution is the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>The sum of the digits is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/d/19d6399b71b30f4b9073c8a8d10e4a69db7c08aa.png" class="latex" alt="$9+9+9=27$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="114" height="14" />. Therefore the number is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/4/6343ae11f7d4980254c30eea36fc6f1a1183f0c2.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot 27 = 162$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />. Since the number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> times the sum of its digits, it must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, therefore also by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, therefore the sum of its digits must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. With this in mind we can conclude that the number must be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, not just by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />. Since the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, it is also divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, therefore the sum of its digits is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, therefore the number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />, which leaves us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a75cef16b301d38a271625130c1aac46a7e9ce72.png" class="latex" alt="$108$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/1/911bd1cd1243d0fb147bd1300b1e2f867a86f1eb.png" class="latex" alt="$162$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. Only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> times its digits, hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/4/3547351abf03ab0b6cbda7b0b905b2e4f6220e1e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{1}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p>
