<p>In a certain year the price of gasoline rose by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> during January, fell by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/8/1183a12017e48198b3352789cc39b223b17945d6.png" class="latex" alt="$20\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> during February, rose by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/96633b73a86b9d9110e1ff824fce08def96ca296.png" class="latex" alt="$25\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" /> during March, and fell by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/e/8de1a36d69a7028480a87b712a7141c651398a06.png" class="latex" alt="$x\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="25" height="14" /> during April. The price of gasoline at the end of April was the same as it had been at the beginning of January. To the nearest integer, what is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/2/842345d8b9391ea18b7ea4664c7e31b1fc7c3ca9.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 12\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 17\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 20\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 25\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 35$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> be the price at the beginning of January.  The price at the end of March was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/b/7db780a30a38dbfd65aaa306357c9205c948e6eb.png" class="latex" alt="$(1.2)(0.8)(1.25)p = 1.2p.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="18" /> Because the price at the end of April was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, the price decreased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/6459f5bed8964a7e0c825cb4a727b2936f6fab14.png" class="latex" alt="$0.2p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="32" height="16" /> during April, and the percent decrease was
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/a/f7a133f61c84005792fb54e3b863469ff7dc7de6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x = 100 \cdot \frac{0.2p}{1.2p} = \frac {100}{6} \approx 16.7.\]" width="226" height="41" />
So to the nearest integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/9/359659c6d36bc0c6e4b36daf21bbe9e930adcffd.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{17}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd5cf5dbc8229738cf06adbf2aa73fd75994b058.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
