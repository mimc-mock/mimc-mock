<p>A flagpole is originally <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> meters tall. A hurricane snaps the flagpole at a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> meters above the ground so that the upper part, still attached to the stump, touches the ground <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> meter away from the base. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/8/d18b099f29e2006dba0ade0d6abcc8f0cf0b970d.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } 2.0 \qquad \text{(B) } 2.1 \qquad \text{(C) } 2.2 \qquad \text{(D) } 2.4 \qquad \text{(E) } 2.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="400" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The broken flagpole forms a right triangle with legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, and hypotenuse <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c445a843f7b9a5ae5e089d62c7a3d22d55a9bee6.png" class="latex" alt="$5-x$" width="41" height="12" />. The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> now states that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/8/2280ebae350361fbb97f83df70548e00050e0acf.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2 + x^2 = (5-x)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/c/d3cea1909af8942ec6b1396f41b6e7e12ed0235a.png" class="latex" alt="$10x = 24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/8/7d8b68ab5b524966d48cb53c25e57a0b8509601f.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{2.4}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="69" height="23" />.
</p><p>(Note that the resulting triangle is the well-known <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/6/c1645fea006e40422df8465ad25c74bc33e615eb.png" class="latex" alt="$5-12-13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="89" height="13" /> right triangle, scaled by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/8/4386436ff16509fe977cc2c9f87c3f170990b8f3.png" class="latex" alt="$1/5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>A right triangle is formed with the bottom of the flagpole, the snapped part, and the ground. One leg is of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and the other is length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a>, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/7/037a3d897de2590489fa217621f1f15ab7d00481.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{x^2+1^2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="72" height="18" /> must be the length of the snapped part of the flagpole. Observe that all the answer choices are rational. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is rational, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/4/c445a843f7b9a5ae5e089d62c7a3d22d55a9bee6.png" class="latex" alt="$5-x$" width="41" height="12" />, which is the snapped part, must also be rational. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/9/069461de83aa25490006a00cfe07f04031894303.png" class="latex" alt="$1, x, 5-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> must form a scaled Pythagorean triple. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/b/e4bb9ba576d1b34375aaee161649ad43c31ad736.png" class="latex" alt="$10, 24, 26$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="70" height="16" /> is a Pythagorean triple, so the corresponding answer must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/0/30044f11ce1fed406f305e002d236d39ce95ffe3.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2.4, 2.6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="16" />. Adding together the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and the snapped part, this does indeed equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, so our solution is done.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> represent the flagpole in the diagram above. After the flagpole breaks at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, its tip lies at point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Since none of the flagpole is destroyed, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/9/eb9f4cb8cd01ce230066f3ce5412abf38dc79b4f.png" class="latex" alt="$DA=DC$" width="83" height="13" />. Therefore, triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8eabc87afe4c11a47e4aa27628d5517af4866b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADC$" width="59" height="13" /> is isosceles.
</p><p>Draw the altitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/9/8d93370bf0c5138121cb37672163df670898ccd1.png" class="latex" alt="$DE \perp AC$" width="82" height="13" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8eabc87afe4c11a47e4aa27628d5517af4866b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADC$" width="59" height="13" /> is isosceles, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/5/6756daa2d442d14b9249c7a3da5b395cfa91e10e.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = EC$" width="81" height="13" />. Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/c/1fc048d1d191847da8b9c72bca6934ebae688907.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AED \sim \triangle ABC$" width="142" height="13" />. Therefore,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/d/97de9345df687bcdd5ca0c87f5b1379129c57426.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} AD &amp;= AE \times \frac{AD}{AE} \\ &amp;= \frac{AC}{2} \times \frac{AC}{AB} \\ &amp;= \frac{AC^2}{2 AB} \\ &amp;= \frac{AB^2 + BC^2}{2 AB} \end{align*}" width="149" height="172" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/8/d88d3b225d4dcf1a78380f5c98940f6107e3deca.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 5$" width="61" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/6/74666022a42f4446ed551033a088108882c5ec09.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9eed01ed1e979df597c2dbf0e747c9cf1c5eaf63.png" class="latex" alt="$AD = \frac{5^2 + 1^2}{2 \cdot 5} = 2.6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="159" height="39" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/2/42251b0751f35ab06a574694095a1e9254aff345.png" class="latex" alt="$x = AB - AD = \boxed{2.4}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="173" height="23" />.
</p>
