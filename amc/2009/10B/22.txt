<p>A cubical  cake with edge length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> inches is iced on the sides and the top. It is cut vertically into three pieces as shown in this top view, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> is the midpoint of a top edge. The piece whose top is triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> cubic inches of cake and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> square inches of icing. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/2/7a2d6387b6c5a4bb8dcfe21d0ebac1fb992d9198.png" class="latex" alt="$c+s$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/a/b9a0b0d2b46c6d8034bbea244b0985f0df2a098d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(1cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt));  draw((-1,-1)--(1,-1)--(1,1)--(-1,1)--cycle); draw((1,1)--(-1,0)); pair P=foot((1,-1),(1,1),(-1,0)); draw((1,-1)--P); draw(rightanglemark((-1,0),P,(1,-1),4));  label(&quot;$M$&quot;,(-1,0),W); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(-0.1,-0.3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-0.4,0.7)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(0.7,0.4)); [/asy]" width="118" height="98" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/4/7846b3bdb289563ee5f441ccdab86f4f1d943845.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } \frac{24}{5} \qquad \text{(B) } \frac{32}{5} \qquad \text{(C) } 8+\sqrt5 \qquad \text{(D) } 5+\frac{16\sqrt5}{5} \qquad \text{(E) } 10+5\sqrt5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="537" height="41" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/9/219b93023f9800bc9603ccb7fe4ecb7ec94fba85.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt));  draw((-1,-1)--(1,-1)--(1,1)--(-1,1)--cycle); draw((1,1)--(-1,0)); pair P=foot((1,-1),(1,1),(-1,0)); draw((1,-1)--P); draw(rightanglemark((-1,0),P,(1,-1),4));  label(&quot;$M$&quot;,(-1,0),W); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(-0.1,-0.3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-0.4,0.7)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(0.7,0.4)); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,(-1,1),NW); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,(1,1),NE); label(&quot;$R$&quot;,(1,-1),SE); label(&quot;$S$&quot;,(-1,-1),SW); label(&quot;$N$&quot;,P,NW); [/asy]" width="228" height="222" />
</p><p>Let's label the points as in the picture above. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/c/87cb9cb353c0ff99455720e9c7b42a2b74b3abf9.png" class="latex" alt="$[RNQ]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="53" height="18" /> be the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/b/25b04d633d24ed5eccd9121566316eec26381a6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle RNQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="17" />. Then the volume of the corresponding piece is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/c/edc9508670d799f0ba58e9a0fe697cb820cd44bb.png" class="latex" alt="$c=2[RNQ]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="18" />. This cake piece has icing on the top and on the vertical side that contains the edge <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/7/9a7c52aff9dd9987076c0ac4fecccf59b6ed8bdb.png" class="latex" alt="$QR$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="16" />. Hence the total area with icing is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/b/7ebfa114e72dc3a17c227630b9493d29a6a02dc1.png" class="latex" alt="$[RNQ]+2^2 = [RNQ]+4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="204" height="20" />. Thus the answer to our problem is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/a/02ad585c3608c2dc0e903c07825aee509477c292.png" class="latex" alt="$3[RNQ]+4$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="95" height="18" />, and all we have to do now is determine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/c/87cb9cb353c0ff99455720e9c7b42a2b74b3abf9.png" class="latex" alt="$[RNQ]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="53" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Introduce a coordinate system where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/2/8a2cad56e46680b3317b6219212c5caa701706e7.png" class="latex" alt="$Q=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/e/60ef96e6598d4c19c77b87cc9b1adf417fb4fe55.png" class="latex" alt="$P=(2,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/e/f4e07e3cadc1797f31292373bcdcffa41c61ddd9.png" class="latex" alt="$R=(0,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />. 
</p><p>In this coordinate system we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d3e88109d69f5e95af509f428e6d52e2cdc785.png" class="latex" alt="$M=(2,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="82" height="18" />, and the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd3cb93d5a71391d67f969e764f66eff46349eff.png" class="latex" alt="$QM$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="16" /> has the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c33aae1795e6aabf8f154459def75fa5a680471e.png" class="latex" alt="$2y-x=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />. 
</p><p>As the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/d/bddf730477ae3ea7754344d502b5b581f2f9a2da.png" class="latex" alt="$RN$" width="30" height="12" /> is orthogonal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/3/fd3cb93d5a71391d67f969e764f66eff46349eff.png" class="latex" alt="$QM$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="16" />, it must have the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b889d489b4d6572873aafb7627082c21c2e3ede2.png" class="latex" alt="$y+2x+q=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="115" height="16" /> for some suitable constant <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" />. As this line contains the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/e/f4e07e3cadc1797f31292373bcdcffa41c61ddd9.png" class="latex" alt="$R=(0,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/0/1b00c96499972dcece6a927ae01957dc79c0be1b.png" class="latex" alt="$q=-2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="15" />.
</p><p>Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76be42ee835b307eb620a487156e9f8988558429.png" class="latex" alt="$x=2y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/c/c0c48cc8223b7d48a9f0b0230d6cbf73e977a4ff.png" class="latex" alt="$y+2x-2=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="115" height="16" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/6/e06aa01176db19c2b8f39fc791a196dd1f7593d5.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac 25$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="45" height="37" />, and then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/8/2a831f9cfec6d92ecad865978fdf89d150c955ba.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac 45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="37" />. 
</p><p>We can note that in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/b/25b04d633d24ed5eccd9121566316eec26381a6f.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle RNQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="17" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the height from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/0/8908a2be2692d4eb840757cd862a1fd76a024398.png" class="latex" alt="$RQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />, hence its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/c/00cd27c4be5b5737396472ae9b5eaa22618c21e3.png" class="latex" alt="$[RNQ] = \frac{x \cdot RQ} 2 = \frac{2x}2 = x = \frac 45$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="253" height="37" />, and therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/8/5e888f0d23d8d7e2b8efae2c596d4eb4f1d46285.png" class="latex" alt="$3[RNQ]+4 = 3\cdot \frac 45 + 4 = \boxed{\frac{32}5 \Longrightarrow B}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="298" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Extend <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/d/bddf730477ae3ea7754344d502b5b581f2f9a2da.png" class="latex" alt="$RN$" width="30" height="12" /> to intersect <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/5/c957496bf7ab118d7d2312a2ce7cc06f96515f97.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt));  draw((-1,-1)--(1,-1)--(1,1)--(-1,1)--cycle); draw((1,1)--(-1,0)); pair P=foot((1,-1),(1,1),(-1,0)); draw((1,-1)--P); draw(rightanglemark((-1,0),P,(1,-1),4)); draw(P -- (0,1));  label(&quot;$M$&quot;,(-1,0),W); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,(-0.1,-0.3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-0.4,0.7)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,(0.7,0.4)); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,(-1,1),NW); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,(1,1),NE); label(&quot;$R$&quot;,(1,-1),SE); label(&quot;$S$&quot;,(-1,-1),SW); label(&quot;$N$&quot;,P,1.5*WNW); label(&quot;$O$&quot;,(0,1),N); [/asy]" width="228" height="222" />
</p><p>It is now obvious that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />. (Imagine rotating the square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259d1d91a5687fe90489d9efeeab80c4de05cf5f.png" class="latex" alt="$PQRS$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="54" height="16" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> clockwise around its center. This rotation will map the segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859356a4f63456a4acbb1f2b191982b4aa400279.png" class="latex" alt="$MQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="16" /> to a segment that is orthogonal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859356a4f63456a4acbb1f2b191982b4aa400279.png" class="latex" alt="$MQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="33" height="16" />, contains <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> and contains the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab17967324fc2b12349c0610c6c256f4c829162.png" class="latex" alt="$PQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />.
</p><p>From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/785cef6fea6a7cd8873b9902d9a2168ccc48c8c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PQM$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="17" /> we can compute that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a955254ceddf29dec9482695458c877c7b6cc233.png" class="latex" alt="$QM = \sqrt{1^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="177" height="20" />.
</p><p>Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/5/785cef6fea6a7cd8873b9902d9a2168ccc48c8c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PQM$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/41184f998af018940f2f4ab3b3cf671b4bbc97d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle NQO$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="17" /> have the same angles and therefore they are similar. The ratio of their sides is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/e/7ce35b95bfd8b8f34fc554ea999c775841c091a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{QM}{OQ} = \frac{\sqrt 5}1 = \sqrt 5$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="139" height="44" />.
</p><p>Hence we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/d/efdce7af899e223a461adccdbcf03716dbca1c6d.png" class="latex" alt="$ON = \frac{PM}{\sqrt 5} = \frac 1{\sqrt 5}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="144" height="42" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/5/fa5eee5abfb046fdea6248d4a0f7c3c9af4ff14d.png" class="latex" alt="$NQ = \frac{PQ}{\sqrt 5} = \frac 2{\sqrt 5}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="138" height="42" />.
</p><p>Knowing this, we can compute the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/41184f998af018940f2f4ab3b3cf671b4bbc97d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle NQO$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="17" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8b61eaf787619b996d732a9176211658082210.png" class="latex" alt="$[NQO] = \frac{ON \cdot NQ}2 = \frac 15$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="194" height="37" />.
</p><p>Finally, we compute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/9/5e914d67030b2d76cda08616d6ca1e99daa80c59.png" class="latex" alt="$[RNQ] = [ROQ] - [NQO] = 1 - \frac 15 = \frac 45,$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="319" height="37" /> and conclude that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a05696585e7b24c735b9ddba2424c6ed0be88add.png" class="latex" alt="$3[RNQ]+4 = 3\cdot \frac 45 + 4 = \boxed{\frac{32}5}.$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="247" height="48" />
</p>
<ul><li>You could also notice that the two triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b37ee2b89daac8cff2c128efeeeb91f8f5e20cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle PMQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/3/5f3202c26f449740e52cb14af53a0978acb8b94a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle NQR$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="61" height="17" /> in the original figure are similar.</li></ul>
