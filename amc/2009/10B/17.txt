<p>Five unit squares are arranged in the coordinate plane as shown, with the lower left corner at the origin. The slanted line, extending from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/d/74db346cfe9282fb9c83277082f8e71fa7e1c01e.png" class="latex" alt="$(c,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/0/9a078b263b69698546e4c4300872e978a126f665.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, divides the entire region into two regions of equal area. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/5/f6555a485643d4132b1cf51c92ce8523144dcfe9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.2cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt));  fill((2/3,0)--(3,3)--(3,1)--(2,1)--(2,0)--cycle,gray);  xaxis(&quot;$x$&quot;,-0.5,4,EndArrow(HookHead,4)); yaxis(&quot;$y$&quot;,-0.5,4,EndArrow(4));  draw((0,1)--(3,1)--(3,3)--(2,3)--(2,0)); draw((1,0)--(1,2)--(3,2)); draw((2/3,0)--(3,3));  label(&quot;$(c,0)$&quot;,(2/3,0),S); label(&quot;$(3,3)$&quot;,(3,3),NE); [/asy]" width="368" height="368" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/5/bb512e74df025e9b7443e81a172114e9fbf25a1d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac12\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac35\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac23\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac34\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac45$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="367" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95ada87f0a4413f618426ff0a6c08e2879704104.png" class="latex" alt="$c\geq 1.5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="54" height="15" /> the shaded area is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/7/15788e198a53ced87268aea78fcde68a84de92c0.png" class="latex" alt="$1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="13" />, which is too little. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/a/43a5a121c6c6d863995ba1ba57c431fae5c3b475.png" class="latex" alt="$c&lt;1.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />, and therefore the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa6bb1c456d71ee24e7a0ee603d226a7992e5da3.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> is indeed inside the shaded part, as shown in the picture.
</p><p>Then the area of the shaded part is one less than the area of the triangle with vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/d/74db346cfe9282fb9c83277082f8e71fa7e1c01e.png" class="latex" alt="$(c,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="37" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/0/9a078b263b69698546e4c4300872e978a126f665.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. The area of the entire triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/c/8cc29e08facbad99fec613b15dd1e24ba3ca271e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3(3-c)}2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="38" />, therefore the area of the shaded part is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/8/4c87d981fe5d658dfab6cb8727f6c0bcb18b49ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7-3c}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="37" />.
</p><p>The entire figure has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, hence we want the shaded part to have area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a3da0818711d86d02d7e709f47544bfe24cb5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 52$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/3/1c34aaf005611ac23a20283296b2fe44a841b3c8.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\frac 23}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="57" height="48" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484f88154fadcf9c781bba19becdae88696b77f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The unit square is of area 1, so the five unit squares have area 5.
Therefore the shaded space must occupy 2.5. The missing unit square is of area 1, and if reconstituted the original triangle would be of area 3.5.
It can then be inferred:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54932b60bc27144ab6048329fa2f62ef8106a35.png" class="latex" alt="$(3-c) * 3 = 7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/c/2dc42bad171eaa9121120de2e20a448142deff9c.png" class="latex" alt="$3-c=\frac{7}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="75" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08f3dbb3e03a475ef2d02f8e6c4b9ab352537b1d.png" class="latex" alt="$3-\frac{7}{3}=c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="76" height="37" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/7/0276aeb2ff8e5b4cc2441f68e97f61b9e540dcfd.png" class="latex" alt="$3-\frac{7}{3} = \frac{9-7}{3} = \frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="149" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/4/6f4f61a9007d3e46e88200ae7179319915581855.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>The shaded space of the object can become a triangle by adding a unit square to its bottom. This triangle has a base length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/7/277ff913d1272481cb651d8888fa6bf0171a3064.png" class="latex" alt="$3-c$" width="39" height="12" /> and a height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. The area of this triangle region is now (using the formula <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/4/244e712b1949bdcec71bf67ea1ed670d250c1da1.png" class="latex" alt="$A=bh/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="74" height="18" /> for a triangle)<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/e/bdee29e9edb55d6c4c27fc1cadae349038ea9637.png" class="latex" alt="$(9-3c)/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />. But, remember that we have to subtract the area of the extra unit square from this area to get the area of the shaded region. 
</p><p>If you add 3 unit squares to the top of the unshaded portion, the figure is now a right trapezoid. Using the formula for a trapezoid, you can derive that its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/d/50d18bc5d265222168bdbab856f48810331eed0b.png" class="latex" alt="$(9+3c)/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />. After you subtract the area of the 3 extra unit squares, we have derived the area of the unshaded portion.
</p><p>Since these areas are both equal, we set them equal to each other and solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cadfce2c02b5fa09a9b39194769480b9a88c3812.png" class="latex" alt="$(9-3c)/2 -1 = (9+3c)/2 -3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="247" height="18" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is now solved to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/7547747b133453d3c209cff6cfbf129aa67a6947.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/4/6f4f61a9007d3e46e88200ae7179319915581855.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
</p><p>~Solution by athens2016
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Test all solutions by solving for the triangle area. When you find a triangle minus 1 equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/d/3adf28c1ab96fc4eddaa890ec574e4cea2caea36.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, that should be your answer. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/4/6f4f61a9007d3e46e88200ae7179319915581855.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h3>
<p>Use the shoelace theorem to get the area of the unshaded portion in terms of a. Divide this area by the whole area which is 5. This fraction should be equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/3/1f3cc0ad966b3e637cd199607cbf0d1befc0e491.png" class="latex" alt="$1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />. You can easily solve for the answer from there.
-mewto
</p>
