<p>Segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/6/9a65c05bd85d2609bea5db2b109ed5f556463511.png" class="latex" alt="$AE$" width="27" height="13" /> intersect at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, as shown, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/8/6e821ade0898eedcb1cf0f0ae3fb14980cd2991d.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=BC=CD=CE$" width="188" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/a/35a31a7b55ee18069f9dcb157537a1f868c8fa77.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle A = \frac 52 \angle B$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="37" />. What is the degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae15cd6fc137587ad1676133c8342dc210dc6364.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle D$" width="28" height="12" />? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/4/2648b032deb3bc4fba6f53bcb3b07b32bcfad484.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2cm); defaultpen(linewidth(.8pt)+fontsize(8pt)); dotfactor=4;  pair C=(0,0), Ep=dir(35), D=dir(-35), B=dir(145); pair A=intersectionpoints(Circle(B,1),C--(-1*Ep))[0]; pair[] ds={A,B,C,D,Ep};  dot(ds); draw(A--Ep--D--B--cycle);  label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,SW); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,NW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,N); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,Ep,E); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,E); [/asy]" width="192" height="132" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/2/832242bbce4c990ccf05ee970a0bd9a40c605a37.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{(A) } 52.5 \qquad \text{(B) } 55 \qquad \text{(C) } 57.7 \qquad \text{(D) } 60 \qquad \text{(E) } 62.5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="418" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is isosceles, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ec657286c4fef5beb788b93ce53761c66577b88.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ACB = \angle CAB$" width="134" height="13" />. 
</p><p>The sum of internal angles of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> can now be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/0/1f063e9cc4bc31d11c186abeb90e0063236ac932.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B + \frac 52 \angle B + \frac 52 \angle B = 6\angle B$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="213" height="37" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/b/83b275c7c9471c1789184e7a6e9884e24e76d206.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle B = 30^\circ$" width="76" height="13" />, and each of the other two angles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/d/dedd77d029c19ec5ed32b0f5b1275ad02b22b1ef.png" class="latex" alt="$75^\circ$" width="24" height="13" />. 
</p><p>Now we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/b/65bb60d589fb4b5aa4ae43f3c499731c48de7258.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DCE = \angle ACB = 75^\circ$" width="185" height="13" />.
</p><p>Finally, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b984c55719cc5f992ee62656d920ac15c78d9137.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CDE$" width="60" height="13" /> is isosceles, hence each of the two remaining angles (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/1/ae15cd6fc137587ad1676133c8342dc210dc6364.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle D$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef820980d93fb66d6f4b2883b9630d6bbdfd2667.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle E$" width="27" height="12" />) is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/3/7433fc481fc9adca45c9adc84623944f4cc9adc6.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{180^\circ - 75^\circ}2 = \frac{105^\circ}2 = \boxed{52.5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="218" height="37" />.
</p>
