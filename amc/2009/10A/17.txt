<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>The situation is shown in the picture below.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/c/32c515543c7a81f453411db67766927addafff00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.6cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(0,0), B=(4,0), C=(4,3), D=(0,3); pair EF=rotate(90)*(D-B); pair E=intersectionpoint( (0,-100)--(0,100), (B-100*EF)--(B+100*EF) ); pair F=intersectionpoint( (-100,3)--(100,3), (B-100*EF)--(B+100*EF) ); draw(A--B--C--D--cycle); draw(B--D, dashed); draw(E--F); draw(A--E, dashed); draw(C--F, dashed); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,SE); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,N); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NW); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,SW); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,NE); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,A--D,W); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,C--D,N); [/asy]" width="225" height="282" />
</p><p>From the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb1064b607676819b46b58a2a13b6f4aea9a22af.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=5$" width="63" height="12" />.
</p><p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e30e7dec97a3f5e84e0de8a12e3056c6c9a77fc.png" class="latex" alt="$EAB$" width="42" height="13" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/5/7e538978649c14e1616fac744e983b5981d0ea8e.png" class="latex" alt="$BAD$" width="43" height="13" />, as they have the same angles. Segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/6/d66492998689e8303534c9e4f3ba1fa650fd41f0.png" class="latex" alt="$BA$" width="28" height="13" /> is perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/3/0b31cd391bebc36897a8c10ea78e5d584980b049.png" class="latex" alt="$DA$" width="29" height="13" />, meaning that angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/9/1e94f1abd32d37d04a5b77e333689bf70ef83864.png" class="latex" alt="$DAB$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/d/1eda04afa4b8a0146840e3993cf91206bf6cb7b8.png" class="latex" alt="$BAE$" width="42" height="13" /> are right angles and congruent. Also, angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/d/afd086bfde3cbcd3899095243a222eee23e40b5c.png" class="latex" alt="$DBE$" width="44" height="12" /> is a right angle. Because it is a rectangle, angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4057c3e63298c2ed6715003afb6f2d9dc6272037.png" class="latex" alt="$BDC$" width="44" height="12" /> is congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/600c2c8bf2070476b38b1f452ef77d07759219f6.png" class="latex" alt="$DBA$" width="44" height="13" /> and angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840cb55c82434b9ae047760636a017e9441e6ca5.png" class="latex" alt="$ADC$" width="43" height="13" /> is also a right angle. By the transitive property:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbfe82ffe084cdc0d2b71c86ca2808be3d164828.png" class="latex" alt="$mADB + mBDC = mDBA + mABE$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="307" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/0/25035825c4e2c1bc8c9ec8672b463cfb449fc42c.png" class="latex" alt="$mBDC = mDBA$" width="144" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/7/d57e1699f0a2a6fd5917fd8b50b25eba9e01265f.png" class="latex" alt="$mADB + mBDC = mBDC + mABE$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="308" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/b/e5b528657ea3e68b8342dbe6592340e41572997f.png" class="latex" alt="$mADB = mABE$" width="142" height="13" />
</p><p>Next, because every triangle has a degree measure of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb1b41850ab676e4d1cf15c69b1609af4befbc5.png" class="latex" alt="$180$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/7/e07a934f110b579c0ee7451176a281ae64e8c426.png" class="latex" alt="$BEA$" width="42" height="13" /> and angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/600c2c8bf2070476b38b1f452ef77d07759219f6.png" class="latex" alt="$DBA$" width="44" height="13" /> are similar. 
</p><p><br />
Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/b/b7bebf8d11d83883302ed2a0e94833e78a2f5817.png" class="latex" alt="$BE/AB = DB/AD$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="18" />, and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/2/ba28adfddc06162f640df5b649392987e4a41ca5.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = AB\cdot DB/AD = 20/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="222" height="18" />.
</p><p>Also triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/5/d454ccad55388ce38766e919a448421c4633323a.png" class="latex" alt="$CBF$" width="43" height="12" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/6/5d6b041f26cd9dbb6669049f9d30a3432fc62542.png" class="latex" alt="$BF/BC = DB/AB$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="18" />, and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/a/73a10e5f78eaf32d45cada6dab8b9762eb2d7d3a.png" class="latex" alt="$BF=BC\cdot DB / AB = 15/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="222" height="18" />.
</p><p>We then have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf063b752f60a99fc163b9ab1cd9a3a2076e0ec6.png" class="latex" alt="$EF = EB+BF = \frac{20}3 + \frac{15}4 = \frac{80 + 45}{12} = \boxed{\frac{125}{12}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="378" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/f/89fff82bb65d0215e49c8c91cb7c553da52205e2.png" class="latex" alt="$BD$" width="29" height="12" /> is the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" />, we can use the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/2/6221c87e92a08dbf0036dd430533fb99c148ec9e.png" class="latex" alt="$BD^2 = EB\cdot BF$" width="133" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Looking at the angles, we see that triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e81e1599a08776bb0799f73128f99332c1d78f.png" class="latex" alt="$BDE$" width="44" height="12" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/5/cf51b47182ee2e945de88c04f653d01d4919d104.png" class="latex" alt="$DCB$" width="44" height="12" />. Because of this, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/2/6c271ed3b5e26d0f0606868be65e5cefa88423b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{CB} = \frac{EB}{DB}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="38" />. From the given information and the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_theorem" class="mw-redirect" title="Pythagorean theorem">Pythagorean theorem</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b44beb78e8be18179bfb7fe6b3424e7fb1aef32.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/6/ea6d56408dbf6eed00eb265d7dc919c687afc12f.png" class="latex" alt="$CB=3$" width="61" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/3/0033fcb53023dac318ccbbb6360a03dae2ca6420.png" class="latex" alt="$DB=5$" width="63" height="12" />. Solving gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/8/2b83bb05bc9a01c2b14712b1dd8b9eed08b6e3f2.png" class="latex" alt="$EB=20/3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="18" />. 
</p><p>We can use the above formula to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/e/65e4f2d5cc59e5ffbed3734130e8c45b643adc91.png" class="latex" alt="$BF$" width="28" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/c/60c7b417a06ab86bc0d8e3f085b9b3b6cbfa3c8e.png" class="latex" alt="$BD^2 = 20/3\cdot BF$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="19" />. Solve to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/c/5cc7de7f10f279639d751ed84460f73bca3f048e.png" class="latex" alt="$BF=15/4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="89" height="18" />. 
</p><p>We now know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bbe91133fdaa92f3e58b137fdb4db178296da61.png" class="latex" alt="$EB$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/e/65e4f2d5cc59e5ffbed3734130e8c45b643adc91.png" class="latex" alt="$BF$" width="28" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf063b752f60a99fc163b9ab1cd9a3a2076e0ec6.png" class="latex" alt="$EF = EB+BF = \frac{20}3 + \frac{15}4 = \frac{80 + 45}{12} = \boxed{\frac{125}{12}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="378" height="48" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>There is a better solution where we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> directly instead of in parts (use similarity). The strategy is similar.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4(Coordinate_Bash)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4.28Coordinate_Bash.29">Solution 4(Coordinate Bash)</span></h3>
<p>To keep things simple, we will use coordinates in only the first quadrant. The picture will look like the diagram above reflected over the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis.It is also worth noting the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> will lie on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the origin, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e208942a293328242e66427058c19f14c8995e.png" class="latex" alt="$A(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/3/5e33b3faf5b170f9b3894d8e3da0e7431d536c41.png" class="latex" alt="$C(4,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, and  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/a/52a32ef5b9109dce8144e1e3a600634eefbed27f.png" class="latex" alt="$B(4,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. We can express segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" /> as the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/6/b461dd0d35c5aaa53b2e8415a45fcf5af1b98a99.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{3x}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="55" height="38" />. 
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" /> is perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/4/204a6f5a6757f5da4c1b3035ad8d806769744e63.png" class="latex" alt="$DB$" width="29" height="12" />, and we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563948106c3332662355412547d99acab218c16f.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> lies on it, we can use this information to find that segment <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" />
is on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c591241be14c44a5eedb90d2c9a4cb0f99fa25e9.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{-4x}{3}+\frac{25}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="114" height="37" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> are on the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis, respectively, we plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />
and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />, we find that point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/a/7ea985e518315d827274fe5cb57ab4e1f718f418.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,\frac{25}{3})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="52" height="37" />, and point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092100478dc38f0b6d19b83fa46a7e8d047d746d.png" class="latex" alt="$(\frac{25}{4},0)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="52" height="38" />. Applying the distance formula,
we obtain that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/2/c128243ea8125d0bb007b8132dcfa44461df73a7.png" class="latex" alt="$EF$" width="28" height="12" />= <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/7/de71bfd477dca3b806354b3d1315bf01b81e5316.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\frac{125}{12}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="43" height="48" />.
