<p>Eric plans to compete in a triathlon. He can average <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> miles per hour in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c339afa03e060d959e4942be7522c0c992f4c41c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" />-mile swim and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> miles per hour in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />-mile run. His goal is to finish the triathlon in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> hours. To accomplish his goal what must his average speed in miles per hour, be for the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />-mile bicycle ride?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/f/fcfa54908e39bf3d85c1a81c6700570d0b25f0ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac{120}{11} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 11 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac{56}{5} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac{45}{4} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 12$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="408" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/1/6f1218dbfae7805ea5564ac7ee2a892d0a846653.png" class="latex" alt="$d=rt$" width="48" height="12" />, Eric takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aacfa503ea0e2162dff1c630f3103a64b002447.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{1}{4}}{2}=\frac{1}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="50" height="40" /> hours for the swim. Then, he takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/2/1523a4d3333b31c89da6e84ea4c313f2aa15850e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{6}=\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="49" height="37" /> hours for the run. 
</p><p>So he needs to take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/8/b680583454ac77930bd09143fbff4a7e3f8f0865.png" class="latex" alt="$2-\frac{5}{8}=\frac{11}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" /> hours for the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> mile bike. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/c/c5c4a867767e25284842b60b5b99217cf68d0bb9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{15}{\frac{11}{8}}=\frac{120}{11} \frac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="116" height="44" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67e21a92ae1b8c2473863b39b7a845e1fc691114.png" class="latex" alt="$\longrightarrow \fbox{A}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="58" height="23" />
</p>
