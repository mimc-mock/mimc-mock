<p>Andrea and Lauren are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> kilometers apart. They bike toward one another with Andrea traveling three times as fast as Lauren, and the distance between them decreasing at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> kilometer per minute. After <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> minutes, Andrea stops biking because of a flat tire and waits for Lauren. After how many minutes from the time they started to bike does Lauren reach Andrea?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/a/08a462b11071b9d29e5236254bd18c9c20b349d3.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 20 \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 30 \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 55 \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 65 \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let their speeds in kilometers per hour be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/d/10db40cc18d7471c355651971b33d25580f36e42.png" class="latex" alt="$v_A$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="18" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/d/28d2763dd690247d2ef2c6a39da93856949dbd77.png" class="latex" alt="$v_L$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="10" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/8/7d8399082f4b025a539c9fc5da1e07750c97851a.png" class="latex" alt="$v_A=3v_L$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="70" height="15" /> and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a28db62a8252c016b21e8f52cc2ea02f0efcbbd0.png" class="latex" alt="$v_A+v_L=60$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="102" height="15" />. (The second equation follows from the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/a/87a7a619f7e42c9dd20c55cf338d69d507745413.png" class="latex" alt="$1\mathrm km/min = 60\mathrm km/h$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="163" height="18" />.) This solves to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7ca96e853175591123da0971db5c692dc32c9ce.png" class="latex" alt="$v_A=45$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="61" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/e/79ec6573e9623729e6e5423328a6b3a4dffa9138.png" class="latex" alt="$v_L=15$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="15" />. 
</p><p>As the distance decreases at a rate of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> kilometer per minute, after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> minutes the distance between them will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/d/17d78ae2f05f140c2c69a76ab90d1bf5d1116205.png" class="latex" alt="$20-5=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> kilometers.
</p><p>From this point on, only Lauren will be riding her bike. As there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> kilometers remaining and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/e/79ec6573e9623729e6e5423328a6b3a4dffa9138.png" class="latex" alt="$v_L=15$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="60" height="15" />, she will need exactly an hour to get to Andrea. Therefore the total time in minutes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/3/49356d877b021d55f7da9f6118dc8d49504e10c0.png" class="latex" alt="$5+60 = \boxed{65}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="23" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Because the speed of Andrea is 3 times as fast as Lauren and the distance between them is decreasing at a rate of 1 kilometer per minute, Andrea's speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919c6a9c0b084df1dfde15943ec5c5897af9f039.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4} \textbf{km/min}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="80" height="38" />, and Lauren's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e95639ca6424f8bcba4c98a8b27d04879b1644.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4} \textbf{km/min}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="80" height="37" />. Therefore, after 5 minutes, Andrea will have biked <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/8/808c9445adbe9db44ee675902e6296f23f125591.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3}{4} \cdot 5 = \frac{15}{4}km$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="107" height="38" />.
</p><p>In all, Lauren will have to bike <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/6/df6a83e3fe5f325cfd7d18e781342d60248804c3.png" class="latex" alt="$20 - \frac{15}{4} = \frac{80}{4} - \frac{15}{4} = \frac{65}{4}km$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="225" height="38" />. Because her speed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e95639ca6424f8bcba4c98a8b27d04879b1644.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{4} \textbf{km/min}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="80" height="37" />, the time elapsed will be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/4/8d4d382cfcbd9d135284df592c2bd2d58992618e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{65}{4}}{\frac{1}{4}} = \boxed{\textbf{D) 65}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="104" height="47" />
</p>
