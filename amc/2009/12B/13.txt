<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/8/ca8d0ec6dc74f13176a38469882c5e79894f7409.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/8/d98d2ed450f84fb3b2599ba598ed5e6e7a69ecfa.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />, and the altitude to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" /> has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. What is the sum of the two possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/2/1d20ecb0f4235fe2d69eb2216c1bc4f60c84fa96.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 15\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 16\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 17\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 18\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 19$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be the foot of the altitude to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7dd2952327a9524b5fe6ea214e619d2b1d8bf71.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline BC$" width="28" height="15" />.  Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/9/a190479975642cc37d54e7f683adac5767ab2b98.png" class="latex" alt="$BD = \sqrt {13^2 - 12^2} = 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="176" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a316d4aa309061065f447301645f68255c1fc992.png" class="latex" alt="$DC = \sqrt {15^2 - 12^2} = 9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="175" height="18" />.  Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a95c92013847b0c7d5ed1e47138e864652b9bfc9.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = BD + BC = 5 + 9 = 14$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="241" height="14" /> or  assume that the triangle is obtuse at angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/8/018c7c616e0b7a272a77970bc3df6e55d2f0be9d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = DC - BD = 9 -5 = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="233" height="13" />.  The sum of the two possible values is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a923faead2cb21f4041dcf99e59c96d6d3aef400.png" class="latex" alt="$14 + 4 = \boxed{18}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="23" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/b/bfbf4df7c25681bb178ddc50ee0c0d656aa25b5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p>
