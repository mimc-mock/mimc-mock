<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> has vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/7/9c73ed8e69dbdf7240eb8185bfaf433b614fc6d8.png" class="latex" alt="$A = (3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/c/53c39306a7f6400bb8570b0394aaa761b8d0abb3.png" class="latex" alt="$B = (0,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is on the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4101381a47c3b67d875532d1ae8149dc863d69.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" />.  What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/3/a238f8130c70d244716a13df0897d09915931922.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 6\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 8\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 10\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 12\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 14$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="369" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Because the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4101381a47c3b67d875532d1ae8149dc863d69.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" /> is parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/8/238f77f5bd36cadced68f029e4554015289e50ee.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {AB}$" width="28" height="15" />, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is independent of the location of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> on that line.  Therefore it may be assumed that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e102c915addbddb07e04205f4aa8f247d404c8.png" class="latex" alt="$(7,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.  In that case the triangle has base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/5/8e5c76d295875eedec88e18c0a7ef4ab99266460.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" /> and altitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, so its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/1/471258839b5cffa6897f9cc509ed80b342243d21.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 = \boxed {6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="102" height="37" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The base of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/0/c10503afb369137ebe6cf9bf859ecd143d64b15b.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = \sqrt{3^2 + 3^2} = 3\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="181" height="19" />.  Its altitude is the distance between the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and the parallel line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/4/3a4101381a47c3b67d875532d1ae8149dc863d69.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/b/79b1804a12f9be0a9d78dd07d54a2bbe10eea0a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 4{\sqrt 2} = 2\sqrt 2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="86" height="41" />. Therefore its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/c/26c1d5b6bf53d6a9614f1a616d29681ae9e8b1cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 12 \cdot 3\sqrt 2 \cdot 2\sqrt 2 = \boxed{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="151" height="37" />.  The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/2/ca2f4283cfd441270cb90bfd2cf89a533f3dde81.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fab711cac413774cb38c162f63d0bf76374d2421.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(0.75cm); defaultpen(0.8); pair A=(3,0), B=(0,3); draw ( (-1,0) -- (9,0), dashed ); draw ( (0,-1) -- (0,9), dashed ); dot(A); dot(B); draw(A--B); draw ( (-1,8) -- (8,-1) ); label( &quot;$A$&quot;, A, S ); label( &quot;$B$&quot;, B, W ); label( &quot;$3$&quot;, A--(0,0), S ); label( &quot;$3$&quot;, B--(0,0), W ); label( &quot;$x+y=7$&quot;, (8,-1), SE ); pair C = intersectionpoint(A--(10,7),(7,0)--(0,7)); draw( A--C, dashed ); draw(rightanglemark(A,C,(7,0))); draw(rightanglemark(C,A,B)); label( &quot;$4$&quot;, A--(7,0), S ); label( &quot;$3\sqrt 2$&quot;, 0.67*B+0.33*A, NE ); label( &quot;$\frac 4{\sqrt 2}$&quot;, A--C, NW ); label( &quot;$\frac 4{\sqrt 2}$&quot;, C--(7,0), NE ); [/asy]" width="408" height="382" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>By Shoelace, our area is:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/9/1396c23f943303e97f684936ca70a7a56859b487.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac {1}{2} \cdot |(3\cdot 3 + 0 \cdot y+ 0 \cdot x)-(0*0+3(x+y))|.\]" width="351" height="37" />
We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/e/dee074fbbe761eeeecb3236e3c1cd9a50f643753.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=7$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="15" /> so we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/7/20734e7f08db10f3e04d7cef82ab45630ef51ce4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac {1}{2} \cdot |9-21|=\boxed 6\]" width="129" height="37" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>WLOG, let the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/f/97fadfe6bdbd1adb164f263b52f2a290723ad45c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> , or any coordinate, for that matter. Applying the shoelace formula, we get the area as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/1/62121a1bb86e20f85f2d22cb6d2bcaffb6faf431.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed 6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" />.
</p><p>~coolmath2017
</p>
