<p>Ten women sit in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> seats in a line. All of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> get up and then reseat themselves using all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> seats, each sitting in the seat she was in before or a seat next to the one she occupied before. In how many ways can the women be reseated?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/a/b7aaa5fe41e553af47b6a76eb278cc49480924e8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 89\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 90\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 120\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 2^{10}\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 2^2 3^8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="422" height="19" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Notice that either a woman stays in her own seat after the rearrangement, or two adjacent women swap places. Thus, our answer is counting the number of ways to arrange 1x1 and 2x1 blocks to form a 1x10 rectangle. This can be done via casework depending on the number of 2x1 blocks. The cases of 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 2x1 blocks correspond to 10, 8, 6, 4, 2, 0 1x1 blocks, and so the sum of the cases is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/5152c401cff4ca8e07112e761a15d3e308249f3b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\binom{10}{0} + \binom{9}{1} + \binom{8}{2} + \binom{7}{3} + \binom{6}{4} + \binom{5}{5} = 1 + 9 + 28 + 35 + 15 + 1 = \boxed{89}.\]" width="533" height="53" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_Shortcut">Solution 1 Shortcut</span></h3>
<p>Recall that the number of ways to arrange 1x1 and 2x1 blocks to form a 1xn rectangle results in <a href="/wiki/index.php/Fibonacci_numbers" class="mw-redirect" title="Fibonacci numbers">Fibonacci numbers</a>. Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57e7427a546847574817bcd46ca81d87e5e14a43.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{89}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" /> is the only fibonacci number, so no calculation is needed for this problem.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74a070eea97d9363f58a257e05e75fc69efa753.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> be the number of possible seating arrangements with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> women. Consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/4/724ce59c04f0542f832a1ddd186b6d3fbb2580fa.png" class="latex" alt="$n \ge 3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="48" height="16" /> and focus on the rightmost woman. If she returns back to her seat, then there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/b/f4b168d3b50d26ac9e4ab6e83c812fd1622ede8f.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{n-1}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="15" /> ways to seat the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bdca93962a2beaffc172ca394e04fe05bd74ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="12" /> women. If she sits in the second to last seat, then the woman who previously sat there must now sit at the rightmost seat. This gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b416a6ee2d55187b352341b14533153ce5748d39.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{n-2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="35" height="15" /> ways to seat the other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/9/ef9b02e32cfba0db0f9038a8d077596720f62e91.png" class="latex" alt="$n-2$" width="41" height="12" /> women, so we obtain the recursion
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/b/f6b0faa0e940a485cfa532b7342c5a1f91768519.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[S_n = S_{n-1}+S_{n-2}.\]" width="144" height="15" />
</p><p>Starting with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/5/375448b8c84bcf0b089347589be7ef2af2431295.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/c/2fc4f386435ab831a101ebaf728d13f51f35f682.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2=2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" /> we can calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/9/5c951c7cead975f8eea52da54dc2b529fee0d9ef.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{10}=\boxed{89}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="82" height="23" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clarification_of_Solution_2">Clarification of Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The seating possibilities of woman #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> become the two cases which we work out. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/4/c74a070eea97d9363f58a257e05e75fc69efa753.png" class="latex" alt="$S_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="19" height="15" /> was defined to be the number of different seating arrangements with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> women.
</p><p>In the first "case" if woman #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> sits in the seat #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, this leads to a similar scenario, but with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> women instead. That means that for this case, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/0/390a30235cff5a7be64f6192ce3b20176d8aedbb.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{9}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> possible arrangements. We don't know how many exactly, but we are able to define it in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />.
</p><p>During the second "case", woman #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> sits in seat #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. This time, woman #9 must go to seat #<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, as she is the only other person who can go there. This leaves us with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" /> women, and we again represent this in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/1/e91dafb3f89d992af872be29e67562c5b611383e.png" class="latex" alt="$S \Rightarrow S_8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="57" height="15" />.
</p><p>Therefore, we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/8/20838b5ca8856f98e9825f2c50e2e21733d63274.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="23" height="15" /> in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/a/07a3fd82bedcbf8d8fe475d9d82fbd04d7ca1c8d.png" class="latex" alt="$S_8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/9/449f9443326f9b0e025114ef8c22b60bb9955459.png" class="latex" alt="$S_9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="15" />, like so:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/5/c45503aac64a96f54f63fbea504108ab68adce5c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[S_{10} = S_8 + S_9.\]" width="112" height="15" />
</p><p>We can then generalize this to say
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/b/f6b0faa0e940a485cfa532b7342c5a1f91768519.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[S_n = S_{n-1}+S_{n-2}.\]" width="144" height="15" />
</p><p>Calculating <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/3/3f366ea6a346d59792ce1fa58dea7fa03ff7ce1c.png" class="latex" alt="$S_1 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="51" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/6/ad6b84b38865bab319505ea57632884fecaa82d1.png" class="latex" alt="$S_2 =2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" /> then following the recursive rule from above, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/b/ffbd7717317b67172b496e912c1edf505e37263f.png" class="latex" alt="$S_{10} = 89 \Rightarrow \boxed{\text{A}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="120" height="23" />.
</p>
