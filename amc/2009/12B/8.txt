<p>When a bucket is two-thirds full of water, the bucket and water weigh <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> kilograms. When the bucket is one-half full of water the total weight is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> kilograms. In terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, what is the total weight in kilograms when the bucket is full of water?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/415cee32b1989440384e5a4dc8a35d59670a3d5d.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ \frac23a + \frac13b\qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ \frac32a - \frac12b\qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ \frac32a + b\qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ \frac32a + 2b\qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 3a - 2b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="601" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the weight of the bucket and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be the weight of the water in a full bucket.  Then we are given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/9/289911478dd514ff5a4f1afad631d5ccd3f4c275.png" class="latex" alt="$x + \frac 23y = a$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad23b7cae0a6e641b801b08c3655b4bad43c9e03.png" class="latex" alt="$x + \frac 12y = b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="87" height="37" />.  Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/2/33255c92a7fb52826d5194282b8b97ac726ece71.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 16y = a-b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/a/76ac8fdb93f3eb20da81a746212abb4079cdb59a.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 6a-6b$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" />.  Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b481ef563c80f1222ecb7314e427ef8d8a01908.png" class="latex" alt="$x = b - \frac 12 (6a-6b) = -3a + 4b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="246" height="37" />.  Finally <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/c/70c03d28a313168d6d8d2844b20bedd059cc6418.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = \boxed {3a-2b}$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="136" height="25" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/3/3a3faf1572d785ef1d5d72f120fb3a16190b4b61.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Imagine that we take three buckets of the first type, to get rid of the fraction. We will have three buckets and two buckets' worth of water.
</p><p>On the other hand, if we take two buckets of the second type, we will have two buckets and enough water to fill one bucket.
</p><p>The difference between these is exactly one bucket full of water, hence the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/4/6940226208dcf0720b3575c4835c2d22c6f64efd.png" class="latex" alt="$3a-2b$" width="58" height="12" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>We are looking for an expression of the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/8/55859668f16a5d3366e3fdc636162a3319720813.png" class="latex" alt="$xa + yb$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />.
</p><p>We must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/476aee07c9af5110b8769e3534ef896fab9368ab.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="15" />, as the desired result contains exactly one bucket. Also, we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43e6d21e174c5fcde0f5332ae7451bc3317440e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac 23 x + \frac 12 y = 1$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="100" height="37" />, as the desired result contains exactly one bucket of water.
</p><p>At this moment, it is easiest to check that only the options (A), (B), and (E) satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/6/476aee07c9af5110b8769e3534ef896fab9368ab.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="15" />, and out of these only (E) satisfies the second equation.
</p><p>Alternatively, we can directly solve the system, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/2/b022dc8ef6d33931fc1ae3d27df3ea0a81b52936.png" class="latex" alt="$x=3$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/d/1ad33aeaf069ca6ae0a40f4cf7b9188a985624be.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="15" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is one bucket plus two-thirds of the total amount of water, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is one bucket plus one-half of the total amount of water, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> would equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/f/a3fc398c16cabfed7880a78338c93dd2c40a8357.png" class="latex" alt="$\cancel{\text{bucket}}+\frac23\cdot\text{water}-(\cancel{\text{bucket}}+\frac12\cdot\text{water})=\frac16\cdot\text{water}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="401" height="37" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> is one-sixth of the total mass of the water. Starting from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we add two one-sixths of the total amount of the water to become full. Therefore, the full bucket of water is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/2/45284e443e2cf32ddccbd01f4f84ec1ba5767f93.png" class="latex" alt="$a+2(a-b)=a+2a-2b=3a-2b\Rightarrow\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="345" height="18" />
</p><p>-<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Sweetmango77" title="User:Sweetmango77">SweetMango77</a>
</p>
