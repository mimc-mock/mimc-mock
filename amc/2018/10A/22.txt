<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/0/2d02bb2275100261109b043261e702124460a83e.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b, c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be positive integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/a/19affe477575850f51b980515bd1f725d01acc01.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a, b)=24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5ed4e93ff016de77db78741b9447e963d7084f.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(b, c)=36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="107" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/8/c984bbbd7306a2b8896f729c16ce1b74e716b988.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(c, d)=54$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ea6c564e6aa0aa6df663863ad89eaecef24a08b.png" class="latex" alt="$70&lt;\gcd(d, a)&lt;100$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="18" />. Which of the following must be a divisor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/e/10ec18258a41d74fe23af3fb4c17d2db6343159e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} \text{ 5} \qquad \textbf{(B)} \text{ 7} \qquad \textbf{(C)} \text{ 11} \qquad \textbf{(D)} \text{ 13} \qquad \textbf{(E)} \text{ 17}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="370" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The GCD information tells us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce5d3cbb9ab0a992ed503c2d4499e4f4af2f9d65.png" class="latex" alt="$36$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/c/b1cb5bdce2d06d2419066a2b55ab01d824217bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Note that we have the prime factorizations:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1ce899386c5efae4ded3e1d6aff780a9b129fb3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 24 &amp;= 2^3\cdot 3,\\ 36 &amp;= 2^2\cdot 3^2,\\ 54 &amp;= 2\cdot 3^3. \end{align*}" width="92" height="71" />
</p><p>Hence we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/7/427543a5ef1b4d7e1eeb754d1b68a915bb7eb6a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a &amp;= 2^3\cdot 3\cdot w\\ b &amp;= 2^3\cdot 3^2\cdot x\\ c &amp;= 2^2\cdot 3^3\cdot y\\ d &amp;= 2\cdot 3^3\cdot z \end{align*}" width="103" height="98" />
for some positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/485d49a1ce6105b267fa45aedcbc484ea035919c.png" class="latex" alt="$w,x,y,z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="11" />. Now if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/23144045c03f52c906e2f5a6c2507296ed68301a.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> would be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5290e655e6ee35ad311faad7ed4d4d58948581.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3\cdot 3^2$" width="44" height="15" /> which is too large, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> does not divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" />. Similarly, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/9/7a98421406b8c376272b2d2400c3066ae2d55043.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(c,d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> would be at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/1/8c1c2bae87c3514a35bea399311d5ca3cde6103a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2\cdot 3^3$" width="44" height="15" /> which is too large, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> does not divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. Therefore,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/f/0ef67de48d6cbf1e2aa30e709598ed5c5bbed23e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\gcd(a,d)=2\cdot 3\cdot \gcd(w,z)\]" width="207" height="18" />
where neither <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> nor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/7/2d79393112e28b3aa413534eac1dee83973f9472.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(w,z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" />. In other words, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/7/2d79393112e28b3aa413534eac1dee83973f9472.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(w,z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="18" /> is divisible only by primes that are at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. The only possible value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/7/7d7ec7e4dceab117b742f35ed322db3b01242386.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a,d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa94edfb9739dcc66dad90f7dd5c148f44a57e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$70$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and which fits this criterion is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/9/9890de151b94d352f0284832d18f687b8237da54.png" class="latex" alt="$78=2\cdot3\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a89edd8ecbff5fd7279880e7a5e17a28cad00bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We can say that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> 'have' <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/f/44f961ba3901b568da8e626acf6de5282a2cce95.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3 \cdot 3$" width="38" height="15" />, that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/2/7b2adb21391bc3c270f616aa907a930d9e76c7f1.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2 \cdot 3^2$" width="44" height="15" />, and that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/f/38f45fc36d742a5d9d40d7761842bfd1bc2983b6.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 \cdot 2$" width="38" height="15" />. Combining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> has (at a minimum) <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/3/ad33b62d99a240b9b91bae1fd0442255f819ffd1.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3 \cdot 3^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="17" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/f/44f961ba3901b568da8e626acf6de5282a2cce95.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3 \cdot 3$" width="38" height="15" /> (and no more powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> because otherwise <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/23144045c03f52c906e2f5a6c2507296ed68301a.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="65" height="18" /> would be different). In addition, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/d/0cd7a350988e704e03c29f16bc2381c34069c690.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 \cdot 2^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="17" />, and thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/f/38f45fc36d742a5d9d40d7761842bfd1bc2983b6.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3 \cdot 2$" width="38" height="15" /> (similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> cannot have any other powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />). We now assume the simplest scenario, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/5/5a574e8cebe5ecde394c9b3ab2bdd62bcf07c32b.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 2^3 \cdot 3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="74" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/0/ee0c6233963b1fe0a3e3c9a3fb273fbf317d3712.png" class="latex" alt="$d = 3^3 \cdot 2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="74" height="17" />. According to this base case, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76915690d9ce9dabe161c773a205094abd0cd4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a, d) = 2 \cdot 3 = 6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="159" height="20" />. We want an extra factor between the two such that this number is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa94edfb9739dcc66dad90f7dd5c148f44a57e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$70$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, and this new factor cannot be divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Checking through, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/a/c0a88e070d02424b96ce4dc88fb085e2ca3afaae.png" class="latex" alt="$6 \cdot 13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" /> is the only one that works. Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/0/de02963a78431c2da51dc09d221564b2c5393825.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />
