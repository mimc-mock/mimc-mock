<p>When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> fair standard <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />-sided dice are thrown, the probability that the sum of the numbers on the top faces is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/c/5ac603cdac4dfed9654cab1540ba3f1927ef6202.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{n}{6^{7}},\]" width="22" height="32" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is a positive integer. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/3/663fec0d21906a8e88f8ade54cbda0edc918880d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }42\qquad \textbf{(B) }49\qquad \textbf{(C) }56\qquad \textbf{(D) }63\qquad \textbf{(E) }84\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Add possibilities. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, listed below.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc42b407b8c7d85fa7bcdd7ebf7b4191cf04576e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4,1,1,1,1,1,1: 7\]" width="135" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/7/997b2b5435a7b7bfdcc7343d05cb9275f32a7e0a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3,2,1,1,1,1,1: 42\]" width="144" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/1/6714cfcffc2d3e61a7a95c562190f6c0c9025b81.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2,2,2,1,1,1,1: 35.\]" width="148" height="16" />
</p><p>Add up the possibilities: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/b/e9b11044edcc86997740ffb921e47529b9db9209.png" class="latex" alt="$35+42+7=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 84}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="177" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Rolling a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> with 7 dice can be represented with stars and bars, with 10 stars and 6 bars. Each star represents one of the dots on the dices' faces and the bars represent separation between different dice. However, we must note that each die must have at least one dot on a face, so there must already be 7 stars predetermined. We are left with 3 stars and 6 bars, which we can rearrange in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/6/d56e7856f4cac7dac3d37c356eadb2022901e8dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{9}{3}=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 84}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="125" height="53" /> ways.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_3_(overkill)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3_.28overkill.29">Solution 3 (overkill)</span></h3>
<p>We can use generating functions, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/5/275c784b05073d53da74fa56c1ca7681f83834e7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+x^2+...+x^6)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="140" height="19" /> is the function for each die. We want to find the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/b/bdb2b080e28bf22191584d4b8d20a6187c8f3192.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{10}$" width="23" height="15" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e805af9ddbeff588bb7e514772f55f8a56ba9db8.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+x^2+...+x^6)^7$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="148" height="19" />, which is the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/48524d3889ec8433cb03669176e738011df245b8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3$" width="16" height="15" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/4/974630844d19daf1576d7a8bd081ccc4feef8d1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{1-x^7}{1-x}\right)^7$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="86" height="47" />. This evaluates to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/3/043520c2a18274baab852195c8a7cec0e345236a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom{-7}{3} \cdot (-1)^3=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 84}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="197" height="53" />
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_4_(Stars_and_Bars)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4_.28Stars_and_Bars.29">Solution 4 (Stars and Bars)</span></h3>
<p>If we let each number take its minimum value of 1, we will get 7 as the minimum sum. So we can do <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to find the number of balls we need to distribute to get three more added to the minimum to get 10, so the problem is asking how many ways can you put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> balls into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" /> boxes. From there we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/b/28b2144c2ed25b08be50646528e96a4b5f934f0d.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{7+3-1}{7-1}=\binom{9}{6}=\boxed{84}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="215" height="53" />
</p>
