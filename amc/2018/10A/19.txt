<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>By Euler's Theorem, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/f/f1f179c38031ec76a0b269aa4d17a01c47662d0d.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{4} \equiv 1 \pmod {10}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="134" height="21" />, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/7/c970393cb0c502c04384537835fc70a7a5ac4518.png" class="latex" alt="$\gcd(a,10)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />. 
Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/8/2a81055808beded1fc7268acd90a0937ca202782.png" class="latex" alt="$m=11,13,17,19$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="136" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/8/7f86978068ea5c49bbbfc94656cfc94bc6d8b05d.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2000,2004,2008,2012,2016$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="248" height="16" /> work. 
</p><p>Also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/2/c52d661da68db5308b820fbdc78bfef6c5856d10.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{\text{any positive integer}}\equiv 1 \pmod {10}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="19" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/a/2ba8d286aec8d410f1e3d7f89b1480699f1d1f7e.png" class="latex" alt="$11^b=(10+1)^b=10^b+10^{b-1}1+...+10(1)+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="373" height="20" />, and the latter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df77fae4176246600bfb38a249360cad8015ab21.png" class="latex" alt="$\pmod {10}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> is clearly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/0/fa04cc089ba32d487230dfab931a567dbfb2ff48.png" class="latex" alt="$m=11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/b/d9b623efeba24d61995ea37fb893752febbc411c.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1999,2001,2002,2003,2005,...,2018$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="316" height="16" /> work (not counting multiples of 4 as we would be double counting if we did). 
</p><p>We can also note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/3/5432077c353a153a4eab3afab82871cac7ce530e.png" class="latex" alt="$19^{2a}\equiv 1 \pmod {10}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="145" height="19" /> because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/5/a95df63da7be3abccb4619874f355e8b896ee75b.png" class="latex" alt="$19^{2a}=361^{a}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="15" />, and by the same logic as why <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/2/c52d661da68db5308b820fbdc78bfef6c5856d10.png" class="latex" alt="$11^{\text{any positive integer}}\equiv 1 \pmod {10}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="246" height="19" />, we are done. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/f/44f8f8d5e5a6438ff5a29a239933e0b5f433af3a.png" class="latex" alt="$m=19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/c/8fc3e91907311cc0bedcb9ffa357d3ec7a23920e.png" class="latex" alt="$n=2002, 2006, 2010, 2014, 2018$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="248" height="16" /> work (not counting any of the aforementioned cases as that would be double counting). 
</p><p>We cannot make any more observations that add more <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e47e7fe0eef055c0ce711529516ea80b26c66a1a.png" class="latex" alt="$m^n$" width="24" height="12" /> with unit digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, hence the number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/7/e47e7fe0eef055c0ce711529516ea80b26c66a1a.png" class="latex" alt="$m^n$" width="24" height="12" /> that have units digit one is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/2/df24faf591f0b44ede401fe259902badaaeeaa0e.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot 5+1\cdot 15+1\cdot 5=40$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="189" height="14" />. And the total number of combinations of an element of the set of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and an element of the set of all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/9/919002a83621e60e9c05fccc75adab538c7bd805.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cdot 20=100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />. Hence the desired probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1f1d8875587f32ba55d179e16e2b16536c7bb96.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{40}{100}=\frac{2}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="67" height="38" />, which is answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/e/24eaa00200852cce86c55fba503842324b2e5ce8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} ~\frac{2}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="59" height="49" />. 
~vsamc
</p>
