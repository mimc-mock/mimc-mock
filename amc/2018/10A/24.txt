<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d78cd4394503dffb3e25f5cd3142ffde2c560fd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = a$" width="62" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/1/69169a29ef334e2a4b42730004c7999a2942fe83.png" class="latex" alt="$BG = x$" width="63" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/c/9dc33aeaeccbf7b4ecfa8153dd683a9cbae29aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$GC = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" />, and the length of the perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/5/6c52a41dcbd739f1d026c5d4f181438b75b76976.png" class="latex" alt="$BC$" width="28" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />. </p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>

Similarly, we find the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/7/45754fa234fa4e8757d97c3e09622970db18825d.png" class="latex" alt="$AGC$" width="42" height="13" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/c/61cb9e2df14f95ad865010002dd70ef6a9a1d500.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \cdot 10 \cdot x \cdot \sin C$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="120" height="37" /> and the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/8/1c85fe92107095e26fb3d596b9a34ff32df8b89d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2} \cdot 6x \cdot 10 \cdot \sin C$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="129" height="37" />. A ratio between these two triangles yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/1/ef13ab958240c3c8026ee6cdaf73677cd35f0430.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[ACG]}{[ABC]} = \frac{x}{6x} = \frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="140" height="43" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/0/a90d7a72ef293f6c08329a8ff633df76ffc90706.png" class="latex" alt="$[AGC] = 20$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="18" />. Now we just need to find the area of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/8/378fc67ebfe29ddad36d7bab7b5851c10738c166.png" class="latex" alt="$AFE$" width="42" height="13" /> and subtract it from the combined areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/7/6975aae6622ad6fa2d1611182399b903b33d105a.png" class="latex" alt="$[ADE]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="51" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/0/a50524dd0627c9c16ce4cd29175bedc175405e21.png" class="latex" alt="$[ACG]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="50" height="18" />, since we count it twice. Note that the angle bisector theorem also applies for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/0/db041d9c0fda44cf24b4f4ae7f02243ef2ea7f92.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ADE$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/3/6034ee931e591dfbde727185967531b5f88d6551.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AE}{AD} = \frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="38" />, so thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15f8e2e67bd0a16207455397c924e7e00dd5e6da.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{EF}{ED} = \frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="69" height="37" /> and we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/f/40f9ee1a0e8d1b44d90400d43cb8f2b94468a5f6.png" class="latex" alt="$[AFE] = \frac{1}{6} \cdot 30 = 5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="154" height="37" />, and the area outside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26e2ad8e5a82c634f3bb79ad068439df0d9df2b3.png" class="latex" alt="$FDBG$" width="58" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/0/8f0c998d61095ccbcd87bd3d7413e762c096c6da.png" class="latex" alt="$[ADE] + [AGC] - [AFE] = 30 + 20 - 5 = 45$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="359" height="18" />, and we finally find  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/4/55457f2887f98e4646a25bc2207803e1d9d3963b.png" class="latex" alt="$[FDBG] = [ABC] - 45 = 120 -45 = \boxed{75}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="332" height="23" />, and we are done. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6:_Areas">Solution 6: Areas</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/c/fec17aeac53afac69550634893242ffecc5cd8fd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(1,3)--(5,0)--cycle); draw((0,0)--(2,2.25)); draw((0.5,1.5)--(2.5,0)); label(&quot;A&quot;,(0,0),SW); label(&quot;B&quot;,(5,0),SE); label(&quot;C&quot;,(1,3),N); label(&quot;G&quot;,(2,2.25),NE); label(&quot;D&quot;,(2.5,0),S); label(&quot;E&quot;,(0.5,1.5),NW); label(&quot;3Y&quot;,(2.5,0.75),N); label(&quot;Y&quot;,(1,0.2),N); label(&quot;X&quot;,(0.5,0.5),N); label(&quot;3X&quot;,(1.25,1.75),N); [/asy]" width="252" height="172" />
Give triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58cf2c01b32821ad23584e33cc96ba86963d7961.png" class="latex" alt="$AEF$" width="42" height="13" /> area X. Then, by similarity, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/3/fa358640918fce3be3ec568697e868444c724fd0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AC}{AE} = \frac{2}{1}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="38" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/f/8af68a1f04db9f1548784f5b53a43d0f8964acbb.png" class="latex" alt="$ACG$" width="42" height="13" /> has area 4X. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/f/17f3b2773f0f367f4a5b60496d3cb309956f27dc.png" class="latex" alt="$FGCE$" width="57" height="12" /> has area 3X.
Doing the same for triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/7/7374374d51cba71d7beeb26e8184d69f43a08d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$AGB$" width="42" height="13" />, we get that triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45d262ec0040725407c96f2c9e81ff7919b0192b.png" class="latex" alt="$AFD$" width="43" height="13" /> has area Y and quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47aaa53b265db8cb1ff667ed708fa5cdbc0ec39c.png" class="latex" alt="$GFDB$" width="58" height="12" /> has area 3Y. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58cf2c01b32821ad23584e33cc96ba86963d7961.png" class="latex" alt="$AEF$" width="42" height="13" /> has the same height as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/d/45d262ec0040725407c96f2c9e81ff7919b0192b.png" class="latex" alt="$AFD$" width="43" height="13" />, the ratios of the areas is equal to the ratios of the bases. Because of the Angle Bisector Theorem, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05f20f09273b6c9e749caf8b142cfedaccc09f24.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{CG}{GB} = \frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="68" height="37" />. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee18d7a558a484b813543414ade5a7827ed70f39.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{[AEF]}{[AFD]} = \frac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="93" height="43" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58cf2c01b32821ad23584e33cc96ba86963d7961.png" class="latex" alt="$AEF$" width="42" height="13" /> has area X, we can write the equation 5X = Y and substitute 5X for Y.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/e/95e21642cc7621170addd6f802e918710adb8397.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(1,3)--(5,0)--cycle); draw((0,0)--(2,2.25)); draw((0.5,1.5)--(2.5,0)); label(&quot;A&quot;,(0,0),SW); label(&quot;B&quot;,(5,0),SE); label(&quot;C&quot;,(1,3),N); label(&quot;G&quot;,(2,2.25),NE); label(&quot;D&quot;,(2.5,0),S); label(&quot;E&quot;,(0.5,1.5),NW); label(&quot;&quot;,(2.5,0.75),N); label(&quot;&quot;,(1,0.2),N); label(&quot;F&quot;, (1, 1.5), N); label(&quot;&quot;,(0.5,1.5),N); label(&quot;&quot;,(1.25,1.75),N); [/asy]" width="252" height="172" />
Now we can solve for X by adding up all the sums. X + 3X + 5X + 15X = 120, so X = 5. Since we want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/a/47aaa53b265db8cb1ff667ed708fa5cdbc0ec39c.png" class="latex" alt="$GFDB$" width="58" height="12" />, we substitute 5 for 15X to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/f/04f0ea7107e7a767532d08bb7db4741e752b37f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{75}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="30" height="23" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/5/e55156a4008b0944ad00d5bc71bc5aa6315aabb7.png" class="latex" alt="$\sim$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" />krishkhushi09
</p>
