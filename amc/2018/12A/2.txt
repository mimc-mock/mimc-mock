<p>While exploring a cave, Carl comes across a collection of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/2/ba2e5f454758b032f3d0b84192fb609c25139815.png" class="latex" alt="$$14$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="27" height="14" /> each, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />-pound rocks worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/2/8620fd780fe6d36b9533cf660e311f0bc145e79a.png" class="latex" alt="$$11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="26" height="14" /> each, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/6/ee6e8cd66adab965db3a961cd262a4ba54e9a132.png" class="latex" alt="$$2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="17" height="14" /> each. There are at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> of each size. He can carry at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pounds. What is the maximum value, in dollars, of the rocks he can carry out of the cave?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/2/aa290196a5030acdc10c3f98109b41f3745218d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 48 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 49 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 50 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 51 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 52$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/2/9320cd65a81ef5333092a94d91e8e5ab5dd002c3.png" class="latex" alt="$$14\div5=$2.80$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="17" /> per pound, and the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />-pound rocks is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/0/620d0bf90f635ed780dd38c053c3dc5015644bf0.png" class="latex" alt="$$11\div4=$2.75$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="17" /> per pound. Clearly, Carl should not carry more than three <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks. Otherwise, he can replace some <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks with some heavier rocks, preserving the weight but increasing the total value. 
</p><p>We perform casework on the number of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks Carl can carry:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/3/9b3113bac03cd60d3bcb8210707c046e48036bbe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{c|c|c||c} &amp; &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.5ex] \boldsymbol{1}\textbf{-Pound Rocks} &amp; \boldsymbol{4}\textbf{-Pound Rocks} &amp; \boldsymbol{5}\textbf{-Pound Rocks} &amp; \textbf{Total Value} \\ \textbf{(}\boldsymbol{$2}\textbf{ Each)} &amp; \textbf{(}\boldsymbol{$11}\textbf{ Each)} &amp; \textbf{(}\boldsymbol{$14}\textbf{ Each)} &amp; \\ [0.5ex] \hline &amp; &amp; &amp; \\ [-2ex] 0 &amp; 2 &amp; 2 &amp; $50 \\  &amp; &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex] 1 &amp; 3 &amp; 1 &amp; $49 \\ &amp; &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex] 2 &amp; 4 &amp; 0 &amp; $48 \\ &amp; &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex] 3 &amp; 0 &amp; 3 &amp; $48 \end{array}\]" width="547" height="143" />
Clearly, the maximum value of the rocks Carl can carry is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/a/8da594b901be552b6abd4f551d43d0d4935dca40.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 50}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="66" height="30" /> dollars.
</p><p><u><b>Remark</b></u>
</p><p>Note that an upper bound of the total value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f750bfb085427a3a2da172a2d6659db2b0aa7844.png" class="latex" alt="$$2.80\cdot18=$50.40,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="154" height="19" /> from which we can eliminate choices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/5729ea372cb53da5f2596d6f802dbd34914d4322.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="17" />
</p><p>~Pyhm2017 (Fundamental Logic)
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM (Reconstruction)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since each rock is worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> times its weight (in pounds), the answer is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/b/c3b428486bbb64204c6cc0d1071bc89bb153d6f8.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot 18=54$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="82" height="13" /> minus the minimum number of rocks we need to make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pounds. Note that we need at least <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> rocks (two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-pound rocks and two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />-pound rocks) to make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/7/5e7a1b6098a8c98b8e4adae526aeef4b91712620.png" class="latex" alt="$18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pounds, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/f/91ffeefe561e39073d8d2f06daa8797232bd495b.png" class="latex" alt="$54-4=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 50}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="145" height="30" />
</p><p>~Kevindujin (Solution)
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM (Revision)
</p>
