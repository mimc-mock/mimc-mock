<p>For positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/4/bb4af373ca27007ad292f3aac68933ec389ee6b9.png" class="latex" alt="$m+10&lt;n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="14" />, both the mean and the median of the set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/b/dfb4201ce0db4289548675ecd1c0f511c329c07d.png" class="latex" alt="$\{m, m+4, m+10, n+1, n+2, 2n\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="281" height="18" /> are equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02dbab3601fa6cce490d54263017048f69e1a35d.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/5729f386104985bdf9d1e4ad4d3c5c8b9c530493.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}20\qquad\textbf{(B)}21\qquad\textbf{(C)}22\qquad\textbf{(D)}23\qquad\textbf{(E)}24$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="367" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The mean and median are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/f/3ff618fec2b3f9d461b341273cfffab03d55dda9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{3m+4n+17}{6}=\frac{m+n+11}{2}=n,\]" width="267" height="37" />so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/8/d584b6bddf47102ff74fc9af1ef0701c463ded07.png" class="latex" alt="$3m+17=2n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="109" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/9/3c9c6f599c84b0cb424fbaa5cfbe529912629389.png" class="latex" alt="$m+11=n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="13" />. Solving this gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/1/041ac9b87ae55bf3c7463e23c8b93cc49cd30623.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(m,n\right)=\left(5,16\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="121" height="18" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/8/768f68255ffcef1b2a07e78c687c21f8ea968b41.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n=\boxed{21}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="23" />. (trumpeter)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>This is an alternate solution if you don't want to solve using algebra. First, notice that the median <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/e/dde27890160d92dd1f8dea64c68915c29759d7e3.png" class="latex" alt="$m+10$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/3/0e3efd9b14723a92c2ae891fe27780d5f8e2b215.png" class="latex" alt="$n+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/9/d598a220a5015165eca588a9ed984fb78340e3e3.png" class="latex" alt="$n=m+11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="13" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04cfdc47110e6ae620054d7ea14adf1013e7094e.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n=2m+11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="137" height="13" />, which must be odd. This leaves two remaining options: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/a/f2a1c26ab4b1a90c116ba26758e98cf860df85b0.png" class="latex" alt="${(B) 21}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/3/ab3938d3d8363de7955893bfd9369f8b14232e18.png" class="latex" alt="${(D) 23}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />. Notice that if the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/313048c3149436615f6cd06b7f1bec83420d6a37.png" class="latex" alt="$(B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is odd, while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is even if the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/4/7b4066cd24c53cd4053f840d210f974f46e1d913.png" class="latex" alt="$(D)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="18" />. Since the average of the set is an integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, the sum of the terms must be even. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/8/b48e047e2245b5fa79edd824ab52821b7df2f163.png" class="latex" alt="$4+10+1+2+2n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="154" height="14" /> is odd by definition, so we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f58efa912a217b84bc41433f2dcaf9bc02428bfb.png" class="latex" alt="$3m+2n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" /> must also be odd, thus with a few simple calculations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is odd. Because all other answers have been eliminated, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/3/313048c3149436615f6cd06b7f1bec83420d6a37.png" class="latex" alt="$(B)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="18" /> is the only possibility left. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/8/768f68255ffcef1b2a07e78c687c21f8ea968b41.png" class="latex" alt="$m+n=\boxed{21}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="23" />. ∎ --anna0kear
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Since the median is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b29e3cb4e43dd45b0f53a6dd0fd07b0fa59537b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m+10+n+1}{2} = n \Rightarrow m+11 = n$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="279" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee1cf11d123c812644103eb9012392034b837e89.png" class="latex" alt="$m= n-11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="12" />. Plug this in for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> values to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4cec710e54788cf6272f114772bfb677af51d4f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{7n-16}{6} = n \Rightarrow 7n-16 = 6n \Rightarrow n= 16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="313" height="37" />. Plug it back in to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/a/11af515956d2fa847b77d1ae0e81258a08a55aa4.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 5$" width="48" height="12" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/3/a230a0933e05ce75778d411a8cba266d0437cee5.png" class="latex" alt="$16 + 5 = \boxed{21}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="103" height="23" />.
</p><p>~ iron
</p>
