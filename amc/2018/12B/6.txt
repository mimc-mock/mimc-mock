<p>Suppose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> cans of soda can be purchased from a vending machine for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> quarters. Which of the following expressions describes the number of cans of soda that can be purchased for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> dollars, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> dollar is worth <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> quarters?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/0/1d0fadfa074d39cf250d4f69f089e5c032c212a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \frac{4DQ}{S} \qquad \textbf{(B) } \frac{4DS}{Q} \qquad \textbf{(C) } \frac{4Q}{DS} \qquad \textbf{(D) } \frac{DQ}{4S} \qquad \textbf{(E) } \frac{DS}{4Q}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="491" height="43" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Each can of soda costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/9/5f9fcd2f008b86d5d425c11d59f44355698ea64e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac QS$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="39" /> quarters, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/7/2b7af4b330f543ba02348ec4d6da1d865906a029.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{Q}{4S}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="27" height="39" /> dollars. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> dollars can purchase <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/3/cd3f0157eac1100ee7ee50175ee11dffb99a1486.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{D}{\left(\tfrac{Q}{4S}\right)}=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } \frac{4DS}{Q}}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="153" height="54" /> cans of soda.
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> is in the unit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/c/ceceb737db1a708f50cc219b04adf43aeda4587a.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{can}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="9" /> On the other hand, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> are both in the unit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/3/993ec0551cb0c018c2bd5bfd2096611644f07e61.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{cost}.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="36" height="12" />
</p><p>The units of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/a/dda5a8ca17248c2042ed8ddb667f0b253e9a76ab.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)},\textbf{(B)},\textbf{(C)},\textbf{(D)},$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="147" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e41e3cd48cee2c6866cfbb4386a8e99d3482f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="26" height="17" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/f/dff8396adab1c07a2b15c6aa0ea2be505648415b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\text{cost}^2}{\text{can}},\text{can},\frac{1}{\text{can}},\frac{\text{cost}^2}{\text{can}},$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="165" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b167f3d2916b881836600505e75f55f4acb0e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{can},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="12" /> respectively. Since the answer is in the unit of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/1/6b167f3d2916b881836600505e75f55f4acb0e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{can},$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="12" /> we eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/2/7e2bc9d2434c5ebd28ce9319839133fb8d899bf6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)},\textbf{(C)},$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="73" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4feb78447beef6c93631b0912fb24e7936b6dad3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="17" /> Moreover, it is clear that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> dollars can purchase more than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82dba5f31862f450980b6e6f8e8e9ae95c88dce1.png" class="latex" alt="$S=\frac{DS}{4Q}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="70" height="43" /> cans of soda, so we eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/5729ea372cb53da5f2596d6f802dbd34914d4322.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(E)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="17" />
</p><p>Finally, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/6/9660aa0d5141585851b8f91b59c7051df5a979f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } \frac{4DS}{Q}}.$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="94" height="54" />
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
