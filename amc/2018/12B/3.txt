<p>A line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> intersects a line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> at the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/0/4a082f6124d87a5160fb9fc5a9558dfd9ac531a6.png" class="latex" alt="$(40,30)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />. What is the distance between the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercepts of these two lines? 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7cfa1a71cd650182f877b8f7ec24b1e017a6508.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 5 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 10 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 20 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 25 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Using point slope form, we get the equations <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/7/be760afa1adc674bbfb772f6c740bfdbf97800bd.png" class="latex" alt="$y-30 = 6(x-40)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e243aeee6356218d5622fb2c672d6c6e22639.png" class="latex" alt="$y-30 = 2(x-40)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="146" height="18" />. Simplifying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/b/b0b73bebe3dd43b644aba5d932f9118ba29d2f3b.png" class="latex" alt="$6x-y=210$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="102" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/a/f4a70b04da404d8090fe80167299e4895d276537.png" class="latex" alt="$2x-y=50$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="93" height="16" />. Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> in both equations and solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> gives the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercepts: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/a/a2a543e51db1a1e80fa299ef0d12a297846626a7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=35$" width="52" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/f/08f7cfaaaac737ada39fc1450e9a5319e01a9038.png" class="latex" alt="$x=25$" width="52" height="12" />, respectively. Thus the distance between them is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/8811363aecd0a8468aac4d4a13aae21d65df9b24.png" class="latex" alt="$35-25=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="149" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>In order for the line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> to travel "up" <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> units (from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />), it must have traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/6/b163b024a04c9f4a491b1a871ee893d32b24b364.png" class="latex" alt="$30/2=15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> units to the right. Thus, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercept is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/9/f791fb9808903d2fd2acdbe2038eac0f111278a2.png" class="latex" alt="$x=40-15=25$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="135" height="13" />. As for the line with slope <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, in order for it to travel "up" <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> units it must have traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/4/af4cc341cdb2642ae62c01b040dadcd35c1245b8.png" class="latex" alt="$30/6=5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" /> units to the right. Thus its <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercept is at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/9/529a23bd2f84e9f35b4fa14a1e4bfd6536b5a228.png" class="latex" alt="$x=40-5=35$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="126" height="13" />. Then the distance between them is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/1/8811363aecd0a8468aac4d4a13aae21d65df9b24.png" class="latex" alt="$35-25=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 10}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="149" height="30" />.
</p>
