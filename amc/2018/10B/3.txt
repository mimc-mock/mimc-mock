<p>In the expression <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/8/30841790d6df05e01324bf04a96c40b291b8da30.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\underline{\qquad}\times\underline{\qquad}\right)+\left(\underline{\qquad}\times\underline{\qquad}\right)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="226" height="18" /> each blank is to be filled in with one of the digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/1/1614f2a4d8d2f0e92b1b76f39417aacfbaba9e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$1,2,3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4054552638cf9f29e857a1306bd3ec31df3c19b6.png" class="latex" alt="$4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> with each digit being used once. How many different values can be obtained?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/f/76facc6cd1c4d16c1234590efc60a2081426f903.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }2 \qquad \textbf{(B) }3\qquad \textbf{(C) }4 \qquad \textbf{(D) }6 \qquad \textbf{(E) }24 \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="362" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1_-_Combinations">Solution 1 - Combinations</span></h2>
<p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/2/54216aeffa0fa278199cd3ceeb64cc8400c4a8cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{4}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" /> ways to choose the pairs, and we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> ways for the values to be switched so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/1/2b10c2126a9784802617cc8f6a217794f92f2875.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{6}{2}=\boxed{3.}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We have four available numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/f/6ff3584203afeaeba1a976ba69d5d362d20328af.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 2, 3, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" />. Because different permutations do not matter because they are all addition and multiplication, if we put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> on the first space, it is obvious there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/3/433d742232f7bf3512c11eb996630ff5ead3641f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="20" height="23" /> possible outcomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/b/afb27f1814a41725b350272ae48ec33ad501a551.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 3, 4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da866442e69c040daf44260b4728d37e98de313.png" class="latex" alt="$4!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="13" /> ways to arrange the numbers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/5/2c5cc1e7f294a032ab77989acd0f30cbf9a011e0.png" class="latex" alt="$2!2!2!$" width="40" height="13" /> overcounts per way due to commutativity. Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/0/3302cc3a407cffa6441c034f37527281f74d8118.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{4!}{2!2!2!}=\boxed{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="91" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
