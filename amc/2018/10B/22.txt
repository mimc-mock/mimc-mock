<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The Pythagorean Inequality tells us that in an obtuse triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/5/3c5379f5d32c8aa53b931a3d02777cb823667588.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{2} + b^{2} &lt; c^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="16" />. The triangle inequality tells us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/5/6e53875273f32921a1a89ab9db9712dce14a12c8.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b &gt; c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="72" height="14" />. So, we have two inequalities:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/685ce0f4c81b985ebdf3b12a304a7164f6ef7fdb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + y^2 &lt; 1\]" width="89" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/1/9b146881400080038376bdbc10aaa2b1a4d5c7e3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x + y &gt; 1\]" width="74" height="15" />
The first equation is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/d/4cd0f9f9869b39e79527f9010293adf7bfe5210f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac14$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="11" height="37" /> of a circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, and the second equation is a line from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/6457ca2dcd40dc21d9aa0db18c66bbac9af4d899.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/4/754e946d1a160f767f0f61cbd9460401c2d1c17f.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
