<p>Sam drove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/7/367f6b15d47102fd8fdfc1bafed72c1daeadb35a.png" class="latex" alt="$96$" width="17" height="12" /> miles in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcfee7bd8a5e15e5f6b7881c138de43651572a9.png" class="latex" alt="$90$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes. His average speed during the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> mph (miles per hour), and his average speed during the second <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> mph. What was his average speed, in mph, during the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e56fc002f05221867837a6a6f9c92b45e3f63097.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 64 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 65 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 66 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 67 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 68$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that Sam's average speed during the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> mph.
</p><p>Recall that a half hour is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes. Therefore, Sam drove <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/4/4e4eb18fa02c1864093dc32cc782b8d84b63d3ff.png" class="latex" alt="$60\cdot0.5=30$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="14" /> miles during the first half hour, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/a/09a9161b4696040343314b2c4a2c94a7955c06ac.png" class="latex" alt="$65\cdot0.5=32.5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" /> miles during the second half hour, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/d/31d81024b51d8c47fa0bab23b0d2a37b2fa44ded.png" class="latex" alt="$x\cdot0.5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" /> miles during the last half hour. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/a/30a75d07f8a489262b5adf18429097191d1c33bb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 30+32.5+x\cdot0.5&amp;=96 \\ x\cdot0.5&amp;=33.5 \\ x&amp;=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 67}. \end{align*}" width="234" height="78" />
~Haha0201 ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Suppose that Sam's average speed during the last <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> mph.
</p><p>Note that Sam's average speed during the entire trip was <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/f/e2f5a44f917d8d1f39f8d5a309bb72a557abb262.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{96}{3/2}=64$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="76" height="43" /> mph. Since Sam drove at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> mph, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> mph, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> mph for the same duration (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> minutes each), his average speed during the entire trip was the average of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> mph, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/9/339baf6df71777af266e6259546917b0742caf2d.png" class="latex" alt="$65$" width="17" height="12" /> mph, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> mph. We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe975d0fb5abc9bab6d3f0277e2e7ed3a1f66a7d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{60+65+x}{3}&amp;=64 \\ 60+65+x&amp;=192 \\ x&amp;=\boxed{\textbf{(D) } 67}. \end{align*}" width="186" height="99" />
~coolmath_2018 ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
