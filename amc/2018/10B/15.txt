<p>A closed box with a square base is to be wrapped with a square sheet of wrapping paper. The box is centered on the wrapping paper with the vertices of the base lying on the midlines of the square sheet of paper, as shown in the figure on the left. The four corners of the wrapping paper are to be folded up over the sides and brought together to meet at the center of the top of the box, point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> in the figure on the right. The box has base length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> and height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />. What is the area of the sheet of wrapping paper?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/9/db9c5fcb99a9df45b2598f29922e31ed17e25f0d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(270pt); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)); filldraw(((3,3)--(-3,3)--(-3,-3)--(3,-3)--cycle),lightgrey); dot((-3,3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-3,3),NW); draw((1,3)--(-3,-1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((-1,3)--(3,-1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((-1,-3)--(3,1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((1,-3)--(-3,1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((0,2)--(2,0)--(0,-2)--(-2,0)--cycle,linewidth(.5)); draw((0,3)--(0,-3),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); draw((3,0)--(-3,0),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); label(&#39;$w$&#39;,(-1,-1),SW); label(&#39;$w$&#39;,(1,-1),SE); draw((4.5,0)--(6.5,2)--(8.5,0)--(6.5,-2)--cycle); draw((4.5,0)--(8.5,0)); draw((6.5,2)--(6.5,-2)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(6.5,0),NW); dot((6.5,0)); [/asy]" width="448" height="245" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/1/a81c089887cb7c4fa61cc6f504c74725f2bef6a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2(w+h)^2 \qquad \textbf{(B) } \frac{(w+h)^2}2 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 2w^2+4wh \qquad \textbf{(D) } 2w^2 \qquad \textbf{(E) } w^2h$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="603" height="39" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Consider one-quarter of the image (the wrapping paper is divided up into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> congruent squares). The length of each dotted line is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />. The area of the rectangle that is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/1/8d131cf4df7bc1b3ad13a217944f06384c81e5f1.png" class="latex" alt="$wh$" width="23" height="12" />. The combined figure of the two triangles with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is a square with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> as its diagonal. Using the Pythagorean Theorem, each side of this square is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/0/e20cec10480b321d1f565fa423c53f3c62b8dc85.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{h}{\sqrt2}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="30" height="44" />. Thus, the area is the side length squared which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c7004e708c5690f4d7f5dce0afd3406b343fda51.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{h^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="39" />. Similarly, the combined figure of the two triangles with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee4b825a2e36ae093ed7be5e4851ef453b34914.png" class="latex" alt="$w$" width="13" height="8" /> is a square with area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7ced80a6b1174094eeea98f5bbdb32272e680b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{w^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="23" height="39" />. Adding all of these together, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d62992ef8f0feef233c2d0c3dd64762a6e04c0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{w^2}{2} + \frac{h^2}{2} + wh$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="115" height="39" />. Since we have four of these areas in the entire wrapping paper, we multiply this by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/3/633b1a4c78c9b68f0408d813e6a14de65225a499.png" class="latex" alt="$4\left(\frac{w^2}{2} + \frac{h^2}{2} + wh\right) = 2\left(w^2 + h^2 + 2wh\right) = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 2(w+h)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="472" height="45" />.
</p><p>The diagram for this solution is shown below:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/1/6313e89b05fec983b4ad73633f620440235eb4f0.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Edited by MRENTHUSIASM */ size(180pt); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)); fill(((0,3)--(-3,3)--(-3,0)--(0,0)--cycle),lightgrey); dot((-3,3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-3,3),NW); draw((-3,0)--(-3,3)--(0,3),linewidth(.5)); draw((0,2)--(-1,3)--(-3,1)--(-2,0),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((0,2)--(-2,0),linewidth(.5)); draw((0,3)--(0,0),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); draw((0,0)--(-3,0),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(-1,1),NW); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(-2,2),SE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-2.5,0.5),NE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-0.5,2.5),SW); label(&quot;$\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-2,3),N); label(&quot;$\frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-0.5,3),N); label(&quot;$\frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(0,2.5),E); label(&quot;$\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(0,1),E); label(&quot;$\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-1,0),S); label(&quot;$\frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-2.5,0),S); label(&quot;$\frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-3,0.5),W); label(&quot;$\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}}$&quot;,(-3,2),W); label(&quot;$wh$&quot;,(-1.5,1.5),red); label(&quot;$\frac{w^2}{4}$&quot;,centroid((-3,3),(-1,3),(-3,1)),red); label(&quot;$\frac{w^2}{4}$&quot;,centroid((0,0),(-2,0),(0,2)),red); label(&quot;$\frac{h^2}{4}$&quot;,centroid((-3,0),(-2,0),(-3,1)),red); label(&quot;$\frac{h^2}{4}$&quot;,centroid((0,3),(-1,3),(0,2)),red); [/asy]" width="295" height="302" />
~Hydroquantum (Solution)
</p><p>~MRENTHUSIASM (Diagram)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The sheet of paper is made out of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> squares, as shown in the diagram in Solution 1. Each square has a side length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/c/81c543028e5e70f70b0b15da4a8ee79d7a0234a7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="77" height="42" />, which we get from the Pythagorean Theorem (a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/7/a47ddd3ed6bdfa6ed83bd3b19783381c616c38d0.png" class="latex" alt="$45^\circ\text{-}45^\circ\text{-}90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="15" /> triangle's legs is the hypotenuse divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/4/114e91200cd4c857d233c7fed6cbe7451f3a82fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />). Thus, to find the area of the entire paper, we square our side length and multiply by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. So, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/8/61808b03abb0c5bb7840fc55742a0c7a899ef9a2.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot\left(\frac{w}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{h}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) } 2(w+h)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="284" height="48" />.
</p><p>~IronicNinja
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The sheet of paper is made out of the surface area of the box plus the sum of the four yellow triangles, as shown below. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/5/a2598ec705885ea931cfa1930a03d01064d39953.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] /* Edited by MRENTHUSIASM */ size(180pt); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)); fill(((3,3)--(-3,3)--(-3,-3)--(3,-3)--cycle),lightgrey); fill((2,0)--(3,1)--(3,-1)--cycle,yellow); fill((-2,0)--(-3,1)--(-3,-1)--cycle,yellow); fill((0,2)--(1,3)--(-1,3)--cycle,yellow); fill((0,-2)--(1,-3)--(-1,-3)--cycle,yellow); draw(((3,3)--(-3,3)--(-3,-3)--(3,-3)--cycle),linewidth(.5)); dot((-3,3)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,(-3,3),NW); draw((1,3)--(-3,-1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((-1,3)--(3,-1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((-1,-3)--(3,1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((1,-3)--(-3,1),dashed+linewidth(.5)); draw((0,2)--(2,0)--(0,-2)--(-2,0)--cycle,linewidth(.5)); draw((0,3)--(0,-3),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); draw((3,0)--(-3,0),linetype(&quot;2.5 2.5&quot;)+linewidth(.5)); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(-1,-1),SW); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(1,-1),SE); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(1,1),NE); label(&quot;$w$&quot;,(-1,1),NW); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(2.5,0.5),NW); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(2.5,-0.5),SW); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-2.5,0.5),NE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-2.5,-0.5),SE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(0.5,2.5),SE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-0.5,2.5),SW); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(0.5,-2.5),NE); label(&quot;$h$&quot;,(-0.5,-2.5),NW); [/asy]" width="302" height="298" />
</p><p>The surface area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/3/6a38bd1af504cc4799dbff0500ade6117a1dec57.png" class="latex" alt="$2w^2 + 2wh + 2wh$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="16" /> which equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/e/fce291436630269239f219f67de040792949c38a.png" class="latex" alt="$2w^2 + 4wh$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="16" />.The four triangles each have a height and a base of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />, so they each have an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c7004e708c5690f4d7f5dce0afd3406b343fda51.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{h^2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="39" />. There are four of them, so multiplied by four is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/2/e72e3d20d1805918b7f3d099ead12c82f27c88fc.png" class="latex" alt="$2h^2$" width="25" height="15" />. Together, paper's area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/789eeecedff435d7e48b9285e39256b950c71436.png" class="latex" alt="$2w^2 + 4wh + 2h^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="133" height="16" />. This can be factored and written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/f/8df6947b72f0823c32cf1faaffbe5256f3a92935.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 2(w+h)^2} \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="124" height="31" />.
</p><p>~Yee2121 (Solution)
