<p>All the roots of the polynomial <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/e/9ee95a55deaeb1ca730505826c05a30c12ac5d1a.png" class="latex" alt="$z^6-10z^5+Az^4+Bz^3+Cz^2+Dz+16$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="320" height="16" /> are positive integers, possibly repeated. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/4/6c4e5132eecb88c702573ad20a95a3f535ab7fb7.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }{-}88 \qquad \textbf{(B) }{-}80 \qquad \textbf{(C) }{-}64 \qquad \textbf{(D) }{-}41\qquad \textbf{(E) }{-}40$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="471" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By Vieta's formulas, the sum of the six roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and the product of the six roots is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. By inspection, we see the roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4b9576f9af1f99f51d809f9f296bed620eff800.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 1, 2, 2, 2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="85" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, so the function is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/3/6936a83c4774aa430b18a7a17694bbd06b84d41f.png" class="latex" alt="$(z-1)^2(z-2)^4=(z^2-2z+1)(z^4-8z^3+24z^2-32z+16)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="475" height="19" />. Therefore, calculating just the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/704e9969bd090004be46cb1cbb380cd1bc2525c0.png" class="latex" alt="$z^3$" width="15" height="15" /> terms, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/0/cb0693a543f52238376e065d88e565cd5fade8b5.png" class="latex" alt="$B = -32 - 48 - 8 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }{-}88}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="248" height="30" />.
</p><p>~JHawk0224
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Using the same method as Solution 1, we find that the roots are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/d/acde51185b656cea19b4517f725e4ae23d00dac3.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 2, 2, 2, 1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> is the negation of the 3rd symmetric sum of the roots. Using casework on the number of 1's in each of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/6/aa605a0d3846a7bc6afee06929ad71b004679b39.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom {6}{3} = 20$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="79" height="53" /> products <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/4/884660660cd0fb1c41f608668b60f116f7e01b49.png" class="latex" alt="$r_a \cdot r_b \cdot r_c,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="11" /> we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/4/f24718ee669124e49dff8e9563ca68a3e55125aa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[B= - \left(\binom {4}{3} \binom {2}{0} \cdot 2^{3} + \binom {4}{2} \binom{2}{1} \cdot 2^{2} \cdot 1 + \binom {4}{1} \binom {2}{2} \cdot 2 \right) = -\left(32+48+8 \right) = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }{-}88}.\]" width="707" height="53" />
~ike.chen
</p>
