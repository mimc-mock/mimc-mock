<p>Suppose that on a parabola with vertex <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d58aa29201da09d8e620f8698e3a37547f6b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$V$" width="14" height="12" /> and a focus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> there exists a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/f/d6fc95ce649c7f42e9a52546763ee1dbf684bb69.png" class="latex" alt="$AF=20$" width="69" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/a/eaa44eb36d4a6061a593fb31c52508c7e4e356ad.png" class="latex" alt="$AV=21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="69" height="13" />. What is the sum of all possible values of the length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/d/dfd3b742254d53e1871a74e65b06b06d305d89d2.png" class="latex" alt="$FV?$" width="37" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/60043e0a55f5371e9930dde816d1104bd6bd1314.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }13 \qquad \textbf{(B) }\frac{40}3 \qquad \textbf{(C) }\frac{41}3 \qquad \textbf{(D) }14\qquad \textbf{(E) }\frac{43}3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="409" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> be the directrix of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17e179805931e8a5004cd6ac6b3ad3a9b5020876.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal P$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="14" />; recall that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/e/17e179805931e8a5004cd6ac6b3ad3a9b5020876.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal P$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="14" /> is the set of points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> such that the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/5/c25436083d2a36da09136e1d9966e871ff0423f3.png" class="latex" alt="$TF$" width="27" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/9866e3a998d628ba0941eb4fea0666ac391d149a.png" class="latex" alt="$Q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> be the orthogonal projections of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/c/63c17c295325f731666c7d74952b563a01e00fcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\ell$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="7" height="13" />, and further let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> be the orthogonal projections of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d58aa29201da09d8e620f8698e3a37547f6b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$V$" width="14" height="12" /> onto <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/6/7563031eab26ad18658cf2f6a8e4948861bb9013.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="16" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/2/4529a7feb059b07b78146d34a3024ffee8ddf79a.png" class="latex" alt="$AF &lt; AV$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="79" height="13" />, there are two possible configurations which may arise, and they are shown below.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/9/bc94cb3aa05d8949f3622223c250dc10bd152d18.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] 	import olympiad; 	size(230); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)+fontsize(11pt)); real d = 1.1, edge = 2.5, Ax = 1.6; real f(real x) { return 1/(4*d) * x * x; } pair V = origin, F = (0,d), la = (-edge,-d), lb = (edge,-d), A = (Ax, f(Ax)); pair P = foot(F,la,lb), Q = foot(A,la,lb), X = foot(F,A,Q), Y = foot(V,A,Q); draw(P--F--A--V--Y^^F--X--Q^^rightanglemark(F,P,la,4)^^rightanglemark(A,Q,lb,4)^^rightanglemark(A,X,F,4)^^rightanglemark(A,Y,V,4),red); draw(graph(f,-2.5,2.5)); draw(la -- lb); dot(F^^A^^V); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,NW); label(&quot;$V$&quot;,V,SW); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,dir(F--A)); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,S,red); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,Q,S,red); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,X,E,red); label(&quot;$Y$&quot;,Y,E,red); [/asy]" width="385" height="218" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/d/b8d2f8b78bd8f950d5ff6fb3ab95036ca940d10c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] 	import olympiad; 	size(200); defaultpen(linewidth(0.8)+fontsize(11pt)); real d = 0.7, edge = 2.5, Ax = 1.9; real f(real x) { return 1/(4*d) * x * x; } pair V = origin, F = (0,d), la = (-edge,-d), lb = (edge,-d), A = (Ax, f(Ax)); pair P = foot(F,la,lb), Q = foot(A,la,lb), X = foot(F,A,Q), Y = foot(V,A,Q); draw(Q--A--F--P^^F--X^^A--V--Y^^rightanglemark(F,P,la,4)^^rightanglemark(A,Q,lb,4)^^rightanglemark(A,X,F,4)^^rightanglemark(A,Y,V,4),red); draw(la -- lb); draw(graph(f,-2.5,2.5)); dot(F^^A^^V); label(&quot;$F$&quot;,F,NW); label(&quot;$V$&quot;,V,SW); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,dir(F--A)); label(&quot;$P$&quot;,P,S,red); label(&quot;$Q$&quot;,Q,S,red); label(&quot;$X$&quot;,X,E,red); label(&quot;$Y$&quot;,Y,E,red); [/asy]" width="335" height="222" />
Set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67cc95380e1eeb560ce11e4fb0a9cfdf45c172b2.png" class="latex" alt="$d = FV$" width="62" height="12" />, which by the definition of a parabola also equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/9/0397e5108d281f9136f1ae009b76d99bacd8ce71.png" class="latex" alt="$VP$" width="28" height="12" />. Then as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/7/fc7e92793a9ac53c34c65daed5934be9a4240fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$AQ = AF = 20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="16" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a53d6d766d6419dc6d2b2e5cb3fc03f5c2e3d8d6.png" class="latex" alt="$AY = 20 - d$" width="102" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/8/0987372755756c6ee15fe74124feca99eaba7b6f.png" class="latex" alt="$AX = |20 - 2d|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="121" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/9/5c99a823846634597565041a563a2efb59baccd9.png" class="latex" alt="$FXYV$" width="59" height="12" /> is a rectangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64f75639cea423dc110b031ca27a050f4788305d.png" class="latex" alt="$FX = VY$" width="83" height="12" />, so by Pythagorean Theorem on triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/9/259bb6257a22298feaf00be011460470587deeb8.png" class="latex" alt="$AFX$" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/1/2a1261c78413cd8b4248120f1458a48c907f6b76.png" class="latex" alt="$AVY$" width="42" height="13" />,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/a/61a87ce497e756a48473054820b6e29e3b4ac1e5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 21^2 - (20 - d)^2 &amp;= AV^2 - AY^2 = VY^2\\ &amp;= FX^2 = AF^2 - AX^2 = 20^2 - (20 - 2d)^2 \end{align*}" width="451" height="47" />
This equation simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbf35ca43b28f99c169558be3fa144300aedb41b.png" class="latex" alt="$3d^2 - 40d + 41 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="16" />, which has solutions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f4dbc54dddd61e8cf8c18f94c017a464f88ecf.png" class="latex" alt="$d = \tfrac{20\pm\sqrt{277}}3$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="94" height="26" />. Both values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> work - the smaller solution with the bottom configuration and the larger solution with the top configuration - and so the requested answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/f/9afa96edf985d9256f5d8d56e3ea1872c6206bd2.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ \tfrac{40}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="63" height="32" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/f/74f4008768812f46db4b252c9e69cf98c5faaa4b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal{P}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="13" height="14" /> be the parabola, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/d/12d58aa29201da09d8e620f8698e3a37547f6b4a.png" class="latex" alt="$V$" width="14" height="12" /> be the origin, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> axis, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/2415b0020fd3ac014cbbeafaea4f543837d36935.png" class="latex" alt="$d=FV$" width="62" height="12" />. The equation of the parabola is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/e/f7e5fb151d773127a88ac4d2e49a442431ef5435.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}=4dy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="18" />. If the coordinates of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b4ea7280b88addac484cf73f439617a9498996.png" class="latex" alt="$(p, q),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="44" height="18" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/5/ba5f9ee97c29f2a36367ce5dd1c1c33a7ae3aaee.png" class="latex" alt="$p^{2}+q^{2}=441$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="18" /> since the distance from the origin to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25d31faf472ddb4c698a14522fe8125c06f8dd8.png" class="latex" alt="$21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. Note also that the parabola is the set of all points equidistant from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> and a line known as its directrix, which in this case is a horizontal line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> units below the origin. Since the distance from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to its directrix is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/a/cba4bccbcf02ccd89a4907b85c1b3a3e02c9a7cd.png" class="latex" alt="$AF,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="30" height="16" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> units above this line and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/0/8d0e49ce313652c1203ffd89e42f0f514afcc0bd.png" class="latex" alt="$q=20-d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="83" height="16" />. Substituting for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/c/a2c892e99a873875055587a463b71e934e745e4b.png" class="latex" alt="$4d(20-d)+(20-d)^{2}=441$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="226" height="19" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/a/85a303b607c4b00292180c7653428a7ac3980d79.png" class="latex" alt="$(20-d)(20+3d)=441,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="192" height="18" /> which simplifies to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/f/c3fd90368167d64c593ff893ccb2fb2280515b77.png" class="latex" alt="$3d^{2}-40d+41=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="16" />. Therefore the sum of all possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/a/8ca2c31ed6616e55fd8ec47ca4d132efc93f5ed1.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{-b}{a}=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~\tfrac{40}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="107" height="32" /> by Viète's Formulas.
</p><p>~sugar_rush
</p>
