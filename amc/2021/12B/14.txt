<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> be a rectangle and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/b/ebbe20733d24579adb2707e9dc19f5c01568a351.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DM}$" width="35" height="15" /> be a segment perpendicular to the plane of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/b/ebbe20733d24579adb2707e9dc19f5c01568a351.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DM}$" width="35" height="15" /> has integer length, and the lengths of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/9/b790cd7e534dde7dfd1e0ab8708c7f80c63cb50e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MA},\overline{MC},$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="79" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/0/e1067e157113377bc8b04c4f96d0eb3caa0f7464.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{MB}$" width="34" height="15" /> are consecutive odd positive integers (in this order). What is the volume of pyramid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/0/b102937e28583fb3eb313ef5a4eae2a6b7a72c2d.png" class="latex" alt="$MABCD?$" width="86" height="13" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/7/237babaa44af634b687e4fcff85d584d40b30ccb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }24\sqrt5 \qquad \textbf{(B) }60 \qquad \textbf{(C) }28\sqrt5\qquad \textbf{(D) }66 \qquad \textbf{(E) }8\sqrt{70}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="472" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7cc298534231e615462c23c9b2d563dc7e5726b6.png" class="latex" alt="$MA=a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="69" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/255cb7d077ce80eebe8b3f33d69837d1e6a8f78d.png" class="latex" alt="$MD=d.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="76" height="14" /> It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/f/80f42ac43386308064bb6ee2617229af815f5220.png" class="latex" alt="$MC=a+2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="101" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/c/c3ca71cab1894044079b34337984504a720a7d65.png" class="latex" alt="$MB=a+4.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="106" height="16" />
</p><p>As shown below, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/6/b26dffdaf7146800f04cef35e039a6e984fcb162.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MAD$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/b/6fb3fc3869e0de5c347520d1596fe011cfb42b47.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MBC$" width="64" height="13" /> are both right triangles.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/f/81f73763c4c24a6da41ac2ad657eaeef36466d15.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(300); import graph3; import solids;  currentprojection=orthographic((0.5,-0.25,-0.5)); triple A, B, C, D, M; A = (-2sqrt(10),0,0); B = (-2sqrt(10),-6sqrt(2),0); C = (0,-6sqrt(2),0); D = (0,0,0); M = (0,0,3); draw(surface(M--A--D--cycle),yellow); draw(surface(M--B--C--cycle),yellow); draw(D--A--B--C^^A--M^^B--M^^C--M^^D--M); draw(C--D,dashed); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^M,linewidth(4.5)); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,2*dir(A-B)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,2*dir(B-A)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,2*dir(C-D)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,2*dir(D-C)); label(&quot;$M$&quot;,M,2*dir((1,1,0))); label(&quot;$a$&quot;,midpoint(M--A),2*dir((-1,-1,0)),red); label(&quot;$d$&quot;,midpoint(M--D),2*dir((1,1,0)),red); label(&quot;$a+2$&quot;,midpoint(M--C),2*dir((1,-1,0)),red); label(&quot;$a+4$&quot;,midpoint(M--B),2*dir((-1,1,0)),red); [/asy]" width="488" height="292" />
By the Pythagorean Theorem, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/6/b968981b68fe2bd10356f37f2ff7e65405cd44c8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{alignat*}{6} AD^2 &amp;= MA^2 - MD^2 &amp;&amp;= a^2 - d^2, \\ BC^2 &amp;= MB^2 - MC^2 &amp;&amp;= (a+4)^2 - (a+2)^2. \end{alignat*}" width="341" height="47" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/4/314bfa1fb251cfe31dbff4a462217c7e4ebce5b6.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=BC$" width="82" height="13" /> in rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/5/42501e1c7100f340da9bd452bf942219ac6b131b.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" /> we equate the expressions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/1/161b162afcb4f7b6a3447e0dc08b3813bdfa113f.png" class="latex" alt="$AD^2$" width="35" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/0/a2030d3443c094b8eb430c7c16b829498fd30d23.png" class="latex" alt="$BC^2,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="40" height="18" /> then rearrange and factor:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/e/61e079414d242e5e0a21622b27d9be7ecf88d8ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a^2 - d^2 &amp;= (a+4)^2 - (a+2)^2 \\ a^2 - d^2 &amp;= 4a + 12 \\ a^2 - 4a - d^2 &amp;= 12 \\ (a-2)^2 - d^2 &amp;= 16 \\ (a+d-2)(a-d-2) &amp;= 16. \end{align*}" width="342" height="128" />
As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/6/f263792fc64acc05e0e47d5de6c0230e2bb27c58.png" class="latex" alt="$a+d-2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/a/c4aea7ff3de9a1dc8763e65f1090b46b7c2772a2.png" class="latex" alt="$a-d-2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="74" height="16" /> have the same parity, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/6/1c6834cc4e36753770d091cee5c17f63debda127.png" class="latex" alt="$a+d-2=8$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="108" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/598d561b2df9b1b310cf4a8f568bf4d1df999a5a.png" class="latex" alt="$a-d-2=2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="113" height="18" /> from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/4946bf7790fc2f082582d337bdfc861da69bf139.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,d)=(7,3).$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="113" height="20" />
</p><p>Applying the Pythagorean Theorem to right <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/6/b26dffdaf7146800f04cef35e039a6e984fcb162.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MAD$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and right <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/5/155fede0237cbcae9cb567591fea7cce1bca8e07.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle MCD,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" /> we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/c/dbc265e277af12c2104cfe2885a22265840d2cfd.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=2\sqrt{10}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="98" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/5/a35e2b91e37b504c33512defcfb11b10f74eb425.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=6\sqrt2,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="23" /> respectively.
</p><p>Let the brackets denote areas. Together, the volume of pyramid <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/4/46473945f3d28b9b1df6ba93795c6c2d261331eb.png" class="latex" alt="$MABCD$" width="77" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/2/832ad1e5bfe11606c247f00dfbba4c6924300e6d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac13\cdot [ABCD]\cdot MD = \frac13\cdot (AD\cdot CD)\cdot MD = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }24\sqrt5}.\]" width="443" height="37" />
~Lopkiloinm ~MRENTHUSIASM
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/d/07daaeb34175de9e89ff0ceb2668cfd950c92814.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=b$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/3/353b86bbdedcf9e4abc7b7d6552d94451bb9bfb4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=a$" width="63" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/1/d31fb34078ad18b2f0e65d3ee7e485ee89effc38.png" class="latex" alt="$MD=x$" width="69" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/1/311d9149f8fcb7ef2f20223e104f83f911027753.png" class="latex" alt="$MC=t$" width="64" height="12" />. It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/9/369a91546611abc61b81d302dd8cbfd6ce085774.png" class="latex" alt="$MA=t-2$" width="95" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/6/e4651fb38d93b28d65b065fd9d9a1995880e11ec.png" class="latex" alt="$MB=t+2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="96" height="14" />.
</p><p>We have three equations:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/f/cefd238910d1fadeedc5037da5f209c9ac81ed1a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} a^2 + x^2 &amp;= t^2, \\ a^2 + b^2 + x^2 &amp;= t^2 + 4t + 4, \\ b^2 + x^2 &amp;= t^2 - 4t + 4. \end{align*}" width="205" height="72" />
Substituting the first and third equations into the second equation, we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/2/ee268bdfef57a2e4b50e31e5d2d4aa70ca7a4e0d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} t^2 - 8t - x^2 &amp;= 0 \\ (t-4)^2 - x^2 &amp;= 16 \\ (t-4-x)(t-4+x) &amp;= 16. \end{align*}" width="213" height="72" />
Therefore, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/2/262741ba3c6ae57bba79c9427d258c845171f01d.png" class="latex" alt="$t = 9$" width="39" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/0/690ef55a607beaf2389d1ee102df817721bc2392.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 3$" width="43" height="12" />.
</p><p>Solving for other values, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/1/0c18f8bf71b0abaa250fe1d11088ccdac7758dbf.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 2\sqrt{10}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f85056b89441a8cbc778845246eb90c7cf22d9.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 6\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="18" />.
The volume is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/a/2badfefaede7b4172e5372d64ef282b4bb4ec17c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{3} abx = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }24\sqrt5}.\]" width="156" height="37" />
</p><p>~jamess2022 (burntTacos)
</p>
