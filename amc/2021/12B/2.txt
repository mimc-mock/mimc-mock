<p>At a math contest, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" /> students are wearing blue shirts, and another <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> students are wearing yellow shirts. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/5/17531b756bc19a5c07c7f1ab8ce1cddf4bb4097c.png" class="latex" alt="$132$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> students are assigned into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d40f3652c1962e98ce050a88c04ed37997c67df.png" class="latex" alt="$66$" width="17" height="12" /> pairs. In exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" /> of these pairs, both students are wearing blue shirts. In how many pairs are both students wearing yellow shirts?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/c/bec6e250ef83ee4f73c41837bccb788498b5a3f6.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~23 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~32 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~37 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~41 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~64$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/b/5abef9fdb0235b2ede2639538b5bd76711e058fa.png" class="latex" alt="$46$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> students paired with a blue partner. The other <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> students wearing blue shirts must each be paired with a partner wearing a shirt of the opposite color. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/e/05e228ac5bee7b9ffb60cacf50f3f2c7c31f8038.png" class="latex" alt="$64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> students remaining. Therefore the requested number of pairs is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/1/b818a9f77fc1aed0671724d853766367a5e3c78d.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{64}{2}=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~32}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="105" height="28" /> ~Punxsutawney Phil
</p>
