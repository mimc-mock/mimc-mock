<p>How many values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/2/d523c1c35268dc2f2a2d6ea8be772659897b3a21.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;\theta\le 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="15" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/8/b58210a8828d746350c9d6235739f48869ca7cf6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1-3\sin\theta+5\cos3\theta = 0?\]" width="197" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/4/f54d10ea6a8b5f9a2d48edf5f0e8f458eff218e2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }2 \qquad \textbf{(B) }4 \qquad \textbf{(C) }5\qquad \textbf{(D) }6 \qquad \textbf{(E) }8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="353" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We rearrange to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/0/6403161c0a12179aebce70cb4619ca393d783ee5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5\cos3\theta = 3\sin\theta-1.\]" width="162" height="13" />
We can graph two functions in this case: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/c/04c00f1ee6cd9b60e50b3b52ecfc6f3c84bdb0cf.png" class="latex" alt="$y=5\cos{3x}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/6/686439407c1a6113389695391daf0e1f2f0593f6.png" class="latex" alt="$y=3\sin{x} -1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="115" height="17" />.
Using transformation of functions, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/6/b06090ad2ad42598f8eb9d3318f12238a2dc6241.png" class="latex" alt="$5\cos{3x}$" width="58" height="12" /> is just a cosine function with amplitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and period <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/1/3a1f43766bf2f5511df35ab6d4a0b84dbcb6dadb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2\pi}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="22" height="37" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/8/5b8d95602401e71129ec7d769fca0f103b2c4c7f.png" class="latex" alt="$3\sin{x} -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="78" height="13" /> is just a sine function with amplitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and shifted <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> unit downward:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac0756ac2aff279bb152a3f7a2f32a2fc7319a66.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph;  size(400,200,IgnoreAspect);  real Sin(real t) {return 3*sin(t) - 1;} real Cos(real t) {return 5*cos(3*t);}  draw(graph(Sin,0, 2pi),red,&quot;$3\sin{x} -1 $&quot;); draw(graph(Cos,0, 2pi),blue,&quot;$5\cos{3x}$&quot;);  xaxis(&quot;$x$&quot;,BottomTop,LeftTicks); yaxis(&quot;$y$&quot;,LeftRight,RightTicks(trailingzero));  add(legend(),point(E),20E,UnFill); [/asy]" width="668" height="335" />
So, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/9/c79d327a2ed1b158c28ca33e5d5a88f2b4d037b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" /> solutions. 
</p><p>~Jamess2022 (burntTacos)
</p>
