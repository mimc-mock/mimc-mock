<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> be an isoceles trapezoid having parallel bases <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084c0b09963f4bb0d8de1a4aa0b0ec3c189b95fc.png" class="latex" alt="$AB&gt;CD.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="86" height="13" /> Line segments from a point inside <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> to the vertices divide the trapezoid into four triangles whose areas are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/c/0fc1edcf20b3841d5e15b61e831325707dd22fb8.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> starting with the triangle with base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/0/ce0bfc603f9a044e308b1a1fdadca917e81ad2dd.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CD}$" width="30" height="15" /> and moving clockwise as shown in the diagram below. What is the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/d/d9d6b1222b0f8d0f90aebb879ae3ae420d33d96c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{CD}?$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="42" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/c/29c42141e2c79d3a16294b55a9be52158c6f9681.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(100); pair A=(-1, 0), B=(1, 0), C=(0.3, 0.9), D=(-0.3, 0.9), P=(0.2, 0.5), E=(0.1, 0.75), F=(0.4, 0.5), G=(0.15, 0.2), H=(-0.3, 0.5);  draw(A--B--C--D--cycle, black);  draw(A--P, black); draw(B--P, black); draw(C--P, black); draw(D--P, black); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,(-1,0)); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,(1,0)); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,(1,-0)); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,(-1,0)); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,E,(0,0)); label(&quot;$3$&quot;,F,(0,0)); label(&quot;$4$&quot;,G,(0,0)); label(&quot;$5$&quot;,H,(0,0)); dot(A^^B^^C^^D^^P); [/asy]" width="382" height="172" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/4/9d4ef6a00f9125c5c10b183be562b516bc372651.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\: 3\qquad\textbf{(B)}\: 2+\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\: 1+\sqrt{6}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\: 2\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\: 3\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="483" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Without the loss of generality, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/26394ab97bace60c3c2285deb5a7c93ac72631b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal T$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="15" height="14" /> have vertices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/d/cad09ae5e841d3b2397dd033a1d0fa97d3b6033e.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = r$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/e/84ec113f1d481e4948a428d080e4d61819582e9b.png" class="latex" alt="$CD = s$" width="62" height="12" />. Also denote by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> the point in the interior of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/26394ab97bace60c3c2285deb5a7c93ac72631b1.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathcal T$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="15" height="14" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" /> be the feet of the perpendiculars from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" />, respectively. Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/c/3fccd3766b839b3f81b17abc9402a362be2a4ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$PX = \tfrac 8r$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="64" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/e/89e422a77f9efcba0101369c5fc84f85b28cd5d5.png" class="latex" alt="$PY = \tfrac 4s$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="62" height="21" />. Now using the formula for the area of a trapezoid yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/0/9e00a7563e000cf1b9723fcc209e423a9ad51a4e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[14 = \frac12\cdot XY\cdot (AB+CD) = \frac12\left(\frac 8r + \frac 4s\right)(r+s) = 6 + 2\cdot\frac rs + 4\cdot\frac sr.\]" width="477" height="43" />
Thus, the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad7b75112937d643c859bb321328f1b6da39e6ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\rho&#160;:= \tfrac rs$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="47" height="18" /> satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/6/836cf63547d3a68536678fcb1cf38d828921e3ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\rho + 2\rho^{-1} = 4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="18" />; solving yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/a/d6a4a4cbbdf73962b78f20ae7539f2e5fa29ecc8.png" class="latex" alt="$\rho = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\: 2+\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="135" height="35" />.
</p><p>(Observe that the given areas of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are irrelevant to the ratio <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/3/8b39cf815494607b5b43de92a75c8f080735feab.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AB}{CD}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="32" height="38" />.)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/f/69fc4b9ed6d75d026557e7b06c2ed483c6ed0eda.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="15" /> be the bottom base, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/b/93b7aa611365e135f58aa09d52043ecd34855fe4.png" class="latex" alt="$b_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="15" /> be the top base, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/d/32d78aae1741ff43c6f40e2077f1ab67900cca2c.png" class="latex" alt="$h_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> be the height of the bottom triangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec781a0e6836ea5e030a44927477cfd5560fecb.png" class="latex" alt="$h_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="15" /> be the height of the top triangle.
Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/c/14c25d6b00187856e9a7587bcb6f5e249e294e92.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1h_1 = 8, b_2h_2 = 4, (b_1+b_2)(h_1+h_2) = 28,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="334" height="18" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a5f149dcdeb523c8c2da6a6ddaefe3017ff43c.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1h_2 + b_2h_1 = 16.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="134" height="15" /> Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59d2917160f9485dfc234024b4fb99d03754f6ff.png" class="latex" alt="$b_2 = 1, h_2 = 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="112" height="16" /> so we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/5/555cae87efc856f48a433fda4ef047ee46fa16f8.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1h_1 = 8, 4b_1+h_1 = 16.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="185" height="16" />
This gives us a quadratic in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f3732bf01d3d0d8050de8b3dc09e61e8fdc2ba1b.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="16" /> ie. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/9/5b9f0748f66ba60aa8a1f5fabdf8c10a55a61a1d.png" class="latex" alt="$4b_1^2+8=16b_1,$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="117" height="19" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/f/a6fa116117d3a9162800b909f0e83a2eebbea53b.png" class="latex" alt="$b_1 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\: 2+\sqrt{2}}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="146" height="35" />
</p><p>- Solution by MathAwesome123, added by ccx09
</p>
