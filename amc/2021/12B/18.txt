</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Using the fact <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/5/bf523c8d600288ee789f84da657762a9854eea0a.png" class="latex" alt="$z\bar{z}=|z|^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="19" />, the equation rewrites itself as
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf0f120e072d806d305b75379a28d1c14f05bdf5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 12z\bar{z}&amp;=2(z+2)(\bar{z}+2)+(z^2+1)(\bar{z}^2+1)+31 \\ -12z\bar{z}+2z\bar{z}+4(z+\bar{z})+8+z^2\bar{z}^2+(z^2+\bar{z}^2)+32&amp;=0 \\ \left((z^2+2z\bar{z}+\bar{z}^2)+4(z+\bar{z})+4\right)+\left(z^2\bar{z}^2-12z\bar{z}+36\right)&amp;=0 \\ (z+\bar{z}+2)^2+(z\bar{z}-6)^2&amp;=0. \end{align*}" width="750" height="103" />
As the two quantities in the parentheses are real, both quantities must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/f/70f6fd554500c22e4c7912a371aa29d849535500.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z+\frac6z=z+\bar{z}=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }-2}.\]" width="215" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/3/5a3f569273c74a546e81f7866bf8a1a8c5e0c535.png" class="latex" alt="$x = z + \frac{6}{z}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="77" height="37" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/0/6103f834f8932c811c9d10fdbbc6c0b718bf3f56.png" class="latex" alt="$z = \frac{x \pm \sqrt{x^2-24}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="142" height="40" />. From the answer choicesï¼Œ we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is real and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/b/e8bc714e810675ad1945776bc21d45df2ceae94f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2&lt;24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/c/09c64668274e9db8756b85351dc786c3aa13404a.png" class="latex" alt="$z = \frac{x \pm i\sqrt{24-x^2}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="148" height="40" />. Then we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/c/7cc3b1bc73810f7a8b0c5fbf3c8ae26f08d0633d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|z|^2 = 6\]" width="57" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/2398d967349e36745df62a0343145ddc978b7b4d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|z+2|^2 = (\frac{x}{2} + 2)^2 + \frac{24-x^2}{4} = 2x+10\]" width="315" height="39" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe59895d0266991ab824b9e40a4c1e1bdfe4e8f9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[|z^2+1|^2 = |xz -6 +1|^2 = \left(\frac{x^2}{2}-5\right)^2 + \frac{x^2(24-x^2)}{4} = x^2 +25\]" width="477" height="46" />
Plugging the above back to the original equation, we have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/d/f4df5ee76c43014b63d6fdfa6651aa77d99f04c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[12*6 = 2(2x+10) + x^2 + 25 + 31\]" width="270" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/0/460dae85e4071005c8891e7f3bcf7c19eb2528ca.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x+2)^2 = 0\]" width="94" height="19" />
So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/c/82c0e37b22abf0bf3b8cd909c1e661027a78f591.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }-2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="113" height="28" />.
