<p>Three balls are randomly and independantly tossed into bins numbered with the positive integers so that for each ball, the probability that it is tossed into bin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/b/65baa1cd247bf018d2dc0ed082fa82a3bf50b343.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{-i}$" width="24" height="15" /> for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/9/a896979d2354a21dd1dc842062e75f7cb0b602f3.png" class="latex" alt="$i=1,2,3,....$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="100" height="16" /> More than one ball is allowed in each bin. The probability that the balls end up evenly spaced in distinct bins is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f28853fb56a9e11580d445ffe40c2c239f0be66.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac pq,$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="17" height="36" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/1/0615acc3725de21025457e7d6f7694dab8e2f758.png" class="latex" alt="$q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="8" height="11" /> are relatively prime positive integers. (For example, the balls are evenly spaced if they are tossed into bins <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/d/c6dd7470632000b66b0544b7a456b09a11341865.png" class="latex" alt="$3,17,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b833a70e5b34465a7e1cc445231d569f21b81da4.png" class="latex" alt="$10.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" />) What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/d/22dd6d9587b2fe28d6b8fd7d058a3e8326763199.png" class="latex" alt="$p+q?$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/f/47f5714954b1b9acbd418fb8ae6c99a6da038b8b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }55 \qquad \textbf{(B) }56 \qquad \textbf{(C) }57\qquad \textbf{(D) }58 \qquad \textbf{(E) }59$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>"Evenly spaced" just means the bins form an arithmetic sequence.
</p><p>Suppose the middle bin in the sequence is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/b/d4baf6d4b1cfa69b68b3204b5e2eba0c5755fe20.png" class="latex" alt="$x-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" /> different possibilities for the first bin, and these two bins uniquely determine the final bin. Now, the probability that these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> bins are chosen is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/0/6709054b4e896d7c95f3d9a0c5163277dca8a378.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot 2^{-3x} = 6\cdot \frac{1}{8^x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="122" height="37" />, so the probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the middle bin is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/5/3b53c998a5c40235f55044ac6075c16841307226.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot\frac{x-1}{8^x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="66" height="37" />. Then, we want the sum
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/e/e4e8a5fe7a2883542996d2dcfb9c853821ba4491.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 6\sum_{x=2}^{\infty}\frac{x-1}{8^x} &amp;= \frac{6}{8}\left[\frac{1}{8} + \frac{2}{8^2} + \frac{3}{8^3}\cdots\right]\\ &amp;= \frac34\left[\left(\frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{8^2} + \frac{1}{8^3}+\cdots \right) + \left(\frac{1}{8^2} + \frac{1}{8^3} + \frac{1}{8^4} + \cdots \right) + \cdots\right]\\ &amp;= \frac34\left[\frac17\cdot \left(1 + \frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{8^2} + \frac{1}{8^3} + \cdots \right)\right]\\ &amp;= \frac34\cdot \frac{8}{49}\\ &amp;= \frac{6}{49} \end{align*}" width="559" height="234" />
The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906b05b5219d98a0f982a40499beea6cb28a1b24.png" class="latex" alt="$6+49=\boxed{\textbf{(A) }55}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="142" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As in solution 1, note that "evenly spaced" means the bins are in arithmetic sequence. We let the first bin be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and the common difference be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Further note that each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/7/347230b1823d4a8f8b932dc6667582c654e800da.png" class="latex" alt="$(a, d)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> pair uniquely determines a set of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> bins. 
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/d/06d5dd57a404f3493262280c2b27fce443b1550c.png" class="latex" alt="$a\geq1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" /> because the leftmost bin in the sequence can be any bin, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/385694583d5b99d2ad7b9180f00a1503aefc698a.png" class="latex" alt="$d\geq1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />, because the bins must be distinct.
</p><p>This gives us the following sum for the probability:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/c/57cd55b3a81dd8db425ebea2b046defc8d3c4211.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 6 \sum_{a=1}^{\infty} \sum_{d=1}^{\infty} 2^{-3a-3d} &amp;= 6 \sum_{a=1}^{\infty} \sum_{d=1}^{\infty} 2^{-3a} \cdot 2^{-3d} \\ &amp;= 6 \left( \sum_{a=1}^{\infty} 2^{-3a} \right) \left( \sum_{d=1}^{\infty} 2^{-3d} \right) \\ &amp;= 6 \left( \sum_{a=1}^{\infty} 8^{-a} \right) \left( \sum_{d=1}^{\infty} 8^{-d} \right) \\ &amp;= 6 \left( \frac{1}{7} \right) \left( \frac{1}{7} \right) \\ &amp;= \frac{6}{49} .\end{align*}" width="344" height="263" />
Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/b/e0b9bef3f4b1e0eeed09a4fde695923377a61c6a.png" class="latex" alt="$6 + 49 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }55}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="141" height="30" />.
</p><p>-Darren Yao
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>This is a slightly messier variant of solution 2. If the first ball is in bin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> and the second ball is in bin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/248ee41acf29d8dac6d244350970db2cb67e3e94.png" class="latex" alt="$j&gt;i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="16" />, then the third ball is in bin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/9/a29d66807625f6c6d2f8a0d1b03d56c80ac91ebc.png" class="latex" alt="$2j-i$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="45" height="16" />. Thus the probability is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/b/f6bbb8f1a7137427644bacd793cdfa55dd8d7412.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} 6\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}\sum_{j=i+1}^\infty2^{-i}2^{-j}2^{-2j+i}&amp;=6\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}\sum_{j=i+1}^\infty2^{-3j}\\ &amp;=6\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}\left(\frac{2^{-3(i+1)}}{1-\tfrac{1}{8}}\right)\\ &amp;=6\sum_{i=1}^\infty\frac{8}{7}\cdot2^{-3}\cdot2^{-3i}\\ &amp;=\frac{6}{7}\sum_{i=1}^\infty2^{-3i}\\ &amp;=\frac{6}{7}\cdot\frac{2^{-3}}{1-\tfrac18}\\ &amp;=\frac{6}{49}. \end{align*}" width="342" height="320" />
Therefore the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/b/e0b9bef3f4b1e0eeed09a4fde695923377a61c6a.png" class="latex" alt="$6 + 49 = \boxed{\textbf{(A) }55}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="141" height="30" />.
</p>
