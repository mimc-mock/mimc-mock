<p>Hiram's algebra notes are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> pages long and are printed on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> sheets of paper; the first sheet contains pages <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, the second sheet contains pages <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and so on. One day he leaves his notes on the table before leaving for lunch, and his roommate decides to borrow some pages from the middle of the notes. When Hiram comes back, he discovers that his roommate has taken a consecutive set of sheets from the notes and that the average (mean) of the page numbers on all remaining sheets is exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. How many sheets were borrowed?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Suppose the roommate took sheets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, or equivalently, page numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f1daa9ab402c89fd76285ac43ae812ff2e5b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$2a-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="12" /> through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" />. Because there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a73329c881449a5a8ef0b8f5a93b1cbdbf925a.png" class="latex" alt="$(2b-2a+2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" /> numbers taken, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/d/7edf4c1d413e4935f8a29164a745534d47e68b62.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{(2a-1+2b)(2b-2a+2)}{2}+19(50-(2b-2a+2))=\frac{50\cdot51}{2} \implies (2a+2b-39)(b-a+1)=\frac{50\cdot13}{2}=25\cdot13.\]" width="813" height="38" /> The first possible solution that comes to mind is if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/f/50f412de169a75ed6ceaed7efb2055549931d1f9.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+2b-39=25, b-a+1=13 \implies a+b=32, b-a=12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="483" height="16" />, which indeed works, giving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/2/062c09b50f8ef1439fedd41be0529c43dd35aae1.png" class="latex" alt="$b=22$" width="49" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/b/32b95a5a1bf6f50913e72972a09d742814553958.png" class="latex" alt="$a=10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/1/a615fde8dfe4496f66a5d93e0f8f8592cfbb5411.png" class="latex" alt="$22-10+1=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~13}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="179" height="30" />.
</p><p>~Lcz

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Suppose the smallest page number borrowed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/9/c99a6aaebc17474bd09ea71d84d4e769a0662b55.png" class="latex" alt="$k,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> pages are borrowed. It follows that the largest page number borrowed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/9/cb9ab1e41e19249c8a4acb58c124d2f194ce8b0e.png" class="latex" alt="$k+n-1.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="14" />
</p><p>We have the following preconditions:
</p>
<ol style="margin-left: 1.5em;">
  <li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> pages are borrowed means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f275b1f33e3c37ece0e41964f0bcb806e95b46b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" /> sheets are borrowed, from which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> must be even.</li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> must be odd, as the smallest page number borrowed is on the right side (odd-numbered).</li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/8/b88c744d5bb3aa7bacbd0d7e131174265343abf7.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots+50=\frac{51(50)}{2}=1275.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="298" height="38" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
  </p><li>The sum of the page numbers borrowed is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/2/4326d881c284f30872311e49d4acabfb3d8e5baf.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(2k+n-1)n}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="116" height="38" /></li><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p></ol>
<p>Together, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/2/3d26522fa2317946c575f464a5e5b36a6a9f1dcc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} \frac{1275-\frac{(2k+n-1)n}{2}}{50-n}&amp;=19 \\ 1275-\frac{(2k+n-1)n}{2}&amp;=19(50-n) \\ 2550-(2k+n-1)n&amp;=38(50-n) \\ 2550-(2k+n-1)n&amp;=1900-38n \\ 650&amp;=(2k+n-39)n. \end{align*}" width="315" height="164" />
The factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/0/12060badf8be82c1d7ae05f240e571d68f73ddfa.png" class="latex" alt="$650$" width="26" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/6/0c6464b7ebe6888c74dfdbd191b263bf05f96827.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1,2,5,10,13,25,26,50,65,130,325,650.\]" width="308" height="16" /> Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is even, we only have a few cases to consider:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b301ae313eb0e7f35385b2c3cfbdab7973327746.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\begin{array}{c|c|c} &amp; &amp; \\ [-2.25ex]  \boldsymbol{n} &amp; \boldsymbol{2k+n-39} &amp; \boldsymbol{k} \\ [0.5ex] \hline  &amp; &amp; \\ [-2ex]  2 &amp; 325 &amp; 181 \\    10 &amp; 65 &amp; 47 \\  26 &amp; 25 &amp; 19 \\  50 &amp; 13 &amp; 1 \\ 130 &amp; 5 &amp; -43 \\ 650 &amp; 1 &amp; -305 \\ \end{array}\]" width="225" height="153" />
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a946c5f17b28dfd7aeceada1761fa35b4575656.png" class="latex" alt="$1\leq k \leq 49,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="16" /> only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/2/a72535da18da8b69b52ae272e4b9839616bcb29f.png" class="latex" alt="$k=47,19,1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" /> are possible:
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/0/5905a5a343d631aaa9e954bda666b2774f77d221.png" class="latex" alt="$k=47,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" /> then there will not be sufficient pages when we take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> pages out starting from page <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/d/c8d06fc910703a003655e44dbcd4f3626ee6d02f.png" class="latex" alt="$47.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="12" /></li></ul>

<p>Therefore, the only possibility is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/b/b9be58d7954461182340af2d6b0b7c0e04787031.png" class="latex" alt="$k=19.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="59" height="14" /> We conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/e/4ced7365b6456073ff6355ebe8bbe90cf81eb3c8.png" class="latex" alt="$n=26$" width="52" height="12" /> pages, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/2/1a2d909c94d28bbd2f6822eb1bafe1a79e70c8ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac n2=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~13}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="33" /> sheets, are borrowed.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/c/dfc16c8051ab4476939cda5798e0118d92c647a2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2nk=2(25-n)(25.5-19)=13(25-n) \implies 13 | n \text{ or } 13 | k\]" width="465" height="18" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/0/640f80d18689f6725dd9c3d76dd5500fc596aad1.png" class="latex" alt="$n, k &lt; 25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="16" /> we have either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/608f4cbd198d2af6bf20b99fc5e126469e09ec84.png" class="latex" alt="$n=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/b/8dbc00a4fb76de2d335a58e6276ef757db6fb3c9.png" class="latex" alt="$k=13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="13" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> be the number of consecutive sheets Hiram’s roommate borrows, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the number of sheets preceding the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> borrowed sheets (i.e. if the friend borrows sheets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/7/8271d4342b38d2ab38bb626ffad8b9dae00b5e2d.png" class="latex" alt="$c=3$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d65baaf9671227780dd04ab952911036aa5c868.png" class="latex" alt="$b=2$" width="40" height="12" />).
</p><p>The sum of the page numbers up till <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> sheets is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/e/41e07e1824e653323b9b462acc306dcd894f639d.png" class="latex" alt="$1+2+3+\cdots + 2b=\frac{2b\cdot(2b+1)}{2} = b(2b+1)$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="371" height="40" />.
The last page number of the borrowed sheets would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/6/32688df2896754d3c8ca0e595c717f9c47c10745.png" class="latex" alt="$2(b+c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />. Therefore, the sum of the remaining page numbers of the sheets after the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> borrowed sheets would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/1/3910389369150a63a5195affca01550f52088802.png" class="latex" alt="$2(b+c)+1 + 2(b+c)+2+\cdots+50$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="293" height="20" />.
</p><p>The total number of page numbers after the borrow would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/a/65addf2e0f171dbf472df78d78b84cabbdd450f4.png" class="latex" alt="$50-2c$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="59" height="15" />.
</p><p>Thus the average of the page numbers after the borrow would be:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/437d3fdb78c468302a73894097f5f6eb42465abb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{b(2b+1)+ 2(b+c)+1 + 2(b+c)+2+\cdots+50}{50-2c} =19.\]" width="431" height="38" />
By the arithmetic series formula, this turns out to be:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18fdd008c82b1f8f620fe6bd443ca2c6737fbf92.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{b(2b+1)+ \frac{(2(b+c)+1+50)\cdot(50-2c-2b)}{2}}{50-2c} =19\]" width="303" height="43" />
because in the changed sum, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" /> numbers minus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/6/9f6072f36e4577a20084d5e518bf7e2f2068ab3d.png" class="latex" alt="$2c$" width="17" height="12" /> borrowed numbers and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" /> numbers from the first <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> sheets.
</p><p>This simplifies to 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/0/590ecd1c116d44d7e93d163540f1c9a1288b85d8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{b(2b+1)+ (2(b+c)+51)\cdot(25-c-b)}{50-2c} =19 \implies 19(50-2c)= b(2b+1)+ (2b+2c+51)\cdot(25-c-b).\]" width="768" height="38" />
Noticing that some terms will cancel, we expand, leading to:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/3/6e3806b1fccef48a25ad0cf19f829f262818184b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[950-38c=2b^2+b+50b-2bc-2b^2+50c-2c^2-2bc+1275-51c-51b\]" width="503" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/c/2acb0b63613e27b01205ce09996e16a4f090d1c9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\implies 950-38c=1275-4bc-c-2c^2 \implies 2c^2+4b-37c=325.\]" width="472" height="17" />
Factoring, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd9c73175f4c1928139b5b49849153a31248c206.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[c(2c+4b-37)=325.\]" width="172" height="18" />
The prime factorization of 325 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/8/55837dbd866aeb30e146c536d8b8f03c5be6c21a.png" class="latex" alt="$5^2\cdot13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="15" />. Recall that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/5/4a52ab962c8037375634eb7241939dbdd2b93b27.png" class="latex" alt="$0\leq c \leq 25$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> could be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>We can rule out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/6/6368d6e5e41a96cb14f765eb5d56fbe31360b5e8.png" class="latex" alt="$c=25$" width="49" height="12" /> since Hiram would have no paper left over, so the average of the page numbers he has would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. We can now plug in the other answers for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> and we if we get a valid answer for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/e/3de790952196c00c8a6d583d82be8dc4b1eb6107.png" class="latex" alt="$c=1 \implies 4b-35=325 \implies 4b=360 \implies b=90$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="419" height="16" />. Since Hiram only has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> sheets, this is clearly wrong and we can rule out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/4/0e4cc644ec8a1c3623c13ed3d14904096a0d87b9.png" class="latex" alt="$c=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57e22d634f556d4775f5449eb67ab1eaeb6be863.png" class="latex" alt="$c=5 \implies 10+4b-37=65 \implies 4b = 92 \implies b=23$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="441" height="16" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/b/c9bbeb7caa1770d1d3cb842655f27e245b08188f.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/9265f5dd6dade78136a6be158ace46bf7c0f8ce3.png" class="latex" alt="$28$" width="17" height="12" /> but we have only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> sheets, this is also implausible so we can rule out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/f/24f7150d029fa128fa37d225f668174382d6219b.png" class="latex" alt="$c=5$" width="40" height="12" />.</li></ul>
<p>Finally, just to make sure, we test <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/1/0c1d4442e0969c8a7ddac69c89b3fb6a3617165c.png" class="latex" alt="$c=13 \implies 26+4b-37=25 \implies 4b=36 \implies b=9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="441" height="16" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is an integer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/2/b220180a8a46aea12c6768e7304976b8c3cf85c5.png" class="latex" alt="$b+c=22$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="79" height="14" />, so everything checks out. The number of consecutive sheets borrowed by Hiram’s friend is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/7/df7028d29d3bff2b408b3d99656174919576e3f1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~13}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="97" height="30" />.
</p><p>~KingRavi
</p>
