What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/b/53bf1afcb274a3bb84338109fffec8a1f1c02008.png" class="latex" alt="$m + n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/0/8b0934660dda855c0527654652e261f6d803d722.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~18\qquad\textbf{(B)} ~27\qquad\textbf{(C)} ~36\qquad\textbf{(D)} ~45\qquad\textbf{(E)} ~54$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>In order to attack this problem, we can use casework on the sign of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/2/e526fbac5ee18e20a7293e9cb9c317989b31988a.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="50" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/8/8482f288a99d173ac59b710ea0aac8599f737a4f.png" class="latex" alt="$|x+y|$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="54" height="20" />.
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/5/a55fa53d0c46e3f52a84a38325a9d50da4487a5e.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|=x-y, |x+y|=x+y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="245" height="18" />
</p><p>Substituting and simplifying, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/c/3ac3db15cc5b4b3cee2b38dc29750e7733bb7e08.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-6x+y^2=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="18" />, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d8e0da30f17b5c652d9e4242b78d98080199882.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-3)^2+y^2=3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />, which gives us a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/4/9e480a6f22c4da5088c9cb25c607a77d30d24c8e.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|=y-x, |x+y|=x+y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="245" height="18" />
</p><p>Substituting and simplifying again, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/1/861bc14ee645853c93c912dfcd98b220c030e108.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2-6y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="18" />, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/103b65a8d00d52833b26c5115e3442a72bd82cbe.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+(y-3)^2=3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />. This gives us a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/b/b3b8eb8e093d1d1c3834d880a7b448b3fa76eeab.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />.
</p><p>Case 3: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/f/13f5f981542a77c2e3eb9521b89d293a7631ebe5.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|=x-y, |x+y|=-x-y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="259" height="18" />
</p><p>Doing the same process as before, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/f/5ff82b0a18b89eaffd703874df0b34f6277f3323.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2+6y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="18" />, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/e/a3eae003c89660da09c455ec136da79175eddbc2.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+(y+3)^2=3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />. This gives us a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/3/073525bcf565b508e59df21b9761303709eb10ef.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />.
</p><p>Case 4: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/2/8a203c9d227029260831c330b5274a1c9046920e.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|=y-x, |x+y|=-x-y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="259" height="18" />
</p><p>One last time: we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/c/f6c4c777db4705a077fd2eaa27c262c08bf01332.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2+6x=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="131" height="18" />, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/f/aafc7cc8fbbaf6620e000fc4e1583d938783e80b.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+3)^2+y^2=3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="142" height="19" />. This gives us a circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/d/e4de5746f4268398948dbb8ae29c897c05f062b8.png" class="latex" alt="$(-3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />.
</p><p>After combining all the cases and drawing them on the Cartesian Plane, this is what the diagram looks like:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/5/6352083edc8b0551fe5673e3d75e6debba348a74.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] size(10cm);  Label f;  f.p=fontsize(7); xaxis(-8,8,Ticks(f, 1.0));  yaxis(-8,8,Ticks(f, 1.0));  draw(arc((-3,0),3,90,270) -- cycle, gray); draw(arc((0,3),3,0,180) -- cycle, gray); draw(arc((3,0),3,-90,90) -- cycle, gray); draw(arc((0,-3),3,-180,0) -- cycle, gray); draw((-3,3)--(3,3)--(3,-3)--(-3,-3)--cycle, grey); [/asy]" width="475" height="475" />
Now, the area of the shaded region is just a square with side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> with four semicircles of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.
The area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c86ffea8ae65f218d65925bc948f66808934e9bc.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot6+4\cdot \frac{9\pi}{2} = 36+18\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="193" height="37" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/d/fad35d38a5edb79b71c330864e1655ddce42ab17.png" class="latex" alt="$36+18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/b/92bb6d107da506cf652da16951543a3654daf88b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }54}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>A somewhat faster variant of solution 1 is to use a bit of symmetry in order to show that the remaining three cases are identical to Case 1 in the above solution, up to rotations by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> about the origin. This allows us to quickly sketch the region after solving Case 1.
</p><p>Upon simplifying Case 1, we obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/c/c6c3d0570f2799a9aa41d9ec1228372dc49bc55a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-3)^2 + y^2 = 3^2$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="145" height="21" /> which is a circle of radius 3 centered at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. We remark that only the points on the semicircle where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/5762417993745557550961acfc269a13d62709e7.png" class="latex" alt="$x \ge 3$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="43" height="15" /> work here, since Case 1 assumes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/1/971166db658c64e91afbd59ae17fd758fde71ac4.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/5/0f50aac3527b29cdf18916914c4b09162a0c7ecf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="75" height="16" />. Next, we observe that an ordered pair is a solution to the given equation if and only if any of its <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> rotations about the origin is a solution. This follows as the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/7/ed76a468719105e206793c453a3b944d56c409b1.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2-3(|x-y|+|x+y|)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="231" height="21" /> is invariant to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> rotations, since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/7/1c71397a41761fa3ae4bc83119c38cd495b29586.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" /> simply represents the square of the distance to the origin (which is unchanged upon rotation), and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/b/c1bf355a6c240f90c683b7d362e46c316c0814f6.png" class="latex" alt="$|x-y|+|x+y|$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="129" height="20" /> is the sum of the distances to the lines <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/1/741a74d86df899c10724dc2f80638f5e7f599194.png" class="latex" alt="$y=x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="44" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f08809818fd50ee3fc13182a3112bc2bd28dccb.png" class="latex" alt="$y=-x$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="11" />, multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/4/2c4dc1409208ff6a3039f4871d85c16763334983.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" /> (also unchanged upon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7dfa8a2a90d521b2b03f5b82a1fb9f85dce88c.png" class="latex" alt="$90^{\circ}$" width="24" height="13" /> rotation).
</p><p>By the above observation, we can quickly sketch the remainder of the region, and the area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/b/92bb6d107da506cf652da16951543a3654daf88b.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }54}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" /> as above.
</p><p>~scrabbler94
