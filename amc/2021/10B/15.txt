<p>The real number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> satisfies the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85f6a1b39cb7d6f92d538a281b16ff07111dfd16.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\frac{1}{x} = \sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/6/bb6ef493b1823aa6364653a6dd63e8a846b82f68.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{11}-7x^{7}+x^3?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="121" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/3/dd35d1b9267519dd511a3a9d53cc4b4fc11a76a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~-1 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~0 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~1 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~2 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~\sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/4/234619b97d92775064c1eeed92c5d2a37681e5dc.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\frac{1}{x}=\sqrt5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/5/e2555472076ded5cfd0eb50ed2727ad2b249edbd.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+2+\frac{1}{x^2}=5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />. We subtract 2 on both sides for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/8/508ce69eec52a34a9348fde256141c94b414a2fa.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+\frac{1}{x^2}=3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="94" height="37" /> and square again, and see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/a/56ad6ad640095b9ca156c372e6bc6cacea0476c2.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4+2+\frac{1}{x^4}=9$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/9/0494a5a8e24852f3e062c427d3bfc0a8bcbefd2e.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4+\frac{1}{x^4}=7$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="37" />. We can divide our original expression of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fab2d2af70a372be209c48d0bffcda0915cb673b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{11}-7x^7+x^3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="16" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/9/4d97ab5c672615853d322347c7b4f396075c9ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$x^7$" width="17" height="15" /> to get that it is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/2/6328ee042bac0a5ba11d4187bd3b71dc5fe50d6a.png" class="latex" alt="$x^7(x^4-7+\frac{1}{x^4})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="123" height="37" />. Therefore because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/a/e9a53ebebefd1f1b7a663437bccb5107f17a3527.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4+\frac{1}{x^4}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="60" height="37" /> is 7, it is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/a/dea108c0fb9460dbc7c63e5b697c5b0bed9d2801.png" class="latex" alt="$x^7(0)=\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="122" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Multiplying both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and using the quadratic formula, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/e/2de8db395a2119a085b6bec7e0f424b5e5fced52.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{5} \pm 1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="41" />. We can assume that it is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/d/dfd7be6c69e189e2989359d8132e05d161acc103.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{5}+1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="58" height="41" />, and notice that this is also a solution the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/2/c/32c76b2637ed1588f05720785daa5cdf9c09e2d6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-x-1=0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="114" height="15" />, i.e. we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/2/b126f3fac467cdac85395e4af077d5f04dc3bd42.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2=x+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="16" />. Repeatedly using this on the given (you can also just note Fibonacci numbers), 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/2/47204d221bc6d1191a37f9eddb8aa12cb232df16.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}  (x^{11})-7x^7+x^3 &amp;= (x^{10}+x^9)-7x^7+x^3 \\ &amp;=(2x^9+x^8)-7x^7+x^3 \\ &amp;=(3x^8+2x^7)-7x^7+x^3 \\ &amp;=(3x^8-5x^7)+x^3 \\ &amp;=(-2x^7+3x^6)+x^3 \\ &amp;=(x^6-2x^5)+x^3 \\ &amp;=(-x^5+x^4+x^3) \\ &amp;=-x^3(x^2-x-1) = \boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0} \end{align*}" width="358" height="222" />
</p><p>~Lcz
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can immediately note that the exponents of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/b/fab2d2af70a372be209c48d0bffcda0915cb673b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{11}-7x^7+x^3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="16" /> are an arithmetic sequence, so they are symmetric around the middle term. So, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/1/bb113cf6865a621f3b4420618e1602172f93590b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{11}-7x^7+x^3 = x^7(x^4-7+\frac{1}{x^4})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="261" height="37" />. We can see that since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85f6a1b39cb7d6f92d538a281b16ff07111dfd16.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\frac{1}{x} = \sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/c/53cfe387c37929386c2262328a157677c992f456.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+2+\frac{1}{x^2} = 5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" /> and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/0/5a07ba4a6842438dc2772cacc547619d67f11992.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+\frac{1}{x^2} = 3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="94" height="37" />. Continuing from here, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/7/ff7423642dfa4ebd89dd3d7617fcfcdf0d2b7784.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4+2+\frac{1}{x^4} = 9$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/1/ba13b2a3f87151d7f33c014cd71ef68c0a2e6169.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-7+\frac{1}{x^4} = 0$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="37" />. We don't even need to find what <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/9/4d97ab5c672615853d322347c7b4f396075c9ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$x^7$" width="17" height="15" /> is! This is since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/1/d21018a99052eca3622e72963e0826f28d6c3d3b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^7\cdot0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="42" height="17" /> is evidently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/c/06c50f428ea4ec9543d49f9e8829bacaf1859d52.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 0}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="57" height="30" />, which is our answer.
</p><p>~sosiaops
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>We begin by multiplying <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/f/85f6a1b39cb7d6f92d538a281b16ff07111dfd16.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\frac{1}{x} = \sqrt{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="96" height="37" /> by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/ace49855b086545a18629fda9bbed5f1f4fc990b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+1 = \sqrt{5}x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="18" />. Now we see this equation: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/9/419e41b3865dab9b9b9b1c527c1ac94f99e464d6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{11}-7x^{7}+x^3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="112" height="16" />. The terms all have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/48524d3889ec8433cb03669176e738011df245b8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3$" width="16" height="15" /> in common, so we can factor that out, and what we're looking for becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0117461f447f7bc05eda7d4e58a12a08d3f30a50.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3(x^8-7x^4+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="19" />. Looking back to our original equation, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/e/ace49855b086545a18629fda9bbed5f1f4fc990b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+1 = \sqrt{5}x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="18" />, which is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/094f8ab3f9682e4b3a58753bc7cd64a724690cb5.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 = \sqrt{5}x-1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="107" height="18" />. Using this, we can evaluate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/0/2f0078b54474db2dbf6219e8386b9b62c4405c94.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4$" width="17" height="15" /> to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/c/4dcb3a2190fd289ea8c3a756f3300e29e4ed5921.png" class="latex" alt="$5x^2-2\sqrt{5}x+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="18" />, and we see that there is another <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" />, so we put substitute it in again, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/4/a548617c508f4c3c0a2cd12ca7786ec906eea293.png" class="latex" alt="$3\sqrt{5}x-4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="75" height="18" />. Using the same way, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1c777b753a5ddb9e42e9f6065680e66b1c5c49b.png" class="latex" alt="$x^8$" width="16" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/8/2e88af1d7bc2cbc8440b912cff37a65691af88fb.png" class="latex" alt="$21\sqrt{5}x-29$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="18" />. We put this into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/0117461f447f7bc05eda7d4e58a12a08d3f30a50.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3(x^8-7x^4+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="128" height="19" />, resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/c/c1c5c054aacb756e79504fbf9b343312f64317ca.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3(0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="39" height="19" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/6/2364210d0f153cd6004e968976affc61f6eeb9b9.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{(B)~0}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="29" />.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>The equation we are given is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/e/e7ed215794e27525af18a7396506bd80a4b5c3e9.png" class="latex" alt="$x+\tfrac{1}{x}=\sqrt{5}...$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="106" height="23" /> Yuck. Fractions and radicals! We multiply both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/0/6d0382f3585219947b495ac2262a81b7b0c40db2.png" class="latex" alt="$x,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="14" height="11" /> square, and re-arrange to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/d/a6d73dbd2a5e862c78fc52bd64a4ef78edea690c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2+1=\sqrt{5}x \implies x^4+2x^2+1=5x^2 \implies x^4-3x^2+1=0.\]" width="478" height="18" /> Now, let us consider the expression we wish to acquire. Factoring out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/b/90b169519409cabf6ba626ec9f7bce0b58a594b8.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="22" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/9/3a97d706499ae248563f55bf52c0da0c30b5bf60.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^3\left(x^8-7x^4+1\right) = x^3\left(x^8+2x^4+1-9x^4\right).\]" width="350" height="22" /> Then, we notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/b/7cbdf206eaa985dc501dbacd37dc6077a13909c4.png" class="latex" alt="$x^8+2x^4+1=\left(x^4+1\right)^2.$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="202" height="25" /> Furthermore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/9/4d9d31973008d7982b93d62abb447e69083c4a01.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^4+1=3x^2 \implies \left(x^4+1\right)^2=x^8+2x^4+1=9x^4.\]" width="399" height="25" /> Thus, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/c/f8c102a6f23e364d2412c9ab6a889053bfbeef68.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^3\left(9x^4-9x^4\right) = x^3 \cdot 0 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}} ~ 0.\]" width="259" height="28" />
