<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> be a positive integer and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> be a digit such that the value of the numeral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/3/8d36ac4d71755454ce69412829bdc76babc31818.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{32d}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="15" /> in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/5/775f06035ecd5475ce472f4321a47e24d2dc5d7d.png" class="latex" alt="$263$" width="26" height="12" />, and the value of the numeral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/0/b000e0abbb0e59ff5f70c20bf929d8d4c23d13ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{324}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="15" /> in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> equals the value of the numeral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d4ef4a70f6bd36bceeeef43973b413698748eaa.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{11d1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="15" /> in base six. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/0/de0410173777540ae6a5ccf0d97f18bf4d2e341a.png" class="latex" alt="$n + d&#160;?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/c/75c83646df733c0fc99777b5fbe814fdfd62f425.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~10 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~11 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~13 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~15 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can start by setting up an equation to convert <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/3/8d36ac4d71755454ce69412829bdc76babc31818.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{32d}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="28" height="15" /> base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> to base 10. To convert this to base 10, it would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/b/e8b8537a449ddf0c656afa8edf112f494dc26ffc.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n+d.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="105" height="16" /> Because it is equal to 263, we can set this equation to 263. Finally, subtract <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> from both sides to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce928aa4ae1483728e7447d5c6b75fb2d2ec7356.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n = 263-d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="153" height="16" />.
</p><p>We can also set up equations to convert <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/0/b000e0abbb0e59ff5f70c20bf929d8d4c23d13ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{324}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="15" /> base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d4ef4a70f6bd36bceeeef43973b413698748eaa.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{11d1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="15" /> base 6 to base 10. The equation to covert <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/0/b000e0abbb0e59ff5f70c20bf929d8d4c23d13ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{324}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="27" height="15" /> base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> to base 10 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/1/1714524b908075f3fde6785790ff622f4c721b21.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n+4.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="104" height="16" /> The equation to convert <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/4/7d4ef4a70f6bd36bceeeef43973b413698748eaa.png" class="latex" alt="$\underline{11d1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="37" height="15" /> base 6 to base 10 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/f/cff24421f03d11ee4cc26f2d2c9c5542c08a3afe.png" class="latex" alt="${6}^3+{6}^2+6d+1.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="16" />
</p><p>Simplify <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/9/c592106a948bf486822aa150d8e64ebfcd0f7ede.png" class="latex" alt="${6}^3+{6}^2+6d+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="126" height="16" /> so it becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/c/2dcc2bc71a434553cba1c00ff48974c12a9fdcee.png" class="latex" alt="$6d+253.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" /> Setting the above equations equal to each other, we have 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/9/f69f161e8a3d2ca81be571694a813984493241ac.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3{n}^2+2n+4 = 6d+253.\]" width="196" height="16" /> 
Subtracting 4 from both sides gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/8/788e1ec122bcfe38508deadf39653a15ae3b6c53.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n = 6d+249.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="165" height="16" />
</p><p>We can then use equations
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/5/355295c88246567fb7a6b4f446070a4c1da8ab22.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3{n}^2+2n = 263-d\]" width="152" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/f/55f3b9b5e4e99b3f6f4e53f205e4dd668c4cd746.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3{n}^2+2n = 6d+249\]" width="160" height="16" />
to solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. Set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/c/1fc23cab9f52bc9c7a741c610ca7251d9b964a5e.png" class="latex" alt="$263-d$" width="59" height="12" /> equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/8/60850dae5f20599e5e0b47d3539d37f619e3d8ae.png" class="latex" alt="$6d+249$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" /> and solve to find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/8/588e07a0cfc1e617ac5d6552a759b3011aa96d6c.png" class="latex" alt="$d=2$" width="42" height="12" />.
</p><p>Plug <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/8/588e07a0cfc1e617ac5d6552a759b3011aa96d6c.png" class="latex" alt="$d=2$" width="42" height="12" /> back into the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/9/ce928aa4ae1483728e7447d5c6b75fb2d2ec7356.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n = 263-d$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="153" height="16" />. Subtract 261 from both sides to get your final equation of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/9/ca93718d6244d60317cab4393e5334d379eb85fb.png" class="latex" alt="$3{n}^2+2n-261 = 0.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="156" height="16" /> We solve using the quadratic formula to find that the solutions are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/e/f8e6b7e5397a45e746da1e87bcc26de1a57ef6c4.png" class="latex" alt="$-10.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> Because the base must be positive, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/6/5b6508a4dcf1d77a85a0ded35969eca8910ce47b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=9.$" width="47" height="12" />
</p><p>Adding 2 to 9 gets <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba152480ec4aa7b8fdc1ccc11cbaee9f61afe77.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~11}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p><p>-Zeusthemoose (edited for readability)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/2952c6b374f39698327fedb0d96a9d4653b0b2c7.png" class="latex" alt="$32d$" width="27" height="12" /> is greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/5/775f06035ecd5475ce472f4321a47e24d2dc5d7d.png" class="latex" alt="$263$" width="26" height="12" /> when both are interpreted in base 10, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Some trial and error gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/f/18fb781bb0840e30c7a894eac0d3a0f67fcd3157.png" class="latex" alt="$n=9$" width="43" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/5/775f06035ecd5475ce472f4321a47e24d2dc5d7d.png" class="latex" alt="$263$" width="26" height="12" /> in base 9 is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/0/4104d093e8cb91695fd6787ce75397863b30c0e2.png" class="latex" alt="$322$" width="26" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/e/0eedb155ce8e177832ffd6603819317451dbf6b1.png" class="latex" alt="$9+2=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~11}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="128" height="28" />. 
</p><p>-SmileKat32
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/5/1155b68ae49c39f3a6468053f31806d7836d7ae1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3n^2 + 2n + d = 263\]" width="151" height="17" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/e/e4eed6e0684258b116f43b7213c4521d55ea2163.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3n^2 + 2n + 4 = 6^3 + 6^2 + 6d + 1\]" width="251" height="17" />
Subtracting the 2nd from the 1st equation we get 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/b/22b56cbca4f68cdc08a37bbca5b4577804ce5316.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*} d-4 &amp;= 263 - (216 + 36 + 6d + 1) \\ &amp;= 263 - 253 - 6d \\ &amp;= 10 - 6d  \end{align*}" width="266" height="63" />
Thus we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c862f1a7fb3753d93eb8c7dc1996057cb3b74663.png" class="latex" alt="$d=2.$" width="46" height="12" />
Substituting into the first, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a73b1370fc2d5942c26e8d80ea077cd69c095413.png" class="latex" alt="$3n^2 + 2n + 2 = 263 \Rightarrow 3n^2 + 2n - 261 = 0.$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="340" height="18" />
Factoring, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/6/586ea221c8078b15e6e56f57b6b15eb4b8fa96b5.png" class="latex" alt="$(n-9)(3n+29)=0.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="171" height="20" />
A digit cannot be negative, so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/6/5b6508a4dcf1d77a85a0ded35969eca8910ce47b.png" class="latex" alt="$n=9.$" width="47" height="12" />
Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/6/dd6b55233e6800b2aa2390ef7e8731a494f14abe.png" class="latex" alt="$d+n=2+9=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} ~11}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="197" height="30" />
</p><p>mathboy282 signing off
~<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/latex.png" class="latex" alt="$\LaTeX$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="46" height="15" /> fixed by Lamboreghini
</p>
