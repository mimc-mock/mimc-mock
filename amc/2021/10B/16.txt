<p>Call a positive integer an uphill integer if every digit is strictly greater than the previous digit. For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/2/0d289e92fe88a46168e7e381397851e30099538f.png" class="latex" alt="$1357, 89,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are all uphill integers, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/d/ccd8c9d7f7982a5b236b88ed511632b21a5f08fb.png" class="latex" alt="$32, 1240,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="66" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/5/a759369df4e2ba711f6001cc9086d93d8549a9cc.png" class="latex" alt="$466$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> are not. How many uphill integers are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c700175e6879884f2a84623c529081d4a5ca9be4.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)} ~4 \qquad\textbf{(B)} ~5 \qquad\textbf{(C)} ~6 \qquad\textbf{(D)} ~7 \qquad\textbf{(E)} ~8$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="348" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The divisibility rule of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is that the number must be congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> mod <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and congruent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> mod <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Being divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> means that it must end with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. We can rule out the case when the number ends with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> immediately because the only integer that is uphill and ends with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> which is not positive. So now we know that the number ends with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Looking at the answer choices, the answer choices are all pretty small, so we can generate all of the numbers that are uphill and are divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. These numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/4/ef40ed0ccaef302433756570c0cc19dc49b3f4cd.png" class="latex" alt="$15, 45, 135, 345, 1245, 12345$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="212" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3deeac91599ed7f0f2b06657dc240239c6fb81e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~6}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="56" height="30" /> numbers.
</p><p>~ilikemath40
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, note how the number must end in either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> in order to satisfying being divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. However, the number can't end in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> because it's not strictly greater than the previous digits. Thus, our number must end in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. We do casework on the number of digits.
</p><p><b>Case 1:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> digit. No numbers work, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers.
</p><p><b>Case 2:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> digits. We have the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/c/3dc0f0d026fbdfebcb587570b34e12e762df2cf4.png" class="latex" alt="$15, 45,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="48" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" />, but <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> isn't an uphill number, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers
</p><p><b>Case 3:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> digits. We have the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/7/6f77bd47066c83d737a5bc88cbdbf1af53140a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$135, 345$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="62" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers.
</p><p><b>Case 4:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> digits. We have the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/3/1136809704582bf0ad1f6ddf3c1dd345a42bfa38.png" class="latex" alt="$1235, 1245$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="80" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6ad6045f59b66f5de989d5f8f2a297b381f2aac.png" class="latex" alt="$2345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, but only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/a/99abbcb682592ee3bb5b25740a2857e45d7f815f.png" class="latex" alt="$1245$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> satisfies this condition, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> number.
</p><p><b>Case 5:</b> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> digits. We have only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/7/70721c830aa84eadb8417a0352d5ae02ead8d5bd.png" class="latex" alt="$12345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="44" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> number.
</p><p>Adding these up, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/4/cd41a86b9874bda9ea0b24babe3863809449fe04.png" class="latex" alt="$2+2+1+1=\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~6}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="184" height="30" />.
</p><p>~JustinLee2017
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Like solution 2, we can proceed by using casework. A number is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> if is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/3/b835f2c6c592edc4583ce996f86bcc0d07ca8da5.png" class="latex" alt="$5.$" width="12" height="12" /> In this case, the units digit must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/21415a2eb558eb7c199a0382fcd50cbeda9299fb.png" class="latex" alt="$5,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="16" /> otherwise no number can be formed.
</p><p><b>Case 1: sum of digits = 6</b>
</p><p>There is only one number, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/1/ce1119733ed51d0c818ac7464686341a08905d3c.png" class="latex" alt="$15.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="13" />
</p><p><b>Case 2: sum of digits = 9</b>
</p><p>There are two numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/7/ad7e2b867321a72e69969ccfdde69141ff469eff.png" class="latex" alt="$135.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="30" height="13" />
</p><p><b>Case 3: sum of digits = 12</b>
</p><p>There are two numbers: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2c72d8d34783d3db7b917c953ca22225f513823.png" class="latex" alt="$345$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/7/5372d66bdeac7be15b202dfdd9f108c986a3757b.png" class="latex" alt="$1245.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" />
</p><p><b>Case 4: sum of digits = 15</b>
</p><p>There is only one number, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/0/760745b8d44b061df443650ab8e8271d61579ca6.png" class="latex" alt="$12345.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="13" />
</p><p>We can see that we have exhausted all cases, because in order to have a larger sum of digits, then a number greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> needs to be used, breaking the conditions of the problem. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/e/69ea5e08f273797514b29a464aa78e43cdc33f79.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)} ~6}.$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="61" height="30" />
</p><p>~coolmath34
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>An integer is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> iff it is divisible by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Divisibility by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> means ending in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, but since no digit is less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, the only uphill integer ending in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> could be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, which is not positive. This means the integer must end in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>All uphill integers ending in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are formed by picking any subset of the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/6/d06a60c2aa2075c3025ac906bb3315b0c768994c.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2,3,4)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="77" height="20" /> of digits (keeping their order), then appending a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. Divisibility by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> means the sum of the digits is a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, so our choice of digits must add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/4/fc4ceaea8c1a5775cb5b82da1a22f231f4567ee1.png" class="latex" alt="$5 \equiv -1 \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="131" height="20" />, so the other digits we pick must add to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> modulo <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/9/a69c2fe6e9b6c093471f0dd7bbabbfdcc4f3b5ec.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,2,3,4) \equiv (1,-1,0,1) \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="262" height="20" />, we can pick either nothing, or one residue <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> (from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />) and one residue <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> (from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />), and we can then optionally add a residue <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> (from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />). This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/b/adbab1f841f7b5ddf146bdf356323f16d89c7e61.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+2\cdot1)\cdot2 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}~6}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="180" height="30" /> possibilities.
</p><p>~<a href="/wiki/index.php/User:Emerald_block" title="User:Emerald block">emerald_block</a>
