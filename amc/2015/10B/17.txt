<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The octahedron is just two congruent pyramids glued together by their base. The base of one pyramid is a rhombus with diagonals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, for an area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/b/75b0f093ae63ec4068a320379b8b6a160ff87613.png" class="latex" alt="$A = 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="55" height="13" />. The height <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />, of one pyramid, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67ce01cd97c81718e787d0abf8093ec9dfc01b05.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" />, so the volume of one pyramid is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/9/9b9eed8a76504e1dc001bcf23f17273afc852821.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{Ah}{3}=5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="61" height="38" />. Thus, the octahedron has volume <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/8/4288a2e711276389f9044617d81dc03f026647b8.png" class="latex" alt="$2\cdot5=\boxed{{(B)}\\10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="114" height="29" />
</p>
