<p>Al, Bill, and Cal will each randomly be assigned a whole number from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, inclusive, with no two of them getting the same number. What is the probability that Al's number will be a whole number multiple of Bill's and Bill's number will be a whole number multiple of Cal's?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/a/abaf0abf7db533b4eae42dd363806df49fb94c44.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \dfrac{9}{1000} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \dfrac{1}{90} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \dfrac{1}{80} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \dfrac{1}{72} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \dfrac{2}{121}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="444" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution">Video Solution</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vulB2z_PdRE&amp;feature=youtu.be">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vulB2z_PdRE&amp;feature=youtu.be</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We can solve this problem with a brute force approach.
</p>
<ul><li>If Cal's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />:
<ul><li>If Bill's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, Al's can be any of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/c/f7c5409dec4471828a76840e3ff65ec2d7860d7e.png" class="latex" alt="$4, 6, 8, 10$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="69" height="16" />.</li>
<li>If Bill's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, Al's can be any of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/6/43680e20fbe5c0f3d6a2619e74dfff9acd187943.png" class="latex" alt="$6, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />.</li>
<li>If Bill's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, Al's can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.</li>
<li>If Bill's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, Al's can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />.</li>
<li>Otherwise, Al's number could not be a whole number multiple of Bill's.</li></ul></li>
<li>If Cal's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />:
<ul><li>If Bill's number is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />, Al's can be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />.</li>
<li>Otherwise, Al's number could not be a whole number multiple of Bill's while Bill's number is still a whole number multiple of Cal's.</li></ul></li>
<li>Otherwise, Bill's number must be greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, i.e. Al's number could not be a whole number multiple of Bill's.</li></ul>
<p>Clearly, there are exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> cases where Al's number will be a whole number multiple of Bill's and Bill's number will be a whole number multiple of Cal's. Since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/6/c2672950ecf00739e1cd6e31d3520e2073d21f65.png" class="latex" alt="$10*9*8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" /> possible permutations of the numbers Al, Bill, and Cal were assigned, the probability that this is true is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/a/c9ace3485c612e82c258b43c99b2176d424588e4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac9{10*9*8}=\boxed{\text{(\textbf C) }\frac1{80}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="165" height="48" />
</p>
