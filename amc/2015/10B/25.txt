How many ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3d9e22097e20a1249b5332202b24941a4bf9e1f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> are possible?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/d/89d75a753449af45da9e28983d617470672219f4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[abc = 2(ab+bc+ac) \quad \text{ or } \quad (a-2)bc = 2a(b+c).\]" width="396" height="18" />

Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86caa5541d61970a8b4bb81cefbb51c5c164f1e5.png" class="latex" alt="$a\le 6$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" />. From the second equation we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/8/e583b2012f12f1e75e592617eeaff88016f1d9f5.png" class="latex" alt="$a &gt; 2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/f/dcf5b63b29391a19f877f895230e1700c7c87843.png" class="latex" alt="$a\in \{3, 4, 5, 6\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="18" />. 
</p>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340605c5f88cf6864adc5b146c3dffca7a45df22.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3$" width="42" height="12" /> we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/f/20f8957e53d1b427f3c4bea42c2f777475cc44ee.png" class="latex" alt="$bc = 6(b+c) \Rightarrow (b-6)(c-6)=36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="277" height="18" />. <ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/5/0c5252a64df98a40335e897265ff4b66a20356c4.png" class="latex" alt="$2bc = 8(b+c) \Rightarrow bc = 4(b+c) \Rightarrow (b-4)(c-4)=16$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="419" height="20" />. We get <b>three</b> roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f9d6c6a6f566bf26846b0ff271c78f6847fcc3.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(4,5,20), (4,6,12), (4,8,8)\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="224" height="18" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/53897e4b47f6049266b4bffcef91bc949385b5e2.png" class="latex" alt="$a=5$" width="42" height="12" /> we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/6/0461bec8d096566cdfcc614d58108583495d3f4e.png" class="latex" alt="$3bc = 10(b+c) \Rightarrow 9bc=30(b+c) \Rightarrow (3b-10)(3c-10)=100$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="491" height="20" />. We get <b>one</b> root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec8375d1d9678724366c63e91315704fab32d5f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(5,5,10)\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="87" height="20" />.</li></ul>
<ul><li>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/5/fa5507ab5a3d1f06941dabd1c26505d646367983.png" class="latex" alt="$a=6$" width="42" height="12" /> we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/757eb1386fccef30fe5ab4abea97d0344be77172.png" class="latex" alt="$4bc = 12(b+c) \Rightarrow bc = 3(b+c) \Rightarrow (b-3)(c-3)=9$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="419" height="20" />. We get <b> one</b> root <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/0/e407317873748ac3009d79a9f6a77318aaf3d755.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(6,6,6)\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="78" height="20" />.</li></ul>
<p>Thus, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/8/7d8f5f4eda652534a927025f5cd62701ef2703e1.png" class="latex" alt="$5+3+1+1 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\; 10}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="190" height="28" /> solutions.

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/9/54931370c041573e3a8cca703c10893e3ad72b48.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2(ab+bc+ca)=abc.\]" width="171" height="18" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Simplification_of_Solution_2">Simplification of Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The surface area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/8/f182b4f8378303d8d89e6b737afdc3df2c421776.png" class="latex" alt="$2(ab+bc+ca)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" />, the volume is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96a81938ca5e71faa6894d9d52ac776c9efbe75d.png" class="latex" alt="$abc$" width="25" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/3/1534ab586ef84be006736521e1d37908b69cecb8.png" class="latex" alt="$2(ab+bc+ca)=abc$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="18" />.
</p><p>Divide both sides by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/08911b936e3accddc46c7e38445f312b17fdffd5.png" class="latex" alt="$2abc$" width="34" height="12" />, we have: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/1/7015aa0f25ec791b015812b9ebf3c0429b13bed5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c}=\frac{1}{2}.\]" width="123" height="37" />

</p><p>So we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/8/9d8f217754c93f26f1d5b73911a7886386c440eb.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3, 4, 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="76" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p><br />
We can say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/2/b028033fd4ec0f01ee75a90576ea3a543ae7f7bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c}=\frac{1}{q}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="84" height="40" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/7/31787d20b673012af7c012bc000c6de0861d5602.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{q} = \frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{a}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="84" height="40" />.
</p><p>Notice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b40d9344b869ae4050bec6777614b9f8d5d183b.png" class="latex" alt="$\emph{\text{immediately}}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="15" /> that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/a/c4acd3609fdb8c049352ac3014278ded2e85ef27.png" class="latex" alt="$b, c &gt; q$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="56" height="16" />. This is our key step.
Then we can say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/b/aab6df573e77fbd06562471a1f7f212f5d3086a8.png" class="latex" alt="$b=q+d$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="72" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59e4a64bb2715fcbaf16d23b8e30b4f27fc771c1.png" class="latex" alt="$c=q+e$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="71" height="14" />. If we clear the fraction about b and c (do the math), our immediate result is that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4cc32219df8957c7ec781b2216a08b8d7401c213.png" class="latex" alt="$de = q^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="57" height="18" />. Realize also that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/0/4c0d55179ca9fcfb18e698091d873f09bfc1ca09.png" class="latex" alt="$d \leq e$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="41" height="15" />. 
</p><p>Now go through cases for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and you end up with the same result. However, now you don't have to guess solutions. For example, when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/340605c5f88cf6864adc5b146c3dffca7a45df22.png" class="latex" alt="$a=3$" width="42" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7defdadfec4d3814791c8549584a05d7dd639ca.png" class="latex" alt="$de = 36$" width="59" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/4/2148012ec7ec5a9debe18798949db1a297332cde.png" class="latex" alt="$d=1, 2, 3, 4, 6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" />.
</p>
