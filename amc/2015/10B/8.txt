<p>The letter F shown below is rotated <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> clockwise around the origin, then reflected in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis, and then rotated a half turn around the origin. What is the final image?
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:Foriginal.png" class="image"><img alt="Foriginal.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//b/b4/Foriginal.png" width="106" height="119" /></a>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:Fproblem.png" class="image"><img alt="Fproblem.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//0/09/Fproblem.png" width="542" height="301" /></a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The first rotation moves the base of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> to the negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis, and the stem to the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis. The reflection then moves the stem to the negative <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-axis, with the base unchanged. Then the half turn moves the stem to the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> axis and the base to the positive <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-axis, choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/87691af2d291d52166ae8d9e750af9e3b7e53931.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathbf{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="29" />.
</p>
