<p>The shaded region below is called a shark's fin falcata, a figure studied by Leonardo da Vinci. It is bounded by the portion of the circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> and center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> that lies in the first quadrant, the portion of the circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/5/85524016576d4135def3798324635e6abd32e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> and center <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/a/e5ac44e0478296923ce638e4b89795e761f25efc.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,\tfrac{3}{2})$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="40" height="21" /> that lies in the first quadrant, and the line segment from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7af424ab2c41e93a9e7ab081dee423809b2ba735.png" class="latex" alt="$(3,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />. What is the area of the shark's fin falcata?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce4e98a2133efe32043ca2e707377730ddad63c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import cse5;pathpen=black;pointpen=black; size(1.5inch); D(MP(&quot;x&quot;,(3.5,0),S)--(0,0)--MP(&quot;\frac{3}{2}&quot;,(0,3/2),W)--MP(&quot;y&quot;,(0,3.5),W)); path P=(0,0)--MP(&quot;3&quot;,(3,0),S)..(3*dir(45))..MP(&quot;3&quot;,(0,3),W)--(0,3)..(3/2,3/2)..cycle; draw(P,linewidth(2)); fill(P,gray); [/asy]" width="182" height="182" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/a/03a66da142f0008a47b442fc8142e3614a3e48ba.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } \dfrac{4\pi}{5} \qquad\textbf{(B) } \dfrac{9\pi}{8} \qquad\textbf{(C) } \dfrac{4\pi}{3} \qquad\textbf{(D) } \dfrac{7\pi}{5} \qquad\textbf{(E) } \dfrac{3\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="427" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The area of the shark's fin falcata is just the area of the quarter-circle minus the area of the semicircle. The quarter-circle has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> so it has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/e/07ea867fa55ecb191f9a1b579700059bed4dc186.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{9\pi}{4}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="22" height="38" />. The semicircle has radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/c/67ce01cd97c81718e787d0abf8093ec9dfc01b05.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> so it has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/7/b774acf584027d9922727c6b1b1e213eb74cc461.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{9\pi}{8}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="22" height="37" />. Thus, the shaded area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/e/8/5e8ee44330e0c8fb74a918149818b4a59ff8d347.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{9\pi}{4}-\dfrac{9\pi}{8}=\boxed{\mathbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{9\pi}{8}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="165" height="48" />
</p>
