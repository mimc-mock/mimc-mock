<p>Among the positive integers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, each of whose digits is a prime number, one is selected at random. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The one digit prime numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. So there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/2/92251f81dfcd78c67dd057d81543703f31419fed.png" class="latex" alt="$4\cdot4=16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> ways to choose a two digit number with both digits as primes and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> ways to choose a one digit prime, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/d/ccd5ace955a316c131942306fda43d6abe43f7f6.png" class="latex" alt="$4+16=20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" /> ways. Out of these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b2497e7b125cd721bab4c6e2385f954fe35cb.png" class="latex" alt="$23$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/78916550838bd31d66e4cdefbc366edb11fd00bd.png" class="latex" alt="$37$" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/a/1ba318f7385187c693cb1859d9b4b1b587e649d9.png" class="latex" alt="$53$" width="17" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5b03f8ead3b3da0bd2cbc1a41d8f01630e638f4.png" class="latex" alt="$73$" width="17" height="12" /> are prime. Thus the probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/c/34c09dfbd7ef1ae58ecaf2a5bf88cf2fa03adc7b.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{8}{20}=\boxed{\textbf{(B)} \dfrac{2}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="99" height="48" />.
