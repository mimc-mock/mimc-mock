<p>Aaron the ant walks on the coordinate plane according to the following rules. He starts at the origin <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/7/6b7c84b8d338464090337f21083f0b4b1e7f659f.png" class="latex" alt="$p_0=(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" /> facing to the east and walks one unit, arriving at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/d/8ad253e7e0042f767f5b3f1bf58c96c3c13314c3.png" class="latex" alt="$p_1=(1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="80" height="18" />. For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/f/7afd5ca011531278306bbde19218bfbd02dc37d8.png" class="latex" alt="$n=1,2,3,\dots$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="106" height="16" />, right after arriving at the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/1/df151678062aa7e93180c689cab0c21a2563e351.png" class="latex" alt="$p_n$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="18" height="11" />, if Aaron can turn <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/e/22ec0d2e4202839322d26a23dcb13f79821df5d7.png" class="latex" alt="$90^\circ$" width="24" height="13" /> left and walk one unit to an unvisited point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b50273c24a79c46b1bb09917df4634e75cc60fc.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="12" />, he does that. Otherwise, he walks one unit straight ahead to reach <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b50273c24a79c46b1bb09917df4634e75cc60fc.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="12" />. Thus the sequence of points continues <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7b97b3a7ea832f09fb54815b9c4edc5feffaac.png" class="latex" alt="$p_2=(1,1), p_3=(0,1), p_4=(-1,1), p_5=(-1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="377" height="18" />, and so on in a counterclockwise spiral pattern. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b406af0fd387a52b5e24c27953f036e3c0e6caa3.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2015}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="11" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/0/880ee7fe403e392728fbe9d8943998d72dead32e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } (-22,-13)\qquad\textbf{(B) } (-13,-22)\qquad\textbf{(C) } (-13,22)\qquad\textbf{(D) } (13,-22)\qquad\textbf{(E) } (22,-13)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="698" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The first thing we would do is track Aaron's footsteps:
</p><p>He starts by taking <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> step East and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> step North, ending at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/7/ae7a465ffe5255961382f5777569608be052b783.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> steps and about to head West.
</p><p>Then he takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> steps West and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> steps South, ending at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/a/c0ad82b10660edf6340c332e04c70ee71a951300.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1,-1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="60" height="18" />) after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/1/ee1a83cfd5c8598dc1fc9fc8ed98d55bebce3ac5.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="13" /> steps, and about to head East.
</p><p>Then he takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> steps East and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> steps North, ending at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b596a120dcf60e33c7bfe19003bb1675bf37a7.png" class="latex" alt="$(2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/98662d9ab8322708539f218c814d9d0213ad3788.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4+6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="14" /> steps, and about to head West.
</p><p>Then he takes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> steps West and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> steps South, ending at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/f/e0f362b2b408e022f4ecbaed09d2f120a4bfd97e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2,-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abef7b7aa79e2fea135c3be34d5e7f1c031b752e.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4+6+8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="102" height="14" /> steps, and about to head East.
</p><p>From this pattern, we can notice that for any integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/2/172e6dadffd9800e796f3f96a1e24ce2a1840f3a.png" class="latex" alt="$k \ge 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" /> he's at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/9/d197c92e7992026dc9444b52e577ec8dba9c9cfc.png" class="latex" alt="$(-k, -k)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" /> after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/6/a56433db12ceceddc68c6a27859d68ad3dc92596.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 4 + 6 + ... + 4k$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="150" height="14" /> steps, and about to head East.  There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/e/b8ea7ee8a282949fd853a01537ec7a2b55e56f75.png" class="latex" alt="$2k$" width="18" height="12" /> terms in the sum, with an average value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/8/2280d83755a0268f07dbb1deb8596909972442f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2 + 4k)/2 = 2k + 1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="156" height="18" />, so:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abe2c1579bcfaa3d307e00f78ea511e026a2b9d7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2 + 4 + 6 + ... + 4k = 2k(2k + 1)\]" width="256" height="18" />
</p><p>If we substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/d/a0dbf4bdff8dce668c17676669a414d2c82c85b9.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 22$" width="52" height="12" /> into the equation:  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/f/bdf7a12d58f42bbd0913022e2354efe78e88f05c.png" class="latex" alt="$44(45) = 1980 &lt; 2015$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="171" height="18" />.  So he has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7f6c79f2148f0cec592c9517b40ee1b82ef22e.png" class="latex" alt="$35$" width="17" height="12" /> moves to go.  This makes him end up at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/e/fdec1f477426c02c3adec542fc8a65ef0382a542.png" class="latex" alt="$(-22+35,-22) = (13,-22) \implies \boxed{\textbf{(D)} (13, -22)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="387" height="29" />.
</p><p><br />
This is just beautiful!!! - sleepypuppy
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We are given that Aaron starts at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/b/c4b86f54134d69cad8793688a431aa907aaf2698.png" class="latex" alt="$(0, 0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and we note that his net steps follow the pattern of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/1178af8fcc5f3f3acb9f151f7dcc2c4eefe88fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-direction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/1178af8fcc5f3f3acb9f151f7dcc2c4eefe88fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$+1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="13" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-direction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-direction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-direction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/6/a26d26d140649aac91fff6adf22f9c795c935801.png" class="latex" alt="$+3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="14" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-direction, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/6/a26d26d140649aac91fff6adf22f9c795c935801.png" class="latex" alt="$+3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="22" height="14" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-direction, and so on, where we add odd and subtract even.
</p><p>We want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/a/4ca5ef1f2e8b3535965b3bded67b3c5f096fdeed.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + ... + 2n = 2015$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="242" height="14" />, but it does not work out cleanly. Instead, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/c/17cbb19dc62f4d1fc3bc66dd53bad943d9a38be7.png" class="latex" alt="$2 + 4 + 6 + ... + 2(44) = 1980$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="232" height="18" />, which means that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/7/8c7f6c79f2148f0cec592c9517b40ee1b82ef22e.png" class="latex" alt="$35$" width="17" height="12" /> extra steps past adding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e4365fd6613b120e5fac37829e0e81dc7313a455.png" class="latex" alt="$-44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="12" /> in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-direction (and the final number we add in the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-direction is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e4365fd6613b120e5fac37829e0e81dc7313a455.png" class="latex" alt="$-44$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="32" height="12" />).
</p><p>So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/f/66f1e35e5d6313625c50a19ca4a161609992e834.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2015} = (0+1-2+3-4+5...-44+35, 0+1-2+3-4+5...-44)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="565" height="18" />.
</p><p>We can group <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/d/6ad67b2aa16baabccc9e934d361debfce4ca2b18.png" class="latex" alt="$1-2+3-4+5...-44$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="189" height="14" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/4/2b4244dafe0b5350b3fd4711e6912b31e736455a.png" class="latex" alt="$(1-2)+(3-4)+(5-6)+...+(43-44) = -22$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="396" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/5/8955fce29b10558d76bddc5a725c4816229d7c20.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2015} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; (13, -22)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="180" height="29" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Looking at his steps, we see that he walks in a spiral shape. At the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/5/8455f3b5cb3b4880b8c9d782a5c1f0334db819eb.png" class="latex" alt="$8$" width="8" height="12" />th step, he is on the bottom right corner of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/5/7b5343f2d9e22816e8de24262565ff2937695b2c.png" class="latex" alt="$3\times 3$" width="40" height="12" /> square centered on the origin. On the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />th step, he is on the bottom right corner of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef7fe7592026585cc36e4d7c6036f19fd8afdc29.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times 5$" width="40" height="12" /> square centered at the origin. It seems that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/2/bd26f70658dc1780d619834c085d117b1b70f790.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{n^2-1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="11" /> is the bottom right corner of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/f/93f761f7fc79f2bec21a2f31c896840d6f36cdc0.png" class="latex" alt="$n\times n$" width="43" height="9" /> square. This makes sense since, after <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb69df5577de55ff087bfc5fd090a6ea28c5c31.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="48" height="15" />, he has been on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/0/6006bba637eef443381fbaff768fa62d9ff41e62.png" class="latex" alt="$n^2$" width="17" height="15" /> dots, including the point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/e/d4e8cf09c5ced63a38ed87c9a03dd9aacf7e490a.png" class="latex" alt="$p_0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="16" height="11" />. Also, this is only for odd <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />, because starting with the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/8/8a8df7312744a199daa5c8008af5b9b4798bfd64.png" class="latex" alt="$1\times 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="12" /> square, we can only add one extra set of dots to each side, so we cannot get even <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/e/c5efab1fe69d482f224850979c04161d3fd44fd5.png" class="latex" alt="$45^2=2025$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="85" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/2/a028948e80563a797a6ab100e88ac90ae20fbc48.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2024}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="36" height="11" /> is the bottom right corner of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/b/c0bb7666bd6e7d779f67760ff823345308f09d0a.png" class="latex" alt="$45\times 45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> square. This point is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/2/f028a936c2d24b386c73a3fab0d820931d90291d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{45-1}{2}=22$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="37" /> over to the right, and therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" /> down, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/1/9a1b44968f8c07eba7287137a0a8d90b9d37460d.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2024}=(22, -22)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="132" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/2/a028948e80563a797a6ab100e88ac90ae20fbc48.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2024}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="36" height="11" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> ahead of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/0/b406af0fd387a52b5e24c27953f036e3c0e6caa3.png" class="latex" alt="$p_{2015}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="35" height="11" />, we go back <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> spaces to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/9/179391096d1f0e80c165353fabba954f26bcd1d1.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; (13, -22)}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="119" height="29" />.
</p>
