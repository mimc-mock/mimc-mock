<p>The line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/2/af2022bc83f2acc8bb5b00fcb2cf789ba4c95962.png" class="latex" alt="$12x+5y=60$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="111" height="16" /> forms a triangle with the coordinate axes. What is the sum of the lengths of the altitudes of this triangle?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We find the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />-intercepts and the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" />-intercepts to find the intersections of the axes and the line. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34ea5b6f6de584d56d19a89cc4923a9d9a5cfa41.png" class="latex" alt="$x=0$" width="43" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/2/c52f2821f1a68dc474c90d7acb0e279c7fca51c6.png" class="latex" alt="$y=12$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="15" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/c/51c80ea5862b45877f08d698a0c893bd2806a2fd.png" class="latex" alt="$x=5$" width="43" height="12" />. Our three vertices are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38ec6a35045b7cc0f04c4989a99570bcc29aee43.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6ab8e2a6c5f571336217a7e364627012c837cae.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,12)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" />. Two of our altitudes are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, and since it is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />-<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> right triangle, the hypotenuse is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. Since the area of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" />, so our final altitude is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/f/02f2139f09ead695f382902b70ad234fc01cd12d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30(2)}{13}=\frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="90" height="39" />. The sum of our altitudes is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/f/d8f209edb73789de0c27ce1757519763f7f59799.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60+156+65}{13}=\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \dfrac{281}{13}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="206" height="48" />. Note that there is no need to calculate the final answer after we know that the third altitude has length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/c/7ac94a5f6297a7d6aadbd7ba04e3a0a732298908.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{60}{13}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> is the only choice with a denominator of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> is relatively prime to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />.
</p>
