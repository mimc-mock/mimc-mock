<p>The town of Hamlet has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> people for each horse, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> sheep for each cow, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> ducks for each person. Which of the following could not possibly be the total number of people, horses, sheep, cows, and ducks in Hamlet?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/6/be694b17d773ff11ab43b01d40f408909b5e4e84.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }41\qquad\textbf{(B) }47\qquad\textbf{(C) }59\qquad\textbf{(D) }61\qquad\textbf{(E) }66$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let the amount of people be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" />, horses be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />, sheep be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />, cows be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and ducks be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" />. 
We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/b/84b671edb3f31393f20c9511c05decd11b87b7c6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3h=p\]" width="52" height="16" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/d/7ad2f752fe1186e20400cef997bd3db46970b043.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[4c=s\]" width="49" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/9/30949cc19375bc9322ebc39d39709149006c05fd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3p=d\]" width="51" height="16" /> Then the total amount of people, horses, sheep, cows, and ducks may be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/a/42a1ba976038bbb7bd99ca65492a25c86bb088d6.png" class="latex" alt="$p+h+s+c+d = 3h+h+4c+c+(3\times3h)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="366" height="18" />. This is equivalent to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/b/36bd1de35e8248f392c4262fd5ebf9ac83cb50ff.png" class="latex" alt="$13h+5c$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="68" height="14" />. Looking through the options, we see <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a21383a2b05dbe4ed4ecd095273f0cabc32b78a6.png" class="latex" alt="$47$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> is impossible to make for integer values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/3/24344a968d5974e1b4ec714736412a3405afa010.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} 47}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>As the solution above says, the total amount of people, horses, sheep, cows, and ducks may be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/b/9db7dd293512fe264bbc465880dab3bbe14eefcf.png" class="latex" alt="$13d+5s$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" />. However, instead of going through each of the solutions and testing the options, you can use the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Chicken_McNugget_Theorem" title="Chicken McNugget Theorem">Chicken McNugget Theorem</a> to find the greatest number of people, horses, sheep, cows, and ducks that cannot be written in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/b/9db7dd293512fe264bbc465880dab3bbe14eefcf.png" class="latex" alt="$13d+5s$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/e/67ead35f5de4609d28fb53abee9698554f3f51f4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[13*5-13-5=47,\]" width="161" height="16" /> so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/3/24344a968d5974e1b4ec714736412a3405afa010.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} 47}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" />.
</p>
