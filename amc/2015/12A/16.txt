<p>Tetrahedron <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff29028b2432b4e2cf3d924ac82114813de066d.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=5$" width="61" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f7661519159a6678741c369a2e897dfd24579670.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=3$" width="60" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/3/a5322c7f896502c208bbb4a354efc8d212620667.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="62" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/d/bbd469ed9ff340d47afa0fd2b9a41b662a9fdfef.png" class="latex" alt="$BD=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/8/6e867467073fae9550c977a21ee2cb19426c4f92.png" class="latex" alt="$AD=3$" width="62" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/d/25dc3032a86bd869e2b6f40a776ca03b979859eb.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=\tfrac{12}5\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="96" height="23" />.  What is the volume of the tetrahedron?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/2/a524c6c44a33bb8d04325509bee01c031d78186d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }3\sqrt2\qquad\textbf{(B) }2\sqrt5\qquad\textbf{(C) }\dfrac{24}5\qquad\textbf{(D) }3\sqrt3\qquad\textbf{(E) }\dfrac{24}5\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="477" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Drop altitudes of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> and triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> down from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" />, respectively. Both will hit the same point; let this point be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" />. Because both triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> and triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> are 3-4-5 triangles, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/7/3873078e03dce6550ab5c8afd497da1f22b716af.png" class="latex" alt="$CT = DT = \dfrac{3\cdot4}{5} = \dfrac{12}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="184" height="37" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef8c07853f9d3cafb46bc949a5f50b427a634496.png" class="latex" alt="$CT^{2} + DT^{2} = 2\left(\frac{12}{5}\right)^{2} = \left(\frac{12}{5}\sqrt{2}\right)^{2} = CD^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="350" height="46" />, it follows that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/2/252b4379bd7d4f56ca4cee487817e25303f43506.png" class="latex" alt="$CTD$" width="42" height="12" /> is a right triangle, meaning that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/f/86fdb1aae2659b4341f6323860c7be113020d29f.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle CTD = 90^\circ$" width="104" height="13" />, and it follows that planes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/a/a6adfd3ad27a2dddeb0af7131d73c33c86991e93.png" class="latex" alt="$ABD$" width="43" height="13" /> are perpendicular to each other. Now, we can treat <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> as the base of the tetrahedron and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/4/264f0c33a54d96dace10debd80b92b6c2d80ddac.png" class="latex" alt="$TD$" width="28" height="12" /> as the height. Thus, the desired volume is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/a/58ae713b93b5e8d0488fd949425fff2dfd0a5000.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[V = \dfrac{1}{3} Bh = \dfrac{1}{3}\cdot[ABC]\cdot TD = \dfrac{1}{3} \cdot 6 \cdot \dfrac{12}{5} = \dfrac{24}{5}\]" width="361" height="37" /> which is answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/515a189da60e45d14676657c952371171e2858e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } \dfrac{24}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/4/0948661e822f2a953c43a57ac9e40b2734476de4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD$" width="29" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" />. We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/6/b06e219dd296fa7c67e8a510d8b9dc842d9f822d.png" class="latex" alt="$CE = \dfrac{6}{5} \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="90" height="37" />, and so by the Pythagorean Theorem <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/0/70016046222cee7d5e67e05bea5a7621b9fb8bc0.png" class="latex" alt="$AE = \dfrac{\sqrt{153}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="97" height="41" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a216050437be6388710bc1358890e2cbcdcfc139.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = \dfrac{\sqrt{328}}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="98" height="41" />. Because the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> of tetrahedron <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> passes touches plane <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7ada763e99852a6e44a15d46cce3c3709a944d5.png" class="latex" alt="$BCD$" width="44" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7baf95ecafc2d7791788ec79aeeae390d60b119.png" class="latex" alt="$BE$" width="28" height="12" />, it is also an altitude of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd85454c0ce4000c0e63b4a1c4a12ef6f8b56e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$ABE$" width="42" height="13" />. The area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> of triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd85454c0ce4000c0e63b4a1c4a12ef6f8b56e9d.png" class="latex" alt="$ABE$" width="42" height="13" /> is, by Heron's Formula, given by
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/d/ecd7a18d42aa428cb4acd4a869efb7f0c32c7226.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[16A^2 = 2a^2 b^2 + 2b^2 c^2 + 2c^2 a^2 - a^4 - b^4 - c^4 = -(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)^2 + 4a^2 b^2.\]" width="497" height="19" />
Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/a/f0afda05a52c5b7653505b24c3b3e35d10f27c15.png" class="latex" alt="$a = AE, b = BE, c = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="180" height="16" /> and performing huge (but manageable) computations yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/7/ef72b5680321165fa9c6ad6b8f8afe99a9092ebd.png" class="latex" alt="$A^2 = 18$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/a/3cad82faa3c807ce8a5d17e45573b0cf0562544a.png" class="latex" alt="$A = 3\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="71" height="18" />. Thus, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" /> is the length of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> of the tetrahedron, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/9/a994fa6023c98ea4fb6d75ddfa2c581f8f1c5583.png" class="latex" alt="$BE \cdot h = 2A = 6\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="157" height="18" />. Our answer is thus
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/7/417426f51d86f6b908bb87306831529d93a55064.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[V = \dfrac{1}{3} Bh = \dfrac{1}{3} h \cdot BE \cdot \dfrac{6\sqrt{2}}{5} = \dfrac{24}{5},\]" width="267" height="41" />
and so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/5/515a189da60e45d14676657c952371171e2858e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) } \dfrac{24}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="69" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Similar to solution 1, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e6a9c34f55e6bf9647cc400a91a99004d5ca42a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CTD$" width="58" height="13" /> is an isosceles right triangle. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> is perpendicular to the plane <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/2/252b4379bd7d4f56ca4cee487817e25303f43506.png" class="latex" alt="$CTD$" width="42" height="12" />. So, we can cut tetrahedron <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> into 2 tetrahedrons <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/5/c/a5c9020d08ccdd4c1451fa07d8331ff43a56abde.png" class="latex" alt="$ACTD$" width="56" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/e/50ec58caf6150c829b01f41d132b75d6b98e2b84.png" class="latex" alt="$BCTD$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="60" height="14" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/6/4e6a9c34f55e6bf9647cc400a91a99004d5ca42a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle CTD$" width="58" height="13" /> as their common base, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/c/69c58bfed7dc75f115a1dd50937a19ab6529aefa.png" class="latex" alt="$BT$" width="27" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/5/8d5b93dbd5e47d3865c56f03ec1213bb82256a67.png" class="latex" alt="$AT$" width="26" height="13" /> as their heights.
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00eb2e5da3586a39b7c2459b636f28806e91753e.png" class="latex" alt="$[CTD]=\frac{1}{2} \cdot CT \cdot DT=\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{12}{5} \cdot \frac{12}{5}= \frac{72}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="329" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/8/0b82e26dac4af525adac3f418b6e40dcc50a09fc.png" class="latex" alt="$V_{ABCD}=V_{ACDT}+V_{BCDT}=\frac{1}{3} \left([CTD] \cdot AT + [CTD] \cdot BT \right)=\frac{1}{3} \cdot [CTD] \cdot (AT+BT)=\frac{1}{3} \cdot [CTD] \cdot AB  =\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{72}{25} \cdot 5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="919" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/c/3ec1089253412f95b92f45c34280a982b4a2d928.png" class="latex" alt="$= \boxed{\textbf{(C) } \dfrac{24}{5}}$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="89" height="49" />
