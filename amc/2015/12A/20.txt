<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/3/6d3908bb6568001af287bff3d6b420a82367f6da.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 8 \cdot 3 = 12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="99" height="37" /> and the perimeter is 18.
</p><p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/81366638e694deb1959c886bb89a07f71eff44a5.png" class="latex" alt="$T&#39;$" width="16" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/c/c6c7ade345e239cecdc3a830ef7966aae353c3aa.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{1}{2} b \sqrt{a^2 - (\dfrac{b}{2})^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="112" height="43" /> and the perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/f/a3f62722cb258211df52beca5271d3f7130ee1fc.png" class="latex" alt="$2a + b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="49" height="14" />.
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a285ac163c84282bb1f65f43db5286edec2d6551.png" class="latex" alt="$2a + b = 18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/109f1ef7a81c7df5cab0138d634daf784a9cf772.png" class="latex" alt="$2a = 18 - b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" />.
</p><p>Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/a/b1a34d74db29437f74d18085ff99f0088deca74f.png" class="latex" alt="$12 = \dfrac{1}{2} b \sqrt{a^2 - (\dfrac{b}{2})^2}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="155" height="43" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/4/824417fa6c08bac0758f0146d7f4a5dcb7070a99.png" class="latex" alt="$48 = b \sqrt{4a^2 - b^2} = b \sqrt{(18 - b)^2 - b^2} = b \sqrt{324 - 36b}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="409" height="32" />.
</p><p>We square and divide 36 from both sides to obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/a/bca40791ca2e4a39a84e1bc4daabe4f3da0ffe3c.png" class="latex" alt="$64 = b^2 (9 - b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="109" height="19" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/9/dd9174b74554fe287ebf2d7583a5806ed7d0e171.png" class="latex" alt="$b^3 - 9b^2 + 64 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="135" height="16" />. Since we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b447277e683dbf98cb0cbcbddf0debfd717e1c0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 8$" width="40" height="12" /> is a solution, we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/d/96d6d95c2e9ed89c3a7618cea76308c041d793cc.png" class="latex" alt="$b - 8$" width="38" height="12" /> to get the other solution. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d370a0645abe2af2116a2b2b3eaa4fdfbc7febf6.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 - b - 8 = 0$" width="109" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/d/bcde2537337dcbdd69ee8c3d8dd54db4a0737c13.png" class="latex" alt="$b = \dfrac{1 + \sqrt{33}}{2} &lt; \dfrac{1 + 6}{2} = 3.5.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="220" height="41" /> The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26b24dfda9c973a310d9a971b5ec21834e73f10d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1.1">Solution 1.1</span></h3>
<p>The area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />, the semiperimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/0/2704c86dbc040d3d9fa78d765cf9574cad77470c.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 9 - \frac12b$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="37" />. Using Heron's formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db6c913dcb9526ed029384cad9ab6dff0e633aed.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{9\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)(9-b)} = 12$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="209" height="53" />. Squaring both sides and simplifying, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62e2f8c391948a4335f19dde426f3da20d78e191.png" class="latex" alt="$-b^3+9b^2-64=0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="16" />. Since we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b447277e683dbf98cb0cbcbddf0debfd717e1c0.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 8$" width="40" height="12" /> is a solution, we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/d/96d6d95c2e9ed89c3a7618cea76308c041d793cc.png" class="latex" alt="$b - 8$" width="38" height="12" /> to get the other solution. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/7/d370a0645abe2af2116a2b2b3eaa4fdfbc7febf6.png" class="latex" alt="$b^2 - b - 8 = 0$" width="109" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/d/bcde2537337dcbdd69ee8c3d8dd54db4a0737c13.png" class="latex" alt="$b = \dfrac{1 + \sqrt{33}}{2} &lt; \dfrac{1 + 6}{2} = 3.5.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="220" height="41" /> The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26b24dfda9c973a310d9a971b5ec21834e73f10d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" />, being isosceles, has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/3/4c3770a98a005e5e16663fbead85d5e23d3c54b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(8)\sqrt{5^2-4^2}=12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="148" height="37" /> and a perimeter of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/3/c2312b9ca68dd42131241ca63d9c6b73ef03991e.png" class="latex" alt="$5+5+8=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" />.
Triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/81366638e694deb1959c886bb89a07f71eff44a5.png" class="latex" alt="$T&#39;$" width="16" height="14" /> similarly has an area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/b/99b22e0843e4becc44d782c604461595b78e9bbc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(b)\bigg(\sqrt{a^2-\frac{b^2}{4}}\bigg)=12$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="179" height="46" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd8d913950dc6bb52df4a68019053c4e79be030a.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+b=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />.
</p><p>Now we apply our computational fortitude.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/4/404f5910ccf93200c3b4bde321a1586da4a749d6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{2}(b)\bigg(\sqrt{a^2-\frac{b^2}{4}}\bigg)=12\]" width="177" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6aca1ba6b0c4299631fcf4a8c25c32df78a52439.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(b)\bigg(\sqrt{a^2-\frac{b^2}{4}}\bigg)=24\]" width="165" height="46" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/d/c2d329c7a0953ecd5c62309eb774cc215b5d576e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(b)\sqrt{4a^2-b^2}=48\]" width="141" height="21" />

<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/7/9a70abc0c3b5c4afa3f8726d5ea9070a2053ad45.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b^2(2a+b)(2a-b)=48^2\]" width="188" height="19" />
Plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/8/fd8d913950dc6bb52df4a68019053c4e79be030a.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+b=18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" /> to obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/2/4423d55d0ee00e5d352e2f78639c7c35fcf659cd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[18b^2(2a-b)=48^2\]" width="143" height="19" />

Plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/4/4a4bbf32d4a3994c6685b528bf94ec17ab8412f7.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=18-b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> to obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/6/0/060d612ce581dcf65e26a38729d2406060adf449.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b^2(18-2b)=128\]" width="137" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/0/d4081941bec5feb1777ce36299433556df4a6e34.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2b^3-18b^2+128=0\]" width="162" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/b/19b04fbeca159dd86c318ef81b24755b2da3f38b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b^3-9b^2+64=0\]" width="134" height="16" />
We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/c/b2ccc9e3c02a4b38f185d5b43a4b7456338e30a6.png" class="latex" alt="$b=8$" width="40" height="12" /> is a valid solution by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/5/5/2554b6496c3b678897e9b060ef00aa9f0a7d7ece.png" class="latex" alt="$T$" width="13" height="12" />. Factoring out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/e/a1e02d0c9d01c03e32236923457c82f2f61e938f.png" class="latex" alt="$b-8$" width="38" height="12" />, we obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/4/2e4792223535e4ce3480ab4f27b923ccf53bf2f3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(b-8)(b^2-b-8)=0 \Rightarrow b^2-b-8=0\]" width="313" height="19" />
Utilizing the quadratic formula gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/9/239db30bdd7a28217f6c751326d20b2c826cbaaa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b=\frac{1\pm\sqrt{33}}{2}\]" width="98" height="41" />
We clearly must pick the positive solution. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Triangle T has perimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/1/54170a459de7dd548185741a0848c81252639d8a.png" class="latex" alt="$5 + 5 + 8 = 18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="113" height="14" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/3/5a3c4d0aae88771fb64acb0a012f086f16502829.png" class="latex" alt="$18 = 2a + b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="91" height="14" />.
</p><p>Using Heron's, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/1/de1e58816486231a29401698cc8fce4127489523.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{(9)(4)^2(1)} = \sqrt{(\frac{2a+b}{2})\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2(\frac{2a-b}{2})}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="323" height="53" />.
</p><p>We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/8/a285ac163c84282bb1f65f43db5286edec2d6551.png" class="latex" alt="$2a + b = 18$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" /> from above so we plug that in, and we also know that then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/a/ffabb821556de6002e92721f5bf519815fdc0c3f.png" class="latex" alt="$2a - b = 18 - 2b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="130" height="13" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/3/e435beb2a9deae97e0567fd82e64a891294b48f6.png" class="latex" alt="$12 = \frac{3b}{2}\sqrt{9-b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="118" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/0/98073a0d344921606c532e4eee3320f3a2839377.png" class="latex" alt="$64 = 9b^2 - b^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="15" />
</p><p>We plug in 3 for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> in the LHS, and we get 54 which is too low. We plug in 4 for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> in the LHS, and we get 80 which is too high. We now know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> is some number between 3 and 4. 
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/4/be4c0d27fc46f001a301b2873c290c638c7f88d9.png" class="latex" alt="$b \geq 3.5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="54" height="15" />, then we would round up to 4, but if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adc6304379943e9adb199e67a25cbbf19cfc3440.png" class="latex" alt="$b &lt; 3.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />, then we would round down to 3. So let us plug in 3.5 for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/5/6f5beac1486d8b43f365cc7215f1d00c7b435fbb.png" class="latex" alt="$67.375$" width="49" height="12" /> which is too high, so we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adc6304379943e9adb199e67a25cbbf19cfc3440.png" class="latex" alt="$b &lt; 3.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="54" height="13" />.
</p><p>Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26b24dfda9c973a310d9a971b5ec21834e73f10d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A) }3}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />
</p>
