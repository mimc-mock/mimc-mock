<p>What is the minimum number of digits to the right of the decimal point needed to express the fraction <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abee710ba93bd9619bd3e0d2821d027f0278f31c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{123456789}{2^{26}\cdot 5^4}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="37" /> as a decimal?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/9/c697c7365e2ad38e52faa848d1cc41f009ae0333.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 4\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 22\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 26\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 30\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 104$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can rewrite the fraction as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/d/b6d710910e5aafbc58b027e85340bedc12d33e5e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{123456789}{2^{22} \cdot 10^4} = \frac{12345.6789}{2^{22}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="199" height="38" />. Since the last digit of the numerator is odd, a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> is added to the right if the numerator is divided by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />, and this will continuously happen because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, itself, is odd. Indeed, this happens twenty-two times since we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> twenty-two times, so we will need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/1/1d111a5e00ee5ea6700bb629994dc629874c505b.png" class="latex" alt="$22$" width="17" height="12" /> more digits. Hence, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b41afc8e90f89771b9e0b99e5de016dd7dac1f17.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 22 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="137" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Multiply the numerator and denominator of the fraction by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/4094584ecd0c794627c19d555a07f50605d7436c.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{22}$" width="21" height="15" /> (which is the same as multiplying by 1) to give <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/d/24d157d54fe3634a637f54319cfbc2c450cdd996.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5^{22} \cdot 123456789}{10^{26}}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="120" height="39" />. Now, instead of thinking about this as a fraction, think of it as the division calculation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/1/ea15d2fc44f0e98bb7cdd011e6c66b864ff6cd8e.png" class="latex" alt="$(5^{22} \cdot 123456789) \div 10^{26}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="185" height="19" /> . The dividend is a huge number, but we know it doesn't have any digits to the right of the decimal point. Also, the dividend is not a multiple of 10 (it's not a multiple of 2), so these 26 divisions by 10 will each shift the entire dividend one digit to the right of the decimal point. Thus, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/5/fb5b036cf7c3a0e86d2d77ee7151f2831c1bcc90.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" /> is the minimum number of digits to the right of the decimal point needed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>The denominator is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/c/8bcc3d1c3d74ff58db775b62c27a77e7b984f5c1.png" class="latex" alt="$10^4 \cdot 2^{22}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />. Each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> adds one digit to the right of the decimal, and each additional <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> adds another digit. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/1/b41afc8e90f89771b9e0b99e5de016dd7dac1f17.png" class="latex" alt="$4 + 22 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="137" height="28" />.
</p>
