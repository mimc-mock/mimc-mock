<p>A league with 12 teams holds a round-robin tournament, with each team playing every other team exactly once. Games either end with one team victorious or else end in a draw. A team scores 2 points for every game it wins and 1 point for every game it draws. Which of the following is NOT a true statement about the list of 12 scores?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/f/2ff63e41107e40bf0223474c343bdf5a4a92eb66.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{There must be an even number of odd scores.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{There must be an even number of even scores.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{There cannot be two scores of }0\text{.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{The sum of the scores must be at least }100\text{.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{The highest score must be at least }12\text{.}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="370" height="96" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2><p>
We can eliminate answer choices <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/6/a46f6af01fcf3bb5f6cac2316bc4de9bb3906bec.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/1/b7193f66028e13f331d53ff5e6942c4b05587465.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> because there are an even number of scores, so if one is false, the other must be false too. Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" /> must be true since every team plays every other team, so it is impossible for two teams to lose every game. Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/0/b305929a95b53f14a0ad53eba1d8560626e1f28b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="28" height="17" /> must be true since each game gives out a total of two points, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f2f414df23bd427f5e77eabc86dd9c5b313e46.png" class="latex" alt="$\binom{12}{2} = 66$" style="vertical-align: -23px" width="91" height="55" /> games, for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/5/17531b756bc19a5c07c7f1ab8ce1cddf4bb4097c.png" class="latex" alt="$132$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> points. Answer choice <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/f/6bfc0829f3f29bbf35d0a861644e00cfef0f50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="28" /> is false. If everyone draws each of their 11 games, then every team will tie for first place with 11 points each.</p><li>
Quick question, why does the answer key say 3?
