<p>Two of the three sides of a triangle are 20 and 15. Which of the following numbers is not a possible perimeter of the triangle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/a/10a731032c53ae3862252152289d1224277b31c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 52\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 57\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 62\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 67\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 72$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Letting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the third side, then by the triangle inequality, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/a/71a10c432509ff670fe7ff0a22770da585fbb483.png" class="latex" alt="$20-15 &lt; x &lt; 20+15$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="175" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/8/7181fcbb3665195a9c6f509a6f59b59fcebc49fe.png" class="latex" alt="$5 &lt; x &lt; 35$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="85" height="13" />. Therefore the perimeter must be greater than 40 but less than 70. 72 is not in this range, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d5e714b804d77b9aa7e45c16fbe405ae72e76a.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \, 72}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="60" height="28" /> is our answer.
</p>
