<p>Integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/8763b276b45bcaa9d42bb9c7638fdd70404e7f55.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;y&gt;0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/e/4de8e71c5517cf931470ee4a9de1af391215eaf3.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+xy=80$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="126" height="16" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/e/54e59c3ca4fc7ec3f402de90142f41b9cfdc58ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 8 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 10 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 15 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 18 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 26$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Use <a href="/wiki/index.php/SFFT" class="mw-redirect" title="SFFT">SFFT</a> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/8/93878f38ef9f5fcbe926cd28ed0ca73f5cde9412.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)(y+1)=81$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />. The terms <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/e/00ed6a7b53eca338b27229604a5e8706ca609ccb.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/f/92f974289e02b578ca91484886e41551ed32163c.png" class="latex" alt="$(y+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> must be factors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, which include <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/5/5d55029dcf0b5a9d762573ee0f8d0b096942684b.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 3, 9, 27, 81$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/7/ab7da572d60053bdb3ab92f2dda44f87cd9dc78d.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/c/c/cccb103143393c0119f5ab61d6d53a852ce70fcf.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1$" width="40" height="13" /> is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/1051e7d41ec0b8bd688a872be17538413f4ee97b.png" class="latex" alt="$27$" width="18" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/e/87edbdda753b32453027dc7cab023c0743b6b243.png" class="latex" alt="$81$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. But if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/7/677170ac927390c7040695bf5690118f2526d143.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1=81$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="83" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/8/ec89f09c84538ec43930ca5d8e5e0957e2000822.png" class="latex" alt="$y=0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/8/398556637e7aa60fc829a2c8354e77b0c1baed8a.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\boxed{\textbf{(E)}\ 26}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="97" height="28" />.
</p>
