<p>Rational numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are chosen at random among all rational numbers in the interval <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/2/cb2c09c4c1e38cc47d3799148e1a4962e4769329.png" class="latex" alt="$[0,2)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="36" height="18" /> that can be written as fractions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/0/c7075ac4cf9ebeb3cfc6c81600ae60d3a822651b.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{d}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="13" height="33" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are integers with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e615b84a56b17140c16006c4778fdccbc3677754.png" class="latex" alt="$1 \le d \le 5$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="75" height="15" />. What is the probability that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/7/887b74a71c3746ec5fd9fcee5cfd177bf7857b95.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(\text{cos}(a\pi)+i\text{sin}(b\pi))^4\]" width="158" height="19" />
is a real number?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b98308e7c8b61b3b4721188e678eb457231838fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{3}{50} \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \frac{4}{25} \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{41}{200} \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \frac{6}{25} \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \frac{13}{50}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="422" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/6/c06854afb10a2f81b2ddd322e22de184958afb48.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi) = x$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f0fdaf0ce1a9bb1f189bd202c617f4868e8fc97.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi) = y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" />. Consider the binomial expansion of the expression:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe580af62ccb9011db0354e18e5736994df1fc83.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^4 + 4ix^{3}y - 6x^{2}y^{2} - 4ixy^3 + y^4.\]" width="256" height="18" />
</p><p>We notice that the only terms with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> are the second and the fourth terms. Thus for the expression to be a real number, either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80384fa8231931383f1c8f07aaf90ef5788c5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbf330abfb62d42567c39040fc195bfdf15e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, or the second term and the fourth term cancel each other out (because in the fourth term, you have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/8/fb8967fa8f1191c7007868db973f7b78ee27012f.png" class="latex" alt="$i^2 = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />). 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/b/8bbec6f3c020e1540ba2deb53c24259a1b40e104.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Case~1:}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" /> Either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80384fa8231931383f1c8f07aaf90ef5788c5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbf330abfb62d42567c39040fc195bfdf15e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>The two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/c/f1c56ae05ba7eda68a32f702bb7ca6188e0755ce.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{a&#39;s}$" width="19" height="11" /> satisfying this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/8/218c6b3e76211f744168e77b8ad9822ccce27d1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/5/85524016576d4135def3798324635e6abd32e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" />, and the two <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a77199e66c327c4bdeb2f9ed1eaa9ce047bd7cce.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{b&#39;s}$" width="19" height="12" /> satisfying this are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> can both be expressed as fractions with a denominator less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> possible values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/6/d96384467795b4b2590a44c7593ce217f621fd84.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[0, 1, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{2}, \frac{1}{3},\]" width="93" height="37" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/a/b6a3aacf909f6b7c2285c48d019e44b0a849f68d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{2}{3}, \frac{4}{3}, \frac{5}{3}, \frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4},\]" width="100" height="38" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/7/e97f8c91026294aa3ba16b5fb51904d9046d0c29.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{5}{4}, \frac{7}{4}, \frac{1}{5}, \frac{2}{5}, \frac{3}{5},\]" width="100" height="38" /> 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/04533746d8fbc090b0b650797e63e464b693dcd3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{4}{5}, \frac{6}{5}, \frac{7}{5}, \frac{8}{5}, \text{and} \frac{9}{5}.\]" width="126" height="37" />
</p><p>Calculating the total number of sets of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> results in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/8/588a3d3a75dbb888495ef4c96883e9714a12b626.png" class="latex" alt="$20 \cdot 20 = 400$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" /> sets.
Calculating the total number of invalid sets (sets where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> doesn't equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/8/218c6b3e76211f744168e77b8ad9822ccce27d1c.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/5/85524016576d4135def3798324635e6abd32e4fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> doesn't equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />), resulting in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/e/efe961951946002648510c4067674406e6b27392.png" class="latex" alt="$(20-2) \cdot (20-2) = 324$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="191" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus the number of valid sets is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/5/1d5db6ea360f9b1367e4db85c18474906401e2c0.png" class="latex" alt="$76$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/6/9c648930ca3574d34e6c5c720106db4211c4e56a.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{Case~2}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="47" height="12" />: The two terms cancel. 
</p><p>We then have: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/b/8fb20bd3a75f170c2f0dafdfe05fa7583e2ac95f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos^3(a\pi) \cdot \sin(b\pi) = \cos(a\pi) \cdot \sin^3(b\pi).\]" width="297" height="20" />
</p><p>So: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/3/823895012a7023a7937814fe53aeb751e16df81e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\cos^2(a\pi) = \sin^2(b\pi),\]" width="157" height="20" />
</p><p>which means for a given value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80384fa8231931383f1c8f07aaf90ef5788c5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbf330abfb62d42567c39040fc195bfdf15e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> valid values(one in each quadrant).
</p><p>When either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80384fa8231931383f1c8f07aaf90ef5788c5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbf330abfb62d42567c39040fc195bfdf15e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> are equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, however, there are only two corresponding values. We don't count the sets where either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80384fa8231931383f1c8f07aaf90ef5788c5c6.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/c/77cbf330abfb62d42567c39040fc195bfdf15e12.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, for we would get repeated sets. We also exclude values where the denominator is an odd number, for we cannot find any corresponding values(for example, if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/0/ac043e0180c67606f9dde95f72b5796854441bff.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{1}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="9" height="21" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/7099205c806b54b82e3627284c199004d946076e.png" class="latex" alt="$\tfrac{3}{10}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="15" height="21" />, which we don't have). Thus the total number of sets for this case is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/f/b4f068deb168a4268421e9270e8ef57993c4548d.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 4 + 2 \cdot 2 = 20$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="126" height="14" />.
</p><p>Thus, our final answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/7/fd73d2e7ca5392e0ad8ca2567405737e01977cee.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{(20 + 76)}{400} = \frac{6}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="121" height="39" />, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/c/dcc93afd012d6af697c4fae71d14de49f021d78e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="37" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Multiplying complex numbers is equivalent to multiplying their magnitudes and summing their angles. In order for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/7/2a79b54351bd10c6a18820ae54d29e809bb7d11f.png" class="latex" alt="$(\cos(a\pi)+i\sin(b\pi))^4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="166" height="19" /> to be a real number, then the angle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ecc671c79ed5aaa37c8e8424e5a21f82c072f06.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)+i\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" /> must be a multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/821d3f11ab7a3e93c7a404a34224228f0449505a.png" class="latex" alt="$45^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="24" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/c/8ecc671c79ed5aaa37c8e8424e5a21f82c072f06.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)+i\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="18" /> satisfies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/b/dab284f67b44fdedc1bf7d08c9031e52adf6da54.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)=0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="94" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/1/8a1406c119e0d3ac896805edcc85eafd1bf1f0fd.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi)=0$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="90" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/2/b225a63c9a23216dbdfeafea1c5558eb3cfaba47.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)=\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="140" height="20" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/4/27413708684df22d3a95ae8ac1fcc97f9e32ea04.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi)=-\sin(b\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="158" height="20" />. 
</p><p>There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/a/2aae6a2f62a3437a1707f0ebe392151398573f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(x\pi) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="90" height="18" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/5/035eeb8edb8d48fb27f890ecff194845fba44e90.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \{0,1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/4/0c4e01502dcf6a1409157e9e5a91800e01f52910.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(x\pi) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" /> at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/5/5c5ea262518647a27bbe96b77d6c6b0e5c5ea53f.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \{\frac12, \frac32\}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="86" height="37" />. The probability of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/4/494d7058d2b6e2e7105dcce15ca97292b664b917.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="88" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/a/01ac19df475912842f9cf43da9535d1790a7f48f.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos(a\pi) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d36eb5c76c7419957c9125abd658611c5a7ade8.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{100}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="118" height="37" /> (We overcounted the case where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/4/cb4d0256b839f604b33e8e8f6227ff76acebcd1a.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin = \cos = 0$" width="103" height="12" />.)
</p><p>We also consider the case where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/4/c9443ac04b130deddb0fe96a948952c94eaa18de.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin(b\pi) = \pm \cos(a\pi)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="154" height="18" />. This only happens when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/b/b2b55f26ed45953c1f6704bb6645726fe98788e6.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \{0,1\}, b = \{\frac12, \frac32\}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="169" height="37" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/e/8ced19cc55161d2a9d682b21659708bf2afc8b8f.png" class="latex" alt="$a,b = \{ \frac14, \frac34, \frac54, \frac74 \}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="143" height="38" />. The probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/f/ebf7d65098554797c23b9297eb42e89bc6f21979.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{100} + \frac{1}{25}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="74" height="37" /> The total probability is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/9/9e9cfd178ee480eddb41dc40c6272681082c0d0a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac15 + \frac{1}{25} = \frac{6}{25} \rightarrow\boxed{\text{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="169" height="37" />
</p><p>~zeric
