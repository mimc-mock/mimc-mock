<p>Sreshtha needs to estimate the quantity <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/5/a45298fa9056b36e1c666ee30086abcb2443528d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b} - c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="33" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/7/9f73d811eca6419d9c9a27423331d71ff06eb957.png" class="latex" alt="$a, b,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="29" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> are large positive integers. She rounds each of the integers so that the calculation will be easier to do mentally. In which of these situations will her answer necessarily be greater than the exact value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/5/a45298fa9056b36e1c666ee30086abcb2443528d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b} - c$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="44" height="33" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/7/d77a41fb998ee001f3a95344a347ea7d1a7dfe6e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \text{She rounds all three numbers up.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \text{She rounds } a \text{ and } b \text{ up, and she rounds } c \text{down.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \text{She rounds } a \text{ and } c \text{ up, and she rounds } b \text{down.} \\ \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \text{She rounds } a \text{ up, and she rounds } b \text{ and } c \text{down.}\\ \qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \text{She rounds } c \text{ up, and she rounds } a \text{ and } b \text{down.}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="375" height="96" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>To maximize our estimate, we want to maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084079bf1d54500f3c59257a7ec3467932b72ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" /> and minimize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, because both terms are positive values. Therefore we round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> down. To maximize <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/4/084079bf1d54500f3c59257a7ec3467932b72ba2.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{a}{b}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="12" height="33" />, round <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> up and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> down. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/1742d593ef6fff4a36153e80d0c3151ac9f16eaf.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow \boxed{\textbf{(D)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />
</p>
