<p>What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> for which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/9/9e931d3820fca8171c0a5788cbc9f5a652ea360f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{\text{log}_2a} + \frac{1}{\text{log}_3a} + \frac{1}{\text{log}_4a} = 1$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="203" height="41" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/2/c223e393487540a4e13959b02dd46bc83a82afef.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 12\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 18\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 24\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 36$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="385" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We use the change of base formula to show that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/3/f73e271cefc08bdc7f68146a8b825b7186a45f32.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_a b = \dfrac{\log_b b}{\log_b a} = \dfrac{1}{\log_b a}.\]" width="187" height="42" />
Thus, our equation becomes
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/2/10290ea33d9b8f7e8c2fce22f914ab6872c10f8a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_a 2 + \log_a 3 + \log_a 4 = 1,\]" width="212" height="17" />
which becomes after combining:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/3/cd3467e85a1cde569caa3051ba6a69ec140d9b84.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_a 24 = 1.\]" width="89" height="17" />
Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/8/9/889b02cf1fc06c35b0822c3e6555b5d52091cfc2.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="52" height="12" />, and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/e/4feb78447beef6c93631b0912fb24e7936b6dad3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(D)}.$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="17" />
</p>
