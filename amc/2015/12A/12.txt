<p>The parabolas <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/3/0d378a20f6ff5fdb410d3cc766e8b29fd870e1fa.png" class="latex" alt="$y=ax^2 - 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/b/a8b1c322d02a45124028f44b1dcd92020ca740d2.png" class="latex" alt="$y=4 - bx^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="89" height="18" /> intersect the coordinate axes in exactly four points, and these four points are the vertices of a kite of area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/d/59d20d448d04681481e2e779ccae06af77a28316.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 1.5\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 2\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2.5\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="377" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Clearly, the parabolas must intersect the x-axis at the same two points. Their distance multiplied by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969fcf63b7f1f3b26d79c923c75bef3a76ffce13.png" class="latex" alt="$4 - (-2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> (the distance between the y-intercepts), all divided by 2 is equal to 12, the area of the kite (half the product of the diagonals). That distance is thus 4, and so the x-intercepts are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/1/b21d1ab618a3acdb90fa926a37a4e4f7568e7b49.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 0), (-2, 0).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" /> Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/2/8c2f27ea1bf92fbbf2ab0a3af4f1110db2f0e396.png" class="latex" alt="$0 = 4a - 2 \rightarrow a = 0.5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="168" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/5/c6568fafb6b25dfe1008e77ed92a97ad30a81271.png" class="latex" alt="$0 = 4 - 4b \rightarrow b = 1.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="154" height="13" /> Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1935e0ef34849e607a1ddf1af5845784839adb1c.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 1.5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="132" height="28" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>The parabolas must intersect the x-axis at the same two points for the kite to form. We find the x values at which they intersect by equating them and solving for x as shown below. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/6/0f601d1ddaf071ba0f385be4de7822e79c0c5034.png" class="latex" alt="$y = ax^2-2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="92" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d66c84377060b3c6314c17ed691d90bbfe01aca.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 4 - bx^2\rightarrow ax^2-2 = 4-bx^2\rightarrow (a+b)x^2 = 6 \rightarrow x = +\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="531" height="43" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/b/2fbf8890eeb8d3cb35d11909c1b5d760bb568bc1.png" class="latex" alt="$-\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="76" height="43" />. The x-values of the y-intercepts is 0, so we plug in zero in each of them and get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/a/62a76677add1d246708c86edfc7c4b8a40184914.png" class="latex" alt="$-2$" width="22" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. The area of a kite is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/5/ef5c6c2088811f159541a5bb36e50a4aecb5afc9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{d_1*d_2}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="53" height="37" />. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/6/44612d9d6ec37da7f4edd0f205d65cdac5954dc0.png" class="latex" alt="$d_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/d/e5ddb87a9b04d53528a050ec7075f38adeb281f2.png" class="latex" alt="$2+4 = 6$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="73" height="14" />. The <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/7/117778db99ba8932dc35128d085c2619f4f690a1.png" class="latex" alt="$d_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="15" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/a/8faf50e63df1785b307efda38115057e9b3ed178.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="71" height="43" />. Solving for the area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/b/14b0db661ff67916b6fdfaa9af1a9e9723384a13.png" class="latex" alt="$\rightarrow \dfrac{1}{2}*(6)*(2*\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}) = 12 \rightarrow (2*\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}) = 4 \rightarrow (\sqrt{\dfrac{6}{a+b}}) = 2 \rightarrow \dfrac{6}{a+b} = 4 \rightarrow  \dfrac{6}{4} = (a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="762" height="43" />, therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/3/1935e0ef34849e607a1ddf1af5845784839adb1c.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 1.5}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="132" height="28" />.
</p>
