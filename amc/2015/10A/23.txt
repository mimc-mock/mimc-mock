<p>The zeroes of the function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/f/a0f0226401b5eb8fafb7cbaed175fcbd0a7a7728.png" class="latex" alt="$f(x)=x^2-ax+2a$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="160" height="19" /> are integers. What is the sum of the possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad274005e946261334794d8f86f18afe302d69f9.png" class="latex" alt="$a?$" width="17" height="12" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/6/57622f0979b9576131025c1f364d7b564bd0e77e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 8\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 16\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 17\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 18$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="376" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>By Vieta's Formula, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the sum of the integral zeros of the function, and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is integral.
</p><p>Because the zeros are integral, the discriminant of the function, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d2918f3b554aa900474257ddd8ca4e12512adf3d.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 - 8a$" width="58" height="15" />, is a perfect square, say <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/5/0e5c83cc0c639fc2ed843bbe2d3d209fb478b104.png" class="latex" alt="$k^2$" width="16" height="15" />. Then adding 16 to both sides and completing the square yields
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f0fc54cac5d6efbe535f2c0592f14c11a50bde5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(a - 4)^2 = k^2 + 16.\]" width="146" height="19" />
Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/5/eb52dc1ab5b31bd0be858299953a121c87631dc8.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4)^2 - k^2 = 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="19" /> and
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/3/8035bbf221fd1d0b33f061a4dea96f3e9d32f20d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[((a-4) - k)((a-4) + k) = 16.\]" width="247" height="18" />
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae87759823804a2d852416e2599be4763f0189b5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4) - k = u$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="121" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/4/77429f40c6a70a7c437b557e0bf992b13ff47944.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-4) + k = v$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="120" height="18" />; then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659c24bb306049854da5e6c4e2a798e54e5e6086.png" class="latex" alt="$a-4 = \dfrac{u+v}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="109" height="35" /> and so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/5/025b538dba348020cff7b03bc92ea8981bdbc019.png" class="latex" alt="$a = \dfrac{u+v}{2} + 4$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="111" height="35" />. Listing all possible <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/1/e4162d79742879fcd196374e6e48a3f896087872.png" class="latex" alt="$(u, v)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> pairs (not counting transpositions because this does not affect (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/7/387885f0e6ec1245f344fd1b192210e4a689e7f6.png" class="latex" alt="$u + v$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="41" height="12" />), <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/1/d819cecbd136814e8e32ea0045847bbb7e18c441.png" class="latex" alt="$(2, 8), (4, 4), (-2, -8), (-4, -4)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="18" />, yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/a/5da24791113f6c68c3628a4f91dd894cfc2f9fd9.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 9, 8, -1, 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="108" height="16" />. These <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> sum to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, so our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db5946ec5998020933dc9ef4064e53212c6ff16.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/c/adc143c3f887fa7e92fd80d91bb22af51b4abd77.png" class="latex" alt="$r_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="13" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b4f3f4b698ce35667432041e168f7653533fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$r_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> be the integer zeroes of the quadratic. Since the coefficient of the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the quadratic can be written as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/4/614c04f57f14bc92e86d7d46dc87d373e868eb2a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x - r_1)(x - r_2)=x^2 - (r_1 + r_2)x + r_1r_2\]" width="317" height="19" />
</p><p>By comparing this with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/7/6d724e3e9b71a7da2b9b9ddd2630915f707de898.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - ax + 2a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="101" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/1/241dc901e5926dcdf5ebe9088377efdd9d039a41.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_1 + r_2 = a\text{ and }r_1r_2 = 2a.\]" width="202" height="15" />
</p><p>Plugging the first equation in the second, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/9/2c9944895d19b5830154a28bc2d0135db44595a1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_1r_2 = 2 (r_1 + r_2).\]" width="135" height="18" /> Rearranging gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/5/385a74a85285f7802322e2a000f55580aee560ad.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[r_1r_2 - 2r_1 - 2r_2 = 0\implies (r_1 - 2)(r_2 - 2) = 4.\]" width="366" height="18" /> These factors can be<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/c/76c6cdac16e2e13cb88903a99fa2d16e4075392a.png" class="latex" alt="$(1, 4), (-1, -4), (4, 1), (-4, -1), (2, 2),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="294" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/8/be8911f3bc861e6fd6245011518900d936f1dcd9.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, -2).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />
</p><p>We want the number of distinct <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142c43f4d3343ae49921f9385fb4078a2e2970ef.png" class="latex" alt="$a = r_1 + r_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="85" height="13" />, and these factors gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/7/247349f2616e60e215adaa20c359eb1451285cab.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -1, 0, 8, 9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="108" height="16" />. So the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/2/31218123ee0f3d06735d90dbf5b850c40af27f1b.png" class="latex" alt="$-1 + 0 + 8 + 9 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }16}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="206" height="28" />.
</p><p><br />
</p>
