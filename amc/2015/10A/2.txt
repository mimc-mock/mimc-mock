<p>A box contains a collection of triangular and square tiles. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> tiles in the box, containing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/3/4131457f67c5dbfd8b1c6be1f74a47595a9bd3be.png" class="latex" alt="$84$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="13" /> edges total. How many square tiles are there in the box?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/a/09a25db44360679e8957a6da81e2854a78406704.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 3\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 5\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 7\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 9\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="357" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> be the amount of triangular tiles and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> be the amount of square tiles.
</p><p>Triangles have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> edges and squares have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> edges, so we have a system of equations.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/f/5df6a591474ffd81edd37d8d9f52f6f476caa531.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> tiles total, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/d/b0dd2ebd4491bc6479d0745f529e4062c6d883ae.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = 25$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />.
</p><p>We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/1/4714cb10142cc9c27e5b5a60ac422e4e299551d7.png" class="latex" alt="$3a + 4b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> edges total, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/a/dba3032971384dbeaad77d25fa1b21e2f2b3cbc5.png" class="latex" alt="$3a + 4b = 84$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="100" height="14" />.
</p><p>Multiplying the first equation by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> on both sides gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/c/08c30f9fa35567e5bccaeab945d741cb6c0f2e04.png" class="latex" alt="$3a + 3b = 3(25) = 75$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="18" />.
</p><p>Second equation minus the first equation gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f0e621ddfd0f3f4728f2161b52587224c273dc7.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 9$" width="40" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8a806de3bdf2c155767a6042823fe88c689ecb.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>If all of the tiles were triangles, there would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7e4c2930d79c57bee209557767b878cd704ea46.png" class="latex" alt="$75$" width="17" height="12" /> edges. This is not enough, so there needs to be some squares. Trading a triangle for a square results in one additional edge each time, so we must trade out <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> triangles for squares. Answer: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8a806de3bdf2c155767a6042823fe88c689ecb.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> be the number of square tiles. A square has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> edges, so the total number of edges from the square tiles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/4/ed4977828049d99f20ccd3b4acb6bbd43c2eb70f.png" class="latex" alt="$4x$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="19" height="12" />. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> total tiles, which means that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/a/54af2a4d611b521e755c7c85c90b02abb570acb0.png" class="latex" alt="$25-x$" width="50" height="12" /> triangle tiles. A triangle has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> edges, so the total number of edges from the triangle tiles is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/1/1115a23338a27ee54415f76a1a9e2d0c4ce48a77.png" class="latex" alt="$3(25-x)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="72" height="18" />. Together, the total number of edges is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/1/f/e1f08ea84427b2154f30d47de2806695fd6bf703.png" class="latex" alt="$4x+3(25-x)=84$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="158" height="18" />. Solving our equation, we get that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/1/5b1d8cdec48abe8293a4169a8afce4fc8a8348e7.png" class="latex" alt="$x=9$" width="43" height="12" /> which means that our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/8/dd8a806de3bdf2c155767a6042823fe88c689ecb.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D) }9}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="56" height="28" />.
</p>
