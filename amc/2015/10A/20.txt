<p>A rectangle with positive integer side lengths in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/759ed967199e05a556aba1e4b227b37095ae3ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{cm}$" width="21" height="7" /> has area <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/0/bb06de78ae4ef6fc2731e0abb6788477c03a7b05.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{cm}^2$" width="27" height="15" /> and perimeter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/9/759ed967199e05a556aba1e4b227b37095ae3ec1.png" class="latex" alt="$\text{cm}$" width="21" height="7" />. Which of the following numbers cannot equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/9/cb92eedeffc22696985f815aba47291279faa1a8.png" class="latex" alt="$A+P$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="50" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/a/79a3cf01f61aa13c3444d9e91f95f0c6c21f041b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }100\qquad\textbf{(B) }102\qquad\textbf{(C) }104\qquad\textbf{(D) }106\qquad\textbf{(E) }108$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the rectangle's length be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and its width be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Its area is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/8/ef870ce9fefe09b80a97b05a7b9198414c1b35f7.png" class="latex" alt="$ab$" width="17" height="12" /> and the perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/a/8ba95891afa865e93ee9ea01d55a42ca847bfa72.png" class="latex" alt="$2a+2b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />.
</p><p>Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/1/f316ee64d5e151ca61ddf2d70d21427873462d0c.png" class="latex" alt="$A + P = ab + 2a + 2b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="172" height="14" />. Factoring, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/5/b45b5626e11a8cf75a34290fa2049524f74ac01c.png" class="latex" alt="$(a + 2)(b + 2) - 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="139" height="18" />.
</p><p>The only one of the answer choices that cannot be expressed in this form is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/9/419924dabadec1cbc40ad235df6230a545e2161f.png" class="latex" alt="$102$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/6/3b67bfabba243dd5b83234a61019e99c18cbbba0.png" class="latex" alt="$102 + 4$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> is twice a prime. There would then be no way to express <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/8/dc81bb8b1c37a6d437bed5cefe57e22af27ba6b4.png" class="latex" alt="$106$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/3/573926d8cff7fd549d948ced4487668620355943.png" class="latex" alt="$(a + 2)(b + 2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" />, keeping <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> as positive integers.
</p><p>Our answer is then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />.
</p><p>Note: The original problem only stated that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> were positive integers, not the side lengths themselves. This rendered the problem unsolvable, and so the AMC awarded everyone 6 points on this problem. This wiki has the corrected version of the problem so that the 2015 AMC 10A test can be used for practice.
</p>
