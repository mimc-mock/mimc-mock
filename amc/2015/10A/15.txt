<p>Consider the set of all fractions <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/2/e32f96b310bbe6cd6981b6c1da83fe7905ea3116.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="12" height="36" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are relatively prime positive integers. How many of these fractions have the property that if both numerator and denominator are increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, the value of the fraction is increased by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/5/2b56bd637cb2c936d539be1b5000a64a898af389.png" class="latex" alt="$10\%$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="32" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/5/c254268ca4a5d58cb8278dfd22ef798652bd1fe2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }0\qquad\textbf{(B) }1\qquad\textbf{(C) }2\qquad\textbf{(D) }3\qquad\textbf{(E) }\text{infinitely many}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="453" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>You can create the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/6/c86d9ee95fe68aaf28d6d87461954fbc8e772e6d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+1}{y+1}=\frac{11x}{10y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="101" height="40" />
</p><p>Cross multiplying and combining like terms gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/9/b69bfd92e44a61b3eee27de867dcdeb4f93298da.png" class="latex" alt="$xy + 11x - 10y = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="153" height="16" />.
</p><p>This can be factored into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/c/d8c748649b701f01be2f705490d6c7efd23e1eab.png" class="latex" alt="$(x - 10)(y + 11) = -110$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="194" height="18" />.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must be positive, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/e/cae4fd68f44ac9b909f8d91f9d498de5759bb45b.png" class="latex" alt="$x &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/2/f6267cf18ccdbddb223019a94263e5b80bafe617.png" class="latex" alt="$y &gt; 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7d330b5007085f3ddea5c7b982fc8d6d03b3558.png" class="latex" alt="$x - 10&gt; -10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/2/2426c21e40a76a08fcf00a98c2c6e93c3624765d.png" class="latex" alt="$y + 11 &gt; 11$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="15" />.
</p><p>Using the factors of 110, we can get the factor pairs: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/f/c8fb62c4db1a1fa0b0389195ecd0c7d03369e2e4.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 110),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="76" height="18" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/0/ff0532723e0773116898dab99414447688f25a63.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, 55),$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1cd2b290bdab78481f9a06ff024626f4c4cd20e.png" class="latex" alt="$(-5, 22).$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />
</p><p>But we can't stop here because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> must be relatively prime.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/7/5f788b31517ad7fd558eb149fd621353e5fd14fb.png" class="latex" alt="$(-1, 110)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/a/04a7a2005b67ab81ee5ff231b2f370f1d71ebe1a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 9$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/c/58c021c64fecaaf4d6a642c4ab3fb106f1598398.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 99$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> are not relatively prime, so this doesn't work.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/3/6d3cd12d31677995bcb39e0282b4abffdd212e99.png" class="latex" alt="$(-2, 55)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/4/62434ae80ca145c20f0fbc7563f5151371ed163c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 8$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba585c0c81fc1f3f70c362d5806356278f287a2.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 44$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="52" height="15" />. This doesn't work.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/d/68d28bf0f875ca05d4d0ed7dcd35c78637e16013.png" class="latex" alt="$(-5, 22)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c43970aa97091f384854d477a1e1e07356887266.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 5$" width="43" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/3/c832e4ebb5fc13830ee1f7897c5ac56e83f94e58.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 11$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="15" />. This does work.
</p><p>We found one valid solution so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e101201addd96f637069ef5899fa2b44223efc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The condition required is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/0/af0fd410f63e9ddc3d86483822a62651f2a5cb65.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x+1}{y+1}=\frac{11}{10}\cdot\frac{x}{y}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="117" height="40" />.
</p><p>Observe that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/e/12ecf0abf79cc52efa674d7e5b935e49cd90f413.png" class="latex" alt="$x+1 &gt; \frac{11}{10}\cdot x$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="111" height="37" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is at most <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/a/f6a1b9d5cec741422bfb37f91db47fd7514f5377.png" class="latex" alt="$9.$" width="12" height="12" />
</p><p>By multiplying by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e00997435b70ac3c4293ac773a5e6e2e5f691d27.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{y+1}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="43" height="37" /> and simplifying we can rewrite the condition as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/4/e8427d6f143a4af41120b0f340ebfbe0f4ff99cd.png" class="latex" alt="$y=\frac{11x}{10-x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="87" height="37" />.  Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are integer, this only has solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/7/ee7ab6aba0d6734ced8bb7bf5bfad36edd3dde0a.png" class="latex" alt="$x\in\{5,8,9\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="93" height="18" />.  However, only the first yields a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> that is relative prime to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.
</p><p>There is only one valid solution so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75e101201addd96f637069ef5899fa2b44223efc.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }1}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="55" height="28" />
</p>
