<p>How many terms are in the arithmetic sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/5/7d55eb78c27dcce1d6292a41d2bfa434703b275e.png" class="latex" alt="$\dotsc$" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa94edfb9739dcc66dad90f7dd5c148f44a57e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$70$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5b03f8ead3b3da0bd2cbc1a41d8f01630e638f4.png" class="latex" alt="$73$" width="17" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/9/d792c19ffbd61d37e6b7f89e0e5eb83fcc820ac1.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 20 \qquad\textbf{(B)} \ 21 \qquad\textbf{(C)} \ 24 \qquad\textbf{(D)} \ 60 \qquad\textbf{(E)} \ 61$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="393" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/0/c10c3f581edf1d01d3f25bbe35ce28b488d1c430.png" class="latex" alt="$73-13 = 60$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />, so the amount of terms in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/030e19e4e2f1c074127cb50581e8496470c5419f.png" class="latex" alt="$13$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/a/5/9a5b4928c8fe50ce3c2428da3bee3505e891b788.png" class="latex" alt="$16$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/5/7d55eb78c27dcce1d6292a41d2bfa434703b275e.png" class="latex" alt="$\dotsc$" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa94edfb9739dcc66dad90f7dd5c148f44a57e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$70$" width="17" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/b/b5b03f8ead3b3da0bd2cbc1a41d8f01630e638f4.png" class="latex" alt="$73$" width="17" height="12" /> is the same as in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/5/7d55eb78c27dcce1d6292a41d2bfa434703b275e.png" class="latex" alt="$\dotsc$" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/1/4b14ef560acadba8244952e9a8d5481f494445dc.png" class="latex" alt="$57$" width="18" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />. 
</p><p>In this sequence, the terms are the multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> going up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> multiples of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" />. 
</p><p>However, the number 0 must also be included, adding another multiple. So, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/a/3ba2aea54fdc49a6bb67bbef45f5b5d0bf1fe60f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Using the formula for arithmetic sequence's nth term, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/0/800ecaf277aca8c342b67984df84ae2d5468379b.png" class="latex" alt="$a + (n-1)d \Longrightarrow13 + (n-1)3 =73, \Longrightarrow n = 21$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="380" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/a/3ba2aea54fdc49a6bb67bbef45f5b5d0bf1fe60f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Minus each of the terms by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> to make the the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/6/8f605f1fb00f809f0990b75fa9ee7a4174a66485.png" class="latex" alt="$1 , 4 , 7,..., 61$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/f/a/afa589297d277f69fbd121ec94b60ec7089a2850.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{61-1}{3}=20, 20 + 1 = 21$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="198" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/a/3ba2aea54fdc49a6bb67bbef45f5b5d0bf1fe60f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Subtract each of the terms by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> to make the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/9/4591a48cff3f51a0afa26cb3cb9c0e70262c80ae.png" class="latex" alt="$3 , 6 , 9,..., 60, 63$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" />. Then divide the each term in the sequence by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/b/4ab0e0387eabf7b3323bd935d4c13c2519fd6237.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3,..., 20, 21$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="118" height="16" />. Now it is clear to see that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/5/f25d31faf472ddb4c698a14522fe8125c06f8dd8.png" class="latex" alt="$21$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> terms in the sequence.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/a/3ba2aea54fdc49a6bb67bbef45f5b5d0bf1fe60f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\ 21}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
