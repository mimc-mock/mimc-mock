<p>Cozy the Cat and Dash the Dog are going up a staircase with a certain number of steps. However, instead of walking up the steps one at a time, both Cozy and Dash jump. Cozy goes two steps up with each jump (though if necessary, he will just jump the last step). Dash goes five steps up with each jump (though if necessary, he will just jump the last steps if there are fewer than 5 steps left). Suppose that Dash takes 19 fewer jumps than Cozy to reach the top of the staircase. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> denote the sum of all possible numbers of steps this staircase can have. What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/7/e77d72b61774ffa9a5f1d9c0027fa70127a4f9cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\; 9 \qquad\textbf{(B)}\; 11 \qquad\textbf{(C)}\; 12 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\; 13 \qquad\textbf{(E)}\; 15$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="382" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We can translate this wordy problem into this simple equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/9/5399c60725f1698fac5b067a5c7b438e9a88236d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lceil \frac{s}{2} \right\rceil - 19 = \left\lceil \frac{s}{5} \right\rceil\]" width="120" height="33" />
</p><p>We will proceed to solve this equation via casework.
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/5/3e52703dde98ecb2e09ef3bb88a685b526a9729b.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lceil \frac{s}{2} \right\rceil = \frac{s}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="67" height="33" />
</p><p>Our equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/3/5d38e3789a8514f0d25b5525029ad11f4659661d.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{s}{2} - 19 = \frac{s}{5} + \frac{j}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="124" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bc53cb24d28e2c2c029b17794d5bfff2190c65.png" class="latex" alt="$j \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is an integer, we quickly find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/b/5db39120014c34e1b098a3090ebd37598244d404.png" class="latex" alt="$j=1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/3/1732b5fde3ea9e2af04da5b4036f9ba1c75f6f53.png" class="latex" alt="$j=4$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> yield <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/1/0a1e1e5f82e4c6fc07cdcbcec374b6643c0b3d31.png" class="latex" alt="$s=64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/f/55f95ddf5cf515c97217148ba9bf64a2cb881f85.png" class="latex" alt="$s=66$" width="50" height="12" />, respectively.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/125c32ac88fa241058a3c2497641ae36149be4c9.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lceil \frac{s}{2} \right\rceil = \frac{s}{2}+\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="103" height="37" />
</p><p>Our equation becomes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/b/d7b04619ed888c495dbe48bc126c5b73f068206a.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{s}{2} +\frac{1}{2} - 19 = \frac{s}{5} + \frac{j}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="160" height="37" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/b/68bc53cb24d28e2c2c029b17794d5bfff2190c65.png" class="latex" alt="$j \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> Using the fact that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> is an integer, we quickly find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/d/dbd69ff270802989ea1341802afdc8b0642e098e.png" class="latex" alt="$j=2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" /> yields <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/2/2c2b348ee51fb0b4b4c9e73b5079bfc456a7b499.png" class="latex" alt="$s=63$" width="50" height="12" />.
</p><p>Summing up we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfcf6090518f8995023f152af4b69eda295ca554.png" class="latex" alt="$63+64+66=193$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />. The sum of the digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1f368a77ceda15d80a4a3e72791b38344731c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>It can easily be seen that the problem can be expressed by the equation:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/3/db31687a3bec78b161b09ab99896aeef70796d67.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lceil \frac{s}{2} \right\rceil - \left\lceil \frac{s}{5} \right\rceil = 19\]" width="123" height="33" />
</p><p>However, because the ceiling function is difficult to work with, we can rewrite the previous equation as:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/2/c42764328029b40c5609a31ec7e07da598642fc4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{s+a}{2} - \frac{s+b}{5} = 19\]" width="149" height="37" /> Where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/6/1c6769dfdbf3f5f94997b0e607168e5b675e1193.png" class="latex" alt="$a \in \{0,1\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="75" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/747d209057eac5140c707c63d1b8e5b4a6b4bc66.png" class="latex" alt="$b \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="18" /> Multiplying both sides by ten and simplifying, we get:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/16918627cb9f43aa1751139fd70144afe783a38e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5s+5a-2s-2b=190\]" width="187" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/f/b1fc57d40a611f2aa8ef2fd2b8c0c47fab989298.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3s = 190+2b-5a\]" width="148" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/8/7e8ef54f46785762b1ca2b76bd7eac3a17afdc4c.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[s = 63 + \frac{1+2b-5a}{3}\]" width="164" height="37" />
</p><p>Because s must be an integer, we need to find the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/5/f95806ff0d6283dfc6d411365e575ff774aa0315.png" class="latex" alt="$2b-5a \equiv 2 \mod 3$" width="152" height="12" />. We solve using casework.
</p><p>Case 1: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/9/b5969c2cfb1ffadf981fbbddcf796c8eb0037546.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 0$" width="42" height="12" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/9/b5969c2cfb1ffadf981fbbddcf796c8eb0037546.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 0$" width="42" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/a/9ca78fb31aff58d22f7b6cb992115e6c9f4de888.png" class="latex" alt="$2b \equiv 2 \mod 3$" width="112" height="12" />. We can easily see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/1/851b03ce92c3ad8da97daf2785cf4e103c7a8d66.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="13" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efb996ca207df5d210cd86914aed764adb55fcb3.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="41" height="13" />, which when plugged into our original equation lead to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/6/9561b03beed2523903545c73e06e3d3ec6a47424.png" class="latex" alt="$s = 64$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/f/55f95ddf5cf515c97217148ba9bf64a2cb881f85.png" class="latex" alt="$s=66$" width="50" height="12" /> respectively.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Case 2: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />
</p><p>If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/d/48d02444a59b917b64e8bd0963b88712fc7fa0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/7/c67aa6afef96734223ccb5c9a8f4e2ec5e919ce5.png" class="latex" alt="$2b-5 \equiv 2 \mod 3$" width="143" height="12" />, which can be rewritten as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/1/571daecb9be5ef1473137a325c2afdcc80c1bcfb.png" class="latex" alt="$2b \equiv 1 \mod 3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="112" height="13" />. We can again easily see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/7/197213da9d06b2c5b59a10ad17f6a4aeb43d6697.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 2$" width="40" height="12" /> is the only solution, which when plugged into our original equation lead to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/e/93e38b2015ba7b34fb77dcf9eb8f0f15fb427078.png" class="latex" alt="$s = 63$" width="50" height="12" />.
</p><p>Adding these together we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/9/df9614cc0696511c8345ff5dceccb141ea8e3ca8.png" class="latex" alt="$64+66+63=193$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />. The sum of the digits is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1f368a77ceda15d80a4a3e72791b38344731c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>As before, we write the equation:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/d/f6d795f179cc739dd37b8bbb59074595a040f926.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left\lceil \frac{s}{2} \right\rceil - 19 = \left\lceil \frac{s}{5} \right\rceil.\]" width="131" height="33" />
</p><p>To get a ballpark estimate of where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> might lie, we remove the ceiling functions to find:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/6/986d6455dc57ae85807487e43faa6e2efc08e091.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{s}{2} - 19 = \frac{s}{5}.\]" width="92" height="32" />
</p><p>This gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/e/80e9e098ff1003ea6f071bb54afa52a3347db1c7.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{3s}{10} = 19$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="64" height="38" />, and thus values for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> will be around <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/6/876a442a3555a1babb308cd3de1ff56404917f77.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{190}{3} = 63.\overline3$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="88" height="37" />.
</p><p>Now, to establish some bounds around this estimated working value, we note that if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/3/6b36a0d42497737fe1bd3cf1a2489568dc6c1c85.png" class="latex" alt="$s=60$" width="50" height="12" />, Cozy takes 30 steps while Dash takes 12, a difference of 18.  If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/d/9fdcbcebf1d4b715c96a4e07b9f2fd3db169b3c2.png" class="latex" alt="$s=70$" width="50" height="12" />, Cozy takes 35 steps while Dash takes 14, a difference of 21.  When <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> increases from a multiple of ten, the difference will never decrease beyond what it is at the multiple of ten, and likewise, when it decreases, it never becomes greater than at the multiple of ten, so any working values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/7/f37bba504894945c07a32f5496d74299a37aa51c.png" class="latex" alt="$s$" width="8" height="8" /> will be between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/9/aa94edfb9739dcc66dad90f7dd5c148f44a57e2c.png" class="latex" alt="$70$" width="17" height="12" />.
</p><p>Then, by inspection, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/9/08993b9d49c904df47e8740963e47cc1d4bdc832.png" class="latex" alt="$s=63, 64,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/4/2d40f3652c1962e98ce050a88c04ed37997c67df.png" class="latex" alt="$66$" width="17" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/b/c0b084535f2f90470f59d0ae5436d79ce9ea7f83.png" class="latex" alt="$\sum s = 193 \implies \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="205" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Notice that the possible number of steps in the staircase is around 60 to 70. By testing all of the values between 60 and 70, we see that 63, 64 and 66 work. Adding those up gives 193, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/e/5ce3c31c88e90ea3db5c8bc5a887fec063ec8dbe.png" class="latex" alt="$1+9+3=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="163" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>We represent C's steps with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94b7568ea9949aef2be0a2cd868aca239fb8b339.png" class="latex" alt="$2c + a = n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="15" /> and D's steps with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba7560b3f0f4d4fa2e348036d7f88554dfe690c.png" class="latex" alt="$5d + b = n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="16" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/5/585563ee63058faea1420ffdad910ddf384babba.png" class="latex" alt="$a \in \{1,2\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="78" height="20" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/b/8db242d270e5514cd190eaa24404364550ec6cd7.png" class="latex" alt="$b \in \{1,2,3,4,5\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="128" height="20" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of steps, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> is the number of jumps C takes bar the last one, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> is the number of jumps D takes bar the last.
The reason for starting at 1 and ending at 5 instead of 0 through 4 is that the last step can be quite problematic to deal with, especially if it is possible to make it in one go, so we treat it as a different jump that can take all possible jump values. We know that D makes it in 19 fewer than C, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86cf5c1ba6b275027d98b8311155efc7ca42afc2.png" class="latex" alt="$c+1-(d+1) = 19 \implies c = 19+d \implies 5d + b = 2(19+d) + a \implies 3d = 38 + a - b$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="692" height="20" />.
</p><p>Now that we have this nice equivalence, we can do the thing and it works. 
</p><p>If we take both sides mod 3 and rearrange, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/f/73fdfc14d81bd88176acb776cf516d32ac3f4acd.png" class="latex" alt="$b \equiv 2+a \pmod{3}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="147" height="20" />
This gives us the following satisfactory <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8da0aef2d19017da7471378386d61620f288f5.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> relational pairs: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/9/7892f10c6e192d8680d53b6db9a59dcb4856aa56.png" class="latex" alt="$\{(1,3),(2,1),(2,4)\}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="157" height="20" />. We can now just find the corresponding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> value for each pair, sum it all up using <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/a/6ba7560b3f0f4d4fa2e348036d7f88554dfe690c.png" class="latex" alt="$5d + b = n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="86" height="16" /> and sum the digits to reveal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f1f368a77ceda15d80a4a3e72791b38344731c0.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\; 13}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" /> as the answer.
</p><p>~ <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/3/8/b38096ce59206eb4c0d50fb9b38f89ef03c30ee0.png" class="latex" alt="$\color{magenta} zoomanTV$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="14" />
</p>
