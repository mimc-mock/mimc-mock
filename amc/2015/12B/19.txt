<p>In <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9e702326a58a2848df8758e667ae40abdbfb150.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C = 90^\circ$" width="75" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/437e65d3735fb1dadf06a8dc9c3d588e451252d1.png" class="latex" alt="$AB = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="70" height="13" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/2/8920176a4e716a1f34ece4dd4064c47332817afb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C,M,E,W,Z,X,Y; A=(2,0); B=(0,2); C=(0,0); M=(A+B)/2; W=(-2,2); Z=(-2,-0); X=(2,4); Y=(4,2); E=(W+Z)/2; draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(W--B--C--Z--cycle); draw(A--B--X--Y--cycle); dot(M); dot(E); label(&quot;W&quot;,W,NW); label(&quot;Z&quot;,Z,SW); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,N); label(&quot;X&quot;,X,NE); label(&quot;Y&quot;,Y,SE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,1.5*plain.W); label(&quot;M&quot;,M,NE); draw(circle(M,sqrt(10))); [/asy]" width="252" height="232" />
First, we should find the center and radius of this circle. We can find the center by drawing the perpendicular bisectors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/f/92f6fdf2d48244959cf6c66b683a59d4e63e5423.png" class="latex" alt="$WZ$" width="33" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/7/2c7774174993ffa29a38cf577ae34d8e0a3a63cb.png" class="latex" alt="$XY$" width="30" height="12" /> and finding their intersection point. This point happens to be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />, the hypotenuse. Let this point be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" />. To find the radius, determine <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f996e2c8cd749cec374c35c1edf3b111653ba484.png" class="latex" alt="$MY$" width="33" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/4/76462718759863e14b44967eaf71bbef762f6314.png" class="latex" alt="$MY^{2} = MA^2 + AY^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="163" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/6/f5637d7edbc6ca9da31085a130a988572a2325a9.png" class="latex" alt="$MA = \frac{12}{2} = 6$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/c/a7c9b83479ebaa8fa2c22834ecfcf64308477fa9.png" class="latex" alt="$AY = AB = 12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="122" height="13" />. Thus, the radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/0/3a0c0d6fd9c2281f6bdde3fcb26552f5e8ff7554.png" class="latex" alt="$=r =MY = 6\sqrt5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="144" height="18" />.
</p><p>Next we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/90663d81d23546bf717871a40845d03186a489ff.png" class="latex" alt="$AC = b$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/7/9d78cd4394503dffb3e25f5cd3142ffde2c560fd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC = a$" width="62" height="12" />. Consider the right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/1/3/21361fc8f29984fa3e95bfc0e578efdbce08c736.png" class="latex" alt="$ACB$" width="42" height="13" /> first. Using the Pythagorean theorem, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/5/35538deb25b7b925f5f7e107d7afa0a31683d320.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + b^2 = 12^2 = 144$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="155" height="16" />. 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/8/418825a05d0e9886d73a80e1a7df49d85c83d235.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] pair A,B,C,M,E,W,Z,X,Y; A=(2,0); B=(0,2); C=(0,0); M=(A+B)/2; W=(-2,2); Z=(-2,-0); X=(2,4); Y=(4,2); E=(W+Z)/2; draw(A--B--C--cycle); draw(W--B--C--Z--cycle); draw(A--B--X--Y--cycle); dot(M); dot(E); label(&quot;W&quot;,W,NW); label(&quot;Z&quot;,Z,SW); label(&quot;C&quot;,C,S); label(&quot;A&quot;,A,S); label(&quot;B&quot;,B,N); label(&quot;X&quot;,X,NE); label(&quot;Y&quot;,Y,SE); label(&quot;E&quot;,E,1.5*plain.W); label(&quot;M&quot;,M,NE); draw(circle(M,sqrt(10))); draw(E--Z--M--cycle,dashed); [/asy]" width="252" height="232" />
Now, we let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/f/92f6fdf2d48244959cf6c66b683a59d4e63e5423.png" class="latex" alt="$WZ$" width="33" height="12" />, and we consider right triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/8/c28c1cd9ed409e3633dc350bd5a1e48542e26a7d.png" class="latex" alt="$ZEM$" width="47" height="12" />. By the Pythagorean theorem, we have that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/c/1eca43cb3fda8b215c759808702db3345614d064.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + \left(a + \frac{b}{2}\right)^2 = r^2 = 180$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="234" height="46" />. Expanding this equation, we get that
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/f/43f7901333b61517b5ece94381e68022db9233a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{1}{4}(a^2+b^2) + a^2 + ab = 180\]" width="209" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/9/299eb10cd4200aeabbdbb914fe050c6e803c16cb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\frac{144}{4} + a^2 + ab = 180\]" width="159" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/1/411303eed0667414fe0af413cadd1b6b60b95235.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a^2 + ab = 144 = a^2 + b^2\]" width="185" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/d/e2d4f97fe7d074acd619a23efc745322170dd825.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[ab = b^2\]" width="55" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/2/4/4246da3143f4b6673903583e9ea6778330c872ec.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[b = a\]" width="41" height="12" />
</p><p>This means that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> is a 45-45-90 triangle, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/5/305703e122052cf42d386f8d3c1bc25f4ceda42d.png" class="latex" alt="$a = b = \frac{12}{\sqrt2} = 6\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="152" height="41" />. Thus the perimeter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/d/4bde7ae2d71d04d22adb245fe47dd6b7b12413c6.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + AB = 12\sqrt2 + 12$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="196" height="18" /> which is answer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/d/0/8d09707f0e315529b5ee0e0a91ab8f0903175b5f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\; 12 + 12\sqrt2}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="128" height="33" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The center of the circle on which <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10cb764f88509fb1c8012366993fdbee98f31bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$W$" width="19" height="12" /> lie must be equidistant from each of these four points. Draw the perpendicular bisectors of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/95915f5885ed19f737ac8338c7c0b1d0e23fb73a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{XY}$" width="31" height="15" /> and of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33f2995384430dc7e21ba193ca137734e9e95ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{WZ}$" width="33" height="15" />. Note that the perpendicular bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33f2995384430dc7e21ba193ca137734e9e95ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{WZ}$" width="33" height="15" /> is parallel to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/b/08b37a0b533fea837a79d8fa1a0c044714550a46.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CW}$" width="34" height="15" /> and passes through the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />. Therefore, the triangle that is formed by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" />, the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" />, and the point at which this perpendicular bisector intersects <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> must be similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" />, and the ratio of a side of the smaller triangle to a side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is 1:2. Consequently, the perpendicular bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/95915f5885ed19f737ac8338c7c0b1d0e23fb73a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{XY}$" width="31" height="15" /> passes through the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />. The perpendicular bisector of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33f2995384430dc7e21ba193ca137734e9e95ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{WZ}$" width="33" height="15" /> must include the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> as well. Since all points on a perpendicular bisector of any two points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> are equidistant from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/d/1/5d1e4485dc90c450e8c76826516c1b2ccb8fce16.png" class="latex" alt="$M$" width="19" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />, the center of the circle must be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />.
</p><p>Now the distance between the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, which is equal to the radius of this circle, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/7/f/87f49990530dd4a37fbab14935f36498bd160295.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{12^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{180}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="146" height="19" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/b/13bf021bbf1bf7645375a7cfb95c232507f1f72a.png" class="latex" alt="$a=AC$" width="61" height="13" />. Then the distance between the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, also equal to the radius of the circle, is given by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/e/e3e9f60064c7ce2228c5916e921768424922bfcc.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + \left(a + \frac{\sqrt{144 - a^2}}{2}\right)^2}$" style="vertical-align: -24px" width="237" height="64" /> (the ratio of the similar triangles is involved here). Squaring these two expressions for the radius and equating the results, we have
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab97fc1184b4ac242e4c907bc8eec196b9cebb55.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2+\left(a+\frac{\sqrt{144-a^2}}{2}\right)^{2} = 180\]" width="265" height="56" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/a/7aaf08c701dbc28d9d5f6ca25ec78558f0f247dd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[144 - a^2 = a\sqrt{144-a^2}\]" width="181" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/125b4789086026bef9b20278858ee9ffe78972b1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(144-a^2)^2 = a^2(144-a^2)\]" width="207" height="19" />
</p><p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cannot be equal to 12, the length of the hypotenuse of the right triangle, we can divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/7/0f7027cb3741161ddf8379a19a2a03f09839f9dd.png" class="latex" alt="$(144-a^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="19" />, and arrive at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/f/84f85056b89441a8cbc778845246eb90c7cf22d9.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 6\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="18" />. The length of other leg of the triangle must be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/c/2fc753efd9835592bd1117441ffc7146b9e2e9f4.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{144-72} = 6\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="141" height="19" />. Thus, the perimeter of the triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/a/a0ab7ccd72b37a804857403daa0f8bf9558933e4.png" class="latex" alt="$12+2(6\sqrt{2}) = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\; 12+12\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="249" height="33" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>In order to solve this problem, we can search for similar triangles. Begin by drawing triangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/a/e2a559986ed5a0ffc5654bd367c29dfc92913c36.png" class="latex" alt="$ABC$" width="42" height="13" /> and squares <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/8/ff85e02805fd5c6ab74f5850f526ddc6167f78e6.png" class="latex" alt="$ABXY$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/3/bf3bceba3d810b0c3ff6a613111f53160f1bf4c8.png" class="latex" alt="$ACWZ$" width="61" height="13" />. Draw segments <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/1/ad1817c383636a4e8f30eb3ebb5c8a26dd172742.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/9/3b925555efec8f323a8a84355f81bc6f03271ceb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{WX}$" width="36" height="15" />. Because we are given points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10cb764f88509fb1c8012366993fdbee98f31bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$W$" width="19" height="12" /> lie on a circle, we can conclude that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b076f2e7f124fc5d8b5ef7b30b95bfcfe85449f.png" class="latex" alt="$WXYZ$" width="64" height="12" /> forms a cyclic quadrilateral. Take <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/5/12517bbc31cb9026c71299b7a0d232d07e60f284.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AC}$" width="28" height="15" /> and extend it through a point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/1/ad1817c383636a4e8f30eb3ebb5c8a26dd172742.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}$" width="28" height="15" />. Now, we must do some angle chasing to prove that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c19d85e710d84056fcadaa3bba513b4d2e935c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle WBX$" width="66" height="13" /> is similar to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/d/ded78e7b31697d5acb30e6f90988261cb05a4d99.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle YAZ$" width="58" height="13" />.

<p>We claim that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a47ca0fe7cb276abc022af6ac88ddae1a9d6894.png" class="latex" alt="$X$" width="15" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/5/ce58e4af225c93d08606c26554caaa5ae32edeba.png" class="latex" alt="$Y$" width="14" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3ead47fb9fb4a4c273feee398f72ff2a09702b84.png" class="latex" alt="$Z$" width="13" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/c/10cb764f88509fb1c8012366993fdbee98f31bc5.png" class="latex" alt="$W$" width="19" height="12" /> lie on a circle if <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/1/e5170b3a48d96da708932026f0e94194f415b0b3.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ACB$" width="58" height="13" /> is an isosceles right triangle.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Note that because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/f/d4fdf3bd26293cffc469314cb1099980fb17fdaa.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle WZA=\angle AYX=90^\circ$" width="190" height="13" />, these two angles inscribe the semicircle defined by diameter <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/4/434b43c3ed50dfcfe55a5a68619bfe75826a1aee.png" class="latex" alt="$WX$" width="35" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> lies on line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/f/8/6f8bb21f4daac6d29938c2850c31631a7e924efb.png" class="latex" alt="$ZA$" width="27" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/e/d2eae7afcdddd1bb9a7ed0d47fb01953eebf2b24.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle XAY=45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="13" /> (because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/8/ff85e02805fd5c6ab74f5850f526ddc6167f78e6.png" class="latex" alt="$ABXY$" width="59" height="13" /> is a square), we can find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/5/ae55f31eec5e26b65587d6ad51d28bf91a82f1fa.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZAY= 180^\circ - 45^\circ = 135^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="219" height="13" />.
</p><p>Now, we can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/6/366d3877c3da3d48b9ba07e5afa00f58d1409b57.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAC=45^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="103" height="13" /> to complete the full <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/9/cf9506d61661a7d046ee93b381ddb704684ffd15.png" class="latex" alt="$360^\circ$" width="33" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is and isosceles right triangle, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/0/c/20cfebabf7ad0c3afb6519f3d0e589e41cf873fd.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=BC=6\sqrt2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="139" height="18" />. So Our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/8/5a8744ff1b51669aaa9ddcebfe6d4db3af1bc9eb.png" class="latex" alt="$6\sqrt2 + 6\sqrt 2 + 12 = \boxed{\textbf{(C) }12+12\sqrt{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="283" height="33" />.
.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5_2">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>We see a circle and little information of position of the shapes inside the triangle, so we think of things associated with circles and think of cyclic quads. We then notice that quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b076f2e7f124fc5d8b5ef7b30b95bfcfe85449f.png" class="latex" alt="$WXYZ$" width="64" height="12" /> is cyclic. Then we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/e/95e48c31c971bceb358ed591a0b14c53fea54701.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle W = \angle X$" width="85" height="12" /> making extensions of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/e/c2edf006dd5689a3b841ac3eb9e6086bfa25e8b7.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{WA}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="35" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/a/bba3704899aa86f3eace66b2bbe084b78320cb5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{XZ}$" width="30" height="15" /> diagonals of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/0/2b076f2e7f124fc5d8b5ef7b30b95bfcfe85449f.png" class="latex" alt="$WXYZ$" width="64" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/6/07651113949294c3a5751c00fad68dcdf61446a0.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \overline{AC}=\overline{AW}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="122" height="18" />. We can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/1/4/614ad11fae41291367ba5eedaebd8d021bfcc837.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AY} = 12\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="97" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/a/82a838f2eee56f358387b1bead77074c67ff39f5.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AZ} = x\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="88" height="21" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/f/38f8e1d8c562e0e782508bfb3e5fb1923c1006ef.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}^2 = x^2 + (x+12\sqrt{2})^2 = 2x^2 + 24x\sqrt{2} + 288$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="374" height="26" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/3/a03c11231a99a571afbcdac439f95dc973ccce75.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZAY = \alpha$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="93" height="14" />. Then we can see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4b2703d6eb2678c68b3c5ec47d299bdc23ee874.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZAC$" width="54" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/a/3cad3e759dc0fd035d18fc83a39242aa4b24b494.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BAY$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/7/f27da4fca6f3fecb215974023aad210b70ec3857.png" class="latex" alt="$45$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> degrees, making <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/0/0f040e665e8ca9d53c17bb0f251153db2967a079.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle ZAY$" width="54" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/3/3e3a8783b12769e52140fe2d37e624128538572b.png" class="latex" alt="$\alpha + 90$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="54" height="15" /> degrees.
</p><p>We can verify with the cosine addition identity that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/3/8a3615d0cbdff90de3bf8778bebf57d812088da0.png" class="latex" alt="$-\cos{\alpha + 90 degree} = \sin{\alpha}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="213" height="18" /> (knowing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/4/3/a43742d6454275111e6652d6d7aaf0a7ab723f10.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\theta + 90 degree} = \cos{\theta}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="18" /> motivates this). By law of cosine's, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/c/b5c244efab2a9992e1685ade4bbc4d2993d6f057.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}^2 = (12\sqrt{2})^2 + x\sqrt{2})^2 - 2\cdot 12\sqrt{2} \cdot x\sqrt{2}cos{\alpha + 90 degree} = 288 + x^2 + 48cos{\alpha + 90 degree} = 288 + 2x^2 + 48x\sin{\alpha}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="916" height="26" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/4/4f43bae270243d086d23357db1731f8591fcd0a3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle{A}$" width="26" height="13" /> is opposite <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/0/34010ac2e57d4967e43a5ffab8b826c41ca8e603.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin{\alpha} = \frac{\sqrt{144-x^2}}{12}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="150" height="43" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dcef7c7a02037496b34177ab5108f7bb6d50103e.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}^2 = 288 + 2x^2 + 4x\sqrt{144-x^2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="262" height="23" />.
</p><p>Setting the first paragraph's <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/8/0/e803e9e0249c83dda855877bb33fa13524f626ae.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{YZ}^2$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="38" height="21" /> equal to the second:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/5/1a5706667ff732178f49ef5c405e9dce18e7f1f9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[288 + x^2 + 4x\sqrt{144-x^2}2x^2 + 24x\sqrt{2} + 288\]" width="341" height="18" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
