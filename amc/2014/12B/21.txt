<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Draw the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> and call the foot <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/9/859ccf4cd60c7bc6b8fa1afc9a42dc811a826d6f.png" class="latex" alt="$L$" width="12" height="12" />. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/a/81a9dfa12681a0c1517ddb3b41e05d1cc0d982ad.png" class="latex" alt="$HL=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/5/d955e610cf3fd94003dcfc4f48a32c9074059f68.png" class="latex" alt="$HJ$" width="28" height="12" />. It is the hypotenuse of both right triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/b/cabcce6d4aa50bad8be3b1fb74936ab555e11f30.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle HGJ$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/1/ec11475036355c2f83064cf0de5cd26856e717fe.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle HLJ$" width="57" height="13" />, and we know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/8/9f8f4956bdae8f7b6afb302cc363158dc756f2bd.png" class="latex" alt="$JG=HL=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="111" height="13" />, so we must have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/2/b92b25ece23cc03166884c5d21c753426ad4579d.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle HGJ\cong\triangle JLH$" width="140" height="13" /> by Hypotenuse-Leg congruence. From this congruence we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/d/17d95bb3853ada137680749d82f0211d3c05f040.png" class="latex" alt="$LJ=HG=BE$" width="132" height="12" />.
</p><p>Notice that all four triangles in this picture are similar. Also, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/4/d649d20e7c046cee27bfab098a724e7d379e3f58.png" class="latex" alt="$LA=HD=EJ$" width="133" height="13" />. So set <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/7/1772ccc1668d4b612874bbf711aef83fc0ee0ed3.png" class="latex" alt="$x=LJ=HG=BE$" width="167" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/5/d/b5df5f10b73fcbd6e650d2b47626071663a0710f.png" class="latex" alt="$y=LA=HD=EJ$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="167" height="16" />. Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/2/f12199c704ba90b0ef41d6fc86182b0f4511eba0.png" class="latex" alt="$BE+EJ+JL+LA=2(x+y)=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="294" height="18" />. This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/c/d2c90eaaae28c69330a7f942b5b2ba0e14a75833.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=\frac{1}{2}=BE+EJ=BJ$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="232" height="37" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/b/abb5588023cfa1ac14643e9778699f03eecc57a3.png" class="latex" alt="$J$" width="12" height="12" /> is the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" />. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/f/cdfea25671bb540ce456b6292d16b2e8a5a1eead.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle AJG$" width="55" height="13" />, along with all other similar triangles in the picture, is a 30-60-90 triangle, and we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/9/659e7e94431ff546f9a68a146ba54b6420316020.png" class="latex" alt="$AG=\sqrt{3} \cdot AJ=\sqrt{3}/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="181" height="21" /> and subsequently <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/b/94bb646912ea1deaf6746cf272e202e879e871a0.png" class="latex" alt="$GD=\frac{2-\sqrt{3}}{2}=KE$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="168" height="41" />. This means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/2/3a2f90ccfff031fdbf518d8f500da64bae77629e.png" class="latex" alt="$EJ=\sqrt{3} \cdot KE=\frac{2\sqrt{3}-3}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="210" height="41" />, which gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c5e4dca1d01c4cac5228f60df046d2579cf909c.png" class="latex" alt="$BE=\frac{1}{2}-EJ=\frac{4-2\sqrt{3}}{2}=2-\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="288" height="41" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/9/169b8d69bd74a91e72ec43123070d770633a340a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="27" height="17" />.
</p>

</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/2/ee2a1c0217a3f9f697c1d142a64052bb7ad8e0bd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[1-\sin(k) = \sin(k)\]" width="145" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/d/a9ddd0bf0cec3a16c281c9de68449bdc1011f225.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\sin(k) = \frac{1}{2}, \cos(k) = \frac{\sqrt 3}{2}\]" width="191" height="41" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/f/09f620032451b8d0e4478015f6305d3a6834c30d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a = \frac{1 - \frac{\sqrt 3}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}} = 2 - \sqrt 3\]" width="170" height="50" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p>Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/5/d955e610cf3fd94003dcfc4f48a32c9074059f68.png" class="latex" alt="$HJ$" width="28" height="12" /> is a diagonal of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/3/c6384882422fca17b6b8bb0cb58a405d46e64590.png" class="latex" alt="$JKHG$" width="59" height="12" />, so it must be equal in length to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/9/59910c059976e19ba94be84e4dab4701a119d5ba.png" class="latex" alt="$FB$" width="28" height="12" />. Therefore, quadrilateral <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/6/c9623a8be8ab919b71ecee458a1923c195a9a775.png" class="latex" alt="$FHJB$" width="56" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/b/a7bc05e8a1c0c5ab2de15a773db4b3fd0bf51793.png" class="latex" alt="$FH\parallel BJ$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="76" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/a/a1a82edef57380985d0a4651d665615f435a7529.png" class="latex" alt="$FB=HJ$" width="82" height="12" />, so it must be either an isosceles trapezoid or a parallelogram. But due to the slope of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/9/59910c059976e19ba94be84e4dab4701a119d5ba.png" class="latex" alt="$FB$" width="28" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/5/d955e610cf3fd94003dcfc4f48a32c9074059f68.png" class="latex" alt="$HJ$" width="28" height="12" />, we see that it must be a parallelogram. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/5/4/c54bb35dc92d366274808aeadcf6244244180481.png" class="latex" alt="$FH=BJ$" width="82" height="12" />. But by the symmetry in rectangle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/e/c6e4b9dcc08bde315900943b03663f36e9373715.png" class="latex" alt="$FEAD$" width="57" height="13" />, we see that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/4/d6486206e34fb860a70a5d621a5575aa4fdf7eb4.png" class="latex" alt="$FH=JA$" width="80" height="13" />. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/2/6/926288a70888e3ce14c0f71a731198235779cd36.png" class="latex" alt="$BJ=FH=JA$" width="131" height="13" />. We also know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/b/26bfe47a566498e6e0f109dd573d073820e37e30.png" class="latex" alt="$BJ+JA=1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="106" height="14" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aaa400de15b483302d50e30f61e1173053165b2.png" class="latex" alt="$BJ=JA=\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="112" height="37" />.
</p><p>As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/c/b0c0185a36a45ee7a0635bcc364017e0c16fc2bf.png" class="latex" alt="$JG=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="58" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/f/6cf6b1965c0ed487a386c848662e067c700d0dd5.png" class="latex" alt="$JA=\frac12$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="61" height="37" />, and as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77dfd3d2bee62255f9a1fa5f40e619250723d639.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GJA$" width="56" height="13" /> is right, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/d/77dfd3d2bee62255f9a1fa5f40e619250723d639.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle GJA$" width="56" height="13" /> must be a 30-60-90 triangle. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/f/01f98074103ea0aed2dfdeeeeb0fa2567b383adb.png" class="latex" alt="$GA=\sqrt{3}/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="94" height="21" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7c99f192f92066b598b37ec32af871813bc5139.png" class="latex" alt="$DG=1-\sqrt{3}/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="127" height="21" />. But by similarity, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/4/a3496242cb32237134d9d790f77ec6b1eb792027.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DHG$" width="62" height="13" /> is also a 30-60-90 triangle, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/1/5f1eea54cdfc5b5d15ae0087827764d98a3efd8f.png" class="latex" alt="$DH=\sqrt{3}-3/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="21" />. But <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/c/64cde519984801c853895d95f51682cebdf64d42.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DHG\cong\triangle EJK$" width="146" height="13" />, hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/e/c/becefd4932c762a3924ef9c67f9579d77e3eaeb1.png" class="latex" alt="$EJ=\sqrt{3}-3/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="123" height="21" />. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/d/e7dceb4fd7496a4ab47ab13bfd185431a9525dd9.png" class="latex" alt="$BJ=1/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="77" height="18" />, this implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/e/03e097f804559bafce3f4f9229178ea0db671f1b.png" class="latex" alt="$BE=BJ-EJ=1/2-\sqrt{3}+3/2=2-\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="356" height="21" />. Thus the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/f/fffc7368e8e7b77c88b0b4bf732c88c60e27b439.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="42" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_6">Solution 6</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/6/836fc8df474c105a5af0235bd19569b3e910150c.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = x$" width="63" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/b/7ab06755f362728feeeb1cbbd488fab08b3e5b6f.png" class="latex" alt="$KE = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/8/ea82ed350a8c12df07ce94a5e4fece197d9abf71.png" class="latex" alt="$DG = y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="63" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/d/4/3d4f8c60ff4c7f12f6c9f0068340941296ea9768.png" class="latex" alt="$AG = 1-y$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/e/f6eac2c1a5c419d59cde33c71e10990bfe377655.png" class="latex" alt="$KJ = x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/0/050192e70ccd2a2f07d8de1c89cc698dc937eab0.png" class="latex" alt="$JG = 1$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="61" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/c/c2cc0b2bb79430bf32d504df1206dd3333fa9fdd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AJ}{JG} = \frac{KE}{JK}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="91" height="39" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/5/1c50a336673b6db245d69f8e23ddb440a579cd8c.png" class="latex" alt="$AJ = \frac{y}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="66" height="34" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/5/fd58855c7f293ccc2d966ccae51b587fe77f1289.png" class="latex" alt="$EJ = 1 - x - \frac{y}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="131" height="34" />, 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/0/2f0169b20945c55de60f329e94c23dd60ad0d2c4.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{AG}{JG} = \frac{EJ}{JK}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="91" height="39" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/5/db5809b5b90f57070b0606c9c388a4e3158c2422.png" class="latex" alt="$1-y = \frac{1 - x - \frac{y}{x}}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="146" height="41" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/8/728deeeb1a071441655b25ddb16e747739e3557c.png" class="latex" alt="$x - xy = 1 - x - \frac{y}{x}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="157" height="34" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/f/a6fde6f9fd2cddf9eca2358f02e071121c55dd41.png" class="latex" alt="$2x^2 - x^2y + y - x = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="176" height="20" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/c/4fce2d831f25cbd63b02ddc99870650b40b6086e.png" class="latex" alt="$AJ^2 + AG^2 = JG^2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="150" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/b/10b06befd63425dc1885a313896d454d35a97e45.png" class="latex" alt="$\left( \frac{y}{x} \right) ^2 + (1-y)^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="163" height="38" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/4/104e75457e06d3dd1fdf61b92fe6cfa4b4fe3448.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{y^2}{x^2} + 1 - 2y + y^2 = 1$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="168" height="41" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/e/37e70046543ceee4bb54d7904072532e27c98f75.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 y -2x^2 + y = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="20" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/5/5456aa005832fe81f1a111836895ef8a06146013.png" class="latex" alt="$(1) + (2)$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="71" height="20" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/f/dbff72bfb472464d1f8c749a4306cddfa4667414.png" class="latex" alt="$2y - x = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="84" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/1/271debc065f7e224cb84abc647ffbdf5a19f8d81.png" class="latex" alt="$y = \frac{x}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="46" height="33" />
</p><p>Substitute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/9/3899217113fe1c926231520b8c167d0b037ddbcf.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 \cdot \frac{x}{2} - 2 x^2 + \frac{x}{2} = 0$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="166" height="34" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/9/379b4eb9dbbd68fc49a5526b11042040d7749adb.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - 4x + 1 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="126" height="18" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/e/ffe05b97cfbaa871ca4de504d0e965fb2f7e7251.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-4}}{2} = 2 \pm \sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="216" height="42" />, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/a/1faf5d41cd05857f8f0cbab1eb5a41bb1a6741f4.png" class="latex" alt="$x &lt; 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/2/1a2bb935ade12d9a5bd62ef9ef47a5212b7fd44c.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}2 - \sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="132" height="35" />
</p><p>~<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/wiki/index.php/User:Isabelchen">isabelchen</a>
