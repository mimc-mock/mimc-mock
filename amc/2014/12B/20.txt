<p>For how many positive integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/8/238e9dc9796c95dfd48409371c1d239cc91df76b.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(x-40) + \log_{10}(60-x) &lt; 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="260" height="18" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/b/04bd0a96dc997a112211865374afdbdff5c9969e.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }10\qquad \textbf{(B) }18\qquad \textbf{(C) }19\qquad \textbf{(D) }20\qquad \textbf{(E) }\text{infinitely many}\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="489" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The domain of the LHS implies that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/1/e61ac4e06141f931a998331029b57ad65f67c533.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[40&lt;x&lt;60\]" width="94" height="13" /> Begin from the left hand side
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/6749fe9dcc8059ce47ccd96766d2b0d9d1f0eb3a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_{10}[(x-40)(60-x)]&lt;2\]" width="210" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/8/dc8b0d61948c16d06b5abed7664d06898e0969c2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x-40)(60-x)&lt;100\]" width="179" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/b/8cb07d38ab8be9ccbb57dd02425ab79f4a9da74d.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[-x^2+100x-2500&lt;0\]" width="181" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/7/dc75fe198e27a34d5b4d4876eed002ce2eb24c26.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x-50)^2&gt;0\]" width="103" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/c/39c9aefab22bafbe506412f1eeed25a27f7cf9d5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x \not = 50\]" width="51" height="17" />
Hence, we have integers from 41 to 49 and 51 to 59. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/2/402b4180ea2e008bf92d91d2f46ad61f12d9bb57.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="58" height="28" /> integers.
</p>
