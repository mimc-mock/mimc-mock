<p>Orvin went to the store with just enough money to buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/1/651ba418a810d971dbc8a326d49a71fe1541fae0.png" class="latex" alt="$30$" width="17" height="12" /> balloons. When he arrived he discovered that the store had a special sale on balloons: buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> balloon at the regular price and get a second at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> off the regular price. What is the greatest number of balloons Orvin could buy?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/a/eca998dd453c14004d7c51fc4f9134508f3f0e73.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 33\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 34\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 38\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 39$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>If every balloon costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> dollars, then Orvin has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/8/6c81a4cab3374601fa0488d219772ef2d0f08696.png" class="latex" alt="$30n$" width="28" height="12" /> dollars.  For every balloon he buys for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> dollars, he can buy another for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/b/05bb4392f23b6656951e63994a2fa0f7da5b1cec.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{2n}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="22" height="37" /> dollars.  This means it costs him <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/1/0e1bf98ef95680be873cf20cf3945f1f3f3e0b55.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{5n}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="22" height="37" /> dollars to buy a bundle of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> balloons.  With <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/8/6c81a4cab3374601fa0488d219772ef2d0f08696.png" class="latex" alt="$30n$" width="28" height="12" /> dollars, he can buy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/4/c04042309dfe4e155a76d0af96d6373c57df1b6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30n}{\frac{5n}{3}} = 18$" style="vertical-align: -19px" width="75" height="44" /> sets of two balloons, so the total number of balloons he can buy is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/7/ba74f5e26ac395e7713e28887a641882d02d9b12.png" class="latex" alt="$18\times2  \implies \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 36 }$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="163" height="28" />
</p>
