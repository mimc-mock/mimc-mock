<p>A truck travels <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/8/528d669ec8c6f2ea3d355747db9269205b379a7f.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{b}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> feet every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> feet in a yard. How many yards does the truck travel in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> minutes?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/a/bfa74c696c99c1e7786968fcaa44ad96aa794573.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } \frac{b}{1080t} \qquad \textbf {(B) } \frac{30t}{b} \qquad \textbf {(C) } \frac{30b}{t}\qquad \textbf {(D) } \frac{10t}{b} \qquad \textbf {(E) } \frac{10b}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="470" height="38" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Converting feet to yards and minutes to second, we see that the truck travels <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/e/83eebc6f294b3469b79317041228d84f68e71d62.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{b}{18}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> yards every <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/d/e0d2bf360290fd61d1c1557e763f2622363b3d35.png" class="latex" alt="$t$" width="6" height="11" /> seconds for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/b/5cb1b41850ab676e4d1cf15c69b1609af4befbc5.png" class="latex" alt="$180$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> seconds. We see that he does <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/5/105e56845e208f995011dffa1f258cfac346407f.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{180}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="29" height="37" /> cycles of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/e/83eebc6f294b3469b79317041228d84f68e71d62.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{b}{18}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="20" height="38" /> yards. Multiplying, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/b/72b6fcfdf5e3fab30682bf9931d050b4ac8ba41f.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{180b}{18t}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="37" height="38" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/6/8365c0a4617d4dfd07108be9fb9a9b6d99d3ff41.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{10b}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="28" height="37" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/f/6bfc0829f3f29bbf35d0a861644e00cfef0f50ca.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="39" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>We set a proportion by letting the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> being the number of feet the truck travels in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> minutes.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/8/4083d9142430d1ba955fd49098a90f2a43e2a96e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{b}{6}}{t}=\frac{x}{180}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="68" height="41" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/d/2fdcf7ee4f4e5343b1b04bc3646f0fe8b19fc001.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{b}{6t}=\frac{x}{180}$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="73" height="38" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/2/3928969cb09d6cc64235ac1dfc96830d2ad04039.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{180b}{6t}=x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="37" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43d436594a0b0384ae938ad34cfec97428fcc022.png" class="latex" alt="$x=\frac{30b}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="62" height="37" />
</p><p>Remember <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number of feet the truck travels, so we divide by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> to convert to yards. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/2/96240f30ab5e7df91d20b05da2d5a0d0aa0592ac.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{x}{3}=\frac{10b}{t}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="66" height="37" />, which corresponds to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6c182d936b9ff33cadf7f6e3391eeddf9248a89.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{(E)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="36" height="28" />
</p>
