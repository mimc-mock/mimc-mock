<p>Two concentric circles have radii <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Two points on the outer circle are chosen independently and uniformly at random. What is the probability that the chord joining the two points intersects the inner circle?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/3/a/83a29f304dcc3ef7bbac2fa79b45a6608aeb705d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac{1}{6}\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ \frac{1}{4}\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2-\sqrt{2}}{2}\qquad \textbf{(D)}\ \frac{1}{3}\qquad \textbf{(E)}\ \frac{1}{2}\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="414" height="41" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>Let the center of the two circles be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />. Now pick an arbitrary point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> on the boundary of the circle with radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. We want to find the range of possible places for the second point, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" />, such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/9/d59f5d75cc27d504ede022aab803742e799709f4.png" class="latex" alt="$AA&#39;$" width="30" height="14" /> passes through the circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. To do this, first draw the tangents from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> to the circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Let the intersection points of the tangents (when extended) with circle of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/9/b1902d279ba37d60bdce4e0e987b7cd19d48974e.png" class="latex" alt="$H$" width="16" height="12" /> be the foot of the altitude from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/3/e33fe7d65facd8868f58b6e94ddc7f153a5a3f9f.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BC}$" width="29" height="15" />. Then we have the following diagram.
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/7/c/b7caeda0d004ad915eefc557ca648dfe2ea51acd.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] scale(200); pair A,O,B,C,H; A = (0,1); O = (0,0); B = (-.866,-.5); C = (.866,-.5); H = (0, -.5); draw(A--C--cycle); draw(A--O--cycle); draw(O--C--cycle); draw(O--H,dashed+linewidth(.7)); draw(A--B--cycle); draw(B--C--cycle); draw(O--B--cycle); dot(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,N); dot(&quot;$O$&quot;,O,NW); dot(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,W); dot(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,E); dot(&quot;$H$&quot;,H,S); label(&quot;$2$&quot;,O--(-.7,-.385),N); label(&quot;$1$&quot;,O--H,E); draw(circle(O,.5)); draw(circle(O,1)); [/asy]" width="245" height="252" />
</p><p>We want to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/f/4fff693c9a4aff817b05b712cd2376cd1673c08d.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC$" width="56" height="12" />, as the range of desired points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" /> is the set of points on minor arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f997f2e439fbb9578ac4d715bc866bbea93ebc2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{BC}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="20" />. This is because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> are part of the tangents, which "set the boundaries" for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/a/3caae9e5b5ddc093627099e8912612c3690d263b.png" class="latex" alt="$OH = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="63" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/b/9/fb943d4373b97579b51fad7d2fd8a9aeb3f113d0.png" class="latex" alt="$OB = 2$" width="62" height="12" /> as shown in the diagram, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/0/d800f57d86eea50e28e17697f4a9c716f3402043.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle OHB$" width="61" height="13" /> is a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/c/47c78b85058ee5e17f88e2cde302e48aad826b35.png" class="latex" alt="$30-60-90$" width="98" height="12" /> triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e3522402f8d6ec1b552febdc7c4f047cecb622.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOH = 60^\circ$" width="106" height="13" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/8/a786db64e476f7e66f7bd93bbf74f55aa210b939.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle BOC = 120^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="113" height="13" />, and the probability <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/9/1090530ae883b627e090bfb5e450ef796febca40.png" class="latex" alt="$A&#39;$" width="17" height="14" /> lies on the minor arc <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/9/f997f2e439fbb9578ac4d715bc866bbea93ebc2a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overarc{BC}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="23" height="20" /> is thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/b/b4bcf04f084c76a6e1e6e776aff23f2522530edf.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{120}{360} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\: \dfrac13}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="113" height="48" />.
</p>
