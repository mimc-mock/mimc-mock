<p>Six regular hexagons surround a regular hexagon of side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> as shown. What is the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/8/f78aa6233f534e73a43d3896751c0c35f8bc342b.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] draw((0,0)--(-5,8.66025404)--(0, 17.3205081)--(10, 17.3205081)--(15,8.66025404)--(10, 0)--(0, 0)); draw((30,0)--(25,8.66025404)--(30, 17.3205081)--(40, 17.3205081)--(45, 8.66025404)--(40, 0)--(30, 0)); draw((30,0)--(25,-8.66025404)--(30, -17.3205081)--(40, -17.3205081)--(45, -8.66025404)--(40, 0)--(30, 0)); draw((0,0)--(-5, -8.66025404)--(0, -17.3205081)--(10, -17.3205081)--(15, -8.66025404)--(10, 0)--(0, 0)); draw((15,8.66025404)--(10, 17.3205081)--(15, 25.9807621)--(25, 25.9807621)--(30, 17.3205081)--(25, 8.66025404)--(15, 8.66025404)); draw((15,-8.66025404)--(10, -17.3205081)--(15, -25.9807621)--(25, -25.9807621)--(30, -17.3205081)--(25, -8.66025404)--(15, -8.66025404)); label(&quot;A&quot;, (0,0), W); label(&quot;B&quot;, (30, 17.3205081), NE); label(&quot;C&quot;, (30, -17.3205081), SE); draw((0,0)--(30, 17.3205081)--(30, -17.3205081)--(0, 0));     //(Diagram Creds-DivideBy0)     [/asy]" width="242" height="252" />
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/f/1dfbfdfadffcbcdb590e3b0e3c7b7d8035f3ae7d.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 2\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf {(B) } 3\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf {(C) } 1+3\sqrt{2} \qquad \textbf {(D) } 2+2\sqrt{3} \qquad \textbf {(E) } 3+2\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="569" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We note that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> triangular sections in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" /> can be put together to form a hexagon congruent to each of the seven other hexagons. By the formula for the area of the hexagon, we get the area for each hexagon as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/0/da093c2e0aaea8f3f6a19ddef6f68bdc978b4a5a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="35" height="41" />. The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/8/1f828f10cad9235a2c72c34369f5fd4a1179e579.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle{ABC}$" width="58" height="13" />, which is equivalent to two of these hexagons together, is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e2cdc7b9807a789060789acadf4ccdb65b14c1f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}  3\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="33" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The measure of an interior angle in a hexagon is 120 degrees. Each side of the 6 triangles that make up the remainder of triangle ABC are isosceles with 2 side lengths of 1 and an angle of 120 degrees. Therefore, by the Law of Cosines, we calculate that the longest side of this triangle is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/7/6c77cf4b63af80bfbc6a9ad9b4b9e8e549e77d78.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="24" height="18" />, so the side length of triangle ABC is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/4/5/e455d80f4657b1112ba302a6e2c42e9c81f780ee.png" class="latex" alt="$2\sqrt{3}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. Using the equilateral triangle area formula, we figure out that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/2/6e2cdc7b9807a789060789acadf4ccdb65b14c1f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}  3\sqrt{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="73" height="33" />.
</p>
