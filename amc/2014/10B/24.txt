<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>We see that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/3/2933f407c21ceb3f3ec8370f43cd5b939250ffca.png" class="latex" alt="$5!$" width="12" height="13" /> total ways to arrange the numbers. However, we can always rotate these numbers so that, for example, the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> is always at the top of the circle. Thus, there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/a/2da866442e69c040daf44260b4728d37e98de313.png" class="latex" alt="$4!$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="12" height="13" /> ways under rotation. Every case has exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> reflection, so that gives us only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/1/161cc110ca9ba317350c76ff9497d466bd9d4190.png" class="latex" alt="$4!/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="31" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> cases, which is not difficult to list out. We systematically list out all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> cases. 
</p><p>Now, we must examine if they satisfy the conditions. We can see that by choosing one number at a time, we can always obtain subsets with sums <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f55e0a11e29af7baee666c19c147e6f7c1923e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />. By choosing the full circle, we can obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/9/f1965fae079a9ba2c0726c307070c2355dfcb213.png" class="latex" alt="$15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. By choosing everything except for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f55e0a11e29af7baee666c19c147e6f7c1923e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, we can obtain subsets with sums of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/f/11f8704e72ea9ca4fab45bba5f4e5d3692c78248.png" class="latex" alt="$10, 11, 12, 13,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="101" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/0/820a20de2847dfdc1bc5cffc8d904933150032bd.png" class="latex" alt="$14$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" />. 
</p><p>This means that we now only need to check for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f8e529f64cbdc5322919e965c1507f05532ca66.png" class="latex" alt="$6, 7, 8,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="47" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, we only need to check each case for whether or not we can obtain <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a0db32027a732ac57d37ef2ae9bb150f65b108.png" class="latex" alt="$7$" width="9" height="12" />. 
</p><p>We can make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> by having <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/4/9344bb6b7bc4be44d4b6250b79f94e363d30892c.png" class="latex" alt="$4, 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="15" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/8/f58d4f4bf5dfba21162d6c564c33a0929376571c.png" class="latex" alt="$3, 2, 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="42" height="16" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/f/3bfbaae6caa92f2c9f580ab0b49aed29a07323cc.png" class="latex" alt="$5, 1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="25" height="16" />.
