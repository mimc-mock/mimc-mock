<p>For how many integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> is the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/9/4599515bf98090f6552262f1a5d8b6b4e7f6e39c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-51x^2+50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="16" /> negative?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/c/01c572433b36181f15913d9761b55cbe6dd0a913.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf {(A) } 8 \qquad \textbf {(B) } 10 \qquad \textbf {(C) } 12 \qquad \textbf {(D) } 14 \qquad \textbf {(E) } 16$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/f/2cf23ed66ff07116998d77f098512f018a7be72f.png" class="latex" alt="$50+1=51$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="90" height="14" />, which motivates us to factor the polynomial as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/3/2/632c5b4bed28e674fca902468be8b2b23275640b.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2-50)(x^2-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="19" />. Since this expression is negative, one term must be negative and the other positive. Also, the first term is obviously smaller than the second, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/b/7dbd87121303b2d77e3882e33fe562db7cfd13fe.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2-50&lt;0&lt;x^2-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="164" height="15" />. Solving this inequality, we find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/a/a3ad5d0adb374a75583ea8b55f34f8c6d71a82fb.png" class="latex" alt="$1&lt;x^2&lt;50$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="93" height="15" />. There are  exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/f/edf074831eb5bc9e61d6d6e09f525a86e3068f6a.png" class="latex" alt="$12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> that satisfy this inequality, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/4/dd491beae61c5edef47e329680386410d3404dbc.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm \{2,3,4,5,6,7\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" />.
</p><p>Thus our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/5/2658a68bf4ca0d7f951e2f9f07f9ed75dc70ef71.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf {(C) } 12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="65" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Since the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/7/5176de6d3e975a5ccf4d2a02e49d5344a548bf0f.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-51x^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="74" height="15" /> part of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/9/4599515bf98090f6552262f1a5d8b6b4e7f6e39c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-51x^2+50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="16" /> has to be less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/b/5bb90d025068754ea0b56f2889bbbe4e93119683.png" class="latex" alt="$-50$" width="31" height="12" /> (because we want <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/9/4599515bf98090f6552262f1a5d8b6b4e7f6e39c.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-51x^2+50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="16" /> to be negative), we have the inequality <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/4/8/54877046fd41e5932bda01d8ca1c69f5668e1278.png" class="latex" alt="$x^4-51x^2&lt;-50 \rightarrow x^2(x^2-51) &lt;-50$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="307" height="19" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> has to be positive, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/f/94fdcc6fbd0dcd081db5901714c7fe3574ef6ce2.png" class="latex" alt="$(x^2-51)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="70" height="19" /> is negative. Then we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5b62f4aa44e4a7727c08c3f34b5fa9601a1be9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2&lt;51$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="15" />. We know that if we find a positive number that works, it's parallel negative will work. Therefore, we just have to find how many positive numbers work, then multiply that by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. If we try <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/3/4c30bb56b31bbcc7533256382af45b150245b2ea.png" class="latex" alt="$1^4-51(1)^4+50 = -50+50 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="257" height="19" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" /> therefore doesn't work. Test two on your own, and then proceed. Since two works, all numbers above <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> that satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5b62f4aa44e4a7727c08c3f34b5fa9601a1be9.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2&lt;51$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="59" height="15" /> work, that is the set {<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/d/69d38f4a998b6012c488399400ee1be980f6ca93.png" class="latex" alt="${2,3,4,5,6,7}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="95" height="16" />}. That equates to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers. Since each numbers' negative counterparts work, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/b/4db2713a807b265001bf321f67c314b4f1ee5500.png" class="latex" alt="$6\cdot2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) }12}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="120" height="28" />.
</p>
