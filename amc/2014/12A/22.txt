<p>The number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f89c2402133dba0ce0d0f5db38c33fd0635466e.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{867}$" width="28" height="15" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/8/b98ae20267e31e37336bcabc2b5e635591ca0250.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2013}$" width="34" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/b/0db2db65006422553ef8b4f8531170496bd48b0d.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2014}$" width="35" height="15" />.  How many pairs of integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/a/7ca10ffe3531cf1faf6222be7eac407c47bef878.png" class="latex" alt="$(m,n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="47" height="18" /> are there such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/4/9b4d5266ee5ddfa2de9e3d4c35b95a67229ca35e.png" class="latex" alt="$1\leq m\leq 2012$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="109" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/d/c0dd87b90d59f4e58a09e5b2e78b8af6511482d5.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5^n&lt;2^m&lt;2^{m+2}&lt;5^{n+1}?\]" width="193" height="15" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/f/b8f65d7dfd34edd356680786e215f513b52cfeba.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }278\qquad \textbf{(B) }279\qquad \textbf{(C) }280\qquad \textbf{(D) }281\qquad \textbf{(E) }282\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="443" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Between any two consecutive powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> there are either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> (because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/3/593edac1c2e377fbe7df18af5a7aa4813fef3972.png" class="latex" alt="$2^2&lt;5^1&lt;2^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="96" height="15" />). Consider the intervals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/8/e08c68fbf366507bda1d3d539b40c1c434b2517e.png" class="latex" alt="$(5^0,5^1),(5^1,5^2),\dots (5^{866},5^{867})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="19" />. We want the number of intervals with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>From the given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/e/abeb0a3a887c2871efdca9a6801c1f3037143f44.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2013}&lt;5^{867}&lt;2^{2014}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="149" height="15" />, we know that these <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/5/e556bd22ea35cf4609d3fc1d7dfc2490fd557d11.png" class="latex" alt="$867$" width="27" height="12" /> intervals together have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> of them have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> of them have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. Thus we have the system
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/8/1580c31f4d58c540f94347ac313ba29d0a32fcda.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x+y=867\]" width="93" height="16" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/b/39b29d4cbbbad5954993ebfc4f5348eb1a5ad481.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2x+3y=2013\]" width="119" height="16" />
from which we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/4/7/747bd99c6d27ef7b5655789652e7f2cf716379b2.png" class="latex" alt="$y=279$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="60" height="16" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The problem is asking for between how many consecutive powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> are there <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />s
</p><p>There can be either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> between any two consecutive powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b216498b4cb64f29f3bfdfa60736dc769f06c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$5^n$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/c/c8c134ae9d194442eec9a0584ff74fc944ce9251.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{n+1}$" width="32" height="15" />. 
</p><p>The first power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b216498b4cb64f29f3bfdfa60736dc769f06c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$5^n$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cfef3a5952e7e3cda7e207e2ab0bffc2f48e8d8.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 5^n$" width="39" height="12" />.
</p><p>The second power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/f/1cfef3a5952e7e3cda7e207e2ab0bffc2f48e8d8.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 5^n$" width="39" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/0/090d69a194f5bc096bc3c48546bc517403d215c0.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 5^n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="42" height="14" />.
</p><p>The third power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> is between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/0/090d69a194f5bc096bc3c48546bc517403d215c0.png" class="latex" alt="$4 \cdot 5^n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="42" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/0/c80ae5979960c1fffcfb2ad37cdb2e5bc9b9580c.png" class="latex" alt="$8 \cdot 5^n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="42" height="14" />, meaning that it can be between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/2/5b216498b4cb64f29f3bfdfa60736dc769f06c7d.png" class="latex" alt="$5^n$" width="17" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/c/c8c134ae9d194442eec9a0584ff74fc944ce9251.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{n+1}$" width="32" height="15" /> or not.
</p><p>If there are only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />s between every consecutive powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f89c2402133dba0ce0d0f5db38c33fd0635466e.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{867}$" width="28" height="15" />, there would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/8/e785fa90219af322135e2c747b41b6bb0f89b04d.png" class="latex" alt="$867*2 = 1734$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="116" height="14" /> power of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />s. However, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/a/2fa725c98dfe19f98809033755b801810bc298aa.png" class="latex" alt="$2013$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> powers of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> before  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/8/2f89c2402133dba0ce0d0f5db38c33fd0635466e.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{867}$" width="28" height="15" />, meaning the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/5/b656c46831ae39a26b85c04f8632971aad7b2361.png" class="latex" alt="$2013 - 1734 = \boxed{\textbf{(B)}279}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="188" height="30" />.
