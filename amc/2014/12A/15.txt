<p>A five-digit palindrome is a positive integer with respective digits <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/2/f8211da70cc567d5d14763c89fd27d304af7581a.png" class="latex" alt="$abcba$" width="43" height="12" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is non-zero.  Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the sum of all five-digit palindromes.  What is the sum of the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/2/8b2b9d5bb2d7fa7bb33cd4410b3fb53d470b6cab.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }9\qquad \textbf{(B) }18\qquad \textbf{(C) }27\qquad \textbf{(D) }36\qquad \textbf{(E) }45\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>For each digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/5/da5ede61811332b7345a9ebab108189119ece645.png" class="latex" alt="$a=1,2,\ldots,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="109" height="16" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/f/c6f654c27c1b832c873f288961f820c99efac337.png" class="latex" alt="$10\cdot10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" /> (ways of choosing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />) palindromes. So the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />s contribute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/a/6aabc91d8e62f4c9b6974c46fc77bbea1fb5b645.png" class="latex" alt="$(1+2+\cdots+9)(100)(10^4+1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="240" height="19" /> to the sum.
For each digit <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/b/ddb17f48c10f56b4582e0bd42b1d634c49db7f8d.png" class="latex" alt="$b=0,1,2,\ldots,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="16" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89a46acdd5c3e4f7a758ef5875dad0b66621c4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" /> (since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/a/67a8181c9d9b8253bd721b620aaabc5b01f4af96.png" class="latex" alt="$a \neq 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="42" height="17" />) palindromes. So the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />s contribute <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/5/b/65b9a4c55a835b7ae12eb096d076ff471ee7746a.png" class="latex" alt="$(0+1+2+\cdots+9)(90)(10^3+10)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="271" height="19" /> to the sum.
Similarly, for each <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/8/3f837a8a2cce456d5c735ee69722be56c173a728.png" class="latex" alt="$c=0,1,2,\ldots,9$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="124" height="16" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/9/a/89a46acdd5c3e4f7a758ef5875dad0b66621c4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="39" height="13" /> palindromes, so the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" /> contributes <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/1/a71a6724ee2854f2493caf9f6569c47d0a339317.png" class="latex" alt="$(0+1+2+\cdots+9)(90)(10^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="231" height="19" /> to the sum.
</p><p>It just so happens that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/b/38b77fb7c65e53c50aeee86e0951039d75743e95.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(1+2+\cdots+9)(100)(10^4+1)+(1+2+\cdots+9)(90)(10^3+10)+(1+2+\cdots+9)(90)(10^2)=49500000\]" width="718" height="19" /> so the sum of the digits of the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245c7c2a25a7c89bb618c9c343e03e007d264fdf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\; 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Notice that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/3/d136e8e1c494afb37065da2b11f47ac9f29425a2.png" class="latex" alt="$10001+ 99999 = 110000.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="195" height="14" /> In fact, ordering the palindromes in ascending order, we find that the sum of the nth palindrome and the nth to last palindrome is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1f7f2fec301458f2ca6d830d8b418233f658fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$110000.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/b/d/3bd3ace4de4e0ababc6bf6cff88d9a9f8f5e78ac.png" class="latex" alt="$9\cdot 10\cdot 10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" /> palindromes, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/3/4739126bff84658daf38a400b94af84c72f11fa0.png" class="latex" alt="$450$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> pairs of palindromes summing to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/f/d1f7f2fec301458f2ca6d830d8b418233f658fc2.png" class="latex" alt="$110000.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" /> Performing the multiplication gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a17e5592430e53342f466335c84110f70eac61b8.png" class="latex" alt="$49500000$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="71" height="13" />, so the sum <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/5/245c7c2a25a7c89bb618c9c343e03e007d264fdf.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}\; 18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="63" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>As shown above, there are a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" /> five-digit palindromes.  We can calculate their sum by finding the expected value of a randomly selected palindrome satisfying the conditions given, then multiplying it by <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" /> to get our sum.  The expected value for the ten-thousands and the units digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/c/00c526a020427c815e8196ee9c272f740cba7302.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1+2+3+\cdots+9}{9}=5$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="183" height="37" />, and the expected value for the thousands, hundreds, and tens digit is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/5/f354d01dddb016b290ed9332fbe2055df94cb849.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{0+1+2+\cdots+9}{10}=4.5$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="197" height="38" />.  Therefore our expected value is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/b/f7b28e6730f60aac54b656452e23997324504d3c.png" class="latex" alt="$5\times10^4+4.5\times10^3+4.5\times10^2+4.5\times10^1+5\times10^0=55,\!000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="490" height="18" />.  Since the question asks for the sum of the digits of the resulting sum, we do not need to keep the trailing zeros of either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/e/96e8dca57eb5f9d0023d6ddef9ed7392109f02cd.png" class="latex" alt="$55,\!000$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="49" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/d/fdd4beaeec1aa15c400a0763d78937b0385924b1.png" class="latex" alt="$900$" width="26" height="12" />.  Thus we only need to calculate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/7/777f1fd44312a46fac78c8ecaf96b9f41c0282b0.png" class="latex" alt="$55\times9=495$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="100" height="13" />, and the desired sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/9/2a9f2c6d5aba65304489e7266c7a591f78ee175f.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B) }18}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="64" height="28" />.
</p>
