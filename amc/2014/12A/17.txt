<p>A <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/6/ab6c9f4be6d9805718461a859fc7bd097c43cdee.png" class="latex" alt="$4\times 4\times h$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" /> rectangular box contains a sphere of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> and eight smaller spheres of radius <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.  The smaller spheres are each tangent to three sides of the box, and the larger sphere is tangent to each of the smaller spheres.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/8/8189a5b5a0917b8c93350827be4038af1839139d.png" class="latex" alt="$h$" width="10" height="12" />?
</p>
<center><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/d/8/9d894dbf42883508d51bf4973c78d1368570c6fa.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] import graph3; import solids; real h=2+2*sqrt(7); currentprojection=orthographic((0.75,-5,h/2+1),target=(2,2,h/2)); currentlight=light(4,-4,4); draw((0,0,0)--(4,0,0)--(4,4,0)--(0,4,0)--(0,0,0)^^(4,0,0)--(4,0,h)--(4,4,h)--(0,4,h)--(0,4,0)); draw(shift((1,3,1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((3,3,1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((3,1,1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((1,1,1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((2,2,h/2))*scale(2,2,2)*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((1,3,h-1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((3,3,h-1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((3,1,h-1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw(shift((1,1,h-1))*unitsphere,gray(0.85)); draw((0,0,0)--(0,0,h)--(4,0,h)^^(0,0,h)--(0,4,h)); [/asy]" width="148" height="252" /></center>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/9/389b17a67cf2fa08d454a756acaa193d4059b72b.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }2+2\sqrt 7\qquad \textbf{(B) }3+2\sqrt 5\qquad \textbf{(C) }4+2\sqrt 7\qquad \textbf{(D) }4\sqrt 5\qquad \textbf{(E) }4\sqrt 7\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="569" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be the point in the same plane as the centers of the top spheres equidistant from said centers. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> be the analogous point for the bottom spheres, and let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be the midpoint of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and the center of the large sphere. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> be the points at which line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/e/57ee5125358c0606c9b588580ddfa66f83e607b7.png" class="latex" alt="$AB$" width="27" height="13" /> intersects the top of the box and the bottom, respectively.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" /> be the center of any of the top spheres (you choose!). We have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/5/81551e5fa78bbcacd178dcc8adaa3eda6fa5603d.png" class="latex" alt="$AO=1\cdot\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/c/d1c65b05e1c58472d877b713ca9e8675a8ea2884.png" class="latex" alt="$CO=3$" width="61" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/4/f84f96feae8ad793266b5212574b23793118eb6e.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=\sqrt{3^2-\left(\sqrt2\right)^2}=\sqrt{7}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="212" height="45" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/e/b/0eba5cb9c105ee77ce1a141d774a7b8965b15ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=\sqrt{7}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="78" height="18" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/b/cfbb4c52e011ceed6e813118a7d59e931af444a2.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AD}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/3/0f32db217d7630882c3441fa8821cd70a6958c3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{BE}$" width="29" height="15" /> are clearly equal to the radius of the small spheres, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Thus the total height is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/1/2a1e9151f6a2875187b1159ae931e65b6eca8439.png" class="latex" alt="$AD+AC+BC+BE=2+2\sqrt7$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="271" height="18" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p><p>~AwesomeToad
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<div class="floatnone"><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2014_AMC_12A_Problem_17.JPG" class="image" title="caption"><img alt="caption" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//thumb/0/0f/2014_AMC_12A_Problem_17.JPG/500px-2014_AMC_12A_Problem_17.JPG" width="500" height="587" srcset="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//0/0f/2014_AMC_12A_Problem_17.JPG 1.5x" /></a></div>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> be the center of the large sphere and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> be the center of any small sphere. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> be a vertex of the rectangular prism closest to point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/5/a055f405829e64a3b70253ab67cb45ed6ed5bb29.png" class="latex" alt="$F$" width="14" height="12" /> be the point on the edge of the prism such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/e/8fe61d9d20e070fbd02e581eca23118337b50ee6.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DF}$" width="30" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/5/c65a0b904f8f63efdc2b5abfef3f4f05b2d3f990.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AF}$" width="28" height="15" /> are perpendicular. Let points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> and point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/2/fa2fa899f0afb05d6837885523503a2d4df434f9.png" class="latex" alt="$E$" width="14" height="12" /> lie on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/5/c65a0b904f8f63efdc2b5abfef3f4f05b2d3f990.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AF}$" width="28" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/e/8fe61d9d20e070fbd02e581eca23118337b50ee6.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DF}$" width="30" height="15" /> respectively such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/e/95eaca439ccbf2863a43b05f4de566ccdabdc392.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CE}$" width="29" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/a/84ae51dd95ca59139201d0895df8311e612782fb.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{CB}$" width="29" height="15" /> are perpendicular at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" />. 
</p><p><br />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is the radii of the spheres, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/f/c3f16fd3457acf873e7d0eac909db5b79b65d000.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=2+1=3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="125" height="14" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/3/c/13c970cbf5e2e7029089517d5e8efbb8cade6d8a.png" class="latex" alt="$CE$" width="28" height="12" /> is the shortest length between the center of a small sphere and the edge of the prism, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/1/b015259a188da5678f8f3c72825040bf4688ca7e.png" class="latex" alt="$CE=\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="77" height="18" />. Similarly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/9/4b906ec2ac3329328dfe6dd9859adcc79b265ddc.png" class="latex" alt="$AF=2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/c/6aca4694f47bf14bfe8136fe16a1e4ed7d663f13.png" class="latex" alt="$CEFB$" width="57" height="12" /> is a rectangle, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/1/4/3141542bd5bad6cf916c1aa2c8ab5ca72dea72f4.png" class="latex" alt="$BF=CE=\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="131" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/9/4b906ec2ac3329328dfe6dd9859adcc79b265ddc.png" class="latex" alt="$AF=2\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="86" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/8/ee88a88c8b906f0b9757e53137c8e3441e681b58.png" class="latex" alt="$AB=AF - BF = \sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="180" height="18" />. Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/d/2cde9d2f000333371e86d6a6564432352285c75d.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=\sqrt{3^2-\left(\sqrt2\right)^2}=\sqrt{7}=EF$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="266" height="45" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/5/6d5c9afa92ae3180f02754c177e530d9d0f8b4d4.png" class="latex" alt="$DE$" width="29" height="12" /> is the length from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to the top of the prism or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/c/1/ec112a86406eb5bb3d28e0aab4284e15d2657925.png" class="latex" alt="$DF=DE+EF=1+\sqrt{7}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="215" height="18" />. The prism is symmetrical, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/b/e/2be76bfbe99b1a975297c32d6c568e411d0249bd.png" class="latex" alt="$h=2DF=\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="139" height="28" />
</p><p>~BJHHar
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Take a cross section and you will see that h is made up of the two radii of the circles plus some radical expression. The only choice satisfying this condition is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/e/35e8ea2d16518197b5113002b8c0c7da6c311a3e.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(A)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="41" height="28" />.
</p>
