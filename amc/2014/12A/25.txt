<p>The parabola <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> has focus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and goes through the points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/3/563948106c3332662355412547d99acab218c16f.png" class="latex" alt="$(4,3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/d/6/7d653d09b4a98dcd868612d2e300e6fa7fd1173b.png" class="latex" alt="$(-4,-3)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="67" height="18" />.  For how many points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/7/1f70b74089fd8d8ba8d1ab8d0a6059be344dfee7.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)\in P$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="78" height="18" /> with integer coordinates is it true that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/b/8abc75fddb857322b10ad73c138a933f863ab191.png" class="latex" alt="$|4x+3y|\leq 1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="130" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/e/4/ee4a8c6cc681cce368d603d6c1bf14dcffc75c95.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }38\qquad \textbf{(B) }40\qquad \textbf{(C) }42\qquad \textbf{(D) }44\qquad \textbf{(E) }46\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="398" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>The parabola is symmetric through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/3/5b35fcfe05549f064cfa1d428f7a395a02836a8e.png" class="latex" alt="$y=- \frac{4}{3}x$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="71" height="37" />, and the common distance is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, so the directrix is the line through <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/8/298ff6780b4764bbb899e9b2c3b4913d1b299f93.png" class="latex" alt="$(1,7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/d/c3d4c2853e090428ccf88173b9633ab8a2869ffa.png" class="latex" alt="$(-7,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />, which is the line <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a4d38879d2c5db2afd19b7e9154d1935fea2661.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[3x-4y = -25.\]" width="119" height="16" /> Using the point-line distance formula, the parabola is the locus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/5/2a5479a13d0f971d8bdfebc9c4fbe79416af5157.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2+y^2 = \frac{\left\lvert 3x-4y+25 \right\rvert^2}{3^2+4^2}\]" width="201" height="43" /> which rearranges to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/7620ff7ecfb997f3a6e3c3f0a1cedc53bc4c91ce.png" class="latex" alt="$(4x+3y)^2 = 25(6x-8y+25)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="237" height="19" />.
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/0/4/8045f13f0897fc96f6a2b2b8cdde295424fa3dec.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 4x+3y \in \mathbb Z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="134" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/4/704e95dfd81ee32a4d1550af309c36e1749468a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lvert m \right\rvert \le 1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />.  Put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/1/181e2f26e63108fd099eb087444952d1d7aa5fd4.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 25k$" width="67" height="12" /> to obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/7/4c787993b04b9cc3d915de495bc242f2eddf9b19.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[25k^2 = 6x-8y+25\]" width="159" height="18" /><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/0/1e0efc6ffc1f03eb606d136abdc43e7ade06d2a9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[25k = 4x+3y.\]" width="116" height="16" />
and accordingly we find by solving the system that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb86e9c39d3fe31d2604f5387047b388fbc78acb.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{1}{2} (3k^2-3) + 4k$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="160" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/7/5873308146a7495eaba587504e825ee7a8c9f931.png" class="latex" alt="$y = -2k^2+3k+2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="146" height="18" />.
</p><p>One can show that the values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> that make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> an integer pair are precisely odd integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />.  For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/9/b998228003a1cb5e4c3c382ce3a9ac24e093058a.png" class="latex" alt="$\left\lvert 25k \right\rvert \le 1000$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="98" height="18" /> this is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/d/96df3e8ba814a58ae5d63904e187851dfb44ae74.png" class="latex" alt="$k= -39,-37,-35,\dots,39$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="205" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> values work and the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/1/a2194b81820e8380be586c1564c3f5d03d5c100d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="40" height="28" />.
</p><p>(Solution by C-273)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The axis of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/4/4b4cade9ca8a2c8311fafcf040bc5b15ca507f52.png" class="latex" alt="$P$" width="14" height="12" /> is inclined at an angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> relative to the coordinate axis, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/2/8/128d07b67009bf58d8577c61924d45bf3d5493cd.png" class="latex" alt="$\tan\theta = \tfrac 34$" style="vertical-align: -7px" width="75" height="23" />. We rotate the coordinate axis by angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> anti-clockwise, so that the parabola now has a vertical symmetry axis relative to the rotated coordinates. Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/8/94881dd279226c33725166ca8c335052a46f62ff.png" class="latex" alt="$(\widetilde{x}, \widetilde{y})$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="44" height="20" /> be the coordinates in the rotated system. Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/3/e53b1fe25be1c679117fb44a6a886fe1247d189a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="40" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/8/94881dd279226c33725166ca8c335052a46f62ff.png" class="latex" alt="$(\widetilde{x}, \widetilde{y})$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="44" height="20" /> are related by 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/1/231132042873ba9a5acaff480764393f31e07f95.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align}   \nonumber x = \widetilde{x}\cos\theta -\widetilde{y}\sin\theta &amp;= \tfrac 45 \widetilde{x} - \tfrac 35 \widetilde{y}, \\    y = \widetilde{x}\sin\theta +\widetilde{y}\cos\theta &amp;= \tfrac 35 \widetilde{x} + \tfrac 45 \widetilde{y} \end{align}" width="361" height="49" />
In the rotated coordinate system, the parabola has focus at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/6/f9603ca3089464e548fc6f1366bc474e7efef8d9.png" class="latex" alt="$(0,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and the two points on it are at <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/e/38ec6a35045b7cc0f04c4989a99570bcc29aee43.png" class="latex" alt="$(5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="38" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/8406e5f7c765b5c5fcd22a3c9a756da79907ca29.png" class="latex" alt="$(-5,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" />. Therefore, the directrix is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/6/2f62550d077179618321f2eb79bec7ddb8fb514c.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{y}=\pm 5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />; we can, WLOG, choose <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/e/bbecdb3a25b338aa49e26f94865b8ec6c694d75c.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{y}=-5$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="59" height="18" />. For a point on the parabola, it is equidistant from the focus and directrix, so the equation of the parabola is 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/0/8e0f3c9159a70f6815e1fbcf6a6e5e521ff2fa6a.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align}\tag{2}   \widetilde{x}^2+\widetilde{y}^2 = (\widetilde{y}+5)^2 \qquad  &amp;\Longrightarrow\qquad \widetilde{y} = \tfrac{1}{10}(\widetilde{x}^2-25) \end{align}" width="424" height="22" />
From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/a/5/3a563b8f29dbd7d80b65b6fbe8c23321ee786a0d.png" class="latex" alt="$|4x+3y|=5\widetilde{x}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="116" height="20" />, so we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/6/6963223a70a161874a4edb9c2937224b5dbcc3f5.png" class="latex" alt="$|\widetilde{x}|&lt;200$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="74" height="20" />. Substituting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/f/62f62a19f0425d8e6121ca0c1cfd3ce96e43c9c5.png" class="latex" alt="$(2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/9/0d9c3b1c142cbd3f8217dabc160c57e4750df241.png" class="latex" alt="$(1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="21" height="18" />, we get
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/4/5044039fcd801065ddbd11809823174aeade1f95.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\begin{align*}   50x &amp;= 40 \widetilde{x} - 3 \widetilde{x}^2 + 75, \\   50y &amp;= 30 \widetilde{x} + 4 \widetilde{x}^2 - 100 \end{align*}" width="178" height="46" />
For <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> to be an integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/4/5b44bcc85172b3c1df5cb5b47076aa67d450411d.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{x}$" width="11" height="13" /> must be a multiple of 5; setting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/d/ddda3f067a0a0d93ffb6db6ba78035f6435f7ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{x}=5a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="15" /> we get 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/6/f76297a5420503f3c791dd1d7e0a75c77c3d3c77.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2x = 8a - 3 a^2 + 3\]" width="141" height="17" />
Now we need <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> to be odd, i.e. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/d/ddda3f067a0a0d93ffb6db6ba78035f6435f7ecd.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{x}=5a$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="55" height="15" /> is an odd multiple of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" />, in which case we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/6/5b69c74ce024bf34b9105766e155ad8219a64e5b.png" class="latex" alt="$y = 3 a + 2 a^2 - 2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="134" height="20" />, which is also an integer. The values that satisfy the given conditions correspond to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/8/b08e20ed044862b41a73d6e475039aa7068befd4.png" class="latex" alt="$\widetilde{x}= \{\pm 5\cdot (2k-1)\mid k = 0, 1, \ldots , 19 \}={-195, -185, -175, ..., 195}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="513" height="20" />, and there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/3/153d574d14ad63993a1f5c2e6f3b62b22ea240d4.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(B)} \ 40}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="65" height="30" /> such numbers.
</p><p>(Solution by Shaddoll)
</p>
