<p>There are exactly <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" /> distinct rational numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/c/9/0c937d816a76e636ba346d281a645bca9bf6a889.png" class="latex" alt="$|k|&lt;200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/3/2a3083567ef6e4c63ddf113aad1c6efad4e79b71.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[5x^2+kx+12=0\]" width="142" height="16" /> has at least one integer solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/9/fc97ef67268cd4e91bacdf12b8901d7036c9a056.png" class="latex" alt="$N$" width="16" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/4/bf4b9f54b97eb84c5fac5810779b2ac92a3016fc.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }6\qquad \textbf{(B) }12\qquad \textbf{(C) }24\qquad \textbf{(D) }48\qquad \textbf{(E) }78\qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="389" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Factor the quadratic into
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/5/fe5199e9f960b0c62e2ab49965e0ff96a0bd31f6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\left(5x + \frac{12}{n}\right)\left(x + n\right) = 0\]" width="180" height="43" />
where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/e/6ee5df0d5b6736df591c2c12ce45cf2d71bba5e7.png" class="latex" alt="$-n$" width="24" height="8" /> is our integer solution. Then, 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/b/3eb94d8ebb2ae4384f3d40a12c2ae537ea961970.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[k = \frac{12}{n} + 5n,\]" width="101" height="37" />
which takes rational values between <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/8/59820a53cf7b362f73593e85ba993fbd3aba6299.png" class="latex" alt="$-200$" width="40" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> when <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/3/073998353c836f3f6e67dfb4eea9588e8800e06f.png" class="latex" alt="$|n| \leq 39$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />, excluding <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/6/2d6fc266463b33c8b1c079a50909f3fd03ed5ffd.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 0$" width="43" height="12" />. This leads to an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/7/7e77fe86c92a5d2fef69e7728acfcbad80c362d8.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 39 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) } 78}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="128" height="28" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Solve for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/c/f/acf467a467306991cbf257dce1a9d3696123e1a7.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[k=-\frac{12}{x}-5x.\]" width="114" height="37" />  Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> can be any integer in the range <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d8a6d739d6ebf4e90398304c7d024a68032a5f7.png" class="latex" alt="$[-39,0)\cup(0,39]$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="127" height="18" />  so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is rational with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/c/85cfc0c2d5ce791db5a0dc62fa47c632d10e390d.png" class="latex" alt="$\lvert k\rvert&lt;200$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="18" />.  Hence, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/b/9ebb33edd6586e83da90f55d23ad5ffe13f4cbd7.png" class="latex" alt="$39+39=\boxed{\textbf{(E) } 78}.$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="149" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Plug in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/7/8b7e8e0360f76071d30522d4979d1c01cb248a5d.png" class="latex" alt="$k=200$" width="61" height="12" /> to find the upper limit. You will find the limit to be a number from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/3/6c3c23a5ac00d0d32f28b3a6cf221965cef9cf04.png" class="latex" alt="$0&lt;x&lt;-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="90" height="13" /> and one that is just below <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/4/6a4e50a9cc464b64c9142990a65a36b20ff99856.png" class="latex" alt="$-39.$" width="35" height="12" /> All the integer values from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/e/bae5aba07d37ff6ff813107e76260fb31ad5794e.png" class="latex" alt="$-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="22" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/7/08763313a15808aa4aec73b1e951c486688c2627.png" class="latex" alt="$-39$" width="31" height="12" /> can be attainable through some value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />. Since the question asks for the absolute value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, we see that the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/0/7f082dd022233f432c345a022127a90c36250476.png" class="latex" alt="$39\cdot2 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)  }78.}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="134" height="28" />
</p><p>iron
</p>
