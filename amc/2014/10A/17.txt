<p>Three fair six-sided dice are rolled. What is the probability that the values shown on two of the dice sum to the value shown on the remaining die?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/7/507886b0828e0f3dceb1c4fb82c359900a2674c8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \dfrac16\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ \dfrac{13}{72}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ \dfrac7{36}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ \dfrac5{24}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ \dfrac29$" style="vertical-align: -13px" width="395" height="38" />
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>First, we note that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f55e0a11e29af7baee666c19c147e6f7c1923e.png" class="latex" alt="$1, 2, 3, 4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="64" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> ways to get sums of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/d/cddcbabf98bdc898829e8753059c7d6fbe15a575.png" class="latex" alt="$2, 3, 4, 5, 6$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="77" height="16" /> respectively--this is not too hard to see. With any specific sum, there is exactly one way to attain it on the other die. This means that the probability that two specific dice have the same sum as the other is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/3/0232e2cd81c4f2dc3f7c46c1875a3ee3578cbe7e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\dfrac16 \left( \dfrac{1+2+3+4+5}{36}\right) = \dfrac{5}{72}.\]" width="228" height="43" /> Since there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/6/d36ed57d9b17cf35aa42a98275a59a994033dacc.png" class="latex" alt="$\dbinom31$" style="vertical-align: -22px" width="33" height="53" /> ways to choose which die will be the one with the sum of the other two, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/e/62eae8688aa930dddd4972841b080a3043820fba.png" class="latex" alt="$3 \cdot \dfrac{5}{72} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)} \: \dfrac{5}{24}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="136" height="48" />.
</p>
