<p>Bridget bakes 48 loaves of bread for her bakery. She sells half of them in the morning for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/959df3ec8a0316741a287c42617c3fa1ac28f37a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 2.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> each. In the afternoon she sells two thirds of what she has left, and because they are not fresh, she charges only half price. In the late afternoon she sells the remaining loaves at a dollar each. Each loaf costs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e005376a0e0dc651089f778b1a1a358d0ff92344.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 0.75$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> for her to make. In dollars, what is her profit for the day?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/f/8ef5286aafd8d3f2f0a66d55957da7a8aaefbd01.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 24\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 36\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 44\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 48\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 52$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="394" height="17" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>She first sells one-half of her <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/f/5afbad0b221f0ef70186fe70b08e6fc1efdd8dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$48$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> loaves, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/a/8ba481345906c0cf1f47a601c92bdba3c196e4b5.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{48}{2}=24$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="64" height="37" /> loaves. Each loaf sells for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/9/959df3ec8a0316741a287c42617c3fa1ac28f37a.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 2.50$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" />, so her total earnings in the morning is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/a/d1a73555236ea88d2364ec2a9f5ec4e28dba4770.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[24\cdot \textdollar 2.50 = \textdollar 60\]" width="123" height="14" />
</p><p>This leaves 24 loaves left, and Bridget will sell <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/8/5381816675e74822277182347e2178e4a5a5bf3a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{2}{3}\times 24=16$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="95" height="37" /> of them for a price of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/6/d/46dcf57db2bc097c9af23af1d000f8de60cafb77.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar\frac{2.50}{2}=\textdollar 1.25$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="110" height="37" />. Thus, her total earnings for the afternoon is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/4/e54a88612e076fcbb746749cde7ab567cc303e23.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[16\cdot \textdollar 1.25  = \textdollar 20\]" width="122" height="14" />
</p><p>Finally, Bridget will sell the remaining <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/d/a2d1a5d748ccb305f58af0ee1a92b54cf106db19.png" class="latex" alt="$24-16=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="91" height="13" /> loaves for a dollar each. This is a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/d/05dfc5dd31fd58fbf85ad1c100c4d5f68157d3b8.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 1\cdot 8 = \textdollar 8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />
</p><p>The total amount of money she makes is equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/2/d/c2d2cc8a49934443e91e9106ae2a9754313f1d28.png" class="latex" alt="$60+20+8=\textdollar 88$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="15" />.
</p><p>However, since Bridget spends <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/0/e005376a0e0dc651089f778b1a1a358d0ff92344.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 0.75$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> making each loaf of bread, the total cost to make the bread is equal to  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79054717079e058e97811a6aefb6c13cf8140345.png" class="latex" alt="$\textdollar 0.75\cdot48=\textdollar 36$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="123" height="14" />.
</p><p>Her total profit is the amount of money she spent subtracted from the amount of money she made, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/0/4a085907343e46e4b1b8c80afdb3cc746896b312.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[88-36=52\implies\boxed{\textbf{(E)} \ 52}\]" width="212" height="28" />
</p>
