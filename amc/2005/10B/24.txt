<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be two-digit integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is obtained by reversing the digits
of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/d/02d24bc38e962a082b7a04fcd4629bb149614248.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - y^2 = m^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="18" /> for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />.
What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/3/523173e1a772e3d0fe571c849c3267be2abb1893.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y + m$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="80" height="14" />?

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969b4af989698bd6e7d17ef55244ccf6c76d1a42.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 10a+b, y = 10b+a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="192" height="16" />. The given conditions imply <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/1/7b18737adf5e499e3c33b3c5516930d328ce5a13.png" class="latex" alt="$x&gt;y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="13" />, which implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/2/082852571ecc48ed136ba13dcffa8f6e40064bbd.png" class="latex" alt="$a&gt;b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="42" height="13" />, and they also imply that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are nonzero. 
</p><p>Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/2/f/a2fae16ea9b6436f4e5c83f4269e70c1dba091df.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - y^2 = (x-y)(x+y) = (9a - 9b)(11a + 11b) = 99(a-b)(a+b) = m^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="576" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Since this must be a perfect square, all the exponents in its prime factorization must be even. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> factorizes into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/e/a7ea2c8fd7106b6df767206838fba6bd664da06f.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 \cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="15" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/f/4bf2cdea3abf6c3ea783ff5a6a9be018c24100b4.png" class="latex" alt="$11|(a-b)(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="129" height="18" />. However, the maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/7/9/779dec1fa400d67f5413e527177df80f6d027f65.png" class="latex" alt="$9-1=8$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/0/7/007842b1816aef98864a703999c61fb56fe4a606.png" class="latex" alt="$11|a+b$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="63" height="18" />. The maximum value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/4/9/f491b5e7215c09280e600a8392e7726ccc8702c9.png" class="latex" alt="$9+8=17$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="82" height="14" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/3/ed32eff390e08407e44423ebea81494172a4037b.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />. 
</p><p>Then, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/1/f/f1f0f5cc563b898212e0a1ca5f520eeb2aa51cfb.png" class="latex" alt="$33^2(a-b) = m^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="125" height="19" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> is a perfect square, but the only perfect squares that are within our bound on <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. We know <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/3/ed32eff390e08407e44423ebea81494172a4037b.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b=11$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="81" height="14" />, and, for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/0/ca0ac3cd47823ab124237d607ea3796c5c4e70a9.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="72" height="13" />, adding equations to eliminate <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/6/22653e91eb777624d3fc62b9a90ab11a59d8adc7.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=12 \Longrightarrow a=6, b=5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="192" height="16" />. Testing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/8/678e8d321bc28e3701dea3719943d09b7ac3555b.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> gives us <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/f/f7fd939b9e8e40df9b0e06ed49188f47a465f2c8.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=15 \Longrightarrow a=\frac{15}{2}, b=\frac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="208" height="37" />, which is impossible, as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> must be digits. Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/045b22f047b7cba2b0a1c3335890b29a4570cf71.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b) = (6,5)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="102" height="18" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/5/a/95a2a0ad0b6fbdd48405f7522b557cc7ebeff6fb.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+m=\boxed{\textbf{(E) }154}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="179" height="30" />.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>The first steps are the same as Solution 1.  Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969b4af989698bd6e7d17ef55244ccf6c76d1a42.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 10a+b, y = 10b+a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="192" height="16" />, where we know that a and b are digits (whole numbers less than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />).  
</p><p>Like Solution 1, we end up getting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/0/a70b754469f39c63a37e2c29b5cf16dad82257d7.png" class="latex" alt="$(9a - 9b)(11a + 11b) = 99(a-b)(a+b) = m^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="358" height="19" />.  This is where the solution diverges.  
</p><p>We know that the left side of the equation is a perfect square because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is an integer.  If we factor <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/3/1/a313bf06349648df7be807bee95b651604ce7a7e.png" class="latex" alt="$99$" width="17" height="12" /> into its prime factors, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/6/ef6ae49f801d52e5c99165b6e03bca65098ea56c.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2\cdot 11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="46" height="15" />.  In order to get a perfect square on the left side, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/7/1771c1b7c25f629fa17aa58115342a387200fc81.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="106" height="18" /> must make both prime exponents even.  Because the a and b are digits, a simple guess would be that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/e/1be092b2283c00c376e719c5f650a15433d43a28.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> (the bigger number) equals <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> while <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2aee761704bb62a779d1bbcb9fb09912bb516f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> is a factor of nine (1 or 9).  The correct guesses are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/6/da6fb663f3ea7e75056e5b89974ecfd5a75c7e67.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 6, b = 5$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> causing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/c/deccc9515430d5e330850cf7fe9a559ae35f947a.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 65, y = 56,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="116" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/a/3eac2a1fc37211987d12dd6a0081bba8a6d041c0.png" class="latex" alt="$m = 33$" width="57" height="12" />.  The sum of the numbers is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b020b2addf1295e816602e0c386c2a749916421.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }154}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Once again, the solution is quite similar as the above solutions. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are two digit integers, we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/9/969b4af989698bd6e7d17ef55244ccf6c76d1a42.png" class="latex" alt="$x = 10a+b, y = 10b+a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="192" height="16" /> and because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/4/cf4bf89f62453a0c2bdb30bcc4161ab099cb56a2.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 - y^2 = (x-y)(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="192" height="19" />, substituting and factoring, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/7/8f7c4f38f5e1ec544bca891b58def58f741bc05c.png" class="latex" alt="$99(a+b)(a-b) = m^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="19" />. 
</p><p>Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/e/07ebd5dc2df651085d6bc7e9b76fd2ef79f17b86.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)(a-b) = \frac{m^2}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="157" height="39" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/a/3fab889ae53723df66b23b053d464890fac1632c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m^2}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="39" /> must be an integer. A quick strategy is to find the smallest such integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/a/3fab889ae53723df66b23b053d464890fac1632c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{m^2}{99}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="39" /> is an integer. We notice that 99 has a prime factorization of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/8/d/d8ddcac7a4230e20900c93f79de8ceb9e3a85ea1.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 \cdot 11.$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="15" />
</p><p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/d/7/fd7cffde2c5f07ed298fca7d6adcfd720bbe4649.png" class="latex" alt="$m^2 = n.$" width="62" height="15" /> Since we need a perfect square and 3 is already squared, we just need to square 11. So <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/3/eb35dce1d10acf9789e63af903fa052f834c42a0.png" class="latex" alt="$3^2 \cdot 11^2$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="53" height="15" /> gives us 1089 as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/5/5/355cd10c4c3876a8bd1d4bf8053a1df21ed09f45.png" class="latex" alt="$m = \sqrt{1089} = 33.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="137" height="18" /> We now get the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/1/d717b7df68089bd0b24d5fe838aa732a354dc828.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)(x+y) = 33^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="161" height="19" />, which we can also write as     <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/5/c/85c6bd5e5f545be63f1ac79ea073c6c935c1277a.png" class="latex" alt="$(x-y)(x+y) = 11^2 \cdot 3^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="190" height="19" />.
</p><p>A very simple guess assumes that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/8/cb8f22fe8b12285f6c25a7352b078216ff2f7d06.png" class="latex" alt="$x-y=3^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="81" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/7/1879e1145b1d7e344fa793a98a011fd1f226f0ed.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=11^2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="90" height="18" /> since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> are positive. Finally, we come to the conclusion that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/3/503691fd771acc6e596537d92bac9dadb7696a54.png" class="latex" alt="$x=65$" width="52" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/2/ed27dc23eb5b804966050e762b7dbd6628d4efed.png" class="latex" alt="$y=56$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/7/2871b391f606ea213aedbf61bf8fa230b9185275.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+m$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="80" height="14" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/5/e/d5eb8726a34932d28103ce7ce17456421222603f.png" class="latex" alt="$=$" style="vertical-align: 2px" width="13" height="5" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b020b2addf1295e816602e0c386c2a749916421.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }154}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />.
</p><p>Note that all of the solutions used <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/d/add2609af1bb7632cc22b68fd49fdd94de06b327.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> as part of their solution.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h2>
<p>Continue the same as Solution <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> until we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/6/f/b6f3bccae16d5e31266370fced24edc41538d5da.png" class="latex" alt="$33$" width="17" height="12" />. Knowing that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/2/97200013eb00f07abe5c958ace0db1977a0d1dc6.png" class="latex" alt="$33^2 = 1089$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="85" height="15" />, we have narrowed down our Pythagorean triples. We know that the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> other squares should be larger than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4edc4613997a910639048b4237ad17260a92852b.png" class="latex" alt="$33^2$" width="24" height="15" />, so we can start testing. 
</p><p>If we start testing the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/7/78792fe4680a164f537ea72a4f900e4471433c3b.png" class="latex" alt="$40$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />s, it is fruitless since the closest to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/d/4edc4613997a910639048b4237ad17260a92852b.png" class="latex" alt="$33^2$" width="24" height="15" /> would be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/e/75eed7c7b1ec5ba25a362dfd9cc3440fdf44e45c.png" class="latex" alt="$33 - 45 - 54$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="101" height="15" /> which is not a Pythagorean triple. We can start by testing out the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/6/906734e2f1010e9a171ffee8050cbc0649cea37c.png" class="latex" alt="$50$" width="17" height="12" />s, and it turns our that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/c/1fcdb6551031b9ffa8bec22d19004eda3c951b8e.png" class="latex" alt="$33 - 56 - 65$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="101" height="15" /> is a Pythagorean triple. Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/1/011ba44f954b4fa0114e4f45723f82397901744e.png" class="latex" alt="$33+56+65$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="101" height="15" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/0/4b020b2addf1295e816602e0c386c2a749916421.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }154}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="72" height="28" />.
</p><p>~Arcticturn
</p>
