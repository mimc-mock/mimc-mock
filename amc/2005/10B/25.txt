<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>The question asks for the maximum possible number of elements. The integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> can be included because you cannot make <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b829a6f0810a25e9ca9f85d690f2bafd0f2c1773.png" class="latex" alt="$125$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> with integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> without the other number being greater than <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. The integers from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> are left. They can be paired so the sum is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b829a6f0810a25e9ca9f85d690f2bafd0f2c1773.png" class="latex" alt="$125$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/a/37a2b2089c7f919e43afb4762dcbe43eeb0aba39.png" class="latex" alt="$25+100$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/c/bfc004ad6c7a7b81eb7b1405dd3380d75c1d23b7.png" class="latex" alt="$26+99$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/5/0455999d3afabc6b81bed067c7cc3e5f4a128ac3.png" class="latex" alt="$27+98$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/5/3958e984d2597b35da7975f4503cdcf965a777f7.png" class="latex" alt="$\ldots$" width="19" height="2" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/7/f67260a1d21df00444c0b427ff9fb43c8719b82f.png" class="latex" alt="$62+63$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" />. That is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/5/4c5e2e4c4950cff11894abd202aa2949e21819c5.png" class="latex" alt="$38$" width="17" height="12" /> pairs, and at most one number from each pair can be included in the set. The total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/e/8/ae8f0aa025292da41a4bee9b2517c995ae58c7bb.png" class="latex" alt="$24 + 38 = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 62}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="148" height="30" />.
</p><p>Also, it is possible to see that since the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/2/9/e290f2cba0ba0e11c0b91630da15a408da2fcf1c.png" class="latex" alt="$24$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="18" height="12" /> are in the set there are only the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e59e2c6e83eb78cca610a5fd4070ae01c8d4ae60.png" class="latex" alt="$100$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> to consider. As <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/6/7/f67260a1d21df00444c0b427ff9fb43c8719b82f.png" class="latex" alt="$62+63$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="58" height="14" /> gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b829a6f0810a25e9ca9f85d690f2bafd0f2c1773.png" class="latex" alt="$125$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />, the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/6/6/66644fd9f411299c3c1eabf151b5f601c9ef6c21.png" class="latex" alt="$25$" width="17" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/6/3e67376d85a21889a01b76d7c9d8c765b2bac379.png" class="latex" alt="$62$" width="17" height="12" /> can be put in subset <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> without having two numbers add up to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/2/b829a6f0810a25e9ca9f85d690f2bafd0f2c1773.png" class="latex" alt="$125$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" />. In this way, subset <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> will have the numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/e/6/3e67376d85a21889a01b76d7c9d8c765b2bac379.png" class="latex" alt="$62$" width="17" height="12" />, and so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/7/7a70b5543f0f4d1f4e2f908db1ccc0c9f68654a6.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 62}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="65" height="30" />.
</p>
