<p>How many positive cubes divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/f/0ffc2c64d7db2d287cb6fe8a7eb1acb3a83018d3.png" class="latex" alt="$3! \cdot 5! \cdot 7!$" width="67" height="13" />&#160;?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/e/51eaf81489e290b727f687aee11023d44131e2f2.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) } 2\qquad \textbf{(B) } 3\qquad \textbf{(C) } 4\qquad \textbf{(D) } 5\qquad \textbf{(E) } 6$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="355" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/2/9122430401be045761bc02e8f332c413575a584e.png" class="latex" alt="$3! \cdot 5! \cdot 7! = (3\cdot2\cdot1) \cdot (5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1) \cdot (7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1) = 2^{8}\cdot3^{4}\cdot5^{2}\cdot7^{1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="583" height="19" />
</p><p>Therefore, a <a href="/wiki/index.php/Perfect_cube" title="Perfect cube">perfect cube</a> that divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/f/0ffc2c64d7db2d287cb6fe8a7eb1acb3a83018d3.png" class="latex" alt="$3! \cdot 5! \cdot 7!$" width="67" height="13" /> must be in the form <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/7/7/e772537d0b691f0684ebed9ddc16bfaded352ed1.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{a}\cdot3^{b}\cdot5^{c}\cdot7^{d}$" width="104" height="15" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/6/a/96ab646de7704969b91c76a214126b45f2b07b25.png" class="latex" alt="$d$" width="9" height="12" /> are <a href="/wiki/index.php/Nonnegative" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonnegative">nonnegative</a> <a href="/wiki/index.php/Multiple" title="Multiple">multiples</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> that are less than or equal to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3b3607827f3fb0e7f9334e9afe9611cb836c8dc.png" class="latex" alt="$8, 5, 2$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="43" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/b/f/5bf27edd6ee0c25241392922eca9b9c9144f4d73.png" class="latex" alt="$1,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> respectively. 
</p><p>So: 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/6/c7675c3b594b7165dcb726bd3cfd306fa5fcafbe.png" class="latex" alt="$a\in\{0,3,6\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="92" height="18" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cde695f2e4542fd01f860a89189f47a27143b66.png" class="latex" alt="$3$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a06570b6c6ff826645a24a1cda0589bf0570792.png" class="latex" alt="$b\in\{0,3\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="73" height="18" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/f/2/5f2d48a509341a94e3a1461f27e9301ec83ad392.png" class="latex" alt="$c\in\{0\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="56" height="18" /> (<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> possibility)
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/a/c8a1ccbb2ee2481ad5a93288ed2fce56146f8cbc.png" class="latex" alt="$d\in\{0\}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="57" height="18" />(<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> possibility)
</p><p>So the number of perfect cubes that divide <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/f/0ffc2c64d7db2d287cb6fe8a7eb1acb3a83018d3.png" class="latex" alt="$3! \cdot 5! \cdot 7!$" width="67" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/0/640145f547950ef1cbfb7e4cd64a5928ad4a1186.png" class="latex" alt="$3\cdot2\cdot1\cdot1 = \boxed{\textbf{(E) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -11px" width="156" height="30" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/e/d/ded703b3a01268a76cdfbff081b4cbb927899dc8.png" class="latex" alt="$3! \cdot 5! \cdot 7! = (3\cdot2\cdot1) \cdot (5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1) \cdot (7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="454" height="18" />
</p><p>In the expression, we notice that there are 3 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/6251edcef979b471d0491c2edc7127345fe07a28.png" class="latex" alt="$3&#39;s$" width="21" height="14" />, 3 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/f/bbf4bd0d713d4cae42912f7ca29fe68bec5f64ec.png" class="latex" alt="$2&#39;s$" width="21" height="14" />, and 3 <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/f/99fd1c19d4fbfae8ddeee2fafd781648083d9580.png" class="latex" alt="$1&#39;s$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="21" height="14" />. This gives us our first 3 cubes: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/a/c4aaecd00c115e0a1f5d807ac00b10f428d07993.png" class="latex" alt="$3^3$" width="15" height="15" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/6/3f6a4ab443c94f13ae3ad0efbc8c6fa07b58a930.png" class="latex" alt="$2^3$" width="15" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/d/56dd47945e92f8205a63aae2eeca65af834868c0.png" class="latex" alt="$1^3$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="15" height="15" />. 
</p><p>However, we can also multiply smaller numbers in the expression to make bigger expressions. For example, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/2/9/d292cd093ec87e208743d247db3f897420d48e9b.png" class="latex" alt="$(2 \cdot 2) \cdot 4 \cdot 4=4 \cdot 4 \cdot 4 = 4^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="207" height="19" /> (one 2 comes from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/5/6859713adc973d3fbe97fafef9ce829757e54b12.png" class="latex" alt="$3!$" width="12" height="13" />, and the other from the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/3/2933f407c21ceb3f3ec8370f43cd5b939250ffca.png" class="latex" alt="$5!$" width="12" height="13" />). Using this method, we find:
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/2/172fada8c3ae831ada2e5f9d8cf3400088f78455.png" class="latex" alt="$(3 \cdot 2) \cdot (3 \cdot 2) \cdot 6 = 6^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="19" />
</p><p>and
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/8/e/58e316f494f9521aee900429553e4110518b6b1c.png" class="latex" alt="$(3 \cdot 4) \cdot (3 \cdot 4) \cdot (2 \cdot 6) = 12^3$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="211" height="19" />
</p><p>So, we have 6 cubes total: <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/7/0/370e75df1b27d4a9a48565aaa89b9e8b083e83c1.png" class="latex" alt="$1^3 ,2^3, 3^3, 4^3, 6^3,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="119" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/f/57f93251c880a97e38d5a4090a665387be05db05.png" class="latex" alt="$12^3$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="27" height="17" /> for a total of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/7/107d22cf8cea8e2bc3744cf7bdac7cdfd2af688c.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(E) }6}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="54" height="28" /> cubes
</p>
