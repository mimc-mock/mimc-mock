<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:Circlenc1.png" class="image"><img alt="Circlenc1.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//8/83/Circlenc1.png" width="279" height="279" /></a>
</p><p>WLOG, Let us assume that the diameter is of length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. 
</p><p>The length of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/1/7/a179ff2638e4799cadd820db205c2beff6299ce9.png" class="latex" alt="$AC$" width="27" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/1/4e1965f8d70832ded66434c75a7bd52b75090af0.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="11" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/9/d/e9dc4f9abdac55c081c747fd2db62ae369497f05.png" class="latex" alt="$CO$" width="28" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/1/9/719f584734f05be763275a9169640467578ded2f.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="37" />.

</p><p>The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d87922337210a9ecb2522803950fde46fe69ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCO$" width="59" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/0/cf09528e2b2a3412fceab4ab4edf9afd6546aa10.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{6}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="79" height="41" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/3/0a3001352ca396a013508a45c7a0cf00411e89df.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\sqrt{2}}{36}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />.
</p><p>The height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" /> can be found using the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/8/d/18d87922337210a9ecb2522803950fde46fe69ec.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCO$" width="59" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/f/f/7ff1d53900652da5f2d40ef5427512642e85c0b7.png" class="latex" alt="$DO$" width="29" height="12" /> as base.
</p><p>Hence, the height of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/5/0d592c8be70c09aea04202effed539c8547adee9.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{36}}{\dfrac{1}{2}\cdot\dfrac{1}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -34px" width="41" height="82" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/d/67d2c9c921d1858b0e8dc8a0168bf9c39faf928a.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{9}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="26" height="41" />. 
</p><p>The diameter is the base for both the triangles <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />,
</p><p>Hence, the ratio of the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" /> to the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" /> is
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/a/2ea4fb5a30f95d27a006a1d43017e239997540a4.png" class="latex" alt="$\dfrac{\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{9}}{\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -39px" width="30" height="87" /> = <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/5/6456cefcb4a5262eee52eda1b544496a5efc20d8.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\textbf{(C) }\frac{1}{3}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="59" height="48" />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Say the center of the circle is point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/d/51da37d984564162c87710ca27bea422f657fb73.png" class="latex" alt="$O$" width="13" height="12" />;

Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/e/e/4eed1e217bb14b892389f5ef8f60042aadba382e.png" class="latex" alt="$CO=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/4/eb465295205ac8c083b62c7acd58fe47323dabd6.png" class="latex" alt="$DO=3$" width="62" height="12" />.
The area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" /> can be expressed as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/b/9/ab9fdf49119b985ef4e1bed9aa7ed2caaea7e654.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(CD)(6)\text{sin }(CDE).$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="168" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092c577cd60014f96b82e82b49430ac1871b50f9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{1}{2}(CD)(6)$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="78" height="37" /> happens to be the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75d6d58b03031bd7cb52a7ca886a8ddf369d21c.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABD$" width="59" height="13" />. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5">Solution 5</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/4/3/d4350c278972068aa00c47f368b984efb308fff2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] unitsize(2.5cm); defaultpen(fontsize(10pt)+linewidth(.8pt)); dotfactor=3; pair O=(0,0), C=(-1/3.0), B=(1,0), A=(-1,0); pair D=dir(aCos(C.x)), E=(-D.x,-D.y); draw(A--B--D--cycle); draw(D--E--C); draw(unitcircle,white); drawline(D,C); dot(O); clip(unitcircle); draw(unitcircle); label(&quot;$E$&quot;,E,SSE); label(&quot;$B$&quot;,B,E); label(&quot;$A$&quot;,A,W); label(&quot;$D$&quot;,D,NNW); label(&quot;$C$&quot;,C,SW); draw(rightanglemark(D,C,B,2)); [/asy]" width="272" height="262" />
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Let the point G be the reflection of point <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/f/9ffb448918db29f2a72f8f87f421b3b3cad18f95.png" class="latex" alt="$D$" width="15" height="12" /> across <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" />. (Point G is on the circle).
</p><p><br />
Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/3/4/2345cf4f7dddab7e3fbb69f1266183fce6d5d1be.png" class="latex" alt="$AC=x$" width="62" height="13" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/5/b456135c0b1ad89a495f98d7721d4eacdccc7b19.png" class="latex" alt="$BC=2x$" width="72" height="12" />. The diameter is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/e/70e634348f8df9eaeac4ced35db64d08a906532e.png" class="latex" alt="$3x$" width="19" height="12" />. To find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/e/27e525b776c49bbe7d0ae66cfc7ab8f58f40624e.png" class="latex" alt="$DC$" width="29" height="12" />, there are two ways (presented here):
</p><p>1. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> is the diameter, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/4/784f65f67919a93e579daa6b35aa994d0c7b85a4.png" class="latex" alt="$CD=CG$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="85" height="14" />. Using power of points,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/3/723ade5bd214e9f80b53d8f81512d4d2ab40b2e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AC\cdot BC=x\cdot2x=2x^{2}=CD^{2} \longrightarrow CD=x\sqrt{2}\]" width="378" height="18" />
2. Use the geometric mean theorem,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/3/723ade5bd214e9f80b53d8f81512d4d2ab40b2e2.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[AC\cdot BC=x\cdot2x=2x^{2}=CD^{2} \longrightarrow CD=x\sqrt{2}\]" width="378" height="18" />
(These are the same equations but obtained through different formulae)
</p><p><br />
Therefore <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/2/ce20e5df9095f58c9db43463874cfe7bd170dd77.png" class="latex" alt="$DG=2x\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="98" height="18" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/9/9/699784c989c3db382520e6f92819e062d239f504.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{DE}$" width="30" height="15" /> is a diameter, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/1/9c1b1ea9c8ebaa33d9a6480b52485e54ae998c0b.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DGE$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="62" height="18" /> is right. By the Pythagorean theorem,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/a/a/caadf32b0b10a9f4bf3f585c0b33b1da0cf777c6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[DE^{2}=GD^{2}+GE^{2} \longrightarrow \left(3x\right)^{2}=\left(2x\sqrt{2}\right)^{2}+GE^{2}\]" width="391" height="35" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/3/f535810c73ffe5cad5cfbab7691e5c738b09b0a1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[9x^{2}=8x^{2}+GE^{2} \longrightarrow GE^{2}=x^{2} \longrightarrow GE=x\]" width="356" height="17" />
</p><p><br />
As established before, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/d/4/1d4679cc3f48dc4d1a567fc49514dd13c83a7ec3.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle DGE$" width="57" height="12" /> is right (if you are unsure, look up "inscribed angle theorem", this is a special case of the theorem where the central angle measures <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/0/3f0cd95f95238159d02dbcea0aca632c2928991c.png" class="latex" alt="$180^{\circ}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="33" height="13" />) so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/6/f269c5510a2fc9f8890ad6289630d92ecb7dc691.png" class="latex" alt="$GE=x$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="65" height="14" /> is the altitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/5/8c5425baeec765203a664dc6bd0f0562f2bf485a.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle DCE$" width="60" height="13" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/1/8119ec74920f1b03bda562d0f43a14ccb55ba4a3.png" class="latex" alt="$DC=x\sqrt{2}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="89" height="18" /> is the base. Therefore
