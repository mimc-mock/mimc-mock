<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/2/7525b2564d035900ed440ae7f4cf3949b7c713b9.png" class="latex" alt="$A(2,2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/9/9491ac2abd87e735bfb06313fb292fbd3e71e5ee.png" class="latex" alt="$B(7,7)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="53" height="18" /> be points in the plane. Define <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> as the region in the first quadrant consisting of those points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c3a2d2224f7d163b46d702132425d47828bf538.png" class="latex" alt="$\triangle ABC$" width="58" height="13" /> is an acute triangle. What is the closest integer to the area of the region <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/e/f/2eff582589d62efa2000431e79df55073d1850bb.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 25     \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 39     \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 51     \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 60      \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/2/5/725584737d8b9165da2946585c60c26fb66110fb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="[asy] Label f;  f.p=fontsize(6); xaxis(-1,15,Ticks(f, 2.0));  yaxis(-1,15,Ticks(f, 2.0));  pair A = MP(&quot;A&quot;,(2,2),SW), B = MP(&quot;B&quot;,(7,7),NE); D(A--B); filldraw((0,4)--(4,0)--(14,0)--(0,14)--cycle,gray); filldraw(CP(0.5(A+B),A),white); D(A); D(B); [/asy]" width="668" height="668" />
</p><p>For angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" /> to be acute, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> must be between the two lines that are perpendicular to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> and contain points <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/9/019e9892786e493964e145e7c5cf7b700314e53b.png" class="latex" alt="$A$" width="13" height="13" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/5/ff5fb3d775862e2123b007eb4373ff6cc1a34d4e.png" class="latex" alt="$B$" width="14" height="12" />. For angle <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> to be acute, first draw a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/2/2/22231a5cdd4fe05a11f4b577e3b81221f3017a85.png" class="latex" alt="$45-45-90$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="98" height="13" /> triangle with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/0/840e2b592390eb6ec918fa6f3292716ce170de66.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline{AB}$" width="28" height="15" /> as the hypotenuse. Note <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/3/c3355896da590fc491a10150a50416687626d7cc.png" class="latex" alt="$C$" width="14" height="12" /> cannot be inside this triangle's circumscribed circle or else <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/2/ef231cd8b3d024d165be26245a618d8a25464245.png" class="latex" alt="$\angle C &gt; 90^\circ$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="75" height="13" />. Hence, the area of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/f/eff43e84f8a3bcf7b6965f0a3248bc4d3a9d0cd4.png" class="latex" alt="$R$" width="14" height="12" /> is the area of the large triangle minus the area of the small triangle minus the area of the circle, which is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/3/4c30506aa167b1e665e0c589c14421720d44a82e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{14^2}{2}-\frac{4^2}{2}-(\frac{5\sqrt{2}}{2})^2\pi=98-8-\frac{25\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="292" height="41" />, which is approximately <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/e/1/6e1fd7cb028e27b8648efbfb002f3e6d4fcb33a5.png" class="latex" alt="$51$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" />. The answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/f/1/ff1cac0ec2caa1645f90b48b2827793673636a84.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="25" height="24" />.
</p>
