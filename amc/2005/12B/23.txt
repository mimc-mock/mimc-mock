<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> be the set of ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/3/c73ed093cc73a8d9a14185fa525e7067e5c3725d.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y,z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" /> of real numbers for which
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/1/c117b87dca578c53d5694e7f783975909325a5bc.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\log_{10}(x+y) = z \text{ and } \log_{10}(x^{2}+y^{2}) = z+1.\]" width="344" height="19" />
There are real numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> such that for all ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/8/0/a80ed325942e6313ca84ecd7f6c161ff56cd11ec.png" class="latex" alt="$(x,y.z)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="55" height="18" /> in <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad28c83c99a8fd0dd2e2e594c9d02ee532765a0a.png" class="latex" alt="$S$" width="12" height="12" /> we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/b/14bd0d09d2089bc2bec61b99d0fe5c924e1ca841.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{3}+y^{3}=a \cdot 10^{3z} + b \cdot 10^{2z}.$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="215" height="18" /> What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/7/917cdf8efc9c4864345e3c006cc837fec14e7cc8.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b?$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="48" height="14" />
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/8/2/9825d1281a3d8e56ec1026ca56ce2d9bfa019c77.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ \frac {15}{2} \qquad  \textbf{(B)}\ \frac {29}{2} \qquad  \textbf{(C)}\ 15 \qquad  \textbf{(D)}\ \frac {39}{2} \qquad  \textbf{(E)}\ 24$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="406" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/4/0b432ab7b5b1ee4a3553310dd8e905b620ea2461.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = s$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="74" height="14" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/9/f/19fb3ec7a08ada7821973712bb5c5b9c75c7667a.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 = t$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="87" height="18" />.   
Then, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/4/c145f1e4078ad4e312341b14f149abe2749cf667.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(s)=z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="79" height="18" /> implies <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/a/a9ae8929c1fb349a5dabd8e68aa1b3656c92af30.png" class="latex" alt="$\log(10s) = z+1= \log(t)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="196" height="18" />,so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/c/0/3c087f5258748e4916a5f7f52fbc385d2278c8b6.png" class="latex" alt="$t=10s$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />.   
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/5/485e3f2dffca626e2329611889f2937a16c7fed6.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3 + y^3 = s\cdot\dfrac{3t-s^2}{2} = s(15s-\dfrac{s^2}{2})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="275" height="39" />.  
Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/b/39ba88f8234323fa41d16e039e36132189fdc4ac.png" class="latex" alt="$s = 10^z$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="57" height="13" />, we find that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142e87a6ea0bba131f602e954eca9a67ac5c9a46.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3 + y^3 = 15\cdot10^{2z} - (1/2)\cdot10^{3z}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="253" height="19" />.  
Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/1/7/c17712e5fa5bdf143aa31b369559391f19b2a11a.png" class="latex" alt="$a+b = \frac{29}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="84" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>First, remember that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/4/6/446930c357814b554e45bce0229906b2cb75fae7.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3 + y^3$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="55" height="18" /> factors to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/6/4/1648197197ec44237d782980d2e8d7909b6b0dff.png" class="latex" alt="$(x + y) (x^2 - xy + y^2)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="168" height="19" />. By the givens, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/3/4c3e4e3c3e160b887b2d0c33aeea5c9b8797b954.png" class="latex" alt="$x + y = 10^z$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/3/e033cec927adcfe073f734b110ef739f29d66b2d.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + y^2 = 10^{z + 1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="18" />. These can be used to find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" />:
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/9/f7967b17ebc5f13cba6c3efe77596eb37bd2163e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[(x + y)^2 = 10^{2z}\]" width="117" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/b/8/7b8e87035760ba6ffc9de644abcc1f99296344ec.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + 2xy + y^2 = 10^{2z}\]" width="163" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/a/51a82b542652c871a2a5bdd04616b8ad6eb73d00.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2xy = 10^{2z} - 10^{z + 1}\]" width="148" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/7/2/272577214ce8a9ae60d5acd64f3eca1781717951.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[xy = \frac{10^{2z} - 10^{z + 1}}{2}\]" width="143" height="38" />
</p><p>Therefore,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/4/014d6c496227809e14206399e7b4a7185307f3c3.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^3 + y^3 = a \cdot 10^{3z} + b \cdot 10^{2z} = 10^z\left(10^{z + 1} - \frac{10^{2z} - 10^{z + 1}}{2}\right)\]" width="453" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/d/4/6d4a9dc625258ca6a010da9c3600772b3bc69a01.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= 10^z\left(10^{z + 1} - \frac{10^{2z} - 10^{z + 1}}{2}\right)\]" width="235" height="43" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/2/9/b2937aa95ed289010ec5a7282e4378f466baa509.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= 10^{2z + 1} - \frac{10^{3z} - 10^{2z + 1}}{2}\]" width="196" height="38" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/d/7/dd740210cb87719fa28a9592eadb74dc59d4f85f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= -\frac{1}{2} \cdot 10^{3z} + \frac{3}{2} \cdot 10^{2z + 1}\]" width="186" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/5/035f86d29f3587edc966682ba6feed38226f692e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[= -\frac{1}{2} \cdot 10^{3z} + 15 \cdot 10^{2z}.\]" width="180" height="37" />
</p><p>It follows that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/3/0/03052bbbe6b519d59a4f15ee7e366973b8e4ca33.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -\frac{1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="59" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/e/34e5f91de9daf5d4420fab8537bbe9377e312200.png" class="latex" alt="$b = 15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="49" height="13" />, thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/2/7e2c43f8a03918c69882e684698c9bf3cf805b27.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b = \frac{29}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="89" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>We can rearrange <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/7/e576098903221c0a82a9da42be1e25ca0995c0c5.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(x+y)=z$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="126" height="18" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38db91c1822f5c1d6a9bd2e5e3887458090260e1.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=10^{z}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/f/15f9ae39b289e1c173cb100fc4666eaaacdb3f11.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_{10}(x^2+y^2)=z+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="172" height="19" /> into <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/e/dbe457e9f97b359ed18b9e5cdbea8936af8bb0b4.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=10^{z+1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="18" />
</p><p>We can then put <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" /> to the third power or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/a/d7a25cb64acdbb10477fceb9f54dd6aff13a1744.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)^{3}=10^{3z}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="119" height="19" />. Basic polynomial multiplication shows us that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/e/0be51ffc49f63270cb5043b98ef074c85f53c626.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)^{3}=x^3+3x^{2}y+3xy^{2}+y^3=10^{3z}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="318" height="19" /> Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/8/2d8f443533a0afa7a01b50cc7398b31267575f23.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3x^{2}y-3xy^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="230" height="18" /> or  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/5/815d1f66597c3f3d4f38af5b567c323dd8c0d705.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3xy(x+y)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="220" height="19" />. We know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38db91c1822f5c1d6a9bd2e5e3887458090260e1.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=10^{z}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> so we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9d8e0e0ece9a80bf3494efad0526113ee90691.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3xy(10^{z})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="19" />.
</p><p>Now we need to find out what <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> is equal to in terms of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />. We will find <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/7/3f79c3676b62fb47243e0d357c87115b6f3395e4.png" class="latex" alt="$xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="19" height="11" /> by squaring <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/3/d/d3da9f8c7fe0473fdd6f8597eff61d71fedda430.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="41" height="14" />. It is basic polynomial multiplication to figure out that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/0/8a0258a361b2d02dc0b97168360497cb8d39dd88.png" class="latex" alt="$(x+y)^{2}=x^2+2xy+y^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="195" height="19" />. We are also given that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/e/dbe457e9f97b359ed18b9e5cdbea8936af8bb0b4.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+y^2=10^{z+1}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="122" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/8/d/38db91c1822f5c1d6a9bd2e5e3887458090260e1.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y=10^{z}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/2/572b3c9b2cdef2f880bbad709b6ca6a9fd0f583c.png" class="latex" alt="$(10^{z})^{2}=10^{z+1}+2xy$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="165" height="19" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/d/49d78e0ce648d74d150bf5d98f3dc1059af8a65c.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2z}=10^{z+1}+2xy$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="150" height="18" />. Rearranging the terms of this equation we obtain that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/a/4/ea4a7fd57642ccfbf1a1eee2635508cb10cb7a80.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=\frac{10^{2z}-10^{z+1}}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="143" height="39" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/6/1f6ba3f35fe7f00a00afc46b2cd15f951ec56122.png" class="latex" alt="$xy=\frac{10^z(10^z-10)}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="152" height="38" />. Now plugging this equation into our original equation  <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/9/fe9d8e0e0ece9a80bf3494efad0526113ee90691.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3xy(10^{z})$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="19" />, we obtain the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/4/1/941e3afceef94c0161c7eb9c764aa6395fee5385.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3(\frac{10^z(10^z-10)}{2})(10^{z})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="308" height="38" /> 
Simple rearranging of this equation yields the result that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/a/5/0a55501d31b12b2f514011f4a682e78f9c1c7b32.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3=10^{3z}-3(\frac{10^{3z}}{2})+30(\frac{10^{2z}}{2})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="285" height="39" />.
Combining like terms we obtain the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/3/8e313c082cd6ea9b39272bccf4cf1679c0e8e4bc.png" class="latex" alt="$x^3+y^3= -(\frac{10^{3z}}{2})+30(\frac{10^{2z}}{2})$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="235" height="39" />.
</p><p>Now we know the coefficients of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/d/64d34c6e0c8eea8a83a27849620144ccfb510217.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{3z}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="15" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/b/c0b059f7296138fb7b425b4e922ac4b4a1ac0563.png" class="latex" alt="$10^{2z}$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="31" height="15" /> which are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/8/5/c853bfb15f8cf5a622c714ab9586566175ef5a90.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{-1}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/c/49c7fb47400f9ab9f4fd8d0bf61d3ff8d0187dcd.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{30}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="20" height="37" /> respectively. Adding the two coefficients we obtain an answer of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/3/a738f6ffebd844e31f10ce01dd67ebcb273b2182.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{29}{2}.$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="25" height="37" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/e/f2e33ab9a00e021af49e888212645d4667903c49.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="26" height="24" /> (Author: David Camacho)
</p>
