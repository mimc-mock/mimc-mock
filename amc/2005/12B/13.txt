<p>Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb87a3665fb59dbcd9596b0df4f43b402970a721.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{x_1}=5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c67cbcfbe58d11956c67bc0409ec1404e702a37.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{x_2}=6$" width="56" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5a02485fa767421348ba260e4388a1825019309.png" class="latex" alt="$6^{x_3}=7$" width="57" height="12" />, ... , <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/3/0937b07c3e346a9cb8d73cd3fa8a2f0109301c4d.png" class="latex" alt="$127^{x_{124}}=128$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" />.  What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/2/cf2fbbd3e4296a47e29abdf2fb1d8cf2cd06ed0f.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1x_2...x_{124}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="80" height="10" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/e/8/8e8874c769ae99cf93e07c8b6afc67378fbaa0cb.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ {{{2}}} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ {{{\frac{5}{2}}}} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ {{{3}}} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ {{{\frac{7}{2}}}} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ {{{4}}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="350" height="37" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We see that we can re-write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb87a3665fb59dbcd9596b0df4f43b402970a721.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{x_1}=5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/6/8c67cbcfbe58d11956c67bc0409ec1404e702a37.png" class="latex" alt="$5^{x_2}=6$" width="56" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/a/f5a02485fa767421348ba260e4388a1825019309.png" class="latex" alt="$6^{x_3}=7$" width="57" height="12" />, ... , <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/3/0937b07c3e346a9cb8d73cd3fa8a2f0109301c4d.png" class="latex" alt="$127^{x_{124}}=128$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="13" /> as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/1/8211404ef93061e622f2026ab7783b588da2e6b2.png" class="latex" alt="$\left(...\left(\left(\left(4^{x_1}\right)^{x_2}\right)^{x_3}\right)...\right)^{x_{124}}=128$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="227" height="23" /> by using substitution. By using the properties of exponents, we know that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/2/d/82d09ece3b46ba4ac4513a85582f5dc413f1d789.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{x_1x_2...x_{124}}=128$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="126" height="13" />. 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/1/6217361ba4a641e2137eeb3ace79ebe00fbc44ed.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{x_1x_2...x_{124}}=128\\2^{2x_1x_2...x_{124}}=2^7\\2x_1x_2...x_{124}=7\\x_1x_2...x_{124}=\dfrac{7}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="126" height="93" />
</p><p>Therefore, the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/6/6867cb238b3ec0641401f5e3e3f9fc1a0ecba186.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\,\dfrac{7}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="56" height="48" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternate_Solution">Alternate Solution</span></h2>
<p>Changing <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/b/8/eb87a3665fb59dbcd9596b0df4f43b402970a721.png" class="latex" alt="$4^{x_1}=5$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="56" height="13" /> to logarithmic form, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/e/e/1eec5d2eadc185970150d658bf20e82dda1d2be5.png" class="latex" alt="${x_1}=\log_{4}{5}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="84" height="17" />.  We can rewrite this as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/7/437ffe69e194483a8d48e92317e50d1e99276f6a.png" class="latex" alt="${x_1}=\dfrac{\log{5}}{\log{4}}$" style="vertical-align: -16px" width="80" height="41" />.  Applying this to the rest, we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/9/e594eea84a16caef95d12cae278d40a5d91d0c52.png" class="latex" alt="$x_1x_2...x_{124}=\dfrac{\log5}{\log4}\cdot\dfrac{\log6}{\log5}\cdot...\cdot\dfrac{\log128}{\log127}=\dfrac{\log5\cdot\log6\cdot...\cdot\log128}{\log4\cdot\log5\cdot...\cdot\log127}=\dfrac{\log128}{\log4}=\log_{4}{128}=\log_{4}{2^7}=7\cdot\log_{4}{2}=7\cdot\dfrac{1}{2}=\boxed{\mathrm{(D)}\,\dfrac{7}{2}}$" style="vertical-align: -18px" width="976" height="48" />
</p>
