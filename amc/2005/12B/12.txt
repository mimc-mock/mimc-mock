<p>The <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_equation" title="Quadratic equation">quadratic equation</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/8/458b4d1201902828abe8b79853ca966928b6c4f1.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+mx+n$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="99" height="16" /> has roots twice those of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/a/7/6a7ca78795861fe42b37d1528e83bd86840b3b51.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2+px+m$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="97" height="18" />, and none of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/c/e/2ceed1f5c723a6e397dd8aa9fb1cfb894e1f6a91.png" class="latex" alt="$m,n,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="39" height="11" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/f/36f73fc1312ee0349b3f3a0f3bd9eb5504339011.png" class="latex" alt="$p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="10" height="11" /> is zero. What is the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/a/b/2ab7f0a5544f40bcbf65297ce862fede3548ddde.png" class="latex" alt="$n/p$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="29" height="18" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/3/ad3799d69d56dd51f832e7a629a2ead7098ac3e3.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A) }\ {{{1}}} \qquad \textbf{(B) }\ {{{2}}} \qquad \textbf{(C) }\ {{{4}}} \qquad \textbf{(D) }\ {{{8}}} \qquad \textbf{(E) }\ {{{16}}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="392" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f705b7c6db594911e9e4a3d3f8fbee1a2abfea.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + px + m = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="18" /> have roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />. Then 
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/3/d6347a5ee25d5b941694ca5cd21c35997e6d31f8.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + px + m = (x-a)(x-b) = x^2 - (a+b)x + ab,\]" width="404" height="19" />
</p><p>so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/1/b/51b634644112024b7d7ff198b4283a6bce1b7e2a.png" class="latex" alt="$p = -(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="101" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/b/d/bbdcf19d4801d74203e8f7aeeebca67c8930e409.png" class="latex" alt="$m = ab$" width="57" height="12" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/c/cdca714c46067aa5f072d52a51c25ad36821b437.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + mx + n = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="132" height="16" /> has roots <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" />, so
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/1/501f7835ffc0da1ceef39eaa3eebb8784db663d4.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2 + mx + n = (x-2a)(x-2b) = x^2 - 2(a+b)x + 4ab,\]" width="442" height="19" />
</p><p>and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/f/6/0f628175c3874d8ac94be8d194d42df54e24d00f.png" class="latex" alt="$m = -2(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="116" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/9/8/c9863bb93d6abee66cbcdb7690b3a4a1b0d8fd5c.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 4ab$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. Thus <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/0/d1097f71fde4cc5f2d89b9b10716987be29af40e.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{p} = \frac{4ab}{-(a+b)} = \frac{4m}{\frac{m}{2}} = \boxed{\textbf{(D) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -20px" width="247" height="46" />. 
</p><p>Indeed, consider the quadratics <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/8/0b8c0a10b2f46e658cb8afe23b66964e770322a2.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + 8x + 16 = 0,\ x^2 + 16x + 64 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="288" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>If the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/d/c/cdca714c46067aa5f072d52a51c25ad36821b437.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + mx + n = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="132" height="16" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/c/6/6c6ea4756efa52a156029529aba37a305a5cb496.png" class="latex" alt="$2a$" width="18" height="12" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/b/a9b30259d95f64f9aa7c6bd36e1583618a23c8bd.png" class="latex" alt="$2b$" width="17" height="12" /> and the roots of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/7/f/07f705b7c6db594911e9e4a3d3f8fbee1a2abfea.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2 + px + m = 0$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="130" height="18" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" />, then using Vieta's formulas,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/8/e/08ecb4147711eacc9e46c3025ecf06f134ac14d1.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2a + 2b = -m\]" width="111" height="14" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/b/02b64c3d1b6523bbb51defb309c6d0549b692b29.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a + b = -p\]" width="86" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/6/c/86c586dee72aa6367bb10d8204969336903bd179.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2a(2b) = n\]" width="84" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/1/4/d14005e53d35d168169a64841d4ceef3b3b42600.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[a(b) = m\]" width="71" height="18" />
Therefore, substituting the second equation into the first equation gives 
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/e/b1ee6cc0acfd106f4f1d26965b77854e3878ea4f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[m = 2(p)\]" width="70" height="18" />
and substituting the fourth equation into the third equation gives
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/b/0bbe2b999bd85bf79835ac644e6dfb2a4587e4d9.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[n = 4(m)\]" width="72" height="18" />
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/f/d0ffba17d89c1ecc0c971fc29fa177f13e976b93.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 8p$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="53" height="16" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/4/3/c4385c14fb274dd0de9cc30bcab312ae31fddfbc.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{n}{p}= \boxed{\textbf{(D) }8}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="97" height="38" />
</p>

</p><p>~ pi_is_3.14
