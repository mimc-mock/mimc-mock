<p>A sequence of complex numbers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/a/39a8c0aeb171ad59cce08ed7ec87d9ba4a83ac6d.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{0}, z_{1}, z_{2}, ...$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="85" height="11" /> is defined by the rule
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/6/1/5615d4f35b70105ed92f07cc305ec89d9a9cdd96.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_{n+1} = \frac {iz_{n}}{\overline {z_{n}}},\]" width="89" height="40" />
</p><p>where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/b/09b0ed391780d182af28b7662c9fed45ce0808b0.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline {z_{n}}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="17" height="13" /> is the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Complex_conjugate" title="Complex conjugate">complex conjugate</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/0/b/c0baa2dc2cf1cd6832376885a2def3e5928e6de1.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{n}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/5/555ad558a9227e54bbf0c1131c9ea47067c9a4e2.png" class="latex" alt="$i^{2}=-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="15" />. Suppose that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/8/3/483a752659c02d1e403e6fcfd03f55447b777200.png" class="latex" alt="$|z_{0}|=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/3/263089c9711dbebb1ce43af336912bc3ef8fe935.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{2005}=1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="14" />. How many possible values are there for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/e/bde52de6af2e23d5c5c091813dfc5e88ace777df.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{0}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/7/9b71ef151728b55f97d15de0c779596e00f83cac.png" class="latex" alt="$\textbf{(A)}\ 1 \qquad  \textbf{(B)}\ 2 \qquad  \textbf{(C)}\ 4 \qquad  \textbf{(D)}\ 2005 \qquad  \textbf{(E)}\ 2^{2005}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="406" height="21" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h2>
<p>Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/b/8/9b82758bbd1e38da673baa1ebf0fdb7c673eb5cc.png" class="latex" alt="$|z_0|=1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="58" height="18" />, let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/4/1f4db771e11bf027671881792a955fba88ce152d.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0=e^{i\theta_0}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="64" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62df86ba6c84c9e8a5008f0ad6bbefa5d9cf301f.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="15" /> is an <a href="/wiki/index.php/Argument" title="Argument">argument</a> of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />. 
We will prove by induction that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/b/5/cb542fa13fb8ca9c4920f51f93baa541168e1757.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n=e^{i\theta_n}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="68" height="18" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/b/b/4bb405f0a0686ab2ba470e26feda2c9550c183d7.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta_n=2^n(\theta_0+\frac{\pi}{2})-\frac{\pi}{2}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="163" height="33" />.
</p><p>Base Case: trivial
</p><p>Inductive Step: Suppose the formula is correct for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f756c0f47c2c000ff11cc29f13381232ff401aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" />, then
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/1/e/01e74ac69a249e45f90adf147c3ebb85f437278e.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_{k+1}=\frac{iz_k}{\overline {z_k}}=i e^{i\theta_k} e^{i\theta_k}=e^{i(2\theta_k+\pi/2)}\]" width="268" height="40" />
Since
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/5/7/457e3e14aad4f7928516575089f38a0e5ad927e6.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2\theta_k+\frac{\pi}{2}=2\cdot 2^n(\theta_0+\frac{\pi}{2})-\pi+\frac{\pi}{2}=2^{n+1}(\theta_0+\frac{\pi}{2})-\frac{\pi}{2}=\theta_{n+1}\]" width="477" height="32" />
the formula is proven
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/9/5/b95b4aa27b6cf34650e1d3b48d5694f9d69748dc.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{2005}=1\Rightarrow \theta_{2005}=2k\pi$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="187" height="15" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> is an integer. Therefore,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/4/d/b4d614845645002f03bb5918558e8cb03b2e28fe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[2^{2005}(\theta_0+\frac{\pi}{2})=(2k+\frac{1}{2})\pi\]" width="206" height="37" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/8/c/b8c7716ce947fab700ffffc659af41384da8058f.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[\theta_0=\frac{k}{2^{2004}}\pi+\left(\frac{1}{2^{2006}}-\frac{1}{2}\right)\pi\]" width="229" height="43" />
The value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/d/62df86ba6c84c9e8a5008f0ad6bbefa5d9cf301f.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="15" /> only matters <a href="/wiki/index.php/Modulo" class="mw-redirect" title="Modulo">modulo</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/9/709acef3551b886e6754de72d59a88f00660f0d3.png" class="latex" alt="$2\pi$" width="19" height="12" />. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/b/3/8b3724d0ef3ca87aa53f29e523c6d9ef5aa63b94.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{k+2^{2005}}{2^{2004}}\pi\equiv\frac{k}{2^{2004}}\pi\mod 2\pi$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="232" height="39" />, k can take values from 0 to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/8/768ede26cd4e5dc6139c1123ea1d03bcda0c513a.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="66" height="15" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/4/d041a818985c30caeef7e41cbeae47b5301049b8.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="88" height="24" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h2>
<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/7/a77f82e4c2d2e3d0e134dfa7b5b92c38f5c79f99.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0 = \cos \theta + i\sin \theta$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="142" height="15" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/4/f/64fb82c88de3f4cf3be269d8897e0ff7783dff54.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_1 = \frac {iz_{0}}{\overline {z_{0}}} = \frac{i(\cos \theta + i\sin \theta)}{\cos \theta - i\sin \theta} = i(\cos \theta + i\sin \theta)^2 = i(\cos 2\theta + i\sin 2\theta) = ie^{i2\theta}\]" width="576" height="41" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/b/f3bbedd4ff0606468250bb3c505467ce03ba30fe.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_2 = \frac {iz_{1}}{\overline {z_{1}}} = \frac{i(\cos 2\theta + i\sin 2\theta)}{\cos 2\theta - i\sin 2\theta} = i(\cos 2\theta + i\sin 2\theta)^{2} = i(\cos 2^2\theta + i\sin 2^2\theta) = ie^{i2^2\theta}\]" width="634" height="41" />
Repeating through this recursive process, we can quickly see that
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/4/2/142c16aecb2b1139ba7294b780a11fc9ddade6ff.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_{2005} = ie^{i2^{2005}\theta} = i(\cos 2^{2005}\theta + i\sin 2^{2005}\theta) = 1\]" width="368" height="23" />
Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/a/6/da6779b3a03972441bb044195cc6d01fd51d44d6.png" class="latex" alt="$\sin 2^{2005}\theta = -1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="117" height="15" />. The solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/2/e/52e8ed7a3ba22130ad3984eb2cd413406475a689.png" class="latex" alt="$\theta$" width="8" height="13" /> are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/2/a725e98f69734d8e380e5982cbc9a7b883219de9.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{\frac{3\pi}{2}+2\pi k} {2^{2005}}$" style="vertical-align: -12px" width="73" height="40" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/d/b0d6d4d2eb0b54823ee0e1f1607d9678ecbb0e8d.png" class="latex" alt="$k = 0,1,2...(2^{2005}-1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="173" height="19" />. Note that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/a/0/aa0a536d1ab17f19aef7e721e75f60139feb11ea.png" class="latex" alt="$\cos 2^{2005}\theta = 0$" width="105" height="15" /> for all <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" />, so the answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/0/4/d041a818985c30caeef7e41cbeae47b5301049b8.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="88" height="24" />. (Author: Patrick Yin)
</p><p>Quick note: the solution forgot the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/8/34857b3ba74ce5cd8607f3ebd23e9015908ada71.png" class="latex" alt="$i$" width="5" height="12" /> in front of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/e/a/9ea31e5c5613f025b5be8aca425d318168491d89.png" class="latex" alt="$z_1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" /> when deriving <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/9/5/29535bc04682b0b477c682ee52dded5983bc41cb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />, so the solution is inaccurate.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h2>
<p>Note that for any complex number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/3/b13f21416d84e13708696f34dea81026cda583c9.png" class="latex" alt="$z$" width="8" height="8" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/5/6/556f55f18c1edb29ca98dd48b492f5115fcb31ca.png" class="latex" alt="$|z|=|\overline z|$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="61" height="18" />. Therefore, the magnitude of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/c/d/5cda19c5b60bd0ad1b0578c72b47af2cff018c06.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{iz_n}{|z_n|}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="30" height="42" /> is always <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />, meaning that all of the numbers in the sequence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f756c0f47c2c000ff11cc29f13381232ff401aeb.png" class="latex" alt="$z_k$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="15" height="10" /> are of magnitude <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />.
</p><p>Another property of complex numbers is that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/a/99a35df9a67c79e44631b57c51c42d84d98ce57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z\overline z=|z|^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="68" height="19" />. For the numbers in our sequence, this means <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/2/8f24175c6995bea2b36ce1edaa88361321f09f72.png" class="latex" alt="$z\overline z=1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="51" height="12" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/9/f/39fb46ac54f3bb997b340d88b9c1f680666b205c.png" class="latex" alt="$\overline z=z^{-1}$" width="59" height="15" />. Rewriting our recursive condition with these facts, we now have
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d8fbf2d5bdf5f2b1df010b1647bfe512d995671.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_{n+1}=\frac{iz_n}{z_n^{-1}}=iz_n^2.\]" width="140" height="42" />
Solving for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6046b5fea971a2f95348ba9ece8e46d4b947c57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> here, we obtain
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/2/5/6252ed8b42eb8b882d78931ffe62e1d4873d4638.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[z_n=\frac{\pm\sqrt{z_{n+1}}}i=-i\cdot(\pm\sqrt{z_{n+1}}).\]" width="248" height="38" />
It is seen that there are two values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/4/6046b5fea971a2f95348ba9ece8e46d4b947c57c.png" class="latex" alt="$z_n$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="16" height="10" /> which correspond to one value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/1/d/91d86ea40c9c122208b0547cbe346e20e7c7ace6.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{n+1}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="32" height="12" />. That means that there are two possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/7/4a7f79d3a679a1740df3720bd2cbbd8177f03083.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{2004}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="34" height="10" />, four possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/4/67478fdf267e9518304ae03d56d9527a94d2edca.png" class="latex" alt="$z_{2003}$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="34" height="10" />, and so on. Therefore, there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/e/a/ceaede13e4a7750e7d95af5d1a7c1f71ec944efb.png" class="latex" alt="$2^{2005}$" width="34" height="15" /> possible values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/a/b/bab9abe98679312d2a3308c76188cf2cbc492f68.png" class="latex" alt="$z_0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="14" height="10" />, giving the answer as <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d8b3ab90b0ae54696bec36837a1195343043f50.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\mathrm{(E)}\text{ }2^{2005}}$" style="vertical-align: -10px" width="80" height="31" />.
</p>
