<p>A positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/0/2/302e99c1c7cd91a6ae371139e5142ccef5977dca.png" class="latex" alt="$60$" width="17" height="12" /> divisors and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/9/40905e7046ff302141d715350424107af2ee3d67.png" class="latex" alt="$7n$" width="19" height="12" /> has <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/e/a/fea5fcc6d66685ee21f722efcd228eb44d38f262.png" class="latex" alt="$80$" width="17" height="12" /> divisors.  What is the greatest integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/c/3/8c325612684d41304b9751c175df7bcc0f61f64f.png" class="latex" alt="$k$" width="9" height="12" /> such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/9/c/d9c75b34bd42bc8596daa00e3f6ddd040a47b4ff.png" class="latex" alt="$7^k$" width="16" height="15" /> divides <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/4/174fadd07fd54c9afe288e96558c92e0c1da733a.png" class="latex" alt="$n$" width="10" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/f/e/dfefd79e7487ad48a38f35d46ed92443682844a5.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ {{{0}}} \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ {{{1}}} \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ {{{2}}} \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ {{{3}}} \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ {{{4}}}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>We may let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/c/9/9c9063417e2ec0261aa4fa97a9ce5ea4e1c38df3.png" class="latex" alt="$n = 7^k \cdot m$" width="81" height="15" />, where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" /> is not divisible by 7. Using the fact that the number of divisors function <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/0/5/b05725a6508fe8df761c6e91d2735b697ebd87d9.png" class="latex" alt="$d(n)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="33" height="18" /> is multiplicative, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/7/b/57b681a19173b376fe8da9375d10e8490158b44b.png" class="latex" alt="$d(n) = d(7^k)d(m) = (k+1)d(m) = 60$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="300" height="20" />. Also, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/a/2/7a23779ca7dfe362ae2b35bd86ffdb792398779e.png" class="latex" alt="$d(7n) = d(7^{k+1})d(m) = (k+2)d(m) = 80$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="326" height="20" />. These numbers are in the ratio 3:4, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/6/5065beb39669decdc0d8b17bf7d1fe67f0e4692c.png" class="latex" alt="$\frac{k+1}{k+2} = \frac{3}{4} \implies k = 2 \Rightarrow \boxed{\mathrm{C}}$" style="vertical-align: -14px" width="228" height="39" />.
</p>
