<p>Let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> be two-digit integers such that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> is obtained by reversing the digits of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. The integers <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/2/092e364e1d9d19ad5fffb0b46ef4cc7f2da02c1c.png" class="latex" alt="$y$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="9" height="11" /> satisfy <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/9/bd9b0749e14ec633274a59bad09c03f3a27e0bff.png" class="latex" alt="$x^{2}-y^{2}=m^{2}$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="103" height="18" /> for some positive integer <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/5/0/f5047d1e0cbb50ec208923a22cd517c55100fa7b.png" class="latex" alt="$m$" width="15" height="8" />. What is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/8/7/2871b391f606ea213aedbf61bf8fa230b9185275.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+m$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="80" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/8/d/68d550b49a434668404ce5216c7d5235f2c5aa6c.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)}\ 88    \qquad \mathrm{(B)}\ 112   \qquad \mathrm{(C)}\ 116   \qquad \mathrm{(D)}\ 144   \qquad \mathrm{(E)}\ 154   \qquad$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="424" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p>let <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/4/78449572731577b2fbbc9e0d64b11005f2a9e1f8.png" class="latex" alt="$x=10a+b$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="92" height="14" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/a/e0a865a2be3ebe9610a3ece120c80347d5b161c4.png" class="latex" alt="$y=10b+a$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="91" height="16" /> where <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/3/8136a7ef6a03334a7246df9097e5bcc31ba33fd2.png" class="latex" alt="$b$" width="8" height="12" /> are nonzero digits.
</p><p>By <a href="/wiki/index.php/Difference_of_squares" title="Difference of squares">difference of squares</a>,
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/8/1b8088e58252db3fe978b651bf66d791f79fb190.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[x^2-y^2=(x+y)(x-y)\]" width="192" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/5/f75db726bece8fe4b6cf1036ff0d80353d418f83.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=(10a+b+10b+a)(10a+b-10b-a)\]" width="320" height="18" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/3/d/43d794211b54da0f1669ac78864b06348267c4fb.png" class="latexcenter" alt="\[=(11(a+b))(9(a-b))\]" width="179" height="18" />
</p><p>For this product to be a square, the factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/6/8/c6878713578626763c38433b3f4c8c2205ad0c15.png" class="latex" alt="$11$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="12" /> must be repeated in either <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/b/e/1be092b2283c00c376e719c5f650a15433d43a28.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2aee761704bb62a779d1bbcb9fb09912bb516f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" />, and given the constraints it has to be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/9/4796b039c76437d4854531dec77460e365129bcc.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)=11$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="95" height="18" />. The factor of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> is already a square and can be ignored. Now <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2aee761704bb62a779d1bbcb9fb09912bb516f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> must be another square, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> cannot be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/c/6/fc606f7f1e530731ab4f1cc364c01dc64a4455ee.png" class="latex" alt="$10$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> or greater then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/2/4f2aee761704bb62a779d1bbcb9fb09912bb516f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a-b)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="52" height="18" /> must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. If <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/7/8/678e8d321bc28e3701dea3719943d09b7ac3555b.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="73" height="13" /> then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/7/d/d7d303726270444ccaa7dda6468e29f180ffbba4.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)+(a-b)=11+4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="203" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/6/dc6c55d8f4e726cd0766ef97fc846854e019058d.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=15$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="13" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/a/15ab853f2f5c89cfa5328b6ed2113d984063b618.png" class="latex" alt="$a=15/2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="69" height="18" />, which is not a digit. Hence the only possible value for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/6/5a697bfdeadf2105048670e6f6c7d4dc6878dc4f.png" class="latex" alt="$a-b$" width="40" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" />. Now we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/6/6/366b555520ae2e14234749fd4398a3d06d6c5acb.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+b)+(a-b)=11+1$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="202" height="18" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/2/8/0282cf4e459c6f04569c6c3a996915c8401bfe9b.png" class="latex" alt="$2a=12$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="60" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/a/5/fa5507ab5a3d1f06941dabd1c26505d646367983.png" class="latex" alt="$a=6$" width="42" height="12" />, then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/f/6/4f69c1ec597d19324140f3130e9d63367f4b69c5.png" class="latex" alt="$b=5$" width="40" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/0/3/503691fd771acc6e596537d92bac9dadb7696a54.png" class="latex" alt="$x=65$" width="52" height="12" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/d/2/ed27dc23eb5b804966050e762b7dbd6628d4efed.png" class="latex" alt="$y=56$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="51" height="16" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/5/7/757562de3e55b66db25d868701e28dfdcf71fbfb.png" class="latex" alt="$m=33$" width="57" height="12" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/c/a6caebb64394712f675a09acf90c06b178888eb1.png" class="latex" alt="$x+y+m=154\Rightarrow\boxed{\mathrm{E}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="184" height="24" />
</p>
