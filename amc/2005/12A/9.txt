<p>There are two values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> for which the equation <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/2/782a7d47e8f237b0150c0a449b6144e2521d2736.png" class="latex" alt="$4x^2 + ax + 8x + 9 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="175" height="16" /> has only one solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. What is the sum of these values of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/a/7/1a73d01dacee48a6f91315c4504f9375b79308ea.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A}) \ -16 \qquad (\mathrm {B}) \ -8 \qquad (\mathrm {C})\ 0 \qquad (\mathrm {D}) \ 8 \qquad (\mathrm {E})\ 20$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="404" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Solution">Video Solution</span></h3>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://youtu.be/3dfbWzOfJAI?t=222">https://youtu.be/3dfbWzOfJAI?t=222</a>
~pi_is_3.14
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/index.php/Quadratic_equation" title="Quadratic equation">quadratic equation</a> always has two roots, unless it has a double root. That means we can write the quadratic as a square, and the coefficients 4 and 9 suggest this. <a href="/wiki/index.php/Completing_the_square" title="Completing the square">Completing the square</a>, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/9/c/49c569048efd381c3a7782e3c40ab200815750c8.png" class="latex" alt="$0 = (2x \pm 3)^2 = 4x^2 \pm 12x + 9$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="238" height="19" />, so <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/f/90f2972f46236c8ddcee36c5ffcd728899c8763d.png" class="latex" alt="$\pm 12 = a + 8 \Longrightarrow a = 4, -20$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="219" height="16" />. The sum of these is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a92cc682c269670d365667578a7eb22b2ef59dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$-20 + 4 = -16 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Another method would be to use the quadratic formula, since our <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/b/76b2878564ab19b80abb21ba964abe90fe120846.png" class="latex" alt="$x^2$" width="16" height="15" /> coefficient is given as 4, the <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" /> coefficient is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/9/f/09fa6605dd2d1c469a7d60f1101607a805f3882b.png" class="latex" alt="$a+8$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="40" height="14" /> and the constant term is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. Hence, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/3/7/93704eea95621c7a54330c5b3b5eb0310cf9de32.png" class="latex" alt="$x = \frac{-(a+8) \pm \sqrt {(a+8)^2-4(4)(9)}}{2(4)}$" style="vertical-align: -17px" width="286" height="45" /> Because we want only a single solution for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />, the determinant must equal 0. Therefore, we can write <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/e/ade250f98817c1f5f4c750a2bbe897e0cf741e21.png" class="latex" alt="$(a+8)^2 - 144 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="144" height="19" /> which factors to <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/f/8/3f848bbaa80b4ad5651460e825fdac317e14ae54.png" class="latex" alt="$a^2 + 16a - 80 = 0$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="140" height="16" />; using <a href="/wiki/index.php/Vieta%27s_formulas" title="Vieta&#39;s formulas">Vieta's formulas</a> we see that the sum of the solutions for <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> is the opposite of the coefficient of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/731bb39bf2d3cd75b6d36fe715742586b69a6bb5.png" class="latex" alt="$-16 \Rightarrow \mathrm{ (A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Using the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Discriminant" title="Discriminant">discriminant</a>, the result must equal <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/c/1/bc1f9d9bf8a1b606a4188b5ce9a2af1809e27a89.png" class="latex" alt="$0$" width="8" height="12" />.
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/0/c/e0ca9ec6a41c35eea966b29ef93a1cac3f4ec56c.png" class="latex" alt="$D = b^2 - 4ac$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="104" height="15" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/3/7/c378eb3b35bbad3915f1f1680101df7e3f8e59ba.png" class="latex" alt="$= (a+8)^2 - 4(4)(9)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="157" height="19" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/0/5/705f72e3619dfb0e5710191b0212bcab66e6cbf6.png" class="latex" alt="$= a^2 + 16a + 64 - 144$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="176" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/9/e/59eddd7e1d93a8e0eea60450b0f46f3ee77b23a9.png" class="latex" alt="$= a^2 + 16a - 80 = 0 \Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="182" height="16" />
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/5/3/153f7cc2df59691506fb856567a5950832c8c9fa.png" class="latex" alt="$(a + 20)(a - 4) = 0$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="152" height="18" />
Therefore, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/d/4/bd4c04e3891a120c9a9d1fa2e33455d70054b025.png" class="latex" alt="$a = -20$" width="65" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/f/9/2f98b3f405113241659d8c8db2ea3480346db4d0.png" class="latex" alt="$a = 4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" />, giving a sum of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/3/1/731bb39bf2d3cd75b6d36fe715742586b69a6bb5.png" class="latex" alt="$-16 \Rightarrow \mathrm{ (A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="86" height="18" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>First, notice that for there to be only <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> root to a quadratic, the quadratic must be a square. Then, notice that the quadratic and linear terms are both squares. Thus, the value of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> must be such that both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/7/1/f715e73e85f95e5638e6f8f40a22d854ced54aa9.png" class="latex" alt="$(2x+3)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/5/41527f69aea88aaeafa362b467b113d3ecfd1fbe.png" class="latex" alt="$(2x-3)^2$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="71" height="19" />. Clearly, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/f/c7ffa3b8792f0170bce1ab6008e907343100d70e.png" class="latex" alt="$a=4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="43" height="12" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/2/f/f2fda6ceb009cfda9fe82a597e0741a79d09a3cd.png" class="latex" alt="$a=-20$" width="65" height="12" />. Hence <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/9/2/a92cc682c269670d365667578a7eb22b2ef59dfa.png" class="latex" alt="$-20 + 4 = -16 \Rightarrow \mathrm{(A)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="174" height="18" />.
</p><p>Solution by franzliszt
</p>
