<p>A faulty car odometer proceeds from digit 3 to digit 5, always skipping the digit 4, regardless of position. If the odometer now reads <tt>002005</tt>, how many miles has the car actually traveled?
<img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/c/d/7cdf4f3f73ea465b4e0ae0e4d69441fa9b2a21ad.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A}) \ 1404 \qquad (\mathrm {B}) \ 1462 \qquad (\mathrm {C})\ 1604 \qquad (\mathrm {D}) \ 1605 \qquad (\mathrm {E})\ 1804$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="478" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solutions">Solutions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_1">Solution 1</span></h3>
<p>We find the number of numbers with a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> and subtract from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5b442c275b4324f100c365b774e872b6b21f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2005$" width="35" height="12" />. Quick counting tells us that there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> numbers with a 4 in the hundreds place, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/f/3/8f37626d49b4082c9102bf930bca51277ff2eb43.png" class="latex" alt="$200$" width="26" height="12" /> numbers with a 4 in the tens place, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/0/3/1038785e77cdf7734f6ee2f8fed6de2ab19acc15.png" class="latex" alt="$201$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="26" height="13" /> numbers with a 4 in the units place (counting <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/0/a/90ae760d1ea10eb7a90d22087b3c7e2393d02117.png" class="latex" alt="$2004$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" />). Now we apply the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Principle_of_Inclusion-Exclusion" title="Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion">Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion</a>. There are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> numbers with a 4 in the hundreds and in the tens, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/3/f8366cd6196dd8a9da6d38a3e9eafb109e99d53e.png" class="latex" alt="$20$" width="17" height="12" /> for both the other two <a href="/wiki/index.php/Intersection" title="Intersection">intersections</a>. The intersection of all three sets is just <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" />. So we get:
</p>
<div style="text-align:center;"><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/a/2/5a216ed52089b61ce91bbe84405a12ad39791de9.png" class="latex" alt="$2005-(200+200+201-20-20-20+2) = 1462 \Longrightarrow \mathrm{(B)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="477" height="18" /></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_2">Solution 2</span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, consider that counting without the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> is almost equivalent to counting in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />; only, in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, the number <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> is not counted. Since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> is skipped, the symbol <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> represents <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> miles of travel, and we have traveled <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/8/c/f8c43547951b56dfd378fe7b48dabb75bf178e79.png" class="latex" alt="$2004_9$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="15" /> miles. By basic conversion, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/6/e/d6ea060a2d1109f602048a7fbab7ef95df4748f0.png" class="latex" alt="$2004_9=9^3(2)+9^0(4)=729(2)+1(4)=1458+4=\boxed{1462}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="463" height="23" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_3">Solution 3</span></h3>
<p>Since any numbers containing one or more <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />s were skipped, we need only to find the numbers that don't contain a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> at all. First we consider <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/0/04003e1a630efc44ec0e7f6e532cf92ad21b6024.png" class="latex" alt="$1999$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />. Single digits are simply four digit numbers with a zero in all but the ones place (this concept applies to double and triple digits numbers as well). From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/c/e/dce34f4dfb2406144304ad0d6106c5382ddd1446.png" class="latex" alt="$1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="8" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/4/0/04003e1a630efc44ec0e7f6e532cf92ad21b6024.png" class="latex" alt="$1999$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" />, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/1/c/41c544263a265ff15498ee45f7392c5f86c6d151.png" class="latex" alt="$2$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities for the thousands place, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" /> possibilities for the hundreds, tens, and ones places. This is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/1/a/b1a73a0e4f763783d2ccdf54724659c9d28c8673.png" class="latex" alt="$2 \cdot 9 \cdot 9 \cdot 9-1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="106" height="13" /> possibilities (because <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/3/4/e34ada25a413c699ddb5f831106d14ab926a7243.png" class="latex" alt="$0000$" width="35" height="12" /> doesn't count) or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/7/7/477e3dcf41ea8406e07974aeb8331a303dfedbcb.png" class="latex" alt="$1457$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="36" height="13" /> numbers. From <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/5/f/05f1ffc8a6829f25b0b0366087a70dfd4e06fef6.png" class="latex" alt="$2000$" width="35" height="12" /> - <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/f/5/9f5b442c275b4324f100c365b774e872b6b21f1e.png" class="latex" alt="$2005$" width="35" height="12" /> there are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/0/1/601a7806cbfad68196c43a4665871f8c3186802e.png" class="latex" alt="$6$" width="8" height="12" /> numbers, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/9/0/79069377f91364c2f87a64e5f9f562a091c8a6c1.png" class="latex" alt="$5$" width="8" height="12" /> of which don't contain a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" />. Therefore the total is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/4/1/3412faa5a13b2a272c3ce6bffdda0b70eeef9774.png" class="latex" alt="$1457 + 5$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="67" height="14" />, or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/7/9/a7946889d88ba7c3d15febd0413b5caa328c6ee8.png" class="latex" alt="$1462$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="35" height="13" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/4/3/843622567e12d686e4e2f94ddfcb444e8ecde0d2.png" class="latex" alt="$\Rightarrow$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="10" /> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/0/d/f0d5b8fdfd2bd98c8be0bad569663660c33f3438.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{\text{B}}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="23" height="23" />.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_4">Solution 4</span></h3>
<p>We seek to find the amount of numbers that contain at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/0/5/4054552638cf9f29e857a1306bd3ec31df3c19b6.png" class="latex" alt="$4,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="13" height="15" /> and subtract this number from <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/f/1/1f11029a8949ae25d6bf047c67be867ddb6180f5.png" class="latex" alt="$2005.$" width="39" height="12" /> 
</p><p>We can simply apply casework to this problem.
</p><p>The amount of numbers with at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> that are one or two digit numbers are <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/6/b/2/6b2a441301885cddf8fd20d7e8b18fe12f0102d2.png" class="latex" alt="$4,14,24,34,40-49,54,\cdots,94$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="242" height="16" /> which gives <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/f/b/efbfe273946ff9f971c5eab331ca39caa6fdf7f4.png" class="latex" alt="$19$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="17" height="13" /> numbers.
</p><p>The amount of three digit numbers with at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/a/f9a071b6d4e258257a78221cc20caddac8166e1a.png" class="latex" alt="$8*19+100=252.$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="148" height="14" /> 
</p><p>The amount of four digit numbers with at least one <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/c/c7cab1a05e1e0c1d51a6a219d96577a16b7abf9d.png" class="latex" alt="$4$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="9" height="12" /> is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/6/2/7626803121e9c8e215b21b668eadba6e9ea17a4c.png" class="latex" alt="$252+1+19=272$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="149" height="14" />
</p><p>This, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/6/db6d8567ffecb53faad8551f84eb7d3c34bad76c.png" class="latex" alt="$2005-19-252-272=1462,$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="240" height="16" /> or <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/0/c/a0cdd2d628e533bf874621ae1b229985f8e2996d.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed{B}.$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="29" height="24" />
</p><p>~coolmath2017
</p>
<h3><span id="Solution_5(Super_fast)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution_5.28Super_fast.29">Solution 5(Super fast)</span></h3>
<p>This is very analogous to base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. But, in base <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />, we don't have a <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/2/bf2c9074b396e3af0dea52d792660eea1c77f10f.png" class="latex" alt="$9$" width="8" height="12" />. So, this means that these are equal except for that base 9 will be one more than the operation here. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/d/2/4d204d44012cb5a2d3e3cc4bd8c389f6766a05d4.png" class="latex" alt="$2005_9 = 9+0+0+1458 = 1463$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="261" height="17" />. <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/b/f/9/bf992dd44a1231dd1ed77cbd4d7eb000e803d1dc.png" class="latex" alt="$1463 - 1 = 1462$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="130" height="15" />
</p><p>Therefore, our answer is <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/8/4/784d396e40d23c4456b7eb5feae0463753e20690.png" class="latex" alt="$\boxed {1462}$" style="vertical-align: -6px" width="49" height="25" />
</p><p>~Arcticturn
</p>
