<p>How many ordered triples of <a href="/wiki/index.php/Integer" title="Integer">integers</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/3/d/f3d9e22097e20a1249b5332202b24941a4bf9e1f.png" class="latex" alt="$(a,b,c)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="54" height="18" />, with <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad2cd546ffbb52fe8a7f8e4d1cb3ec38047627e0.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" />, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/c/c/4cca117c11e298720e03826725b6d317e21ef64f.png" class="latex" alt="$b\ge 1$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="15" />, and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/5/6/e566834db376ac33941a38556b9eaa9c03580d7b.png" class="latex" alt="$c \ge 0$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="15" />, satisfy both <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/8/f98ca0255041d279a45b055e80cd8dabe8e87c62.png" class="latex" alt="$\log_a b = c^{2005}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="100" height="19" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/7/5/1752f0800b74f9fa5f314f6b0458c28d9f72b786.png" class="latex" alt="$a + b + c = 2005$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="129" height="14" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/6/5/a658cdbc840d9f28f81b4efa23eacc5b0b865f5b.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(A)} \ 0 \qquad \mathrm{(B)} \ 1 \qquad \mathrm{(C)} \ 2 \qquad \mathrm{(D)} \ 3 \qquad \mathrm{(E)} \ 4$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="342" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/e/8aee18467dee74ba653d43b3f8edf561f6692bb5.png" class="latex" alt="$a^{c^{2005}} = b$" width="72" height="18" />
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Casework" title="Casework">Casework</a> upon <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/3/3/7/3372c1cb6d68cf97c2d231acc0b47b95a9ed04cc.png" class="latex" alt="$c$" width="8" height="8" />:
</p>
<ul><li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/d/b/4/db496a2fade0867e2dedc83ca9cf6262560559a1.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 0$" width="40" height="12" />: Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/9/f/99fab2bc419928deb9a3e33867a7d259fb4382c6.png" class="latex" alt="$a^0 = b \Longrightarrow b = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="128" height="15" />. Thus we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/d/8/0d819584e4f799c65fd66748bfc018a190b2b2a7.png" class="latex" alt="$(2004,1,0)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="83" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/5/3/5/5351998230e216d9d0f2884c159326cfe6622263.png" class="latex" alt="$c = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="40" height="12" />: Then <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/c/e/1ce38c5747d8bc0f9b2248addba8d67d3f8cf307.png" class="latex" alt="$a^1 = b \Longrightarrow a = b$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="130" height="15" />. Thus we get <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/4/a/3/4a3d9502f1fb35e36b9172e44525fdd4723a2af4.png" class="latex" alt="$(1002,1002,1)$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="110" height="18" />.</li>
<li><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/e/6/c/e6c519570f5d0a43e91e6548d78f173edfb2d5b0.png" class="latex" alt="$c \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="40" height="14" />: Then the exponent of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/7/d/c7d457e388298246adb06c587bccd419ea67f7e8.png" class="latex" alt="$a$" width="9" height="8" /> becomes huge, and since <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/a/d/2/ad2cd546ffbb52fe8a7f8e4d1cb3ec38047627e0.png" class="latex" alt="$a \ge 2$" style="vertical-align: -2px" width="42" height="14" /> there is no way we can satisfy the second condition. Hence we have two ordered triples <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/4/8/2484f88154fadcf9c781bba19becdae88696b77f.png" class="latex" alt="$\mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="25" height="18" />.</li></ul>
