<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/Square" title="Square">Square</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> is inside the square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> so that each side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> can be extended to pass through a vertex of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />. Square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" /> has side length <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/c/f/1/cf1ef7fc51a23e91f950d353b999d30c957268e1.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt {50}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" /> and <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/7/e/d/7edf3afba191eb9958ab78c986da35e33e1681b3.png" class="latex" alt="$BE = 1$" style="vertical-align: 0px" width="61" height="13" />. What is the area of the inner square <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" />?
</p><p><img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/1/0/810dfcecc71022276e274ca22ebc746a5c2e7fcb.png" class="latex" alt="$(\mathrm {A}) \ 25 \qquad (\mathrm {B}) \ 32 \qquad (\mathrm {C})\ 36 \qquad (\mathrm {D}) \ 40 \qquad (\mathrm {E})\ 42$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="387" height="18" />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solution">Solution</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2005_12A_AMC-7b.png" class="image"><img alt="2005 12A AMC-7b.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//f/f8/2005_12A_AMC-7b.png" width="366" height="362" /></a>
</p><p>Arguable the hardest part of this question is to visualize the diagram. Since each side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> can be extended to pass through a vertex of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/f/9/e/f9efaf9474c5658c4089523e2aff4e11488f8603.png" class="latex" alt="$ABCD$" width="57" height="13" />, we realize that <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> must be tilted in such a fashion. Let a side of <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/9/7/3/9735b37ac43861ef9e105bff1711f117917b09ec.png" class="latex" alt="$EFGH$" width="59" height="12" /> be <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/6/e/26eeb5258ca5099acf8fe96b2a1049c48c89a5e6.png" class="latex" alt="$x$" width="10" height="8" />. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/index.php/File:2005_12A_AMC-7.png" class="image"><img alt="2005 12A AMC-7.png" src="https://wiki-images.artofproblemsolving.com//e/ec/2005_12A_AMC-7.png" width="419" height="402" /></a>
</p><p>Notice the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Right_triangle" title="Right triangle">right triangle</a> (in blue) with legs <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/0/b/2/0b2ef0d728e55041038290fd992ffb8da668b2ea.png" class="latex" alt="$1, x+1$" style="vertical-align: -3px" width="58" height="15" /> and <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hypotenuse" title="Hypotenuse">hypotenuse</a> <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/8/a/6/8a6d1b584a42c88275d2ae86a59079aad25c034f.png" class="latex" alt="$\sqrt{50}$" style="vertical-align: -1px" width="33" height="18" />. By the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Pythagorean_Theorem" title="Pythagorean Theorem">Pythagorean Theorem</a>, we have <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/1/1/e/11e20007dad331a20146f0f381d71966cfc600d7.png" class="latex" alt="$1^2 + (x+1)^2 = (\sqrt{50})^2 \Longrightarrow (x+1)^2 = 49 \Longrightarrow x = 6$" style="vertical-align: -4px" width="409" height="21" />. Thus, <img src="//latex.artofproblemsolving.com/2/d/3/2d30f9bca378f43e18618306b3247b27128bf96f.png" class="latex" alt="$[EFGH] = x^2 = 36\ \mathrm{(C)}$" style="vertical-align: -5px" width="185" height="20" />
</p>
